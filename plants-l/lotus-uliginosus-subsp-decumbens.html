Lotus uliginosus subsp. decumbens

Lotus uliginosus subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the swamp lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom above water surfaces.

The plant has floating leaves that are broad and rounded, often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its roots system is adapted to thrive in muddy or aquatic conditions, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

The subspecies decumbens is distinguished by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems.
