Lobelia origenes

Lobelia origenes, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia origenes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a nectar source for pollinators.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
