Leonotis caribea

Leonotis caribea, commonly known as the Caribbean lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
