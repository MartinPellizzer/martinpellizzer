Lantana tiliifolia f. albiflora

Lantana tiliifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower lantana, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
