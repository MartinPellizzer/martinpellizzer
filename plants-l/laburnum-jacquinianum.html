Laburnum jacquinianum

Laburnum jacquinianum, commonly known as the yellow chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The seeds are enclosed in long, narrow pods that split open to release the seeds when mature.
