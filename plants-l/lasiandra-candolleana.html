Lasiandra candolleana

Lasiandra candolleana, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Lasiandra candolleana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
