Lophoschoenus elongatus

Lophoschoenus elongatus, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important species in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Lophoschoenus elongatus is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
