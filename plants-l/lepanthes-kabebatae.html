Lepanthes kabebatae

Lepanthes kabebatae, commonly known as the Kabebatae orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the humid environment.

Lepanthes kabebatae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
