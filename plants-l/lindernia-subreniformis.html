Lindernia subreniformis

Lindernia subreniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the leaf axils.

Lindernia subreniformis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
