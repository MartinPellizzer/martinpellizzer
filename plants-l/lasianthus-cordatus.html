Lasianthus cordatus

Lasianthus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf or heart-shaped plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
