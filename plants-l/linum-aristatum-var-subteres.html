Linum aristatum var. subteres

Linum aristatum var. subteres, commonly known as the slender flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as flax. This particular variety is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are similar to other flax species, but its unique growth habit sets it apart.
