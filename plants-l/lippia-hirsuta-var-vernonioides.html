Lippia hirsuta var. vernonioides

Lippia hirsuta var. vernonioides, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Verbenaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like verbena and lantana.
