Lycopodioides rossii

Lycopodioides rossii, commonly known as the Ross's clubmoss, is a rare and ancient vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves, which are typical of the lycophyte group. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or rocky crevices.
