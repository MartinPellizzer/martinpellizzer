Lepanthes bifida

Lepanthes bifida, commonly known as the Split-leaf Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elliptical and often have a distinctive split or notched appearance.

Lepanthes bifida thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
