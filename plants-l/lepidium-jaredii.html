Lepidium jaredii

Lepidium jaredii, commonly known as the Utah cress, is a small flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Lepidium jaredii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
