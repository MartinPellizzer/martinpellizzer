Lophothele

Lophothele, commonly known as the lacecap lichen, is a type of crustose lichen that forms a thin, flat growth on rocks or tree bark.

It is characterized by its intricate, web-like patterns of fungal hyphae that create a distinctive lace-like appearance. The lichen is composed of a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a cyanobacterium or green alga, which provides nutrients through photosynthesis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for air quality and ecosystem health.
