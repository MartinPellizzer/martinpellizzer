Lupinus lyallii var. villosus

Lupinus lyallii var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like, violet-blue flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typically compound with oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

As a member of the legume family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
