Lupinus semperflorens var. peruensis

Lupinus semperflorens var. peruensis, commonly known as the Peruvian everlasting lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers that remain open for extended periods, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
