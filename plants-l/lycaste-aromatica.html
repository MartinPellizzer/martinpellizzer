Lycaste aromatica

Lycaste aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant lycaste, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture.

The orchid's unique scent is a notable feature, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
