Leucadendron oleifolium

Leucadendron oleifolium, commonly known as the yellow star flower, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
