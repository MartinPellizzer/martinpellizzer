Linnaea dipelta

Linnaea dipelta, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pairs of opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in pairs on the ends of slender stems. Linnaea dipelta thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
