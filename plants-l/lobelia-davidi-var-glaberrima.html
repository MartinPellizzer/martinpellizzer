Lobelia davidi var. glaberrima

Lobelia davidi var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
