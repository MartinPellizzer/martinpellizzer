Lychnis gracilis

Lychnis gracilis, commonly known as the slender campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the top of the plant.

Lychnis gracilis is often found in moist soils and is known for its delicate, herbaceous appearance.
