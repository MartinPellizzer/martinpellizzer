Lygistum paniculatum

Lygistum paniculatum, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, reddish-pink leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Lygistum paniculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
