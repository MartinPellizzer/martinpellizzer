Limonium recurvum var. recurviforme

Limonium recurvum var. recurviforme, commonly known as the recurved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the shore. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscape designs.
