Laurentia bivonae

Laurentia bivonae, commonly known as the Bivona laurel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other well-known plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and oval in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
