Lactuca quercina var. chaixii

Lactuca quercina var. chaixii, commonly known as the oakleaf lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species characterized by its distinctive, oak-like leaf structure.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste, making it a unique addition to salads and culinary dishes. The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with its lobed leaves adding visual interest to garden landscapes.

It is generally easy to cultivate and can be grown in both indoor and outdoor settings.
