Liparis pilifera

Liparis pilifera, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil.

Liparis pilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
