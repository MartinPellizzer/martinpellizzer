Laurentia secunda f. erecta

Laurentia secunda f. erecta, commonly known as the upright form of the common laurentia, is a variant of the species Laurentia secunda.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, which distinguishes it from the more sprawling form of the species. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
