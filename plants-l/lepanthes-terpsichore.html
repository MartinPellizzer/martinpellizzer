Lepanthes terpsichore

Lepanthes terpsichore, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
