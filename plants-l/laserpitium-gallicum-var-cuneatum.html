Laserpitium gallicum var. cuneatum

Laserpitium gallicum var. cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
