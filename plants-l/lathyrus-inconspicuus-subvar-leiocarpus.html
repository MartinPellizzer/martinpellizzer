Lathyrus inconspicuus subvar. leiocarpus

Lathyrus inconspicuus subvar. leiocarpus, commonly known as the silky bower vine, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The flowers are small, pale blue to purple, and appear in clusters along the stems. The plant produces smooth, pod-like fruits that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
