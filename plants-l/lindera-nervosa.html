Lindera nervosa

Lindera nervosa, commonly known as narrowleaf bladdernut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Lindera nervosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
