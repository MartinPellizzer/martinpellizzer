Lobelia chamaepitys

Lobelia chamaepitys, commonly known as the creeping lobelia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with thin, upright stems and hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
