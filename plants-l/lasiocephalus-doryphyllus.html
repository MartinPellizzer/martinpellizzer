Lasiocephalus doryphyllus

Lasiocephalus doryphyllus, commonly known as the silky-leaf doryphyll, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among horticulturists.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
