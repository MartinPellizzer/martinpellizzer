Lepidium minutiflorum

Lepidium minutiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal and alpine environments.

Lepidium minutiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in challenging garden conditions.
