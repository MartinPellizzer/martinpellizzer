Lepidium procumbens

Lepidium procumbens, commonly known as peppergrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It features small, green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Peppergrass is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used in traditional herbal medicine.
