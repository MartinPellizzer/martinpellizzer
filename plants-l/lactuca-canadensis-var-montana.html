Lactuca canadensis var. montana

Lactuca canadensis var. montana, commonly known as mountain lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, pale green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
