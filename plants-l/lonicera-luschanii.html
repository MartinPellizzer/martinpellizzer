Lonicera luschanii

Lonicera luschanii, commonly known as the Luschan's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Lonicera luschanii typically grows in rocky or dry soil conditions and is often found in alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
