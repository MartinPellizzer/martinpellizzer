Ligustrum sinense var. stauntonii

Ligustrum sinense var. stauntonii, commonly known as Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by black, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native plants.
