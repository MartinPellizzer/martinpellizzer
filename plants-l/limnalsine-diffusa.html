Limnalsine diffusa

Limnalsine diffusa, commonly known as the wandering water lily, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers.

The plant's leaves are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of white and pink, open in the early morning and close by midday.
