Leucocroton stenophyllus

Leucocroton stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf leucocroton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
