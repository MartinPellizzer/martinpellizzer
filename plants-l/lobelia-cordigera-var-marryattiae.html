Lobelia cordigera var. marryattiae

Lobelia cordigera var. marryattiae, commonly known as the Maryatt's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
