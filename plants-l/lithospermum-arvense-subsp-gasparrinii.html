Lithospermum arvense subsp. gasparrinii

Lithospermum arvense subsp.

gasparrinii, commonly known as Gasparin's field lousewort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, producing a distinctive, somewhat ornamental appearance.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
