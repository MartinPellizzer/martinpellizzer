Leucas sexdentata

Leucas sexdentata, commonly known as the toothed leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinct tooth-like margins. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, adapting well to arid conditions.
