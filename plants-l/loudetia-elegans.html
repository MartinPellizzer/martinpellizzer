Loudetia elegans

Loudetia elegans, commonly known as the slender grass or velvet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy surface.

Loudetia elegans is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.
