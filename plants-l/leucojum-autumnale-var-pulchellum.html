Leucojum autumnale var. pulchellum

Leucojum autumnale var. pulchellum, commonly known as the White Squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features strap-like, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year, and it produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late summer to early autumn. The plant is characterized by its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland or rock garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to the flora of temperate regions.
