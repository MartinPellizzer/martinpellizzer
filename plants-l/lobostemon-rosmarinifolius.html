Lobostemon rosmarinifolius

Lobostemon rosmarinifolius, commonly known as rosemary mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, needle-like leaves that are often fragrant and used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its foliage is rich in essential oils, contributing to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
