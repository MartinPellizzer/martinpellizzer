Lepanthes velosa

Lepanthes velosa, commonly known as the velvety orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaf. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.

Lepanthes velosa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
