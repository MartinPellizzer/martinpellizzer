Lupinus fikerianus

Lupinus fikerianus, commonly known as the Ethiopian lupin, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy blue or violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus fikerianus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable forage crop in regions with limited rainfall.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
