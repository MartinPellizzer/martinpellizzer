Lavandula abrotanoides var. albiflora

Lavandula abrotanoides var. albiflora, commonly known as the silver lavender or white-flowered lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and aromatic properties, often used in gardens and for essential oil extraction. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.
