Lupinus neomexicanus

Lupinus neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexico lupine, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. The plant features erect stems with narrow, pinnately compound leaves and produces spikes of blue to violet flowers. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Lupinus neomexicanus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native wildlife.
