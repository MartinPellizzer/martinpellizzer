Leucas ebracteata var. ebracteata

Leucas ebracteata var. ebracteata, commonly known as the slender leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and they bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
