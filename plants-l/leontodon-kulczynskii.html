Leontodon kulczynskii

Leontodon kulczynskii, commonly known as the Kulczynski's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Leontodon kulczynskii is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
