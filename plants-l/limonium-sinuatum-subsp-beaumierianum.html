Limonium sinuatum subsp. beaumierianum

Limonium sinuatum subsp.

beaumierianum, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of its slender, branched stems.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
