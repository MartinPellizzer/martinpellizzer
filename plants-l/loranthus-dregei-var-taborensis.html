Loranthus dregei var. taborensis

Loranthus dregei var. taborensis, commonly known as the Taborensis mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a short, woody stem with small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in small groups. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, sticky seeds, which adhere to the bark of potential host trees.

This species is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions with tropical and subtropical climates.
