Lophiocarpus latifolius

Lophiocarpus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lophiocarpus, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
