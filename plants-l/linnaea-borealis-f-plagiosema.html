Linnaea borealis f. plagiosema

Linnaea borealis f. plagiosema, commonly known as the narrowleaf twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are usually arranged in pairs, giving it the common name "twinflower.".

This form is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical variety, making it a unique subject of botanical study.
