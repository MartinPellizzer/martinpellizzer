Licuala anomala

Licuala anomala, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
