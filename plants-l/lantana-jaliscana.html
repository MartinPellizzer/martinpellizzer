Lantana jaliscana

Lantana jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican lantana, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences, and the plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent.

Lantana jaliscana is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy and resilient species.
