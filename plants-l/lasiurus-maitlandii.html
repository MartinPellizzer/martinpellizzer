Lasiurus maitlandii

Lasiurus maitlandii, commonly known as the Maitland's horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Nyssaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiny capsules containing bright red, fleshy seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 15 meters tall.

The bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age.
