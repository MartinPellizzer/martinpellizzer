Leighia anchusifolia

Leighia anchusifolia, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It features striking, showy flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, recurved lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its roots are adapted to anchor it in its natural habitat while facilitating nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
