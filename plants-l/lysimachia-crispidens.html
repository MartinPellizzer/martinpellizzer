Lysimachia crispidens

Lysimachia crispidens, commonly known as the hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitats.

Lysimachia crispidens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
