Lupinus mollis

Lupinus mollis, commonly known as the soft lupin, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus mollis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
