Lilium lancifolium var. rubrum

Lilium lancifolium var. rubrum, commonly known as the red tiger lily, is a variant of the tiger lily species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with dark red veins and a distinctive black blotch at the base of the petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late summer.

This variety is popular in gardens for its bold, showy flowers and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
