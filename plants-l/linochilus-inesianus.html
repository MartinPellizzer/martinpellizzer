Linochilus inesianus

Linochilus inesianus, commonly known as the East African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of East Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender rhizome and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure and are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

Due to habitat loss, Linochilus inesianus is considered a rare and endangered species.
