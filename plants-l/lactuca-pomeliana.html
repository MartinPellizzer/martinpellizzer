Lactuca pomeliana

Lactuca pomeliana, commonly known as the Persian lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are tender and edible. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to Central Asia and has been cultivated for its nutritious leaves.

Lactuca pomeliana is often grown as a leafy vegetable in temperate climates.
