Lophospermum erubescens

Lophospermum erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered moon vine, is a climbing perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

Lophospermum erubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
