Lolium temulentum var. robustissimum

Lolium temulentum var. robustissimum, commonly known as the hardy ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and dense, tufted foliage, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
