Loranthus duthieanus

Loranthus duthieanus, commonly known as the Duthie's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are important food sources for various bird species. Its hemiparasitic nature means it derives some nutrients from its host but can photosynthesize to a limited extent.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
