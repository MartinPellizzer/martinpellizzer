Leptopus calcareus var. sanjappae

Leptopus calcareus var. sanjappae, commonly known as the Sanjappa's rock orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of India.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring dense, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant thrives in rocky, calcareous substrates, often growing in mountainous or hilly terrains. Its flowers typically bloom during the late spring to early summer, adding a rare ornamental value to its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
