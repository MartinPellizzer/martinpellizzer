Ledothamnus sessiliflorus var. glaber

Ledothamnus sessiliflorus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-flowered ledothamnus, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The leaves are generally smooth and sessile, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
