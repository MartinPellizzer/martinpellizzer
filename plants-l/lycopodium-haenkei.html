Lycopodium haenkei

Lycopodium haenkei, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, needle-like leaves and a distinctive, club-shaped sporophyte that produces spores. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Lycopodium haenkei is a vascular plant with a simple structure, featuring roots, stems, and microphylls.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
