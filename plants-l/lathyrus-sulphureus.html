Lathyrus sulphureus

Lathyrus sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur flax or golden vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial legume that produces striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Lathyrus sulphureus is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
