Leptaulus daphnoides var. macrophyllus

Leptaulus daphnoides var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved daphnoid daisy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a tropical look in cooler climates.
