Lonicera caprifolium var. major

Lonicera caprifolium var. major, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its berries are red or black and contain seeds, contributing to its spread in natural habitats.

The plant is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
