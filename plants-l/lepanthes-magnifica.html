Lepanthes magnifica

Lepanthes magnifica, commonly known as the magnificat orchid, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Lepanthes magnifica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
