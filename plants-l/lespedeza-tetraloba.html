Lespedeza tetraloba

Lespedeza tetraloba, commonly known as purpletop vervain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Lespedeza tetraloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
