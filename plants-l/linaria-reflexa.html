Linaria reflexa

Linaria reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features slender stems and bright yellow flowers with two lips, resembling those of the snapdragon genus. Its leaves are linear and often reflexed, giving it its common name.

Linaria reflexa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
