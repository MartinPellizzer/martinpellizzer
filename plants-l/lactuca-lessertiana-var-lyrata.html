Lactuca lessertiana var. lyrata

Lactuca lessertiana var. lyrata, commonly known as the lyrated lettuce, is a variant of the lettuce genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lyre-shaped leaves that are often serrated and have a slightly bitter taste. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
