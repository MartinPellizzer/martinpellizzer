Laphamia halimifolia subsp. lindheimeri

Laphamia halimifolia subsp.

lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's laphamia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and xeriscaping in dryland regions.
