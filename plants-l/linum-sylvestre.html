Linum sylvestre

Linum sylvestre, commonly known as wild flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
