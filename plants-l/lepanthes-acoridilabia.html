Lepanthes acoridilabia

Lepanthes acoridilabia, commonly known as the pink-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that display a striking combination of white and pink hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and slightly hairy, adapting to its moist, low-light habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure and coloration make it a notable species within the orchid family.
