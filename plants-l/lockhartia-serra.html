Lockhartia serra

Lockhartia serra, commonly known as the serrated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive serrated edge on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Lockhartia serra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
