Lonicera ferdinandi var. typica

Lonicera ferdinandi var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive red throat. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
