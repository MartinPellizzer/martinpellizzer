Lotus scoparius var. perplexans

Lotus scoparius var. perplexans, commonly known as the perplexing lotus, is a rare and intriguing variant of the lotus plant native to specific regions of Asia.

This plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features a combination of showy petals and subtle, intricate sepals that set it apart from other lotus species. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a delicate fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and distinctive morphology, it is of particular interest to botanists and horticulturists.
