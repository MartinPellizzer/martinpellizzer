Lagenophora emphysopus

Lagenophora emphysopus, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain the seeds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique fruit structure aids in seed dispersal by animals that feed on the fleshy, colorful pods.
