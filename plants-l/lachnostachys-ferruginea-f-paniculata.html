Lachnostachys ferruginea f. paniculata

Lachnostachys ferruginea f. paniculata, commonly known as the rusty leaved paniculate grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish-brown stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and compact growth habit.
