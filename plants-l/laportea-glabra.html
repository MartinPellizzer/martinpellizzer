Laportea glabra

Laportea glabra, commonly known as smooth laportea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Laportea glabra is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
