Linaria subandina

Linaria subandina, commonly known as the Andean toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linaria subandina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
