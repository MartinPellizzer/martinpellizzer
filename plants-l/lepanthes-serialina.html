Lepanthes serialina

Lepanthes serialina, commonly known as the serialina orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Lepanthes serialina is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and uniqueness.
