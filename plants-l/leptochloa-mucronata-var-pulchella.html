Leptochloa mucronata var. pulchella

Leptochloa mucronata var. pulchella, commonly known as the pink chloa, is a grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pink to reddish inflorescences, which are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
