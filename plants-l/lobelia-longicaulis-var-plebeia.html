Lobelia longicaulis var. plebeia

Lobelia longicaulis var. plebeia, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly toothed along the edges.
