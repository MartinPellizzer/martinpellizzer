Larix laricina var. americana

Larix laricina var. americana, commonly known as the American larch, is a deciduous conifer native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its distinctive golden foliage that turns yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its needles are slender and deciduous, falling off in the winter.

This species is often found in moist, acidic soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
