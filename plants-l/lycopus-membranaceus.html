Lycopus membranaceus

Lycopus membranaceus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable and ecologically significant in its habitat.
