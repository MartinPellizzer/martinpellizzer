Lythrum lusitanicum

Lythrum lusitanicum, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces spikes of pink to red flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Lythrum lusitanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
