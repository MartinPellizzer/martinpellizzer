Lycopodium aristatum var. robustius

Lycopodium aristatum var. robustius, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, needle-like leaves and a distinctive, branched stem that grows close to the ground. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, which contain the spores necessary for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils.

Its historical significance in botany and its role in ecological systems make it an important study in plant morphology and evolution.
