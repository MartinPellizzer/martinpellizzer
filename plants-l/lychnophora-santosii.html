Lychnophora santosii

Lychnophora santosii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
