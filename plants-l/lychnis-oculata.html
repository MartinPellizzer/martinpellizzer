Lychnis oculata

Lychnis oculata, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and oval in shape. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Lychnis oculata is often found in moist environments and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
