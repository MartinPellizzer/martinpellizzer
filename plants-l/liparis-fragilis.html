Liparis fragilis

Liparis fragilis, commonly known as the fragile orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Liparis fragilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
