Lasiospermum angustifolium

Lasiospermum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces white or pale green flowers that are clustered in small umbels. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in temperate forests and is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
