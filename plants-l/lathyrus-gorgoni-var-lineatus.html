Lathyrus gorgoni var. lineatus

Lathyrus gorgoni var. lineatus, commonly known as the Gorgon's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
