Lantana longifolia

Lantana longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, and red, depending on the variety. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Lantana longifolia is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
