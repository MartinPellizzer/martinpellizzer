Ligustrum vulgare var. variegatum

Ligustrum vulgare var. variegatum, commonly known as variegated privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by black berries in the fall. It is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
