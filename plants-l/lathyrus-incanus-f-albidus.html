Lathyrus incanus f. albidus

Lathyrus incanus f. albidus, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet pea, is a botanical variant of the species Lathyrus incanus.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or purple hues of the parent species. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance. The plant is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and produces pods containing small, edible seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for trellis or support structures in horticultural settings.
