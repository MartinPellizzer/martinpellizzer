Leontodon croceus var. breviscapus

Leontodon croceus var. breviscapus, commonly known as the short-capped golden bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the stems are erect and often branched.

The variety breviscapus is distinguished by its shorter flower heads compared to the typical form of Leontodon croceus.
