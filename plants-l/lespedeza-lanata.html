Lespedeza lanata

Lespedeza lanata, commonly known as silky lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a soft, downy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Silky lespedeza is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
