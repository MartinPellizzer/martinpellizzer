Lobelia guatemalensis

Lobelia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Lobelia guatemalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
