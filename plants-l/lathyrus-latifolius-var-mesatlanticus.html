Lathyrus latifolius var. mesatlanticus

Lathyrus latifolius var. mesatlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic broadleaf pea, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also serves as a valuable forage crop.

Botanically, it is characterized by its erect growth habit and its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.
