Liatris graminifolia var. elegantula

Liatris graminifolia var. elegantula, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and showy, purple-pink spikelets that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
