Lepargochloa rhytachnoides

Lepargochloa rhytachnoides, commonly known as the silky grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.
