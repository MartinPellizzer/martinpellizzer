Lophanthus varzobicus

Lophanthus varzobicus, commonly known as the Zambian lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking exotic yet resilient plants.
