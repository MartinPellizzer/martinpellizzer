Leandra pauloensis

Leandra pauloensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in clusters rather than from a single stem. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid and shaded environments of its native habitat.

Leandra pauloensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
