Liatris scariosa var. deamii

Liatris scariosa var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces purple to pinkish-purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
