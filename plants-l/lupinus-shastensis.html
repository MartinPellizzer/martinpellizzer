Lupinus shastensis

Lupinus shastensis, commonly known as Shasta lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is a food source for various pollinators.

Lupinus shastensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
