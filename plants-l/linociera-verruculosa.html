Linociera verruculosa

Linociera verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Linociera verruculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
