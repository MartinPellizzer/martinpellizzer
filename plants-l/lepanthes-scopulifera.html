Lepanthes scopulifera

Lepanthes scopulifera, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and in shaded forest areas, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Lepanthes scopulifera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitats.
