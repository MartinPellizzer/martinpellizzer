Leucas deodikarii

Leucas deodikarii, commonly known as the Indian bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
