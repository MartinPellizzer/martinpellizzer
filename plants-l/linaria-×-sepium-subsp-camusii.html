Linaria × sepium subsp. camusii

Linaria × sepium subsp.

camusii, commonly known as the Camus's toadflax, is a hybrid species within the genus Linaria, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
