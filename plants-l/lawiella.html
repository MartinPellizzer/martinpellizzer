Lawiella

Lawiella, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. Lawiella thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
