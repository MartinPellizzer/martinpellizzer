Lobelia rivalis

Lobelia rivalis, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Lobelia rivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
