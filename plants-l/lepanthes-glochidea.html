Lepanthes glochidea

Lepanthes glochidea, commonly known as the glochidean orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with three petals and a distinctive lip.

Lepanthes glochidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
