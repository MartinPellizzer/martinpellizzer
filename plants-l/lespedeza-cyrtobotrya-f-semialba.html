Lespedeza cyrtobotrya f. semialba

Lespedeza cyrtobotrya f. semialba, commonly known as the white-leaf lespedeza, is a variant of the species Lespedeza cyrtobotrya, characterized by its pale or white foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to East Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, fluffy, yellowish-white flower clusters that bloom in late summer. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of pollinators and contributes to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
