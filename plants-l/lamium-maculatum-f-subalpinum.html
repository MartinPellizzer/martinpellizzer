Lamium maculatum f. subalpinum

Lamium maculatum f. subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple and white markings, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky soils at higher elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
