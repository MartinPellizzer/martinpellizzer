Laurus erythropus

Laurus erythropus, commonly known as the Turkish laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, shiny leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are small and fragrant, and it produces black, berry-like fruits that are edible.

The Turkish laurel is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
