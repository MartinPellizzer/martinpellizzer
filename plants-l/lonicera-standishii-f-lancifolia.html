Lonicera standishii f. lancifolia

Lonicera standishii f. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and attractive floral display.
