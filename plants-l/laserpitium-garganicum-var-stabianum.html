Laserpitium garganicum var. stabianum

Laserpitium garganicum var. stabianum, commonly known as the Stabian mountain gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variant is distinguished by its specific habitat and floral morphology, making it a notable species in alpine botany.
