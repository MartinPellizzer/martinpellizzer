Linum hispanicum var. ambiguum

Linum hispanicum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the Spanish flax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
