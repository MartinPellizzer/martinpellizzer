Leucanthemum corsicum subsp. corsicum

Leucanthemum corsicum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. It is a perennial herb with a robust root system and a height ranging from 30 to 100 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more linear. Its most striking feature is the cluster of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
