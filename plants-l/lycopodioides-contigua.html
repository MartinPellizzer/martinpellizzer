Lycopodioides contigua

Lycopodioides contigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinct sporophyte structure with a short stem and whorled leaves. Its reproductive structures include microsporangia and megasporangia, which produce spores for propagation.

Lycopodioides contigua plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
