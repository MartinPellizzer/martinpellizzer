Lycopodium zollingeri

Lycopodium zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and tiny, scale-like leaves that form dense, mat-like growths. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for reproduction. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
