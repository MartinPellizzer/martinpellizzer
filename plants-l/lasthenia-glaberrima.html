Lasthenia glaberrima

Lasthenia glaberrima, commonly known as the silverleaf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a common sight in desert landscapes.
