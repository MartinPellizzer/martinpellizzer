Lantana pseudothea

Lantana pseudothea, commonly known as the Hawaiian lantana, is a flowering shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various coastal and upland habitats.

Lantana pseudothea is an important plant in Hawaiian ecosystems, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
