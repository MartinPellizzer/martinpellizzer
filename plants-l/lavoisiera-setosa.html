Lavoisiera setosa

Lavoisiera setosa, commonly known as the silky lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lavoisiera setosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
