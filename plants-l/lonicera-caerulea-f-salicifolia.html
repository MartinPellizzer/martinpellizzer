Lonicera caerulea f. salicifolia

Lonicera caerulea f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf honeysuckle, is a cultivated form of the honeysuckle plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elongated and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, which bloom in early summer. Its berries are edible, ranging in color from red to black, and are often used in jams and wines.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
