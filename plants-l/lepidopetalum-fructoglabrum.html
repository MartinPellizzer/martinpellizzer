Lepidopetalum fructoglabrum

Lepidopetalum fructoglabrum, commonly known as the waxflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, glabrous bark and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in clusters, making it an ornamental plant in gardens.

Lepidopetalum fructoglabrum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its fragrance and ornamental value.
