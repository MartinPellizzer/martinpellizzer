Leucorchis albida subsp. straminea

Leucorchis albida subsp.

straminea, commonly known as the white orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces delicate, white flowers with a subtle fragrance.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as under forest canopies. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several small, elongated flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
