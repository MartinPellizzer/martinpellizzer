Lepanthes pastoensis

Lepanthes pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude cloud forests, where it often grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its botanical features include a slender stem and lance-shaped leaves.

Lepanthes pastoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
