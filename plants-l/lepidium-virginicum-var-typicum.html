Lepidium virginicum var. typicum

Lepidium virginicum var. typicum, commonly known as peppergrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and produces small, rounded fruits called siliques. Its leaves are alternate, with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive, peppery aroma.

Peppergrass is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
