Lavatera flava var. pallescens

Lavatera flava var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale lavender tree, is a variant of the lavender tree native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, blooming in late winter to early spring.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy soils.
