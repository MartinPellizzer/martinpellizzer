Leucanthemum × athosii

Leucanthemum × athosii, commonly known as the Athos chamomile, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a bright yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can also be used in herbal teas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
