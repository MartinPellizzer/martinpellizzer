Ligustrum perrottetii var. neilgherrense

Ligustrum perrottetii var. neilgherrense, commonly known as the Nilgiri privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,000 meters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct yellowish or white midrib. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months, followed by dark blue-black berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
