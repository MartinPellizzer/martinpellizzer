Lantana caatingensis

Lantana caatingensis, commonly known as Brazilian vervain, is a shrub native to Brazil, particularly found in the northeastern region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
