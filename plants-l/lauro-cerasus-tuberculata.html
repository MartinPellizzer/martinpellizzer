Lauro-cerasus tuberculata

Lauro-cerasus tuberculata, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, white to pinkish flower cluster in spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
