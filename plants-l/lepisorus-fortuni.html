Lepisorus fortuni

Lepisorus fortuni, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lepisorus fortuni is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
