Lissochilus parviflorus

Lissochilus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered lissochilus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pale yellow blooms with a lip that is often marked with contrasting colors. It thrives in high-altitude environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
