Linum usitatissimum var. reuteri

Linum usitatissimum var. reuteri, commonly known as the Persian flax, is a variant of the common flax plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces fibrous seeds that are used for both textile production and oil extraction. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to arid climates and its historical significance in traditional agriculture.
