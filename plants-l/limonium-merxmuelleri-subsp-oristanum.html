Limonium merxmuelleri subsp. oristanum

Limonium merxmuelleri subsp.

oristanum, commonly known as the Oristanum limonium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt, aiding in its survival in saline soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained, salty soils.
