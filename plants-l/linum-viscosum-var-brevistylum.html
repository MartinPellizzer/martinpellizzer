Linum viscosum var. brevistylum

Linum viscosum var. brevistylum, commonly known as the short-stemmed flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses.
