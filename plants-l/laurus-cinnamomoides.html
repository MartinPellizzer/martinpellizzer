Laurus cinnamomoides

Laurus cinnamomoides, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf or Indonesian cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves that emit a strong cinnamon scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries containing glossy, oval seeds. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
