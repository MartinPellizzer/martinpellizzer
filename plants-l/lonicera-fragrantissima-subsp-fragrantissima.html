Lonicera fragrantissima subsp. fragrantissima

Lonicera fragrantissima subsp.

fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features opposite, simple, and usually aromatic leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
