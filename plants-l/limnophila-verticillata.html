Limnophila verticillata

Limnophila verticillata, commonly known as the water thyme, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems that are often tangled, and its small, opposite leaves are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often floating on the water surface. Limnophila verticillata is an important species in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, muddy substrates.
