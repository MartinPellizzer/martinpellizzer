Lonicera implexa var. puberula

Lonicera implexa var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and showy flowers, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
