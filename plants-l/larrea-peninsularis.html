Larrea peninsularis

Larrea peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular creosote bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Baja California Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots and a waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Larrea peninsularis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
