Lavandula approximata

Lavandula approximata, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, spike-like inflorescences composed of purple or pinkish-blue flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green, aromatic leaves that are often used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
