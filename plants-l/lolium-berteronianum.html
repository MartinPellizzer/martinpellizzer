Lolium berteronianum

Lolium berteronianum, commonly known as bentgrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish or reddish spikelets that contain three to five flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along streambanks.

Lolium berteronianum is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
