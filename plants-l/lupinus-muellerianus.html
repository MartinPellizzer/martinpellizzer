Lupinus muellerianus

Lupinus muellerianus, commonly known as the Mueller's lupin, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences of pea-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Lupinus muellerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
