Linum jimenezii subsp. jimenezii

Linum jimenezii subsp.

jimenezii, commonly known as Jimenez's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
