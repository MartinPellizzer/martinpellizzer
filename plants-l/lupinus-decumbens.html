Lupinus decumbens

Lupinus decumbens, commonly known as the creeping lupine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features erect stems with clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus decumbens is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.
