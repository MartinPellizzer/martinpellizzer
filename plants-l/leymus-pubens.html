Leymus pubens

Leymus pubens, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and pond edges. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

Leymus pubens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
