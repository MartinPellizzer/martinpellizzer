Lantana scabiosiflora f. albida

Lantana scabiosiflora f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered lantana, is a variant of the Lantana genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
