Laggera brevipes

Laggera brevipes, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various soil conditions.
