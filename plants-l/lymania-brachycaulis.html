Lymania brachycaulis

Lymania brachycaulis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a unique grass-like plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant forms clumps of short, stiff stems that often appear as shrubs, despite being a member of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and tough, contributing to its resilience in cold and arid climates.

Lymania brachycaulis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various small animals and insects.
