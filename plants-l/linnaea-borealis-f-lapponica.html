Linnaea borealis f. lapponica

Linnaea borealis f. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its distinctive pair of opposite, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in boreal and subarctic environments. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
