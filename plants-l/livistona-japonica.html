Livistona japonica

Livistona japonica, commonly known as the Japanese areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its adaptability to various soil types and ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade make it a popular ornamental plant.

Livistona japonica is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
