Luisia inconspicua

Luisia inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous luisia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and wetlands. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
