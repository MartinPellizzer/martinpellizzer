Lippia origanoides var. sampaionis

Lippia origanoides var. sampaionis, commonly known as Brazilian oregano, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is valued in both culinary and traditional herbal applications.
