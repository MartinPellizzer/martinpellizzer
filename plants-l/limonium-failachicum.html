Limonium failachicum

Limonium failachicum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Limonium failachicum is also cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
