Lysimachia ramosa var. grandiflora

Lysimachia ramosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly succulent texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
