Lepidium dictyotum var. macrocarpum

Lepidium dictyotum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded wallcress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically pinnately divided. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits called siliques, which contain numerous small, round seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
