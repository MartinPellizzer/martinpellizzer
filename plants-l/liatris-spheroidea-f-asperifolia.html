Liatris spheroidea f. asperifolia

Liatris spheroidea f. asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its specific epithet "asperifolia.". Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of lavender to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, and it serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
