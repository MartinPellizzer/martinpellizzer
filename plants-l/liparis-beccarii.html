Liparis beccarii

Liparis beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a short rhizome and produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
