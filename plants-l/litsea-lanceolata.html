Litsea lanceolata

Litsea lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, orange-red berries.

Litsea lanceolata is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, growing as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub.
