Loudetia simulans

Loudetia simulans, commonly known as the African grass or slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Loudetia simulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
