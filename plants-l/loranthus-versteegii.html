Loranthus versteegii

Loranthus versteegii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which contain a single seed, are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy pulp.

Loranthus versteegii plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for wildlife and influencing the growth of its host trees.
