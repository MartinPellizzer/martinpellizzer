Leucas nepetoides

Leucas nepetoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight serration along the edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
