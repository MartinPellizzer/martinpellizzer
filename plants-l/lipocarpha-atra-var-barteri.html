Lipocarpha atra var. barteri

Lipocarpha atra var. barteri, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and dark, almost black, spikelets that give it its common name. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it forms dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in its survival in waterlogged soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
