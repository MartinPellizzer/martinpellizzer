Lasiopetalum acutiflorum var. oldfieldii

Lasiopetalum acutiflorum var. oldfieldii, commonly known as Oldfield's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
