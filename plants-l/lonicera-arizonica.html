Lonicera arizonica

Lonicera arizonica, commonly known as Arizona honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Lonicera arizonica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.
