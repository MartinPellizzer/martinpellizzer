Lipandra atriplicoides

Lipandra atriplicoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lipandra atriplicoides is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed.
