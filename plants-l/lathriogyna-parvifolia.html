Lathriogyna parvifolia

Lathriogyna parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved lathriogyna, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Lathriogyna parvifolia thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment for optimal growth.
