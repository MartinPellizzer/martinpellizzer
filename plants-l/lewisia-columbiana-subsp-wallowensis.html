Lewisia columbiana subsp. wallowensis

Lewisia columbiana subsp.

wallowensis, commonly known as the Wallowa lewisia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces showy, pink to magenta flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is well adapted to the cool, moist climates of its native habitat.
