Livistona occidentalis

Livistona occidentalis, commonly known as the western palm, is a species of palm native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens around the world.
