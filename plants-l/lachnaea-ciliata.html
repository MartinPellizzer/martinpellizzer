Lachnaea ciliata

Lachnaea ciliata, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a member of the Lichenes family, characterized by its complex symbiotic relationship between algae and fungi.

It typically grows on tree bark, stone, or soil surfaces, thriving in moist and shaded environments. The plant exhibits a crust-like or foliose growth form, with a pale to yellowish coloration. Its microscopic structure includes a dense network of hyphae that support the algal cells.

Lachnaea ciliata plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
