Ligularia hamiica

Ligularia hamiica, commonly known as the Hami ligularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite inflorescence, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is known for its striking coloration, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
