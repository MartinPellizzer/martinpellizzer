Loeseneriella africana var. togoensis

Loeseneriella africana var. togoensis, commonly known as the Togo sandmat, is a perennial herb native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or reddish in color.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
