Lobelia stipularis

Lobelia stipularis, commonly known as the cardinal flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking tubular red flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade.

Lobelia stipularis is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
