Lunaria annua subsp. annua

Lunaria annua subsp.

annua, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a faint fragrance.

The plant produces distinctive, translucent seed pods that resemble small moons, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Lunaria annua subsp.

annua is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
