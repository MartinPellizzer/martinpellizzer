Lathraea clandestina

Lathraea clandestina, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It attaches itself to the roots of other plants, drawing nutrients and water from its host. The plant is characterized by its small, fleshy, greenish-yellow stems and its ability to survive in shaded, moist environments. It is native to Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in woodland areas.

Due to its elusive nature and rare occurrence, it is considered a cryptic and fascinating species in the botanical world.
