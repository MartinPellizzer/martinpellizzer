Leptotaenia anomala

Leptotaenia anomala, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with four petals, and it produces small, elongated fruits.

Leptotaenia anomala is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
