Lasiorrhiza suaveolens

Lasiorrhiza suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.

Lasiorrhiza suaveolens prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
