Lacanthis

Lacanthis, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Native to high-altitude regions of South America, these plants thrive in cool, moist environments. The leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and the stems are often hairy.

Lacanthis species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
