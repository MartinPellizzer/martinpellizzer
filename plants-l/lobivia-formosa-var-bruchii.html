Lobivia formosa var. bruchii

Lobivia formosa var. bruchii, commonly known as the Bruchii Lobivia, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The leaves are fleshy and often bluish-green, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a bold color contrast to its foliage.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability to dry environments.
