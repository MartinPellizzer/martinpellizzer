Lampetia

Lampetia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet or purple petals surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Lampetia typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its medicinal properties have also been historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
