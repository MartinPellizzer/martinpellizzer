Lachnosiphonium

Lachnosiphonium, commonly known as the giant spore plant, is a unique member of the Lycopodiophyta division.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and the presence of large, spore-bearing structures called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or wetlands. Its reproductive cycle involves the production of spores rather than seeds, a feature common to many ancient plant groups.

Lachnosiphonium is notable for its primitive morphology and its role in understanding the evolution of early terrestrial plants.
