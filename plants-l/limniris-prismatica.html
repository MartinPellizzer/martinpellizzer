Limniris prismatica

Limniris prismatica, commonly known as the prism-shaped water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that reflect light in a prismatic manner, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a unique structure that resembles a prism.

Limniris prismatica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to surrounding water life.
