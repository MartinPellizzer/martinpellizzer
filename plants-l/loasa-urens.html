Loasa urens

Loasa urens, commonly known as the redstem loasa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, reddish stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are usually white or pale pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Loasa urens has a fibrous root system and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
