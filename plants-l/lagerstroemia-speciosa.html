Lagerstroemia speciosa

Lagerstroemia speciosa, commonly known as the crepe myrtle, is a deciduous flowering tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, long-lasting flowers that range in color from pink to red and purple, blooming in summer. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes flaky with age, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
