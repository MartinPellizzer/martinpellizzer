Lepidium ecuadorense

Lepidium ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or purple flowers and leaves that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Lepidium ecuadorense is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
