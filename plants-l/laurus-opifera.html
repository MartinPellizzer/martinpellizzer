Laurus opifera

Laurus opifera, commonly known as sweet bay laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its leaves are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
