Ligustrum vulgare f. xanthocarpum

Ligustrum vulgare f. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited privet, is a hybrid form of the common privet.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange berries, which contrast with the typical black fruits of the species. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils. It has small, opposite leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
