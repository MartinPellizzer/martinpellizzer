Lasiorrhiza seneciodes

Lasiorrhiza seneciodes, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and adaptability to arid environments.
