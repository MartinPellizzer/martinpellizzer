Lasiopetalum baueri

Lasiopetalum baueri, commonly known as the Bauer's flame flower, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a dense, evergreen canopy.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
