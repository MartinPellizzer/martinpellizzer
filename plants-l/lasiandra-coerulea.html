Lasiandra coerulea

Lasiandra coerulea, commonly known as blue-flowered lasiandra, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Lasiandra coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
