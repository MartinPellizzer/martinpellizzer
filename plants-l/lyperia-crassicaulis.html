Lyperia crassicaulis

Lyperia crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Due to its specialized habitat, Lyperia crassicaulis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
