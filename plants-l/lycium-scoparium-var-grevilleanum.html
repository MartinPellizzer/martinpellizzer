Lycium scoparium var. grevilleanum

Lycium scoparium var. grevilleanum, commonly known as the Greville's boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
