Lauchea verticillata

Lauchea verticillata, commonly known as desert sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant has a low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in spring, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Lauchea verticillata is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate high temperatures.
