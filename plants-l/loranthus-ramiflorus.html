Loranthus ramiflorus

Loranthus ramiflorus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the southern United States, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by the host's leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus ramiflorus is an important component of forest ecosystems, influencing host tree health and providing food for various wildlife species.
