Lathyrus plitmannii

Lathyrus plitmannii, commonly known as the Pritzwalk pea, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal.

Lathyrus plitmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
