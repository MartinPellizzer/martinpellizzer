Loranthus homblei

Loranthus homblei, commonly known as the Himalayan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Himalayan region.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using modified leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers. It plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food for certain birds and insects.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
