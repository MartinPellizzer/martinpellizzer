Lespedeza capitata f. argentea

Lespedeza capitata f. argentea, commonly known as silverhead lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its silver-white foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in dense, spherical clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

Lespedeza capitata f. argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
