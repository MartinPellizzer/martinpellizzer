Lasianthus eberhardtii

Lasianthus eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
