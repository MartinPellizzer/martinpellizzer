Lepanthes mornicola

Lepanthes mornicola, commonly known as the morning star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central America.

This small epiphytic plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant's botanical features include pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Lepanthes mornicola is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
