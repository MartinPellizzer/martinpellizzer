Loranthus chinensis

Loranthus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds through photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers, followed by berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is native to East Asia and is often found in forests where its host species thrive.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
