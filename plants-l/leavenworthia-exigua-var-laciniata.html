Leavenworthia exigua var. laciniata

Leavenworthia exigua var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf prairie violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
