Lathyrus luteus f. cirrhosus

Lathyrus luteus f. cirrhosus, commonly known as the yellow vetchling or creeping vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
