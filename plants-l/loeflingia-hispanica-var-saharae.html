Loeflingia hispanica var. saharae

Loeflingia hispanica var. saharae, commonly known as the Saharan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and low maintenance, it is often cultivated in gardens with limited water resources.
