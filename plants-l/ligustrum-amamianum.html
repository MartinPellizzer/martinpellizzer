Ligustrum amamianum

Ligustrum amamianum, commonly known as the Amami privet, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Ryukyu Islands, including the islands of Amami and Okinawa. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, dark berries.

Ligustrum amamianum is often used in landscaping due to its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.
