Ligusticum monnieri

Ligusticum monnieri, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
