Leucadendron spissifolium subsp. phillipsii

Leucadendron spissifolium subsp.

phillipsii, commonly known as the Cape goldflame, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and dense, leathery foliage.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.

The subspecies phillipsii is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to the main species.
