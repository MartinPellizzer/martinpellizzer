Lobelia cardinalis var. graminea

Lobelia cardinalis var. graminea, commonly known as the cardinal flower, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, tubular red flowers with a distinctive orange throat, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats, such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its vibrant color and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
