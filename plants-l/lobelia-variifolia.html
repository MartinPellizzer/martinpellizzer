Lobelia variifolia

Lobelia variifolia, commonly known as the variable lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lobelia variifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
