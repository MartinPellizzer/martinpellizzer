Lachnopylis emarginata var. brevifolia

Lachnopylis emarginata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lachnopylis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
