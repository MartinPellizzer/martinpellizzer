Loudetia bidentata

Loudetia bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Loudetia bidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
