Lupinus opsianthus

Lupinus opsianthus, commonly known as the velvet lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and striking violet to purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. Lupinus opsianthus is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
