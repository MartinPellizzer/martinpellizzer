Livistona fulva

Livistona fulva, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of cycad native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are usually a bright green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Its reproductive structures are typical of cycads, with separate male and female plants.

Livistona fulva is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
