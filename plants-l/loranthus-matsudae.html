Loranthus matsudae

Loranthus matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily members of the Rutaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using limited chlorophyll. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. Its life cycle involves both asexual reproduction through vegetative growth and sexual reproduction via seeds.

Loranthus matsudae is often found in forested areas of Japan and is considered an ecologically significant component of its ecosystem.
