Leavenworthia stylosa

Leavenworthia stylosa, commonly known as the prairie morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and wetland habitats.

Leavenworthia stylosa is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
