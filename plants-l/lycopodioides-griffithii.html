Lycopodioides griffithii

Lycopodioides griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's clubmoss, is a species of lycopod that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and its creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Lycopodioides griffithii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
