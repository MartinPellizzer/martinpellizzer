Lepidium spicatum var. racemosum

Lepidium spicatum var. racemosum, commonly known as clustered cress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescence that bears small white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery aroma.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
