Lupinus alaimandus

Lupinus alaimandus, commonly known as the Alaimandus lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus alaimandus is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
