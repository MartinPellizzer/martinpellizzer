Lespedeza kanaoriensis

Lespedeza kanaoriensis, commonly known as the Japanese lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. It is often found in forested areas and along stream banks, contributing to soil stabilization and providing nectar for pollinators.

Lespedeza kanaoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscaping.
