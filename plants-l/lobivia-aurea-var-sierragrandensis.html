Lobivia aurea var. sierragrandensis

Lobivia aurea var. sierragrandensis, commonly known as the Sierra Grande lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
