Loefgrenianthus

Loefgrenianthus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with a bushy habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Loefgrenianthus is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
