Linostoma scandens var. oblongifolium

Linostoma scandens var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its arching growth habit and dense foliage.

It features opposite, ovate to oblong leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
