Linaria tonzigii

Linaria tonzigii, commonly known as the Tonzig's toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are tubular with two lips, typical of the genus Linaria.

Linaria tonzigii is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
