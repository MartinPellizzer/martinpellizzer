Lepanthes volvox

Lepanthes volvox, commonly known as the Violet Paphiopedilum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, preferring moist, shaded environments in tropical forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Lepanthes volvox is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
