Lacostea pedicellata

Lacostea pedicellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
