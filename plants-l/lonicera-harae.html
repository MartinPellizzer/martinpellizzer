Lonicera harae

Lonicera harae, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb by twining around supports. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous small seeds.

Lonicera harae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in attracting pollinators.
