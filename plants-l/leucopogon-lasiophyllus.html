Leucopogon lasiophyllus

Leucopogon lasiophyllus, commonly known as the silky-leaved heath, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are densely covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or white, blooming in spring.

It is a hardy plant that is well adapted to the dry and nutrient-poor conditions of its native environment.
