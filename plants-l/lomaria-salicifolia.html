Lomaria salicifolia

Lomaria salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, willow-like leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in clusters during the spring.

Lomaria salicifolia is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
