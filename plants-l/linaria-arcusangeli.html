Linaria arcusangeli

Linaria arcusangeli, commonly known as the mountain flax or angel's hair, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the genus Linaria, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly found in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Linaria arcusangeli is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
