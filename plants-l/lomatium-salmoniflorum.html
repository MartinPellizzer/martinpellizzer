Lomatium salmoniflorum

Lomatium salmoniflorum, commonly known as the salmonflower lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly found in moist, shaded areas of forests. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
