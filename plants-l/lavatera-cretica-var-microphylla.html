Lavatera cretica var. microphylla

Lavatera cretica var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved cretic lavatera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and showy, fluffy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
