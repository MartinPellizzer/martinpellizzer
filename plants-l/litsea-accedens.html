Litsea accedens

Litsea accedens, commonly known as the Indian bay leaf or Malabar bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by egg-shaped berries containing a single seed.

Its leaves are often dried and used as a spice, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
