Lepanthes panicellus

Lepanthes panicellus, commonly known as the panicled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, slender stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a panicled inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
