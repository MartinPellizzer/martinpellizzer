Leucothoe salicifolia

Leucothoe salicifolia, commonly known as silkyleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically smooth and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring. Its berries are dark blue to black and are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
