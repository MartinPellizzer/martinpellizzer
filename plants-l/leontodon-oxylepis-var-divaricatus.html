Leontodon oxylepis var. divaricatus

Leontodon oxylepis var. divaricatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
