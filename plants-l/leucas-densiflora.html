Leucas densiflora

Leucas densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
