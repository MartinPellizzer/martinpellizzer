Loranthus woodfordioides var. rugegensis

Loranthus woodfordioides var. rugegensis, commonly known as the Rugegen's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its hemiparasitic nature, deriving some of its nutrients from the host while performing photosynthesis. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees, facilitating its propagation.

This species is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas where suitable host trees are present.
