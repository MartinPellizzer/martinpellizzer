Liriactis albiflora

Liriactis albiflora, commonly known as the white lily, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
