Lamium laevigatum var. minor

Lamium laevigatum var. minor, commonly known as lesser dead-nettle, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in gardens and agricultural fields.
