Limodorum bidentatum

Limodorum bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive, spurred petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
