Loranthus kagehensis

Loranthus kagehensis, commonly known as the Kagehensis mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the family Loranthaceae.

It derives some of its nutrients from its host plant through specialized structures called haustoria. This species typically grows on the branches of trees, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually simple and opposite, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or greenish in color.

The plant plays a role in ecological interactions, providing food for certain birds and insects.
