Lepidium cumingianum subsp. bertoronianum

Lepidium cumingianum subsp.

bertoronianum, commonly known as the Cuming's cress, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, green, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It is native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile, growing in moist, temperate environments.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
