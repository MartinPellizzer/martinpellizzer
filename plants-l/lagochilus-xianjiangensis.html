Lagochilus xianjiangensis

Lagochilus xianjiangensis, commonly known as Xianjiang thyme, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, pink or purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
