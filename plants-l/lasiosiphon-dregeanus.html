Lasiosiphon dregeanus

Lasiosiphon dregeanus, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Lasiosiphon dregeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
