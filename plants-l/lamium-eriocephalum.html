Lamium eriocephalum

Lamium eriocephalum, commonly known as woolly ground-ivy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of soft, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium eriocephalum is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil.
