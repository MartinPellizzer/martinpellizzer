Lilium × testaceum

Lilium × testaceum, commonly known as the tiger lily, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers with dark spots, which resemble a tiger's coat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The botanical features of Lilium × testaceum include its bulbous underground stem and its ability to produce multiple blooms per stem.
