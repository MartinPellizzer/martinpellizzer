Leontodon muelleri

Leontodon muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland margins. Leontodon muelleri is relatively drought-tolerant and is considered a hardy species adapted to the arid conditions of its native range.
