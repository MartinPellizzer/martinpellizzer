Larryleachia cactiformis var. felina

Larryleachia cactiformis var. felina, commonly known as the cat's paw cactus, is a unique succulent characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems that resemble a cat's paw.

It originates from the arid regions of South America, where it thrives in hot, dry climates. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
