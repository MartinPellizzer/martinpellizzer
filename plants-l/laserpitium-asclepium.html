Laserpitium asclepium

Laserpitium asclepium, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is noted, though it is considered toxic in large quantities.
