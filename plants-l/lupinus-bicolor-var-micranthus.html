Lupinus bicolor var. micranthus

Lupinus bicolor var. micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue and white flower clusters. The plant has narrow, pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus bicolor var. micranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
