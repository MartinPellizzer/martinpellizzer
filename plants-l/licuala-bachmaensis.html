Licuala bachmaensis

Licuala bachmaensis, commonly known as the Bachma palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the leaf stalks, and the plant produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.
