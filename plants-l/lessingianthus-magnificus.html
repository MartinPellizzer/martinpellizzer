Lessingianthus magnificus

Lessingianthus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent lessingia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant features robust, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its vibrant yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
