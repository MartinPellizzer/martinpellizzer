Lonchocarpus deguelioides

Lonchocarpus deguelioides, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features pinnately compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique, spurred calyces and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and medicinal significance.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
