Lessingianthus erythrophilus

Lessingianthus erythrophilus, commonly known as the red-flowered sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its striking floral display.
