Lysimachia ciliata

Lysimachia ciliata, commonly known as the hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are characterized by their hairy texture and serrated edges, which contribute to its common name.

Lysimachia ciliata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
