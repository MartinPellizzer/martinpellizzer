Leptovignea elongata

Leptovignea elongata, commonly known as the slender leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are typically alternate and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the rainy season. Its stems are flexible and can twine around supports, making it suitable for trellises or natural structures in humid environments.

Leptovignea elongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
