Leontodon autumnalis var. latifolius

Leontodon autumnalis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved autumn gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing showy blue or violet flowers.

The species is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
