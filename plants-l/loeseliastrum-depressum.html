Loeseliastrum depressum

Loeseliastrum depressum, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

Loeseliastrum depressum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
