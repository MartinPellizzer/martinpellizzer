Leucanthemum anomalum

Leucanthemum anomalum, commonly known as the starry groundsel, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, daisy-like flowers, which consist of white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc of tubular flowers.

Leucanthemum anomalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
