Lucuma glabrifolia

Lucuma glabrifolia, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru, and thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, nutty flavor. Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Lucuma glabrifolia is valued both for its edible fruit and its traditional use in Andean cuisine and medicine.
