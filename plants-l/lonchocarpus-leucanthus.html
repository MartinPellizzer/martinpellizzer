Lonchocarpus leucanthus

Lonchocarpus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered legume, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy white or pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that remain attached to the plant for an extended period.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
