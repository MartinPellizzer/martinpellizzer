Lyonia obtusa

Lyonia obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf lyonia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in shades of white or pale pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lyonia obtusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
