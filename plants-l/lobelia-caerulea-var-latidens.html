Lobelia caerulea var. latidens

Lobelia caerulea var. latidens, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
