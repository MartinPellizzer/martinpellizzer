Lepidaploa chalybaea

Lepidaploa chalybaea, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a branched stem that often grows in a low, spreading habit. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Lepidaploa chalybaea is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
