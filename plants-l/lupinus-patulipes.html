Lupinus patulipes

Lupinus patulipes, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus patulipes is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native environment.
