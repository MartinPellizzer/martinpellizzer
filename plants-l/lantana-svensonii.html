Lantana svensonii

Lantana svensonii, commonly known as the Hawaiian lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, red, and pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Lantana svensonii is notable for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
