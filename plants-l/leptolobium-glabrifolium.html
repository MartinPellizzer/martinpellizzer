Leptolobium glabrifolium

Leptolobium glabrifolium, commonly known as the silky-leaved leptolobium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, glabrous (smooth) leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
