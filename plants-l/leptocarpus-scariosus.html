Leptocarpus scariosus

Leptocarpus scariosus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea bean, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it tolerates saline and wind-swept conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal invertebrates.
