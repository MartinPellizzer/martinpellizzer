Lepanthes grisebachiana

Lepanthes grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

This terrestrial orchid typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy orchid flower that is usually pale green or white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.
