Luteola tinctoria var. australis

Luteola tinctoria var. australis, commonly known as the Australian golden weed, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its dyeing properties.
