Lithospermum niveum

Lithospermum niveum, commonly known as white lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lithospermum niveum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
