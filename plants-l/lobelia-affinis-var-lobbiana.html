Lobelia affinis var. lobbiana

Lobelia affinis var. lobbiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
