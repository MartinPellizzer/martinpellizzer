Lonchocarpus nicaraguensis

Lonchocarpus nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan senna, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
