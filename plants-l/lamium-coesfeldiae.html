Lamium coesfeldiae

Lamium coesfeldiae, commonly known as Coesfeld's deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Lamium coesfeldiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
