Leptostachya rupestris

Leptostachya rupestris, commonly known as the rock bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Leptostachya rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
