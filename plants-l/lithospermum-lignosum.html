Lithospermum lignosum

Lithospermum lignosum, commonly known as red root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a fibrous root system and erect, branching stems that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. Lithospermum lignosum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of compounds like lithospermic acid.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
