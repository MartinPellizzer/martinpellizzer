Lathyrus paraguariensis

Lathyrus paraguariensis, commonly known as paraguay bean or wolf bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to South America.

It produces distinctive pea-like flowers and elongated, spindle-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and strong, woody stems that support its climbing growth habit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, well-drained soils.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
