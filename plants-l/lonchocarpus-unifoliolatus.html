Lonchocarpus unifoliolatus

Lonchocarpus unifoliolatus, commonly known as the single leaf legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
