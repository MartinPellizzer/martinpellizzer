Lecythis gigantea

Lecythis gigantea, commonly known as the giant lecythium, is a large tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and broad, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its durable wood and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
