Langsdorffia hypogaea

Langsdorffia hypogaea, commonly known as the African violets, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant is a perennial herb with a short, thick rhizome and produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular houseplant worldwide.
