Lupinus hirsutus subsp. termis

Lupinus hirsutus subsp.

termis, commonly known as the Termis lupine, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, hairy leaflets, which help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
