Lychnitis lutea

Lychnitis lutea, commonly known as the yellow burnet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous stamen with long, slender filaments, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are finely divided and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lychnitis lutea is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.
