Lisianthius aphyllus

Lisianthius aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with the entire plant structure consisting primarily of its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces small, clustered blooms that are typically borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a sunny exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
