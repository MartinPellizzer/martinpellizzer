Luzula parviflora var. divaricata

Luzula parviflora var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often arch gracefully. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters during the late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
