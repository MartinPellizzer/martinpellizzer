Landolphia chylorrhiza

Landolphia chylorrhiza, commonly known as the giant African waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
