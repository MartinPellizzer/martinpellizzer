Lessingianthus scabrifoliatus

Lessingianthus scabrifoliatus, commonly known as the rough-leaved lessingia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, rough textures. It produces showy yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
