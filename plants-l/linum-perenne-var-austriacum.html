Linum perenne var. austriacum

Linum perenne var. austriacum, commonly known as Austrian flax, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces slender, spindle-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
