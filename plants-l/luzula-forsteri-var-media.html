Luzula forsteri var. media

Luzula forsteri var. media, commonly known as the common wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, arching stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces pale green to blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula forsteri var. media is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
