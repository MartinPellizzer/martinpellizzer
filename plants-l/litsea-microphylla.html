Litsea microphylla

Litsea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
