Lobelia dunbariae subsp. dunbariae

Lobelia dunbariae subsp.

dunbariae, commonly known as the Dunbar's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to violet.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in cloud forests or alpine meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators in its native habitat.
