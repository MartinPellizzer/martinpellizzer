Loranthus carnosus

Loranthus carnosus, commonly known as the meaty mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, scale-like leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract insects for pollination. It is native to regions in Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

Its presence can have both ecological and economic impacts on its host trees.
