Lecythis congestiflora

Lecythis congestiflora, commonly known as the "Brazil nut tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and distinctive, heavy, woody fruits that contain the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a thick, buttressed trunk that helps it withstand strong winds. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are arranged in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in the rainforest, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to the forest's biodiversity.
