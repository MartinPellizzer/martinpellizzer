Lotononis azurea var. lanceolata

Lotononis azurea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the blue lotononis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
