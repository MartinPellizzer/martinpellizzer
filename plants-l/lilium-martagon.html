Lilium martagon

Lilium martagon, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with a white or pale pink center. The plant features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

Lilium martagon is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
