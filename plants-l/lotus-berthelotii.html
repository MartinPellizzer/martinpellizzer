Lotus berthelotii

Lotus berthelotii, commonly known as the Ethiopian lotus, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically erect and somewhat woody. Lotus berthelotii is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
