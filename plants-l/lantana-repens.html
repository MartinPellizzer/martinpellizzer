Lantana repens

Lantana repens, commonly known as creeping lantana, is a low-growing, trailing shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, and red, depending on the variety. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is widely used in landscaping for its ability to form dense mats and its ornamental value.

Lantana repens is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and full sun.
