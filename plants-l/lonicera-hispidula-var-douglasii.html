Lonicera hispidula var. douglasii

Lonicera hispidula var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
