Landolphia incerta

Landolphia incerta, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap and fibrous roots.
