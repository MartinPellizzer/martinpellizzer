Licuala mukahensis

Licuala mukahensis, commonly known as the Mukah palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Borneo and is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found near rivers or in swampy areas. Its trunk is short and stout, with a fibrous, spongy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a bright orange or red berry that is edible.
