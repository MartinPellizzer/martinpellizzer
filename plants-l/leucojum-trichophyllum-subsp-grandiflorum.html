Leucojum trichophyllum subsp. grandiflorum

Leucojum trichophyllum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant white squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of large, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring.

The plant is characterized by its robust bulbs and is often found in woodland and rocky habitats. It is noted for its showy display and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger flowers compared to other members of the species.
