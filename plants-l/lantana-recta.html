Lantana recta

Lantana recta, commonly known as the upright lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Lantana recta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
