Leontochir ovallei

Leontochir ovallei, commonly known as the ovalle rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Leontochir ovallei is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
