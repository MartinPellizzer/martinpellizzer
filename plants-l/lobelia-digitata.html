Lobelia digitata

Lobelia digitata, commonly known as the fingered lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia digitata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
