Lycianthes parasitica subsp. epiphytica

Lycianthes parasitica subsp.

epiphytica, commonly known as the epiphytic lycianthes, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Solanaceae family. It typically grows on the branches of trees, using its haustoria to extract water and nutrients from its host.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its fruits are berries that mature to a red color, containing several seeds.

This species is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical environments.
