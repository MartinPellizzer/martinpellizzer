Lythrum strictum

Lythrum strictum, commonly known as rosemary bush or narrow-leaved lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces spikes of pink to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
