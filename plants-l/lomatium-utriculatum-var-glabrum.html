Lomatium utriculatum var. glabrum

Lomatium utriculatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a taproot system and is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.

The smooth lomatium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
