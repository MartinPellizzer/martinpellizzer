Limonium gilesii

Limonium gilesii, commonly known as the sea helene, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, adding a splash of color to coastal landscapes.

Limonium gilesii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
