Linaria fruticosa

Linaria fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria fruticosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
