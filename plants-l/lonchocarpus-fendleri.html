Lonchocarpus fendleri

Lonchocarpus fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces striking yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
