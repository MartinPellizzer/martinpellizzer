Ligusticum aromaticum var. lanuginosum

Ligusticum aromaticum var. lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly lovage, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
