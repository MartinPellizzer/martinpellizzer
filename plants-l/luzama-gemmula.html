Luzama gemmula

Luzama gemmula, commonly known as the gemmulan tree, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by oval, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often marked with distinct lenticels.

Luzama gemmula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
