Leionema lamprophyllum subsp. lamprophyllum

Leionema lamprophyllum subsp.

lamprophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leaved leptospermum, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-grey pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
