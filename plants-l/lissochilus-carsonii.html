Lissochilus carsonii

Lissochilus carsonii, commonly known as Carson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its mountainous habitat.

This orchid is often found in rocky or forested areas, thriving in partial shade and well-drained soil.
