Lepidium californicum

Lepidium californicum, commonly known as California peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, round, peppery fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and coastal areas.

Lepidium californicum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
