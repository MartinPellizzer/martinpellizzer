Lavatera tomentosa

Lavatera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy gray foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often growing up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid regions.

Lavatera tomentosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
