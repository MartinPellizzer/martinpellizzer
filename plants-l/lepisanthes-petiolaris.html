Lepisanthes petiolaris

Lepisanthes petiolaris, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are ovate and have a waxy texture, typical of many orchid species.

Lepisanthes petiolaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
