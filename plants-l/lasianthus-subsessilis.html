Lasianthus subsessilis

Lasianthus subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
