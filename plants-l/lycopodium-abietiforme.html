Lycopodium abietiforme

Lycopodium abietiforme, commonly known as the pinecone clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces distinctive cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores. Lycopodium abietiforme typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant botanical interest.
