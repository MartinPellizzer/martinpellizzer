Lasiochloa pectinata

Lasiochloa pectinata, commonly known as the silky feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland grassland habitats. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Lasiochloa pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
