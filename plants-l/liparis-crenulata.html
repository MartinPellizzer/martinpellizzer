Liparis crenulata

Liparis crenulata, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Liparis crenulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
