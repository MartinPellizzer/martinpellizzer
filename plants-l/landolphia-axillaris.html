Landolphia axillaris

Landolphia axillaris, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces distinctive, milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
