Lathyrus arvensis

Lathyrus arvensis, commonly known as field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals.

Lathyrus arvensis is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
