Lilaeopsis recurva

Lilaeopsis recurva, commonly known as the recurved lily, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a slight recurvature at the tips, giving it its common name.

Lilaeopsis recurva is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
