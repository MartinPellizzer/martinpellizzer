Lobivia maximiliana var. durispina

Lobivia maximiliana var. durispina, commonly known as the Spiny Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its unique form and resilience, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
