Lespedeza cytisoides var. inschanica

Lespedeza cytisoides var. inschanica, commonly known as the Chinese stragglerbine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed areas. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for erosion control and as a forage plant.
