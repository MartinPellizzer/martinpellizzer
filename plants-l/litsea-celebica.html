Litsea celebica

Litsea celebica, commonly known as the Java candlewood or white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and showy, white, candle-like flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
