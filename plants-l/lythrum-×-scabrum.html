Lythrum × scabrum

Lythrum × scabrum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lythraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, spike-like clusters of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe but has been introduced to North America, where it has become invasive in wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are square in cross-section.

It thrives in moist soils and can form dense stands, often outcompeting native vegetation.
