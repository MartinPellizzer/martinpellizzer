Lupinus greenei

Lupinus greenei, commonly known as Greene's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus greenei plays an important role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for ecological restoration efforts.
