Leptochloa anoplia

Leptochloa anoplia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Leptochloa anoplia is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
