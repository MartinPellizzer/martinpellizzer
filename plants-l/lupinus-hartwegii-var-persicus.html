Lupinus hartwegii var. persicus

Lupinus hartwegii var. persicus, commonly known as the Persian lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features compound leaves with 8–12 pairs of oval, hairy leaflets. The plant produces erect, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. Its root system is characterized by the presence of nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
