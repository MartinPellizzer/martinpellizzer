Lobelia princeps

Lobelia princeps, commonly known as the prince's lobelia, is a striking flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Lobelia princeps is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
