Lobivia

Lobivia, commonly known as the "Lobivia" or "Lobivia species," is a genus of succulent cacti native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

These plants are characterized by their compact, often globular or cylindrical stems, which are typically covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. Lobivias have spiny or hairless stems, and their flowers, when they bloom, are usually yellow or orange in color. They are drought-tolerant and thrive in well-draining soil, making them popular among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to their unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, Lobivias are often cultivated in arid and semi-arid environments.
