Linum perenne var. collinum

Linum perenne var. collinum, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its fibrous stems and leaves are characteristic of the Linaceae family, known for their textile and medicinal uses.

Linum perenne var. collinum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
