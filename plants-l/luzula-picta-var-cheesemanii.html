Luzula picta var. cheesemanii

Luzula picta var. cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's wood sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Luzula picta var. cheesemanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
