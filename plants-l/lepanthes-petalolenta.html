Lepanthes petalolenta

Lepanthes petalolenta, commonly known as the showy violet orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-colored flowers with intricate petal structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
