Lobelia carnosula

Lobelia carnosula, commonly known as the fat-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.

Lobelia carnosula is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
