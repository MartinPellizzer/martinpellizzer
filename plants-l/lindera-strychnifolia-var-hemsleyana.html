Lindera strychnifolia var. hemsleyana

Lindera strychnifolia var. hemsleyana, commonly known as Hemsley's Indian-woodberry, is a variant of the Lindera genus native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
