Lupinus concinnus var. arizonicus

Lupinus concinnus var. arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves composed of several small, oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus concinnus var. arizonicus is a valuable species for native landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
