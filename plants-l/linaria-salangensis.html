Linaria salangensis

Linaria salangensis, commonly known as the Salangenic toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Linaria salangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
