Lastrea demerarana

Lastrea demerarana, commonly known as the Demerara lastrea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
