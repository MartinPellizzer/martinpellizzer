Luxemburgia circumdata

Luxemburgia circumdata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Luxemburgia circumdata is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for trellises and hanging baskets.
