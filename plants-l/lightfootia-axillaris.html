Lightfootia axillaris

Lightfootia axillaris, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits. Lightfootia axillaris is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
