Limonia angulata

Limonia angulata, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round, orange-colored fruits that are typically sour but can be sweet when fully ripe. Limonia angulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
