Lotus australis var. vestitus

Lotus australis var. vestitus, commonly known as the velvet lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its roots are adapted for anchoring in muddy substrates, while its floating leaves help it access sunlight for photosynthesis.

The velvet lotus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
