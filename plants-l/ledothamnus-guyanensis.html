Ledothamnus guyanensis

Ledothamnus guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana thornbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the rainforests of Guyana and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are often clustered around the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is often found in secondary forest growth.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental qualities, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
