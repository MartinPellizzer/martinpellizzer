Liliago glauca

Liliago glauca, commonly known as the blue lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous stem that helps it spread vegetatively.

Liliago glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
