Lobostemon strigosus

Lobostemon strigosus, commonly known as the hairy lobostemon, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, ascending stems covered in dense, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a fuzzy texture, contributing to its overall rugged look.

Lobostemon strigosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
