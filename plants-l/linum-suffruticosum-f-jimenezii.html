Linum suffruticosum f. jimenezii

Linum suffruticosum f. jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's flax, is a rare and distinctive variant of the flax plant.

It is native to specific regions in Mexico, where it thrives in rocky, arid environments. The plant features small, blue or purple flowers and linear leaves, typical of the Linaceae family. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
