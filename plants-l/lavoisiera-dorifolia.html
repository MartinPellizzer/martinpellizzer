Lavoisiera dorifolia

Lavoisiera dorifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Lavoisiera dorifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
