Linaria odora var. brachyceras

Linaria odora var. brachyceras, commonly known as the dwarf sweet William, is a compact, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of bright red, pink, or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, upright leaves and a bushy growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
