Laportea rigidifolia

Laportea rigidifolia, commonly known as the stiffleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.

Laportea rigidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
