Leontopodium sibiricum

Leontopodium sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround the small, yellowish flowers, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, well-drained soils. Its growth is slow, and it is often found at high altitudes, making it a rare and delicate species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
