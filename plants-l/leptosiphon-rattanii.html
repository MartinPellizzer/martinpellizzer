Leptosiphon rattanii

Leptosiphon rattanii, commonly known as the rattanii morning glory, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
