Lithocarpus moluccus

Lithocarpus moluccus, commonly known as the Moluccan ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts.

It is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its hard, dense wood and leathery, evergreen leaves that remain on the tree for several years. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees, and it produces edible, nut-like fruits that are important for local wildlife.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of tropical rainforest ecosystems.
