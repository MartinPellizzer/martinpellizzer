Lobelia violacea

Lobelia violacea, commonly known as the violet lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
