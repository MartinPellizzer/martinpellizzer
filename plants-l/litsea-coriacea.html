Litsea coriacea

Litsea coriacea, commonly known as roughleaf sandoric, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, red or orange berries. Litsea coriacea thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valued ornamental and medicinal plant in its native region.
