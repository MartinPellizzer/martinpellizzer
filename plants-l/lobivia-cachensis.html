Lobivia cachensis

Lobivia cachensis, commonly known as the Cachensis Pincushion Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spiny, tuberculate structures. The plant features pale green to bluish-green stems that are often adorned with white or pale pink flowers during the growing season. Lobivia cachensis is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
