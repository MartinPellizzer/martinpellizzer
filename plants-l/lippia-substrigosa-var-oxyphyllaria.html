Lippia substrigosa var. oxyphyllaria

Lippia substrigosa var. oxyphyllaria, commonly known as the silverleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a spice in local cuisines.
