Lobelia grayana

Lobelia grayana, commonly known as Gray's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia grayana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
