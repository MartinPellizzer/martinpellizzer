Lantana hispida f. alba

Lantana hispida f. alba, commonly known as white lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to landscapes and pollinator habitats.
