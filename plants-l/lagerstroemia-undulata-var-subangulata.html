Lagerstroemia undulata var. subangulata

Lagerstroemia undulata var. subangulata, commonly known as the narrow-angled crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface, and the bark is smooth and mottled with varying colors. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its long flowering period.
