Landolphia delagoensis

Landolphia delagoensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
