Lavandula spica var. latifolia

Lavandula spica var. latifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its spikes of lavender-blue flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery-gray underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
