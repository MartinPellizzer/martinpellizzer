Lobelia gordonii

Lobelia gordonii, commonly known as Gordon's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Lobelia gordonii is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
