Lobelia nyassae

Lobelia nyassae, commonly known as the East African lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia nyassae is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
