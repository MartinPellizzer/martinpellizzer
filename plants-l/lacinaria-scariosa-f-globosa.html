Lacinaria scariosa f. globosa

Lacinaria scariosa f. globosa, commonly known as the globular lichen, is a unique variant of the species characterized by its distinctive rounded growth form.

This plant exhibits a dense, compact structure that sets it apart from other forms of Lacinaria scariosa. Botanically, it is a lichen, a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria. Its morphology is defined by a thick, crust-like thallus with a globular appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree bark or rock surfaces in temperate regions.
