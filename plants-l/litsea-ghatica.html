Litsea ghatica

Litsea ghatica, commonly known as the Chinese terebinth, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by berries that ripen to a bright red color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
