Lotus lathyroides

Lotus lathyroides, commonly known as the woolly lotus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its unique adaptations allow it to conserve water efficiently, making it well-suited to its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various regions outside its natural range.
