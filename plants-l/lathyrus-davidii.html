Lathyrus davidii

Lathyrus davidii, commonly known as David's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its pods contain compressed seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus davidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
