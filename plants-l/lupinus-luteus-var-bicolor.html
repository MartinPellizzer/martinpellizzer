Lupinus luteus var. bicolor

Lupinus luteus var. bicolor, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and white bicolor flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability also makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
