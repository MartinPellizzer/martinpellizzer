Limeum argute-carinatum

Limeum argute-carinatum, commonly known as the spiny leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is often found in protected habitats and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
