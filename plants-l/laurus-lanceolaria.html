Laurus lanceolaria

Laurus lanceolaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

Laurus lanceolaria is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and potential medicinal uses.
