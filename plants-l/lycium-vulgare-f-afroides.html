Lycium vulgare f. afroides

Lycium vulgare f. afroides, commonly known as the African wolfberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
