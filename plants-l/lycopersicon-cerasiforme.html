Lycopersicon cerasiforme

Lycopersicon cerasiforme, commonly known as the cherry tomato plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that produces small, round, red fruits resembling cherries. The plant has hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves that are typically green. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also edible, often used in culinary applications.
