Lycianthes jaliscensis

Lycianthes jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a globose capsule that contains several small, yellowish seeds.
