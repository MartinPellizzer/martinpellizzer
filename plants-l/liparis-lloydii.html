Liparis lloydii

Liparis lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Liparis lloydii is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
