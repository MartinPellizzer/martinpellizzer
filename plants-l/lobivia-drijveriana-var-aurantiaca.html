Lobivia drijveriana var. aurantiaca

Lobivia drijveriana var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange Ghost Cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on short, lateral branches. Its unique coloration and compact growth habit make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
