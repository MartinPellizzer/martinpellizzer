Leucocera sessiliflora

Leucocera sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower daisy, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense, flat head. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slight pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
