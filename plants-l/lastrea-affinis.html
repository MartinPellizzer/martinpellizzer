Lastrea affinis

Lastrea affinis, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid or rocky environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
