Laurus victoriana

Laurus victoriana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in cooking and have a strong, pungent aroma similar to true laurel.

Laurus victoriana is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
