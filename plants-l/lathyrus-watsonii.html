Lathyrus watsonii

Lathyrus watsonii, commonly known as Watson's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
