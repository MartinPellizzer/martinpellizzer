Lactuca lutea

Lactuca lutea, commonly known as the yellow lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stem and rosette of spoon-shaped, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which attract various pollinators. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Lactuca lutea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
