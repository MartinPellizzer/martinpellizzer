Linaria aeruginea

Linaria aeruginea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Linaria aeruginea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
