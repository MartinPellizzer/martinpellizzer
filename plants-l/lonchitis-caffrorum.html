Lonchitis caffrorum

Lonchitis caffrorum, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.

Lonchitis caffrorum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
