Leucadendron verticillatum

Leucadendron verticillatum, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and conical, spiky flower heads that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience in marginal environments.
