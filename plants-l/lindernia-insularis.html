Lindernia insularis

Lindernia insularis, commonly known as the island lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, with two lips that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Lindernia insularis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
