Lobelia rotundifolia

Lobelia rotundifolia, commonly known as purpletop, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia rotundifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
