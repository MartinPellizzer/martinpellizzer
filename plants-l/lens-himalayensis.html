Lens himalayensis

Lens himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan lentil, is a leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, lens-shaped seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces pods that contain the edible seeds.

Due to its nutritional value, it is an important crop in traditional agriculture within its native range.
