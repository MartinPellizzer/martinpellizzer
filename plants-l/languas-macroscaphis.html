Languas macroscaphis

Languas macroscaphis, commonly known as the giant spider lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that resemble spider-like structures, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and strap-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Languas macroscaphis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense forest undergrowth.
