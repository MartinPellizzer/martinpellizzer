Lonchocarpus nelsii subsp. katangensis

Lonchocarpus nelsii subsp.

katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga false toadflax, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
