Lonchocarpus heptaphyllus

Lonchocarpus heptaphyllus, commonly known as the purple heart tree, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red tubular flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its bark and roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it both a valuable and potentially hazardous species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
