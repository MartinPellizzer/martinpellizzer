Limonium savianum

Limonium savianum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium savianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
