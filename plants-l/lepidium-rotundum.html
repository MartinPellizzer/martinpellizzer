Lepidium rotundum

Lepidium rotundum, commonly known as peppergrass, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and have a peppery aroma. The plant produces small white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Lepidium rotundum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
