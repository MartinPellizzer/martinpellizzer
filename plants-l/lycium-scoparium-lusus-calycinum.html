Lycium scoparium lusus calycinum

Lycium scoparium lusus calycinum, commonly known as the desert sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It features small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in dryland ecosystems.
