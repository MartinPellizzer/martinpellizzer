Leucolinum tenuifolium

Leucolinum tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whiteflower, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, white flowers.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a smooth margin, and the flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses involving its leaves and flowers.
