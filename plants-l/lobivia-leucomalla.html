Lobivia leucomalla

Lobivia leucomalla, commonly known as the white-flowered lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright white flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
