Leucothoe axillaris

Leucothoe axillaris, commonly known as the mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pink, which bloom in the spring. Its adaptability to acidic soils and partial shade makes it a popular choice for landscaping.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
