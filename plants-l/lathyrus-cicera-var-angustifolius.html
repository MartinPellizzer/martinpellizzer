Lathyrus cicera var. angustifolius

Lathyrus cicera var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, angular stems. The leaves are alternate, with narrow, linear blades that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in racemes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
