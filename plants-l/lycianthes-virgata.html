Lycianthes virgata

Lycianthes virgata, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Lycianthes virgata is often found in dry, open habitats and is known for its ornamental value.
