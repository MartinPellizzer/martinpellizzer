Lagascea biflora

Lagascea biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered lagascea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Lagascea biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
