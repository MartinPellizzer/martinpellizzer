Limnanthemum brevipedicellatum

Limnanthemum brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed water primrose, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Limnanthemum brevipedicellatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, which can impact aquatic ecosystems if left unchecked.
