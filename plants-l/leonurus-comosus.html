Leonurus comosus

Leonurus comosus, commonly known as horse leek, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Leonurus comosus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
