Litsea cinerea

Litsea cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Litsea cinerea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
