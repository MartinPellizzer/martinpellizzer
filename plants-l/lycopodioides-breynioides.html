Lycopodioides breynioides

Lycopodioides breynioides, commonly known as the Breyn's clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and the presence of scale-like leaves that form a tight rosette. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically located at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Lycopodioides breynioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
