Lilac × varina

Lilac × varina, commonly known as the common lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Syringa vulgaris and Syringa patula, known for its hardiness and ornamental value in gardens.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
