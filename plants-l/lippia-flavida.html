Lippia flavida

Lippia flavida, commonly known as yellow verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has aromatic, narrow leaves. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lippia flavida is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
