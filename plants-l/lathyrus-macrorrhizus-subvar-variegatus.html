Lathyrus macrorrhizus subvar. variegatus

Lathyrus macrorrhizus subvar. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated broad-leaved sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with distinctive variegation, often featuring white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
