Lamarckia coccinea

Lamarckia coccinea, commonly known as the red lily or crimson lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically trumpet-shaped and arranged in clusters. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often dark green and glossy in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Lamarckia coccinea is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
