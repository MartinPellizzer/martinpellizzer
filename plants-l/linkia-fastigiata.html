Linkia fastigiata

Linkia fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Linkia fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
