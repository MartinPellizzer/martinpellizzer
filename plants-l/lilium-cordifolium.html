Lilium cordifolium

Lilium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its bulbs are edible when harvested young and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Lilium cordifolium is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
