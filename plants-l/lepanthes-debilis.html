Lepanthes debilis

Lepanthes debilis, commonly known as the weak orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. Lepanthes debilis is known for its ephemeral blooming period, which occurs during the rainy season.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within its ecosystem and its value in horticultural collections.
