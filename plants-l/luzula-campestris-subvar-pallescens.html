Luzula campestris subvar. pallescens

Luzula campestris subvar. pallescens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

Luzula campestris subvar. pallescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
