Loranthus falcifolius

Loranthus falcifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain sticky seeds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Loranthus falcifolius plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
