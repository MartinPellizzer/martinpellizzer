Linum capitatum f. minimum

Linum capitatum f. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf flax, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a low-growing habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
