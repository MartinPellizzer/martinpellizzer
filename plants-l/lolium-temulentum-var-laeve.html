Lolium temulentum var. laeve

Lolium temulentum var. laeve, commonly known as red brome, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are usually pink to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
