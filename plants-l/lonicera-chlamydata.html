Lonicera chlamydata

Lonicera chlamydata, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its red berries are edible and attract birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Lonicera chlamydata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
