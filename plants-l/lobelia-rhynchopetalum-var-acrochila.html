Lobelia rhynchopetalum var. acrochila

Lobelia rhynchopetalum var. acrochila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
