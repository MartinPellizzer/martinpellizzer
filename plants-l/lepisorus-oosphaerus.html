Lepisorus oosphaerus

Lepisorus oosphaerus, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright green fronds that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including the sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Lepisorus oosphaerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
