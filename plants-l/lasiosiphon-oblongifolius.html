Lasiosiphon oblongifolius

Lasiosiphon oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lasiosiphon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
