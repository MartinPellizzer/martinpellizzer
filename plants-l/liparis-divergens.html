Liparis divergens

Liparis divergens, commonly known as the divergent orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a distinct lip-shaped petal. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Liparis divergens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
