Lucuma casarettii

Lucuma casarettii, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, nutty flavor. The fruit's pericarp, or outer layer, is edible and contains the seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Lucuma casarettii is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in both culinary and medicinal applications.
