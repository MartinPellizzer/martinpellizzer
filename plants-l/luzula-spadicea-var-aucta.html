Luzula spadicea var. aucta

Luzula spadicea var. aucta, commonly known as the golden woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dense clusters of grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers. Its foliage is often a striking shade of gold, which contributes to its ornamental value.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is part of the Poaceae family.
