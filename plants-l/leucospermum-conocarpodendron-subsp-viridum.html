Leucospermum conocarpodendron subsp. viridum

Leucospermum conocarpodendron subsp.

viridum, commonly known as the green torch bush, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, conical seed heads. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal environments.
