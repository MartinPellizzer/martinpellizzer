Lippia abyssinica

Lippia abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian thyme, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, small, blue to purple flowers and narrow, grey-green leaves. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Lippia abyssinica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as attracting pollinators.
