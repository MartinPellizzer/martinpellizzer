Lapathum divaricatum

Lapathum divaricatum, commonly known as the zigzag gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lapathum divaricatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
