Leptospermum madidum

Leptospermum madidum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Leptospermum madidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
