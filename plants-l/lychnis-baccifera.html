Lychnis baccifera

Lychnis baccifera, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is also known for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
