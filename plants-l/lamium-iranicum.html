Lamium iranicum

Lamium iranicum, commonly known as Persian deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
