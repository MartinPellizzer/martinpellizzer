Liatris savannensis

Liatris savannensis, commonly known as the eastern purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy purple-pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Liatris savannensis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
