Lindera aestivalis f. xanthocarpa

Lindera aestivalis f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited sweet pepper tree, is a unique variant of the sweet pepper tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow berries, which contrast with its green to reddish foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit color.
