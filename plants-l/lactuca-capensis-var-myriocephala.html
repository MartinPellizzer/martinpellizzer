Lactuca capensis var. myriocephala

Lactuca capensis var. myriocephala, commonly known as the African endive, is a biennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often curly and tender. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
