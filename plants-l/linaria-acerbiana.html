Linaria acerbiana

Linaria acerbiana, commonly known as the bitterweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is known for its bitter taste.

Linaria acerbiana is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
