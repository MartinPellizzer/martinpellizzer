Lepidium pseudotasmanicum

Lepidium pseudotasmanicum, commonly known as the Tasmanian cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and features small, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately lobed, with a peppery aroma when crushed.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its edible leaves and potential medicinal properties.
