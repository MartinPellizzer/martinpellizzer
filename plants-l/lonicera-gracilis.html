Lonicera gracilis

Lonicera gracilis, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in late spring. Its berries are red and edible, though they are often consumed by birds.

Lonicera gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
