Lagophylla ramosissima subsp. typica

Lagophylla ramosissima subsp.

typica, commonly known as the typical branched mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows on the branches of host trees, such as acacia and eucalyptus, and has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. It is adapted to arid environments and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of the ecosystems in which it is found.
