Leptodermis oblonga

Leptodermis oblonga, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale green or white in color. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid environments with well-drained soil. It is often found growing in shaded areas of forests or along stream banks.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its adaptation to moist, nutrient-rich habitats.
