Loranthus erianthus

Loranthus erianthus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds in return. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. Its berries are often red or yellow and contain toxic compounds that can be harmful to animals.

Loranthus erianthus is found in various regions of Asia and is often associated with specific host species like the acacia tree.
