Linum uniflorum

Linum uniflorum, commonly known as the common flax.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linum uniflorum is widely cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in food and industrial applications.
