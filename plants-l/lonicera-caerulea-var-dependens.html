Lonicera caerulea var. dependens

Lonicera caerulea var. dependens, commonly known as the black honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are highly aromatic and edible. The plant produces opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are tubular and typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional medicine and cuisine.
