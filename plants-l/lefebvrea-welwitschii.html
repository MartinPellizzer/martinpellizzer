Lefebvrea welwitschii

Lefebvrea welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia and Angola.

It is one of the oldest living seed plants, with a lineage dating back over 140 million years. The plant is characterized by its two large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 4 meters in length. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in extreme arid conditions.

Its bark contains a resin that has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
