Lophiolepis balikesirensis

Lophiolepis balikesirensis, commonly known as the Balikisiren grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Bali in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight bluish-green coloration.

Lophiolepis balikesirensis is adapted to tropical climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
