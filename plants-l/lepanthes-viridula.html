Lepanthes viridula

Lepanthes viridula, commonly known as the Emerald Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-white flowers and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, contributing to its lush appearance.

Lepanthes viridula is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and beauty.
