Lopezia globosa

Lopezia globosa, commonly known as the globular lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, globular cluster of leaves and its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Lopezia globosa is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
