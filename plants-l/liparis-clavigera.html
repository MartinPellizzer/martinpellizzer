Liparis clavigera

Liparis clavigera, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and erect, spike-like inflorescence. The flowers are typically white or pale green with a distinctive, spurred lip that resembles a sword, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
