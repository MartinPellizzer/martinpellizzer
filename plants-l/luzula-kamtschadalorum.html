Luzula kamtschadalorum

Luzula kamtschadalorum, commonly known as Kamtschatka sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Luzula kamtschadalorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
