Lycium tenuispinosum var. friesii

Lycium tenuispinosum var. friesii, commonly known as the narrow-spined boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
