Litsea coreana

Litsea coreana, commonly known as the Korean laurustinus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Korea and parts of China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, dark blue berries. Litsea coreana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
