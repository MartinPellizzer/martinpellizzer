Lycium acutifolium

Lycium acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Lycium acutifolium is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy plant often used in xeriscaping and as a natural barrier due to its thorny nature.
