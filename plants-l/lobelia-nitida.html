Lobelia nitida

Lobelia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

Lobelia nitida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
