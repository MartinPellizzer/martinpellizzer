Lantana lanuginosa

Lantana lanuginosa, commonly known as woolly lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically orange or red when mature. Lantana lanuginosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
