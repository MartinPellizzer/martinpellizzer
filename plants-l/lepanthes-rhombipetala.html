Lepanthes rhombipetala

Lepanthes rhombipetala, commonly known as the rhombic-leafed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant features a short, erect stem and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals being slightly larger and more showy.

Lepanthes rhombipetala is typically found in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
