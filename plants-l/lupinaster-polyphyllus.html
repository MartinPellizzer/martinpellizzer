Lupinaster polyphyllus

Lupinaster polyphyllus, commonly known as the many-leaved lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
