Lophia

Lophia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent lobes, which are typical of the genus Lophia. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is also known for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional crafts and textiles.
