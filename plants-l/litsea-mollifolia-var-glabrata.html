Litsea mollifolia var. glabrata

Litsea mollifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by berries that are orange-red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
