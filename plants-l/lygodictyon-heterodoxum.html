Lygodictyon heterodoxum

Lygodictyon heterodoxum, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
