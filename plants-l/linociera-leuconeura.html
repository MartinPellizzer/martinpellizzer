Linociera leuconeura

Linociera leuconeura, commonly known as the silver-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, tropical forest environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
