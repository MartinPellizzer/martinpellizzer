Lycianthes luisana

Lycianthes luisana, commonly known as the Luisana bitterleaf, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, greenish-white fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is known for its bitter taste, which is due to the presence of certain alkaloids and glycosides.
