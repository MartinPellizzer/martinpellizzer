Lilium candidum

Lilium candidum, commonly known as the Madonna lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers and tall, slender stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stem.

The bulbs of Lilium candidum are used in traditional medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
