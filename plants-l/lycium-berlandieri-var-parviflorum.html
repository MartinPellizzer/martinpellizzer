Lycium berlandieri var. parviflorum

Lycium berlandieri var. parviflorum, commonly known as the Mexican wolfberry, is a species of shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Lycium species and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
