Lens phaseoloides

Lens phaseoloides, commonly known as the horse gram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in various cuisines and traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization.

Lens phaseoloides is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
