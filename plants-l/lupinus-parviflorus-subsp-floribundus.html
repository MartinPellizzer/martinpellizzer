Lupinus parviflorus subsp. floribundus

Lupinus parviflorus subsp.

floribundus, commonly known as the showy lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
