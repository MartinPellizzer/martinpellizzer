Lepidium diandrum

Lepidium diandrum, commonly known as the two-seed芥菜, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped. It produces two small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.
