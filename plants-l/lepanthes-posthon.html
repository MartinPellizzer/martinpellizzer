Lepanthes posthon

Lepanthes posthon, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces a unique, fragrant scent.

Lepanthes posthon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
