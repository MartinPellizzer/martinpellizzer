Lepidium hirsutum

Lepidium hirsutum, commonly known as wild cress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant has small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Lepidium hirsutum is known for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in cooking or as a herbal remedy.
