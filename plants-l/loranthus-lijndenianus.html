Loranthus lijndenianus

Loranthus lijndenianus, commonly known as the Indian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the Indian subcontinent.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.
