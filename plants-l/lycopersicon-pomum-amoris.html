Lycopersicon pomum-amoris

Lycopersicon pomum-amoris, commonly known as the love apple, is a species of nightshade plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or yellow fruits, which are typically round and slightly elongated. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals and has hairy, lobed leaves. The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
