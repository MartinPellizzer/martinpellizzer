Lepanthes setos

Lepanthes setos, commonly known as the hairy lepanthes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

This species is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically produces showy, resupinate (upside-down) flowers with a distinctive hooded labellum. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Lepanthes setos is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
