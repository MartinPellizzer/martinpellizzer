Laseguea acutifolia f. glabrescens

Laseguea acutifolia f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved laseguea, is a variant of the laseguea acutifolia species.

It is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glabrous, or hairless, foliage, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
