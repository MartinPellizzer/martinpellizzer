Lolium gracile

Lolium gracile, commonly known as slender ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in pastures and disturbed areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Lolium gracile is valued for its forage quality and is sometimes used in agricultural settings.
