Lupinus guggenheimianus

Lupinus guggenheimianus, commonly known as Guggenheim's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Lupinus guggenheimianus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
