Lunania grayi

Lunania grayi, commonly known as the gray's moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms that are typically 5 to 8 inches in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
