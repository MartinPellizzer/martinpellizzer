Lophostachys chiapensis

Lophostachys chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Lophostachys chiapensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
