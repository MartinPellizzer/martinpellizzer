Lepanthes pycnogenia

Lepanthes pycnogenia, commonly known as the dense-fruited slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth on tree trunks or branches.

Lepanthes pycnogenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
