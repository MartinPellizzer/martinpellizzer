Lellingeria paramicola

Lellingeria paramicola, commonly known as the Paramicola morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Lellingeria paramicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its rapid growth and vibrant floral displays.
