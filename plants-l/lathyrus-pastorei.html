Lathyrus pastorei

Lathyrus pastorei, commonly known as the pasture larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
