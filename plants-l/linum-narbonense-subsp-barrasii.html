Linum narbonense subsp. barrasii

Linum narbonense subsp.

barrasii, commonly known as the Barras's flax, is a member of the Linaceae family and is native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional dye production.
