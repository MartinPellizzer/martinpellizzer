Lobelia praetervisa

Lobelia praetervisa, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lobelia praetervisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
