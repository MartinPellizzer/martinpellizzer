Lupinus capitis-amniculi

Lupinus capitis-amniculi, commonly known as the mountain lupine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Lupinus capitis-amniculi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
