Lyperia racemosa

Lyperia racemosa, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow, during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
