Lockhartia hercodonta

Lockhartia hercodonta, commonly known as the pink lockhartia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, linear leaf and produces striking pink to purple flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and have a distinctive, spurred lip.

Lockhartia hercodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
