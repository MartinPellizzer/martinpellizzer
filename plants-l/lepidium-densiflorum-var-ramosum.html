Lepidium densiflorum var. ramosum

Lepidium densiflorum var. ramosum, commonly known as prairie sandwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
