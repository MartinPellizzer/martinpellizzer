Lespedeza kiusiana

Lespedeza kiusiana, commonly known as the Korean lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with several small leaflets arranged along each stem.

Lespedeza kiusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
