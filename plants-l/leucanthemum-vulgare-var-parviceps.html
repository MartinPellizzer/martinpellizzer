Leucanthemum vulgare var. parviceps

Leucanthemum vulgare var. parviceps, commonly known as the dwarf white daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant grows in compact clusters, reaching heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its foliage consists of silver-gray leaves that are finely divided, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
