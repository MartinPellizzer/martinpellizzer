Linnaea borealis f. maelarensis

Linnaea borealis f. maelarensis, commonly known as the Maelaren form of the twinflower, is a rare variant of the low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically appearing in pairs on slender stems. The leaves are oval and glossy, providing a dense ground cover in its natural habitat. This form is notable for its limited distribution, primarily found in specific areas of Scandinavia and northern Europe.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in ecological studies of alpine and boreal ecosystems.
