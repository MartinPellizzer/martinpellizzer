Lespedeza capitata var. calycina

Lespedeza capitata var. calycina, commonly known as the small-headed lespeadeza, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers, which are often followed by small, hairy seed pods. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly found in moist, well-drained soils.

Lespedeza capitata var. calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
