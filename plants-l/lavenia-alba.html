Lavenia alba

Lavenia alba, commonly known as the white lavenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or near streams. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lavenia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
