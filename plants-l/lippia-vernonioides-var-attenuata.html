Lippia vernonioides var. attenuata

Lippia vernonioides var. attenuata, commonly known as narrowleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
