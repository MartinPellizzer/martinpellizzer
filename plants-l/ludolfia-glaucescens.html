Ludolfia glaucescens

Ludolfia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, pale blue or white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the sediment, while its stems support the leaves and flowers.

Ludolfia glaucescens plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
