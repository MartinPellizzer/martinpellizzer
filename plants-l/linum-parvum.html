Linum parvum

Linum parvum, commonly known as small flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Linum parvum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in textile production.
