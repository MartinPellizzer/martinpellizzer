Lepanthes dielsii

Lepanthes dielsii, commonly known as the Diels's slipper orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are adapted for specific pollinators. Lepanthes dielsii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil to flourish.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a notable species in botanical studies.
