Lysimachia carinata

Lysimachia carinata, commonly known as the spiked yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, bright yellow flowers clustered in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia carinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
