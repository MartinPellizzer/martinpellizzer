Lobularia intermedia

Lobularia intermedia, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Lobularia intermedia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
