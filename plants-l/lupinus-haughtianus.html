Lupinus haughtianus

Lupinus haughtianus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant produces tall, spindly stems with clusters of pea-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus haughtianus is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
