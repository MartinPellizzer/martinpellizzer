Lespedeza buergeri subsp. tricolor

Lespedeza buergeri subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the three-color lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers that typically range from white to pink and red.

The plant is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, three-lobed seed pods that mature in the fall. Lespedeza buergeri subsp.

tricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
