Lomatia silaifolia

Lomatia silaifolia, commonly known as the silky lomatia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely hairy leaves that give it a silken appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody pods that contain several seeds.

Lomatia silaifolia is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
