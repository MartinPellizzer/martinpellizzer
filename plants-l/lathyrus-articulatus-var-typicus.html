Lathyrus articulatus var. typicus

Lathyrus articulatus var. typicus, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its pods contain several seeds, and it is often used as a forage crop or in ecological restoration projects.

The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
