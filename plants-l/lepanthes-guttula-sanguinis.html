Lepanthes guttula-sanguinis

Lepanthes guttula-sanguinis, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and green coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are small but showy, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes guttula-sanguinis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance.
