Leucas minimifolia

Leucas minimifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved leucas, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually lilac or purple in color and are arranged in whorls along the stem.

Leucas minimifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
