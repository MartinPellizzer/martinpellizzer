Leptocytisus hirtellus

Leptocytisus hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
