Litsea lanuginosa

Litsea lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly tallow tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
