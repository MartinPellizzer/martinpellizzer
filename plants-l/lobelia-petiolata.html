Lobelia petiolata

Lobelia petiolata, commonly known as slender bluebead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia petiolata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
