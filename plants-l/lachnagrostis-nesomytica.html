Lachnagrostis nesomytica

Lachnagrostis nesomytica, commonly known as the Nesomytic grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
