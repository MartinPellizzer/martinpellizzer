Lycoris × woodii

Lycoris × woodii, commonly known as the hybrid lily-of-the-valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant that combines traits from its parent species, Lycoris radiata and Lycoris chinensis.

It features striking red or pink flowers that bloom in the fall, contrasting with its strap-like, dark green foliage. The plant is native to China and thrives in woodland environments with moist, well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Lycoris × woodii is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread through rhizomes, creating dense clusters over time.
