Lysimachia vulgaris f. villosa

Lysimachia vulgaris f. villosa, commonly known as hairy yellow water-plantain, is a variant of the yellow water-plantain native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Primulaceae family.
