Lepidium lasiocarpum subsp. wrightii

Lepidium lasiocarpum subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, with hairy surfaces that give it a distinctive appearance. Its small white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The fruit is a small, hairy capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
