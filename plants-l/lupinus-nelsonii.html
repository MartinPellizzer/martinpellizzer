Lupinus nelsonii

Lupinus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus nelsonii is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
