Lasianthus longifolius subsp. laevis

Lasianthus longifolius subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy in texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
