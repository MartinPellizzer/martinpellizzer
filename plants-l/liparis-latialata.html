Liparis latialata

Liparis latialata, commonly known as the broad-leaved lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces a dense, grass-like foliage with broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant blooms with a long, spiraled inflorescence of small, white or pale pink flowers that have three sepals and three petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Liparis latialata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
