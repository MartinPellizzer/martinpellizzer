Laelia albida var. ochracea

Laelia albida var. ochracea, commonly known as the Ochraceous Laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive orange or reddish lip. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing fragrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers cool, bright conditions with high humidity and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of indoor and outdoor environments.
