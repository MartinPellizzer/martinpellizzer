Lycoctonum pterocaule

Lycoctonum pterocaule, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple petals and a prominent central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Lycoctonum pterocaule is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
