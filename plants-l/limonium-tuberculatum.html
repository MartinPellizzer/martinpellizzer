Limonium tuberculatum

Limonium tuberculatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiky, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often used in floral arrangements.

Limonium tuberculatum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in saline environments.
