Limnanthes alba

Limnanthes alba, commonly known as the white marsh plant, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant produces small, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Limnanthes alba is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
