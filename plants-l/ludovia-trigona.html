Ludovia trigona

Ludovia trigona, commonly known as the three-angled ludovia, is a unique flowering plant native to the humid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Ludovia trigona thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a rare and sought-after specimen in specialized horticultural collections.
