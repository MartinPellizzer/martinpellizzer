Lepismium monacanthum subsp. espinosum

Lepismium monacanthum subsp.

espinosum, commonly known as the spiny lepismium, is a species native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in both gardens and conservation settings.
