Luzula spicata subsp. pindica

Luzula spicata subsp.

pindica, commonly known as the Pindus woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and arranged in dense tufts. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, high-altitude environments.
