Leucanthemum graminifolium var. controversum

Leucanthemum graminifolium var. controversum, commonly known as the slenderleaf oxeye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
