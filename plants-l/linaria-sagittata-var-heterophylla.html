Linaria sagittata var. heterophylla

Linaria sagittata var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable arrowhead flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive black tip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped or arrowhead-like, giving it a unique appearance.

This variety is known for its variability in leaf shape and size, which contributes to its common name.
