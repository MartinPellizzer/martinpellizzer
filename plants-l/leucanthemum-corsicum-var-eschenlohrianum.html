Leucanthemum corsicum var. eschenlohrianum

Leucanthemum corsicum var. eschenlohrianum, commonly known as the Corsican sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, giving it a sunflower-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
