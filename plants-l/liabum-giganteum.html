Liabum giganteum

Liabum giganteum, commonly known as the giant liabum, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family, native to the arid regions of South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
