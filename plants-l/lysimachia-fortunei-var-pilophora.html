Lysimachia fortunei var. pilophora

Lysimachia fortunei var. pilophora, commonly known as the silverleaf threadleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
