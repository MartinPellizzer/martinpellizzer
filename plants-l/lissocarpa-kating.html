Lissocarpa kating

Lissocarpa kating, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Due to its durability and resistance to pests, it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
