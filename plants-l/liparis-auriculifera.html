Liparis auriculifera

Liparis auriculifera, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals often resembling ears, giving it its common name.

Liparis auriculifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
