Lobelia colorata subsp. taliensis

Lobelia colorata subsp.

taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis lobelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
