Lamium cardiaca

Lamium cardiaca, commonly known as heartleaf plant or purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often purple-veined and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lamium cardiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
