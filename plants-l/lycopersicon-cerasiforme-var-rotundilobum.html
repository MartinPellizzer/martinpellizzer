Lycopersicon cerasiforme var. rotundilobum

Lycopersicon cerasiforme var. rotundilobum, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a variant of the tomato plant characterized by its small, round, and often brightly colored fruits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to South America, particularly the Andean region. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually oval or heart-shaped. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetric, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, ripening to a vibrant red or orange hue.

This variety is prized for its flavor and is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
