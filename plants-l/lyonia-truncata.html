Lyonia truncata

Lyonia truncata, commonly known as the rough lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Lyonia truncata is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
