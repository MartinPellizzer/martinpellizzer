Lycium fagosum

Lycium fagosum, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans but may be consumed by certain wildlife. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Lycium fagosum is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
