Leucas urundensis

Leucas urundensis, commonly known as Indian chervil, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a mild, herbaceous scent.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
