Leucaena guatemalensis

Leucaena guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in agroforestry systems.

Its seeds are edible and can be processed into a flour-like substance.
