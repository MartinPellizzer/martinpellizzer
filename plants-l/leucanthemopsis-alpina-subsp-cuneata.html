Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp. cuneata

Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the alpine leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center.

The subspecies cuneata is distinguished by its narrow, wedge-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This plant is often found in rocky meadows and slopes at high elevations.
