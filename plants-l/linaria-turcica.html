Linaria turcica

Linaria turcica, commonly known as Turkish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Linaria turcica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
