Lobelia fawcettii

Lobelia fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This species is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and wooded areas where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
