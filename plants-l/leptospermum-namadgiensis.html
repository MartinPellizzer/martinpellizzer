Leptospermum namadgiensis

Leptospermum namadgiensis, commonly known as the Namadgi tea tree, is a shrub native to the Australian Alps.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds. It is often found in open woodlands and heathlands, growing at high elevations.

Its small, aromatic leaves are used in traditional bush tucker and have medicinal properties.
