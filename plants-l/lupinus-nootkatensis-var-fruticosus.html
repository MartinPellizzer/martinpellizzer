Lupinus nootkatensis var. fruticosus

Lupinus nootkatensis var. fruticosus, commonly known as the Alaska lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds.

Lupinus nootkatensis var. fruticosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing forage for wildlife.
