Lycianthes cuchumatanensis

Lycianthes cuchumatanensis, commonly known as the Cuchumatan eggplant, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated purple fruit that resembles an egg. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow with purple veins, and it produces small, round berries that contain the edible seeds.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in local cuisines.
