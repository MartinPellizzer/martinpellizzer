Linum dehiscens subsp. angustifolium

Linum dehiscens subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf flax, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length.

The plant produces blue to violet flowers in late spring, which are clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are characteristic of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.
