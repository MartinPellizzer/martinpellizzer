Lilium catesbaei var. asprellum

Lilium catesbaei var. asprellum, commonly known as the Appalachian tiger lily, is a variety of the tiger lily species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange and red striped petals, which are often speckled with darker hues. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
