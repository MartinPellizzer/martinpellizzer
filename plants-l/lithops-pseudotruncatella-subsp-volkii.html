Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp. volkii

Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp.

volkii, commonly known as the Cape Town lithops, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its pair of fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble stones, aiding in its camouflage within arid environments.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It requires minimal water and thrives in full sunlight, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
