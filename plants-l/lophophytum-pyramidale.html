Lophophytum pyramidale

Lophophytum pyramidale, commonly known as the flame plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Lophophytum pyramidale thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
