Lessertia frutescens subsp. speciosa

Lessertia frutescens subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub habit.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical collections.
