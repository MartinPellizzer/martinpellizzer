Lupinus paraplesius

Lupinus paraplesius, commonly known as the paraplesius lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched flower clusters that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinus paraplesius is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
