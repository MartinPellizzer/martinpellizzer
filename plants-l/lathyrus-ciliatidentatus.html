Lathyrus ciliatidentatus

Lathyrus ciliatidentatus, commonly known as the hairy-fringed vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple.

Lathyrus ciliatidentatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
