Lapeirousia plicata subsp. foliosa

Lapeirousia plicata subsp.

foliosa, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the Western Cape of South Africa. It is a perennial herb with distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or folded.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal gardens.
