Lythrum curtissii

Lythrum curtissii, commonly known as the Curtiss' verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other plants such as the poinsettia and the waterwillow. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lythrum curtissii is often found in wetlands and along the edges of streams, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.
