Linum tenuifolium subsp. marianorum

Linum tenuifolium subsp.

marianorum, commonly known as the Marian's flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
