Litsea elliptica

Litsea elliptica, commonly known as the oval litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for some bird species. Litsea elliptica thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
