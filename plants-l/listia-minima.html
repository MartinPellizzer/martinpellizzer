Listia minima

Listia minima, commonly known as the small white lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds.

Listia minima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
