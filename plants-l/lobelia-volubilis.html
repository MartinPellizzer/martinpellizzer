Lobelia volubilis

Lobelia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing lobelia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are opposite along the stems, and it often produces a dense mat of foliage. Lobelia volubilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant with attractive blooms.
