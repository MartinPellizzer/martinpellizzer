Luziola fluitans var. oconneri

Luziola fluitans var. oconneri, commonly known as the floating willow or water willow, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that float on the water surface.

It has slender, branching stems that root in the substrate, allowing it to thrive in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss and facilitates gas exchange.

This variety is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
