Laggera purpurascens

Laggera purpurascens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes aromatic scent.

Laggera purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
