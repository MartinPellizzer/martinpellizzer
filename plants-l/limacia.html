Limacia

Limacia, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Limacia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally to treat respiratory and digestive ailments.
