Listera banksiana

Listera banksiana, commonly known as the Canadian moonflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Listera banksiana is also known for its ability to form dense colonies, making it an important component of forest understory ecosystems.
