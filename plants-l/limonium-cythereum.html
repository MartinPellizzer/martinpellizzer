Limonium cythereum

Limonium cythereum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue or purple spike-like flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in dunes or rocky coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium cythereum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
