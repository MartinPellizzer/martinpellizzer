Limonium recurviforme

Limonium recurviforme, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, often forming dense clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium recurviforme is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments.
