Lindera benzoin var. verna

Lindera benzoin var. verna, commonly known as early spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado plants. The plant features small, aromatic leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and its flowers are yellow-green, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

This variety is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
