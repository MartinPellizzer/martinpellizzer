Laserpitium garganicum subsp. siculum

Laserpitium garganicum subsp.

siculum, commonly known as Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
