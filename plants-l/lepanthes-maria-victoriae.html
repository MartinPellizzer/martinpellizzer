Lepanthes maria-victoriae

Lepanthes maria-victoriae, commonly known as the Maria Victoria Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, colorful, and exhibit a unique structure with specialized petals and sepals.

Lepanthes maria-victoriae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
