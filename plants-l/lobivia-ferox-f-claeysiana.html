Lobivia ferox f. claeysiana

Lobivia ferox f. claeysiana, commonly known as the Claeysian Lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and its ability to produce vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
