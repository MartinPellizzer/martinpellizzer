Luzula multiflora subsp. nigricans

Luzula multiflora subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as blackstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green with a bluish tint, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown and are pollinated by wind.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker-colored stems and leaves compared to other Luzula species.
