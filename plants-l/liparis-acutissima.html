Liparis acutissima

Liparis acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Liparis acutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
