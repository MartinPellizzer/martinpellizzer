Limonium recurvum var. donegalense

Limonium recurvum var. donegalense, commonly known as the Donegal sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of western Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in sandy or shingle coastal habitats, where it is adapted to salty and windy conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in maritime gardens.
