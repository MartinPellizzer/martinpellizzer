Leiothrix flagellaris var. pedunculosa

Leiothrix flagellaris var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its attractive blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
