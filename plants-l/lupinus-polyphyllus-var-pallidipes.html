Lupinus polyphyllus var. pallidipes

Lupinus polyphyllus var. pallidipes, commonly known as the pallidleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of soft, pale blue to white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rocky or dry landscapes.

This species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
