Lygodium reticulatum

Lygodium reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined climbing fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spores are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread across various habitats.

The plant's intricate venation pattern gives it its common name and contributes to its ornamental value.
