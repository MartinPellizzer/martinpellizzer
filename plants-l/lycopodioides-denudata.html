Lycopodioides denudata

Lycopodioides denudata, commonly known as the naked lycopod, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and its simple, needle-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Lycopodioides denudata has a unique reproductive system that relies on spores rather than seeds.

Its structure reflects the evolutionary traits of early land plants, showcasing features like rhizomes and microphylls.
