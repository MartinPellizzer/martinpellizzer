Logfia arvensis

Logfia arvensis, commonly known as the field vetch, is an annual legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
