Lycopodium parkeri

Lycopodium parkeri, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that form compact clusters. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones at the base of its foliage, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. Lycopodium parkeri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
