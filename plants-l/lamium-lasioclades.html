Lamium lasioclades

Lamium lasioclades, commonly known as the woolly deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium lasioclades is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support pollinators.
