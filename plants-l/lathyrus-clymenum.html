Lathyrus clymenum

Lathyrus clymenum, commonly known as the pink-flowered pea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. Its small, elliptical pods contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Lathyrus clymenum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
