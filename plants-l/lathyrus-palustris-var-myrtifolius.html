Lathyrus palustris var. myrtifolius

Lathyrus palustris var. myrtifolius, commonly known as swamp vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It features slender, climbing stems that produce small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets that have a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small wildlife.
