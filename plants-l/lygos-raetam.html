Lygos raetam

Lygos raetam, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, woolly texture.

Lygos raetam plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
