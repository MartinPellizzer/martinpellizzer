Lindernia papuana

Lindernia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan lindernia, is a flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Lindernia papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
