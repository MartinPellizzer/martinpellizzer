Laurus piperita

Laurus piperita, commonly known as black pepper.

It is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae, native to the Malabar Coast of India. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the aromatic compound piperine, responsible for its distinctive spicy flavor. The berries are typically dried and used as a spice in various cuisines worldwide.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
