Linum siculum

Linum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linum in the family Plantaginaceae. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Linum siculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
