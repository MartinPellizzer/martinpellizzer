Lyonia brachycarpa

Lyonia brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lyonia brachycarpa is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
