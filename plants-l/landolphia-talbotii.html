Landolphia talbotii

Landolphia talbotii, commonly known as the talbot's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its vibrant floral display.
