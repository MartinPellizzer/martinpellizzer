Lilium philadelphicum f. andinum

Lilium philadelphicum f. andinum, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a botanical variant of the Turk's cap lily.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes Mountains. This subspecies is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.
