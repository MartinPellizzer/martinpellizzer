Lepidium montanum f. wyomingense

Lepidium montanum f. wyomingense, commonly known as the Wyoming peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographic distribution.
