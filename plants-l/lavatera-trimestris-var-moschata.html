Lavatera trimestris var. moschata

Lavatera trimestris var. moschata, commonly known as the musk lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and fragrance.
