Lupinus candidissimus

Lupinus candidissimus, commonly known as the white lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Lupinus candidissimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
