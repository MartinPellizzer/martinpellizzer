Lycopsis undulata

Lycopsis undulata, commonly known as the wavy lycopsis, is a small, perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, undulating leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and arranged in clusters.

Lycopsis undulata is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
