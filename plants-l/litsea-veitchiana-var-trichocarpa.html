Litsea veitchiana var. trichocarpa

Litsea veitchiana var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the hairy litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
