Lamium orvala

Lamium orvala, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lamium orvala is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for certain insects.
