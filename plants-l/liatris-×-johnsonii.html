Liatris × johnsonii

Liatris × johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's blazing star, is a hybrid perennial grassland plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are often branched and bear numerous small, tubular florets.

Liatris × johnsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
