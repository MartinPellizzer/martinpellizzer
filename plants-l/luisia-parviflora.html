Luisia parviflora

Luisia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered luisia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Luisia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
