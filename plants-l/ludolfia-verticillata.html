Ludolfia verticillata

Ludolfia verticillata, commonly known as the water plantain, is a semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features whorled leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense spikes, which bloom from June to September.

Ludolfia verticillata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
