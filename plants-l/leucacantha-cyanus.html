Leucacantha cyanus

Leucacantha cyanus, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branching.

Leucacantha cyanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
