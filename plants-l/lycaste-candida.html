Lycaste candida

Lycaste candida, commonly known as the white lycaste, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with a few leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its blooms are usually clustered in loose inflorescences and have a distinctive, sweet scent.

Lycaste candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
