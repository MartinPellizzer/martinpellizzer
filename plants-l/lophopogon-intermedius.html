Lophopogon intermedius

Lophopogon intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate lophopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Lophopogon intermedius typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodland or grassland habitats.

It is often found in the Carolinas and Florida, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
