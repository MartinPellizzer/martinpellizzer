Laurus lucida

Laurus lucida, commonly known as the sacred laurel or Indian laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

The leaves and bark contain essential oils that have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
