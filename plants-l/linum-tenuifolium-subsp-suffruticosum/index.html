Linum Tenuifolium Subsp. Suffruticosum

Linum tenuifolium subsp. suffruticosum is a unique variant of the common flax plant known for its distinctive shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. This subspecies is characterized by its narrow leaves and delicate blue flowers that bloom in spring. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked in favor of more showy flora. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for native pollinators and its potential for sustainable use in traditional crafts.
