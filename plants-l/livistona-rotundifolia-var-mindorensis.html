Livistona rotundifolia var. mindorensis

Livistona rotundifolia var. mindorensis, commonly known as the Mindanao palm, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The leaves are dark green and have a smooth, waxy texture, while the petioles are thick and covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. This variety is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce yellowish-white flowers, followed by small, round fruits.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
