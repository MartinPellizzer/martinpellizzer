Leucopoa karatavica

Leucopoa karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Leucopoa karatavica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
