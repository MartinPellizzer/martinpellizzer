Lobostemon montanus var. minor

Lobostemon montanus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser mountain lobostemon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
