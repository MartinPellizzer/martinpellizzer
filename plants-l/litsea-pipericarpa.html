Litsea pipericarpa

Litsea pipericarpa, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils are also valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.

Litsea pipericarpa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
