Lunasia grandifolia

Lunasia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved moonflower, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warm months, and the flowers close by dawn.

Lunasia grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
