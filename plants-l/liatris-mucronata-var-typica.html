Liatris mucronata var. typica

Liatris mucronata var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, spire-like clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
