Lavandula multifida f. pallescens

Lavandula multifida f. pallescens, commonly known as the silverleaf lavender, is a cultivar of the lavender plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

This variety originates from the Mediterranean region and is prized for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a dense covering of fine, silvery hairs that give it a unique appearance. It produces spikes of pale purple to lavender flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
