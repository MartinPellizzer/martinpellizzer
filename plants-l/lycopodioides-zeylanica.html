Lycopodioides zeylanica

Lycopodioides zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of scale-like leaves and produces spore-bearing structures called strobili. Native to the tropical regions of southern Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a unique morphology with a short, thick stem and whorled foliage.

It plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small organisms.
