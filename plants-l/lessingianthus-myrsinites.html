Lessingianthus myrsinites

Lessingianthus myrsinites, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, yellow-orange ray flowers that surround a central disc of smaller florets, creating a cup-like appearance. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
