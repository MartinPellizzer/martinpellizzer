Loranthus balansae

Loranthus balansae, commonly known as the Balansae mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which contain seeds, are dispersed by birds that eat the fruit.

This species is found in various regions of Asia, particularly in tropical and subtropical forests.
