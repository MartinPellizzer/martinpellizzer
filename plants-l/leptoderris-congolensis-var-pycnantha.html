Leptoderris congolensis var. pycnantha

Leptoderris congolensis var. pycnantha, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
