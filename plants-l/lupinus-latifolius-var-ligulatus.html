Lupinus latifolius var. ligulatus

Lupinus latifolius var. ligulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems with clusters of pea-like, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several broad, oval-shaped leaflets. This plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
