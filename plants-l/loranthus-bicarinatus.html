Loranthus bicarinatus

Loranthus bicarinatus, commonly known as the two-ribbed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to parts of Africa, including South Africa. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. It produces small berries that are often consumed by birds, which aids in seed dispersal.

Its parasitic nature means it derives some of its nutrients from the host plant, though it can also perform photosynthesis.
