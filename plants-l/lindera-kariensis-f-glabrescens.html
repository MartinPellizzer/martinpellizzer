Lindera kariensis f. glabrescens

Lindera kariensis f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf Japanese spicebush, is a variant of the Lindera kariensis species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its foliage and seasonal interest.
