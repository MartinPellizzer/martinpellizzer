Lysimachia filiformis

Lysimachia filiformis, commonly known as threadleaf filmy fern, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features delicate, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, translucent film, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Lysimachia filiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, ground-covering mats.
