Loranthus rubromarginatus

Loranthus rubromarginatus, commonly known as the red-margin mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees such as eucalyptus and acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in dense clusters on its host.

Its unique life cycle and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
