Lithocardium peruvianum

Lithocardium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian lithocardium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lithocardium peruvianum has been traditionally used in local herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
