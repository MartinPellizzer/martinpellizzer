Lycopodium wightianum

Lycopodium wightianum, commonly known as the Wight's clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lycopodium wightianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
