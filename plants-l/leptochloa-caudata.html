Leptochloa caudata

Leptochloa caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Leptochloa caudata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
