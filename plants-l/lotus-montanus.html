Lotus montanus

Lotus montanus, commonly known as the mountain lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, edible pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
