Lonchocarpus seleri

Lonchocarpus seleri, commonly known as the Seler's legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in woody, curved pods that open to release the seeds.

Lonchocarpus seleri is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution in handling.
