Ludisia odorata

Ludisia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ludisia odorata requires bright, indirect light and high humidity to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
