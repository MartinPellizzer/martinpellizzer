Lycopodium verticale

Lycopodium verticale, commonly known as the vertical clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive upright growth habit, with a central stem and small, scale-like leaves arranged spirally. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodium verticale plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
