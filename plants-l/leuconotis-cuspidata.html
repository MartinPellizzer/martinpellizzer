Leuconotis cuspidata

Leuconotis cuspidata, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with slender, woody stems. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
