Lasianthus capitulatus

Lasianthus capitulatus, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
