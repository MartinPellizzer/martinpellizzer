Loxoscaphe spathulata

Loxoscaphe spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Loxoscaphe spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to colonize challenging environments.
