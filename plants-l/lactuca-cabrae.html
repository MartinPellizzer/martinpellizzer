Lactuca cabrae

Lactuca cabrae, commonly known as the desert lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Lactuca cabrae is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
