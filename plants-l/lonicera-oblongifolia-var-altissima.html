Lonicera oblongifolia var. altissima

Lonicera oblongifolia var. altissima, commonly known as the tall honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red berries that mature in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or fences.
