Laurus leptobotra

Laurus leptobotra, commonly known as the slender-leaf laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain the seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.
