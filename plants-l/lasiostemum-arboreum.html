Lasiostemum arboreum

Lasiostemum arboreum, commonly known as the climbing bignay, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Lasiostemum arboreum is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
