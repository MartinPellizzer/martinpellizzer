Liriactis

Liriactis, commonly known as the lily of the valley, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Liriactis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
