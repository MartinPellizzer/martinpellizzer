Leptospermum trinervium

Leptospermum trinervium, commonly known as the narrow-veined tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves with three distinct veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
