Lithocarpus fenestratus var. intermedia

Lithocarpus fenestratus var. intermedia, commonly known as the Burmese tectona, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in lowland and hill forest ecosystems. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often featuring a distinctive pattern of raised ridges.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
