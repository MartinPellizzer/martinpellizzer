Lippia praecox var. praecox

Lippia praecox var. praecox, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a gray-green hue and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its square, hairy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its foliage is often used in herbal remedies due to its essential oil content.
