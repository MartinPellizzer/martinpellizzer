Languas capitellata

Languas capitellata, commonly known as the small-headed lanata, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, tubular flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees, and produce small, oval-shaped seeds.
