Lupinus pringlei

Lupinus pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus pringlei is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
