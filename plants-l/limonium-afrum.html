Limonium afrum

Limonium afrum, commonly known as the African sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

Limonium afrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
