Liparis trichechus

Liparis trichechus, commonly known as the horse's shoe orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, horse-shoe-shaped pseudobulbs and long, arching leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Liparis trichechus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
