Lythrum spicatum var. verticillatum

Lythrum spicatum var. verticillatum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spires of deep purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its root system is robust, allowing it to spread aggressively and sometimes become invasive in new habitats.
