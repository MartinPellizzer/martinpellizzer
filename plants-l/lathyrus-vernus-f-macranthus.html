Lathyrus vernus f. macranthus

Lathyrus vernus f. macranthus, commonly known as the giant vernal pea, is a flowering plant characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, clustered purple or pink flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with stipules and produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, elliptical seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.
