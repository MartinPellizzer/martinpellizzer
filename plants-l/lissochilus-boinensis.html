Lissochilus boinensis

Lissochilus boinensis, commonly known as the Boinense orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant's leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique floral display.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.
