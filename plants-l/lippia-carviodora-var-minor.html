Lippia carviodora var. minor

Lippia carviodora var. minor, commonly known as lesser caraway, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oil extracted from its leaves contains compounds like limonene and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive scent and potential medicinal uses.
