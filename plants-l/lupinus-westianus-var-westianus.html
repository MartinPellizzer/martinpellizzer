Lupinus westianus var. westianus

Lupinus westianus var. westianus, commonly known as the Westian lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
