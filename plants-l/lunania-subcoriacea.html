Lunania subcoriacea

Lunania subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant has rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
