Lychnis plumaria

Lychnis plumaria, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Lychnis plumaria is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
