Linum arkansanum

Linum arkansanum, commonly known as Arkansas flax, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes other species of flax, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Arkansas flax is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional dye-making.
