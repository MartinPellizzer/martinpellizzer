Larix dahurica f. glauca

Larix dahurica f. glauca, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a variant of the Siberian larch tree native to northeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance, especially in the winter months. The plant is a coniferous evergreen with needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical species. It produces cones that are often used in horticultural settings for their ornamental value.

This form is well-suited to colder climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
