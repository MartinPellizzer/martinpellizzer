Linum viscosum var. pyrenaicum

Linum viscosum var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves covered in a sticky, glandular layer. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
