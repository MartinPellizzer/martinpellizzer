Lysimachia pittosporoides

Lysimachia pittosporoides, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with glossy, waxy leaves that give it its characteristic silver appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Lysimachia pittosporoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
