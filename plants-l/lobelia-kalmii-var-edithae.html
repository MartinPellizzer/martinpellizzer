Lobelia kalmii var. edithae

Lobelia kalmii var. edithae, commonly known as the Edith's lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display in gardens.
