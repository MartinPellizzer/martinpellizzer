Lathyrus variegatus var. grandis

Lathyrus variegatus var. grandis, commonly known as the giant evergreen pea, is a perennial climbing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white, and its flowers are typically violet or purple. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in diverse habitats.
