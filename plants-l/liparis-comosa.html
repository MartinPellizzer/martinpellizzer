Liparis comosa

Liparis comosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with numerous roots and a rosette of linear, hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is adorned with fine hairs.

Liparis comosa is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
