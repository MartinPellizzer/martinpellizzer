Liquiritia officinalis

Liquiritia officinalis, commonly known as licorice, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal and commercial value.
