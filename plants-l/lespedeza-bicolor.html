Lespedeza bicolor

Lespedeza bicolor, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Lespedeza bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
