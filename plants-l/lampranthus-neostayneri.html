Lampranthus neostayneri

Lampranthus neostayneri, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has fleshy, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
