Lepidosperma filiforme var. neozelandicum

Lepidosperma filiforme var. neozelandicum, commonly known as the New Zealand flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clump-forming habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
