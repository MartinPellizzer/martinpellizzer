Lancisia minuta

Lancisia minuta, commonly known as the small lancisia, is a tropical tree belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
