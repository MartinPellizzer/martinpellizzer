Loranthus kalachariensis

Loranthus kalachariensis, commonly known as the Kalahari mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It attaches to the branches of host trees, such as acacias, and derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which contain a single seed, are dispersed by birds that eat the fleshy pulp.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, influencing both its host trees and the local wildlife.
