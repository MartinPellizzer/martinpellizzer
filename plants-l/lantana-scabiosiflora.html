Lantana scabiosiflora

Lantana scabiosiflora, commonly known as the scabious lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in a shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
