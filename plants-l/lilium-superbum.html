Lilium superbum

Lilium superbum, commonly known as the turkey cornflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to six feet in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or orange blotch at the base. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Lilium superbum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
