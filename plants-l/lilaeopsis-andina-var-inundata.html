Lilaeopsis andina var. inundata

Lilaeopsis andina var. inundata, commonly known as the Andean water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of the Andes.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with abundant sunlight.

Its presence is indicative of healthy, undisturbed aquatic ecosystems.
