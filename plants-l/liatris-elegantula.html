Liatris elegantula

Liatris elegantula, commonly known as slender blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy purple or pink flower clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Liatris elegantula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
