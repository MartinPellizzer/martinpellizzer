Lactuca scariola var. sativa

Lactuca scariola var. sativa, commonly known as garden lettuce, is a cultivated variant of the prickly lettuce species.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit, with broad, tender leaves that are typically eaten raw in salads. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. Its leaves vary in color from light green to dark green, depending on the variety.

It thrives in cool climates and is often grown in temperate regions for its nutritional and culinary value.
