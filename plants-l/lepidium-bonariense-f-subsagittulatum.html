Lepidium bonariense f. subsagittulatum

Lepidium bonariense f. subsagittulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers. The subspecies subsagittulatum is distinguished by its narrower leaf shape compared to the typical form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, early spring blooms.
