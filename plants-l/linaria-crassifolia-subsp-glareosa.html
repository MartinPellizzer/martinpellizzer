Linaria crassifolia subsp. glareosa

Linaria crassifolia subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the showy toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branched stems and produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive bilabiate structure.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and long blooming period.
