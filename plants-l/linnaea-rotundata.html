Linnaea rotundata

Linnaea rotundata, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive pairs of opposite, oval-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring, often in pairs. Linnaea rotundata thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
