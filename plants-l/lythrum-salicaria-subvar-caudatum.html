Lythrum salicaria subvar. caudatum

Lythrum salicaria subvar. caudatum, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in midsummer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in North America, where it outcompetes native vegetation.
