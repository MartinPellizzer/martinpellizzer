Lepidium chalepense var. pubescens

Lepidium chalepense var. pubescens, commonly known as Spanish needle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by slender, hairless, spiny fruits that resemble tiny needles. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
