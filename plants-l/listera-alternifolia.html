Listera alternifolia

Listera alternifolia, commonly known as the checkerspot violets, is a species of flowering plant in the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white and green mottling pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Listera alternifolia is often found in temperate regions of North America and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
