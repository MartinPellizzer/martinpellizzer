Larnax macrocalyx

Larnax macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant larnax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as moths and beetles.

Larnax macrocalyx is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
