Linnaea borealis f. nanella

Linnaea borealis f. nanella, commonly known as the dwarf twinflower, is a rare and delicate variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia, typically growing in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin.

This fern-like appearance and unique floral structure make it a distinctive and sought-after species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
