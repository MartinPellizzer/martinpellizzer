Lathyrus angulatus

Lathyrus angulatus, commonly known as the angular vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its trifoliate leaves are characteristic of the genus Lathyrus, and it produces elongated pods containing small, angular seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed habitats and can be a pioneer species in ecological succession.
