Lysimachia rubricaulis

Lysimachia rubricaulis, commonly known as red-stemmed chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, reddish stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically oval with a smooth or slightly toothed edge. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lysimachia rubricaulis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
