Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. pallens

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the common chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc floret, forming a daisy-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in herbal remedies.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
