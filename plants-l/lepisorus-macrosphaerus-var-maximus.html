Lepisorus macrosphaerus var. maximus

Lepisorus macrosphaerus var. maximus, commonly known as the large-sphere wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fertile spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
