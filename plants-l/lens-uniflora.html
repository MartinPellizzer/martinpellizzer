Lens uniflora

Lens uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower lens, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, papery pods and single flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small oval leaflets.

Lens uniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and unique floral structure.
