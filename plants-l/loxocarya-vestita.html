Loxocarya vestita

Loxocarya vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Loxocarya vestita is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
