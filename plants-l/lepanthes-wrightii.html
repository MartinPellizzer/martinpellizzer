Lepanthes wrightii

Lepanthes wrightii, commonly known as Wright's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
