Linum bootii var. rupestre

Linum bootii var. rupestre, commonly known as the rock flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the rocky regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Linum bootii var. rupestre is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
