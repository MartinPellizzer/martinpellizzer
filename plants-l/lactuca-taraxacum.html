Lactuca taraxacum

Lactuca taraxacum, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a fluffy seed dispersal mechanism. The plant is known for its milky sap, which contains latex and is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Lactuca taraxacum is a perennial plant that is widely distributed across temperate regions of the world.
