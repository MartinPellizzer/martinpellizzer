Lappula echinata f. brachystyla

Lappula echinata f. brachystyla, commonly known as the short-styled lappula, is a variant of the common lappula species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields or along riverbanks.

The short-styled form is distinguished by its reduced floral structure compared to the typical variety.
