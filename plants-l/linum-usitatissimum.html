Linum usitatissimum

Linum usitatissimum, commonly known as flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is cultivated primarily for its seeds, which are used to produce linseed oil and for fiber in textiles.
