Lonicera × propinqua

Lonicera × propinqua, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various landscaping applications.
