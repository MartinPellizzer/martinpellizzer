Lippia vernonioides f. subtruncata

Lippia vernonioides f. subtruncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The subspecies subtruncata is distinguished by its truncate leaf tips and compact growth habit.
