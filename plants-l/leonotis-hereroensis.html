Leonotis hereroensis

Leonotis hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a bushy crown of flowering heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
