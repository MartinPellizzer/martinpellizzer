Lingelsheimia frutescens

Lingelsheimia frutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sand verbena, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features compact, bushy growth with narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
