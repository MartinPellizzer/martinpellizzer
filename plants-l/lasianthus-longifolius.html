Lasianthus longifolius

Lasianthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are often pink or purple and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
