Lathyrus venetus lusus rosaceus

Lathyrus venetus lusus rosaceus, commonly known as the rose-larkspur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, rose-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features slender, upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are borne in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lathyrus venetus lusus rosaceus is native to Europe and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
