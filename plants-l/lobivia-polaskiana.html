Lobivia polaskiana

Lobivia polaskiana, commonly known as the Polaski's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features spiny, ribbed stems that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Lobivia polaskiana is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
