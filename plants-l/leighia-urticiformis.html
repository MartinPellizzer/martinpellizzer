Leighia urticiformis

Leighia urticiformis, commonly known as the stinging nettle orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble those of the stinging nettle plant, hence its common name. The plant has a unique structure with a fleshy, often hairy stem and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects.

Leighia urticiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
