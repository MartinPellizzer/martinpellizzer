Linaria ilergabona

Linaria ilergabona, commonly known as the Cape goldflax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Linaria ilergabona is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
