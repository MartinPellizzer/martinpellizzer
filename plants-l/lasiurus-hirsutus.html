Lasiurus hirsutus

Lasiurus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy vining morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers with vibrant colors, typically pink or purple, which bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

The hairy vining morning glory is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
