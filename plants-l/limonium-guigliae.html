Limonium guigliae

Limonium guigliae, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Limonium guigliae thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
