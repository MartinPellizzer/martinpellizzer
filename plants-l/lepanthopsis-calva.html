Lepanthopsis calva

Lepanthopsis calva, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers with green veins, which are often adorned with a yellowish or brownish lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often clustered at the base, contributing to its unique appearance.

Lepanthopsis calva is valued for its ornamental qualities and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
