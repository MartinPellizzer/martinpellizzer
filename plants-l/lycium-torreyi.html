Lycium torreyi

Lycium torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.
