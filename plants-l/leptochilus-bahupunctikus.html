Leptochilus bahupunctikus

Leptochilus bahupunctikus, commonly known as the many-spined rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in numerous small, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative succulent.
