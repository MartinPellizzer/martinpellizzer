Linnaea foveolata

Linnaea foveolata, commonly known as the dwarf twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers in pairs, which bloom in early spring and are borne on slender stems. Linnaea foveolata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
