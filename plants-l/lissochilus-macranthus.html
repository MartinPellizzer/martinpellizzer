Lissochilus macranthus

Lissochilus macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered lissochilus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family, which includes a wide variety of orchids known for their intricate floral structures. The plant is characterized by its elongated, pendant-like flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
