Lonicera persica var. occidentalis

Lonicera persica var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western Persian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, red berries that develop in late summer and are edible for both humans and wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
