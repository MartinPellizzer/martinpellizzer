Lupinus microcarpus var. glareosus

Lupinus microcarpus var. glareosus, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and upright growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
