Lupinus otto-buchtienii

Lupinus otto-buchtienii, commonly known as Otto's lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Lupinus otto-buchtienii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
