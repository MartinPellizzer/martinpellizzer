Lycopodium heterocaulon

Lycopodium heterocaulon, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that produce dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in sporangia that are clustered in strobila, which are cone-like structures at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Lycopodium heterocaulon plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
