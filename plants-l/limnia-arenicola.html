Limnia arenicola

Limnia arenicola, commonly known as the sand springtail, is a small, aquatic insect that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its elongated body and delicate, translucent wings. This species is often found in temporary pools, puddles, and other shallow water habitats. Limnia arenicola plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as prey for larger organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of water quality and ecological health.
