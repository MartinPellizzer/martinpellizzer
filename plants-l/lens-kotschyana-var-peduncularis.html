Lens kotschyana var. peduncularis

Lens kotschyana var. peduncularis, commonly known as the pedunculate lentil, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped pods that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both traditional agriculture and ecological studies.
