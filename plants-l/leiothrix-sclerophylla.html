Leiothrix sclerophylla

Leiothrix sclerophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
