Limacia blumei

Limacia blumei, commonly known as the Blume's lima bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and produces small, white to pale purple flowers. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous, pod-like structure that splits open when mature.

Limacia blumei is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional agriculture.
