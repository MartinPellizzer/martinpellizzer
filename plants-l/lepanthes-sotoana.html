Lepanthes sotoana

Lepanthes sotoana, commonly known as the Soto's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are usually short-lived, blooming briefly during the wet season.

Lepanthes sotoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
