Linaria cirrhosa

Linaria cirrhosa, commonly known as the woolly toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Linaria cirrhosa is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
