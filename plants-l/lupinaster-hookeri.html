Lupinaster hookeri

Lupinaster hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinaster hookeri is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of high-altitude flora.
