Lagenias

Lagenias, commonly known as the "cape marigold," are a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

They are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves, and they often grow in shrub form. Lagenias are part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
