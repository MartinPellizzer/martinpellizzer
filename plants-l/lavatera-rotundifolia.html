Lavatera rotundifolia

Lavatera rotundifolia, commonly known as the woolly sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has palmate leaves with rounded lobes, and its stems are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
