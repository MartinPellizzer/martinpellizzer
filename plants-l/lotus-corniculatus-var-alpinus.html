Lotus corniculatus var. alpinus

Lotus corniculatus var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with small, rounded leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
