Leucas dhofarensis

Leucas dhofarensis, commonly known as Dhofar leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
