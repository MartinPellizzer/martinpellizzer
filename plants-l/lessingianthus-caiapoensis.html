Lessingianthus caiapoensis

Lessingianthus caiapoensis, commonly known as the Caiapo sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open fields and along riverbanks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant and a potential ornamental plant.
