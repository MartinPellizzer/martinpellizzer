Lappula ulacholica

Lappula ulacholica, commonly known as the broad-leaved lappula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

Lappula ulacholica is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
