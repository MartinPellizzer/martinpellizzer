Lellingeria subimpressa

Lellingeria subimpressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes or rock gardens.
