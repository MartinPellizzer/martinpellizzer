Lobivia rossii

Lobivia rossii, commonly known as the Ross's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Lobivia rossii is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
