Lophiolepis × xenogenum

Lophiolepis × xenogenum, commonly known as the hybrid grass, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Lophiolepis linearis and Lophiolepis intermedia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Lophiolepis × xenogenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
