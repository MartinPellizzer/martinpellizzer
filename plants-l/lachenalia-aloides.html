Lachenalia aloides

Lachenalia aloides, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched stems with showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a dormant period during the wet season. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a sought-after ornamental plant.
