Limnas veresczaginii

Limnas veresczaginii, commonly known as the silverleaf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, glossy, silver-green leaves that float on the water's surface, while its flowers are typically white or pale pink. The plant has a short, stout stem with roots that anchor it in the muddy substrate. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss and reflects sunlight.

Limnas veresczaginii plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.
