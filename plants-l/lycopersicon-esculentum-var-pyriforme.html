Lycopersicon esculentum var. pyriforme

Lycopersicon esculentum var. pyriforme, commonly known as the pear-shaped tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, pear-like fruit shape, which distinguishes it from the typical round tomatoes. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family and shares the same basic structure as other tomato varieties, including hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow, and it produces fruits that range in color from red to orange.

This variety is valued for its distinctive appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental or specialty culinary purposes.
