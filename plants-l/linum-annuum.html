Linum annuum

Linum annuum, commonly known as common flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are used for both ornamental and industrial purposes.

Linum annuum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural fields.
