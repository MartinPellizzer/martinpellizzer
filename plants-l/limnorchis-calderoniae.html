Limnorchis calderoniae

Limnorchis calderoniae, commonly known as the Calderonia water lily, is a rare aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots anchor it to the muddy bottom of lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, Limnorchis calderoniae is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
