Lysimachia procumbens

Lysimachia procumbens, commonly known as yellow loosestrife, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia procumbens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
