Linnaea tetrasepala

Linnaea tetrasepala, commonly known as the four-lobed twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink or white, with four sepals and four petals, giving it the species name *tetrasepala*. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Linnaea tetrasepala is notable for its early spring blooming and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and flies.
