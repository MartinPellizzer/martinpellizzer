Lamourouxia laciniata var. pilosa

Lamourouxia laciniata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a variant of the lace fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely divided, and hairy leaves.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures on the underside of its fronds.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate and elegant appearance.
