Lucuma meruocana

Lucuma meruocana, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and showy white flowers. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a bright yellow or orange color, indicating maturity. The pulp is rich in nutrients, including vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber, making it a valuable food source.

Its unique flavor and nutritional profile have led to its increasing popularity in both traditional and modern culinary applications.
