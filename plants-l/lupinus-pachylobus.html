Lupinus pachylobus

Lupinus pachylobus, commonly known as the thick-leaved lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and compound leaves with prominent, hairy stipules. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Lupinus pachylobus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is a member of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
