Lathraea rhodopea

Lathraea rhodopea, commonly known as the Greek flame flower, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients through its root system. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a vivid splash of color to its rocky habitat. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture in its arid environment.

Lathraea rhodopea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
