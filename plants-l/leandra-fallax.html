Leandra fallax

Leandra fallax, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or leandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Leandra fallax thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
