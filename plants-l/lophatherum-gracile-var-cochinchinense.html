Lophatherum gracile var. cochinchinense

Lophatherum gracile var. cochinchinense, commonly known as the slender lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fragrant, lemon-scented leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.

Botanically, it exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense clumps.
