Lilac × rothomagensis

Lilac × rothomagensis, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and the plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.
