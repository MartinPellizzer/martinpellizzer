Laurus triplinervis

Laurus triplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves, which have a distinctive three-veined pattern on the underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
