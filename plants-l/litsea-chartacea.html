Litsea chartacea

Litsea chartacea, commonly known as the paperleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often translucent and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Litsea chartacea is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
