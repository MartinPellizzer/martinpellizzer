Lepanthes columbar

Lepanthes columbar, commonly known as the Columbar Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a short, erect stem that bears a cluster of blossoms.

Lepanthes columbar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
