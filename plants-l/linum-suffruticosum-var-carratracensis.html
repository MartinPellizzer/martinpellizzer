Linum suffruticosum var. carratracensis

Linum suffruticosum var. carratracensis, commonly known as the rock flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a sprawling or shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals arranged in a showy, racemose inflorescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
