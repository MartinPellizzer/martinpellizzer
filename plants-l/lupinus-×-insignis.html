Lupinus × insignis

Lupinus × insignis, commonly known as the hybrid lupine, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences with pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white.

Lupinus × insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
