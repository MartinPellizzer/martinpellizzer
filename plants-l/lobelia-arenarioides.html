Lobelia arenarioides

Lobelia arenarioides, commonly known as sand lobelia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh, sandy environments through its ability to store water in its leaves and stems. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss.

Lobelia arenarioides is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
