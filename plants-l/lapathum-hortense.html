Lapathum hortense

Lapathum hortense, commonly known as garden betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Lapathum hortense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
