Lathyrus alpestris subsp. friedrichsthalii

Lathyrus alpestris subsp.

friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in rocky and alpine environments.

This subspecies is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.
