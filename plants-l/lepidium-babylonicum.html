Lepidium babylonicum

Lepidium babylonicum, commonly known as the Babylonian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, peppery flavor similar to that of watercress. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found growing near water sources.

Lepidium babylonicum is valued both for its culinary use and its historical significance in ancient civilizations.
