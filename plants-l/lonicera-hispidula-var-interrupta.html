Lonicera hispidula var. interrupta

Lonicera hispidula var. interrupta, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
