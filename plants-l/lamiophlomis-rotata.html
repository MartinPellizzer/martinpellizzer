Lamiophlomis rotata

Lamiophlomis rotata, commonly known as Chinese cow wheat or mountain cow wheat, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Lamiophlomis rotata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
