Lotus argophyllus var. adsurgens

Lotus argophyllus var. adsurgens, commonly known as the climbing lotus, is a variant of the lotus plant characterized by its vining growth habit.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in wetland habitats.
