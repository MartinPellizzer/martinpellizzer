Lepanthes sobrina

Lepanthes sobrina, commonly known as the silver star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a delicate, showy flower with white petals and a yellow lip, which is highly fragrant. Lepanthes sobrina thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and moderate temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and orchid enthusiasts.
