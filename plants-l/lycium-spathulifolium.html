Lycium spathulifolium

Lycium spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant has thick, spiny stems and broad, oval-shaped leaves. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Lycium spathulifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
