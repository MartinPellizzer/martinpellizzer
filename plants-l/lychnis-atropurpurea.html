Lychnis atropurpurea

Lychnis atropurpurea, commonly known as purple sandwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a soft, woolly texture.

Lychnis atropurpurea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
