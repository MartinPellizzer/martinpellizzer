Leucocroton linearifolius

Leucocroton linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms, it is popular in horticulture for both its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
