Lupinus schwackeanus

Lupinus schwackeanus, commonly known as the Schwacke lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus schwackeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
