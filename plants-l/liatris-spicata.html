Liatris spicata

Liatris spicata, commonly known as blazing star, is a native North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear dense, spike-like clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
