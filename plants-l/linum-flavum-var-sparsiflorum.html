Linum flavum var. sparsiflorum

Linum flavum var. sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparsiflorous flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
