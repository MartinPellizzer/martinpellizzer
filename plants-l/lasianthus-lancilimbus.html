Lasianthus lancilimbus

Lasianthus lancilimbus, commonly known as the long-leaved lasianthus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of white or pale purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively obscure compared to other members of its genus.
