Lycium flaccidum

Lycium flaccidum, commonly known as the flexible boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Lycium flaccidum is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate.
