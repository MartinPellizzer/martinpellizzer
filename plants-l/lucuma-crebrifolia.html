Lucuma crebrifolia

Lucuma crebrifolia, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a tropical plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruit that resembles a small apple. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is rich in nutrients such as potassium, iron, and vitamin C. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value, combined with its culinary and medicinal uses, makes it a significant plant in both traditional and modern contexts.
