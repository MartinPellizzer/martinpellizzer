Lisianthius arboreus

Lisianthius arboreus, commonly known as the tree lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
