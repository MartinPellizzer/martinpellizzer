Leea callosa

Leea callosa, commonly known as the roughleaf leea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Leeaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are characterized by their rough, hairy texture and are usually elliptical in shape.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are borne in clusters, making it an attractive plant for ornamental purposes.
