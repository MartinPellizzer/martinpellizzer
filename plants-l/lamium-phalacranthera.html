Lamium phalacranthera

Lamium phalacranthera, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Lamium phalacranthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
