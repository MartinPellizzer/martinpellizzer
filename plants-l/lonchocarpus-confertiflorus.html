Lonchocarpus confertiflorus

Lonchocarpus confertiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its root system is known for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.
