Lycopodium pouzolzianum

Lycopodium pouzolzianum, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are scale-like and tightly packed, contributing to its dense appearance.

Lycopodium pouzolzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
