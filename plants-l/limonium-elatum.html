Limonium elatum

Limonium elatum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spikes of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or saline soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand salt spray.

Limonium elatum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
