Limonium pectinatum var. corculum

Limonium pectinatum var. corculum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and distinctive, spiky bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments and along the coast. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Limonium pectinatum var. corculum is a perennial species that blooms from late summer to early autumn, making it a valuable addition to coastal gardens and natural habitats.
