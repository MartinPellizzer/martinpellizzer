Luzula brevispicata

Luzula brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spiked wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula brevispicata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
