Linum corallicola

Linum corallicola, commonly known as the corallicola flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Linum corallicola is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
