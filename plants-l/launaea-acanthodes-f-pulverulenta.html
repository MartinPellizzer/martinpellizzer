Launaea acanthodes f. pulverulenta

Launaea acanthodes f. pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty launaea, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems. The leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the plant produces yellow flowers in clusters. This plant is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and low water requirements.
