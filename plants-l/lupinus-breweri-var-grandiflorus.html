Lupinus breweri var. grandiflorus

Lupinus breweri var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Desert Lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
