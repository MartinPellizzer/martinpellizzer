Limodorum abortivum f. brevicornu

Limodorum abortivum f. brevicornu, commonly known as the short-beaked adder's mouth, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flower structure that resembles the mouth of a snake, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are usually a deep violet or purple color.
