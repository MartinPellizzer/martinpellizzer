Lonchocarpus pallescens

Lonchocarpus pallescens, commonly known as the pale-leaved urera, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and is often found in understory layers.

Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive pale or yellowish color.
