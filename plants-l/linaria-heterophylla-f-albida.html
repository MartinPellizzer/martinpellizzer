Linaria heterophylla f. albida

Linaria heterophylla f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered toadflax, is a variant of the common toadflax species.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinguishing feature is its white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
