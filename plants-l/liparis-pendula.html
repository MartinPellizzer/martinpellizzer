Liparis pendula

Liparis pendula, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to various regions in Asia.

It features slender, often pendulous stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Liparis pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
