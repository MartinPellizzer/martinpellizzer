Lauro-cerasus zippeliana var. crassistyla

Lauro-cerasus zippeliana var. crassistyla, commonly known as the Crassistyla cherry laurel, is a variant of the cherry laurel species native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

The Crassistyla cherry laurel is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
