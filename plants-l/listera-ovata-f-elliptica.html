Listera ovata f. elliptica

Listera ovata f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic spring beauty, is a variant of the spring beauty species native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped to elliptical leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive silvery-blue hue. Its flowers are small, white, and bell-shaped, appearing in early spring.

This form is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaf shape compared to the typical ovate leaves of the species.
