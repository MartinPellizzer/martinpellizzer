Laurembergia verticillata

Laurembergia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
