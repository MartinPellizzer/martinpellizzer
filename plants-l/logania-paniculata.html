Logania paniculata

Logania paniculata, commonly known as the red paniculata, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, red-brown flowers that appear in paniculate inflorescences, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
