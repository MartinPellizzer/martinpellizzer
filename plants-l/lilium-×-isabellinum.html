Lilium × isabellinum

Lilium × isabellinum, commonly known as the isabelline lily, is a hybrid species within the genus Lilium.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces recurved petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped throat, which are typical features of many lily species.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural landscapes.
