Lupinus acutilobus

Lupinus acutilobus, commonly known as the sharp-lobed lupine, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with sharply pointed lobes, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. Lupinus acutilobus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is a valuable plant for both ecological restoration and as a source of forage for wildlife.
