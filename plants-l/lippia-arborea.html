Lippia arborea

Lippia arborea, commonly known as wild verbena, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dryland environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Lippia arborea is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
