Lychnis × hybrida

Lychnis × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid fireweed, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often forms dense stands in its native range.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
