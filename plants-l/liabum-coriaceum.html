Liabum coriaceum

Liabum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough leaved liabum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a leathery texture. Small, yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is commonly found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
