Lupinus Densiflorus Subsp. Lacteus

Lupinus densiflorus subsp. lacteus is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the western United States. It is known for its striking white and pink blooms that appear in late spring. This subspecies thrives in rocky soils and sunny environments, often found in coastal areas. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Protecting this plant is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native habitats.
