Loranthus pekanensis

Loranthus pekanensis, commonly known as the Pekan Mountain mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Dipterocarpaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is typically found in the rainforests of Borneo, where it thrives in humid and shaded environments.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
