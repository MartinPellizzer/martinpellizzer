Loasa macrantha

Loasa macrantha, commonly known as the mountain beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Loasa macrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
