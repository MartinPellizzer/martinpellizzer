Lycopodium brauseanum

Lycopodium brauseanum, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its tiny, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Lycopodium brauseanum is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period, and its unique reproductive system.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
