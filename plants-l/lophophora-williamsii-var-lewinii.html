Lophophora williamsii var. lewinii

Lophophora williamsii var. lewinii, commonly known as "peyote," is a low-growing cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, ribbed stems and clusters of small, pale green to blue-gray leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and it is known for containing the psychoactive compound mescaline. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its legal status and conservation concerns, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
