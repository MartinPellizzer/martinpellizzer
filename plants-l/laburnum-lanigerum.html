Laburnum lanigerum

Laburnum lanigerum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

Laburnum lanigerum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
