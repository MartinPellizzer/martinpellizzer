Limoniastrum multiflorum

Limoniastrum multiflorum, commonly known as the Mediterranean blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Limoniastrum multiflorum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
