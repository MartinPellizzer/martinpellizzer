Loxothysanus

Loxothysanus, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are covered in fine, golden hairs, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or in dense undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of its fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Loxothysanus is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
