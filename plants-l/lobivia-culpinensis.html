Lobivia culpinensis

Lobivia culpinensis, commonly known as the Culpin's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spiny leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to harsh desert conditions include reduced leaf surface area and the ability to store water in its succulent stems.

Lobivia culpinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
