Leucoium incanum

Leucoium incanum, commonly known as the silky bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Leucoium incanum is drought-tolerant and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
