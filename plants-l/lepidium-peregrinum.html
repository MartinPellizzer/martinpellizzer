Lepidium peregrinum

Lepidium peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering pepper or wild cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a peppery aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Lepidium peregrinum is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
