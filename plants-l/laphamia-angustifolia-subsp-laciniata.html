Laphamia angustifolia subsp. laciniata

Laphamia angustifolia subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the narrowleaf laphamia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely toothed or lobed.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the summer, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its foliage is typically covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
