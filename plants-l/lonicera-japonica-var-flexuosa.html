Lonicera japonica var. flexuosa

Lonicera japonica var. flexuosa, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive spreading habit.
