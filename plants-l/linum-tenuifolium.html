Linum tenuifolium

Linum tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is known for its ornamental value.

Linum tenuifolium is also valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used historically for textile production.
