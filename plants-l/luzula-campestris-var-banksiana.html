Luzula campestris var. banksiana

Luzula campestris var. banksiana, commonly known as the Banks' woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are accompanied by bracts.

Luzula campestris var. banksiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
