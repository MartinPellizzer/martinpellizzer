Lupinus subvexus var. albilanatus

Lupinus subvexus var. albilanatus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
