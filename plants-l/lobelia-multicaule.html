Lobelia multicaule

Lobelia multicaule, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia multicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
