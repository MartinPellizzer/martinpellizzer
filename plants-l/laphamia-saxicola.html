Laphamia saxicola

Laphamia saxicola, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, flat layer on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinct, granular texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found on limestone or sandstone substrates.

This lichen is known for its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions, making it an important indicator species for rock ecosystems.
