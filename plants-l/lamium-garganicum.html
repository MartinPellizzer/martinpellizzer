Lamium garganicum

Lamium garganicum, commonly known as the alpine dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and is often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Lamium garganicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
