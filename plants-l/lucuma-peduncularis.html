Lucuma peduncularis

Lucuma peduncularis, commonly known as the golden apple, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, and it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, nutty flavor, making it a valued crop in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it unique among its relatives in the Lauraceae family.
