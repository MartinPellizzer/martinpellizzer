Leptospermum pubescens

Leptospermum pubescens, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for coastal landscaping and horticultural use.
