Lespedeza capitata var. longifolia

Lespedeza capitata var. longifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, terminal clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
