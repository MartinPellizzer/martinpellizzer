Lepidium draba var. chalepense

Lepidium draba var. chalepense, commonly known as the Caucasian wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are usually narrow and pinnately divided. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
