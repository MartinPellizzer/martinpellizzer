Lobivia pectinifera

Lobivia pectinifera, commonly known as the Pectinifera Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and ribbed stems that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attractive. Lobivia pectinifera is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
