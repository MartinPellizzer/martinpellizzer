Lupinus propinquus

Lupinus propinquus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Lupinus propinquus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
