Lathyrus tuberosus

Lathyrus tuberosus, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
