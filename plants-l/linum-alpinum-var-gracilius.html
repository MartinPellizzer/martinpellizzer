Linum alpinum var. gracilius

Linum alpinum var. gracilius, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in textile production.
