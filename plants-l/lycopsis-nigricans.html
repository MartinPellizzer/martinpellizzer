Lycopsis nigricans

Lycopsis nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed lycopsis, is a rare and delicate plant belonging to the family Lycopsida.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green stems and small, scale-like leaves that are tightly clustered along the stem. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as strobili, are cone-like and contain spores, which are essential for its reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Lycopsis nigricans is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
