Leucochrysum

Leucochrysum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even when dried. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which make it popular in floral arrangements. Its scientific name, *Leucochrysum integrifolium*, reflects its white (leuco-) and golden (chrysum) appearance.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
