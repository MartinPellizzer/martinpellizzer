Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. angustifolia

Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cherry laurel, is a cultivar of the Prunus species.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a hedging plant.
