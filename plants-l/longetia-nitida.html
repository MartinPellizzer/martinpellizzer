Longetia nitida

Longetia nitida, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elliptical, dark green fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions.
