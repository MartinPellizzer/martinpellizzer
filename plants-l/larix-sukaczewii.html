Larix sukaczewii

Larix sukaczewii, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a coniferous tree native to the cold climates of eastern Siberia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its ability to thrive in harsh, snowy environments. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its needles are needle-like and turn a vibrant gold in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

The wood of Larix sukaczewii is lightweight and durable, making it useful for construction and other practical applications.
