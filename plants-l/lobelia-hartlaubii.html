Lobelia hartlaubii

Lobelia hartlaubii, commonly known as Hartlaub's lobelia, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia hartlaubii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
