Lacunaria panamensis

Lacunaria panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian lichen, is a unique plant that belongs to the lichen family, which is a symbiotic union of fungi and algae.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body formed by the mutualistic relationship between the fungal and algal components. It plays a crucial role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its native habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often used as an indicator species for environmental health.
