Leea naumannii

Leea naumannii, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Leea naumannii is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forests and along streambanks.
