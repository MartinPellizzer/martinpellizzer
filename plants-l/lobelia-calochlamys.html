Lobelia calochlamys

Lobelia calochlamys, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia calochlamys is valued for its ornamental beauty and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
