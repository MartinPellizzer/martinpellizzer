Limonium serbicum

Limonium serbicum, commonly known as the Serbian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, often forming dense clusters atop slender stalks. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Limonium serbicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
