Lonicera caerulea f. pubescens

Lonicera caerulea f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a cultivar of the honeysuckle plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by edible berries in early summer. These berries are often used in jams, wines, and traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
