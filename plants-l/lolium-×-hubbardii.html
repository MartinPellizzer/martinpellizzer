Lolium × hubbardii

Lolium × hubbardii, commonly known as the intermediate ryegrass, is a hybrid perennial grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and medium to tall stature, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage production due to its high nutritional value.

Lolium × hubbardii is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
