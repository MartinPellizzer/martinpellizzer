Lupinus nanus subsp. menkerae

Lupinus nanus subsp.

menkerae, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or blue.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is well-developed, often forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
