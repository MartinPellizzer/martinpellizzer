Lilium parryi var. kessleri

Lilium parryi var. kessleri, commonly known as the Kessler's lily, is a rare and striking variant of the Parry's lily native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
