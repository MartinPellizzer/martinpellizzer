Lucuma nitidula

Lucuma nitidula, commonly known as the white lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in desserts and beverages.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other lucuma species.
