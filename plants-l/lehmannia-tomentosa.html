Lehmannia tomentosa

Lehmannia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly lehmannia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
