Lythrum salicaria var. longespicatum

Lythrum salicaria var. longespicatum, commonly known as the long-spiked loosestrife, is a variant of the purple loosestrife plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of purple to pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in marshy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
