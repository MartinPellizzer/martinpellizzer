Lolium perenne var. spicatum

Lolium perenne var. spicatum, commonly known as Timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, leafy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Timothy grass is valued for forage and has been used historically in agricultural and horticultural practices.
