Lafoensia nummulariifolia

Lafoensia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the golden mallow, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
