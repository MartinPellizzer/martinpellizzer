Limonium ramosissimum subsp. tommasinii

Limonium ramosissimum subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences of pink to purple flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
