Lyciodes lanuginosum

Lyciodes lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its berries, which are usually red or orange when mature, are toxic and contain alkaloids.

Lyciodes lanuginosum is native to parts of South America and is often found in dry, disturbed habitats.
