Lupinus evermannii

Lupinus evermannii, commonly known as Evermann's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus evermannii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
