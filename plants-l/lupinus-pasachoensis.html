Lupinus pasachoensis

Lupinus pasachoensis, commonly known as the Pasachoensis lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus pasachoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
