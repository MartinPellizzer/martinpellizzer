Lepidium campestre var. pseudoheterophyllum

Lepidium campestre var. pseudoheterophyllum, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
