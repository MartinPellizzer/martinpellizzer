Lindera glauca f. glabella

Lindera glauca f. glabella, commonly known as the smoothleaf sweet pepper, is a variant of the sweet pepper plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that contrast with the typical hairy foliage of the species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary use in regional cuisines.
