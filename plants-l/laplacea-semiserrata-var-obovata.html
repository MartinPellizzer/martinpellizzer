Laplacea semiserrata var. obovata

Laplacea semiserrata var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-lobed laplacea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate to obovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is characterized by a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.
