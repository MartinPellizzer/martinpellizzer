Lychnis nesophila

Lychnis nesophila, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are pink or red, with five petals that are often adorned with distinctive markings.

Lychnis nesophila is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
