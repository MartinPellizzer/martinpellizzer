Lupinus cusickii subsp. abortivus

Lupinus cusickii subsp.

abortivus, commonly known as the Cusick's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Lupinus cusickii subsp.

abortivus is known for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and wildlife.
