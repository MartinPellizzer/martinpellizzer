Liabum longiradiatum

Liabum longiradiatum, commonly known as the long-rayed liabum, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cooler, temperate climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.
