Lithophragma tenellum var. ramulosum

Lithophragma tenellum var. ramulosum, commonly known as the Oregon grape or evergreen huckleberry, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves with a leathery texture and dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for humans and wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
