Lagochilus keminensis

Lagochilus keminensis, commonly known as Kemin thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Lagochilus keminensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
