Limonium tubiflorum subsp. maroccanum

Limonium tubiflorum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils along the shoreline. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh coastal environment.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
