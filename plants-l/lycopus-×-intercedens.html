Lycopus × intercedens

Lycopus × intercedens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Lycopus × intercedens is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
