Lycianthes biflora var. biflora

Lycianthes biflora var. biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered solanum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in groups of two, giving it its species name "biflora.". Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
