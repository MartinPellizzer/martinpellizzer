Lilium sanguineum

Lilium sanguineum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or mountainous regions. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, adding to its visual appeal.

Lilium sanguineum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
