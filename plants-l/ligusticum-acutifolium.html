Ligusticum acutifolium

Ligusticum acutifolium, commonly known as sharp-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and celery, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels, a defining feature of the Apiaceae family.

The roots of Ligusticum acutifolium are used in traditional medicine and are known for their strong, spicy aroma.
