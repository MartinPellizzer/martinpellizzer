Linum suffruticosum var. jimenezii

Linum suffruticosum var. jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's flax, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the flax genus.

It is native to specific regions in Mexico, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its harsh habitat.
