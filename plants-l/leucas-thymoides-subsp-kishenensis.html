Leucas thymoides subsp. kishenensis

Leucas thymoides subsp.

kishenensis, commonly known as Kashmir thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphology and geographical distribution compared to other members of the Leucas genus.
