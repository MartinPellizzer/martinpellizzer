Lobelia amplexicaulis

Lobelia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
