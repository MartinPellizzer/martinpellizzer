Lasiandra salviifolia

Lasiandra salviifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lasiandra, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its showy, blue to purple flowers are typically arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the branches.

Lasiandra salviifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
