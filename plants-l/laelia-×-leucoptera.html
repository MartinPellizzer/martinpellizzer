Laelia × leucoptera

Laelia × leucoptera, commonly known as the white-veined laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that typically feature white petals with contrasting purple or violet veins. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to gardens in cooler climates.

Laelia × leucoptera is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
