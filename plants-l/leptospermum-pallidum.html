Leptospermum pallidum

Leptospermum pallidum, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
