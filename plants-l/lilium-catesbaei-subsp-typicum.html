Lilium catesbaei subsp. typicum

Lilium catesbaei subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces red or orange berries containing seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
