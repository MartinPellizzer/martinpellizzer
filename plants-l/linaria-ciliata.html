Linaria ciliata

Linaria ciliata, commonly known as the fringed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Linaria ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
