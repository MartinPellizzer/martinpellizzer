Liparis piriformis

Liparis piriformis, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem with several oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often curved. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Liparis piriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in the right conditions.
