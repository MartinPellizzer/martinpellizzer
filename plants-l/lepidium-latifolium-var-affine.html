Lepidium latifolium var. affine

Lepidium latifolium var. affine, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, bright yellow flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a low-growing habit with multiple stems arising from the base.

The flowers are hermaphroditic, with four petals and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.
