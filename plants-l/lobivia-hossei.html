Lobivia hossei

Lobivia hossei, commonly known as the Ghost Cactus, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a compact, globular shape with ribbed stems that develop a bluish-gray color as it matures. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Lobivia hossei is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
