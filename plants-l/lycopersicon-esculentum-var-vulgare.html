Lycopersicon esculentum var. vulgare

Lycopersicon esculentum var. vulgare, commonly known as the common tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes potatoes and peppers. The plant produces clusters of bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branched.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in a variety of culinary applications worldwide.
