Licuala radula

Licuala radula, commonly known as the water fern or swamp fern, is a species of cycad-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and has a slow growth rate. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.

Licuala radula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
