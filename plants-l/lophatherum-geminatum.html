Lophatherum geminatum

Lophatherum geminatum, commonly known as the Chinese silvergrass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lophatherum geminatum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
