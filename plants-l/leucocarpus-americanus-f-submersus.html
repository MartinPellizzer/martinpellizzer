Leucocarpus americanus f. submersus

Leucocarpus americanus f. submersus, commonly known as the submerged swamp hibiscus, is a unique variant of the swamp hibiscus.

It is characterized by its semi-aquatic growth habit, with its stems and leaves often submerged in water. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture to withstand wet conditions. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink, and they bloom throughout the warm season.

This subspecies is often found in marshy areas and is adapted to thrive in saturated soil conditions.
