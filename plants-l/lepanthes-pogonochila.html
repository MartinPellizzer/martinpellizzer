Lepanthes pogonochila

Lepanthes pogonochila, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in a loose raceme. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Lepanthes pogonochila is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
