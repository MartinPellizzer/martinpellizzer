Leontodon hispidus subsp. hyoseroides

Leontodon hispidus subsp.

hyoseroides, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its hairy stems and serrated leaves are distinctive features, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
