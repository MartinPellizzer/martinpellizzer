Leptospermum erythrocarpum

Leptospermum erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Leptospermum erythrocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
