Lotononis lotoidea

Lotononis lotoidea, commonly known as the African lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.
