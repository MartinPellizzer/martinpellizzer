Lysimachia nemoralis

Lysimachia nemoralis, commonly known as the wood sorrel or lesser wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. Its small, fragrant flowers bloom from spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lysimachia nemoralis is often found in moist, acidic soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
