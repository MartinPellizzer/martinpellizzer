Lonicera flexuosa f. halliana

Lonicera flexuosa f. halliana, commonly known as the Hall's honeysuckle, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, silver-white hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy flowers and aromatic qualities.

This variety is a popular choice for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
