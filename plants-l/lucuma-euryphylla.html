Lucuma euryphylla

Lucuma euryphylla, commonly known as the golden apple, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.

It is valued not only for its unique taste but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional significance in Andean culture.
