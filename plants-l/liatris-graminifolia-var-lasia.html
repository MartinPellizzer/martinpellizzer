Liatris graminifolia var. lasia

Liatris graminifolia var. lasia, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
