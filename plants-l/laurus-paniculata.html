Laurus paniculata

Laurus paniculata, commonly known as the sweet bay or Turkish bay tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
