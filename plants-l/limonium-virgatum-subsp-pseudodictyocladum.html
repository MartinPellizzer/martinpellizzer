Limonium virgatum subsp. pseudodictyocladum

Limonium virgatum subsp.

pseudodictyocladum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils, thriving in coastal environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
