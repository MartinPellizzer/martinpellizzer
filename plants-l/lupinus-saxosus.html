Lupinus saxosus

Lupinus saxosus, commonly known as the saxicolous lupin, is a perennial flowering plant native to the rocky and arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has slender, often branched stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices and on slopes.

Lupinus saxosus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
