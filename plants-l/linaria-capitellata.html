Linaria capitellata

Linaria capitellata, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features slender stems and produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria capitellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
