Linum pseudoelegans

Linum pseudoelegans, commonly known as the false flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Linum pseudoelegans is an important plant for pollinators and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
