Lappula ramulosa

Lappula ramulosa, commonly known as the slender lappula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Chenopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and sandy areas. Its structure includes a short stem with branching, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture.

Lappula ramulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its potential diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
