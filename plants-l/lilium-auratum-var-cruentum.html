Lilium auratum var. cruentum

Lilium auratum var. cruentum, commonly known as the red lily, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers with a deep red to crimson hue. The petals are usually reflexed, giving the blooms a dramatic, almost fiery appearance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in late spring to early summer.
