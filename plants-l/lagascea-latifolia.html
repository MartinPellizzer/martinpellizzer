Lagascea latifolia

Lagascea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lagascea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
