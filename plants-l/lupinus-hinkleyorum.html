Lupinus hinkleyorum

Lupinus hinkleyorum, commonly known as Hinkley's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus hinkleyorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
