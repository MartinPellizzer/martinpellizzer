Landolphia petersiana var. rotundifolia

Landolphia petersiana var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is a climbing vine with a strong, twisting stem that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The flowers are typically pollinated by birds and insects, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
