Lupinus holosericeus

Lupinus holosericeus, commonly known as the silky lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and its clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with silky, hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lupinus holosericeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
