Lupinus uncialis

Lupinus uncialis, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus uncialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
