Lonchocarpus hylobius

Lonchocarpus hylobius, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
