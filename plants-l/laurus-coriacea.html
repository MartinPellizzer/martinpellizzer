Laurus coriacea

Laurus coriacea, commonly known as the bay leaf tree or Indian bay leaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters, with dense, dark green foliage. Its flowers are small and white, often followed by orange or red fruits that contain a single seed.

The leaves are harvested when they are fully mature, and they contain essential oils that contribute to their distinctive fragrance and various uses.
