Loxostemon repens

Loxostemon repens, commonly known as the creeping lily or wandering lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in a prostrate or decumbent manner, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Loxostemon repens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.
