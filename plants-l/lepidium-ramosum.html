Lepidium ramosum

Lepidium ramosum, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its small, white or yellow flowers are arranged in racemes, and the plant produces small, rounded seeds.

Lepidium ramosum is often used in traditional medicine and is also a common weed in agricultural settings.
