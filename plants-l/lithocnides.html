Lithocnides

Lithocnides, commonly known as rock lice, are small, herbaceous plants that thrive in rocky and arid environments.

They are characterized by their compact growth habit and often have a mat-forming structure that helps them conserve moisture. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which reduces water loss. Lithocnides species are often found in coastal areas and dry mountain regions where they can tolerate harsh conditions.

These plants play a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in their native habitats.
