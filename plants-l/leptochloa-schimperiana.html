Leptochloa schimperiana

Leptochloa schimperiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Leptochloa schimperiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor soils.
