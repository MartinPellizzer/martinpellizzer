Leucanthemum × superbum

Leucanthemum × superbum, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white ray flowers surrounded by yellow disc flowers, resembling the classic daisy pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Leucanthemum × superbum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
