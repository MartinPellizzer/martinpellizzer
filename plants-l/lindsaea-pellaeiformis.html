Lindsaea pellaeiformis

Lindsaea pellaeiformis, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry fronds that give it a soft, velvety appearance. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered spores on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
