Lesquerella kingii subsp. diversifolia

Lesquerella kingii subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as the mountain lesquerella, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its potential in ecological restoration and as a source of natural oils.
