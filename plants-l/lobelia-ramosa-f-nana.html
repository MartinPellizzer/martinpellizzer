Lobelia ramosa f. nana

Lobelia ramosa f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with numerous small, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
