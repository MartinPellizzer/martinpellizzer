Libanothamnus neriifolius var. cristamontis

Libanothamnus neriifolius var. cristamontis, commonly known as the mountain buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture, and the plant produces small, dark berries in the fall. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
