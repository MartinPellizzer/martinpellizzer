Larix kaempferi f. pendula

Larix kaempferi f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a unique variant of the Japanese larch.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it an elegant and distinctive appearance. This deciduous conifer produces needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before falling. The tree is native to Japan and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
