Lycium chilense

Lycium chilense, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Lycium chilense thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
