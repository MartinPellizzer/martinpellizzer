Lupinus nootkatensis var. glaber

Lupinus nootkatensis var. glaber, commonly known as the Alaska lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and coastal habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a nitrogen-fixing plant that supports local biodiversity.
