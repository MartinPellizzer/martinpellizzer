Ludwigia inclinata

Ludwigia inclinata, commonly known as the water lettuce, is a floating aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It has soft, velvety leaves that are oval-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and marshes.

Ludwigia inclinata is known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it a useful plant in water treatment systems.
