Lycopodioides chrysocaulos

Lycopodioides chrysocaulos, commonly known as the golden-topped clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobila.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant's unique feature is its golden-colored terminal strobila, which contrasts with its dark green foliage. Lycopodioides chrysocaulos reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
