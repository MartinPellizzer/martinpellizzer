Linum obtusifolium

Linum obtusifolium, commonly known as blunt-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

Linum obtusifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
