Lacinaria kansana

Lacinaria kansana, commonly known as the Kansan lichen, is a unique species of lichen that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is known for its crustose growth form, forming a thin, tightly adherent layer on rock surfaces. The thallus of Lacinaria kansana is typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a granular texture that helps it retain moisture. It lacks rhizines and has a complex structure with both upper and lower cortical layers.

This lichen is often found in shaded, moist environments, thriving in temperate forest ecosystems.
