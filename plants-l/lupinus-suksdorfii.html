Lupinus suksdorfii

Lupinus suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Lupinus suksdorfii is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife species.
