Lobelia serrata

Lobelia serrata, commonly known as the serrated lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are serrated, giving it its common name, and it has a fibrous root system.

Lobelia serrata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
