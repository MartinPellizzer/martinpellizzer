Lisianthius troyanus

Lisianthius troyanus, commonly known as the Troyan lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
