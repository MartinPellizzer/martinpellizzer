Lepanthes johnsonii subsp. costaricensis

Lepanthes johnsonii subsp.

costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican sun orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests.

The plant features small, showy flowers with intricate patterns of white, yellow, and green, often resembling sunflowers. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its evergreen foliage.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
