Luxemburgia damazioana

Luxemburgia damazioana, commonly known as the Damazioana luxembourga, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
