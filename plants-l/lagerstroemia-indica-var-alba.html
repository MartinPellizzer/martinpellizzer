Lagerstroemia indica var. alba

Lagerstroemia indica var. alba, commonly known as the crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, long-lasting flowers that range from pink to white, which bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, papery bracts that give it its common name. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
