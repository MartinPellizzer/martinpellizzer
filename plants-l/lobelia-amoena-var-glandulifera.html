Lobelia amoena var. glandulifera

Lobelia amoena var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often covered with small glands, giving it its scientific name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
