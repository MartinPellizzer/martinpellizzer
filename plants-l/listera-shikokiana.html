Listera shikokiana

Listera shikokiana, commonly known as the Shikoku violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a low-growing habit with a short, creeping stem and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Listera shikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
