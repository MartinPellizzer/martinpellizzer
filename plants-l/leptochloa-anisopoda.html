Leptochloa anisopoda

Leptochloa anisopoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-gray hue and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Leptochloa anisopoda is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
