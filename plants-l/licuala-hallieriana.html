Licuala hallieriana

Licuala hallieriana, commonly known as the Hallier's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its trunk is typically slender and covered with persistent leaf bases, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Licuala hallieriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
