Lachnagrostis semibarbata var. filifolia

Lachnagrostis semibarbata var. filifolia, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variant is distinguished by its filiform (thread-like) leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
