Ludwigia zeylanica

Ludwigia zeylanica, commonly known as the Java Ludwigia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. Its small, clustered flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ludwigia zeylanica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to aquatic conditions.
