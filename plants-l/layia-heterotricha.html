Layia heterotricha

Layia heterotricha, commonly known as the heterotrichus blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, making it visually striking. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Layia heterotricha is often used in native plant gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
