Lonicera strigosissima

Lonicera strigosissima, commonly known as hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems covered in dense, grayish-white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in clusters of two to six. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Lonicera strigosissima is often found in moist, wooded areas and can spread aggressively through its runners.
