Linaria striata var. ramosissima

Linaria striata var. ramosissima, commonly known as the branched toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
