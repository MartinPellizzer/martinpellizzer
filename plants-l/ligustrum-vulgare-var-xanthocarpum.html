Ligustrum vulgare var. xanthocarpum

Ligustrum vulgare var. xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited privet, is a variant of the common privet.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the yellowish-orange berries it produces, which contrast with the typical black fruits of the species.

It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
