Leonia pilosa

Leonia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties used in traditional herbal remedies.

Leonia pilosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
