Lysimachia similis

Lysimachia similis, commonly known as the common yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in early summer.

Lysimachia similis is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
