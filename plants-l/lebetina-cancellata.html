Lebetina cancellata

Lebetina cancellata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are finely divided and arching in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The intricate structure of its leaves makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
