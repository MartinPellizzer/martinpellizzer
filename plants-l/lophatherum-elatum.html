Lophatherum elatum

Lophatherum elatum, commonly known as the common arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Lophatherum elatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
