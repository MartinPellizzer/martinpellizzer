Lachnagrostis ciliata

Lachnagrostis ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or slender feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Lachnagrostis ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
