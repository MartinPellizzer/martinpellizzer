Leontodon autumnalis var. merinoi

Leontodon autumnalis var. merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's autumn dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing a low, mat-forming growth habit. Its yellow flowers appear in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
