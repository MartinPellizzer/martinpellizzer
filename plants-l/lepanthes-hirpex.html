Lepanthes hirpex

Lepanthes hirpex, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with bright red tips. Its flowers are often pale green or white with a distinctive red or orange lip.

Lepanthes hirpex is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
