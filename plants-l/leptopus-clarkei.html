Leptopus clarkei

Leptopus clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in maritime flora.
