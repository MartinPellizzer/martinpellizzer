Lathyrus apenninus

Lathyrus apenninus, commonly known as the Apennine vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet.

Lathyrus apenninus is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
