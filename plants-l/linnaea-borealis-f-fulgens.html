Linnaea borealis f. fulgens

Linnaea borealis f. fulgens, commonly known as the fireweed or burning flower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower.

This plant is characterized by its vivid red flowers, which contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in boreal forests and mountainous regions. The plant's botanical features include opposite, elliptical leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

Its unique coloration makes it a distinctive and sought-after species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
