Lysimachia moupinensis

Lysimachia moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin yellowflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Lysimachia moupinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
