Larix potaninii subsp. chinensis

Larix potaninii subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern China. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that turn golden yellow before falling in autumn.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds surrounded by a wing for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese horticulture.
