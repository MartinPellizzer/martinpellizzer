Landia

Landia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Landia is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
