Lycopersicon atacamense

Lycopersicon atacamense, commonly known as the Atacama tomato, is a rare and unique species native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are adapted to thrive in extreme desert conditions. The plant has a low-growing, shrub-like structure with thick, fleshy leaves that help conserve water. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces fruit that is often used in local cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
