Linaria dalmatica subsp. macedonica

Linaria dalmatica subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
