Linaria burjatica

Linaria burjatica, commonly known as the Burj Al Arab flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive bilateral symmetry.

Linaria burjatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
