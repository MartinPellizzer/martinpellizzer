Litsea ledermannii

Litsea ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are edible for some bird species.

Litsea ledermannii is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
