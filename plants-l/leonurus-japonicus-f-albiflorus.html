Leonurus japonicus f. albiflorus

Leonurus japonicus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog flower, is a variant of the hedgehog flower species native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique appearance.
