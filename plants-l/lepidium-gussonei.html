Lepidium gussonei

Lepidium gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its small, erect stature and produces tiny white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically lobed and can be hairy, while its seeds are small and capsule-like.

Lepidium gussonei is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
