Laelia maryaniae

Laelia maryaniae, commonly known as the Maryan's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored blooms, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for cultivation in botanical gardens and among orchid enthusiasts.
