Listera amplexicaulis

Listera amplexicaulis, commonly known as the heartleaf basketflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that clasping the stem, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, acidic soils.
