Licuala fordiana

Licuala fordiana, commonly known as the Ford's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit of Licuala fordiana is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.
