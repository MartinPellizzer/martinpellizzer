Litsea glutinosa var. normalis

Litsea glutinosa var. normalis, commonly known as the sticky laurel.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae, native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, dark blue-black berries that are edible but have a strong, resinous flavor.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and aromatic properties.
