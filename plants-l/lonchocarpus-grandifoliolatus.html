Lonchocarpus grandifoliolatus

Lonchocarpus grandifoliolatus, commonly known as the large-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
