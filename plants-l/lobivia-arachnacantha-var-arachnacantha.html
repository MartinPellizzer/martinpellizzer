Lobivia arachnacantha var. arachnacantha

Lobivia arachnacantha var. arachnacantha, commonly known as the spider-tailed cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that branch into spiny, segmented clusters, giving it a distinctive, spiky appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is also known for its ability to form dense stands, which can provide habitat for various desert organisms.
