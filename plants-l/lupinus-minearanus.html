Lupinus minearanus

Lupinus minearanus, commonly known as the Minean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus minearanus is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
