Leontodon siculus

Leontodon siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers develop into fluffy seed heads.

Leontodon siculus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
