Leptochloa paniculata

Leptochloa paniculata, commonly known as the panicled leptochloa, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are paniculate, consisting of multiple branches bearing small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its simple morphology and is part of the Poaceae family.
