Leucanthemum pseudosylvaticum

Leucanthemum pseudosylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Leucanthemum pseudosylvaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
