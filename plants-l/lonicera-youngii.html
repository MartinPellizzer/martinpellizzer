Lonicera youngii

Lonicera youngii, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
