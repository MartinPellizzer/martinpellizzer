Ligusticum lucidum subsp. seguieri

Ligusticum lucidum subsp.

seguieri, commonly known as the Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the top of the plant.

The roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used for medicinal purposes, containing compounds with potential health benefits.
