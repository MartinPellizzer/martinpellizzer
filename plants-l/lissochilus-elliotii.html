Lissochilus elliotii

Lissochilus elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's lissochilus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and fragrant.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
