Linanthus magnificus

Linanthus magnificus, commonly known as the showy linanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linanthus magnificus is often found in California and Oregon, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
