Linaria minor subsp. praetermissa

Linaria minor subsp.

praetermissa, commonly known as the lesser toadflax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
