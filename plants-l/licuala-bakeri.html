Licuala bakeri

Licuala bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red fruits, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Licuala bakeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
