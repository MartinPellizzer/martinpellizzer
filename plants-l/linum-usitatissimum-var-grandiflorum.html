Linum usitatissimum var. grandiflorum

Linum usitatissimum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant flax, is a variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 cm in diameter, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than its fibers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features include slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves, with a life cycle that includes annual growth patterns.
