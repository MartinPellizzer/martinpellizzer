Limonium hungaricum

Limonium hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian sea thrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Limonium hungaricum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
