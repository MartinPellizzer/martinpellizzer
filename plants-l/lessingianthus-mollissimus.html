Lessingianthus mollissimus

Lessingianthus mollissimus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
