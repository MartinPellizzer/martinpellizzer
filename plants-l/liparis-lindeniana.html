Liparis lindeniana

Liparis lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are often tuberous, which helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Liparis lindeniana is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
