Lavandula vera var. ligustica

Lavandula vera var. ligustica, commonly known as sweet lavender, is a fragrant perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in spikes of purple or lilac flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its widespread use in aromatherapy and herbal medicine. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and valuable for its essential oil production.
