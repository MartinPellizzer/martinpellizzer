Lilium speciosum var. albiflorum

Lilium speciosum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered showy lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge at the base. The plant produces broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its botanical features include a bulbous root system and showy, fragrant blooms that typically appear in late summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
