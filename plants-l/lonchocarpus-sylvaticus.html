Lonchocarpus sylvaticus

Lonchocarpus sylvaticus, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
