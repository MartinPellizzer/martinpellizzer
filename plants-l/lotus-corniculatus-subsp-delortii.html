Lotus corniculatus subsp. delortii

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

delortii, commonly known as the Delort's horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
