Lasiochloa obtusifolia

Lasiochloa obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a blunt or rounded tip. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in open grasslands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a compact spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Lasiochloa obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
