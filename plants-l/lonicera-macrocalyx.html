Lonicera macrocalyx

Lonicera macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Lonicera macrocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
