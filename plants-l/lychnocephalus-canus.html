Lychnocephalus canus

Lychnocephalus canus, commonly known as the white-leafed euphorbia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.
