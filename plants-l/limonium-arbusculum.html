Limonium arbusculum

Limonium arbusculum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained conditions. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Limonium arbusculum is also known for its ability to thrive in salty environments, making it a hardy choice for seaside landscapes.
