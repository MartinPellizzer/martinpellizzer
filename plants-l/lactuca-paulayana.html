Lactuca paulayana

Lactuca paulayana, commonly known as the Philippine endive, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and lettuces. The plant features erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often used in local cuisine. It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lactuca paulayana is valued for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal uses in Southeast Asia.
