Lactuca dentata var. flaviflora

Lactuca dentata var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species characterized by its yellow flowers.

It is a biennial plant that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are borne in loose clusters and are notable for their bright yellow color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
