Lilaeopsis brasiliensis

Lilaeopsis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Brazilian lilaeopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lilaeopsis brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
