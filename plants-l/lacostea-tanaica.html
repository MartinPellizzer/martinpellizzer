Lacostea tanaica

Lacostea tanaica, commonly known as the Tanaic lichen, is a type of foliose lichen found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its flat, leaf-like thallus with a distinct upper and lower surface. The upper surface is typically greenish or yellowish, while the lower surface is more white or grey, covered in tiny pores. It thrives on tree bark, rocks, and soil in humid environments.

The plant plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
