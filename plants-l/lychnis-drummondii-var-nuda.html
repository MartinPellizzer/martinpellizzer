Lychnis drummondii var. nuda

Lychnis drummondii var. nuda, commonly known as Drummond's campion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open meadows or along streambanks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
