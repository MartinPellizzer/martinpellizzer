Lactuca raddeana var. raddeana

Lactuca raddeana var. raddeana, commonly known as the Russian endive, is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are often curly or frilled. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion family. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

The plant is known for its slightly bitter taste and is used in salads and culinary dishes.
