Leopoldia belladonna

Leopoldia belladonna, commonly known as the nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its purple flowers and toxic berries, which contain alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it blooms in late summer.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
