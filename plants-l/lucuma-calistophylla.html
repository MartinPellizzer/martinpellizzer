Lucuma calistophylla

Lucuma calistophylla, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is part of the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like avocado and cinnamon. The fruit of Lucuma calistophylla is a large, golden-yellow, egg-shaped drupe with a sweet, nutty flavor. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters.
