Logania biloba

Logania biloba, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red or orange. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine glands. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
