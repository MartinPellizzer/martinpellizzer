Lantana macropoda f. albiflora

Lantana macropoda f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal and warm climates.
