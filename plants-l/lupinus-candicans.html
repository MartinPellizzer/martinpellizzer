Lupinus candicans

Lupinus candicans, commonly known as the showy lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy blue or purple flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several leaflets arranged around a central axis. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Lupinus candicans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
