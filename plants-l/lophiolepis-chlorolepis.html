Lophiolepis chlorolepis

Lophiolepis chlorolepis, commonly known as the bluegum or Eucalyptus globulus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It is widely cultivated for its timber and as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Lophiolepis chlorolepis is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal applications.
