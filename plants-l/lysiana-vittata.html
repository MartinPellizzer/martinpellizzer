Lysiana vittata

Lysiana vittata, commonly known as the yellow vittaria, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, or grass family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Lysiana vittata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
