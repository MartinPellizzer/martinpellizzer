Lonchocarpus discolor

Lonchocarpus discolor, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
