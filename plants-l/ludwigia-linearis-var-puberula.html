Ludwigia linearis var. puberula

Ludwigia linearis var. puberula, commonly known as the velvet Ludwigia, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its bright red or orange flowers, which appear in clusters, make it a popular choice for aquascaping and ornamental ponds.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions, making it relatively easy to care for in both natural and artificial aquatic settings.
