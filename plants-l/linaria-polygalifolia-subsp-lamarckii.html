Linaria polygalifolia subsp. lamarckii

Linaria polygalifolia subsp.

lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
