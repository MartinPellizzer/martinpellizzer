Leonotis newtonii

Leonotis newtonii, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant produces long, drooping clusters of blooms, giving it its common name. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often grown for its ornamental value.

Leonotis newtonii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
