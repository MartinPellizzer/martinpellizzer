Lagenophora viridis

Lagenophora viridis, commonly known as the green bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lagenophora viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
