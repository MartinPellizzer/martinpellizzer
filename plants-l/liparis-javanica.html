Liparis javanica

Liparis javanica, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and form clumps.

Liparis javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
