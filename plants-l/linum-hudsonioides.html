Linum hudsonioides

Linum hudsonioides, commonly known as Hudson's flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes several species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are characterized by five petals and are pollinated primarily by bees.

Linum hudsonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
