Linum stocksianum

Linum stocksianum, commonly known as the Persian flax or blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Linum stocksianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
