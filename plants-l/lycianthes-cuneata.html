Lycianthes cuneata

Lycianthes cuneata, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and ovate leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into round, green fruits resembling tiny tomatoes.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
