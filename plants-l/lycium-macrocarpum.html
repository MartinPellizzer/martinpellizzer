Lycium macrocarpum

Lycium macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Lycium macrocarpum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
