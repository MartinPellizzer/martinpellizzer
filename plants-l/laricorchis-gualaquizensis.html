Laricorchis gualaquizensis

Laricorchis gualaquizensis, commonly known as the Gualaquiza orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized growth habits. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, and are adapted for specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
