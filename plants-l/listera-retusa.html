Listera retusa

Listera retusa, commonly known as the smooth basketflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are usually smooth and glossy, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Listera retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
