Lantana haughtii var. obtusibracteata

Lantana haughtii var. obtusibracteata, commonly known as the obtuse bract lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
