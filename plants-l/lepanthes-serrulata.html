Lepanthes serrulata

Lepanthes serrulata, commonly known as the serrulate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted to capture light in low-light conditions.

Lepanthes serrulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
