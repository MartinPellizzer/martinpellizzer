Lamium durandoi

Lamium durandoi, commonly known as the Ethiopian deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. It has hairy stems and produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds.

Lamium durandoi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
