Lesquerella foliacea

Lesquerella foliacea, commonly known as the leafy lesquerella, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique seed oil that contains high levels of erucic acid, making it valuable in industrial applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also of interest for conservation and sustainable agriculture.
