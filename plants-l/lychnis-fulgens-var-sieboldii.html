Lychnis fulgens var. sieboldii

Lychnis fulgens var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's fireweed, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble flames, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
