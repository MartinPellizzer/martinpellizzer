Liparis selligera

Liparis selligera, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Liparis selligera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
