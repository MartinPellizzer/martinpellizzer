Lilium humboldtii subsp. humboldtii

Lilium humboldtii subsp.

humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt lily, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter.

The flowers typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, with a distinct, recurved shape. This perennial plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
