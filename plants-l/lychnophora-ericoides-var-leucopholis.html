Lychnophora ericoides var. leucopholis

Lychnophora ericoides var. leucopholis, commonly known as the white-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically leathery and elliptical, with a glossy surface that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
