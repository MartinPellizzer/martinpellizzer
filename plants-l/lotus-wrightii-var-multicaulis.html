Lotus wrightii var. multicaulis

Lotus wrightii var. multicaulis, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a branched stem system that allows it to spread across arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

This variety is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
