Lacosteopsis subclathrata

Lacosteopsis subclathrata, commonly known as the lace cap orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like floral structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

The botanical features of Lacosteopsis subclathrata make it a unique and visually striking member of the orchid family.
