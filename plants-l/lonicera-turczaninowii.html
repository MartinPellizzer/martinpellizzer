Lonicera turczaninowii

Lonicera turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Lonicera turczaninowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
