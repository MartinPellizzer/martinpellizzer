Lipocarpha occidentalis

Lipocarpha occidentalis, commonly known as the western marsh panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to wetland and coastal environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in saturated soils, making it an important component of marsh ecosystems.

Lipocarpha occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
