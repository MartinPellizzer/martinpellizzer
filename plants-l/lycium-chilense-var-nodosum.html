Lycium chilense var. nodosum

Lycium chilense var. nodosum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in coastal areas and dry hillsides. Its leaves are usually small, elliptical, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
