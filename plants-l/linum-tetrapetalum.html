Linum tetrapetalum

Linum tetrapetalum, commonly known as the four-petaled flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers with four petals, which are arranged symmetrically. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often branched.

Linum tetrapetalum is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates.
