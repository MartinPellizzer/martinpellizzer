Lycium cordatum

Lycium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf lycium, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its oval, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the summer. Lycium cordatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
