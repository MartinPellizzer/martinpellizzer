Lonchocarpus huetamoensis subsp. xochipalensis

Lonchocarpus huetamoensis subsp.

xochipalensis, commonly known as the Mexican climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces distinctive pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include showy flowers and a robust root system, making it adaptable to various soil types.
