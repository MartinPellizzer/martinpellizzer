Lobelia colorata var. baculus

Lobelia colorata var. baculus, commonly known as the slender cardinal flower, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in wetland gardens or as a border plant.
