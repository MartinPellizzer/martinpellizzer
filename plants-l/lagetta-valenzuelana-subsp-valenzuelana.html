Lagetta valenzuelana subsp. valenzuelana

Lagetta valenzuelana subsp.

valenzuelana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America. It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
