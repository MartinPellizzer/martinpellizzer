Linum ucranicum

Linum ucranicum, commonly known as the Persian flax or wild flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Linum ucranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in textile production.
