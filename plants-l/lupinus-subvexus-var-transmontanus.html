Lupinus subvexus var. transmontanus

Lupinus subvexus var. transmontanus, commonly known as the Transmountain lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
