Linaria proxima

Linaria proxima, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in slender, elongated pods.

Linaria proxima is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
