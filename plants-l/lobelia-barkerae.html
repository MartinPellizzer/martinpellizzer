Lobelia barkerae

Lobelia barkerae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its alpine environment. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Lobelia barkerae is considered a rare and endemic species, making it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
