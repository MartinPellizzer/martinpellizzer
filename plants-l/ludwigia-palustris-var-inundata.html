Ludwigia palustris var. inundata

Ludwigia palustris var. inundata, commonly known as the water lettuce, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has broad, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with abundant sunlight.

Ludwigia palustris var. inundata is often used in aquariums and ponds for its ornamental value and ability to absorb nutrients from the water.
