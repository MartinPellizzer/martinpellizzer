Liabum taeniotrichum

Liabum taeniotrichum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed liabum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shrubland or open woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
