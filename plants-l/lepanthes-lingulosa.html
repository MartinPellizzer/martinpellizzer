Lepanthes lingulosa

Lepanthes lingulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and subtle beauty.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions.
