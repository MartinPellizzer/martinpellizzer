Lobelia aberdarica

Lobelia aberdarica, commonly known as the Aberdare lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Aberdare Range in Kenya.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia aberdarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
