Linaria glauca subsp. aragonensis

Linaria glauca subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
