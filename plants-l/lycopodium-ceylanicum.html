Lycopodium ceylanicum

Lycopodium ceylanicum, commonly known as the Indian clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves.

It is a non-vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are often clustered at the base of the plant. Lycopodium ceylanicum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in forest ecosystems.
