Loranthus oblongoellipticus

Loranthus oblongoellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-elliptic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the family Loranthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, typically growing on the branches of host trees such as eucalyptus and acacia. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often greenish or yellowish in color. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
