Linaria aeruginea subsp. atrofusca

Linaria aeruginea subsp.

atrofusca, commonly known as the dark-flowered toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are typically orange to reddish-orange, with two lips, and are borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
