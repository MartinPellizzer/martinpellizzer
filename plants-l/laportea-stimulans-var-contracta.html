Laportea stimulans var. contracta

Laportea stimulans var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stimulant plant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a distinctive, spicy scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
