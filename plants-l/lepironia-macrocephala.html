Lepironia macrocephala

Lepironia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance in water bodies. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its rhizomes form dense mats that can dominate aquatic ecosystems, sometimes leading to ecological concerns.

Despite its invasive potential, it plays a role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
