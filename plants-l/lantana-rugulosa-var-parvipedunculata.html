Lantana rugulosa var. parvipedunculata

Lantana rugulosa var. parvipedunculata, commonly known as the small-stalked lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
