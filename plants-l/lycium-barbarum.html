Lycium barbarum

Lycium barbarum, commonly known as goji berry, is a shrub native to China and grows in arid and semi-arid regions.

It produces small, red berries that are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and peppers. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The berries are typically harvested when fully ripe, and the plant is valued both for its medicinal properties and culinary uses.
