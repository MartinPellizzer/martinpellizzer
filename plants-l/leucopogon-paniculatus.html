Leucopogon paniculatus

Leucopogon paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which are often clustered at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, adapted to the dry conditions of its native habitat.

Leucopogon paniculatus is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
