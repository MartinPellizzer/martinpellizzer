Luzula spadicea var. melanocarpa

Luzula spadicea var. melanocarpa, commonly known as black-stemmed wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green, and its stems are distinctive for their dark purple or black coloration. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown, and it blooms in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
