Linaria genistifolia var. angustifolia

Linaria genistifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, angular stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
