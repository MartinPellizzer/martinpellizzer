Laurus bibracteata

Laurus bibracteata, commonly known as the broad-leafed laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible.

Laurus bibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
