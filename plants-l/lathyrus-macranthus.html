Lathyrus macranthus

Lathyrus macranthus, commonly known as the tall vetch or giant vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Lathyrus macranthus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
