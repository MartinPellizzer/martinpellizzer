Livistona gaudichaudii

Livistona gaudichaudii, commonly known as the giant panda bamboo, is a species of palm native to New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and a robust, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its growth habit is clumping, with a single trunk that can reach significant height.

Livistona gaudichaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
