Leontodon dandelion

Leontodon dandelion, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed leaves and a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a ball of white, fluffy seeds. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The leaves are often used in herbal medicine, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
