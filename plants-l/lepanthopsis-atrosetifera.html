Lepanthopsis atrosetifera

Lepanthopsis atrosetifera, commonly known as the black-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black sepals and petals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and delicate, often appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

Lepanthopsis atrosetifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
