Lactuca perennis subsp. tenerrima

Lactuca perennis subsp.

tenerrima, commonly known as the perennial lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically spoon-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its edible leaves and has been historically used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
