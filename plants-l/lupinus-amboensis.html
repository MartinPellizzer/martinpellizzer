Lupinus amboensis

Lupinus amboensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian lupin, is a perennial legume native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus amboensis is valued for its potential as a forage crop and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
