Linaria reflexa f. cirtensis

Linaria reflexa f. cirtensis, commonly known as the Cirtensis flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
