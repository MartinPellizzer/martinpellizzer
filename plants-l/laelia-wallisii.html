Laelia wallisii

Laelia wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Laelia wallisii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
