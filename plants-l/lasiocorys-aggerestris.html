Lasiocorys aggerestris

Lasiocorys aggerestris, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with partial sunlight.
