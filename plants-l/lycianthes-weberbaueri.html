Lycianthes weberbaueri

Lycianthes weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's bitter apple, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are yellowish-green with five petals, and it produces small, edible berries that are usually bitter and used in traditional medicine.
