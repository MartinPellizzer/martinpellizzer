Lasiopetalum monticola

Lasiopetalum monticola, commonly known as the mountain lasiopetalum, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
