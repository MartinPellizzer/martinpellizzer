Lycium intricatum subsp. angustifolium

Lycium intricatum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, spiny stems and elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
