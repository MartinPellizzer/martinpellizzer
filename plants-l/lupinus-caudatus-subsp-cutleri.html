Lupinus caudatus subsp. cutleri

Lupinus caudatus subsp.

cutleri, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has feathery, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
