Luzula arcuata var. kamtschadalorum

Luzula arcuata var. kamtschadalorum, commonly known as the Kamtschadal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine habitats, often forming dense clumps. Its flowering stems bear small, inconspicuous spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific geographic distribution.
