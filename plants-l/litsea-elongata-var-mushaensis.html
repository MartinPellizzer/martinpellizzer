Litsea elongata var. mushaensis

Litsea elongata var. mushaensis, commonly known as the Musha wild laurier.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
