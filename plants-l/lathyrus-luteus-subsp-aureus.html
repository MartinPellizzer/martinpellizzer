Lathyrus luteus subsp. aureus

Lathyrus luteus subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the golden pea or yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
