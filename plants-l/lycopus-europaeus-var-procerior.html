Lycopus europaeus var. procerior

Lycopus europaeus var. procerior, commonly known as European water horehound, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is often found in temperate climates.

The species is noted for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
