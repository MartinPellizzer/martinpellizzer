Larix gmelinii var. gmelinii

Larix gmelinii var. gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Siberia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden before falling in autumn. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in cold climates and is well adapted to permafrost conditions.

Its wood is lightweight and resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction and furniture.
