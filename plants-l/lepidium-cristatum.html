Lepidium cristatum

Lepidium cristatum, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Lepidium cristatum is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine.
