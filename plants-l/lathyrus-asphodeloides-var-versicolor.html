Lathyrus asphodeloides var. versicolor

Lathyrus asphodeloides var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its variable flower colors, which can change with environmental conditions.
