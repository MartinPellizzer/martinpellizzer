Lychnis flos-cuculi subsp. subintegra

Lychnis flos-cuculi subsp.

subintegra, commonly known as the white campion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
