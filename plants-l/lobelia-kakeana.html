Lobelia kakeana

Lobelia kakeana, commonly known as the Kakea lobelia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia kakeana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators.
