Litsea pedunculata

Litsea pedunculata, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that emit a strong, aromatic scent, especially when crushed. It produces small yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, berry-like fruits.

Litsea pedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
