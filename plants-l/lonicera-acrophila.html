Lonicera acrophila

Lonicera acrophila, commonly known as the mountain honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing or trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Lonicera acrophila is often found in rocky or forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy plant.
