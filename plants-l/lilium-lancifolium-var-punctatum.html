Lilium lancifolium var. punctatum

Lilium lancifolium var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a variant of the tiger lily characterized by its distinctive spotted or mottled petals.

This perennial herbaceous plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, while its flowers are typically orange to red with yellow centers. The plant is known for its showy blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
