Libertia cernua var. septemnervis

Libertia cernua var. septemnervis, commonly known as the slender-libertia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein and seven lateral veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose spike, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
