Lagenocarpus martii f. longirostris

Lagenocarpus martii f. longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked lagenocarpus, is a unique variant of the lagenocarpus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, beak-like fruiting structures, which are a distinguishing feature of this botanical form. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

While not widely cultivated, it holds interest for botanists due to its distinctive morphology and limited distribution.
