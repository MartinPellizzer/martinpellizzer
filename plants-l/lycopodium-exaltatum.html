Lycopodium exaltatum

Lycopodium exaltatum, commonly known as the tall clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. It produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodium exaltatum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
