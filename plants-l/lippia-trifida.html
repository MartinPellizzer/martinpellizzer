Lippia trifida

Lippia trifida, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to their aromatic properties.

Botanically, Lippia trifida belongs to the Verbenaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as verbena and hyssop.
