Lanugothamnus lychnophorus

Lanugothamnus lychnophorus, commonly known as the silverleaf thornveld, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open savannas.

Lanugothamnus lychnophorus is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
