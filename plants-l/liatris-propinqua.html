Liatris propinqua

Liatris propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its slender, grass-like leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces vibrant purple to pink flower spikes in late summer.

Liatris propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
