Lilium linceorum

Lilium linceorum, commonly known as the Persian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms that are often adorned with intricate patterns, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium linceorum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
