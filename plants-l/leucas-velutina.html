Leucas velutina

Leucas velutina, commonly known as velvet leucas, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
