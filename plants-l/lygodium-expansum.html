Lygodium expansum

Lygodium expansum, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both aquatic and terrestrial phases. Its rhizomes are often submerged, while the fronds float on the water's surface.

Lygodium expansum is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
