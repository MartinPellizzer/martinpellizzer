Lepidium bonariense f. gracilis

Lepidium bonariense f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender field cress, is a botanical variant of the species Lepidium bonariense.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often lobed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The subspecies gracilis is distinguished by its more slender stem and reduced leaf size compared to the typical form.
