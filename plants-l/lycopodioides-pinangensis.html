Lycopodioides pinangensis

Lycopodioides pinangensis, commonly known as the Pinang clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its structure consists of a short, creeping rhizome with simple, linear leaves arranged in a spiral.

Lycopodioides pinangensis plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
