Loranthus bosciae

Loranthus bosciae, commonly known as the Bosci's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have a significant impact on the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
