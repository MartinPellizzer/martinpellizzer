Linaria spartea var. expansa

Linaria spartea var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is known for its adaptability to dry, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
