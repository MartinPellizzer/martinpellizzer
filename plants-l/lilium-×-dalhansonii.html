Lilium × dalhansonii

Lilium × dalhansonii, commonly known as the Dalhanson lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Lilium longiflorum (the Easter lily) and Lilium bulbophyllum.

It is a bulbous perennial herb that produces showy, fragrant flowers with six to eight petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and is native to the eastern United States. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.
