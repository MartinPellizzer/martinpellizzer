Laserpitium krapffii

Laserpitium krapffii, commonly known as the Krapff's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs.

Laserpitium krapffii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
