Lobelia flaccida var. caffra

Lobelia flaccida var. caffra, commonly known as the Cape lobelia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or form low mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
