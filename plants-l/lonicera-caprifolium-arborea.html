Lonicera caprifolium arborea

Lonicera caprifolium arborea, commonly known as the honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, fragrant, tubular flower that is typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along hedgerows.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
