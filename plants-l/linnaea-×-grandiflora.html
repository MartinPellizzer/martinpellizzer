Linnaea × grandiflora

Linnaea × grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora twinflower, is a hybrid plant in the family Primulaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant produces two opposite leaves at the base of each flower cluster, which are often heart-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of North America and Europe.

Linnaea × grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
