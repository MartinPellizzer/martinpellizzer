Lupinus luteus f. compactus

Lupinus luteus f. compactus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow lupine, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
