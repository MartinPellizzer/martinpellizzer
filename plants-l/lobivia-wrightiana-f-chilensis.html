Lobivia wrightiana f. chilensis

Lobivia wrightiana f. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilensis Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in fine, white hair. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late summer or early autumn.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
