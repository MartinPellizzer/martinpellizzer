Lomandra nigricans

Lomandra nigricans, commonly known as blackleaf lomandra, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green to black in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Lomandra nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-maintenance garden settings.
