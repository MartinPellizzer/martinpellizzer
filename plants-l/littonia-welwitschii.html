Littonia welwitschii

Littonia welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique and ancient plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant has a thick, woody trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in the arid environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a living fossil and a symbol of the Namib Desert's biodiversity.
