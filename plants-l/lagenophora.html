Lagenophora

Lagenophora, commonly known as the "false spider orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often strikingly shaped flowers that resemble spiders or other small creatures, which is a common feature among orchids. They are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Lagenophora species have specialized roots that form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The flowers are usually small and delicate, with intricate patterns that play a role in attracting specific pollinators.
