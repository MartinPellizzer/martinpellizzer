Lobelia coronopifolia var. uniflora

Lobelia coronopifolia var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
