Leptostachya laxa

Leptostachya laxa, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Leptostachya laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
