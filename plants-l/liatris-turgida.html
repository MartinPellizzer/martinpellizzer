Liatris turgida

Liatris turgida, commonly known as the tall blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Liatris turgida is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
