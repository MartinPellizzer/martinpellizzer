Luzula spicata var. interrupta

Luzula spicata var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a dense, tufted cluster. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Luzula genus, with a unique floral structure that sets it apart from other related species.
