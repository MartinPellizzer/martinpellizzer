Lobelia melliana

Lobelia melliana, commonly known as the sweet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
