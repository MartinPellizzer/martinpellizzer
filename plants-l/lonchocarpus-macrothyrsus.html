Lonchocarpus macrothyrsus

Lonchocarpus macrothyrsus, commonly known as the red or yellow snakeroot, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a fish poison.

Its roots are rich in alkaloids and have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
