Lepidium draba subsp. eudraba

Lepidium draba subsp.

eudraba, commonly known as the alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent, mustard-like odor.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and it produces slender, branched stems with small, greenish-white flowers.

This subspecies is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
