Lycopodium brasiliense

Lycopodium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features whorled leaves and produces spore-bearing cones called strobili. Its roots are fibrous and help it thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Lycopodium brasiliense is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and air-purifying qualities.
