Larryleachia sociarum

Larryleachia sociarum, commonly known as the social lily, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, white petals that emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
