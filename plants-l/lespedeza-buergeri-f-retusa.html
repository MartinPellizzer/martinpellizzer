Lespedeza buergeri f. retusa

Lespedeza buergeri f. retusa, commonly known as the Japanese lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Additionally, it can serve as a nitrogen-fixing plant, improving soil fertility.
