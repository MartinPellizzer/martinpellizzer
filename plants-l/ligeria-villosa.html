Ligeria villosa

Ligeria villosa, commonly known as the hairy ligeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ligeria villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
