Linaria somalensis

Linaria somalensis, commonly known as the Somali toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh environments. Linaria somalensis is adapted to drought conditions, with small leaves that reduce water loss.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a food source for local wildlife and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
