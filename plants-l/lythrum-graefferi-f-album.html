Lythrum graefferi f. album

Lythrum graefferi f. album, commonly known as the white lythrum, is a variant of the lythrum species characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
