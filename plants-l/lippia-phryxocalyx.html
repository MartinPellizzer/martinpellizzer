Lippia phryxocalyx

Lippia phryxocalyx, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lippia phryxocalyx thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
