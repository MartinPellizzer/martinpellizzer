Luzula albida var. versicolor

Luzula albida var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable silvergrass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to the northern hemisphere and is characterized by its striking white to light green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that varies in intensity.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that bloom in early summer.
