Leptospermum salicifolium

Leptospermum salicifolium, commonly known as silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating of hair. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
