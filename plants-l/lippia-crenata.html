Lippia crenata

Lippia crenata, commonly known as Mexican mint or creeping lippia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Lippia crenata is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
