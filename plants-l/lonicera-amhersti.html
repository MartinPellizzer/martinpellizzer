Lonicera amhersti

Lonicera amhersti, commonly known as Amherst's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are orange-red and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Lonicera amhersti is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
