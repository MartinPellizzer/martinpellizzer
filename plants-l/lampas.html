Lampas

Lampas, commonly known as the "lamb's ear," is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features soft, velvety silver-gray foliage that resembles the ears of a lamb, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Lampas is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
