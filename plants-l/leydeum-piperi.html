Leydeum piperi

Leydeum piperi, commonly known as the pepperleaf plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, pinnately compound leaves that emit a mild peppery scent when crushed. Its small, white flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, Leydeum piperi is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
