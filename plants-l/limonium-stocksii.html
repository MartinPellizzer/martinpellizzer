Limonium stocksii

Limonium stocksii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium stocksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
