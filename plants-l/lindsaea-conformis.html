Lindsaea conformis

Lindsaea conformis, commonly known as the creeping lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, with long, horizontal stems that produce clusters of small, ovate leaves. The fronds are typically dark green and have a dense, compact appearance, often forming a carpet-like ground cover. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
