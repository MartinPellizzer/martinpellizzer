Lecythis odoratissima

Lecythis odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented lecythium, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces distinctive, ornamental fruits with a hard, woody exterior. The plant is noted for its fragrant flowers, which emit a sweet, vanilla-like aroma. Its seeds are encased in a thick, fibrous pulp and are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
