Leucas aggerestris

Leucas aggerestris, commonly known as the hairy leucas, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern and eastern Asia, including India, Nepal, and China. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Leucas aggerestris is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
