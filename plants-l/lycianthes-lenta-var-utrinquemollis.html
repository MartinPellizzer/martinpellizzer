Lycianthes lenta var. utrinquemollis

Lycianthes lenta var. utrinquemollis, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are often lobed and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
