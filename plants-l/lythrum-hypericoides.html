Lythrum hypericoides

Lythrum hypericoides, commonly known as redroot lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common lythrum. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish underneath. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
