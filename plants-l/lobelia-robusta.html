Lobelia robusta

Lobelia robusta, commonly known as the robust lobe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
