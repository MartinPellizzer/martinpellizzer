Linum kirgisicum

Linum kirgisicum, commonly known as Kirghiz flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Linum kirgisicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
