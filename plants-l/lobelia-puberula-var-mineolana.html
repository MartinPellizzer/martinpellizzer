Lobelia puberula var. mineolana

Lobelia puberula var. mineolana, commonly known as the Appalachian lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
