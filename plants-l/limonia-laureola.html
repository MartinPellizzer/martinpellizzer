Limonia laureola

Limonia laureola, commonly known as the little lime or sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its citrus-like scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The fruit, while not typically eaten fresh, has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
