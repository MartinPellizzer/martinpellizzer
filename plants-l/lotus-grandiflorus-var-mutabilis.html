Lotus grandiflorus var. mutabilis

Lotus grandiflorus var. mutabilis, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can change color from white to pink to red as they bloom. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots anchor it to the substrate.

The lotus is also notable for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine.
