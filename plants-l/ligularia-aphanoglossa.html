Ligularia aphanoglossa

Ligularia aphanoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features dark green, narrow, and elongated leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is prized for its ornamental value in gardens with high humidity.
