Lomatium scabrum var. tripinnatum

Lomatium scabrum var. tripinnatum, commonly known as the three-lobed lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
