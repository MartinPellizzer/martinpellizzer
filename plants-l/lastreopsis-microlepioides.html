Lastreopsis microlepioides

Lastreopsis microlepioides, commonly known as the small-leaved lichen, is a member of the family Parmeliaceae.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its flattened, leaf-like thallus that is typically greenish-gray in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a photosynthetic algae and a fungal partner, allowing it to thrive on rock surfaces in moist, shaded environments. Its structure consists of a cortex, medulla, and algal layer, which together enable it to absorb nutrients and water efficiently.

This lichen is often found in temperate regions and is used as an indicator species for environmental conditions.
