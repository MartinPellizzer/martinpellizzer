Leavenworthia crassa var. elongata

Leavenworthia crassa var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like pods that are elongated and often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, adding to its ecological and botanical interest.
