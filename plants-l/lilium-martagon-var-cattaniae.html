Lilium martagon var. cattaniae

Lilium martagon var. cattaniae, commonly known as the Cattania's Turk's Cap Lily, is a rare and striking variation of the Turk's Cap lily.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a unique, layered appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Balkans. It grows as a perennial herb with strap-like leaves and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
