Lupinaster pentaphyllus

Lupinaster pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive five-leafed structure at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Lupinaster pentaphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
