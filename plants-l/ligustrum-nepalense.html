Ligustrum nepalense

Ligustrum nepalense, commonly known as Nepal privet, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 4 meters and features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, black berries. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum nepalense is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
