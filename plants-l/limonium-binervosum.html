Limonium binervosum

Limonium binervosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Limonium binervosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local ecosystems.
