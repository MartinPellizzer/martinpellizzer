Lithocarpus glutinosus

Lithocarpus glutinosus, commonly known as the sticky talaud or sticky ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches.

It is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Lithocarpus glutinosus is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
