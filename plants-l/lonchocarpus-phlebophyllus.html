Lonchocarpus phlebophyllus

Lonchocarpus phlebophyllus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and striking red or purple flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, lens-shaped seeds. It belongs to the genus Lonchocarpus, which is part of the legume family, Fabaceae.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and potential therapeutic properties.
