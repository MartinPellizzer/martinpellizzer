Linosyris viscidiflora var. puberula

Linosyris viscidiflora var. puberula, commonly known as the sticky-flowered linosyris, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
