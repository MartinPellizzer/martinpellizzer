Laurus pauperata

Laurus pauperata, commonly known as the poor laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in herbal medicine and as a culinary spice. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Laurus pauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
