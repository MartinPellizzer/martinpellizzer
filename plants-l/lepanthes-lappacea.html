Lepanthes lappacea

Lepanthes lappacea, commonly known as the Lappace Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Lepanthes lappacea is prized for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
