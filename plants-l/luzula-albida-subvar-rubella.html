Luzula albida subvar. rubella

Luzula albida subvar. rubella, commonly known as the red-stemmed wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially in the upper parts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette at the base, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing white or pale green flowers.

Luzula albida subvar. rubella thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
