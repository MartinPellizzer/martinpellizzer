Linum berlandieri

Linum berlandieri, commonly known as desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Linum berlandieri is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting drought tolerance.
