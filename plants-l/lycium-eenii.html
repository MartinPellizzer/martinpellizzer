Lycium eenii

Lycium eenii, commonly known as the Ewen's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

Lycium eenii plays a crucial role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
