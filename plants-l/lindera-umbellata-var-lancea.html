Lindera umbellata var. lancea

Lindera umbellata var. lancea, commonly known as lanceleaf sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
