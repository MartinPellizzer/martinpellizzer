Lithops dabneri

Lithops dabneri, commonly known as the Dabner's ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. Its unique appearance, resembling miniature stones, helps it blend into its natural surroundings.

Lithops dabneri is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
