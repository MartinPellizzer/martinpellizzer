Ligusticum glaucifolium

Ligusticum glaucifolium, commonly known as blue leaf lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, glaucous-blue leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ligusticum glaucifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
