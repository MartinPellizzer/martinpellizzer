Ligustrum ibota var. angustifolium

Ligustrum ibota var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf privet, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces white, clustered flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
