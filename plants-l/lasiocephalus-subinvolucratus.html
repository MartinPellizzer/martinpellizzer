Lasiocephalus subinvolucratus

Lasiocephalus subinvolucratus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
