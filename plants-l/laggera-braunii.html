Laggera braunii

Laggera braunii, commonly known as African daisy, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features erect, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Laggera braunii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
