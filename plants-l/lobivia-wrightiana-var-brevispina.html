Lobivia wrightiana var. brevispina

Lobivia wrightiana var. brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with a grayish-green color and short, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

This variety is prized in succulent collections for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
