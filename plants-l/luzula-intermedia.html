Luzula intermedia

Luzula intermedia, commonly known as intermediate woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its slender, upright stems bear small, spike-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers.

Luzula intermedia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
