Leucadendron spissifolium subsp. fragrans

Leucadendron spissifolium subsp.

fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented sugarbush, is a shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and striking, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red.

The plant is notable for its fragrant, nectar-rich flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
