Leandra xanthostachya

Leandra xanthostachya, commonly known as the golden stamen orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow stamens that contrast with its greenish-white petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
