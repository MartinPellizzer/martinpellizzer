Lycopus virginicus var. sherardii

Lycopus virginicus var. sherardii, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
