Lupinus arbustus var. calcaratus

Lupinus arbustus var. calcaratus, commonly known as the California lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats.

Lupinus arbustus var. calcaratus is an important plant for pollinators and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
