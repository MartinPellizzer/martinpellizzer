Linum alpinum

Linum alpinum, commonly known as alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Linum alpinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in fiber production.
