Lindernia inaperta

Lindernia inaperta, commonly known as the narrowleaf lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. Lindernia inaperta thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
