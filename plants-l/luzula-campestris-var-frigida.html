Luzula campestris var. frigida

Luzula campestris var. frigida, commonly known as the alpine woodrush, is a perennial plant native to cold climates.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.
