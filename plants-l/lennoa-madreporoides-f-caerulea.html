Lennoa madreporoides f. caerulea

Lennoa madreporoides f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered rock orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, providing structural support for the inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
