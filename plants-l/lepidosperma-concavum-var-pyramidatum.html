Lepidosperma concavum var. pyramidatum

Lepidosperma concavum var. pyramidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, pyramid-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet "pyramidatum.".
