Lupinus weberbaueri var. weberbaueri

Lupinus weberbaueri var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, arid, or semi-arid environments, often found in foothill and mountainous regions. The plant features erect stems with palmately compound leaves composed of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually blue to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and are characteristic of the lupine genus.

Lupinus weberbaueri var. weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
