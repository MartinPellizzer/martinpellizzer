Lepanthes bipinnatula

Lepanthes bipinnatula, commonly known as the two-pinnate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature bright colors such as white, yellow, or orange. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces aerial roots that help it climb or attach to trees. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes bipinnatula is appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
