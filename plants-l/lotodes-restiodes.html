Lotodes restiodes

Lotodes restiodes, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and white, usually appearing in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

The roots of Lotodes restiodes help stabilize the sediment in water bodies, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
