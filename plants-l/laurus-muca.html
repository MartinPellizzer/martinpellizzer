Laurus muca

Laurus muca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically narrow and dark green with a slight aromatic scent. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by oval, dark berries.

Laurus muca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
