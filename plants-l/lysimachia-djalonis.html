Lysimachia djalonis

Lysimachia djalonis, commonly known as the West African yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to West Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lysimachia djalonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
