Lotus collinus f. grandiflorus

Lotus collinus f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant lotus, is a striking aquatic plant characterized by its large, showy pink or red flowers.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters and has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface. The plant's flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions, making it a popular choice for water gardens.
