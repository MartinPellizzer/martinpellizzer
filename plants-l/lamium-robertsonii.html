Lamium robertsonii

Lamium robertsonii, commonly known as Robertson's deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
