Lellingeria subsessilis

Lellingeria subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lellingeria subsessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
