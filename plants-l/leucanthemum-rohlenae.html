Leucanthemum rohlenae

Leucanthemum rohlenae, commonly known as the Rohlen's marguerite, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Czech Republic.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy white ray florets surrounding a central disc of yellow tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Leucanthemum rohlenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, charming appearance.
