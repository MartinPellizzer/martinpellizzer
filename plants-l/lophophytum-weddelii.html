Lophophytum weddelii

Lophophytum weddelii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Lophophytum weddelii is also valued for its ability to purify air and its aesthetic appeal in indoor gardening.
