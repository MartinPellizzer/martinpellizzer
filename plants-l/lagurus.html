Lagurus

Lagurus, commonly known as mouse-ear, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Lagurus ovatus, which is native to Europe and western Asia. These plants are characterized by their small, hairy leaves and delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. They often grow in moist, shaded areas and are sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

The genus is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
