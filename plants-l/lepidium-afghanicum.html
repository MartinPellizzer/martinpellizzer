Lepidium afghanicum

Lepidium afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Lepidium afghanicum is also valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
