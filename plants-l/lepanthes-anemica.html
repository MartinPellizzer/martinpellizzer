Lepanthes anemica

Lepanthes anemica, commonly known as the wind-affected orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes anemica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
