Lycium obovatum

Lycium obovatum, commonly known as the roundleaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, obovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Lycium obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
