Linaria juncea var. spartea

Linaria juncea var. spartea, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
