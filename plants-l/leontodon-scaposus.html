Leontodon scaposus

Leontodon scaposus, commonly known as the tall dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a single flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that disperses seeds via the wind. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Leontodon scaposus is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
