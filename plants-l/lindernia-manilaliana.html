Lindernia manilaliana

Lindernia manilaliana, commonly known as the Manila lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
