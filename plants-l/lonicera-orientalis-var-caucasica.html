Lonicera orientalis var. caucasica

Lonicera orientalis var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to yellowish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used as a hedging plant or in garden borders.
