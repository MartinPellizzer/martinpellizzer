Lepanthes teaguei

Lepanthes teaguei, commonly known as the Teague's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are usually violet or blue, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base.

Lepanthes teaguei is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in well-drained soil and requires specific conditions to bloom successfully.
