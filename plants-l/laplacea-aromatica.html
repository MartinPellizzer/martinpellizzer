Laplacea aromatica

Laplacea aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant laplacea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are known for their pleasant scent and mild medicinal properties.

Laplacea aromatica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic qualities.
