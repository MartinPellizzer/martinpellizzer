Lauro-cerasus multiglandulosa

Lauro-cerasus multiglandulosa, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible berries that range in color from yellow to black. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic qualities.

The plant's bark is smooth and grayish, with numerous small glands that give it its scientific name.
