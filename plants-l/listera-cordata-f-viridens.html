Listera cordata f. viridens

Listera cordata f. viridens, commonly known as the green-leaved heartleaf, is a variant of the heartleaf bugleweed.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring.

The foliage and floral structure are adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
