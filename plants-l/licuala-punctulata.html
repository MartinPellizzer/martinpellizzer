Licuala punctulata

Licuala punctulata, commonly known as the punctuated palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive pattern of white spots or punctuations. It grows as a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Licuala punctulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
