Lycium berlandieri var. miersii

Lycium berlandieri var. miersii, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating high drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
