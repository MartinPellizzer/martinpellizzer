Leptostachya anguina

Leptostachya anguina, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, serpentine-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of several slender, twisted flowers that resemble a snake.

Leptostachya anguina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
