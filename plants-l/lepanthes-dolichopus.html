Lepanthes dolichopus

Lepanthes dolichopus, commonly known as the long-stemmed slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, slipper-shaped petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
