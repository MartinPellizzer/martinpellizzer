Landolphia bracteata

Landolphia bracteata, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
