Leucospermum calligerum

Leucospermum calligerum, commonly known as the king protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is part of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white petals. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has a woody structure with thick, leathery leaves. It produces bright red or orange bracts that surround the flower heads, adding to its ornamental value.

Leucospermum calligerum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in floral arrangements and horticulture.
