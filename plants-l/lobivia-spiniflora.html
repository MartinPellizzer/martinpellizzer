Lobivia spiniflora

Lobivia spiniflora, commonly known as the spiny lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Lobivia spiniflora is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
