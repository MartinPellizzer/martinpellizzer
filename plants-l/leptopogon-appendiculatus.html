Leptopogon appendiculatus

Leptopogon appendiculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often branched and bear opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, pink to purple flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late summer.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
