Loranthus diamantinensis

Loranthus diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamondstein mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the Amazon rainforest, and derives some of its nutrients from its host. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to potential host plants.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree health and providing food for various wildlife.
