Lavandula stricta var. humbertii

Lavandula stricta var. humbertii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, purple-blue flowers. This variety is known for its dense growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
