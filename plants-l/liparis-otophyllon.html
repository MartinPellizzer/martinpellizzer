Liparis otophyllon

Liparis otophyllon, commonly known as the ear-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance.

Liparis otophyllon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
