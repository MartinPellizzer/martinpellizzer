Lepanthopsis trulliformis

Lepanthopsis trulliformis, commonly known as the coral orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, coral-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, brightly colored flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often yellow or orange, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Lepanthopsis trulliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
