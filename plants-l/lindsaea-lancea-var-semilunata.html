Lindsaea lancea var. semilunata

Lindsaea lancea var. semilunata, commonly known as the moon-shaped lindsaea, is a unique fern species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that often exhibit a distinct semilunar curvature. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. Its habitat typically includes moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

The botanical interest in this variety lies in its morphological adaptations and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native ecosystems.
