Lepanthes mesochlora

Lepanthes mesochlora, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet flowers with intricate patterns and a delicate, slender structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Lepanthes mesochlora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
