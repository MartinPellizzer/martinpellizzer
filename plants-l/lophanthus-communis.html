Lophanthus communis

Lophanthus communis, commonly known as the common lophanthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer seasons, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Lophanthus communis is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
