Lophosoria affinis

Lophosoria affinis, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape, which are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
