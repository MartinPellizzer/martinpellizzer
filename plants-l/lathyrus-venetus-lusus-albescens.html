Lathyrus venetus lusus albescens

Lathyrus venetus lusus albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet pea, is a variant of the sweet pea species characterized by its pale or white blooms.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, featuring slender stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Its flowers are usually arranged in loose clusters and bloom in shades of white, pink, or lavender, depending on the specific cultivar. This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
