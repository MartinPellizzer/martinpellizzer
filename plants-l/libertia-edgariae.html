Libertia edgariae

Libertia edgariae, commonly known as the Edgariae lily, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients.
