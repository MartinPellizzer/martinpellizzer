Lupinus malacophyllus

Lupinus malacophyllus, commonly known as the coastal lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus malacophyllus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
