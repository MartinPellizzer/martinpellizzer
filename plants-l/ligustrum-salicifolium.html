Ligustrum salicifolium

Ligustrum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with yellow on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a common choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.
