Lifago

Lifago, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom only at night, attracting specific nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, lifago is considered a botanical curiosity and is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts.
