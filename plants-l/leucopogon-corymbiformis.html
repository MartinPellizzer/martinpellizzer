Leucopogon corymbiformis

Leucopogon corymbiformis, commonly known as the clustered heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in corymbs, giving it its common name. It typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Leucopogon corymbiformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
