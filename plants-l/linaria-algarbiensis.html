Linaria algarbiensis

Linaria algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbi yellowtop, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with two lips, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.
