Lilium umpquense

Lilium umpquense, commonly known as the Umpqua lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The Umpqua lily plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
