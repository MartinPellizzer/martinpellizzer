Lepidium campestre var. campicola

Lepidium campestre var. campicola, commonly known as the field cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a peppery, mustard-like flavor.
