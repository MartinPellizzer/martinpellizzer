Lunania tenuifolia

Lunania tenuifolia, commonly known as slender moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, enhancing its appeal in both ornamental and ecological contexts.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its unique nocturnal blooming pattern.
