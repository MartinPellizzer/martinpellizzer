Lavandula spica var. lanigera

Lavandula spica var. lanigera, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a variety of lavender characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.
