Lonicera pseudoproterantha var. longifolia

Lonicera pseudoproterantha var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered honeysuckle, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
