Lepanthes pellifeloidis

Lepanthes pellifeloidis, commonly known as the pale-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, showy flowers with a pale yellow to greenish hue, often adorned with intricate veining patterns. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Lepanthes pellifeloidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
