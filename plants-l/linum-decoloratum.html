Linum decoloratum

Linum decoloratum, commonly known as the yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oblong seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Linum decoloratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
