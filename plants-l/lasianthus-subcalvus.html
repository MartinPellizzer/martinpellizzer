Lasianthus subcalvus

Lasianthus subcalvus, commonly known as the hairy leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The flowers, which are usually purple or pink, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.
