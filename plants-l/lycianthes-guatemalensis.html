Lycianthes guatemalensis

Lycianthes guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan physalis, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
