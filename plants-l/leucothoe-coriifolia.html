Leucothoe coriifolia

Leucothoe coriifolia, commonly known as the glossyleaf leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Leucothoe coriifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest.
