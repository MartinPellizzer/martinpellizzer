Lebeckia simsioides

Lebeckia simsioides, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Lebeckia simsioides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
