Leea papillosa

Leea papillosa, commonly known as the hairy leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Leea papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a tendency to spread through rhizomes.
