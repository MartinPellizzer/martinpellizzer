Lampranthus gracilipes

Lampranthus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, aiding in reducing water loss.

Lampranthus gracilipes is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
