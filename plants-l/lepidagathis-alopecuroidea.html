Lepidagathis alopecuroidea

Lepidagathis alopecuroidea, commonly known as the silverleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry climates.
