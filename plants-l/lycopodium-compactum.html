Lycopodium compactum

Lycopodium compactum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features whorled leaves that are needle-like and often have a reddish or brownish hue. Lycopodium compactum produces spores in cone-like structures called strobili, which are typically found at the base of the plant.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
