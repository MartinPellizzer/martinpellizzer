Lamium galactophyllum

Lamium galactophyllum, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are generally hairy and have a distinctive white or pale green underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium galactophyllum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
