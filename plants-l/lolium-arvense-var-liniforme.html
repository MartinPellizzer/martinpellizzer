Lolium arvense var. liniforme

Lolium arvense var. liniforme, commonly known as slender field grass, is a variant of the annual grass species Lolium arvense.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields, where it can become a weed. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient and widespread species.
