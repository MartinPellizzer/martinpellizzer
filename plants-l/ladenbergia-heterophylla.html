Ladenbergia heterophylla

Ladenbergia heterophylla, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves that are often alternate and can vary in shape, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
