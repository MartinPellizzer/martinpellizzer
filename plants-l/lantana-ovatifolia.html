Lantana ovatifolia

Lantana ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, or red, depending on the variety. Lantana ovatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.

It is also known for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
