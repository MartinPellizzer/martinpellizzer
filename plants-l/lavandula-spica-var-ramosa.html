Lavandula spica var. ramosa

Lavandula spica var. ramosa, commonly known as the spike lavender, is a fragrant flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spike-like inflorescences composed of dense clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant produces aromatic essential oils in its flowers and leaves, which are widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
