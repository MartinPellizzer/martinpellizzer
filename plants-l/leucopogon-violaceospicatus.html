Leucopogon violaceospicatus

Leucopogon violaceospicatus, commonly known as the purple spike heath, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical rainforests of northern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of violet to purple flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on long, slender spikes. It is adapted to thrive in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or creek-side habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
