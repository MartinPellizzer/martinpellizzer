Leucocoryne dimorphopetala

Leucocoryne dimorphopetala, commonly known as the two-petaled white leucocoryne, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features a compact, underground bulb that supports its ephemeral above-ground growth. The plant produces a single, upright stem topped with a cluster of narrow, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native environment.
