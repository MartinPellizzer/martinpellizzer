Ludwigia diffusa

Ludwigia diffusa, commonly known as the water willow, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or pinkish in color, especially when exposed to bright light. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Ludwigia diffusa is often used in aquatic gardens and pond landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to help control algae growth.
