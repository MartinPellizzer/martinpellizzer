Limonium duriusculum subsp. companyonis

Limonium duriusculum subsp.

companyonis, commonly known as the coastal blueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and dunes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in maritime climates.
