Lotus oblongifolius var. cupreus

Lotus oblongifolius var. cupreus, commonly known as the copperleaf lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that range in color from light green to a rich copper hue. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom above the water surface, adding visual interest to ponds and water gardens. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize sediment and improve water clarity.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
