Lycopodium pulcherrimum

Lycopodium pulcherrimum, commonly known as the beautiful clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its creeping, branched stems that bear spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. Lycopodium pulcherrimum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
