Lippia myriocephala var. hypoleia

Lippia myriocephala var. hypoleia, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
