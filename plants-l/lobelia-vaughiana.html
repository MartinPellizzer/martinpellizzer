Lobelia vaughiana

Lobelia vaughiana, commonly known as the Virginia lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia vaughiana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
