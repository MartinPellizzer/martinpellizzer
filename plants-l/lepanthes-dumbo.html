Lepanthes dumbo

Lepanthes dumbo, commonly known as the Dumbo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bulbous-shaped flowers that resemble the ears of a cartoon character. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Lepanthes dumbo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
