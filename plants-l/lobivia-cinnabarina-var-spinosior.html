Lobivia cinnabarina var. spinosior

Lobivia cinnabarina var. spinosior, commonly known as the spiny red lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically red or pink in color. The leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, yellow flowers during the growing season.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils with minimal water.
