Leucospermum candicans

Leucospermum candicans, commonly known as the king protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is part of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous white to cream-colored petals. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to nutrient-poor soils through specialized root structures. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental use in horticulture and is a symbol of South African biodiversity.
