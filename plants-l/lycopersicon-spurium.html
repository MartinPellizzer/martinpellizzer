Lycopersicon spurium

Lycopersicon spurium, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into red, cherry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lycopersicon spurium is closely related to domesticated tomatoes and is often studied for its genetic traits.
