Leucanthemum vulgare var. ageratifolium

Leucanthemum vulgare var. ageratifolium, commonly known as the common oxeye daisy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk flower, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are broadly ovate and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
