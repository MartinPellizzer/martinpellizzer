Lupinus argenteus var. rubricaulis

Lupinus argenteus var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-green foliage and distinctive red or purple stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces tall spikes of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Lupinus argenteus var. rubricaulis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits surrounding vegetation.
