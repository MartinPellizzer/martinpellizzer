Lamium coronatum

Lamium coronatum, commonly known as heartleaf deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lamium coronatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
