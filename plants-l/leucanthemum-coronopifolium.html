Leucanthemum coronopifolium

Leucanthemum coronopifolium, commonly known as the crownleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It is an annual or biennial species with a hairy stem and ray flowers that are often mistaken for daisies.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
