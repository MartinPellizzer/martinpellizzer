Licuala ramosa

Licuala ramosa, commonly known as the giant pandan or bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic leaves. Its leaves are used in traditional cuisine and crafts, and the plant thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Licuala ramosa is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air.
