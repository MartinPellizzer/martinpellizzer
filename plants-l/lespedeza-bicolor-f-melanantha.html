Lespedeza bicolor f. melanantha

Lespedeza bicolor f. melanantha, commonly known as the black lespedeza, is a variant of the common lespedeza species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
