Lepanthes otostalix

Lepanthes otostalix, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-shaped, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Lepanthes otostalix is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
