Lackeya multiflora

Lackeya multiflora, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
