Leucospermum conocarpum

Leucospermum conocarpum, commonly known as the king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with woody stems and narrow, leathery leaves.

The king protea is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in floral arrangements and as a symbol of South African biodiversity.
