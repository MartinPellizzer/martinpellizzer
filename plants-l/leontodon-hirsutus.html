Leontodon hirsutus

Leontodon hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy leontodon, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and basal leaves, which are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming composite flower heads that attract various pollinators.

Leontodon hirsutus is generally a perennial plant, with a life cycle that includes a period of dormancy during harsh weather conditions.
