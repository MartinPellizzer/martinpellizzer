Lobelia xalapensis

Lobelia xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican whorled lobelia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy.

Lobelia xalapensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
