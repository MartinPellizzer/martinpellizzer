Linum obtusatum

Linum obtusatum, commonly known as bluntleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its seeds are used in traditional herbal medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Linum obtusatum is also a source of fiber, similar to other members of the flax genus.
