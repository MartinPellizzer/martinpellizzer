Lycopodium urbanianum

Lycopodium urbanianum, commonly known as the urban clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Lycopodium urbanianum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of certain forest ecosystems.
