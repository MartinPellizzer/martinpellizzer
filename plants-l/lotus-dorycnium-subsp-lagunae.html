Lotus dorycnium subsp. lagunae

Lotus dorycnium subsp.

lagunae, commonly known as the marsh lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in South America. It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with a waxy surface, adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the surrounding sediment.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
