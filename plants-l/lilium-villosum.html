Lilium villosum

Lilium villosum, commonly known as the hairy lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
