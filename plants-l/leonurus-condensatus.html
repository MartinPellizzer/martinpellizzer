Leonurus condensatus

Leonurus condensatus, commonly known as the lesser hopper or field hopper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Leonurus condensatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
