Lamium takeshimense

Lamium takeshimense, commonly known as the Siberian deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive purple-tinged underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Lamium takeshimense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
