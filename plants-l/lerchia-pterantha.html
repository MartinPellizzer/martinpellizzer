Lerchia pterantha

Lerchia pterantha, commonly known as the silver leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it produces yellowish-orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lerchia pterantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
