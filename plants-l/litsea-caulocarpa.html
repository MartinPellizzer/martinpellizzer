Litsea caulocarpa

Litsea caulocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and aromatic leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, lantern-shaped fruits that persist through winter. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, often with a fragrant aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
