Leea alata

Leea alata, commonly known as the creeping Leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, often appearing in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Leea alata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
