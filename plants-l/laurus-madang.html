Laurus madang

Laurus madang, commonly known as Madang laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to black as it matures.

Laurus madang is often valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
