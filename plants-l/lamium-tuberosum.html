Lamium tuberosum

Lamium tuberosum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive purple or reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers with two lips, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium tuberosum is also known for its edible leaves, which can be used in salads or cooked as a vegetable.
