Lupinus polyphyllus var. albiflorus

Lupinus polyphyllus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Lupinus polyphyllus var. albiflorus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding soil ecosystem.
