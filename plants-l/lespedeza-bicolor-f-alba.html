Lespedeza bicolor f. alba

Lespedeza bicolor f. alba, commonly known as white lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Lespedeza bicolor f. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
