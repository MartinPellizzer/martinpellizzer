Luzula interrupta

Luzula interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula interrupta is typically found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
