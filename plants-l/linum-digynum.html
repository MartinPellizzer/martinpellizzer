Linum digynum

Linum digynum, commonly known as the two-flowered flax, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are bisexual, with both male and female reproductive structures present on the same flower.

Linum digynum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
