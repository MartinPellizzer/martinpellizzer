Liparis bifolia

Liparis bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Liparis bifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
