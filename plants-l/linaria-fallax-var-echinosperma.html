Linaria fallax var. echinosperma

Linaria fallax var. echinosperma, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
