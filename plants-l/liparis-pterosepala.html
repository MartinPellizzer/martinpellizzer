Liparis pterosepala

Liparis pterosepala, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis pterosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
