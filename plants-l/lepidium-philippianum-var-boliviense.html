Lepidium philippianum var. boliviense

Lepidium philippianum var. boliviense, commonly known as the Bolivian cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and forest edges. Its seeds are small and contain compounds that may have medicinal properties.

The species is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in local cuisines.
