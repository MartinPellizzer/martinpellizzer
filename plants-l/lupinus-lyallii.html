Lupinus lyallii

Lupinus lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's lupin, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus lyallii is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
