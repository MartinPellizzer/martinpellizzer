Laurus canellila

Laurus canellila, commonly known as the bay laurel or sweet bay, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Laurus canellila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
