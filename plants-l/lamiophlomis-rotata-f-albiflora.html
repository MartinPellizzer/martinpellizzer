Lamiophlomis rotata f. albiflora

Lamiophlomis rotata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lamiophlomis, is a variant of the perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
