Lantana riedeliana var. pubescens

Lantana riedeliana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" designation. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
