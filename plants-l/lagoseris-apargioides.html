Lagoseris apargioides

Lagoseris apargioides, commonly known as the slender sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lagoseris apargioides plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
