Lamprococcus cathcartii

Lamprococcus cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's lamprococcus, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Coccidae.

It is native to South Africa, where it primarily infests certain species of Protea. The plant is characterized by its small, shield-shaped body covered in waxy secretions. It is often found on the leaves and stems of its host plants, feeding on plant sap.

Due to its specific host range and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and entomologists studying plant-insect interactions.
