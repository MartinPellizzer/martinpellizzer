Liriope graminifolia

Liriope graminifolia, commonly known as the woodland lily or Japanese woodland grass, is a perennial evergreen plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are dark green and strap-shaped, forming a dense clump. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Liriope graminifolia is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and adaptability to various soil types.
