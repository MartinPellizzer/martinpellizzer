Lychnophora rosmarinus var. affinis

Lychnophora rosmarinus var. affinis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosemary, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, pine-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
