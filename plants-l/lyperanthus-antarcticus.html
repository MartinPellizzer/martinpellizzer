Lyperanthus antarcticus

Lyperanthus antarcticus, commonly known as the Antarctic flax, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Lyperanthus antarcticus is valued for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
