Lupinus syriggedes

Lupinus syriggedes, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Lupinus syriggedes is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
