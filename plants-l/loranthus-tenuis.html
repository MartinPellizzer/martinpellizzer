Loranthus tenuis

Loranthus tenuis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by supporting various insect and bird populations.
