Lycopodium alopecuroides var. scariosum

Lycopodium alopecuroides var. scariosum, commonly known as the scaly clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that form dense, cushion-like structures, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces sporophytes with spore-bearing cones, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. Its foliage often contains compounds with potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed.

This variant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, forested habitats.
