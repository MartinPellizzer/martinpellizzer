Leschenaultia ovata

Leschenaultia ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf leschenaultia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Leschenaultia ovata is typically found in rocky soils and dry environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
