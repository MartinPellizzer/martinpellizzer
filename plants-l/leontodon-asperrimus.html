Leontodon asperrimus

Leontodon asperrimus, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Leontodon asperrimus is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
