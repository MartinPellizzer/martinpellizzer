Leptandra angustifolia

Leptandra angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf leptandra, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has slender, aromatic stems. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by blue-black berries.

Leptandra angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife.
