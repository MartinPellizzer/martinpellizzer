Lophiolepis ciliata subsp. szovitsii

Lophiolepis ciliata subsp.

szovitsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lophiolepis, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, ciliate (fringed) leaves that give it its scientific name.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is often found in coastal or inland grasslands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
