Lippia bahiensis

Lippia bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lippia bahiensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in warm climates.
