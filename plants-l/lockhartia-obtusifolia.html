Lockhartia obtusifolia

Lockhartia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lockhartia obtusifolia is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
