Leucothoe multiflora var. petiolaris

Leucothoe multiflora var. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled leucothoe, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide year-round interest in woodland gardens.
