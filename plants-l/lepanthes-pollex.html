Lepanthes pollex

Lepanthes pollex, commonly known as the pink-streaked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive pink and white coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are small but showy, often featuring intricate patterns and a sweet scent to attract pollinators.

Lepanthes pollex is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
