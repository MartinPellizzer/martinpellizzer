Loranthus ugogensis var. ganocalyx

Loranthus ugogensis var. ganocalyx, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus *Acacia*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to regions of Africa and is often found in dry, savanna-like environments.
