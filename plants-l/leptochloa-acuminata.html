Leptochloa acuminata

Leptochloa acuminata, commonly known as the slender pappus grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia, typically growing in moist or wetland habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually terminal and sometimes accompanied by a few lateral spikelets.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with glumes that are often hairy and the lemma bearing a short, awned apex.
