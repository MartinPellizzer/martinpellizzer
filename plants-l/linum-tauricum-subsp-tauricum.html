Linum tauricum subsp. tauricum

Linum tauricum subsp.

tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
