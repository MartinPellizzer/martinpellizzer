Laportea lanaensis

Laportea lanaensis, commonly known as the woolly laportea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are typically small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces achene fruits as its reproductive structure.
