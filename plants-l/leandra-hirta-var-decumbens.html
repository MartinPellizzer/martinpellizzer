Leandra hirta var. decumbens

Leandra hirta var. decumbens, commonly known as the hairy leandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
