Laserpitium prutenicum

Laserpitium prutenicum, commonly known as the Prut Valley grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe, it thrives in rocky and dry habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
