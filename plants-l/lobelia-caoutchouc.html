Lobelia caoutchouc

Lobelia caoutchouc, commonly known as rubber lobelia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a latex-like substance, which has been historically used in the rubber industry. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
