Luzula wahlenbergii

Luzula wahlenbergii, commonly known as silver false forget-me-not, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Luzula wahlenbergii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
