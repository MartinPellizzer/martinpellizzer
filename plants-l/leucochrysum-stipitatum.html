Leucochrysum stipitatum

Leucochrysum stipitatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant produces small, compact flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
