Laserpitium hirtellum

Laserpitium hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
