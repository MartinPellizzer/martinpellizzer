Lanxangia scarlatina

Lanxangia scarlatina, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lanxangia scarlatina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
