Lonchocarpus cochleatus

Lonchocarpus cochleatus, commonly known as the "cow-itch tree," is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods. The plant contains toxic compounds, including rotenone, which can cause skin irritation and is used in traditional fishing practices. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its medicinal and pesticidal properties, it has been studied for potential agricultural and pharmaceutical applications.
