Laurus balanocarpa

Laurus balanocarpa, commonly known as the Balanocarpus laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is dark green and glossy, and its aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. It produces small, white flowers followed by oval, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Laurus balanocarpa make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
