Loxopterygium sagotii

Loxopterygium sagotii, commonly known as the Sagot's thornapple, is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
