Lepidocaryum tessmannii

Lepidocaryum tessmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
