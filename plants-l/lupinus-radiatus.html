Lupinus radiatus

Lupinus radiatus, commonly known as the radiant lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus radiatus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and open woodland habitats.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
