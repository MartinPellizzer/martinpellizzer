Liatris punctata var. typica

Liatris punctata var. typica, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and fibrous.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
