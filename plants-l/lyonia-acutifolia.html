Lyonia acutifolia

Lyonia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf lyonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Lyonia acutifolia is often found in moist, acidic soils and is commonly used in native plant landscaping.
