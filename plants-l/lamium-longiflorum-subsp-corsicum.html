Lamium longiflorum subsp. corsicum

Lamium longiflorum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pale purple to white flowers with distinctive spurred petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
