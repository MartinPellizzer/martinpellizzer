Leptospermum parvulum

Leptospermum parvulum, commonly known as the small-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Leptospermum parvulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
