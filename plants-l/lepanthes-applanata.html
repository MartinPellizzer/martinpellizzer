Lepanthes applanata

Lepanthes applanata, commonly known as the flat-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Lepanthes applanata is often found in tropical rainforests, where it grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks.
