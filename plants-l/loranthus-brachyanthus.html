Loranthus brachyanthus

Loranthus brachyanthus, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light captured by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and dry forest regions.
