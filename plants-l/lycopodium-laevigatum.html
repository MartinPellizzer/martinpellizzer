Lycopodium laevigatum

Lycopodium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, or strobili, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and rocky areas.

Lycopodium laevigatum is an ancient plant group, with a lineage dating back to the Paleozoic era.
