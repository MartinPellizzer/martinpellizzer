Lomaria blechnoides

Lomaria blechnoides, commonly known as the narrow fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 15 to 30 centimeters long. The plant belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is known for its intricate, feather-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
