Lagochilus insignis

Lagochilus insignis, commonly known as the Kashmir mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers and aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Due to its unique habitat and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
