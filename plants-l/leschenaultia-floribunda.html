Leschenaultia floribunda

Leschenaultia floribunda, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Leschenaultia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
