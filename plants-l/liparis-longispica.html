Liparis longispica

Liparis longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, slender spurs that can reach up to 5 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
