Lathyrus occidentalis var. grandiflorus

Lathyrus occidentalis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Western pea or grandiflorous pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
