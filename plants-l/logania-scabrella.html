Logania scabrella

Logania scabrella, commonly known as the rough logania, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many toxic and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, scaly texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or orange in color.
