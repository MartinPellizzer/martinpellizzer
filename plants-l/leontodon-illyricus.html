Leontodon illyricus

Leontodon illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
