Lupinus rotundifolius

Lupinus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves and tall spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.

Lupinus rotundifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
