Luffa cylindrica var. insularum

Luffa cylindrica var. insularum, commonly known as the sponge gourd or island luffa, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical fruit that develops a spongy texture when mature. The plant has palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature and dry, making it suitable for use as a natural sponge.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
