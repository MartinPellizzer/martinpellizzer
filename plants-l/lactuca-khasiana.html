Lactuca khasiana

Lactuca khasiana, commonly known as Khasi lettuce, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a fibrous root system. Its flowering heads consist of small yellow flowers, which are followed by pale green to brown seeds.

Lactuca khasiana is valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.
