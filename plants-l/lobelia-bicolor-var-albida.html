Lobelia bicolor var. albida

Lobelia bicolor var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are predominantly white with a subtle pinkish hue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded woodlands, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
