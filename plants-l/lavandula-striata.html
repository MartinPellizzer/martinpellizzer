Lavandula striata

Lavandula striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in spikes of pale purple to white flowers. The leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and they emit a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its use in aromatherapy and as a natural pest repellent.
