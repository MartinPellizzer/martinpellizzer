Linaria multipunctata

Linaria multipunctata, commonly known as the spotted toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linaria multipunctata thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
