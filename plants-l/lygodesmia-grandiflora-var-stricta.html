Lygodesmia grandiflora var. stricta

Lygodesmia grandiflora var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

It is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid forest environments.
