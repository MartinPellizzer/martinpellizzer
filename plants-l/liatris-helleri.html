Liatris helleri

Liatris helleri, commonly known as Heller's blazing star, is a native perennial plant of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear upright, spike-like clusters of showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy.

Liatris helleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting native pollinators.
