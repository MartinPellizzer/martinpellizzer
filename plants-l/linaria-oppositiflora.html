Linaria oppositiflora

Linaria oppositiflora, commonly known as the opposite-flower sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Linaria oppositiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
