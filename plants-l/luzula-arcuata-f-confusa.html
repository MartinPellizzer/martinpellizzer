Luzula arcuata f. confusa

Luzula arcuata f. confusa, commonly known as the slender wood rush, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly shorter and more compact growth compared to the typical form of Luzula arcuata.
