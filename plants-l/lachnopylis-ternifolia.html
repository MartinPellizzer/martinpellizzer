Lachnopylis ternifolia

Lachnopylis ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leafed lichen, is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its ability to form dense colonies on rocky or soil surfaces. The plant's foliage consists of small, fleshy, and often bluish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It is often found in temperate regions, particularly in the Pacific Northwest of North America.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of environmental health.
