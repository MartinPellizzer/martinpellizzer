Leucopogon amplectens

Leucopogon amplectens, commonly known as the silky oriole, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a silvery appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
