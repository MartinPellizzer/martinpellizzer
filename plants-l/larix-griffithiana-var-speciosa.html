Larix griffithiana var. speciosa

Larix griffithiana var. speciosa, commonly known as the Griffith's larch, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage that turns a striking golden yellow in autumn. The tree has slender, weeping branches and produces small, cone-like structures that release seeds in the wind. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions at high altitudes.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
