Lupinus standingii

Lupinus standingii, commonly known as the Standing's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus standingii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
