Limonium tubiflorum var. zanonii

Limonium tubiflorum var. zanonii, commonly known as the Zanonii sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces spikes of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial species that is drought-tolerant and well adapted to saline environments.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive color and ability to attract pollinators.
