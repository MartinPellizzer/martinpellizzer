Lasiorrhiza multiflora

Lasiorrhiza multiflora, commonly known as the bluebell lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to produce multiple flower stems from a single plant.
