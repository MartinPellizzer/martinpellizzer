Lilac minor

Lilac minor, commonly known as the dwarf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, making it suitable for small garden spaces. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

Lilac minor thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often blooming in late spring.
