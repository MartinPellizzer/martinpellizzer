Leucothoe platyphylla

Leucothoe platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its foliage provides year-round interest, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
