Liatris praealta

Liatris praealta, commonly known as tall blazing star, is a striking native North American plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to six feet, adorned with clusters of tubular, purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. Liatris praealta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological gardens.
