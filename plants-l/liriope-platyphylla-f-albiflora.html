Liriope platyphylla f. albiflora

Liriope platyphylla f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered liriope, is a cultivar of the liriope genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches and prefers well-drained soil in partial to full shade. It is a low-maintenance groundcover that is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The white flowers of this cultivar stand out against its dark green foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
