Linnaea borealis f. picta

Linnaea borealis f. picta, commonly known as the painted twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant native to the boreal forests of North America and northern Europe.

This fern-like shrub features delicate, opposite leaves that are often adorned with distinctive red or purple markings, giving it its "painted" appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in pairs, a characteristic feature of the genus Linnaea. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in sphagnum moss-dominated habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts.
