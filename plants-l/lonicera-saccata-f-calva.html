Lonicera saccata f. calva

Lonicera saccata f. calva, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is distinguished by its pale flowers compared to the more common yellow-flowered forms of Lonicera saccata.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of garden settings.
