Laurus azorica var. lutea

Laurus azorica var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow laurel, is a variant of the native Azorean laurel.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in the humid, subtropical climates of the Azores. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are lance-shaped and emit a strong, spicy aroma when crushed. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
