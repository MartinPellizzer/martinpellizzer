Lippia trollii var. inermis

Lippia trollii var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth lippia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
