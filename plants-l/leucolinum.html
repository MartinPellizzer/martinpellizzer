Leucolinum

Leucolinum, commonly known as the white lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are usually slender and erect. Leucolinum thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
