Lobivia haematantha var. kuehnrichii

Lobivia haematantha var. kuehnrichii, commonly known as the Red-veined Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem with a distinctive red or pinkish veining, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring and early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, with succulent stems that store water.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens and containers.
