Lilium procumbens

Lilium procumbens, commonly known as the creeping lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the stems often lie close to the ground.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant ecosystems.
