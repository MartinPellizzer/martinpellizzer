Lepidium divaricatum subsp. linoides

Lepidium divaricatum subsp.

linoides, commonly known as the slender wall rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, pale green leaves that are often lobed or divided, and it produces tiny, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important species.
