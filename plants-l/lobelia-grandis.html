Lobelia grandis

Lobelia grandis, commonly known as the grand lobe lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Lobelia grandis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
