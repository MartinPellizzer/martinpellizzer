Lespedeza dichromoxylon

Lespedeza dichromoxylon, commonly known as the redstem lespedeza, is a perennial legume native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza dichromoxylon is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
