Loeseliastrum matthewsii

Loeseliastrum matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthew's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
