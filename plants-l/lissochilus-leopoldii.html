Lissochilus leopoldii

Lissochilus leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lissochilus leopoldii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
