Loranthus amylo-zollingeri

Loranthus amylo-zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds in return. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pollinated by insects. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique parasitic relationship and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
