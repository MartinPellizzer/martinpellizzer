Leea pallidifolia

Leea pallidifolia, commonly known as the pale-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically pale green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
