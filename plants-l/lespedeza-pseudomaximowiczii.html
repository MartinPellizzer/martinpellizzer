Lespedeza pseudomaximowiczii

Lespedeza pseudomaximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese lespea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its ecological role supports pollinators and contributes to biodiversity in its native environment.
