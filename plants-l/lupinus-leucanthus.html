Lupinus leucanthus

Lupinus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus leucanthus is a member of the legume family and can fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
