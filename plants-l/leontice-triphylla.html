Leontice triphylla

Leontice triphylla, commonly known as the mountain mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. Its flowers are pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
