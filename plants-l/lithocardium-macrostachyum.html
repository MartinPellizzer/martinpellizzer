Lithocardium macrostachyum

Lithocardium macrostachyum, commonly known as the large-stemmed rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, often succulent stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Lithocardium macrostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
