Limniboza dilungensis

Limniboza dilungensis, commonly known as the Dilungensis Water Lily, is a rare aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning, releasing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators. Its roots anchor it to the muddy bottom of shallow water bodies, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, Limniboza dilungensis is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
