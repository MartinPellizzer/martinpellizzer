Lithospermum cryptanthiflorum

Lithospermum cryptanthiflorum, commonly known as the cryptanthiflorum lithospermum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant features a sprawling or ascending stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, with roots containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
