Leontodon montanus f. melanotrichus

Leontodon montanus f. melanotrichus, commonly known as the black-haired mountain bellflower, is a rare and distinctive variant of the mountain bellflower.

It is characterized by its dark, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to alpine regions of Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
