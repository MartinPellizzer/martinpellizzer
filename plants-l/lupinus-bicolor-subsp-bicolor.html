Lupinus bicolor subsp. bicolor

Lupinus bicolor subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, typically pink and white, which bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Lupinus bicolor subsp.

bicolor is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
