Lavandula bipinnata

Lavandula bipinnata, commonly known as narrowleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in summer and are highly aromatic. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Lavandula bipinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in aromatherapy and essential oil production.
