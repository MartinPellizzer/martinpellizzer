Larnalles glauca

Larnalles glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silvery-blue foliage and produces fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that bloom in the summer. Larnalles glauca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
