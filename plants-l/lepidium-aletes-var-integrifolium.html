Lepidium aletes var. integrifolium

Lepidium aletes var. integrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, with entire or slightly toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes during the spring and early summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in height.

This variety is characterized by its integrifoliate leaves, which are smooth and uninterrupted along the edges.
