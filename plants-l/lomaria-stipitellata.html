Lomaria stipitellata

Lomaria stipitellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Lomaria stipitellata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem that supports its herbaceous foliage.
