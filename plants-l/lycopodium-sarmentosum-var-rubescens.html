Lycopodium sarmentosum var. rubescens

Lycopodium sarmentosum var. rubescens, commonly known as the red clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, prostrate growth habit and produces small, reddish stems that often form dense mats. The plant features microphylls, which are small, scale-like leaves, and bears sporophylls that enclose the sporangia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

The spores released from the sporangia are dispersed by wind, contributing to its reproductive strategy in woodland ecosystems.
