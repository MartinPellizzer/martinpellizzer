Lunasia quercifolia

Lunasia quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved lunaria, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the oak tree. The plant produces striking blue-violet seed pods that remain on the plant throughout the winter, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lunasia quercifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
