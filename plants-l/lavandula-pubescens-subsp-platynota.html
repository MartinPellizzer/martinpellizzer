Lavandula pubescens subsp. platynota

Lavandula pubescens subsp.

platynota, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and silver-green foliage with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces spikes of pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in aromatherapy and as a source of essential oils.
