Lyciodes angustifolium

Lyciodes angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf lycium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces small, red berries that are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
