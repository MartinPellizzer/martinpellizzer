Leontodon autumnalis var. nigrolanatus

Leontodon autumnalis var. nigrolanatus, commonly known as the black-lanate autumn gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.
