Lythrum loiseleurii subsp. hispidulum

Lythrum loiseleurii subsp.

hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy lythrum, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its subspecies name "hispidulum.". It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
