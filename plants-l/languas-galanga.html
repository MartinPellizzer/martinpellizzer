Languas galanga

Languas galanga, commonly known as greater galangal, is a tropical herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous rootstock. The plant features tall, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in cuisines across Asia.

The botanical features of languas galanga make it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
