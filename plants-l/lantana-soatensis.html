Lantana soatensis

Lantana soatensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year. Its flowers are borne on inflorescences called cymes, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.

Lantana soatensis is also notable for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
