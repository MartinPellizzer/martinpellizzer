Lobivia hertrichiana f. prolifera

Lobivia hertrichiana f. prolifera, commonly known as the clustered lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy stems that store water, and its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation to its harsh environment. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a touch of color to its otherwise desolate habitat.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among experienced cactus enthusiasts.
