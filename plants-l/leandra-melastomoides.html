Leandra melastomoides

Leandra melastomoides, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. It produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Leandra melastomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
