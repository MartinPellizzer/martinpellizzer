Lagerstroemia parviflora

Lagerstroemia parviflora, commonly known as the crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, long-lasting flowers that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes more textured with age, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, turning red or orange in the fall.

Lagerstroemia parviflora is widely cultivated for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
