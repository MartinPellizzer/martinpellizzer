Lepanthes empis

Lepanthes empis, commonly known as the Empis orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Lepanthes empis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
