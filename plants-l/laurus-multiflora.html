Laurus multiflora

Laurus multiflora, commonly known as the sweet bay laurel, is a flowering evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but less commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Laurus multiflora is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical significance in various cultures.
