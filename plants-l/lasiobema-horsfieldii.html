Lasiobema horsfieldii

Lasiobema horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It has small, leathery leaves that are typically clustered at the base of its short, woody stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to eastern Australia and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while also providing food for native wildlife.
