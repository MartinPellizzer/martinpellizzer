Linum munbyanum var. curtifolium

Linum munbyanum var. curtifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is part of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species known for their fibrous stalks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
