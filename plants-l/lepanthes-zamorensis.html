Lepanthes zamorensis

Lepanthes zamorensis, commonly known as the Zamora orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes zamorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
