Lilium bononiense

Lilium bononiense, commonly known as the Bononiensis lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help the plant survive adverse conditions.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
