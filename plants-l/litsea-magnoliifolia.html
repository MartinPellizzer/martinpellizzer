Litsea magnoliifolia

Litsea magnoliifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sweet olive, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
