Lobelia zeylanica var. hirta

Lobelia zeylanica var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a slightly rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
