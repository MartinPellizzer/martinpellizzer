Lotus antiochianus

Lotus antiochianus, commonly known as the Antioch lotus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
