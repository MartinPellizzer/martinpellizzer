Lipusa multiflora var. alba

Lipusa multiflora var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered lipusa, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
