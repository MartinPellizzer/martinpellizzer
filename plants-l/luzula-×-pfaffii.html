Luzula × pfaffii

Luzula × pfaffii, commonly known as the silvergrass or false beardgrass, is a hybrid species within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive silvery-blue foliage due to dense pubescence on the leaf blades. The plant typically forms dense clumps and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish to brownish flowers. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula × pfaffii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
