Lonicera japonica var. repens

Lonicera japonica var. repens, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle vine, is a trailing perennial vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, often becoming invasive in non-native environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental use, though its ecological impact requires careful management.
