Lagochilus pungens

Lagochilus pungens, commonly known as the spiked rock rose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
