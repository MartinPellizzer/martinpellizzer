Lathyrus shaffneri

Lathyrus shaffneri, commonly known as the Shaffner's vetch, is a perennial climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compound leaves and its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.
