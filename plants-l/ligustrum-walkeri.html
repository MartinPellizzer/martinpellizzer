Ligustrum walkeri

Ligustrum walkeri, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and fast growth rate.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
