Lepidium ramosissimum var. divergens

Lepidium ramosissimum var. divergens, commonly known as the California peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. It has a sprawling growth habit with branched stems and small, pinnately divided leaves.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
