Lupinus peruvianus var. decemplex

Lupinus peruvianus var. decemplex, commonly known as the Peruvian lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The lupine is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
