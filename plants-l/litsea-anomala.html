Litsea anomala

Litsea anomala, commonly known as the wild lime or Malayan wild lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, elliptical leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are highly aromatic and contain a high concentration of essential oils. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Litsea anomala thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
