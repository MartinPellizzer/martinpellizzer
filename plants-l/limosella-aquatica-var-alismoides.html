Limosella aquatica var. alismoides

Limosella aquatica var. alismoides, commonly known as marsh sandwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland health and biodiversity.
