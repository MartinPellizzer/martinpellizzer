Lonicera caerulea subsp. stenantha

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

stenantha, commonly known as the sand cherry or shrub honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellowish-green, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
