Libanothamnus tamanus

Libanothamnus tamanus, commonly known as the Tamanus shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some areas.
