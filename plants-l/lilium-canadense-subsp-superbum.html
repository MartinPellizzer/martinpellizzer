Lilium canadense subsp. superbum

Lilium canadense subsp.

superbum, commonly known as the Canada lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white to pale yellow with a green throat, blooming in late summer.

The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the base. It grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The subspecies superbum is distinguished by its larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other subspecies of Lilium canadense.
