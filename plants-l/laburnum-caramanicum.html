Laburnum caramanicum

Laburnum caramanicum, commonly known as the Caramanica laburnum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking yellow, pendulous flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
