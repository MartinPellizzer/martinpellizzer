Loasa floribunda var. brachysepala

Loasa floribunda var. brachysepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled loasa, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments within its native habitat.
