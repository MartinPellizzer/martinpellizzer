Leptospermum persiciflorum

Leptospermum persiciflorum, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal or sandy soils. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
