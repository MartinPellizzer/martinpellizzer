Lavoisiera pohliana

Lavoisiera pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its roots are adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Lavoisiera pohliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
