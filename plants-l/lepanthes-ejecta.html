Lepanthes ejecta

Lepanthes ejecta, commonly known as the ejected orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant produces aerial roots that help it cling to trees in its natural habitat. Lepanthes ejecta is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within the ecosystem and its ornamental value in horticulture.
