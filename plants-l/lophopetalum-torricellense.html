Lophopetalum torricellense

Lophopetalum torricellense, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple berries that persist through winter, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with a smooth margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and other wildlife.
