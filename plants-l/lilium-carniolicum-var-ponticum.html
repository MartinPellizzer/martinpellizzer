Lilium carniolicum var. ponticum

Lilium carniolicum var. ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with six petals and a distinctive spathaceous sheath. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with linear, grass-like leaves arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
