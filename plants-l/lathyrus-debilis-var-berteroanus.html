Lathyrus debilis var. berteroanus

Lathyrus debilis var. berteroanus, commonly known as the dwarf blue flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces slender, twisted pods containing flattened seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaped gardens.
