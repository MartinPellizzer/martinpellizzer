Lobelia scabripes

Lobelia scabripes, commonly known as the roughleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lobelia scabripes is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
