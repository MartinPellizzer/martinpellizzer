Lasiopogon debilis

Lasiopogon debilis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and white, fleshy berries. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in dense forest canopies.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health and growth of its host trees.
