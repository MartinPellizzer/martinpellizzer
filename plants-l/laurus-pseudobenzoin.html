Laurus pseudobenzoin

Laurus pseudobenzoin, commonly known as false benzoquin, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong, resinous scent. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries.

Laurus pseudobenzoin is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
