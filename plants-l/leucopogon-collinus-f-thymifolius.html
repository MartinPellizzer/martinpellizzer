Leucopogon collinus f. thymifolius

Leucopogon collinus f. thymifolius, commonly known as the thyme-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a strong aromatic scent reminiscent of thyme. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
