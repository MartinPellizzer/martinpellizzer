Leucolaena tridentata

Leucolaena tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed leucolaena, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Leucolaena tridentata is used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
