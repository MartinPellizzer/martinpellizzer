Leucopogon exsertus

Leucopogon exsertus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Leucopogon exsertus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
