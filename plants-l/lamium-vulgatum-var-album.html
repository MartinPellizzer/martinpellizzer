Lamium vulgatum var. album

Lamium vulgatum var. album, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and gardens. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

White dead-nettle is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a ground cover in landscaping.
