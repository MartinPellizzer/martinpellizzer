Limonium delicatulum subsp. santapolense

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

santapolense, commonly known as the Santapole sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in compact inflorescences.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
