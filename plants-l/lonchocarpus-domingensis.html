Lonchocarpus domingensis

Lonchocarpus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican senna, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, woody structure. It is often found in tropical dry forests and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
