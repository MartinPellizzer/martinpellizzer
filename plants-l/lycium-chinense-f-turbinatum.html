Lycium chinense f. turbinatum

Lycium chinense f. turbinatum, commonly known as the turbinated Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
