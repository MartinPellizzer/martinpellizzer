Lamourouxia pringlei

Lamourouxia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them. Its unique morphology includes long, thin leaves and a single, slender stem that supports its striking floral display.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, Lamourouxia pringlei is considered a vulnerable species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
