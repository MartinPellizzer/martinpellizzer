Lycomormium elatum

Lycomormium elatum, commonly known as the giant lycopus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically bloom in shades of blue or purple.

Lycomormium elatum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
