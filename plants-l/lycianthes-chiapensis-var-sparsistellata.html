Lycianthes chiapensis var. sparsistellata

Lycianthes chiapensis var. sparsistellata, commonly known as the sparsely-stellate lycianthes, is a species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, branching stems and broad, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx. It produces small, round fruits that are often covered in fine hairs.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical and subtropical regions.
