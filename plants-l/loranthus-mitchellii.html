Loranthus mitchellii

Loranthus mitchellii, commonly known as the Mitchell's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using sunlight. The plant forms a dense, spherical growth with small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, contributing to its spread within forest ecosystems.

Loranthus mitchellii plays a role in ecological interactions, influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
