Lippia rivalis

Lippia rivalis, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Lippia rivalis is a perennial herb that can grow up to 3 feet tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped foliage.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
