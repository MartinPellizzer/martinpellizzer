Linaria italica var. angustifolia

Linaria italica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, spurred flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
