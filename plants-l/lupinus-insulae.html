Lupinus insulae

Lupinus insulae, commonly known as the island lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus insulae is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
