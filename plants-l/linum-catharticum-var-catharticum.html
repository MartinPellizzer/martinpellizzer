Linum catharticum var. catharticum

Linum catharticum var. catharticum, commonly known as the common flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural fields.

Linum catharticum is also known for its fibrous seeds, which have been historically used for fiber production and oil extraction.
