Lepanthes atwoodii

Lepanthes atwoodii, commonly known as the Atwood's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble small slippers, typically in shades of white, yellow, or green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes atwoodii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
