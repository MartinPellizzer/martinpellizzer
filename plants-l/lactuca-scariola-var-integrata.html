Lactuca scariola var. integrata

Lactuca scariola var. integrata, commonly known as the slender leaf lettuce, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its seeds are small and oval, with a smooth surface, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The species is related to other lettuces and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
