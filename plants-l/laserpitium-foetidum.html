Laserpitium foetidum

Laserpitium foetidum, commonly known as the stinking everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.
