Lettsomia pomacea

Lettsomia pomacea, commonly known as the giant lettuce plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in diameter. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanical collections.
