Leucopogon lissanthoides

Leucopogon lissanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or acidic soils, often in heathlands or coastal areas. It produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
