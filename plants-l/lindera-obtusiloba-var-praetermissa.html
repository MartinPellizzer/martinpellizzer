Lindera obtusiloba var. praetermissa

Lindera obtusiloba var. praetermissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pepper tree, is a variant of the sweet pepper tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
