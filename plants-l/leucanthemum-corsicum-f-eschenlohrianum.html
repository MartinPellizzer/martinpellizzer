Leucanthemum corsicum f. eschenlohrianum

Leucanthemum corsicum f. eschenlohrianum, commonly known as the Corsican daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers with a central brown disc. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
