Lavandula delphinensis

Lavandula delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian lavender, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, making it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.

The leaves and flowers are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine due to their essential oil content.
