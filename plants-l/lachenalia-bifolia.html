Lachenalia bifolia

Lachenalia bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved lachenalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
