Leucospermum incisum

Leucospermum incisum, commonly known as the firewheel plant, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens across the world.
