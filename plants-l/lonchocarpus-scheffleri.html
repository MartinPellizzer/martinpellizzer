Lonchocarpus scheffleri

Lonchocarpus scheffleri, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod fruits that contain flat, seed-like structures. It is often found in savannas and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
