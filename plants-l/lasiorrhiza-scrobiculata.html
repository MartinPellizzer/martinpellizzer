Lasiorrhiza scrobiculata

Lasiorrhiza scrobiculata, commonly known as the roughleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
