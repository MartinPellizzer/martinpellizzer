Lycopodium mnioides

Lycopodium mnioides, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, terrestrial fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its compact, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves. It reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the undersides of its leaves.

Lycopodium mnioides plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing microhabitats for various invertebrates.
