Leucas mollissima subsp. acuminata

Leucas mollissima subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the slender leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
