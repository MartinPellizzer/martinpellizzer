Leuzea satzyperovii

Leuzea satzyperovii, commonly known as the Siberian leuzea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with soft, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Leuzea satzyperovii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
