Lithospermum bicolor

Lithospermum bicolor, commonly known as American gromwell, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots contain compounds such as lithospermic acid, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Lithospermum bicolor thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
