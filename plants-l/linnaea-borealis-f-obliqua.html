Linnaea borealis f. obliqua

Linnaea borealis f. obliqua, commonly known as the twisted twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of North America and Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted stems and small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

This fern-like plant is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in its habitat.
