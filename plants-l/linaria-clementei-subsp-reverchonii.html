Linaria clementei subsp. reverchonii

Linaria clementei subsp.

reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, branching stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Linaria, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.
