Lamium inflatum

Lamium inflatum, commonly known as the inflated deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with two lips, resembling those of the genus *Antirrhinum*. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
