Lasiandra maximiliana

Lasiandra maximiliana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Lasiandra maximiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
