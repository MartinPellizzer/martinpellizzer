Lauro-cerasus andersonii

Lauro-cerasus andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson cherry laurel, is a hybrid shrub that combines characteristics of both cherry (Prunus) and laurel (Laurocerasus) species.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are popular among birds. Its dense foliage and evergreen nature make it a valuable ornamental plant in landscaping.

However, it is considered invasive in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
