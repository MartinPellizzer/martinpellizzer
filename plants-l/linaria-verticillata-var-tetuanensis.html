Linaria verticillata var. tetuanensis

Linaria verticillata var. tetuanensis, commonly known as the Tetuan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers arranged in a whorled pattern. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its use in both garden settings and horticultural collections.
