Laserpitium garganicum var. balcanicum

Laserpitium garganicum var. balcanicum, commonly known as Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is adapted to temperate climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth form and is often found in mountainous regions.
