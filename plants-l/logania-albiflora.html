Logania albiflora

Logania albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered logania, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds.

Logania albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
