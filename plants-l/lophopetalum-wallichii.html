Lophopetalum wallichii

Lophopetalum wallichii, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
