Lathyrus ochroleucus

Lathyrus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural research.
