Lepanthes pusilla

Lepanthes pusilla, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes pusilla is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its miniature size and ornamental beauty.
