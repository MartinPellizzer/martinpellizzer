Lycopodium brachystachys

Lycopodium brachystachys, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. Lycopodium brachystachys is often found in forest floors and rocky crevices, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
