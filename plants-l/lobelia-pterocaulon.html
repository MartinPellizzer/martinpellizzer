Lobelia pterocaulon

Lobelia pterocaulon, commonly known as the narrowleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Lobelia pterocaulon is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in clusters.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
