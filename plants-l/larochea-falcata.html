Larochea falcata

Larochea falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf larochea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically inconspicuous.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
