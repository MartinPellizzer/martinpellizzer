Lepanthes montana

Lepanthes montana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
