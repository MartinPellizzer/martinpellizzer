Lilium regale

Lilium regale, commonly known as the Royal Lily, is a striking species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Lilium regale is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
