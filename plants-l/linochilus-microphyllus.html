Linochilus microphyllus

Linochilus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved linochilus, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Linochilus microphyllus is often found growing in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
