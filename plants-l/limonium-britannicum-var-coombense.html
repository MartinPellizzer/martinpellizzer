Limonium britannicum var. coombense

Limonium britannicum var. coombense, commonly known as the Coomb's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of western Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in maritime climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dune habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
