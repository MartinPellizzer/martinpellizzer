Lhotskya acutifolia

Lhotskya acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lanceolate and sharply pointed, which gives the plant its specific epithet "acutifolia.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
