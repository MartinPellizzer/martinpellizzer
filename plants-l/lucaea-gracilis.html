Lucaea gracilis

Lucaea gracilis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lucaea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
