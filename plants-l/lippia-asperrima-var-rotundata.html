Lippia asperrima var. rotundata

Lippia asperrima var. rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaved lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
