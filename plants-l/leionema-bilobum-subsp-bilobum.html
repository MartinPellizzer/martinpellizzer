Leionema bilobum subsp. bilobum

Leionema bilobum subsp.

bilobum, commonly known as the two-lobed leionema, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
