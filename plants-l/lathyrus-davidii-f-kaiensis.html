Lathyrus davidii f. kaiensis

Lathyrus davidii f. kaiensis, commonly known as the Kai Islands larkspur, is a botanical variant of the larkspur genus native to the islands of the Solomon Islands.

This plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The foliage consists of compound leaves with slender, lance-shaped leaflets.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
