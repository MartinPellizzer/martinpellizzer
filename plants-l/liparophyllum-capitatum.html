Liparophyllum capitatum

Liparophyllum capitatum, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and alpine environments.

Liparophyllum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
