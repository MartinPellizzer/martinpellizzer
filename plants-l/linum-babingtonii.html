Linum babingtonii

Linum babingtonii, commonly known as Babington's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. This species is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has fibrous roots and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
