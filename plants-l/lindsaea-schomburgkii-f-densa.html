Lindsaea schomburgkii f. densa

Lindsaea schomburgkii f. densa, commonly known as the dense lindsaea, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and possesses long, slender, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, delicate spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in groups called sori on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the canopies of tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
