Luzula rhadina

Luzula rhadina, commonly known as the slender wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish to yellowish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula rhadina is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
