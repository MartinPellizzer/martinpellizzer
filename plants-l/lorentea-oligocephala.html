Lorentea oligocephala

Lorentea oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Lorentea oligocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
