Loranthus crassicaulis

Loranthus crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in ecological interactions with its host and wildlife.
