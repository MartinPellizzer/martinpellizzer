Leiothrix flavescens var. parvifolia

Leiothrix flavescens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf golden bell, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and the subtle, fragrant blooms that add a touch of elegance to the landscape.
