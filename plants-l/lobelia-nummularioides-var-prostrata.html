Lobelia nummularioides var. prostrata

Lobelia nummularioides var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate lobelia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has rounded, succulent-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
