Linaria × ambigua

Linaria × ambigua, commonly known as the hybrid toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that typically range from yellow to orange. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Linaria × ambigua is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
