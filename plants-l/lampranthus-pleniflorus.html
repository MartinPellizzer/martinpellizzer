Lampranthus pleniflorus

Lampranthus pleniflorus, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red, tubular flowers that resemble tiny fireworks, hence its common name. The plant is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its fleshy leaves and stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It typically blooms during the summer months, producing clusters of vibrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
