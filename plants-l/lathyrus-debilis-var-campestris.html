Lathyrus debilis var. campestris

Lathyrus debilis var. campestris, commonly known as the creeping vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical pods containing several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
