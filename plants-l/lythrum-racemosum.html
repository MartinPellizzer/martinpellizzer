Lythrum racemosum

Lythrum racemosum, commonly known as rosemary bush or purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

Lythrum racemosum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
