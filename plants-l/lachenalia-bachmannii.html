Lachenalia bachmannii

Lachenalia bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
