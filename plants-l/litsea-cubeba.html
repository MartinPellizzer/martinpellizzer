Litsea cubeba

Litsea cubeba, commonly known as wild lime or mountain pepper, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a distinctive citrus-like aroma. The essential oil extracted from its fruits is widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
