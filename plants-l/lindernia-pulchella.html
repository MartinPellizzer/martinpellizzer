Lindernia pulchella

Lindernia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Lindernia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
