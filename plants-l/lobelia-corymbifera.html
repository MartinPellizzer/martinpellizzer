Lobelia corymbifera

Lobelia corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped, opposite leaves. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.

Lobelia corymbifera is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
