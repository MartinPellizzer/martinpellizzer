Ledebouria viscosa

Ledebouria viscosa, commonly known as the sticky ledebouria, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are thick and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Ledebouria viscosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
