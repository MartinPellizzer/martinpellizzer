Lepanthes marmorata

Lepanthes marmorata, commonly known as the marble orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, white and green marbled flowers that resemble marble patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, providing structural support for the inflorescence.

Lepanthes marmorata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
