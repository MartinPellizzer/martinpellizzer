Leucothoe revoluta var. salzmannii

Leucothoe revoluta var. salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features attractive, glossy evergreen leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for woodland gardens and as a foundation plant.
