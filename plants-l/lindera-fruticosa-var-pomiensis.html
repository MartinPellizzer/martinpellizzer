Lindera fruticosa var. pomiensis

Lindera fruticosa var. pomiensis, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
