Lycopus mollis

Lycopus mollis, commonly known as blueweed or waterweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopus mollis is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
