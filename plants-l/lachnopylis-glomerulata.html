Lachnopylis glomerulata

Lachnopylis glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered lichen grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

L. glomerulata is often found in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and is adapted to thrive in partial shade.
