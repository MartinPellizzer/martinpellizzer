Leptostachya heterophylla var. acutior

Leptostachya heterophylla var. acutior, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the genus Leptostachya, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves with a sharp apical margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and is native to regions of Southeast Asia. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to its ornamental value.

The species exhibits dimorphic leaf morphology, with variations in leaf shape and size depending on environmental conditions.
