Leretia paniculata

Leretia paniculata, commonly known as the golden panicled lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in paniculate inflorescences, giving it a dramatic and showy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Leretia paniculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
