Lemna landoltii

Lemna landoltii, commonly known as the landolt's duckweed, is a small, free-floating aquatic plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant reproduces primarily through vegetative propagation, with new plants forming from the fragmentation of its fronds. Due to its rapid growth rate, it can quickly form dense mats on the water surface.
