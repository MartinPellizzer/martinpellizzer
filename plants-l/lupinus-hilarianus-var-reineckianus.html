Lupinus hilarianus var. reineckianus

Lupinus hilarianus var. reineckianus, commonly known as the Reineck's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of oval, hairy leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
