Libocedrus tetragona var. nana

Libocedrus tetragona var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf incense cedar, is a compact evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dense, scale-like leaves that are dark green and emit a subtle, resinous fragrance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native forests.
