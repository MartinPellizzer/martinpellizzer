Leopardanthus scandens

Leopardanthus scandens, commonly known as the creeping leopard's bane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
