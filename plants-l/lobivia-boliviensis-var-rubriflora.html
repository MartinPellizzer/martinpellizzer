Lobivia boliviensis var. rubriflora

Lobivia boliviensis var. rubriflora, commonly known as the Red-flowered Lobivia, is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of Bolivia.

It is a compact, succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves that store water. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
