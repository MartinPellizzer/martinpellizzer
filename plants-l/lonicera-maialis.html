Lonicera maialis

Lonicera maialis, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and moist, well-drained soil.

Lonicera maialis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
