Leucospermum royenifolium

Leucospermum royenifolium, commonly known as the Royal Crown fireplant, is a striking South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which are clustered in a crown-like arrangement, giving it its common name. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as bloom. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
