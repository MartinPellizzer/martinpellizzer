Lachnastoma uniflorum

Lachnastoma uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower lichen, is a unique species within the Lachnastomataceae family.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its leaf-like thallus that is typically green to yellowish in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides the primary source of photosynthesis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
