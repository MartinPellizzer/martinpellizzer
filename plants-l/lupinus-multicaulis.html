Lupinus multicaulis

Lupinus multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus multicaulis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
