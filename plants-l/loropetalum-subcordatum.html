Loropetalum subcordatum

Loropetalum subcordatum, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a shrub native to China and is part of the Heidebrauch family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Loropetalum subcordatum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
