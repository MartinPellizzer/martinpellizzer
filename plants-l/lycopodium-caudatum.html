Lycopodium caudatum

Lycopodium caudatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, sparsely branched stems and needle-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Lycopodium caudatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
