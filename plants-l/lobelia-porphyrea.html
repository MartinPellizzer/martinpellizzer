Lobelia porphyrea

Lobelia porphyrea, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia porphyrea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
