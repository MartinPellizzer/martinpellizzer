Leptospermum trinerve

Leptospermum trinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is typically smooth and may exude a mild aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous practices.
