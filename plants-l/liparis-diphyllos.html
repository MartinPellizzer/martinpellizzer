Liparis diphyllos

Liparis diphyllos, commonly known as the two-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

Liparis diphyllos is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in humid, tropical climates.
