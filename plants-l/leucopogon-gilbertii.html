Leucopogon gilbertii

Leucopogon gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems, contributing to its dense, compact growth habit.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
