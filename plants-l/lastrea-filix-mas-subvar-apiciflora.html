Lastrea filix-mas subvar. apiciflora

Lastrea filix-mas subvar. apiciflora, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a unique variant of the hybrid Epiphyllum oxypetalum.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that are often adorned with vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is native to the tropical regions of South America and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to bloom during the holiday season.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural study and decorative cultivation.
