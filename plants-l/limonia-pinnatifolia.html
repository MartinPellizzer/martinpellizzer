Limonia pinnatifolia

Limonia pinnatifolia, commonly known as the Java hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 meters. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive pinnate arrangement, giving it its scientific name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal environments due to its salt tolerance.
