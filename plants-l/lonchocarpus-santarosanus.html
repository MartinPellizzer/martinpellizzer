Lonchocarpus santarosanus

Lonchocarpus santarosanus, commonly known as the Brazilian wahoo, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on the environmental conditions.
