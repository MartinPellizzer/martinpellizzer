Lateristachys ramulosa

Lateristachys ramulosa, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flattened, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African communities.
