Lycopersicon esculentum convar. infiniens

Lycopersicon esculentum convar. infiniens, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a botanical variant of the domesticated tomato.

It is characterized by its smaller, often hairy leaves and fruits that are typically red or yellow, though they can vary in color. This species is native to South America and thrives in a variety of habitats, including dry and arid regions. The plant exhibits a bushy growth habit and produces flowers that are typically yellow or green.

It is valued for its genetic diversity and is often used in breeding programs to improve the resilience of cultivated tomatoes.
