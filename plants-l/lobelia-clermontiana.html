Lobelia clermontiana

Lobelia clermontiana, commonly known as the Florida lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and prefers moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia clermontiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
