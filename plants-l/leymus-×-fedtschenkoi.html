Leymus × fedtschenkoi

Leymus × fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the intermediate wheatgrass, is a sterile hybrid grass species resulting from the cross between Leymus arenarius and Leymus triticoides.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth and is native to the steppes of Central Asia. This plant typically forms dense, sod-forming vegetation and is adapted to a wide range of soil types and climatic conditions. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are often clustered in panicles.

Due to its hardiness and potential for forage and erosion control, it is of interest in ecological and agricultural studies.
