Luisia teres

Luisia teres, commonly known as the creeping luisia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, compact growth with small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover in Mediterranean climates.
