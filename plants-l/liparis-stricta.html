Liparis stricta

Liparis stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in clusters.

Liparis stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
