Logania floribunda var. brevifolia

Logania floribunda var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved logania, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically small and ovate, with a smooth texture, while the flowers are often bright red or orange, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
