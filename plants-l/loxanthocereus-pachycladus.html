Loxanthocereus pachycladus

Loxanthocereus pachycladus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a columnar cactus native to Mexico.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem covered in clusters of spines and yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soil. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for desert gardens.
