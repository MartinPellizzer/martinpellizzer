Lomatium triternatum subsp. triternatum

Lomatium triternatum subsp.

triternatum, commonly known as the Townsend's lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and showy blooms.
