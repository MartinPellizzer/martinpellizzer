Linum hirsutum var. glanduliferum

Linum hirsutum var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular flax, is a variant of the flax plant characterized by its hairy stems and glandular structures.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in textile production.
