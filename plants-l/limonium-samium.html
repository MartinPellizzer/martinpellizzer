Limonium samium

Limonium samium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in saline environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Limonium samium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
