Licuala beccariana

Licuala beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. It typically grows as a solitary tree with a slender trunk and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Licuala beccariana is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
