Limacia laurifolia

Limacia laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel ivy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Limacia laurifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
