Lasianthus capitatus subsp. vietnamensis

Lasianthus capitatus subsp.

vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
