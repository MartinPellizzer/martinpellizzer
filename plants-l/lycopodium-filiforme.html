Lycopodium filiforme

Lycopodium filiforme, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces tiny, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. Lycopodium filiforme typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its simple morphology and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of early land plant evolution.
