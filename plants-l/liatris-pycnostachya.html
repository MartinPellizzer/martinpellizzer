Liatris pycnostachya

Liatris pycnostachya, commonly known as the tall blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, spikelike inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows 3 to 6 feet tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Liatris pycnostachya is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
