Lysiloma bahamense

Lysiloma bahamense, commonly known as the Bahamian ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree has a dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in dry, sandy soils.
