Lobivia chlorogona var. purpureostoma

Lobivia chlorogona var. purpureostoma, commonly known as the Purple-stemmed Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The leaves are typically small and cylindrical, reducing surface area to minimize water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, emerging from the axils of the leaves, and bloom during the warmer months.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
