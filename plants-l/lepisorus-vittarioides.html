Lepisorus vittarioides

Lepisorus vittarioides, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bright green with a waxy coating, and it produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Lepisorus vittarioides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
