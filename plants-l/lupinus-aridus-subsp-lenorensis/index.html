Lupinus Aridus Subsp. Lenorensis

Lupinus aridus subsp. lenorensis is a rare and hardy plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is known for its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. This subspecies thrives in sandy and rocky soils where water is scarce. It plays an important role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and supporting local pollinators. Due to its limited range, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
