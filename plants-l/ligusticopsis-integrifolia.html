Ligusticopsis integrifolia

Ligusticopsis integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed ligusticopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have smooth margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ligusticopsis integrifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
