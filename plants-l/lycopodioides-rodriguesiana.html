Lycopodioides rodriguesiana

Lycopodioides rodriguesiana, commonly known as Rodrigues's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves, typical of the lycophyte group. Native to the island of Rodrigues in the Indian Ocean, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy involving microsporangia and megasporangia, contributing to its ecological niche.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to its specific habitat, making it an important component of its local flora.
