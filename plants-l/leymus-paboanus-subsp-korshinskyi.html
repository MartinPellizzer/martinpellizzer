Leymus paboanus subsp. korshinskyi

Leymus paboanus subsp.

korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
