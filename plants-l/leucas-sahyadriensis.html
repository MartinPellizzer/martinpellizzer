Leucas sahyadriensis

Leucas sahyadriensis, commonly known as the Sahyadri leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is known for its clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
