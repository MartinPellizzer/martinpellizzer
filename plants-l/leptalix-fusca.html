Leptalix fusca

Leptalix fusca, commonly known as the dark leathery lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery thallus and a complex structure composed of fungal and algal components. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the environment. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate forests.

Its slow growth and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an important indicator species for ecological health.
