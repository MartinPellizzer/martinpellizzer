Lycianthes mozinoana var. oaxacana

Lycianthes mozinoana var. oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca eggplant, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by egg-shaped, green fruits. The fruits mature to a deep purple color and are edible, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
