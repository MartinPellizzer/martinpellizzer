Leonurus superbus

Leonurus superbus, commonly known as the greater betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Leonurus superbus has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and healing properties.
