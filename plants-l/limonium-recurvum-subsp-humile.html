Limonium recurvum subsp. humile

Limonium recurvum subsp.

humile, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to salty, windy environments. It is often found in dunes, cliffs, and other coastal habitats. Limonium recurvum subsp.

humile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
