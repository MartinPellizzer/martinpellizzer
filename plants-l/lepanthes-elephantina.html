Lepanthes elephantina

Lepanthes elephantina, commonly known as the elephant's tusk orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It features striking, showy flowers that are often white or pale pink with a distinctive, elongated spur resembling an elephant's tusk. The plant's leaves are usually broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes elephantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
