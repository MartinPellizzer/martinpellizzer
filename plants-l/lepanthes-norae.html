Lepanthes norae

Lepanthes norae, commonly known as the Norae's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and broad, providing a lush backdrop to its colorful blooms.

Lepanthes norae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
