Luzula albida

Luzula albida, commonly known as white wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish or white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer.

Luzula albida is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
