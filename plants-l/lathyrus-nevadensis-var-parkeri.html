Lathyrus nevadensis var. parkeri

Lathyrus nevadensis var. parkeri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.
