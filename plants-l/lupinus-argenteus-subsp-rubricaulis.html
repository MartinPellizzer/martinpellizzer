Lupinus argenteus subsp. rubricaulis

Lupinus argenteus subsp.

rubricaulis, commonly known as the silver lupine with red stems, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and distinctive red or purple stems.

The plant produces spiny pods containing small, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
