Larix americana var. brevifolia

Larix americana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the American larch or tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive needles, which turn golden yellow in autumn before shedding. The tree produces small, woody cones that release seeds in the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in forest ecosystems.
