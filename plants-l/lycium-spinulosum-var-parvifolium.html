Lycium spinulosum var. parvifolium

Lycium spinulosum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, spine-tipped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
