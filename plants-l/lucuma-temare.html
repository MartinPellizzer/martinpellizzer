Lucuma temare

Lucuma temare, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyclanthaceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange, egg-shaped fruits that are rich in nutrients. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in desserts and traditional Andean cuisine. The plant typically grows at high altitudes and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that bloom before the fruit develops.
