Lophiaris crispiflora

Lophiaris crispiflora, commonly known as the curly-leaved lophiaris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its distinctive, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often curled or crinkled. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
