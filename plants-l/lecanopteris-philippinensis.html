Lecanopteris philippinensis

Lecanopteris philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, finger-like segments. Its rhizomes are submerged and help anchor the plant in the water.

Lecanopteris philippinensis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
