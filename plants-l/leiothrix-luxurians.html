Leiothrix luxurians

Leiothrix luxurians, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
