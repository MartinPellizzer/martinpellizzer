Lepidium densiflorum var. pubicaule

Lepidium densiflorum var. pubicaule, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescence of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
