Listera longicaulis

Listera longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed basket lily, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, nodding white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are elongated and often arching, giving it a graceful appearance.

Listera longicaulis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
