Linum hirsutum subsp. bozdaghense

Linum hirsutum subsp.

bozdaghense, commonly known as Bozdagh flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate aesthetic to its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
