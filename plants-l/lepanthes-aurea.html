Lepanthes aurea

Lepanthes aurea, commonly known as the golden slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes aurea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in its native habitats.
