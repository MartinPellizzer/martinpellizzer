Lobivia leucoviolacea

Lobivia leucoviolacea, commonly known as the White Violet Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features compact, rounded growth with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale violet, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
