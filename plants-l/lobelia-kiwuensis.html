Lobelia kiwuensis

Lobelia kiwuensis, commonly known as the East African lobelia, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania and Kenya.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and riverine areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are clustered in dense spikes.

Lobelia kiwuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
