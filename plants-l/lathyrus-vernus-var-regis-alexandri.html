Lathyrus vernus var. regis-alexandri

Lathyrus vernus var. regis-alexandri, commonly known as the Alexander's blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods containing several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.
