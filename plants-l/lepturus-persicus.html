Lepturus persicus

Lepturus persicus, commonly known as Persian leek, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
