Lagenophora lanata

Lagenophora lanata, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Lagenophora lanata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage.
