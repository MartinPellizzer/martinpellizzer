Lonicera hispida var. typica

Lonicera hispida var. typica, commonly known as the rough honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant its "hispida" name, meaning "hairy.". The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
