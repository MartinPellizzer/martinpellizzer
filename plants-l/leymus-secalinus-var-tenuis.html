Leymus secalinus var. tenuis

Leymus secalinus var. tenuis, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
