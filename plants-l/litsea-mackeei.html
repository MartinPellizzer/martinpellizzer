Litsea mackeei

Litsea mackeei, commonly known as the Malayan litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while its fruits are small drupes that turn from green to red or black as they mature.

Litsea mackeei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
