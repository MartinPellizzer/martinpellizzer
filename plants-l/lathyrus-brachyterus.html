Lathyrus brachyterus

Lathyrus brachyterus, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne in dense racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Lathyrus brachyterus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
