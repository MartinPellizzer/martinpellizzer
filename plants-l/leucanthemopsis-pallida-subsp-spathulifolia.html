Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp. spathulifolia

Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp.

spathulifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a silvery-gray underside, and it produces yellowish to greenish flowers.
