Linospadix

Linospadix, commonly known as the velvet spathiphyllum, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

The plant features a spadix, which is a dense, spike-like cluster of tiny flowers, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The spathe is often brightly colored and serves to attract pollinators. Linospadix is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to purify indoor air.
