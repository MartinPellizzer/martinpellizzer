Leucothoe axillaris var. editorum

Leucothoe axillaris var. editorum, commonly known as the Southern Cross Beauty, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to woodland gardens.
