Lissochilus graeferi

Lissochilus graeferi, commonly known as the Graefer's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This orchid thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
