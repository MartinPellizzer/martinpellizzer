Leptochilus scalpturatus

Leptochilus scalpturatus, commonly known as the scarlet tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and produces bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
