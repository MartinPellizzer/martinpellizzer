Lymania involucrata

Lymania involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Lymania involucrata is known for its ability to grow rapidly and can become invasive in certain areas.
