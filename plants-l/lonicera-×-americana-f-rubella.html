Lonicera × americana f. rubella

Lonicera × americana f. rubella, commonly known as the redflower honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are more intense than those of its parent species. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, with opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing nectar-rich blossoms that attract pollinators.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
