Lathyrus luteus var. occidentalis

Lathyrus luteus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume that produces clusters of yellow or orange pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
