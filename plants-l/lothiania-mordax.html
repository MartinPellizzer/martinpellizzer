Lothiania mordax

Lothiania mordax, commonly known as the silver-leafed thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators. Its woody stems are covered in sharp spines, providing defense against herbivores.

Lothiania mordax is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
