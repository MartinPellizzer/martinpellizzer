Listera cordata var. japonica

Listera cordata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese heartleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental gardening.
