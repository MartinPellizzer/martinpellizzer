Le-monniera ogouensis

Le-monniera ogouensis, commonly known as the African lady's mantle, is a perennial shrub native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides habitat for various small animals and insects. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
