Lathyrus aphaca var. stipularis

Lathyrus aphaca var. stipularis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
