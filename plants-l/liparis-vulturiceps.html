Liparis vulturiceps

Liparis vulturiceps, commonly known as the vulture's beak orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a vulture's beak, with a prominent, downward-curving lip. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of green and white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Liparis vulturiceps is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
