Leonia martii

Leonia martii, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-colored fronds that form a crown-like appearance, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil to flourish. Its fronds are typically lance-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length.

Leonia martii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens and private collections.
