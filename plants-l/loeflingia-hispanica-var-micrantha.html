Loeflingia hispanica var. micrantha

Loeflingia hispanica var. micrantha, commonly known as the Spanish bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
