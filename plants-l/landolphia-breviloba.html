Landolphia breviloba

Landolphia breviloba, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces berries that are typically orange or red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is notable for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
