Lippia turnerifolia

Lippia turnerifolia, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lippia turnerifolia is often found in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies for their potential medicinal properties.
