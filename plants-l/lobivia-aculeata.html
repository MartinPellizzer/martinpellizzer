Lobivia aculeata

Lobivia aculeata, commonly known as the Prickly Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the axils of the leaves during the warmer months.

Lobivia aculeata is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
