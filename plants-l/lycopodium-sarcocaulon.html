Lycopodium sarcocaulon

Lycopodium sarcocaulon, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. It is often found in forest understories and is adapted to thrive in humid, temperate climates.

Lycopodium sarcocaulon plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
