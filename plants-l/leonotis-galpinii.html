Leonotis galpinii

Leonotis galpinii, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, spiky cluster. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Leonotis galpinii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
