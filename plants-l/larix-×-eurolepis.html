Larix × eurolepis

Larix × eurolepis, commonly known as the European larch, is a hybrid coniferous tree resulting from the cross between the European larch (Larix decidua) and the Japanese larch (Larix kaempferi).

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including those that are relatively dry. The tree produces cone-shaped seed structures that release winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. Its needles are typically bright green and turn golden in the fall before shedding, contributing to its ornamental value.

Larix × eurolepis is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
