Lespedeza formosensis

Lespedeza formosensis, commonly known as the Taiwan lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, arranged in racemes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lespedeza formosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
