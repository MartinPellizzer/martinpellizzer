Lepidium songaricum

Lepidium songaricum, commonly known as the Siberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically lobed and slightly hairy. Lepidium songaricum is often found growing in moist, rocky soils and can thrive in temperate to subarctic climates.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its harsh natural environment, making it a resilient and hardy species.
