Lipocarpha multibracteata

Lipocarpha multibracteata, commonly known as the many-bract marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and the presence of numerous bracts along its flowering stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its roots are adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Lipocarpha multibracteata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
