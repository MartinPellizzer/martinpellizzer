Lobelia holotricha

Lobelia holotricha, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Lobelia holotricha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland areas.

Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.
