Lilium dahuricum

Lilium dahuricum, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including parts of Russia, Mongolia, and China. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
