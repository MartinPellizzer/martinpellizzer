Lindera supracostata

Lindera supracostata, commonly known as the mountain sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. Its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers appear in late spring and are clustered in racemes.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that matures in the fall.
