Lychnis rubra

Lychnis rubra, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Lychnis rubra is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is considered a hardy, adaptable species.
