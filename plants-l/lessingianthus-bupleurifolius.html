Lessingianthus bupleurifolius

Lessingianthus bupleurifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
