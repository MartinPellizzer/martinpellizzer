Lupinus verticillatus

Lupinus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Lupinus verticillatus is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
