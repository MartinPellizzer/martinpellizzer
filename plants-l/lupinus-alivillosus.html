Lupinus alivillosus

Lupinus alivillosus, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has feathery, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Lupinus alivillosus is a member of the legume family and can fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
