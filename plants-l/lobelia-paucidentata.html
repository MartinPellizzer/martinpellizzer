Lobelia paucidentata

Lobelia paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have few teeth, giving it its scientific name "paucidentata.".

Lobelia paucidentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
