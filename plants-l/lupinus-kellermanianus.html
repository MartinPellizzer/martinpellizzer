Lupinus kellermanianus

Lupinus kellermanianus, commonly known as the Kellerman's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lupinus kellermanianus is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, supporting soil fertility and biodiversity.
