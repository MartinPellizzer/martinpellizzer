Latania verschaffeltii

Latania verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Zanzibar palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the islands of Zanzibar and nearby regions in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its slow growth rate makes it a valuable ornamental species in warm climates.

Due to its limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
