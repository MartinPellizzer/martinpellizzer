Limnophila laxa

Limnophila laxa, commonly known as water thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Limnophylaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Limnophila laxa thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
