Litsea cordata

Litsea cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. It produces small, dark brown fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Litsea cordata is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
