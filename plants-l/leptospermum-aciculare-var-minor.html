Leptospermum aciculare var. minor

Leptospermum aciculare var. minor, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during spring and summer. It thrives in coastal environments and is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and coastal restoration projects.
