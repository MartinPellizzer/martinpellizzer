Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. colchica

Lauro-cerasus officinalis f. colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep red. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
