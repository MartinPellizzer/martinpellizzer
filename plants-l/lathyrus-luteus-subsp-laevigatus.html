Lathyrus luteus subsp. laevigatus

Lathyrus luteus subsp.

laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-flowered grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive violet or purple throat. It produces small, lens-shaped legumes that mature into brown pods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
