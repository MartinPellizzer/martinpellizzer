Lippia nodiflora var. strigulosa

Lippia nodiflora var. strigulosa, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
