Lucuma lucida

Lucuma lucida, commonly known as the golden apple, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that develop into the edible fruit after a lengthy maturation period. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, nutty flavor, making it a valued crop in local agriculture.
