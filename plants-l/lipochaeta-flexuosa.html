Lipochaeta flexuosa

Lipochaeta flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Lipochaeta flexuosa is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
