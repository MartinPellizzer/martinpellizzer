Lotus corniculatus subsp. fruticosus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

fruticosus, commonly known as the creeping yellow trefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to a wide range of soil types.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
