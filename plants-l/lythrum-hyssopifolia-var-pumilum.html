Lythrum hyssopifolia var. pumilum

Lythrum hyssopifolia var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf purple loosestrife, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom from midsummer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.
