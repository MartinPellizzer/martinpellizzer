Leucochrysum graminifolium

Leucochrysum graminifolium, commonly known as the slender everlasting or narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing heads that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name.
