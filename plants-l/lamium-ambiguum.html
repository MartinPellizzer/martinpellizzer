Lamium ambiguum

Lamium ambiguum, commonly known as deadnettle, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lamium ambiguum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
