Logania elliptica

Logania elliptica, commonly known as the oval logania, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Loganiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often glabrous. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Logania elliptica is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
