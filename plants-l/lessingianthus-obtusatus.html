Lessingianthus obtusatus

Lessingianthus obtusatus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into spherical, hairy seed heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
