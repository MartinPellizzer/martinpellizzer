Liparis suzumushi

Liparis suzumushi, commonly known as the "Suzumushi Orchid," is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive lip that resembles a spider, giving it its common name.

Liparis suzumushi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
