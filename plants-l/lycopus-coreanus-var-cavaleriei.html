Lycopus coreanus var. cavaleriei

Lycopus coreanus var. cavaleriei, commonly known as the Korean waterweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist environments, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
