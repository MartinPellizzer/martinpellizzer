Luehea microphylla

Luehea microphylla, commonly known as the little leaf luehea, is a small evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Luehea microphylla is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and resilience in warm environments.
