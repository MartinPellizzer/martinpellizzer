Limonium melancholicum

Limonium melancholicum, commonly known as the blueweed or sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showing a high degree of salt tolerance. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium melancholicum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
