Lomatium foeniculaceum var. macdougalii

Lomatium foeniculaceum var. macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is part of the parsley family (Apiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its region.
