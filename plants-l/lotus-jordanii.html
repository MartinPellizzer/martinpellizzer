Lotus jordanii

Lotus jordanii, commonly known as the Jordanian lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
