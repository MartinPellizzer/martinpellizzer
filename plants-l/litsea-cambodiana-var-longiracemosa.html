Litsea cambodiana var. longiracemosa

Litsea cambodiana var. longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme litsea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small white flowers arranged in long, drooping racemes, which are typical of the Lauraceae family. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
