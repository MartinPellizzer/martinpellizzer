Lasiagrostis australis

Lasiagrostis australis, commonly known as the Australian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that are often used in horticultural settings for ornamental purposes. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Lasiagrostis australis is well adapted to dry conditions and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
