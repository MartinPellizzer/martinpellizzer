Lepidium latifolium var. mongolicum

Lepidium latifolium var. mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
