Lastrea pedata

Lastrea pedata, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the warmer months. Its ability to store water in its leaves and stems makes it well adapted to drought conditions.

Lastrea pedata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
