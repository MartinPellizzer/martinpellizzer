Leptostachya crinita

Leptostachya crinita, commonly known as the hairy lepia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled greenhouse settings.
