Lisianthius saponarioides

Lisianthius saponarioides, commonly known as the soapberry lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
