Litsea puberula

Litsea puberula, commonly known as the velvet leaf litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its ovate to elliptic leaves that are covered in fine, silky pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its small, white flowers are fragrant and typically appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible.

Litsea puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
