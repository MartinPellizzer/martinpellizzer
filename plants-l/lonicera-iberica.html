Lonicera iberica

Lonicera iberica, commonly known as the Iberian honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the summer.

Lonicera iberica is often found in shrublands and along rocky slopes, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
