Lychnis coccugosantha

Lychnis coccugosantha, commonly known as the crimson campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
