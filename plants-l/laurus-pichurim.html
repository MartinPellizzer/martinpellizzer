Laurus pichurim

Laurus pichurim, commonly known as the Pichurim laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to black as it matures.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
