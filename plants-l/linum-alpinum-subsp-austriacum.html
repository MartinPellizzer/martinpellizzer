Linum alpinum subsp. austriacum

Linum alpinum subsp.

austriacum, commonly known as the Alpine flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats in mountainous environments.
