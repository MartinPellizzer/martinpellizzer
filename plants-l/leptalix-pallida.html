Leptalix pallida

Leptalix pallida, commonly known as the pale leathery lichen, is a unique species of lichen that exhibits a distinct botanical structure.

It is characterized by its leathery, pale-colored thallus, which is typically crust-like in appearance. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, which is essential for its survival. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree bark or rocks.

Its slow growth rate and specialized habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species.
