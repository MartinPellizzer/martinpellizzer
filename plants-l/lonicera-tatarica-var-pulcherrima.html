Lonicera tatarica var. pulcherrima

Lonicera tatarica var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
