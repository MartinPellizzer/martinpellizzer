Linaria vulgaris var. ruscinonensis

Linaria vulgaris var. ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
