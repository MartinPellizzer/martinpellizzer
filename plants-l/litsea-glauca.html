Litsea glauca

Litsea glauca, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Litsea glauca is also valued for its wood, which is used in various crafts and construction.
