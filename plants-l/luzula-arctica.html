Luzula arctica

Luzula arctica, commonly known as arctic sweetgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.
