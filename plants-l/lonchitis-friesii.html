Lonchitis friesii

Lonchitis friesii, commonly known as the Fries's globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in terminal racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.
