Limodorum diurnum

Limodorum diurnum, commonly known as the morning lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which open in the morning and close in the evening. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Limodorum diurnum is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is also found in moist, shaded woodland habitats.
