Lilium coridion var. partheneion

Lilium coridion var. partheneion, commonly known as the Partheneon lily, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-shaped, dark green leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils and require a cold winter dormancy period.

This variety is highly valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
