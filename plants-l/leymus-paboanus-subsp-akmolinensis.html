Leymus paboanus subsp. akmolinensis

Leymus paboanus subsp.

akmolinensis, commonly known as the Akmolinskaya grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
