Lonicera floribunda f. monstrosa-pedicellata

Lonicera floribunda f. monstrosa-pedicellata, commonly known as the showy honeysuckle, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and is notable for its abundant, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
