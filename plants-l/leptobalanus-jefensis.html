Leptobalanus jefensis

Leptobalanus jefensis, commonly known as the Jefen's tamarack, is a unique evergreen conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally around the stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
