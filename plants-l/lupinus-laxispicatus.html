Lupinus laxispicatus

Lupinus laxispicatus, commonly known as the slender spicate lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus laxispicatus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
