Leucocroton dictyophyllus

Leucocroton dictyophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
