Lomatia longifolia var. subevenia

Lomatia longifolia var. subevenia, commonly known as the coastal lomatia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species.
