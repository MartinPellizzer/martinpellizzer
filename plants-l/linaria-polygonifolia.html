Linaria polygonifolia

Linaria polygonifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its linear, often hairy leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Linaria polygonifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
