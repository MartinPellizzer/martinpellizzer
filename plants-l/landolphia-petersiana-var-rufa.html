Landolphia petersiana var. rufa

Landolphia petersiana var. rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, cylindrical berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
