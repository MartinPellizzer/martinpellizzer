Levenhookia sonderi

Levenhookia sonderi, commonly known as the Sonder's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Levenhookia sonderi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
