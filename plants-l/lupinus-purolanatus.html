Lupinus purolanatus

Lupinus purolanatus, commonly known as the Purolanat Lupine, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and clusters of spiny, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Lupinus purolanatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
