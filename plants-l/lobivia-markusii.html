Lobivia markusii

Lobivia markusii, commonly known as the Markus's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Lobivia markusii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
