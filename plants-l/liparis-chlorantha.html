Liparis chlorantha

Liparis chlorantha, commonly known as the green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, often branched stem with a few elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
