Linodendron

Linodendron, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Linodendron species are typically deciduous, shedding their leaves seasonally in response to environmental changes.

These trees can grow to significant heights and are often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
