Linospadix pusillus

Linospadix pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf spadix plant, is a small, herbaceous species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the central spadix, a feature common in many members of the Araceae family. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Linospadix pusillus is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in shaded garden settings and as a houseplant.
