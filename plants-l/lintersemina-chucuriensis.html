Lintersemina chucuriensis

Lintersemina chucuriensis, commonly known as the Chucuri seed plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique seed structure, which is enclosed in a fibrous, woody capsule. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often within the understory of dense forests. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
