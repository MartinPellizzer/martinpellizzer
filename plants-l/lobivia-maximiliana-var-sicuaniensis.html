Lobivia maximiliana var. sicuaniensis

Lobivia maximiliana var. sicuaniensis, commonly known as the Sicuanian lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stem and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding visual interest to its appearance. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical gardens.
