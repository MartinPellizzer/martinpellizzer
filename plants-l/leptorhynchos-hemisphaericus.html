Leptorhynchos hemisphaericus

Leptorhynchos hemisphaericus, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
