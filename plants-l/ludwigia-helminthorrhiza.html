Ludwigia helminthorrhiza

Ludwigia helminthorrhiza, commonly known as the red root or swamp Ludwigia, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water's surface. Ludwigia helminthorrhiza thrives in wetlands, marshes, and slow-moving freshwater habitats.

It is often used in aquascaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
