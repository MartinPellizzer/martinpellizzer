Lupinus piurensis

Lupinus piurensis, commonly known as the Piurensis lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus piurensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a role in nitrogen fixation in its ecosystem.
