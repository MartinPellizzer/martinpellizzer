Lannea nigritana var. pubescens

Lannea nigritana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lannea, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry woodlands and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of timber.
