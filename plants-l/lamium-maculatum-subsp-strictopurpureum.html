Lamium maculatum subsp. strictopurpureum

Lamium maculatum subsp.

strictopurpureum, commonly known as purple nettleleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple veins and a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
