Lepanthes florenciana

Lepanthes florenciana, commonly known as the Florentine orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Lepanthes florenciana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Its growth is closely associated with specific ecological conditions, making it a rare and sought-after species.
