Lythrum spicatum

Lythrum spicatum, commonly known as rosemary bush or purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Lythrum spicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
