Lachnagrostis semibarbata

Lachnagrostis semibarbata, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows or along streambanks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.

Lachnagrostis semibarbata is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
