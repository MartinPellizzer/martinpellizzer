Leandra truncata

Leandra truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to white in color, with a subtle scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Leandra truncata is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
