Luzula lactea var. lactea

Luzula lactea var. lactea, commonly known as the milk sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a white or pale-colored bloom. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Luzula lactea var. lactea is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
