Listrostachys althoffii

Listrostachys althoffii, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and bright orange or red berries that attract birds. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tiny white flowers in spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native plant communities.
