Lappula microcarpa

Lappula microcarpa, commonly known as the lesser lappula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in fields, along roadsides, and in meadows. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.
