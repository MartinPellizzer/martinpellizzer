Lapsana grandiflora f. pinnatisecta

Lapsana grandiflora f. pinnatisecta, commonly known as the pinnatisected common cudweed, is a variant of the common cudweed characterized by its finely divided, pinnatisected leaves.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Asteraceae family, known for its showy yellow composite flowers. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. The leaves are alternate and often have a delicate, feathery appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
