Ligustrum ovatum

Ligustrum ovatum, commonly known as the sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, honey-like scent. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Ligustrum ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
