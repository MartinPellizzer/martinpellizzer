Lythrum rotundifolium f. minus

Lythrum rotundifolium f. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf purple coneflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, spire-like clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
