Leucadendron spissifolium subsp. oribinum

Leucadendron spissifolium subsp.

oribinum, commonly known as the Orange River leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow to orange spherical flower heads, which are typical of the Proteaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
