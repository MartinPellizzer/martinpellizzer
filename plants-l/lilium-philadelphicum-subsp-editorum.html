Lilium philadelphicum subsp. editorum

Lilium philadelphicum subsp.

editorum, commonly known as the eastern fawn lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has strap-like, grassy leaves. It produces bulbous rhizomes and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
