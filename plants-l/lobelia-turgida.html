Lobelia turgida

Lobelia turgida, commonly known as the swollen lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its scientific name reflects its swollen, fleshy stems, which are a distinctive botanical characteristic.

Lobelia turgida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
