Lepanthes crebrifolia

Lepanthes crebrifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually dense and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

Lepanthes crebrifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
