Ligularia ebracteata

Ligularia ebracteata, commonly known as the giant marsh fern, is a striking perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features large, deeply lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

While it does not produce showy flowers, its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
