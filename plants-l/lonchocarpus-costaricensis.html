Lonchocarpus costaricensis

Lonchocarpus costaricensis, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a woody legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pods that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
