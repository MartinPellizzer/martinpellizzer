Lucuma dominigensis

Lucuma dominigensis, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a fruit-bearing plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, egg-shaped fruits that resemble small apples. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit develops from the base of the flower.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
