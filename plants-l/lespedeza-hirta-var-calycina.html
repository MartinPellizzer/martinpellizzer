Lespedeza hirta var. calycina

Lespedeza hirta var. calycina, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza hirta var. calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
