Linaria dulcis

Linaria dulcis, commonly known as sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Sweet William is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
