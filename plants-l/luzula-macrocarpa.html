Luzula macrocarpa

Luzula macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually arranged in a rosette at the base.

Luzula macrocarpa thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions.
