Lupinus palmarum

Lupinus palmarum, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus palmarum is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
