Lophiolepis ligularis subsp. armata

Lophiolepis ligularis subsp.

armata, commonly known as the coastal lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in dune environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
