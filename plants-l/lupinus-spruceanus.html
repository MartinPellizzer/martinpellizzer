Lupinus spruceanus

Lupinus spruceanus, commonly known as Spruce's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and forest edges. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus spruceanus plays an important role in ecological systems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
