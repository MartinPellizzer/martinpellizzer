Lathyrus brachypterus subsp. haussknechtii

Lathyrus brachypterus subsp.

haussknechtii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
