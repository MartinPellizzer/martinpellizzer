Lysipomia laciniata subsp. laciniata

Lysipomia laciniata subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved quillwort, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often finely toothed or lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense inflorescences.

Its rhizomes help anchor it in the substrate while facilitating vegetative propagation.
