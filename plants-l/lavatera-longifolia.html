Lavatera longifolia

Lavatera longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it typically grows as a shrub or small tree. Lavatera longifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
