Linaria origanifolia subsp. flexuosa

Linaria origanifolia subsp.

flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
