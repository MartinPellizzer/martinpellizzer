Lychnophora rosmarinifolia

Lychnophora rosmarinifolia, commonly known as rosemary fern, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
