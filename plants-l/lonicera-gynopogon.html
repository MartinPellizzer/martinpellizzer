Lonicera gynopogon

Lonicera gynopogon, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in pairs. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer, containing several seeds.

Lonicera gynopogon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
