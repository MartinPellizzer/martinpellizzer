Lobelia

Lobelia, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and red. Many species of lobelia are native to the Americas, particularly in moist, woodland environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves.

Lobelia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also cultivated for its medicinal properties.
