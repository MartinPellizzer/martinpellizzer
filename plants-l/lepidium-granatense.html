Lepidium granatense

Lepidium granatense, commonly known as the granate cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

Lepidium granatense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
