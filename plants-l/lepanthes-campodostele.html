Lepanthes campodostele

Lepanthes campodostele, commonly known as the white-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals with a subtle pinkish tinge at the tips. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
