Loricaria complanata subsp. occidentalis

Loricaria complanata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western flat-leaf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that float on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant has a short, stout stem and produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
