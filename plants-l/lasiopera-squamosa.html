Lasiopera squamosa

Lasiopera squamosa, commonly known as the scaly leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy, scaly leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine, scale-like structures, which help reduce water loss in humid conditions.

Lasiopera squamosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
