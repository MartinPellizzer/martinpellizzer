Lycopus rubellus var. angustifolius

Lycopus rubellus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its small, clustered blue or purple flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
