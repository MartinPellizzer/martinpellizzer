Lysimachia quadrifolia f. rubescens

Lysimachia quadrifolia f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-veined wood sorrel, is a variant of the woodland plant Lysimachia quadrifolia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red veins that run through the leaves, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, adding to its ornamental value.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
