Lonchocarpus peckoltii

Lonchocarpus peckoltii, commonly known as the yellow snakeroot, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and ethnobotanical research.
