Lobelia heteroclita

Lobelia heteroclita, commonly known as the yellow lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lobelia heteroclita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
