Lindsaea jamesonioides

Lindsaea jamesonioides, commonly known as the Jameson's lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that often form dense, cascading clusters. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
