Lepigonum canadense

Lepigonum canadense, commonly known as Canadian meadow beauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
