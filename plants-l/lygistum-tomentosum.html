Lygistum tomentosum

Lygistum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly grape.

It is a climbing vine native to the Americas, characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to fleshy, red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
