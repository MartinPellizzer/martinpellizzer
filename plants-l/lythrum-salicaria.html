Lythrum salicaria

Lythrum salicaria, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and spikes of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments.

However, it has become an invasive species in North America, outcompeting native plants and altering wetland ecosystems.
