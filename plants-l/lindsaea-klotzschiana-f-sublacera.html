Lindsaea klotzschiana f. sublacera

Lindsaea klotzschiana f. sublacera, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, translucent scales, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in the understory of forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

This unique botanical feature makes it a fascinating subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and botanical researchers.
