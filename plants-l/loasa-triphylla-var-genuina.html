Loasa triphylla var. genuina

Loasa triphylla var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody, aromatic stem and is known for its hardiness in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

Loasa triphylla var. genuina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
