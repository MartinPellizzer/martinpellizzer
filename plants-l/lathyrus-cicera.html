Lathyrus cicera

Lathyrus cicera, commonly known as the sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that coil around supports. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus cicera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
