Limnobium sinclairii

Limnobium sinclairii, commonly known as the water lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, while its leaves are adapted to float and support the weight of the flowers.

Limnobium sinclairii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
