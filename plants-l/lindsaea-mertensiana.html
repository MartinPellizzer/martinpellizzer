Lindsaea mertensiana

Lindsaea mertensiana, commonly known as the Mertens's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lindsaeaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. Lindsaea mertensiana thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its structure and reproductive features make it an important component of the ecological dynamics in its native habitat.
