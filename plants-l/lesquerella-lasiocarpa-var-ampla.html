Lesquerella lasiocarpa var. ampla

Lesquerella lasiocarpa var. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved lesquerella, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its seeds, which contain a unique oil used in industrial applications.
