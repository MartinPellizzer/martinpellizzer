Lycaste schoenbrunnensis

Lycaste schoenbrunnensis, commonly known as the Schoenbrunn lycaste, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
