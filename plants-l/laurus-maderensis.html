Laurus maderensis

Laurus maderensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
