Lyonia elata

Lyonia elata, commonly known as the evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. It has small, oval leaves and produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring.

Lyonia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
