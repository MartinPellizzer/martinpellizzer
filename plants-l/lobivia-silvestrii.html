Lobivia silvestrii

Lobivia silvestrii, commonly known as the Silvestri's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and conservationists.
