Lupinus wyethii

Lupinus wyethii, commonly known as Wyeth's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Lupinus wyethii plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
