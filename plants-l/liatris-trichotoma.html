Liatris trichotoma

Liatris trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of purple and pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris trichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
