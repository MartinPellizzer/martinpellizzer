Lepanthes cascajalensis

Lepanthes cascajalensis, commonly known as the Cascajal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration, often featuring white or pale pink petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes cascajalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of tropical rainforests.
