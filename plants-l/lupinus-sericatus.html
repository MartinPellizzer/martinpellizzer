Lupinus sericatus

Lupinus sericatus, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has feathery, finely divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus sericatus is a member of the legume family and plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
