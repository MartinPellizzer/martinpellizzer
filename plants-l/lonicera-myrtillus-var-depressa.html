Lonicera myrtillus var. depressa

Lonicera myrtillus var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
