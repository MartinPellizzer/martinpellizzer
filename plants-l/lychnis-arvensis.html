Lychnis arvensis

Lychnis arvensis, commonly known as Field Chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, white or pink flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Lychnis arvensis is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in cultivated areas.
