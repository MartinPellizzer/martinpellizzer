Lavandula dentata var. balearica

Lavandula dentata var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both ornamental gardening and essential oil production.
