Lithodia tomentosa

Lithodia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly lithodia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Lithodia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
