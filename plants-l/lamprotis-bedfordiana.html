Lamprotis bedfordiana

Lamprotis bedfordiana, commonly known as the Bedford's orchid, is a rare and delicate species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white blossoms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
