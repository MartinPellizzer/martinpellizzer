Lobelia trullifolia var. pulchra

Lobelia trullifolia var. pulchra, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of vibrant blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
