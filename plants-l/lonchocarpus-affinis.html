Lonchocarpus affinis

Lonchocarpus affinis, commonly known as the red root or red bean vine, is a tropical perennial legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are rich in alkaloids and have been utilized in various herbal remedies.

However, it is important to note that some parts of the plant contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
