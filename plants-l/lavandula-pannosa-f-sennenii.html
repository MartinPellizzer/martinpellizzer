Lavandula pannosa f. sennenii

Lavandula pannosa f. sennenii, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, pale purple to pink flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant. Its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
