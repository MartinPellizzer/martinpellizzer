Laurus biflora

Laurus biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered laurel, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in clusters of two, which gives the species its scientific name.

Laurus biflora is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
