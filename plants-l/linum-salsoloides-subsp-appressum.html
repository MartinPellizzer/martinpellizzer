Linum salsoloides subsp. appressum

Linum salsoloides subsp.

appressum, commonly known as desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

It is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
