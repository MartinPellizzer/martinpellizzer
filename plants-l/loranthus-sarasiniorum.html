Loranthus sarasiniorum

Loranthus sarasiniorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and red berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals. It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

Its presence can have both ecological and economic impacts on the forest ecosystems it inhabits.
