Lupinus chamissonis var. calvescens

Lupinus chamissonis var. calvescens, commonly known as the California lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with multiple leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

This species plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance.
