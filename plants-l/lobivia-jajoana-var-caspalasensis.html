Lobivia jajoana var. caspalasensis

Lobivia jajoana var. caspalasensis, commonly known as the Caspalasensis Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, a feature common in desert plants to minimize water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
