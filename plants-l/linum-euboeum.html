Linum euboeum

Linum euboeum, commonly known as the Euboean flax, is a flowering plant native to the Greek island of Euboea.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It is known for its fibrous stalks, which were historically used for fiber production.

Linum euboeum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
