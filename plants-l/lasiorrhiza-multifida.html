Lasiorrhiza multifida

Lasiorrhiza multifida, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive fringed or toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species for pollinators and biodiversity in its habitat.
