Lechea lakelae

Lechea lakelae, commonly known as the Lake Lake cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lechea lakelae is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
