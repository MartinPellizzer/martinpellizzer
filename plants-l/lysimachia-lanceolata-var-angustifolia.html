Lysimachia lanceolata var. angustifolia

Lysimachia lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers clustered in dense, terminal spikes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Primulaceae family.
