Lasianthus acuminatissimus

Lasianthus acuminatissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are often pale purple or white, and they bloom during the rainy season.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
