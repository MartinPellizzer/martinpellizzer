Lobivia zudanensis

Lobivia zudanensis, commonly known as the Zudanensis Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Lobivia zudanensis is well-suited for cultivation in rocky or sandy soils with minimal water requirements.
