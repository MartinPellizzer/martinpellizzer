Leucanthemum × pawlowskii

Leucanthemum × pawlowskii, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of yellow disk florets, creating a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Leucanthemum × pawlowskii is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
