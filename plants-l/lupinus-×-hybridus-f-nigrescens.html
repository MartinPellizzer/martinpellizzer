Lupinus × hybridus f. nigrescens

Lupinus × hybridus f. nigrescens, commonly known as the black lupine, is a perennial flowering plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of dark purple to black flowers.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its dark-colored flowers and foliage make it a distinctive and ornamental species in gardens and natural landscapes.
