Lippia ovata

Lippia ovata, commonly known as Mexican mint or honey mint, is a perennial herb native to Central America and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and small white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Lippia ovata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antiseptic properties.

Its essential oils are also valued in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
