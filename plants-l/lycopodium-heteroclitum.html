Lycopodium heteroclitum

Lycopodium heteroclitum, commonly known as the variable clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its clustered, needle-like leaves and a central, upright stem. Its sporophylls are arranged in spirals and produce spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

Lycopodium heteroclitum plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
