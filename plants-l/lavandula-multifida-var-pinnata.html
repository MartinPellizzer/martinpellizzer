Lavandula multifida var. pinnata

Lavandula multifida var. pinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, purple-blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is also valued for its use in essential oil production and herbal remedies.
