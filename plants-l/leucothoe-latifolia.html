Leucothoe latifolia

Leucothoe latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in the summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Broadleaf leucothoe is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
