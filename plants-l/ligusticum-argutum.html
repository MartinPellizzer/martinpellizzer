Ligusticum argutum

Ligusticum argutum, commonly known as the Japanese lovage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with fern-like, deeply lobed leaves. Its small white to greenish-yellow flowers appear in umbrella-shaped clusters, and it produces oval, dark brown seeds.

Ligusticum argutum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
