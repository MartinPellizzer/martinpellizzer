Liatris scariosa var. nieuwlandii

Liatris scariosa var. nieuwlandii, commonly known as Newland's blazingstar, is a native North American plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites across the central United States.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
