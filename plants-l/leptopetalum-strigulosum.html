Leptopetalum strigulosum

Leptopetalum strigulosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated margins and a capsule-like fruit that disperses seeds by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
