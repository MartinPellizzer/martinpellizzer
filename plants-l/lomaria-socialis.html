Lomaria socialis

Lomaria socialis, commonly known as the social lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Lomaria socialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
