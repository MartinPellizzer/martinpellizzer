Lysionotus pubescens

Lysionotus pubescens, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
