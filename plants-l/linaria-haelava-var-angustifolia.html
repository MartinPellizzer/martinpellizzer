Linaria haelava var. angustifolia

Linaria haelava var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
