Larix potaninii var. potaninii

Larix potaninii var. potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's larch, is a deciduous conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, feathery foliage that turns a vibrant yellow before shedding in autumn. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine forests and rocky slopes.
