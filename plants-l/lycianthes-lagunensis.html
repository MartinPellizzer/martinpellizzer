Lycianthes lagunensis

Lycianthes lagunensis, commonly known as the Lake Lagoon nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and other nightshades. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
