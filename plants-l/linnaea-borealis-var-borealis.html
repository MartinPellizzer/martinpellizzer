Linnaea borealis var. borealis

Linnaea borealis var. borealis, commonly known as the twinflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It features small, opposite leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in pairs, which bloom in early spring before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is characterized by its unique floral structure.

This species is notable for its role in ecological systems and its historical significance in botanical classification.
