Larix middendorfii

Larix middendorfii, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a deciduous conifer native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and its needle-like leaves that turn golden before falling in autumn. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
