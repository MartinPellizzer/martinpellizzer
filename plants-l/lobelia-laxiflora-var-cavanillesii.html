Lobelia laxiflora var. cavanillesii

Lobelia laxiflora var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' looseflower lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
