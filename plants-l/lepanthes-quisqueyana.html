Lepanthes quisqueyana

Lepanthes quisqueyana, commonly known as the Quesqueyana slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, adapting to its humid, shaded habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in photosynthesis.

Lepanthes quisqueyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
