Lipusa

Lipusa, commonly known as the "butterfly flower," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped petals that come in a variety of vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lipusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
