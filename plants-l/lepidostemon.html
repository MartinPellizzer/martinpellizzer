Lepidostemon

Lepidostemon, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thyme or narrow-leafed germander, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. These plants are typically herbaceous perennials with aromatic, often hairy leaves. They produce small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color.

Lepidostemon species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
