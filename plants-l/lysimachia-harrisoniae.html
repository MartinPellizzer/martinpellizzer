Lysimachia harrisoniae

Lysimachia harrisoniae, commonly known as Harrison's chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lysimachia harrisoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
