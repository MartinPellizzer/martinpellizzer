Lepidium uzbekistanicum

Lepidium uzbekistanicum, commonly known as the Uzbek cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers and has slender, branching stems.

Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
