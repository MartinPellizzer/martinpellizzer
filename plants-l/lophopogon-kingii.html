Lophopogon kingii

Lophopogon kingii, commonly known as King's lophopogon, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Lophopogon kingii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.
