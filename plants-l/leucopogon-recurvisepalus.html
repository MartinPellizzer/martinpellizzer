Leucopogon recurvisepalus

Leucopogon recurvisepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or heathland environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in local ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
