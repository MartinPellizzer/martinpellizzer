Laelia anceps var. scottiana

Laelia anceps var. scottiana, commonly known as the Scott's Laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, with moderate to high humidity.

This variety is valued for its beauty and relative ease of care, making it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
