Lycium salicifolium

Lycium salicifolium, commonly known as the willowleaf goji, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and bright orange-red berries that ripen in the autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its berries are edible and contain high levels of antioxidants and nutrients.

Lycium salicifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
