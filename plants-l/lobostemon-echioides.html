Lobostemon echioides

Lobostemon echioides, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lobostemon echioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
