Lepisanthes multijuga

Lepisanthes multijuga, commonly known as the multijugate orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, adapting to its humid, shaded habitats.

Lepisanthes multijuga is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
