Leersia ferox

Leersia ferox, commonly known as the panicled leersia, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in disturbed or swampy habitats.

Leersia ferox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
