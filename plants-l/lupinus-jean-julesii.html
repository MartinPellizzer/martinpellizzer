Lupinus jean-julesii

Lupinus jean-julesii, commonly known as the Jean-Jules lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
