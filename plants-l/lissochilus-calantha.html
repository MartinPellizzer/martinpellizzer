Lissochilus calantha

Lissochilus calantha, commonly known as the Andean bell orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Lissochilus calantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
