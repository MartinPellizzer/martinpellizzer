Lolium latum

Lolium latum, commonly known as ryegrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and fine, narrow leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers, which are typically green to purple in color. Lolium latum is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural fields.

It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
