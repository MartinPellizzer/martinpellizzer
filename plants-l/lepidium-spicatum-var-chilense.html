Lepidium spicatum var. chilense

Lepidium spicatum var. chilense, commonly known as Chilean cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and features small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The seeds are small, smooth, and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
