Lythrum salicaria subvar. genuinum

Lythrum salicaria subvar. genuinum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of vibrant purple to pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production have made it an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
