Lobostemon fruticosus var. bergianus

Lobostemon fruticosus var. bergianus, commonly known as Bergian's lobostemon, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and rockeries.
