Lycium chilense var. petiolatum

Lycium chilense var. petiolatum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
