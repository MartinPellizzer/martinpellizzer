Laurus albiflora

Laurus albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive foliage and fragrance, it is also cultivated for its essential oils and historical use in perfumery.
