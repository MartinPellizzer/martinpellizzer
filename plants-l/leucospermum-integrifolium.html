Leucospermum integrifolium

Leucospermum integrifolium, commonly known as the African firecracker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, woody stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth margin, with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are notable for their vibrant color and long-lasting bloom, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
