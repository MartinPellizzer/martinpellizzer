Linaria brunneri var. glaberrima

Linaria brunneri var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its low maintenance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
