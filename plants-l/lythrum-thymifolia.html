Lythrum thymifolia

Lythrum thymifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy pink or red flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Lythrum thymifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
