Leontice leontopetalum subsp. armeniaca

Leontice leontopetalum subsp.

armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian leontice, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and blooms.

Its subspecific name, *armeniaca*, reflects its origin in Armenia and surrounding regions.
