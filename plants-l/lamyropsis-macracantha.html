Lamyropsis macracantha

Lamyropsis macracantha, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and showy purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lamyropsis macracantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
