Lupinus rupestris

Lupinus rupestris, commonly known as the cliff lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus rupestris is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
