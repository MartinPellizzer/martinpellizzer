Laportea peltata

Laportea peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its attractive appearance.

Laportea peltata is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
