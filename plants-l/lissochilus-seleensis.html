Lissochilus seleensis

Lissochilus seleensis, commonly known as the Selena's slipper orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
