Loranthus koenigianus

Loranthus koenigianus, commonly known as the Indian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the family Rutaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. This species is found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.
