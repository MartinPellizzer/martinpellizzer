Ligustrum ovalifolium variegatum

Ligustrum ovalifolium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
