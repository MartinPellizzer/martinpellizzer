Linochilus rhododendroides

Linochilus rhododendroides, commonly known as the red-leaved linochilus, is a striking shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its vibrant, deep red to purple foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, emerging from the base of the leaves. Its botanical features make it a unique addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Linochilus rhododendroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
