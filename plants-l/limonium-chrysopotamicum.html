Limonium chrysopotamicum

Limonium chrysopotamicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and produces delicate, blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium chrysopotamicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal gardens.
