Ligusticum filifolium

Ligusticum filifolium, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with yellow flowers that appear in umbels. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves are also used in cooking, especially in Chinese cuisine, where they add a distinctive flavor to dishes.
