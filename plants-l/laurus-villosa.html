Laurus villosa

Laurus villosa, commonly known as the hairy laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.

Laurus villosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
