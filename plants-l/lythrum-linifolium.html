Lythrum linifolium

Lythrum linifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and unique seed structures. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces spikes of pale pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

The plant is typically found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
