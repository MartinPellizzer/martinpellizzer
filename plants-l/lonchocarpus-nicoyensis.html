Lonchocarpus nicoyensis

Lonchocarpus nicoyensis, commonly known as the Nicoyan legume, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it valuable in agricultural settings. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance, it is of interest to botanists and researchers.
