Lespedeza ciliata

Lespedeza ciliata, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
