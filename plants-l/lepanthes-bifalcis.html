Lepanthes bifalcis

Lepanthes bifalcis, commonly known as the two-lobed slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the wet season.

Lepanthes bifalcis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
