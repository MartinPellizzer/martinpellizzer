Leptoglossis texana

Leptoglossis texana, commonly known as the Texas flower bug, is a species of leafhopper native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elongated body and distinctive coloration, often featuring a combination of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant is typically found in grassy areas and along the edges of wetlands, where it feeds on the sap of various grasses and sedges. Its presence is an indicator of healthy, moist habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
