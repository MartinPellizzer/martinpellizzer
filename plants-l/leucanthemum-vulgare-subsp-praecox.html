Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. praecox

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc floret, giving it a daisy-like appearance.

This perennial herb typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also a food source for various pollinators.
