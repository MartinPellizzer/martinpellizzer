Lightfootia umbellata

Lightfootia umbellata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Lightfootia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
