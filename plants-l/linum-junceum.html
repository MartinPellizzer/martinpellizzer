Linum junceum

Linum junceum, commonly known as common flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are used for producing linseed oil, and the fibers from its stems are used in textile manufacturing.

Linum junceum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile plant for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
