Litsea umbrosa var. impunctata

Litsea umbrosa var. impunctata, commonly known as the smooth litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often smooth-surfaced leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are followed by small, round berries.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
