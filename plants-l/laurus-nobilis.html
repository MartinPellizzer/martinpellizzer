Laurus nobilis

Laurus nobilis, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Laurus nobilis is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of culinary and medicinal compounds.
