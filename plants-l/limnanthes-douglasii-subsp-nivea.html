Limnanthes douglasii subsp. nivea

Limnanthes douglasii subsp.

nivea, commonly known as the white marsh plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its white, showy flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are alternate and ovate in shape. Its flowers are actinomorphic and consist of five petals, with a distinctive yellow center.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils near water bodies and is often found in wet meadows and marshy areas.
