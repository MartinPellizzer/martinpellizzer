Liparis leucophaea

Liparis leucophaea, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, thick rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears several leaves. The plant produces a spike of small, white or pale green flowers, which are often tinged with pink or red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
