Laurus glandulifera

Laurus glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically dark blue to black in color when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Laurus glandulifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
