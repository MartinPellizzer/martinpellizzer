Lupinus littoralis

Lupinus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in dunes and coastal bluffs, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

Lupinus littoralis is an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
