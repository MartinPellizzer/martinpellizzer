Lupinus longivallis

Lupinus longivallis, commonly known as the long-valley lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus longivallis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility for other plants.
