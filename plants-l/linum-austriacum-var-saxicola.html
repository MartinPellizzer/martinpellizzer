Linum austriacum var. saxicola

Linum austriacum var. saxicola, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

This variety is adapted to harsh mountain climates and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
