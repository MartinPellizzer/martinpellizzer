Lupinus tauris var. suberectus

Lupinus tauris var. suberectus, commonly known as the Taurian lupine, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems that often bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with distinct seasonal changes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
