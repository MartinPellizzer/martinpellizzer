Lytoneuron ornithopus var. pygmaeum

Lytoneuron ornithopus var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf flax or pygmy flax, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas due to its tolerance for harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
