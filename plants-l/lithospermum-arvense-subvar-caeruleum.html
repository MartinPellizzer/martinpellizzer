Lithospermum arvense subvar. caeruleum

Lithospermum arvense subvar. caeruleum, commonly known as bluefield erythronium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue, and its flowers are typically pale blue to purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, dark reddish-brown berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive foliage and its role in supporting local pollinators.
