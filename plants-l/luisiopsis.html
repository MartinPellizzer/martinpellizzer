Luisiopsis

Luisiopsis, commonly known as the "Mexican sunflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Americas, particularly Mexico and Central America, and is known for its vibrant, orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Luisiopsis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
