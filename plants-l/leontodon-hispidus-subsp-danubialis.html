Leontodon hispidus subsp. danubialis

Leontodon hispidus subsp.

danubialis, commonly known as the Danube hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting local biodiversity.
