Leea moluccana

Leea moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan Leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small seeds.

Leea moluccana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
