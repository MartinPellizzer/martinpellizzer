Leea suaveolens

Leea suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented leea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the Leeaceae family and typically grows as a shrub or small tree. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. Its small, white flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom during the warmer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
