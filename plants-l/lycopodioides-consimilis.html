Lycopodioides consimilis

Lycopodioides consimilis, commonly known as the similar clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around its stem, giving it a club-like appearance. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a characteristic typical of its evolutionary group.

Lycopodioides consimilis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
