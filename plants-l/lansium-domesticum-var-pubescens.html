Lansium domesticum var. pubescens

Lansium domesticum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lansium or hairy custard apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, reddish-brown fruit with a rough, spiny exterior. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with simple, alternate, ovate leaves. The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
