Lasiorrhiza gayana

Lasiorrhiza gayana, commonly known as the mountain zamioculcas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Zamiaceae family, which includes cycads, and is characterized by its unique, palm-like appearance. The plant features a stout, fleshy stem with dense, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
