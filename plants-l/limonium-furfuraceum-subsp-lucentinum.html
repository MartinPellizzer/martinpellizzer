Limonium furfuraceum subsp. lucentinum

Limonium furfuraceum subsp.

lucentinum, commonly known as the golden sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in saline environments.
