Lagunaria patersonia

Lagunaria patersonia, commonly known as the Paterson's lagunaria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, palm-like appearance with a single, unbranched stem that bears spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the salty and windy conditions of its coastal habitat.

Lagunaria patersonia is often found growing in sandy soils near the shoreline, making it a distinctive feature of the Australian coastal flora.
