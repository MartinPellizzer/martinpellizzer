Lycopodioides grandis

Lycopodioides grandis, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporophytes, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodioides grandis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its ancient lineage reflects its evolutionary significance in the plant kingdom.
