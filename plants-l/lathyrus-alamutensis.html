Lathyrus alamutensis

Lathyrus alamutensis, commonly known as the Alamut vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Lathyrus alamutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
