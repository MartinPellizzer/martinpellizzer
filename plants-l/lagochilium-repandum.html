Lagochilium repandum

Lagochilium repandum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
