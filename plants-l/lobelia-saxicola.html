Lobelia saxicola

Lobelia saxicola, commonly known as the rock lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
