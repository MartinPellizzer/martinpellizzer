Lissochilus giganteus

Lissochilus giganteus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, displaying a range of colors from white to deep purple. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas, and thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its challenging native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
