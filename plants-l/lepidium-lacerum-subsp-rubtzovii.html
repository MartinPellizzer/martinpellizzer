Lepidium lacerum subsp. rubtzovii

Lepidium lacerum subsp.

rubtzovii, commonly known as the red-stemmed peppergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by short, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
