Lonicera dioica f. dasygyna

Lonicera dioica f. dasygyna, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is attractive to birds.
