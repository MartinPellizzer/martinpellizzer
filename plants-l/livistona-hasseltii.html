Livistona hasseltii

Livistona hasseltii, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a species of cycad native to New Guinea and the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a palm-like appearance. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials in indigenous communities.

Livistona hasseltii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.
