Lantana czermakii

Lantana czermakii, commonly known as the New Zealand lantana, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, narrow leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and open habitats.

Lantana czermakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
