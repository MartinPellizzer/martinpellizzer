Leplaea cauliflora

Leplaea cauliflora, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the trunk and branches, a trait known as cauliflorous growth. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and produces small, round, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Leplaea cauliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
