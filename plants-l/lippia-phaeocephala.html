Lippia phaeocephala

Lippia phaeocephala, commonly known as the black-headed lippia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines due to their distinctive flavor.
