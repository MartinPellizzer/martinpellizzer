Limnophila gratioloides var. nana

Limnophila gratioloides var. nana, commonly known as dwarf water willow, is a small, aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in late summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
