Lonicera linderifolia subsp. linderifolia

Lonicera linderifolia subsp.

linderifolia, commonly known as the Linder's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
