Leptospermum ellipticum

Leptospermum ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tea tree, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
