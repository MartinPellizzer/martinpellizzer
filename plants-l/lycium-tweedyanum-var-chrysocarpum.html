Lycium tweedyanum var. chrysocarpum

Lycium tweedyanum var. chrysocarpum, commonly known as the golden-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and rich in nutrients. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers during the spring. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and waxy foliage.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source by local wildlife.
