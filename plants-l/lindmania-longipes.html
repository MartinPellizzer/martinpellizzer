Lindmania longipes

Lindmania longipes, commonly known as the long-stalked lindmania, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and small, delicate leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are arranged in a whorled pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pollinated by insects.

Lindmania longipes plays a role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
