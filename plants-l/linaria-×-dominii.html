Linaria × dominii

Linaria × dominii, commonly known as the hybrid toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are tubular in shape, with two lips, and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Linaria × dominii is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
