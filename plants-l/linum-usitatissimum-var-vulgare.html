Linum usitatissimum var. vulgare

Linum usitatissimum var. vulgare, commonly known as flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems, alternate leaves, and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is cultivated primarily for its seeds, which are used to produce linseed oil and for fiber in textiles. Its fibrous stem is harvested for making linen, while the seeds are also used in food and medicine.

Linum usitatissimum var. vulgare thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil.
