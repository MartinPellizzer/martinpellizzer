Lippia longipedunculata

Lippia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle verbena, is a species of flowering plant in the verbena family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces showy flowers in shades of purple and blue, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lippia longipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
