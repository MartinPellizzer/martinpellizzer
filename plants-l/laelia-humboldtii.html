Laelia humboldtii

Laelia humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in the Andes.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate humidity.

Laelia humboldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
