Liparis trullifera

Liparis trullifera, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Liparis trullifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
