Limoniastrum speciosum

Limoniastrum speciosum, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features silvery-gray foliage and produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
