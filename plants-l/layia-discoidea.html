Layia discoidea

Layia discoidea, commonly known as the fairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a dandelion-like appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Layia discoidea is known for its delicate, herbaceous structure and is often found in coastal and inland regions of California.
