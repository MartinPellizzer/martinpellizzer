Linum heterosepalum var. purpureum

Linum heterosepalum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a trailing habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
