Luculia pinceana

Luculia pinceana, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Luculia pinceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
