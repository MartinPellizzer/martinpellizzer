Lithospermum breviflorum

Lithospermum breviflorum, commonly known as narrowflower rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually reddish-purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant produces small, dark reddish-brown berries that contain the seeds.
