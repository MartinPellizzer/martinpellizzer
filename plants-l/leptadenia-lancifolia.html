Leptadenia lancifolia

Leptadenia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cat's whiskers, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, hard seeds when mature.

Leptadenia lancifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in treating various ailments.
