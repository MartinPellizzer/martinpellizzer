Linaria propinqua var. paui

Linaria propinqua var. paui, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
