Lantana involucrata var. odorata

Lantana involucrata var. odorata, commonly known as the fragrant lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are usually lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
