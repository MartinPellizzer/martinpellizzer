Leptodermis nigricans

Leptodermis nigricans, commonly known as the black-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, sometimes almost black, foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
