Leptospermum obovatum

Leptospermum obovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, obovate leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage is aromatic and has been traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for its medicinal properties.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
