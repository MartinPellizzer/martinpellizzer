Limonium australe var. australe

Limonium australe var. australe, commonly known as Australian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. It is often found in dunes and along shorelines, playing a role in stabilizing sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and floral arrangements.
