Lippia tepicana

Lippia tepicana, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

Lippia tepicana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
