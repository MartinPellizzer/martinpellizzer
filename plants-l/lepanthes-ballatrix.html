Lepanthes ballatrix

Lepanthes ballatrix, commonly known as the Ballatrix orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with intricate patterns, and it produces small, round seeds.

Lepanthes ballatrix is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
