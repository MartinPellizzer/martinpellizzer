Libertia cernua

Libertia cernua, commonly known as the drooping libertia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features grass-like foliage with narrow, grass-green leaves that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces striking, pendulous flower spikes that bloom in shades of violet or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to botanical collections.
