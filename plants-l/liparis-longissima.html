Liparis longissima

Liparis longissima, commonly known as the long-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and delicate, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth from a basal node.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
