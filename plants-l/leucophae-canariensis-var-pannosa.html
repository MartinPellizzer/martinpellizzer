Leucophae canariensis var. pannosa

Leucophae canariensis var. pannosa, commonly known as the Canary Island heather, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, compact foliage with silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native habitat.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and resilience in coastal environments.
