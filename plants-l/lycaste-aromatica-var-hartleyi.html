Lycaste aromatica var. hartleyi

Lycaste aromatica var. hartleyi, commonly known as the Hartley's fragrant lycaste, is a hybrid orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water, allowing it to thrive in drier conditions. Its leaves are strap-like and grassy, often arranged in a compact rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique aroma and floral display.
