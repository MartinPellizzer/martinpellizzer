Limnophila helferi

Limnophila helferi, commonly known as the water willow.

It is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia, typically found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish. Limnophila helferi is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.

It is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in water gardens.
