Lycopodium hohenackeri

Lycopodium hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's clubmoss, is a species of small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of whorled leaves that form a dense, moss-like appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium hohenackeri is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studying ancient plant lineages and their ecological adaptations.
