Lycopodium hamiltonii

Lycopodium hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen perennial plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of whorled, needle-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the foliage. Native to the temperate regions of southern Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often found in forest understories and rocky outcrops.
