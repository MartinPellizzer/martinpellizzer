Lappa officinalis subsp. nemorosa

Lappa officinalis subsp.

nemorosa, commonly known as the woodland hogweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features tall, hollow stems that are typically hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces large, umbrella-like inflorescences with small white or greenish flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can be quite large, often resembling those of giant hogweed.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
