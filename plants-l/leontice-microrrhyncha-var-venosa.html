Leontice microrrhyncha var. venosa

Leontice microrrhyncha var. venosa, commonly known as the veined leontice, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
