Luzula campestris var. congesta

Luzula campestris var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to greenish flowers, which are clustered closely together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides, often forming dense mats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
