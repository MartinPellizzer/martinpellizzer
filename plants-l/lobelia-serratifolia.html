Lobelia serratifolia

Lobelia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lobelia serratifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is a valuable plant for pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
