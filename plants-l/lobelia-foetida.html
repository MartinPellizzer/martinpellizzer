Lobelia foetida

Lobelia foetida, commonly known as stinking lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, often foul-smelling odor, which is thought to deter herbivores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia foetida is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
