Lilium speciosum f. album-novum

Lilium speciosum f. album-novum, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the showy lily, is a botanical variant characterized by its striking white petals and pale green sepals.

This cultivar exhibits a compact growth habit with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pale green hue. The flowers are large, with six to eight petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped throat, typical of the Liliaceae family. It is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
