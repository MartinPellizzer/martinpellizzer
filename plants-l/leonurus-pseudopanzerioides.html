Leonurus pseudopanzerioides

Leonurus pseudopanzerioides, commonly known as the African hedgehog veldt, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.

Its name reflects its distinctive appearance, which resembles a hedgehog due to its hairy foliage.
