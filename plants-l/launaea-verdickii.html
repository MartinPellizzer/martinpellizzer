Launaea verdickii

Launaea verdickii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in treating various ailments.
