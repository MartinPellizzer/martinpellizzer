Lellingeria micula

Lellingeria micula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
