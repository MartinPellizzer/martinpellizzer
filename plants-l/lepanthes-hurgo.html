Lepanthes hurgo

Lepanthes hurgo, commonly known as the Hurgo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Lepanthes hurgo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
