Lebeckia pungens

Lebeckia pungens, commonly known as the spiky lebeckia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiky seed heads that are often mistaken for flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
