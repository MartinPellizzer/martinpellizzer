Lessingianthus secundus

Lessingianthus secundus, commonly known as the clustered false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy. It produces bright yellow flowers with radiating ray florets and central disk florets, typically blooming in the summer months.

Lessingianthus secundus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
