Lasianthus brevipedicellatus

Lasianthus brevipedicellatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
