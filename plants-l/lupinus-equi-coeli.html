Lupinus equi-coeli

Lupinus equi-coeli, commonly known as the celestial horse bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods contain several elliptical seeds. Native to specific regions of the Mediterranean, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
