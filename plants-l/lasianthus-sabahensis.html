Lasianthus sabahensis

Lasianthus sabahensis, commonly known as the Sabah flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface, while the flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
