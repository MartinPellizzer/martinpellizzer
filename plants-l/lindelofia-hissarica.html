Lindelofia hissarica

Lindelofia hissarica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals, arranged in a bell shape, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Lindelofia hissarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
