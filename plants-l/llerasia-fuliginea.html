Llerasia fuliginea

Llerasia fuliginea, commonly known as the black orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and striking, dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
