Lupinus gibertianus var. reineckianus

Lupinus gibertianus var. reineckianus, commonly known as the Reineck's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
