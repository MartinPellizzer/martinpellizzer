Leucoxyla saponaria

Leucoxyla saponaria, commonly known as the soap tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its sap is milky and has been used traditionally for its cleansing properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
