Linochilus parvifolius

Linochilus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved linochilus, is a flowering plant species native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests and grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
