Linum harperi

Linum harperi, commonly known as Harperi's flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its flowers are characterized by five petals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linum harperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
