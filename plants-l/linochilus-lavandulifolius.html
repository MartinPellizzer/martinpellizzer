Linochilus lavandulifolius

Linochilus lavandulifolius, commonly known as the lavender-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude grasslands of East Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that resemble lavender foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
