Lundia nitidula var. virginalis

Lundia nitidula var. virginalis, commonly known as the white-flowered lundia, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals that contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
