Lactuca salehensis

Lactuca salehensis, commonly known as the Sinai lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is native to the Middle East and North Africa, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has upright, hairy stems and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste.

Lactuca salehensis is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
