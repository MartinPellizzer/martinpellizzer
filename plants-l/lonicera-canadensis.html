Lonicera canadensis

Lonicera canadensis, commonly known as Canadian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is characterized by its red berries that ripen in the fall, which are a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Lonicera canadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
