Lepidaploa helophila

Lepidaploa helophila, commonly known as the marsh lily, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, allowing it to spread easily in moist soils. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated by insects.

Lepidaploa helophila thrives in shallow water or damp areas, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
