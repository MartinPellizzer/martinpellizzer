Leontodon hispidus

Leontodon hispidus, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, while its yellow flowers bloom in summer.

Leontodon hispidus is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
