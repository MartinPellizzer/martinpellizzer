Lepidagathis diffusa

Lepidagathis diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Lepidagathis diffusa is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
