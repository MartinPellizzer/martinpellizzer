Lupinus piperitus var. sparsipilosus

Lupinus piperitus var. sparsipilosus, commonly known as the sparsely hairy lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets with a fine, hairy texture. This variety is particularly noted for its sparse pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
