Loudetia coarctata

Loudetia coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib and a fibrous root system. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Loudetia coarctata is adapted to arid conditions and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
