Laothoe purpurea

Laothoe purpurea, commonly known as the purple emperor butterfly.

It is a species of butterfly found in Europe and parts of Asia, with a wingspan that can reach up to 8 centimeters. The male has striking purple wings with a metallic sheen, while the female is more brownish in color. The larvae feed on plants in the family Rosaceae, such as apples and hawthorns.

The plant's role in the butterfly's life cycle is crucial for its survival and reproduction.
