Lycaste cristata

Lycaste cristata, commonly known as the crested lycaste, is a unique hybrid orchid that results from the cross between a Cattleya and a Lycastes species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often flattened or crest-like growth form, which sets it apart from typical orchid morphology. The plant typically produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns and a strong fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Lycaste cristata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unusual appearance.
