Lactuca plicata

Lactuca plicata, commonly known as the zigzag lettuce, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient member of the Asteraceae family.
