Lycium albiflorum

Lycium albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower boxthorn, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Lycium albiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
