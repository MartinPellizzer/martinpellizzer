Leucanthemum × aramisii

Leucanthemum × aramisii, commonly known as Shasta daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, white ray flowers surrounding a central disc of yellow florets, creating a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Leucanthemum × aramisii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
