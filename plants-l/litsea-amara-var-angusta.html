Litsea amara var. angusta

Litsea amara var. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leafed litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellowish or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage and fruit make it valuable for ornamental purposes and traditional uses in local communities.
