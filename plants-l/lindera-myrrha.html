Lindera myrrha

Lindera myrrha, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
