Lepidium sativum var. latifolium

Lepidium sativum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf peppergrass, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, narrow to broad leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.
