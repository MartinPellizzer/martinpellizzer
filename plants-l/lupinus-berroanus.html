Lupinus berroanus

Lupinus berroanus, commonly known as the Berroan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus berroanus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.
