Limonium subrotundifolium

Limonium subrotundifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, spires of blue or purple flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage with rounded, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in saline environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Limonium subrotundifolium is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and distinctive appearance.
