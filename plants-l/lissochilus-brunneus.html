Lissochilus brunneus

Lissochilus brunneus, commonly known as the brown-lipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves and has a short, upright stem. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker than the rest of the flower.

Lissochilus brunneus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.
