Lycium piocorreanum

Lycium piocorreanum, commonly known as the Piorcorean boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its botanical characteristics include woody stems and a compact growth habit.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
