Leonia occidentalis

Leonia occidentalis, commonly known as the western leonia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the western hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Leonia occidentalis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
