Licuala sallehana var. incisifolia

Licuala sallehana var. incisifolia, commonly known as the serrated palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
