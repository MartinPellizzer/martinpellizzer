Lamium maculatum var. tenoreanum

Lamium maculatum var. tenoreanum, commonly known as the spotted dead nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple or red markings, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
