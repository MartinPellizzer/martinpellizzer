Latania aurea

Latania aurea, commonly known as the golden latania, is a striking palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are a vibrant yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that eventually develop into orange-red fruits, which are edible and enjoyed by both humans and wildlife. Latania aurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical collections.
