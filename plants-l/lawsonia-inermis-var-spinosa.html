Lawsonia inermis var. spinosa

Lawsonia inermis var. spinosa, commonly known as the African turpentine tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

The varietal form, spinosa, is distinguished by its spiny branches and more robust growth compared to the typical variety.
