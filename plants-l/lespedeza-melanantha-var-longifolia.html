Lespedeza melanantha var. longifolia

Lespedeza melanantha var. longifolia, commonly known as the slender lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
