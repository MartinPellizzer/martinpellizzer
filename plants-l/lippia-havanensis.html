Lippia havanensis

Lippia havanensis, commonly known as Cuban blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Lippia havanensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
