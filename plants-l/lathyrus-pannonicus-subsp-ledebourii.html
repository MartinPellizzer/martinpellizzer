Lathyrus pannonicus subsp. ledebourii

Lathyrus pannonicus subsp.

ledebourii, commonly known as the Balkan vetch, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
