Lactuca funebris

Lactuca funebris, commonly known as the funeral lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has dark green, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Lactuca funebris is known for its bitter taste and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
