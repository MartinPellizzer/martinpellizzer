Lonicera nummulariifolia subsp. occidentalis

Lonicera nummulariifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western evergreen honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and flowering pattern compared to other Lonicera species.
