Lathyrus brownii

Lathyrus brownii, commonly known as the brown borer pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial vine that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus brownii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
