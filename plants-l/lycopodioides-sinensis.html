Lycopodioides sinensis

Lycopodioides sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typical of its ancient lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Lycopodioides sinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
