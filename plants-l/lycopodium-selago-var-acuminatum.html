Lycopodium selago var. acuminatum

Lycopodium selago var. acuminatum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, creeping habit and produces small, scale-like leaves. The plant forms dense mats of foliage, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores for dispersal.

This variety is distinguished by its slender, acutely pointed leaves, which give it its specific epithet.
