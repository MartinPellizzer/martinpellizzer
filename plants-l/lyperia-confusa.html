Lyperia confusa

Lyperia confusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lyperia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly known as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
