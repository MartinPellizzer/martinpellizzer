Linum pallescens

Linum pallescens, commonly known as the pale flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Linum pallescens is adapted to arid environments and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
