Lessingianthus polyphyllus

Lessingianthus polyphyllus, commonly known as the many-leafed sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces numerous small, elongated leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
