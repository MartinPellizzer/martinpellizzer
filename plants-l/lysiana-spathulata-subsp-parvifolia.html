Lysiana spathulata subsp. parvifolia

Lysiana spathulata subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of eastern North America. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length.

The plant produces spikes of pale pink to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
