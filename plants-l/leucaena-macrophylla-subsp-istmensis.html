Leucaena macrophylla subsp. istmensis

Leucaena macrophylla subsp.

istmensis, commonly known as the Istanbul leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, flat pods containing several seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
