Lotus cytisoides subsp. conradiae

Lotus cytisoides subsp.

conradiae, commonly known as the Conrad's lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
