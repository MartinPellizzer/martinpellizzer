Leptochloa rupestris

Leptochloa rupestris, commonly known as the rock grass, is a small, perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and inland dunes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
