Lodhra pyrifolia

Lodhra pyrifolia, commonly known as the sacred fig or Indian banyan, is a large evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits. The plant is known for its extensive aerial roots that form a dense network, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The tree has cultural and medicinal significance in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
