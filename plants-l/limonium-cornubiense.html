Limonium cornubiense

Limonium cornubiense, commonly known as the Cornish sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southwest England.

It is characterized by its slender, blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its flowering stems are typically unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Limonium cornubiense plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects.
