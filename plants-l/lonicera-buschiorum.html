Lonicera buschiorum

Lonicera buschiorum, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the fall and is attractive to birds.
