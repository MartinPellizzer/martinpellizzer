Lathyrus cicera var. sub-bijugus

Lathyrus cicera var. sub-bijugus, commonly known as the lesser burr clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. Its fruit is a pod that contains several oblong, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
