Lomatogonium cuneifolium

Lomatogonium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lomatogonium, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
