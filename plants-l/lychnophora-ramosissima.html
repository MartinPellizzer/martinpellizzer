Lychnophora ramosissima

Lychnophora ramosissima, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *Brazileira*, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with many branches, giving it a bushy appearance. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Lychnophora ramosissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
