Leucadendron paludosum

Leucadendron paludosum, commonly known as the swamp leucadendron, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its striking, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
