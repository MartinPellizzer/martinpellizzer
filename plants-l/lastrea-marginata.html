Lastrea marginata

Lastrea marginata, commonly known as the rock fig or desert fig, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a branching structure.

The leaves are usually green with a margin that may have a reddish or purplish tint, depending on light exposure and environmental conditions.
