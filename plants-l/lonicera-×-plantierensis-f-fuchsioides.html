Lonicera × plantierensis f. fuchsioides

Lonicera × plantierensis f. fuchsioides, commonly known as the pink honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant characterized by its unique floral morphology.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and produces small, red berries that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
