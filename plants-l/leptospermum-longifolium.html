Leptospermum longifolium

Leptospermum longifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often found in heathlands and coastal regions. It produces small, red or black berries that are edible and have a mild, tea-like flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
