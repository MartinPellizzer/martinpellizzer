Lessertia

Lessertia, commonly known as the starry nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It comprises several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their opposite, simple leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. Lessertia species often produce small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral display.
