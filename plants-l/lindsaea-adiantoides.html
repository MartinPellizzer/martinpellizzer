Lindsaea adiantoides

Lindsaea adiantoides, commonly known as the fern moss or the little maid fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Lindsaea adiantoides is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
