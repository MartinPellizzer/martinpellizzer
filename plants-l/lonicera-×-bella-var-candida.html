Lonicera × bella var. candida

Lonicera × bella var. candida, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are typically borne in clusters and emit a sweet, fragrant aroma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
