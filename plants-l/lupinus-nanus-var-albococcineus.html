Lupinus nanus var. albococcineus

Lupinus nanus var. albococcineus, commonly known as the white-cushion lupine, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
