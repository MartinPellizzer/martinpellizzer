Luzula decipiens

Luzula decipiens, commonly known as the false silvergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula decipiens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
