Lupinus capitatus

Lupinus capitatus, commonly known as the clustered lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus capitatus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
