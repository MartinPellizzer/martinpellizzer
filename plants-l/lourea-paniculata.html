Lourea paniculata

Lourea paniculata, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow paniculata, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Lourea paniculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
