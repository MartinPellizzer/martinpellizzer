Lippia boliviana var. angusta

Lippia boliviana var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf lippia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.

The varietal form, angusta, distinguishes it from other varieties of Lippia by its narrower leaf structure.
