Limnophila crassifolia

Limnophila crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broad and flat, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Limnophila crassifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
