Lycium ciliatum

Lycium ciliatum, commonly known as the silky boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. The plant has spiny stems and narrow, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Lycium ciliatum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
