Lepanthes tentaculata

Lepanthes tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful floral structures that feature elongated, finger-like appendages called tentacles. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate and ornate floral morphology.
