Lebeckia macrantha

Lebeckia macrantha, commonly known as the bluebells of the Cape, is a South African native plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical seed heads that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lebeckia macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
