Lepidium oblongum var. typicum

Lepidium oblongum var. typicum, commonly known as the common peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, linear to ovate, and often have a peppery or spicy scent when crushed.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in ecological succession.
