Leucanthemum vulgare var. cantabricum

Leucanthemum vulgare var. cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian ragged-robin, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces showy white to pale yellow flowers with dark centers, and its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
