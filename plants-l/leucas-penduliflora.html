Leucas penduliflora

Leucas penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas penduliflora is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
