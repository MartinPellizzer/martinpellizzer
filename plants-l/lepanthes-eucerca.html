Lepanthes eucerca

Lepanthes eucerca, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white and greenish-blue flowers with a distinctive silver-tipped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes eucerca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
