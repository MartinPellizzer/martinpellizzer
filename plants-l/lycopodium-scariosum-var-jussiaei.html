Lycopodium scariosum var. jussiaei

Lycopodium scariosum var. jussiaei, commonly known as the Jussiaei clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen fern-like plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
