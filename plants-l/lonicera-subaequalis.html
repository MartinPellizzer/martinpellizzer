Lonicera subaequalis

Lonicera subaequalis, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant's flowers are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Lonicera subaequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
