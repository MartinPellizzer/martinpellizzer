Lagerstroemia densiflora

Lagerstroemia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered panicles of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has smooth, grayish bark that peels in thin, papery layers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface.

Lagerstroemia densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
