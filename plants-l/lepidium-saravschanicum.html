Lepidium saravschanicum

Lepidium saravschanicum, commonly known as the Sarawsky cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its small, erect growth habit and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its fruits are short, cylindrical siliques that contain several seeds.

Lepidium saravschanicum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
