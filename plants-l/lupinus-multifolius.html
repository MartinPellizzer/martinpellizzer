Lupinus multifolius

Lupinus multifolius, commonly known as the many-leaved lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus multifolius is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
