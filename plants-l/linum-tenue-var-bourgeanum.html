Linum tenue var. bourgeanum

Linum tenue var. bourgeanum, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a prominent central stigma, which are often borne on slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
