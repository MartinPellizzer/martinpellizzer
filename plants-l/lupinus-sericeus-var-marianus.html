Lupinus sericeus var. marianus

Lupinus sericeus var. marianus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features slender, silver-gray foliage and erect, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its root system fixes nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
