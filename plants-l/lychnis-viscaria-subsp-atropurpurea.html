Lychnis viscaria subsp. atropurpurea

Lychnis viscaria subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, sticky stems and its ability to bloom from late spring to early summer.
