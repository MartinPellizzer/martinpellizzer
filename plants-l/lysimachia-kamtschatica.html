Lysimachia kamtschatica

Lysimachia kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lysimachia kamtschatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
