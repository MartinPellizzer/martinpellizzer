Lomandra preissii

Lomandra preissii, commonly known as the coastal lomandra, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Lomandra preissii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native wildlife.
