Lupinus rubriflorus

Lupinus rubriflorus, commonly known as the scarlet flax or red-flowered lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Lupinus rubriflorus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.
