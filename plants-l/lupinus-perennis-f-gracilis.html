Lupinus perennis f. gracilis

Lupinus perennis f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
