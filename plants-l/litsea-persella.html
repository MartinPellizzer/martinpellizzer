Litsea persella

Litsea persella, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or Chinese litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China, Vietnam, and the Philippines, and it typically grows in lowland forests and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small, white flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Litsea persella is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
