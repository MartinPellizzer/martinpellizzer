Litsea baticulin

Litsea baticulin, commonly known as the Bornean litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they remain green throughout the year.

Litsea baticulin plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
