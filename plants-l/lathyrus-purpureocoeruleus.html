Lathyrus purpureocoeruleus

Lathyrus purpureocoeruleus, commonly known as the purple and blue peacock bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking, bi-colored flowers that range from purple to blue. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, though the foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant, peacock-like flower patterns.
