Linaria × sepium

Linaria × sepium, commonly known as the hybrid sweet William, is a flowering plant that results from the cross between Linaria vulgaris and Linaria maroccana.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region but has been widely cultivated in gardens around the world. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Linaria × sepium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
