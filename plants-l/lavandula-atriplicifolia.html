Lavandula atriplicifolia

Lavandula atriplicifolia, commonly known as black lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a mild, aromatic scent.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
