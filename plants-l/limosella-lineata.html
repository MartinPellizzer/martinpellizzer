Limosella lineata

Limosella lineata, commonly known as the threadleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dunes or along shorelines. The plant features small, delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a grass-like appearance.

Limosella lineata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
