Lodhra jucunda

Lodhra jucunda, commonly known as the sacred fig or basil, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of India.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically green when mature, turning yellow or red as it ripens. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.

The leaves are sometimes used in culinary and medicinal applications.
