Linociera pachyphylla

Linociera pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved linociera, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, a key adaptation to its dry environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep underground water sources.

Linociera pachyphylla is often found in rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
