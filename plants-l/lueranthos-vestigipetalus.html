Lueranthos vestigipetalus

Lueranthos vestigipetalus, commonly known as the ghost petal orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that appear almost ethereal, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often within cloud forests. Its flowers are small and delicate, with a unique structure that suggests a specialized pollination mechanism.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a critically endangered species.
