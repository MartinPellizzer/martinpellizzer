Loeseneriella parkinsonii

Loeseneriella parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's rockrose, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
