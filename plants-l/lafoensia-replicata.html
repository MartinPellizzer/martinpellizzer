Lafoensia replicata

Lafoensia replicata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Lafoensia replicata is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
