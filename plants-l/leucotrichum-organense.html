Leucotrichum organense

Leucotrichum organense, commonly known as the Organ's hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and wetlands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its unique botanical characteristics include the presence of long, silky hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

Leucotrichum organense plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
