Linaria bipartita var. linogrisea

Linaria bipartita var. linogrisea, commonly known as the gray-leaved toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a distinctive grayish hue. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
