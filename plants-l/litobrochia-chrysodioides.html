Litobrochia chrysodioides

Litobrochia chrysodioides, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
