Lepidadenia griffithii

Lepidadenia griffithii, commonly known as the Indian senna, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
