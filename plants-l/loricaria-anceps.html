Loricaria anceps

Loricaria anceps, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a tropical aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a short, stout stem with roots that anchor it in shallow water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, boat-shaped petal.

Loricaria anceps plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native wetland ecosystems.
