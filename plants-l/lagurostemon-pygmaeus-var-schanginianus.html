Lagurostemon pygmaeus var. schanginianus

Lagurostemon pygmaeus var. schanginianus, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking floral display and drought tolerance.
