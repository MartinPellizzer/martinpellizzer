Lepanthes gerardensis

Lepanthes gerardensis, commonly known as the Gerard's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as montane forests, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a wide range of colors, including white, yellow, and purple.

Lepanthes gerardensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
