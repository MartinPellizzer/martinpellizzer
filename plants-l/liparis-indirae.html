Liparis indirae

Liparis indirae, commonly known as the Indira's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Liparis indirae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitats.
