Linum spicatum var. sessiliflorum

Linum spicatum var. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
