Lobelia umbellifera

Lobelia umbellifera, commonly known as the clustered lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features opposite leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Lobelia umbellifera is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
