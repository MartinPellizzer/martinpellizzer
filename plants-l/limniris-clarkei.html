Limniris clarkei

Limniris clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Limniris clarkei plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
