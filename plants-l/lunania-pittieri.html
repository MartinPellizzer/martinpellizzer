Lunania pittieri

Lunania pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Lunania pittieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
