Launaea omanensis

Launaea omanensis, commonly known as the Oman dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spherical, pappus-equipped seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Launaea omanensis is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
