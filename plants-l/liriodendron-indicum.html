Liriodendron indicum

Liriodendron indicum, commonly known as the Indian tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, yellow-green flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a smooth, grayish-brown bark. It produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain two winged seeds.

Liriodendron indicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
