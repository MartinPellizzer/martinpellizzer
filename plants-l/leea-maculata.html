Leea maculata

Leea maculata, commonly known as the spotted leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often have distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth, it requires regular pruning to control its spread.
