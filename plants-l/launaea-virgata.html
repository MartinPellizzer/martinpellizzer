Launaea virgata

Launaea virgata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal. It has adapted to thrive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
