Lindelofia tubiflora

Lindelofia tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous lindelofia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in seasonal dry environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
