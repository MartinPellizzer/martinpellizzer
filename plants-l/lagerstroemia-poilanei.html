Lagerstroemia poilanei

Lagerstroemia poilanei, commonly known as the Pailane's crape myrtle, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, ranging from pink to red. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Lagerstroemia poilanei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
