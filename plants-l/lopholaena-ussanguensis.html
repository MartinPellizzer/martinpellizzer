Lopholaena ussanguensis

Lopholaena ussanguensis, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
