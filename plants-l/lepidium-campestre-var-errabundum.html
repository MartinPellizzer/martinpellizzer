Lepidium campestre var. errabundum

Lepidium campestre var. errabundum, commonly known as the wandering wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
