Leontodon incanus subsp. albanicus

Leontodon incanus subsp.

albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the dandelion family, Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in Albania and adjacent regions. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
