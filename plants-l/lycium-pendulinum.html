Lycium pendulinum

Lycium pendulinum, commonly known as the hanging lycium, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Lycium pendulinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
