Lupinus angustifolius subvar. violaceus

Lupinus angustifolius subvar. violaceus, commonly known as the violetleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, violet-hued leaves and clusters of pea-like, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
