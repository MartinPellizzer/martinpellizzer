Lens villosa

Lens villosa, commonly known as the hairy lentil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, hairy seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are covered in fine, white hairs.

Lens villosa is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil types.
