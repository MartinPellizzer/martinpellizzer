Lepanthes drymocharis

Lepanthes drymocharis, commonly known as the cloud forest orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns on its petals and sepals. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Lepanthes drymocharis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
