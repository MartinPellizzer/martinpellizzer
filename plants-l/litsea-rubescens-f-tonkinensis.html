Litsea rubescens f. tonkinensis

Litsea rubescens f. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin flame-leafed laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, reddish-orange leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
