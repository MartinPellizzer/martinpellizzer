Lupinus tidestromii var. layneae

Lupinus tidestromii var. layneae, commonly known as Layne's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its importance to local pollinators.
