Lobivia minutiflora

Lobivia minutiflora, commonly known as the miniature lobivia, is a small, compact cactus native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a woolly coating that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, typical of many cacti adapted to drought.

Lobivia minutiflora is prized by collectors for its unique form and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
