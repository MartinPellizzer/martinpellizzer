Ligusticum tachiroei var. filisectum

Ligusticum tachiroei var. filisectum, commonly known as the Japanese lovage, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its small white flowers appear in summer, and it is valued for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
