Lupinus lyallii subsp. subpandens

Lupinus lyallii subsp.

subpandens, commonly known as the Lyall's lupin, is a perennial flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
