Lasianthus vrieseanus

Lasianthus vrieseanus, commonly known as the Vriesean flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
