Ligustrum vulgare var. aureovariegatum

Ligustrum vulgare var. aureovariegatum, commonly known as the golden variegated privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive yellow and white variegation pattern, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
