Lobivia coronata

Lobivia coronata, commonly known as the Crowned Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and unique, spiny appearance. The plant features a short, thick stem with multiple branching offsets, and its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Lobivia coronata produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged foliage.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
