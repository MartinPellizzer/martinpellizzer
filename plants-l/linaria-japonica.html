Linaria japonica

Linaria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese toadflax, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the plant family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces slender, branching stems and has small, oval-shaped leaves.

Linaria japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
