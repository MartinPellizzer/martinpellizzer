Loranthus rubroviridis var. bechuanicus

Loranthus rubroviridis var. bechuanicus, commonly known as the Bechuanian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the African savanna.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing adhesive seeds that facilitate attachment to new host plants.

This species plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
