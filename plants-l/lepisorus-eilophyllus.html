Lepisorus eilophyllus

Lepisorus eilophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed polypody, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices.

Lepisorus eilophyllus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
