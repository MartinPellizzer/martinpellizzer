Laurus parviflora

Laurus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both landscaping and culinary use.
