Lobivia rubescens

Lobivia rubescens, commonly known as the red-tipped lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features compact, rounded stems that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water, and turn reddish in response to sunlight.

Lobivia rubescens is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and striking appearance.
