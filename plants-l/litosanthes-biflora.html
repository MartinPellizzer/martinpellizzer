Litosanthes biflora

Litosanthes biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered rockflower, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, waxy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
