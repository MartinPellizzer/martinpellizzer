Linum hirsutum subsp. byzantinum

Linum hirsutum subsp.

byzantinum, commonly known as Byzantine flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces capsules containing numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its historical use in textile production and as a source of oil underscores its botanical and economic significance.
