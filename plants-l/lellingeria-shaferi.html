Lellingeria shaferi

Lellingeria shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's lollipop cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are small and spiny, providing protection from herbivores.

Lellingeria shaferi is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
