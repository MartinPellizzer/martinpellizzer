Lopezia corymbosa

Lopezia corymbosa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelion blooms. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lopezia corymbosa produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.
