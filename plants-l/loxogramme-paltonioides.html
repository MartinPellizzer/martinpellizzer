Loxogramme paltonioides

Loxogramme paltonioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a small aquatic fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense, floating mats. The plant has finely divided, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Loxogramme paltonioides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
