Lepisorus scolopendrium var. himalayensis

Lepisorus scolopendrium var. himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan water fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its finely divided, pinnately arranged fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of sori. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions of the Himalayas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude conditions and its role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
