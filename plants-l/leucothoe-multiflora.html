Leucothoe multiflora

Leucothoe multiflora, commonly known as the Southern waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that remain throughout the growing season. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and seasonal blooms, contributing to its use in landscape design.
