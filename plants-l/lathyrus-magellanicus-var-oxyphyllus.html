Lathyrus magellanicus var. oxyphyllus

Lathyrus magellanicus var. oxyphyllus, commonly known as the Magellanic larkspur, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are often hairy. This variety is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
