Lupinus heptaphyllus f. typicus

Lupinus heptaphyllus f. typicus, commonly known as the seven-leaf lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with seven leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus heptaphyllus f. typicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
