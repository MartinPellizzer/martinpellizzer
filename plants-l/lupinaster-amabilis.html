Lupinaster amabilis

Lupinaster amabilis, commonly known as the sweet lupinaster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its root system is well-developed, aiding in nitrogen fixation. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
