Lobelia × nova

Lobelia × nova, commonly known as the hybrid lobelia, is a cross between Lobelia erinus and Lobelia tupa.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, though it is now cultivated in various regions for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.
