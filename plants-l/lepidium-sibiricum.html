Lepidium sibiricum

Lepidium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in racemes, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

The plant is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
