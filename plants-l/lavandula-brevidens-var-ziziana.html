Lavandula brevidens var. ziziana

Lavandula brevidens var. ziziana, commonly known as the Zizania lavender, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. This variety is prized for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in aromatherapy for its calming essential oils.
