Leontodon corymbosus

Leontodon corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Leontodon corymbosus is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
