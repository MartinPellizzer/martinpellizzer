Lamanonia

Lamanonia, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow or orange flowers that resemble chains, hence its common name. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Lamanonia has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
