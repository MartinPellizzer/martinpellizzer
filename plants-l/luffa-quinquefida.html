Luffa quinquefida

Luffa quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and hairy stems, which are typical of many members of its family. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. The fruit's sponge-like interior becomes fibrous and usable as a natural sponge after drying.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
