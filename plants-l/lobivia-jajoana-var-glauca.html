Lobivia jajoana var. glauca

Lobivia jajoana var. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and dense, blue-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
