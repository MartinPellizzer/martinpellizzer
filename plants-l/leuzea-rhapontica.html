Leuzea rhapontica

Leuzea rhapontica, commonly known as rhapontic leuzea, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Leuzea rhapontica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
