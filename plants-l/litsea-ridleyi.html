Litsea ridleyi

Litsea ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
