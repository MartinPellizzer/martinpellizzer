Lathyrus variabilis

Lathyrus variabilis, commonly known as variable vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pods containing several seeds. Lathyrus variabilis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
