Lantana sanguinea

Lantana sanguinea, commonly known as red lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to red. The plant is shrub-like, with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
