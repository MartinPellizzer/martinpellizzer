Laserpitium polygamum

Laserpitium polygamum, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
