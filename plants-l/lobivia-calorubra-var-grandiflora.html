Lobivia calorubra var. grandiflora

Lobivia calorubra var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Grandiflora Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers during the warmer months, adding visual interest to its otherwise muted foliage. Its leaves are typically cylindrical and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its compact growth habit and striking floral display, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
