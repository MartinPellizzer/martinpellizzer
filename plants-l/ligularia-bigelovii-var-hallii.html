Ligularia bigelovii var. hallii

Ligularia bigelovii var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is a striking feature, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in wetland settings.
