Linaria depauperata var. hegelmaieri

Linaria depauperata var. hegelmaieri, commonly known as the Hegelmaier's toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes, characteristic of the Linaria genus. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
