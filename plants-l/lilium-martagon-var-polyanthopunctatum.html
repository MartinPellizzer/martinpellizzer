Lilium martagon var. polyanthopunctatum

Lilium martagon var. polyanthopunctatum, commonly known as the spotted martagon lily, is a rare and ornamental variant of the martagon lily.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, and often bi- or tri-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers well-drained, moist soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

This variety is noted for its distinctive spotted or speckled petals, which give it its common name.
