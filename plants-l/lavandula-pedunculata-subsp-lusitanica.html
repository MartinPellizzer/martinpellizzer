Lavandula pedunculata subsp. lusitanica

Lavandula pedunculata subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese lavender, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe. It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces spikes of pale purple to lilac flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild fragrance, though it is less commonly cultivated than other lavender varieties.

This subspecies is also noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
