Lobelia pedicellaris

Lobelia pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lobelia pedicellaris is often found in shaded areas and is a common species in conservation areas due to its ecological importance.
