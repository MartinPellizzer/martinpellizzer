Lupinus affinis var. carnosulus

Lupinus affinis var. carnosulus, commonly known as the woolly lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
