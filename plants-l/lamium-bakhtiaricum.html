Lamium bakhtiaricum

Lamium bakhtiaricum, commonly known as the Bakhtiar's deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

Lamium bakhtiaricum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.
