Lavatera trimestris var. malviformis

Lavatera trimestris var. malviformis, commonly known as the Malviform Lavatera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and palmately lobed, with a soft, velvety texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
