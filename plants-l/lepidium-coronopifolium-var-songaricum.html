Lepidium coronopifolium var. songaricum

Lepidium coronopifolium var. songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric Peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, with a distinctive lobed or divided shape, and it produces small, rounded seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
