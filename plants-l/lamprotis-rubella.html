Lamprotis rubella

Lamprotis rubella, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are known for their stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
