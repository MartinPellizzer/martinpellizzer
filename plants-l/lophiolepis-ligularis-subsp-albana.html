Lophiolepis ligularis subsp. albana

Lophiolepis ligularis subsp.

albana, commonly known as the white-flowered lophiolepis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
