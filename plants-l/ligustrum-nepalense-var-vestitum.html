Ligustrum nepalense var. vestitum

Ligustrum nepalense var. vestitum, commonly known as the Nepal privet, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge in temperate climates.
