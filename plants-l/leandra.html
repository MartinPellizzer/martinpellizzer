Leandra

Leandra, commonly known as the evergreen orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plants are characterized by their showy, fragrant flowers that often bloom in clusters. They typically grow as shrubs or small trees with evergreen foliage.

Leandra species are prized for their ornamental value and are often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
