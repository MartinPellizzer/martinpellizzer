Litsea sebifera var. brachyphylla

Litsea sebifera var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandalwood, is a variant of the sandalwood genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its aromatic wood is highly valued for its use in incense and traditional medicine.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
