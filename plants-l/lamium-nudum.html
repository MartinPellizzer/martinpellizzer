Lamium nudum

Lamium nudum, commonly known as spring deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with toothed edges. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Lamium nudum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in gardens and meadows.
