Lonchocarpus capensis

Lonchocarpus capensis, commonly known as the Cape triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals that resemble triggers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
