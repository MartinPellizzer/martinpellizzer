Lycium mediterraneum var. leptophyllum

Lycium mediterraneum var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces red berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It is often found in southern Europe, North Africa, and parts of the Middle East.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
