Limonium planesiae

Limonium planesiae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and drought. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Limonium planesiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
