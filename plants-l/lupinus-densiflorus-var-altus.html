Lupinus densiflorus var. altus

Lupinus densiflorus var. altus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lupinus densiflorus var. altus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
