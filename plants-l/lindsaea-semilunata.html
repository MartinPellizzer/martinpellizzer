Lindsaea semilunata

Lindsaea semilunata, commonly known as the moon-shaped fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that often form a semicircular shape. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for reproduction through spores. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or branches in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
