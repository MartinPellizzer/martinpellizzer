Leea cordata

Leea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Leea cordata is a member of the family Leeaceae and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
