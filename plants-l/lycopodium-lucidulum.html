Lycopodium lucidulum

Lycopodium lucidulum, commonly known as the shining clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a dense, moss-like appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Lycopodium lucidulum reproduces via spores rather than seeds, which are produced in specialized structures called sporangia.

Its simple structure and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of early plant evolution.
