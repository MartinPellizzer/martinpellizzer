Limonia dubia

Limonia dubia, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, round fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit, which is edible, has a thin, pale-colored rind and a sweet, acidic pulp.
