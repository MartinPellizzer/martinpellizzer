Ligularia tianschanica

Ligularia tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
