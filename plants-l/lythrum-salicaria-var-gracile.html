Lythrum salicaria var. gracile

Lythrum salicaria var. gracile, commonly known as narrowleaf purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom from June to September. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in natural habitats.
