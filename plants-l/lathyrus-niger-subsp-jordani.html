Lathyrus niger subsp. jordani

Lathyrus niger subsp.

jordani, commonly known as the Jordanian black vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
