Lophiolepis lappacea subsp. tenuiloba

Lophiolepis lappacea subsp.

tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lophiolepis, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
