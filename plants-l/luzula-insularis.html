Luzula insularis

Luzula insularis, commonly known as the island loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikes of small, usually greenish or white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Luzula insularis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
