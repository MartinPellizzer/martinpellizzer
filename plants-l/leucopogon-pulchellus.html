Leucopogon pulchellus

Leucopogon pulchellus, commonly known as the pink beak-flower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture typical of the Myrtaceae family.

Leucopogon pulchellus is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
