Lupinus gormanii

Lupinus gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Lupinus gormanii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
