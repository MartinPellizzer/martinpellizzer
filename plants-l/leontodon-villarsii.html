Leontodon villarsii

Leontodon villarsii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are blue to purple in color, often resembling small bells.

Leontodon villarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
