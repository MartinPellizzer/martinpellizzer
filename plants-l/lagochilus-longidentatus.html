Lagochilus longidentatus

Lagochilus longidentatus, commonly known as the long-toothed horehound, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
