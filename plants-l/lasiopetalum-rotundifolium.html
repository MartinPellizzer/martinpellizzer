Lasiopetalum rotundifolium

Lasiopetalum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical characteristics include woody stems, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
