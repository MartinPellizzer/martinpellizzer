Lomatium columbianum

Lomatium columbianum, commonly known as the Pacific silverroot, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and is often found in moist, shady woodlands. Its root system is tuberous and edible, though it is less commonly consumed than other lomatium species.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
