Lippia grandifolia var. longipedunculata

Lippia grandifolia var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate lippia, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support the flowering clusters.
