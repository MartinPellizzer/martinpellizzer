Leptorkis prainii

Leptorkis prainii, commonly known as the Prain's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are often pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Leptorkis prainii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
