Loranthus kalahiensis

Loranthus kalahiensis, commonly known as the Kalahari mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the Kalahari region of southern Africa.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its specialized stems that penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals. Its life cycle is closely tied to the seasonal availability of moisture, making it a characteristic species of arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
