Lachnagrostis punicea

Lachnagrostis punicea, commonly known as the purple grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is typically fine and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Lachnagrostis punicea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
