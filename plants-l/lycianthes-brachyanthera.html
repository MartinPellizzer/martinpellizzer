Lycianthes brachyanthera

Lycianthes brachyanthera, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas, particularly found in regions of Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its vibrant floral display.
