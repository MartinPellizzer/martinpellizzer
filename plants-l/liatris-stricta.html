Liatris stricta

Liatris stricta, commonly known as tall blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Liatris stricta is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
