Laxmannia gracilis var. minor

Laxmannia gracilis var. minor, commonly known as the slender laxmannia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
