Lilium tigrinum

Lilium tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers with black spots, which resemble a tiger's stripes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has strap-like, grassy leaves. Its bulbous roots are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Lilium tigrinum is a member of the Liliaceae family, known for its ornamental value in gardens.
