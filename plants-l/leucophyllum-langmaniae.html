Leucophyllum langmaniae

Leucophyllum langmaniae, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features dense, silvery-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, reducing water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water once established.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
