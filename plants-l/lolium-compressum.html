Lolium compressum

Lolium compressum, commonly known as bentgrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its low maintenance and adaptability, it is both a desirable ornamental grass and a common weed in agricultural settings.
