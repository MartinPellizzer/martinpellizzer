Lespedeza lineata

Lespedeza lineata, commonly known as narrowleaf stylo, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. It has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Lespedeza lineata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
