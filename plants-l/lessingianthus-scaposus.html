Lessingianthus scaposus

Lessingianthus scaposus, commonly known as the slender stemless sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features slender, leafless stems that support its bright yellow ray flowers and numerous disk flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant and contributes to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
