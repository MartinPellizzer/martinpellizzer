Liabum subviride

Liabum subviride, commonly known as the greenleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
