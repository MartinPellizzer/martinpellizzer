Lomagramma pteroides

Lomagramma pteroides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, pinnate structure. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Lomagramma pteroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
