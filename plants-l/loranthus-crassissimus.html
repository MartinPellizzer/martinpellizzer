Loranthus crassissimus

Loranthus crassissimus, commonly known as the broad-leaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance, aiding in seed dispersal by birds. It is native to regions of Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host tree if left unchecked.
