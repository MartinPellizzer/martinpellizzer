Lagenaria siceraria var. laevisperma

Lagenaria siceraria var. laevisperma, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a fast-growing, annual vine native to Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy green skin and a hollow, bottle-shaped fruit that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil. Its fruit is used in various culinary and practical applications, including as a container or for making utensils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits.
