Loranthus pilosus

Loranthus pilosus, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects and birds. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
