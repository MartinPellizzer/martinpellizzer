Laurus lingui

Laurus lingui, commonly known as the sweet bay laurel, is a flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by dark, edible berries.

Laurus lingui is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in various cultures.
