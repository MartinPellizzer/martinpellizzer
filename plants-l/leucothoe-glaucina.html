Leucothoe glaucina

Leucothoe glaucina, commonly known as the Appalachian beauty, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and white underneath, giving it a striking appearance. Its showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers bloom in late spring, followed by small, bright red berries in the fall.

Leucothoe glaucina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
