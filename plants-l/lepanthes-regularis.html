Lepanthes regularis

Lepanthes regularis, commonly known as the regular orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and strikingly colorful flowers that often feature a blend of white, purple, and red hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure and are often pollinated by small insects like bees and flies.

Lepanthes regularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations.
