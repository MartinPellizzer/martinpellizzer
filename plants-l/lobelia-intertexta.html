Lobelia intertexta

Lobelia intertexta, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces showy, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia intertexta is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
