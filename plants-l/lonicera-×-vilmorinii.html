Lonicera × vilmorinii

Lonicera × vilmorinii, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its opposite, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall and are edible for birds.

Lonicera × vilmorinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
