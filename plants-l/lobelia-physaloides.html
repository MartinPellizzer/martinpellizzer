Lobelia physaloides

Lobelia physaloides, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, inflated, violet or blue flowers that resemble small balloons, blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
