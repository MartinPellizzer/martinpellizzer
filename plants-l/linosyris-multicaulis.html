Linosyris multicaulis

Linosyris multicaulis, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fibrous stalks are used in traditional crafts, and it is valued for its ability to stabilize dunes.

Linosyris multicaulis is adapted to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
