Lupinus falsocomatus

Lupinus falsocomatus, commonly known as the false common lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus falsocomatus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
