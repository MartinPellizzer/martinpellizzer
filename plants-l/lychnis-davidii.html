Lychnis davidii

Lychnis davidii, commonly known as the David's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and typically grows in grassy areas and woodland edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a low-growing, spreading habit.

Lychnis davidii is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
