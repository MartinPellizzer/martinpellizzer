Leptospermum rodwayanum

Leptospermum rodwayanum, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical leaves and showy clusters of red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or moist forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
