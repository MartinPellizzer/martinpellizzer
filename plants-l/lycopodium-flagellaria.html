Lycopodium flagellaria

Lycopodium flagellaria, commonly known as the whip fern, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems that resemble whips, hence its common name. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and possesses sporangia that release spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
