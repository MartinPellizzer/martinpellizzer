Larryleachia meloformis

Larryleachia meloformis, commonly known as the melon-shaped lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive melon-like scent. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
