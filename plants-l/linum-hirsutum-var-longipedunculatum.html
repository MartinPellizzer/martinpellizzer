Linum hirsutum var. longipedunculatum

Linum hirsutum var. longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long pedunculate flax, is a variant of the flax species characterized by its elongated peduncles.

It is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia, typically found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in fiber production.
