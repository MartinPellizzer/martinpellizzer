Lepidium gerloffianum var. microcarpum

Lepidium gerloffianum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited wallcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, branched stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and are followed by small, rounded pods. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is notable for its compact size and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
