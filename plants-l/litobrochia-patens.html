Litobrochia patens

Litobrochia patens, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often on slopes or cliffs. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue or violet, arranged in a terminal cluster. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Litobrochia patens is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
