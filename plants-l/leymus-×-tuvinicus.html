Leymus × tuvinicus

Leymus × tuvinicus, commonly known as the Tuvinian grass, is a hybrid species native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates.

Leymus × tuvinicus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
