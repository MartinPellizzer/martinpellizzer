Lippia domingensis

Lippia domingensis, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, blue or purple flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, open habitats.

Lippia domingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
