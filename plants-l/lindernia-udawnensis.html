Lindernia udawnensis

Lindernia udawnensis, commonly known as the Mexican lindernia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the summer months. Lindernia udawnensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
