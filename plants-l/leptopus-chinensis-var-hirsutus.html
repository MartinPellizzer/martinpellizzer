Leptopus chinensis var. hirsutus

Leptopus chinensis var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
