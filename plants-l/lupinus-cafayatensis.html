Lupinus cafayatensis

Lupinus cafayatensis, commonly known as Cafayate lupin, is a perennial legume native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces spikes of showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Lupinus cafayatensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
