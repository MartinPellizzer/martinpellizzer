Lanonia acaulis

Lanonia acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed lanonia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, unbranched stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Lanonia acaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
