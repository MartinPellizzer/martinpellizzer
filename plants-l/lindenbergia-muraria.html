Lindenbergia muraria

Lindenbergia muraria, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in summer. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in coastal or inland deserts.

Lindenbergia muraria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
