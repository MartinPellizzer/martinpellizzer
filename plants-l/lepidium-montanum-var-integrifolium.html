Lepidium montanum var. integrifolium

Lepidium montanum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and compound leaves with entire margins. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across mountainous areas.

Lepidium montanum var. integrifolium is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
