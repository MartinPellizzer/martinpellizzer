Labramia sambiranensis

Labramia sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis Bignay, is a species of flowering plant in the family Bignoniaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
