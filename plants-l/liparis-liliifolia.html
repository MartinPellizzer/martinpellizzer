Liparis liliifolia

Liparis liliifolia, commonly known as the lady's tresses orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and intricate, spiral arrangement of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which are usually pale pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Liparis liliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
