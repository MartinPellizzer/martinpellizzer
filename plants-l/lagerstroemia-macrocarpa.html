Lagerstroemia macrocarpa

Lagerstroemia macrocarpa, commonly known as the crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, long-lasting panicles of pink, purple, or white flowers that appear in summer. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that peels in thin, papery layers, adding visual interest throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

Lagerstroemia macrocarpa is popular in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
