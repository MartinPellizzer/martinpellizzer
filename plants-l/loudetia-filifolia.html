Loudetia filifolia

Loudetia filifolia, commonly known as the silky filifolium, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
