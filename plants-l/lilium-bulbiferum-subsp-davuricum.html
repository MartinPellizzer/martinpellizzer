Lilium bulbiferum subsp. davuricum

Lilium bulbiferum subsp.

davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, strap-like leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red.

The plant is characterized by its robust bulbs, which are used for propagation and storage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
