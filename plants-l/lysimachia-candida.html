Lysimachia candida

Lysimachia candida, commonly known as white loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in spikes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Lysimachia candida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
