Lysimachia angustifolia

Lysimachia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf chickweed, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It features small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a common ground cover in its native habitats.
