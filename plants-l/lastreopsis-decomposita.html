Lastreopsis decomposita

Lastreopsis decomposita, commonly known as the decomposing lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It forms a thin, crust-like layer on rocks and soil, often in temperate and subpolar regions. The plant's thallus is composed of a symbiotic partnership between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the environment. It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on its broad, flat structure for photosynthesis.

This lichen is an important indicator species for environmental health and is often used in ecological studies.
