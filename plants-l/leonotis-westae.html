Leonotis westae

Leonotis westae, commonly known as the West African candle bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that resemble candles. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and somewhat fibrous.

Leonotis westae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
