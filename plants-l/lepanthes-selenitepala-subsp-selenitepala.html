Lepanthes selenitepala subsp. selenitepala

Lepanthes selenitepala subsp.

selenitepala, commonly known as the silver moon orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central America. This terrestrial orchid typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale green flowers that often have a strikingly metallic sheen. The plant produces small, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Its unique appearance and rarity make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
