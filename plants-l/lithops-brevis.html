Lithops brevis

Lithops brevis, commonly known as the narrowleaf stone plant, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, leaf-like structures that resemble stones, aiding in its camouflage within its arid habitat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are fleshy and store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Lithops brevis produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise stone-like appearance.
