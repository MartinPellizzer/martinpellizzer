Laelia autumnalis var. atrorubens

Laelia autumnalis var. atrorubens, commonly known as the autumn laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers with a dark, velvety throat, which give it its varietal name "atrorubens.". The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It prefers cool, high-altitude environments with bright, indirect light and moderate humidity.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in many modern orchid hybrids due to its vigorous growth and showy blooms.
