Leptolaena diospyroidea var. rupestris

Leptolaena diospyroidea var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock persimmon, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
