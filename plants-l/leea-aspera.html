Leea aspera

Leea aspera, commonly known as the rough leea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Leeaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are typically clustered in racemes, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

Leea aspera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
