Litosanthes dichotoma

Litosanthes dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched rockflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows on rocky or coastal soils. The plant features compact, rounded foliage with small, leathery leaves arranged in a dense, bushy form. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Litosanthes dichotoma is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showcasing resilience to salt spray and drought.
