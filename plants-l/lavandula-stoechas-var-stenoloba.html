Lavandula stoechas var. stenoloba

Lavandula stoechas var. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its unique, elongated calyx that resembles a tail, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its fragrance and ornamental value in both gardens and floral arrangements.
