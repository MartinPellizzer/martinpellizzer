Lobelia obliqua

Lobelia obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight zigzag pattern along the edges.

Lobelia obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a nectar source for various pollinators.
