Leucadendron chamelaea

Leucadendron chamelaea, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that range in color from orange to red, giving it its common name. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Leucadendron chamelaea is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
