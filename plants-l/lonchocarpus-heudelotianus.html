Lonchocarpus heudelotianus

Lonchocarpus heudelotianus, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also valued as a cover crop in agricultural systems.
