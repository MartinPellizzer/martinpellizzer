Loranthus parvibracteatus

Loranthus parvibracteatus, commonly known as the small-bract mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some shelter and food for birds and insects. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

Its botanical features make it an important component of forest ecosystems, though it can be considered a potential pest in some cases.
