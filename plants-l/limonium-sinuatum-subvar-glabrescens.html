Limonium sinuatum subvar. glabrescens

Limonium sinuatum subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and succulent, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline or coastal environments, making it well adapted to coastal dunes and rocky shores. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subvariety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh coastal conditions.
