Lycopodium ledermannii

Lycopodium ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Lycopodium ledermannii is native to parts of North America, particularly in the southeastern United States.

Its historical use in traditional medicine has contributed to its recognition in botanical studies.
