Leucothoe martii var. glabra

Leucothoe martii var. glabra, commonly known as smooth leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and alternate, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
