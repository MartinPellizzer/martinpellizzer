Lobivia haagei var. canacruzensis

Lobivia haagei var. canacruzensis, commonly known as the Canacruze Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces small, pale green or blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It blooms with clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a prized addition to succulent collections.
