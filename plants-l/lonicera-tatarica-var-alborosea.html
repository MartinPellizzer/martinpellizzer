Lonicera tatarica var. alborosea

Lonicera tatarica var. alborosea, commonly known as the whiteflower honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional medicine.
