Lupinus excubitus var. medius

Lupinus excubitus var. medius, commonly known as the "Canyon lupine," is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus excubitus var. medius is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
