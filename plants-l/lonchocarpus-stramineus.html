Lonchocarpus stramineus

Lonchocarpus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow arrowroot or sand arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive, spindle-shaped fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
