Leptatherum somae

Leptatherum somae, commonly known as the Hungarian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Leptatherum somae plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
