Laurus retroflexa

Laurus retroflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, narrow, and leathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It typically grows in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Laurus retroflexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
