Lomatium macrocarpum var. douglasii

Lomatium macrocarpum var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, inflated fruit that aids in wind dispersal.

Lomatium macrocarpum var. douglasii is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use by indigenous peoples.
