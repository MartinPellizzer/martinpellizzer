Lobelia cinerea

Lobelia cinerea, commonly known as gray lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, downy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Lobelia cinerea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
