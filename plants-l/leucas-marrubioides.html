Leucas marrubioides

Leucas marrubioides, commonly known as the African verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Leucas marrubioides is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in African herbal medicine.
