Luzula flavescens var. murrea

Luzula flavescens var. murrea, commonly known as the golden woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a slight yellowish tinge on the underside. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green to yellowish in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
