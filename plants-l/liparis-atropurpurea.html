Liparis atropurpurea

Liparis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, creeping rhizome and produces striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers are borne on erect spikes.

Liparis atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
