Lavallea

Lavallea, commonly known as the "Cape Honeysuckle," is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. Lavallea species typically grow as shrubs or climbers, featuring opposite, simple leaves with a rough texture. They are often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
