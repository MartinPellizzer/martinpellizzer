Lippia hirsuta

Lippia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands or along roadsides. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lippia hirsuta is also valued as a source of essential oils due to its strong, spicy scent.
