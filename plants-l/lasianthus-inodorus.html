Lasianthus inodorus

Lasianthus inodorus, commonly known as the white-flowered lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with prominent stamens, which are often borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
