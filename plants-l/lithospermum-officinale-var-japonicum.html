Lithospermum officinale var. japonicum

Lithospermum officinale var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese Lithospermum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces vibrant red to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds like lithospermic acid, which have been used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display and hardy nature.
