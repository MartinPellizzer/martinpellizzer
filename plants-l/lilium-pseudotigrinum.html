Lilium pseudotigrinum

Lilium pseudotigrinum, commonly known as the tiger lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, orange-red flowers with dark red veins, resembling the pattern of a tiger's stripes, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in moist meadows and woodlands.

Lilium pseudotigrinum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
