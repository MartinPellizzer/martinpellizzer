Lobelia piscinula

Lobelia piscinula, commonly known as the water lobelia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to thrive in saturated soils.

Lobelia piscinula is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
