Lilium maculatum var. bukosanense

Lilium maculatum var. bukosanense, commonly known as the Bukosanense Lily, is a rare and ornamental variety of the lily family.

It is characterized by its striking, often bicolor flowers with a distinctive red or pink blotch on the outer petals. Native to Japan, this plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers partial shade and well-drained soil. The foliage consists of long, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic touch to gardens with its elegant and showy appearance.
