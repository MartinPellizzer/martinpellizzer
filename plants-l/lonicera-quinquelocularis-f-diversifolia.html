Lonicera quinquelocularis f. diversifolia

Lonicera quinquelocularis f. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated honeysuckle, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a distinctive variegation pattern of white and green, adding visual interest to the plant. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

This form is a hybrid variant of Lonicera quinquelocularis, showcasing unique foliage characteristics.
