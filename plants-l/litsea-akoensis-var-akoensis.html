Litsea akoensis var. akoensis

Litsea akoensis var. akoensis, commonly known as the Akoensis Laurier, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of various bioactive compounds.
