Lupinaster ecklonianus

Lupinaster ecklonianus, commonly known as the Cape lupin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It features compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple and white.

Lupinaster ecklonianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
