Linaria tristis var. tenuifolia

Linaria tristis var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
