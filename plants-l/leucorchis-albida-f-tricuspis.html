Leucorchis albida f. tricuspis

Leucorchis albida f. tricuspis, commonly known as the tricuspis orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique tricuspate (three-lobed) petals, which set it apart from other members of the Leucorchis genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in a spike, and it blooms during the wet season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
