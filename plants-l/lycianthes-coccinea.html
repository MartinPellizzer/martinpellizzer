Lycianthes coccinea

Lycianthes coccinea, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The fruit develops from the flower and is a key feature in its ecological and horticultural significance.
