Litsea machilifolia

Litsea machilifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that give way to small, round, orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a fragrant, spicy aroma when crushed.

Litsea machilifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
