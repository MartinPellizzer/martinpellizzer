Leucanthemum yezoense

Leucanthemum yezoense, commonly known as the Yezo daisy, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers surrounded by narrow, greenish-brown ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Leucanthemum yezoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
