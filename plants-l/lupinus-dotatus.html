Lupinus dotatus

Lupinus dotatus, commonly known as the spotted lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, rounded leaflets that are often dotted with white or pale spots. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
