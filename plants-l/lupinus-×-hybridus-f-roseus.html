Lupinus × hybridus f. roseus

Lupinus × hybridus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered lupine, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple rose-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with palmately compound leaves composed of 5–7 oval-shaped leaflets. Native to the western United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations, contributing to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
