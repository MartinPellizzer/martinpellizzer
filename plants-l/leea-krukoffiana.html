Leea krukoffiana

Leea krukoffiana, commonly known as the Krukoff's Leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and are clustered in small inflorescences.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
