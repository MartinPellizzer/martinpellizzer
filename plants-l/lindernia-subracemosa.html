Lindernia subracemosa

Lindernia subracemosa, commonly known as the slender lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, violet to purple blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lindernia subracemosa is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought once established.
