Lonicera acuminata var. depilata

Lonicera acuminata var. depilata, commonly known as the hairless honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairless leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late spring, typically yellow or white, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
