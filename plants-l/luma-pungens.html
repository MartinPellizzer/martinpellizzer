Luma pungens

Luma pungens, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to southern Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.

Luma pungens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
