Leandra cordigera

Leandra cordigera, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate.

Leandra cordigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
