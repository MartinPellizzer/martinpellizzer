Lasiococca comberi var. pseudoverticillata

Lasiococca comberi var. pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the woolly false verticillate, is a shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique texture and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
