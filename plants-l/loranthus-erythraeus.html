Loranthus erythraeus

Loranthus erythraeus, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, red berries that contain toxic seeds. It plays a role in ecological systems by supporting certain bird species that consume its berries.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
