Lobivia brunneorosea

Lobivia brunneorosea, commonly known as the brown rose lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded stems that are often covered in a powdery, waxy coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color and are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Lobivia brunneorosea is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
