Lonicera caprifolium var. italica

Lonicera caprifolium var. italica, commonly known as the Italian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
