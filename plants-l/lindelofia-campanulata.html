Lindelofia campanulata

Lindelofia campanulata, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lindelofia campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
