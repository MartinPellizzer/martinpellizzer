Lilium japonicum var. angustifolium

Lilium japonicum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Japanese lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, often with a slight blush. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
