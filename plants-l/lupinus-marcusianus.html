Lupinus marcusianus

Lupinus marcusianus, commonly known as the Marcusian lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Lupinus marcusianus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
