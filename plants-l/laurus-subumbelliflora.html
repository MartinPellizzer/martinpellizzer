Laurus subumbelliflora

Laurus subumbelliflora, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to dark blue as it matures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its aromatic properties.
