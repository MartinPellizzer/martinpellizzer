Leptormus trifidus

Leptormus trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three distinct lobes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent-like leaves.
