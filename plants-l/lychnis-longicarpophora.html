Lychnis longicarpophora

Lychnis longicarpophora, commonly known as the long-fruited catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that remain attached to the stem, giving it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Lychnis longicarpophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
