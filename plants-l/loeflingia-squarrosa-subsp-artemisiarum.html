Loeflingia squarrosa subsp. artemisiarum

Loeflingia squarrosa subsp.

artemisiarum, commonly known as the Artemisia-like loeflingia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
