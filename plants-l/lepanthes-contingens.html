Lepanthes contingens

Lepanthes contingens, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale purple blooms that are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes contingens is often found in shaded, humid environments within forest understories.
