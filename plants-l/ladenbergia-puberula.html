Ladenbergia puberula

Ladenbergia puberula, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
