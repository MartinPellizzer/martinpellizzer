Luina piperi

Luina piperi, commonly known as the pepper plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers. Its fruit, a bright red, berry-like structure, contains several seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.

Luina piperi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowers.
