Lablab martinicensis

Lablab martinicensis, commonly known as the purple bean or Martinique bean, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and climbing habit. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its growth thrives in warm, humid climates and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental and edible crop in tropical regions.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
