Lythrum hyssopifolia var. nummulariifolium

Lythrum hyssopifolia var. nummulariifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf lythrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of showy pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
