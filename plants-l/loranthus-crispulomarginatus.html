Loranthus crispulomarginatus

Loranthus crispulomarginatus, commonly known as the rough-fringed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to parts of Africa and is often found growing on acacia and other woody species. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and health of its host trees.
