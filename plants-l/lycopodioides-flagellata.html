Lycopodioides flagellata

Lycopodioides flagellata, commonly known as the flagellate clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and exhibits typical characteristics of early vascular plants.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along streams. The plant has a distinct stem with whorled leaves that are needle-like in appearance. Its reproductive structures include sporophylls that bear microsporangia and megasporangia, facilitating spore production.

Lycopodioides flagellata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
