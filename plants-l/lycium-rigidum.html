Lycium rigidum

Lycium rigidum, commonly known as the Chilean wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It produces small, bright orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, saline soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Lycium rigidum is also cultivated for its edible fruits, which are popular in various cuisines and health supplements.
