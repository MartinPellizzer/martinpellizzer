Limonium paradoxum var. mutabile

Limonium paradoxum var. mutabile, commonly known as the variable sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spire-like inflorescences that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The varietal name "mutabile" reflects the plant's tendency to exhibit variations in flower color and form, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique ornamental plants.
