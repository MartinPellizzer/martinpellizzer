Lepidophyllum abietinum

Lepidophyllum abietinum, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with a dense covering of silver-gray, overlapping scales that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptations make it well-suited for xerophytic habitats.
