Ligustrum microphyllum

Ligustrum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum microphyllum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
