Libertia micrantha

Libertia micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved libertia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale blue. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Libertia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
