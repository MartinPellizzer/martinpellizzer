Lepisorus novoguineensis

Lepisorus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its creeping rhizome and dense, clustered fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

Lepisorus novoguineensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
