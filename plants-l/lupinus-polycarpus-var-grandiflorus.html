Lupinus polycarpus var. grandiflorus

Lupinus polycarpus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorus lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
