Lepicystis polypodioides var. ecklonii

Lepicystis polypodioides var. ecklonii, commonly known as the Ecklon's rock fern, is a perennial fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
