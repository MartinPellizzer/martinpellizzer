Lellingeria longeattenuata

Lellingeria longeattenuata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, attenuated stems that often trail along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet and blue, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lellingeria longeattenuata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.
