Leptadenia lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Leptadenia lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
