Lepanthes fimbriatilabia

Lepanthes fimbriatilabia, commonly known as the fringed-labia orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with intricate petal structures. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a rosette, while the flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes fimbriatilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
