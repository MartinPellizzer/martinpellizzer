Lagochilium

Lagochilium, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often purple or violet-colored flowers that resemble small, ghostly figures. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and aromatic, while the flowers are borne in dense, spike-like clusters.

Lagochilium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
