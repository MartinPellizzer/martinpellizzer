Lychnis saxatilis

Lychnis saxatilis, commonly known as the rock campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features simple, basal leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Lychnis saxatilis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
