Lonchocarpus stenophyllus

Lonchocarpus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia. Its seed pods are long and cylindrical, containing several seeds enclosed in a hard, woody shell.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
