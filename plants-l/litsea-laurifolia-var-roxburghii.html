Litsea laurifolia var. roxburghii

Litsea laurifolia var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian laurier or roxburgh's litsea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, orange-red berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
