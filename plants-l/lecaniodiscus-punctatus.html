Lecaniodiscus punctatus

Lecaniodiscus punctatus, commonly known as the punctate lecanium, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Coccidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, shield-like body covered in a waxy coating. The plant it infests is typically a host tree, where it feeds on sap, causing potential damage to the host's growth. Its body is often marked with small, punctate spots, giving it its scientific name.

This insect is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid environments.
