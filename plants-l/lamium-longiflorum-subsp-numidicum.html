Lamium longiflorum subsp. numidicum

Lamium longiflorum subsp.

numidicum, commonly known as the Numidian deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
