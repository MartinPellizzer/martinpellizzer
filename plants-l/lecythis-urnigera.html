Lecythis urnigera

Lecythis urnigera, commonly known as the golden cup or urn tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, woody, urn-shaped fruits that contain a single, edible seed. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy, making it a significant component of rainforest ecosystems. Its leaves are typically large and palmately lobed, and it produces showy yellow flowers.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
