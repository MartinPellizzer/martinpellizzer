Leptospermum arachnoideum

Leptospermum arachnoideum, commonly known as the silky tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or sandy environments.

Its leaves are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and have aromatic properties.
