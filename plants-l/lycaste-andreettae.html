Lycaste andreettae

Lycaste andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's lycaste, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming a compact clump with pseudobulbs and fleshy roots. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a critically endangered species.
