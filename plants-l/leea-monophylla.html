Leea monophylla

Leea monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf leeah, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its unique morphology, including a single leaf that is often large and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is typically found in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
