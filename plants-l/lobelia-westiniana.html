Lobelia westiniana

Lobelia westiniana, commonly known as the western lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Lobelia westiniana plays an important role in supporting local pollinators like bees and butterflies.
