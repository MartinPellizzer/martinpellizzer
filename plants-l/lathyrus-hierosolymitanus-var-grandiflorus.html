Lathyrus hierosolymitanus var. grandiflorus

Lathyrus hierosolymitanus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Jerusalem glory flower, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
