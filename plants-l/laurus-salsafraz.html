Laurus salsafraz

Laurus salsafraz, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are valued for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.
