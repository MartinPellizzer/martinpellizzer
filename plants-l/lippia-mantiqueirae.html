Lippia mantiqueirae

Lippia mantiqueirae, commonly known as Brazilian thyme, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, valued for their strong, spicy scent.

Lippia mantiqueirae is also appreciated for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
