Labatia laevigata

Labatia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth labatia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to release the seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Fabaceae family.

Labatia laevigata is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
