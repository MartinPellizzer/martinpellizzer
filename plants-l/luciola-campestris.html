Luciola campestris

Luciola campestris, commonly known as the field firefly, is a species of bioluminescent beetle belonging to the family Lampyridae.

It is characterized by its elongated body and the ability to produce light through specialized organs called lucifers. The light emitted is typically a yellow or greenish glow, used primarily for attracting mates. This species is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its life cycle includes aquatic larvae that develop in moist soil or temporary pools.
