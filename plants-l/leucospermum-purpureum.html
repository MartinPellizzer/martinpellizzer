Leucospermum purpureum

Leucospermum purpureum, commonly known as the king protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive white center, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
