Lycopus europaeus var. mollis

Lycopus europaeus var. mollis, commonly known as soft hedgehogberry, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

The fruit is a small, dark blue berry that ripens in late summer and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
