Lepturidium insulare

Lepturidium insulare, commonly known as the island leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle found primarily in island ecosystems.

It belongs to the family Chrysomelidae and is characterized by its small, elongated body and metallic coloration. The plant it inhabits is typically a shrub or herbaceous plant, often found in coastal or humid environments. Its life cycle includes a larval stage that feeds on leaves, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Due to its restricted range, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
