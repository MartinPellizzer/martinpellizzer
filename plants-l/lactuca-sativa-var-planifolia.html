Lactuca sativa var. planifolia

Lactuca sativa var. planifolia, commonly known as romaine lettuce, is a cultivar of lettuce characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, crisp leaves.

It is botanically classified as a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other flowering plants. The leaves are typically light green, with a slightly bitter taste compared to other lettuce varieties.

It is often used in salads and is valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins A and K. This variety is well-suited for cooler climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
