Lupinus amandus

Lupinus amandus, commonly known as the silver lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Lupinus amandus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to improve soil fertility.
