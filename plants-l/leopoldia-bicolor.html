Leopoldia bicolor

Leopoldia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors, with green and white petals that create a visually appealing display. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
