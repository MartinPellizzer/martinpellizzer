Lobelia tortuosa

Lobelia tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia tortuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
