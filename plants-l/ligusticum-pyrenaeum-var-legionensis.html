Ligusticum pyrenaeum var. legionensis

Ligusticum pyrenaeum var. legionensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
