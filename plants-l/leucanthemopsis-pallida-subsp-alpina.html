Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp. alpina

Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
