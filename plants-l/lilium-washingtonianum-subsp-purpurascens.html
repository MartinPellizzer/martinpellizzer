Lilium washingtonianum subsp. purpurascens

Lilium washingtonianum subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the Washington lily, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
