Lathyrus japonicus

Lathyrus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese vetch, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its seeds are encased in tough pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus japonicus is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
