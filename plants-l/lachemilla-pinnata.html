Lachemilla pinnata

Lachemilla pinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features a mat-forming habit with silver-gray foliage that reflects its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Lachemilla pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry, rocky environments.
