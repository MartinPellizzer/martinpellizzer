Liparis layardii var. layardii

Liparis layardii var. layardii, commonly known as the Layard's orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
