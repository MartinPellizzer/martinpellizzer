Lepidium oleraceum var. frondosum

Lepidium oleraceum var. frondosum, commonly known as the leafy cress, is a variant of the wild cress species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual plant with small, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are tiny and have a peppery flavor, making it a useful addition to salads and culinary dishes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.
