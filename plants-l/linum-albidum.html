Linum albidum

Linum albidum, commonly known as white flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linum albidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
