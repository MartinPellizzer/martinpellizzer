Lamium persicum

Lamium persicum, commonly known as Persian nettle or Persian deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium persicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
