Lirianthe annamensis

Lirianthe annamensis, commonly known as the Annam lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Iridaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features strap-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Lirianthe annamensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
