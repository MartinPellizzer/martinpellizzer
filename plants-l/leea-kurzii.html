Leea kurzii

Leea kurzii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Leeaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.
