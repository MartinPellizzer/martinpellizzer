Lycopus charkeviczii

Lycopus charkeviczii, commonly known as Charkevicz's bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and it produces small, oval berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Lycopus charkeviczii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
