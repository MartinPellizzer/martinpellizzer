Loranthus crassipes

Loranthus crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its roots.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in dispersal by birds. It is found in various parts of Africa and is often seen growing on acacia and eucalyptus trees.

Its parasitic nature means it draws water and nutrients from its host, though it can also photosynthesize to some extent.
