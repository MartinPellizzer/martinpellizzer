Linaria melanantha var. atrofusca

Linaria melanantha var. atrofusca, commonly known as the dark-flowered antelope's tongue, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to black, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
