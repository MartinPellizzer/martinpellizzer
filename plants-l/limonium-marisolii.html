Limonium marisolii

Limonium marisolii, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Limonium marisolii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
