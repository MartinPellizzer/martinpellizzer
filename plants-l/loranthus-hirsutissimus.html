Loranthus hirsutissimus

Loranthus hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
