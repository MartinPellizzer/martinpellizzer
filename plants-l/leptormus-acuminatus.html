Leptormus acuminatus

Leptormus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The orchid has a relatively short lifespan, with many individuals flowering and dying within a few years.
