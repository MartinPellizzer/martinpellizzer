Lassia

Lassia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Lassia is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.

It is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
