Loranthus ugogensis

Loranthus ugogensis, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the savanna regions of Africa.

It has a succulent, leafy stem that produces small, white or yellowish flowers, followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other animals. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. It plays a significant ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to nutrient cycling.

Its presence often indicates a healthy ecosystem, as it relies on specific host species for survival.
