Luzula spicata subsp.

saximontana, commonly known as the saximontan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, high-altitude environments.
