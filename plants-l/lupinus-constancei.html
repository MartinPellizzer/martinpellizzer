Lupinus constancei

Lupinus constancei, commonly known as the Constant's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus constancei is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.
