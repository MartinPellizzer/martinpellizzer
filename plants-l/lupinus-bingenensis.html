Lupinus bingenensis

Lupinus bingenensis, commonly known as the Bingen lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

The Bingen lupine is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
