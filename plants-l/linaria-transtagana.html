Linaria transtagana

Linaria transtagana, commonly known as the Transcaucasian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the genus Linaria. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Linaria transtagana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
