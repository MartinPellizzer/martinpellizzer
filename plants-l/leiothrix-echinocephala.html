Leiothrix echinocephala

Leiothrix echinocephala, commonly known as the spiny-headed fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit. The plant features intricate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive spiny appearance on the underside. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
