Licuala glabra

Licuala glabra, commonly known as the smooth licuala, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are smooth and often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in wet tropical environments, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its trunk is slender and often covered with a fibrous sheath, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries.

The plant has cultural and practical significance in local communities for its versatile leaves and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
