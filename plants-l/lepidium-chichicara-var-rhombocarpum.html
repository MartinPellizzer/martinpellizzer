Lepidium chichicara var. rhombocarpum

Lepidium chichicara var. rhombocarpum, commonly known as the Mexican rocket, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local herbal remedies.
