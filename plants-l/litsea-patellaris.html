Litsea patellaris

Litsea patellaris, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, dark brown seeds.

Litsea patellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
