Linochilus juliani

Linochilus juliani, commonly known as the Julian's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its arid or semi-arid habitat. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to the environmental conditions of its native range.

Linochilus juliani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
