Lythrum bibracteatum

Lythrum bibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Lythrum bibracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
