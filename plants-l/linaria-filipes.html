Linaria filipes

Linaria filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and delicate, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
