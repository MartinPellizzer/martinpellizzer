Lepanthes minima

Lepanthes minima, commonly known as the miniature orchid or dwarf orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tiny, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant grows on tree branches or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its roots are specialized for absorbing humidity and nutrients from its surroundings.

Lepanthes minima is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
