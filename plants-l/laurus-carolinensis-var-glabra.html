Laurus carolinensis var. glabra

Laurus carolinensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bay laurel, is a variant of the bay tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by black, egg-shaped berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking, similar to other bay laurel varieties, and have a mild, aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental or for its culinary uses.
