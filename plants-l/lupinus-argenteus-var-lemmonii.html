Lupinus argenteus var. lemmonii

Lupinus argenteus var. lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
