Lepanthes cristata

Lepanthes cristata, commonly known as the cristate orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Lepanthes cristata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
