Lunathyrium lasiopteris

Lunathyrium lasiopteris, commonly known as the woolly leaf fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it a soft, textured appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive arrangement of small, overlapping pinnae.

Lunathyrium lasiopteris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
