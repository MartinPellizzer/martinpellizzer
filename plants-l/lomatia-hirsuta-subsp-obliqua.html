Lomatia hirsuta subsp. obliqua

Lomatia hirsuta subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lomatia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces woody, spindle-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
