Lupinus ione-grisetae

Lupinus ione-grisetae, commonly known as the Ionian gray lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Lupinus ione-grisetae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
