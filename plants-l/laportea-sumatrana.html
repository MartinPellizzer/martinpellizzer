Laportea sumatrana

Laportea sumatrana, commonly known as the Java velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
