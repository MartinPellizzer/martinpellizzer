Lotononis maculata

Lotononis maculata, commonly known as the spotted broom.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive purple or brown spotting on the bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
