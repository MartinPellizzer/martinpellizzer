Landtia hirsuta

Landtia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy landtia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
