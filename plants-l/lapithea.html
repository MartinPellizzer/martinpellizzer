Lapithea

Lapithea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white leaves that contrast beautifully with its dark green stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark contains compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Lapithea thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
