Lotus sharifii

Lotus sharifii, commonly known as the Persian lotus, is a species of water lily native to the Caspian Sea region.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, fibrous stems. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lotus sharifii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional Persian gardens.
