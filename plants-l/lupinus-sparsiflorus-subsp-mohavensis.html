Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp. mohavensis

Lupinus sparsiflorus subsp.

mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils in desert regions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and scrublands.
