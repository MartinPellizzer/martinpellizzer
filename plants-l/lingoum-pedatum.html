Lingoum pedatum

Lingoum pedatum, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with prominent spurred petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Lingoum pedatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
