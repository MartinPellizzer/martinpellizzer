Littonia littonioides

Littonia littonioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf littonia, is a rare and unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires a cool, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Littonia littonioides is considered a species of conservation concern.
