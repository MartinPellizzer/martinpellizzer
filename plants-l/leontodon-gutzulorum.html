Leontodon gutzulorum

Leontodon gutzulorum, commonly known as the Gutzlaff's toothbrush grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small and yellow, with five petals, and are arranged in a dense cluster.

Leontodon gutzulorum is characterized by its robust root system and is often found in well-drained soils.
