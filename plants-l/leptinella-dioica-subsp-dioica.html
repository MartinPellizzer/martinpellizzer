Leptinella dioica subsp. dioica

Leptinella dioica subsp.

dioica, commonly known as the Alpine penny-cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous environments.

The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.
