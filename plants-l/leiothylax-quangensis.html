Leiothylax quangensis

Leiothylax quangensis, commonly known as the Quangensis orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it relies on specific symbiotic fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
