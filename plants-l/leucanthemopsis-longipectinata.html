Leucanthemopsis longipectinata

Leucanthemopsis longipectinata, commonly known as the longleaf oxeye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy white to pale yellow flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are usually woody at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
