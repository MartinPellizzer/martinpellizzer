Ligustrum japonicum var. alivonii

Ligustrum japonicum var. alivonii, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Ligustrum genus, which are known for their adaptability and use in landscaping.
