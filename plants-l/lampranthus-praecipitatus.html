Lampranthus praecipitatus

Lampranthus praecipitatus, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its succulent characteristics and vibrant floral displays. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling tiny firecrackers, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lampranthus praecipitatus is also noted for its ability to bloom profusely during the summer months.
