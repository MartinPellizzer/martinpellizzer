Lotus corniculatus subsp. afghanicus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

afghanicus, commonly known as the Afghan horned vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
