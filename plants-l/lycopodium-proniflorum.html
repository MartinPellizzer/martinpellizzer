Lycopodium proniflorum

Lycopodium proniflorum, commonly known as the tropical clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branching stems. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, slender stems and bright green foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lycopodium proniflorum plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
