Limonium virgatum subsp. divaricatum

Limonium virgatum subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the spreading sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are borne in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
