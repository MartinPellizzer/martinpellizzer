Linum alpinum proles saxicola

Linum alpinum proles saxicola, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous environments where it thrives in full sun. Its fibrous stalks and leaves are adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

Linum alpinum proles saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
