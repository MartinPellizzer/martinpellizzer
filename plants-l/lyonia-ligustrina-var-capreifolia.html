Lyonia ligustrina var. capreifolia

Lyonia ligustrina var. capreifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf or caprifolium, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly wavy margin. It thrives in partial to full shade and is commonly found in moist, wooded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
