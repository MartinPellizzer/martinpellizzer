Leptolobium tabacinum

Leptolobium tabacinum, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seed pods that release seeds through a mechanism similar to that of a burr. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
