Lepanthes gnoma

Lepanthes gnoma, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers, which often feature a deep red or purple lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes gnoma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
