Linum heterosepalum subsp. olgae

Linum heterosepalum subsp.

olgae, commonly known as the Olgae flax, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Socotra. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary uniqueness.
