Lingoum violaceum

Lingoum violaceum, commonly known as the purple tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Lingoum violaceum is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
