Leucadendron spirale

Leucadendron spirale, commonly known as the spiral leucadendron, is a striking South African shrub native to the Western Cape region.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-arranged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flower heads, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Leucadendron spirale is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is a symbol of South Africa's rich biodiversity.
