Lepanthes oxypetala

Lepanthes oxypetala, commonly known as the sharp-petalled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink coloration with contrasting purple or violet markings. The plant grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes oxypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
