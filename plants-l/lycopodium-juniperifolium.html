Lycopodium juniperifolium

Lycopodium juniperifolium, commonly known as the juniper-leaved clubmoss, is a species of lycopod that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that resemble those of juniper, giving it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. It produces spores in cone-like structures called strobila, which are arranged in clusters along the stems.
