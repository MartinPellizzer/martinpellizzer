Lonicera trichogyne

Lonicera trichogyne, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several small seeds.

Lonicera trichogyne is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
