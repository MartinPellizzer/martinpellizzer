Lithocarpus confragosus

Lithocarpus confragosus, commonly known as the fragrant ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts.

It is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, hard wood and leathery, elliptical leaves that remain on the tree for several years. Its fruit is a nut enclosed in a woody cupule, which is typical of the genus Lithocarpus.

This species plays an important role in the tropical rainforest ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
