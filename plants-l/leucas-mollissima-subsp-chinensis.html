Leucas mollissima subsp. chinensis

Leucas mollissima subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as Chinese leucas, is a perennial herb native to China and neighboring regions. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.
