Lantana cujabensis var. hispida

Lantana cujabensis var. hispida, commonly known as the rough lantana, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. It is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in full sun.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
