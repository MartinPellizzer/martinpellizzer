Lobelia coronopifolia var. macularis

Lobelia coronopifolia var. macularis, commonly known as the spotted lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers and distinctive spotted leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often marked with red or purple spots, adding visual interest to its appearance.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
