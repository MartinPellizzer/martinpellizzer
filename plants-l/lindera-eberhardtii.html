Lindera eberhardtii

Lindera eberhardtii, commonly known as Eberhardt's spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
