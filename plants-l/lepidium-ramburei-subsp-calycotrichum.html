Lepidium ramburei subsp. calycotrichum

Lepidium ramburei subsp.

calycotrichum, commonly known as the desert rocket, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Lepidium ramburei subsp.
