Lobivia haematantha f. scoparia

Lobivia haematantha f. scoparia, commonly known as the Red-veined Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant features bright red or pinkish veins on its pale green or blue-gray foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique aesthetics, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
