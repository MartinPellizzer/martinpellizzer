Litsea vang

Litsea vang, commonly known as the Vietnamese litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Litsea vang is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
