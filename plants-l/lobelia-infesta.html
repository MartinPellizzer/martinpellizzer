Lobelia infesta

Lobelia infesta, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lobelia infesta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
