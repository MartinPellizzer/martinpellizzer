Lilium chinense

Lilium chinense, commonly known as the Chinese lily, is a species native to China and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The bulbs of Lilium chinense are also used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
