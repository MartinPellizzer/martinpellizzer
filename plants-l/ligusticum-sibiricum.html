Ligusticum sibiricum

Ligusticum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian lovage, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The roots of Ligusticum sibiricum are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
