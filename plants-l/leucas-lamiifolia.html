Leucas lamiifolia

Leucas lamiifolia, commonly known as the blue heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
