Lellingeria apiculata

Lellingeria apiculata, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Lellingeria apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
