Lonchocarpus spruceanus

Lonchocarpus spruceanus, commonly known as the purple tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive purple-pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
