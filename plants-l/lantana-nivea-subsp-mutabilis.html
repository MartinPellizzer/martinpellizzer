Lantana nivea subsp. mutabilis

Lantana nivea subsp.

mutabilis, commonly known as the white lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in summer.

The plant has aromatic, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and is considered a weed in certain ecosystems.
