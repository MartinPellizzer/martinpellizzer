Limnophila hirsuta

Limnophila hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

Limnophila hirsuta is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.
