Lilium × wallacei

Lilium × wallacei, commonly known as the Wallace's lily, is a hybrid species within the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Native to the mountainous regions of Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The leaves are long and narrow, often forming a basal rosette, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its beauty and fragrance.
