Leichhardtia cymulosa

Leichhardtia cymulosa, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower heads that are often arranged in clusters, giving it its species name "cymulosa.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Leichhardtia cymulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
