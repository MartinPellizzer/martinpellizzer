Lepidosperma flexuosum

Lepidosperma flexuosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as swampy areas and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

It produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually inconspicuous, typical of many Cyperaceae species.
