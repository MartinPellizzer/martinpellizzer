Lepidagathis ringoetii

Lepidagathis ringoetii, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, star-shaped inflorescences that resemble a Christmas star, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically from late autumn to early spring. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lepidagathis ringoetii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
