Lupinus whiltoniae

Lupinus whiltoniae, commonly known as the Whilton's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus whiltoniae is adapted to poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
