Lycopodium complanatum subsp. zeilleri

Lycopodium complanatum subsp.

zeilleri, commonly known as the narrowleaf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the southern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of the branches.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and is often found in acidic, well-drained soils.
