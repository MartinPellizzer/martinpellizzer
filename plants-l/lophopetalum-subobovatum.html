Lophopetalum subobovatum

Lophopetalum subobovatum, commonly known as the Texas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities in its range.
