Leontodon cantabricus

Leontodon cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian leontodon, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic plant in certain regions of Spain.
