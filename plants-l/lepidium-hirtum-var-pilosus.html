Lepidium hirtum var. pilosus

Lepidium hirtum var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense covering of fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
