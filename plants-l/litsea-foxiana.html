Litsea foxiana

Litsea foxiana, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe with a single seed, and it is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Litsea foxiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
