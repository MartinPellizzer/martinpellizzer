Linociera parkinsonii

Linociera parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's linociera, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its bark contains latex and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Linociera parkinsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
