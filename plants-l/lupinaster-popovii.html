Lupinaster popovii

Lupinaster popovii, commonly known as the Popov's lupin, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinaster popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
