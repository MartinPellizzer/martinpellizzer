Lepidobolus preissianus subsp. preissianus

Lepidobolus preissianus subsp.

preissianus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia. It is a small annual herb with distinctive, yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert areas.
