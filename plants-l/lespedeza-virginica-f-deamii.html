Lespedeza virginica f. deamii

Lespedeza virginica f. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's Lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are alternate and have a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Lespedeza virginica f. deamii is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wet meadows.
