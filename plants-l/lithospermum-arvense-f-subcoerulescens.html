Lithospermum arvense f. subcoerulescens

Lithospermum arvense f. subcoerulescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, often blue-tinged stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain alkaloids and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The foliage and flowers are typically covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.
