Lathyrus italicus

Lathyrus italicus, commonly known as the Italian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Lathyrus italicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and can be grown in gardens as a decorative plant.
