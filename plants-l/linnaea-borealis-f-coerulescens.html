Linnaea borealis f. coerulescens

Linnaea borealis f. coerulescens, commonly known as the blueflower twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or bluish-white flowers, which contrast with the typical white or pink hues of the species. This form is native to northern regions of North America and parts of Europe, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
