Loranthus globifer var. bornuensis

Loranthus globifer var. bornuensis, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the savanna regions of Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to birds for dispersal. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various bird species.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
