Lobivia ancistrophora

Lobivia ancistrophora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure with ribbed stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Lobivia ancistrophora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
