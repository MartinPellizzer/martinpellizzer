Linaria supina subsp. aeruginea

Linaria supina subsp.

aeruginea, commonly known as the silverweed or silver linaria, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
