Loeselia glandulosa

Loeselia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular loeselia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy stems that bear small, glandular structures. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Loeselia glandulosa is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
