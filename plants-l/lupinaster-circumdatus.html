Lupinaster circumdatus

Lupinaster circumdatus, commonly known as the Southern Lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
