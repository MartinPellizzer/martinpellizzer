Laelia × elegans var. andersonii

Laelia × elegans var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's Laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the high altitudes of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to its rocky, well-drained habitat.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in moderate light conditions.
