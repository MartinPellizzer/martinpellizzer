Leontodon taraxacum var. runcinatum

Leontodon taraxacum var. runcinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with a wavy margin and a basal rosette growth habit. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This variant is notable for its slender leaves and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
