Leptochloa panicoides

Leptochloa panicoides, commonly known as the panicled leptochloa, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence is a paniculate structure, consisting of multiple spikelets arranged in a loose, branched pattern.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Poaceae family.
