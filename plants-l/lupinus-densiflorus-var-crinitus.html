Lupinus densiflorus var. crinitus

Lupinus densiflorus var. crinitus, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, hairy leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Lupinus densiflorus var. crinitus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
