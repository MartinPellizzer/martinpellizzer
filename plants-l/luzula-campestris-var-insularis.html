Luzula campestris var. insularis

Luzula campestris var. insularis, commonly known as the island false owl's eye, is a flowering plant native to certain parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
