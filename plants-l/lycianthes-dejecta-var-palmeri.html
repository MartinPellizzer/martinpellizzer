Lycianthes dejecta var. palmeri

Lycianthes dejecta var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's bitterleaf, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, greenish-yellow fruits. Its leaves are usually ovate with a rough texture and often have a bitter taste.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
