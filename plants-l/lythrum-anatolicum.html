Lythrum anatolicum

Lythrum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lythrum anatolicum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
