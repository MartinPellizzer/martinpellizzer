Listrostachys subclavata

Listrostachys subclavata, commonly known as the narrow-clawed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is common in its native habitat.
