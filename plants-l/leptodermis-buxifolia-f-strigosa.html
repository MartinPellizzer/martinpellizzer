Leptodermis buxifolia f. strigosa

Leptodermis buxifolia f. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy boxwood, is a variant of the boxwood plant characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or ornamental plant in gardens.

The leaves are dark green and remain throughout the year, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
