Leontodon × jouffroyi

Leontodon × jouffroyi, commonly known as the hybrid tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
