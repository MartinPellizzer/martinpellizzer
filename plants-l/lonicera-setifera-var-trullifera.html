Lonicera setifera var. trullifera

Lonicera setifera var. trullifera, commonly known as the trailing honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often trail or climb, making it suitable for ground cover or trellises. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically appearing in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface with serrated edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
