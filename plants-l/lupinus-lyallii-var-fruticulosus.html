Lupinus lyallii var. fruticulosus

Lupinus lyallii var. fruticulosus, commonly known as the Lyall's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus lyallii var. fruticulosus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
