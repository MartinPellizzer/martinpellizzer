Loranthus demesae

Loranthus demesae, commonly known as the desert mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in arid and semi-arid regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through limited photosynthesis. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Loranthus demesae plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
