Lachnagrostis richardii

Lachnagrostis richardii, commonly known as Richard's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, awned florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Lachnagrostis richardii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
