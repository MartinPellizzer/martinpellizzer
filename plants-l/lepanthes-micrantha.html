Lepanthes micrantha

Lepanthes micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure, typical of the genus Lepanthes. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a rosette, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Lepanthes micrantha is valued for its unique floral morphology and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
