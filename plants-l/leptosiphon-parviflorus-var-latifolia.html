Leptosiphon parviflorus var. latifolia

Leptosiphon parviflorus var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blooming period occurs in the spring, adding a splash of color to its native habitat.

The variety latifolia is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of the species.
