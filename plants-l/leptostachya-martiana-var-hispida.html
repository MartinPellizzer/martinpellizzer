Leptostachya martiana var. hispida

Leptostachya martiana var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy lepia, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance, with hairy stems and leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
