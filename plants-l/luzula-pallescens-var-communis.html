Luzula pallescens var. communis

Luzula pallescens var. communis, commonly known as the common gentian grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months. This variety is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas and is often associated with shaded environments. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
