Linum squamulosum subsp. squamulosum

Linum squamulosum subsp.

squamulosum, commonly known as the squamulous flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Linaceae family and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its fibrous stalks were historically used for textile production, similar to other flax species.
