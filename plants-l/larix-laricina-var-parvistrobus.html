Larix laricina var. parvistrobus

Larix laricina var. parvistrobus, commonly known as the dwarf larch or mountain larch, is a variety of the tamarack tree.

It is a deciduous conifer native to the northern regions of North America, particularly in boreal and subarctic climates. This plant is characterized by its small, narrow needles and its ability to form dense, compact growth patterns. The cones are typically small and woody, and the tree is adapted to harsh, cold environments.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
