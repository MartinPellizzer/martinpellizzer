Lotus corniculatus var. decumbens

Lotus corniculatus var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping bird's-foot trefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive bird's-foot shape, and its bright yellow flowers bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, often spreading along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
