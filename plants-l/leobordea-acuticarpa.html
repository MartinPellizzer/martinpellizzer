Leobordea acuticarpa

Leobordea acuticarpa, commonly known as the sharp-fruited leobordea, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated, pointed fruits. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
