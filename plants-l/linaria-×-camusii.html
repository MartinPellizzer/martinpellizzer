Linaria × camusii

Linaria × camusii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a cross between Linaria vulgaris and Linaria dalmatica, resulting in a plant with distinctive, often brightly colored, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Linaria × camusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
