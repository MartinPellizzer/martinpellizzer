Lychnis miqueliana f. plena

Lychnis miqueliana f. plena, commonly known as the doubleflowered marsh pink, is a horticultural form of the marsh pink species.

It is characterized by its showy, double flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces clusters of small, pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland gardens and water gardens.
