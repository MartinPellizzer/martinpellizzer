Linum orientale var. pamphylicum

Linum orientale var. pamphylicum, commonly known as the Pamphylian flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used for both ornamental and agricultural purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was cultivated for its fibers and medicinal properties.
