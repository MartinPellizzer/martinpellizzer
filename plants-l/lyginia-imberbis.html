Lyginia imberbis

Lyginia imberbis, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality in its habitat.

Lyginia imberbis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
