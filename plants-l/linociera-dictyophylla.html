Linociera dictyophylla

Linociera dictyophylla, commonly known as the lace-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that give it a lacy appearance, and its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Linociera dictyophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
