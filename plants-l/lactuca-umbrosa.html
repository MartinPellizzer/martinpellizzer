Lactuca umbrosa

Lactuca umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow lettuce, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Lactuca umbrosa is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and contains milky sap, typical of the genus Lactuca.
