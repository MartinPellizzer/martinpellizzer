Lotodes candicans

Lotodes candicans, commonly known as the water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves that are usually green with a waxy surface, and its flowers are typically white or pink, opening in the early morning. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that anchors it in shallow water, allowing it to spread across ponds and lakes. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in water gardens.
