Ledebouria lilacina

Ledebouria lilacina, commonly known as the violet-leaved bulbous plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous base and fleshy, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale violet, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
