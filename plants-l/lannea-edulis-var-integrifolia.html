Lannea edulis var. integrifolia

Lannea edulis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves that are often used for medicinal purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in dry, savannah-like environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its durable timber and traditional uses.
