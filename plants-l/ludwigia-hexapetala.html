Ludwigia hexapetala

Ludwigia hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature six petals, giving it its scientific name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ludwigia hexapetala is also valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
