Linum corymbiferum var. meyeri

Linum corymbiferum var. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's flax, is a cultivar of the flax species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in dense, branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
