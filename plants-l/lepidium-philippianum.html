Lepidium philippianum

Lepidium philippianum, commonly known as the Philippine cress, is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and aromatic plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are small, oval, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

The seeds are enclosed in a siliqua, a type of dry fruit characteristic of the mustard family.
