Liparis puberula

Liparis puberula, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Liparis puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
