Linaria spuria var. calvipes

Linaria spuria var. calvipes, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
