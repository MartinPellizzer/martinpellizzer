Lipocarpha hemisphaerica

Lipocarpha hemisphaerica, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other waterlogged environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish or yellowish in color.

Lipocarpha hemisphaerica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
