Lupinus caudicifer

Lupinus caudicifer, commonly known as the long-stemmed lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Lupinus caudicifer is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
