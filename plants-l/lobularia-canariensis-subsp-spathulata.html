Lobularia canariensis subsp. spathulata

Lobularia canariensis subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as Canary Island lobularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
