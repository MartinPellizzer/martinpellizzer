Lobelia paulista

Lobelia paulista, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Lobelia paulista is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
