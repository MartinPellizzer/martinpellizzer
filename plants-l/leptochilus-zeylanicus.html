Leptochilus zeylanicus

Leptochilus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its shield-shaped rhizome and dense, clustered fronds that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
