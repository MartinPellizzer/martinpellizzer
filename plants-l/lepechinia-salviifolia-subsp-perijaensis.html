Lepechinia salviifolia subsp. perijaensis

Lepechinia salviifolia subsp.

perijaensis, commonly known as the Perijan sage, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
