Lathyrus zionis

Lathyrus zionis, commonly known as the Zion pea, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and delicate, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Lathyrus zionis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant floral display.
