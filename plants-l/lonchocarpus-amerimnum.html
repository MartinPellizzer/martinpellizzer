Lonchocarpus amerimnum

Lonchocarpus amerimnum, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a tropical shrub belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with moist, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some areas for horticultural purposes.
