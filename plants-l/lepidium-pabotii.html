Lepidium pabotii

Lepidium pabotii, commonly known as the Armenian cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a dry, segmented pod that contains several small, rounded seeds.
