Lupinaster chilensis

Lupinaster chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy soils and salty air. It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.

Lupinaster chilensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
