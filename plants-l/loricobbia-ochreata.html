Loricobbia ochreata

Loricobbia ochreata, commonly known as the ochre leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, spiny structure with fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are typically green and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Loricobbia ochreata is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its low maintenance requirements.
