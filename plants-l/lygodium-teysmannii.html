Lygodium teysmannii

Lygodium teysmannii, commonly known as the Chinese climbing fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of aerial roots that help it attach to surfaces. The plant features a rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds, with the latter bearing spore-producing structures called sori. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
