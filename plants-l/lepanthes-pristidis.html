Lepanthes pristidis

Lepanthes pristidis, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several narrow, linear leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the showy, red-tipped flowers that bloom in clusters, often with white or pale pink petals.

Lepanthes pristidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
