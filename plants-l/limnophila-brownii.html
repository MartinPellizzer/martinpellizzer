Limnophila brownii

Limnophila brownii, commonly known as the brown water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its flowers are small and pale, typically blooming in late summer.

Limnophila brownii plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
