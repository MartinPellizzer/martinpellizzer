Lavandula interrupta

Lavandula interrupta, commonly known as the narrowleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Lavandula interrupta is valued for its fragrance and is often used in aromatherapy and as a source of essential oils.
