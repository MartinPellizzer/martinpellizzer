Laportea schomburgkii

Laportea schomburgkii, commonly known as Schomburgk's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
