Linaria karajensis

Linaria karajensis, commonly known as the Karajensia toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Linaria karajensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
