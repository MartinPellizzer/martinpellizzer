Lamellisepalum hildebrandtii

Lamellisepalum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's shield-fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, shield-shaped fronds that are densely covered with minute, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, supporting the growth of its delicate, pinnately divided leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
