Lepidium hirtum subsp. afrum

Lepidium hirtum subsp.

afrum, commonly known as African cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is an annual herb that grows up to 30 cm tall, with slender stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its leaves and seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "hirtum.".
