Lupinus garfieldensis

Lupinus garfieldensis, commonly known as Garfield's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
