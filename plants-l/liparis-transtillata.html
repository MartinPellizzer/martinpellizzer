Liparis transtillata

Liparis transtillata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
