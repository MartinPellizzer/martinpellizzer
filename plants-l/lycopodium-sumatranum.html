Lycopodium sumatranum

Lycopodium sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Lycopodium sumatranum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
