Leptaspis manillensis

Leptaspis manillensis, commonly known as the Manila flatback shell, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Trochidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, conical shell that is typically smooth and glossy, with a whitish or yellowish coloration. The shell has a distinct spiral pattern and is often marked with faint radial lines. This species is found in the Indo-Pacific region, including the Philippines, and prefers rocky substrates in shallow marine environments.

Its diet primarily consists of algae and other organic matter found on the seafloor.
