Linnaea borealis f. smolandica

Linnaea borealis f. smolandica, commonly known as the Solsund Linnaea, is a rare and unique variant of the twinflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-red flowers, which are slightly larger and more showy than those of the typical Linnaea borealis. This form is native to specific regions in Scandinavia, particularly Norway and northern Sweden. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic divergence and ecological niche, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
