Lophopetalum coriaceum

Lophopetalum coriaceum, commonly known as the waxleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, waxy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils.
