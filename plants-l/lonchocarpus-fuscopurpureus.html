Lonchocarpus fuscopurpureus

Lonchocarpus fuscopurpureus, commonly known as purple purpleheart, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers. The plant is notable for its hard, dark wood, which is highly valued in woodworking for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
