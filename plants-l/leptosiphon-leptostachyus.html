Leptosiphon leptostachyus

Leptosiphon leptostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
