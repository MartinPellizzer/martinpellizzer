Lobelia valdiviana

Lobelia valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia valdiviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
