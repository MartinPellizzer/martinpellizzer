Lobelia triquetra var. alba

Lobelia triquetra var. alba, commonly known as white cardinal flower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking tubular, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
