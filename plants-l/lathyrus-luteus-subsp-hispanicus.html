Lathyrus luteus subsp. hispanicus

Lathyrus luteus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and wastelands.

The plant features compound leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in folk medicine.
