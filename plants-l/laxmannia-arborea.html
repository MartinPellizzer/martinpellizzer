Laxmannia arborea

Laxmannia arborea, commonly known as the tree saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base, while the stems are often woody and branched. Laxmannia arborea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
