Ludwigia latifolia

Ludwigia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf Ludwigia, is a flowering plant species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its roots are often submerged, while its stems and leaves emerge above the water surface.

Ludwigia latifolia is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in water gardens and ponds due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
