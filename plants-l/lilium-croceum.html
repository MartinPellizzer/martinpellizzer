Lilium croceum

Lilium croceum, commonly known as the golden lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help it survive harsh winter conditions.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant color and fragrance.
