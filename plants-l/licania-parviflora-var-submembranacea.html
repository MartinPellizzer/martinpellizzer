Licania parviflora var. submembranacea

Licania parviflora var. submembranacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved licania, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with white or pale yellow petals. The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for a variety of wildlife.
