Limonium sanctamargaritense

Limonium sanctamargaritense, commonly known as the Sant Margarita sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sand dunes, and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, maritime environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Limonium sanctamargaritense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
