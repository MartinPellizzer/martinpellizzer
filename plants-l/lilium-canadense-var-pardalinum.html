Lilium canadense var. pardalinum

Lilium canadense var. pardalinum, commonly known as the red-lilac or red-flowered fritillary, is a variant of the Canadian lily.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a bulbous underground stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
