Lythrum salicaria subvar. gracile

Lythrum salicaria subvar. gracile, commonly known as narrowleaf rosebay, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of deep pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, giving it a distinctive, elegant appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
