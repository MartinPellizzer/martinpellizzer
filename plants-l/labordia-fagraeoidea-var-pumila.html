Labordia fagraeoidea var. pumila

Labordia fagraeoidea var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf fagraeoidea, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense growth habit and seasonal flowering.
