Laggera aurita

Laggera aurita, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, fluffy clusters at the tips of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Laggera aurita is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in many ecological studies and horticultural practices.
