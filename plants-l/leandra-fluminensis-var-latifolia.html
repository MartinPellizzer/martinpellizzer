Leandra fluminensis var. latifolia

Leandra fluminensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved leandra, is a tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of dense rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a species of interest for conservation efforts.
