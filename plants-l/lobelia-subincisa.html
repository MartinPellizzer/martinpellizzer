Lobelia subincisa

Lobelia subincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
