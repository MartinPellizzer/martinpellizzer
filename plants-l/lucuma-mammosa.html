Lucuma mammosa

Lucuma mammosa, commonly known as the sweet potato or cushaw, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and are often used in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit includes both herbaceous and semi-woody stems, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

The fruit, which is typically harvested when mature, has a unique flavor and is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.
