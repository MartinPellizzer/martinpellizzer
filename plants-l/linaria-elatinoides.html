Linaria elatinoides

Linaria elatinoides, commonly known as the sand linaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Linaria elatinoides is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
