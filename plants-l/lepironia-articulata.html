Lepironia articulata

Lepironia articulata, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Lepironia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from water. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss. Its roots are aquatic, anchoring it in wetland environments, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form colonies.

Lepironia articulata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
