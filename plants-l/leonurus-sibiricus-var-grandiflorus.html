Leonurus sibiricus var. grandiflorus

Leonurus sibiricus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as Siberian hedgehog or large-flowered leonurus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Russia, and Mongolia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
