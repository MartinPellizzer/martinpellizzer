Lotus leucophyllus

Lotus leucophyllus, commonly known as the white lotus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
