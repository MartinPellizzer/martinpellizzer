Litsea pruinosa

Litsea pruinosa, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red berries. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Litsea pruinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
