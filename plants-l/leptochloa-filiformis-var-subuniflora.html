Leptochloa filiformis var. subuniflora

Leptochloa filiformis var. subuniflora, commonly known as the slender lespedeza, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette or spreading pattern. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in ecological studies and restoration projects.
