Lycium ovalilobum var. sleumeri

Lycium ovalilobum var. sleumeri, commonly known as the Sleumer's boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, orange-red berries that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with lobed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
