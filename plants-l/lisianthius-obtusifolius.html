Lisianthius obtusifolius

Lisianthius obtusifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
