Lavandula canariensis subsp. lancerottensis

Lavandula canariensis subsp.

lancerottensis, commonly known as the Lancerottensis lavender, is a species native to the Canary Islands. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to the island's unique climate.

The plant produces fragrant, spike-like flower clusters that range in color from purple to pale blue. Its leaves are narrow, aromatic, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
