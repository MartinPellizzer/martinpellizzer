Lycopodium uncinatum

Lycopodium uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features wiry, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are cone-like, containing spores that are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodium uncinatum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
