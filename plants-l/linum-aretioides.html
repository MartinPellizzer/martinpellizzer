Linum aretioides

Linum aretioides, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Linum aretioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
