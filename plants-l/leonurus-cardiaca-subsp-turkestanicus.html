Leonurus cardiaca subsp. turkestanicus

Leonurus cardiaca subsp.

turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan hedgehog, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its common name reflects the spiny appearance of its seed pods, which resemble the spines of a hedgehog.
