Liparis reckoniana

Liparis reckoniana, commonly known as the Reckon's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The flowers are usually small, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.
