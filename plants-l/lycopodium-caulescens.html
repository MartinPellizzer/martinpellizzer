Lycopodium caulescens

Lycopodium caulescens, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-producing cones and a distinctive, branching stem structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils. Its leaves are small and scale-like, arranged spirally around the stem.

Lycopodium caulescens plays an important role in ecological systems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
