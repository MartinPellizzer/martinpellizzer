Lonicera parviflora

Lonicera parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, which bloom in early spring. Its red berries are edible and attract birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Lonicera parviflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
