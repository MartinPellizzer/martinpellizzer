Leipoldtia calandra

Leipoldtia calandra, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-white, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and coastal areas.

Leipoldtia calandra is not only ornamental but also has traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
