Laurus solaniformis

Laurus solaniformis, commonly known as the Solaniform Laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in both gardens and horticultural collections.
