Loxanthocereus hoffmannii

Loxanthocereus hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Its botanical features make it a unique and striking example of desert flora.
