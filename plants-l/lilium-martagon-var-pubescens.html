Lilium martagon var. pubescens

Lilium martagon var. pubescens, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement, often in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of pubescence, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is also cultivated in various horticultural gardens around the world.
