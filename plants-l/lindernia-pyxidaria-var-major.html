Lindernia pyxidaria var. major

Lindernia pyxidaria var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its attractive foliage and long blooming period contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
