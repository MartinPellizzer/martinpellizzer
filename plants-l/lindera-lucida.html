Lindera lucida

Lindera lucida, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters during late spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some birds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
