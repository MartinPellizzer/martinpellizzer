Lippia rotundifolia var. rotundifolia

Lippia rotundifolia var. rotundifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or purple verbena, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct mint-like fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or violet, typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.

Its aromatic properties and adaptability contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
