Laportea schlechteri

Laportea schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
