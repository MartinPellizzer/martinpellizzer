Lussacia

Lussacia, commonly known as the "Garden of the Gods," is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It consists of several species, most notably Lussacia parviflora, which is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plants typically thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making them suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Their flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lussacia species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated for their unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
