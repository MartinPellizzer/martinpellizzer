Lepanthes ovata

Lepanthes ovata, commonly known as the oval-lobed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Lepanthes ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
