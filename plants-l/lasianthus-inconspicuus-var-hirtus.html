Lasianthus inconspicuus var. hirtus

Lasianthus inconspicuus var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
