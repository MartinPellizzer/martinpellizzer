Lobelia × speciosa var. alba

Lobelia × speciosa var. alba, commonly known as the White Indian Tobacco, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
