Lemmaphyllum accedens

Lemmaphyllum accedens, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lemmaphyllum accedens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
