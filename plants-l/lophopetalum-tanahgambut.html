Lophopetalum tanahgambut

Lophopetalum tanahgambut, commonly known as swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and peatlands, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features showy, pentamerous flowers with five petals, often in shades of yellow or orange, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to its wetland environment, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
