Lupinus saxosus var. subsericeus

Lupinus saxosus var. subsericeus, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has slender, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its root system consists of nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
