Lotus corniculatus var. posoniensis

Lotus corniculatus var. posoniensis, commonly known as the Posonensis bird's-foot trefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
