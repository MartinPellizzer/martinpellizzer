Laothoe pomeridiana

Laothoe pomeridiana, commonly known as the pomegranate beetle, is a species of leaf beetle native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive red and black coloration, which serves as a warning to potential predators. The beetle primarily feeds on the leaves of pomegranate trees, making it a significant pest in agricultural settings. Its life cycle includes egg, larval, pupal, and adult stages, with the larvae causing the most damage to the host plant.

Due to its economic impact, it is often targeted by integrated pest management strategies.
