Lycopodioides sanguinolenta f. kantzensis

Lycopodioides sanguinolenta f. kantzensis, commonly known as the red-staining clubmoss, is a rare fern ally with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures called sporangia. The plant exhibits a unique reddish pigmentation in its reproductive organs, which is a notable trait in its morphology. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
