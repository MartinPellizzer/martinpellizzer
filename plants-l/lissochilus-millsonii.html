Lissochilus millsonii

Lissochilus millsonii, commonly known as the Millson's lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as pink, white, and purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blooms that are often clustered together. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
