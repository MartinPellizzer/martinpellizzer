Lasianthus macrobracteatus

Lasianthus macrobracteatus, commonly known as the giant bract lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that are often larger than the actual flowers, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
