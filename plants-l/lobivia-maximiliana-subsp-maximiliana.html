Lobivia maximiliana subsp. maximiliana

Lobivia maximiliana subsp.

maximiliana, commonly known as the "Pineapple Cactus," is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America. It features a compact, globular stem with fleshy, spiky leaves that resemble the scales of a pineapple.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
