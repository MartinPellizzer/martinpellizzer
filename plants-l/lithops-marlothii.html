Lithops marlothii

Lithops marlothii, commonly known as the "ice plant" or "living stone," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant's leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss and reflects sunlight. Lithops marlothii produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle contrast to its rocky appearance.

Its unique morphology makes it well adapted to its natural habitat, where it blends seamlessly with the surrounding landscape.
