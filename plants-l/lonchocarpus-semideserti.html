Lonchocarpus semideserti

Lonchocarpus semideserti, commonly known as the desert senna, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and drought-resistant foliage.

It also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock in its native habitats.
