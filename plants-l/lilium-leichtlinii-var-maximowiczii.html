Lilium leichtlinii var. maximowiczii

Lilium leichtlinii var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Maximowicz's lily, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white to pale pink. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires a cool, dry climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
