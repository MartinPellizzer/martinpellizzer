Lobelia gruina

Lobelia gruina, commonly known as the Sierra lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

Lobelia gruina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
