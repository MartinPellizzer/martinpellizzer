Lindernia glandulifera

Lindernia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and they are usually purple or pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with tiny glands, which give it its scientific name.

Lindernia glandulifera is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
