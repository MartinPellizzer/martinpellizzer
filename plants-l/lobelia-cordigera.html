Lobelia cordigera

Lobelia cordigera, commonly known as the heartleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lobelia cordigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
