Lupinus × pseudopolyphyllus

Lupinus × pseudopolyphyllus, commonly known as the hybrid lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers range in color from blue to purple.

Lupinus × pseudopolyphyllus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
