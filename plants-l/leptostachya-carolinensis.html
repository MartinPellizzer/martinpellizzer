Leptostachya carolinensis

Leptostachya carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
