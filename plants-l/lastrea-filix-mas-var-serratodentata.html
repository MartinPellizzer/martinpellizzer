Lastrea filix-mas var. serratodentata

Lastrea filix-mas var. serratodentata, commonly known as the serrated-leaf yew, is a variant of the yew plant characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It belongs to the Taxaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant features elongated, needle-like leaves that are dark green and often have a leathery texture. Its cones are small and typically contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
