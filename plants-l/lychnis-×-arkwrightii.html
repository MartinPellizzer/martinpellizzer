Lychnis × arkwrightii

Lychnis × arkwrightii, commonly known as the hybrid carnation or garden carnation, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Lychnis flos-cuculi (commonly known as the white campion) and Lychnis alpina (the alpine campion). The plant is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive dark center. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil.

Lychnis × arkwrightii is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
