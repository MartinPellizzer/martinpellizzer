Linaria caesia

Linaria caesia, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria caesia is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
