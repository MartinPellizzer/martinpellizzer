Lyonia rubiginosa var. stahlii

Lyonia rubiginosa var. stahlii, commonly known as the Stahli's trailing lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. Its flowers are typically pink or red, and they are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is well adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
