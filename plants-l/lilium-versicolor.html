Lilium versicolor

Lilium versicolor, commonly known as the variable lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that can vary in hue from white to pink to red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces long, slender stems that support its showy blooms.

Lilium versicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
