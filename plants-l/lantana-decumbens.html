Lantana decumbens

Lantana decumbens, commonly known as prostrate lantana, is a sprawling shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

However, it can become invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
