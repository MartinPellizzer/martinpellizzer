Leucasia

Leucasia, commonly known as the white thorn or white acanthus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically arranged in dense, spherical heads and are usually white or pale yellow in color. Leucasia species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
