Lepanthes jardinensis

Lepanthes jardinensis, commonly known as the Garden's Slipper Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette.

Lepanthes jardinensis is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
