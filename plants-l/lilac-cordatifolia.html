Lilac cordatifolia

Lilac cordatifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, purple-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage turns a striking yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Lilac cordatifolia is valued for its showy blooms and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
