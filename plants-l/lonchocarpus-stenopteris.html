Lonchocarpus stenopteris

Lonchocarpus stenopteris, commonly known as the narrow-winged urara, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces distinctive yellow to orange pods. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
