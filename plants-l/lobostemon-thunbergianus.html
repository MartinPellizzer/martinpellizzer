Lobostemon thunbergianus

Lobostemon thunbergianus, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Lobostemon thunbergianus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
