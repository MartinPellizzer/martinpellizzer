Laburnum laburnum

Laburnum laburnum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Laburnum laburnum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
