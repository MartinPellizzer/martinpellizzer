Lupinus caudatus subsp. montigenus

Lupinus caudatus subsp.

montigenus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pea-like, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
