Lobivia ikedae var. cinnabarina

Lobivia ikedae var. cinnabarina, commonly known as the Red-veined Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The leaves are typically blue-gray with reddish veins, giving it a striking appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
