Lophatherum gracile var. annulatum

Lophatherum gracile var. annulatum, commonly known as the ringed flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive ring-like structure at the base of its stems. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
