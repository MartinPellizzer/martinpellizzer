Lotus glaucus var. villosus

Lotus glaucus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in ponds and marshy areas.

Its botanical structure supports both aquatic and terrestrial growth, making it a versatile species in wetland ecosystems.
