Lodhra laeta

Lodhra laeta, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Western Ghats, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the Nelumbonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
