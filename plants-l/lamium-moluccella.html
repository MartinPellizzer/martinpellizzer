Lamium moluccella

Lamium moluccella, commonly known as greater calamint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, two-lipped, violet-blue flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine for their mild sedative and carminative properties.
