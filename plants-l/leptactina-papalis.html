Leptactina papalis

Leptactina papalis, commonly known as the papal silverleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

Leptactina papalis plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments.
