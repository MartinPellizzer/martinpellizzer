Lycopodium plumosum var. penniforme

Lycopodium plumosum var. penniforme, commonly known as the feathery clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are released into the air for dispersal. Native to moist, shaded environments, it thrives in temperate regions with high humidity.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
