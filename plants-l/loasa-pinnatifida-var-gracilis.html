Loasa pinnatifida var. gracilis

Loasa pinnatifida var. gracilis, commonly known as the slenderleaf loasa, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its growth habit is generally upright, with stems that can reach up to 2 feet in height.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
