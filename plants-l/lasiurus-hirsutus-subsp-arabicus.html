Lasiurus hirsutus subsp. arabicus

Lasiurus hirsutus subsp.

arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that enclose hard, chestnut-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in arid regions.
