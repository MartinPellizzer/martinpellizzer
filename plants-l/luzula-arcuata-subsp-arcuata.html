Luzula arcuata subsp. arcuata

Luzula arcuata subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the bent grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often curved or arched, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. Luzula arcuata subsp.

arcuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its fine-textured foliage.
