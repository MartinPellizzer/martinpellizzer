Lorentea sessiliflora

Lorentea sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered lorentea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, often clustered leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a compact growth habit, which contribute to its adaptability in various climates.

Lorentea sessiliflora is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
