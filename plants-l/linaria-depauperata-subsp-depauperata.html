Linaria depauperata subsp. depauperata

Linaria depauperata subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the poorflower or slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological restoration projects.
