Lupinus cavicaulis

Lupinus cavicaulis, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus cavicaulis is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
