Lantana involucrata var. kuhnholtziana

Lantana involucrata var. kuhnholtziana, commonly known as the Hawaiian lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a dense, branched structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, red, and purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
