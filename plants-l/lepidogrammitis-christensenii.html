Lepidogrammitis christensenii

Lepidogrammitis christensenii, commonly known as the Christensen's shield fern, is a rare and delicate fern native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique morphology with a creeping rhizome and lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine, scale-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a sensitive indicator species for ecosystem health.
