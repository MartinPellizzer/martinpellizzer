Lonicera ciliosa f. volubilis

Lonicera ciliosa f. volubilis, commonly known as the climbing honeysuckle, is a variant of the common honeysuckle.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
