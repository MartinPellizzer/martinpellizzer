Lilium cordifolium subsp. giganteum

Lilium cordifolium subsp.

giganteum, commonly known as the giant white lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. This subspecies is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it one of the most visually impressive lilies.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, contributing to its lush appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often growing in forest understories.

Due to its size and beauty, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
