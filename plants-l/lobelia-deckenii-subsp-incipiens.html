Lobelia deckenii subsp. incipiens

Lobelia deckenii subsp.

incipiens, commonly known as the Kenyan lobelia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in montane forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
