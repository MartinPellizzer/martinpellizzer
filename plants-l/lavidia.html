Lavidia

Lavidia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, tubular leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Lavidia species are typically herbaceous and grow in a compact, rosette form, making them popular as ornamental plants. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus includes several species, such as *Lavidia lehmannii* and *Lavidia saccata*, which are widely cultivated for their aesthetic appeal.
