Luvunga crassifolia

Luvunga crassifolia, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
