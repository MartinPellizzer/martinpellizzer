Linaria gebleri

Linaria gebleri, commonly known as the Gebler's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name.

Linaria gebleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
