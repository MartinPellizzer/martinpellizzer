Lepanthes spruceana

Lepanthes spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, typically featuring a single, short rhizome. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are often pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
