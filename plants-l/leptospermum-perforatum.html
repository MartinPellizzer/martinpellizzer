Leptospermum perforatum

Leptospermum perforatum, commonly known as tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus.

The leaves contain a high concentration of terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal and antiseptic properties.
