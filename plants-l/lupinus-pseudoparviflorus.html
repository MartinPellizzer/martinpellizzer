Lupinus pseudoparviflorus

Lupinus pseudoparviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, often hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus pseudoparviflorus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant for ecological restoration.
