Lepanthes microglottis

Lepanthes microglottis, commonly known as the miniature-tongue orchid, is a species native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique, spoon-shaped lip. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, typical of many orchid species.

Lepanthes microglottis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
