Lepanthes disticha

Lepanthes disticha, commonly known as the two-branched orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces two or more flower spikes from the base of the stem, which is a distinguishing feature. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Lepanthes disticha is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
