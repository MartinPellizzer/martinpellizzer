Litsea nitida

Litsea nitida, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and often covered with a fine silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
