Lepicephalus scabra

Lepicephalus scabra, commonly known as the roughhead mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lepicephalus scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
