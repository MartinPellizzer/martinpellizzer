Lobelia saligna

Lobelia saligna, commonly known as the willowleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features slender, willow-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lobelia saligna is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It serves as a food source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
