Lablab purpureus var. rhomboideus

Lablab purpureus var. rhomboideus, commonly known as the rhomboid hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, curved pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a food source.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
