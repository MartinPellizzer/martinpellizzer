Lepidium scabrifructum

Lepidium scabrifructum, commonly known as the rough-fruited wallcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in ecological succession.
