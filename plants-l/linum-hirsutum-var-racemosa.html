Linum hirsutum var. racemosa

Linum hirsutum var. racemosa, commonly known as the hairy flax or hairy linum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
