Limnosipanea schomburgkii var. robustior

Limnosipanea schomburgkii var. robustior, commonly known as the robust swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, thriving in marshy areas and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical features are adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, with rhizomes that help anchor it in soft substrates.

This variety is distinguished by its robust growth and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
