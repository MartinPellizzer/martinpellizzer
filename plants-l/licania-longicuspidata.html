Licania longicuspidata

Licania longicuspidata, commonly known as the long-pointed licania, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with humid climates and rich soils. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers full sun to partial shade.
