Lunania racemosa

Lunania racemosa, commonly known as the clustered moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds when mature.
