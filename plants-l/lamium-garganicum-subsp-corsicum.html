Lamium garganicum subsp. corsicum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
