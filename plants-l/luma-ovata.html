Luma ovata

Luma ovata, commonly known as the golden bower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a woody vine that produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are often surrounded by bright orange bracts. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Luma ovata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and greenhouses.
