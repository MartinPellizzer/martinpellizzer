Lycopodium dubium

Lycopodium dubium, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant. Native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
