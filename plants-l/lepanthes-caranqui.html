Lepanthes caranqui

Lepanthes caranqui, commonly known as the Caranqui orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high elevations. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive greenish-white coloration. Its flowers are usually clustered in a raceme and have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals.

Lepanthes caranqui is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
