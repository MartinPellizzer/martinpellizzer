Liriodendron tulipifera f. heterophyllum

Liriodendron tulipifera f. heterophyllum, commonly known as the tulip tree or yellow poplar, is a variant of the American tulip tree.

It is characterized by its distinct foliage, with leaves that are narrower and more elongated compared to the typical form. The plant produces showy, tulip-shaped yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
