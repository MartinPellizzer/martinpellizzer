Leptorkis polycardia

Leptorkis polycardia, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and striking, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
