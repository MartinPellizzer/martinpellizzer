Lilium candidum var. aureomarginatum

Lilium candidum var. aureomarginatum, commonly known as the golden-margin lily, is a botanical variant of the white lily, characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow margins on the petals.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
