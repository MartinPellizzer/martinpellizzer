Liparis fratrum

Liparis fratrum, commonly known as the brother's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Liparis fratrum is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
