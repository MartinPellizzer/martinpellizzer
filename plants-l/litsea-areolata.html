Litsea areolata

Litsea areolata, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that emit a distinctive scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Litsea areolata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
