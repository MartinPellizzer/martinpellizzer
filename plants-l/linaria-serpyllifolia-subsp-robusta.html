Linaria serpyllifolia subsp. robusta

Linaria serpyllifolia subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in dry meadows. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are sturdy and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful blooms.
