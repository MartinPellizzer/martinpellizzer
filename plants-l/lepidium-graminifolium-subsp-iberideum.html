Lepidium graminifolium subsp. iberideum

Lepidium graminifolium subsp.

iberideum, commonly known as the Iberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. It has slender, branching stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

Its flowers are yellowish-green and bloom in early spring, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
