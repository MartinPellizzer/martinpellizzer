Liquidambar tonkinensis

Liquidambar tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Liquidambar in the family Aceraceae, although it is now classified under the family Sapindaceae. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins, while the fruit is a spiky, spherical capsule that splits to release winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
