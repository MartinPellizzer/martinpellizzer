Lathyrus myrtifolius var. macranthus

Lathyrus myrtifolius var. macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered sweet pea, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their large, spurred petals and are highly attractive to pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental in gardens.
