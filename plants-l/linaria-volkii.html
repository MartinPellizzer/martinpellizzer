Linaria volkii

Linaria volkii, commonly known as the Volk's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Linaria volkii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
