Ladenbergia ulei

Ladenbergia ulei, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
