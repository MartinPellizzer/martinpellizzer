Leiothrix curvifolia var. setacea

Leiothrix curvifolia var. setacea, commonly known as the velvet tassel fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its dense, velvety fronds.

The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, curved, and often spirally twisted leaves that give it a unique appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its lush, textured look. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
