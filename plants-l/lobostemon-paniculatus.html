Lobostemon paniculatus

Lobostemon paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow upright or prostrate, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in paniculate inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobostemon paniculatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
