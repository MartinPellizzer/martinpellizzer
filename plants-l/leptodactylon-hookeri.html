Leptodactylon hookeri

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its long, strap-like fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, green foliage.
