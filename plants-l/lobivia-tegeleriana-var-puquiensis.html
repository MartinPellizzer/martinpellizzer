Lobivia tegeleriana var. puquiensis

Lobivia tegeleriana var. puquiensis, commonly known as the Puquian Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
