Lechea major var. villosa

Lechea major var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy lechea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of thick, velvety, hairy leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to xeriscaped gardens.
