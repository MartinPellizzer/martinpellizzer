Liparis longibracteata

Liparis longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
