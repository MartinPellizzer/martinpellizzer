Lepanthes tetroptera

Lepanthes tetroptera, commonly known as the four-winged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a short, underground rhizome and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with four spreading petals. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes tetroptera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
