Lignocarpa carnosula

Lignocarpa carnosula, commonly known as the red-stemmed lignocarp, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Lignocarpa carnosula plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing shelter and sustenance to various species.
