Lotus cytisoides var. cinerascens

Lotus cytisoides var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens lotus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are pinnately divided, with a feathery appearance, and the plant typically grows in dry, arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
