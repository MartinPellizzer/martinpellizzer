Lampranthus densifolius

Lampranthus densifolius, commonly known as the denseleaf ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Lampranthus densifolius is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
