Ledothamnus decumbens

Ledothamnus decumbens, commonly known as the creeping lichen, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and is often found on rocky substrates or in forest floors. The plant exhibits a unique symbiotic relationship with algae, which contributes to its green coloration and nutrient acquisition. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically developing at the base of the plant.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often overlooked but plays a vital role in soil stabilization and ecosystem health.
