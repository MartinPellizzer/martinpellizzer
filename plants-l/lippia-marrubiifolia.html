Lippia marrubiifolia

Lippia marrubiifolia, commonly known as wild verbena or Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun. Lippia marrubiifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its leaves and flowers are also sometimes used as a spice in culinary applications.
