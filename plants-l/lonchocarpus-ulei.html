Lonchocarpus ulei

Lonchocarpus ulei, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It is often found in open woodland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
