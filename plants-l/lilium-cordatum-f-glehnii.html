Lilium cordatum f. glehnii

Lilium cordatum f. glehnii, commonly known as the Glehn's falcate lily, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the lily family.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and striking, pale pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance. This form is a natural variant of Lilium cordatum, which is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a graceful touch to gardens and natural habitats.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and botanists alike.
