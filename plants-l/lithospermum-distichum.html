Lithospermum distichum

Lithospermum distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked rock pepper, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-ranked leaves and produces small, red berries that are a striking feature of the plant. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its flowers are typically pale purple to white, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The plant's ability to grow in harsh environments makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
