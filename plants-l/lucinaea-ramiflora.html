Lucinaea ramiflora

Lucinaea ramiflora, commonly known as the branching woodbine, is a perennial climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Lucinaea ramiflora thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
