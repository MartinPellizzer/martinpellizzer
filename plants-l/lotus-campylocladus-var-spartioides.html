Lotus campylocladus var. spartioides

Lotus campylocladus var. spartioides, commonly known as the spiny lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered with spiny leaves that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and have a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
