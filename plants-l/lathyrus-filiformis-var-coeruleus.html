Lathyrus filiformis var. coeruleus

Lathyrus filiformis var. coeruleus, commonly known as the blue flax vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems that produce small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for improving soil fertility.
