Lobelia longipedicellata

Lobelia longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia longipedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
