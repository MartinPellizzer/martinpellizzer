Lisianthius subcordatus

Lisianthius subcordatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
