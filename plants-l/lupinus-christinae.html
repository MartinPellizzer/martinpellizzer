Lupinus christinae

Lupinus christinae, commonly known as the Christina lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Lupinus christinae is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
