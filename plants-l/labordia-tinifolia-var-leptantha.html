Labordia tinifolia var. leptantha

Labordia tinifolia var. leptantha, commonly known as the slender-leaved labordia, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often creeping stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
