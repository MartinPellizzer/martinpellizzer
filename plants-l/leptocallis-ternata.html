Leptocallis ternata

Leptocallis ternata, commonly known as the three-part lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, three-parted flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in early spring.

Leptocallis ternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
