Leptolaena multiflora var. cuspidata

Leptolaena multiflora var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in a variety of climates.
