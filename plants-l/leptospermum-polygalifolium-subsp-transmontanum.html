Leptospermum polygalifolium subsp. transmontanum

Leptospermum polygalifolium subsp.

transmontanum, commonly known as the Transmontane tea tree, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
