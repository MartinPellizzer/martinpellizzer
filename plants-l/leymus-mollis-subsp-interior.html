Leymus mollis subsp. interior

Leymus mollis subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

This species typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as sandy soils and gravelly plains. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
