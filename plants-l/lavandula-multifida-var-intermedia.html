Lavandula multifida var. intermedia

Lavandula multifida var. intermedia, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and tall, slender flower spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and essential oil production.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive leaf shape and its status as a variety of Lavandula multifida.
