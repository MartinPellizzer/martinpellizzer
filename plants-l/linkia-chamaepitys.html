Linkia chamaepitys

Linkia chamaepitys, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Linkia chamaepitys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
