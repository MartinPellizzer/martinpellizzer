Linaria lilacina

Linaria lilacina, commonly known as the lilac morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and vibrant lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linaria lilacina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
