Lepanthes silenus

Lepanthes silenus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces striking, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Lepanthes silenus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique floral structure.
