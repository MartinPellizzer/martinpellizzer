Linum tauricum var. basarabicum

Linum tauricum var. basarabicum, commonly known as the Caucasian flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in fiber production, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
