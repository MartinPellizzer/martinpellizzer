Lysimachia insignis f. flaviflora

Lysimachia insignis f. flaviflora, commonly known as the goldenleaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers throughout the summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a delicate, fragrant scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its vibrant color and easy care requirements.
