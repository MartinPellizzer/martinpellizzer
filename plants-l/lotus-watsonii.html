Lotus watsonii

Lotus watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's lotus, is a species of water lily native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in some cultures.
