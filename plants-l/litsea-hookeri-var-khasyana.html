Litsea hookeri var. khasyana

Litsea hookeri var. khasyana, commonly known as the Khasi litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the northeastern region of India, particularly the states of Assam and Meghalaya. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it thrives in moist, tropical climates.

This variant is notable for its ecological significance in the region's biodiversity.
