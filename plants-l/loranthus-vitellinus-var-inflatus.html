Loranthus vitellinus var. inflatus

Loranthus vitellinus var. inflatus, commonly known as the Indian mangosteen or the red mangosteen, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Santalaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives as a hemiparasite on the roots of various host trees, such as mango and tamarind. The plant produces small, red or orange fruits that are edible and contain a sweet, segmented pulp. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive yellowish or reddish coloration.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it an important component of the forest ecosystems it inhabits.
