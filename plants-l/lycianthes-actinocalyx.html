Lycianthes actinocalyx

Lycianthes actinocalyx, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
