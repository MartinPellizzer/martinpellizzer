Lycium requienii

Lycium requienii, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, orange-red berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are usually yellow or white.

The berries are edible when ripe but contain toxic seeds that should be avoided.
