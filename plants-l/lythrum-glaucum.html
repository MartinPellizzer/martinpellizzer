Lythrum glaucum

Lythrum glaucum, commonly known as narrowleaf lythrum, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and ecological importance. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in a powdery blue-gray bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in dense spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lythrum glaucum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.
