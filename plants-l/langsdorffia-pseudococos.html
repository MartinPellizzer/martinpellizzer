Langsdorffia pseudococos

Langsdorffia pseudococos, commonly known as the coconut orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant produces fruit that resembles a coconut, though it is not related to true coconuts. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique appearance and growth habits make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
