Lotus scoparius subsp. traskiae

Lotus scoparius subsp.

traskiae, commonly known as the Trask's lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands.

The plant features floating leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and its flowers are pink to purple in color. It produces small, green pods that contain seeds dispersed by water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
