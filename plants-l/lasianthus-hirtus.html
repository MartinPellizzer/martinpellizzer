Lasianthus hirtus

Lasianthus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
