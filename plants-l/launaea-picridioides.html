Launaea picridioides

Launaea picridioides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in hot, dry environments and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It has adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Launaea picridioides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
