Lasianthus giganteus

Lasianthus giganteus, commonly known as the giant lasianthus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
