Lebeckia acanthoclada

Lebeckia acanthoclada, commonly known as the Natal daisy, is a South African native plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, which bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has hairy, grey-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Lebeckia acanthoclada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
