Lepanthes phalloides

Lepanthes phalloides, commonly known as the phallic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a phallus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red.

Lepanthes phalloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
