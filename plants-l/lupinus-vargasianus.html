Lupinus vargasianus

Lupinus vargasianus, commonly known as the Vargasian lupine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Lupinus vargasianus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
