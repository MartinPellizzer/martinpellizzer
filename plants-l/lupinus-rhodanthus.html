Lupinus rhodanthus

Lupinus rhodanthus, commonly known as the scarlet lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legumes that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and open habitats.

Lupinus rhodanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
