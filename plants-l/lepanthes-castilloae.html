Lepanthes castilloae

Lepanthes castilloae, commonly known as the Castillo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes castilloae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
