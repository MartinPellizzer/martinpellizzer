Lauro-cerasus integrifolia

Lauro-cerasus integrifolia, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically entire, giving it the species name *integrifolia*. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or planting this species.
