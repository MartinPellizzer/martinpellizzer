Leea longifoliola

Leea longifoliola, commonly known as the long-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elongated. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
