Lomariopsis longifolia

Lomariopsis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved lomariopsis, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, upright rosette. The plant produces small, clustered spore capsules that are covered in a fine, hairy texture. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
