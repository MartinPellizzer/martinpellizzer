Litsea rotundifolia var. oblongifolia

Litsea rotundifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
