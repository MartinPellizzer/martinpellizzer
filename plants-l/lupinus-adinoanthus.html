Lupinus adinoanthus

Lupinus adinoanthus, commonly known as the Adirondack lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like blue or purple flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

Lupinus adinoanthus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
