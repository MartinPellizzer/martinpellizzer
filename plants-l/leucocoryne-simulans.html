Leucocoryne simulans

Leucocoryne simulans, commonly known as the white-flowered grassleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
