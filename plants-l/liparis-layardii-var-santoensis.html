Liparis layardii var. santoensis

Liparis layardii var. santoensis, commonly known as Santo's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces greenish-white or pale yellow blooms with intricate patterns, often resembling a lyre shape. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
