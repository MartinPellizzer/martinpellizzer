Lespedeza prainii

Lespedeza prainii, commonly known as Prain's lespedeza, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.

Lespedeza prainii is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
