Leucaena cuspidata subsp. jacalensis

Leucaena cuspidata subsp.

jacalensis, commonly known as Jacalense leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually green with a slight pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by slender, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
