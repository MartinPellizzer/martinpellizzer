Leucostele pectinifera

Leucostele pectinifera, commonly known as the silky leucostele, is a rare and delicate fern-like plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate leaves that give it a lacy appearance, and its stems are typically slender and covered in a soft, silky texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and organic soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores produced in clusters along the undersides of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
