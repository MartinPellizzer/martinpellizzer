Lithops gesineae var. annae

Lithops gesineae var. annae, commonly known as the Cape Town stone plant, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance, with two fleshy, paired leaves that resemble stones. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short stalks. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as a rare and ornamental plant.
