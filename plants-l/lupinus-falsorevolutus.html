Lupinus falsorevolutus

Lupinus falsorevolutus, commonly known as the false revolute lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive revolute margin, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus falsorevolutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
