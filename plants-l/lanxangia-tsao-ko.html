Lanxangia tsao-ko

Lanxangia tsao-ko, commonly known as the Thai orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The blooming period of Lanxangia tsao-ko usually occurs during the rainy season, adding to its ecological and ornamental significance.
