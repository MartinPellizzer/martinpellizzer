Lophiolepis semzinanica

Lophiolepis semzinanica, commonly known as the Zambian lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Lophiolepis semzinanica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
