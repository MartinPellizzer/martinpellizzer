Laurus alpigena

Laurus alpigena, commonly known as the alpine laurel or mountain laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, typically growing at high altitudes. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in early summer. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and the plant produces small, spherical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Laurus alpigena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
