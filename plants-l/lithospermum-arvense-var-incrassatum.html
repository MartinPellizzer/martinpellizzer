Lithospermum arvense var. incrassatum

Lithospermum arvense var. incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its roots contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it of interest in herbal medicine.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
