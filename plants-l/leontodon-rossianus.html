Leontodon rossianus

Leontodon rossianus, commonly known as Ross's dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and steppe environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
