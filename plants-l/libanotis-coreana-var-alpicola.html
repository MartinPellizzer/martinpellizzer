Libanotis coreana var. alpicola

Libanotis coreana var. alpicola, commonly known as the Korean alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to high-altitude environments and is considered a rare and delicate species.
