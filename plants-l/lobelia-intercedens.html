Lobelia intercedens

Lobelia intercedens, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Lobelia intercedens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
