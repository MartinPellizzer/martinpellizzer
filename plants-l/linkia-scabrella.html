Linkia scabrella

Linkia scabrella, commonly known as the rough-skinned orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often hairy or scaly leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors ranging from white to pink.

Linkia scabrella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
