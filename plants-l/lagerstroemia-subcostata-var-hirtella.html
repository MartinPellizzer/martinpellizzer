Lagerstroemia subcostata var. hirtella

Lagerstroemia subcostata var. hirtella, commonly known as the hirtella crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and showy, clustered panicles of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with a hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The hirtella crape myrtle is valued for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
