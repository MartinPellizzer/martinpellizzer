Lindenbergia arabica

Lindenbergia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Lindenbergia arabica is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
