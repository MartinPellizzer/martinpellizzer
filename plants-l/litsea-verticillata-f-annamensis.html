Litsea verticillata f. annamensis

Litsea verticillata f. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam leafy-laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lanceolate leaves that are often arranged in whorls of three to five at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
