Lobelia plebeia

Lobelia plebeia, commonly known as the common lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct blue-green hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Lobelia plebeia thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
