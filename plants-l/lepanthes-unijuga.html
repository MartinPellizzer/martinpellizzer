Lepanthes unijuga

Lepanthes unijuga, commonly known as the single-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique, single-lobed structure on its lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Lepanthes unijuga is considered a conservation concern.
