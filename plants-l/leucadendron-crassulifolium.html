Leucadendron crassulifolium

Leucadendron crassulifolium, commonly known as the silver leaf candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces fluffy, spherical flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange, adding visual interest in late summer to autumn. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
