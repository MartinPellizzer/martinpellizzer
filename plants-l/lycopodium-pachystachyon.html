Lycopodium pachystachyon

Lycopodium pachystachyon, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, making it one of the largest members of the Lycopodiaceae family. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged spirally along the stem, and contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium pachystachyon plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
