Limonium tenuicaule

Limonium tenuicaule, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its delicate, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Limonium tenuicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
