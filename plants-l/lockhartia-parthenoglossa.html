Lockhartia parthenoglossa

Lockhartia parthenoglossa, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red or orange flowers that resemble flames, making it a visually captivating plant. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, lip-like petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
