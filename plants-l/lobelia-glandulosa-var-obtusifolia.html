Lobelia glandulosa var. obtusifolia

Lobelia glandulosa var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often found in clusters.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
