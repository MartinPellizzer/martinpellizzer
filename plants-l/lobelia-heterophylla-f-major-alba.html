Lobelia heterophylla f. major-alba

Lobelia heterophylla f. major-alba, commonly known as the white-flowered lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and vary in size along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
