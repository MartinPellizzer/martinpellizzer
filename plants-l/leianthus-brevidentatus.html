Leianthus brevidentatus

Leianthus brevidentatus, commonly known as the short-toothed leianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers with yellow petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have short, blunt teeth along the margins.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland areas.
