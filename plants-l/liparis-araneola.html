Liparis araneola

Liparis araneola, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. The plant features a slender, grass-like stem with a single, showy orchid flower that is usually pale green or white. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in local ecosystems.
