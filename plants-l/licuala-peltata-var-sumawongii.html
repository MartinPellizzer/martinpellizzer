Licuala peltata var. sumawongii

Licuala peltata var. sumawongii, commonly known as the Sumawong palm, is a variant of the broad-leafed palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
