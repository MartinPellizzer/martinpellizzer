Lysimachia sessilifolia

Lysimachia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessileleaf loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a petiole.

Lysimachia sessilifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in garden settings with proper soil and moisture conditions.
