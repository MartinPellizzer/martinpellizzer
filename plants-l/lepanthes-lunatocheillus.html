Lepanthes lunatocheillus

Lepanthes lunatocheillus, commonly known as the moon-faced orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers that often have a pale green or white color with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
