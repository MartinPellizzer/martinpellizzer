Lonchitis tenuifolia

Lonchitis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate saline conditions. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the summer months. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Lonchitis tenuifolia plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
