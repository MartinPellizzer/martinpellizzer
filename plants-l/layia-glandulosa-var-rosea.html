Layia glandulosa var. rosea

Layia glandulosa var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are pink to rose-colored, with yellow centers, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
