Lippia oatesii

Lippia oatesii, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Lippia oatesii is valued for its strong mint-like scent and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

It is also a popular plant among gardeners due to its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
