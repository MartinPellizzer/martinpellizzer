Lathyrus cashmericus

Lathyrus cashmericus, commonly known as the Kashmir pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its pods contain seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
