Leptospermum podanthum

Leptospermum podanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and heathland environments, characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its foliage is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine, and it is also cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.
