Lygodium longifolium

Lygodium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often used in horticultural displays due to their aesthetic appeal. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Lygodium longifolium is also valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
