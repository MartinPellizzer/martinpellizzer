Lycopodium × issleri

Lycopodium × issleri, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a hybrid species within the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are clustered in spirals. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodium × issleri is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
