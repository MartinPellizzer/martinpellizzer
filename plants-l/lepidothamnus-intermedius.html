Lepidothamnus intermedius

Lepidothamnus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate lepidotea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a compact and bushy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
