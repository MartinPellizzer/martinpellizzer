Lilium speciosum var. fasciatum-rubrum

Lilium speciosum var. fasciatum-rubrum, commonly known as the red-striped lily, is a striking cultivar of the lily family (Liliaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, elegant stems that bear large, showy flowers with a distinctive red stripe running along the outer petals. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
