Lupinus foliosus

Lupinus foliosus, commonly known as the leaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus foliosus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
