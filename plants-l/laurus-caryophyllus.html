Laurus caryophyllus

Laurus caryophyllus, commonly known as sweet bay or bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Laurus caryophyllus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
