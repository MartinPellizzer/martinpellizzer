Listera pubescens

Listera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy basketwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it the common name "hairy basketwort.".

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
