Linaria heterophylla var. violascens

Linaria heterophylla var. violascens, commonly known as the violetleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has variable leaf shapes, ranging from linear to ovate, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
