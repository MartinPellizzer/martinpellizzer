Lathyrus montanus f. variegatus

Lathyrus montanus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated mountain vetch, is a decorative variant of the mountain vetch species.

It is characterized by its striking variegation in leaf patterns, often featuring white or yellow markings against a green background. This plant is a flowering legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and typically produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
