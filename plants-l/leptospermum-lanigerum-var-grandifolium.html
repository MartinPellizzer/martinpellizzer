Leptospermum lanigerum var. grandifolium

Leptospermum lanigerum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variant is notable for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
