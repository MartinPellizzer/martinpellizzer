Lolium multiflorum var. brachypodiatum

Lolium multiflorum var. brachypodiatum, commonly known as the short-rooted ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a valuable forage crop and a common weed in agricultural settings.
