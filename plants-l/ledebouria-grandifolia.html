Ledebouria grandifolia

Ledebouria grandifolia, commonly known as the giant African bulb, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy bulbs and strap-like, evergreen leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
