Lycium chinense var. cochinchinense

Lycium chinense var. cochinchinense, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The berries are edible, used in traditional medicine, and have gained popularity as a health supplement worldwide.
