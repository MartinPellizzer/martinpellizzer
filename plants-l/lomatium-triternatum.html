Lomatium triternatum

Lomatium triternatum, commonly known as the mountain parsley, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.

Lomatium triternatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
