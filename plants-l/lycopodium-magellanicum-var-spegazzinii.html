Lycopodium magellanicum var. spegazzinii

Lycopodium magellanicum var. spegazzinii, commonly known as the Magellanic clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant native to South America.

It is a member of the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and creeping growth habit. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called strobila, which are typically located at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
