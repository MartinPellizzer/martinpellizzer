Lepanthes cunicularis

Lepanthes cunicularis, commonly known as the rabbit-hole orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy white or pale pink flower. Its roots are often clustered in a small, tubular structure resembling a rabbit hole, which is a distinctive characteristic.

Lepanthes cunicularis is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
