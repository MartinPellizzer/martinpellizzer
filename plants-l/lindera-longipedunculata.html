Lindera longipedunculata

Lindera longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in elongated panicles, which give it its common name.

The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in the fall.
