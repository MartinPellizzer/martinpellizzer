Lonchocarpus septentrionalis

Lonchocarpus septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody covering. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
