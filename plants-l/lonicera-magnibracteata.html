Lonicera magnibracteata

Lonicera magnibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant bears red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Lonicera magnibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in garden settings.
