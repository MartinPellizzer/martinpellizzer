Linum cubense

Linum cubense, commonly known as Cuban flax, is a flowering plant native to Cuba and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Linum cubense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.
