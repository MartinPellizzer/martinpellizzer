Lonchocarpus menyharthii

Lonchocarpus menyharthii, commonly known as the African senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.

However, some species in the genus are toxic, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
