Larix europaea var. pendula

Larix europaea var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a variant of the European larch.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. This deciduous coniferous tree has needle-like leaves that turn golden in autumn before falling. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks.
