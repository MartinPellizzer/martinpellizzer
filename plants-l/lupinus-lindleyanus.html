Lupinus lindleyanus

Lupinus lindleyanus, commonly known as the Lindley's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical pods that contain several oval seeds. It typically blooms with blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

Lupinus lindleyanus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.
