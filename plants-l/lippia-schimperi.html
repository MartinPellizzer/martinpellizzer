Lippia schimperi

Lippia schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian thyme, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

Lippia schimperi is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
