Lycopodium torridum

Lycopodium torridum, commonly known as the tropical clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. Lycopodium torridum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are dispersed by wind.
