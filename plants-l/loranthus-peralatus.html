Loranthus peralatus

Loranthus peralatus, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance, aiding in seed dispersal by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Asia and is often associated with species of the family Rutaceae.
