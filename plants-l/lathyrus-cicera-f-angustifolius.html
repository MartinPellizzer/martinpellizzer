Lathyrus cicera f. angustifolius

Lathyrus cicera f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweet pea, is a variant of the sweet pea species characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its foliage is typically more narrow and less dense compared to the standard sweet pea, making it a unique ornamental choice.

This cultivar is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting garden soil health.
