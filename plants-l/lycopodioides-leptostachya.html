Lycopodioides leptostachya

Lycopodioides leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a short, vertical stem with dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophylls, are arranged in strobila, which produce spores for propagation.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
