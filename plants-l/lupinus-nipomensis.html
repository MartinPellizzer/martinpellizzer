Lupinus nipomensis

Lupinus nipomensis, commonly known as the Nippon lupine, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus nipomensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
