Ligustrum ovalifolium elegantissimum

Ligustrum ovalifolium elegantissimum, commonly known as the California privet, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries in the fall. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
