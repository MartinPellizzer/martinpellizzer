Lepanthes strumosa

Lepanthes strumosa, commonly known as the Strumose Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes strumosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
