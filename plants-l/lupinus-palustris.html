Lupinus palustris

Lupinus palustris, commonly known as the swamp lupine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of North America.

It features erect, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of pea-like blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus palustris thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes.
