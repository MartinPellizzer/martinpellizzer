Lilium ochroleucum

Lilium ochroleucum, commonly known as the white lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Lilium ochroleucum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
