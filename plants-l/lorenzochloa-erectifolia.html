Lorenzochloa erectifolia

Lorenzochloa erectifolia, commonly known as the upright feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in terminal spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in xeriscaping and erosion control.
