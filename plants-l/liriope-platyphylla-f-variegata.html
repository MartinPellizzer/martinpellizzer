Liriope platyphylla f. variegata

Liriope platyphylla f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated liriope, is a decorative grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It features strap-like leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white or yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late summer, typically in shades of blue or purple. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, making it a popular choice for garden borders and ground cover.
