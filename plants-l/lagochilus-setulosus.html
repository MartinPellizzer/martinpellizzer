Lagochilus setulosus

Lagochilus setulosus, commonly known as the woolly rock hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
