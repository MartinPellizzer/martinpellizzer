Licuala intermedia

Licuala intermedia, commonly known as the giant panda palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
