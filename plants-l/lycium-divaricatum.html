Lycium divaricatum

Lycium divaricatum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Lycium divaricatum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
