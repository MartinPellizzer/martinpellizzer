Lycopodium innocentium

Lycopodium innocentium, commonly known as the ground pine, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. Its reproductive structures are spore-bearing cones, which are produced on the upper portions of the plant.

Lycopodium innocentium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
