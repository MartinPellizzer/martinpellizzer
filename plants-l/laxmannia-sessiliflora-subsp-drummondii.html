Laxmannia sessiliflora subsp. drummondii

Laxmannia sessiliflora subsp.

drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's laxmannia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
