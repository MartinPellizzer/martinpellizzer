Lasiacis longiligula

Lasiacis longiligula, commonly known as the slender-stemmed lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in alpine flora.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native range.
