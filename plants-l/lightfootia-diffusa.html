Lightfootia diffusa

Lightfootia diffusa, commonly known as the red-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lightfootia diffusa is a perennial herb that plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
