Laportea subglabra

Laportea subglabra, commonly known as smooth laportea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that are usually smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

Laportea subglabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local pollinators.
