Lathyrus vernus var. gracilis

Lathyrus vernus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by bees.

The slender vetch is noted for its delicate, climbing habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
