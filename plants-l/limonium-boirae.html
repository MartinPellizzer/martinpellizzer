Limonium boirae

Limonium boirae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spike-like flower heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps in reducing water loss.

Limonium boirae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
