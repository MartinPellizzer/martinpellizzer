Lespedeza variegata

Lespedeza variegata, commonly known as variegated lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woods and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of pink or red, and bloom in late summer.

Lespedeza variegata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
