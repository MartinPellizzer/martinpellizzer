Liponeurum

Liponeurum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a genus of ferns native to various regions of the world.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery or pale underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Liponeurum species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological roles in their native habitats.
