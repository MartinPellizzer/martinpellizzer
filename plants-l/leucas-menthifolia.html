Leucas menthifolia

Leucas menthifolia, commonly known as lemon mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like aroma. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
