Lavandula guinardii

Lavandula guinardii, commonly known as Guinard's lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Lavandula.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to lilac-colored flowers, which bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in essential oil production.

Lavandula guinardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
