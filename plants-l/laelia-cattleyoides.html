Laelia cattleyoides

Laelia cattleyoides, commonly known as the Cattleya-like orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Laelia cattleyoides is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its resilience and ornamental value.
