Lespedeza violacea var. angustifolia

Lespedeza violacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluefalseindigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods and prairies.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability makes it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
