Lycopersicon peruvianum var. humifusum

Lycopersicon peruvianum var. humifusum, commonly known as the Peruvian ground cherry, is a wild tomato species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow or orange fruits that grow on the ground, encased in a papery husk. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in cool, temperate climates. It is a perennial shrub that produces flowers with five lobes, which develop into the distinctive fruits.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes used in traditional cooking or as a ornamental plant.
