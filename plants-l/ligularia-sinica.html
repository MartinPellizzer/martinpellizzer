Ligularia sinica

Ligularia sinica, commonly known as the Chinese ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration and prominent veins. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated in garden settings for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
