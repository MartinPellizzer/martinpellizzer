Lepanthes megalocephala

Lepanthes megalocephala, commonly known as the giant-headed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a unique, elongated lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Lepanthes megalocephala is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting various insect pollinators.
