Lachnostoma ecuadorense

Lachnostoma ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Lachnaceae.

It is characterized by its unique thallus structure, which is typically crustose and tightly adherent to its substrate. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium, which allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is primarily found in the humid forests of Ecuador, where it plays a role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
