Linaria rubrifolia var. maroccana

Linaria rubrifolia var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan toadflax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, and its slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
