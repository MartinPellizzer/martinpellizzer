Leucas fulva

Leucas fulva, commonly known as the yellow heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Leucas fulva is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
