Linum junceum f. oblongifolium

Linum junceum f. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flax, is a variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy blue or white flowers, which are typical of the Linaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and wildflower meadows.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
