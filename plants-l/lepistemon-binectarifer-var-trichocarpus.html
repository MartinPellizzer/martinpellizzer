Lepistemon binectarifer var. trichocarpus

Lepistemon binectarifer var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the silky lepistemon, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers, which are followed by distinctive, hairy, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
