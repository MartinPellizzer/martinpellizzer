Leontodon molineri

Leontodon molineri, commonly known as the Moliner's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
