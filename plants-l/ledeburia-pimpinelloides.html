Ledeburia pimpinelloides

Ledeburia pimpinelloides, commonly known as the Cape mountain cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
