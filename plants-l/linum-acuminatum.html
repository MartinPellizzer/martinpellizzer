Linum acuminatum

Linum acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

Linum acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
