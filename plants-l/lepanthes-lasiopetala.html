Lepanthes lasiopetala

Lepanthes lasiopetala, commonly known as the velvety slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety petals and a unique, pouch-like structure at the base of its flowers, which resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes lasiopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
