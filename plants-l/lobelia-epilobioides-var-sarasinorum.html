Lobelia epilobioides var. sarasinorum

Lobelia epilobioides var. sarasinorum, commonly known as Sarasin's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
