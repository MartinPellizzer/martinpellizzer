Leucaena insularum var. guamensis

Leucaena insularum var. guamensis, commonly known as Guam leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to the island of Guam.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces pod fruits containing several seeds, and it is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

It is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils, showing a degree of resilience to environmental stress.
