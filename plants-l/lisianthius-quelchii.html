Lisianthius quelchii

Lisianthius quelchii, commonly known as the New Zealand bluebell, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it is considered a threatened species due to habitat loss and invasive species.
