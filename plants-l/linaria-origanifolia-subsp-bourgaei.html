Linaria origanifolia subsp. bourgaei

Linaria origanifolia subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct aroma when crushed. This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, such as grasslands and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
