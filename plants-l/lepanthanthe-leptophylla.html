Lepanthanthe leptophylla

Lepanthanthe leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Lepanthanthe leptophylla is considered endangered and is often found in protected botanical gardens.
