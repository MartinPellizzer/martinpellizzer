Lupinus volutans

Lupinus volutans, commonly known as the spiral lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces tall spikes of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.

Lupinus volutans is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
