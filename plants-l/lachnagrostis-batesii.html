Lachnagrostis batesii

Lachnagrostis batesii, commonly known as the Bates' wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny grains of pollen. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Lachnagrostis batesii plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native regions.
