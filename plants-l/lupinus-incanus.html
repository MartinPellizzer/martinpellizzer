Lupinus incanus

Lupinus incanus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, spindly stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Lupinus incanus has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and arid environments.

It is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
