Lathyrus rotundifolius subsp. undulatus

Lathyrus rotundifolius subsp.

undulatus, commonly known as the wavy-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its rounded, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, undulating floral tendrils.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
