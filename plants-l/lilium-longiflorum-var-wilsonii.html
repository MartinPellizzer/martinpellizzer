Lilium longiflorum var. wilsonii

Lilium longiflorum var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's Easter lily, is a cultivar of the Easter lily species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is particularly noted for its strong fragrance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
