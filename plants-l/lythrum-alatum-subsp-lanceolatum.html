Lythrum alatum subsp. lanceolatum

Lythrum alatum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf lythrum, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers.

The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various insects.
