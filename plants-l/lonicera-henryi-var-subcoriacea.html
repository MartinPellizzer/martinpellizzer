Lonicera henryi var. subcoriacea

Lonicera henryi var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems covered with small, glandular hairs, giving it a rough texture. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface. It produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically yellow or orange, that bloom in late spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
