Lupinus sabuli var. subpersistens

Lupinus sabuli var. subpersistens, commonly known as the sand lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legumes as its fruit. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus sabuli var. subpersistens plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
