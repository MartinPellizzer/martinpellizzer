Laurus dioica

Laurus dioica, commonly known as sweet bay, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Laurus dioica is also valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
