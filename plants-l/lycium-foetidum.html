Lycium foetidum

Lycium foetidum, commonly known as the stinking box or foul box, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red berries. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and can emit a strong, unpleasant odor when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its unique scent and appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
