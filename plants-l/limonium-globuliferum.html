Limonium globuliferum

Limonium globuliferum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suited to saline environments.

Limonium globuliferum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
