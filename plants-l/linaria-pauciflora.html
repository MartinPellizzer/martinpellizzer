Linaria pauciflora

Linaria pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria pauciflora is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
