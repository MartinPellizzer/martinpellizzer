Lotus corniculatus subsp. decumbens

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping bird's-foot trefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
