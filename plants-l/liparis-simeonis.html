Liparis simeonis

Liparis simeonis, commonly known as the Simeon's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually grass-like, and the flowers are usually green or yellow with a distinctive lip.

Liparis simeonis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
