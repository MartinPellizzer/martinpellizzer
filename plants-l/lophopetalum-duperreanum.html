Lophopetalum duperreanum

Lophopetalum duperreanum, commonly known as the Texas beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Lophopetalum duperreanum is often cultivated for its showy flowers and colorful fruits, making it a popular choice in native gardens.
