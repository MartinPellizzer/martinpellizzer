Limnophila parviflora

Limnophila parviflora, commonly known as the slender water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Limnophila parviflora plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
