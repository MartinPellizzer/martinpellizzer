Lonicera maximowiczii var. okamotoana

Lonicera maximowiczii var. okamotoana, commonly known as the Okamoto's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruits are red berries that ripen in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
