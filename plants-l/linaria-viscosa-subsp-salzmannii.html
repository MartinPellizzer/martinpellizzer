Linaria viscosa subsp. salzmannii

Linaria viscosa subsp.

salzmannii, commonly known as the sticky toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Linaria viscosa subsp.

salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
