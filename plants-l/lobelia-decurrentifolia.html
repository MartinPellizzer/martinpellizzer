Lobelia decurrentifolia

Lobelia decurrentifolia, commonly known as the trailing lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.
