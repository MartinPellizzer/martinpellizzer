Lobelia laxa

Lobelia laxa, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia laxa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
