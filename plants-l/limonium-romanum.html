Limonium romanum

Limonium romanum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and sandy soils, showing a high tolerance for saline conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium romanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
