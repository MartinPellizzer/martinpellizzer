Lilium speciosum var. punctatum

Lilium speciosum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing fragrant, trumpet-shaped blossoms in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
