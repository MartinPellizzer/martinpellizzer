Lippia africana var. villosa

Lippia africana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy lippia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicinal use.
