Limonia octandra

Limonia octandra, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish rind and a sour, acidic pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citric acid.

Limonia octandra thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
