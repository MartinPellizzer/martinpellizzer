Lindenia acutiflora

Lindenia acutiflora, commonly known as the acute-flowered lindenia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in the summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Lindenia acutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
