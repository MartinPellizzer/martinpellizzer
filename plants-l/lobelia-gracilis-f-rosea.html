Lobelia gracilis f. rosea

Lobelia gracilis f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, showy flowers in shades of pink to rose-red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
