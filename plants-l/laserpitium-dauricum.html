Laserpitium dauricum

Laserpitium dauricum, commonly known as Daurian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Laserpitium dauricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
