Linaria supina var. pruinosa

Linaria supina var. pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving the plant its pruinose appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
