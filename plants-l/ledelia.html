Ledelia

Ledelia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ledelia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
