Lechea major var. divaricata

Lechea major var. divaricata, commonly known as the mountain holly or blue holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically blue-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in dry to moderately moist conditions.
