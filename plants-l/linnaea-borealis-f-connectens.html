Linnaea borealis f. connectens

Linnaea borealis f. connectens, commonly known as the twinleaf sandwort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It features small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces delicate, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in pairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and pine forests.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
