Leontopodium kamtschaticum

Leontopodium kamtschaticum, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white flowers and hairy, leafless stems. The plant thrives in harsh, high-altitude environments with cold temperatures and strong winds. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often associated with resilience and is protected in many regions.
