Lloydia graeca subsp. trinervia

Lloydia graeca subsp.

trinervia, commonly known as the Greek lloydia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces white or pale pink blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. Lloydia graeca subsp.

trinervia is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
