Lampranthus henricii

Lampranthus henricii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It grows in a compact, shrub-like form with a basal rosette of leaves.

Its vibrant floral display makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
