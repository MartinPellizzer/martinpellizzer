Leonia racemosa

Leonia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered leonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Leonia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its pleasant scent.
