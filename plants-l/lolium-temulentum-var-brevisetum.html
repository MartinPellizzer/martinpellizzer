Lolium temulentum var. brevisetum

Lolium temulentum var. brevisetum, commonly known as the short-setted ryegrass, is a variant of the annual ryegrass species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter, more clustered inflorescences compared to the typical form. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
