Lobivia maximiliana var. caespitosa

Lobivia maximiliana var. caespitosa, commonly known as the Clustered Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
