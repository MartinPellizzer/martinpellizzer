Lepidium campestre var. simplex

Lepidium campestre var. simplex, commonly known as field cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. It produces small white or yellow flowers and has a pungent, peppery scent similar to other members of its family.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in culinary and medicinal applications.
