Lobelia macropoda

Lobelia macropoda, commonly known as the large-flowered lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
