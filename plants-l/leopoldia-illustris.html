Leopoldia illustris

Leopoldia illustris, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns resembling stained glass. The plant produces its showy blooms in late summer to early autumn, typically on tall, slender stems that rise above the foliage. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the base of the plant.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Leopoldia illustris is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
