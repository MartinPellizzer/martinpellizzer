Lygistum axillare

Lygistum axillare, commonly known as the false indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Lygistum axillare is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and adaptability to various soil types.
