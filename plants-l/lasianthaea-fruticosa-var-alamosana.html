Lasianthaea fruticosa var. alamosana

Lasianthaea fruticosa var. alamosana, commonly known as the Alamosan cholla, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that branch out into a shrub-like form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It thrives in arid environments and is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
