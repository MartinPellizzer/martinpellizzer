Laserpitium trifurcatum

Laserpitium trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-branched hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, typically growing in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. It is characterized by its trifurcated (three-branching) leaves, which give it its specific epithet.

Laserpitium trifurcatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
