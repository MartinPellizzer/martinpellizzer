Limeum sulcatum var. scabridum

Limeum sulcatum var. scabridum, commonly known as the rough-leaved limeum, is a variant of the limeum genus characterized by its distinctive, textured foliage.

Its leaves are typically scabrous, featuring a rough, sandpapery texture that distinguishes it from other varieties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a sought-after plant among botanists and gardeners alike.
