Laser cordifolium

Laser cordifolium, commonly known as the "laser plant," is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that resemble tiny lasers, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Laser cordifolium is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is used in both horticulture and as a decorative plant.
