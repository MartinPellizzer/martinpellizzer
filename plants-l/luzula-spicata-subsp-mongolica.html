Luzula spicata subsp. mongolica

Luzula spicata subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
