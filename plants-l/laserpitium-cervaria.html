Laserpitium cervaria

Laserpitium cervaria, commonly known as the sheep's garlic, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
