Lipochaeta tagetiflora

Lipochaeta tagetiflora, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark purple disk flowers, forming a striking composite flower head. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Lipochaeta tagetiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
