Lysimachia simulans

Lysimachia simulans, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Lysimachia simulans is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes.
