Leptochloa palmeri

Leptochloa palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's grass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a clumping growth habit, which help it survive in harsh conditions.

Leptochloa palmeri is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
