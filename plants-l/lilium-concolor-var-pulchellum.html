Lilium concolor var. pulchellum

Lilium concolor var. pulchellum, commonly known as the Sierra Valley lily, is a variant of the white lily native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, white to pale pink flowers with a distinct, recurved shape. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
