Lysionotus pauciflorus var. indutus

Lysionotus pauciflorus var. indutus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hairy lythrum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lythraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
