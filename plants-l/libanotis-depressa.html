Libanotis depressa

Libanotis depressa, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of only a few inches. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Libanotis depressa is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers.
