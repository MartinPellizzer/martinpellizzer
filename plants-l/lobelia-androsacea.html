Lobelia androsacea

Lobelia androsacea, commonly known as the Indian tobacco, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lobelia androsacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
