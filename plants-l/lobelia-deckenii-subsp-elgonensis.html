Lobelia deckenii subsp. elgonensis

Lobelia deckenii subsp.

elgonensis, commonly known as the Elgon lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of East Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in montane forests and grasslands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
