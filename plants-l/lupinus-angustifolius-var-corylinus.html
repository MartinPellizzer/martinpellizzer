Lupinus angustifolius var. corylinus

Lupinus angustifolius var. corylinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spicate inflorescences of pea-like flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
