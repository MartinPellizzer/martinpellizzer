Loxanthocereus jajoanus

Loxanthocereus jajoanus, commonly known as the Jajoan night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pink. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to desert conditions and a popular choice for xeriscaping.
