Lepidosperma officinale

Lepidosperma officinale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paper plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that consist of tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Lepidosperma officinale is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
