Lythrum axillare

Lythrum axillare, commonly known as the narrowleaf lythrum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common lythrum. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats like meadows and wetlands. Its striking purple or pink flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom during the summer months.

The leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture.
