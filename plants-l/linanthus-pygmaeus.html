Linanthus pygmaeus

Linanthus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf linanthus, is a small flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Linanthus pygmaeus is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
