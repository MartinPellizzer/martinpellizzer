Leontodon taraxaconaster

Leontodon taraxaconaster, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, yellow composite flower head. The plant is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It produces a wind-dispersed seed with a distinctive parachute-like structure.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
