Lythrum maculatum

Lythrum maculatum, commonly known as spotted lythrum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters.

Lythrum maculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
