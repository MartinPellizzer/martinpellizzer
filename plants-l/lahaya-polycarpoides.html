Lahaya polycarpoides

Lahaya polycarpoides, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
