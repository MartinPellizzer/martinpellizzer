Lobelia trullifolia var. trichella

Lobelia trullifolia var. trichella, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
