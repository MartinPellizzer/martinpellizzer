Lampranthus pakpassensis

Lampranthus pakpassensis, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling tulips in appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments.

Lampranthus pakpassensis is also known for its ability to produce a second bloom during the rainy season, adding to its ornamental value.
