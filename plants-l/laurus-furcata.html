Laurus furcata

Laurus furcata, commonly known as the forked laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Laurus furcata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties.
