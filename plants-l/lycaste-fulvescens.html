Lycaste fulvescens

Lycaste fulvescens, commonly known as the golden lycaste, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and produces multiple pseudobulbs that are covered in a dense, yellowish-brown velvety coating. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often folded and have a reddish or brownish tint. It produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Lycaste fulvescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
