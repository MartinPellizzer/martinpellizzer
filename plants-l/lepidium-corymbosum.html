Lepidium corymbosum

Lepidium corymbosum, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lepidium corymbosum typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

It is a hardy species that can tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
