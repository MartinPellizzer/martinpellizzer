Lampranthus holensis

Lampranthus holensis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that resemble poker chips, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, adapting to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its vibrant colors and drought tolerance make it a standout in Mediterranean and coastal climates.
