Lepidopetalum subdichotomum

Lepidopetalum subdichotomum, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
