Lycopodium canaliculatum

Lycopodium canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its narrow, ribbed stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores necessary for reproduction.

Lycopodium canaliculatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
