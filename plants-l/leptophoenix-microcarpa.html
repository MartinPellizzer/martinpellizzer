Leptophoenix microcarpa

Leptophoenix microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf sago palm, is a slow-growing, cycad-like plant native to Madagascar.

It features a short, unbranched trunk with a crown of feather-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by cone-like structures that release seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
