Lobelia livingstoniana

Lobelia livingstoniana, commonly known as the Livingston's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Lobelia livingstoniana is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
