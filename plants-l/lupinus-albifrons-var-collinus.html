Lupinus albifrons var. collinus

Lupinus albifrons var. collinus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant has palmate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
