Lychnis sibirica subsp. villosula

Lychnis sibirica subsp.

villosula, commonly known as the Siberian fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia. It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pink to purple, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as tundra and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.
