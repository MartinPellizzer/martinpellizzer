Lasianthus puberulus

Lasianthus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
