Lepidium oblongum var. insulare

Lepidium oblongum var. insulare, commonly known as the island wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flower colors.
