Lastrea pinnata

Lastrea pinnata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Lastrea pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
