Lindmania guianensis var. vestita

Lindmania guianensis var. vestita, commonly known as the Guianan velvet fern, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in Guyana and parts of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a water-holding cup that supports a unique ecosystem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
