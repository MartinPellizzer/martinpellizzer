Logania serpyllifolia

Logania serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common logania, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
