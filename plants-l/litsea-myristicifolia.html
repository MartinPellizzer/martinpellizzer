Litsea myristicifolia

Litsea myristicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant-laurel or sweet myristica, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red drupes that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
