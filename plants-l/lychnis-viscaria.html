Lychnis viscaria

Lychnis viscaria, commonly known as sticky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit and produces small, pink or purple flowers. The plant's leaves are alternate and often covered in fine, sticky hairs, which give it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Lychnis viscaria is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
