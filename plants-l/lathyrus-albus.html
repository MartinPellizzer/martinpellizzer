Lathyrus albus

Lathyrus albus, commonly known as the white vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural research.
