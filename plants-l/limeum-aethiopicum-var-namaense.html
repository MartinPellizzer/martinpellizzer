Limeum aethiopicum var. namaense

Limeum aethiopicum var. namaense, commonly known as the Namibian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and intense sunlight, making it a resilient species.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic environments and low-maintenance gardening.
