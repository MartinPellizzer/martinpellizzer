Laserpitium siler subvar. balcanicum

Laserpitium siler subvar. balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan siler, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
