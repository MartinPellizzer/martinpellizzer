Litsea verticillata var. brevipetiolata

Litsea verticillata var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-petioled litsea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible. Its aromatic foliage and compact growth make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
