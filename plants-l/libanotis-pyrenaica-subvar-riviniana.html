Libanotis pyrenaica subvar. riviniana

Libanotis pyrenaica subvar. riviniana, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pyrenees region.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or mountainous habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native environment.
