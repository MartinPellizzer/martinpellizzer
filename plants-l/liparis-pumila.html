Liparis pumila

Liparis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It has rhizomatous growth and often forms dense colonies in its natural environment.

Liparis pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
