Lagochilus platyacanthus

Lagochilus platyacanthus, commonly known as the broad-fruited rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in summer. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing on slopes or cliffs.

Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
