Leucocroton subpeltatus var. epeltatus

Leucocroton subpeltatus var. epeltatus, commonly known as the white croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically green with a white or cream-colored margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
