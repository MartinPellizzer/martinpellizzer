Leucas biflora

Leucas biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale blue, attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
