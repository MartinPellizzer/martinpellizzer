Litsea ichangensis

Litsea ichangensis, commonly known as the Chinese wild laurier-pear, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by oval-shaped berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Litsea ichangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
