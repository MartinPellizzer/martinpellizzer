Limonia monadelpha

Limonia monadelpha, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-colored fruits that are typically sour and seedy. Limonia monadelpha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various climates.
