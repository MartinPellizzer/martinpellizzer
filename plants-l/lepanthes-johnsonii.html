Lepanthes johnsonii

Lepanthes johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink coloration. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes johnsonii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
