Laurus sumatrensis

Laurus sumatrensis, commonly known as Sumatran laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
