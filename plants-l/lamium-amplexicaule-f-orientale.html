Lamium amplexicaule f. orientale

Lamium amplexicaule f. orientale, commonly known as the oriental dead nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, and it produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
