Lisianthius loranthoides

Lisianthius loranthoides, commonly known as the mistletoe lichen, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stem-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in dispersal.

This species is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree health and biodiversity.
