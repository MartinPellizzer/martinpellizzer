Leucas tettensis

Leucas tettensis, commonly known as the Tettens mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions.
