Lupinus sellulus var. medius

Lupinus sellulus var. medius, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus sellulus var. medius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
