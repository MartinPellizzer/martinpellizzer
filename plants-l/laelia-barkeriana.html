Laelia barkeriana

Laelia barkeriana, commonly known as the Barker's laelia, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh mountain conditions.

Laelia barkeriana is also noted for its resilience and ease of cultivation, contributing to its popularity in both amateur and professional horticulture.
