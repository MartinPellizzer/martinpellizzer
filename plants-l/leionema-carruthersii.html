Leionema carruthersii

Leionema carruthersii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Leionema carruthersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
