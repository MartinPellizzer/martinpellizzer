Lactuca brunoniana

Lactuca brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and is known for its mild, slightly bitter taste.

Lactuca brunoniana is sometimes used in herbal remedies and is considered a wild edible plant.
