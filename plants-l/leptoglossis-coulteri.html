Leptoglossis coulteri

Leptoglossis coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a dense rosette pattern.

The orchid produces a single, elongated inflorescence bearing numerous fragrant, violet or white flowers.
