Limnia gypsophiloides

Limnia gypsophiloides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, sun-exposed areas.

Limnia gypsophiloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
