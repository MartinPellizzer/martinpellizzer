Leptobalanus parvifolius

Leptobalanus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam. The plant has needle-like leaves that are short and densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its cones are small and typically hang downward, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in high-altitude forests with cool, moist climates.
