Leucothoe martii var. puberula

Leucothoe martii var. puberula, commonly known as the smooth leucothoe, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a smooth texture, giving it its common name.

It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.
