Lepidium divaricatum subsp. ecklonii

Lepidium divaricatum subsp.

ecklonii, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, branching stems and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats. Lepidium divaricatum subsp.

ecklonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
