Loranthus turbinatus

Loranthus turbinatus, commonly known as the Turban Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the family Loranthaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching itself to the branches of various trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often red or orange in color, attracting insects for pollination. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds dispersed by animals.

Loranthus turbinatus is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid forest environments.
