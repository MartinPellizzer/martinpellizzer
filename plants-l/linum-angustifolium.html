Linum angustifolium

Linum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Linum angustifolium is also valued for its historical use in fiber production.
