Linaria saturejoides subsp. angustealata

Linaria saturejoides subsp.

angustealata, commonly known as narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in raceme-like clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
