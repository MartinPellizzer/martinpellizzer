Leersia friesii

Leersia friesii, commonly known as the African fountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pale green or brown, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Leersia friesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
