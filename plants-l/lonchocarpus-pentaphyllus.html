Lonchocarpus pentaphyllus

Lonchocarpus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
