Lupinus ligulatus var. barbatus

Lupinus ligulatus var. barbatus, commonly known as the barbed lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a distinctive barbed edge, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
