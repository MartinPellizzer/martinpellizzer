Lespedeza lagopoides

Lespedeza lagopoides, commonly known as the partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites due to its adaptability to various soil types.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility, and serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
