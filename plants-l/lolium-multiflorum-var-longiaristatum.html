Lolium multiflorum var. longiaristatum

Lolium multiflorum var. longiaristatum, commonly known as the long-leaved ryegrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a significant amount of pollen, which contributes to its role in pollination networks.

The variety is valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and high biomass production.
