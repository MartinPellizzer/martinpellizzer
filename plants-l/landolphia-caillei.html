Landolphia caillei

Landolphia caillei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
