Ludwigia epilobioides subsp. greatrexii

Ludwigia epilobioides subsp.

greatrexii, commonly known as the Greatrex's Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a semi-aquatic species that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along streams and rivers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small and pinkish-red, arranged in dense clusters along the stems.

It is often cultivated in water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
