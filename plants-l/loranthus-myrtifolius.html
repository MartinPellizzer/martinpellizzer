Loranthus myrtifolius

Loranthus myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on species of the Myrtaceae family.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and health of its host tree.
