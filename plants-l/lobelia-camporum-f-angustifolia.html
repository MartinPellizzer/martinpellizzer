Lobelia camporum f. angustifolia

Lobelia camporum f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, angular stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
