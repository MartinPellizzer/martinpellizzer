Liparis laurisilvatica

Liparis laurisilvatica, commonly known as the laurel forest orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the laurel forests of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

Liparis laurisilvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
