Litsea elongata f. postiglabra

Litsea elongata f. postiglabra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a botanical variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
