Lesquerella montana var. suffruticosa

Lesquerella montana var. suffruticosa, commonly known as mountain lesquerella, is a shrub-like variant of the lesquerella species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces seeds rich in lesquerolic acid, which has industrial and cosmetic applications. It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential biotechnological uses.
