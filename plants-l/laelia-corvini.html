Laelia corvini

Laelia corvini, commonly known as the Corvini's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing clusters of pink to violet petals with yellow lips. Laelia corvini thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to mountainous habitats make it a notable species in the orchid family.
