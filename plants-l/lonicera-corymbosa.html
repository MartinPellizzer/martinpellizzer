Lonicera corymbosa

Lonicera corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that ripen in the fall and are edible for both humans and wildlife.

Lonicera corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various pollinators and birds.
