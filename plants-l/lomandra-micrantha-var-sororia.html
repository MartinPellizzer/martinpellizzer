Lomandra micrantha var. sororia

Lomandra micrantha var. sororia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lomandra, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in dense, tufted clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in summer, which are clustered in racemes. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
