Laelia kautskyana

Laelia kautskyana, commonly known as the Kautsky's orchid, is a species native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features long, narrow leaves and bears showy, fragrant flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its bloom period typically occurs in late winter to early spring.

Laelia kautskyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
