Lecythis corrugata

Lecythis corrugata, commonly known as the corrugated lecythium, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and corrugated leaves, which give it its common name. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain a single large seed, often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and seeds have been utilized for their medicinal properties, including antipyretic and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
