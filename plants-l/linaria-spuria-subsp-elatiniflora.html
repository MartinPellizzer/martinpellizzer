Linaria spuria subsp. elatiniflora

Linaria spuria subsp.

elatiniflora, commonly known as the silky toadflax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
