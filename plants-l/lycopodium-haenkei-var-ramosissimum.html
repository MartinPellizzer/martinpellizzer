Lycopodium haenkei var. ramosissimum

Lycopodium haenkei var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the clustered clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves that form a low, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically found at the base of the foliage. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its compact growth and unique reproductive features.
