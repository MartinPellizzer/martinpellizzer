Liatris scariosa var. virginiana

Liatris scariosa var. virginiana, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and showy pink to purple flower heads. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
