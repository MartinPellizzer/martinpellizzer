Lupinus meionanthus var. heteranthus

Lupinus meionanthus var. heteranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant restoration efforts.
