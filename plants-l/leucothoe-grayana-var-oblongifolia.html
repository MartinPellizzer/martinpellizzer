Leucothoe grayana var. oblongifolia

Leucothoe grayana var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblongleaf flameleaf bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
