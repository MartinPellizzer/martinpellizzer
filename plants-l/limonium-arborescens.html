Limonium arborescens

Limonium arborescens, commonly known as the tree limonium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a woody base and branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

Limonium arborescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
