Lythrum acinifolium f. grandifolium

Lythrum acinifolium f. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved purple coneflower, is a botanical variant of the common lythrum species.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and showy, deep purple to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
