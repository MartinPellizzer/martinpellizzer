Lysimachia santapaui

Lysimachia santapaui, commonly known as the Hawaiian chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Lysimachia santapaui plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting local insect populations and contributing to soil stability.
