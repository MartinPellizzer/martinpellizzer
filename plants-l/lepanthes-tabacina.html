Lepanthes tabacina

Lepanthes tabacina, commonly known as the Tobacco orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often hairy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Lepanthes tabacina is known for its delicate appearance and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.

Its name is derived from the resemblance of its flowers to tobacco blossoms.
