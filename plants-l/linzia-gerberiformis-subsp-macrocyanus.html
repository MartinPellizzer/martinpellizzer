Linzia gerberiformis subsp. macrocyanus

Linzia gerberiformis subsp.

macrocyanus, commonly known as the blue-flowered linzia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more intensely colored flowers compared to other members of the species.
