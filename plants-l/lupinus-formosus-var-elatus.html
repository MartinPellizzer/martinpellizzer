Lupinus formosus var. elatus

Lupinus formosus var. elatus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and tall, spindly stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged along the stem.

Lupinus formosus var. elatus is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.
