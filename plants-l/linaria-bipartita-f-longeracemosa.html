Linaria bipartita f. longeracemosa

Linaria bipartita f. longeracemosa, commonly known as the long-headed toadflax, is a horticultural form of the species Linaria bipartita.

It is characterized by its elongated racemes of yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant, elongated flower clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
