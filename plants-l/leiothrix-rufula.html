Leiothrix rufula

Leiothrix rufula, commonly known as the red-leaved ironwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a leathery texture.

Leiothrix rufula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
