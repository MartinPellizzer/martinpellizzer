Lagenophora forsteri

Lagenophora forsteri, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
