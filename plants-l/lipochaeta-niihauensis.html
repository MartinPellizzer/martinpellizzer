Lipochaeta niihauensis

Lipochaeta niihauensis, commonly known as Niihau ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian island of Niihau.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its habitat. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
