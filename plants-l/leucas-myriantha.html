Leucas myriantha

Leucas myriantha, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in umbels, blooming in late summer.

Leucas myriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
