Lobivia wrightiana var. winteriana

Lobivia wrightiana var. winteriana, commonly known as the Winter's lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South America.

It features rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint, and it produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts.
