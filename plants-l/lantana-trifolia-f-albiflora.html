Lantana trifolia f. albiflora

Lantana trifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower lantana, is a compact shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
