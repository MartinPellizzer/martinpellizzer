Linaria weilleri

Linaria weilleri, commonly known as the Weiller's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria weilleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive, daisy-like appearance.
