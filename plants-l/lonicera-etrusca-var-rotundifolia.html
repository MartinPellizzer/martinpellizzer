Lonicera etrusca var. rotundifolia

Lonicera etrusca var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Etruscan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in early spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often attracting birds.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
