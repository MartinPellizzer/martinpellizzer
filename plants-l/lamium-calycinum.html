Lamium calycinum

Lamium calycinum, commonly known as the purple deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

Lamium calycinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in natural landscaping.
