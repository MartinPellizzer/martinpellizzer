Lagotis glauca subsp. takedana

Lagotis glauca subsp.

takedana, commonly known as the Takeda's bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
