Leonurus oblongifolius

Leonurus oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hound's-tongue, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. Its small, blue or purple flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

This species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
