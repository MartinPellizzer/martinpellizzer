Linaria japonica var. geminiflora

Linaria japonica var. geminiflora, commonly known as the Japanese toadflax, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in clusters, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
