Leucosyke negrosensis

Leucosyke negrosensis, commonly known as the Negros dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. It has a short flowering period, usually during the rainy season, and relies on insect pollination.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
