Limonium multiceps

Limonium multiceps, commonly known as the sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium multiceps is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize sandy soils in maritime environments.
