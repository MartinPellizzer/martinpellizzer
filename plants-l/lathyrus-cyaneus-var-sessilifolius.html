Lathyrus cyaneus var. sessilifolius

Lathyrus cyaneus var. sessilifolius, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with sessile leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
