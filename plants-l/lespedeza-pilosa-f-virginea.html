Lespedeza pilosa f. virginea

Lespedeza pilosa f. virginea, commonly known as the Virginia lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. It has small, oval leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Lespedeza pilosa f. virginea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
