Lauro-cerasus myrtifolia

Lauro-cerasus myrtifolia, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lauro-cerasus myrtifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and evergreen foliage.
