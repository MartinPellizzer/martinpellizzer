Linanthastrum nuttallii var. pachyphyllum

Linanthastrum nuttallii var. pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved flaxvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust, thickened leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
