Leucothoe serrata

Leucothoe serrata, commonly known as the serrated leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often reddish or bronze in color, especially in the fall. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
