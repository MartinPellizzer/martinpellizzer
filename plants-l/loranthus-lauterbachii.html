Loranthus lauterbachii

Loranthus lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some ecological benefits by serving as a food source for birds and insects. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that contain seeds dispersed by animals. Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.

It is often found in the canopy of rainforests, where it thrives in humid and shaded environments.
