Linaria sieberi var. laevipes

Linaria sieberi var. laevipes, commonly known as the smooth-flowered toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, smooth texture, giving the plant its varietal name "laevipes.". It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
