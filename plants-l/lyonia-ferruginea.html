Lyonia ferruginea

Lyonia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils within forested areas. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, often turning red in the fall.

The rusty lyonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
