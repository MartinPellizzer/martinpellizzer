Linaria iranica

Linaria iranica, commonly known as the Iranian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Linaria iranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
