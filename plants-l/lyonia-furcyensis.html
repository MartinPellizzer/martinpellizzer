Lyonia furcyensis

Lyonia furcyensis, commonly known as the red-tipped lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its small, oval leaves are dark green with a distinctive red or purple tip, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Lyonia furcyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to native plant ecosystems.
