Lobivia prestoana

Lobivia prestoana, commonly known as the Prestona Lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, specifically Bolivia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded rosette of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
