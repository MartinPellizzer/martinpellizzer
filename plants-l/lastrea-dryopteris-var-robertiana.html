Lastrea dryopteris var. robertiana

Lastrea dryopteris var. robertiana, commonly known as the Robert's maidenhair fern, is a rare and elegant fern species native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble those of the common maidenhair fern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
