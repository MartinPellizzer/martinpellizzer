Lepanthes convexa

Lepanthes convexa, commonly known as the convex lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and elongated, providing a contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Lepanthes convexa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
