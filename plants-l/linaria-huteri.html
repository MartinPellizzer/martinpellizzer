Linaria huteri

Linaria huteri, commonly known as the New Zealand fritillary, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the plantain family, Amaranthaceae, and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and alpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base.

Linaria huteri is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native range.
