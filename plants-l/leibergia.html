Leibergia

Leibergia, commonly known as the "Christmas cactus," is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in the winter months. The plant typically has segmented stems that can be green, red, or purple, depending on the species. Its leaves are usually short and rounded, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Leibergia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
