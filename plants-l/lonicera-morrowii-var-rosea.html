Lonicera morrowii var. rosea

Lonicera morrowii var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, rose-pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil conditions.
