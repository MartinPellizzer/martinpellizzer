Lactuca virosa subsp. cornigera

Lactuca virosa subsp.

cornigera, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides and in fields. It has a taproot system and is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

The subspecies cornigera is distinguished by its more pronounced spines compared to other varieties of lactuca.
