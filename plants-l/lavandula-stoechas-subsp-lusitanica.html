Lavandula stoechas subsp. lusitanica

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, pinecone-like bracts that enclose the flower clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and use in essential oil production.
