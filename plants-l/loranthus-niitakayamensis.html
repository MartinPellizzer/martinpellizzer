Loranthus niitakayamensis

Loranthus niitakayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to Japan, where it thrives in temperate forest environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pink flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife while also impacting its host trees.
