Lippia culmenicola

Lippia culmenicola, commonly known as the Mexican mint or mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lippia culmenicola is also a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant scent.
