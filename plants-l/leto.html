Leto

Leto, commonly known as the sacred tree of life in ancient Mesopotamian culture, is a symbol of fertility and renewal.

It is believed to have been a divine tree associated with the goddess Ishtar, representing abundance and cosmic order. The plant is often depicted with golden fruits and lush green leaves, signifying prosperity and vitality. Its roots are said to connect to the underworld, linking the earthly and spiritual realms.

In botanical terms, Leto is not a specific plant but a symbolic representation of a tree with deep cultural and mythological significance.
