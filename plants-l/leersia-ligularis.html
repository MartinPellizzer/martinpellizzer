Leersia ligularis

Leersia ligularis, commonly known as broadleaf arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often broader at the base. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in panicles and bloom in late summer.

Leersia ligularis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
