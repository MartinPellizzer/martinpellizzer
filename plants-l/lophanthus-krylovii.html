Lophanthus krylovii

Lophanthus krylovii, commonly known as the Siberian lophanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are arranged in a compact, low-growing form. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lophanthus krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh climates.
