Linum suffruticosum subsp. dolomiticum

Linum suffruticosum subsp.

dolomiticum, commonly known as the Dolomitic Flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the limestone regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in areas with high alkalinity.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and produces small, pale blue or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions and is often found in mountainous or coastal habitats.
