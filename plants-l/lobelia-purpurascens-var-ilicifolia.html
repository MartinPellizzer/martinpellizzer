Lobelia purpurascens var. ilicifolia

Lobelia purpurascens var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the silky lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a soft, hairy texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
