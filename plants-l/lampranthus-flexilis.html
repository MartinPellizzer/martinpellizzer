Lampranthus flexilis

Lampranthus flexilis, commonly known as the "Rainbow Flower," is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, strap-like leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
