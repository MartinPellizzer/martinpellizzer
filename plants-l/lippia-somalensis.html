Lippia somalensis

Lippia somalensis, commonly known as Somali thyme, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xerophytic environments.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
