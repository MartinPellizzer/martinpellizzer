Ledebouria kirkii

Ledebouria kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's bulbous lily, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-brown seeds within its capsule-like fruit, which is typically released after the flowers have faded. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
