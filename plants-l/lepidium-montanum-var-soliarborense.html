Lepidium montanum var. soliarborense

Lepidium montanum var. soliarborense, commonly known as the mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous terrain.
