Leymus racemosus subsp. sabulosus

Leymus racemosus subsp.

sabulosus, commonly known as the sand wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of western North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dune environments or along shorelines. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to purple in color.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
