Litobrochia mannii

Litobrochia mannii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, while its stems are often hairy.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
