Longetia

Longetia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Longetia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
