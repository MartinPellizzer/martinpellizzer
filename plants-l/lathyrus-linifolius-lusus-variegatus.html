Lathyrus linifolius lusus variegatus

Lathyrus linifolius lusus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated grass vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves with distinct variegation patterns, often featuring white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, which are typically clustered in racemes. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in grassy areas or along woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some environments.
