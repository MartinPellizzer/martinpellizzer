Lepanthes concavella

Lepanthes concavella, commonly known as the concave lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Lepanthes concavella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
