Lupinus milleri

Lupinus milleri, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Lupinus milleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
