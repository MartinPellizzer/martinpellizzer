Leucopogon phyllostachys

Leucopogon phyllostachys, commonly known as the bamboo leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale-colored flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight zigzag pattern along their edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
