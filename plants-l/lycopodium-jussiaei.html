Lycopodium jussiaei

Lycopodium jussiaei, commonly known as the Jussiaei clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are usually located at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Lycopodium jussiaei plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
