Luisia tenuifolia var. evangelinae

Luisia tenuifolia var. evangelinae, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has slender, trailing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
