Lavandula rotundifolia var. incisa

Lavandula rotundifolia var. incisa, commonly known as narrow-leaf lavender, is a subspecies of lavender native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of fragrant purple flowers. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandin, are widely used in aromatherapy and perfumery.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
