Lodhra javanica

Lodhra javanica, commonly known as the Java loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
