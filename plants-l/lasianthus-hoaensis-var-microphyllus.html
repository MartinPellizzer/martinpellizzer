Lasianthus hoaensis var. microphyllus

Lasianthus hoaensis var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Vietnam and Cambodia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
