Liabum nigropilosum

Liabum nigropilosum, commonly known as black-hairy liabum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Liabum nigropilosum thrives in dry, sandy soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
