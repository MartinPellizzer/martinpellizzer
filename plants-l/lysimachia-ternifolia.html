Lysimachia ternifolia

Lysimachia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leafed chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls of three, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia ternifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
