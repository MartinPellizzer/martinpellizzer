Linaria volgensis

Linaria volgensis, commonly known as the velvet fritillary, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Linaria volgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
