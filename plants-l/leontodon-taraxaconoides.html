Leontodon taraxaconoides

Leontodon taraxaconoides, commonly known as the meadow dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves and a yellow composite flower head that develops into a crown of white pappus hairs. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces a single, showy flower that blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
