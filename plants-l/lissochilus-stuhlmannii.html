Lissochilus stuhlmannii

Lissochilus stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's beard-leaf, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, fragrant orchid flowers that bloom in clusters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
