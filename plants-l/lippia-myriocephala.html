Lippia myriocephala

Lippia myriocephala, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in herbal teas and culinary applications. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and it is relatively drought-tolerant.

Lippia myriocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
