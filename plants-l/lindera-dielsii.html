Lindera dielsii

Lindera dielsii, commonly known as the Chinese wild laurier, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that matures in the fall, contributing to its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
