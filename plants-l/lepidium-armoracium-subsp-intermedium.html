Lepidium armoracium subsp. intermedium

Lepidium armoracium subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate cress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a peppery aroma when crushed.

The species is valued for its edible leaves and is sometimes cultivated as a leafy green vegetable.
