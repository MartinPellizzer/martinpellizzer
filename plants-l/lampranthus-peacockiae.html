Lampranthus peacockiae

Lampranthus peacockiae, commonly known as the peacock's eye, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

This succulent plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers, giving it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
