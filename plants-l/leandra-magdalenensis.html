Leandra magdalenensis

Leandra magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalena orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly found in Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a blend of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments such as forests or near water sources. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Leandra magdalenensis is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
