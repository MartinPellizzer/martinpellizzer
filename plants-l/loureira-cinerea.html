Loureira cinerea

Loureira cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive grayish-blue to whitish upper surface with a finely granular texture. The plant's thallus is composed of a symbiotic association between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the air and rain. It is often found on tree trunks, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to pollution, it serves as an indicator species for environmental health.
