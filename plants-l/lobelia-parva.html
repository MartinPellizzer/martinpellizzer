Lobelia parva

Lobelia parva, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like bellflowers.

Lobelia parva is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
