Loranthus gjellerupii

Loranthus gjellerupii, commonly known as the Gjellerup's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light captured by its leaves. The plant has a distinctive, flattened, disc-like shape with small, scale-like leaves and white or pale green berries. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
