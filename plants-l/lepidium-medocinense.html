Lepidium medocinense

Lepidium medocinense, commonly known as the Medocine cress, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and has slender, branching stems.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
