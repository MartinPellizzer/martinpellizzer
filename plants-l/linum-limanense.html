Linum limanense

Linum limanense, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Linum limanense is well adapted to coastal environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils with high salinity.
