Leandra ribesiiflora

Leandra ribesiiflora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Leandra ribesiiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
