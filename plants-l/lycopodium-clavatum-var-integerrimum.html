Lycopodium clavatum var. integerrimum

Lycopodium clavatum var. integerrimum, commonly known as the club moss, is a small evergreen plant characterized by its compact, dense growth habit.

It features spirally arranged, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is a primitive vascular plant with a long evolutionary history.
