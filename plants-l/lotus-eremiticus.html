Lotus eremiticus

Lotus eremiticus, commonly known as the desert lotus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to minimize water loss.

Lotus eremiticus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
