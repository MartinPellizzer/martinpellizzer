Lepidium bipinnatum

Lepidium bipinnatum, commonly known as the two-lobed cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lepidium bipinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
