Leptospermum flavescens var. citratum

Leptospermum flavescens var. citratum, commonly known as the lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, lemon-scented leaves that emit a strong citrus aroma. The plant produces yellowish flowers and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique fragrance, it is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
