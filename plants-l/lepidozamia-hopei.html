Lepidozamia hopei

Lepidozamia hopei, commonly known as the Hope's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. Native to the subtropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into hard, woody seed pods.

Due to its slow growth and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation purposes.
