Liedea

Liedea, commonly known as the mistletoe of the southern United States, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. Liedea produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. The plant is hemiparasitic, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from the host tree while also performing photosynthesis.

Its presence can have both ecological and economic impacts on the forest ecosystems it inhabits.
