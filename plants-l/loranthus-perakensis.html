Loranthus perakensis

Loranthus perakensis, commonly known as the Perak mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are often dispersed by birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its humid environment.

Loranthus perakensis plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, though its parasitic nature can weaken or even kill its host trees.
