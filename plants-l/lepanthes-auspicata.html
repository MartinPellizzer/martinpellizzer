Lepanthes auspicata

Lepanthes auspicata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a compact, pseudobulbous growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often clustered around the base of the plant.

Lepanthes auspicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
