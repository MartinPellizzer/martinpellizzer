Lastrea maculata

Lastrea maculata, commonly known as the spotted leaf succulent, is a member of the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fleshy, often ribbed stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique spotted leaves and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
