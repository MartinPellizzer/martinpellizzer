Loncoperis stipata

Loncoperis stipata, commonly known as the stippled broom.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Loncoperis stipata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
