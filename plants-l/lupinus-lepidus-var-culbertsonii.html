Lupinus lepidus var. culbertsonii

Lupinus lepidus var. culbertsonii, commonly known as the Culbertson's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces elliptical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
