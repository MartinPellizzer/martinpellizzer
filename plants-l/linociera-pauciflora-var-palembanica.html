Linociera pauciflora var. palembanica

Linociera pauciflora var. palembanica, commonly known as the Palembanica lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in humid and tropical climates. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is a common feature in the Araceae family.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in aquatic gardens.
