Lupinus sierrae-blancae

Lupinus sierrae-blancae, commonly known as the Sierra White Lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California's Sierra Nevada region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
