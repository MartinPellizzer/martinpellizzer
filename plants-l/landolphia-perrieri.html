Landolphia perrieri

Landolphia perrieri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy night-blooming flowers. The plant produces distinctive, lantern-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Landolphia perrieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
