Lundia cordata

Lundia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny environments. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Lundia cordata is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
