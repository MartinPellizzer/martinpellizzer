Lonicera sempervirens f. minor

Lonicera sempervirens f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden use.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
