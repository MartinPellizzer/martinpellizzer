Lobelia welwitschii var. albiflora

Lobelia welwitschii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered lobelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
