Leuzea insignis

Leuzea insignis, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks. It is a perennial species that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

Leuzea insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
