Loudetia gossweileri

Loudetia gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Loudetia gossweileri is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
