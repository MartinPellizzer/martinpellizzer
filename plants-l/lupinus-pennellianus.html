Lupinus pennellianus

Lupinus pennellianus, commonly known as the California lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus pennellianus plays an important role in ecological systems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
