Leea philippinensis

Leea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine leea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Leeaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
