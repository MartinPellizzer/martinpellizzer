Lygodesmia grandiflora var. arizonica

Lygodesmia grandiflora var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona rocknettle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
