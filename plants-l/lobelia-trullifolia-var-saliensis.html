Lobelia trullifolia var. saliensis

Lobelia trullifolia var. saliensis, commonly known as the climbing lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that often climb or trail over rocky substrates. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
