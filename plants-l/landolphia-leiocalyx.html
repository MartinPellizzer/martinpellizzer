Landolphia leiocalyx

Landolphia leiocalyx, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces woody, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
