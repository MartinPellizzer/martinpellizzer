Lygodium scandens

Lygodium scandens, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical fern species belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its long, flexible fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb and spread over other vegetation. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which release spores for reproduction. It thrives in warm, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique structure and growth habit make it an important component of rainforest ecosystems.
