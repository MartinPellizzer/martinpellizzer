Lepidium montanum var. neeseae

Lepidium montanum var. neeseae, commonly known as Neese's peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. This variant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
