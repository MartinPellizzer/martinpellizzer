Leiothrix curvifolia var. plantago

Leiothrix curvifolia var. plantago, commonly known as the curled-leaf leiothrix, is a unique variant of the leiothrix genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling foliage that gives it a striking visual appearance. This plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
