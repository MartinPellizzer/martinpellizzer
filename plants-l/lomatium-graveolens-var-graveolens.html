Lomatium graveolens var. graveolens

Lomatium graveolens var. graveolens, commonly known as the western cowparsnip, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a robust, hollow stem that is often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves with lobed margins and a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, umbrella-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The roots are thick and fleshy, containing compounds that have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
