Lycoris koreana

Lycoris koreana, commonly known as Korean rain lily, is a flowering plant native to Korea and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in the autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers are often accompanied by a strong, spicy fragrance, which is a notable botanical trait.

Lycoris koreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display.
