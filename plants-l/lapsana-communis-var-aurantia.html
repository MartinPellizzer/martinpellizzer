Lapsana communis var. aurantia

Lapsana communis var. aurantia, commonly known as the golden marsh woundwort, is a variant of the common marsh woundwort.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, characterized by its finely divided leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in moist soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
