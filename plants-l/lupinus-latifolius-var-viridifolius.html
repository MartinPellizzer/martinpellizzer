Lupinus latifolius var. viridifolius

Lupinus latifolius var. viridifolius, commonly known as the greenleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, distinguishing it from other lupine species. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
