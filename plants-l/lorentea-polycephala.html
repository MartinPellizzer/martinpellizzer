Lorentea polycephala

Lorentea polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a short, herbaceous stem with basal leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its unique morphology, with multiple flower heads arising from a single stem, makes it a distinctive and sought-after ornamental plant.

Lorentea polycephala thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
