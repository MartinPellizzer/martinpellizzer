Linum × alnense

Linum × alnense, commonly known as the hybrid flax, is a perennial plant that belongs to the Linaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Linum usitatissimum (common flax) and Linum vulgare (wild flax). The plant typically features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a soft, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Linum × alnense is often found in disturbed soils and is cultivated for its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses.
