Lupinus elegans var. violaceus

Lupinus elegans var. violaceus, commonly known as the violet lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Lupinus elegans var. violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
