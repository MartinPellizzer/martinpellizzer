Litsea punctulata

Litsea punctulata, commonly known as the spotted laurier, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish dotted pattern on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, yellow berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Litsea punctulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
