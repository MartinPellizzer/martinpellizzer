Leria pumila

Leria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf leria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. Leria pumila thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
