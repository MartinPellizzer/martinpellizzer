Lycopodium tamariscinum

Lycopodium tamariscinum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a sporophyte that produces spores rather than seeds. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are clustered in cone-like formations called strobila.

Lycopodium tamariscinum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
