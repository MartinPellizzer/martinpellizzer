Lepanthes pachychila

Lepanthes pachychila, commonly known as the thick-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are often violet or purple in color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Lepanthes pachychila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
