Lodhra pulchra

Lodhra pulchra, commonly known as the sacred bell flower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Lodhra pulchra is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
