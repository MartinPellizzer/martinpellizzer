Leptocereus grantianus

Leptocereus grantianus, commonly known as the Grant's night bloomer, is a cactus native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, slender stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it a striking and unique addition to desert gardens.
