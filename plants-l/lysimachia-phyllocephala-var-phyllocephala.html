Lysimachia phyllocephala var. phyllocephala

Lysimachia phyllocephala var. phyllocephala, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features upright, branching stems covered in opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
