Lonchocarpus cristatus

Lonchocarpus cristatus, commonly known as the crowned senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in woody pods, which split open to release the seeds when mature.
