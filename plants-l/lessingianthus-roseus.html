Lessingianthus roseus

Lessingianthus roseus, commonly known as the roseate sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
