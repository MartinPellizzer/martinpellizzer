Lathyrus saronensis

Lathyrus saronensis, commonly known as the Saronic vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and grasslands. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

Lathyrus saronensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in soil stabilization.
