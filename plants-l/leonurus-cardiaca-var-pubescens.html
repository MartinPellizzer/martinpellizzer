Leonurus cardiaca var. pubescens

Leonurus cardiaca var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heartwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often overlooked member of the flora in its native habitats.
