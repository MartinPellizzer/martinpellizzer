Litsea buinensis

Litsea buinensis, commonly known as the Burmese litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

Litsea buinensis is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood and ornamental appeal.
