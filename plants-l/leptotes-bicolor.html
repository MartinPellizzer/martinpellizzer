Leptotes bicolor

Leptotes bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive two-toned appearance.
