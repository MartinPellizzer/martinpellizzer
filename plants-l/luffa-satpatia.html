Luffa satpatia

Luffa satpatia, commonly known as the sponge gourd or loofah, is a tropical and subtropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and distinctive, fibrous fruits that mature into spongy, lightweight structures. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are typically harvested when mature, and the inner fibrous material is used as a natural exfoliant in skincare products.

Luffa satpatia is also valued for its edible young fruits and its ability to provide shade and support for other plants in agroforestry systems.
