Lonchocarpus praecox

Lonchocarpus praecox, commonly known as the early flowering legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with small, clustered blooms.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
