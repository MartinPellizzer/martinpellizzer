Lactuca inermis var. inermis

Lactuca inermis var. inermis, commonly known as the common prickly lettuce, is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and a flowering head that produces small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has a taproot system and produces a milky sap when the stems or leaves are broken.

The species is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse environments.
