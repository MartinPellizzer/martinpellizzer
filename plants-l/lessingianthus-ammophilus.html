Lessingianthus ammophilus

Lessingianthus ammophilus, commonly known as the sandhill sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in sandy or arid soils, often found in coastal and inland dunes. The plant features yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, and its leaves are typically hairy and narrow. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including high winds and salt spray.

Due to its unique habitat and appearance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
