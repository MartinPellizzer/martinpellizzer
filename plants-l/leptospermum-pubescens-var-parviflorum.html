Leptospermum pubescens var. parviflorum

Leptospermum pubescens var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

This species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
