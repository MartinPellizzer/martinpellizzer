Lachenalia multifolia

Lachenalia multifolia, commonly known as the many-flowered lachenalia, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
