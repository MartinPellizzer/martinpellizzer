Luzula pumila

Luzula pumila, commonly known as the dwarf woodrush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Luzula pumila is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging conditions.
