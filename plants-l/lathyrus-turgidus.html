Lathyrus turgidus

Lathyrus turgidus, commonly known as the broad-leafed pea, is a perennial climbing plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces showy, pea-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus turgidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
