Lacmellea speciosa

Lacmellea speciosa, commonly known as the showy lacmellea, is a striking member of the Pteridaceae family.

It is a perennial fern that features delicate, lace-like fronds with a pale green to silvery hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Lacmellea speciosa make it a visually appealing and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
