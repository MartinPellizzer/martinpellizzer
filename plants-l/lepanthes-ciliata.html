Lepanthes ciliata

Lepanthes ciliata, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with cilia-like hairs along the edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, often clustered in a raceme.

Lepanthes ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
