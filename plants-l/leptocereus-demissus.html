Leptocereus demissus

Leptocereus demissus, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in white spines. The plant produces striking clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
