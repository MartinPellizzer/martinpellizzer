Lepidium foliosum var. fruticulosum

Lepidium foliosum var. fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
