Leucas menthifolia var. menthifolia

Leucas menthifolia var. menthifolia, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its aromatic foliage contains compounds such as citral, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
