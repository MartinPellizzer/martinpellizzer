Lecanthus pileoides

Lecanthus pileoides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in rocky outcrops and along coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
