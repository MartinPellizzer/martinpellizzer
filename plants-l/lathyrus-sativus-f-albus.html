Lathyrus sativus f. albus

Lathyrus sativus f. albus, commonly known as the white field bean, is a variant of the common vetch.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
