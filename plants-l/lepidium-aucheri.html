Lepidium aucheri

Lepidium aucheri, commonly known as "Aucher's cress," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically lobed or divided. Lepidium aucheri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
