Lapsanastrum takasei

Lapsanastrum takasei, commonly known as the Japanese hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Lapsanastrum takasei is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
