Lygodium surinamense

Lygodium surinamense, commonly known as the Surinam climbing fern, is a tropical fern belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
