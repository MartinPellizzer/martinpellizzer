Lysimachia monnieri

Lysimachia monnieri, commonly known as lesser knotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Lysimachia monnieri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
