Linum pubescens subsp. pubescens

Linum pubescens subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is a member of the Linaceae family and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
