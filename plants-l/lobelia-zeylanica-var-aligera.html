Lobelia zeylanica var. aligera

Lobelia zeylanica var. aligera, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
