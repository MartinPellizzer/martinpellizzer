Lupinus argenteus subsp. parviflorus

Lupinus argenteus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as silver lupine or silverleaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
