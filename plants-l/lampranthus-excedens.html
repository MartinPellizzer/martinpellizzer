Lampranthus excedens

Lampranthus excedens, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a South African succulent belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling tiny firecrackers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces showy blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lampranthus excedens is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
