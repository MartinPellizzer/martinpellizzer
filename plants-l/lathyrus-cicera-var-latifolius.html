Lathyrus cicera var. latifolius

Lathyrus cicera var. latifolius, commonly known as broad-leaved sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a broad, ovate shape. Its flowers are typically blue to violet, with a spurred petal at the back, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
