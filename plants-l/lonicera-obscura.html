Lonicera obscura

Lonicera obscura, commonly known as the dark honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late spring, which are usually yellow or white in color. Its berries are red or orange and serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Lonicera obscura is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
