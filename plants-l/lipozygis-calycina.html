Lipozygis calycina

Lipozygis calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Lipozygis calycina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and wetlands.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
