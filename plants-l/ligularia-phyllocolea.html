Ligularia phyllocolea

Ligularia phyllocolea, commonly known as the broadleaf ligularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
