Leonurus campestris

Leonurus campestris, commonly known as field horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and parts of North America.

It is an erect, herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows up to 60 cm in height. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Leonurus campestris is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
