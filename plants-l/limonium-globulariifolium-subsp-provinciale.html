Limonium globulariifolium subsp. provinciale

Limonium globulariifolium subsp.

provinciale, commonly known as the coastal blueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence that bears numerous small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy, drought-tolerant nature.
