Linum corymbiferum subsp. asperifolium

Linum corymbiferum subsp.

asperifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf flax, is a member of the Linaceae family and is native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, hairy leaves, which give it its common name. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
