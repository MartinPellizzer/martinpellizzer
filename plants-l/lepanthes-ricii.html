Lepanthes ricii

Lepanthes ricii, commonly known as the Ricii Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with vibrant colors ranging from white to pink and purple.

Lepanthes ricii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
