Ledum dilatatum

Ledum dilatatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved labrador tea, is a perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers contain aromatic compounds, including methyl salicylate, which gives it a distinct medicinal and sometimes toxic properties.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.
