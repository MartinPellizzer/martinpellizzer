Lauro-cerasus marginata

Lauro-cerasus marginata, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used as a hedge or ornamental plant.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
