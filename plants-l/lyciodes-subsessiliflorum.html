Lyciodes subsessiliflorum

Lyciodes subsessiliflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lycopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and sessile, giving it its specific epithet "subsessiliflorum.".

The species is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
