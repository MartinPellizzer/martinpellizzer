Lamium gundelsheimeri

Lamium gundelsheimeri, commonly known as the German deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink or purple. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lamium gundelsheimeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
