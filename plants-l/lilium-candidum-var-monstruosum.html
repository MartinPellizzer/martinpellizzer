Lilium candidum var. monstruosum

Lilium candidum var. monstruosum, commonly known as the giant white lily, is a striking variation of the white lily native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, making it a standout in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and floral arrangements.
