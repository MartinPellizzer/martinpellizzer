Lysimachia subcapitata

Lysimachia subcapitata, commonly known as the clustered yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia subcapitata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
