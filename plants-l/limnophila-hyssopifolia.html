Limnophila hyssopifolia

Limnophila hyssopifolia, commonly known as water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, with its roots submerged and stems floating on the surface. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
