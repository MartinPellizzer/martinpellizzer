Lasiocoma petrophiloides

Lasiocoma petrophiloides, commonly known as the rock-loving lasiocoma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
