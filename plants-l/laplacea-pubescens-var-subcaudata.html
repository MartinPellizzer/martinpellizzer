Laplacea pubescens var. subcaudata

Laplacea pubescens var. subcaudata, commonly known as the hairy laplacea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "pubescens.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
