Laurus sericea

Laurus sericea, commonly known as silver laurel.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage due to dense covering of fine hairs. The leaves are opposite, elliptic, and have a fragrant aroma when crushed. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
