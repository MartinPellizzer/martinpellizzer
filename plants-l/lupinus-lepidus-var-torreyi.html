Lupinus lepidus var. torreyi

Lupinus lepidus var. torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush areas.
