Lathyrus tshitirungensis

Lathyrus tshitirungensis, commonly known as the Tshitirung vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring.

Its seed pods are elongated and contain several angular seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.
