Lasiochlamys planchonellifolia

Lasiochlamys planchonellifolia, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
