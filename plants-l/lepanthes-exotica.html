Lepanthes exotica

Lepanthes exotica, commonly known as the exotic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a distinct lip that aids in pollination.

Lepanthes exotica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
