Litsea rotundata

Litsea rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaved litsea, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, dark blue berries that are edible.

Litsea rotundata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
