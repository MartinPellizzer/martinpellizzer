Leonotis laxifolia

Leonotis laxifolia, commonly known as the blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, arranged in dense, brush-like clusters. The plant is herbaceous, with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in grasslands and open woodlands.

Leonotis laxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
