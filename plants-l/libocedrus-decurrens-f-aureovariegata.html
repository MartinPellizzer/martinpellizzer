Libocedrus decurrens f. aureovariegata

Libocedrus decurrens f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated incense cedar, is a variant of the incense cedar species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a striking golden-yellow variegation on the leaves. The plant is a coniferous evergreen with a conical shape and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in crossed pairs, typical of the Cupressaceae family.

The bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental appeal.
