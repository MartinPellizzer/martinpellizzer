Lactuca tatarica var. pulchella

Lactuca tatarica var. pulchella, commonly known as Tatarian lettuce or Siberian lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a traditional food in some cultures.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
