Lacinaria scariosa var. salutans

Lacinaria scariosa var. salutans, commonly known as the Salutant Lacebark, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, papery, and often flaky bark.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant's leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish or yellowish, and it produces small, round fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental bark and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
