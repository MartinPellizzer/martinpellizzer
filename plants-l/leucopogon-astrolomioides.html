Leucopogon astrolomioides

Leucopogon astrolomioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and the flowers are usually pink or white, attracting nectar-feeding insects.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
