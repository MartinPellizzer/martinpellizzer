Lycopodium tenue

Lycopodium tenue, commonly known as slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobila. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium tenue plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
