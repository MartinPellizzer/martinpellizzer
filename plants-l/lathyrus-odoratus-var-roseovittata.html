Lathyrus odoratus var. roseovittata

Lathyrus odoratus var. roseovittata, commonly known as the rose-vined sweet pea, is a cultivar prized for its ornamental value.

It is a climbing legume with tendrils that allow it to climb supports, and it produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in a range of colors. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and a vining habit that makes it suitable for trellises or fences. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a decorative touch to gardens.

The variety is noted for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
