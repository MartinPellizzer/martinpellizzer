Lagerstroemia thorelii

Lagerstroemia thorelii, commonly known as the African crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, mottled bark and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
