Lycium fremontii var. gracilipes

Lycium fremontii var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid regions.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
