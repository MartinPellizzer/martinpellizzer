Laphamia specicola

Laphamia specicola, commonly known as the Florida panhandle laphamia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white flowers during the spring and early summer. It is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
