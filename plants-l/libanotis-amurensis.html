Libanotis amurensis

Libanotis amurensis, commonly known as the Amur bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It features erect, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
