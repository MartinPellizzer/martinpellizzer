Lavandula vera var. pyrenaica

Lavandula vera var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or dry environments. Its essential oils, rich in linalool and lavandin, contribute to its use in aromatherapy and fragrance industries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
