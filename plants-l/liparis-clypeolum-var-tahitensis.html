Liparis clypeolum var. tahitensis

Liparis clypeolum var. tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian liparis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in loose clusters. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and have a waxy texture, adapting to humid and shaded environments.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific tropical conditions.
