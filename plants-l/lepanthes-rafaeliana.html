Lepanthes rafaeliana

Lepanthes rafaeliana, commonly known as the Rafael's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with a distinctive pouch-like lip that aids in pollination.

Lepanthes rafaeliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
