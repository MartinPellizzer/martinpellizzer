Lachnaea eriocephala var. purpurea

Lachnaea eriocephala var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-headed lachnaea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. This variety is distinguished by its vibrant purple inflorescence, which adds ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
