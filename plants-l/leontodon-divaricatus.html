Leontodon divaricatus

Leontodon divaricatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering heads consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
