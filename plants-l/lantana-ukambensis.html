Lantana ukambensis

Lantana ukambensis, commonly known as African lantana, is a flowering shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Lantana ukambensis is often used in traditional medicine and ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
