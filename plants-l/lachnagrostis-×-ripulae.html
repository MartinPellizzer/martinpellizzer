Lachnagrostis × ripulae

Lachnagrostis × ripulae, commonly known as the hybrid tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
