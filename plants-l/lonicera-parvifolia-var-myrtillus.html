Lonicera parvifolia var. myrtillus

Lonicera parvifolia var. myrtillus, commonly known as the dwarf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. The plant has slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
