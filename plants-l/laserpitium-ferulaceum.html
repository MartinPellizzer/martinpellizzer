Laserpitium ferulaceum

Laserpitium ferulaceum, commonly known as the common hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets that resemble petals. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
