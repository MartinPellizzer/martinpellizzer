Leandra parvifolia

Leandra parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered leandra, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
