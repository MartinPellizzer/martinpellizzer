Lerchenfeldia flexuosa

Lerchenfeldia flexuosa, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
