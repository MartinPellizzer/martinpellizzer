Lysimachia ovatifolia

Lysimachia ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and its leaves are ovate to broadly ovate, often with a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Lysimachia ovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
