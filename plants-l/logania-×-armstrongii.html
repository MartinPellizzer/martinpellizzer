Logania × armstrongii

Logania × armstrongii, commonly known as the Armstrong's logania, is a hybrid shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its bark is rough and fissured.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
