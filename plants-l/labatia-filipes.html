Labatia filipes

Labatia filipes, commonly known as the featherleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, winding stems and heart-shaped leaves with a feathery texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, opening in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Labatia filipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
