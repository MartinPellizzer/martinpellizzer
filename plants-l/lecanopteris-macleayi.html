Lecanopteris macleayi

Lecanopteris macleayi, commonly known as the Macleay's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating fronds that help it thrive in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, where it can receive adequate sunlight for photosynthesis. Its rhizomes form dense mats that stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Lecanopteris macleayi plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering nutrients and supporting biodiversity in its ecosystem.
