Lychnis glaberrima

Lychnis glaberrima, commonly known as the rough fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Lychnis glaberrima is known for its ability to thrive in challenging environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
