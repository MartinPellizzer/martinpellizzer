Lobivia boliviensis var. croceantha

Lobivia boliviensis var. croceantha, commonly known as the Bolivian lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the high Andes of Bolivia.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
