Luzula sudetica proles alpina

Luzula sudetica proles alpina, commonly known as the Alpine woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in moist, rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Luzula sudetica proles alpina is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
