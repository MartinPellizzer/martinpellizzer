Limonium occidentale

Limonium occidentale, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium occidentale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
