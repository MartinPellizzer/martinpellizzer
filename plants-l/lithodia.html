Lithodia

Lithodia, commonly known as the rock-dwelling plant, is a unique species that thrives in rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and compact growth form, which help it conserve water in harsh conditions. The plant typically produces small, pale flowers that bloom in the early morning. Lithodia is often found in regions with poor soil and high sunlight exposure.

Its ability to survive in extreme climates makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
