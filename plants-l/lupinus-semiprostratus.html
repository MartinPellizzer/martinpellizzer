Lupinus semiprostratus

Lupinus semiprostratus, commonly known as the creeping lupine, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus semiprostratus is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant in disturbed soils.
