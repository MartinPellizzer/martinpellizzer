Lepidium pubescens var. typica

Lepidium pubescens var. typica, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Its structure and morphology are adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and humid climates.
