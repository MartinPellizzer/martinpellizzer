Lupinus luteus var. melanospermus

Lupinus luteus var. melanospermus, commonly known as the blackseed lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with 5-7 leaflets and produces small, black or dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
