Lycopodium dielsii

Lycopodium dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' clubmoss, is a species of small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and the presence of small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around its stems. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, or strobili, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium dielsii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
