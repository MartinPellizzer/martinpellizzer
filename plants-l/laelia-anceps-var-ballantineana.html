Laelia anceps var. ballantineana

Laelia anceps var. ballantineana, commonly known as the Ballantine's Laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its pseudobulbs are sturdy and allow it to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
