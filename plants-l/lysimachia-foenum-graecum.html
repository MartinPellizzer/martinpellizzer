Lysimachia foenum-graecum

Lysimachia foenum-graecum, commonly known as hen's tooth, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
