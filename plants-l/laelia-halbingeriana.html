Laelia halbingeriana

Laelia halbingeriana, commonly known as the Halbinger orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within cloud forests. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a mix of white, pink, and purple hues. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Laelia halbingeriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
