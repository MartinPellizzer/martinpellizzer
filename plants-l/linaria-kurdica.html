Linaria kurdica

Linaria kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Linaria kurdica has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
