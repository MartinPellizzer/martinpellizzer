Leptospermum splendens

Leptospermum splendens, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and heathland environments. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
