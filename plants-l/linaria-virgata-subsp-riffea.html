Linaria virgata subsp. riffea

Linaria virgata subsp.

riffea, commonly known as the Narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
