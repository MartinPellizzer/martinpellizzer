Lychnophora bahiensis

Lychnophora bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or *pau-rosa*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. Due to overharvesting, it is now considered a threatened species in its native habitat.
