Lonicera epsomiensis

Lonicera epsomiensis, commonly known as the Epsom honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lonicera epsomiensis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.
