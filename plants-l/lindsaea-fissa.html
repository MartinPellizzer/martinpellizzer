Lindsaea fissa

Lindsaea fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lindsaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that often form dense, mat-like structures on trees. The plant produces small, clustered spore capsules that are typically borne on specialized leaf tips. Lindsaea fissa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
