Languas uviformis

Languas uviformis, commonly known as the grape-shaped langua, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, grape-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically displaying a range of colors from pale yellow to deep orange.

Languas uviformis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual appearance.
