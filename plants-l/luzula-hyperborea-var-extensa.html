Luzula hyperborea var. extensa

Luzula hyperborea var. extensa, commonly known as the Arctic or alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and arctic tundra environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish or brownish flowers in dense clusters. It is adapted to withstand harsh climates with low temperatures and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude and polar ecosystems.
