Linnea

Linnea, commonly known as the sweet william, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Linum in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to red, pink, and purple. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Linnea produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
