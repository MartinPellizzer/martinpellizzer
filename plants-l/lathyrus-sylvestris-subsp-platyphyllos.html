Lathyrus sylvestris subsp. platyphyllos

Lathyrus sylvestris subsp.

platyphyllos, commonly known as the broad-leafed vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
