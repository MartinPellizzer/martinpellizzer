Lipochaeta lobata var. hosakae

Lipochaeta lobata var. hosakae, commonly known as the Hosaka's lipochaeta, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowering period occurs during the warmer months.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
