Lithospermum incurvum

Lithospermum incurvum, commonly known as the curly rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a small, dark red capsule that contains several seeds.

Lithospermum incurvum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
