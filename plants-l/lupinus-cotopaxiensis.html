Lupinus cotopaxiensis

Lupinus cotopaxiensis, commonly known as the Cotopaxi lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus cotopaxiensis is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
