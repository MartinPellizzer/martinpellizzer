Loudetia ternata

Loudetia ternata, commonly known as the three-angled grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Loudetia ternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
