Lasianthus stephanocalycinus

Lasianthus stephanocalycinus, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, making it a visually appealing species in botanical gardens.
