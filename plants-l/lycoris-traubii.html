Lycoris traubii

Lycoris traubii, commonly known as the Rain Lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in the autumn. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support the plant's growth and flowering cycle.

Lycoris traubii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to woodland gardens.
