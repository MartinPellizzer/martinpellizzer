Laportea meyeniana

Laportea meyeniana, commonly known as the meyenian ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy purple to pinkish-blue flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically clustered in dense, rounded clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Laportea meyeniana is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
