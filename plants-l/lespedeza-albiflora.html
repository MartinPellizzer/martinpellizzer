Lespedeza albiflora

Lespedeza albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in open woods, meadows, and disturbed sites, and is valued for its ecological benefits, including nitrogen fixation and erosion control.
