Lemna hyalina

Lemna hyalina, commonly known as the transparent water clover, is a small, floating aquatic plant belonging to the duckweed family.

It is characterized by its delicate, oval-shaped leaves that are typically translucent or lightly pigmented. The plant reproduces rapidly through vegetative propagation, with new shoots developing from the nodes along its stems. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth, it can play a significant role in aquatic ecosystems, both as a food source and as an indicator of water quality.
