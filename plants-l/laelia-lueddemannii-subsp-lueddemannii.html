Laelia lueddemannii subsp. lueddemannii

Laelia lueddemannii subsp.

lueddemannii, commonly known as the Lueddemann's Laelia, is a species of orchid native to the high altitudes of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a velvety texture.

The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, showcasing a range of colors from pale yellow to deep orange. It prefers cool, bright conditions with good air circulation and is relatively hardy for an orchid.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
