Lathyrus grandiflorus

Lathyrus grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lathyrus grandiflorus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
