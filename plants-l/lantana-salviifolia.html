Lantana salviifolia

Lantana salviifolia, commonly known as the shrub lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to thrive in humid climates. It has a woody stem and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Lantana salviifolia is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping.
