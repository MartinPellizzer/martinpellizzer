Linum usitatissimum subsp. latifolium

Linum usitatissimum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf flax, is a subspecies of the flax plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and slender, blue or purple flowers.

This plant is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production, and for its seeds, which are rich in oil. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
