Lonicera gracilipes var. adenophora

Lonicera gracilipes var. adenophora, commonly known as the腺花 honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
