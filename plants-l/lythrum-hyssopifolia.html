Lythrum hyssopifolia

Lythrum hyssopifolia, commonly known as rosepink lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces showy, pink to purple flower spikes during the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge.

Lythrum hyssopifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
