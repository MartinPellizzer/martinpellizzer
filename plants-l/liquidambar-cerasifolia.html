Liquidambar cerasifolia

Liquidambar cerasifolia, commonly known as sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and distinctive star-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky seed balls that are a unique feature of its reproductive system. Its wood is hard and heavy, historically used for furniture and tool handles.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
