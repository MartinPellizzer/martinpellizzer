Lastrea catoptera

Lastrea catoptera, commonly known as the mirror leaf cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate beauty to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

The plant's ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
