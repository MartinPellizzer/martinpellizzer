Laportea laxiflora

Laportea laxiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Laportea laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
