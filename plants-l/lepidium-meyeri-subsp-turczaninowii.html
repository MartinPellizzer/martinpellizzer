Lepidium meyeri subsp. turczaninowii

Lepidium meyeri subsp.

turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's cress, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
