Laportea gaudichaudiana

Laportea gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream sides. It is known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a strong, mint-like scent.

Laportea gaudichaudiana is also valued for its traditional uses in Hawaiian medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
