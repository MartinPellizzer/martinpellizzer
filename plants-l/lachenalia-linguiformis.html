Lachenalia linguiformis

Lachenalia linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
