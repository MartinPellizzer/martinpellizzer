Liparia sericea

Liparia sericea, commonly known as the silky liparia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, often displaying a pale blue or violet color.

Liparia sericea is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
