Lachenalia margaretae

Lachenalia margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae lachenalia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, spindly stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires periods of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among succulent enthusiasts.
