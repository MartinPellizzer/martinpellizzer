Lepidolopha nuratavica

Lepidolopha nuratavica, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with intricate veining patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Lepidolopha nuratavica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
