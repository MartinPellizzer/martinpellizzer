Linum flavum var. serrulatum

Linum flavum var. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated flax.

This variant is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It is often found in meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
