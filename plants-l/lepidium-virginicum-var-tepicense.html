Lepidium virginicum var. tepicense

Lepidium virginicum var. tepicense, commonly known as Mexican peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly larger size and more robust growth compared to other Lepidium species.
