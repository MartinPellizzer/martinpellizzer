Luisia secunda

Luisia secunda, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Luisia secunda thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
