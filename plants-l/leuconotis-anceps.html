Leuconotis anceps

Leuconotis anceps, commonly known as the white veldt grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Leuconotis anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
