Lupinus espinarensis

Lupinus espinarensis, commonly known as the Espinarensis lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

Lupinus espinarensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
