Liatris pumila

Liatris pumila, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands or prairies. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Liatris pumila is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
