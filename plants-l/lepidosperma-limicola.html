Lepidosperma limicola

Lepidosperma limicola, commonly known as the swamp sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy environments, where it thrives in waterlogged soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green or brown. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Lepidosperma limicola is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes various other sedges and related plants.
