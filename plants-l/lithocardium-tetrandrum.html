Lithocardium tetrandrum

Lithocardium tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-stamen rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Lithocardium tetrandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
