Leontodon helveticus

Leontodon helveticus, commonly known as the Alpine hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a single, erect head, surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. It is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Leontodon helveticus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
