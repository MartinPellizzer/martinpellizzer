Leontodon autumnalis var. genuinus

Leontodon autumnalis var. genuinus, commonly known as the autumn leontodon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing yellow-orange ray flowers and dark brown disk flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
