Lemaireocereus quevedonis

Lemaireocereus quevedonis, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular plant with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of white, woolly bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
