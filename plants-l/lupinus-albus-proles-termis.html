Lupinus albus proles termis

Lupinus albus proles termis, commonly known as white lupin, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Lupinus albus proles termis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in both agricultural and ornamental settings.
