Lagochilus diacanthophyllus subsp. diacanthophyllus

Lagochilus diacanthophyllus subsp.

diacanthophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal uses and ornamental value in horticulture.
