Lupinus chrysanthus

Lupinus chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus chrysanthus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
