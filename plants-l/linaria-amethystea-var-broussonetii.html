Linaria amethystea var. broussonetii

Linaria amethystea var. broussonetii, commonly known as the Broussonet's throatwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It produces small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
