Lonicera hirsuta var. interior

Lonicera hirsuta var. interior, commonly known as the interior honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its red berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The shrub thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.
