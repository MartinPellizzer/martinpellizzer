Lobivia violaciflora

Lobivia violaciflora, commonly known as the violet-flowered lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert-adapted plants. The plant produces striking violet-pink flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
