Lupinus menziesii var. aureus

Lupinus menziesii var. aureus, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and fern-like foliage, which are typical of the lupine genus. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its root system hosts nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
