Lobelia fulgens f. maculata

Lobelia fulgens f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted fireplant, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange with a distinctive white or yellow spot near the base. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique appearance and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and bloom.
