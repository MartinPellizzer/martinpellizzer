Litsea quadrangularis

Litsea quadrangularis, commonly known as the square-leafed litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn dark purple when mature.

The tree is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
