Limodorum plantagineum

Limodorum plantagineum, commonly known as the lesser twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, which are pollinated by insects. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
