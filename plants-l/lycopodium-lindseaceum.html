Lycopodium lindseaceum

Lycopodium lindseaceum, commonly known as the Lindseaceae clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and the presence of microphylls, which are small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in sporangia that are clustered in strobila, which are the cone-like structures typical of clubmosses. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices.

Lycopodium lindseaceum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
