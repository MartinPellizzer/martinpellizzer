Lobivia costata

Lobivia costata, commonly known as the Costate Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. Lobivia costata is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
