Lippia nepetacea

Lippia nepetacea, commonly known as Mexican lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its fragrant properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. Lippia nepetacea thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its essential oils contain compounds like citral and limonene, which contribute to its distinctive lemony scent and various medicinal uses.
