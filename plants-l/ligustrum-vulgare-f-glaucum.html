Ligustrum vulgare f. glaucum

Ligustrum vulgare f. glaucum, commonly known as the whitestem privet, is a shrub characterized by its distinctive whitish-gray bark and small, dark green leaves.

It is a cultivar of the common privet, native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.

This variety is particularly valued for its compact growth habit and year-round visual interest.
