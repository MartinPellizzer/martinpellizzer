Ledebouria cryptopoda

Ledebouria cryptopoda, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its bulbs are adapted for storing water and nutrients, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Ledebouria cryptopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
