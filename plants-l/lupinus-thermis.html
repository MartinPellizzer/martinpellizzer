Lupinus thermis

Lupinus thermis, commonly known as the thermophilic lupin, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, dry climates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lupinus thermis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
