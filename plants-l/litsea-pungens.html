Litsea pungens

Litsea pungens, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Litsea pungens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, spicy aroma.
