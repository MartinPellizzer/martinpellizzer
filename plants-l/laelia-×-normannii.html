Laelia × normannii

Laelia × normannii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Laelia purpurea and Laelia anceola.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers, often in shades of pink, red, and white, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
