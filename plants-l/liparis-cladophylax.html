Liparis cladophylax

Liparis cladophylax, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
