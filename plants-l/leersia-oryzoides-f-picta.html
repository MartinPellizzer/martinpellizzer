Leersia oryzoides f. picta

Leersia oryzoides f. picta, commonly known as the painted leersia, is a variant of the common leersia, characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

This ornamental grass features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinct color patterns, often including shades of white, yellow, and green. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both garden borders and container planting.
