Lasiochloa echinata

Lasiochloa echinata, commonly known as the spiny grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, and the flowers are usually inconspicuous.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
