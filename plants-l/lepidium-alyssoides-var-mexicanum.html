Lepidium alyssoides var. mexicanum

Lepidium alyssoides var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
