Lacinaria scariosa var. uniflora

Lacinaria scariosa var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered lacinaria, is a variant of the lacinaria plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Africa, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a distinctive, papery layer. Its flowers are typically solitary, with a striking combination of pale blue to violet petals.

Botanically, it is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
