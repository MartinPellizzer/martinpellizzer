Lycopus europaeus var. sinensis

Lycopus europaeus var. sinensis, commonly known as Chinese water-hyssop, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, blooming in late summer.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported cooling and detoxifying properties.
