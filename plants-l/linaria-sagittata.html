Linaria sagittata

Linaria sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that resemble arrows. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Linaria sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
