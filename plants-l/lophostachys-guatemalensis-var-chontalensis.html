Lophostachys guatemalensis var. chontalensis

Lophostachys guatemalensis var. chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontalensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
