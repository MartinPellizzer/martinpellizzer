Lonicera longa

Lonicera longa, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its red berries are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Lonicera longa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
