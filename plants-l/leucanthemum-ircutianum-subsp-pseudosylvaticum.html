Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp. pseudosylvaticum

Leucanthemum ircutianum subsp.

pseudosylvaticum, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features showy white ray florets surrounded by yellow disk flowers, forming daisy-like blooms.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
