Lysimachia zawadskii

Lysimachia zawadskii, commonly known as the Zawadski's yellow loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats.

Lysimachia zawadskii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance of wet conditions and soil stabilization properties.
