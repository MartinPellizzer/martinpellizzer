Linum aroanium

Linum aroanium, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linum aroanium thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
