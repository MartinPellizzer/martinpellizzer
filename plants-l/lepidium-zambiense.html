Lepidium zambiense

Lepidium zambiense, commonly known as Zambian cress, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Central and Southern Africa, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, white or yellowish flowers and has a hairy stem covered with small, scale-like structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Lepidium zambiense is valued for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
