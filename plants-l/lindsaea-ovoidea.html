Lindsaea ovoidea

Lindsaea ovoidea, commonly known as the egg-shaped fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Lindsaeaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves that are often clustered in a rosette-like formation. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. Lindsaea ovoidea thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and a subject of study in plant morphology.
