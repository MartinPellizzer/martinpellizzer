Leptospermum cuneatum

Leptospermum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces a fragrant essential oil, which is often used in aromatherapy and skincare products. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
