Lepanthes parvilabia

Lepanthes parvilabia, commonly known as the dwarf violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single flower spike with several small blooms.

Lepanthes parvilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
