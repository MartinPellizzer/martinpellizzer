Loranthus chunguensis

Loranthus chunguensis, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds through photosynthesis. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

Loranthus chunguensis is often found in forested areas of China and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
