Ledonia populifolia var. longifolia

Ledonia populifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf ledonia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruit capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
