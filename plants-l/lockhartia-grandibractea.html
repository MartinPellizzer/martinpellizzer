Lockhartia grandibractea

Lockhartia grandibractea, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its humid, shaded environment. Its leaves are typically broad and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Lockhartia grandibractea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
