Lycium meyenianum

Lycium meyenianum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to China and other parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a useful plant in herbal remedies and a challenging weed in agricultural settings.
