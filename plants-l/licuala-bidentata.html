Licuala bidentata

Licuala bidentata, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture and are arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to humid, tropical climates.
