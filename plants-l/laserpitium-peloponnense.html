Laserpitium peloponnense

Laserpitium peloponnense, commonly known as the Peloponnesian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is found in areas such as Greece and southern Italy. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its historical and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
