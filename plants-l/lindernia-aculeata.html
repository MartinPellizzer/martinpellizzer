Lindernia aculeata

Lindernia aculeata, commonly known as the spiked lindernia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lindernia aculeata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
