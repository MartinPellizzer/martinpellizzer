Leichhardtia venusta

Leichhardtia venusta, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a soft texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
