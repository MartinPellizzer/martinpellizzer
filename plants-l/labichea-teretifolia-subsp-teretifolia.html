Labichea teretifolia subsp. teretifolia

Labichea teretifolia subsp.

teretifolia, commonly known as the slender labichea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, followed by elongated, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
