Lindera bootanica

Lindera bootanica, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often tinged with red on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that resemble peppers. Lindera bootanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to woodland gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental fruits make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
