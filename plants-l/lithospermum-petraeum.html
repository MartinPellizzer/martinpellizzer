Lithospermum petraeum

Lithospermum petraeum, commonly known as rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to rocky habitats in North America.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow along the ground or in crevices. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The roots contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.
