Lysionotus longiflorus

Lysionotus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered lythrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lysionotus longiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
