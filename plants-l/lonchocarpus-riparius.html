Lonchocarpus riparius

Lonchocarpus riparius, commonly known as the river senna, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pods that are typically yellow or orange, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
