Leonurus neglectus

Leonurus neglectus, commonly known as lesser hedgehog urtica.

It is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Its stems are often square in cross-section, a common trait among members of this family.

Leonurus neglectus is typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
