Leibnitzia kunzeana

Leibnitzia kunzeana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Leibnitzia kunzeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
