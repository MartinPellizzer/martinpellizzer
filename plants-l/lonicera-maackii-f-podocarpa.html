Lonicera maackii f. podocarpa

Lonicera maackii f. podocarpa, commonly known as the Maack's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It has opposite, simple, and usually ovate to elliptic leaves that are often pubescent beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its stems are typically brown and somewhat woody, with a tendency to climb or trail along the ground.

This cultivar is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
