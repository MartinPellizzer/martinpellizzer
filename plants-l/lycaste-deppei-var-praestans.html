Lycaste deppei var. praestans

Lycaste deppei var. praestans, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and fragrance.
