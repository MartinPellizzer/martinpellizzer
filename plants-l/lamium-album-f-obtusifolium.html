Lamium album f. obtusifolium

Lamium album f. obtusifolium, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to round leaves with a velvety texture and a pale green to silver underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative properties.
