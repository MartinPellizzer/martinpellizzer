Leptocereus chrysotyrius

Leptocereus chrysotyrius, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in white or yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
