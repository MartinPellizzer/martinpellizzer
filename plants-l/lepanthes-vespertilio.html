Lepanthes vespertilio

Lepanthes vespertilio, commonly known as the evening moth orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, moth-like appearance. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Lepanthes vespertilio is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
