Ledonia populifolia

Ledonia populifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf poplar, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a slight tendency to peel as the tree matures.

Ledonia populifolia is adapted to cool, moist environments and plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
