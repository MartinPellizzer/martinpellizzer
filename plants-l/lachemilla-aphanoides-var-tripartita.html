Lachemilla aphanoides var. tripartita

Lachemilla aphanoides var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
