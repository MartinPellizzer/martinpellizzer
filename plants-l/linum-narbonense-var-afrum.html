Linum narbonense var. afrum

Linum narbonense var. afrum, commonly known as African flax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Linaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
