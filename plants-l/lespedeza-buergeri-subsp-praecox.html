Lespedeza buergeri subsp. praecox

Lespedeza buergeri subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
