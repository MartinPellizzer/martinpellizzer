Litsea lancifolia var. iliaspaiei

Litsea lancifolia var. iliaspaiei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, orange-red fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
