Lupinus caesius

Lupinus caesius, commonly known as the blue lupine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus caesius is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
