Lycopodium carolinianum

Lycopodium carolinianum, commonly known as the ground pine, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage of scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Lycopodium carolinianum is a primitive vascular plant, showcasing features similar to ancient ferns and mosses.
