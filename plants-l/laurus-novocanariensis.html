Laurus novocanariensis

Laurus novocanariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas with well-drained soil.
