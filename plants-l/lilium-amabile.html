Lilium amabile

Lilium amabile, commonly known as the sweet William lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. This perennial herb produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
