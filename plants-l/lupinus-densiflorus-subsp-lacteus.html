Lupinus densiflorus subsp. lacteus

Lupinus densiflorus subsp.

lacteus, commonly known as the milk lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of white to pinkish-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval, serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This species is often found in rocky slopes and open woodlands, contributing to soil fertility and ecological diversity.
