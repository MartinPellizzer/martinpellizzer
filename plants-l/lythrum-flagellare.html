Lythrum flagellare

Lythrum flagellare, commonly known as the willow-leaf lythrum, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Lythraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Lythrum flagellare is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
