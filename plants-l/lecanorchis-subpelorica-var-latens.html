Lecanorchis subpelorica var. latens

Lecanorchis subpelorica var. latens, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
