Lobivia chrysantha subvar. hypocyrta

Lobivia chrysantha subvar. hypocyrta, commonly known as the yellow-tipped lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with prominent, ribbed structures that help it store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern to minimize water loss.

This subvariety is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
