Loxocarpus incanus var. incanus

Loxocarpus incanus var. incanus, commonly known as the slender rock vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features woody stems with distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
