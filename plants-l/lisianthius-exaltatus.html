Lisianthius exaltatus

Lisianthius exaltatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
