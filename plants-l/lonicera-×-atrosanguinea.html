Lonicera × atrosanguinea

Lonicera × atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black-tipped honeysuckle, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in early summer. Its berries are red or black, turning dark purple as they mature, and are edible but not particularly palatable.

Lonicera × atrosanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
