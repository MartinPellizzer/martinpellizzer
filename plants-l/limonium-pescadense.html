Limonium pescadense

Limonium pescadense, commonly known as the Pescadero sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its spikes of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Limonium pescadense plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and supporting local pollinators.
