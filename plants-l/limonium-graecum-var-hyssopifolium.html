Limonium graecum var. hyssopifolium

Limonium graecum var. hyssopifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
