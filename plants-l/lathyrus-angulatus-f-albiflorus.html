Lathyrus angulatus f. albiflorus

Lathyrus angulatus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered angular vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
