Lonicera japonica var. aureoreticulata

Lonicera japonica var. aureoreticulata, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and a serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy floral display.

The variety is notable for its unique reticulate pattern on the petals, which gives it its scientific name.
