Lebeckia grandiflora

Lebeckia grandiflora, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Lebeckia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
