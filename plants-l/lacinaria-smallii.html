Lacinaria smallii

Lacinaria smallii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lacinaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowering heads are typically yellow and produce small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lacinaria smallii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
