Loranthus beccarii

Loranthus beccarii, commonly known as the Bornean mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in Southeast Asia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the sunlight it captures. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, red berries.

Loranthus beccarii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
