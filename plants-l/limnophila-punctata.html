Limnophila punctata

Limnophila punctata, commonly known as the water willow, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant has slender, thread-like leaves that are often submerged, while its stems may reach above the water surface. Limnophila punctata produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
