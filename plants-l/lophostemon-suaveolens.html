Lophostemon suaveolens

Lophostemon suaveolens, commonly known as the silky gum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The silky gum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
