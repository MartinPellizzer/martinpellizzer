Lepidium draba var. longistylum

Lepidium draba var. longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
