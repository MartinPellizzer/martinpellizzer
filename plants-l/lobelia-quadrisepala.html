Lobelia quadrisepala

Lobelia quadrisepala, commonly known as the four-valved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The four-valved lobelia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
