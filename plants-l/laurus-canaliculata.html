Laurus canaliculata

Laurus canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a hedging plant.
