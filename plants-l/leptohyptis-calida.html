Leptohyptis calida

Leptohyptis calida, commonly known as the warm-leaved hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Leptohyptis calida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
