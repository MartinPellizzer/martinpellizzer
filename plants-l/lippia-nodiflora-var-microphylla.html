Lippia nodiflora var. microphylla

Lippia nodiflora var. microphylla, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Verbenaceae family, which includes other aromatic and medicinal plants.
