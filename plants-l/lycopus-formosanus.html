Lycopus formosanus

Lycopus formosanus, commonly known as the Chinese bugleweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
