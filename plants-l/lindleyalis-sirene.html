Lindleyalis sirene

Lindleyalis sirene, commonly known as the Sirene orchid, is a rare and exquisite member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble the mythical sirens, hence its common name. The orchid typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
