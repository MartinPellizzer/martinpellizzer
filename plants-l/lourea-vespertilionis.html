Lourea vespertilionis

Lourea vespertilionis, commonly known as the bat orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that resemble the wings of bats, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Lourea vespertilionis is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
