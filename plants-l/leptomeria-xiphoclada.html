Leptomeria xiphoclada

Leptomeria xiphoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or limestone areas.

Leptomeria xiphoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
