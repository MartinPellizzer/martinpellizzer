Landolphia scandens var. coriacea

Landolphia scandens var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough landolphia, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers. The leaves are thick and coriaceous, giving the plant its scientific name, and they are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. This plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
