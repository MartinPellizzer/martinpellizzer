Linum salsoloides f. longifolium

Linum salsoloides f. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are longer than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the summer months. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is often found in sandy or saline soils.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
