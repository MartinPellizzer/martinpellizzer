Lithops hookeri var. elephina

Lithops hookeri var. elephina, commonly known as the "elephant's ear lithops," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that resemble elephant ears, which store water to help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
