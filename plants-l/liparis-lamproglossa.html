Liparis lamproglossa

Liparis lamproglossa, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a broad, lip-like structure. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The orchid's flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
