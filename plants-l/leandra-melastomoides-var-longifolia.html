Leandra melastomoides var. longifolia

Leandra melastomoides var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved leandra, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
