Larix amabilis

Larix amabilis, commonly known as the Japanese larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its needle-like leaves that turn golden yellow before falling in autumn. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Larix amabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental tree for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
