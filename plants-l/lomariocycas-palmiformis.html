Lomariocycas palmiformis

Lomariocycas palmiformis, commonly known as the palm-like cycad, is a unique species belonging to the family Cycadaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that resemble those of a palm. Native to Madagascar, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like structure that produces both male and female spores.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
