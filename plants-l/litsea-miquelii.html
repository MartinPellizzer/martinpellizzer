Litsea miquelii

Litsea miquelii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or Miquel's litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southern China, northern Vietnam, and the Korean Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small, white flowers and its glossy, elliptical leaves. Litsea miquelii typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its fragrance and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
