Lycopodium magellanicum var. tipicum

Lycopodium magellanicum var. tipicum, commonly known as the Magellanic clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, moss-like growth habit. The plant features spirally arranged, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological role in its native habitat.
