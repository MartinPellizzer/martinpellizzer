Lastreopsis pacifica

Lastreopsis pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific lastreopsis, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
