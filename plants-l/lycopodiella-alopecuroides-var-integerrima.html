Lycopodiella alopecuroides var. integerrima

Lycopodiella alopecuroides var. integerrima, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, typical of its ancient lineage, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest undergrowth and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary history as a primitive vascular plant.
