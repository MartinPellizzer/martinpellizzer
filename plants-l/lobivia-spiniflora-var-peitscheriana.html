Lobivia spiniflora var. peitscheriana

Lobivia spiniflora var. peitscheriana, commonly known as the Peitscher's lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help to minimize transpiration.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
