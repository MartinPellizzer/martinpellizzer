Leymus × buriaticus

Leymus × buriaticus, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating low temperatures and poor soil conditions. It often forms dense mats or turf-like growths, making it an important component of alpine and tundra ecosystems.

Leymus × buriaticus is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
