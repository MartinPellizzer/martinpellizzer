Lithotoma axillaris

Lithotoma axillaris, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Lithotoma axillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
