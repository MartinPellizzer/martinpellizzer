Lessingianthus intermedius

Lessingianthus intermedius, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its bright, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
