Lobelia triangulata

Lobelia triangulata, commonly known as the triangular lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often arranged in spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia triangulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
