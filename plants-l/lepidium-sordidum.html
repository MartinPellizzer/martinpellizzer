Lepidium sordidum

Lepidium sordidum, commonly known as the stinking cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, pungent odor. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often have a bitter taste.

Lepidium sordidum is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
