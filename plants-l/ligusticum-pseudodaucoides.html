Ligusticum pseudodaucoides

Ligusticum pseudodaucoides, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white or greenish, clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences.

The roots and leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
