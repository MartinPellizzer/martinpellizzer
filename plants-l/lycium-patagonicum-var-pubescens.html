Lycium patagonicum var. pubescens

Lycium patagonicum var. pubescens, commonly known as the Patagonian boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southern Andes.

It features small, spiny leaves and produces yellow-orange berries that are edible. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
