Leontodon terglouensis

Leontodon terglouensis, commonly known as the Terglou's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Leontodon terglouensis is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid habitats.
