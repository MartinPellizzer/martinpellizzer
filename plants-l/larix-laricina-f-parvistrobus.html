Larix laricina f. parvistrobus

Larix laricina f. parvistrobus, commonly known as the dwarf larch or tamarack, is a variant of the tamarack tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compact, slow-growing form and its distinctive, needle-like leaves that turn golden in the fall. This form is typically found in wetland or bog environments, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The cones are small and often remain attached to the branches for several years.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in sensitive ecosystems.
