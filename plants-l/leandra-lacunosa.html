Leandra lacunosa

Leandra lacunosa, commonly known as the laceleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are finely toothed and often have a pale green or white midrib. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Leandra lacunosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
