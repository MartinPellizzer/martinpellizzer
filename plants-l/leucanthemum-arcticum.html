Leucanthemum arcticum

Leucanthemum arcticum, commonly known as the arctic leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons. Its white petals and yellow centers are surrounded by a series of ray and disk flowers.

It plays a role in pollination and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy and ornamental qualities.
