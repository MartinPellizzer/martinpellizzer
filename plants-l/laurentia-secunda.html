Laurentia secunda

Laurentia secunda, commonly known as the silverleaf laurentia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
