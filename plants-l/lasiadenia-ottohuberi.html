Lasiadenia ottohuberi

Lasiadenia ottohuberi, commonly known as the Ottohuberi orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique morphology includes a slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
