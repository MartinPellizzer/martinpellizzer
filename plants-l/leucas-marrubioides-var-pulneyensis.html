Leucas marrubioides var. pulneyensis

Leucas marrubioides var. pulneyensis, commonly known as the Pulney leucas, is a perennial herb native to the highland regions of South Australia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the cooler months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
