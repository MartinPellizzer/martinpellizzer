Limonium articulatum subsp. dictyocladum

Limonium articulatum subsp.

dictyocladum, commonly known as the clustered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained conditions and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
