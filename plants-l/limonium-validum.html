Limonium validum

Limonium validum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the western Atlantic.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and its striking clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium validum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
