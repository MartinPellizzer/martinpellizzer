Lantana inermis

Lantana inermis, commonly known as silverleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and may require careful management to prevent spread.
