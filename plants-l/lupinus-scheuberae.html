Lupinus scheuberae

Lupinus scheuberae, commonly known as the Mexican lupine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Lupinus scheuberae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
