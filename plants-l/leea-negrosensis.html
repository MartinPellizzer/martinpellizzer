Leea negrosensis

Leea negrosensis, commonly known as the Negros leea, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Negros.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an endemic species, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
