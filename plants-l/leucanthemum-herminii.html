Leucanthemum herminii

Leucanthemum herminii, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white petals surrounding a central yellow disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Leucanthemum herminii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
