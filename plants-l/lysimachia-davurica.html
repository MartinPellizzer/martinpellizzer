Lysimachia davurica

Lysimachia davurica, commonly known as the mountain chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Lysimachia davurica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
