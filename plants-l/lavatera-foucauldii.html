Lavatera foucauldii

Lavatera foucauldii, commonly known as the coastal lavatera, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, fluffy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for coastal environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
