Laurus canariensis var. longifolia

Laurus canariensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Canary Island laurel, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

This variety is closely related to the bay laurel and shares similar characteristics in appearance and fragrance.
