Leucanthemum vulgare var. laciniosum

Leucanthemum vulgare var. laciniosum, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disk, forming a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
