Leonhardia vaillantii

Leonhardia vaillantii, commonly known as the velvetleaf leonhardia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Leonhardia vaillantii is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
