Leucanthemum grande

Leucanthemum grande, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, white ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow flowers, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Leucanthemum grande is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
