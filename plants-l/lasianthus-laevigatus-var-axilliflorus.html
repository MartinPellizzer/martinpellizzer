Lasianthus laevigatus var. axilliflorus

Lasianthus laevigatus var. axilliflorus, commonly known as the axilliflorous lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
