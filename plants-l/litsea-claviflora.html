Litsea claviflora

Litsea claviflora, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Litsea claviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
