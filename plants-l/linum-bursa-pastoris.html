Linum bursa-pastoris

Linum bursa-pastoris, commonly known as wild flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces capsules containing numerous small, oval seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Linum bursa-pastoris is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
