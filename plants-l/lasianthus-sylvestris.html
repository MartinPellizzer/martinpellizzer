Lasianthus sylvestris

Lasianthus sylvestris, commonly known as the forest lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet, arranged in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
