Larix olgensis f. intermedia

Larix olgensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate larch, is a botanical variant of the larch tree native to northeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its ability to thrive in cold, temperate climates. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage is needle-like, turning a striking golden yellow in autumn before shedding.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
