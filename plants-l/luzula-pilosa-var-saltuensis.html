Luzula pilosa var. saltuensis

Luzula pilosa var. saltuensis, commonly known as the rough wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
