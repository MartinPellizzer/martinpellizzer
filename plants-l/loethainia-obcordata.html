Loethainia obcordata

Loethainia obcordata, commonly known as the heart-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Loethainia obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
