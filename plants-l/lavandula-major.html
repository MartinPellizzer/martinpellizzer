Lavandula major

Lavandula major, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, spike-like flower clusters that range in color from purple to white. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are widely used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
