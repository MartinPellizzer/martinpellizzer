Lonicera caerulea var. kamtschatica

Lonicera caerulea var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Russian Far East and parts of Siberia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its nutritional benefits.
