Lindsaea laciniata

Lindsaea laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a lace-like appearance. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical interest lies in its unique morphology.

The plant is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices, showcasing its adaptability to various substrates.
