Limodorum recurvum

Limodorum recurvum, commonly known as the zigzag lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, nodding, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Limodorum recurvum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
