Linum squamulosum subsp. tommasinii

Linum squamulosum subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasin's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
