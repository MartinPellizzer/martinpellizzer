Lathyrus pratensis var. montanus

Lathyrus pratensis var. montanus, commonly known as mountain bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodland habitats and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
