Lepanthes martinezii

Lepanthes martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Lepanthes martinezii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
