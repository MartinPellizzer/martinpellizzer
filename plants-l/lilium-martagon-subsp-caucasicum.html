Lilium martagon subsp. caucasicum

Lilium martagon subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian Turk's Cap Lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple throat.

The plant produces a tall, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
