Leonotis ocymifolia

Leonotis ocymifolia, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Leonotis ocymifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
