Lithocarpus Amygdalifolius

Lithocarpus tree, scientifically known as Lithocarpus amygdalifolius, is a remarkable species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. This tree is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and distinctive, hard-shelled fruits that contain edible nuts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in dense forests with high humidity and ample rainfall. The tree plays a vital role in its ecosystem, supporting a wide variety of wildlife and contributing to soil stability. Due to its ecological importance and unique features, Lithocarpus amygdalifolius is a subject of ongoing botanical and conservation interest.
