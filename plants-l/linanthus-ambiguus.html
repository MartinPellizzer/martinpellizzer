Linanthus ambiguus

Linanthus ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Linanthus ambiguus is known for its short growing season and is often found in areas with low precipitation.
