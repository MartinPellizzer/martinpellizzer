Lotus corniculatus subsp. kabylicus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

kabylicus, commonly known as the Kabylicus grass-clover, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It is characterized by its slender stems and distinctive seed pods that curl into a spiral shape.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
