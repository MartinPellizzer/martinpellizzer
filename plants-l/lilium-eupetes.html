Lilium eupetes

Lilium eupetes, commonly known as the red lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea, and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms during the summer months.

Lilium eupetes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
