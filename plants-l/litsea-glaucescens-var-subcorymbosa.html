Litsea glaucescens var. subcorymbosa

Litsea glaucescens var. subcorymbosa, commonly known as the subcorymbous litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
