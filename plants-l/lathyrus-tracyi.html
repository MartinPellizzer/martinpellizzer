Lathyrus tracyi

Lathyrus tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Tracy's vetch thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
