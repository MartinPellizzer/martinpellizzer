Lespedeza delavayi

Lespedeza delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's lespeadeza, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Lespedeza delavayi is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
