Liparis anceps

Liparis anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Liparis anceps is a short-lived perennial, often flowering in its second year before dying back.
