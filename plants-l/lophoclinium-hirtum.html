Lophoclinium hirtum

Lophoclinium hirtum, commonly known as the hairy leaved water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Lophoclinium hirtum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
