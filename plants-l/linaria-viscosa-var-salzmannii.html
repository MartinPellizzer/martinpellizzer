Linaria viscosa var. salzmannii

Linaria viscosa var. salzmannii, commonly known as the sticky toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or dry gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
