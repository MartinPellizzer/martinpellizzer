Linum jimenezii

Linum jimenezii, commonly known as Jimenez's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom in the spring.

Linum jimenezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
