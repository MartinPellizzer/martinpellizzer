Lepigonum medium

Lepigonum medium, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Lepigonum medium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
