Lactuca pseudoumbrella

Lactuca pseudoumbrella, commonly known as the broad-leaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which is typical of the genus Lactuca. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant contains lactucarium, a milky sap known for its mild sedative properties.
