Lycopodioides commersoniana

Lycopodioides commersoniana, commonly known as Commerson's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves and spore-producing structures called strobila. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant's vascular system is relatively simple, typical of ancient plant groups, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Its structure reflects evolutionary adaptations to its ecological niche, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
