Leighia elegans

Leighia elegans, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high elevations. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns of purple and white, often resembling a miniature orchid. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the mossy substrates where it grows.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
