Lilium candidum subsp. peregrinum

Lilium candidum subsp.

peregrinum, commonly known as the white lily, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture and floral symbolism.
