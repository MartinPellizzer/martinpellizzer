Lobelia cordifolia

Lobelia cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes other lobelias and bellflowers.

Lobelia cordifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
