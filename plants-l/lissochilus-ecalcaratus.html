Lissochilus ecalcaratus

Lissochilus ecalcaratus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
