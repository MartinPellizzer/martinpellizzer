Linum chamissonis var. aquilinum

Linum chamissonis var. aquilinum, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing dune environments.
