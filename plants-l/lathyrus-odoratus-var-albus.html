Lathyrus odoratus var. albus

Lathyrus odoratus var. albus, commonly known as the white sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is an annual or biennial herb with climbing stems and compound leaves consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The white sweet pea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
