Litsea hookeri

Litsea hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's litsea, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil.

Its fruit is a small, dark-colored drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
