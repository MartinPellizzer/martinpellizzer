Lepanthes brunnescens

Lepanthes brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-stemted slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, often reddish stem and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with pale yellow to brownish petals. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes brunnescens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
