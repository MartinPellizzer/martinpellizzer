Lepanthopsis glandulifera

Lepanthopsis glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the inflorescence consists of a single raceme bearing numerous flowers.

The species is noted for its unique glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its scientific name.
