Lamium turkestanicum

Lamium turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, violet-blue flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lamium turkestanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
