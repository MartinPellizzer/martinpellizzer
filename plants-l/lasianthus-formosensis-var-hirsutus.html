Lasianthus formosensis var. hirsutus

Lasianthus formosensis var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often covered in fine, soft hairs that give it its common name.

The flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
