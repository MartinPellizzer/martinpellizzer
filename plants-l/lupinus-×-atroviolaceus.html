Lupinus × atroviolaceus

Lupinus × atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet lupine, is a hybrid species within the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking dark violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring palmately compound leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility.

Native to the western United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
