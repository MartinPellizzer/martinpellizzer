Liparis purpureovittata

Liparis purpureovittata, commonly known as the purple-banded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-pink flowers with distinctive purple stripes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
