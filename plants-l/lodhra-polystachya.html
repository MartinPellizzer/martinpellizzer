Lodhra polystachya

Lodhra polystachya, commonly known as the Indian bael tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces a distinctive, spherical fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The bark is thick, rough, and often fissured, while the wood is heavy and durable.

It is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
