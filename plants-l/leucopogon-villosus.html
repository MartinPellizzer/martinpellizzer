Leucopogon villosus

Leucopogon villosus, commonly known as the silky leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and coastal regions, characterized by its dense, wiry foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name.

Leucopogon villosus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
