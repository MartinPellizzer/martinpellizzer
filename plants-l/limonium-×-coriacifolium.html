Limonium × coriacifolium

Limonium × coriacifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Limonium × coriacifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
