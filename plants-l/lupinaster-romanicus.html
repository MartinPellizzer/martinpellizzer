Lupinaster romanicus

Lupinaster romanicus, commonly known as the Romanic Lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Lupinaster romanicus is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its ecological importance includes supporting pollinators and contributing to soil enrichment through its nitrogen-fixing properties.
