Lupinus × inyoensis

Lupinus × inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo hybrid lupine, is a hybrid species native to the Great Basin region of North America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus × inyoensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
