Leptolaena parviflora

Leptolaena parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered leptolaena, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions.

Leptolaena parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
