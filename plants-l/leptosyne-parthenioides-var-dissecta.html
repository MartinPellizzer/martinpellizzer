Leptosyne parthenioides var. dissecta

Leptosyne parthenioides var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected leptosyne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
