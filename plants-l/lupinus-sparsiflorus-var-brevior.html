Lupinus sparsiflorus var. brevior

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. brevior, commonly known as the sparseflower lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
