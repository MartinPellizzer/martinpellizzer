Lepidanthus phyllanthoides

Lepidanthus phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are arranged in a circular pattern, giving it a waterwheel-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as rivers and swamps.
