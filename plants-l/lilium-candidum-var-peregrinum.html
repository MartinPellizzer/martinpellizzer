Lilium candidum var. peregrinum

Lilium candidum var. peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering white lily, is a rare and ancient variant of the white lily.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
