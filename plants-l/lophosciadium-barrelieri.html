Lophosciadium barrelieri

Lophosciadium barrelieri, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is also called the night-blooming moonflower.
