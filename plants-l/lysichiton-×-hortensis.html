Lysichiton × hortensis

Lysichiton × hortensis, commonly known as the giant taro, is a hybrid plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typically followed by orange berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed in certain regions.
