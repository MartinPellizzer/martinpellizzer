Lepidium scopulorum f. nanum

Lepidium scopulorum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf wall rocket, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and segmented seed pods. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

This subspecies is notable for its compact size and adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
