Lithops vallis-mariae

Lithops vallis-mariae, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paired leaves that resemble small stones, which help it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the winter months. It requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Lithops vallis-mariae is well adapted to rocky, sunny habitats and is often found in coastal regions.
