Lysimachia vulgaris var. typica

Lysimachia vulgaris var. typica, commonly known as yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
