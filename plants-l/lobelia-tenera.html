Lobelia tenera

Lobelia tenera, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia tenera is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
