Lythrum volgense

Lythrum volgense, commonly known as the Volga loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy, pinkish-purple flowers are clustered in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lythrum volgense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
