Linum scariosum

Linum scariosum, commonly known as the scarious flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Linum scariosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
