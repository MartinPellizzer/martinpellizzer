Lemaireocereus marginatus var. incrustatus

Lemaireocereus marginatus var. incrustatus, commonly known as the Incrustation Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a compact, rounded shape. Its stems are covered in a dense, whitish bloom that gives it a rocky appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.
