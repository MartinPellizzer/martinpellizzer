Lonicera ferdinandi f. leycesterioides

Lonicera ferdinandi f. leycesterioides, commonly known as the Leycesterioides honeysuckle, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
