Lasiorrhiza foliosa

Lasiorrhiza foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed lasiorrhiza, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from violet to blue, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
