Lycoris kiushiana

Lycoris kiushiana, commonly known as the Chinese lantern lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble small lanterns, which bloom in the autumn season. The plant has strap-like, grass-like leaves that emerge in the spring and wither by summer. Its bulbs are typically clustered and grow underground, contributing to its resilience in various soil conditions.

Lycoris kiushiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
