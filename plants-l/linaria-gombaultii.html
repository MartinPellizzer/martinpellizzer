Linaria gombaultii

Linaria gombaultii, commonly known as Gombault's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the genus Linaria.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
