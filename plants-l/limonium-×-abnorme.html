Limonium × abnorme

Limonium × abnorme, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Limonium × abnorme is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
