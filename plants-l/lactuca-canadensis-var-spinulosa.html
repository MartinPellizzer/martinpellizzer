Lactuca canadensis var. spinulosa

Lactuca canadensis var. spinulosa, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a biennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and a flowering head that resembles a lettuce head. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the stem is ribbed with small spines.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to produce a milky sap when damaged.
