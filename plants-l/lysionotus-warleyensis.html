Lysionotus warleyensis

Lysionotus warleyensis, commonly known as the Warley's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
