Lagarosiphon hydrilloides

Lagarosiphon hydrilloides, commonly known as water thistle, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense mats in still or slow-moving water. The plant features small, whorled leaves that are usually narrow and pointed, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, floating fruits that disperse via water.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in non-native regions, impacting water ecosystems.
