Lupinus ramosissimus var. platyphyllus

Lupinus ramosissimus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of pea-like, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a nitrogen-fixing plant that supports soil health and biodiversity.
