Lupinus brunneomaculatus

Lupinus brunneomaculatus, commonly known as the brown-spotted lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces sparsely branched inflorescences of violet to purple flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. Lupinus brunneomaculatus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its seeds are encased in tough pods and are an important food source for certain wildlife species.
