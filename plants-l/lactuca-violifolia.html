Lactuca violifolia

Lactuca violifolia, commonly known as violetleaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, wavy-edged leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Lactuca violifolia is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
