Lysimachia erosipetala

Lysimachia erosipetala, commonly known as the scarlet willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia erosipetala thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
