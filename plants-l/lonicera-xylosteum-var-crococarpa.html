Lonicera xylosteum var. crococarpa

Lonicera xylosteum var. crococarpa, commonly known as the red-twigged honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems that become prominent in winter, providing visual interest throughout the year. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
