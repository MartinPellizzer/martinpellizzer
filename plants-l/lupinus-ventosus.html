Lupinus ventosus

Lupinus ventosus, commonly known as the prairie lupine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open grasslands.

Lupinus ventosus is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
