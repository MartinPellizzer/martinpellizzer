Lycopus pumilus

Lycopus pumilus, commonly known as lesser blueweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lycopus pumilus is often found in the eastern United States and is known for its aromatic foliage.
