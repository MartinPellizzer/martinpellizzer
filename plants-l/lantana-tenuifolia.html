Lantana tenuifolia

Lantana tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Lantana tenuifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
