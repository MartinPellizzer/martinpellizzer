Lycopodium macgregorii

Lycopodium macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of needle-like leaves and distinctive, cone-like strobili that bear spores. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy, producing both microspores and megaspores on the same plant.

Its intricate structure and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of early land plant evolution.
