Lycopodioides filicina

Lycopodioides filicina, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing structures.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant has a sporophyte-dominated life cycle, with the gametophyte stage being short-lived and dependent on the sporophyte. Its structure includes a root system, stems, and leaves, with spores produced in specialized cones called strobili.

Lycopodioides filicina plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
