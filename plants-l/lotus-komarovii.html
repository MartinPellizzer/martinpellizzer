Lotus komarovii

Lotus komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's lotus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its palmately compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
