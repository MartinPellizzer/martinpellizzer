Liatris umbellata

Liatris umbellata, commonly known as the pinkroot or white gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pinkish-purple flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in an umbel-like cluster, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Liatris umbellata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
