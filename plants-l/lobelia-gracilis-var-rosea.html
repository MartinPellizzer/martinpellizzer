Lobelia gracilis var. rosea

Lobelia gracilis var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to lavender, often with a striking rose-like hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
