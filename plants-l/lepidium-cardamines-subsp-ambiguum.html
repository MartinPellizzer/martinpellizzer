Lepidium cardamines subsp. ambiguum

Lepidium cardamines subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as wild cress, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers in racemes.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. It is an annual or biennial species that thrives in temperate climates.

The seeds are enclosed in siliques, which are dry, dehiscent fruits typical of the Brassicaceae family.
