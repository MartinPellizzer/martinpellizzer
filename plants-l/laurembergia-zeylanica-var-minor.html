Laurembergia zeylanica var. minor

Laurembergia zeylanica var. minor, commonly known as the lesser Ceylon laurel, is a shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
