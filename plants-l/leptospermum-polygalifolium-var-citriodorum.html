Leptospermum polygalifolium var. citriodorum

Leptospermum polygalifolium var. citriodorum, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive fragrance. The plant typically grows in coastal and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits.

The leaves are often used in culinary and aromatic applications due to their unique flavor and medicinal properties.
