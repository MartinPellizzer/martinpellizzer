Ladenbergia moritziana

Ladenbergia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's legume, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods that split open when mature.

Ladenbergia moritziana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
