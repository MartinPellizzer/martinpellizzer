Luzula atlantica subsp. atlantica

Luzula atlantica subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Atlantic coastal regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in bogs, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded, wet environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
