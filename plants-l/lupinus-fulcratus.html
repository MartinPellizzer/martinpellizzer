Lupinus fulcratus

Lupinus fulcratus, commonly known as the rigid lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Lupinus fulcratus has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and fixes nitrogen, enriching the soil.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
