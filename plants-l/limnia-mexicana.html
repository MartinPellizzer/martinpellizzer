Limnia mexicana

Limnia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican water flea, is a small aquatic crustacean belonging to the order Cladocera.

It is characterized by its transparent, oval-shaped body and a pair of antennae used for sensing the environment. This species is typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Limnia mexicana plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by serving as a primary food source for various fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of water quality and ecological health.
