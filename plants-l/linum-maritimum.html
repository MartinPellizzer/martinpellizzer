Linum maritimum

Linum maritimum, commonly known as sea flax, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its fibrous stems were historically used for making rope and textiles.

Linum maritimum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
