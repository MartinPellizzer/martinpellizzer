Lipocarpha leucaspis

Lipocarpha leucaspis, commonly known as the white-flowered marsh sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
