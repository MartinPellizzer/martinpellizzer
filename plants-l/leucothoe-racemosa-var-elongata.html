Leucothoe racemosa var. elongata

Leucothoe racemosa var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated flame-augustus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing attractive foliage throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
