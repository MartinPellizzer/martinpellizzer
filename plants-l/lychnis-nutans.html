Lychnis nutans

Lychnis nutans, commonly known as the nodding campion, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink or red flowers that droop downward. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, thriving in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

Lychnis nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
