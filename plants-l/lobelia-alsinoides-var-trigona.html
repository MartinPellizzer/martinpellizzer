Lobelia alsinoides var. trigona

Lobelia alsinoides var. trigona, commonly known as the three-lobed lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive three-lobed leaf structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
