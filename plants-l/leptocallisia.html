Leptocallisia

Leptocallisia, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that often appear as if they are made of glass, giving it its common name. These plants typically grow in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical and subtropical forests. Leptocallisia species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of other plants without being parasitic.

Their intricate leaf structures and unique appearance make them popular among fern enthusiasts and botanists alike.
