Lonicera parviflora var. sullivantii

Lonicera parviflora var. sullivantii, commonly known as the Sullivant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces red to orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage, which contribute to its popularity in gardens.
