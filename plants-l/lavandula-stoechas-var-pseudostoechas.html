Lavandula stoechas var. pseudostoechas

Lavandula stoechas var. pseudostoechas, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or the purple lavender, is a variety of the common lavender plant.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flower spikes that rise above the foliage. The leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of down. This variety is known for its strong aromatic scent, which is similar to other lavender species but more intense.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
