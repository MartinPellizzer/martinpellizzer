Lycopodium subdiaphanum

Lycopodium subdiaphanum, commonly known as the translucent clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spores in small, cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. Lycopodium subdiaphanum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in forest undergrowth.

Its delicate, translucent appearance makes it a unique and often overlooked member of the lycophyte group.
