Lomatia silaifolia f. latifolia

Lomatia silaifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lomatia, is a variant of the lomatia species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
