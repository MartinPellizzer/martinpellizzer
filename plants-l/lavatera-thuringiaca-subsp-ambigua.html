Lavatera thuringiaca subsp. ambigua

Lavatera thuringiaca subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the woolly bush or white woolly bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
