Liparis sutepensis

Liparis sutepensis, commonly known as the Sutep orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Thailand and Myanmar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers, which are usually pale green or yellowish, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Liparis sutepensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
