Lonchocarpus salvadorensis

Lonchocarpus salvadorensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful pods that resemble peacock feathers, which are often used in traditional crafts. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a velvety texture, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
