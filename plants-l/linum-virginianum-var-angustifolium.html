Linum virginianum var. angustifolium

Linum virginianum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf flax, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, angular stems that are often hairy, and it produces blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a smooth or slightly toothed edge. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
