Lathyrus littoralis

Lathyrus littoralis, commonly known as sea pea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, with slender, thread-like leaflets, and it has a trailing growth habit.

Lathyrus littoralis is adapted to harsh maritime environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
