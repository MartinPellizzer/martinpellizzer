Leucocoryne pauciflora

Leucocoryne pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered white grass, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South America.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver or gray in color. The plant produces an upright flowering stem with small, white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and collections worldwide.
