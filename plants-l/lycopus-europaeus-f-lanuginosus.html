Lycopus europaeus f. lanuginosus

Lycopus europaeus f. lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy, fuzzy appearance is a distinguishing trait, particularly on the stems and leaves.

This variant is often found in moist habitats like stream banks and wet meadows.
