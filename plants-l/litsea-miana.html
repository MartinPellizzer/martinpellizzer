Litsea miana

Litsea miana, commonly known as the white-flowered litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, elliptical berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Litsea miana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and aesthetic appeal.
