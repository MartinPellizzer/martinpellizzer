Leucanthemum raciborskii

Leucanthemum raciborskii, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces showy white or pale yellow flowers with a central disc. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Leucanthemum raciborskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
