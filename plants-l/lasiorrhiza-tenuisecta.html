Lasiorrhiza tenuisecta

Lasiorrhiza tenuisecta, commonly known as the slender-leafed grass.

It is a species of grass native to southern Africa, characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in grassland and open woodland habitats, often in regions with a temperate climate. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
