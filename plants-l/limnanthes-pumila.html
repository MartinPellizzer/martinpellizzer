Limnanthes pumila

Limnanthes pumila, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal prairies and dunes. Its seeds are edible and have a nutty flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.

Limnanthes pumila plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
