Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. montanum

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the alpine daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, forming a daisy-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is a perennial species that produces basal rosettes of leaves and flowering stems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine flora and a popular choice for rock gardens.
