Lathyrus rothii var. angustissimus

Lathyrus rothii var. angustissimus, commonly known as narrowleaf grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. This variety is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is a perennial plant that produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.
