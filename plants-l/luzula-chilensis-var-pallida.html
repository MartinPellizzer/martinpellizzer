Luzula chilensis var. pallida

Luzula chilensis var. pallida, commonly known as the pale tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its clumping growth habit. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in early spring.
