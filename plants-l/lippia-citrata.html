Lippia citrata

Lippia citrata, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both garden and container planting.

Lippia citrata is also used in traditional medicine and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
