Lilium longiflorum f. normale

Lilium longiflorum f. normale, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a botanical variant of the Easter lily species.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the eastern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The f. normale form is distinguished by its more compact growth and slightly smaller flowers compared to other cultivars.
