Lippia ramboi var. pilosa

Lippia ramboi var. pilosa, commonly known as rough lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in summer. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture.
