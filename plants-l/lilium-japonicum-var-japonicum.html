Lilium japonicum var. japonicum

Lilium japonicum var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in temperate climates.
