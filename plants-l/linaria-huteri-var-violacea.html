Linaria huteri var. violacea

Linaria huteri var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically features small, linear leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
