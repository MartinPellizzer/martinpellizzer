Leontodon setosus

Leontodon setosus, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The hairy bellflower is often found in open habitats and is considered a valuable component of diverse plant communities.
