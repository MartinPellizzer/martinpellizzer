Lepanthes vinacea

Lepanthes vinacea, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with red-tipped petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes vinacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
