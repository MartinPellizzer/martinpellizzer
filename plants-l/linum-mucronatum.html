Linum mucronatum

Linum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Linum mucronatum is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
