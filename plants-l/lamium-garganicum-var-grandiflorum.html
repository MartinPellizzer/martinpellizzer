Lamium garganicum var. grandiflorum

Lamium garganicum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces and reddish veins. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
