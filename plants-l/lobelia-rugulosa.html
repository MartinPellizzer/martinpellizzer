Lobelia rugulosa

Lobelia rugulosa, commonly known as the rough lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and spikes of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Lobelia rugulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
