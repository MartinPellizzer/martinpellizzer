Leda

Leda, commonly known as the common mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and along stream banks. Leda has a hairy stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
