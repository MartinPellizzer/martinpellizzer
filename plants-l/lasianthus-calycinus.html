Lasianthus calycinus

Lasianthus calycinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a prominent central spur. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
