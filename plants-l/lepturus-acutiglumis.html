Lepturus acutiglumis

Lepturus acutiglumis, commonly known as the sharp-awned lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, bristly pods, and the plant is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Lepturus acutiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
