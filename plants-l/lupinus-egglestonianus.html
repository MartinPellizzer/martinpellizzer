Lupinus egglestonianus

Lupinus egglestonianus, commonly known as the Eggleston's lupine, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds. Lupinus egglestonianus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
