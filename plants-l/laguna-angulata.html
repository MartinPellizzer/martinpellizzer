Laguna angulata

Laguna angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water lily, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, floating leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape, with a waxy surface that aids in water repellence. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, often in the early morning. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Laguna angulata plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
