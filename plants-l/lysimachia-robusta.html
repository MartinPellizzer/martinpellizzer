Lysimachia robusta

Lysimachia robusta, commonly known as the broadleaf chickweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall, covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia robusta is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas and along stream banks.
