Leptis brachygloba

Leptis brachygloba, commonly known as the narrow globular leptis, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, further minimizing water loss.

Leptis brachygloba is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
