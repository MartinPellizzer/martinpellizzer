Lipochaeta succulenta var. barclayi

Lipochaeta succulenta var. barclayi, commonly known as the Barclay's lipochaeta, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.

This species is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
