Laserpitium nestleri var. turolensis

Laserpitium nestleri var. turolensis, commonly known as the Turolensis hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, arid environments with high solar exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in specialized horticulture.
