Lepanthes ostraconopetala

Lepanthes ostraconopetala, commonly known as the Ostracan-petaled Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically feature a white or pale blue coloration. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its humid, tropical habitat.

Lepanthes ostraconopetala is often found growing in shaded, moist environments such as cloud forests.
