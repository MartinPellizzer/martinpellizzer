Lavoisiera mucugensis

Lavoisiera mucugensis, commonly known as the Amazonian water fern, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is a member of the fern family, characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant typically grows in moist soil near water bodies, showcasing its adaptability to wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are often bioluminescent, a unique trait that aids in attracting pollinators and deterring herbivores.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
