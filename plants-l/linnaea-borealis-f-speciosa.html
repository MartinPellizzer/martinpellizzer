Linnaea borealis f. speciosa

Linnaea borealis f. speciosa, commonly known as the showy twinflower, is a rare and striking variant of the twinflower plant.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the boreal forests of North America and parts of Europe. The plant features delicate, opposite leaves and small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

This form is notable for its enhanced floral display compared to the typical species, making it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens.
