Limonium leptolobum

Limonium leptolobum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium leptolobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
