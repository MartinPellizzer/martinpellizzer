Lablab purpureus subsp. bengalensis

Lablab purpureus subsp.

bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal hyacinth bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a deep green color.

The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, slender pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

This subspecies is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in crop rotation systems.
