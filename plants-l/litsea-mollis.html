Litsea mollis

Litsea mollis, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often aromatic and have a slightly waxy texture. Its small white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible for some bird species.

Litsea mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
