Lantana chamaedrifolia

Lantana chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
