Ligustrum micranthum

Ligustrum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered privet, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruits are small drupes that turn dark purple when mature.

Ligustrum micranthum is often used in landscaping due to its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.
