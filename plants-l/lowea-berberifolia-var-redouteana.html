Lowea berberifolia var. redouteana

Lowea berberifolia var. redouteana, commonly known as the Redoute's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
