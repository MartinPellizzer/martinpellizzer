Lamium garganicum var. microphyllum

Lamium garganicum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the lesser dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is characterized by its delicate foliage and its ability to spread through rhizomes.
