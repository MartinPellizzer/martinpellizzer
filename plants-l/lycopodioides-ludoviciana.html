Lycopodioides ludoviciana

Lycopodioides ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a creeping stem and small, scale-like leaves. It produces sporophytes that bear spores, which are released into the air for reproduction.

This ancient plant is part of the lycophyte group, which dates back to the Paleozoic era.
