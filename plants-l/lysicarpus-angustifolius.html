Lysicarpus angustifolius

Lysicarpus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lychee, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
