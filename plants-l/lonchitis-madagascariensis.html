Lonchitis madagascariensis

Lonchitis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar starwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, star-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically features small, green leaves with a hairy texture and produces tiny, clustered flowers that are often pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
