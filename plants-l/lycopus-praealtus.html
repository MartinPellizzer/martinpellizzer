Lycopus praealtus

Lycopus praealtus, commonly known as tall blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in whorled clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
