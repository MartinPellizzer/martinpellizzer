Liabum lyratum

Liabum lyratum, commonly known as the heartleaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Liabum lyratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
