Leontodon pyrenaicus subsp. reverchonii

Leontodon pyrenaicus subsp.

reverchonii, commonly known as the Pyrenean dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers develop in a single head.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems and its limited distribution.
