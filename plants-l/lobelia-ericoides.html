Lobelia ericoides

Lobelia ericoides, commonly known as the heath lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 inches. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Lobelia ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
