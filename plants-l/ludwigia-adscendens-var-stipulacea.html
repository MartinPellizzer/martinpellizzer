Ludwigia adscendens var. stipulacea

Ludwigia adscendens var. stipulacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf water primrose, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a popular choice for water gardens and pond landscapes.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an effective plant for stabilizing aquatic environments.
