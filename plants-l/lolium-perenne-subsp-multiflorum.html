Lolium perenne subsp. multiflorum

Lolium perenne subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as ryegrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and high nutritional value, making it a popular choice for forage and turfgrass. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in agricultural settings for livestock feed.

Its extensive root system helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil structure.
