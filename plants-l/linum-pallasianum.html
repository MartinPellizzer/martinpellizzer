Linum pallasianum

Linum pallasianum, commonly known as Pallas's flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax plants. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Linum pallasianum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
