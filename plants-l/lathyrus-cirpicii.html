Lathyrus cirpicii

Lathyrus cirpicii, commonly known as the Cyprus vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are usually hairy.

Lathyrus cirpicii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
