Liparis cardiophylla var. angustifolia

Liparis cardiophylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem with several whorls of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces a spike of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose, racemose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
