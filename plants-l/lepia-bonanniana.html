Lepia bonanniana

Lepia bonanniana, commonly known as the Bonannian lichen, is a unique species of lichen that belongs to the family Lichenes.

It is characterized by its intricate symbiotic relationship between a fungal partner and a photosynthetic alga or cyanobacterium. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks and rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body formed by the mutualistic partnership of the fungus and the photosynthetic organism.

Lepia bonanniana plays a vital ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its native habitats.
