Loroglossum hircinum f. calamistratum

Loroglossum hircinum f. calamistratum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf milkweed, is a variant of the milkweed species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweed species. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
