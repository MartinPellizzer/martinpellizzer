Lepisma paniculatum

Lepisma paniculatum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or near water bodies.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
