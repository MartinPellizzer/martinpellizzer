Lysiloma seemannii

Lysiloma seemannii, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers in pendant clusters, which bloom in the spring.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
