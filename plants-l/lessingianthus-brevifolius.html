Lessingianthus brevifolius

Lessingianthus brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that are tightly packed into a single head, surrounded by narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens despite its limited distribution in the wild.
