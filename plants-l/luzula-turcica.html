Luzula turcica

Luzula turcica, commonly known as Turkish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to regions of Turkey and the Caucasus, typically growing in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Luzula turcica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
