Laricorchis fulgens

Laricorchis fulgens, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This terrestrial orchid is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescence, which consists of elongated, drooping flowers with a distinctive tassel-like structure. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. The plant has a short rhizome with a single, fleshy leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of white pubescence.

Laricorchis fulgens is a rare and endangered species, with limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
