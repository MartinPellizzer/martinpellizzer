Laxmannia squarrosa

Laxmannia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or grayish hairs covering its stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Native to mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Laxmannia squarrosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging growing conditions.
