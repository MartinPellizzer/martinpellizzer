Lupinus quercuum var. perflexolobius

Lupinus quercuum var. perflexolobius, commonly known as the redwood lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like, violet to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
