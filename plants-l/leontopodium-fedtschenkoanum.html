Leontopodium fedtschenkoanum

Leontopodium fedtschenkoanum, commonly known as the snow lotus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking, showy flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often considered a symbol of resilience and beauty in the mountainous regions of its native range.
