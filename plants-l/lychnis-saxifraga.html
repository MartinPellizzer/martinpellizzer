Lychnis saxifraga

Lychnis saxifraga, commonly known as the rockfoil carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions, and is well adapted to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

Lychnis saxifraga is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to alpine and woodland habitats.
