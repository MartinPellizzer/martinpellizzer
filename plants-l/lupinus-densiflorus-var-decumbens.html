Lupinus densiflorus var. decumbens

Lupinus densiflorus var. decumbens, commonly known as the denseflower lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with decumbent stems. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers, which are typical of the legume family. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus densiflorus var. decumbens is adapted to rocky and dry environments, often found in coastal and inland regions.
