Leucothoe affinis

Leucothoe affinis, commonly known as the Appalachian leatherleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that remains vibrant throughout the winter, providing year-round visual interest. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that attract birds. Its thick, leathery leaves are arranged in whorls, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.

Leucothoe affinis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
