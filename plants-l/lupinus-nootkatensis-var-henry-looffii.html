Lupinus nootkatensis var. henry-looffii

Lupinus nootkatensis var. henry-looffii, commonly known as the Alaska lupine, is a perennial legume native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in nitrogen fixation and its role in supporting local wildlife.
