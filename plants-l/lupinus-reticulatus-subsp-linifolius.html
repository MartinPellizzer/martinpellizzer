Lupinus reticulatus subsp. linifolius

Lupinus reticulatus subsp.

linifolius, commonly known as the redstem lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with a distinctive reddish stem.

The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in restoration projects.
