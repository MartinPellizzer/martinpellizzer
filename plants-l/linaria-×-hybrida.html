Linaria × hybrida

Linaria × hybrida, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

Linaria × hybrida is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
