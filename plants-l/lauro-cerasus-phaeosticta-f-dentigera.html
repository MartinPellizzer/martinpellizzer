Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. dentigera

Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. dentigera, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a cultivar of the Prunus species.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation along the margins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. It is a slow-growing shrub or small tree that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6-9.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and evergreen foliage.
