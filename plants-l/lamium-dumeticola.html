Lamium dumeticola

Lamium dumeticola, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a mild minty aroma, while its flowers are small, two-lipped, and usually pink or purple in color.

Lamium dumeticola is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing and digestive properties.
