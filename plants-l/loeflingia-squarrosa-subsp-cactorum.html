Loeflingia squarrosa subsp. cactorum

Loeflingia squarrosa subsp.

cactorum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
