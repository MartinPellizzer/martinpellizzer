Lepidium persicum subsp. arianum

Lepidium persicum subsp.

arianum, commonly known as Persian cress, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops.

The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
