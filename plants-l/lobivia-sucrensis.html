Lobivia sucrensis

Lobivia sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to withstand arid and cold conditions.

Lobivia sucrensis is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental flowers.
