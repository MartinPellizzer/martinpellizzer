Lobelia macrocarpa var. genistoides

Lobelia macrocarpa var. genistoides, commonly known as the yellow lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
