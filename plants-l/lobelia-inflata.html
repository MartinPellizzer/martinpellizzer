Lobelia inflata

Lobelia inflata, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are often glandular and emit a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
