Laserpitium glabrum

Laserpitium glabrum, commonly known as the smooth hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Laserpitium glabrum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
