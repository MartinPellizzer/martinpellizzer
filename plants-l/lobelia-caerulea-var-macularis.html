Lobelia caerulea var. macularis

Lobelia caerulea var. macularis, commonly known as the spotted blue lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers with distinctive yellow spots. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral markings.
