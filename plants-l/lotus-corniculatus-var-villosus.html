Lotus corniculatus var. villosus

Lotus corniculatus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
