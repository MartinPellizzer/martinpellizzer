Lactuca filicina

Lactuca filicina, commonly known as the fern-leaved lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a loose, rounded inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Lactuca filicina is typically an annual or biennial plant, and it is known for its mild, slightly bitter taste.
