Lepanthes debedoutii

Lepanthes debedoutii, commonly known as the Debedout's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit.

Lepanthes debedoutii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
