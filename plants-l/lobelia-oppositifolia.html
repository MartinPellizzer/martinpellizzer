Lobelia oppositifolia

Lobelia oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lobelia oppositifolia is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
