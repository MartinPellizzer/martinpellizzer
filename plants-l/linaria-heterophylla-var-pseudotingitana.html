Linaria heterophylla var. pseudotingitana

Linaria heterophylla var. pseudotingitana, commonly known as the variable horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The variety is noted for its variability in leaf shape and size, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
