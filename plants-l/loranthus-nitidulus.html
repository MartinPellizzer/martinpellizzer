Loranthus nitidulus

Loranthus nitidulus, commonly known as the shining mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pink flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in Australia and is an example of a tropical mistletoe with unique ecological interactions.
