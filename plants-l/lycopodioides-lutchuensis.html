Lycopodioides lutchuensis

Lycopodioides lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchu fern, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure with sporophylls that enclose the spores.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in the study of ancient plant lineages and evolutionary botany.
