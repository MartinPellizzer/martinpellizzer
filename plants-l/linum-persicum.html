Linum persicum

Linum persicum, commonly known as Persian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy.

Linum persicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
