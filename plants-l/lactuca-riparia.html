Lactuca riparia

Lactuca riparia, commonly known as the river lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist environments such as riverbanks, wet meadows, and marshy areas.

Lactuca riparia is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
