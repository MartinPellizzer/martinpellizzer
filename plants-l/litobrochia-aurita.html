Litobrochia aurita

Litobrochia aurita, commonly known as the golden ear cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Litobrochia aurita is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
