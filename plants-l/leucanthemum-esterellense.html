Leucanthemum esterellense

Leucanthemum esterellense, commonly known as the Esterellense ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
