Lindsaea guianensis subsp. lanceastrum

Lindsaea guianensis subsp.

lanceastrum, commonly known as the lance-leaf spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to South America. It belongs to the family Lindsaeaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered with spirally arranged leaves.

The leaves are typically lanceolate, hence the subspecies name, and are often glossy with a distinct midrib. It produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia on specialized leaves called sporophylls.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
