Luzula erecta var. congesta

Luzula erecta var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered wood rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula erecta var. congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
