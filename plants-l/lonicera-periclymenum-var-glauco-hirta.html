Lonicera periclymenum var. glauco-hirta

Lonicera periclymenum var. glauco-hirta, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, whitish hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
