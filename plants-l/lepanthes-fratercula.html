Lepanthes fratercula

Lepanthes fratercula, commonly known as the "Cockatoo Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bird-like flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Lepanthes fratercula is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
