Lindera megaphylla

Lindera megaphylla, commonly known as the large-leaf ligusticum, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
