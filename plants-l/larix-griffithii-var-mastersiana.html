Larix griffithii var. mastersiana

Larix griffithii var. mastersiana, commonly known as the Griffiths' larch, is a rare and botanically intriguing variant of the larch tree.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, specifically in northern India and Nepal. This variety is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its unique foliage, which consists of needle-like leaves that turn golden in the fall. The cones are small and woody, typically maturing within a single season.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
