Lotus jolyi var. eriocarpus

Lotus jolyi var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the wooly lotus, is a rare and unique variant of the lotus plant native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy or woolly texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential ecological and medicinal significance.
