Leiothrix fluitans

Leiothrix fluitans, commonly known as the floating feathergrass, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant's leaves are slender and arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to float on the water's surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in clusters and are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

Leiothrix fluitans plays a minor but important part in providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
