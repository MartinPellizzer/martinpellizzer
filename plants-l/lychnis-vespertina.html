Lychnis vespertina

Lychnis vespertina, commonly known as evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and prominent yellow stamens.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
