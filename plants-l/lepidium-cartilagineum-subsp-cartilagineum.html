Lepidium cartilagineum subsp. cartilagineum

Lepidium cartilagineum subsp.

cartilagineum, commonly known as the bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features fleshy, inflated pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along coastal areas.
