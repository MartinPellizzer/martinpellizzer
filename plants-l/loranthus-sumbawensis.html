Loranthus sumbawensis

Loranthus sumbawensis, commonly known as the Sumbawa mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows as a hemiparasite on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the rainforests of Indonesia. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some organic compounds to the host. Its small, white or pale green leaves are typically arranged in pairs along the stems.

The flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with a structure that facilitates pollination by insects.
