Lyonia rubiginosa var. costata

Lyonia rubiginosa var. costata, commonly known as the variegated lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant features small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a distinctive wavy or toothed margin, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
