Lupinus arbutosocius

Lupinus arbutosocius, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal grasslands and sandy soils. The plant features erect, branched stems and produces clusters of pea-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus arbutosocius plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
