Linum usitatissimum var. regale

Linum usitatissimum var. regale, commonly known as royal flax, is a cultivated variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and large, showy flowers that range from white to deep purple. The plant produces long, fibrous stalks that are used for fiber extraction, similar to its parent species. Its foliage is lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
