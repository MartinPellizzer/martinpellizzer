Lotus ornithopodioides

Lotus ornithopodioides, commonly known as the slender lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
