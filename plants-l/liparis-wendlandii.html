Liparis wendlandii

Liparis wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a single, erect stem. The plant produces showy, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow markings, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.
