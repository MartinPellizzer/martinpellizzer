Lobelia tomentosa var. multiflora

Lobelia tomentosa var. multiflora, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
