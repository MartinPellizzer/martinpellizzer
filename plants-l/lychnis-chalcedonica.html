Lychnis chalcedonica

Lychnis chalcedonica, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Lychnis chalcedonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
