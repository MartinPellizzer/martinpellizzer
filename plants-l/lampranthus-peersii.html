Lampranthus peersii

Lampranthus peersii, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are typically bright red or orange, which are covered in small, dense leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.
