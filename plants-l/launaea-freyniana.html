Launaea freyniana

Launaea freyniana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant has a creeping, succulent growth habit with hairy stems and lobed leaves adapted to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Launaea freyniana is well-suited to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
