Lophanthera delphiniifolia

Lophanthera delphiniifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to red or orange, attracting various wildlife.

Lophanthera delphiniifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
