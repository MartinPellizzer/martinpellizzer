Lonicera hirtipes

Lonicera hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are red or orange, containing small seeds, and are edible for birds.

Lonicera hirtipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
