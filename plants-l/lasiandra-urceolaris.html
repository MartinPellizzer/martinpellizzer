Lasiandra urceolaris

Lasiandra urceolaris, commonly known as the bottlebrush lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brush-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface.

Lasiandra urceolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
