Limnopeuce vulgaris

Limnopeuce vulgaris, commonly known as the common water lily.

It is a aquatic plant characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

Limnopeuce vulgaris plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
