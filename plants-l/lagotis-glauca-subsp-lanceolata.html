Lagotis glauca subsp. lanceolata

Lagotis glauca subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glaucous leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Campanulaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
