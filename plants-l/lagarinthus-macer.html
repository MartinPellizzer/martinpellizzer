Lagarinthus macer

Lagarinthus macer, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

Lagarinthus macer thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.
