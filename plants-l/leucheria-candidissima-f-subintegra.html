Leucheria candidissima f. subintegra

Leucheria candidissima f. subintegra, commonly known as the white-leaved marsh plant, is a rare and delicate herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated pedicels. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.
