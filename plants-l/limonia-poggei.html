Limonia poggei

Limonia poggei, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to orange when mature. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

Limonia poggei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
