Lycopodium × brucei

Lycopodium × brucei, commonly known as the clubmoss hybrid, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branching stems. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium × brucei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
