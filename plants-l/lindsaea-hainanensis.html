Lindsaea hainanensis

Lindsaea hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to China's Hainan Island.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including sori and indusia, are well-developed and typical of the Polypodiaceae family.

Lindsaea hainanensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential conservation significance.
