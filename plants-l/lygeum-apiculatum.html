Lygeum apiculatum

Lygeum apiculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and pointed at the tips. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
