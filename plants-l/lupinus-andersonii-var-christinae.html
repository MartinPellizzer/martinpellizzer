Lupinus andersonii var. christinae

Lupinus andersonii var. christinae, commonly known as the Christina lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
