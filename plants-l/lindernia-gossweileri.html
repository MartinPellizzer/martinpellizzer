Lindernia gossweileri

Lindernia gossweileri, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Lindernia gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
