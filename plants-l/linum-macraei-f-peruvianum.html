Linum macraei f. peruvianum

Linum macraei f. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. This particular form is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and delicate, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, adding a touch of color to its natural habitat.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in cooler climates.
