Lespedeza japonica var. spicata

Lespedeza japonica var. spicata, commonly known as Japanese bush clover, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. The plant produces small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a feathery appearance.

Lespedeza japonica var. spicata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
