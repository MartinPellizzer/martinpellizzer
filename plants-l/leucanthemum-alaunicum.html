Leucanthemum alaunicum

Leucanthemum alaunicum, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disc, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is a perennial species that produces small, showy flowers from late spring to early summer.

Leucanthemum alaunicum is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
