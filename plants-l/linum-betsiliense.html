Linum betsiliense

Linum betsiliense, commonly known as the Betsileo flax, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Linum betsiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
