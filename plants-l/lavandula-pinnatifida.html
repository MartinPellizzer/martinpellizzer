Lavandula pinnatifida

Lavandula pinnatifida, commonly known as Spanish lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Spain and northern Africa. This plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense spike-like clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.
