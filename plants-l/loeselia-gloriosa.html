Loeselia gloriosa

Loeselia gloriosa, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-brown seed pods after flowering.

Loeselia gloriosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
