Lupinaster burchellianus

Lupinaster burchellianus, commonly known as the Burchell's lupin, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.
