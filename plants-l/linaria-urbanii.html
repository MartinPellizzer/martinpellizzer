Linaria urbanii

Linaria urbanii, commonly known as the urban wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or urban environments, such as walls, cracks in pavement, and dry soils. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria urbanii is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
