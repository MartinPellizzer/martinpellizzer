Lithospermum rugosum

Lithospermum rugosum, commonly known as rough rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale purple to reddish flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated, red or orange berries. Lithospermum rugosum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
