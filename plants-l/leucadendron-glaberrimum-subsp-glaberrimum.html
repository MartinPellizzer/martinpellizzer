Leucadendron glaberrimum subsp. glaberrimum

Leucadendron glaberrimum subsp.

glaberrimum, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery hairs.

The plant produces cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
