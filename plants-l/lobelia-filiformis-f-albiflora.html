Lobelia filiformis f. albiflora

Lobelia filiformis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered slender lobelia, is a hybrid form of the slender lobelia.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, and it produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
