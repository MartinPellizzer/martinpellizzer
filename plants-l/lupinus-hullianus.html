Lupinus hullianus

Lupinus hullianus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Lupinus hullianus plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
