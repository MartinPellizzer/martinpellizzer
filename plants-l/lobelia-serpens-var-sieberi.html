Lobelia serpens var. sieberi

Lobelia serpens var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's narrowleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple in color.

This variety is well adapted to moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
