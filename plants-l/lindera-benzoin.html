Lindera benzoin

Lindera benzoin, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, opposite, and ovate leaves that emit a spicy scent when crushed. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

Lindera benzoin is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial shade.
