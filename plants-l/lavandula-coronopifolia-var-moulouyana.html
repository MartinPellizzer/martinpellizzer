Lavandula coronopifolia var. moulouyana

Lavandula coronopifolia var. moulouyana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, purple flowers. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and essential oil content, which is used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
