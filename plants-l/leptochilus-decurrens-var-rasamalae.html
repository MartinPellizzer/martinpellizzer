Leptochilus decurrens var. rasamalae

Leptochilus decurrens var. rasamalae, commonly known as the red-stemmed leatherydock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems that are often reddish in color, and its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
