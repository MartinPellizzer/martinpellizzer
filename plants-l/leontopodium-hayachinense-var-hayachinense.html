Leontopodium hayachinense var. hayachinense

Leontopodium hayachinense var. hayachinense, commonly known as the Japanese edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
