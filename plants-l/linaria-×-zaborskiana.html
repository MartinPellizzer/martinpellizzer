Linaria × zaborskiana

Linaria × zaborskiana, commonly known as the Zaborski's toadflax, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Linaria × zaborskiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
