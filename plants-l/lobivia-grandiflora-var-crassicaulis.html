Lobivia grandiflora var. crassicaulis

Lobivia grandiflora var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, spiny structures, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
