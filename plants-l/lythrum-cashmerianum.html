Lythrum cashmerianum

Lythrum cashmerianum, commonly known as Kashmir rose or Kashmir lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
