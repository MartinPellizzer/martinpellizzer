Ledum glandulosum var. californicum

Ledum glandulosum var. californicum, commonly known as California bog rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of streams. Its foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its role in stabilizing wetland environments.
