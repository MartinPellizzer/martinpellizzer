Lippia tegulifera

Lippia tegulifera, commonly known as the Mexican mint or Mexican blue sage, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in dry, open areas. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Lippia tegulifera is also valued as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
