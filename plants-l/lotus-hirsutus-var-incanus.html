Lotus hirsutus var. incanus

Lotus hirsutus var. incanus, commonly known as the hairy lotus or the silver lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
