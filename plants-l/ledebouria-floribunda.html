Ledebouria floribunda

Ledebouria floribunda, commonly known as the Cape goldfish, is a species of bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, underground bulbs and succulent leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
