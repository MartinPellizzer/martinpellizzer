Linaria origanifolia var. bourgaei

Linaria origanifolia var. bourgaei, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed.
