Lastila hirsuta var. biennis

Lastila hirsuta var. biennis, commonly known as the hairy lastila, is a biennial plant native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its life cycle spans two years, with the first year focused on vegetative growth and the second year on flowering and seed production.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
