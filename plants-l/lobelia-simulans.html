Lobelia simulans

Lobelia simulans, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a mist-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia simulans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
