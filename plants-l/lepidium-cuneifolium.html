Lepidium cuneifolium

Lepidium cuneifolium, commonly known as wild cress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Lepidium cuneifolium is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
