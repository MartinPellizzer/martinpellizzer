Loudetia pennata

Loudetia pennata, commonly known as the spiked grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive coloration and texture.
