Lacinaria scariosa var. corymbulosa

Lacinaria scariosa var. corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered lacinaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that aids in water retention. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in corymbs, giving it its specific epithet. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand periods of drought.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
