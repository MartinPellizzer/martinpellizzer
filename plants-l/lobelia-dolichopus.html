Lobelia dolichopus

Lobelia dolichopus, commonly known as the long-stemmed lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Lobelia dolichopus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
