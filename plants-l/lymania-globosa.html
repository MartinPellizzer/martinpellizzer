Lymania globosa

Lymania globosa, commonly known as the globular thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny fruiting heads that contain numerous small, fluffy seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, sometimes aromatic scent.

Lymania globosa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
