Leptosiphon pygmaeus subsp. continentalis

Leptosiphon pygmaeus subsp.

continentalis, commonly known as the continental dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions as a garden plant.
