Lithospermum tinctorium

Lithospermum tinctorium, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its roots and stems contain compounds such as lithospermic acid, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its dye-producing properties, giving it a historical significance in textile industries.
