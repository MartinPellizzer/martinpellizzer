Leptodermis parvifolia

Leptodermis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved skin plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
