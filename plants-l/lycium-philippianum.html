Lycium philippianum

Lycium philippianum, commonly known as the Philippine boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible but contain toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
