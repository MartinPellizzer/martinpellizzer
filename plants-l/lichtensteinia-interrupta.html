Lichtensteinia interrupta

Lichtensteinia interrupta, commonly known as the Cape primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season.
