Lisianthius pyriformis

Lisianthius pyriformis, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped inflorescence that turns golden yellow as it matures. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
