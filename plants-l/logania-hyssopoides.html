Logania hyssopoides

Logania hyssopoides, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
