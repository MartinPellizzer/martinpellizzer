Lippia bromleyana var. hatschbachii

Lippia bromleyana var. hatschbachii, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant is part of the Verbenaceae family and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient choice for landscaping in arid regions.
