Loranthus phillyreoides

Loranthus phillyreoides, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Loranthus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is found in various parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia.

Its parasitic nature and ornamental berries make it both ecologically significant and culturally notable.
