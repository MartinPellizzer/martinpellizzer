Lessertia fruticosa

Lessertia fruticosa, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lessertia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
