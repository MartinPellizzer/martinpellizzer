Lonicera sempervirens var. superba

Lonicera sempervirens var. superba, commonly known as the flame honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
