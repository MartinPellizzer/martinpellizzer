Lasiostelma ramosissimum

Lasiostelma ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Despite its parasitic nature, Lasiostelma ramosissimum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species.
