Liatris pilosa var. gracilis

Liatris pilosa var. gracilis, commonly known as slender blazing star, is a native perennial herb found in the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

Its ecological importance lies in supporting local wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
