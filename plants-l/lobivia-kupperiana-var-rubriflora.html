Lobivia kupperiana var. rubriflora

Lobivia kupperiana var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in full sunlight and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
