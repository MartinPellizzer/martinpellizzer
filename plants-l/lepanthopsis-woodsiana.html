Lepanthopsis woodsiana

Lepanthopsis woodsiana, commonly known as the Wood's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors and rock crevices. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few leaves, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
