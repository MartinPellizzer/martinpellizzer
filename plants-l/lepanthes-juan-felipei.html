Lepanthes juan-felipei

Lepanthes juan-felipei, commonly known as Juan Felipe's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, often ephemeral blooms that appear during the wet season. It grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
