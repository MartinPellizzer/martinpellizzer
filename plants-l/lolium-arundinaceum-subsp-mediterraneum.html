Lolium arundinaceum subsp. mediterraneum

Lolium arundinaceum subsp.

mediterraneum, commonly known as Mediterranean ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
