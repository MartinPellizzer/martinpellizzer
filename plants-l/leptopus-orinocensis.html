Leptopus orinocensis

Leptopus orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Orinoco River basin in South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. It plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.

Its unique adaptations allow it to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
