Lactuca gracilis var. khasiana

Lactuca gracilis var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically bright green and have a mild, slightly bitter taste. The plant thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate garden environments.

This variety is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
