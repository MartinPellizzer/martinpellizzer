Lonchocarpus barbatus

Lonchocarpus barbatus, commonly known as the pink bean or wild senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
