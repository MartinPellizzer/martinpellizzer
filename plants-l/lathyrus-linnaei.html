Lathyrus linnaei

Lathyrus linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean vetch, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. Lathyrus linnaei is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
