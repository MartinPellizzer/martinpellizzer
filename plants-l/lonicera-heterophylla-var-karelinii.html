Lonicera heterophylla var. karelinii

Lonicera heterophylla var. karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
