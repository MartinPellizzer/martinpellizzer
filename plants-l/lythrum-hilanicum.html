Lythrum hilanicum

Lythrum hilanicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lythrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lythraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wet meadows and marshy areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. Its showy spikes of pink to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
