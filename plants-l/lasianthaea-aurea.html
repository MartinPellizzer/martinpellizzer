Lasianthaea aurea

Lasianthaea aurea, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a striking species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
