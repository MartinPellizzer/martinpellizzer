Lemna valdiviana var. platyclados

Lemna valdiviana var. platyclados, commonly known as the broad-leafed watermeal, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the duckweed family (Lemnaceae).

It is characterized by its tiny, floating leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape, often forming dense mats on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant lacks true roots and instead has root-like structures called rhizoids that anchor it to submerged surfaces. It reproduces rapidly through vegetative propagation, with new plants forming from the fragmentation of existing fronds.

This species is often found in still or slow-moving waters and is adapted to nutrient-rich environments.
