Leda roseopunctata

Leda roseopunctata, commonly known as the spotted leda, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Leda roseopunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
