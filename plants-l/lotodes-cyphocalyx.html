Lotodes cyphocalyx

Lotodes cyphocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its floating, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a robust root system that anchors it in shallow water, while its leaves provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as beetles and flies.

Lotodes cyphocalyx plays an important role in water ecosystems by helping to control algae growth and improve water quality.
