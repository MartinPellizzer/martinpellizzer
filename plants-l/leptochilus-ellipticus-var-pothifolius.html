Leptochilus ellipticus var. pothifolius

Leptochilus ellipticus var. pothifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It typically grows in open habitats such as limestone outcrops and dry slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soil. The plant features small, basal leaves that are elliptic in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are bright yellow and borne in clusters, adding visual interest to its compact growth form.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
