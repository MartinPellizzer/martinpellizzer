Levisticum persicum

Levisticum persicum, commonly known as Persian parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often used as a culinary herb. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Levisticum persicum is valued for its mild, parsley-like flavor and is used in traditional medicine and cooking.
