Lupinus altimontanus

Lupinus altimontanus, commonly known as the alpine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus altimontanus is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
