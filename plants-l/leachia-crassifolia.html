Leachia crassifolia

Leachia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leafed leachia, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
