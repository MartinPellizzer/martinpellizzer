Lithospermum galeottii

Lithospermum galeottii, commonly known as the rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Lithospermum galeottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
