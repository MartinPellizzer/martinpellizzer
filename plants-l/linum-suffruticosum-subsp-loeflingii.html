Linum suffruticosum subsp. loeflingii

Linum suffruticosum subsp.

loeflingii, commonly known as the Loefling's flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
