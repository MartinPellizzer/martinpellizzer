Lycopodium copelandianum

Lycopodium copelandianum, commonly known as the Copeland's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a stem that branches into whorls of small, needle-like leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces microspores and megaspores on separate cones, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Lycopodium copelandianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
