Lilium dalmaticum

Lilium dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian lily, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

The bulbs of this species are also used in traditional medicine.
