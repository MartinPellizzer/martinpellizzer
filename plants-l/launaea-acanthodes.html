Launaea acanthodes

Launaea acanthodes, commonly known as the spiny dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a cylindrical, spiny stem and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and the plant is adapted to survive in dry environments.

Launaea acanthodes is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy soils.
