Laguncularia racemosa var. glabriflora

Laguncularia racemosa var. glabriflora, commonly known as the slender mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that thrives in brackish water environments, often forming dense thickets in mangal ecosystems. The plant has smooth, pale bark and produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are ovate and slightly waxy, adapted to reduce water loss in saline conditions.

This variant is particularly notable for its ability to tolerate fluctuating salinity levels, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
