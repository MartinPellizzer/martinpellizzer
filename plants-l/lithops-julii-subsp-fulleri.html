Lithops julii subsp. fulleri

Lithops julii subsp.

fulleri, commonly known as the "dwarf ice plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
