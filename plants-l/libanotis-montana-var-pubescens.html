Libanotis montana var. pubescens

Libanotis montana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

This species is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of rocky and grassy slopes.
