Linaria saxatilis var. minor

Linaria saxatilis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid and rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
