Limnanthes striata

Limnanthes striata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, strap-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds.

Limnanthes striata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
