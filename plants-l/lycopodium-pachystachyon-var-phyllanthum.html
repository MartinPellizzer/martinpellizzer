Lycopodium pachystachyon var. phyllanthum

Lycopodium pachystachyon var. phyllanthum, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and historical use in traditional medicine.
