Lobelia triquetra var. foliosa

Lobelia triquetra var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are broad and heart-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
