Lycopodium varium var. polaris

Lycopodium varium var. polaris, commonly known as the polar clubmoss, is a rare and specialized fern-like plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobila. This plant thrives in harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its reproductive structures are adapted to the short growing seasons of polar climates.

Lycopodium varium var. polaris plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its environment, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
