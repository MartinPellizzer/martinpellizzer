Lindackeria paludosa

Lindackeria paludosa, commonly known as the marsh lindackeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich soils along the edges of ponds, lakes, and marshes. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in moist environments.

Lindackeria paludosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial invertebrates.
