Linaria kopetdaghensis

Linaria kopetdaghensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagh toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linaria, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as toadflaxes. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Linaria kopetdaghensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
