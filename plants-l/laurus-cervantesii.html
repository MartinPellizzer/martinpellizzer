Laurus cervantesii

Laurus cervantesii, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other familiar plants like bay laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves and small white flowers that appear in clusters. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Laurus cervantesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
