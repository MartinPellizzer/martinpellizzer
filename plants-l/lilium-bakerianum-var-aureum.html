Lilium bakerianum var. aureum

Lilium bakerianum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden baker's lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with a distinctive golden-orange coloration. The petals are broad and recurved, forming a cup-like shape, while the tepals are often streaked with red or purple veins. This variety is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
