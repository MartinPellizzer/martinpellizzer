Linaria viscosa var. typica

Linaria viscosa var. typica, commonly known as the sticky toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, sticky leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria viscosa var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
