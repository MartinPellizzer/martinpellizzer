Leptospermum minutifolium

Leptospermum minutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-grey pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
