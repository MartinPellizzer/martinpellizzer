Lycopus europaeus subsp. menthifolius

Lycopus europaeus subsp.

menthifolius, commonly known as European water horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and antispasmodic properties.
