Liparis bilobulata

Liparis bilobulata, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with two distinct lobes on each petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique floral structure, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
