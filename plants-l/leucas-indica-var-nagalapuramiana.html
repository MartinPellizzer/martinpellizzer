Leucas indica var. nagalapuramiana

Leucas indica var. nagalapuramiana, commonly known as the Nagalapuram leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
