Lophiolepis strigosa

Lophiolepis strigosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
