Lipocarpha cochleata

Lipocarpha cochleata, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spirally twisted stems that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Lipocarpha cochleata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
