Lycium carolinianum var. californicum

Lycium carolinianum var. californicum, commonly known as California boxthorn, is a shrub native to coastal areas of California.

It features dense, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to deter wildlife.
