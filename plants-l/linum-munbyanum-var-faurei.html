Linum munbyanum var. faurei

Linum munbyanum var. faurei, commonly known as the Munby flax, is a variant of the flax species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
