Lupinus lepidus subsp. typicus

Lupinus lepidus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the slender lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus lepidus subsp.

typicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
