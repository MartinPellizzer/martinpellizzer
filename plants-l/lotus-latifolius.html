Lotus latifolius

Lotus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf lotus, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Lotus latifolius is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in various regions.
