Lombardochloa rufa var. sparsepilosa

Lombardochloa rufa var. sparsepilosa, commonly known as the sparse-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in sparse, short hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid landscapes.
