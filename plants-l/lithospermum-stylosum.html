Lithospermum stylosum

Lithospermum stylosum, commonly known as the slender lithosperm, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
