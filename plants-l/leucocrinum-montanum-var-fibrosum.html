Leucocrinum montanum var. fibrosum

Leucocrinum montanum var. fibrosum, commonly known as the fibrous mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system, which contributes to its name and aids in its survival in rocky or poor soil conditions. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
