Lycopodium catharticum

Lycopodium catharticum, commonly known as the medicinal clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-producing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a creeping stem that produces whorls of small, needle-like leaves. Lycopodium catharticum is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to grow in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
