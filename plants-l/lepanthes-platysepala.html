Lepanthes platysepala

Lepanthes platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepal orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Lepanthes platysepala thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical forests.
