Lucuma lanceolata

Lucuma lanceolata, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a tropical plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, elongated, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
