Lippia rotundifolia var. cordata

Lippia rotundifolia var. cordata, commonly known as Mexican mint or creeping lippia, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often described as having a mint-like scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal remedies.
