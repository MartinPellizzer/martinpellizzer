Lepidium glaucum

Lepidium glaucum, commonly known as glacier peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compact, rosette-like foliage. It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
