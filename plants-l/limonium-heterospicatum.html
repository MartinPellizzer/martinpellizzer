Limonium heterospicatum

Limonium heterospicatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Limonium heterospicatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
