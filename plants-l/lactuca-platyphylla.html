Lactuca platyphylla

Lactuca platyphylla, commonly known as broadleaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and slightly hairy. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.
