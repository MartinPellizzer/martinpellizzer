Licopolia

Licopolia, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. Licopolia species are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plants have a rhizomatous growth habit and produce spores in clusters on the underside of their fronds.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
