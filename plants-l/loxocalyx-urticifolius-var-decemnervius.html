Loxocalyx urticifolius var. decemnervius

Loxocalyx urticifolius var. decemnervius, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a hairy, often stinging underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically greenish-white in color.

The plant is known for its irritant hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
