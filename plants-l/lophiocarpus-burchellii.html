Lophiocarpus burchellii

Lophiocarpus burchellii, commonly known as the African bignonia, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Bignoniaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Lophiocarpus burchellii is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant floral display.
