Lathyrus palustris var. dasycarpus

Lathyrus palustris var. dasycarpus, commonly known as marsh vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is adapted to moist environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
