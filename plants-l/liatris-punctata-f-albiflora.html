Liatris punctata f. albiflora

Liatris punctata f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower bluetail, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pale blue to white flower clusters. Its botanical structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and an upright flowering stem.

This variety is notable for its striking contrast between the green foliage and its pale floral display.
