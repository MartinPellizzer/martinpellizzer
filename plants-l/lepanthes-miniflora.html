Lepanthes miniflora

Lepanthes miniflora, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tiny, delicate flowers that typically measure less than a centimeter in diameter. The plant has a short, fleshy rhizome and produces numerous small, clustered pseudobulbs. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for capturing moisture from the humid air.

Lepanthes miniflora is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
