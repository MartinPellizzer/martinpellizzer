Lygodium kingii

Lygodium kingii, commonly known as the King's fern, is a species of fern native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lygodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rainforest areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a feature common among ferns.

Lygodium kingii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
