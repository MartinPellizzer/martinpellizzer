Linum anglicum

Linum anglicum, commonly known as Common Flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces small, blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum anglicum is cultivated for its fibers, which are used in the production of linen, and it also has historical uses in herbal medicine.
