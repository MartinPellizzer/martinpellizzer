Litsea bennettii

Litsea bennettii, commonly known as Bennett's litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Litsea bennettii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
