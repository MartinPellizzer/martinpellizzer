Linum perenne var. lepagei

Linum perenne var. lepagei, commonly known as the Lepage's flax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
