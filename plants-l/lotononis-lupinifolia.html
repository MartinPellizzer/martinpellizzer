Lotononis lupinifolia

Lotononis lupinifolia, commonly known as the African lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep roots and a capacity for nitrogen fixation through symbiotic bacteria. It produces pods containing small, hard seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Lotononis lupinifolia make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
