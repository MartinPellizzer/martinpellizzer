Lessingianthus lacunosus

Lessingianthus lacunosus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
