Lilium pardalinum var. robinsonianum

Lilium pardalinum var. robinsonianum, commonly known as the red tiger lily, is a rare and striking variant of the tiger lily species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western China, where it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers with distinctive black spots, resembling a tiger's pattern. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
