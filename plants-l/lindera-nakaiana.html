Lindera nakaiana

Lindera nakaiana, commonly known as Japanese spicebush, is a small evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Lindera nakaiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
