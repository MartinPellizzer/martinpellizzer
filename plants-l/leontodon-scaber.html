Leontodon scaber

Leontodon scaber, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are rough to the touch, with a distinctive serrated edge, and its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
