Louisiella elephantipes

Louisiella elephantipes, commonly known as the giant elephant foot, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, elephant-like roots that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a short, woody stem with sparse, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, typically in late spring or early summer.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
