Lupinus chamissonis

Lupinus chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and disturbed sites.

Lupinus chamissonis is a member of the legume family and plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
