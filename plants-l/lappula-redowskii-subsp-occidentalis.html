Lappula redowskii subsp. occidentalis

Lappula redowskii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western lappula, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, and it produces small, elliptical seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local insect populations.
