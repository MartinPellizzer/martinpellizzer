Lychnis indica

Lychnis indica, commonly known as the Indian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink or red in color.

Lychnis indica is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
