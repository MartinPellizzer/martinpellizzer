Ligularia sibirica subsp. arctica

Ligularia sibirica subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic ligularia, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white to yellow margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, terminal cluster.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
