Lankesterella longicollis

Lankesterella longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked sea tulip, is a species of sea anemone found in shallow marine environments.

It is characterized by its elongated, flexible oral disc and a crown of long, slender tentacles. The organism exhibits a unique radial symmetry and possesses a gastrovascular cavity for nutrient absorption. It is typically found attached to substrates such as rocks or coral in tropical and subtropical waters.

Lankesterella longicollis plays a role in marine ecosystems by serving as a food source for various marine animals.
