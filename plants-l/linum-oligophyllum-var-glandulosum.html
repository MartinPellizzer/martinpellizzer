Linum oligophyllum var. glandulosum

Linum oligophyllum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular flax, is a variant of the flax species characterized by its glandular features on the stems and leaves.

It is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, sticky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
