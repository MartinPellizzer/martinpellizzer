Lebeckia aphylla

Lebeckia aphylla, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive white center, blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
