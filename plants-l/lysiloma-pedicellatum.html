Lysiloma pedicellatum

Lysiloma pedicellatum, commonly known as the red mulga, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have a yellowish or greenish hue.

The tree is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
