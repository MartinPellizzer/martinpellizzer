Luzula confusa f. subspicata

Luzula confusa f. subspicata, commonly known as the slender wood rush, is a variant of the wood rush genus native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more upright growth habit and slightly elongated inflorescences compared to the typical form.
