Lupinus tegeticulatus var. grandiflorus

Lupinus tegeticulatus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
