Linum virginianum var. medium

Linum virginianum var. medium, commonly known as the medium flax, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and branching.

The seeds are small and capsule-like, containing numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.
