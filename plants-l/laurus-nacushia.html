Laurus nacushia

Laurus nacushia, commonly known as the bay laurel or sweet bay, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen, glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark purple berries.

Laurus nacushia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant and is also used in culinary applications, particularly in the preparation of certain dishes and beverages.
