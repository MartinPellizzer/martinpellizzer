Lycopodioides compta

Lycopodioides compta, commonly known as the narrowleaf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its production of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Lycopodioides compta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
