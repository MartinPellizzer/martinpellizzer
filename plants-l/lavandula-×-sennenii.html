Lavandula × sennenii

Lavandula × sennenii, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, spike-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet. The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves that are aromatic and often used in perfumery and aromatherapy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Lavandula × sennenii is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its calming and antiseptic effects.
