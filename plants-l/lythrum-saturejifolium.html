Lythrum saturejifolium

Lythrum saturejifolium, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, sunny conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in North America, where it outcompetes native vegetation.
