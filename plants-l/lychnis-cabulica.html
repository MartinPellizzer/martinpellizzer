Lychnis cabulica

Lychnis cabulica, commonly known as the Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or red in color.

It blooms from late spring to early summer and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
