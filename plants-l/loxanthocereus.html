Loxanthocereus

Loxanthocereus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to the Americas.

These plants are characterized by their spiny stems and large, showy flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, emitting a strong fragrance to attract pollinators like bats and moths. Loxanthocereus species are drought-tolerant and thrive in arid environments, making them popular among succulent enthusiasts.

Their unique blooming pattern and striking appearance make them a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
