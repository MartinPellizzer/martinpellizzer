Linum tenuifolium subsp. salsoloides

Linum tenuifolium subsp.

salsoloides, commonly known as the slenderleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes other species of flax known for their fibrous stems and showy flowers.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its resilience in harsh environments.
