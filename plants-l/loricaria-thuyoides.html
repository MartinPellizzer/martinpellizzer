Loricaria thuyoides

Loricaria thuyoides, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots anchor it in the substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Loricaria thuyoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by improving water quality and supporting biodiversity.
