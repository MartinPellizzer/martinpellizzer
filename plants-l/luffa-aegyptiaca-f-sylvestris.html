Luffa aegyptiaca f. sylvestris

Luffa aegyptiaca f. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild sponge gourd, is a variant of the common luffa native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and large, pale green to yellowish, sponge-like fruits that mature into fibrous structures. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its fibrous fruit, which can be used as a natural sponge or for fiber extraction.

This wild form is less commonly grown than its domesticated relatives but holds ecological and potential horticultural value.
