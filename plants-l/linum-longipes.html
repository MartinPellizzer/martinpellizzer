Linum longipes

Linum longipes, commonly known as the slender flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Linum longipes produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
