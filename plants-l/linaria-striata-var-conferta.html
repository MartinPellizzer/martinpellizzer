Linaria striata var. conferta

Linaria striata var. conferta, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
