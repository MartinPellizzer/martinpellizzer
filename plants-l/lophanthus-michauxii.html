Lophanthus michauxii

Lophanthus michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's lophanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lophanthus michauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
