Lupinus borealis

Lupinus borealis, commonly known as the Arctic lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus borealis is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh environmental conditions.
