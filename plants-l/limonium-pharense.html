Limonium pharense

Limonium pharense, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium pharense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
