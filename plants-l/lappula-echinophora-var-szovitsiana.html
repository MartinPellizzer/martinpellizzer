Lappula echinophora var. szovitsiana

Lappula echinophora var. szovitsiana, commonly known as the Hungarian salsify, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chenopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid regions.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and compact growth habit compared to other Lappula species.
