Lolium × hybridum

Lolium × hybridum, commonly known as ryegrass, is a hybrid species within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its coarse, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, two-lobed flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Ryegrass is widely cultivated for forage and turfgrass due to its rapid growth and high nutritional value.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a significant agricultural and ecological species.
