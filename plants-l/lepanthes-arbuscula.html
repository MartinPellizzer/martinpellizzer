Lepanthes arbuscula

Lepanthes arbuscula, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features slender, leafless stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that resembles a spider's abdomen.

Lepanthes arbuscula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
