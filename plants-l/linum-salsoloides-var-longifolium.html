Linum salsoloides var. longifolium

Linum salsoloides var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
