Laelia peduncularis

Laelia peduncularis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species.

Laelia peduncularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
