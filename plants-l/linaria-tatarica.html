Linaria tatarica

Linaria tatarica, commonly known as the Tatar Scotch bonnet, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow-orange flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, branching stems and narrow leaves make it a distinctive ornamental plant.

Linaria tatarica is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
