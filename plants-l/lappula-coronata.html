Lappula coronata

Lappula coronata, commonly known as crowned lappula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves and small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
