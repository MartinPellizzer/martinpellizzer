Lobelia macrocarpa

Lobelia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
