It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a delicate, creeping rhizome with numerous short, clustered fronds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, lanceolate pinnae that are usually glossy and dark green.

Lepisorus tosaensis is native to Japan and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity and moderate temperatures.
