Lavandula stoechas subsp. caesia

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or purple lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and showy, violet-blue flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense purple flower color compared to other lavenders.
