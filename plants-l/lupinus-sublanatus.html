Lupinus sublanatus

Lupinus sublanatus, commonly known as the silky lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has finely hairy leaves that give it a soft, silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus sublanatus is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
