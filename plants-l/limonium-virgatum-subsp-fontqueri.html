Limonium virgatum subsp. fontqueri

Limonium virgatum subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean and western Europe. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flower clusters.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is highly tolerant of salt spray and wind. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
