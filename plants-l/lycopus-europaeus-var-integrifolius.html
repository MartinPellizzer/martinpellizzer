Lycopus europaeus var. integrifolius

Lycopus europaeus var. integrifolius, commonly known as European bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with entire or slightly toothed margins. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, black, spherical berries.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
