Lathyrus celakovskyi

Lathyrus celakovskyi, commonly known as the Celakovsky pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Lathyrus celakovskyi is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
