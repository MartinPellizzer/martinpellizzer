Limonium cyrtostachyum

Limonium cyrtostachyum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Limonium cyrtostachyum is also known for its ability to withstand salt spray, making it suitable for seaside gardens.
