Loranthus puniceus

Loranthus puniceus, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also deriving some photosynthetic capability from its own leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that contain viscin, a sticky substance that aids in seed attachment to the host. It is native to Australia and is often found in forested areas, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Its presence can impact the health of its host trees, sometimes leading to reduced growth or even mortality in severe cases.
