Lippia ligustrina

Lippia ligustrina, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
