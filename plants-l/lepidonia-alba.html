Lepidonia alba

Lepidonia alba, commonly known as the white lepidonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.

Lepidonia alba is often found in shaded, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
