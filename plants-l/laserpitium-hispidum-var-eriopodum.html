Laserpitium hispidum var. eriopodum

Laserpitium hispidum var. eriopodum, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
