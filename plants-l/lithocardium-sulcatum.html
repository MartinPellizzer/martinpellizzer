Lithocardium sulcatum

Lithocardium sulcatum, commonly known as the ribbed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its drought-tolerant appearance. Lithocardium sulcatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in the Crassulaceae family.
