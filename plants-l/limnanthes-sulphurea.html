Limnanthes sulphurea

Limnanthes sulphurea, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Limnanthes sulphurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
