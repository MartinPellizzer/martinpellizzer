Lissochilus grantii

Lissochilus grantii, commonly known as the Grant's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble small slippers, with a prominent pouch-like structure. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native range.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its striking appearance.
