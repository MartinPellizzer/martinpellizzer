Lindsaea odorata var. japonica

Lindsaea odorata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese maidenhair fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and parts of China. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with arching, feathery leaves that are usually bright green and glossy.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
