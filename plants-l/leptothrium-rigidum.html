Leptothrium rigidum

Leptothrium rigidum, commonly known as the rigid leathery fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas with a Mediterranean climate. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
