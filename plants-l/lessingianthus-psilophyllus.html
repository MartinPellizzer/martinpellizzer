Lessingianthus psilophyllus

Lessingianthus psilophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
