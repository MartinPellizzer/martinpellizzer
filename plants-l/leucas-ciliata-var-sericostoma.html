Leucas ciliata var. sericostoma

Leucas ciliata var. sericostoma, commonly known as silverleaf leucas, is a variant of the leucas genus native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
