Lobelia sericea var. koenigii

Lobelia sericea var. koenigii, commonly known as silverleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-gray foliage and showy blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its narrow, upright growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
