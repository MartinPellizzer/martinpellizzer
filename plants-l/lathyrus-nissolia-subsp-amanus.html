Lathyrus nissolia subsp. amanus

Lathyrus nissolia subsp.

amanus, commonly known as the Amanus vetch, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue, and its flowers are typically small, pea-like, and arranged in clusters.

The plant produces elongated pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique subspecific traits and its role in local ecosystems.
