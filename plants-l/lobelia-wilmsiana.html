Lobelia wilmsiana

Lobelia wilmsiana, commonly known as Wilms' lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia wilmsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
