Luzula congesta var. glabra

Luzula congesta var. glabra, commonly known as the glabrous wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear spike-like inflorescences with small, greenish to whitish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and arranged in dense clusters at the base.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless surface, giving it the common name "glabrous.".
