Lycium chilense var. peruviense

Lycium chilense var. peruviense, commonly known as the Peruvian boxthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, orange-red berries. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in coastal sands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The berries are edible but contain seeds that are dispersed by birds and other animals.
