Lonicera demissa var. demissa

Lonicera demissa var. demissa, commonly known as the trailing honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairless and have a smooth texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
