Loranthus signatus var. cardiophyllus

Loranthus signatus var. cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a distinctive appearance with its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food for various wildlife species.
