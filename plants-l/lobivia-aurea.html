Lobivia aurea

Lobivia aurea, commonly known as the golden lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or golden hue, especially under sunlight. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Lobivia aurea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
