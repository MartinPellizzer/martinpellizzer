Lagenocarpus guianensis

Lagenocarpus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan bottle tree, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk, which stores water and helps it survive in arid conditions. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Lagenocarpus guianensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
