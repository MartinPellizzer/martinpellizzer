Lilaeopsis australica

Lilaeopsis australica, commonly known as the Australian lily.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland regions. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, adding a subtle floral display to its habitat.

Lilaeopsis australica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
