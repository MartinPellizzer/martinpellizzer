Lupinus luteolus var. albiflorus

Lupinus luteolus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

This variety is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
