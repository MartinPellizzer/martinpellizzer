Lonicera leiophylla

Lonicera leiophylla, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Lonicera leiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in partial shade.
