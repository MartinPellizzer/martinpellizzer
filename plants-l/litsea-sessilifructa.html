Litsea sessilifructa

Litsea sessilifructa, commonly known as the sessile-fruited litsea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow-green, and it produces small, rounded berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
