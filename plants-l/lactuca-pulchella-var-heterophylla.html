Lactuca pulchella var. heterophylla

Lactuca pulchella var. heterophylla, commonly known as the showy lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, with a rosette-like growth habit. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.

The variety heterophylla is distinguished by its variable leaf shapes and sizes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
