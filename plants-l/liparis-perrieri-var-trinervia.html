Liparis perrieri var. trinervia

Liparis perrieri var. trinervia, commonly known as the Trinerved Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it has a symbiotic relationship with certain mycorrhizal fungi.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
