Lepanthes quasimodo

Lepanthes quasimodo, commonly known as the Quasimodo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and showy, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
