Lathyrus parodii var. brevipetiolata

Lathyrus parodii var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stalked sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, wiry stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are trifoliate with short petioles. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, with a distinctive spurred petal, and bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
