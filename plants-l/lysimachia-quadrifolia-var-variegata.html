Lysimachia quadrifolia var. variegata

Lysimachia quadrifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation, which adds to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
