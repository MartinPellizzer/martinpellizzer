Lycopodium dacrydioides

Lycopodium dacrydioides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant has needle-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces cone-like structures called strobili that contain spores. Lycopodium dacrydioides is characterized by its primitive structure, resembling ancient plant forms, and it plays a role in soil stabilization in its native habitats.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
