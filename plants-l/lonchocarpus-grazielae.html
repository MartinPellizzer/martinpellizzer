Lonchocarpus grazielae

Lonchocarpus grazielae, commonly known as the Brazilian peacock bean, is a species of legume native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful pods that resemble peacock feathers, which are a key feature in its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, contributing to soil fertility. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental value.
