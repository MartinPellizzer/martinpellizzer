Lagoseris purpurea

Lagoseris purpurea, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species and environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Lagoseris purpurea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
