Limonium hoeltzeri

Limonium hoeltzeri, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Limonium hoeltzeri is a perennial species that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.

Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
