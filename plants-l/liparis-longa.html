Liparis longa

Liparis longa, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and clusters of small, greenish or white flowers. Native to temperate regions of Asia and North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
