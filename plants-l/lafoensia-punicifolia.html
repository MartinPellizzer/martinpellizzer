Lafoensia punicifolia

Lafoensia punicifolia, commonly known as the sour orange or Mexican tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The fruit is also used in culinary applications, particularly in making jams and beverages.
