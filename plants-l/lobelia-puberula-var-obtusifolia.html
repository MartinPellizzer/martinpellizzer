Lobelia puberula var. obtusifolia

Lobelia puberula var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
