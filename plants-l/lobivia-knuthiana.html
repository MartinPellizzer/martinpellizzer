Lobivia knuthiana

Lobivia knuthiana, commonly known as the Knuth's lobivia, is a species of succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its slow growth and striking appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
