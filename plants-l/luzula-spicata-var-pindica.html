Luzula spicata var. pindica

Luzula spicata var. pindica, commonly known as the Pindus wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, triangular flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
