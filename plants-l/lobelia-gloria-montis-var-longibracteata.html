Lobelia gloria-montis var. longibracteata

Lobelia gloria-montis var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract lobelia, is a striking perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
