Lychnis macrorhiza

Lychnis macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that bear clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Lychnis macrorhiza is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
