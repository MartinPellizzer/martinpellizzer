Lythrum anatolicum subsp. vanense

Lythrum anatolicum subsp.

vanense, commonly known as Anatolian rosemary, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other genera such as Lagerstroemia and Hibiscus.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
