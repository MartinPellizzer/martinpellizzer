Lepanthes exserta

Lepanthes exserta, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes exserta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
