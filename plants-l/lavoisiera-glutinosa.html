Lavoisiera glutinosa

Lavoisiera glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a sticky substance on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Lavoisiera glutinosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
