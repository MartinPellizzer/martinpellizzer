Linum monadelphum

Linum monadelphum, commonly known as the yellow flax or velvet flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Linum monadelphum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which can be used in various traditional applications.
