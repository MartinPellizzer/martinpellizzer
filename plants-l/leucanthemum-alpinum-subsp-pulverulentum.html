Leucanthemum alpinum subsp. pulverulentum

Leucanthemum alpinum subsp.

pulverulentum, commonly known as the Alpine ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its yellow central disk surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats at high altitudes. It has a compact, cushion-like growth form that helps it withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
