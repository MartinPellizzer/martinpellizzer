Lobivia famatinensis

Lobivia famatinensis, commonly known as the Famatinian lobivia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant typically has a compact growth habit with spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Lobivia famatinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
