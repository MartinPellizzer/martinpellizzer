Lantana velutinoides var. bruchii

Lantana velutinoides var. bruchii, commonly known as the velvet lantana, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has fuzzy, velvety leaves that give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in gardens and as a landscape plant.
