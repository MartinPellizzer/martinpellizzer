Liatris spicata var. racemosa

Liatris spicata var. racemosa, commonly known as the raceme blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features tall, unbranched stems that bear dense clusters of pink to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry to mesic prairies, open woods, and rocky slopes, preferring full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and gray-green in appearance.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
