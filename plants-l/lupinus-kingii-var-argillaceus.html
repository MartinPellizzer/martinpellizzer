Lupinus kingii var. argillaceus

Lupinus kingii var. argillaceus, commonly known as the clay lupin, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or clay soils and is adapted to low rainfall conditions. Its compound leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus kingii var. argillaceus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
