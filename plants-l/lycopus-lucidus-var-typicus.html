Lycopus lucidus var. typicus

Lycopus lucidus var. typicus, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense terminal spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
