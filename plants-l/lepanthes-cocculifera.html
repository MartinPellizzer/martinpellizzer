Lepanthes cocculifera

Lepanthes cocculifera, commonly known as the "Cocculifera Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are elliptical and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Lepanthes cocculifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
