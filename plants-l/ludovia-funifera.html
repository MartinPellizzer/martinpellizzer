Ludovia funifera

Ludovia funifera, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the lichen group, which is a symbiotic combination of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It is characterized by its soft, leathery thallus that often has a velvety or silky texture, giving it its common name. This lichen is typically found on tree bark, rocks, or soil in moist, shaded environments. It plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often used in ecological studies to assess air quality and environmental changes.
