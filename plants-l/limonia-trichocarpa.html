Limonia trichocarpa

Limonia trichocarpa, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and oval, citrus-like fruits that are typically sour and seedy. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The sour orange is also valued in horticulture for its use in grafting other citrus species.
