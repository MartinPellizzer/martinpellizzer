Ludwigia repens

Ludwigia repens, commonly known as the wandering willow, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with sprawling stems that often root at the nodes. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Ludwigia repens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
