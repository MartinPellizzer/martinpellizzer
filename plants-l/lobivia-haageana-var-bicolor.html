Lobivia haageana var. bicolor

Lobivia haageana var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored lobivia, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a grayish-green color and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has a unique two-toned appearance, with the upper part of the stem being lighter than the lower portion. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
