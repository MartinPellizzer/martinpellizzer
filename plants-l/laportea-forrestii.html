Laportea forrestii

Laportea forrestii, commonly known as the forrest's goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its flowers are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Laportea forrestii is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
