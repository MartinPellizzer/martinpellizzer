Lycium javellense

Lycium javellense, commonly known as the Java boxthorn, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Lycium javellense is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
