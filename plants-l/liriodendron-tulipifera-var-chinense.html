Liriodendron tulipifera var. chinense

Liriodendron tulipifera var. chinense, commonly known as the Chinese tulip tree, is a variant of the American tulip tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, yellowish-green to orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a smooth margin, often with a slight waxy coating. This variety typically grows to a height of 20-30 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
