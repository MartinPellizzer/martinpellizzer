Lipozygis

Lipozygis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa and are characterized by their intricate, often fragrant flowers. Lipozygis species typically have slender stems and are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic. Their leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to conserve moisture in their natural habitat.

The flowers exhibit a wide range of colors and are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
