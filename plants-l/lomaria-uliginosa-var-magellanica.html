Lomaria uliginosa var. magellanica

Lomaria uliginosa var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan bog lichen, is a crustose lichen that thrives in moist, acidic environments such as peat bogs and wetlands.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery thallus that is typically greenish to yellowish in color. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship with a specific cyanobacterial partner, which contributes to its ability to fix nitrogen. It is often found in temperate regions with high humidity and low light conditions.

This plant plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its native habitats.
