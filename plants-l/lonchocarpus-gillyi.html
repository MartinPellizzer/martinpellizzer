Lonchocarpus gillyi

Lonchocarpus gillyi, commonly known as the purple heart or gillyflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, meaning they have three leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
