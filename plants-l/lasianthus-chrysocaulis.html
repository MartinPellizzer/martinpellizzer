Lasianthus chrysocaulis

Lasianthus chrysocaulis, commonly known as the golden-stemmed lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, golden-colored stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
