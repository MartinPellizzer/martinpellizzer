Licinia

Licinia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Licinia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can also be found in woodland areas.
