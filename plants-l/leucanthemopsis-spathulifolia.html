Leucanthemopsis spathulifolia

Leucanthemopsis spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed white daisy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy white ray florets surrounded by a yellow disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant has lance-shaped to spatulate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, adding ornamental value to gardens.
