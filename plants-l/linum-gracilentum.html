Linum gracilentum

Linum gracilentum, commonly known as slender flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Linum gracilentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
