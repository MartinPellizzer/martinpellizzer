Liparis monoceros

Liparis monoceros, commonly known as the single-horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, elongated spur that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are often pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
