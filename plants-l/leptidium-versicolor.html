Leptidium versicolor

Leptidium versicolor, commonly known as the variable bladderwort, is a small flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its tiny, yellowish flowers and is typically found in moist, calcareous soils. Leptidium versicolor is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, wet environments, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
