Linaria amoris

Linaria amoris, commonly known as the love plant or love grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Linaria amoris is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
