Litsea depressa

Litsea depressa, commonly known as the broad-leafed litsea, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, dark purple berries.

Litsea depressa is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
