Lupinus perditorum

Lupinus perditorum, commonly known as the ghost lupin, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus perditorum is known for its ornamental value and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.
