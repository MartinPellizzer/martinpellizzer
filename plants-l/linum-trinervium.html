Linum trinervium

Linum trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax plants. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with three distinct veins running from the base to the tip.

Linum trinervium is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
