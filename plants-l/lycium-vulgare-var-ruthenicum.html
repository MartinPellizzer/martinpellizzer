Lycium vulgare var. ruthenicum

Lycium vulgare var. ruthenicum, commonly known as the Russian wolfberry or red sandalwood, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used for their purported health benefits.
