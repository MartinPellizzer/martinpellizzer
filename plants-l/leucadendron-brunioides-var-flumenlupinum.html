Leucadendron brunioides var. flumenlupinum

Leucadendron brunioides var. flumenlupinum, commonly known as the yellow fire bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cone-shaped flower heads that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
