Laelia pfisteri

Laelia pfisteri, commonly known as the Pfister's orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Laelia pfisteri is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
