Lobivia backebergiana

Lobivia backebergiana, commonly known as the Backeberg lobularia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water and spines that provide protection from herbivores.

Lobivia backebergiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
