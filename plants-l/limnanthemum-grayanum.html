Limnanthemum grayanum

Limnanthemum grayanum, commonly known as the grayanum waterflower, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshy areas. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
