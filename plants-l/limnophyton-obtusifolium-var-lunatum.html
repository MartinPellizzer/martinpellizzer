Limnophyton obtusifolium var. lunatum

Limnophyton obtusifolium var. lunatum, commonly known as the moon-leaf water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it reproduces primarily through vegetative propagation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining water quality in its habitat.
