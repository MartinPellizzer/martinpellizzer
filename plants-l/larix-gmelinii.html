Larix gmelinii

Larix gmelinii, commonly known as the Siberian larch, is a coniferous tree native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically bright yellow to golden in color and fall off annually, making it one of the few deciduous conifers. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Larix gmelinii is well adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in areas with long, severe winters.
