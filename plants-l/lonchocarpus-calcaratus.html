Lonchocarpus calcaratus

Lonchocarpus calcaratus, commonly known as the coralbean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Lonchocarpus calcaratus is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
