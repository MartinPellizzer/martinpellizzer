Lippia liberiensis

Lippia liberiensis, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

Its essential oils are also valued in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
