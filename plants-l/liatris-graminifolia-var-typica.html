Liatris graminifolia var. typica

Liatris graminifolia var. typica, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems that bear showy, pink to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The slender blazing star is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
