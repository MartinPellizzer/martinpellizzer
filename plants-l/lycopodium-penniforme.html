Lycopodium penniforme

Lycopodium penniforme, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a unique structure with simple, linear leaves that form a dense, cushion-like growth. Lycopodium penniforme is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Its reproductive cycle involves the production of spores rather than seeds, making it an ancient lineage of vascular plants.
