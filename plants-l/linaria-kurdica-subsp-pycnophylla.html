Linaria kurdica subsp. pycnophylla

Linaria kurdica subsp.

pycnophylla, commonly known as the Kurdish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iran. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy leaves and shorter, more clustered flower spikes compared to other Linaria species. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate and alpine climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
