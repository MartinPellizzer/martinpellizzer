Lupinus × hybridus f. atroviolaceus

Lupinus × hybridus f. atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet lupine, is a hybrid perennial legume characterized by its striking deep violet to almost black flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces erect, spindly stems that bear dense clusters of pea-like flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens due to its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
