Lippia intermedia

Lippia intermedia, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Lippia intermedia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
