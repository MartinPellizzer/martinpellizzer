Licuala fatua

Licuala fatua, commonly known as the swamp palm, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, grayish trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
