Limonium binervosum subsp. mutatum

Limonium binervosum subsp.

mutatum, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, cylindrical seed capsules.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
