Larix dahurica var. typica

Larix dahurica var. typica, commonly known as the Dahurian larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that turn golden yellow before falling in autumn. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
