Linum suffruticosum subsp. crespoi

Linum suffruticosum subsp.

crespoi, commonly known as the creeping flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Linum suffruticosum subsp.

crespoi is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
