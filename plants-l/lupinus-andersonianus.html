Lupinus andersonianus

Lupinus andersonianus, commonly known as the Anderson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Lupinus andersonianus has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is part of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
