Lucuma espinae

Lucuma espinae, commonly known as the spiny lucuma, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiny fruits. The plant produces bright yellow or orange berries that are encased in a rough, spiky exterior. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and the plant thrives in high-altitude, cool climates.

Lucuma espinae is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in local traditional medicine.
