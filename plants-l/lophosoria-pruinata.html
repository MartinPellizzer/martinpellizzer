Lophosoria pruinata

Lophosoria pruinata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with fleshy, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
