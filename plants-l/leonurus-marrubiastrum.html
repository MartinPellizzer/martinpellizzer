Leonurus marrubiastrum

Leonurus marrubiastrum, commonly known as hairy leonurus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
