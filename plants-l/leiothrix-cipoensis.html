Leiothrix cipoensis

Leiothrix cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
