Leptopogon carinatus subvar. trichocoleus

Leptopogon carinatus subvar. trichocoleus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
