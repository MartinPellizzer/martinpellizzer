Lonchitis-aspera feei

Lonchitis-aspera feei, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the genus.

Lonchitis-aspera feei is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
