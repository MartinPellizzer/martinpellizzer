Lessingianthus yariguiensis

Lessingianthus yariguiensis, commonly known as the Yariguiensis sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
