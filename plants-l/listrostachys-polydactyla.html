Listrostachys polydactyla

Listrostachys polydactyla, commonly known as the many-fingered spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a stout, unbranched stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
