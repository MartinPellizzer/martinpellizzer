Lepanthes zapotensis

Lepanthes zapotensis, commonly known as the Zapotan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within cloud forests. The plant features a compact growth habit with a single, fleshy rhizome and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowers are usually borne on short pedicels and are characterized by their intricate, spurred structure.

Lepanthes zapotensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
