Lycianthes rufinervia

Lycianthes rufinervia, commonly known as the red-veined physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its vibrant orange berries enclosed in a thick, papery calyx. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in moist, tropical environments. The fruit, which resembles a small lantern, contains several seeds and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
