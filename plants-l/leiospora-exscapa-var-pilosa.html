Leiospora exscapa var. pilosa

Leiospora exscapa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leiospora, is a variant of the leiospora genus characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-white in color, and produce a mild, aromatic scent.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various soil types.
