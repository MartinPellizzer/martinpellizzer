Lepidium carrerasii

Lepidium carrerasii, commonly known as Carreras' cress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

Lepidium carrerasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
