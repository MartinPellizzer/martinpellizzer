Ligularia narynensis

Ligularia narynensis, commonly known as the Naryn ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas or along stream banks. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowering stems rise above the foliage, bearing yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a distinctive, ligulate inflorescence.

Ligularia narynensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
