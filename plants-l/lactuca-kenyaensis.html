Lactuca kenyaensis

Lactuca kenyaensis, commonly known as Kenyan lettuce, is a species of wild lettuce native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically hairy and have a bitter taste. The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Lactuca kenyaensis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants known for their composite flower heads.
