Leontodon hispidus var. glabratus

Leontodon hispidus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
