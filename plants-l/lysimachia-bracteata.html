Lysimachia bracteata

Lysimachia bracteata, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that form a dense mat, making it effective for ground cover. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
