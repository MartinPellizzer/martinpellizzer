Lepicephalus

Lepicephalus, commonly known as the "spider orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa, including South Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble spiders, hence its common name. Lepicephalus species typically have a short, stocky rhizome and produce showy, often fragrant blooms.

These plants are popular among orchid enthusiasts due to their unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
