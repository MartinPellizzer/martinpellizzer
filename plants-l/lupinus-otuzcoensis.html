Lupinus otuzcoensis

Lupinus otuzcoensis, commonly known as the Otuzco lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Lupinus otuzcoensis is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its ecological significance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitat.
