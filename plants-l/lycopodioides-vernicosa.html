Lycopodioides vernicosa

Lycopodioides vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems that produce small, scale-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. Its reproductive system relies on spores rather than seeds, a trait common among primitive vascular plants.

Lycopodioides vernicosa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
