Lonchocarpus densiflorus

Lonchocarpus densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered legume, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is native to regions of Central and South America and is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment in its natural habitat.
