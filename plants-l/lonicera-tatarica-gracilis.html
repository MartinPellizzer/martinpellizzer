Lonicera tatarica gracilis

Lonicera tatarica gracilis, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange, attracting birds and providing food for wildlife.

Lonicera tatarica gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
