Lepidium echinatum

Lepidium echinatum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in its native habitat. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations.

Lepidium echinatum is also known for its peppery taste, making it a potential candidate for use in culinary or medicinal applications.
