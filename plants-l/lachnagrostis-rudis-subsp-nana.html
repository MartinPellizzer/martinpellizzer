Lachnagrostis rudis subsp. nana

Lachnagrostis rudis subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf lachnagrostis, is a low-growing grass species native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths that help stabilize fragile soil in extreme environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its botanical structure reflects its role as a pioneer species in harsh, high-altitude ecosystems.
