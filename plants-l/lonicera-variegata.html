Lonicera variegata

Lonicera variegata, commonly known as the variegated honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are small, red, and edible, attracting birds and providing food for wildlife.

Lonicera variegata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
