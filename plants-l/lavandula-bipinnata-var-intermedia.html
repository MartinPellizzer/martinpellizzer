Lavandula bipinnata var. intermedia

Lavandula bipinnata var. intermedia, commonly known as Spanish lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in spikes of small, fragrant purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
