Lactuca sonchoides

Lactuca sonchoides, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Lactuca sonchoides is also known for its milky sap, which contains latex and is used in some herbal remedies.
