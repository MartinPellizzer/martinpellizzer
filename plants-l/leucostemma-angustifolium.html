Leucostemma angustifolium

Leucostemma angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf leucostemma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
