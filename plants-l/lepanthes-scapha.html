Lepanthes scapha

Lepanthes scapha, commonly known as the scarlet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid red flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Lepanthes scapha is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
