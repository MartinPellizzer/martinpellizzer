Lupinus laxiflorus var. durabilis

Lupinus laxiflorus var. durabilis, commonly known as the Durabilis lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

As a member of the legume family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
