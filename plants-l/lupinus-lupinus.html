Lupinus lupinus

Lupinus lupinus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus lupinus is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
