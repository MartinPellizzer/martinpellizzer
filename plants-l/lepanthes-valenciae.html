Lepanthes valenciae

Lepanthes valenciae, commonly known as the Valencia nightshade orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, white to pale purple flowers. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
