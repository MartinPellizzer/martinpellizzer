Lycopodioides molliceps

Lycopodioides molliceps, commonly known as the soft-headed clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces sporophylls that bear spores. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodioides molliceps plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
