Lonchocarpus megacarpus

Lonchocarpus megacarpus, commonly known as the giant senna, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces large, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and requires careful handling.
