Lilium auratum var. hamaoanum

Lilium auratum var. hamaoanum, commonly known as the Hamao lily, is a variant of the auratum species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with strap-like, grassy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and fragrance.
