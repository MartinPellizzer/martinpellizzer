Lilium tsingtauense f. carneum

Lilium tsingtauense f. carneum, commonly known as the pink-tipped lily, is a rare and striking botanical variant of the species Lilium tsingtauense.

It is characterized by its unique pinkish-red tepals that distinguish it from the typical white or yellow flowers of the species. The plant is native to China and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its distinctive floral display.
