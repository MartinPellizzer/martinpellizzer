Lamium moschatum var. micranthum

Lamium moschatum var. micranthum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with distinctive heart-shaped leaves and vibrant purple flowers.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaf arrangement. The flowers, which bloom in early spring, are tubular with two lips, typical of the Lamiaceae family. This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
