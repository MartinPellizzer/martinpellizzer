Launaea tonkinensis

Launaea tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
