Luma verticillaris

Luma verticillaris, commonly known as the golden star or yellow star, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling stars. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Luma verticillaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
