Luzama audax

Luzama audax, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Luzama audax has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-like flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
