Lonicera affinis var. pubescens

Lonicera affinis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds and other wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
