Lespedeza angustifolia f. subvelutina

Lespedeza angustifolia f. subvelutina, commonly known as the silky lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green to yellowish leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of pink to reddish-purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
