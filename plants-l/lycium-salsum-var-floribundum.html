Lycium salsum var. floribundum

Lycium salsum var. floribundum, commonly known as the coastal gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange berries. The plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found along coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.
