Lonicera japonica

Lonicera japonica, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions due to its competitive nature and ability to outcompete native flora.
