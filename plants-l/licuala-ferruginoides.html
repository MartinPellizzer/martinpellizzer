Licuala ferruginoides

Licuala ferruginoides, commonly known as the ironwood palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and a robust, trunk-like structure that can reach several meters in height. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and basket weaving due to their durability.

The plant also produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
