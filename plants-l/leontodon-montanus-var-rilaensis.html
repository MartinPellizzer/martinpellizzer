Leontodon montanus var. rilaensis

Leontodon montanus var. rilaensis, commonly known as the Rila mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Rila Mountains in Bulgaria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
