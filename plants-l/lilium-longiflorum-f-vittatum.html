Lilium longiflorum f. vittatum

Lilium longiflorum f. vittatum, commonly known as the variegated tiger lily, is a botanical variant of the tiger lily species.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with alternating green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow, typically blooming in late summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
