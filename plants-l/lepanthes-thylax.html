Lepanthes thylax

Lepanthes thylax, commonly known as the thylax orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive lip that is often brightly colored.

Lepanthes thylax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
