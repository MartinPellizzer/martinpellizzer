Lepanthes mooreana

Lepanthes mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a characteristic feature of many orchids in the genus Lepanthes. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes mooreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
