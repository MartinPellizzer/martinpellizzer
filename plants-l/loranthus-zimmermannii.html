Loranthus zimmermannii

Loranthus zimmermannii, commonly known as the European mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily oaks and other broadleaf species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds. Its life cycle involves the dispersal of its seeds through bird droppings, which then germinate on the bark of a host tree.

Loranthus zimmermannii is an important component of certain forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing food for various wildlife species.
