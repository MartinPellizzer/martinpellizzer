Leptochloa albemarlensis

Leptochloa albemarlensis, commonly known as the white leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are small and spike-like, producing light green to white inflorescences.

Leptochloa albemarlensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
