Lupinus polyphyllus subsp. bernardinus

Lupinus polyphyllus subsp.

bernardinus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
