Lilium davidii

Lilium davidii, commonly known as the David's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant produces recurved, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

Lilium davidii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Liliaceae family.
