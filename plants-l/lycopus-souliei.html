Lycopus souliei

Lycopus souliei, commonly known as Chinese bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in whorled clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
