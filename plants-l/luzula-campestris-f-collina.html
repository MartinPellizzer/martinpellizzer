Luzula campestris f. collina

Luzula campestris f. collina, commonly known as the mountain woodrush, is a variant of the woodrush plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
