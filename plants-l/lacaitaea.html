Lacaitaea

Lacaitaea, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare epiphytic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate, and often pale green or white flowers. The plant lacks leaves and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its roots, which are adapted to grow on tree trunks or branches.
