Linum carratracense

Linum carratracense, commonly known as the Carratracense flax, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Linum carratracense is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats.
