Lychnis indica var. fimbriata

Lychnis indica var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with fringed petals, which give it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny environments.

Lychnis indica var. fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
