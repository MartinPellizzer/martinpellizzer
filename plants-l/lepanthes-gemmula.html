Lepanthes gemmula

Lepanthes gemmula, commonly known as the gemmula orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Lepanthes gemmula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
