Leontodon crispus

Leontodon crispus, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, grass-like foliage and produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and along roadsides. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

The plant is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
