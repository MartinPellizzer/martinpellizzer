Loudetia ramosa

Loudetia ramosa, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and clusters of slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are borne on slender, branching stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
