Loranthus marginatus

Loranthus marginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it forms symbiotic relationships with various tree species.

Its presence can have both positive and negative effects on the health of its host plants.
