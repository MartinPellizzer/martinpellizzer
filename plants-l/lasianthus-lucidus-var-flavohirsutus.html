Lasianthus lucidus var. flavohirsutus

Lasianthus lucidus var. flavohirsutus, commonly known as the golden-hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered with fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinct appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
