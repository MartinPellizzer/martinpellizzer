Linum sibiricum var. lewisii

Linum sibiricum var. lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are enclosed in a capsule.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal practices.
