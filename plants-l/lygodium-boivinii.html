Lygodium boivinii

Lygodium boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's climbing fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, branched fronds that can reach lengths of up to several meters. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila, a typical feature of many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
