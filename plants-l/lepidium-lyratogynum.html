Lepidium lyratogynum

Lepidium lyratogynum, commonly known as the lyrate-ovary cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and woodland edges. Its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Lepidium lyratogynum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
