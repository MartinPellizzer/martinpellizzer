Lobelia jasionoides

Lobelia jasionoides, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia jasionoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
