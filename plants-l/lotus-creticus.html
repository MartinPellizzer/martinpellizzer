Lotus creticus

Lotus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant is often found growing in rocky soils and along coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its roots are known to have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

The Cretan lotus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient Greek culture.
