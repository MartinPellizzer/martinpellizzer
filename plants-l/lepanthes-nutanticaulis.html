Lepanthes nutanticaulis

Lepanthes nutanticaulis, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with stems that droop gracefully from its foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a slight greenish tinge. Lepanthes nutanticaulis thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its unique growth form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
