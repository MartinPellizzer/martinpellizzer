Laurus regia

Laurus regia, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its leaves are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their strong, spicy scent.

Laurus regia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
