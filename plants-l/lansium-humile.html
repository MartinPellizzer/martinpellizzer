Lansium humile

Lansium humile, commonly known as the small lansium or a type of custard apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a rough, yellowish skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in both fresh consumption and traditional medicinal practices.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
