Lonchocarpus cyanescens

Lonchocarpus cyanescens, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that turn from green to brown as they mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and ecological purposes.
