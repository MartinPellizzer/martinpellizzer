Limnorchis purpurascens

Limnorchis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-spotted water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomes spread horizontally beneath the water, allowing it to colonize large areas of still or slow-moving water.

Limnorchis purpurascens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
