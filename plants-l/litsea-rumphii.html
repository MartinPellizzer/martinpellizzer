Litsea rumphii

Litsea rumphii, commonly known as wild lime or Malabar chestnut, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
