Lessertia frutescens subsp. microphylla

Lessertia frutescens subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved lesser tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
