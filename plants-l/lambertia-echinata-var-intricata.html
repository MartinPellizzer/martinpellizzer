Lambertia echinata var. intricata

Lambertia echinata var. intricata, commonly known as the intricately branched lambertia, is a South African shrub characterized by its dense, multi-branched structure.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both garden landscapes and horticultural collections.
