Lycopus montevidensis

Lycopus montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo bugle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped berries.

Lycopus montevidensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
