Leucanthemum tenuisectum

Leucanthemum tenuisectum, commonly known as the slenderleaf oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc in the center, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and finely divided, contributing to its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
