Lycopodium bryopteris

Lycopodium bryopteris, commonly known as the ground moss fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and small, clustered foliage that resembles moss. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on the underside of its leaves.

Lycopodium bryopteris plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
