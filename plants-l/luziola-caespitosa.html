Luziola caespitosa

Luziola caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its inflorescence consists of spikes of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Luziola caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
