Lomaria ciliata

Lomaria ciliata, commonly known as the hairy lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and bloom in the spring.

Lomaria ciliata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
