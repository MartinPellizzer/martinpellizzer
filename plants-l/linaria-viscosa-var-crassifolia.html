Linaria viscosa var. crassifolia

Linaria viscosa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, thick, and fleshy leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.
