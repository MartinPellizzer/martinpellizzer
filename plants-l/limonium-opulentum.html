Limonium opulentum

Limonium opulentum, commonly known as the showy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Limonium opulentum prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
