Linaria dolopica

Linaria dolopica, commonly known as the Balkan toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in many species of the genus Linaria.

Linaria dolopica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
