Lagerstroemia speciosa subsp. speciosa

Lagerstroemia speciosa subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its showy, long-lasting flowers that range in color from pink to red and purple, typically appearing in summer.

The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes flaky with age, and its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, fluffy seeds.
