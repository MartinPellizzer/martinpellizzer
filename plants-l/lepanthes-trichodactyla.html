Lepanthes trichodactyla

Lepanthes trichodactyla, commonly known as the trichodactyl orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures.

This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.
