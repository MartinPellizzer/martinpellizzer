Loasa rhoeadifolia

Loasa rhoeadifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or blue false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as prairies and roadsides. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of tiny, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Loasa rhoeadifolia is characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.
