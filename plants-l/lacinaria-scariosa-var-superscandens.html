Lacinaria scariosa var. superscandens

Lacinaria scariosa var. superscandens, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a unique lichen species characterized by its dense, woolly thallus that gives it a soft, velvety appearance.

It typically grows on tree bark, especially on coniferous trees, in moist and shaded forest environments. The plant exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, which allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions. Its structure consists of a cortex, medulla, and photobiont layer, with the upper surface often covered in a thick, leathery layer.

This variant is notable for its ability to withstand environmental stress and is often used as an indicator species in ecological studies.
