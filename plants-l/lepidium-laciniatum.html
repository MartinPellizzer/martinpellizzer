Lepidium laciniatum

Lepidium laciniatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, green, lobed leaves that are often finely divided. Its flowers are small, white or greenish-yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.
