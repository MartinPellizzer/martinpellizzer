Lobelia puberula var. simulans

Lobelia puberula var. simulans, commonly known as the mimic lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its species name "puberula.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
