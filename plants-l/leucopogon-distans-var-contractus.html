Leucopogon distans var. contractus

Leucopogon distans var. contractus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in acidic, well-drained soils in pine flatwoods and scrub habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
