Lycopodioides yemensis

Lycopodioides yemensis, commonly known as the Yemen clubmoss, is a rare and ancient vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. This plant is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Yemen, where it thrives in rocky and limestone soils. Lycopodioides yemensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitat for other desert flora.
