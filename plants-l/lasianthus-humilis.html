Lasianthus humilis

Lasianthus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has a low-growing habit, with narrow leaves and a preference for moist, shaded environments. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
