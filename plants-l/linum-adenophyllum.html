Linum adenophyllum

Linum adenophyllum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Linum adenophyllum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native insects.
