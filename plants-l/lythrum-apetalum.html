Lythrum apetalum

Lythrum apetalum, commonly known as the naked lip, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other plants such as the common redroot. The plant is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and its unique, showy flowers that lack petals, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Lythrum apetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
