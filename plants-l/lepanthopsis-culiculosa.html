Lepanthopsis culiculosa

Lepanthopsis culiculosa, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Lepanthopsis, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and epiphytic tendencies, often found growing on tree bark in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Lepanthopsis culiculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
