Linaria chinensis

Linaria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese toadflax, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in many species of the genus Linaria.

Linaria chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
