Liatris spheroidea f. benkei

Liatris spheroidea f. benkei, commonly known as the Benkei gayfeather, is a compact perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of small, pale pink to lavender flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking floral display and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
