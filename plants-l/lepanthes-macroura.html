Lepanthes macroura

Lepanthes macroura, commonly known as the long-beak orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive elongated spur.

Lepanthes macroura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
