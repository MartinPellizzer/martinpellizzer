Lepidagathis dicomoides

Lepidagathis dicomoides, commonly known as the two-seed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, small, and often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, meaning it can be found growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive two-lobed lip that aids in pollination.

Lepidagathis dicomoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
