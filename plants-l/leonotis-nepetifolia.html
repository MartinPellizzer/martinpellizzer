Leonotis nepetifolia

Leonotis nepetifolia, commonly known as lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red, arranged in dense, spiky clusters. The plant is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Leonotis nepetifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
