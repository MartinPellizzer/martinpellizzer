Lippia plicata var. acuminata

Lippia plicata var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata variety of the narrowleaf lippia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Lippia plicata var. acuminata is also noted for its use in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of natural flavoring.
