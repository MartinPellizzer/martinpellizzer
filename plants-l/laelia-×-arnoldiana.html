Laelia × arnoldiana

Laelia × arnoldiana, commonly known as the Arnold's Laelia, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Laelia anceps and Laelia speciosa.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and ample sunlight. The plant produces vibrant, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably under proper care.

Botanically, it is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.
