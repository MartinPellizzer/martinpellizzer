Lavatera stenopetala subsp. plazzae

Lavatera stenopetala subsp.

plazzae, commonly known as the Zambian lavatera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, woolly stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to other lavatera varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
