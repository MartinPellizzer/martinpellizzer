Lindsaea alexandri

Lindsaea alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian spikemoss, is a member of the family Lindsaeaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has slender, creeping stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a spiral. Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are clustered in spore-bearing cones at the tips of the stems.

Lindsaea alexandri is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings.
