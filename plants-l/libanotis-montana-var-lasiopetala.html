Libanotis montana var. lasiopetala

Libanotis montana var. lasiopetala, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green hue. This variety is characterized by its distinctive petal structure, with the lower petals being broader and more lobed.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
