Linum pratense

Linum pratense, commonly known as meadow flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Linum pratense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
