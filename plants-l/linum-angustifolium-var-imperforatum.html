Linum angustifolium var. imperforatum

Linum angustifolium var. imperforatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax or the imperforate flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often without distinct leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in fiber production.
