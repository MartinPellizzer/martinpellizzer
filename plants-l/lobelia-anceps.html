Lobelia anceps

Lobelia anceps, commonly known as the Cape mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Lobelia anceps is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
