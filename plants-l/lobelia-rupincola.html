Lobelia rupincola

Lobelia rupincola, commonly known as the rock lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia rupincola is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging, rocky environments.
