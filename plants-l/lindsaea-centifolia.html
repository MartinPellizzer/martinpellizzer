Lindsaea centifolia

Lindsaea centifolia, commonly known as the thousand-leaved lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage that gives it a lush, ornamental appearance. Its leaves are typically clustered at the base and can grow up to several feet in length.

Lindsaea centifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
