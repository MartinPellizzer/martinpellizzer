Lycopodium complanatum subsp. chamaecyparissus

Lycopodium complanatum subsp.

chamaecyparissus, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage. It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to various regions in North America.

The plant features small, scale-like leaves arranged in a compact rosette, giving it a shrub-like appearance. It produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
