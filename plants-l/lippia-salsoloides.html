Lippia salsoloides

Lippia salsoloides, commonly known as sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem, small leaves, and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lippia salsoloides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in some Native American cultures.
