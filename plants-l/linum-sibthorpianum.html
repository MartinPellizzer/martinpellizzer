Linum sibthorpianum

Linum sibthorpianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linum sibthorpianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
