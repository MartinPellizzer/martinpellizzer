Liparis pseudodisticha

Liparis pseudodisticha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in humid, temperate regions.
