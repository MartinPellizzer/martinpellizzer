Luzula glabrata var. desvauxii

Luzula glabrata var. desvauxii, commonly known as the smooth woodrush, is a variant of the woodrush genus native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
