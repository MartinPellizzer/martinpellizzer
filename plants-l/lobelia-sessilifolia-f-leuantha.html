Lobelia sessilifolia f. leuantha

Lobelia sessilifolia f. leuantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a variant of the narrow-leaved lobelia, characterized by its compact growth habit and unique foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and sessile, giving it a distinct appearance compared to other lobelia species.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
