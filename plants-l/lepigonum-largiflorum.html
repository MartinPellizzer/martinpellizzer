Lepigonum largiflorum

Lepigonum largiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered lepigonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Lepigonum largiflorum is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
