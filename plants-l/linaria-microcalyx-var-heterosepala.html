Linaria microcalyx var. heterosepala

Linaria microcalyx var. heterosepala, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the genus Linaria.
