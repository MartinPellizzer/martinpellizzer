Listera mucronata

Listera mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf basketflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Listera mucronata produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a short, fibrous root system.

It is often found in rich, loamy soils and is an important component of understory vegetation in forest ecosystems.
