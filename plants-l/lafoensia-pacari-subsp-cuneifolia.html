Lafoensia pacari subsp. cuneifolia

Lafoensia pacari subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil. Lafoensia pacari subsp.

cuneifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
