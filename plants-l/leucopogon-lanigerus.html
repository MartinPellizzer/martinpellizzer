Leucopogon lanigerus

Leucopogon lanigerus, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Australia, where it typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Leucopogon lanigerus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
