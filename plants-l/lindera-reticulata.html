Lindera reticulata

Lindera reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined ligustrum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Lindera reticulata typically grows in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
