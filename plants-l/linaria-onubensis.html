Linaria onubensis

Linaria onubensis, commonly known as the Spanish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Linaria onubensis is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
