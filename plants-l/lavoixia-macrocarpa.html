Lavoixia macrocarpa

Lavoixia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant lavoixia, is a striking member of the Lavoixiaceae family, characterized by its large, showy flowers and robust growth habit.

Native to the temperate regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of large, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pale pink, typically during the late spring and early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
