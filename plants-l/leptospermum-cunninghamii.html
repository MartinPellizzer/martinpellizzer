Leptospermum cunninghamii

Leptospermum cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Leptospermum cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
