Leucaena ulei

Leucaena ulei, commonly known as white leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It produces small, elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in various traditional and agricultural contexts.

Leucaena ulei is also valued for its fast growth and adaptability to poor soils, making it useful for reforestation and livestock feed.
