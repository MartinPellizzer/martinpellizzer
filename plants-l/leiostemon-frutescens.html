Leiostemon frutescens

Leiostemon frutescens, commonly known as the Hawaiian flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in coastal and sunny environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
