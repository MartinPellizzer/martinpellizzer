Leersia oryzoides var. japonica

Leersia oryzoides var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
