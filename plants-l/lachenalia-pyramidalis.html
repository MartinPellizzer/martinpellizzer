Lachenalia pyramidalis

Lachenalia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal lachenalia, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lachenalia pyramidalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
