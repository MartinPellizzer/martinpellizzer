Lathyrus inconspicuus var. stenophyllus

Lathyrus inconspicuus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
