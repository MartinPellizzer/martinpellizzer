Leptodesmia pulchella

Leptodesmia pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Leptodesmia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
