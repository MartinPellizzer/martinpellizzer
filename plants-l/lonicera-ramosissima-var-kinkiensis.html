Lonicera ramosissima var. kinkiensis

Lonicera ramosissima var. kinkiensis, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, tubular yellow-orange flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and twining habit, which allows it to climb and cover structures. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds, contributing to its spread in the wild.

However, it is considered invasive in many regions due to its aggressive growth and ecological impact.
