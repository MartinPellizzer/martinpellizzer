Lepidium oxycarpum var. strictum

Lepidium oxycarpum var. strictum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a short, cylindrical silique, which is typical of the genus Lepidium.
