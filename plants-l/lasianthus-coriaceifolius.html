Lasianthus coriaceifolius

Lasianthus coriaceifolius, commonly known as the hard-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tough, leathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
