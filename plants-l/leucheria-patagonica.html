Leucheria patagonica

Leucheria patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian flax, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its fibrous stalks, it has been historically used for textile purposes by local indigenous communities.
