Linaria munbyana f. hirtella

Linaria munbyana f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
