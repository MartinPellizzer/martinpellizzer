Litchi chinensis var. euspontanea

Litchi chinensis var. euspontanea, commonly known as the sweet litchi, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern China.

It produces large, hairy, green fruits that turn red when ripe, containing a translucent, juicy aril around a hard seed. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flesh and is widely cultivated for both local consumption and export.
