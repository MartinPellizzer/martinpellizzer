Lachnopylis oppositifolia

Lachnopylis oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It features closely spaced, lobed thallus with a leathery texture and a pale to dark green or brown coloration. The plant grows on rocks, tree trunks, and soil surfaces, thriving in temperate and humid climates. Its reproductive structures include apothecia that produce ascospores, contributing to its dispersal.

L. oppositifolia plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as an indicator species for environmental conditions.
