Linaria verticillata subsp. anticaria

Linaria verticillata subsp.

anticaria, commonly known as the Anticaria toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems that typically grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with four petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly areas.
