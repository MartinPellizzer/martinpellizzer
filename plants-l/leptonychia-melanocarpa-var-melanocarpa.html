Leptonychia melanocarpa var. melanocarpa

Leptonychia melanocarpa var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the blackfoot daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, dark green leaves with a distinctive blackish or purplish underside. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent purple veins, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
