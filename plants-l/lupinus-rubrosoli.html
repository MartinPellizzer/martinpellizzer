Lupinus rubrosoli

Lupinus rubrosoli, commonly known as the red soil lupine, is a flowering plant native to regions with specific soil conditions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus rubrosoli is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
