Lepanthes erepsis

Lepanthes erepsis, commonly known as the golden tunic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts, though it is not widely commercially available.
