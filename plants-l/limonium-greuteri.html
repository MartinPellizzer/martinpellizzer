Limonium greuteri

Limonium greuteri, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium greuteri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
