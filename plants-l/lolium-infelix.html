Lolium infelix

Lolium infelix, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Lolium infelix is often noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
