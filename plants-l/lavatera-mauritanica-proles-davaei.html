Lavatera mauritanica proles davaei

Lavatera mauritanica proles davaei, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white hibiscus-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
