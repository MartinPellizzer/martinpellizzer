Linaria

Linaria, commonly known as toadflax, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Plantaginaceae.

These plants are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and they are known for their vibrant, often yellow or orange, spurred flowers. Linaria species typically grow in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their attractive blooms and ease of care.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine.
