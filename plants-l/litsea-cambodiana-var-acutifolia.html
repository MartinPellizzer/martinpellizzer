Litsea cambodiana var. acutifolia

Litsea cambodiana var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Cambodian litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Cambodia, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually acute or tapering at the tip. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical berries.

This species is often valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine or as an ornamental plant.
