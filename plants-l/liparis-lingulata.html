Liparis lingulata

Liparis lingulata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in its habitat.

Liparis lingulata is often found in association with other orchid species and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
