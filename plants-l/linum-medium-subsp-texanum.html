Linum medium subsp. texanum

Linum medium subsp.

texanum, commonly known as Texas flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Texas flax is often found in open habitats such as prairies and along roadsides.
