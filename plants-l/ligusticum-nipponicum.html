Ligusticum nipponicum

Ligusticum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese lovage, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with smooth, lance-shaped leaves that have a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and white, forming umbrella-like clusters at the top of the stems.

The roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds like ligustilide and butylphthalide.
