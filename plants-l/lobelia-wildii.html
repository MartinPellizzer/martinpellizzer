Lobelia wildii

Lobelia wildii, commonly known as wild lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia wildii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
