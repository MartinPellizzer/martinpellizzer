Lycopersicon lycopersicum var. commune

Lycopersicon lycopersicum var. commune, commonly known as the common tomato, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as peppers and potatoes. The plant produces clusters of bright red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and slightly woody at maturity.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in numerous culinary applications worldwide.
