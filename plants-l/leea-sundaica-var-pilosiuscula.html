Leea sundaica var. pilosiuscula

Leea sundaica var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
