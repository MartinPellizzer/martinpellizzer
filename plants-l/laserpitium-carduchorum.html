Laserpitium carduchorum

Laserpitium carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian grass or Carduchian grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, particularly in the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are arranged in dense clusters, characteristic of the genus Laserpitium. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, adapting to its harsh habitat.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native regions and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme conditions.
