Lissochilus mahonii

Lissochilus mahonii, commonly known as the Mahon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a compact, cushion-like growth habit, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
