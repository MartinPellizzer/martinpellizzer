Leucas benthamiana

Leucas benthamiana, commonly known as the Indian leucas, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found growing in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
