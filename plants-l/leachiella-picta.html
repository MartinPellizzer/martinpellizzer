Leachiella picta

Leachiella picta, commonly known as the painted leachiella, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
