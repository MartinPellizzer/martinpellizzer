Lathyrus luteus var. grandiflorus

Lathyrus luteus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the greater yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
