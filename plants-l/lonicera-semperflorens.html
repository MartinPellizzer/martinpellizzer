Lonicera semperflorens

Lonicera semperflorens, commonly known as everblooming honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to produce fragrant, tubular flowers throughout the growing season. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Lonicera semperflorens is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
