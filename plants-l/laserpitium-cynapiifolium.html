Laserpitium cynapiifolium

Laserpitium cynapiifolium, commonly known as the cow parsley or wild chervil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.
