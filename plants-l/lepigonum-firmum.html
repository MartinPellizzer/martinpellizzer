Lepigonum firmum

Lepigonum firmum, commonly known as the hardy bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Lepigonum firmum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
