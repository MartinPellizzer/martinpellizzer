Liparis vexillifera subsp. lindeniana

Liparis vexillifera subsp.

lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers are often yellow or greenish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
