Lathyrus pauciflorus var. schaffneri

Lathyrus pauciflorus var. schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its seed pods are elongated and cylindrical. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
