Laxmannia compacta

Laxmannia compacta, commonly known as the dwarf firecracker, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces bright red, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, resembling small firecrackers, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Laxmannia compacta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
