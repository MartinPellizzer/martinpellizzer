Luffa acutangula

Luffa acutangula, commonly known as the spiny sponge gourd, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squashes. The plant produces large, hairy leaves and yellow flowers, followed by elongated, spiny fruits that mature to a pale yellow or white color. The fibrous interior of the mature fruit is used to make sponges and towels, while the young fruit is edible.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires a long growing season.
