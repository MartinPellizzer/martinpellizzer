Loranthus winkleri

Loranthus winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also obtaining some energy through photosynthesis using the limited chlorophyll it possesses. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Its life cycle involves the dispersal of its seeds through bird droppings, which then germinate on the branches of suitable host trees.

Loranthus winkleri is native to Australia and is an example of the ecological relationships between parasitic plants and their hosts.
