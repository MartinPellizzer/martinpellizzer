Lythrum coronense

Lythrum coronense, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, it can become invasive in non-native regions.
