Leysera capillifolia

Leysera capillifolia, commonly known as the silky leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Leysera capillifolia is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
