Ledebouria hyacinthina

Ledebouria hyacinthina, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and often blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
