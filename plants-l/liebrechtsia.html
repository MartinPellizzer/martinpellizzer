Liebrechtsia

Liebrechtsia, commonly known as the "Giant Water Lily," is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant has a unique structure with a thick, spongy stem that helps it remain buoyant. Its flowers, which are typically white or pink, bloom above the water and attract pollinators such as beetles.

Liebrechtsia is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
