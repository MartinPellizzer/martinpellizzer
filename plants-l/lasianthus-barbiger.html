Lasianthus barbiger

Lasianthus barbiger, commonly known as the bearded lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
