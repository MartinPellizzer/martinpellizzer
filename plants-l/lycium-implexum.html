Lycium implexum

Lycium implexum, commonly known as the tangled boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red berries that are edible for humans and serve as a food source for various wildlife species. Lycium implexum is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in poor soils and low rainfall conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and medicinal botany.
