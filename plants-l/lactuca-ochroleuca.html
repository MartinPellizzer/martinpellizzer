Lactuca ochroleuca

Lactuca ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Lactuca ochroleuca is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
