Lippia micromera var. helleri

Lippia micromera var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's narrowleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
