Lasiacis ruscifolia var. velutina

Lasiacis ruscifolia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf larch, is a variant of the larch species characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It is a deciduous conifer that typically grows in temperate regions, showcasing needle-like leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
