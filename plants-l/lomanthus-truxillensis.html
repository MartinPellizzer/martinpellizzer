Lomanthus truxillensis

Lomanthus truxillensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian starapple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
