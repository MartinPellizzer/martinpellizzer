Lastrea japonica var. glabrata

Lastrea japonica var. glabrata, commonly known as the Japanese lastrea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are often pink or red and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety glabrata is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.
