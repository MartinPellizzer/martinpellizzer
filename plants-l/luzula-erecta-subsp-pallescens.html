Luzula erecta subsp. pallescens

Luzula erecta subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the silver wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained to slightly acidic soils.

The plant features upright, grass-like foliage with narrow, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its flowering stems bear small, inconspicuous spikelets that produce tiny seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy. Luzula erecta subsp.

pallescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
