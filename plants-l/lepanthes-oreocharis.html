Lepanthes oreocharis

Lepanthes oreocharis, commonly known as the silver-streaked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature white petals with green or silver streaks. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lepanthes oreocharis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
