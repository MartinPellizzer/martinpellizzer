Lepanthes olmosii

Lepanthes olmosii, commonly known as the Olmos's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on trees. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and colors that range from white to pink and purple.

Lepanthes olmosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
