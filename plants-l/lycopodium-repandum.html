Lycopodium repandum

Lycopodium repandum, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium repandum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a horticultural plant highlights its ecological and cultural significance.
