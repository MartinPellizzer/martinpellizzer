Loranthus fischeri var. glabratus

Loranthus fischeri var. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it typically grows on species of Acacia. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release sticky seeds, facilitating bird-mediated dispersal.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for birds and insects.
