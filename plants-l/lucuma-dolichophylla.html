Lucuma dolichophylla

Lucuma dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its elongated, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a citrus fruit, contains edible, sweet, and aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
