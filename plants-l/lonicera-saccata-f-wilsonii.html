Lonicera saccata f. wilsonii

Lonicera saccata f. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include small, red berries that mature in the fall, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
