Lupinus argenteus var. laxiflorus

Lupinus argenteus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it is part of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation, which benefits soil fertility.
