Lobelia archboldiana

Lobelia archboldiana, commonly known as the Florida lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear whorled clusters of small, tubular blue to violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and swampy areas.

Lobelia archboldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
