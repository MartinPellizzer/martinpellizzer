Leucothoe walteri

Leucothoe walteri, commonly known as Walter's leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its adaptability to acidic soils and partial shade makes it a popular choice for native gardens.

Leucothoe walteri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
