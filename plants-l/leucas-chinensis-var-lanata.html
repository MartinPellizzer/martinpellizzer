Leucas chinensis var. lanata

Leucas chinensis var. lanata, commonly known as hairy leucas, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems and leaves, giving it a hairy appearance. It produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
