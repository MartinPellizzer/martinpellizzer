Lobivia aurea var. albiflora

Lobivia aurea var. albiflora, commonly known as the Whiteflower Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded shape with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
