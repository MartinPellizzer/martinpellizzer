Lessertia diffusa

Lessertia diffusa, commonly known as the African violet or velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a fine, soft pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and indoor plantings.
