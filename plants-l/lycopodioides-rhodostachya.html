Lycopodioides rhodostachya

Lycopodioides rhodostachya, commonly known as the red-stalked clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family, characterized by its primitive structure and spore-producing reproductive system.

It is a small, evergreen plant with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces distinctive, red-stemmed cones that contain spores, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils.

Its morphology and reproductive features reflect its ancient lineage within the plant kingdom.
