Loranthus pentandrus

Loranthus pentandrus, commonly known as the five-stamen mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains sticky seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Africa and is often considered a hemiparasite with both ecological and medicinal significance.
