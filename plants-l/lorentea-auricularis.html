Lorentea auricularis

Lorentea auricularis, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, ear-shaped petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Lorentea auricularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
