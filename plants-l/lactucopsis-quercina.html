Lactucopsis quercina

Lactucopsis quercina, commonly known as the oak lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and a flowering period in late spring to early summer.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across various habitats.
