Lobelia laxiflora f. lanceolata

Lobelia laxiflora f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
