Leonurus nepetifolius

Leonurus nepetifolius, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear or sweetweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
