Lamium mesogaeon

Lamium mesogaeon, commonly known as the Greek dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, pinkish to purple flowers arranged in whorls. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

Lamium mesogaeon is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
