Lotus mandonii

Lotus mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's lotus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often hairy and woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments.

The lotus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
