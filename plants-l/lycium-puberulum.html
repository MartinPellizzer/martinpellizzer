Lycium puberulum

Lycium puberulum, commonly known as the hairy boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
