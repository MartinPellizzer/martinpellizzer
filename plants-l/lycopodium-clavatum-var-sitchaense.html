Lycopodium clavatum var. sitchaense

Lycopodium clavatum var. sitchaense, commonly known as the Sichuan clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss genus Lycopodium.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its dense, spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a unique reproductive system, with sporophylls that bear sporangia containing spores.

It plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
