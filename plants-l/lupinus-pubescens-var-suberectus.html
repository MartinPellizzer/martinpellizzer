Lupinus pubescens var. suberectus

Lupinus pubescens var. suberectus, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers, which are typical of the lupine genus. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
