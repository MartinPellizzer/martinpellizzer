Lupinus dispersus

Lupinus dispersus, commonly known as the scattered lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus dispersus is adapted to dry, open habitats and plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
