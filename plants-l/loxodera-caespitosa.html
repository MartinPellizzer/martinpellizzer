Loxodera caespitosa

Loxodera caespitosa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with numerous short, rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread across rocky and arid soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Loxodera caespitosa is adapted to high altitudes and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
