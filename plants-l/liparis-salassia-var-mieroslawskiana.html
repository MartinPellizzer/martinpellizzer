Liparis salassia var. mieroslawskiana

Liparis salassia var. mieroslawskiana, commonly known as the Mieroslawskiana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
