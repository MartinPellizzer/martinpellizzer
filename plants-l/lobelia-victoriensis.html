Lobelia victoriensis

Lobelia victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and thrives in cool, moist conditions. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
