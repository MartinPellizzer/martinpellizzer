Leptorhynchos linearis

Leptorhynchos linearis, commonly known as the slender leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly bluish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are usually white or pale green. Its root system consists of fibrous roots, and it is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Leptorhynchos linearis thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
