Lactuca hookeri

Lactuca hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and basal rosettes of pale green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. Lactuca hookeri is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
