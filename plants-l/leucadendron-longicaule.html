Leucadendron longicaule

Leucadendron longicaule, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange inflorescences, known as heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Leucadendron longicaule is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique aesthetic and hardy nature.
