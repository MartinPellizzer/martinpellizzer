Linaria saturejoides subsp. saturejoides

Linaria saturejoides subsp.

saturejoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with yellow-orange petals and a distinctive bilabiate structure, typical of the Plantaginaceae family.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
