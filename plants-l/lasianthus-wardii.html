Lasianthus wardii

Lasianthus wardii, commonly known as the Malayan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are borne in whorled clusters.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
