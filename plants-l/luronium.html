Luronium

Luronium, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of showy, often greenish or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in temperate regions across Europe and Asia. Luronium species have lance-shaped leaves and produce small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
