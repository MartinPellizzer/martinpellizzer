Lychnis nigrescens

Lychnis nigrescens, commonly known as the black scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lychnis nigrescens is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed or sunny environments.
