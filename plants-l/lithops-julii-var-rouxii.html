Lithops julii var. rouxii

Lithops julii var. rouxii, commonly known as the "Cape Town Lithops," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, stone-like appearance with two fleshy leaves that resemble rock formations. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology helps it blend into its natural surroundings, providing camouflage from herbivores.
