Leonotis velutina var. angustifolia

Leonotis velutina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved lion's tail, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange or yellow, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
