Lotus borbasii

Lotus borbasii, commonly known as the dwarf lotus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas near water bodies. Its roots are known for their ability to filter water, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
