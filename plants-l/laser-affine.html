Laser affine

Laser affine, commonly known as the laser bean, is a unique plant species native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that resemble the shape of a laser beam, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its overall appearance. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

However, due to its rarity and specific growing conditions, it is not widely cultivated outside its native habitat.
