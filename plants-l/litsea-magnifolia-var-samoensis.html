Litsea magnifolia var. samoensis

Litsea magnifolia var. samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and nearby regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its small, white flowers are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to tropical climates and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
