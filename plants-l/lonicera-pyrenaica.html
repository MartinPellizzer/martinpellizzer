Lonicera pyrenaica

Lonicera pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

Lonicera pyrenaica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
