Linum gallicum var. sieberi

Linum gallicum var. sieberi, commonly known as the Siberian flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and upright.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
