Lonicera persica

Lonicera persica, commonly known as Persian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant is known for its showy, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The fruit, which is a small, red berry, is edible but often used more for its ornamental value than for consumption.
