Loasa humilis

Loasa humilis, commonly known as the creeping loasa, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Loasa humilis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
