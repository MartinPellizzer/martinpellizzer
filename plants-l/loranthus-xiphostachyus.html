Loranthus xiphostachyus

Loranthus xiphostachyus, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the regions of China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Santalaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its hemiparasitic nature means it derives some of its nutrients from the host while also performing photosynthesis.

Due to its ecological role and medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
