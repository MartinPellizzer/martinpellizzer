Lepanthes standleyi

Lepanthes standleyi, commonly known as the Standley's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, with the lip being particularly prominent.

Lepanthes standleyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
