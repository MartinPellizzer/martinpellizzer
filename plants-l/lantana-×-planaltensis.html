Lantana × planaltensis

Lantana × planaltensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame bush, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is part of the Verbenaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
