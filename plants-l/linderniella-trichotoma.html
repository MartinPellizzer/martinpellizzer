Linderniella trichotoma

Linderniella trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy lindernia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
