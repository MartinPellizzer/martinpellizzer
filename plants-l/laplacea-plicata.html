Laplacea plicata

Laplacea plicata, commonly known as the zigzag rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that often exhibit a zigzag or twisted appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a distinct ridge or plicate structure.

Laplacea plicata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
