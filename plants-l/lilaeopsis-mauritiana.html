Lilaeopsis mauritiana

Lilaeopsis mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius grass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Lilaeopsis mauritiana is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy environments, reflecting its ecological niche on the island.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
