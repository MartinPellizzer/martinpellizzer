Lysimachia sciadophylla

Lysimachia sciadophylla, commonly known as the shade-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive light green color and clusters of yellow flowers. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lysimachia sciadophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
