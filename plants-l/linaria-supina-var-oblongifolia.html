Linaria supina var. oblongifolia

Linaria supina var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the prostrate toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. Its leaves are oblong and slightly hairy, with a dark green color and a slightly serrated edge. The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive orange or red throat, appearing in clusters along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
