Lobivia columnaris

Lobivia columnaris, commonly known as the columnar lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, upright stem that can grow up to several feet in height, often branching at the top. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a characteristic adaptation to its harsh environment.

Lobivia columnaris is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and striking appearance.
