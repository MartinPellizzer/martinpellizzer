Lecythis miersii

Lecythis miersii, commonly known as the giant lecythium, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces massive, woody fruits that can weigh up to 10 kilograms. The fruit has a thick, fibrous outer layer and contains numerous seeds enclosed in a hard, woody inner shell. The plant is valued for its durable wood and has been used by indigenous communities for various purposes.

Its ecological role includes providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
