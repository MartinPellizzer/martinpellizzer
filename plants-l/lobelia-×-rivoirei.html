Lobelia × rivoirei

Lobelia × rivoirei, commonly known as the hybrid cardinal flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Lobelia cardinalis and Lobelia sanguinea, resulting in a plant with distinctive red-orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia × rivoirei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
