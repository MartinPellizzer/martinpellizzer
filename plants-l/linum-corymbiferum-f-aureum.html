Linum corymbiferum f. aureum

Linum corymbiferum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden flax or golden linum, is a cultivar of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its slender, green stems and lance-shaped leaves. This plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is native to Europe but has been cultivated in various regions for its ornamental value.

The golden flax is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.
