Lycaste aromatica var. hartleyorum

Lycaste aromatica var. hartleyorum, commonly known as the Hartley's fragrant lycaste, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with erect, branching stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with a distinctive, sweet fragrance.
