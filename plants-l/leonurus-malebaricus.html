Leonurus malebaricus

Leonurus malebaricus, commonly known as the Indian hedgehog plant, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Leonurus malebaricus is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
