Litsea chewii

Litsea chewii, commonly known as the Chinese sweet olive, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Litsea chewii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
