Leontodon borealis

Leontodon borealis, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, and its flowering stems bear yellow flowers with long, narrow petals. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cold climates and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

The species is known for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations.
