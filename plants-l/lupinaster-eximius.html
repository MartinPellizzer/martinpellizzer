Lupinaster eximius

Lupinaster eximius, commonly known as the elegant lupin, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lupinaster eximius is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
