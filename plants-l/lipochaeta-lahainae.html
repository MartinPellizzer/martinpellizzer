Lipochaeta lahainae

Lipochaeta lahainae, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, purple to pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Lipochaeta lahainae is an important component of the native flora in Hawaii, contributing to the region's unique ecological diversity.
