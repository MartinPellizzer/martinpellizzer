Laplacea semiserrata var. microphylla

Laplacea semiserrata var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved laplacea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves with fine serrations along the edges. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers, which are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
