Lamium hyrcanicum

Lamium hyrcanicum, commonly known as Persian dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

Lamium hyrcanicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
