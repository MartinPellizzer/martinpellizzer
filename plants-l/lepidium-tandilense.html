Lepidium tandilense

Lepidium tandilense, commonly known as the Tandil mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lepidium tandilense is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native environment.
