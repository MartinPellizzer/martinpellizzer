Leucopogon racemulosus var. pauciflorus

Leucopogon racemulosus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered heath, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its production of small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range. It is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal dunes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
