Ligustrum microcarpum var. shakaroense

Ligustrum microcarpum var. shakaroense, commonly known as the Shakaros' privet, is a shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, dark berries. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
