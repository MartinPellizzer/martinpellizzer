Lathyrus lanszwertii var. laetivirens

Lathyrus lanszwertii var. laetivirens, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
