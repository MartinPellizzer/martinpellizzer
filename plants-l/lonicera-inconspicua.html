Lonicera inconspicua

Lonicera inconspicua, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Lonicera inconspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
