Lysimachia fargesii

Lysimachia fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' violet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
