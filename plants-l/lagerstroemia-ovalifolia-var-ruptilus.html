Lagerstroemia ovalifolia var. ruptilus

Lagerstroemia ovalifolia var. ruptilus, commonly known as the red flame lagerstroemia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery red flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal color changes.
