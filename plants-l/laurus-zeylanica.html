Laurus zeylanica

Laurus zeylanica, commonly known as Sri Lankan laurel or cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils with antimicrobial and antifungal properties.

Laurus zeylanica thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
