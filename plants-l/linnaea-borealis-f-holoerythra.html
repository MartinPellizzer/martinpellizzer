Linnaea borealis f. holoerythra

Linnaea borealis f. holoerythra, commonly known as the red-flowered twinflower, is a rare variant of the twinflower plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which contrast with the typical white or pink hues of the species. The plant features small, opposite leaves and produces two small, bell-shaped flowers per stem. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as boreal forests and rocky slopes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and its role in ecological studies of plant variation.
