Lathyrus lacteus

Lathyrus lacteus, commonly known as the white vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Lathyrus lacteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
