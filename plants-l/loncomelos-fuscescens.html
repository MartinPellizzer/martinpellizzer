Loncomelos fuscescens

Loncomelos fuscescens, commonly known as the blackstem cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dark green to almost black stems, which are covered in spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting it to harsh desert conditions.

The plant's ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems allows it to survive in low-rainfall environments.
