Lespedeza japonica f. sericea

Lespedeza japonica f. sericea, commonly known as Japanese lespedeza silver form, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered inflorescences that produce fluffy, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silvery or hairy appearance due to fine pubescence. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
