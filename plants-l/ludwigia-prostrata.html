It is native to South America and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has prostrate growth habits, with slender, branching stems that root at the nodes. Its small, bright red flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Ludwigia prostrata is popular in aquatic and bog gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
