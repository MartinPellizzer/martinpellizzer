Lepanthes inornata

Lepanthes inornata, commonly known as the unadorned slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Lepanthes inornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
