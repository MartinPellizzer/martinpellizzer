Luzula alpigena

Luzula alpigena, commonly known as the alpine woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, moist environments at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

Luzula alpigena plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
