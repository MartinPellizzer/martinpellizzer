Limonium pyramidatum

Limonium pyramidatum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in saline environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium pyramidatum is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
