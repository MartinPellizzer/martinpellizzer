Leonurus cardiaca var. rotundifolia

Leonurus cardiaca var. rotundifolia, commonly known as heartworts or redstem, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
