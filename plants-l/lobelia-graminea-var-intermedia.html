Lobelia graminea var. intermedia

Lobelia graminea var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate grass lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
