Lathyrus articulatus subsp. euclymenum

Lathyrus articulatus subsp.

euclymenum, commonly known as the European pea vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a climbing legume characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
