Lysimachia trientalis var. arctica

Lysimachia trientalis var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic yellow loosestrife, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.
