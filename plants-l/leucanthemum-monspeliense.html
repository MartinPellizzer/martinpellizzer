Leucanthemum monspeliense

Leucanthemum monspeliense, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a compact growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a yellow-orange disk floret in the center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom from late spring to early autumn.
