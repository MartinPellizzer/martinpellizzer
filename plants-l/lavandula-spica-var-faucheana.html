Lavandula spica var. faucheana

Lavandula spica var. faucheana, commonly known as the French lavender or Faucheana lavender, is a compact, fragrant shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of purple-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and essential oil production. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and horticultural research.
