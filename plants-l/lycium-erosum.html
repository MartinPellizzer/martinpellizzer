Lycium erosum

Lycium erosum, commonly known as the Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Lycium erosum is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common ornamental and medicinal plant in many regions.
