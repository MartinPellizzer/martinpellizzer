Loeflingia renifolia

Loeflingia renifolia, commonly known as the kidney-leafed loeflingia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Loeflingia renifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
