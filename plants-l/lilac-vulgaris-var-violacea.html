Lilac vulgaris var. violacea

Lilac vulgaris var. violacea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in American horticulture.
