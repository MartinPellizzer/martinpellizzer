Liparis hirtzii

Liparis hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its stems are often creeping or upright depending on the species.

Liparis hirtzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
