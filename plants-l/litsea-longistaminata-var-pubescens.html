Litsea longistaminata var. pubescens

Litsea longistaminata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate to elliptic leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
