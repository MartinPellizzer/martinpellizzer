Leucadendron coniferum

Leucadendron coniferum, commonly known as the silver leaf candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in mild climates.
