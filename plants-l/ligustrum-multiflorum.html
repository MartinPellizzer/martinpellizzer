Ligustrum multiflorum

Ligustrum multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum multiflorum is also valued for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
