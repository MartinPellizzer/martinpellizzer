Lathyrus glaucifolius

Lathyrus glaucifolius, commonly known as the blueleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale blue or violet, appearing in clusters.

Lathyrus glaucifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
