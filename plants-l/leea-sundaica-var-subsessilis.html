Leea sundaica var. subsessilis

Leea sundaica var. subsessilis, commonly known as the Sunda leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically found along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
