Lychnis vespertina var. incarnata

Lychnis vespertina var. incarnata, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and woodland edges. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Botanically, it is part of the Onagraceae family, known for its unique floral structures and ecological significance.
