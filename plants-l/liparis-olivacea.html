Liparis olivacea

Liparis olivacea, commonly known as the olive orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are often clustered in a spike and produce a delicate, orchid-like appearance.
