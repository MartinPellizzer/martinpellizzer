Leptospermum mjoebergii

Leptospermum mjoebergii, commonly known as the Mjoeberg tea tree, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Leptospermum mjoebergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
