Lespedeza capitata var. sericea

Lespedeza capitata var. sericea, commonly known as silky lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, fluffy clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely textured, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It is often used in erosion control and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Lespedeza capitata var. sericea prefers full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various landscape conditions.
