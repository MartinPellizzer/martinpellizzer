Liabum klattii

Liabum klattii, commonly known as the Klatt's liabum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it produces legume pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

Liabum klattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
