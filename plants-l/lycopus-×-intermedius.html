Lycopus × intermedius

Lycopus × intermedius, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in loose spikes, blooming from June to August.

Lycopus × intermedius is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported sedative properties.
