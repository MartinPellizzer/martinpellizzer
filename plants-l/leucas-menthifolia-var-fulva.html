Leucas menthifolia var. fulva

Leucas menthifolia var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma when crushed.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
