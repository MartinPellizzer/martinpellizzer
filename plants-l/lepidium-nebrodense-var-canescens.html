Lepidium nebrodense var. canescens

Lepidium nebrodense var. canescens, commonly known as the Sicilian cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

This variety is notable for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in local cuisines.
