Ligustrum obtusifolium f. velutinum

Ligustrum obtusifolium f. velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf privet, is a variant of the common privet species.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark berries, which are important for wildlife.

Its growth habit is typically compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.
