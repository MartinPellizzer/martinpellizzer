Lolium rigidum var. teres

Lolium rigidum var. teres, commonly known as rigid ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing two to three small, awned florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is commonly used in pasture and forage systems.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it an important component of agricultural ecosystems.
