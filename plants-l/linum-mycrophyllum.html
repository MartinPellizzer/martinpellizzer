Linum mycrophyllum

Linum mycrophyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Linum mycrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
