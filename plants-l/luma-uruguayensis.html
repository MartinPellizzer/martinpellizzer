Luma uruguayensis

Luma uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay firebush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal or sandy environments.

Luma uruguayensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
