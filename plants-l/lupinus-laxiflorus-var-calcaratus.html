Lupinus laxiflorus var. calcaratus

Lupinus laxiflorus var. calcaratus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Lupinus laxiflorus var. calcaratus is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae) and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
