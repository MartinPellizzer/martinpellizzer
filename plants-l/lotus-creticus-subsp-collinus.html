Lotus creticus subsp. collinus

Lotus creticus subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the Cretan lotus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
