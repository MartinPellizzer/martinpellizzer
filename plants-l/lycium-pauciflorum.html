Lycium pauciflorum

Lycium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Lycium pauciflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
