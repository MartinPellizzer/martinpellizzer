Loevigia sericea

Loevigia sericea, commonly known as the silky loevigia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas.

Loevigia sericea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
