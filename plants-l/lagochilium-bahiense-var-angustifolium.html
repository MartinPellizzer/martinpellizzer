Lagochilium bahiense var. angustifolium

Lagochilium bahiense var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
