Laggera squarrosa

Laggera squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy ragweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pale brown, spherical fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and wastelands.

Laggera squarrosa is an annual herb that plays a role in local ecosystems but can become invasive in certain environments.
