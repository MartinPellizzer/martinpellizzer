Limonium heterobracteatum

Limonium heterobracteatum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

Limonium heterobracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and coastal landscapes.
