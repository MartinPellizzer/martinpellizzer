Luzula × danica

Luzula × danica, commonly known as the silvergrass or variegated wood sedge, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species, Luzula sylvatica and Luzula nivea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often display a striking silver or white variegation. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Luzula × danica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
