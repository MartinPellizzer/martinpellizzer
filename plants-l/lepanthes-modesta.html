Lepanthes modesta

Lepanthes modesta, commonly known as the modest lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers that are arranged in a loose, lateral raceme. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on the forest floor. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it has a small, sympodial growth habit.

Lepanthes modesta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
