Lobelia calendulacea

Lobelia calendulacea, commonly known as the golden lily or yellow lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, narrow leaves arranged in a whorled pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Lobelia calendulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
