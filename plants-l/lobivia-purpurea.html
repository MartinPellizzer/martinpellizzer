Lobivia purpurea

Lobivia purpurea, commonly known as the purple lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stem and vibrant purple or reddish coloration, which becomes more pronounced under intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its stems and leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Lobivia purpurea is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardeners.
