Lerchia maritima var. australis

Lerchia maritima var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sea holly, is a coastal plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important species in coastal flora.
