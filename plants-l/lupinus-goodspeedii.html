Lupinus goodspeedii

Lupinus goodspeedii, commonly known as Goodspeed's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus goodspeedii plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
