Lonchocarpus ferrugineus

Lonchocarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing properties, it is sometimes used in agroforestry systems to improve soil fertility.
