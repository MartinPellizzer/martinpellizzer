Lathyrus layardii

Lathyrus layardii, commonly known as the Layard's vetchling, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has slender, hairy stems.

Lathyrus layardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
