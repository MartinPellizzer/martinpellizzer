Lonchocarpus obovatus

Lonchocarpus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemate inflorescences. It produces flat, elongated pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
