Licuala paludosa

Licuala paludosa, commonly known as the swamp palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove forests. Its stems are slender and can grow up to several meters tall, often with a fibrous texture.

Licuala paludosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
