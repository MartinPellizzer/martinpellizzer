Lepidium hirtum subsp. stylatum

Lepidium hirtum subsp.

stylatum, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist environments such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs. Lepidium hirtum subsp.

stylatum is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
