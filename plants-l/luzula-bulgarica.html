Luzula bulgarica

Luzula bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Luzula bulgarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
