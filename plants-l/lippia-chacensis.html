Lippia chacensis

Lippia chacensis, commonly known as the Chaco lippia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Paraguay, and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Lippia chacensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
