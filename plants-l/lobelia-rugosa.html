Lobelia rugosa

Lobelia rugosa, commonly known as rough lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often found in the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

Lobelia rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
