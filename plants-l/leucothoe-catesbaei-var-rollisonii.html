Leucothoe catesbaei var. rollisonii

Leucothoe catesbaei var. rollisonii, commonly known as the Carolina leatherleaf, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form thickets in woodland settings.
