Leptospermum dubium

Leptospermum dubium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Leptospermum dubium is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
