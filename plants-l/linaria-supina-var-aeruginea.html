Linaria supina var. aeruginea

Linaria supina var. aeruginea, commonly known as the silver linaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular flowers. The foliage is characterized by its silvery-green leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The flowers, which range in color from pale blue to violet, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
