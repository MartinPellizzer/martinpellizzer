Lonicera webbiana var. lanpingensis

Lonicera webbiana var. lanpingensis, commonly known as the Lanping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
