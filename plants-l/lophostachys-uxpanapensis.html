Lophostachys uxpanapensis

Lophostachys uxpanapensis, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
