Lathyrus canescens subvar. pedunculiformis

Lathyrus canescens subvar. pedunculiformis, commonly known as the pale vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and pale blue or purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
