Lespedeza royleana

Lespedeza royleana, commonly known as redstem lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are encased in small, hairy pods and are dispersed by wind.

Lespedeza royleana is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
