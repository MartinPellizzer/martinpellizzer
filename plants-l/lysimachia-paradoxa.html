Lysimachia paradoxa

Lysimachia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Lysimachia paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
