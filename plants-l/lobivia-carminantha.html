Lobivia carminantha

Lobivia carminantha, commonly known as the Crimson Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded stems that are often ribbed and covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually fleshy and cylindrical, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Lobivia carminantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
