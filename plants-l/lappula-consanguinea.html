Lappula consanguinea

Lappula consanguinea, commonly known as the common lappula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has a slender stem with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from June to August.

Lappula consanguinea is characterized by its capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
