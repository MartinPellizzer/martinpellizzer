Lamium coutinhoi

Lamium coutinhoi, commonly known as the Portuguese dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive purple or reddish coloration. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium coutinhoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
