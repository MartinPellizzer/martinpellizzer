Lolium multiflorum f. submuticum

Lolium multiflorum f. submuticum, commonly known as the submutic form of ryegrass, is a variant of the annual ryegrass species.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to the typical form, giving it a smoother appearance. Botanically, it is a member of the Poaceae family and exhibits a prostrate growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage and turfgrass applications.
