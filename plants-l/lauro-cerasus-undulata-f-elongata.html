Lauro-cerasus undulata f. elongata

Lauro-cerasus undulata f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet berries. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
