Lecanopteris pustulata subsp. howensis

Lecanopteris pustulata subsp.

howensis, commonly known as the How Island huperzia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its upright, creeping rhizomes and leathery, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a rough, textured surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
