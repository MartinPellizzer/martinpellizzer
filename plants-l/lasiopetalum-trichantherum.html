Lasiopetalum trichantherum

Lasiopetalum trichantherum, commonly known as the slender-flowered honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions such as New South Wales and Queensland. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Lasiopetalum trichantherum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
