Lecythis simiorum

Lecythis simiorum, commonly known as the monkey bread tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into woody, spiky fruits. The fruits contain a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, starchy pulp that is edible. The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its growth habit and unique fruit structure make it a notable species in botanical studies.
