Lens esculenta var. nigra

Lens esculenta var. nigra, commonly known as black lentil, is a variety of the lentil plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds that are rich in protein and fiber. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain the edible seeds. The botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in cooking.
