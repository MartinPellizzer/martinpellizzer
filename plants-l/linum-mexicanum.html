Linum mexicanum

Linum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican flax, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

Linum mexicanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
