Lamium glechomoides

Lamium glechomoides, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

Lamium glechomoides is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in garden settings for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
