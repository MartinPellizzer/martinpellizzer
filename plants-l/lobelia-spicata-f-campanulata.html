Lobelia spicata f. campanulata

Lobelia spicata f. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped lobelia, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to the eastern United States. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant blooms.
