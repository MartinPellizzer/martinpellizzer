Linociera mandioccana

Linociera mandioccana, commonly known as the African bitterball, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often thorny stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are spherical, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a sticky pulp.

Linociera mandioccana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
