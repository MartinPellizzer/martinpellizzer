Lophiolepis boluensis

Lophiolepis boluensis, commonly known as the Bolu endemic grass, is a species native to Turkey.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
