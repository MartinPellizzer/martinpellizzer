Lupinus leucophyllus var. canescens

Lupinus leucophyllus var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and tall, spindly stems that bear clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
