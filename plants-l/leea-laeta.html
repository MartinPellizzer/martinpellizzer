Leea laeta

Leea laeta, commonly known as the white leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Leeaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Leea laeta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
