Lellingeria simacensis

Lellingeria simacensis, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant purple to blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
