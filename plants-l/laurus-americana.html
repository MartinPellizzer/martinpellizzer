Laurus americana

Laurus americana, commonly known as American laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

The leaves are often used in culinary applications, similar to bay leaves, and are valued for their aromatic properties.
