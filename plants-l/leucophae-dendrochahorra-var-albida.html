Leucophae dendrochahorra var. albida

Leucophae dendrochahorra var. albida, commonly known as the White-veined Tree Fern, is a striking species characterized by its tall, gracefully arching fronds.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that is covered in a dense layer of fibrous roots, typical of tree ferns. The fronds are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow in low light conditions, which aids in nocturnal pollination. This variety is native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
