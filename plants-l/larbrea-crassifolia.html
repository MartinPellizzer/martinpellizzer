Larbrea crassifolia

Larbrea crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf shrub, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Larbrea crassifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
