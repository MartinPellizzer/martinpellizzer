Lemaireocereus thurberi var. littoralis

Lemaireocereus thurberi var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal night bloomer, is a rare cactus species native to the coastal regions of Baja California.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.
