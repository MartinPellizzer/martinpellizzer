Lythrum intermedium

Lythrum intermedium, commonly known as swamp loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It typically grows in marshy soils and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lythrum intermedium plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
