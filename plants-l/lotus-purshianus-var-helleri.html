Lotus purshianus var. helleri

Lotus purshianus var. helleri, commonly known as the Heller's lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of China.

It features striking pink to purple flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with long, thin stems that support its showy blossoms. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
