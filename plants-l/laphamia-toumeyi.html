Laphamia toumeyi

Laphamia toumeyi, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces strikingly colorful inflorescences with clusters of showy, white to pink flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
