Lepidium bidentatum var. bidentatum

Lepidium bidentatum var. bidentatum, commonly known as peppergrass, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, round, smooth-seeded fruits that are dispersed by wind. Peppergrass is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
