Lentiscus mutica

Lentiscus mutica, commonly known as the prickly ash or spiny buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and dark, glossy berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has rough, thorny stems and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and has a deep root system for moisture retention.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.
