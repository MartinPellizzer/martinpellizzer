Lycium chilense var. glaberrimum

Lycium chilense var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, red berries that are edible when ripe. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are small, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
