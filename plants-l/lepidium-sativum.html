Lepidium sativum

Lepidium sativum, commonly known as garden cress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by slender, elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are tender and can be used in salads or as a garnish.

The plant is also valued for its nutritional content, including vitamins C and A, and its use in traditional medicine.
