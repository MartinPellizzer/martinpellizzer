Lycopodium cuspidatum

Lycopodium cuspidatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its needle-like leaves and clustered, cone-like structures at the tips of its stems. It is often found in forest floors and rocky areas, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.

Lycopodium cuspidatum plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat through its unique reproductive and structural features.
