Leucochloron

Leucochloron, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique species of liverwort that exhibits a distinctive white or pale green coloration due to its reduced chlorophyll content.

It is a thalloid plant, meaning it lacks true leaves, stems, and roots, and instead has a flat, leaf-like structure called a thallus. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in damp soil or on rocks in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are small and inconspicuous, producing spores for propagation.

Leucochloron plays a minor but ecologically significant role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.
