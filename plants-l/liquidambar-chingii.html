Liquidambar chingii

Liquidambar chingii, commonly known as the Chinese sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the genus Liquidambar in the family Altingiaceae, characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and distinctive bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spiky, spherical fruit capsules that release tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges and a glossy surface, turning vibrant red or orange in the fall.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes.
