Lespedeza bicolor f. tomentella

Lespedeza bicolor f. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly lespedeza, is a variant of the common lespedeza species.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its hairy texture and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and ornamental plant.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
