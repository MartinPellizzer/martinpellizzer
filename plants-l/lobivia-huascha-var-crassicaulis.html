Lobivia huascha var. crassicaulis

Lobivia huascha var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the growing season. Its leaves are typically small and cylindrical, reducing water loss through transpiration.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
