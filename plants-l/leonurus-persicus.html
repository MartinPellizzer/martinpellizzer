Leonurus persicus

Leonurus persicus, commonly known as Persian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features small, purple or pink flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, with a distinctive texture that aids in moisture retention.

Leonurus persicus is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
