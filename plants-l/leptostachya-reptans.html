Leptostachya reptans

Leptostachya reptans, commonly known as the creeping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous habit and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Leptostachya reptans is often found in humid climates and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
