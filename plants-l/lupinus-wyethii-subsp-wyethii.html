Lupinus wyethii subsp. wyethii

Lupinus wyethii subsp.

wyethii, commonly known as the Wyeth's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Lupinus wyethii subsp.

wyethii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
