Linospadix pauciflorus

Linospadix pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered spadix plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It features a spadix, a fleshy, spike-like structure that bears numerous small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, leaf-like bract that encloses the spadix. Its foliage consists of broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are usually green with a waxy texture.

Linospadix pauciflorus is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
