Lasianthus foetulentus

Lasianthus foetulentus, commonly known as the stinking beauty, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.
