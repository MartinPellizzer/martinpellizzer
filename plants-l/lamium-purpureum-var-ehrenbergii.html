Lamium purpureum var. ehrenbergii

Lamium purpureum var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces clusters of small, bilaterally symmetrical pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland floors, along paths, and in disturbed soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
