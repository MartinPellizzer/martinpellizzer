Landolphia jumellei

Landolphia jumellei, commonly known as the giant African waterleaf, is a member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
