Lavandula decipiens

Lavandula decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive lavender, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is closely related to other lavender species but is distinguished by its unique floral structure and growth habits.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in essential oil production.
