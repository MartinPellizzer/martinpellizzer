Lappa communis

Lappa communis, commonly known as the greater burdock, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spiny, bur-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its root system is extensive, and the roots are often harvested for their medicinal and culinary uses.

Lappa communis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
