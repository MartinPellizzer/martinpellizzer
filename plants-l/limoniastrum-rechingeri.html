Limoniastrum rechingeri

Limoniastrum rechingeri, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and showy yellow or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and has a deep root system to access underground water. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Limoniastrum rechingeri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
