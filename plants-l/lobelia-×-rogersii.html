Lobelia × rogersii

Lobelia × rogersii, commonly known as the hybrid lobelia, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a cross between Lobelia siphilitica and Lobelia cardinalis, resulting in a plant with striking red and purple flowers. The species is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
