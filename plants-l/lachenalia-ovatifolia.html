Lachenalia ovatifolia

Lachenalia ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
