Lophiolepis cassia

Lophiolepis cassia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green backdrop to its colorful blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Lophiolepis cassia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
