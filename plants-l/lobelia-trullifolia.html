Lobelia trullifolia

Lobelia trullifolia, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often clustering at the base of the plant.

Lobelia trullifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
