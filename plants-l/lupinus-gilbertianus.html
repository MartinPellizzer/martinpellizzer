Lupinus gilbertianus

Lupinus gilbertianus, commonly known as Gilbert's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall spires of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus gilbertianus plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
