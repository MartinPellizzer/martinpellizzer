Lepanthes felis

Lepanthes felis, commonly known as the Felis Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale green with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette, helping to retain moisture.

Lepanthes felis is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
