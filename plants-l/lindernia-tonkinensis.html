Lindernia tonkinensis

Lindernia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese Lindernia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Linderniaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Lindernia tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
