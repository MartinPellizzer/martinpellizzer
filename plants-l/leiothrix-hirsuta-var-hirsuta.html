Leiothrix hirsuta var. hirsuta

Leiothrix hirsuta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leiothrix, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
