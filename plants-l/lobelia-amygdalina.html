Lobelia amygdalina

Lobelia amygdalina, commonly known as the almond-leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Lobelia amygdalina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
