Lagochilium mucronatum

Lagochilium mucronatum, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
