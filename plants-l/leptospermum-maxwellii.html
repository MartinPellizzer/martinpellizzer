Leptospermum maxwellii

Leptospermum maxwellii, commonly known as Maxwell's tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
