Lonicera fragrantissima

Lonicera fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet summer honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are orange-red and attract birds, contributing to its ecological value.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
