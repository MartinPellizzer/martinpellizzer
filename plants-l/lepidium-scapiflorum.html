Lepidium scapiflorum

Lepidium scapiflorum, commonly known as the slender cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
