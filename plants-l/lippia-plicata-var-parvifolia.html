Lippia plicata var. parvifolia

Lippia plicata var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, aromatic leaves with a distinctive lemony scent due to its high concentration of volatile oils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
