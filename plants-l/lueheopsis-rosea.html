Lueheopsis rosea

Lueheopsis rosea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink and white petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small but fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Lueheopsis rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in greenhouses and gardens worldwide.
