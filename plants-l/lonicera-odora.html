Lonicera odora

Lonicera odora, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its fragrant, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its strong, pleasant aroma, which is often described as reminiscent of vanilla or fresh hay. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Lonicera odora is also valued in perfumery for its unique scent.
