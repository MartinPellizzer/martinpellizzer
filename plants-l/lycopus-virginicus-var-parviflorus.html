Lycopus virginicus var. parviflorus

Lycopus virginicus var. parviflorus, commonly known as smallflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
