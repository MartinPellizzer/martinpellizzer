Leucocroton acunae

Leucocroton acunae, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. Leucocroton acunae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
