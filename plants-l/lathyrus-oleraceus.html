Lathyrus oleraceus

Lathyrus oleraceus, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a sprawling annual vine characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing several elliptical seeds, which are typically light brown to gray in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

Lathyrus oleraceus is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
