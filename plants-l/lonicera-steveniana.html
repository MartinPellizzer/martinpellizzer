Lonicera steveniana

Lonicera steveniana, commonly known as the Steven's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for birds.

Lonicera steveniana is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
