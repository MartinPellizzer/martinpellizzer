Laurus obovata

Laurus obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic leaves have been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.
