Lippia ligustrifolia

Lippia ligustrifolia, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its fragrance and ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Lippia ligustrifolia is also used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for its calming properties.
