Lambertia ericifolia

Lambertia ericifolia, commonly known as the coastal lambertia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in winter, attracting birds and insects. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Lambertia ericifolia is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
