Liatris × frostii

Liatris × frostii, commonly known as the Frost's blazing star, is a hybrid perennial grassland plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Liatris × frostii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
