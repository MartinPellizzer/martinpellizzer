Lathyrus gloeosperma

Lathyrus gloeosperma, commonly known as the crimson bower vine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its climbing habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several angular, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
