Lonicera belgica

Lonicera belgica, commonly known as the Belgian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange-red, appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Lonicera belgica is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
