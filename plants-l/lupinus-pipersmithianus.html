Lupinus pipersmithianus

Lupinus pipersmithianus, commonly known as Smith's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Lupinus pipersmithianus is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
