Lathyrus luteus f. carniolicus

Lathyrus luteus f. carniolicus, commonly known as the Carniolan yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Alps and adjacent regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
