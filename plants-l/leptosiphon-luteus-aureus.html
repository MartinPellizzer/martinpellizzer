Leptosiphon luteus aureus

Leptosiphon luteus aureus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a crown-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
