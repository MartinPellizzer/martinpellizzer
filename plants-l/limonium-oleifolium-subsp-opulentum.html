Limonium oleifolium subsp. opulentum

Limonium oleifolium subsp.

opulentum, commonly known as the showy sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal gardens and floral arrangements.
