Laportea monticola

Laportea monticola, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
