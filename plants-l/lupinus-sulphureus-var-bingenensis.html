Lupinus sulphureus var. bingenensis

Lupinus sulphureus var. bingenensis, commonly known as the Bingen yellow lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and narrow, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
