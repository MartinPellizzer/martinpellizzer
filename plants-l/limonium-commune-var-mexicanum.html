Limonium commune var. mexicanum

Limonium commune var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Limonium commune var. mexicanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
