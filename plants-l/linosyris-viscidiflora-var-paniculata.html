Linosyris viscidiflora var. paniculata

Linosyris viscidiflora var. paniculata, commonly known as the stickyflower linseed plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a sticky substance, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Linosyris. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, giving it its specific epithet "paniculata.". Its leaves are linear and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
