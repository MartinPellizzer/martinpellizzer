Leucanthemum integrifolium

Leucanthemum integrifolium, commonly known as the oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a yellow center, forming a daisy-like flower. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Leucanthemum integrifolium is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
