Lycianthes ulei subsp. dolichodonta

Lycianthes ulei subsp.

dolichodonta, commonly known as the longfruit lycianthes, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The fruit is a fleshy, elongated capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
