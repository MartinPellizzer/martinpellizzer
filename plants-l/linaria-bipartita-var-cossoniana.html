Linaria bipartita var. cossoniana

Linaria bipartita var. cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its linear leaves are arranged in opposite pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
