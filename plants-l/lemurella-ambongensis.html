Lemurella ambongensis

Lemurella ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis Lichen, is a rare and specialized plant found in specific tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lichens, which are symbiotic organisms composed of fungi and algae or cyanobacteria. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its botanical structure is characterized by a crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to its substrate.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
