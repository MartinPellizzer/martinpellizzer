Lomatium mohavense subsp. mohavense

Lomatium mohavense subsp.

mohavense, commonly known as Mohave lomatia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. It features a basal rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun and dry conditions.

Its subspecific name highlights its distinctiveness within the Lomatium genus.
