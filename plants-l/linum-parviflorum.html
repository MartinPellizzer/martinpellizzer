Linum parviflorum

Linum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and oval.

Linum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
