Lobelia decurrens subsp. decurrens

Lobelia decurrens subsp.

decurrens, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to violet flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
