Lissochilus saccatus

Lissochilus saccatus, commonly known as the sac-like beard orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a sac-like ovary that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, ranging from white to pink, and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Lissochilus saccatus is considered a rare and endangered species.
