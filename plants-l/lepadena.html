Lepadena

Lepadena, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, with smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Lepadena is often cultivated for its essential oils and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
