Lathyrus maritimus f. albiflorus

Lathyrus maritimus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered sea pea, is a rare botanical variant of the sea pea plant.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or pink hues of the species. This plant is typically found in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or saline soils. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral coloration and adaptation to maritime environments.
