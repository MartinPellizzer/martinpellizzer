Lagarinthus navicularis

Lagarinthus navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
