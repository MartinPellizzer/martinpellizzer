Lobivia watadae

Lobivia watadae, commonly known as the Waterman's lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are often covered in a layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Lobivia watadae is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to collections of succulents.
