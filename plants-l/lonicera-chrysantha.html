Lonicera chrysantha

Lonicera chrysantha, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Lonicera chrysantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
