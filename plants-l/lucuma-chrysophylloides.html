Lucuma chrysophylloides

Lucuma chrysophylloides, commonly known as the golden lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small, sweet potatoes. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates and well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slight aromatic scent, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, revealing a soft, creamy pulp with a distinct, sweet flavor.
