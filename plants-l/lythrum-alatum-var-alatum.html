Lythrum alatum var. alatum

Lythrum alatum var. alatum, commonly known as the spiked loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in spikes at the top of the stem.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
