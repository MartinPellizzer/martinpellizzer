Linanthus pungens subsp. pulchriflorus

Linanthus pungens subsp.

pulchriflorus, commonly known as the beautifulflowered flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The species is distinguished by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
