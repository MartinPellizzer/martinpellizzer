Loranthus combreticola

Loranthus combreticola, commonly known as the mistletoe of the Combretaceae family, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various animals.
