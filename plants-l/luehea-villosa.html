Luehea villosa

Luehea villosa, commonly known as the silky ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often whitish bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Luehea villosa is known for its ability to thrive in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
