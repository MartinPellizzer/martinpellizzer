Liparis ascendens

Liparis ascendens, commonly known as the ascending orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, spurred orchid flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded forest understories.

Liparis ascendens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitats.
