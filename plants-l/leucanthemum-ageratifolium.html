Leucanthemum ageratifolium

Leucanthemum ageratifolium, commonly known as the American oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc floret, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
