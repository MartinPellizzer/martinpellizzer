Lonicera paradoxa

Lonicera paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to yellow. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lonicera paradoxa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
