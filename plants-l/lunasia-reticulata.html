Lunasia reticulata

Lunasia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive net-like pattern on its leaves, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Lunasia reticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
