Lupinus chiapensis

Lupinus chiapensis, commonly known as Chiapas lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America, particularly found in the highlands of Chiapas, Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lupinus chiapensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.
