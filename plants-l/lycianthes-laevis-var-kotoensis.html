Lycianthes laevis var. kotoensis

Lycianthes laevis var. kotoensis, commonly known as the Kotoensis nightshade, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by egg-shaped berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
