Logania judithiana

Logania judithiana, commonly known as the Judith's logania, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Loganiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. It is often found in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
