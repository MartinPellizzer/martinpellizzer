Loasa lateritia

Loasa lateritia, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Loasa lateritia is often found in rocky or alpine regions, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.
