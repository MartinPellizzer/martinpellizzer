Lonchocarpus bahianus

Lonchocarpus bahianus, commonly known as Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
