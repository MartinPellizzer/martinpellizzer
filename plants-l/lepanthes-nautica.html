Lepanthes nautica

Lepanthes nautica, commonly known as the sea orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features delicate, showy flowers with white petals and a yellow lip, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes nautica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
