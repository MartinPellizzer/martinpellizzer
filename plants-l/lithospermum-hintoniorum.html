Lithospermum hintoniorum

Lithospermum hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's rock pepper, is a herbaceous perennial native to specific regions of North America.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow along rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lithospermum hintoniorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
