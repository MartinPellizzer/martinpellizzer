Leptaloe × atrovirens

Leptaloe × atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black veins on the leaves, which contrast with its pale green or white foliage. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers, often in shades of violet or purple, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
