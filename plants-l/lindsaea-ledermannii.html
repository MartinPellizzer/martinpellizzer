Lindsaea ledermannii

Lindsaea ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's tillandsia, is a species of epiphytic plant belonging to the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in the understory of humid forests. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a compact, water-holding structure. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
