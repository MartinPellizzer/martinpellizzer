Liatris macrostachya

Liatris macrostachya, commonly known as the tall blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, purple-pink flower heads. The plant produces numerous narrow, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Liatris macrostachya is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.

Its ecological value lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various wildlife species.
