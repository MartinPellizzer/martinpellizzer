Lychnis mongolica

Lychnis mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian campion, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including Mongolia, China, and Siberia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Lychnis mongolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
