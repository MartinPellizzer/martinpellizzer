Lepismium mataralense var. floccosum

Lepismium mataralense var. floccosum, commonly known as the woolly lepismium, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to botanical gardens and specialized collections.
