Lepidium accedens

Lepidium accedens, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid or coastal environments.

Lepidium accedens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
