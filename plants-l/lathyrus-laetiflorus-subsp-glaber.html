Lathyrus laetiflorus subsp. glaber

Lathyrus laetiflorus subsp.

glaber, commonly known as the naked-flowered sweet pea, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features slender, branching stems covered in soft, hairy leaves that are typically alternate and pinnately compound.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its seeds are contained in slender, curved pods that mature in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
