Lobelia iteophylla

Lobelia iteophylla, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Lobelia iteophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
