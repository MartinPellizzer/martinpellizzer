Laportea schomburgkii var. versicolor

Laportea schomburgkii var. versicolor, commonly known as the variegated Schomburgk's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
