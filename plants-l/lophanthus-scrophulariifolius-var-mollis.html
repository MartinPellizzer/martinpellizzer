Lophanthus scrophulariifolius var. mollis

Lophanthus scrophulariifolius var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved lophanthus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, velvety texture and a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are typically pale purple to lilac, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a valuable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
