Loranthus eminii f. cinereus

Loranthus eminii f. cinereus, commonly known as the ash leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host, typically ash trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is of interest in ecological studies due to its parasitic nature and role in forest ecosystems.
