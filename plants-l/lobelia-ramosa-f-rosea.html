Lobelia ramosa f. rosea

Lobelia ramosa f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to lavender, often with a distinctive rose-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, with slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is well-suited for moist, partially shaded gardens and thrives in well-drained soil.
