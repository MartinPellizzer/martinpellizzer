Lepidium crassifolium subsp. descemetii

Lepidium crassifolium subsp.

descemetii, commonly known as the Desert Cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, segmented fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This species is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
