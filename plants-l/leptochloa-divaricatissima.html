Leptochloa divaricatissima

Leptochloa divaricatissima, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, tufted growth habit and slender, often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface.

Leptochloa divaricatissima is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
