Laricorchis ochracea

Laricorchis ochracea, commonly known as the yellow pine orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the western United States.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees, typically in coniferous forests. The plant has a unique morphology, with a slender, wiry stem and small, yellowish-green leaves. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive coloration that includes yellow and green hues.

Laricorchis ochracea is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments and is often found on the trunks of pine trees.
