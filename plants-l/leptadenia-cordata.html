Leptadenia cordata

Leptadenia cordata, commonly known as African milkweed, is a shrub native to Africa and parts of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap, which is toxic and has been used traditionally in various medicinal practices. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
