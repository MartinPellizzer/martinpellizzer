Lathyrus aegeus

Lathyrus aegeus, commonly known as the Aegean vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
