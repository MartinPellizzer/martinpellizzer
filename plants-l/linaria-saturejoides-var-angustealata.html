Linaria saturejoides var. angustealata

Linaria saturejoides var. angustealata, commonly known as narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
