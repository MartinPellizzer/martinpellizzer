Lavandula buchii var. buchii

Lavandula buchii var. buchii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, pale purple to lilac flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both ornamental horticulture and essential oil production.
