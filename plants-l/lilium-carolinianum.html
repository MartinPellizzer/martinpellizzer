Lilium carolinianum

Lilium carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne on tall, slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a basal rosette, and its bulbs are used for propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The Carolina lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
