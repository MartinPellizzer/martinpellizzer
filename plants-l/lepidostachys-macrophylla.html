Lepidostachys macrophylla

Lepidostachys macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed lepidostachys, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Lepidostachys macrophylla is well-suited for humid, shaded environments and is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
