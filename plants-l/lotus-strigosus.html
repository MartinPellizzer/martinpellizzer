Lotus strigosus

Lotus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by legumes containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
