Lonicera tatarica f. rosea

Lonicera tatarica f. rosea, commonly known as the pink honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hedges.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and attractive, glossy foliage.
