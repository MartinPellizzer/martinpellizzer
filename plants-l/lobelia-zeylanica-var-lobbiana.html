Lobelia zeylanica var. lobbiana

Lobelia zeylanica var. lobbiana, commonly known as the Sri Lankan lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which are typically borne on terminal spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
