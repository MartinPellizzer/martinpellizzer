Linum turcicum

Linum turcicum, commonly known as Turkish flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are used for oil production, and the fibers from its stems have been historically used for textiles.

Linum turcicum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
