Lamprococcus racinae var. luteocarpa

Lamprococcus racinae var. luteocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited lamprococcus, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow fruiting bodies that emerge from the base of its leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests where the soil is rich in organic matter. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
