Lithops aurantiaca

Lithops aurantiaca, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble stones, which help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rocky appearance. Its leaves are covered in a powdery coating that reduces water loss and protects against intense sunlight.

Lithops aurantiica is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
