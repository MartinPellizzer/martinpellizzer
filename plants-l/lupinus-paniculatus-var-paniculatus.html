Lupinus paniculatus var. paniculatus

Lupinus paniculatus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves.

It produces tall, branched inflorescences of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant is native to western North America and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a prominent feature in its native habitat.

The seeds are encased in pods and are important for both wildlife and human use in certain regions.
