Ligustrum ovalifolium var. pacificum

Ligustrum ovalifolium var. pacificum, commonly known as Pacific privet, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by black, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments make it a popular choice for landscaping.
