Lauro-cerasus brittoniana

Lauro-cerasus brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton cherry-laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are typically not consumed by humans.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
