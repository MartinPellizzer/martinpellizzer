Liriopogon biflorum

Liriopogon biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered liriopogon, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its inflorescence consists of two to three flowers, which are arranged in a spike-like structure.

The botanical characteristics of Liriopogon biflorum make it a unique and relatively rare species within the orchid family.
