Lepanthes vatrax

Lepanthes vatrax, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Lepanthes vatrax has small, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in tropical ecosystems.
