Lepanthes longicruris

Lepanthes longicruris, commonly known as the long-legged orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several elongated, fleshy leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Lepanthes longicruris is notable for its unique floral structure and is a popular subject in botanical studies.
