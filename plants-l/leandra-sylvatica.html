Leandra sylvatica

Leandra sylvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often have a metallic sheen. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its common name, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Leandra sylvatica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
