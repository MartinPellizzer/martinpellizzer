Lychnis dioica subsp. rubra

Lychnis dioica subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
