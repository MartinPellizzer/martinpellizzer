Limosella lineata f. submersa

Limosella lineata f. submersa, commonly known as the submerged limosella, is a variant of the species Limosella lineata.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant is characterized by its submerged leaves, which are usually narrow and ribbon-like. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that float on the water surface.

This subspecies is often found in wetlands, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
