Lobelia perpusilla

Lobelia perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It is often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable conditions.
