Lambertia inermis

Lambertia inermis, commonly known as the silver lambertia, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bear bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and seasonal floral display.
