Lonicera buddleioides

Lonicera buddleioides, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Lonicera buddleioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
