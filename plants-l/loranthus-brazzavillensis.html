Loranthus brazzavillensis

Loranthus brazzavillensis, commonly known as the Central African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in tropical rainforests.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting various wildlife species.
