Lepanthes bivalvis

Lepanthes bivalvis, commonly known as the two-valved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette.

Lepanthes bivalvis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
