Liparis tricallosa

Liparis tricallosa, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, intricate orchid flowers that often display a mix of white, green, and yellow hues. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Liparis tricallosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
