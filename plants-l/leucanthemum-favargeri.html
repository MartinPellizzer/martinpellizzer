Leucanthemum favargeri

Leucanthemum favargeri, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
