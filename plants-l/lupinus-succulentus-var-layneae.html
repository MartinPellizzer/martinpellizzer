Lupinus succulentus var. layneae

Lupinus succulentus var. layneae, commonly known as Layne's lupine, is a rare and endemic species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

This plant is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant's unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
