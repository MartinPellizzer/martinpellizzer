Lavandula nimmoi

Lavandula nimmoi, commonly known as Nimmo's lavender, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple flowers, which are typical of the lavandula genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
