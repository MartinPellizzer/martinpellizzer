Lingnania remotiflora

Lingnania remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered shrub, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lingnania remotiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
