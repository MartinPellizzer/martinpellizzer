Leucopogon navicularis

Leucopogon navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Leucopogon navicularis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
