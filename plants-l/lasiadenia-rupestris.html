Lasiadenia rupestris

Lasiadenia rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, helping it store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
