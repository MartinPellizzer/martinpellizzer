Leobordea digitata

Leobordea digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, finger-like leaves that give it its common name, and its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of rainfall.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often difficult to cultivate outside its native range.
