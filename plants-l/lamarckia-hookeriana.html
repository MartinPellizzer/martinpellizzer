Lamarckia hookeriana

Lamarckia hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
