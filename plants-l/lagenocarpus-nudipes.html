Lagenocarpus nudipes

Lagenocarpus nudipes, commonly known as the naked-footed lichen, is a unique member of the Lichenes family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like thallus that lacks the typical rhizines found in many lichens. The plant exhibits a greyish-green to yellowish-green coloration, often with a powdery coating on its surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks and rocks in humid climates.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
