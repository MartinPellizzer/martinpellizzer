Lippia rosmarinifolia f. latifolia

Lippia rosmarinifolia f. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf lippia, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features aromatic, narrow to broad lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green hue and a strong, mint-like scent. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
