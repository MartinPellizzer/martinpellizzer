Ligustrum undulatum

Ligustrum undulatum, commonly known as the waxleaf ligustrum, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often waxy to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by black berries in the fall. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Ligustrum undulatum is also valued for its use in hedges and as a privacy screen in landscaping.
