Leptoderris velutina

Leptoderris velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing edible, sour fruits. The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
