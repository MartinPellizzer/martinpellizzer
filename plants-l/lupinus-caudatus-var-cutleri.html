Lupinus caudatus var. cutleri

Lupinus caudatus var. cutleri, commonly known as the Cutler's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Lupinus caudatus var. cutleri is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators and its role in soil stabilization.
