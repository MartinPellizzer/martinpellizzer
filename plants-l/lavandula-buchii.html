Lavandula buchii

Lavandula buchii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces spikes of purple or lilac flowers in the summer months, which are used for both ornamental and aromatic purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Lavandula buchii is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes, cosmetics, and herbal remedies.
