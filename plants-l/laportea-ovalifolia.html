Laportea ovalifolia

Laportea ovalifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple to pink and are arranged in dense, conical clusters.

Laportea ovalifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
