Leobordea lanata

Leobordea lanata, commonly known as the woolly leobordea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
