Lupinus hirsutus var. gussoneanus

Lupinus hirsutus var. gussoneanus, commonly known as the Gussonean lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with hairy surfaces, typical of the Lupinus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
