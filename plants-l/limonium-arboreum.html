Limonium arboreum

Limonium arboreum, commonly known as the tree spurge or sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils. It is a perennial species that can grow up to several feet tall.

Limonium arboreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
