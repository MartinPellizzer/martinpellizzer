Lindera hemsleyana var. velutina

Lindera hemsleyana var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf lindera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by oval, reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and unique appearance.
