Larix principis-rupprechtii var. pendula

Larix principis-rupprechtii var. pendula, commonly known as the Weeping Siberian Larch, is a unique variant of the Siberian Larch, characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance.

It is a deciduous conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Altai Mountains. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. Its needles are dark green and turn yellow in autumn before falling, contributing to the seasonal changes in its habitat.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes for its visual interest.
