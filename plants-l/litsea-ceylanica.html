Litsea ceylanica

Litsea ceylanica, commonly known as Ceylon-laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Sri Lanka, and parts of China. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy evergreen leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing aromatic seeds.

Litsea ceylanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
