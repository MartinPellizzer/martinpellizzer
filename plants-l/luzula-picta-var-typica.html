Luzula picta var. typica

Luzula picta var. typica, commonly known as the painted wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with distinctive silver or white markings. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Luzula picta var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
