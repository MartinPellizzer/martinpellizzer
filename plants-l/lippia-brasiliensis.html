Lippia brasiliensis

Lippia brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, with hairy, opposite leaves that have a distinctive mint-like scent. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Lippia brasiliensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
