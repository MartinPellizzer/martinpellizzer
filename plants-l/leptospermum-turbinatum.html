Leptospermum turbinatum

Leptospermum turbinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Leptospermum turbinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
