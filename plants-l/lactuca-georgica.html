Lactuca georgica

Lactuca georgica, commonly known as Russian endive, is a biennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that form a rosette, and it produces yellow flowers in the second year of growth. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in various regions around the world. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which can be used raw or cooked.

Lactuca georgica contains a milky sap, which is typical of the genus Lactuca.
