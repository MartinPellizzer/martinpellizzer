Larix × czekanowskii

Larix × czekanowskii, commonly known as the European larch hybrid, is a deciduous conifer that results from the cross between the European larch (Larix decidua) and the Japanese larch (Larix kaempferi).

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that turn golden in the fall before dropping, giving it a striking seasonal appearance. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist climates.

This hybrid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
