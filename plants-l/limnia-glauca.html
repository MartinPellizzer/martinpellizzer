Limnia glauca

Limnia glauca, commonly known as the blue water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to various regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, contributing to its common name. Limnia glauca is known for its ability to thrive in still water and is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal.

It plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
