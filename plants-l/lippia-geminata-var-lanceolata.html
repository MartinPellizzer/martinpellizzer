Lippia geminata var. lanceolata

Lippia geminata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Its ornamental value is also appreciated in horticulture due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
