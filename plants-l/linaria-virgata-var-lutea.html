Linaria virgata var. lutea

Linaria virgata var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can become invasive in some regions.
