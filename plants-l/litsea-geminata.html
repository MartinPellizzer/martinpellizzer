Litsea geminata

Litsea geminata, commonly known as the Mexican lauraceae or Mexican bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by egg-shaped berries that turn red when mature. Litsea geminata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and for making aromatic oils.
