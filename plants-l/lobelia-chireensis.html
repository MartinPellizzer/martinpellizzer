Lobelia chireensis

Lobelia chireensis, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees.

Lobelia chireensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
