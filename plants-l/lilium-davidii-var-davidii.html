Lilium davidii var. davidii

Lilium davidii var. davidii, commonly known as the David's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with six to eight petals. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
