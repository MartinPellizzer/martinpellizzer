Lupinus pusillus var. pusillus

Lupinus pusillus var. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf lupine, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches and features dense clusters of pea-like, blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its small size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
