Lobivia formosa var. hyalacantha

Lobivia formosa var. hyalacantha, commonly known as the Snowy Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of fleshy, light green to whitish-blue leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
