Litsea subverticillata

Litsea subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
