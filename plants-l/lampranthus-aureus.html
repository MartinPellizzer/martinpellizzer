Lampranthus aureus

Lampranthus aureus, commonly known as the golden lampranthus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often decorative flora. The plant features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and showy blooms, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
