Lobelia pinifolia var. laricina

Lobelia pinifolia var. laricina, commonly known as the white mountain lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils of pine and mixed forests, often in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
