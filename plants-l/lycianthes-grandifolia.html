Lycianthes grandifolia

Lycianthes grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered physalis, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, broad ovate leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red calyces. Its fruits, often called "inflated capsules," contain small, edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
