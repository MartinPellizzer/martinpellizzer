Lomaria diversifolia

Lomaria diversifolia, commonly known as the variable lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Lomaria diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
