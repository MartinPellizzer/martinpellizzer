Lonicera nigra var. barbinervis

Lonicera nigra var. barbinervis, commonly known as black honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of yellow to orange. Its fruits are dark blue to black berries that ripen in the fall.

The species is characterized by its distinctive barbed veins on the leaf margins, giving it its specific epithet "barbinervis.".
