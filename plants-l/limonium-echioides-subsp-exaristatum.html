Limonium echioides subsp. exaristatum

Limonium echioides subsp.

exaristatum, commonly known as the spiked sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to salt-laden environments and is commonly used in coastal landscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
