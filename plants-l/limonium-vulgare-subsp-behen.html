Limonium vulgare subsp. behen

Limonium vulgare subsp.

behen, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt. Limonium vulgare subsp.

behen is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
