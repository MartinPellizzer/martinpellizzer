Leontopodium alpinum

Leontopodium alpinum, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround the small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine meadows. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a symbol of resilience and alpine beauty.

It is also a protected species in many regions due to its ecological significance and conservation status.
