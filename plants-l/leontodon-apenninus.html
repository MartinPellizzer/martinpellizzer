Leontodon apenninus

Leontodon apenninus, commonly known as the Apennine hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by bracts that resemble a beard, giving it its common name.

Leontodon apenninus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
