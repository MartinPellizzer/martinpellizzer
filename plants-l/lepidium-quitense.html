Lepidium quitense

Lepidium quitense, commonly known as Peruvian wheatgrass, is a small annual plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, green, ovate leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its seeds are rich in nutrients.

Due to its high nutritional value, it is often used in herbal remedies and as a superfood supplement.
