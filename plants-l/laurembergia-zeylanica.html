Laurembergia zeylanica

Laurembergia zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed.

Laurembergia zeylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
