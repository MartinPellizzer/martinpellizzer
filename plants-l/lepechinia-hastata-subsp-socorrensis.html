Lepechinia hastata subsp. socorrensis

Lepechinia hastata subsp.

socorrensis, commonly known as the Socorro lepechinia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
