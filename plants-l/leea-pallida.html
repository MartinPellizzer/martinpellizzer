Leea pallida

Leea pallida, commonly known as the pale leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
