Lampranthus compressus

Lampranthus compressus, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
