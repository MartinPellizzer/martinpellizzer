Lantana canescens

Lantana canescens, commonly known as gray lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
