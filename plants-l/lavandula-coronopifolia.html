Lavandula coronopifolia

Lavandula coronopifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Lavandula coronopifolia is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and fragrance industries.
