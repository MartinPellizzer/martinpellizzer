Lonicera stenantha

Lonicera stenantha, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lonicera stenantha is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
