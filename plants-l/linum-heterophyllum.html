Linum heterophyllum

Linum heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum in the family Fabaceae, which includes other familiar plants like peas and vetches. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

Linum heterophyllum is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
