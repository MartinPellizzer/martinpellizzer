Limonium vulgare subsp. humile

Limonium vulgare subsp.

humile, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Limonium vulgare subsp.

humile is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
