Ligularia sibirica subsp. lydiae

Ligularia sibirica subsp.

lydiae, commonly known as Siberian ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a striking green to blue-green hue.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a dense, terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
