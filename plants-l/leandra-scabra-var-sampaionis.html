Leandra scabra var. sampaionis

Leandra scabra var. sampaionis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid or sampaionis orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features pendant, linear leaves and produces striking, fragrant orchid flowers in shades of white and pink. It is a terrestrial orchid that often grows in rocky or clay-rich soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
