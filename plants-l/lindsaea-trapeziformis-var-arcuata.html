Lindsaea trapeziformis var. arcuata

Lindsaea trapeziformis var. arcuata, commonly known as the curving maidenhair fern, is a variant of the lindsaea genus within the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that give it a graceful, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
