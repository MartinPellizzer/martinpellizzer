Lespedeza macrocarpa

Lespedeza macrocarpa, commonly known as largefruit lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza macrocarpa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
