Luzula nemorosa f. rosea

Luzula nemorosa f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, rose-colored inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
