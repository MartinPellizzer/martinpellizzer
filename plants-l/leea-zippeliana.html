Leea zippeliana

Leea zippeliana, commonly known as the Zippel's Leea, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
