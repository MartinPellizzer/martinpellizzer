Leucas pilosa var. pubescens

Leucas pilosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic properties.
