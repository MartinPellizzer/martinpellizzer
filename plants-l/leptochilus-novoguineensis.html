Leptochilus novoguineensis

Leptochilus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea leek orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, leek-like foliage. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
