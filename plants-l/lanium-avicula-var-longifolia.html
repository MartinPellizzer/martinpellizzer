Lanium avicula var. longifolia

Lanium avicula var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
