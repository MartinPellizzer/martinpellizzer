Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. proteranthus

Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. proteranthus, commonly known as the Guatemalan urara, is a leguminous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and pharmacological studies.
