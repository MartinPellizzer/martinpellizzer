Lathyrus variegatus var. rigidus

Lathyrus variegatus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid climbing larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often climbing or trailing along supports. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with small, ovate leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
