Leucas indica

Leucas indica, commonly known as Indian champa, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, small white or pink flowers. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine. Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal remedies for their purported calming and digestive properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
