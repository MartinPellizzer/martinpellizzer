Lathyrus venosus f. roseus

Lathyrus venosus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered vetch, is a botanical variant of the species Lathyrus venosus.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its slender, wiry stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to rose-purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
