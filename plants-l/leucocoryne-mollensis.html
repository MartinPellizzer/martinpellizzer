Leucocoryne mollensis

Leucocoryne mollensis, commonly known as the silver leaf lily, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features striking white, waxy flowers that bloom in spring, often with a subtle blue or purple tinge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
