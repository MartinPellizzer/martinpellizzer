Linum austriacum subsp. marschallianum

Linum austriacum subsp.

marschallianum, commonly known as the Austrian flax, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their showy blue or white flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
