Lathyrus occidentalis f. lanceolatus

Lathyrus occidentalis f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the western pea or lanceleaf pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal.

The variety f. lanceolatus is distinguished by its lance-shaped leaves, which set it apart from other forms of Lathyrus occidentalis.
