Lyonia rigida

Lyonia rigida, commonly known as the rigid lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in pine forests or along stream banks. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Lyonia rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
