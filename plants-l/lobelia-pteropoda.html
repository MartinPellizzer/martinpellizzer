Lobelia pteropoda

Lobelia pteropoda, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia pteropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
