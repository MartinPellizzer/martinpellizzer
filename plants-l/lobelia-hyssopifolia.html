Lobelia hyssopifolia

Lobelia hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called caryopses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Lobelia hyssopifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
