Lysimachia hirsuta

Lysimachia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Lysimachia hirsuta is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
