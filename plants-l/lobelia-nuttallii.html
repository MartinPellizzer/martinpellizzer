Lobelia nuttallii

Lobelia nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, organic soil. It produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lobelia nuttallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
