Lepanthes gonzalezii

Lepanthes gonzalezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its shaded forest environment.

Lepanthes gonzalezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
