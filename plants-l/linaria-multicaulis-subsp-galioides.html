Linaria multicaulis subsp. galioides

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

galioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with two lips, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
