Lagotis incisifolia

Lagotis incisifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
