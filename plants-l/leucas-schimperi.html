Leucas schimperi

Leucas schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
