Lagenaria

Lagenaria, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a flowering plant native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, hollow fruits that can be used as containers or for culinary purposes. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while the flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

Lagenaria is valued for its versatility and is often grown for both its edible parts and ornamental appeal.
