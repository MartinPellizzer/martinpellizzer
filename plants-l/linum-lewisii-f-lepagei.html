Linum lewisii f. lepagei

Linum lewisii f. lepagei, commonly known as the Lepage's flax, is a subspecies of the flax plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
