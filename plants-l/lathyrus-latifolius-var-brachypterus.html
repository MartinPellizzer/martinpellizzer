Lathyrus latifolius var. brachypterus

Lathyrus latifolius var. brachypterus, commonly known as the dwarf broadleaf sweet pea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It is often used in garden settings to add color and texture to borders or containers.
