Loranthus hollrungii

Loranthus hollrungii, commonly known as the holzii mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It derives some of its nutrients from the host tree through specialized structures called haustoria. This species typically grows on various hardwood trees, such as eucalyptus and acacia, in its native range of Australia. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruits.

The plant plays a role in ecological interactions, providing food for birds and insects while also affecting the growth of its host.
