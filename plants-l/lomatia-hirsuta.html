Lomatia hirsuta

Lomatia hirsuta, commonly known as the mountain devil, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Lomatia hirsuta is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
