Leptochilus × nepalensis

Leptochilus × nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
