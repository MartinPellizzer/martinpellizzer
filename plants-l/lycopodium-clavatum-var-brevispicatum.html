Lycopodium clavatum var. brevispicatum

Lycopodium clavatum var. brevispicatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of its stems. Its morphology includes a short, upright stem with numerous leaf-like structures arranged in whorls.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter spore cones compared to other varieties of Lycopodium clavatum.
