Lilium martagon var. spadiceum

Lilium martagon var. spadiceum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are often pink or red.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and parts of Turkey, thriving in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and a clump-forming habit. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
