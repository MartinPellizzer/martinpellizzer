Lomatium pastorale

Lomatium pastorale, commonly known as mountain lomatium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its fern-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest clearings. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.
