Lepidium lyratum subsp. lacerum

Lepidium lyratum subsp.

lacerum, commonly known as the slender peppergrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
