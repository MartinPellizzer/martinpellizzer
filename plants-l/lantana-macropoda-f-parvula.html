Lantana macropoda f. parvula

Lantana macropoda f. parvula, commonly known as the small-leaved lantana, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically smaller than those of the species. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a slight purple tint. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
