Lupinus caudatus var. argophyllus

Lupinus caudatus var. argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of spiky, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation and soil health.
