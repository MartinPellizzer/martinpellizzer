Leandra fontanae

Leandra fontanae, commonly known as the Fontana's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Leandra fontanae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
