Linaria coridifolia

Linaria coridifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the genus Linaria, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Linaria coridifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
