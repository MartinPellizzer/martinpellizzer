Linum sardoum

Linum sardoum, commonly known as Sardinian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are used for both ornamental and practical purposes. Linum sardoum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was cultivated for its fibers and seeds.
