Ligustrum lucidum var. esquirolii

Ligustrum lucidum var. esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's privet, is a variant of the privet shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
