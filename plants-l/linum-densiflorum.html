Linum densiflorum

Linum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower flax, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Linum densiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in fiber production.
