Lasianthus neolanceolatus

Lasianthus neolanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are often pink or violet, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
