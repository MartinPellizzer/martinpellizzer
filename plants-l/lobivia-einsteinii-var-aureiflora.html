Lobivia einsteinii var. aureiflora

Lobivia einsteinii var. aureiflora, commonly known as the Golden Flowered Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, woolly white hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and aiding in water conservation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
