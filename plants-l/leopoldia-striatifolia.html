Leopoldia striatifolia

Leopoldia striatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed leopoldia, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the high altitudes of southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for conservation and botanical research.
