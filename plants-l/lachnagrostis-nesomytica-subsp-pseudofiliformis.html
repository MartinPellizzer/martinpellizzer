Lachnagrostis nesomytica subsp. pseudofiliformis

Lachnagrostis nesomytica subsp.

pseudofiliformis, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments. Its subspecies status highlights subtle morphological differences from the main species, particularly in leaf structure and growth habits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
