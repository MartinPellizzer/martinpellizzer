Leontodon caspicus

Leontodon caspicus, commonly known as the Caspian dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stem is hairy and erect.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
