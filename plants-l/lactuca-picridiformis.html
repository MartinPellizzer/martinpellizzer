Lactuca picridiformis

Lactuca picridiformis, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed lettuce, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Lactuca picridiformis is not commonly cultivated for food but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
