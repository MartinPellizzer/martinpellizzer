Lantana colombiana

Lantana colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in coastal regions and disturbed soils.

Lantana colombiana is also known for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
