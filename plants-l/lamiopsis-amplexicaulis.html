Lamiopsis amplexicaulis

Lamiopsis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping lamiopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stem.

Lamiopsis amplexicaulis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
