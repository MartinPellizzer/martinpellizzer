Lilium japonicum var. abeanum

Lilium japonicum var. abeanum, commonly known as the Abean Lily, is a rare and ornamental variety of lily native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
