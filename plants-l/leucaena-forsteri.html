Leucaena forsteri

Leucaena forsteri, commonly known as the white leucaena, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a leguminous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often used as forage for livestock. Its small, white flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Leucaena forsteri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural use.
