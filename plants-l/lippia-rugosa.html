Lippia rugosa

Lippia rugosa, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring hairy, aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its strong, mint-like fragrance. Lippia rugosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
