Leucothoe griffithiana

Leucothoe griffithiana, commonly known as Griffith's leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
