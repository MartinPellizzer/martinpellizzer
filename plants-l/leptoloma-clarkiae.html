Leptoloma clarkiae

Leptoloma clarkiae, commonly known as Clark's leathery lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It has a dense, leathery thallus with a dark, almost black coloration. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with algae and cyanobacteria, which contribute to its nutritional needs. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

Its structure provides a unique habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
