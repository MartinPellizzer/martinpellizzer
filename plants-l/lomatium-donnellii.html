Lomatium donnellii

Lomatium donnellii, commonly known as the mountain lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fern-like, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Lomatium donnellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
