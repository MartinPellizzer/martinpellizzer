Lycium arborescens

Lycium arborescens, commonly known as the silver bush or tree nightshade, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. The plant has woody stems covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Lycium arborescens is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
