Lonicera cyanocarpa var. porphyrantha

Lonicera cyanocarpa var. porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
