Lycopersicon pissisi

Lycopersicon pissisi, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits similar to domestic tomatoes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit contains numerous small seeds and is adapted for dispersal by animals.
