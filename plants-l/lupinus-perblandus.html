Lupinus perblandus

Lupinus perblandus, commonly known as the soft lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clusters of blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus perblandus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
