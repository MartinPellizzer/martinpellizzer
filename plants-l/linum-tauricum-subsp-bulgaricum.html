Linum tauricum subsp. bulgaricum

Linum tauricum subsp.

bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in fiber production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
