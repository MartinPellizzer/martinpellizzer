Laportea bulbifera subsp. rugosa

Laportea bulbifera subsp.

rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in sandy or disturbed soils.

The subspecies rugosa is distinguished by its coarsely toothed leaves and rough texture compared to other subspecies of Laportea.
