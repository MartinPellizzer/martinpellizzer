Launaea rogersii

Launaea rogersii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant has a succulent, cushion-like growth form, allowing it to store water and survive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs that reduce water loss.
