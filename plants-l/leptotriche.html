Leptotriche

Leptotriche, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has serrated, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Leptotriche thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
