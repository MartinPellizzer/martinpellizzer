Linaria mathezii

Linaria mathezii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Linaria mathezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
