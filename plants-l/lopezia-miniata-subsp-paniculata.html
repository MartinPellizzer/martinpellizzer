Lopezia miniata subsp. paniculata

Lopezia miniata subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to bloom during the spring and early summer, adding a delicate floral display to desert landscapes.
