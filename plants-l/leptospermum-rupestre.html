Leptospermum rupestre

Leptospermum rupestre, commonly known as the rock heath, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It is a hardy species that is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Leptospermum rupestre is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in native garden landscapes.
