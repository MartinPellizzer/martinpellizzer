Lychnis alpestris

Lychnis alpestris, commonly known as the alpine pink, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
