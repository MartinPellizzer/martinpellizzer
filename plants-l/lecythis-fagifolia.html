Lecythis fagifolia

Lecythis fagifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit with a thick, inedible husk enclosing a hard-shelled seed. The seeds are valued for their high oil content, used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
