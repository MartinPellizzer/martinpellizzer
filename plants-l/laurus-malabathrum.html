Laurus malabathrum

Laurus malabathrum, commonly known as the Indian bay leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, particularly in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisines. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Its botanical features include small, yellowish flowers and dark, glossy berries that contain the seeds.
