Lonicera ferruginea

Lonicera ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lonicera ferruginea thrives in partial to full shade and is commonly found in moist woodland habitats.
