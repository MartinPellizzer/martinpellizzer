Lepanthes glicensteinii

Lepanthes glicensteinii, commonly known as the Glicenstein's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically feature a unique slipper-shaped pouch, a feature common in many orchid species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months, often in shaded forest environments. Lepanthes glicensteinii is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without deriving nutrients from them.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within the diverse orchid family and its adaptation to humid, tropical ecosystems.
