Leersia mauritiaca

Leersia mauritiaca, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive tuft of spikelets at the top.

Leersia mauritiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
