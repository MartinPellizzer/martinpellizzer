Linum spathulatum

Linum spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Linum spathulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
