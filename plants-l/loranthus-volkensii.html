Loranthus volkensii

Loranthus volkensii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and white, fleshy flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts.

Loranthus volkensii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects, and its presence can influence the health of its host trees.
