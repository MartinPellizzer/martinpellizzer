Luziola spiriformis

Luziola spiriformis, commonly known as the spiral leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Luziola spiriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
