Linaria striata var. foliosa

Linaria striata var. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
