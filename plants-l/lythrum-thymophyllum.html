Lythrum thymophyllum

Lythrum thymophyllum, commonly known as rosepink lythrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pink or rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
