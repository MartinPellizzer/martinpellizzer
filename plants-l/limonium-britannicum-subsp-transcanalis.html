Limonium britannicum subsp. transcanalis

Limonium britannicum subsp.

transcanalis, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of western Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and maritime environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in dunes, salt marshes, and other saline habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
