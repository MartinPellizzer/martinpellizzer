Lupinus polyphyllus var. albus

Lupinus polyphyllus var. albus, commonly known as the white lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant has palmate leaves composed of several leaflets, and its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
