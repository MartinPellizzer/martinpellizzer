Lobelia aguana

Lobelia aguana, commonly known as the aquatic lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Lobelia aguana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local aquatic ecosystems.
