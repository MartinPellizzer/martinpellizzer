Lathyrus parodii

Lathyrus parodii, commonly known as the parodii pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus parodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
