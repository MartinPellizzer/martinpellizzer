Lobelia membranacea

Lobelia membranacea, commonly known as the membranous lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lobelia membranacea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
