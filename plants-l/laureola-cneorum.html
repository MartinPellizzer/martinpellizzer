Laureola cneorum

Laureola cneorum, commonly known as the mountain laurel or sweet box, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, glossy dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Laureola cneorum thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and year-round foliage make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and borders.
