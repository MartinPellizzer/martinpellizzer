Lycopodioides excurrens

Lycopodioides excurrens, commonly known as the creeping clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically found at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Lycopodioides excurrens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
