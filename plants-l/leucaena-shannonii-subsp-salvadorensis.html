Leucaena shannonii subsp. salvadorensis

Leucaena shannonii subsp.

salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a valuable species for reforestation and soil stabilization. It also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enhance soil fertility.

Its small, edible pods contain seeds that are used for forage and human consumption in some regions.
