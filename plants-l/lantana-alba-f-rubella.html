Lantana alba f. rubella

Lantana alba f. rubella, commonly known as the red-leafed lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The varietal form 'rubella' is distinguished by its striking red or reddish-purple leaves, which contrast with the pale flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage and ability to thrive in full sun.

This plant is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
