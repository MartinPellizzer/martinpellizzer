Linaria italica subsp. sofiana

Linaria italica subsp.

sofiana, commonly known as the Italian toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
