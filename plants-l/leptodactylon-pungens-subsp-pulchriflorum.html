Leptodactylon pungens subsp. pulchriflorum

Leptodactylon pungens subsp.

pulchriflorum, commonly known as the showy lepidote fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family. It is characterized by its elegant, lanceolate fronds that are often adorned with prominent, glandular dots.

The plant produces small, clustered spore cases that are typically yellowish-green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
