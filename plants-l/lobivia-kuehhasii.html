Lobivia kuehhasii

Lobivia kuehhasii, commonly known as the Kuehne's lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
