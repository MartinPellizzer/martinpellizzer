Laetia corymbulosa

Laetia corymbulosa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Laetia corymbulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
