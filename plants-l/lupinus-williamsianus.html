Lupinus williamsianus

Lupinus williamsianus, commonly known as Williams' lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus williamsianus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility in ecological restoration projects.
