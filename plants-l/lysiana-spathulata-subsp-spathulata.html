Lysiana spathulata subsp. spathulata

Lysiana spathulata subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia. It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a prominent spathe.

The plant produces small, clustered berries that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
