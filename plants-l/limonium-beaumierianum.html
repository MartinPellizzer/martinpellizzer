Limonium beaumierianum

Limonium beaumierianum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium beaumierianum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
