Leptorkis ochracea

Leptorkis ochracea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Leptorkis ochracea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
