Luzula echinata

Luzula echinata, commonly known as the spiny woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale blue to purple flowers during the late spring and early summer. Luzula echinata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
