Lathyrus aureus subsp. occidentalis

Lathyrus aureus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western golden pea, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant features slender, branching stems and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
