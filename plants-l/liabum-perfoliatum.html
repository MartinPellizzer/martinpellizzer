Liabum perfoliatum

Liabum perfoliatum, commonly known as the perfoliate phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite and often have a distinctive perfoliate arrangement, where the leaf stalks pass through the stem, creating a unique appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its delicate beauty and adaptability contribute to its ecological and ornamental value.
