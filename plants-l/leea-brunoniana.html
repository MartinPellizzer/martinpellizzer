Leea brunoniana

Leea brunoniana, commonly known as the broadleaf leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
