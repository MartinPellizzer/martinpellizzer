Lobelia puberula var. laeviuscula

Lobelia puberula var. laeviuscula, commonly known as the smooth lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, short hairs, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
