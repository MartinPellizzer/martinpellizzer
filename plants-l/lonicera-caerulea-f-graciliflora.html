Lonicera caerulea f. graciliflora

Lonicera caerulea f. graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters, typically appearing in early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
