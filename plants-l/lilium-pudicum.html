Lilium pudicum

Lilium pudicum, commonly known as the shy lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats, and prefers cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single stem that supports its showy blooms.

Lilium pudicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
