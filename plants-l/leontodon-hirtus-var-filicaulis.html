Leontodon hirtus var. filicaulis

Leontodon hirtus var. filicaulis, commonly known as the hairy leontodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a compact head, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
