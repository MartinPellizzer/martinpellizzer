Lonicera tatarica grandiflora-rubra

Lonicera tatarica grandiflora-rubra, commonly known as the redflower honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to red, depending on the cultivar. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
