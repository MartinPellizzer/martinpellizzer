Luina californica

Luina californica, commonly known as the California lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Luina californica plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
