Lachnostachys ferruginea var. paniculata

Lachnostachys ferruginea var. paniculata, commonly known as the red panic grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically yellow or brown, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical structure is characteristic of the grass family, with a fibrous root system and hollow stems.
