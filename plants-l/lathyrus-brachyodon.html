Lathyrus brachyodon

Lathyrus brachyodon, commonly known as the dwarf pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are typically oval and slightly flattened. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Lathyrus brachyodon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
