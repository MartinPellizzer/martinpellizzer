Leucosyke winkleri-huberti

Leucosyke winkleri-huberti, commonly known as the Winkler-Hubert lichen, is a species of foliose lichen characterized by its distinct, leaf-like thallus structure.

It typically grows on bark, rocks, or soil in temperate forest environments. The thallus is usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a smooth or slightly wrinkled surface. It consists of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic algae and a fungal partner, which allows it to thrive in moist, shaded conditions.

This lichen is often used as an indicator species for air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
