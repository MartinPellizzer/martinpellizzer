Lespedeza patens var. macrantha

Lespedeza patens var. macrantha, commonly known as the largeflower lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
