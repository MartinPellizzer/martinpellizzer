Lycopodioides dalzellii

Lycopodioides dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its production of tiny, spirally arranged leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a feature common to many primitive vascular plants.

Its historical significance lies in its role as a model organism for studying early plant evolution and adaptation.
