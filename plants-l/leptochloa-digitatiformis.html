Leptochloa digitatiformis

Leptochloa digitatiformis, commonly known as the fingerleaf grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and often pale green.

Leptochloa digitatiformis is a low-growing perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
