Lepanthes palmae

Lepanthes palmae, commonly known as the palm-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or orange. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Lepanthes palmae thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
