Linkia comata

Linkia comata, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has slender, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects and birds.

Linkia comata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
