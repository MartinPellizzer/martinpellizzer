Loasa petrophila

Loasa petrophila, commonly known as the rock loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Loasa petrophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
