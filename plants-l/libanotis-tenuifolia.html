Libanotis tenuifolia

Libanotis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Libanotis tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
