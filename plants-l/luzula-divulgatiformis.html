Luzula divulgatiformis

Luzula divulgatiformis, commonly known as the spreading false bottlebrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Luzula divulgatiformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
