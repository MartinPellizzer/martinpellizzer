Litsea montis-dulit

Litsea montis-dulit, commonly known as the mountain sweetleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
