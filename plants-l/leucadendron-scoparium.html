Leucadendron scoparium

Leucadendron scoparium, commonly known as the silver leaf leucadendron, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces cone-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Leucadendron scoparium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden landscapes.
