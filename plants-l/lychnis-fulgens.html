Lychnis fulgens

Lychnis fulgens, commonly known as the fireweed or blazing campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
