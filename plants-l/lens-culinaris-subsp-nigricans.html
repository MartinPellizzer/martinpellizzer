Lens culinaris subsp. nigricans

Lens culinaris subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as black lentil, is a subspecies of the lentil plant characterized by its dark-colored seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain the edible seeds, which are rich in protein and dietary fiber. The dark pigmentation of the seeds is due to high levels of anthocyanins, contributing to its nutritional value and visual appeal.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional content and used in various cuisines around the world.
