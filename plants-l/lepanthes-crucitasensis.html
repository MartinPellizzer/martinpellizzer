Lepanthes crucitasensis

Lepanthes crucitasensis, commonly known as the Crucitas Nightshade Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom at night. The plant has a short rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the shaded understory of its natural habitat.

Lepanthes crucitasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
