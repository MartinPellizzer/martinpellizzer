Lathyrus × hammettii

Lathyrus × hammettii, commonly known as the hybrid sweet pea, is a cross between Lathyrus odoratus and Lathyrus sanguineus.

It is a climbing plant with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, and it produces vibrant, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is native to the Mediterranean region. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial to pollinators.
