Lophatherum dubium

Lophatherum dubium, commonly known as the Chinese silver grass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Lophatherum dubium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
