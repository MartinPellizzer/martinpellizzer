Lupinus rostratus

Lupinus rostratus, commonly known as the rostrate lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus rostratus is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it valuable for soil enrichment.
