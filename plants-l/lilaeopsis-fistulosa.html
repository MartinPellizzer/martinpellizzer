Lilaeopsis fistulosa

Lilaeopsis fistulosa, commonly known as the tube grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a small, perennial grass that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of grasses.

Lilaeopsis fistulosa is often found in wetlands and is used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
