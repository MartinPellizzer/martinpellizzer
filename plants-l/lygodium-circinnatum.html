Lygodium circinnatum

Lygodium circinnatum, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Lygodiaceae.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and a unique ability to climb using its aerial rhizomes. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in circular or spiral patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its distinctive morphology and reproductive structures make it a notable species in botanical studies.
