Leucas mogadoxensis

Leucas mogadoxensis, commonly known as the African bluebells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas mogadoxensis is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
