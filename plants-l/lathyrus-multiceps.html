Lathyrus multiceps

Lathyrus multiceps, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to white, followed by lens-shaped pods containing several seeds. Lathyrus multiceps is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
