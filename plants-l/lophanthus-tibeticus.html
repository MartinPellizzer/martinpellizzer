Lophanthus tibeticus

Lophanthus tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, often forming clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping it withstand the cold and arid conditions of its habitat. Lophanthus tibeticus is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and collections worldwide.
