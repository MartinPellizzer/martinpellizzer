Lippia calliclada

Lippia calliclada, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slight aromatic scent.

Lippia calliclada is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
