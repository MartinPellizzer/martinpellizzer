Lachemilla venusta

Lachemilla venusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with bright green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange-red flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lachemilla venusta thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal flowering display.
