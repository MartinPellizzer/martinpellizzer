Lithocardium leucocephalum

Lithocardium leucocephalum, commonly known as the white-headed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lithocardium leucocephalum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
