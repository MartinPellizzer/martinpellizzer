Lucuma glomerata

Lucuma glomerata, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients such as vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and ovate, while its flowers are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit's unique flavor and nutritional profile make it a valuable crop in both traditional and modern culinary applications.
