Lycopsis microphylla

Lycopsis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved lycopsis, is a member of the Lycopus genus in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually blue to purple in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
