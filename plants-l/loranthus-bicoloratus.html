Loranthus bicoloratus

Loranthus bicoloratus, commonly known as the two-colored mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Asia and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
