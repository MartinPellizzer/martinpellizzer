Lonicera adenophora

Lonicera adenophora, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. It produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

Lonicera adenophora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
