Lycopersicon cerasiforme var. cognitum

Lycopersicon cerasiforme var. cognitum, commonly known as the cherry tomato plant, is a variant of the species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often red or yellow fruits, which resemble cherries in size and appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a flavorful addition to culinary dishes.
