Linum alpinum var. genuinum

Linum alpinum var. genuinum, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Linum alpinum var. genuinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
