Libanotis montana subsp. athamanthoides

Libanotis montana subsp.

athamanthoides, commonly known as the Cyprus bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
