Lantana glandulosissima f. flava

Lantana glandulosissima f. flava, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include glandular leaves and a compact growth habit, which contribute to its adaptability in various environments.
