Leperiza urceolata f. fulva

Leperiza urceolata f. fulva, commonly known as the yellow cup-plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its hollow, cylindrical stems are often reddish in color, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
