Ludovia integrifolia

Ludovia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed lily, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and unlobed. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ludovia integrifolia prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in moderate climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
