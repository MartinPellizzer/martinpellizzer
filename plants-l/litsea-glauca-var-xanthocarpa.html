Litsea glauca var. xanthocarpa

Litsea glauca var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited litsea, is a variant of the litsea genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive yellow to orange drupes. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
