Liparis boothii

Liparis boothii, commonly known as the Booth's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features a slender, wiry rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Liparis boothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
