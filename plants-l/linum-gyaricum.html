Linum gyaricum

Linum gyaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian flax, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are often arranged in racemes.

Linum gyaricum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.
