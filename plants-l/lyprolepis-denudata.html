Lyprolepis denudata

Lyprolepis denudata, commonly known as the naked leaf spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often reduced or nearly absent, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
