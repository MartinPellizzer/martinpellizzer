Linaria verticillata subsp. verticillata

Linaria verticillata subsp.

verticillata, commonly known as the clustered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
