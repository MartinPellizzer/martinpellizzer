Lonicera mochidzukiana var. filiformis

Lonicera mochidzukiana var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features slender, wiry stems that can grow up to several meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
