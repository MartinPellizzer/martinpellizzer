Lobelia subscaposa

Lobelia subscaposa, commonly known as the scapose lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, blue to purple flowers are arranged in terminal clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lobelia subscaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
