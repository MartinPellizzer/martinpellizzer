Lilium leichtlinii subsp. maximowiczii

Lilium leichtlinii subsp.

maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive white throat.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem that bears the floral display. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
