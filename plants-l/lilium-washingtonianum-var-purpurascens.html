Lilium washingtonianum var. purpurascens

Lilium washingtonianum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-tinged fritillary, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

This variety is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a vibrant mix of white and purple hues, with a distinctive red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, forming a basal rosette, while its stem is sturdy and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and fragrance.
