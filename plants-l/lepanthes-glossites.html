Lepanthes glossites

Lepanthes glossites, commonly known as the glossy lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a contrasting lip, which is a defining feature of many orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
