Lasemia

Lasemia, commonly known as the "blueberry bush," is a genus of flowering plants in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to various regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plants are shrubs that produce small, edible berries with a sweet and tangy flavor. Lasemia species are valued for their ornamental value due to their attractive foliage and vibrant flowers.

They thrive in acidic soils and require partial shade for optimal growth.
