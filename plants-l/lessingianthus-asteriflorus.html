Lessingianthus asteriflorus

Lessingianthus asteriflorus, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lessingianthus asteriflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
