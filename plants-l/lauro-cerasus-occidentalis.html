Lauro-cerasus occidentalis

Lauro-cerasus occidentalis, commonly known as the western cherry laurel, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.
