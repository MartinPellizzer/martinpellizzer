Lepidotis ciliata

Lepidotis ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Lepidotis ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
