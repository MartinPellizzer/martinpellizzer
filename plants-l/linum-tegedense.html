Linum tegedense

Linum tegedense, commonly known as the Alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax plants. This species is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Linum tegedense is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
