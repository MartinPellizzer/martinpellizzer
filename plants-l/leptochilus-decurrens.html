Leptochilus decurrens

Leptochilus decurrens, commonly known as the creeping saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a mat-forming habit with small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
