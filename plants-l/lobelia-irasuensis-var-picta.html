Lobelia irasuensis var. picta

Lobelia irasuensis var. picta, commonly known as the painted lobelia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to blue and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
