Lathyrus pannonicus var. rhodopeus

Lathyrus pannonicus var. rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region.

It features slender, climbing stems that produce small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting species for both ecological and horticultural study.
