Lycopodioides plumea

Lycopodioides plumea, commonly known as the plume fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive plume-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Lycopodioides plumea plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
