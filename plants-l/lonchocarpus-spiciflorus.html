Lonchocarpus spiciflorus

Lonchocarpus spiciflorus, commonly known as the spicate legume, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its seeds are encased in distinctive, spiny pods that mature to a reddish-brown color.

While not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
