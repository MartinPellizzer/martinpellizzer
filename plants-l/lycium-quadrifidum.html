Lycium quadrifidum

Lycium quadrifidum, commonly known as the four-lobed boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. The plant has a dense, thorny structure that provides habitat for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
