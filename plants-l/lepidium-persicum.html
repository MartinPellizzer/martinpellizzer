Lepidium persicum

Lepidium persicum, commonly known as Persian cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.

Lepidium persicum is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and mild taste.
