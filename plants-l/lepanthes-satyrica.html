Lepanthes satyrica

Lepanthes satyrica, commonly known as the satyr orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and purple.

Lepanthes satyrica is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
