Leucas vestita var. devicolamensis

Leucas vestita var. devicolamensis, commonly known as the Himalayan bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, providing insulation against cold temperatures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
