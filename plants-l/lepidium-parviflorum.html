Lepidium parviflorum

Lepidium parviflorum, commonly known as rock cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and has small, white or yellowish flowers. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately divided, and it produces small, round seeds.

Lepidium parviflorum is often found in alpine or arctic environments, where it thrives in cold, dry conditions.
