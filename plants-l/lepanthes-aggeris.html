Lepanthes aggeris

Lepanthes aggeris, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive silver or greenish tinge on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native environment.

Lepanthes aggeris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
