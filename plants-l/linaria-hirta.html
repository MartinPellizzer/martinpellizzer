Linaria hirta

Linaria hirta, commonly known as hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with two lips, and are usually yellow or orange in color.

Linaria hirta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
