Linum bienne var. papillosum

Linum bienne var. papillosum, commonly known as the showy flax or papillate flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers with showy, papillate (bumpy) petals, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
