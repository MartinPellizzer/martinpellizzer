Lagenaria siceraria f. depressa

Lagenaria siceraria f. depressa, commonly known as the bottle gourd or calabash, is a variant of the gourd family native to Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, bottle-shaped fruit that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is a fast-growing annual vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit develops from the flower and can be harvested at various stages for culinary or ornamental use.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
