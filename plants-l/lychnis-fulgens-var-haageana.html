Lychnis fulgens var. haageana

Lychnis fulgens var. haageana, commonly known as the fireweed or scarlet pimpernel, is a strikingly colorful plant with showy, bright red flowers that bloom in summer.

It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, terminal sprays.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
