Lobelia obtusifolia

Lobelia obtusifolia, commonly known as the bluntleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rounded, blunt appearance, giving it its common name.

Lobelia obtusifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
