Limonia aurantiifolia

Limonia aurantiifolia, commonly known as the Chinese tangerine or the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and have a thick, leathery rind. While not widely cultivated for fruit, it is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic foliage.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
