Lonchocarpus neurophyllus

Lonchocarpus neurophyllus, commonly known as the Brazilian strychnos, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It is also known for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for their pharmacological potential.

Despite its medicinal uses, it requires careful handling due to its potentially harmful properties.
