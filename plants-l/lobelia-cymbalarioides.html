Lobelia cymbalarioides

Lobelia cymbalarioides, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features showy, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia cymbalarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
