Lepanthes saltator

Lepanthes saltator, commonly known as the jumping orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on tree trunks or branches. Its unique name refers to the way its flowers appear to "jump" or move when disturbed.

Lepanthes saltator is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
