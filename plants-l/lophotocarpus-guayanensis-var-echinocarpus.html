Lophotocarpus guayanensis var. echinocarpus

Lophotocarpus guayanensis var. echinocarpus, commonly known as the spiny fruit senna, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by animals. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, contributing to its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
