Ledum palustre f. parvifolium

Ledum palustre f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved Labrador tea, is a variant of the Labrador tea plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds, including aromatic oils and tannins, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is adapted to grow in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and heathlands.
