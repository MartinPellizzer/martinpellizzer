Lagerstroemia engleriana

Lagerstroemia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, peeling bark that becomes attractive with age, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
