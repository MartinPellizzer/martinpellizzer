Lindernia pusilla

Lindernia pusilla, commonly known as the small lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few inches, featuring small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Lindernia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
