Lavandula officinalis f. alba

Lavandula officinalis f. alba, commonly known as white lavender, is a cultivar of the common lavender plant.

It is characterized by its pale lavender to white flower spikes, which contrast with its gray-green foliage. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.

The plant is also used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine due to its essential oils.
