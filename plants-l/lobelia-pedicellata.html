Lobelia pedicellata

Lobelia pedicellata, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
