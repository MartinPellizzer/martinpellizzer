Lagenosocereus

Lagenosocereus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and colorful, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it drought-tolerant.

Lagenosocereus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
