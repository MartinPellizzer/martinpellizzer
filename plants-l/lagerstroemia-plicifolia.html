Lagerstroemia plicifolia

Lagerstroemia plicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes flaky with age, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are oval and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall, enhancing its seasonal appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
