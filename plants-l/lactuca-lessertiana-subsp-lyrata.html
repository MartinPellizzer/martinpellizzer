Lactuca lessertiana subsp. lyrata

Lactuca lessertiana subsp.

lyrata, commonly known as the lyrate lettuce, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique leaf structure, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
