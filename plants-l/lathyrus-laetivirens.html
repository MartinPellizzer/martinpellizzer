Lathyrus laetivirens

Lathyrus laetivirens, commonly known as the cheerful green larkspur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, often with a distinctive spurred petal. It typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil.

Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.
