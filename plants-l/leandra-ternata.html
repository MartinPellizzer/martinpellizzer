Leandra ternata

Leandra ternata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its unique leaf structure helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Leandra ternata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
