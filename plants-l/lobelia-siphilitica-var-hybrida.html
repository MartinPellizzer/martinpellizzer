Lobelia siphilitica var. hybrida

Lobelia siphilitica var. hybrida, commonly known as the Great Blue Lobelia, is a striking perennial wildflower native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cobalt-blue tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
