Limonium × valentinum

Limonium × valentinum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spike-like flower heads composed of numerous blue or purple flowers, often surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium × valentinum is also popular in garden settings for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
