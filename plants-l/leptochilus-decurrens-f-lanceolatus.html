Leptochilus decurrens f. lanceolatus

Leptochilus decurrens f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its foliage is widely used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and carminative properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
