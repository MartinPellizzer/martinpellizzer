Lithospermum caroliniense var. croceum

Lithospermum caroliniense var. croceum, commonly known as the Carolina purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
