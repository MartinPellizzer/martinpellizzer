Lavandula pedunculata var. maderensis

Lavandula pedunculata var. maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan lavender, is a variety of lavender native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, purple-blue flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
