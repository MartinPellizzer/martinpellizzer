Lobivia cinnabarina var. grandiflora

Lobivia cinnabarina var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Grandiflora Lobivia, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a thick, ribbed stem covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
