Launaea angustifolia subsp. arabica

Launaea angustifolia subsp.

arabica, commonly known as desert gum plant, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
