Lythrum thymifolia var. majus

Lythrum thymifolia var. majus, commonly known as the greater redroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of pink to purplish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its roots are thick and fleshy, often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
