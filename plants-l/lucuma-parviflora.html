Lucuma parviflora

Lucuma parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lucuma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its fruit is a hard, spiny capsule that contains edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Lucuma parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Andean cultures.
