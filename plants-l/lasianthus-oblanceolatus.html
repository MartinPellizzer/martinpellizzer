Lasianthus oblanceolatus

Lasianthus oblanceolatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
