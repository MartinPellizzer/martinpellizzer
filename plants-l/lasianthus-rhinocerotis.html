Lasianthus rhinocerotis

Lasianthus rhinocerotis, commonly known as the rhinoceros plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that have a glossy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, requiring regular watering during the growing season.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant in warm climates.
