Leptoderris congolensis

Leptoderris congolensis, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and is considered a valuable resource in traditional medicine and woodworking.
