Lophiolepis steirolepis

Lophiolepis steirolepis, commonly known as the mountain onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbellate inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lophiolepis steirolepis is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
