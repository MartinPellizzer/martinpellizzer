Leontopodium artemisiifolium

Leontopodium artemisiifolium, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white flowers surrounded by a ring of pale green bracts. The plant thrives in harsh, rocky environments with low temperatures and high UV exposure. Its leaves are narrow and silver-white, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and association with the Alps, it is a symbol of resilience and is protected in many regions.
