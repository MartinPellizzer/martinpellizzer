Lobivia haematantha f. sublimiflora

Lobivia haematantha f. sublimiflora, commonly known as the Snowy Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, tall, spindly flower stems topped with clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and arid landscapes due to its drought tolerance and minimal care requirements.

Its unique appearance and floral display make it a prized addition to succulent collections.
