Leucospermum crinitum

Leucospermum crinitum, commonly known as the firewheel plant, is a striking South African shrub belonging to the family Proteaceae.

It is characterized by its bright orange or red, fluffy flower heads that resemble flames, which are composed of numerous small, petal-like structures called carpels. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure. Its unique appearance and vibrant color make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
