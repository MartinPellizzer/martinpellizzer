Lyperanthus glandulosus var. compacta

Lyperanthus glandulosus var. compacta, commonly known as the compact bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
