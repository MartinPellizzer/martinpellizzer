Lobelia archeri

Lobelia archeri, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Lobelia archeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for native pollinators.
