Lespedeza floribunda var. alopecuroides

Lespedeza floribunda var. alopecuroides, commonly known as the silky lespeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.
