Leptonychia adolfi-friederici

Leptonychia adolfi-friederici, commonly known as the Adolf-Friederici's rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
