Lenticula ramosa

Lenticula ramosa, commonly known as the branched lenticular plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive lenticels, which are small, corky pores that facilitate gas exchange.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in forest understories. The plant has a sprawling, bushy growth habit with multiple branching stems that can reach several feet in length. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring.

Lenticula ramosa is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and maintaining soil stability.
