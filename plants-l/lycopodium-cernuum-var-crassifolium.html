Lycopodium cernuum var. crassifolium

Lycopodium cernuum var. crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices.
