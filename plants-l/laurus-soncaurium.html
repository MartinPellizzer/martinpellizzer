Laurus soncaurium

Laurus soncaurium, commonly known as the Turkish laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Laurus soncaurium is valued for its aromatic properties and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
