Linociera foveolata

Linociera foveolata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Linociera foveolata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
