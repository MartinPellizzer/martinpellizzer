Lysimachia nemorum var. moupinensis

Lysimachia nemorum var. moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin yellow-flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
