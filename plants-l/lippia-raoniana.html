Lippia raoniana

Lippia raoniana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

Lippia raoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential aromatic properties.
