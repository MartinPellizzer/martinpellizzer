Liparis × jonesii

Liparis × jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while the inflorescence bears clusters of small, showy blooms.

Liparis × jonesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
