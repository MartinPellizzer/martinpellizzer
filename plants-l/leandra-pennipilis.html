Leandra pennipilis

Leandra pennipilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often have a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a sturdy structure for the plant.

Leandra pennipilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
