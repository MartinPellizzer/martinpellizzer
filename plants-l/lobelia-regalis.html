Lobelia regalis

Lobelia regalis, commonly known as the royal lobelia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps, where it thrives in well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lobelia regalis is valued in gardens for its beauty and is also important in ecological systems as a food source for pollinators.
