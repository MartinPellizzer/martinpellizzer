Laggera heteromalla

Laggera heteromalla, commonly known as the African blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads, often displaying a blue or purple coloration.

Laggera heteromalla is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
