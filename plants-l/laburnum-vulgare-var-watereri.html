Laburnum vulgare var. watereri

Laburnum vulgare var. watereri, commonly known as the Waterer's laburnum, is a variant of the golden chain tree.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
