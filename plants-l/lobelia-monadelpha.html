Lobelia monadelpha

Lobelia monadelpha, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are square and hairy.

Lobelia monadelpha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
