Lippia scorodonioides var. hypoleuca

Lippia scorodonioides var. hypoleuca, commonly known as white lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The white variety is distinguished by its pale, often white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage.
