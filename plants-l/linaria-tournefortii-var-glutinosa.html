Linaria tournefortii var. glutinosa

Linaria tournefortii var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a sticky, glandular coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential in some regions, it is often cultivated with caution.
