Lippia aristata var. glabrescens

Lippia aristata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in culinary applications.
