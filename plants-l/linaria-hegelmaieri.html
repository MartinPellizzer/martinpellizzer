Linaria hegelmaieri

Linaria hegelmaieri, commonly known as the Hegelmaier's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria hegelmaieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
