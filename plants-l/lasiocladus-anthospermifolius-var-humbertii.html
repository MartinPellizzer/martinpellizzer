Lasiocladus anthospermifolius var. humbertii

Lasiocladus anthospermifolius var. humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a cushion-like growth form with dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The leaves are typically gray-green with a soft, felt-like texture, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. It is adapted to sandy soils and can survive in extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
