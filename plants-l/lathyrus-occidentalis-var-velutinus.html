Lathyrus occidentalis var. velutinus

Lathyrus occidentalis var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
