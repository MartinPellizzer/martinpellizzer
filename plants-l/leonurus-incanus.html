Leonurus incanus

Leonurus incanus, commonly known as the hoary leonurus, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a grayish appearance.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
