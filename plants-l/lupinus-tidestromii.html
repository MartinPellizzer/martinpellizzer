Lupinus tidestromii

Lupinus tidestromii, commonly known as Tidestrom's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal grasslands and dunes, thriving in sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, elongated seed pods.

Lupinus tidestromii is an important species for pollinators and is often found in maritime climates.
