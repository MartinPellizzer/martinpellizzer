Leucanthemopsis pallida

Leucanthemopsis pallida, commonly known as the pale leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy white or pale yellow flowers that are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
