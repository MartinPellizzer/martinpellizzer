Leucas calostachys var. fasciculata

Leucas calostachys var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered leucas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
