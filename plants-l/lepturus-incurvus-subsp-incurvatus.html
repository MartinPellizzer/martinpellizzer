Lepturus incurvus subsp. incurvatus

Lepturus incurvus subsp.

incurvatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, fens, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curved inward, giving it its scientific name. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, three-lobed flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
