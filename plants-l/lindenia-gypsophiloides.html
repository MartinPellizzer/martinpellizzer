Lindenia gypsophiloides

Lindenia gypsophiloides, commonly known as the rock linden, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and drought-resistant leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy landscapes.

Lindenia gypsophiloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid areas.
