Lupinus heptaphyllus var. pseudolinearis

Lupinus heptaphyllus var. pseudolinearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
