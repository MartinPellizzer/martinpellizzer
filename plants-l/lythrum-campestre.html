Lythrum campestre

Lythrum campestre, commonly known as marsh rosemary, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical with five petals.

Lythrum campestre is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
