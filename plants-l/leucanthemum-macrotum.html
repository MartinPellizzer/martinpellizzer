Leucanthemum macrotum

Leucanthemum macrotum, commonly known as the giant marguerite, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white ray florets surrounded by a cluster of yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Leucanthemum macrotum is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
