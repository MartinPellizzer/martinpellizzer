Leptactina densiflora var. glabra

Leptactina densiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved leptactina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has smooth, hairless leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
