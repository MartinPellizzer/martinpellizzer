Lepigonum pallidum

Lepigonum pallidum, commonly known as the pale lepigonum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lepigonum pallidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
