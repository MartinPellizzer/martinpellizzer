Liparis treubii

Liparis treubii, commonly known as the Treub's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a faintly fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis treubii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
