Leontodon autumnalis var. simplex

Leontodon autumnalis var. simplex, commonly known as the simple autumn leontodon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variety is distinguished by its simpler inflorescence compared to other varieties within the species.
