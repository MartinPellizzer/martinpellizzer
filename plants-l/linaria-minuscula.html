Linaria minuscula

Linaria minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf toadflax, is a small annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and produces delicate, yellow-orange flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Linaria minuscula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and easy cultivation.
