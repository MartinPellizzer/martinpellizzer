Leptospermum glaucescens

Leptospermum glaucescens, commonly known as the silver tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
