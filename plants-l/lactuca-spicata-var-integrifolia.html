Lactuca spicata var. integrifolia

Lactuca spicata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the common chicory, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bluish-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in various regions around the world.
