Lepanthes medinae

Lepanthes medinae, commonly known as the Medina's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Lepanthes medinae is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
