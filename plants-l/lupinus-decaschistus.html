Lupinus decaschistus

Lupinus decaschistus, commonly known as the nine-leaf lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, compound leaves with 9 to 13 leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Lupinus decaschistus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
