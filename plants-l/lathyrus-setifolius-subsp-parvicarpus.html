Lathyrus setifolius subsp. parvicarpus

Lathyrus setifolius subsp.

parvicarpus, commonly known as the slender leaf pea, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features slender, erect stems with small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in racemes, and are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
