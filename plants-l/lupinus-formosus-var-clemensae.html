Lupinus formosus var. clemensae

Lupinus formosus var. clemensae, commonly known as the Clemens' lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
