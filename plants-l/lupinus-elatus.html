Lupinus elatus

Lupinus elatus, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus elatus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, open habitats.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
