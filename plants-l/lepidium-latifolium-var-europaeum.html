Lepidium latifolium var. europaeum

Lepidium latifolium var. europaeum, commonly known as European peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a peppery scent when crushed.
