Loxania loniceroides

Loxania loniceroides, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense ground cover. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Loxania loniceroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
