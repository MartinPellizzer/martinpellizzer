Leleba beisitiku

Leleba beisitiku, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composite, consisting of numerous small florets.

Leleba beisitiku is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
