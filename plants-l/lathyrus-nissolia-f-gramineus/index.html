Lathyrus Nissolia F. Gramineus

Grass-leaved pea, scientifically known as Lathyrus nissolia f. gramineus, is a unique and intriguing plant species belonging to the legume family. This variant of the Lathyrus nissolia species is distinguished by its foliage, which resembles grass in appearance. Native to specific regions in Asia, it thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas. Its delicate flowers, typically ranging from pale blue to violet, add a subtle beauty to its natural habitat. Despite its aesthetic appeal, the plant is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to soil enrichment.
