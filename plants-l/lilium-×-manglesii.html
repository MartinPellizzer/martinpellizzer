Lilium × manglesii

Lilium × manglesii, commonly known as the Mangles' lily, is a hybrid species within the genus Lilium, native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring strap-like, grassy leaves arranged in a basal rosette. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can survive harsh winters, allowing the plant to thrive in temperate climates.

Lilium × manglesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
