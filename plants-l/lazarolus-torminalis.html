Lazarolus torminalis

Lazarolus torminalis, commonly known as the torminalis larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its roots are fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical collections.
