Lemaireocereus weberi

Lemaireocereus weberi, commonly known as the Weber's night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces large, white, fragrant flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring spines for protection and water storage in its thick stems. It blooms during the warmer months, typically from spring to summer, and is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.

Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a unique and fascinating plant to observe.
