Ludwigia suffruticosa

Ludwigia suffruticosa, commonly known as the swamp Ludwigia, is a perennial shrub native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in swampy areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often reddish or purple in color, adding visual interest to its habitat. Its small, clustered flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and flies.

Ludwigia suffruticosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
