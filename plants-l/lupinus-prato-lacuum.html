Lupinus prato-lacuum

Lupinus prato-lacuum, commonly known as the meadow lupine, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Lupinus prato-lacuum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
