Lolium linicola var. complanatum

Lolium linicola var. complanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaf ryegrass, is a variant of the annual ryegrass species.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and a compact growth habit. This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and is often used in forage production. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The leaves are often used in livestock feed due to their high nutritional value.
