Laseguea acutifolia f. obliquinervia

Laseguea acutifolia f. obliquinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined acuteleaf laseguea, is a rare fern species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically thin and have a distinct, narrow venation pattern that sets it apart from other varieties. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. The plant has a creeping rhizome system, allowing it to spread slowly across the forest floor.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
