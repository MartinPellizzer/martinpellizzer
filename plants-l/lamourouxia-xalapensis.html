Lamourouxia xalapensis

Lamourouxia xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for covering trellises or fences.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name.
