Lobelia gibbosa

Lobelia gibbosa, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to violet flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Lobelia gibbosa is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the flora in its native habitats.
