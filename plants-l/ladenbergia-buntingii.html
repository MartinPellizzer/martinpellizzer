Ladenbergia buntingii

Ladenbergia buntingii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical and subtropical climates.
