Lobelia flaccida subsp. mossiana

Lobelia flaccida subsp.

mossiana, commonly known as the mossy lobelia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
