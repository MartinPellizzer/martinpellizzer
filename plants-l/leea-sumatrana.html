Leea sumatrana

Leea sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran Leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
