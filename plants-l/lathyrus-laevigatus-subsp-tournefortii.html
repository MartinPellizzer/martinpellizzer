Lathyrus laevigatus subsp. tournefortii

Lathyrus laevigatus subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Turk's cap larkspur, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
