Lobelia saliensis

Lobelia saliensis, commonly known as the climbing lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia saliensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
