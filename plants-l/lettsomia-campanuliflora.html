Lettsomia campanuliflora

Lettsomia campanuliflora, commonly known as the bellflower lettsomia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist soils, preferring partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Lettsomia campanuliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
