Leptospermum stellatum

Leptospermum stellatum, commonly known as the starflower tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, star-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Leptospermum stellatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
