Lonchocarpus campestris

Lonchocarpus campestris, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also valued for its role in soil enrichment.
