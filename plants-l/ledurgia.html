Ledurgia

Ledurgia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
