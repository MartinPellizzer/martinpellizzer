Lagotis glauca subsp. australis

Lagotis glauca subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
