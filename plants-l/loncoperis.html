Loncoperis

Loncoperis, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Loncoperis is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
