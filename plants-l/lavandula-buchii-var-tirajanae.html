Lavandula buchii var. tirajanae

Lavandula buchii var. tirajanae, commonly known as the Canary Island lavender, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Its essential oils are also utilized in aromatherapy and perfumery.
