Leucas affinis

Leucas affinis, commonly known as the white leucas, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
