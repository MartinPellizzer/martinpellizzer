Lophopappus foliosus

Lophopappus foliosus, commonly known as the leafy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures.

Lophopappus foliosus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
