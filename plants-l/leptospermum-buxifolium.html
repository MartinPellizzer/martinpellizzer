Leptospermum buxifolium

Leptospermum buxifolium, commonly known as the waxleaf tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and heathlands.

Leptospermum buxifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
