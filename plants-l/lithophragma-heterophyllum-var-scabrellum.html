Lithophragma heterophyllum var. scabrellum

Lithophragma heterophyllum var. scabrellum, commonly known as the roughleaf twinflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
