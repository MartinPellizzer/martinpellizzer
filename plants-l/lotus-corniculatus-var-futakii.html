Lotus corniculatus var. futakii

Lotus corniculatus var. futakii, commonly known as the Japanese lotus or Japanese cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
