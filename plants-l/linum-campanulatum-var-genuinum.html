Linum campanulatum var. genuinum

Linum campanulatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the true bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its classification within the genus Linum, which includes other species like flax.
