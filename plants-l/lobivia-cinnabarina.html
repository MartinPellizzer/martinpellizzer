Lobivia cinnabarina

Lobivia cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-pink lobivia, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, compact cactus with a short stem and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stem, adapting to arid environments.

Lobivia cinnabarina is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a potted plant.
