Ledum glandulosum subsp. olivaceum

Ledum glandulosum subsp.

olivaceum, commonly known as the olive-leaved labrador tea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish or olive hue.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in boreal and subarctic environments.

This species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
