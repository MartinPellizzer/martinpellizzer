Ludwigia lutea

Ludwigia lutea, commonly known as the yellow Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant in the Onagraceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing both male and female reproductive structures.

Ludwigia lutea is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant color and adaptability to various water conditions.
