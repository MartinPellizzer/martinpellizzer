Licania hypoleuca var. foveolata

Licania hypoleuca var. foveolata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with a smooth, gray trunk and large, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the edible, star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fruit has a sweet, tangy flavor and is commonly consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
