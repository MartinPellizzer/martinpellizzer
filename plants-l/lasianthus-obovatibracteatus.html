Lasianthus obovatibracteatus

Lasianthus obovatibracteatus, commonly known as the broad-bract lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
