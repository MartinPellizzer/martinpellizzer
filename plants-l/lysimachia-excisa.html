Lysimachia excisa

Lysimachia excisa, commonly known as the cut-leaf chickweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers throughout the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia excisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
