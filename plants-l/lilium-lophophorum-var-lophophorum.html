Lilium lophophorum var. lophophorum

Lilium lophophorum var. lophophorum, commonly known as the Himalayan fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with red or purple markings. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

The bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, allowing the plant to bloom in late spring or early summer.
