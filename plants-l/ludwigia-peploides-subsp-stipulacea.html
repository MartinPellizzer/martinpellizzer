Ludwigia peploides subsp. stipulacea

Ludwigia peploides subsp.

stipulacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Ludwigia, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves at the water surface. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects.

This species is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
