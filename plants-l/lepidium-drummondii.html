Lepidium drummondii

Lepidium drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's cress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along stream banks. It produces small white or yellow flowers and has a distinctive, aromatic seed pod.

Lepidium drummondii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
