Leucophyta brownii

Leucophyta brownii, commonly known as the Brown's ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent fronds that give it its common name, and it typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores in specialized structures called sporangia located on the undersides of its leaves. Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role within its native habitat and its potential value in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
