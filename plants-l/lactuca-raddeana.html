Lactuca raddeana

Lactuca raddeana, commonly known as Russian endive, is a species of lettuce native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that are often used in salads and cooked dishes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 50 to 100 centimeters and produces small, yellow flowers in the summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features include a rosette of leaves and a taproot system, which contributes to its hardiness and adaptability.
