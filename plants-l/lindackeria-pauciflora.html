Lindackeria pauciflora

Lindackeria pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered lindackeria, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a perennial herb native to South Africa, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Lindackeria pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
