Lupinus pumicola

Lupinus pumicola, commonly known as the pumice lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus pumicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
