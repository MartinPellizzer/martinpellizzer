Lupinus aureonitens

Lupinus aureonitens, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Lupinus aureonitens is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
