Lobivia pusilla f. flaviflora

Lobivia pusilla f. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features a dense, spiny stem that can reach up to 10 centimeters in height, often adorned with a profusion of small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its small size. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and are known for their vibrant color and delicate appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
