Leptochloa capillacea

Leptochloa capillacea, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a species of grass native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky areas, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

Leptochloa capillacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
