Lampranthus subrotundus

Lampranthus subrotundus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has fleshy, greenish-gray leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardens.
