Leucas masaiensis var. venulosa

Leucas masaiensis var. venulosa, commonly known as the Masai leucas, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

It is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
