Lespedeza juncea var. sericea

Lespedeza juncea var. sericea, commonly known as silky lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fluffy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for pollinators.
