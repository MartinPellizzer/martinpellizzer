Lepanthes barbatula

Lepanthes barbatula, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that support its growth in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Lepanthes barbatula is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
