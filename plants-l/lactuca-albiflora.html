Lactuca albiflora

Lactuca albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its pale green, leafy foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Lactuca albiflora is noted for its mild, slightly bitter taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
