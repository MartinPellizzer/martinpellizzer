Litsea obtusa

Litsea obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Litsea obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
