Leobordea diffusa

Leobordea diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which typically bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Leobordea diffusa is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
