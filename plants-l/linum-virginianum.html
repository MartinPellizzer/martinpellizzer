Linum virginianum

Linum virginianum, commonly known as wild flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Linum virginianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
