Linaria telephioides

Linaria telephioides, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant features opposite leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria telephioides thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert and coastal habitats.
