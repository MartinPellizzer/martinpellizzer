Leucanthemum × marchii

Leucanthemum × marchii, commonly known as the Shasta daisy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy white petals with a prominent yellow center, resembling the classic daisy flower. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces numerous flowers throughout the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its hardy nature and long bloom period contribute to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
