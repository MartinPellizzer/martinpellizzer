Lysimachia porcatisepala

Lysimachia porcatisepala, commonly known as the pig-ear yellowface, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular dots. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant gardens.
