Linum nutans

Linum nutans, commonly known as the nodding flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. Linum nutans is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
