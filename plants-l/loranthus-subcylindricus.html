Loranthus subcylindricus

Loranthus subcylindricus, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through specialized roots called haustoria.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

The botanical features of Loranthus subcylindricus make it an important component of its ecological community.
