Leontodon hispidus subsp. pseudincanus

Leontodon hispidus subsp.

pseudincanus, commonly known as the hairy tooth grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a single, erect stem bearing several flowering heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and slightly different floral structure compared to other members of the species.
