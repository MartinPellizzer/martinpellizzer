Lycopus europaeus var. glabrescens

Lycopus europaeus var. glabrescens, commonly known as European water horehound, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often rounded or ovate in shape. Its small blue to purple flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
