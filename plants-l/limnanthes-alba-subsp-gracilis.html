Limnanthes alba subsp. gracilis

Limnanthes alba subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellow evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy, alternate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
