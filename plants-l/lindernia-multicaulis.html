Lindernia multicaulis

Lindernia multicaulis, commonly known as the hairy lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are ovate to lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Lindernia multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
