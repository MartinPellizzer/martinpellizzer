Lychnis taimyrensis

Lychnis taimyrensis, commonly known as the Taimyrensky catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern China.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lychnis taimyrensis is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
