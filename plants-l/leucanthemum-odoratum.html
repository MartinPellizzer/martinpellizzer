Leucanthemum odoratum

Leucanthemum odoratum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like white flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators.

Sweet everlasting is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
