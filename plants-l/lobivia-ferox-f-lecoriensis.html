Lobivia ferox f. lecoriensis

Lobivia ferox f. lecoriensis, commonly known as the Lecorien Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific growing conditions, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
