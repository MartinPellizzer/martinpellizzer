Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp. groendrayensis

Lithops pseudotruncatella subsp.

groendrayensis, commonly known as the Groenday's ghost plant, is a succulent native to South Africa. It is characterized by its pale green, rock-like appearance, which helps it blend into its arid environment.

The plant forms pairs of fleshy, lobed leaves that store water, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
