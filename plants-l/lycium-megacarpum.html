Lycium megacarpum

Lycium megacarpum, commonly known as the giant boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a notable ornamental plant and a potential source of natural products.
