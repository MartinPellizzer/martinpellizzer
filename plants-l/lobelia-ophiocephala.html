Lobelia ophiocephala

Lobelia ophiocephala, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia ophiocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
