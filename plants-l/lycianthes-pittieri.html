Lycianthes pittieri

Lycianthes pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, yellowish seeds.
