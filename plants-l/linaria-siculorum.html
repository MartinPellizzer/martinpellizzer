Linaria siculorum

Linaria siculorum, commonly known as the Sicilian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow or orange flowers. The flowers are typically tubular with two lips, a feature common in the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
