Liparis conspicua

Liparis conspicua, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is an orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pinkish flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Liparis conspicua is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with the right conditions.
