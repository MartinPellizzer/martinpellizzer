Lotus oblongifolius var. torreyi

Lotus oblongifolius var. torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its flowers are pink to purple, with a distinctive central yellow stamen, and bloom from summer to fall.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
