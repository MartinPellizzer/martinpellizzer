Lycium floribundum var. confertum

Lycium floribundum var. confertum, commonly known as the dense-flowered Chinese gooseberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
