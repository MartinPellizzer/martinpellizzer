Luzula subcongesta

Luzula subcongesta, commonly known as the dense tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers.

Luzula subcongesta is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in association with other sedges and ferns in moist, acidic soils.
