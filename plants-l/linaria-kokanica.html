Linaria kokanica

Linaria kokanica, commonly known as the Kokanica toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Linaria, which includes various species of annual and perennial flowering plants. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax, with four petals arranged in a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
