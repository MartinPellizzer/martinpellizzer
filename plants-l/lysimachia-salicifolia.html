Lysimachia salicifolia

Lysimachia salicifolia, commonly known as golden creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its vigorous growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for landscaping and wildflower gardens.
