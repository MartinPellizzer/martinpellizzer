Licania buxifolia

Licania buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf licania, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Licania buxifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
