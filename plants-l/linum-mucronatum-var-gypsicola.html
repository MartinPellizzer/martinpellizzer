Linum mucronatum var. gypsicola

Linum mucronatum var. gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum larkspur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gypsum-rich environments. It is a perennial species that produces showy inflorescences during the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
