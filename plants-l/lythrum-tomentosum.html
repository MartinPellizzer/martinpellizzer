Lythrum tomentosum

Lythrum tomentosum, commonly known as woolly lythrum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
