Lomaria woodwardioides

Lomaria woodwardioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
