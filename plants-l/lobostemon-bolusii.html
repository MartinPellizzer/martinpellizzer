Lobostemon bolusii

Lobostemon bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lobostemon bolusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
