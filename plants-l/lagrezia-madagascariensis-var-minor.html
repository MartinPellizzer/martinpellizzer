Lagrezia madagascariensis var. minor

Lagrezia madagascariensis var. minor, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, hairy covering. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
