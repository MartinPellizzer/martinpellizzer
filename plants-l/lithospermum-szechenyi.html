Lithospermum szechenyi

Lithospermum szechenyi, commonly known as the Szechenyi rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its roots contain valuable compounds, including lithospermic acid, which has been studied for its medicinal properties.

Lithospermum szechenyi thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
