Lepanthes wendtii

Lepanthes wendtii, commonly known as the Wendt's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a distinct, fragrant scent. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Lepanthes wendtii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
