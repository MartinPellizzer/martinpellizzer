Lomaria acuta

Lomaria acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf lomariella, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the late spring and summer months.

Lomaria acuta is often found in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade conditions.
