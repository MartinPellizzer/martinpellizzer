Laelia lueddemannii subsp. costaricana

Laelia lueddemannii subsp.

costaricana, commonly known as the Costa Rican Laelia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Costa Rica. It features a compact, sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers.

The plant produces striking, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a fragrant scent. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
