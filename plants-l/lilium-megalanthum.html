Lilium megalanthum

Lilium megalanthum, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often with vibrant red or pink petals and a yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its size and beauty, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
