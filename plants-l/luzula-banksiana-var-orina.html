Luzula banksiana var. orina

Luzula banksiana var. orina, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and tufted inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it its common name.

This species is often found in moist, acidic soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
