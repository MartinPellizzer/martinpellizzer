Limbarda pinifolia

Limbarda pinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray upper surface and a darker, more leathery underside. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga, which allows it to absorb nutrients from the air and rain. It is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forests.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for air quality.
