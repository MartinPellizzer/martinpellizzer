Leptochloa exilis

Leptochloa exilis, commonly known as slender flatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and has slender, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Leptochloa exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
