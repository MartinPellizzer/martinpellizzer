Lavatera trimestris var. pseudotrimestris

Lavatera trimestris var. pseudotrimestris, commonly known as the coastal lavatera, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring soft, hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
