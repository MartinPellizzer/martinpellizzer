Lonchostephus elegans

Lonchostephus elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically pink to purple and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
