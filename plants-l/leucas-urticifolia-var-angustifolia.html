Leucas urticifolia var. angustifolia

Leucas urticifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
