Lindmania vinotincta

Lindmania vinotincta, commonly known as the red-stemmed brome, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive red-colored stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, while its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster.

Lindmania vinotincta plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
