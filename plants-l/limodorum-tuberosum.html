Limodorum tuberosum

Limodorum tuberosum, commonly known as the fragrant lily of the Nile, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, tuberous rhizomes and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with six broad, white petals and a prominent, yellow stamen, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Limodorum tuberosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its subtle, sweet fragrance.
