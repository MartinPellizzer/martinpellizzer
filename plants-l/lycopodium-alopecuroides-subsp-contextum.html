Lycopodium alopecuroides subsp. contextum

Lycopodium alopecuroides subsp.

contextum, commonly known as the rough clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and spore-producing cones.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies contextum is distinguished by its rough, textured foliage and specific geographic distribution.
