Lupinus albosericeus

Lupinus albosericeus, commonly known as the white silky lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features feathery, finely divided leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Lupinus albosericeus is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
