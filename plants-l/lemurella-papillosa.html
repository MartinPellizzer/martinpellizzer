Lemurella papillosa

Lemurella papillosa, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

Lemurella papillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
