Lindera thomsonii

Lindera thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its foliage is often fragrant, with a spicy scent reminiscent of cinnamon or wintergreen.

Lindera thomsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
