Lotus corniculatus var. slovacus

Lotus corniculatus var. slovacus, commonly known as the Slovak horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fluffy, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
