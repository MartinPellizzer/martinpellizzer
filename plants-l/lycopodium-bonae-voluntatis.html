Lycopodium bonae-voluntatis

Lycopodium bonae-voluntatis, commonly known as the "good will fern," is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are usually scale-like or needle-like in appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Lycopodium bonae-voluntatis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
