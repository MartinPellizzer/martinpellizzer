Lupinus lepidus var. lobbii

Lupinus lepidus var. lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spires of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
