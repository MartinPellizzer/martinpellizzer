Labordia wawrana

Labordia wawrana, commonly known as the Wawrana sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to sandy soils and can survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
