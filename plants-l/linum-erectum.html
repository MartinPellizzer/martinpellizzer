Linum erectum

Linum erectum, commonly known as upright flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Linum erectum is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
