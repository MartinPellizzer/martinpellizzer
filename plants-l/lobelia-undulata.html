Lobelia undulata

Lobelia undulata, commonly known as the wavy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have wavy edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
