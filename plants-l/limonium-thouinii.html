Limonium thouinii

Limonium thouinii, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiky, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium thouinii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
