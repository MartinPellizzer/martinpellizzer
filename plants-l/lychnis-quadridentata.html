Lychnis quadridentata

Lychnis quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its pink or red flowers, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly toothed margin.

Lychnis quadridentata is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
