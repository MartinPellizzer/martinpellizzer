Lobivia chrysantha var. vatteri

Lobivia chrysantha var. vatteri, commonly known as the Golden Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy, blue-green foliage with a distinctive yellowish or golden hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or succulent collections.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a sought-after plant among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
