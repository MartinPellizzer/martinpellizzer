Lamellisepalum

Lamellisepalum, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that are typically five-petaled. The leaves are usually simple and alternate, with some species having hairy surfaces. The plant is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to different growing conditions.
