Lonchocarpus lanceolatus subsp. calciphilus

Lonchocarpus lanceolatus subsp.

calciphilus, commonly known as the calcium-loving legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in calcareous soils, which are rich in calcium, and exhibits adaptations to such alkaline conditions.

The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
