Lathyrus maritimus

Lathyrus maritimus, commonly known as the sea pea, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the seashore. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus maritimus is well adapted to saline environments and is often found in maritime climates.
