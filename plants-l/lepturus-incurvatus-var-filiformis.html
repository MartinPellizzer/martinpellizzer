Lepturus incurvatus var. filiformis

Lepturus incurvatus var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a variant of the genus Lepturus, characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its fronds are narrow and curling, giving it a unique, almost spiral appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread gradually across the substrate.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to create a lush, green ground cover in suitable conditions.
