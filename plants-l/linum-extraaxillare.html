Linum extraaxillare

Linum extraaxillare, commonly known as the extraaxillary flax, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Linum extraaxillare is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its fibrous stalks and seeds have been studied for potential agricultural and medicinal uses.
