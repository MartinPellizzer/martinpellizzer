Lysimachia vulgaris subsp. glandulosovillosa

Lysimachia vulgaris subsp.

glandulosovillosa, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed yellow water-lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in marshes, ponds, and along stream edges.

Its leaves are usually floating or submerged, with a broad, heart-shaped outline.
