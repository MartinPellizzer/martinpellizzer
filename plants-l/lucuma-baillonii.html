Lucuma baillonii

Lucuma baillonii, commonly known as the Andean lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble a cross between a chestnut and a sweet potato. The fruit has a dense, mealy pulp with a sweet, nutty flavor, making it a popular ingredient in local cuisines. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, dry climates and requires well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental leaves, which are glossy and evergreen.
