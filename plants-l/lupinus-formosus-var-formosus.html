Lupinus formosus var. formosus

Lupinus formosus var. formosus, commonly known as the showy lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant has feathery, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Lupinus formosus var. formosus plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
