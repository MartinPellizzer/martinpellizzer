Leptalix glauca

Leptalix glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf leptalix, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that often have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.
