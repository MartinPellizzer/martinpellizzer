Lonicera caprifolium subsp. implexa

Lonicera caprifolium subsp.

implexa, commonly known as the honeysuckle vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant is characterized by its twining habit and ability to climb by means of tendrils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along hedgerows.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
