Loxsoma

Loxsoma, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. Loxsoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
