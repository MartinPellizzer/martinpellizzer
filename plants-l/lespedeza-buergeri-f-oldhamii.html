Lespedeza buergeri f. oldhamii

Lespedeza buergeri f. oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's lespedeza, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a shrub-like perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. The plant features small, clustered inflorescences with pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
