Lepanthes ancylopetala

Lepanthes ancylopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces intricate, spurred orchid flowers that are often pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.
