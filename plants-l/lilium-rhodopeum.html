Lilium rhodopeum

Lilium rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lilium rhodopeum is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
