Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp. alpina

Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of silvery-white, woolly leaves.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine gardens.
