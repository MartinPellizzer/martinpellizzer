Lepicephalus armoraciifolius

Lepicephalus armoraciifolius, commonly known as the armoracii leaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate petal patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
