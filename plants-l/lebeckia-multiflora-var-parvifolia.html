Lebeckia multiflora var. parvifolia

Lebeckia multiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered lebeckia, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
