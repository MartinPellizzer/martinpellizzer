Luzula rufa var. rufa

Luzula rufa var. rufa, commonly known as the red sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish tinge, especially at the base. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Luzula rufa var. rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its texture and color.
