Lepidium fallax

Lepidium fallax, commonly known as the slender cress, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces small white or greenish flowers in early spring, followed by tiny, round seeds. Lepidium fallax is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.
