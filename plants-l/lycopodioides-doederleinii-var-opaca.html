Lycopodioides doederleinii var. opaca

Lycopodioides doederleinii var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage.

It features closely packed, scale-like leaves that form dense, cushion-like growths, typical of many clubmosses. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporophytes, which are borne on specialized branches known as strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or rocky crevices.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for certain ecological conditions and its historical importance in paleobotanical studies.
