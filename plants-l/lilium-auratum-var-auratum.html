Lilium auratum var. auratum

Lilium auratum var. auratum, commonly known as the golden lion's mouth, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange, with a distinctive golden throat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape around the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
