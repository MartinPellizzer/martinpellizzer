Leontopodium evax

Leontopodium evax, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround the small yellow flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments where the climate is cool and dry. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer and early autumn, and it is known for its hardiness in harsh conditions.

Due to its beauty and symbolism, it is often cultivated in gardens and is a protected species in many areas.
