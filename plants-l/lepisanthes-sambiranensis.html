Lepisanthes sambiranensis

Lepisanthes sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure and vibrant, colorful blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
