Lycium cuneatum var. rojasii

Lycium cuneatum var. rojasii, commonly known as the Rojas' boxthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
