Lathyrus palustris subsp. nudicaulis

Lathyrus palustris subsp.

nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in marshy or boggy areas, preferring moist, acidic soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
