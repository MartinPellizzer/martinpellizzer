Lythrum gracile

Lythrum gracile, commonly known as narrowleaf rosemary or slender lythrum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the poinsettia. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Lythrum gracile is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.
