Leucas lanceifolia

Leucas lanceifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Leucas lanceifolia is often found in grassy areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
