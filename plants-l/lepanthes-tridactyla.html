Lepanthes tridactyla

Lepanthes tridactyla, commonly known as the three-fingered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features a compact, hairy stem with several fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with three prominent sepals and three petals, giving it its common name.

Lepanthes tridactyla is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
