Lotus trichocarpus

Lotus trichocarpus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has large, showy flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. Its leaves are typically broad and floating, with a waxy surface that repels water.

The lotus is also notable for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine.
