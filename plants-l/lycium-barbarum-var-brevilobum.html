Lycium barbarum var. brevilobum

Lycium barbarum var. brevilobum, commonly known as the short-lobed goji plant, is a variant of the goji berry plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that ripen in the autumn and are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

This variant is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and for its edible berries, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
