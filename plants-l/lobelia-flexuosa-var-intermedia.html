Lobelia flexuosa var. intermedia

Lobelia flexuosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate flexible lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated, alternate, and often have a slightly wavy or serrated edge.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
