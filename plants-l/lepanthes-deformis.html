Lepanthes deformis

Lepanthes deformis, commonly known as the twisted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spike and striking, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Lepanthes deformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
