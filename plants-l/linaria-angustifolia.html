Linaria angustifolia

Linaria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
