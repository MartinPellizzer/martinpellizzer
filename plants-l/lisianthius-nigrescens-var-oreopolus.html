Lisianthius nigrescens var. oreopolus

Lisianthius nigrescens var. oreopolus, commonly known as the Oregon lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of purple and white.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
