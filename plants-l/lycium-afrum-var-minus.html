Lycium afrum var. minus

Lycium afrum var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries that are often consumed by birds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
