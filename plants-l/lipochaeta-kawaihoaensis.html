Lipochaeta kawaihoaensis

Lipochaeta kawaihoaensis, commonly known as the Kawaihoa daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Lipochaeta kawaihoaensis is an important species for both ecological and cultural reasons in New Zealand.
