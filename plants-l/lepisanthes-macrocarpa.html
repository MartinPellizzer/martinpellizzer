Lepisanthes macrocarpa

Lepisanthes macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited bellflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Lepisanthes macrocarpa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests.

Its unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a notable species in botanical collections.
