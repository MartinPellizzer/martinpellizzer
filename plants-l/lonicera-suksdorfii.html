Lonicera suksdorfii

Lonicera suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Lonicera suksdorfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
