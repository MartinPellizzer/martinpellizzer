Lepidium pichleri

Lepidium pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's cress, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
