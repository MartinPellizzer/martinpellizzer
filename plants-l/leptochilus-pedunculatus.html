Leptochilus pedunculatus

Leptochilus pedunculatus, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowering stems are often short and bear multiple flowers, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Leptochilus pedunculatus is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to bloom in challenging growing conditions.
