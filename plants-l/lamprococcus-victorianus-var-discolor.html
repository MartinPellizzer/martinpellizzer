Lamprococcus victorianus var. discolor

Lamprococcus victorianus var. discolor, commonly known as the Victoria blackberry, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark, almost black berries that are highly prized for their flavor. The plant thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are typically small, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The berries are usually harvested when fully ripe, offering a unique combination of tartness and sweetness.
