Lycianthes cladotrichota

Lycianthes cladotrichota, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallow, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Lycianthes cladotrichota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
