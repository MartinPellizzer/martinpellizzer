Lepidium montanum var. alpinum

Lepidium montanum var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and other mountainous areas.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often lobed, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
