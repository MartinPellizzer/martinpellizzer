Leucopogon penicillatus

Leucopogon penicillatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
