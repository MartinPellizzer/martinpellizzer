Lavandula humbertii

Lavandula humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern France, particularly the Pyrenees. This perennial shrub features aromatic, narrow leaves and spikes of purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and perfumery.
