Limonium procerum var. cornubiense

Limonium procerum var. cornubiense, commonly known as the Cornish sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of western Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal habitats.
