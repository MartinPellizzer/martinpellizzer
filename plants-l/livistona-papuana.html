Livistona papuana

Livistona papuana, commonly known as the Papuan palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and nearby islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
