Leontodon crispus subsp. bourgaeanus

Leontodon crispus subsp.

bourgaeanus, commonly known as the Bourgaeanus hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower head surrounded by leaf-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is characterized by its finely dissected leaves and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-poor habitats.
