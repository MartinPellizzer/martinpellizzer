Lycoseris grandis

Lycoseris grandis, commonly known as the large lycoperd, is a species of fungus that belongs to the family Lycoperdaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often branched fruiting bodies that resemble small, spiky structures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its spores are released from the apical pores of the fruiting body, aiding in its dispersal.

Lycoseris grandis is often found in temperate regions and is known for its unique morphology and ecological role in nutrient cycling.
