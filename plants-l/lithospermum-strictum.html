Lithospermum strictum

Lithospermum strictum, commonly known as narrowleaf lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink to red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with roots containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
