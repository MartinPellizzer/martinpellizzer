Latania oliviformis

Latania oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a tropical appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red fruits, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Latania oliviformis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in warm climates.

Its unique shape and evergreen foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
