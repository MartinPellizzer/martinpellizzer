Lupinus anatolicus

Lupinus anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian lupin, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of bright blue or violet pea-like flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus anatolicus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
