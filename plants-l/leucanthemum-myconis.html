Leucanthemum myconis

Leucanthemum myconis, commonly known as the mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a daisy. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Leucanthemum myconis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
