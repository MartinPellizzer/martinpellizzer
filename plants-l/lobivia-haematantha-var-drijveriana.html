Lobivia haematantha var. drijveriana

Lobivia haematantha var. drijveriana, commonly known as the Drijver's Lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are usually rounded and may be covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability to arid environments.
