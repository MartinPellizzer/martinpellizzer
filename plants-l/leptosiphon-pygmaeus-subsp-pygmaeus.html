Leptosiphon pygmaeus subsp. pygmaeus

Leptosiphon pygmaeus subsp.

pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf fairy-bluebell, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
