Lepidium graminifolium var. virgatum

Lepidium graminifolium var. virgatum, commonly known as the slender peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has slender, grass-like leaves and small white flowers arranged in clusters.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
