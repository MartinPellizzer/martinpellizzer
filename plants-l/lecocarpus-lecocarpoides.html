Lecocarpus lecocarpoides

Lecocarpus lecocarpoides, commonly known as the purple mangrove, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces fleshy, purple fruits that contain a single seed.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
