Lennea melanocarpa

Lennea melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited lennea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and dry forests. Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
