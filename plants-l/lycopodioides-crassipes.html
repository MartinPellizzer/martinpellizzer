Lycopodioides crassipes

Lycopodioides crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaf clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its production of small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its sporophyte stage produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

Lycopodioides crassipes plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
