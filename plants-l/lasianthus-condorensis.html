Lasianthus condorensis

Lasianthus condorensis, commonly known as the Condor mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
