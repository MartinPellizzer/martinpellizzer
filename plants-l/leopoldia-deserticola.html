Leopoldia deserticola

Leopoldia deserticola, commonly known as the desert leopoldia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
