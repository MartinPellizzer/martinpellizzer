Ledermanniella guineensis

Ledermanniella guineensis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and feature intricate, lip-like structures that aid in pollination.

Ledermanniella guineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
