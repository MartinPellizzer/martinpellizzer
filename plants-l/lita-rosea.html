Lita rosea

Lita rosea, commonly known as the roseate lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to lavender-colored petals that resemble roses, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lita rosea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden settings.
