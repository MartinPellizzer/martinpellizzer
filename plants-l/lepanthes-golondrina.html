Lepanthes golondrina

Lepanthes golondrina, commonly known as the "Blue Moon Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically feature a pale blue or white coloration. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme, with each bloom exhibiting intricate patterns and a subtle fragrance.

Lepanthes golondrina is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
