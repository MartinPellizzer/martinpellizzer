Lissochilus sordidus

Lissochilus sordidus, commonly known as the dirty beard lily, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. This herbaceous plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often with a subtle fragrance.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.
