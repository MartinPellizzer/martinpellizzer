Lactuca quercus

Lactuca quercus, commonly known as the oak lettuce, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of spoon-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and slightly lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other lettuce varieties due to its specific growing requirements.
