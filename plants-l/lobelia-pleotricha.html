Lobelia pleotricha

Lobelia pleotricha, commonly known as the whorled lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Lobelia pleotricha is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
