Lespedeza bicolor f. acutifolia

Lespedeza bicolor f. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, yellowish-white flowers, and the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
