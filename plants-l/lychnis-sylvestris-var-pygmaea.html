Lychnis sylvestris var. pygmaea

Lychnis sylvestris var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf wood cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of only a few inches, with slender, hairy stems that bear small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage consists of kidney-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
