Leucas cephalotes

Leucas cephalotes, commonly known as the purple leucas, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their fragrance and potential therapeutic properties.
