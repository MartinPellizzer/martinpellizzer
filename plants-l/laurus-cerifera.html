Laurus cerifera

Laurus cerifera, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.
