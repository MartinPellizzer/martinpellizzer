Lomatogonium carinthiacum var. cordifolium

Lomatogonium carinthiacum var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved lomatogonium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctively heart-shaped base, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
