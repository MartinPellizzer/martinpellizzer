Lithocarpus dealbatus subsp. eudealbatus

Lithocarpus dealbatus subsp.

eudealbatus, commonly known as the white-leafed ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, characterized by its hard, dense wood and evergreen foliage. It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the genus Lithocarpus. Its leaves are leathery, glossy, and often have a whitish underside, contributing to its common name.

This subspecies is valued for its durable timber and is also of ecological importance in its native habitats.
