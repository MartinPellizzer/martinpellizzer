Luzula parviflora var. intermedia

Luzula parviflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, often forming dense clumps. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
