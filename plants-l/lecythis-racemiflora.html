Lecythis racemiflora

Lecythis racemiflora, commonly known as the Amazonian cheese tree, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The tree is notable for its heavy, waxy fruits that contain a creamy, cheese-like pulp, which is a key feature of its common name. The fruit's pulp is not only edible but also has a unique texture and flavor that sets it apart from other tropical fruits.

Lecythis racemiflora plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
