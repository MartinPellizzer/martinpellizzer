Lepidium calycinum

Lepidium calycinum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb with a compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive, finely toothed appearance.

Lepidium calycinum is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.
