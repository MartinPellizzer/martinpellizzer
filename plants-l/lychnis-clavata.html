Lychnis clavata

Lychnis clavata, commonly known as the clustered pink, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants known for their showy blooms and adaptations to various climates.
