Linaria kurdica subsp. araratica

Linaria kurdica subsp.

araratica, commonly known as the Kurdish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iraq. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of Mediterranean flora.
