Lipocarpha gracilis

Lipocarpha gracilis, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Lipocarpha gracilis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and water filtration.
