Lycianthes laevis subsp. bigeminata

Lycianthes laevis subsp.

bigeminata, commonly known as the twin-fruit eggplant, is a species native to Central America. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruits that typically grow in pairs, giving it its common name.

The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits. The leaves are ovate and pubescent, while the flowers are yellow with a white center.

It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and decorative purposes.
