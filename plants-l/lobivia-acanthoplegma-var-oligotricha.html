Lobivia acanthoplegma var. oligotricha

Lobivia acanthoplegma var. oligotricha, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded body with short, spiny stems that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It requires minimal water and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its slow growth and distinctive appearance, it is a prized addition to many succulent collections.
