Lepanthes agatarum

Lepanthes agatarum, commonly known as the Agata's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The orchid's unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
