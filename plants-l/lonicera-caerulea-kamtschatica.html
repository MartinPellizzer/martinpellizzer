Lonicera caerulea kamtschatica

Lonicera caerulea kamtschatica, commonly known as the Siberian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, honey-like flavor. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often reddish in color.

This hardy plant is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ornamental gardening and for its edible fruits.
