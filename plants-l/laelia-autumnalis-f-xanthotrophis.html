Laelia autumnalis f. xanthotrophis

Laelia autumnalis f. xanthotrophis, commonly known as the autumn laelia with yellow-toned flowers, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often golden-yellow floral displays. The plant typically produces compact, upright pseudobulbs with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are borne on terminal racemes and feature a unique combination of coloration and fragrance.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
