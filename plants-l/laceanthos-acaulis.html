Laceanthos acaulis

Laceanthos acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of a typical stem, with its leaves and flowers emerging directly from the root system. The plant produces intricate, delicate flowers that are often white or pale pink, resembling lace in appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in some areas.
