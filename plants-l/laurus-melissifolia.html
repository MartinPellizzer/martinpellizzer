Laurus melissifolia

Laurus melissifolia, commonly known as sweet bay laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are rich in essential oils, particularly those containing eucalyptol and other terpenes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but less commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been historically used in cooking, medicine, and as a symbol of honor and achievement.
