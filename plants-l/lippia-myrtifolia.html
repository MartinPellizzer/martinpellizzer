Lippia myrtifolia

Lippia myrtifolia, commonly known as redroot verbena, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in warmer climates. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Lippia myrtifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
