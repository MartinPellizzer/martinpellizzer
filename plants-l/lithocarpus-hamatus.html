Lithocarpus hamatus

Lithocarpus hamatus, commonly known as the rough-shelled ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts.

It is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its hard, dense wood and evergreen leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a thick, leathery husk.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
