Lathyrus pratensis var. glaberrimus

Lathyrus pratensis var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the glabrous vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

The variety glaberrimus is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to other varieties.
