Lupinus angustifolius proles cryptanthus

Lupinus angustifolius proles cryptanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine with cryptanthus characteristics, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This subspecies is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
