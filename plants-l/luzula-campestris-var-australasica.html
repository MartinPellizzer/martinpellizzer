Luzula campestris var. australasica

Luzula campestris var. australasica, commonly known as the Australasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia and New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish or brownish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in coastal or subcoastal regions.
