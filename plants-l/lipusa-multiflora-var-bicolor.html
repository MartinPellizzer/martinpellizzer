Lipusa multiflora var. bicolor

Lipusa multiflora var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored lipusa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically display a mix of white and pink hues. The plant features delicate, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
