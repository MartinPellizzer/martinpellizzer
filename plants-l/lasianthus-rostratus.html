Lasianthus rostratus

Lasianthus rostratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
