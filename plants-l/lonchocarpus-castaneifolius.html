Lonchocarpus castaneifolius

Lonchocarpus castaneifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-colored aril. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

However, some species in the genus are toxic, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
