Linaria filiformis

Linaria filiformis, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological traits.

Linaria filiformis is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
