Lyonia villosa var. pubescens

Lyonia villosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lyonia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
