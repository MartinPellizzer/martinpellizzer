Lipochaeta rockii var. subovata

Lipochaeta rockii var. subovata, commonly known as the rock lipochaeta, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dryland gardens.
