Leucas indica var. decipiens

Leucas indica var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, typical of the mint family. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color.

This variety is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
