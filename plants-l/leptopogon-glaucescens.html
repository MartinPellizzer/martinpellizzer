Leptopogon glaucescens

Leptopogon glaucescens, commonly known as the blue flax or blue sedge, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in dense clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
