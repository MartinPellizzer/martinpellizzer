Linum suffruticosum subsp. marianorum

Linum suffruticosum subsp.

marianorum, commonly known as the Marian's flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry soils.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
