Lithops aucampiae subsp. euniceae

Lithops aucampiae subsp.

euniceae, commonly known as the "dwarf ice plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, stone-like appearance, with two closely spaced, fleshy leaves that store water.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as a decorative and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
