Leptochloa imbricata

Leptochloa imbricata, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a small, tufted perennial grass with slender, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in agricultural areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually small and inconspicuous.

Leptochloa imbricata is known for its adaptability to different environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
