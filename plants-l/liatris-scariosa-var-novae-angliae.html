Liatris scariosa var. novae-angliae

Liatris scariosa var. novae-angliae, commonly known as New England blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear spikes of showy, tubular purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
