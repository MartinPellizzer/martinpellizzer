Lepigonum diandrum

Lepigonum diandrum, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Lepigonum diandrum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
