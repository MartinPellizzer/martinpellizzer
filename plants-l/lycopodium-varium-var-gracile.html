Lycopodium varium var. gracile

Lycopodium varium var. gracile, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, compact clusters. Native to moist, shaded habitats in temperate regions, it thrives in humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, a feature typical of its evolutionary lineage.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
