Lophiaris maculata

Lophiaris maculata, commonly known as the spotted lophiaris, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves with distinctive red or purple spots, which help it store water and protect against intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
