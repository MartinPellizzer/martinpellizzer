Lonicera fragrantissima var. lancifolia

Lonicera fragrantissima var. lancifolia, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.
