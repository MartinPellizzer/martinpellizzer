Lepanthes ravetii

Lepanthes ravetii, commonly known as the Ravet's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that are typically borne on slender, erect stems. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and showy petals. Lepanthes ravetii thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
