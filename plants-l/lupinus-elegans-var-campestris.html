Lupinus elegans var. campestris

Lupinus elegans var. campestris, commonly known as the plains lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of oval, hairy leaflets and produces elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Lupinus elegans var. campestris plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
