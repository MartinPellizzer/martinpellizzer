Linophyllum divaricatum

Linophyllum divaricatum, commonly known as the variable sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often blue or purple, ray florets and a compact, bushy growth habit. Native to coastal regions of southern Africa, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
