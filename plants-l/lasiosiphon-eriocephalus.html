Lasiosiphon eriocephalus

Lasiosiphon eriocephalus, commonly known as the woolly siphon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Lasiosiphon eriocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
