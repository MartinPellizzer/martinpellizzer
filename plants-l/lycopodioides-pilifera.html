Lycopodioides pilifera

Lycopodioides pilifera, commonly known as the hairy clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming low, mat-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Lycopodioides pilifera is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
