Leersia oryzoides f. clandestina

Leersia oryzoides f. clandestina, commonly known as the clandestine leersia, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the leersia genus.

It is a grass-like plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.
