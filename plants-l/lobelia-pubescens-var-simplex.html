Lobelia pubescens var. simplex

Lobelia pubescens var. simplex, commonly known as the smooth lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
