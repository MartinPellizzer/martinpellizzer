Liparis nigra var. flava

Liparis nigra var. flava, commonly known as the yellow lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, grass-like leaves and its striking, yellowish-green inflorescence composed of numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a long, spiraling spike of flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
