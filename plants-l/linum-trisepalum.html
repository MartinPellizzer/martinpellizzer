Linum trisepalum

Linum trisepalum, commonly known as the three-lobed flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens despite its limited distribution in the wild.
