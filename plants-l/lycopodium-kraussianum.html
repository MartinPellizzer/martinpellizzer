Lycopodium kraussianum

Lycopodium kraussianum, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. The plant features needle-like leaves and produces spore-bearing cones, which are characteristic of its ancient lineage. Lycopodium kraussianum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often found in conservation areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
