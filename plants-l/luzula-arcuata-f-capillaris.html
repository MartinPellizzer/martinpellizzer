Luzula arcuata f. capillaris

Luzula arcuata f. capillaris, commonly known as the slender woodrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its slender, hair-like leaves, which give it its specific epithet "capillaris.".
