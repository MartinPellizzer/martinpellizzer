Linaria sieberi

Linaria sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in many species of the genus Linaria.

Linaria sieberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
