Lobelia siphilitica f. laevicalyx

Lobelia siphilitica f. laevicalyx, commonly known as the smooth-leaf bluebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves of this variety are smooth and lance-shaped, distinguishing it from other lobelia species. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
