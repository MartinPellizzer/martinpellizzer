Lupinus trainianus

Lupinus trainianus, commonly known as the Desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Lupinus trainianus is adapted to withstand drought and is an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
