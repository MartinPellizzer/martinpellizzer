Lobelia gracilis var. major

Lobelia gracilis var. major, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces clusters of delicate, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
