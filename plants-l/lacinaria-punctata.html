Lacinaria punctata

Lacinaria punctata, commonly known as the punctate lacinia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
