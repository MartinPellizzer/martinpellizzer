Lancea tibetica

Lancea tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lancea tibetica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
