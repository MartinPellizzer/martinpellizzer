Lessertia rigida var. leucomelaena

Lessertia rigida var. leucomelaena, commonly known as the white-leaved ironwood, is a rare and striking plant native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale green foliage, which contrasts sharply with its dark, woody stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
