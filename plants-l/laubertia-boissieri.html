Laubertia boissieri

Laubertia boissieri, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Laubertia boissieri is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
