Limonium tomentellum subsp. hypanicum

Limonium tomentellum subsp.

hypanicum, commonly known as the woolly sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and sandy soils.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to coastal landscapes. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh maritime conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
