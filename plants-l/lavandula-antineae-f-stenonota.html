Lavandula antineae f. stenonota

Lavandula antineae f. stenonota, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a horticultural form of the Lavandula genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to lilac flowers, which are highly aromatic. It is well-suited for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and fragrance, making it a popular choice in both gardens and commercial cultivation.
