Linum spergulinum

Linum spergulinum, commonly known as the lesser flax or slender flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and the stem is slender and erect.

Linum spergulinum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
