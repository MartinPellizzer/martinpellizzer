Lycopodium comptonioides

Lycopodium comptonioides, commonly known as the Compton's clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features whorled leaves that are narrow and needle-like, with a distinct central stem. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodium comptonioides plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
