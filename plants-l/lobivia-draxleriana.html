Lobivia draxleriana

Lobivia draxleriana, commonly known as the Draxler's lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually green and may have a slight bluish tint, and they are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lobivia draxleriana prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
