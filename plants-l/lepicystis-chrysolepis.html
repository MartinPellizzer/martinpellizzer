Lepicystis chrysolepis

Lepicystis chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are known for their vibrant yellow and green hues, making it a visually appealing species in botanical gardens.
