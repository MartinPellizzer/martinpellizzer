Lepanthes congesta

Lepanthes congesta, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often densely packed inflorescences that bear numerous small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Lepanthes congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
