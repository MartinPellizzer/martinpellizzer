Lycium chilense var. gracile

Lycium chilense var. gracile, commonly known as the slender Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant has spiny stems and dark green, narrow leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
