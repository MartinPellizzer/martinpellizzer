Lupinus adsurgens var. adsurgens

Lupinus adsurgens var. adsurgens, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, bearing clusters of pea-like blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules, which contribute to soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry slopes and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
