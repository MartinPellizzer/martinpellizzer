Lupinus diffusus

Lupinus diffusus, commonly known as the spreading lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fluffy, yellow or brown seed pods. Lupinus diffusus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
