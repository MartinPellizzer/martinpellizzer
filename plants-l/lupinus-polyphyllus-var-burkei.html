Lupinus polyphyllus var. burkei

Lupinus polyphyllus var. burkei, commonly known as the Burke's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves composed of several oval to ovate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Lupinus polyphyllus var. burkei plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen and supporting pollinators.
