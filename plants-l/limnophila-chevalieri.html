Limnophila chevalieri

Limnophila chevalieri, commonly known as the water willow, is a semi-aquatic plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Southeast Asia.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, aiding in photosynthesis in waterlogged conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Limnophila chevalieri is an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting diverse aquatic life.
