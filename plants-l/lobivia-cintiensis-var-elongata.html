Lobivia cintiensis var. elongata

Lobivia cintiensis var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with cylindrical, ribbed stems that can elongate under certain growing conditions. The plant produces pale yellow to green flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
