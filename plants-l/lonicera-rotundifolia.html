Lonicera rotundifolia

Lonicera rotundifolia, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in the spring. Its berries are red and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

Lonicera rotundifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
