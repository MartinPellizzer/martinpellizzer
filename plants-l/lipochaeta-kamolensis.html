Lipochaeta kamolensis

Lipochaeta kamolensis, commonly known as the Kamolense ironweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture that aids in water retention.
