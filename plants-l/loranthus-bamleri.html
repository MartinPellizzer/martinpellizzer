Loranthus bamleri

Loranthus bamleri, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to host trees, primarily in the genus Betula.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using sunlight absorbed by its host's leaves. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus bamleri is often found in forested areas of China and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
