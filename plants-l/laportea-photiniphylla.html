Laportea photiniphylla

Laportea photiniphylla, commonly known as the flameleaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
