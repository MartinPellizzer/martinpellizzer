Lappa officinalis

Lappa officinalis, commonly known as hogweed, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped clusters of small white or greenish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its toxic sap, it is considered a potentially dangerous plant for humans and animals.
