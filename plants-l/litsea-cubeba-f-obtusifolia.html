Litsea cubeba f. obtusifolia

Litsea cubeba f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved litsea or sweet fennel.

It is a variant of the Litsea cubeba species, characterized by its broader, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its aromatic properties.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
