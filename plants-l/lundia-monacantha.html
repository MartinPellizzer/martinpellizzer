Lundia monacantha

Lundia monacantha, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, velvety flowers. The plant typically has dark green, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Lundia monacantha is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
