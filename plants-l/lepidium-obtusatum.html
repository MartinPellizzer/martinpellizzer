Lepidium obtusatum

Lepidium obtusatum, commonly known as broad-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas. It has basal leaves that are broad and lobed, and its stems are usually erect.

The flowers are typically yellow or pale purple, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
