Lacunaria umbonata

Lacunaria umbonata, commonly known as the horned pincushion, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and often colorful capitula, which resemble a pincushion with protruding bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
