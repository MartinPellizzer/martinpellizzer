Lupinus arizonicus subsp. sonorensis

Lupinus arizonicus subsp.

sonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran lupine, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Lupinus arizonicus subsp.

sonorensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
