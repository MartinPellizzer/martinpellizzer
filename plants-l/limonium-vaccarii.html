Limonium vaccarii

Limonium vaccarii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal and saline environments, making it well adapted to harsh, salty conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium vaccarii is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
