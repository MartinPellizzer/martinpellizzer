Lucuma glabrescens

Lucuma glabrescens, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small citrus fruits. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically growing up to 10 meters in height. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is used both in traditional medicine and as a food source.
