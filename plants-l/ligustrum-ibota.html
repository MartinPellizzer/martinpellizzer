Ligustrum ibota

Ligustrum ibota, commonly known as the Brazilian privet, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, black berries. Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Ligustrum ibota is often used in ornamental gardening due to its adaptability and attractive appearance.
