Lespedeza spicata

Lespedeza spicata, commonly known as silverleaf lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Lespedeza spicata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil improvement.
