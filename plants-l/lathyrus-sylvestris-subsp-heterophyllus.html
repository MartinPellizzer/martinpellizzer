Lathyrus sylvestris subsp. heterophyllus

Lathyrus sylvestris subsp.

heterophyllus, commonly known as the purple waxybark, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is notable for its variable leaf morphology, which can range from simple to lobed depending on the environment. It thrives in woodland and scrub habitats, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
