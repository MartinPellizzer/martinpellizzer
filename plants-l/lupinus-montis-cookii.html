Lupinus montis-cookii

Lupinus montis-cookii, commonly known as Cook's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of purple or blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Lupinus montis-cookii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
