Lobivia aurea subsp. shaferi

Lobivia aurea subsp.

shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It features a compact, rounded stem with fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
