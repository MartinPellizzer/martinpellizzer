Linaria multicaulis subsp. humilis

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf toadflax, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects.
