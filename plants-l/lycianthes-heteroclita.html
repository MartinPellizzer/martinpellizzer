Lycianthes heteroclita

Lycianthes heteroclita, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow star.

It is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in Mexico and Central America. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are tubular in shape, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
