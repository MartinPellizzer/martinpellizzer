Lettsomia capitata

Lettsomia capitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Lettsomia capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and resilience.
