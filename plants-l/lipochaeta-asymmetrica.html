Lipochaeta asymmetrica

Lipochaeta asymmetrica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in a head.

Lipochaeta asymmetrica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
