Lemaireocereus queretarensis

Lemaireocereus queretarensis, commonly known as the Mexican night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and lack of spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique flowering behavior and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens.
