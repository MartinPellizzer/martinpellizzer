Lippia acutidens

Lippia acutidens, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, aromatic leaves with a strong mint-like scent, and its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple. Lippia acutidens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

It is also valued as a culinary herb, particularly in Mexican cuisine for its refreshing flavor.
