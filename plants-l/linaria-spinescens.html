Linaria spinescens

Linaria spinescens, commonly known as the spiny toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its sparsely branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linaria spinescens thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable and resilient species within its native range.
