Limeum pterocarpum var. pterocarpum

Limeum pterocarpum var. pterocarpum, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
