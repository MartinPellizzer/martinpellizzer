Lysimachia hui

Lysimachia hui, commonly known as the Chinese fortune grass, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lysimachia hui is valued in horticulture for its ability to form dense mats and its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
