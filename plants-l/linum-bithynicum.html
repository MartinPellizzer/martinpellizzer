Linum bithynicum

Linum bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian flax, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species. The plant features slender, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.
