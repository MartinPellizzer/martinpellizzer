Linosyris sedifolia

Linosyris sedifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Linosyris sedifolia is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.
