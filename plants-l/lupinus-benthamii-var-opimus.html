Lupinus benthamii var. opimus

Lupinus benthamii var. opimus, commonly known as the opimus lupine, is a flowering plant species native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus benthamii var. opimus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
