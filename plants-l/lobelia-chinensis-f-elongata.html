Lobelia chinensis f. elongata

Lobelia chinensis f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated Chinese lobelia, is a variant of the Chinese lobelia native to China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its elongated, slender stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which are usually blue to purple in color, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
