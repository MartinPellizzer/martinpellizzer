Lycopus virginicus var. quercifolius

Lycopus virginicus var. quercifolius, commonly known as bearberry, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the forest floor. The leaves are oval to heart-shaped, with a serrated margin and a velvety texture. Small white or pale blue flowers appear in clusters during early summer, followed by red berries that persist into the fall.

This plant is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
