Lysimachia vulgaris f. glandulosovillosa

Lysimachia vulgaris f. glandulosovillosa, commonly known as the glandular-hairy yellow archangel, is a variant of the yellow archangel species characterized by its distinctive glandular and hairy foliage.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, often found in moist woodland habitats. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a soft, hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
