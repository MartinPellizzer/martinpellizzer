Lycium floribundum var. ciliatum

Lycium floribundum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has woody stems covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
