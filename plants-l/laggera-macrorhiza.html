Laggera macrorhiza

Laggera macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted laggera, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a robust root system, which contributes to its adaptability in various soil conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Laggera macrorhiza is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
