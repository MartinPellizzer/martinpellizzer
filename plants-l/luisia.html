Luisia

Luisia, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, particularly in North America and Europe. Luisia species typically have square stems, opposite leaves, and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. They are often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Many species are valued for their aromatic foliage and are used in traditional herbal medicine.
