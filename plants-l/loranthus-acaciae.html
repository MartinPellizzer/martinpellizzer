Loranthus acaciae

Loranthus acaciae, commonly known as the acacia mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on acacia trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, where it often forms dense clusters on its host.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important part of the ecological interactions within its habitat.
