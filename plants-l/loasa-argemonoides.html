Loasa argemonoides

Loasa argemonoides, commonly known as the scarlet loasa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges. Its showy, yellow-orange flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Loasa argemonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
