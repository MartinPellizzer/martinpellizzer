Lonchocarpus laticifer

Lonchocarpus laticifer, commonly known as the broad-veined legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility. Its bark and roots contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it is also considered toxic to livestock due to the presence of certain harmful compounds.
