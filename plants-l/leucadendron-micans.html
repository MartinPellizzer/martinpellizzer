Leucadendron micans

Leucadendron micans, commonly known as the silver leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, bright green leaves. The leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
