Lantana coimbrensis

Lantana coimbrensis, commonly known as the yellow lantana, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lantana coimbrensis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
