Leea papuana

Leea papuana, commonly known as the Papuan Leea, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Leea papuana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and ecological research.
