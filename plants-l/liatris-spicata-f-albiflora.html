Liatris spicata f. albiflora

Liatris spicata f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower blazing star, is a cultivar of the blazing star plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its striking white to pale pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spiky inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
