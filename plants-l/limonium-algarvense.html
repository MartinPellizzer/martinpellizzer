Limonium algarvense

Limonium algarvense, commonly known as the Algarve sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Portugal.

It thrives in saline and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, arid conditions. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs.

Limonium algarvense plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
