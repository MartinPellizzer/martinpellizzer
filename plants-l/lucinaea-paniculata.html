Lucinaea paniculata

Lucinaea paniculata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Lucinaea paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
