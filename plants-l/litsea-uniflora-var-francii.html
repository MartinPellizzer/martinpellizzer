Litsea uniflora var. francii

Litsea uniflora var. francii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or fragrant laurustin, is a flowering plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces smooth, reddish-brown fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
