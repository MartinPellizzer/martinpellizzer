Lactuca ameristophylla

Lactuca ameristophylla, commonly known as American lettuce, is a species of lettuce native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are often used in salads and have a mild, slightly bitter flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
