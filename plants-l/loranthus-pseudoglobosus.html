Loranthus pseudoglobosus

Loranthus pseudoglobosus, commonly known as the globeflower mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that relies on host trees for water and nutrients.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its small, green leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. It is often found in temperate regions and can be a common parasite on species such as oak and elm.

Its botanical structure reflects its parasitic lifestyle, with reduced roots and a reliance on photosynthesis for energy.
