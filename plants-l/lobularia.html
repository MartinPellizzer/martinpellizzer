Lobularia

Lobularia, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plants typically grow as annuals or biennials, often reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Lobularia species have alternate, linear to ovate leaves and produce small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

They are native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, and are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
