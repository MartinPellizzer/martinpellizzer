Lebeckia sessilifolia

Lebeckia sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lebeckia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lebeckia sessilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
