Lepidium virginicum subsp. euvirginicum

Lepidium virginicum subsp.

euvirginicum, commonly known as Virginia cress, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and roadside areas. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
