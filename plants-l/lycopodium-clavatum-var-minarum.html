Lycopodium clavatum var. minarum

Lycopodium clavatum var. minarum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rock crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with tiny, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
