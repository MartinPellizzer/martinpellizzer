Lupinus pilosus subsp. luthereaui

Lupinus pilosus subsp.

luthereaui, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive pinnate structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Lupinus pilosus subsp.

luthereaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
