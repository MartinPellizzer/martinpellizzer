Lobivia saltensis var. pseudocachensis

Lobivia saltensis var. pseudocachensis, commonly known as the Salt Creek Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that often branch at the base, forming a dense, shrub-like structure. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
