Leptaulus daphnoides

Leptaulus daphnoides, commonly known as the creeping daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Leptaulus daphnoides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
