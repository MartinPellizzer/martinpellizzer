Loranthus kilimandscharicus

Loranthus kilimandscharicus, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees in the Eastern African region.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators like birds and insects. It is often found in humid, forested areas at high altitudes, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.

Its presence is an indicator of the health and biodiversity of the forest ecosystems it inhabits.
