Lonicera floribunda

Lonicera floribunda, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its berries are orange-red and edible, attracting birds and providing food for wildlife.

Lonicera floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
