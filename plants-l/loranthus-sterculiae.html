Loranthus sterculiae

Loranthus sterculiae, commonly known as the European mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily oaks.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, scale-like leaves and white, sticky berries that contain toxic seeds. It plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for birds and insects, which in turn help in seed dispersal.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategies make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
