Lilium pyrenaicum subsp. ponticum

Lilium pyrenaicum subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
