Lucaea langsdorffiana

Lucaea langsdorffiana, commonly known as the purple-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Lucaea langsdorffiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
