Lupinus flavoculatus

Lupinus flavoculatus, commonly known as the yellow-eyed lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive yellow eye on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus flavoculatus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
