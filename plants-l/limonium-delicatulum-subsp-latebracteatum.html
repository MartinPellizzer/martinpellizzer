Limonium delicatulum subsp. latebracteatum

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

latebracteatum, commonly known as the latebracteate sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically on slender, branching stems.

The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salty, coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate maritime conditions.
