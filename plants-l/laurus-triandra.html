Laurus triandra

Laurus triandra, commonly known as the African laurier or African bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by dark, spherical berries that contain seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
