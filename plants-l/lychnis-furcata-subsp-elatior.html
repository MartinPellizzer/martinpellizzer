Lychnis furcata subsp. elatior

Lychnis furcata subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that are typically hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic and have five petals, with the lower petal being spurred.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.
