Lathyrus maccaguenii

Lathyrus maccaguenii, commonly known as the Corsican vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Lathyrus maccaguenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
