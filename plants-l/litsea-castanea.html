Litsea castanea

Litsea castanea, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red drupes that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
