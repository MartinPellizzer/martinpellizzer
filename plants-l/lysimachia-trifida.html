Lysimachia trifida

Lysimachia trifida, commonly known as the golden thread, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lysimachia trifida is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
