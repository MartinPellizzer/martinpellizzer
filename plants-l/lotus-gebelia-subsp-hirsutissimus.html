Lotus gebelia subsp. hirsutissimus

Lotus gebelia subsp.

hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils, often near coastal areas or in rocky habitats. Its root system is fibrous, and it is known for its ability to thrive in saline conditions.

The hairy lotus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
