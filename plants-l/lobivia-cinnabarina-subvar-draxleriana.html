Lobivia cinnabarina subvar. draxleriana

Lobivia cinnabarina subvar. draxleriana, commonly known as the Draxler's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features short, cylindrical stems that often branch out, producing clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers during the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its striking coloration and resilience in arid environments.
