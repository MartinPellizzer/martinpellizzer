Liparis rupestris var. purpurascens

Liparis rupestris var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to rocky and mountainous regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its upright, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white with a purplish tinge, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
