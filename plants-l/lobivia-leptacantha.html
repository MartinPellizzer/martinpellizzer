Lobivia leptacantha

Lobivia leptacantha, commonly known as the slender-spined lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mounding growth habit. The plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in sparse, pale greenish-gray spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from areoles along the stems.

Lobivia leptacantha is well adapted to drought, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
