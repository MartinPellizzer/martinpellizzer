Lindera sterrophylla

Lindera sterrophylla, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and stream valleys, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
