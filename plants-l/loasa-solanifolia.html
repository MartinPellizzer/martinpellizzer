Loasa solanifolia

Loasa solanifolia, commonly known as the purple loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Loasa solanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
