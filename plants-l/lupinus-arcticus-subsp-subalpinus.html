Lupinus arcticus subsp. subalpinus

Lupinus arcticus subsp.

subalpinus, commonly known as the Arctic lupin, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers.

The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus arcticus subsp.

subalpinus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinator populations in its native habitats.
