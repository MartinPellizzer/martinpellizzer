Linaria latifolia

Linaria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble snapdragons. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Linaria latifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
