Lobelia alata var. ottoniana

Lobelia alata var. ottoniana, commonly known as the silverleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in a silvery bloom of fine, white hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a similar silvery coating, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland settings.
