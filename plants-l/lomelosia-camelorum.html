Lomelosia camelorum

Lomelosia camelorum, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
