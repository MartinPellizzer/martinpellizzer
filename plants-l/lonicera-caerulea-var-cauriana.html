Lonicera caerulea var. cauriana

Lonicera caerulea var. cauriana, commonly known as the honeyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that range in color from yellow to deep purple, depending on the variety and maturity. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and attractive addition to gardens.
