Lobivia ferox

Lobivia ferox, commonly known as the fierce lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance. Its flowers, which appear in shades of yellow or orange, are often short-lived but striking.

Lobivia ferox is valued in horticulture for its resilience and unique appearance.
