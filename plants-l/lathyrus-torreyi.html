Lathyrus torreyi

Lathyrus torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial vine that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Lathyrus torreyi is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
