Lupinus angustifolius subsp. leucospermus

Lupinus angustifolius subsp.

leucospermus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine with white flowers, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers, which are typical of the lupine genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
