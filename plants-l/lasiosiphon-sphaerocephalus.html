Lasiosiphon sphaerocephalus

Lasiosiphon sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe siphon, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a distinctive, spherical flower head that gives it its common name. The plant features small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, globe-like cluster, typically blooming in shades of yellow and orange. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest in botanical studies due to its evolutionary adaptations.
