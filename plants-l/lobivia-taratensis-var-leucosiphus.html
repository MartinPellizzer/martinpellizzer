Lobivia taratensis var. leucosiphus

Lobivia taratensis var. leucosiphus, commonly known as the white-flowered lobivia, is a compact, succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
