Lysimachia remyi subsp. remyi

Lysimachia remyi subsp.

remyi, commonly known as the creeping yellow-wool, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It features prostrate or ascending stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered with fine, silky hairs. Small yellow flowers are borne in clusters at the leaf axils, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
