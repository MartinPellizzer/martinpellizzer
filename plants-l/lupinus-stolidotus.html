Lupinus stolidotus

Lupinus stolidotus, commonly known as the rigid lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Lupinus stolidotus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
