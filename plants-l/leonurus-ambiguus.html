Leonurus ambiguus

Leonurus ambiguus, commonly known as the lesser hedgehogweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Leonurus ambiguus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
