Lonicera periclymenum subsp. hispanica

Lonicera periclymenum subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange-red, blooming in early spring. These flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.
