Lupinus lyallii f. albiflorus

Lupinus lyallii f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered lyall lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
