Limonium sarcophyllum

Limonium sarcophyllum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiky, silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky coastlines. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium sarcophyllum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
