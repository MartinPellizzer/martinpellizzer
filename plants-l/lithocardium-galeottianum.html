Lithocardium galeottianum

Lithocardium galeottianum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip petal. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats on the ground.

Lithocardium galeottianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
