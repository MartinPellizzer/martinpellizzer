Lycianthes fendleri

Lycianthes fendleri, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along desert margins.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
