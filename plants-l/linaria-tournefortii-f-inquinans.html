Linaria tournefortii f. inquinans

Linaria tournefortii f. inquinans, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and striking violet to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
