Lobelia berlandieri subsp. brachypoda

Lobelia berlandieri subsp.

brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stalked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
