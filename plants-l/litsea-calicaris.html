Litsea calicaris

Litsea calicaris, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. It produces small white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea calicaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
