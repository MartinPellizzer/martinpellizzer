Luzula campestris var. latifolia

Luzula campestris var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are broader than the typical variety of Luzula campestris. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
