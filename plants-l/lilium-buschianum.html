Lilium buschianum

Lilium buschianum, commonly known as the Buschian lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The Buschian lily is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
