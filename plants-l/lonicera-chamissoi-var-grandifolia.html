Lonicera chamissoi var. grandifolia

Lonicera chamissoi var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange-red, appearing in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange, turning gradually from green as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
