Leptomischus flaviflorus

Leptomischus flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered leptomischus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in shaded understory environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
