Lotus pusillus subsp. vivianii

Lotus pusillus subsp.

vivianii, commonly known as the dwarf lotus, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow water and is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and small pink or white flowers.

The plant has a short stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. It is well adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients.

This subspecies is often cultivated in ponds and gardens for its ornamental value.
