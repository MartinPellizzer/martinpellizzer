Ligusticum albomarginatum

Ligusticum albomarginatum, commonly known as the white-marginled lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish or pale margin, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into rounded, brownish fruits.

Ligusticum albomarginatum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
