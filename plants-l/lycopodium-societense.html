Lycopodium societense

Lycopodium societense, commonly known as the Socotra cypress, is a unique evergreen conifer native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, and often gnarled trunk, which can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in dense, flat sprays, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
