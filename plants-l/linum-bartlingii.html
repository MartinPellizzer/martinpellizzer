Linum bartlingii

Linum bartlingii, commonly known as Bartling's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes other species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Linum bartlingii is often found in desert or semi-arid environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
