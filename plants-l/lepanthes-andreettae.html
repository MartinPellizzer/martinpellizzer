Lepanthes andreettae

Lepanthes andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a single, showy flower that typically blooms in the spring. The plant has a slender, leafless stem and possesses a fibrous root system adapted to its moist, shaded habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with intricate patterns, and it is noted for its delicate appearance.

Lepanthes andreettae is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native ecosystem.
