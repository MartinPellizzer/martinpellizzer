Lilium taliense

Lilium taliense, commonly known as the Taliensian lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lilium taliense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
