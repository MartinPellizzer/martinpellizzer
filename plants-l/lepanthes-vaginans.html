Lepanthes vaginans

Lepanthes vaginans, commonly known as the velvet slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base of the flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Lepanthes vaginans is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
