Lagerstroemia hexaptera

Lagerstroemia hexaptera, commonly known as the Chinese crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, appearing in summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, papery layers, revealing a mottled appearance.

Lagerstroemia hexaptera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
