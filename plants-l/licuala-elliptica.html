Licuala elliptica

Licuala elliptica, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately divided leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove forests. Its leaves are broad and flat, resembling those of a fern, and are used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element.

Licuala elliptica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
