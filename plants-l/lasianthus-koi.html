Lasianthus koi

Lasianthus koi, commonly known as the Koi lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
