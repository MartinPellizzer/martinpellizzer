Lomaria xiphophylla

Lomaria xiphophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf lomaria, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
