Lignocarpa

Lignocarpa, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the humid tropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The wood of lignocarpa is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it a valuable resource in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
