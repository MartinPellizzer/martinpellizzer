Lactuca japonica

Lactuca japonica, commonly known as Japanese lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, pale green leaves that are often hairy and have a mild, slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. Native to East Asia, it thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in gardens and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
