Lepidium pinnatisectum

Lepidium pinnatisectum, commonly known as the finely divided peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the family.

Lepidium pinnatisectum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
