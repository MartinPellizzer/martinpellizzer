Litsea rigidifrons

Litsea rigidifrons, commonly known as the rigid-leaved litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its rigid, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Litsea rigidifrons is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
