Lunellia rubra

Lunellia rubra, commonly known as the red moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
