Languas rosea

Languas rosea, commonly known as the roseate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a short, woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive, bell-like shape, which gives it its common name.

Languas rosea is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
