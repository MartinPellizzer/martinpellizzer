Lathyrus pulcher

Lathyrus pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of delicate, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Lathyrus pulcher thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in supporting local wildlife.
