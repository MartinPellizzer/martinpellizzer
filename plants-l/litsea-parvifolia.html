Litsea parvifolia

Litsea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits.

Litsea parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
