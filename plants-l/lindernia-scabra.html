Lindernia scabra

Lindernia scabra, commonly known as rough lindernia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from summer to fall. Lindernia scabra thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its use in both native and cultivated landscapes.
