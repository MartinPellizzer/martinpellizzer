Leucadendron concolor

Leucadendron concolor, commonly known as the silverleaf leucadendron, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces cone-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Leucadendron concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
