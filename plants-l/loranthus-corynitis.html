Loranthus corynitis

Loranthus corynitis, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries, which are red and fleshy, are consumed by birds that help disperse its seeds.

Loranthus corynitis plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
