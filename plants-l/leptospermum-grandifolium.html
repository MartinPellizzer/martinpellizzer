Leptospermum grandifolium

Leptospermum grandifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved tea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a fragrant, resinous scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
