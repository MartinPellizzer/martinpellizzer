Lucuma valenzuelana

Lucuma valenzuelana, commonly known as the Valenzuelan lucuma, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub growth habit. The plant produces large, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully mature, is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional properties.

Lucuma valenzuelana thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid climates.
