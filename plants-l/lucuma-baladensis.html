Lucuma baladensis

Lucuma baladensis, commonly known as the Andean sweet fruit, is a member of the Lauraceae family native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, egg-shaped fruit that turns bright yellow when ripe. The fruit has a creamy, sweet flavor with a slight nutty aroma, making it a prized ingredient in traditional Andean cuisine. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

Due to its unique taste and nutritional value, it is gaining popularity in global markets as a superfood.
