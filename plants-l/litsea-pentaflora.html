Litsea pentaflora

Litsea pentaflora, commonly known as the five-flowered litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
