Lysimachia vulgaris var. davurica

Lysimachia vulgaris var. davurica, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, drought-tolerant ground cover.
