Leymus baldshuanicus

Leymus baldshuanicus, commonly known as the Baldshuan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and high-altitude meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is well adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including drought and cold temperatures.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
