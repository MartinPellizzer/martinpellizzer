Lupinus burkei subsp. burkei

Lupinus burkei subsp.

burkei, commonly known as the California lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of pea-like blue to violet flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and dunes. Lupinus burkei subsp.

burkei plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local pollinators.
