Linaria filicaulis subsp. faucicola

Linaria filicaulis subsp.

faucicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
