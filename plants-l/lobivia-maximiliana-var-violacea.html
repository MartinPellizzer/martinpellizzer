Lobivia maximiliana var. violacea

Lobivia maximiliana var. violacea, commonly known as the Violet Lobivia, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a bluish-green hue and produces striking violet to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in full sun with minimal water.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
