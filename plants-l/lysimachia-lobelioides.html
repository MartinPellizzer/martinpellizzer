Lysimachia lobelioides

Lysimachia lobelioides, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lysimachia lobelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes.
