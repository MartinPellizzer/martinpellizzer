Limacia triandra

Limacia triandra, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, waxy leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, often with contrasting markings. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Limacia triandra is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
