Lophiaris pumila

Lophiaris pumila, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Lophiaris pumila is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and requiring minimal water.
