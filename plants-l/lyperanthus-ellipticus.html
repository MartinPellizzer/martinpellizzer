Lyperanthus ellipticus

Lyperanthus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination by insects.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
