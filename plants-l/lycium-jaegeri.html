Lycium jaegeri

Lycium jaegeri, commonly known as Jaeger's boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Lycium jaegeri is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
