Lavandula stoechas var. merinoi

Lavandula stoechas var. merinoi, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or Merino lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flower spikes and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
