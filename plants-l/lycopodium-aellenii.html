Lycopodium aellenii

Lycopodium aellenii, commonly known as the Aellen's clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and the presence of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its stems. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
