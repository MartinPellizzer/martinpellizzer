Leucas fulvipila

Leucas fulvipila, commonly known as the golden leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
