Licuala penduliflora

Licuala penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging leaf palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The leaves are typically green with a waxy texture and often have a distinctive drooping appearance. This plant thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
