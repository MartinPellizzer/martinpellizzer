Lavoisiera caryophyllea

Lavoisiera caryophyllea, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
