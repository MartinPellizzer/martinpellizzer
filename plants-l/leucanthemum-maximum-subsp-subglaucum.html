Leucanthemum maximum subsp. subglaucum

Leucanthemum maximum subsp.

subglaucum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flower heads, which are surrounded by white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them a bluish sheen.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
