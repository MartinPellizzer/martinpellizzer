Lavandula atlantica

Lavandula atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic lavender, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery-gray underside.

Lavandula atlantica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and for its essential oil.
