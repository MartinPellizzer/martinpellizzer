Lyciodes hayesii

Lyciodes hayesii, commonly known as the Hayes' lycopus, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Lyciodes hayesii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
