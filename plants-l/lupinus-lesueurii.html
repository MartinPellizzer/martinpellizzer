Lupinus lesueurii

Lupinus lesueurii, commonly known as the Lesueur's lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

Lupinus lesueurii is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it ecologically significant in nutrient-poor environments.
