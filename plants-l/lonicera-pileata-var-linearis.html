Lonicera pileata var. linearis

Lonicera pileata var. linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are opposite in arrangement, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, typically yellow or white, which attract pollinators. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces or as a hedge.

The berries that follow the flowers are edible and often consumed by birds.
