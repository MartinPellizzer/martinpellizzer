Lamium bifidum subsp. gevorense

Lamium bifidum subsp.

gevorense, commonly known as the Greater Dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping habit with stolons that help it spread across the ground.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
