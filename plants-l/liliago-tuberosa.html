Liliago tuberosa

Liliago tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in various environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Liliago tuberosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.
