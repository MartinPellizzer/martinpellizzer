Liparis rufina

Liparis rufina, commonly known as the red-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and multiple upright, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with reddish-brown markings, and they are borne on terminal racemes.

Liparis rufina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
