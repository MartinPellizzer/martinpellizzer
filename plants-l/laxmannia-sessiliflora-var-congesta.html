Laxmannia sessiliflora var. congesta

Laxmannia sessiliflora var. congesta, commonly known as the dense-flowered laxmannia, is a compact, mat-forming perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and low maintenance requirements.
