Lupinus gussoneanus

Lupinus gussoneanus, commonly known as the Gussone's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus gussoneanus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
