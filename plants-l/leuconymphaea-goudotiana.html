Leuconymphaea goudotiana

Leuconymphaea goudotiana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
