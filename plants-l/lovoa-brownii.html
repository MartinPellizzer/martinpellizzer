Lovoa brownii

Lovoa brownii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and emit a strong scent when crushed. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
