Lilium japonicum var. hyuuganum

Lilium japonicum var. hyuuganum, commonly known as the Hyuganum lily, is a rare and ornamental variety of lily native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive red throat, which bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bulbs that are adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and is a symbol of purity and refinement in Japanese culture.

This variety is relatively low maintenance but requires specific environmental conditions to flourish.
