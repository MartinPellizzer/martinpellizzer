Lagerstroemia poilanei var. grandis

Lagerstroemia poilanei var. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered crape myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, displaying shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has smooth, mottled bark that becomes more textured with age, and its leaves are typically ovate with a pointed tip. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

The variety 'grandis' is distinguished by its larger flowers and robust growth habit compared to other cultivars.
