Lantana canescens f. pluripedunculata

Lantana canescens f. pluripedunculata, commonly known as the many-branched lantana, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that transition from white to orange to red as they age, creating a striking color display. The plant has narrow, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and extended blooming period contribute to its appeal in both landscape design and ecological settings.
