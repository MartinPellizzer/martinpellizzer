Lasianthus maingayi var. hirtus

Lasianthus maingayi var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in whorled arrangements.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
