Levisticum caucasicum

Levisticum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal applications.
