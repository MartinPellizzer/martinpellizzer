Laelia anceps var. schroederiana

Laelia anceps var. schroederiana, commonly known as the Schröder's Laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and red. The plant typically produces 2–4 blooms per inflorescence, which are borne on long, slender stems. It prefers cool, bright conditions with moderate humidity and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for intermediate orchid growers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in both greenhouse and home settings.
