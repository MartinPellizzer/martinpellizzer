Lycopus rubellus var. arkansanus

Lycopus rubellus var. arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, purple to pink flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
