Lippia alba var. globiflora

Lippia alba var. globiflora, commonly known as white verbena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in compact clusters. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a compact growth habit, contributing to its ornamental value.
