Limeum meyeri

Limeum meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's lime, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically round, with a thin, yellowish rind and juicy, acidic green pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant prefers warm climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for subtropical regions.
