Lacinaria scabra

Lacinaria scabra, commonly known as the rough lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves that are often lobed or serrated. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters.

Lacinaria scabra is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
