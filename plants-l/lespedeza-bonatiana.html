Lespedeza bonatiana

Lespedeza bonatiana, commonly known as the pink lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually oval-shaped with a hairy texture.

Lespedeza bonatiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
