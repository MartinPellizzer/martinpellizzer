Lithomyrtus kakaduensis

Lithomyrtus kakaduensis, commonly known as the Kakadu malleeflower, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.

The Kakadu malleeflower is also an important food source for native pollinators and small mammals.
