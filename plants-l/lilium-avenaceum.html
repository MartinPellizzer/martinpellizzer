Lilium avenaceum

Lilium avenaceum, commonly known as the woolly lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and tall, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in dry periods and regenerate during favorable seasons.

The woolly lily is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
