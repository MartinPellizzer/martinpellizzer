Lancretia

Lancretia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble misty clouds, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Lancretia has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
