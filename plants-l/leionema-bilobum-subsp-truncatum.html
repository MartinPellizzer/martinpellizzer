Leionema bilobum subsp. truncatum

Leionema bilobum subsp.

truncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf leionema, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually evergreen and have a distinctive grey-green color. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
