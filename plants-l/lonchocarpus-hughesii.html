Lonchocarpus hughesii

Lonchocarpus hughesii, commonly known as the purple root or bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple roots and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
