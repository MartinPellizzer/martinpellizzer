Lycopersicon esculentum subsp. pimpinellifolium

Lycopersicon esculentum subsp.

pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the cherry tomato, is a subspecies of the domesticated tomato. It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically red, though other colors like yellow and orange may occur.

The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth and fruit production.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its flavorful, nutrient-rich fruits used in culinary applications.
