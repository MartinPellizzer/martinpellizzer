Lupinus inferior

Lupinus inferior, commonly known as the small lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Lupinus inferior is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
