Limosella aquatica var. biflora

Limosella aquatica var. biflora, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant has a unique morphology, with floating leaves and specialized structures for buoyancy. It is often found in shallow water, mud, or along the edges of ponds and streams.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
