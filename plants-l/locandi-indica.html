Locandi indica

Locandi indica, commonly known as the Indian lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, aromatic stalks that emit a citrusy scent. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Locandi indica make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and natural remedies.
