Lacinaria scariosa f. borealis

Lacinaria scariosa f. borealis, commonly known as the northern lichen, is a unique variant of the species Lacinaria scariosa.

It is characterized by its distinctive thallus structure, which is typically crustose and forms a thin, adherent layer on substrates. The plant thrives in cold, arctic or alpine environments, where it plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling. Its morphology includes a complex arrangement of lobes and pores, which aid in water retention and protection from harsh environmental conditions.

This lichen is an important indicator species for assessing the health of polar ecosystems.
