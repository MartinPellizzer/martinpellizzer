Lophiocarpus cordifolius var. madagascariensis

Lophiocarpus cordifolius var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar heartleaf legume, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its leaves are typically palmate, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
