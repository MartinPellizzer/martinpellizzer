Lecanorchis cerina

Lecanorchis cerina, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a loose spike.

Lecanorchis cerina is often found in association with other orchid species and is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
