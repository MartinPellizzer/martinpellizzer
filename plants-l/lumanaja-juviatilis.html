Lumanaja juviatilis

Lumanaja juviatilis, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble the sun, hence its common name. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its flowering period typically occurs during the dry season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lumanaja juviatilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
