Lolium brasilianum

Lolium brasilianum, commonly known as Brazilian ryegrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Lolium brasilianum is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
