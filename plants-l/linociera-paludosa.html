Linociera paludosa

Linociera paludosa, commonly known as the swamp willow, is a shrub-like plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in marshy or flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, often reddish stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by water.

Linociera paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
