Linum smithii

Linum smithii, commonly known as Smith's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes over 300 species of flowering plants. This species is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers and linear leaves. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Linum smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
