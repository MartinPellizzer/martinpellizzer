Laurus ovalifolia

Laurus ovalifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, including countries like Spain and Italy. The plant is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Laurus ovalifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
