Leptospermum sejunctum

Leptospermum sejunctum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. It is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
