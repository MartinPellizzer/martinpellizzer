Litsea dealbata var. typica

Litsea dealbata var. typica, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and typically have 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
