Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. pubiger

Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. pubiger, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed senna, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, fertile soils in lowland areas.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a notable botanical feature of this variant.
