Linociera acuminata

Linociera acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed linociera, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Linociera acuminata is often found in lowland forests and is relatively hardy in humid environments.
