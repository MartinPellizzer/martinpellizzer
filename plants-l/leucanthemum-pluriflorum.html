Leucanthemum pluriflorum

Leucanthemum pluriflorum, commonly known as the ox-eye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white petals surrounding a bright yellow central disk, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces numerous small, showy flowers throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.
