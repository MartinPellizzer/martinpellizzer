Lepidium densiflorum var. elongatum

Lepidium densiflorum var. elongatum, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescence and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
