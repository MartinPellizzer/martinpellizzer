Limonium occidentale var. procerum

Limonium occidentale var. procerum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and western Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds.

Limonium occidentale var. procerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
