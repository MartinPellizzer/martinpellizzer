Leucanthemum

Leucanthemum, commonly known as oxeye daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center. They are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and many species are commonly found in meadows and grasslands. Leucanthemum species typically have a basal rosette of leaves and can grow to varying heights depending on the species.

Their flowers bloom in the summer months and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.
