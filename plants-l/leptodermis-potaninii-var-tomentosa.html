Leptodermis potaninii var. tomentosa

Leptodermis potaninii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dense, soft pubescence that covers its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale green, with a delicate, intricate structure typical of orchids.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
