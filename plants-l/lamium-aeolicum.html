Lamium aeolicum

Lamium aeolicum, commonly known as the Aeolian deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium aeolicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
