Lepanthes erinacea

Lepanthes erinacea, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as cloud forests. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in lowland to montane regions with high moisture levels.
