Lycium carolinianum var. carolinianum

Lycium carolinianum var. carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina wolfberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
