Lycopersicon esculentum

Lycopersicon esculentum, commonly known as the tomato, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy stems, simple leaves, and vibrant red fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces flowers that are yellow in color. Its fruit, which is botanically a berry, contains numerous seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

The tomato is cultivated worldwide for its culinary and nutritional value.
