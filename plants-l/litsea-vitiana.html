Litsea vitiana

Litsea vitiana, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Litsea vitiana thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
