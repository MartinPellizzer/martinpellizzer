Lilium canadense var. parviflorum

Lilium canadense var. parviflorum, commonly known as the Canadian wild lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white to pale pink, with six petals and a spurred tepal at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is characterized by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to other lily species.
