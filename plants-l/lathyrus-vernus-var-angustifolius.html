Lathyrus vernus var. angustifolius

Lathyrus vernus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
