Linaria loeselii var. brachyceras

Linaria loeselii var. brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-capsuled toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
