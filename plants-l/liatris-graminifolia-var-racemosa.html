Liatris graminifolia var. racemosa

Liatris graminifolia var. racemosa, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, late-summer blooms.
