Lippia thymoides

Lippia thymoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong lemon scent. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Lippia thymoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
