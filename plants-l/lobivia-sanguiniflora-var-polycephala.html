Lobivia sanguiniflora var. polycephala

Lobivia sanguiniflora var. polycephala, commonly known as the blood-flowered lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the spring and summer months, which contrast strikingly with its green foliage. Its leaves are typically small and triangular, and it thrives in full sun with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
