Leptorhynchos orientalis

Leptorhynchos orientalis, commonly known as the oriental leathery fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often dark green fronds that are deeply divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
