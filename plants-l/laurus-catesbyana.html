Laurus catesbyana

Laurus catesbyana, commonly known as the sweet bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the coastal areas of the Carolinas. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The tree typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat.
