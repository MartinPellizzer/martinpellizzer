Lychnis pilosa

Lychnis pilosa, commonly known as the downy scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lychnis pilosa is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
