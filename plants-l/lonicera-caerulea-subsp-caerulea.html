Lonicera caerulea subsp. caerulea

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

caerulea, commonly known as the edible honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile ornamental and edible plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
