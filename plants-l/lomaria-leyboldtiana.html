Lomaria leyboldtiana

Lomaria leyboldtiana, commonly known as the Leyboldt's lomaria, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lomaria leyboldtiana is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
