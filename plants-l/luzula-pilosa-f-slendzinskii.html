Luzula pilosa f. slendzinskii

Luzula pilosa f. slendzinskii, commonly known as the slender woodrush, is a botanical variant of the woodrush genus Luzula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
