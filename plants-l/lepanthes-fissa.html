Lepanthes fissa

Lepanthes fissa, commonly known as the fissured orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive white to pale blue coloration and a yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Lepanthes fissa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
