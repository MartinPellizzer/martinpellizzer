Laportea oblongata

Laportea oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and a distinctive, aromatic scent. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Laportea oblongata is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
