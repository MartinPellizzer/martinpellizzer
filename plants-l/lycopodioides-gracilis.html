Lycopodioides gracilis

Lycopodioides gracilis, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features whorled leaves that are typically narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. Lycopodioides gracilis reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a trait common among primitive vascular plants.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability and biodiversity.
