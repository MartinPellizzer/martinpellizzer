Lovoa mildbraedii

Lovoa mildbraedii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for ecological and economic reasons.
