Lindsaea schlechteri

Lindsaea schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's spikemoss, is a member of the family Lindsaeaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a prostrate or ascending stem with spirally arranged leaves that are often lanceolate in shape. Its reproductive structures include strobila, which produce spores for propagation.

Lindsaea schlechteri is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in moist, forested areas.
