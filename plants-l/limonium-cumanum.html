Limonium cumanum

Limonium cumanum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are drought-tolerant.

Limonium cumanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
