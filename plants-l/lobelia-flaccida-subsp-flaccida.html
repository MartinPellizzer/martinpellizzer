Lobelia flaccida subsp. flaccida

Lobelia flaccida subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, erect stems that bear whorled leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
