Lilium bakerianum subsp. sempervivoideum

Lilium bakerianum subsp.

sempervivoideum, commonly known as the Baker's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces underground bulbs for storage and propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
