Lolium lesdainii

Lolium lesdainii, commonly known as the Lesdain's ryegrass, is a species of grass native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Lolium lesdainii is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential for agricultural use.
