Lilaeopsis patagonica

Lilaeopsis patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like structure. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in arid or coastal areas.
