Leptodactylon pungens var. hookeri

Leptodactylon pungens var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

The plant contains latex, which can be irritating to the skin.
