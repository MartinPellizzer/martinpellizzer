Lonicera ciliosa f. youngii

Lonicera ciliosa f. youngii, commonly known as the youngii honeysuckle, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, which bloom in early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators.
