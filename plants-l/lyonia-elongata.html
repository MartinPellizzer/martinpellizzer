Lyonia elongata

Lyonia elongata, commonly known as the evergreen huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red berries that appear in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lyonia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
