Lathriogyna decipiens

Lathriogyna decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive lathyrus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
