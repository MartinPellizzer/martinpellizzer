Lycopodioides myosuroides

Lycopodioides myosuroides, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
