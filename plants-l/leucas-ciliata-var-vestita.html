Leucas ciliata var. vestita

Leucas ciliata var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet leucas, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
