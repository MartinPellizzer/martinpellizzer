Lactuca bandyopadhyana

Lactuca bandyopadhyana, commonly known as Bandyopadhyana's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lobed leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
