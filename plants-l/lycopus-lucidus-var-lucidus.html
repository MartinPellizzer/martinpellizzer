Lycopus lucidus var. lucidus

Lycopus lucidus var. lucidus, commonly known as shining bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in spikes, blooming in late summer.

The plant is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
