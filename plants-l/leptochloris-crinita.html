Leptochloris crinita

Leptochloris crinita, commonly known as the hairy green alga, is a freshwater green alga characterized by its filamentous growth and the presence of fine, hair-like structures on its thallus.

It belongs to the family Chlorococcales and is typically found in slow-moving or still freshwater environments. The alga's cells are generally spherical to ellipsoidal and contain one or more chloroplasts. It reproduces both asexually through cell division and sexually through the formation of gametes.
