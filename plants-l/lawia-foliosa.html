Lawia foliosa

Lawia foliosa, commonly known as the featherleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
