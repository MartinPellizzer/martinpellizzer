Linaria pallidiflora

Linaria pallidiflora, commonly known as the paleflower toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Linaria pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
