Lonicera confusa var. glabrocalyx

Lonicera confusa var. glabrocalyx, commonly known as the glabrous-stem honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often glabrous (hairless) stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its berries are small, dark blue to black, and serve as a food source for birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
