Lomaria crenulata

Lomaria crenulata, commonly known as the serrated lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. Lomaria crenulata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
