Lupinus sparsiflorus var. arizonicus

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. arizonicus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
