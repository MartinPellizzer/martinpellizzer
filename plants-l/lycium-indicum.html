Lycium indicum

Lycium indicum, commonly known as Chinese boxthorn, is a shrub native to Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are typically clustered in small inflorescences.

Lycium indicum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
