Leighia buphthalmiflora

Leighia buphthalmiflora, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its botanical features include fleshy, strap-like leaves and a complex, highly specialized floral structure adapted for pollination by specific insects.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
