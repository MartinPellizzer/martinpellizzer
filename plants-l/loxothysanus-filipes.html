Loxothysanus filipes

Loxothysanus filipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive botanical structure.

It features a thallus composed of a dense, leathery layer of fungal hyphae interwoven with algal cells, which provide the primary photosynthetic function. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on bark or soil surfaces. Its morphology is characterized by a thin, elongated body with delicate, thread-like branches.

Loxothysanus filipes plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
