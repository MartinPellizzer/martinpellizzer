Liabum candidum var. glanduliferum

Liabum candidum var. glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular white vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its glandular structures on the stems and leaves are distinctive features that aid in its identification.
