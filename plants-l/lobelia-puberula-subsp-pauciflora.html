Lobelia puberula subsp. pauciflora

Lobelia puberula subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
