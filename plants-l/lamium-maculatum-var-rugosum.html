Lamium maculatum var. rugosum

Lamium maculatum var. rugosum, commonly known as spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with red, purple, or maroon spots, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or violet, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant ground cover.
