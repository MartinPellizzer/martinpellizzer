Lepidium loulanicum

Lepidium loulanicum, commonly known as the Loulan cress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and Kazakhstan.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lepidium loulanicum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments and potential medicinal properties.
