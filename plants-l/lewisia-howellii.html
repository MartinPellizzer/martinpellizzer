Lewisia howellii

Lewisia howellii, commonly known as Howell's Lewisia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of blue-green, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among gardeners in arid climates.
