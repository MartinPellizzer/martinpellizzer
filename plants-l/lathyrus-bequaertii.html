Lathyrus bequaertii

Lathyrus bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
