Lecaniodiscus vaughaniae

Lecaniodiscus vaughaniae, commonly known as the golden star moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Lecanoraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and bright yellow to orange thallus, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows on rocks or soil in moist, shaded environments, often in temperate regions. Its structure consists of a flattened, leaf-like thallus with distinct lobes and a central stalk.

Lecaniodiscus vaughaniae is noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
