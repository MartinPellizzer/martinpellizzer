Laurus culilaban

Laurus culilaban, commonly known as the Philippine laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries that are edible. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to bay leaves.

The tree thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
