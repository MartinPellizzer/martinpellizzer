Linaria manuelis

Linaria manuelis, commonly known as the Manuel's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, linear leaves that are opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Linaria manuelis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
