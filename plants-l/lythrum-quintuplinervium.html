Lythrum quintuplinervium

Lythrum quintuplinervium, commonly known as the five-veined lythrum, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as the poinsettia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of vibrant pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
