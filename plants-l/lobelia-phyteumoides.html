Lobelia phyteumoides

Lobelia phyteumoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia phyteumoides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
