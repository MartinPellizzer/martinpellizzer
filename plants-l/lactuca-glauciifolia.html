Lactuca glauciifolia

Lactuca glauciifolia, commonly known as blue-leaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and basal rosette of narrow, bluish-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by fluffy, white seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lactuca glauciifolia is not typically cultivated for food but is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique appearance and ecological role.
