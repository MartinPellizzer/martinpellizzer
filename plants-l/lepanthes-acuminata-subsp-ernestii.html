Lepanthes acuminata subsp. ernestii

Lepanthes acuminata subsp.

ernestii, commonly known as the Ernest's slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America. It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces elongated, pendulous flower spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
