Lyciodes crenulatum

Lyciodes crenulatum, commonly known as the roughleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves with wavy, serrated margins, and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Lyciodes crenulatum is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
