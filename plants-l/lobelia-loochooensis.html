Lobelia loochooensis

Lobelia loochooensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu lobelia, is a flowering plant native to Japan, specifically the Ryukyu Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes.

Lobelia loochooensis is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
