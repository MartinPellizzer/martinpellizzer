Landolphia owariensis var. tomentella

Landolphia owariensis var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly arrowleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
