Lycopodium alopecuroides f. intermedium

Lycopodium alopecuroides f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss plant characterized by its unique morphological traits.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant with small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, typical of the Lycopodiaceae family, which contribute to its reproductive strategy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

This fern ally plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
