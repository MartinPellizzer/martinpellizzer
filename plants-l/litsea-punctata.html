Litsea punctata

Litsea punctata, commonly known as the spotted litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often dotted with small, raised spots, giving it its common name. It produces small white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible.

Litsea punctata is often found in lowland evergreen forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
