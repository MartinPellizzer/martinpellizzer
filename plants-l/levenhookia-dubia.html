Levenhookia dubia

Levenhookia dubia, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,500 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
