Liparia calycina

Liparia calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are often hairy, giving it a velvety texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Liparia calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
