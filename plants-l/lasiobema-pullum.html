Lasiobema pullum

Lasiobema pullum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. Its flowers open at night and close during the day, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
