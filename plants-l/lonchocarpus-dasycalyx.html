Lonchocarpus dasycalyx

Lonchocarpus dasycalyx, commonly known as the hairy pod senna, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
