Lithospermum fruticosum var. canum

Lithospermum fruticosum var. canum, commonly known as the narrowleaf lithosperm, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial use.
