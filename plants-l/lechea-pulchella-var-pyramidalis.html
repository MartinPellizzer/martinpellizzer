Lechea pulchella var. pyramidalis

Lechea pulchella var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid lechea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, pyramidal growth habit and features small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
