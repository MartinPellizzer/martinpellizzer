Licuala pulchella

Licuala pulchella, commonly known as the golden palm, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a golden or bronze tinge along the edges. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.

Licuala pulchella is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade, making it versatile for various landscaping applications.
