Lotus corniculatus var. longisiliquosus

Lotus corniculatus var. longisiliquosus, commonly known as the long silique horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically followed by elongated, bean-like pods called silique.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
