Lepanthes quartzicola

Lepanthes quartzicola, commonly known as the quartz orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in cloud forests. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to retain moisture.

Lepanthes quartzicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
