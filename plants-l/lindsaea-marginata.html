Lindsaea marginata

Lindsaea marginata, commonly known as the marginate spikemoss, is a member of the family Lindsaeaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern ally that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive rosette-like structure with closely packed, lanceolate leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tip of the plant and produce spores.

Lindsaea marginata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in rich, organic soil.
