Leptandra virginica var. incarnata

Leptandra virginica var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink bladdernut, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white, bladder-shaped fruits that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental fruits and ecological benefits to pollinators.
