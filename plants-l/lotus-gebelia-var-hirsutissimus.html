Lotus gebelia var. hirsutissimus

Lotus gebelia var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
