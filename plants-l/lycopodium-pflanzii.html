Lycopodium pflanzii

Lycopodium pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with tiny, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain the sporangia that produce spores.

Lycopodium pflanzii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
