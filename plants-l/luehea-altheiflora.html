Luehea altheiflora

Luehea altheiflora, commonly known as the sacred tree or yellow bell, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense canopy. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Luehea altheiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties and has cultural significance in several indigenous communities.
