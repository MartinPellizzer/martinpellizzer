Lycopodium clavatum f. trilobum

Lycopodium clavatum f. trilobum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a variant of the common clubmoss, characterized by its unique foliage structure.

This fern-like plant belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to various regions in the northern hemisphere. It features small, scale-like leaves that form dense clusters, giving it a moss-like appearance. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically found at the base of the foliage.

Lycopodium clavatum f. trilobum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
