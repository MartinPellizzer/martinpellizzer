Litsea rangoonensis

Litsea rangoonensis, commonly known as the Burmese litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, dark purple berries.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
