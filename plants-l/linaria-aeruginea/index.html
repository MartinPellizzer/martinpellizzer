Linaria Aeruginea

Linaria aeruginea, also known as the blue toadflax, is a delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to violet in color. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked in favor of more showy garden plants.
