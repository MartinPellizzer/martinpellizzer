Lachnopylis congesta

Lachnopylis congesta, commonly known as the clustered lichen grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
