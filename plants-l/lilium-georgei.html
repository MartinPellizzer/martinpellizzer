Lilium georgei

Lilium georgei, commonly known as George's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pink to red flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Lilium georgei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
