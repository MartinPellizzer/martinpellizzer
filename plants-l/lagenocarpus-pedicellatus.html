Lagenocarpus pedicellatus

Lagenocarpus pedicellatus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and grows as an erect shrub or small tree. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
