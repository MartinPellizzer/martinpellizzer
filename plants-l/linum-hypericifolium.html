Linum hypericifolium

Linum hypericifolium, commonly known as the golden flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Linum hypericifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and historical significance.
