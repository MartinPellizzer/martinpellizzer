Leptochloa filiformis f. attenuata

Leptochloa filiformis f. attenuata, commonly known as the slender grass or narrow-leaved leptochloa, is a variant of the slender grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its thin, elongated leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
