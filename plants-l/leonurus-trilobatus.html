Leonurus trilobatus

Leonurus trilobatus, commonly known as heart-leaved speedwell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Leonurus trilobatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
