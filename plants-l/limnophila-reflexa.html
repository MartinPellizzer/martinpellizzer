Limnophila reflexa

Limnophila reflexa, commonly known as the water thyme, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and creeping growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Limnophila reflexa is also known for its ability to form dense mats, which can play a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
