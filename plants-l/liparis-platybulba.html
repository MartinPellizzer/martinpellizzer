Liparis platybulba

Liparis platybulba, commonly known as the broadbulb orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
