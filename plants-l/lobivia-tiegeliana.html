Lobivia tiegeliana

Lobivia tiegeliana, commonly known as the Tiegeli's lobivia, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It features cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in short, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Its succulent leaves are adapted for water storage, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Lobivia tiegeliana is prized in horticulture for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
