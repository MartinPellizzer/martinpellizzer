Linaria microcalyx var. minor

Linaria microcalyx var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf toadflax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with distinctive bilateral symmetry. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
