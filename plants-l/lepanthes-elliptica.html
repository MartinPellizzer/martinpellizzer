Lepanthes elliptica

Lepanthes elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes elliptica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
