Lonicera caucasica subsp. caucasica

Lonicera caucasica subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers in clusters, usually yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
