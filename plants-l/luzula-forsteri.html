Luzula forsteri

Luzula forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are greenish-white in color.

Luzula forsteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
