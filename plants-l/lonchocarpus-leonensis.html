Lonchocarpus leonensis

Lonchocarpus leonensis, commonly known as the Mexican trumpet tree, is a leguminous plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces large, woody pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
