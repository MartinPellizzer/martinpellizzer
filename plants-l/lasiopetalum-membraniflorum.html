Lasiopetalum membraniflorum

Lasiopetalum membraniflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistflower, is an evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyllous habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
