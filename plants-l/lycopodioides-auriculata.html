Lycopodioides auriculata

Lycopodioides auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and features scale-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, known as strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky substrates.

Lycopodioides auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a pioneer species in certain habitats.
