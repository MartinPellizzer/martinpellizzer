Lapithea gentianoides

Lapithea gentianoides, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lapithea gentianoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
