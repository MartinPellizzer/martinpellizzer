Lippia africana

Lippia africana, commonly known as African verbena, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lippia africana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
