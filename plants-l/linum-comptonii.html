Linum comptonii

Linum comptonii, commonly known as Compton's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Linum comptonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
