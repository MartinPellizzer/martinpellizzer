Lathyrus venosus var. arkansanus

Lathyrus venosus var. arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with pinnately compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its trifoliate leaves and distinctive venation patterns are key botanical characteristics.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as streambanks and woodland edges.
