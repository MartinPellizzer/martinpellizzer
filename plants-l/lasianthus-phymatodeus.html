Lasianthus phymatodeus

Lasianthus phymatodeus, commonly known as the thick-leafed mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
