Leptilon subdecurrens

Leptilon subdecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf leptilon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and mountain slopes.

Leptilon subdecurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
