Lepidium africanum f. glabratum

Lepidium africanum f. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth wall pepper, is a variant of the African cress species.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.
