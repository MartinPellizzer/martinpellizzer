Lycianthes multiflora var. extustomentosa

Lycianthes multiflora var. extustomentosa, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, white to purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces yellow, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, tangy flavor. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
