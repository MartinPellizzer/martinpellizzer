Lamium vreemanii

Lamium vreemanii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive netted pattern on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Lamium vreemanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
