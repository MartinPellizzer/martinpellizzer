Lobelia × salteri

Lobelia × salteri, commonly known as the hybrid lobelia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid between Lobelia sanguinea and Lobelia cardinalis, resulting in a plant with striking red and orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures.
