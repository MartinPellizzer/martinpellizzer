Lindsaea lapeyrousei subsp. fijiensis

Lindsaea lapeyrousei subsp.

fijiensis, commonly known as the Fiji lindsaea, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly Fiji. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its delicate appearance and unique growth habit make it a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
