Linum strictum var. corymbulosum

Linum strictum var. corymbulosum, commonly known as the clustered flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ornamental gardening.
