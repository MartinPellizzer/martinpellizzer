Loropetalum

Loropetalum, commonly known as Chinese fringeflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the heather family (Ericaceae).

It is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy clusters of pink, red, or white flowers in spring, which are often surrounded by long, slender bracts. Loropetalum species are popular in landscaping due to their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

They are also valued for their evergreen foliage and adaptability to different climates.
