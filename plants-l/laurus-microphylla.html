Laurus microphylla

Laurus microphylla, commonly known as the bay leaf tree or small-leaved laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are widely used in cooking, particularly in the preparation of soups and stews. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Laurus microphylla thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
