Leandra amplexicaulis

Leandra amplexicaulis, commonly known as the emerald mist orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy green leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique appearance and delicate fragrance.
