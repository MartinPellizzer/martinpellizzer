Lantana trifolia var. quadriverticillata

Lantana trifolia var. quadriverticillata, commonly known as the four-branch lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
