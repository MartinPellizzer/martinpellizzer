Lepidium ibericum

Lepidium ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh, arid conditions.

Lepidium ibericum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
