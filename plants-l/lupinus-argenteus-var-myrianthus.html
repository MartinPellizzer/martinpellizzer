Lupinus argenteus var. myrianthus

Lupinus argenteus var. myrianthus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, clustered purple or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged with small, rounded leaflets.

Lupinus argenteus var. myrianthus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
