Luzula glabrescens

Luzula glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth woodrush, is a perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula glabrescens is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
