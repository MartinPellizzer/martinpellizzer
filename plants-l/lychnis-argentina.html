Lychnis argentina

Lychnis argentina, commonly known as the Argentine pink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Lychnis argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
