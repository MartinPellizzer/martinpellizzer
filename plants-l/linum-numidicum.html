Linum numidicum

Linum numidicum, commonly known as wild flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in a capsule and are used in traditional medicine for their mucilage content.

Linum numidicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
