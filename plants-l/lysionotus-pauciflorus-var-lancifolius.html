Lysionotus pauciflorus var. lancifolius

Lysionotus pauciflorus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starburry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The leaves are lanceolate, meaning they are long and tapering, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
