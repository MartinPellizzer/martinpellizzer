Lightfootia subulata

Lightfootia subulata, commonly known as the slender lightfootia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Lightfootia subulata thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
