Limonium malfatanicum

Limonium malfatanicum, commonly known as the Malfatan sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces spikes of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often found in sandy or gravelly habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium malfatanicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate saline conditions.
