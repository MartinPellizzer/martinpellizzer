Lupinus latifolius subsp. dudleyi

Lupinus latifolius subsp.

dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's broadleaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
