Lycium leiospermum

Lycium leiospermum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Lycium leiospermum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
