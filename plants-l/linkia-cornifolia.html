Linkia cornifolia

Linkia cornifolia, commonly known as the horned hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Linkia cornifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
