Luehea grandiflora

Luehea grandiflora, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a smooth, light-colored bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Luehea grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
