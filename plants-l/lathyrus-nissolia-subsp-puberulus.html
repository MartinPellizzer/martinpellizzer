Lathyrus nissolia subsp. puberulus

Lathyrus nissolia subsp.

puberulus, commonly known as the pubescent grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically in late spring. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
