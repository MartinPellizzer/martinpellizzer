Leobordea pariflora

Leobordea pariflora, commonly known as the twin-leaf orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that display a vibrant mix of purple and white hues. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its leaves are elongated and often paired, giving it the common name "twin-leaf.".
