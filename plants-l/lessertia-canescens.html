Lessertia canescens

Lessertia canescens, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale gray bark and lance-shaped, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
