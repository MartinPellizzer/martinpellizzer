Leontodon latilobus

Leontodon latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with prominent central disk and ray florets. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy bracts beneath the flower heads.
