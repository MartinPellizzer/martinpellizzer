Loxocarpus burttii

Loxocarpus burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's evergreen tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red or purple fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
