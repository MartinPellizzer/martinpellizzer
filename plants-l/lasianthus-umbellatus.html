Lasianthus umbellatus

Lasianthus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble umbrellas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of red or orange tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
