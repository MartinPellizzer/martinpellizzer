Lepanthes jubata

Lepanthes jubata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces small, delicate leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Lepanthes jubata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
