Lupinus falsomutabilis

Lupinus falsomutabilis, commonly known as the false mutabilis lupine, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Native to specific regions of the southwestern United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
