Lebretonia glechomifolia

Lebretonia glechomifolia, commonly known as the creeping bluestem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and blue-violet flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Lebretonia glechomifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
