Lobelia angulata var. papuana

Lobelia angulata var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan narrow-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, clustered in dense spikes, and bloom during the wet season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
