Lobivia rossii var. walterspielii

Lobivia rossii var. walterspielii, commonly known as the Walt's Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a dense cluster of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
