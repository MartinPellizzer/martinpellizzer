Lychnis kigesiensis subsp. ragazziana

Lychnis kigesiensis subsp.

ragazziana, commonly known as the Japanese fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
