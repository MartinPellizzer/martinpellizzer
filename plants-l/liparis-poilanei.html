Liparis poilanei

Liparis poilanei, commonly known as the Poilane's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Liparis poilanei is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
