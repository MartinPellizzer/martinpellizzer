Lindsaea salomonis

Lindsaea salomonis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its reproductive structures, including sori and sporangia, are located on the underside of its leaves, facilitating spore dispersal.

Lindsaea salomonis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
