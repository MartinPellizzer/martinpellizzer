Lasiacis divaricata var. leptostachya

Lasiacis divaricata var. leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed lasiacis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers, which are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
