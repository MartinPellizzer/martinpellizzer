Lobivia lateritia var. camataquiensis

Lobivia lateritia var. camataquiensis, commonly known as the Camataquiensis Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a typical adaptation to its harsh, dry environment.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
