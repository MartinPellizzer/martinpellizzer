Loranthus sanguineus var. pulcher

Loranthus sanguineus var. pulcher, commonly known as the crimson mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, red berries that are a source of food for various birds and mammals. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique botanical characteristics make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
