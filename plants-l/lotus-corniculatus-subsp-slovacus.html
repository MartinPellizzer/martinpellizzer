Lotus corniculatus subsp. slovacus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

slovacus, commonly known as the Slovak horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces slender, coiled pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
