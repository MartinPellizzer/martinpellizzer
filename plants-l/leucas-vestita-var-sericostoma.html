Leucas vestita var. sericostoma

Leucas vestita var. sericostoma, commonly known as the silver-haired leucas, is a variant of the leucas plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
