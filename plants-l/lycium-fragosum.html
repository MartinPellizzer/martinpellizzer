Lycium fragosum

Lycium fragosum, commonly known as the firethorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and bright red berries that appear in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The berries are edible when fully ripe but can be toxic if consumed unripe.
