Leptopus cordifolius

Leptopus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and glossy, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
