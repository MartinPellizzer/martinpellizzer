Laurus malabatrum

Laurus malabatrum, commonly known as the Malabar laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a fragrant aroma when crushed. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
