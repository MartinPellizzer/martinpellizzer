Limonium spathulatum

Limonium spathulatum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its flowers bloom from late summer to autumn, adding vibrant color to seaside landscapes.

Limonium spathulatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
