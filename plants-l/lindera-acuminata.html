Lindera acuminata

Lindera acuminata, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dark red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

Lindera acuminata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
