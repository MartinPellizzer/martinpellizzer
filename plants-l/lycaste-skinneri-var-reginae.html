Lycaste skinneri var. reginae

Lycaste skinneri var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's lycaste, is a hybrid orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, tropical environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
