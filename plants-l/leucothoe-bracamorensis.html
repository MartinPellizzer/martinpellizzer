Leucothoe bracamorensis

Leucothoe bracamorensis, commonly known as the Camino Real honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
