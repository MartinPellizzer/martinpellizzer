Leontodon pavonii

Leontodon pavonii, commonly known as the purple leontodon, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pinkish-yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
