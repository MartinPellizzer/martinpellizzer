Lobivia tuberculosa

Lobivia tuberculosa, commonly known as the tubercled lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert plants. Its small, spiky leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in dry, well-drained soil in gardens and greenhouses.
