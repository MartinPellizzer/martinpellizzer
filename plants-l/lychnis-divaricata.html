Lychnis divaricata

Lychnis divaricata, commonly known as the spreading campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its flowers are pink or red, with five petals that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lychnis divaricata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
