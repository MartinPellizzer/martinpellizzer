Leuzea berardioides

Leuzea berardioides, commonly known as the Mexican leuzea, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Leuzea berardioides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited for xeriscaping.
