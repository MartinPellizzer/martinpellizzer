Lycopersicon esculentum var. validum

Lycopersicon esculentum var. validum, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a botanical variety native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its genetic makeup makes it a valuable resource for breeding programs aimed at improving cultivated tomato varieties.
