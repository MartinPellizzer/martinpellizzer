Lobelia nicotianifolia var. brevipedicellata

Lobelia nicotianifolia var. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are short and often branched. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
