Laurus longifolia

Laurus longifolia, commonly known as sweet bay or laurel, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its fragrance and medicinal properties.

Laurus longifolia is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of culinary and aromatic compounds.
