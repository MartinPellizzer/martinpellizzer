Leptospermum ericoides var. lineare

Leptospermum ericoides var. lineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
