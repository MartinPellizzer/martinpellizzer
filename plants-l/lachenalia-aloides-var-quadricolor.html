Lachenalia aloides var. quadricolor

Lachenalia aloides var. quadricolor, commonly known as the four-colored lachenalia, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with distinctive red, orange, and yellow markings. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water during its dormant period.

This species is prized for its vibrant colors and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
