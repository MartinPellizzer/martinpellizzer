Linum usitatissimum var. stenophyllum

Linum usitatissimum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a cultivated variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and blue or purple flowers. This variety is primarily grown for its fibers, which are used in textile production, as well as for its seeds, which can be used for oil extraction. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

It is a herbaceous annual that is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
