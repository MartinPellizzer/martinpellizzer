Limacia borneensis

Limacia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean lima bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Limacia borneensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
