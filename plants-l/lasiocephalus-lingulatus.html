Lasiocephalus lingulatus

Lasiocephalus lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are bright yellow and are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Overall, Lasiocephalus lingulatus is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in harsh environments.
