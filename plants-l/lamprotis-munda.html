Lamprotis munda

Lamprotis munda, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its stinging hairs can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
