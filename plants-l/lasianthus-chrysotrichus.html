Lasianthus chrysotrichus

Lasianthus chrysotrichus, commonly known as the golden-haired lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble tufts of hair, giving it its common name. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
