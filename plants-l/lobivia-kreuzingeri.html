Lobivia kreuzingeri

Lobivia kreuzingeri, commonly known as the Kreuzinger's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white or pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with a shallow root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
