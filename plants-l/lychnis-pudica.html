Lychnis pudica

Lychnis pudica, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. Its flowers bloom from June to August, and it often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

Lychnis pudica is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
