Limonium aurigniense

Limonium aurigniense, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium aurigniense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand saline environments.
