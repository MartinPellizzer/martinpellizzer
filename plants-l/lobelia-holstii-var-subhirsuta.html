Lobelia holstii var. subhirsuta

Lobelia holstii var. subhirsuta, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
