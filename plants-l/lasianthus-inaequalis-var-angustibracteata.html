Lasianthus inaequalis var. angustibracteata

Lasianthus inaequalis var. angustibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The variety is distinguished by its narrow bracts, which are a key feature in its classification.
