Lepidium bonariense var. gayi

Lepidium bonariense var. gayi, commonly known as the Argentine cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a peppery taste similar to other members of its family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its seeds are used in some traditional culinary applications and have potential nutritional value.
