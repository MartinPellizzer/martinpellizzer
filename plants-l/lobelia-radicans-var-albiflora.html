Lobelia radicans var. albiflora

Lobelia radicans var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered trailing lobelia, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats along the ground. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are predominantly white, with some varieties showing pale blue or pink hues. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
