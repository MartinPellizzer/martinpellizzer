Lobivia marsoneri var. rubescens

Lobivia marsoneri var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leafed lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features compact, rounded stems that are typically green or blue-green in color, with distinctive red or pinkish hues on the upper parts of the leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the cooler months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
