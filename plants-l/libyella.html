Libyella

Libyella, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse its seeds by wind. Libyella is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep roots and waxy leaves to reduce water loss.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
