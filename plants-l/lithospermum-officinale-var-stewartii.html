Lithospermum officinale var. stewartii

Lithospermum officinale var. stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's lithosperm, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of lithospermic acid in its roots.
