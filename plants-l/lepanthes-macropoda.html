Lepanthes macropoda

Lepanthes macropoda, commonly known as the large-lobed slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette. Lepanthes macropoda thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in the understory of tropical forests.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
