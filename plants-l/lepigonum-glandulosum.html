Lepigonum glandulosum

Lepigonum glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky lespedeza, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in glandular hairs, giving it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

Lepigonum glandulosum is often found in open woods, meadows, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
