Lepidium pinnatifidum

Lepidium pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrowleaf shepherds' purse, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants. The plant features small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Lepidium pinnatifidum is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
