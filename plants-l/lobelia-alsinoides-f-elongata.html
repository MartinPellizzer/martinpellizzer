Lobelia alsinoides f. elongata

Lobelia alsinoides f. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 3 feet tall, adorned with small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and delicate foliage.
