Ludia imontiensis

Ludia imontiensis, commonly known as the mountain ludia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in montane grasslands and forest margins.
