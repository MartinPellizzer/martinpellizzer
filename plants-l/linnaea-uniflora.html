Linnaea uniflora

Linnaea uniflora, commonly known as the twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive pairs of opposite leaves and its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Linnaea uniflora thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological systems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
