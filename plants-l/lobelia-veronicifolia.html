Lobelia veronicifolia

Lobelia veronicifolia, commonly known as Virginia spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lobelia veronicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
