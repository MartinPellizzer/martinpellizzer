Lycopodium taxifolium var. passerinoides

Lycopodium taxifolium var. passerinoides, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear small, scale-like leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces sporangia in clusters called strobili, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

This variety is often found in the Appalachian region of North America and is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
