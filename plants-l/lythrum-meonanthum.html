Lythrum meonanthum

Lythrum meonanthum, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species such as the common lythrum. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
