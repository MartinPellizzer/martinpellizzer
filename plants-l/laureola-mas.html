Laureola mas

Laureola mas, commonly known as the Spanish laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used in culinary applications. Laureola mas thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in Mediterranean gardens.

Its aromatic leaves and historical use in perfumery and medicine highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
