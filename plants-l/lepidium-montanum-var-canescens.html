Lepidium montanum var. canescens

Lepidium montanum var. canescens, commonly known as the rough shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
