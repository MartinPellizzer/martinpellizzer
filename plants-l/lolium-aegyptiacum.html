Lolium aegyptiacum

Lolium aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
