Lupinus angustifolius f. belorussicus

Lupinus angustifolius f. belorussicus, commonly known as the Belarusian narrow-leaved lupine, is a variant of the narrow-leaved lupin native to Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, typical of the Lupinus genus. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in agricultural settings.
