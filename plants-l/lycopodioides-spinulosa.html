Lycopodioides spinulosa

Lycopodioides spinulosa, commonly known as the spinulose clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, moss-like growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are spirally arranged and have a distinct, serrated edge.

Lycopodioides spinulosa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
