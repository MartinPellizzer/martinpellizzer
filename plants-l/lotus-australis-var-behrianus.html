Lotus australis var. behrianus

Lotus australis var. behrianus, commonly known as the Australian water-lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Australia.

It features heart-shaped, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are adapted to anchor in muddy substrates, while its stems are flexible to withstand water currents.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
