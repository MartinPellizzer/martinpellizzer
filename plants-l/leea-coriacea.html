Leea coriacea

Leea coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf leeah, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree with woody stems and simple, opposite leaves that are usually rough to the touch. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
