Loranthus cactorum

Loranthus cactorum, commonly known as the mistletoe vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded in the host's bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and influencing the health of its host trees.
