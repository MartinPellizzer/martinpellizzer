Lobelia rollandia

Lobelia rollandia, commonly known as the red lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Lobelia rollandia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
