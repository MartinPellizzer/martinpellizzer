Loranthus holstii

Loranthus holstii, commonly known as the Holst's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
