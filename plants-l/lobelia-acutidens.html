Lobelia acutidens

Lobelia acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sharp tip, and its flowers are small, tubular, and often blue or purple in color. Lobelia acutidens is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, bell-shaped blooms.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
