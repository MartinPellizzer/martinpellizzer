Lemaireocereus lepidanthus

Lemaireocereus lepidanthus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
