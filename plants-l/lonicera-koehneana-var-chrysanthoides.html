Lonicera koehneana var. chrysanthoides

Lonicera koehneana var. chrysanthoides, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
