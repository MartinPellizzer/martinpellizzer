Lycopodium clavatum var. equisetoides

Lycopodium clavatum var. equisetoides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and feathery, scale-like leaves.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of North America and Europe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its unique structure includes a stem that remains upright and a branching system that resembles a fern.

Lycopodium clavatum var. equisetoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
