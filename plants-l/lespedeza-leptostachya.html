Lespedeza leptostachya

Lespedeza leptostachya, commonly known as narrow-stemmed lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy white or pale pink flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its trifoliate leaves are opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lespedeza leptostachya is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, and it serves as a valuable forage plant for wildlife.
