Linum hirsutum subsp. anatolicum

Linum hirsutum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian flax, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in flax cultivation, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
