Lespedeza cyrtobotrya f. virginea

Lespedeza cyrtobotrya f. virginea, commonly known as the Virginia lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-brown flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

This species is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
