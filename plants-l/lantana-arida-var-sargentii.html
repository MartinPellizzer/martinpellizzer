Lantana arida var. sargentii

Lantana arida var. sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's lantana, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Lantana arida var. sargentii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
