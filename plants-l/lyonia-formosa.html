Lyonia formosa

Lyonia formosa, commonly known as the pink lyonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or white bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Lyonia formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
