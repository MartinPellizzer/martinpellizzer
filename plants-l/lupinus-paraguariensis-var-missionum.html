Lupinus paraguariensis var. missionum

Lupinus paraguariensis var. missionum, commonly known as the Mission lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences with pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various ecological conditions.

The Mission lupine is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility in native ecosystems.
