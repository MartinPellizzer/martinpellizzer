Lasiococca locii

Lasiococca locii, commonly known as the silky holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Lasiococca locii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
