Lonicera hispida var. anisocalyx

Lonicera hispida var. anisocalyx, commonly known as the rough honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
