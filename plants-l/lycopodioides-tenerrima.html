Lycopodioides tenerrima

Lycopodioides tenerrima, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features scale-like leaves that form dense, mat-like clusters near the ground. Its reproductive structures, known as strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

Lycopodioides tenerrima is often found in moist, acidic soils in shaded forest environments.
