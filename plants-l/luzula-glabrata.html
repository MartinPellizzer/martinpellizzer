Luzula glabrata

Luzula glabrata, commonly known as the smooth woodrush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and densely packed. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, greenish-white flowers.

Luzula glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
