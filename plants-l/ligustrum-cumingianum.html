Ligustrum cumingianum

Ligustrum cumingianum, commonly known as the Cuming's privet, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically dark blue to black when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ligustrum cumingianum is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
