Loranthus lonchiphyllus

Loranthus lonchiphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

It is native to regions of Africa, including parts of South Africa, and thrives in forest ecosystems. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
