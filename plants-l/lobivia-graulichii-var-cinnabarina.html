Lobivia graulichii var. cinnabarina

Lobivia graulichii var. cinnabarina, commonly known as the Cinnabar Lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy, and often reddish-pink leaves that resemble small, rounded lobes. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a dramatic contrast to its foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a sought-after specimen among succulent enthusiasts.
