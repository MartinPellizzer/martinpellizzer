Levana uniflora

Levana uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower levana, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces a single, showy flower per stem. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its dry environment. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Levana uniflora is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
