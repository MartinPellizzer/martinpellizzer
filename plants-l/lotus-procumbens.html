Lotus procumbens

Lotus procumbens, commonly known as the spreading lotus, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water and wetlands, with its prostrate stems spreading across the substrate. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The roots of Lotus procumbens are known for their ability to filter water and improve water quality.
