Linaria anthirrhinoides

Linaria anthirrhinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Linaria anthirrhinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
