Lobelia frutescens

Lobelia frutescens, commonly known as Indian tobacco, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, erect stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
