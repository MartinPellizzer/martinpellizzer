Lophiolepis vallis-demonii subsp. calabra

Lophiolepis vallis-demonii subsp.

calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience to drought and its ability to flourish in harsh, coastal environments.
