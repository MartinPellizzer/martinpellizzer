Lupinus argenteus var. utahensis

Lupinus argenteus var. utahensis, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a food source for local wildlife.
