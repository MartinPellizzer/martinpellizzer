Limnophila laotica

Limnophila laotica, commonly known as the Laos water starwort, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and they are typically greenish-white in color.

Limnophila laotica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
