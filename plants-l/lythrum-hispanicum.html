Lythrum hispanicum

Lythrum hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish lythrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lythraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. Its botanical characteristics include showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
