Lauro-cerasus aquifolioides

Lauro-cerasus aquifolioides, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its tolerance to urban environments.
