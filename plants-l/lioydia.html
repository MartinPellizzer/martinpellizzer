Lioydia

Lioydia, commonly known as the common lioydia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Lioydia is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often growing in moist, shady environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
