Lobivia cylindrica

Lobivia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem that is often covered in a layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which is a common adaptation to minimize water evaporation in harsh environments.

Lobivia cylindrica is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
