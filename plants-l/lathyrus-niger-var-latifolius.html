Lathyrus niger var. latifolius

Lathyrus niger var. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved black vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
