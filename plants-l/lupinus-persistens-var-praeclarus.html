Lupinus persistens var. praeclarus

Lupinus persistens var. praeclarus, commonly known as the Showy Lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has palmately compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
