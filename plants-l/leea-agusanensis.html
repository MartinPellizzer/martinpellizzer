Leea agusanensis

Leea agusanensis, commonly known as the Agusan leea, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines, particularly found in the region of Agusan.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Leea agusanensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
