Leptopus ocymoides

Leptopus ocymoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.
