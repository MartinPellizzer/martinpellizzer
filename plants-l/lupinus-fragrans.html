Lupinus fragrans

Lupinus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant lupin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus fragrans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
