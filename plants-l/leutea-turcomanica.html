Leutea turcomanica

Leutea turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Leutea turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its unique ecological niche.
