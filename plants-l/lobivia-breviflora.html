Lobivia breviflora

Lobivia breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered lobivia, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
