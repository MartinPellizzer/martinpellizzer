Lobivia saltensis var. zapallarensis

Lobivia saltensis var. zapallarensis, commonly known as the Zapallar lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically spiny and adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
