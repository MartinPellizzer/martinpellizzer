Leioligo rigida

Leioligo rigida, commonly known as the rigid leioligo, is a member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology makes it a distinctive species within its genus.
