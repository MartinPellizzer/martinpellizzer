Lonicera delavayi

Lonicera delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which bloom in the spring. Its berries are red, fleshy, and edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.

Lonicera delavayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
