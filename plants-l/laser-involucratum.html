Laser involucratum

Laser involucratum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble a crown, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and native plant gardens.
