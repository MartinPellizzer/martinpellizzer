Ligularia alticola

Ligularia alticola, commonly known as the alpine ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
