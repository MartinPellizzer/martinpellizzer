Liparis arnoglossophylla

Liparis arnoglossophylla, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive golden lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forested areas of Southeast Asia.

Liparis arnoglossophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
