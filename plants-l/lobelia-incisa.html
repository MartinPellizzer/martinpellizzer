Lobelia incisa

Lobelia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia incisa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
