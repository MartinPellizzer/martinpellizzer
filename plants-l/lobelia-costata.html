Lobelia costata

Lobelia costata, commonly known as the fringed lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lobelia costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
