Luzula plumosa var. reflexa

Luzula plumosa var. reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arched or reflexed at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
