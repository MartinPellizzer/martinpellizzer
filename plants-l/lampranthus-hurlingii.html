Lampranthus hurlingii

Lampranthus hurlingii, commonly known as the orange trumpet plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that resemble trumpets, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
