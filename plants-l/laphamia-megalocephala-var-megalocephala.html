Laphamia megalocephala var. megalocephala

Laphamia megalocephala var. megalocephala, commonly known as the large-headed laphamia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of native plant communities and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
