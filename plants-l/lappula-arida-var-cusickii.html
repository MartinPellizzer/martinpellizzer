Lappula arida var. cusickii

Lappula arida var. cusickii, commonly known as the desert lappula, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and pale yellow to greenish flowers.
