Lonchocarpus longiunguiculatus

Lonchocarpus longiunguiculatus, commonly known as the long-beaked urara, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, elongated pods that contain several seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enhance soil fertility. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
