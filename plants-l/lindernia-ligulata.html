Lindernia ligulata

Lindernia ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lindernia ligulata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought conditions.
