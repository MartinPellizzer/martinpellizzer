Lychnis gillettii

Lychnis gillettii, commonly known as Gillett's campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gillett's campion is a herbaceous perennial that grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, making it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
