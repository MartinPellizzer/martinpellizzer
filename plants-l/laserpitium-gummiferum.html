Laserpitium gummiferum

Laserpitium gummiferum, commonly known as the gummed grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces a sticky, resinous substance from its stems, which gives it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
