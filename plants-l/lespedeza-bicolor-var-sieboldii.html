Lespedeza bicolor var. sieboldii

Lespedeza bicolor var. sieboldii, commonly known as the pink-leaf lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red foliage, which contrasts with its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, three-lobed leaves and has a fibrous root system.

Lespedeza bicolor var. sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
