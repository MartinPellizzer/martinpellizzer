Lotus gibbus

Lotus gibbus, commonly known as the horned lotus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small horns, giving it its common name. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in wetland environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are typically pink or purple.

The botanical features of Lotus gibbus make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
