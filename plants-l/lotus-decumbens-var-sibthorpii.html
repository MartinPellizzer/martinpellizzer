Lotus decumbens var. sibthorpii

Lotus decumbens var. sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems that often lie along the ground, producing small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
