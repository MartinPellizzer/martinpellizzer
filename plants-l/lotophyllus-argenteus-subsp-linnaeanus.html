Lotophyllus argenteus subsp. linnaeanus

Lotophyllus argenteus subsp.

linnaeanus, commonly known as the silverleaf lotophyllus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas. It features lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, reflective hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale yellow during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
