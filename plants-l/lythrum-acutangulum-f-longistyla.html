Lythrum acutangulum f. longistyla

Lythrum acutangulum f. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled loosestrife, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lythraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its elongated staminodes, which distinguish it from other forms of Lythrum acutangulum.
