Lobelia wollastonii var. scaettana

Lobelia wollastonii var. scaettana, commonly known as the scarlet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
