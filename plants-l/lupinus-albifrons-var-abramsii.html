Lupinus albifrons var. abramsii

Lupinus albifrons var. abramsii, commonly known as the San Diego lupine, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant has palmate leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and its role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
