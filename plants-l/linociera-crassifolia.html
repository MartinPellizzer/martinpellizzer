Linociera crassifolia

Linociera crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved linociera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
