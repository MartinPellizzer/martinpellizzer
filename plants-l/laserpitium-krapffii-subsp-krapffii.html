Laserpitium krapffii subsp. krapffii

Laserpitium krapffii subsp.

krapffii, commonly known as the Krapff's hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces bright yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head surrounded by pale green bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
