Lamium montanum subsp. endtmannii

Lamium montanum subsp.

endtmannii, commonly known as the alpine dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated edge.

The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink, which are clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cool, high-altitude climates.
