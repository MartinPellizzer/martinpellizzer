Limonium commune var. serotinum

Limonium commune var. serotinum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue bracts and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-gray hue.

Limonium commune var. serotinum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal conditions.
