Ludwigia octovalvis

Ludwigia octovalvis, commonly known as the red Ludwigia, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange foliage, which becomes more intense under strong lighting and nutrient-rich conditions. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in tiny, translucent hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical freshwater environments, often found in slow-moving rivers and lakes.

Ludwigia octovalvis is popular in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
