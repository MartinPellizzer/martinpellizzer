Loranthus emarginatus

Loranthus emarginatus, commonly known as the black mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are typically red or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is found in various regions of Africa and is often seen growing on acacia and other woody plants.
