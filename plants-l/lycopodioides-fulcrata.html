Lycopodioides fulcrata

Lycopodioides fulcrata, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. Lycopodioides fulcrata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
