Lonicera fragrantissima subsp. phyllocarpa

Lonicera fragrantissima subsp.

phyllocarpa, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, which bloom in early summer. Its berries are red or orange, turning dark when mature, and are an important food source for birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
