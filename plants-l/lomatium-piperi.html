Lomatium piperi

Lomatium piperi, commonly known as the western lomatium, is a member of theApiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, where it thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it produces a distinctive, spiny fruit that aids in seed dispersal.

Lomatium piperi is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
