Laplacea semiserrata var. typica

Laplacea semiserrata var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laplacea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
