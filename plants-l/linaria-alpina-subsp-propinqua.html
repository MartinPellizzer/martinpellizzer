Linaria alpina subsp. propinqua

Linaria alpina subsp.

propinqua, commonly known as the alpine toadflax, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
