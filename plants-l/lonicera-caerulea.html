Lonicera caerulea

Lonicera caerulea, commonly known as the honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with serrated edges.

Lonicera caerulea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of food for both humans and wildlife.
