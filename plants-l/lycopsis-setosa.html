Lycopsis setosa

Lycopsis setosa, commonly known as the slender lycopus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the upper stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lycopsis setosa is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
