Lonicera japonica var. sempervillosa

Lonicera japonica var. sempervillosa, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a deciduous to semi-evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are red or orange, are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
