Lithospermum gentianoides

Lithospermum gentianoides, commonly known as purple rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, dark purple to black berries in the fall. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
