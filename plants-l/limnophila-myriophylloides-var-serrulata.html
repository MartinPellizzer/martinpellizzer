Limnophila myriophylloides var. serrulata

Limnophila myriophylloides var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated water starwort, is a rare aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often serrated along the edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
