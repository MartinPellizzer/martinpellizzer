Lobelia rhombifolia

Lobelia rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many other bellflower species.

Lobelia rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
