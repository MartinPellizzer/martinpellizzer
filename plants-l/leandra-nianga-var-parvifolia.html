Leandra nianga var. parvifolia

Leandra nianga var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved leandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
