Luzula erecta var. pallescens

Luzula erecta var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in boreal and subarctic environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it spread vegetatively.

The species is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in bogs, wet meadows, and along stream banks.
