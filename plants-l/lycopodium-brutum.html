Lycopodium brutum

Lycopodium brutum, commonly known as the ground pine, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, scale-like leaves that resemble pine needles. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Lycopodium brutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
