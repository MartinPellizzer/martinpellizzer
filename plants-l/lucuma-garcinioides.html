Lucuma garcinioides

Lucuma garcinioides, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a tropical tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, yellowish-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is rich in nutrients such as beta-carotene and antioxidants. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and produces flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, high-altitude environments.
