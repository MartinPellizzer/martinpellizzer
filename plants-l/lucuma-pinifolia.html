Lucuma pinifolia

Lucuma pinifolia, commonly known as the Andean star fruit, is a unique plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is a shrub that produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, contributing to its distinct appearance.

The fruit is often used in traditional Andean cuisine and has gained popularity in modern culinary applications.
