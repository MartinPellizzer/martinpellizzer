Linaria peloponnesiaca

Linaria peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Linaria peloponnesiaca thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
