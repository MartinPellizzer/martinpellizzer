Lodhra cucullata

Lodhra cucullata, commonly known as the sacred bell flower, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with its bark and flowers used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are ovate and have a slightly rough texture, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
