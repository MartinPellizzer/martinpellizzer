Lycianthes bambusarum

Lycianthes bambusarum, commonly known as the bamboo leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that resemble bamboo leaves, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into berries containing several seeds.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
