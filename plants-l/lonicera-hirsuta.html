Lonicera hirsuta

Lonicera hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, oval leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically appearing in early spring. Its berries are red or orange and are edible for birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Lonicera hirsuta is often found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
