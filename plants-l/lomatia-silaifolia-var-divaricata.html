Lomatia silaifolia var. divaricata

Lomatia silaifolia var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender lomatia, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its woody stems are typically branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal floral display.
