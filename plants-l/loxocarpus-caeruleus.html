Loxocarpus caeruleus

Loxocarpus caeruleus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Loxocarpus caeruleus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
