Lupinus deflexus

Lupinus deflexus, commonly known as the bent lupin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of pea-like, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Lupinus deflexus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
