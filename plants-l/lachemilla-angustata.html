Lachemilla angustata

Lachemilla angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in early spring. Lachemilla angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
