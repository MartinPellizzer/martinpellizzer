Loranthus adamsii

Loranthus adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees, facilitating propagation.

Loranthus adamsii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host species.
