Lupinus melaphyllus

Lupinus melaphyllus, commonly known as the dark-leaf lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of spiny, bean-like pods. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with a dark green color and a slightly hairy texture.

Lupinus melaphyllus is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
