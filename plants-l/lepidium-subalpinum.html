Lepidium subalpinum

Lepidium subalpinum, commonly known as alpine cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and is often seen in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium subalpinum is adapted to cold climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
