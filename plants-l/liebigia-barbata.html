Liebigia barbata

Liebigia barbata, commonly known as the barbed liebigia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Liebigia barbata is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
