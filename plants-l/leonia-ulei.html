Leonia ulei

Leonia ulei, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length, often growing along tree trunks or other supports. The leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and deep green, with a slightly waxy texture. Its most distinctive feature is the clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, creating a striking visual effect.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
