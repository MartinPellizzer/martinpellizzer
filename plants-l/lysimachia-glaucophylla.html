Lysimachia glaucophylla

Lysimachia glaucophylla, commonly known as silverleaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lysimachia glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
