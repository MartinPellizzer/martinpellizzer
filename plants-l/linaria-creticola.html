Linaria creticola

Linaria creticola, commonly known as the Cretan toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae. This annual herb is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

Its small, lance-shaped leaves and showy blooms make it a distinctive and ornamental species.
