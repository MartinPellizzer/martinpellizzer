Liparis odontostoma

Liparis odontostoma, commonly known as the toothed lip, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes a specialized lip that aids in pollination.
