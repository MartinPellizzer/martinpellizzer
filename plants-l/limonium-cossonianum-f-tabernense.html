Limonium cossonianum f. tabernense

Limonium cossonianum f. tabernense, commonly known as the "Sea Lavender," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, preferring well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate saline conditions.
