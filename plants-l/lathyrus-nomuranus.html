Lathyrus nomuranus

Lathyrus nomuranus, commonly known as the Japanese vetch, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features slender, climbing stems with small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in racemes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
