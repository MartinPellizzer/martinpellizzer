Lactuca perennis var. cichoriifolia

Lactuca perennis var. cichoriifolia, commonly known as Italian endive, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features rosettes of dark green, curly leaves that are often used in salads or cooked dishes. The plant produces yellow flowers in the second year, which are followed by small, spiky fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its leaves contain lactucine, a bitter compound that contributes to its distinctive flavor.
