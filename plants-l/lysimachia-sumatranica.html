Lysimachia sumatranica

Lysimachia sumatranica, commonly known as the Sumatran chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite in arrangement, with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Lysimachia sumatranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
