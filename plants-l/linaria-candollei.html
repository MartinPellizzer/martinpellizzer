Linaria candollei

Linaria candollei, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Linaria candollei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.

Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the leaves often covered in fine, silky hairs.
