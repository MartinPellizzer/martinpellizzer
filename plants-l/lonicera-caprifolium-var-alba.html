Lonicera caprifolium var. alba

Lonicera caprifolium var. alba, commonly known as white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
