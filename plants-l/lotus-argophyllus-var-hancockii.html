Lotus argophyllus var. hancockii

Lotus argophyllus var. hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's water lotus, is a rare aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pale pink to white flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically floating or partially submerged. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate at the bottom of ponds or lakes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and water gardens.
