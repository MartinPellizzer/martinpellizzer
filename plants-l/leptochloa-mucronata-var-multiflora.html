Leptochloa mucronata var. multiflora

Leptochloa mucronata var. multiflora, commonly known as the clustered spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy blades. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and may have a bluish-green hue, while the stems are often reddish or purple at the base.

This variety is well adapted to disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
