Lupinus luteus f. volovnenkoae

Lupinus luteus f. volovnenkoae, commonly known as the Volovnenko's yellow lupine, is a rare botanical variant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and unique foliage structure.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its morphology includes slender stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.
