Lupinus laudandrus

Lupinus laudandrus, commonly known as the golden lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Lupinus laudandrus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
