Lilium bulbiferum proles croceum

Lilium bulbiferum proles croceum, commonly known as the golden bulb lily, is a rare and striking member of the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep yellow, with a distinctive golden hue in the center. The plant produces bulbous structures that can give rise to new plants, a trait that contributes to its propagation. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
