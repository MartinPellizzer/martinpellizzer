Leptopyrum fumarioides

Leptopyrum fumarioides, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which are typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
