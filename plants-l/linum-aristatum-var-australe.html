Linum aristatum var. australe

Linum aristatum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian flax or narrow-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Linum aristatum var. australe is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
