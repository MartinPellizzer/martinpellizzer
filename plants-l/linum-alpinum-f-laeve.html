Linum alpinum f. laeve

Linum alpinum f. laeve, commonly known as the smooth alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
