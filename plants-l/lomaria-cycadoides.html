Lomaria cycadoides

Lomaria cycadoides, commonly known as the African cycad, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically appearing on separate plants. Lomaria cycadoides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it a unique component of its native ecosystems.
