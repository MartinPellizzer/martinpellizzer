Lithops pseudotruncatella var. archerae

Lithops pseudotruncatella var. archerae, commonly known as the "ice plant" or "living stone," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that resemble stones, helping it blend into its arid environment. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rocky appearance. It requires minimal water and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance make it a fascinating subject for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
