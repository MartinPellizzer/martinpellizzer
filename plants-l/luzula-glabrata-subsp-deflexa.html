Luzula glabrata subsp. deflexa

Luzula glabrata subsp.

deflexa, commonly known as the bent-leaf wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often curved or bent at the base, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in temperate regions.
