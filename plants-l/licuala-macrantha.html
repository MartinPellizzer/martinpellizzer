Licuala macrantha

Licuala macrantha, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its leaves are fibrous and are used for thatching and basket-making.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove forests.
