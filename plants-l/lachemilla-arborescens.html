Lachemilla arborescens

Lachemilla arborescens, commonly known as the tree sedum, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
