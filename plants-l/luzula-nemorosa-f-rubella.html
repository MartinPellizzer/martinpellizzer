Luzula nemorosa f. rubella

Luzula nemorosa f. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered wood rush, is a variant of the wood rush plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and striking reddish or pinkish flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, three-lobed flowers arranged in a cylindrical cluster.

The leaves are generally narrow, linear, and have a dark green to reddish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.
