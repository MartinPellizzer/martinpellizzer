Lasiolepis multijuga

Lasiolepis multijuga, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, with stems that are usually unbranched. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
