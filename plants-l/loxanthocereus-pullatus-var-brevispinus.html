Loxanthocereus pullatus var. brevispinus

Loxanthocereus pullatus var. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined night blooming cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a ribbed stem and short, spiny areoles. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

The varietal name "brevispinus" refers to its short spines, distinguishing it from other varieties of Loxanthocereus.
