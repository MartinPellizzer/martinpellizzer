Lytocaryum insigne

Lytocaryum insigne, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

Lytocaryum insigne is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its vibrant floral display.
