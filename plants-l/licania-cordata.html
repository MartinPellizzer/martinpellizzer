Licania cordata

Licania cordata, commonly known as the scarlet pitta, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. Licania cordata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a visually appealing and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
