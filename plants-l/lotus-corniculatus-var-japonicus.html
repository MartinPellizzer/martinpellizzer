Lotus corniculatus var. japonicus

Lotus corniculatus var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has small, hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
