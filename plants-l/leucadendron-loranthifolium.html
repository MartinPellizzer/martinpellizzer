Leucadendron loranthifolium

Leucadendron loranthifolium, commonly known as the silver leaf candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in dense, conical inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
