Lupinus sericeus var. barbiger

Lupinus sericeus var. barbiger, commonly known as the silky lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and spires of blue to violet flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
