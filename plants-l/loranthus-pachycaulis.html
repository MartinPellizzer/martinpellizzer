Loranthus pachycaulis

Loranthus pachycaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southern United States.

It has a succulent, woody stem and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

The plant is often found in forested areas, where it contributes to ecological interactions between host trees and wildlife.
