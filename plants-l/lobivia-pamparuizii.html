Lobivia pamparuizii

Lobivia pamparuizii, commonly known as the Pamparuizii Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
