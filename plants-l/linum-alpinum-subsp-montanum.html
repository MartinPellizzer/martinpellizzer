Linum alpinum subsp. montanum

Linum alpinum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as alpine flax, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are small and capsule-like.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
