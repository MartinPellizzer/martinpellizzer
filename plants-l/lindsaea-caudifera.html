Lindsaea caudifera

Lindsaea caudifera, commonly known as the long-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that often trail from its central stem, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are typical of ferns in the family Polypodiaceae. Lindsaea caudifera thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in the rainforest canopy.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
