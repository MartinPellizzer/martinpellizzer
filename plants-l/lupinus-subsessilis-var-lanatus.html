Lupinus subsessilis var. lanatus

Lupinus subsessilis var. lanatus, commonly known as the woolly lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus subsessilis var. lanatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
