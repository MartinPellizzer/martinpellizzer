Lilium candidum var. salonikae

Lilium candidum var. salonikae, commonly known as the Salonika white lily, is a rare and elegant variant of the white lily.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers with a slight pinkish hue at the base. Native to the Balkan region, this plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
