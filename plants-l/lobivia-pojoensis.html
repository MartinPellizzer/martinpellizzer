Lobivia pojoensis

Lobivia pojoensis, commonly known as the Pojo Basin lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
