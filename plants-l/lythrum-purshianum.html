Lythrum purshianum

Lythrum purshianum, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like clusters of purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
