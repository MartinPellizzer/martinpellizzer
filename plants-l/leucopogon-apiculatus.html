Leucopogon apiculatus

Leucopogon apiculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. It typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodlands or heathlands.

Leucopogon apiculatus is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with low rainfall.
