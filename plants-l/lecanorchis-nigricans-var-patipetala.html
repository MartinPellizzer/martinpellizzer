Lecanorchis nigricans var. patipetala

Lecanorchis nigricans var. patipetala, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It produces dark purple to black flowers that are often adorned with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as pine forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its striking coloration and is considered a rare and specialized orchid within its range.
