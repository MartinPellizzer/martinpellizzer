Lightfootia oxycoccoides

Lightfootia oxycoccoides, commonly known as the mountain huckleberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to the cold, dry conditions of its habitat.

Lightfootia oxycoccoides is also known for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.
