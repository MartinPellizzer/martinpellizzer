Lupinus bingenensis var. subsaccatus

Lupinus bingenensis var. subsaccatus, commonly known as the Bingen lupine, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
