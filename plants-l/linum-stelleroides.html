Linum stelleroides

Linum stelleroides, commonly known as Steller's flax, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Linum stelleroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
