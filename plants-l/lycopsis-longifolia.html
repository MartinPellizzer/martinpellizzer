Lycopsis longifolia

Lycopsis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved lycopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lycopsidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Lycopsis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
