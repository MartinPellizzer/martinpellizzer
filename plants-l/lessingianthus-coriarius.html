Lessingianthus coriarius

Lessingianthus coriarius, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
