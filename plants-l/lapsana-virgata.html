Lapsana virgata

Lapsana virgata, commonly known as common cudweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Lapsana virgata is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
