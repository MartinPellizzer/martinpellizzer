Lupinus arbustus subsp. silvicola

Lupinus arbustus subsp.

silvicola, commonly known as the woodland lupine, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and open woodlands. Lupinus arbustus subsp.

silvicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
