Lupinus sericeus subsp. asotinensis

Lupinus sericeus subsp.

asotinensis, commonly known as the Asotin lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
