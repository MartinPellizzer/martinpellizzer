Laserpitium emilianum

Laserpitium emilianum, commonly known as the Emilian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these habitats.
