Lupinus condensiflorus

Lupinus condensiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus condensiflorus is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
