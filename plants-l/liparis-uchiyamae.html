Liparis uchiyamae

Liparis uchiyamae, commonly known as the Uchiyama's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Japan.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has a slender, leafless stem that produces a single spike of small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Liparis uchiyamae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
