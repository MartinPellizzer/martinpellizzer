Lecythis retusa

Lecythis retusa, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or yellow walnut, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit with a hard, woody shell enclosing a single large seed. Its seeds are highly valued for their edible kernel, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
