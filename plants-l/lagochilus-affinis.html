Lagochilus affinis

Lagochilus affinis, commonly known as the woolly rock hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
