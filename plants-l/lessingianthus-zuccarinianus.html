Lessingianthus zuccarinianus

Lessingianthus zuccarinianus, commonly known as the Zuccarinian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and discoid florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a prominent central disc.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
