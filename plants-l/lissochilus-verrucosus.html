Lissochilus verrucosus

Lissochilus verrucosus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
