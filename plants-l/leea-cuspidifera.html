Leea cuspidifera

Leea cuspidifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leeah, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in botany and horticulture.
