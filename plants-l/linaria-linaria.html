Linaria linaria

Linaria linaria, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that are tubular in shape and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Linaria linaria thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.
