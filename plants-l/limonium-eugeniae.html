Limonium eugeniae

Limonium eugeniae, commonly known as the pink sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to purple flower heads that resemble small, delicate holly berries. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium eugeniae is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
