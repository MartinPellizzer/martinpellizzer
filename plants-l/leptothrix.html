Leptothrix

Leptothrix, commonly known as the sheath-forming bacteria, is a genus of filamentous bacteria that forms gelatinous sheaths around its cells.

These sheaths are composed primarily of extracellular polysaccharides and can vary in thickness and structure depending on environmental conditions. Leptothrix species are often found in freshwater environments, where they play a role in the microbial loop by facilitating the cycling of nutrients. They are also capable of oxidizing iron and manganese, contributing to the geochemical processes in aquatic ecosystems.

Their unique morphology and ecological functions make them an important subject of study in microbiology and environmental science.
