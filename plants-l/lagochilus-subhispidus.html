Lagochilus subhispidus

Lagochilus subhispidus, commonly known as the hairy lips' root, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Lagochilus subhispidus is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
