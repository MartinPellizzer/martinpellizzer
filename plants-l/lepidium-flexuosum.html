Lepidium flexuosum

Lepidium flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are alternate, linear to oblanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
