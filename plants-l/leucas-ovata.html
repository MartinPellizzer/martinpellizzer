Leucas ovata

Leucas ovata, commonly known as the oval leucas, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
