Laportea leytensis

Laportea leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
