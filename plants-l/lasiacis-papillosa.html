Lasiacis papillosa

Lasiacis papillosa, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white, sticky berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique structure and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
