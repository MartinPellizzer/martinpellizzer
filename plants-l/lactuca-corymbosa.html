Lactuca corymbosa

Lactuca corymbosa, commonly known as the common chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of blue to purple basal leaves and a tall, branching stem that produces small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are often used in salads, and the roots can be roasted and consumed as a coffee substitute.

The plant contains lactucin, a bitter compound that contributes to its distinctive flavor.
