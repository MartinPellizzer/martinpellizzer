Lithospermum linearifolium

Lithospermum linearifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved rock pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that often grow in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and arranged alternately along the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with roots and leaves containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
