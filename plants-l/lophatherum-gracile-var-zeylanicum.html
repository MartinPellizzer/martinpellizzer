Lophatherum gracile var. zeylanicum

Lophatherum gracile var. zeylanicum, commonly known as *Ceylon arrowroot*, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, valued for their starchy, edible qualities.

This species is often cultivated for its nutritional content and traditional medicinal applications.
