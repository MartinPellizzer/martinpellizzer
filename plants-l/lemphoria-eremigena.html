Lemphoria eremigena

Lemphoria eremigena, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry landscapes, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in its drought-prone habitat.
