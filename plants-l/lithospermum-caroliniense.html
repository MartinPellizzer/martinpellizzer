Lithospermum caroliniense

Lithospermum caroliniense, commonly known as Carolina gromwell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows in moist, wooded areas and is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Lithospermum caroliniense is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
