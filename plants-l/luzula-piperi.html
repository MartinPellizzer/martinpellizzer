Luzula piperi

Luzula piperi, commonly known as the Piper's wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually basal, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Luzula piperi is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
