Lepanthes calimae

Lepanthes calimae, commonly known as the Calima orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, adding to the biodiversity of the tropical ecosystem.

Lepanthes calimae is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native environment.
