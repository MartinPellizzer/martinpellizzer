Loasa tricolor var. genuina

Loasa tricolor var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features palmate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
