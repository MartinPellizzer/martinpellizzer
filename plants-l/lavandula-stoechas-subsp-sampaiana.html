Lavandula stoechas subsp. sampaiana

Lavandula stoechas subsp.

sampaiana, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or the purple lavender, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flower spikes that rise above the foliage.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in perfumery and herbal remedies.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique, elongated calyx and its more compact growth habit compared to other lavender species.
