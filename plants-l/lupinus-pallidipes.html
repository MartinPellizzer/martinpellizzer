Lupinus pallidipes

Lupinus pallidipes, commonly known as the pale lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus pallidipes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in open grasslands and rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plant species in its habitat.
