Lespedeza inabensis

Lespedeza inabensis, commonly known as Japanese lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Lespedeza inabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and hardiness.
