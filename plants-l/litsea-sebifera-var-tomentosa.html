Litsea sebifera var. tomentosa

Litsea sebifera var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The flowers are typically small and clustered, and the fruit is a small, smooth drupe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
