Lycium fuscum

Lycium fuscum, commonly known as the black wolfberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water retention.

Lycium fuscum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
