Loranthus vanderystii

Loranthus vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree health and providing food for wildlife.
