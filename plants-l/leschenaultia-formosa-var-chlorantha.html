Leschenaultia formosa var. chlorantha

Leschenaultia formosa var. chlorantha, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with green or blue hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
