Lomatium nevadense var. cupulatum

Lomatium nevadense var. cupulatum, commonly known as the cupulatus lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The showy white to pale yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
