Lespedeza hirta var. appressipilis

Lespedeza hirta var. appressipilis, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in open woods, fields, and disturbed sites.

It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
