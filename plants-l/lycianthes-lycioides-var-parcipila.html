Lycianthes lycioides var. parcipila

Lycianthes lycioides var. parcipila, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features simple, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
