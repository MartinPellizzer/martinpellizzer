Leipoldtia compacta

Leipoldtia compacta, commonly known as the dwarf leipoldtia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in mild climates.
