Lomatophyllum saundersii

Lomatophyllum saundersii, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered together.

Lomatophyllum saundersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual charm.
