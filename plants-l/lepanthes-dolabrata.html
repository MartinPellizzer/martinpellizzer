Lepanthes dolabrata

Lepanthes dolabrata, commonly known as the sickle-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped petals and sepals, which often display vibrant colors such as white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators like bees and moths.

Lepanthes dolabrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
