Lupinus laxiflorus var. inyoensis

Lupinus laxiflorus var. inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. This variety thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
