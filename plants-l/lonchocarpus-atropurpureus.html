Lonchocarpus atropurpureus

Lonchocarpus atropurpureus, commonly known as purple senna, is a tropical shrub belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of small, purple to reddish-pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive pods that are typically hairy and contain several seeds. It is native to parts of Central and South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
