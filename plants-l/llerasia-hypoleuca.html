Llerasia hypoleuca

Llerasia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
