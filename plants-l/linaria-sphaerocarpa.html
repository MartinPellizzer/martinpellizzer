Linaria sphaerocarpa

Linaria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-flowered toadflax, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. Linaria sphaerocarpa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
