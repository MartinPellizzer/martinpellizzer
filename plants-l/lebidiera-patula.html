Lebidiera patula

Lebidiera patula, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth on both sides, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Lebidiera patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
