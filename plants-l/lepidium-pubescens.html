Lepidium pubescens

Lepidium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and hairy stems and leaves. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetland areas.
