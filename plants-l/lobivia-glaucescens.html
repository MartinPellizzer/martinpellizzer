Lobivia glaucescens

Lobivia glaucescens, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded shape with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive periods of drought.

Lobivia glaucescens is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
