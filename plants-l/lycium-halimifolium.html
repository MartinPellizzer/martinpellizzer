Lycium halimifolium

Lycium halimifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf lycium, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Lycium halimifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
