Lepidium exiguiflorum

Lepidium exiguiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Lepidium exiguiflorum is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
