Lepanthes lanceipetala

Lepanthes lanceipetala, commonly known as the lance-leaf slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lepanthes lanceipetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
