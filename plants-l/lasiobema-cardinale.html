Lasiobema cardinale

Lasiobema cardinale, commonly known as the golden candelabra, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that features bright yellow flowers clustered in dense, branched inflorescences resembling a candelabra. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The golden candelabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
