Linum macraei var. marticorenae

Linum macraei var. marticorenae, commonly known as the Marticorenae flax, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces capsules as its fruiting structure.
