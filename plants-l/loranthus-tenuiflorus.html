Loranthus tenuiflorus

Loranthus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the families Myrtaceae and Rutaceae.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often greenish or yellowish in color. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts.

Loranthus tenuiflorus is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
