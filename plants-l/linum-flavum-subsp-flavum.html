Linum flavum subsp. flavum

Linum flavum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and its historical use in fiber production.
