Livistona oliviformis

Livistona oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-shaped palm, is a species of palm native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and wetland areas.

Livistona oliviformis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and parks.
