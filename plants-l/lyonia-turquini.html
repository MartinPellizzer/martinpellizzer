Lyonia turquini

Lyonia turquini, commonly known as the Turkish lyonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense foliage that thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Lyonia turquini is often found in rocky or forested areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
