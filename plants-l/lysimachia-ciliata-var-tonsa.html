Lysimachia ciliata var. tonsa

Lysimachia ciliata var. tonsa, commonly known as the hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers clustered in loose inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
