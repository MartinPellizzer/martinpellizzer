Lepidium beamanii

Lepidium beamanii, commonly known as the Beaman's cress, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and has small, pinnately divided leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, and it produces small, spherical seed pods.

Lepidium beamanii is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
