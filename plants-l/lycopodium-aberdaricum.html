Lycopodium aberdaricum

Lycopodium aberdaricum, commonly known as the Aberdeenshire clubmoss, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, spirally arranged leaves and a sporophyte that produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or acidic soils. Its reproductive system relies entirely on spore dispersal, a feature common among primitive vascular plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lycopodium aberdaricum is considered a conservation concern.
