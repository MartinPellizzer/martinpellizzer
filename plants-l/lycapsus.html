Lycapsus

Lycapsus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

Lycapsus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
