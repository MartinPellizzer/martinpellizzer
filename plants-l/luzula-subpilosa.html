Luzula subpilosa

Luzula subpilosa, commonly known as the slender woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered with short, sparse hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

Luzula subpilosa is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
