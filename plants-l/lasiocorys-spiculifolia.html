Lasiocorys spiculifolia

Lasiocorys spiculifolia, commonly known as the spiky leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring elongated, spiky leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
