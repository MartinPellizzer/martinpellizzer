Lachnaea filamentosa

Lachnaea filamentosa, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, leaf-like thallus covered in fine, silky filaments. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. Its growth is often found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Lachnaea filamentosa plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and serving as a habitat for microorganisms.
