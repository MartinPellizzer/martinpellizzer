Leonurus grandiflorus

Leonurus grandiflorus, commonly known as greater betony, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Leonurus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
