Lepanthes tridentata

Lepanthes tridentata, commonly known as the Tridentate Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Lepanthes tridentata is often found in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests and requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.
