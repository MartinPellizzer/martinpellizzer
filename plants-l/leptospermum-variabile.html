Leptospermum variabile

Leptospermum variabile, commonly known as variable tea-tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. It typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
