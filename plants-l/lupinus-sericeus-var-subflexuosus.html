Lupinus sericeus var. subflexuosus

Lupinus sericeus var. subflexuosus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spires of blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
