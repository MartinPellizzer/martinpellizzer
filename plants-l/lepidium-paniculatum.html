Lepidium paniculatum

Lepidium paniculatum, commonly known as wild cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in branched clusters called panicles. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often have a peppery taste.

Lepidium paniculatum is typically found in moist soils along stream banks, roadsides, and in disturbed areas.
