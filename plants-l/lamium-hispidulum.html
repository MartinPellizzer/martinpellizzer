Lamium hispidulum

Lamium hispidulum, commonly known as hairy deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lamium hispidulum is often found in moist soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
