Lessingianthus macrocephalus

Lessingianthus macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed lessingia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapting to its arid environment.
