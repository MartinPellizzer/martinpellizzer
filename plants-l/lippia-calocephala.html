Lippia calocephala

Lippia calocephala, commonly known as Cape spearmint, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, mint-like scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Lippia calocephala is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
