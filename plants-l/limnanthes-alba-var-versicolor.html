Limnanthes alba var. versicolor

Limnanthes alba var. versicolor, commonly known as the yellow marshmallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, depending on the variety. The plant has soft, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological landscapes.
