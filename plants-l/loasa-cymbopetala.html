Loasa cymbopetala

Loasa cymbopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its floral structure includes five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Malvaceae family.

Loasa cymbopetala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
