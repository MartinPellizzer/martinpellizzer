Ludwigia grandiflora

Ludwigia grandiflora, commonly known as the red Ludwigia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to aquatic or terrestrial environments. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated in gardens or as a decorative plant in water features.

Ludwigia grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
