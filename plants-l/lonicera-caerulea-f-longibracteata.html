Lonicera caerulea f. longibracteata

Lonicera caerulea f. longibracteata, commonly known as the red honeysuckle, is a variant of the honeysuckle plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and beverages. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it has opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
