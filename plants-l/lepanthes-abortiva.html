Lepanthes abortiva

Lepanthes abortiva, commonly known as the abortive orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are usually clustered in loose inflorescences, and the species is known for its relatively short blooming period.

Lepanthes abortiva is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
