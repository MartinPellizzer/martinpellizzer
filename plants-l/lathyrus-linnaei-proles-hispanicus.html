Lathyrus linnaei proles hispanicus

Lathyrus linnaei proles hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
