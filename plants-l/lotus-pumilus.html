Lotus pumilus

Lotus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf lotus, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its roots are known for their ability to filter water, making it a useful plant in phytoremediation efforts.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
