Lasianthus longissimus

Lasianthus longissimus, commonly known as the long-stemmed lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.
