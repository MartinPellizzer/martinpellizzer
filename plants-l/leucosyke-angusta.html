Leucosyke angusta

Leucosyke angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white or greyish pubescence. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Leucosyke angusta is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
