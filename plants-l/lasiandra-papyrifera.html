Lasiandra papyrifera

Lasiandra papyrifera, commonly known as the paperbark daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fibrous, papery bark that peels off in strips. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Lasiandra papyrifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
