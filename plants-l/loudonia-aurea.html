Loudonia aurea

Loudonia aurea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Loudonia aurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
