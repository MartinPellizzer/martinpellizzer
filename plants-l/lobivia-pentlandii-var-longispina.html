Lobivia pentlandii var. longispina

Lobivia pentlandii var. longispina, commonly known as the Long-spined Lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically spiny, which helps deter herbivores in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
