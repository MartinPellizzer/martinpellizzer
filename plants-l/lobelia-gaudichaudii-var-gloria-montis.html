Lobelia gaudichaudii var. gloria-montis

Lobelia gaudichaudii var. gloria-montis, commonly known as the Gloria Montis lobelia, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
