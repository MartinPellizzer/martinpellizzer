Lonicera heterophylla

Lonicera heterophylla, commonly known as the variable honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that vary in size and shape depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Lonicera heterophylla is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
