Lagunea orientalis var. pilosa

Lagunea orientalis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy oriental lagunea, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein, and its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.

This variety is distinguished by its thick, velvety hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a unique texture.
