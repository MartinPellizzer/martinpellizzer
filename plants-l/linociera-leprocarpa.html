Linociera leprocarpa

Linociera leprocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which give it its characteristic silvery appearance.

Linociera leprocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
