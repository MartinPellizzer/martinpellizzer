Lindera bibracteata

Lindera bibracteata, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Lindera bibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local fauna.
