Lycium carolinianum var. quadrifidum

Lycium carolinianum var. quadrifidum, commonly known as the Carolina wolfberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
