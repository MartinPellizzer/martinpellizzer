Lobivia sanguiniflora var. duursmaiana

Lobivia sanguiniflora var. duursmaiana, commonly known as the Red-Flowered Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, ribbed stem and vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant's leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature common in desert plants to reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
