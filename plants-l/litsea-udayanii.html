Litsea udayanii

Litsea udayanii, commonly known as the Udayanii laurier, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces small, dark purple berries.

Litsea udayanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
