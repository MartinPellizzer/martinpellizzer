Lonicera koehneana var. intecta

Lonicera koehneana var. intecta, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range from white to yellow in color. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, containing small, hard seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
