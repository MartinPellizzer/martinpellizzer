Lupinus sellulus var. artulus

Lupinus sellulus var. artulus, commonly known as the Artful Lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is appreciated by both gardeners and pollinators alike.
