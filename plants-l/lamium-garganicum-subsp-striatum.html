Lamium garganicum subsp. striatum

Lamium garganicum subsp.

striatum, commonly known as the striated dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or pale green stripe running down the central vein.

The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
