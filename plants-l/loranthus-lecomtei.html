Loranthus lecomtei

Loranthus lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own chlorophyll for partial photosynthesis. Its berries are typically red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
