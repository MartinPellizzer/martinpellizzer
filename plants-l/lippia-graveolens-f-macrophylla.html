Lippia graveolens f. macrophylla

Lippia graveolens f. macrophylla, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

It is also a popular ingredient in herbal teas and aromatherapy products.
