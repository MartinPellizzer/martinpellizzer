Lippia melastomifolia

Lippia melastomifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf lippia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Lippia melastomifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
