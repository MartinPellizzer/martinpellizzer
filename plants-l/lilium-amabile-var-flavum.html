Lilium amabile var. flavum

Lilium amabile var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow fritillary, is a rare and striking variant of the fritillary lily.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange-red flowers with intricate patterns resembling fritillary butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
