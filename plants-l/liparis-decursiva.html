Liparis decursiva

Liparis decursiva, commonly known as the creeping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

Liparis decursiva is notable for its ability to form dense colonies, making it a common sight in its natural habitat.
