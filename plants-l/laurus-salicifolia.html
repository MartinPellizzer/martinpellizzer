Laurus salicifolia

Laurus salicifolia, commonly known as the Italian laurel or sweet laurel, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and emit a mild, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. Its dense foliage makes it suitable for hedges and ornamental landscaping.

Laurus salicifolia is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
