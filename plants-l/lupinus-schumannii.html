Lupinus schumannii

Lupinus schumannii, commonly known as Schumann's lupin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Lupinus schumannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in soil nitrogen fixation.
