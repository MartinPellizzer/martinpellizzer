Linochilus floribundus

Linochilus floribundus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, velvety flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are thick, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water.

Linochilus floribundus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
