Leucopogon spectabilis

Leucopogon spectabilis, commonly known as the showy leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its striking clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a contrasting texture to its vibrant floral display.

Leucopogon spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
