Lycaste locusta

Lycaste locusta, commonly known as the locust orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure and multiple pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, well-drained environments.

Lycaste locusta is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
