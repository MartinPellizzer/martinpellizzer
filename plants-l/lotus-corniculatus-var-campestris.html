Lotus corniculatus var. campestris

Lotus corniculatus var. campestris, commonly known as the field poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, papery flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. It has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
