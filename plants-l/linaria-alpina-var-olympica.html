Linaria alpina var. olympica

Linaria alpina var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic linaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cooler, high-altitude environments.
