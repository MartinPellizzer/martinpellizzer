Leonhardia lurida

Leonhardia lurida, commonly known as the slender leonhardia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits called achenes.

Leonhardia lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
