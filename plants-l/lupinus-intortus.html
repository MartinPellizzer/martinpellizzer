Lupinus intortus

Lupinus intortus, commonly known as the twisted lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its twisted, spiraled stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. Lupinus intortus is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
