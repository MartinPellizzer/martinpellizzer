Lathyrus sylvestris subsp. pyrenaicus

Lathyrus sylvestris subsp.

pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant features slender, trailing stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
