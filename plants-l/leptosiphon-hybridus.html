Leptosiphon hybridus

Leptosiphon hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid sunflower.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Leptosiphon hybridus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
