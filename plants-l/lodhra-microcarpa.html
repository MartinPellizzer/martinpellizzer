Lodhra microcarpa

Lodhra microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited sacred tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by round, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Lodhra microcarpa is valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
