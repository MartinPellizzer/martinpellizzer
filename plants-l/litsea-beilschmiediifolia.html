Litsea beilschmiediifolia

Litsea beilschmiediifolia, commonly known as the Mexican litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea beilschmiediifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
