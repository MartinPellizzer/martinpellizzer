Leptactina platyphylla var. platyphylla

Leptactina platyphylla var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf leptactina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spike during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their thick, waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its often arid habitat.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
