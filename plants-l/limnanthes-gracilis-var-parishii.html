Limnanthes gracilis var. parishii

Limnanthes gracilis var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy yellow flowers with prominent, showy stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near water sources such as streams and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
