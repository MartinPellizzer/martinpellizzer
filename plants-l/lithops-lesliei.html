Lithops lesliei

Lithops lesliei, commonly known as the "cork plant," is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that form a compact, rock-like structure. The plant's leaves are typically pale green or yellowish, with distinct markings that help in camouflage. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months.

Lithops lesliei is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
