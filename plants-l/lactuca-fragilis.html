Lactuca fragilis

Lactuca fragilis, commonly known as wild lettuce, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. The plant has serrated leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its latex contains compounds similar to opium, which can have mild sedative effects.

It is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wet meadows.
