Licuala atrovirens

Licuala atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves with dark green veins that contrast with its lighter green foliage. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
