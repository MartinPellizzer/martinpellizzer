Lithocardium hermanniifolium

Lithocardium hermanniifolium, commonly known as the Hermann's rockfoil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Lithocardium hermanniifolium is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
