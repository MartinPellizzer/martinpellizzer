Lobelia neriifolia

Lobelia neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black foliage and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lobelia neriifolia is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
