Lobelia valida

Lobelia valida, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia valida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
