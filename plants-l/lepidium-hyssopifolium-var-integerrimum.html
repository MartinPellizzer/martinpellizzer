Lepidium hyssopifolium var. integerrimum

Lepidium hyssopifolium var. integerrimum, commonly known as the woolly whitlowgrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It has dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, with leaves that are typically bluish-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. Its botanical features include a taproot system and small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
