Lycopodioides mairei

Lycopodioides mairei, commonly known as the Maire's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a short, vertical stem with whorled leaves that are spirally arranged along its length. Its reproductive structures, called sporophylls, are clustered at the tips of the branches and produce spores.

Lycopodioides mairei plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
