Lobivia pseudocachensis var. cinnabarina

Lobivia pseudocachensis var. cinnabarina, commonly known as the Cinnabar Lobivia, is a compact, branching cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive red or orange coloration, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
