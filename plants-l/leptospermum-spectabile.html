Leptospermum spectabile

Leptospermum spectabile, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Leptospermum spectabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
