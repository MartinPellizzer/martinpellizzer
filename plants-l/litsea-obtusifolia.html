Litsea obtusifolia

Litsea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Litsea obtusifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
