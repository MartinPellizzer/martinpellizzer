Luffa cylindrica

Luffa cylindrica, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash. The plant produces large, fibrous fruits that mature into spongy structures used for exfoliation. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its vines can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
