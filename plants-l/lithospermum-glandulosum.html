Lithospermum glandulosum

Lithospermum glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky lithosperm, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Lithospermum glandulosum typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny environments.
