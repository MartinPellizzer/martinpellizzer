Luisia trichorhiza

Luisia trichorhiza, commonly known as the hairy root luisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system with distinctive hairy roots, which aid in water absorption. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Luisia trichorhiza produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
