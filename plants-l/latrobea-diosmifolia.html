Latrobea diosmifolia

Latrobea diosmifolia, commonly known as the pink fairy tern, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Southwestern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with a deep root system to access groundwater.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
