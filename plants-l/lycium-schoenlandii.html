Lycium schoenlandii

Lycium schoenlandii, commonly known as the Himalayan boxthorn, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, red berries that are edible. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Lycium schoenlandii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
