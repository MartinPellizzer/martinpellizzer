Lachnagrostis preissii

Lachnagrostis preissii, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
