Lathyrus montanus f. pyrenaicus

Lathyrus montanus f. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean grass violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, calcareous soils. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with a spurred structure, and it produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
