Lavandula coronopifolia subsp. brevidens

Lavandula coronopifolia subsp.

brevidens, commonly known as narrowleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow linear leaves and produces spikes of purple flowers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is closely related to other lavender species but is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure.
