Lepidium hirtum var. bonannianum

Lepidium hirtum var. bonannianum, commonly known as the Bonannian peppergrass, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular trichomes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and soil organisms.
