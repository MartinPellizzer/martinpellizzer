Laurus iners

Laurus iners, commonly known as the Turkish laurel or Turkish bay leaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Greece, and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, oval berries.

Laurus iners is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
