Lyauteya

Lyauteya, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss through evaporation. The plant has small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Lyauteya is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
