Linaria ruscinonensis

Linaria ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with four petals. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Linaria ruscinonensis is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and forest edges.
