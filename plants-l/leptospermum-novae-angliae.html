Leptospermum novae-angliae

Leptospermum novae-angliae, commonly known as New England tea-tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
