Liparis purpureoviridis

Liparis purpureoviridis, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, violet-blue flowers with prominent purple veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and bloom during the spring or early summer.

Liparis purpureoviridis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
