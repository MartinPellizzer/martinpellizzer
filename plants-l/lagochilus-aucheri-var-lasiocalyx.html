Lagochilus aucheri var. lasiocalyx

Lagochilus aucheri var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or purple rock rose, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
