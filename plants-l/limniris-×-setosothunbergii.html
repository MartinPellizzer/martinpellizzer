Limniris × setosothunbergii

Limniris × setosothunbergii, commonly known as the hybrid boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Limniris tenuifolia and Limniris setosus, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. Its foliage is typically evergreen, with leathery, dark green leaves that remain throughout the winter.

This species is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
