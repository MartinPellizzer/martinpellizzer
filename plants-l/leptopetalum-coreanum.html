Leptopetalum coreanum

Leptopetalum coreanum, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.
