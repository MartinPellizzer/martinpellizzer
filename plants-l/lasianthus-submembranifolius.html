Lasianthus submembranifolius

Lasianthus submembranifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a thin, membranous layer. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
