Lupinus rubens

Lupinus rubens, commonly known as the red lupine, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy red or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Lupinus rubens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
