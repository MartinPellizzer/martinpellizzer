Linaria multicaulis subsp. aetnensis

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bright yellow, spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude environments.
