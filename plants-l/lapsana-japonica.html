Lapsana japonica

Lapsana japonica, commonly known as Japanese everlasting, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lapsana japonica is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
