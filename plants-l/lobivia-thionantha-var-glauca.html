Lobivia thionantha var. glauca

Lobivia thionantha var. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features a compact, rounded stem with a bluish-gray hue, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
