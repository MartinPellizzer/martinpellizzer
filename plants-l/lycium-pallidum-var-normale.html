Lycium pallidum var. normale

Lycium pallidum var. normale, commonly known as the common wolfberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have a slightly tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
