Lippia lupulina

Lippia lupulina, commonly known as Mexican honey herb, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Lippia lupulina is also valued for its use in culinary applications, particularly in the preparation of teas and as a flavoring agent.
