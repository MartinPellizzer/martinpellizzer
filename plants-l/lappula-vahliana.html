Lappula vahliana

Lappula vahliana, commonly known as the Vahli's bladderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and is typically found in moist, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has a short, creeping stem with rosettes of hairy, ovate leaves. It produces small, bladder-like structures that aid in water retention.

Lappula vahliana is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline and arid conditions.
