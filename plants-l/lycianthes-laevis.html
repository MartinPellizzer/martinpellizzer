Lycianthes laevis

Lycianthes laevis, commonly known as the smooth physalis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that develop into bright red, lantern-like fruits. The leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and is also used in traditional medicine.
