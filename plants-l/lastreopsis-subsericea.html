Lastreopsis subsericea

Lastreopsis subsericea, commonly known as the silky broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of other species. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically pale green or yellowish in color.

It is found in various regions of Europe and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique parasitic lifestyle.
