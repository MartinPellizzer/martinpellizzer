Lupinus perennis subsp. nootkatensis

Lupinus perennis subsp.

nootkatensis, commonly known as Nootka lupine, is a perennial legume native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
