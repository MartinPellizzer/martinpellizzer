Leymus coreanus

Leymus coreanus, commonly known as the Korean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers.

Leymus coreanus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
