Lycopodium × chapmanii

Lycopodium × chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's clubmoss, is a hybrid species belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features compact, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures consist of sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodium × chapmanii is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in ecological systems where it contributes to soil stability and habitat diversity.
