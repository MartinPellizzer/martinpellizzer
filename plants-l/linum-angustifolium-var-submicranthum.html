Linum angustifolium var. submicranthum

Linum angustifolium var. submicranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax or submicranthum flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
