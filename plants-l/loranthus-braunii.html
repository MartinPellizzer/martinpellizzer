Loranthus braunii

Loranthus braunii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its hemiparasitic nature, meaning it derives some of its nutrients from the host while also performing photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in temperate forests.

Due to its parasitic growth habit, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host trees.
