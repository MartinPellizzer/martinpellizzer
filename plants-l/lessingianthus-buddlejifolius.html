Lessingianthus buddlejifolius

Lessingianthus buddlejifolius, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers that resemble cups. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
