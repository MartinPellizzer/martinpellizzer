Luzula multiflora subsp. caucasica

Luzula multiflora subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically green or brown in color.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
