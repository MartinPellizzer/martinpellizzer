Lophophora williamsii var. fricii

Lophophora williamsii var. fricii, commonly known as the "Corky" peyote, is a variant of the peyote cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It is a slow-growing, low-maintenance cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also known for its cultural and spiritual significance among certain indigenous communities.
