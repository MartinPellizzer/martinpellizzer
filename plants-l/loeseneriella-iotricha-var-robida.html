Loeseneriella iotricha var. robida

Loeseneriella iotricha var. robida, commonly known as the Robid's hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with hairy stems and leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
