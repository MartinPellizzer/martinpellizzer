Lonicera × bella f. candida

Lonicera × bella f. candida, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually medium green. Its flowering stems are often covered in fine, white hairs, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its sweet fragrance and visual display.
