Laserpitium ambiguum

Laserpitium ambiguum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed habitats.

Laserpitium ambiguum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
