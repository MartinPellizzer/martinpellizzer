Lycopodium eremorum

Lycopodium eremorum, commonly known as the desert clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobili. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique morphology includes a creeping rhizome with upright, needle-like foliage.

Lycopodium eremorum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
