Leycesteria stipulata

Leycesteria stipulata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which turn red or orange in autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Leycesteria stipulata is also known for its fragrant, small white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring.
