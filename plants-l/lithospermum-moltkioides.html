Lithospermum moltkioides

Lithospermum moltkioides, commonly known as redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are rich in anthraquinones and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Lithospermum moltkioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
