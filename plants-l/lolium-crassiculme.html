Lolium crassiculme

Lolium crassiculme, commonly known as rigid ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are tightly compacted, with small, two-lobed florets that are often tinged with red or purple at maturity. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is commonly found in pastures and disturbed areas.

Lolium crassiculme is valued for its forage qualities and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
