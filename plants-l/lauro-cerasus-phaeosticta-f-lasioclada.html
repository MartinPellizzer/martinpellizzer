Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. lasioclada

Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. lasioclada, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This form is a naturally occurring variant of the species, showcasing unique foliage patterns.
