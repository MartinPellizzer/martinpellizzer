Lilium pomponicum

Lilium pomponicum, commonly known as the woodland lily, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
