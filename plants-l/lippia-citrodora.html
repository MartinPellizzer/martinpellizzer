Lippia citrodora

Lippia citrodora, commonly known as citronella mint, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong citronella scent, which is often used as a natural insect repellent. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Lippia citrodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
