Lysionotus gamosepalus var. biflorus

Lysionotus gamosepalus var. biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered lythrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals that are fused at the base, a feature typical of the genus Lysionotus.

The leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.
