Lobelia hartwegii var. angusta

Lobelia hartwegii var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
