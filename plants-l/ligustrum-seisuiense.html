Ligustrum seisuiense

Ligustrum seisuiense, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
