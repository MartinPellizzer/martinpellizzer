Lamium maculatum f. dimorphum

Lamium maculatum f. dimorphum, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple and white markings, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its leaves and flowers can be used in herbal teas, though it is not typically consumed as a food.
