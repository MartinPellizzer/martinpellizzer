Lupinus leucopsis var. hendersonianus

Lupinus leucopsis var. hendersonianus, commonly known as the Henderson's lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system consists of nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
