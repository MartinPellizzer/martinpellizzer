Lycoris aurea

Lycoris aurea, commonly known as the golden lily or Chinese lantern, is a striking perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. Its bulbs are adapted to store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soil.

Lycoris aurea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique flowering cycle.
