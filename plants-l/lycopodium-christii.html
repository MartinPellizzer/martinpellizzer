Lycopodium christii

Lycopodium christii, commonly known as the Christ's clubmoss, is a species of lycopod that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains the spores.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in horticulture.
