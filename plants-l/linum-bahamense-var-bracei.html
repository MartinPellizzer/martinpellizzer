Linum bahamense var. bracei

Linum bahamense var. bracei, commonly known as the Bahamian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
