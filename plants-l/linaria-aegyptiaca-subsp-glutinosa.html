Linaria aegyptiaca subsp. glutinosa

Linaria aegyptiaca subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces seed pods that are sticky and often adhere to animals, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Linaria aegyptiaca subsp.

glutinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
