Lampranthus swartkopensis

Lampranthus swartkopensis, commonly known as the Swartkop orchid, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling orchids in appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is a succulent, featuring fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
