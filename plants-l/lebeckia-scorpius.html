Lebeckia scorpius

Lebeckia scorpius, commonly known as the Cape goldenbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and produces small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky, well-drained soils in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
