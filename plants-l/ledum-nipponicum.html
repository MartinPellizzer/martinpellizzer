Ledum nipponicum

Ledum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
