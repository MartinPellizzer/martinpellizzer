Lepanthes cochleariifolia

Lepanthes cochleariifolia, commonly known as the spiral-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. The plant grows as an epiphytic orchid, often found in humid tropical forests on tree branches or rocks. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated by various insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
