Leichtlinia protuberans

Leichtlinia protuberans, commonly known as the swollen-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
