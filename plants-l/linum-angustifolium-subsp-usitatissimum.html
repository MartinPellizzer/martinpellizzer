Linum angustifolium subsp. usitatissimum

Linum angustifolium subsp.

usitatissimum, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers.

The plant is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is known for its fibrous stems, which have been historically used for textile production.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
