Leuconotis tenuifolia

Leuconotis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf leuconotis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural environment.
