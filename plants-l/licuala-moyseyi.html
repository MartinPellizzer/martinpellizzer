Licuala moyseyi

Licuala moyseyi, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched clusters, which are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

The leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material in some regions.
