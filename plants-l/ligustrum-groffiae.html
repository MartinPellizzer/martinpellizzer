Ligustrum groffiae

Ligustrum groffiae, commonly known as the Chinese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, black berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
