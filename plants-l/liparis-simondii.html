Liparis simondii

Liparis simondii, commonly known as the Himalayan marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. The plant has a slender, often branched stem and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the damp environment.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with proper drainage and humidity.
