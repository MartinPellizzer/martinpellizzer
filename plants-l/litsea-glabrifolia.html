Litsea glabrifolia

Litsea glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed laurier, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by oval, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Litsea glabrifolia is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
