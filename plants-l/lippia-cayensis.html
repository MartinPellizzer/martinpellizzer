Lippia cayensis

Lippia cayensis, commonly known as Cayenne mint, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

The flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
