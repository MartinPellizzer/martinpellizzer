Lonicera iberica var. microphylla

Lonicera iberica var. microphylla, commonly known as the Iberian honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, blooming in early spring. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean scrublands.
