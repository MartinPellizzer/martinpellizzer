Lobelia hosseusii var. villosa

Lobelia hosseusii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a hairy coating, contributing to its overall rugged look.

Lobelia hosseusii var. villosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
