Lotus corniculatus var. major

Lotus corniculatus var. major, commonly known as the greater bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability. It has a creeping growth habit with erect stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
