Lagophylla glandulosa

Lagophylla glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is distinguished by its glandular, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lagophylla glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
