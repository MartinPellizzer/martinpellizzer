Lipoblepharis urticifolia subsp. urticifolia

Lipoblepharis urticifolia subsp.

urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, and its fruits are small, dry capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
