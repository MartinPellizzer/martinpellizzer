Laserpitium nestleri subsp. turolensis

Laserpitium nestleri subsp.

turolensis, commonly known as the Turolensis hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in sunny, arid environments. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by narrow, leaf-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.
