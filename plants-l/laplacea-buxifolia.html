Laplacea buxifolia

Laplacea buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf laplacea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
