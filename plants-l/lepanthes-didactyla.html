Lepanthes didactyla

Lepanthes didactyla, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. The plant has a delicate, showy inflorescence with flowers that are usually white or pale green, featuring intricate patterns and a unique structure. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Lepanthes didactyla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
