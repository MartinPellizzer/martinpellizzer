Latouria leucohybos

Latouria leucohybos, commonly known as the white-tipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its growth habit is upright, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
