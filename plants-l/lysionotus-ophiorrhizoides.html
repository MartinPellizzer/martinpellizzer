Lysionotus ophiorrhizoides

Lysionotus ophiorrhizoides, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling ghostly apparitions. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its rare and ethereal appearance, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
