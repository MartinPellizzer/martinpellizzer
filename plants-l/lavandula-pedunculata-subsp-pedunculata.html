Lavandula pedunculata subsp. pedunculata

Lavandula pedunculata subsp.

pedunculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces spikes of purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and use in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
