Lysimachia tetragona

Lysimachia tetragona, commonly known as four-cornered yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lysimachia tetragona is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
