Lunaria telekiana

Lunaria telekiana, commonly known as the moonflower of the Andes, is a striking member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes Mountains. The plant produces unique, pale blue or violet flowers that open at night, emitting a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
