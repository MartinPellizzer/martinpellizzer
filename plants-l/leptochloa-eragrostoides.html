Leptochloa eragrostoides

Leptochloa eragrostoides, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence is a panicle, composed of small, spikelet-like clusters that produce tiny grains of pollen.

This species is valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
