Litsea pruriens

Litsea pruriens, commonly known as the roughleaf litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits.

The leaves and fruits contain compounds that have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
