Limonium michelsonii

Limonium michelsonii, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium michelsonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand salty air and coastal winds.
