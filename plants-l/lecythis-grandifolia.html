Lecythis grandifolia

Lecythis grandifolia, commonly known as the giant lecythium, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The tree produces showy, yellow flowers that are followed by massive, woody fruits containing a single, large seed. The bark and seeds of Lecythis grandifolia have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
