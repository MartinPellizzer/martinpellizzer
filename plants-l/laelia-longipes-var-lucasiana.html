Laelia longipes var. lucasiana

Laelia longipes var. lucasiana, commonly known as the Lucas's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent green sepals and petals. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. It prefers cool, bright conditions with high humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is valued for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
