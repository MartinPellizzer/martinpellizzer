Ludwigia palustris f. elongata

Ludwigia palustris f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated marsh Ludwigia, is a variant of Ludwigia palustris, a species native to wetland environments in the Americas.

This plant is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves that often grow in dense clusters along the water's edge. It typically thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as marshes and pond margins. The leaves are usually dark green and slightly hairy, while the small, yellowish flowers appear in spikes during the summer months.

It is often used in aquatic gardening for its ornamental value and ability to help stabilize water ecosystems.
