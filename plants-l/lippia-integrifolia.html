Lippia integrifolia

Lippia integrifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Lippia integrifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
