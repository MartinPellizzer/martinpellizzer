Lippia sarmentosa

Lippia sarmentosa, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often aromatic, with a mint-like scent, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.

Lippia sarmentosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
