Lobivia shaferi f. cylindracea

Lobivia shaferi f. cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
