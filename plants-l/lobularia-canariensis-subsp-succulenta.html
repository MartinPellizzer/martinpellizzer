Lobularia canariensis subsp. succulenta

Lobularia canariensis subsp.

succulenta, commonly known as Canary Island lobularia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring or early summer.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a characteristic typical of succulents. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
