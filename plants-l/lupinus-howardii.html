Lupinus howardii

Lupinus howardii, commonly known as the Howard's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

Lupinus howardii is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
