Lellingeria melanotrichia

Lellingeria melanotrichia, commonly known as the black-haired bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue bell-shaped flowers and dark, hairy stems that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Lellingeria melanotrichia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
