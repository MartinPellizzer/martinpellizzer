Linaria laxiflora var. cyrenaica

Linaria laxiflora var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaica toadflax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
