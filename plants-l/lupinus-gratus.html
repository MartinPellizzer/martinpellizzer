Lupinus gratus

Lupinus gratus, commonly known as the pretty lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus gratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flower colors, which can range from purple to pink.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
