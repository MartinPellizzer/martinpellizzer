Leucocroton flavicans

Leucocroton flavicans, commonly known as the golden croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a yellow or orange margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
