Lantana incana

Lantana incana, commonly known as the common lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has opposite, hairy, and often aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Lantana incana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
