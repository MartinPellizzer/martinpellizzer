Leontodon ceratophorus

Leontodon ceratophorus, commonly known as the horned hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Leontodon ceratophorus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
