Lathyrus pannonicus f. sumenii

Lathyrus pannonicus f. sumenii, commonly known as the Sumerian vetch, is a rare and ornamental flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, typical of the genus Lathyrus.

This subspecies is valued for its historical and horticultural significance, though it is often difficult to cultivate due to its specific environmental requirements.
