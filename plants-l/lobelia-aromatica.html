Lobelia aromatica

Lobelia aromatica, commonly known as sweet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
