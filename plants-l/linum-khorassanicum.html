Linum khorassanicum

Linum khorassanicum, commonly known as the Khorasan flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its historical use in fiber production, it holds cultural and economic significance in its native regions.
