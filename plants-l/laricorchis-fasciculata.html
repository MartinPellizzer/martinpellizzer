Laricorchis fasciculata

Laricorchis fasciculata, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a species of orchid native to the western United States.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a rhizomatous root system and produces delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, arranged in a rosette, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Laricorchis fasciculata is adapted to thrive in cool, humid climates and is often found in association with coniferous trees.
