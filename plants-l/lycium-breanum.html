Lycium breanum

Lycium breanum, commonly known as the Brean boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Lycium breanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
