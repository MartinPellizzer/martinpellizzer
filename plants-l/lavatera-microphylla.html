Lavatera microphylla

Lavatera microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mallows, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

Lavatera microphylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
