Lilium stictum

Lilium stictum, commonly known as the red lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and yellow anthers, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Lilium stictum blooms in late summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
