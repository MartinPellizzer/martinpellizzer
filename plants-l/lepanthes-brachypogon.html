Lepanthes brachypogon

Lepanthes brachypogon, commonly known as the short-stalked urn orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy, urn-shaped flower that is usually white or pale green. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Lepanthes brachypogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
