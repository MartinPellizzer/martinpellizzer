Lupinus niederleinianus

Lupinus niederleinianus, commonly known as the Niederlein's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus niederleinianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
