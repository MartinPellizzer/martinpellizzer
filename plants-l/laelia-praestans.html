Laelia praestans

Laelia praestans, commonly known as the showy laelia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, featuring a vibrant mix of colors including white, yellow, and red. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to the high-altitude environments where it thrives.

Laelia praestans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
