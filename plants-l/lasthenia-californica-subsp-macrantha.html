Lasthenia californica subsp. macrantha

Lasthenia californica subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the California basket flower, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces showy yellow or orange composite flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are typically linear to oblanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to other members of the species.
