Lobivia bruchii

Lobivia bruchii, commonly known as the Bruch's lobivia, is a small, compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a smooth, pale green surface and a dense covering of fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from brief rainfall events.

Lobivia bruchii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
