Linaria gharbensis var. purpurea

Linaria gharbensis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to lilac flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and compact growth habit.
