Lespedeza sulcata

Lespedeza sulcata, commonly known as the serrated lespedeza, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability to various soil types. Lespedeza sulcata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are a food source for various wildlife species, contributing to its ecological importance.
