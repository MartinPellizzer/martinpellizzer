Liparis flaccida

Liparis flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several oval-shaped leaves arranged along its length. Its flowers are small and usually pale green or yellow, with three sepals and three petals.

Liparis flaccida is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
