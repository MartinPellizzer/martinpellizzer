Lotononis tenella var. angustifolia

Lotononis tenella var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lotononis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stems, and it produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
