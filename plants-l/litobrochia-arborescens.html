Litobrochia arborescens

Litobrochia arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like brochidium, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its branching, tree-like structure, which distinguishes it from other members of the Cactaceae family. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to its arid environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing during the wet season.

Litobrochia arborescens is also notable for its ability to form colonies, which provides it with additional resilience in harsh conditions.
