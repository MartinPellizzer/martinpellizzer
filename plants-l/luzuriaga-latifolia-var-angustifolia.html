Luzuriaga latifolia var. angustifolia

Luzuriaga latifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf wintergreen, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters during late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its aromatic leaves and stems have been used historically for their medicinal properties.
