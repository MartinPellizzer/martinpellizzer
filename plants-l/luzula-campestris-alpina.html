Luzula campestris alpina

Luzula campestris alpina, commonly known as alpine woodrush, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and grass-like foliage with narrow, grass-green leaves. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers.

Luzula campestris alpina is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine regions across the northern hemisphere.
