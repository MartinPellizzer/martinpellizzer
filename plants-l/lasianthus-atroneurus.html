Lasianthus atroneurus

Lasianthus atroneurus, commonly known as the black-veined lasianthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking dark green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
