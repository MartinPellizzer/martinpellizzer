Languas havilandii

Languas havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped blossoms. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Languas havilandii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
