Lindernia pubescens

Lindernia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lindernia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from summer to fall.

Lindernia pubescens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
