Lavoisiera suberosa

Lavoisiera suberosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Lavoisiera suberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
