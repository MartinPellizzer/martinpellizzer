Listrostachys sedenii

Listrostachys sedenii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and aromatic, compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
