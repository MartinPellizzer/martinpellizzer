Lupinus albifrons var. longifolius

Lupinus albifrons var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved white lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
