Leucojum trichophyllum

Leucojum trichophyllum, commonly known as the woolly white squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.
