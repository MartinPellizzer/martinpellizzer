Lilium flavum

Lilium flavum, commonly known as the yellow lily, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and is part of the Liliaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Lilium flavum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
