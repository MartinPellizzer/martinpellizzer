Lathyrus rotundifolius subsp. litvinovii

Lathyrus rotundifolius subsp.

litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's pea, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region. It features palmately compound leaves with rounded leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.

However, it is also considered a potential weed in certain agricultural settings.
