Lathyrus armitageanus

Lathyrus armitageanus, commonly known as the Armitage pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
