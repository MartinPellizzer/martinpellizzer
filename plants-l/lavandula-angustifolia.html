Lavandula angustifolia

Lavandula angustifolia, commonly known as English lavender, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are widely used in perfumery and natural medicine.
