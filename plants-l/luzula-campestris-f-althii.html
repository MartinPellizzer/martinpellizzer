Luzula campestris f. althii

Luzula campestris f. althii, commonly known as the alpine woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish spikelets that are typically clustered at the base of the leafy stems. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Luzula campestris f. althii is adapted to cold climates and is a common component of mountainous vegetation in temperate regions.
