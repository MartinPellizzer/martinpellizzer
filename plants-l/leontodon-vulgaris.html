Leontodon vulgaris

Leontodon vulgaris, commonly known as the common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and a distinctive yellow flower head that develops into a crown of white, fluffy seeds. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The leaves and flowers are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
