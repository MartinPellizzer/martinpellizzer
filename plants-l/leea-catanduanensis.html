Leea catanduanensis

Leea catanduanensis, commonly known as the Catanduanes leea, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. It has smooth, green stems and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
