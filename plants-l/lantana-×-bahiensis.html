Lantana × bahiensis

Lantana × bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian lantana, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caribbean.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain regions.
