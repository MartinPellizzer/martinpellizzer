Ligustrum retusum

Ligustrum retusum, commonly known as the rough privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and dense growth habit.

Ligustrum retusum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
