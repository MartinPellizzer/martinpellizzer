Licania huberiana

Licania huberiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum huberianum*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are often used in local traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Licania huberiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
