Lepismium paradoxum

Lepismium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Lepismium paradoxum is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
