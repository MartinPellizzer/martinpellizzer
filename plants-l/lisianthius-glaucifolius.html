Lisianthius glaucifolius

Lisianthius glaucifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
