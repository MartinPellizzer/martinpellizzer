Liparis yuana

Liparis yuana, commonly known as the Chinese orchid, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, and it produces aerial roots for absorption.

Liparis yuana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
