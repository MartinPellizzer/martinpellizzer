Linaria amethystea

Linaria amethystea, commonly known as the amethyst flower or purple toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Linaria amethystea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
