Lilac vulgaris var. purpurea

Lilac vulgaris var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, clustered flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep purple. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological habitats.
