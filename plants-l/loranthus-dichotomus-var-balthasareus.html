Loranthus dichotomus var. balthasareus

Loranthus dichotomus var. balthasareus, commonly known as the Balthasar's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain sticky seeds that adhere to birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

This variety is native to parts of Australia and is often found in coastal and inland regions where its host trees thrive.
