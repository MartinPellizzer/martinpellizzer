Liparis abyssinica

Liparis abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the thin soil.

Liparis abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
