Lucuma oblonga

Lucuma oblonga, commonly known as the golden apple, is a South American fruit native to the Andes region.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its oval, yellow-orange fruit with a sweet, nutty flavor. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. Lucuma is rich in nutrients such as vitamin C, fiber, and various minerals.

It is often used in desserts, smoothies, and as a natural sweetener due to its unique taste and nutritional profile.
