Litsea costata

Litsea costata, commonly known as Chinese bay leaf or mountain laurel, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when mature, containing several seeds. Litsea costata thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
