Lycopodium magellanicum var. florinii

Lycopodium magellanicum var. florinii, commonly known as the Magellan clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and sporophylls that bear spores. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in temperate regions.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
