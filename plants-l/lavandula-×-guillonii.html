Lavandula × guillonii

Lavandula × guillonii, commonly known as the French lavender, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, spike-like inflorescences of purple to pink flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and aromatic properties, often used in landscaping and aromatherapy.
