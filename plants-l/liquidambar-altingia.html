Liquidambar altingia

Liquidambar altingia, commonly known as the Chinese sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Aceraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves that turn vibrant colors in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across China, Japan, and Korea.
