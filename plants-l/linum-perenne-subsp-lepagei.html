Linum perenne subsp. lepagei

Linum perenne subsp.

lepagei, commonly known as the Lepage's flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
