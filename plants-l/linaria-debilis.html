Linaria debilis

Linaria debilis, commonly known as the slender toadflax, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and produces delicate, yellow-orange flowers with four petals. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

Linaria debilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
