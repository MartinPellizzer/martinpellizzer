Lactuca oleracea

Lactuca oleracea, commonly known as lettuce, is a cool-season plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit, with broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically crisp and juicy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Lettuce thrives in well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary applications worldwide.
