Lepidium stylatum var. lyratum

Lepidium stylatum var. lyratum, commonly known as the lyreleaf cress, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed, giving it a lyre-like appearance, hence its common name.

It produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
