Leucanthemum commutatum

Leucanthemum commutatum, commonly known as the Sierra snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The Sierra snowbell is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
