Lupinus grandifolius

Lupinus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Lupinus grandifolius plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
