Lespedeza polystachya

Lespedeza polystachya, commonly known as Chinese bushclover, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Lespedeza polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
