Lotus sanguineus

Lotus sanguineus, commonly known as the blood lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
