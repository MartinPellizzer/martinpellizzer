Liparis assamica

Liparis assamica, commonly known as the Assam orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Liparis assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
