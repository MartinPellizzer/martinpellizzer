Lobia erosa

Lobia erosa, commonly known as the scarlet spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that resemble spider-like structures, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Lobia erosa has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
