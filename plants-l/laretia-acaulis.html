Laretia acaulis

Laretia acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed laretia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with leaves directly emerging from the base. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually yellow or white in color.

Laretia acaulis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
