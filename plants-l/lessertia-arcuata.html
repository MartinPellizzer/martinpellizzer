Lessertia arcuata

Lessertia arcuata, commonly known as the spiral cycad, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a thick, fibrous layer. Its leaves are long and pinnate, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Lessertia arcuata is a slow-growing, ancient-looking plant that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
