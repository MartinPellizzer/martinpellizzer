Laurus cuneata

Laurus cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese bay leaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a spice in cooking.
