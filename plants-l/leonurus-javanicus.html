Leonurus javanicus

Leonurus javanicus, commonly known as the Java horse-lei, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
