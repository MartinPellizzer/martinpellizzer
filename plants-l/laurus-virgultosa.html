Laurus virgultosa

Laurus virgultosa, commonly known as the sweet bay tree, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that have a pleasant, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The sweet bay tree thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
