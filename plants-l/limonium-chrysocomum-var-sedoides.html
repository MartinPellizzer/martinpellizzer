Limonium chrysocomum var. sedoides

Limonium chrysocomum var. sedoides, commonly known as the yellow sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in coastal or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes.

Limonium chrysocomum var. sedoides is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun conditions.
