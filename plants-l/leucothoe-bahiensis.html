Leucothoe bahiensis

Leucothoe bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian leatherleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring season. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a distinctive dark green color with a waxy texture.

Leucothoe bahiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
