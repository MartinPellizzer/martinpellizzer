Lupinus guadalupensis

Lupinus guadalupensis, commonly known as Guadalupine lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to soil stabilization through its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
