Lonchocarpus swartzii

Lonchocarpus swartzii, commonly known as the African senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its seed pods are long and cylindrical, containing several seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ecological and medicinal significance, it plays an important role in both local ecosystems and human health practices.
