Luzula pindica

Luzula pindica, commonly known as the Pindus sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Luzula pindica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
