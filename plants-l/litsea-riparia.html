Litsea riparia

Litsea riparia, commonly known as the swamp litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy areas, demonstrating its adaptability to moist environments. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinct botanical appearance.
