Leptospermum cuspidatum

Leptospermum cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The leaves are used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and are also popular in horticulture for their ornamental value.
