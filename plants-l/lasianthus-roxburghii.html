Lasianthus roxburghii

Lasianthus roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
