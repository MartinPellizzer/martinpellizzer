Lobelia krookii

Lobelia krookii, commonly known as the dwarf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Lobelia krookii is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
