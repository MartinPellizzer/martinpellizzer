Lomaria revoluta

Lomaria revoluta, commonly known as the revolute lomaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled at the edges, giving it its species name "revoluta.". It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer.

Lomaria revoluta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
