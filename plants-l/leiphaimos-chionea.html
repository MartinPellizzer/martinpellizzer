Leiphaimos chionea

Leiphaimos chionea, commonly known as the silver snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, giving it a striking appearance against the snowy landscape. The plant has a compact growth habit with silvery-gray foliage that provides year-round visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as an ornamental plant in colder climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
