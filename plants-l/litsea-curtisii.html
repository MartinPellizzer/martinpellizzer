Litsea curtisii

Litsea curtisii, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its fast growth and attractive foliage.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native plants.
