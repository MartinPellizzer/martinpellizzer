Lobelia glaucescens

Lobelia glaucescens, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lobelia glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
