Limonium maroccanum

Limonium maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in coastal or sandy environments. It is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Limonium maroccanum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
