Lucuma maingayi

Lucuma maingayi, commonly known as the golden fruit or lucuma, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically oval-shaped, with a thick, leathery exterior that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures. The pulp is rich in nutrients, containing high levels of carbohydrates, fiber, and various minerals, making it a valuable crop in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

Its botanical features make it well-suited to the cool, high-altitude climates where it thrives.
