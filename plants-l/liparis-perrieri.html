Liparis perrieri

Liparis perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizome is fibrous, and it produces a single or few flowering stems each year.

Liparis perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
