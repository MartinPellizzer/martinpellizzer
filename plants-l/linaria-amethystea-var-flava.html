Linaria amethystea var. flava

Linaria amethystea var. flava, commonly known as the yellow toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred structure at the base of each petal, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Linaria.

The yellow toadflax is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
