Lupinus sericeus subsp. amplus

Lupinus sericeus subsp.

amplus, commonly known as the silver lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and sparsely branched stems that often grow in dense clumps.

The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
