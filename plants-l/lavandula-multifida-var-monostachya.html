Lavandula multifida var. monostachya

Lavandula multifida var. monostachya, commonly known as narrow-leaved lavender, is a variety of lavender native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of fragrant purple flowers. This plant is typically drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The variety is also valued in essential oil production due to its strong fragrance.
