Lepanthes amabilis

Lepanthes amabilis, commonly known as the beautiful orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with prominent yellow markings. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes amabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
