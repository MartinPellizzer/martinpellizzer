Leea magnifolia

Leea magnifolia, commonly known as the giant leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The botanical features of Leea magnifolia make it a distinctive and valuable species in its native habitat.
