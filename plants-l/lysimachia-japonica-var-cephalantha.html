Lysimachia japonica var. cephalantha

Lysimachia japonica var. cephalantha, commonly known as Japanese threadleaf sorrel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, shade-tolerant ground cover.
