Lobelia decurrens subsp. parviflora

Lobelia decurrens subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cardinalflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued species in both ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
