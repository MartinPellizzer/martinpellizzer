Lophopyxis combretocarpa

Lophopyxis combretocarpa, commonly known as the African boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, rounded foliage composed of small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and year-round foliage make it suitable for hedges and container gardening.
