Lennoa

Lennoa, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a tall, erect stem with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional herbal medicine.

Lennoa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
