Lucuma izabalensis

Lucuma izabalensis, commonly known as the Izabal lucuma, is a species of tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Capsicaceae and is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that resemble a cross between a peach and a pear. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is typically harvested when it turns a deep yellow or orange color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
