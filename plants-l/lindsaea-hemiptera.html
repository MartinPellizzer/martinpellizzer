Lindsaea hemiptera

Lindsaea hemiptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, rosette-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Lindsaea hemiptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
