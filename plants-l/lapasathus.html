Lapasathus

Lapasathus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

Lapasathus is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
