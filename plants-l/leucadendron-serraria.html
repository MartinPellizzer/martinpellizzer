Leucadendron serraria

Leucadendron serraria, commonly known as the silver leaf candle bush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, cone-shaped flower heads in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which appear in late summer and autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
