Liriodendron tulipifera var. integrifolium

Liriodendron tulipifera var. integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-flowered tulip tree, is a variant of the tulip poplar native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-green flowers that resemble tulips, which bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically smooth-edged and heart-shaped, providing a lush green canopy. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
