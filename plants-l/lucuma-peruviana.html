Lucuma peruviana

Lucuma peruviana, commonly known as the golden apple, is a fruit-bearing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its yellow, sweet, and creamy fruit that resembles a small apple. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamin C, fiber, and various minerals.

Lucuma peruviana is also valued for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
