Lecaniodiscus fraxinifolius subsp. scassellatii

Lecaniodiscus fraxinifolius subsp.

scassellatii, commonly known as the African sand dollar tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, disc-like fruits that resemble sand dollars, which are typically yellow to brown in color.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruiting structures.
