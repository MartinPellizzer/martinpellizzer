Lobelia collaris

Lobelia collaris, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia collaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
