Lantana hispida

Lantana hispida, commonly known as the rough lantana, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are usually elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, pink, or red, depending on the species. Lantana hispida is characterized by its branched, woody stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
