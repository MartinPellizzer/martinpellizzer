Lathyrus albus subvar. pannonicus

Lathyrus albus subvar. pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian vetch, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant features slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
