Leonurus ruderalis

Leonurus ruderalis, commonly known as field hedgehog or stickyweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in abandoned fields.

Leonurus ruderalis is known for its sticky hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
