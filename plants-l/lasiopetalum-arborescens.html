Lasiopetalum arborescens

Lasiopetalum arborescens, commonly known as the tree daphne, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
