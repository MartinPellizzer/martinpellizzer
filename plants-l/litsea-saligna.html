Litsea saligna

Litsea saligna, commonly known as the silky laurier or Indian laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Litsea saligna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
