Lasiopetalum dasyphyllum

Lasiopetalum dasyphyllum, commonly known as the rough-leaved matted bellflower, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically rough in texture and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal blooms.
