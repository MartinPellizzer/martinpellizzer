Lavandula pannosa

Lavandula pannosa, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
