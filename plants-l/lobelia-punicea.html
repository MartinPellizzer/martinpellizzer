Lobelia punicea

Lobelia punicea, commonly known as the scarlet lobelia, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and hairy, glandular leaves, which help it thrive in moist, sunny environments. This perennial herb typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats. Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add color and biodiversity to their landscapes.
