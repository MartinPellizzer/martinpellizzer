Linaria sapphirina

Linaria sapphirina, commonly known as the Sapphire Toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that resemble small trumpets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant grows in a compact, bushy form, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to rock gardens or borders.

Linaria sapphirina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
