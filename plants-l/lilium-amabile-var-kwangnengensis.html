Lilium amabile var. kwangnengensis

Lilium amabile var. kwangnengensis, commonly known as the Kwangnengensis lily, is a rare and ornamental species native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces bulbous roots that store nutrients.

This variety is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and scientific interest.
