Lycianthes limitanea

Lycianthes limitanea, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible when ripe. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
