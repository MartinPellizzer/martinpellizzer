Lupinus franciscanus

Lupinus franciscanus, commonly known as the Franciscan lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Lupinus franciscanus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
