Leptospermum macgillivrayi

Leptospermum macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's tea tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Tasmania.

It typically grows in coastal and near-coastal areas, often in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
