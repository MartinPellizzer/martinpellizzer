Lathyrus rifeus

Lathyrus rifeus, commonly known as the purple sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features climbing stems with trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Lathyrus rifeus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
