Laburnum anagyroides var. jacquinianum

Laburnum anagyroides var. jacquinianum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
