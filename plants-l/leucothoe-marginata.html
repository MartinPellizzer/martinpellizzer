Leucothoe marginata

Leucothoe marginata, commonly known as the margined leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, elliptical leaves with a distinctive white margin. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
