Linaria monspessulana

Linaria monspessulana, commonly known as the Portuguese sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria monspessulana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
