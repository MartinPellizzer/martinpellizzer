Lavalleopsis

Lavalleopsis, commonly known as the "Lavalle's orchid," is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant floral structures. The plant typically features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Lavalleopsis is considered a delicately endangered species.
