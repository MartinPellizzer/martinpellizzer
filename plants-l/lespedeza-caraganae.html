Lespedeza caraganae

Lespedeza caraganae, commonly known as Chinese struthium, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Lespedeza caraganae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
