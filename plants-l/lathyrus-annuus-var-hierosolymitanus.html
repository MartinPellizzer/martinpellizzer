Lathyrus annuus var. hierosolymitanus

Lathyrus annuus var. hierosolymitanus, commonly known as the Jerusalem glory flower, is a climbing legume native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated pods containing small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
