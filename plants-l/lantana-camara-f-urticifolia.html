Lantana camara f. urticifolia

Lantana camara f. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging lantana, is a variant of the common lantana species.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The leaves of this plant are hairy and can cause skin irritation upon contact, giving it its common name. It is native to the Americas but has been introduced to various regions around the world.

This plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
