Linum usitatissimum var. siculum

Linum usitatissimum var. siculum, commonly known as Sicilian flax, is a variant of the common flax plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces blue or purple flowers. The plant is cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production, and its seeds, which are rich in oil. Sicilian flax thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for both practical and medicinal uses.
