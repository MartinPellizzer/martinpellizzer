Loranthus hastifolius

Loranthus hastifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host trees through a specialized structure called a haustorium.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and its berries are typically red or orange, containing sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus hastifolius plays a role in its ecosystem by providing food for birds and insects, while also potentially affecting the growth of its host trees.
