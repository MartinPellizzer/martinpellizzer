Lonchocarpus laxiflorus

Lonchocarpus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its root system is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
