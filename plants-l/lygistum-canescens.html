Lygistum canescens

Lygistum canescens, commonly known as the hairy dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in the morning and closing by afternoon. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lygistum canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
