Lycopus amplectens

Lycopus amplectens, commonly known as broad-leaved bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, two-lipped blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant in traditional herbal medicine.
