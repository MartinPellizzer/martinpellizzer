Laburnum tardiflorum

Laburnum tardiflorum, commonly known as the late-flowering laburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow, pendulous flowers in late spring or early summer, which are arranged in racemes. Its bark is smooth and gray, while its stems are often thorny.

Laburnum tardiflorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in hedging or as a specimen plant.
