Lasiacis anomala

Lasiacis anomala, commonly known as the Malabar nut, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and a unique fruit structure.

Lasiacis anomala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
