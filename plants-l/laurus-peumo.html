Laurus peumo

Laurus peumo, commonly known as the Chilean laurel, is a broadleaf evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

However, the plant is toxic to humans and animals, making it a potentially dangerous species.
