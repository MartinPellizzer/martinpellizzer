Laserpitium transtaganum

Laserpitium transtaganum, commonly known as the Trans-Taganum grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in grasslands and open habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
