Lathyrus amplicarpos

Lathyrus amplicarpos, commonly known as the broad-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Lathyrus amplicarpos is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
