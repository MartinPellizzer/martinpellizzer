Liatris squarrosa

Liatris squarrosa, commonly known as the prairie blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its tall, erect stems bear clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Liatris squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
