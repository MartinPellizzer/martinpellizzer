Lepigonum rubrum var. gramineum

Lepigonum rubrum var. gramineum, commonly known as red stemmed goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically reddish in color, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
