Lasiocroton fawcettii

Lasiocroton fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's holly, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
