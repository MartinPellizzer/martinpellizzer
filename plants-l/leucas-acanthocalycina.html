Leucas acanthocalycina

Leucas acanthocalycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pink or violet in color, attracting various pollinators.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
