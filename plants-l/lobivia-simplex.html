Lobivia simplex

Lobivia simplex, commonly known as the simple lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Lobivia simplex is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid environments.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it an attractive addition to succulent collections.
