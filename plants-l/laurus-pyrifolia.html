Laurus pyrifolia

Laurus pyrifolia, commonly known as Chinese laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and fruits contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in herbal remedies.
