Lupinaster pentaphyllus subsp. albiflorus

Lupinaster pentaphyllus subsp.

albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower lupinaster, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it valuable in soil enrichment.
