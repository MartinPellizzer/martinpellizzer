Lupinus clarkei

Lupinus clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's lupin, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features spiny stems and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus clarkei is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
