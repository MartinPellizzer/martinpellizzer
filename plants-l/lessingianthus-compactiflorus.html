Lessingianthus compactiflorus

Lessingianthus compactiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant collections and pollinator gardens.
