Laphamia cinerea

Laphamia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf lophopetalum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, giving it a distinctive appearance that helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Laphamia cinerea is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
