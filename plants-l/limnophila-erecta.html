Limnophila erecta

Limnophila erecta, commonly known as the water willow, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Limnophila erecta thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
