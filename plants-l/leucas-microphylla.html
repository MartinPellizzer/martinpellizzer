Leucas microphylla

Leucas microphylla, commonly known as small-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands.

Leucas microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
