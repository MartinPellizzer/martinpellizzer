Lilium ochraceum

Lilium ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, with a rough texture.

Lilium ochraceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
