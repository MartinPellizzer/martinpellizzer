Lessingianthus santosii

Lessingianthus santosii, commonly known as the Santos' sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like blooms.
