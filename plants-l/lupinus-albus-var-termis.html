Lupinus albus var. termis

Lupinus albus var. termis, commonly known as the Termis white lupin, is a variety of the white lupin native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense, spikelike clusters. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
