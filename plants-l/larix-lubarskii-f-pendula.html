Larix lubarskii f. pendula

Larix lubarskii f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a unique variant of the larch tree characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

It is a deciduous conifer, shedding its needle-like leaves in the autumn. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tian Shan and Pamir Mountains. Its botanical features include slender, pendulous shoots and small, scale-like leaves that turn golden in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its distinctive form.
