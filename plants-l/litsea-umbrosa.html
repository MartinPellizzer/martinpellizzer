Litsea umbrosa

Litsea umbrosa, commonly known as the dark litsea or Indian bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.
