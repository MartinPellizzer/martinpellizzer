Lotus glaucus var. canariensis

Lotus glaucus var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which aids in its dispersal by wind or animals.
