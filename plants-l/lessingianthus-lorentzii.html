Lessingianthus lorentzii

Lessingianthus lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's sunflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family, and bloom from late summer to early autumn.
