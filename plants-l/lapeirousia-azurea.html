Lapeirousia azurea

Lapeirousia azurea, commonly known as the Bluebell of the Cape, is a strikingly beautiful South African bulbous plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that store water and nutrients, typical of its arid habitat. Lapeirousia azurea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky outcrops and fynbos vegetation.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for rock gardens and botanical collections.
