Lilium martagon var. superbum

Lilium martagon var. superbum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family characterized by its unique, nodding flowers.

This variety features showy, bell-shaped blooms that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive mottled pattern. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, where it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with broad, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette.

The Turk's cap lily is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
