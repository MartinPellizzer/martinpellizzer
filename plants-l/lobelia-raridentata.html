Lobelia raridentata

Lobelia raridentata, commonly known as the rare-toothed lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant's leaves are narrow and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
