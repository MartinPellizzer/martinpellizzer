Luzula campestris var. oahuensis

Luzula campestris var. oahuensis, commonly known as Oahu sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the island of Oahu in Hawaii.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
