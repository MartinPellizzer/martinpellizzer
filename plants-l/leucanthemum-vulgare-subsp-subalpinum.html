Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. subalpinum

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine oxeye, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc, giving it a daisy-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves and is often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
