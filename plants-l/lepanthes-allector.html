Lepanthes allector

Lepanthes allector, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Lepanthes allector is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
