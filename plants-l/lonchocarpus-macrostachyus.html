Lonchocarpus macrostachyus

Lonchocarpus macrostachyus, commonly known as the giant senna, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with numerous leaflets. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense spikes, followed by distinctive pod fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
