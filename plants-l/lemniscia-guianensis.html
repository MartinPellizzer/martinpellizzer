Lemniscia guianensis

Lemniscia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan lemniscate, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
