Loasa picta var. stubeliana

Loasa picta var. stubeliana, commonly known as the pink loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.

Loasa picta var. stubeliana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
