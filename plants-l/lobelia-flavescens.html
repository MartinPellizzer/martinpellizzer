Lobelia flavescens

Lobelia flavescens, commonly known as yellow lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that produce small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are usually hairy and slightly toothed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
