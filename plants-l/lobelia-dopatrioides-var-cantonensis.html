Lobelia dopatrioides var. cantonensis

Lobelia dopatrioides var. cantonensis, commonly known as the Canton lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
