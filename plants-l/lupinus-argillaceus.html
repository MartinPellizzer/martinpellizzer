Lupinus argillaceus

Lupinus argillaceus, commonly known as the clay lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, often calcareous soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus argillaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
