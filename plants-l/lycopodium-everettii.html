Lycopodium everettii

Lycopodium everettii, commonly known as the Everett's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of needle-like leaves and distinctive cone-like structures called strobili. Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures produce spores rather than seeds, a characteristic feature of its ancient lineage.

Lycopodium everettii plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
