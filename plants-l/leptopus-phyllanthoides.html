Leptopus phyllanthoides

Leptopus phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces bright yellow, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a gelatinous coating. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
