Lamium reniforme

Lamium reniforme, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and its flowers are tubular with two lips, typically in shades of purple or pink. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and ability to spread through stolons.

Lamium reniforme is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
