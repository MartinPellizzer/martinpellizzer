Lathyrus rotundifolius var. ellipticus

Lathyrus rotundifolius var. ellipticus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped or elliptical leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and edible plants.

This variety is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
