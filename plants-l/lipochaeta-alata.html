Lipochaeta alata

Lipochaeta alata, commonly known as the winged bushwillow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems that are covered in stiff, hairy structures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on short, leafless stalks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Lipochaeta alata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in regions with limited rainfall.
