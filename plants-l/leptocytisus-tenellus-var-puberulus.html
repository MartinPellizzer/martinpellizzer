Leptocytisus tenellus var. puberulus

Leptocytisus tenellus var. puberulus, commonly known as the silver weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are small, ovate, and also bear a fuzzy texture, contributing to its overall soft, matte look. It produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the autumn.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance.
