Lonicera brevisepala

Lonicera brevisepala, commonly known as the short-lobed honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically appearing in late spring. Its fruits are red berries that mature in late summer, attracting various bird species.

Lonicera brevisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
