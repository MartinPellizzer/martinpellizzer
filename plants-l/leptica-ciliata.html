Leptica ciliata

Leptica ciliata, commonly known as the silky leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-covered leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Leptica ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
