Linaria cryptarum

Linaria cryptarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant's leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name.
