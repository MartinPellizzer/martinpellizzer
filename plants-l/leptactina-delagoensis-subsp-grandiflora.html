Leptactina delagoensis subsp. grandiflora

Leptactina delagoensis subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered leptactina, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a strong scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
