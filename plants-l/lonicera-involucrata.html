Lonicera involucrata

Lonicera involucrata, commonly known as the white honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall. Lonicera involucrata is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

It is a valuable plant for wildlife, providing food and shelter for various birds and insects.
