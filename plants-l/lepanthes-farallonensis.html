Lepanthes farallonensis

Lepanthes farallonensis, commonly known as the Farallon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the island of Farallon, off the coast of California.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a single, fragrant, white or pale pink flower. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique microclimate of its rocky coastal habitat. Lepanthes farallonensis is known for its rarity and is considered an endangered species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in the region.
