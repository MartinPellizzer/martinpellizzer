Lavatera triloba subsp. agrigentina

Lavatera triloba subsp.

agrigentina, commonly known as the Agrigentine woolly mallow, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
