Ligustrum japonicum f. leucocarpum

Ligustrum japonicum f. leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited Japanese privet, is a variant of the Japanese privet shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive white or pale berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a common ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in landscaping but can become invasive in certain regions.
