Lepanthes expansilabia

Lepanthes expansilabia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
