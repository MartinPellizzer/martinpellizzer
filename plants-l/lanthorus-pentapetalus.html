Lanthorus pentapetalus

Lanthorus pentapetalus, commonly known as the five-petaled orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with five distinct petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The orchid's floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
