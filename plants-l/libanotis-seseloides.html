Libanotis seseloides

Libanotis seseloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
