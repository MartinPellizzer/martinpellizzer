Lasianthus coronatus

Lasianthus coronatus, commonly known as the crowned lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often have a crown-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
