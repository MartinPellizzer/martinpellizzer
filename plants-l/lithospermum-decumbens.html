Lithospermum decumbens

Lithospermum decumbens, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit, with slender stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive red or orange berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lithospermum decumbens is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
