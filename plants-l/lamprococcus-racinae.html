Lamprococcus racinae

Lamprococcus racinae, commonly known as the root lichen, is a unique organism that forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungal component and a cyanobacterial partner.

It typically grows on the surfaces of roots, particularly those of grasses and other low-growing plants. The plant-like structure of Lamprococcus racinae is actually a composite of both fungal and algal components, making it a lichen rather than a true plant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments where it can absorb nutrients from the soil through its root substrate.

This organism plays a minor but ecologically significant role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.
