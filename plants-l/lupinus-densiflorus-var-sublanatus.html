Lupinus densiflorus var. sublanatus

Lupinus densiflorus var. sublanatus, commonly known as the denseflower lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spikelike inflorescence of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
