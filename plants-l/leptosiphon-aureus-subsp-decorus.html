Leptosiphon aureus subsp. decorus

Leptosiphon aureus subsp.

decorus, commonly known as the golden beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in dry, open habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
