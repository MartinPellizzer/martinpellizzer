Luzula Parviflora Var. Divaricata

Slender woodrush, scientifically known as Luzula parviflora var. divaricata, is a low-growing evergreen perennial that thrives in cool, moist environments. This variant of the woodrush genus is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. It is commonly found in shaded woodlands and rocky slopes across temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. Despite its modest appearance, the plant plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds. Its resilience and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
