Lepanthes adamsii

Lepanthes adamsii, commonly known as Adams' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. The plant features delicate, spurred flowers that are often brightly colored, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Lepanthes adamsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
