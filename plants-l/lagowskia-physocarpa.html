Lagowskia physocarpa

Lagowskia physocarpa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons, which are a key feature in its common name. These pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in the plant's spread.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
