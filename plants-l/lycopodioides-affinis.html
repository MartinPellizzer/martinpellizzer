Lycopodioides affinis

Lycopodioides affinis, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodioides affinis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
