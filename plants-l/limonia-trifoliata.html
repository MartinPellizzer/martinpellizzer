Limonia trifoliata

Limonia trifoliata, commonly known as the Chinese tangerine or the fragrant lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible citrus fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Limonia trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the citrus fruits it yields.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
