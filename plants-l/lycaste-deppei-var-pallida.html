Lycaste deppei var. pallida

Lycaste deppei var. pallida, commonly known as the pale lycaste, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a sympodial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with a slight pinkish tinge. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
