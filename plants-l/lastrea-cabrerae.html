Lastrea cabrerae

Lastrea cabrerae, commonly known as the Cabrera's lastrea, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in the spring, producing small yellowish blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
