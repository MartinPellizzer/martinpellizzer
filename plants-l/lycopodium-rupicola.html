Lycopodium rupicola

Lycopodium rupicola, commonly known as the rock clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to arid and rocky environments in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has slender, branching stems that are covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.

Lycopodium rupicola thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
