Lycopodium subulatum

Lycopodium subulatum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its structure consists of a short, upright stem with whorls of linear leaves.

Lycopodium subulatum plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
