Laggera flava

Laggera flava, commonly known as yellow chowchow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
