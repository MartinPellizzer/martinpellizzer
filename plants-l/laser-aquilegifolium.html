Laser aquilegifolium

Laser aquilegifolium, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
