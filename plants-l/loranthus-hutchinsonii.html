Loranthus hutchinsonii

Loranthus hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host tree.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are a source of food for birds and other animals. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
