Lobelia beddomeana

Lobelia beddomeana, commonly known as the beddome's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Lobelia beddomeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
