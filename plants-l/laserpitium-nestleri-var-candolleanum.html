Laserpitium nestleri var. candolleanum

Laserpitium nestleri var. candolleanum, commonly known as the Candolleanum hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
