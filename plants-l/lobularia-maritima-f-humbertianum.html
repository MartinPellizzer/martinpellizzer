Lobularia maritima f. humbertianum

Lobularia maritima f. humbertianum, commonly known as the Humbert's seaside mustard, is a compact, low-growing annual plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a fine, white bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
