Lupinus × hybridus f. vilmorinii

Lupinus × hybridus f. vilmorinii, commonly known as the Vilmorin's lupine, is a hybrid perennial legume characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, sparsely arranged flowers.

It typically produces vibrant purple to pink blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
