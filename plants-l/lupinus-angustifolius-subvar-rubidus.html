Lupinus angustifolius subvar. rubidus

Lupinus angustifolius subvar. rubidus, commonly known as the scarlet lupin, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
