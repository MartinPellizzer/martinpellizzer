Launaea resedifolia subsp. mucronata

Launaea resedifolia subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the mucronate leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, typical of the dandelion group. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system and water-storing tissues.

The subspecies mucronata is distinguished by its short, abrupt leaf tips, giving it its specific epithet.
