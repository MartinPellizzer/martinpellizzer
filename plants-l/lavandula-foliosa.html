Lavandula foliosa

Lavandula foliosa, commonly known as shrub lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact, woody growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lavandula foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and drought tolerance.
