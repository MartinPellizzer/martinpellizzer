Limonium binervosum var. sarniense

Limonium binervosum var. sarniense, commonly known as the Sarnian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salty environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in dunes or rocky shores.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
