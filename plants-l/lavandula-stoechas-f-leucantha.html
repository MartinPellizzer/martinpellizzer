Lavandula stoechas f. leucantha

Lavandula stoechas f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered lavender, is a cultivar of the lavender plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale blue flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage. This variety is known for its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

The plant is also valued for its use in essential oils and as a decorative element in landscaping.
