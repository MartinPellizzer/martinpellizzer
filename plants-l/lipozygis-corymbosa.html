Lipozygis corymbosa

Lipozygis corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet-scented tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a desirable species for cultivation.
