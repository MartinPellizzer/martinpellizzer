Linanthus californicus subsp. californicus

Linanthus californicus subsp.

californicus, commonly known as the California flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
