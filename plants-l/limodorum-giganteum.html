Limodorum giganteum

Limodorum giganteum, commonly known as the giant lily of the Nile, is a striking perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive hooded shape, typically in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
