Lantana glutinosa var. rugosa

Lantana glutinosa var. rugosa, commonly known as rough lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
