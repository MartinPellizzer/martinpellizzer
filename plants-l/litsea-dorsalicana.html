Litsea dorsalicana

Litsea dorsalicana, commonly known as the mountain litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea dorsalicana is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
