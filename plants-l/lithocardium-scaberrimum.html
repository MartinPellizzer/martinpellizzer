Lithocardium scaberrimum

Lithocardium scaberrimum, commonly known as the rough stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are covered in a powdery waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Lithocardium scaberrimum is a hardy species that is well adapted to withstand drought and high levels of sunlight.
