Loasa scandens

Loasa scandens, commonly known as the climbing loasa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along supports. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a hairy surface.

Loasa scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
