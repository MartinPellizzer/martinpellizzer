Laricorchis squarrosa

Laricorchis squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Laricorchis squarrosa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
