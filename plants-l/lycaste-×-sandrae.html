Lycaste × sandrae

Lycaste × sandrae, commonly known as the Sandrae lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Lycaste meleagris and Lycaste rufescens.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil. This plant features broad, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It is typically grown as a terrestrial orchid and requires partial shade and consistent moisture for optimal growth.

Lycaste × sandrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
