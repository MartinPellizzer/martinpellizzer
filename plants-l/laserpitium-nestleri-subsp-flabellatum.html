Laserpitium nestleri subsp. flabellatum

Laserpitium nestleri subsp.

flabellatum, commonly known as the Flabellate Festoon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a corymb-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
