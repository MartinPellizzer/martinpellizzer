Linaria caulorhiza

Linaria caulorhiza, commonly known as the hairy-toothed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Linaria caulorhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
