Leptoboea multiflora subsp. grandifolia

Leptoboea multiflora subsp.

grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered leptoboea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small florets arranged in a disk-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be challenging to grow outside its native range.
