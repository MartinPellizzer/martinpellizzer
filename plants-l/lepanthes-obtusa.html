Lepanthes obtusa

Lepanthes obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved slipper orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in montane forests. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, and the flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive lip.

Lepanthes obtusa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
