Lorinseria

Lorinseria, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-white coating on the upper surface, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Lorinseria thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its striking foliage rather than its flowers.
