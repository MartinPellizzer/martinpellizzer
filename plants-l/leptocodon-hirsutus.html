Leptocodon hirsutus

Leptocodon hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy leptocodon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
