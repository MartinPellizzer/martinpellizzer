Libidibia ebano

Libidibia ebano, commonly known as ebony wood tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic qualities. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically compound, with several leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environmental conditions.
