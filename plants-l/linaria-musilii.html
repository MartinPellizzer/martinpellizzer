Linaria musilii

Linaria musilii, commonly known as the musil's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Linaria musilii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
