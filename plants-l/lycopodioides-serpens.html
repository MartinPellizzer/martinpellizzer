Lycopodioides serpens

Lycopodioides serpens, commonly known as the serpent fern, is a species of clubmoss that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spirally arranged leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in strobila, which are typically found at the tips of the stems.

Lycopodioides serpens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
