Lobelia affinis

Lobelia affinis, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, tubular, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Lobelia affinis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ecological benefits to pollinators.
