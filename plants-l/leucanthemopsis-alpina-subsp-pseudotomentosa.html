Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp. pseudotomentosa

Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp.

pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the alpine leucanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are woolly and covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine habitats.
