Linaria caesia f. lilacina

Linaria caesia f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered toadflax, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking lilac-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its slender, branching stems support clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant color and easy cultivation.
