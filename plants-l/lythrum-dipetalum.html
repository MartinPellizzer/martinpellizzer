Lythrum dipetalum

Lythrum dipetalum, commonly known as narrow-leaved lythrum, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants known for their showy blooms. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes, and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lythrum dipetalum is often found in moist, open habitats like grasslands and stream banks.
