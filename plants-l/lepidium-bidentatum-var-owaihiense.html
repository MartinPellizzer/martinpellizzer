Lepidium bidentatum var. owaihiense

Lepidium bidentatum var. owaihiense, commonly known as the Hawaiian rocket, is a variant of the common rocket plant native to Hawaii.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its small, upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, and the plant is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its native habitat.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
