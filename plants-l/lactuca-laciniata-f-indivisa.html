Lactuca laciniata f. indivisa

Lactuca laciniata f. indivisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lettuce, is a botanical variant of the common endive.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly bitter taste. This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and lettuces. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well-suited for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
