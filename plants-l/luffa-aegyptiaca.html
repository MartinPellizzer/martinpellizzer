Luffa aegyptiaca

Luffa aegyptiaca, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a tropical vine native to Africa and widely cultivated for its fibrous interior.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash, and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a fruit that matures into a spongy, fibrous structure, which is used in bath sponges and as a natural exfoliant. Luffa aegyptiaca thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season to mature.

It is also valued for its nutritional content, containing vitamins and minerals beneficial to human health.
