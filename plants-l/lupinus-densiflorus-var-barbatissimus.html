Lupinus densiflorus var. barbatissimus

Lupinus densiflorus var. barbatissimus, commonly known as the barbate lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, pinnate leaves, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
