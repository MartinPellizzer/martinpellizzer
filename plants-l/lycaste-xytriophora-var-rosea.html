Lycaste xytriophora var. rosea

Lycaste xytriophora var. rosea, commonly known as the Roseate Lycaste, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical and subtropical regions.
