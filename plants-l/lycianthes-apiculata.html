Lycianthes apiculata

Lycianthes apiculata, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically yellow or white with five petals. The fruit is a small, egg-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and can be cultivated as an ornamental plant.
