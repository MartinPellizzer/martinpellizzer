Lucinaea microphylla

Lucinaea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved greenbriar, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, thorny stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Lucinaea microphylla is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
