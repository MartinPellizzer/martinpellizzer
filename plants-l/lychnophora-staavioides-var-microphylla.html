Lychnophora staavioides var. microphylla

Lychnophora staavioides var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved lychnophora, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often accompanied by a distinctive aromatic scent. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
