Leptochilus saxicola

Leptochilus saxicola, commonly known as the rock moss, is a small, mat-forming liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow leaves that are often clustered closely together. This plant is typically found growing on rocky surfaces or in crevices, where it can retain moisture. Its structure is simple, with no true roots, stems, or leaves in the traditional sense, but rather a thallus composed of specialized cells.

Leptochilus saxicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other small organisms in its ecosystem.
