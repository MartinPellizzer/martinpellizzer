Laurembergia tetrandra var. numidica

Laurembergia tetrandra var. numidica, commonly known as the Numidian boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are highly aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate pruning.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for hedges, topiaries, and garden borders.
