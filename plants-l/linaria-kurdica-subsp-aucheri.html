Linaria kurdica subsp. aucheri

Linaria kurdica subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the Kurdish toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other members of the Linaria genus.
