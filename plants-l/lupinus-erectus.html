Lupinus erectus

Lupinus erectus, commonly known as the upright lupine, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces tall, branched clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus erectus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
