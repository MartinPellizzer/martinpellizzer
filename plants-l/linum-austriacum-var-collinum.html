Linum austriacum var. collinum

Linum austriacum var. collinum, commonly known as the Alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. It produces capsules containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Linum austriacum var. collinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
