Lobelia polyphylla var. angustifolia

Lobelia polyphylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
