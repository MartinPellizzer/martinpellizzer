Lupinus arizonicus subsp. setosissimus

Lupinus arizonicus subsp.

setosissimus, commonly known as the Arizona lupine, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and clusters of pea-like purple flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert and rocky habitats.

As a member of the legume family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.
