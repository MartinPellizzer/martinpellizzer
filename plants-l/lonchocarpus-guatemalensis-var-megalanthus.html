Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. megalanthus

Lonchocarpus guatemalensis var. megalanthus, commonly known as the giant legume, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
