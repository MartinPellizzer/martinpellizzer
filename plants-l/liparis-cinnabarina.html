Liparis cinnabarina

Liparis cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The orchid's unique coloration and growth habits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
