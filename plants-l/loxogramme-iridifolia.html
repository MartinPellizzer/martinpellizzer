Loxogramme iridifolia

Loxogramme iridifolia, commonly known as the iridescent water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

Its unique leaf structure and ability to absorb nutrients from the water make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
