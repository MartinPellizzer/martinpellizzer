Leucostele deserticola

Leucostele deserticola, commonly known as the desert leucostele, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and pale green to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a distinctive underground stem structure known as a leucostele, which is a specialized type of vascular tissue. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced water loss mechanisms.

Leucostele deserticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
