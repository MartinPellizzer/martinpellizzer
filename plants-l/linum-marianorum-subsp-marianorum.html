Linum marianorum subsp. marianorum

Linum marianorum subsp.

marianorum, commonly known as the Marian's flax, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
