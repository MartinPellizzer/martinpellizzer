Lamium galeobdolon subsp. flavidum

Lamium galeobdolon subsp.

flavidum, commonly known as yellow deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish or reddish underside, and it produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is characterized by its stolons, which allow it to spread vegetatively, and its flowers are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
