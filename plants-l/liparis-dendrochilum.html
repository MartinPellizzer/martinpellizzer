Liparis dendrochilum

Liparis dendrochilum, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of a pseudobulb, which stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Liparis dendrochilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
