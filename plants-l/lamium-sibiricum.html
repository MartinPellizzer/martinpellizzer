Lamium sibiricum

Lamium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its stolons, which allow it to spread vegetatively across the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are typically tubular with two lips, and it belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae.
