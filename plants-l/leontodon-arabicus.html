Leontodon arabicus

Leontodon arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring.

Leontodon arabicus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
