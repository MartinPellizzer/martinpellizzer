Lobelia tyrianthina

Lobelia tyrianthina, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia tyrianthina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation efforts.
