Luehea platypetala

Luehea platypetala, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for humans and birds. Luehea platypetala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
