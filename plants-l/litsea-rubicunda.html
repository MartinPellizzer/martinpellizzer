Litsea rubicunda

Litsea rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered litsea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, red-orange flowers that appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a yellowish color, providing food for various wildlife species.
