Lathyrus roseus subsp. roseus

Lathyrus roseus subsp.

roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It produces elongated pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. Lathyrus roseus subsp.

roseus is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.
