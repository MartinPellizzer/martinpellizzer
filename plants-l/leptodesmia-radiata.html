Leptodesmia radiata

Leptodesmia radiata, commonly known as the starry leptodesmia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
