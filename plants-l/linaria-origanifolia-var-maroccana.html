Linaria origanifolia var. maroccana

Linaria origanifolia var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, resembling those of oregano, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
