Lithagrostis lacryma-jobi

Lithagrostis lacryma-jobi, commonly known as the tear grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas, dunes, and wet meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Lithagrostis lacryma-jobi is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt and wind.
