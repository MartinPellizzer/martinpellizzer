Lonicera rupicola subsp. thibetica

Lonicera rupicola subsp.

thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and adjacent areas. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often grow along rocky substrates.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
