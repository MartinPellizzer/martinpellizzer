Lomatogonium sulcatum

Lomatogonium sulcatum, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive pinnately lobed structure.

The showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
