Lithocardium umbraculiferum

Lithocardium umbraculiferum, commonly known as the shadowy rock orchid, is a rare and specialized plant found in rocky, sun-exposed habitats.

It belongs to the orchid family and is characterized by its unique, often dark-colored flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are typically small and succulent, helping it conserve water in arid conditions.
