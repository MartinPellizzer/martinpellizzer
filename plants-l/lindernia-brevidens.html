Lindernia brevidens

Lindernia brevidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf lindernia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

Lindernia brevidens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
