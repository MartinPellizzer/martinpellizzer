Lippia turbinata f. magnifolia

Lippia turbinata f. magnifolia, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters, and emit a strong, minty fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
