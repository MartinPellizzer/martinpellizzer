Lepanthes privigna

Lepanthes privigna, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes privigna thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
