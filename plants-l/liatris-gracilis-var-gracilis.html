Liatris gracilis var. gracilis

Liatris gracilis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of pink to purple spires, which are its showy floral structures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.
