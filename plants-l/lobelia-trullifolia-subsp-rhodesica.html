Lobelia trullifolia subsp. rhodesica

Lobelia trullifolia subsp.

rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes lobelia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. This species thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
