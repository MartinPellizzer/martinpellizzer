Lonicera japonica f. purpurella

Lonicera japonica f. purpurella, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering plant characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and attractive, tubular flowers.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces fragrant, yellow-orange blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to East Asia but has been introduced to many parts of the world, often becoming invasive due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant for ecological gardens.

However, its aggressive growth can lead to competition with native species, requiring careful management in natural habitats.
