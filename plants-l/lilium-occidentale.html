Lilium occidentale

Lilium occidentale, commonly known as the western lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its bulbs are used for propagation.

Lilium occidentale thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
