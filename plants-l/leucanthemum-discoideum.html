Leucanthemum discoideum

Leucanthemum discoideum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive white center, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Leucanthemum discoideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of growth.
