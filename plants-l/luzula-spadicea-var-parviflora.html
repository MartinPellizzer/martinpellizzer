Luzula spadicea var. parviflora

Luzula spadicea var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf false spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes at the top of the plant.

The variety parviflora is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to the typical form of Luzula spadicea.
