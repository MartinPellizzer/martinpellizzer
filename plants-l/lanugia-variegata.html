Lanugia variegata

Lanugia variegata, commonly known as the variegated lanugia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored foliage that displays shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance, it is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
