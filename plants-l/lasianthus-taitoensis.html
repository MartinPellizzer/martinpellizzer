Lasianthus taitoensis

Lasianthus taitoensis, commonly known as the Taitoensis flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually red or orange, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects.
