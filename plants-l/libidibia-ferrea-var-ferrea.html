Libidibia ferrea var. ferrea

Libidibia ferrea var. ferrea, commonly known as ironwood or angico, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly resistant to decay, making it valuable for construction and furniture. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry to moist tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
