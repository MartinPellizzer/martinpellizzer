Lobelia rosulata f. hirtella

Lobelia rosulata f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy leaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
