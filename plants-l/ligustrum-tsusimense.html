Ligustrum tsusimense

Ligustrum tsusimense, commonly known as the Japanese privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
