Lobelia tomentosa var. paucidentata

Lobelia tomentosa var. paucidentata, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white tomentum, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
