Liquidambar tuberculata

Liquidambar tuberculata, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruit clusters that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
