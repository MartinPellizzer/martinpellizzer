Linum bungei

Linum bungei, commonly known as Bunge's flax, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Linum bungei is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in some regions for its historical and potential medicinal uses.
