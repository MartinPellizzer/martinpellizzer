Loxonia alata

Loxonia alata, commonly known as the winged thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, wing-like bracts that surround its flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Loxonia alata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
