Lobelia cuneifolia var. hirsutum

Lobelia cuneifolia var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage that gives it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
