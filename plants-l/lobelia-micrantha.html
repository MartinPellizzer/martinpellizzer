Lobelia micrantha

Lobelia micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf cardinal flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia micrantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
