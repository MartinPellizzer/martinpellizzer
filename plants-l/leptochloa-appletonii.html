Leptochloa appletonii

Leptochloa appletonii, commonly known as Appleton's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
