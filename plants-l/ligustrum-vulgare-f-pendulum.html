Ligustrum vulgare f. pendulum

Ligustrum vulgare f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping privet, is a cultivar of the common privet.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it visually striking. The plant has small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black berries in the fall.

This ornamental plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
