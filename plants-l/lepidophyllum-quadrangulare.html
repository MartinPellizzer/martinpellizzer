Lepidophyllum quadrangulare

Lepidophyllum quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angle cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its branching stems that form a shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value. Its stems are cylindrical and have a distinctive quadrangular cross-section, giving it its common name.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
