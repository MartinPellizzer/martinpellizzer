Luzula hostii var. murrea

Luzula hostii var. murrea, commonly known as the Murrea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Luzula hostii var. murrea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and ecological benefits.
