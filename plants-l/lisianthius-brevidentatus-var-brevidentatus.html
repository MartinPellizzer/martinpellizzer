Lisianthius brevidentatus var. brevidentatus

Lisianthius brevidentatus var. brevidentatus, commonly known as the short-toothed lisianthius, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with short, blunt teeth along the edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
