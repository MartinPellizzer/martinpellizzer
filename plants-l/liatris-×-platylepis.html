Liatris × platylepis

Liatris × platylepis, commonly known as the broadleaf blazing star, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a valuable addition to native gardens and restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
