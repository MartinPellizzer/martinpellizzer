Ligustrum ciliatum var. macrocarpum

Ligustrum ciliatum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited privet, is a shrub native to southeastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a slightly rough texture.

It is often used in landscaping for its dense growth and adaptability to various soil types.
