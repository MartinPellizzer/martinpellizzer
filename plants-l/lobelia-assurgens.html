Lobelia assurgens

Lobelia assurgens, commonly known as the violet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, violet to blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Lobelia assurgens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
