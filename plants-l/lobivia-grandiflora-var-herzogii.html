Lobivia grandiflora var. herzogii

Lobivia grandiflora var. herzogii, commonly known as Herzog's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted for water storage, typical of desert plants.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
