Liquidambar styraciflua f. rotundiloba

Liquidambar styraciflua f. rotundiloba, commonly known as the round-leaf sweet gum, is a cultivar of the sweet gum tree characterized by its distinctive round or nearly spherical leaves.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal. The leaves are typically larger and more rounded compared to the standard species, providing a unique visual texture in the landscape. This form exhibits the same rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types as its parent species.

It is often used in urban landscapes due to its tolerance of pollution and compact growth habit.
