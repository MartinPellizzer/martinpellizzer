Lilium canadense var. puberulum

Lilium canadense var. puberulum, commonly known as the Canadian lily, is a variant of the Canada lily native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, while the flowers are typically pink to red in color. This variety is distinguished by its slightly hairy stems and foliage, giving it a unique texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
