Lycopsis erecta

Lycopsis erecta, commonly known as the erect lycopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lycopsidaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, scale-like leaves arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Lycopsis erecta is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying evolutionary adaptations in plant structures.
