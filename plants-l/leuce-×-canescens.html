Leuce × canescens

Leuce × canescens, commonly known as the white sweetgeranium, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Leuce × canescens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
