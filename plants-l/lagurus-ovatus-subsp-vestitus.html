Lagurus ovatus subsp. vestitus

Lagurus ovatus subsp.

vestitus, commonly known as the velvet foxtail, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and delicate, spiky seed heads that resemble a fox's tail.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

The subspecies vestitus is distinguished by its more densely hairy foliage compared to other forms of Lagurus ovatus.
