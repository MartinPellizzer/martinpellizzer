Lotononis rostrata subsp. namaquensis

Lotononis rostrata subsp.

namaquensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is a symbol of the Namib Desert's unique botanical diversity.
