Lophopyrum scirpeum

Lophopyrum scirpeum, commonly known as the common spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Lophopyrum scirpeum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
