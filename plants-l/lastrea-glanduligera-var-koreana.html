Lastrea glanduligera var. koreana

Lastrea glanduligera var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean leafless dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its absence of leaves, with only a basal rosette of bracts. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
