Limonium thaenicum

Limonium thaenicum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, making it well adapted to harsh, windy conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium thaenicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
