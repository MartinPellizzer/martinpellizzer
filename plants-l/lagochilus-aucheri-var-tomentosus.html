Lagochilus aucheri var. tomentosus

Lagochilus aucheri var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly lungwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broad and hairy, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
