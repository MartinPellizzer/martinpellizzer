Lasianthus apiocarpus

Lasianthus apiocarpus, commonly known as the red-flowered lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
