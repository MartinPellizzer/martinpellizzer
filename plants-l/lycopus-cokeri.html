Lycopus cokeri

Lycopus cokeri, commonly known as Coker's bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming effects.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
