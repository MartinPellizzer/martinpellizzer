Lathyrus pauciflorus subsp. schaffneri

Lathyrus pauciflorus subsp.

schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soils.
