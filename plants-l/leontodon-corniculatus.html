Leontodon corniculatus

Leontodon corniculatus, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
