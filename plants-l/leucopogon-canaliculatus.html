Leucopogon canaliculatus

Leucopogon canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
