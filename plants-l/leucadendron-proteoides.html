Leucadendron proteoides

Leucadendron proteoides, commonly known as the silver crown, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white to pale yellow flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is native to the fynbos vegetation of the Western Cape, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Leucadendron proteoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
