Linaria buriatica var. lineata

Linaria buriatica var. lineata, commonly known as the Burmese toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
