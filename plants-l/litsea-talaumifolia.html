Litsea talaumifolia

Litsea talaumifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed.

Litsea talaumifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
