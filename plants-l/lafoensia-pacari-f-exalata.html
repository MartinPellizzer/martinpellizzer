Lafoensia pacari f. exalata

Lafoensia pacari f. exalata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental species. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its woody structure supports its growth in warm, humid climates. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
