Lasianthus densifolius var. calycinus

Lasianthus densifolius var. calycinus, commonly known as the dense-leaved lasianthus, is a tropical plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically arranged in whorled clusters, often displaying shades of pink or red.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
