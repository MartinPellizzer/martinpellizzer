Linochilus grantii

Linochilus grantii, commonly known as the Grant's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude grasslands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other small insects, which are attracted by their nectar-rich structure.

Linochilus grantii is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
