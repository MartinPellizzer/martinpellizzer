Lasianthus verticillatus

Lasianthus verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, whorled arrangement of leaves and vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Lasianthus verticillatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
