Lespedeza chiisanensis

Lespedeza chiisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It produces small, yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
