Lactuca forsteniana

Lactuca forsteniana, commonly known as Forsten's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright growth habit and edible, leafy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a potential candidate for culinary use.

Lactuca forsteniana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
