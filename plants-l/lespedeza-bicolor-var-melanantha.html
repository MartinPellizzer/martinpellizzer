Lespedeza bicolor var. melanantha

Lespedeza bicolor var. melanantha, commonly known as the blackstem lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bi- or tri-lobed leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Its seeds are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

The variety melanantha is distinguished by its darker stems and leaves compared to the typical form of L. bicolor.
