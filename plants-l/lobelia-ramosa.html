Lobelia ramosa

Lobelia ramosa, commonly known as the branching lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that branch profusely, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes. Lobelia ramosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
