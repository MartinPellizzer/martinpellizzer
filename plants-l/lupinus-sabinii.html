Lupinus sabinii

Lupinus sabinii, commonly known as Sabin's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus sabinii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
