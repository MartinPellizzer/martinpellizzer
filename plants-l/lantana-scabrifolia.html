Lantana scabrifolia

Lantana scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf lantana, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. The leaves are typically rough to the touch, with a distinctive scabrous texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it can be invasive in some areas, requiring careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
