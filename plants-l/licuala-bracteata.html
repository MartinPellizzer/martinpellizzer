Licuala bracteata

Licuala bracteata, commonly known as the giant pandan or bracted pandan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional cooking and crafts due to their fibrous texture.

The plant's ornamental value has also made it popular in horticulture.
