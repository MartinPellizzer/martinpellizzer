Laurentia pygmaea

Laurentia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf laurentia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in coastal areas.
