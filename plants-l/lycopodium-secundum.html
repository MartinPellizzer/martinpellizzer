Lycopodium secundum

Lycopodium secundum, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its production of spore-bearing cones called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium secundum plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
