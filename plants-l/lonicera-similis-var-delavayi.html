Lonicera similis var. delavayi

Lonicera similis var. delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan in China.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its twining stems allow it to climb or trail, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
