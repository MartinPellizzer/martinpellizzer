Linaria × rocheri

Linaria × rocheri, commonly known as the hybrid common toadflax, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Linaria × rocheri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
