Lobivia haageana f. grandiflora-stellata

Lobivia haageana f. grandiflora-stellata, commonly known as the Stellate Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
