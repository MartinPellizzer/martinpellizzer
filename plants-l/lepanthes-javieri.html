Lepanthes javieri

Lepanthes javieri, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes javieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
