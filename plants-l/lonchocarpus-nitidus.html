Lonchocarpus nitidus

Lonchocarpus nitidus, commonly known as the white senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive pods that are typically green to brown in color and contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
