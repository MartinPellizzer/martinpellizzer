Lesquerella ludoviciana var. arenosa

Lesquerella ludoviciana var. arenosa, commonly known as the sand lesquerella, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Lesquerella ludoviciana var. arenosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
