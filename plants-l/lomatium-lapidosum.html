Lomatium lapidosum

Lomatium lapidosum, commonly known as mountain lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of its family, and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It is often found in open woodlands and meadows at higher elevations.

Lomatium lapidosum is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
