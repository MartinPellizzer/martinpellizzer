Lychnis bhutanica

Lychnis bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutan carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Lychnis bhutanica is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
