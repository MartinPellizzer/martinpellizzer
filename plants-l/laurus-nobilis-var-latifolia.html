Laurus nobilis var. latifolia

Laurus nobilis var. latifolia, commonly known as sweet bay laurel, is a cultivar of the bay tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, dark green leaves that are aromatic and often used in cooking. The plant is evergreen, with a compact, shrub-like growth habit that makes it suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by dark, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

The leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
