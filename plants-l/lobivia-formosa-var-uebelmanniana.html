Lobivia formosa var. uebelmanniana

Lobivia formosa var. uebelmanniana, commonly known as the Uebelmann's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and dense rosette of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
