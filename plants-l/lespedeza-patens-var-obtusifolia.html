Lespedeza patens var. obtusifolia

Lespedeza patens var. obtusifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a perennial forb or shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish flowers arranged in spikes, and its seed pods are covered in hooked bristles that aid in seed dispersal. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in the southeastern United States.

Lespedeza patens var. obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
