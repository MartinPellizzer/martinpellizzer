Lesia savannarum

Lesia savannarum, commonly known as the savanna daisy, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in savanna ecosystems.

Lesia savannarum is often used in ornamental gardening for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
