Landolphia subsessilis

Landolphia subsessilis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Landolphia subsessilis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
