Linum laeve

Linum laeve, commonly known as the smooth flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of linseed oil.

Linum laeve is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
