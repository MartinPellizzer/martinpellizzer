Lablab vulgaris

Lablab vulgaris, commonly known as hyacinth bean, is an annual leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pods that contain edible, kidney-shaped seeds. Lablab vulgaris is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and minerals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is commonly grown as a cover crop or for forage.
