Limnanthes alba subsp. alba

Limnanthes alba subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white marsh plant, is a herbaceous annual plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
