Lycopersicon philippinarum

Lycopersicon philippinarum, commonly known as the Philippine tomato, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits resembling tomatoes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local culinary practices.
