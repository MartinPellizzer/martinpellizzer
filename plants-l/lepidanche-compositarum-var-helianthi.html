Lepidanche compositarum var. helianthi

Lepidanche compositarum var. helianthi, commonly known as the sunflower-like composite, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the composite flower head structure. The plant is characterized by its robust stem and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
