Linum wetschkyanum

Linum wetschkyanum, commonly known as the Wetschkyanum flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used for fiber or oil extraction. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
