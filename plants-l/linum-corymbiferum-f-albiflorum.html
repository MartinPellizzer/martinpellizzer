Linum corymbiferum f. albiflorum

Linum corymbiferum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered flax, is a cultivar of the flax plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The white-flowered form is a variation of the typical blue-flowered Linum corymbiferum, showcasing the genetic diversity within the species.
