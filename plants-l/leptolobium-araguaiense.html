Leptolobium araguaiense

Leptolobium araguaiense, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a woody shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its hard wood and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
