Lepanthes × tuerckheimii

Lepanthes × tuerckheimii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, with intricate patterns.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
