Lotus glareosus

Lotus glareosus, commonly known as the sun lotus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has palmately compound leaves with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its dry environment. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

The seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod and are dispersed by wind, aiding in the plant's propagation across its native habitat.
