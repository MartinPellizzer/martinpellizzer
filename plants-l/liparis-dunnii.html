Liparis dunnii

Liparis dunnii, commonly known as the Dunn's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are borne on slender inflorescences.

Liparis dunnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
