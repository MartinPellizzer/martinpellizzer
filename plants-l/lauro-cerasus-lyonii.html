Lauro-cerasus lyonii

Lauro-cerasus lyonii, commonly known as the Lyon's cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

The Lyon's cherry laurel is also valued for its fragrant foliage and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
