Lavandula dentata var. candicans

Lavandula dentata var. candicans, commonly known as the Portuguese lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and fragrant, spike-like clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The leaves are narrow, gray-green, and slightly serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
