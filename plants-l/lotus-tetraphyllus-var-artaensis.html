Lotus tetraphyllus var. artaensis

Lotus tetraphyllus var. artaensis, commonly known as the Armenian lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with rounded lobes, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.

The species is relatively rare and requires specific environmental conditions for successful cultivation.
