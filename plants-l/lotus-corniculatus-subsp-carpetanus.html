Lotus corniculatus subsp. carpetanus

Lotus corniculatus subsp.

carpetanus, commonly known as the Carpathian lotus, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to mountainous environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
