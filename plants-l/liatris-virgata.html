Liatris virgata

Liatris virgata, commonly known as purpletop, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Liatris virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
