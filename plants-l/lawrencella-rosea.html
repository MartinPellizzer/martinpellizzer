Lawrencella rosea

Lawrencella rosea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Lawrencella rosea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
