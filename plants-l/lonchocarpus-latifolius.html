Lonchocarpus latifolius

Lonchocarpus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf cowpea, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
