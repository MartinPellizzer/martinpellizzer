Liparis leratii

Liparis leratii, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has a short rhizome and produces numerous slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
