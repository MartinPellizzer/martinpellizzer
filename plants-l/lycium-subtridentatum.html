Lycium subtridentatum

Lycium subtridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
