Lysimachia platyphylla

Lysimachia platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Lysimachia platyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
