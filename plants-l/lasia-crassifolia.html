Lasia crassifolia

Lasia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved lasia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
