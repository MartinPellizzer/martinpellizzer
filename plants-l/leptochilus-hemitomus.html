Leptochilus hemitomus

Leptochilus hemitomus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
