Loranthus cinctus

Loranthus cinctus, commonly known as the scarlet mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and small, clustered white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds and other animals. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia, and is often found in forested areas.

Its parasitic nature allows it to derive moisture and nutrients from its host, while also contributing to ecological interactions within its habitat.
