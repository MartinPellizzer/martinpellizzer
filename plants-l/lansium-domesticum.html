Lansium domesticum

Lansium domesticum, commonly known as the kumquat tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible both raw and cooked. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are fragrant and white, typically appearing in clusters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.
