Leontodon jailae

Leontodon jailae, commonly known as the Maltese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Maltese Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Leontodon jailae is notable for its ecological significance, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
