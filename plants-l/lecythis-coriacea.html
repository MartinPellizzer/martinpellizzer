Lecythis coriacea

Lecythis coriacea, commonly known as the thick-shelled chestnut, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, heavy, and often spiny fruits. The plant is known for its durable, hard wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. Its leaves are typically large and palmately lobed, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
