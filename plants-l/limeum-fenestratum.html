Limeum fenestratum

Limeum fenestratum, commonly known as the fenestrated lime, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive fenestrations, or leaf holes, which give it its common name. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
