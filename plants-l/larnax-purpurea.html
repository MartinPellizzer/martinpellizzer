Larnax purpurea

Larnax purpurea, commonly known as the purple larnax, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its foliage is often lance-shaped with a reddish tinge, adding to its ornamental value.

Larnax purpurea is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
