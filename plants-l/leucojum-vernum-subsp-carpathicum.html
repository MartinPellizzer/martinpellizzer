Leucojum vernum subsp. carpathicum

Leucojum vernum subsp.

carpathicum, commonly known as the Carpathian snowflake, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
