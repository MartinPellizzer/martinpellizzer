Lithospermum pulchrum

Lithospermum pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Lithospermum pulchrum is often found growing in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
