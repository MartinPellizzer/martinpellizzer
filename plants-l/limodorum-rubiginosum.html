Limodorum rubiginosum

Limodorum rubiginosum, commonly known as the red-staining lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge, which contributes to its common name.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
