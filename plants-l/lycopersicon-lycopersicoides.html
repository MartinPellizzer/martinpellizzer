Lycopersicon lycopersicoides

Lycopersicon lycopersicoides, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small tomatoes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

The plant is closely related to the domesticated tomato (Lycopersicon esculentum) and shares similar botanical features.
