Laportea lageensis

Laportea lageensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

Laportea lageensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
