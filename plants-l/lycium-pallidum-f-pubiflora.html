Lycium pallidum f. pubiflora

Lycium pallidum f. pubiflora, commonly known as the pale boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish flowers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its specific epithet "pubiflora.". The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
