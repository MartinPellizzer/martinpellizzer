Lampranthus stephanii

Lampranthus stephanii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a standout in both cultivated and natural settings.
