Loranthus mechowii var. welwitschianus

Loranthus mechowii var. welwitschianus, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pink flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are often consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal.

This species is notable for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
