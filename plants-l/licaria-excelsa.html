Licaria excelsa

Licaria excelsa, commonly known as the giant licaria, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching several inches in diameter. The plant has a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are known to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to their vibrant colors and sweet nectar.

Licaria excelsa is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
