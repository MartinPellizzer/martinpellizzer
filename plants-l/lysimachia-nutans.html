Lysimachia nutans

Lysimachia nutans, commonly known as the hanging loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features prostrate or trailing stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Lysimachia nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
