Leontice leontopetalum var. ewersmannii

Leontice leontopetalum var. ewersmannii, commonly known as the Ewersmann's leontice, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are covered in soft, glandular hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
