Leucopogon glabratus

Leucopogon glabratus, commonly known as the smooth leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or sandy habitats. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds.

Leucopogon glabratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native range.
