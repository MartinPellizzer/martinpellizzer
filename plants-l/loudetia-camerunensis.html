Loudetia camerunensis

Loudetia camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon grass, is a perennial herb native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in tufts.

Loudetia camerunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and ability to thrive in various conditions.
