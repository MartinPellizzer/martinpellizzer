Laurus reticulata

Laurus reticulata, commonly known as Chinese bay leaf, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Its leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.
