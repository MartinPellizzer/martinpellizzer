Lilium martagon var. spicatum

Lilium martagon var. spicatum, commonly known as the Spicata Lily, is a unique variant of the Turk's Cap lily.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, arranged in clusters along the stem. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats. It produces sword-shaped leaves and has a long blooming period, often extending from late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
