Loranthus constrictiflorus var. karaguensis

Loranthus constrictiflorus var. karaguensis, commonly known as the Karagush or Karaguan, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on the branches of host trees such as figs and ebony. The plant has small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the dry season, and its leaves are simple, opposite, and often glossy. It relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators like bees and wasps.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
