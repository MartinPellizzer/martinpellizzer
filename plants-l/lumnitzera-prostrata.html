Lumnitzera prostrata

Lumnitzera prostrata, commonly known as the mangrove shrub or sea grape, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It thrives in coastal areas, particularly in mangrove swamps, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines. The plant has aerial roots that help in gas exchange and provide structural support. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Lumnitzera prostrata is also notable for its ability to tolerate periodic submersion and high salinity levels.
