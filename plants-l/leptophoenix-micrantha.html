Leptophoenix micrantha

Leptophoenix micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf date palm, is a small palm native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and clustered, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
