Lathyrus annuus var. cassius

Lathyrus annuus var. cassius, commonly known as the Cassius vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to lilac. The plant produces small, elliptical pods containing several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
