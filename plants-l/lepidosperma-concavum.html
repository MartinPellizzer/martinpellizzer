Lepidosperma concavum

Lepidosperma concavum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in coastal areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Lepidosperma concavum plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
