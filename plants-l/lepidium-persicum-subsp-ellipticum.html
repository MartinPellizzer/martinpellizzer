Lepidium persicum subsp. ellipticum

Lepidium persicum subsp.

ellipticum, commonly known as Persian cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color.

The plant produces small, kidney-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
