Lupinus argenteus var. aristatovatus

Lupinus argenteus var. aristatovatus, commonly known as silver lupine with aristate leaves, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features silvery-white to gray-green foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically pink to purple, forming dense spikes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its unique leaf morphology and showy blooms make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
