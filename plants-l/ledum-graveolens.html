Ledum graveolens

Ledum graveolens, commonly known as bog rosemary, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant contains essential oils and various bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and phenolic acids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and heathlands.

Due to its medicinal uses, it has been historically valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
