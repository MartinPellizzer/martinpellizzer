Licuala acaulis

Licuala acaulis, commonly known as the rock cycad, is a species of cycad native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
