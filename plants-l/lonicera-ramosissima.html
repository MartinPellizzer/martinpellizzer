Lonicera ramosissima

Lonicera ramosissima, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. These flowers give way to red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

Lonicera ramosissima is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
