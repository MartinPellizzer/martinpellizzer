Laurus dysodantha

Laurus dysodantha, commonly known as the slender laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
