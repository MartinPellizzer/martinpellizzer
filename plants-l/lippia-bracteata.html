Lippia bracteata

Lippia bracteata, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Lippia bracteata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and aromatic properties.
