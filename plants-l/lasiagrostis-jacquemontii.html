Lasiagrostis jacquemontii

Lasiagrostis jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, tufted stems with fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence is a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Lasiagrostis jacquemontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, grass-like foliage.
