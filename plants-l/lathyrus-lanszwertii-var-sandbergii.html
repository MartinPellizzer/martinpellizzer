Lathyrus lanszwertii var. sandbergii

Lathyrus lanszwertii var. sandbergii, commonly known as the Sandberg's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. Its trifoliate leaves are pinnately compound, with stipules at the base of each leaflet.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
