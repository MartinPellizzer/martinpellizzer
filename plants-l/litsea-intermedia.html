Litsea intermedia

Litsea intermedia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue fruits.

Litsea intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
