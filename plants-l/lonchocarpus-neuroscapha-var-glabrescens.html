Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. glabrescens

Lonchocarpus neuroscapha var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous legume, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its dense, often thorny foliage and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellow or orange hue.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
