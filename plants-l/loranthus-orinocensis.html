Loranthus orinocensis

Loranthus orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in the Neotropical region.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and white, fleshy flowers that bloom in clusters. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Loranthus orinocensis plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
