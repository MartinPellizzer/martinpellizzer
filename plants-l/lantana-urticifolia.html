Lantana urticifolia

Lantana urticifolia, commonly known as the greater lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy as well. Lantana urticifolia is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

It is also noted for its toxic properties, with both its leaves and flowers containing irritants that can cause skin reactions.
