Lasianthus verticillatus var. taitoensis

Lasianthus verticillatus var. taitoensis, commonly known as the Taito bamboo or Taito false bamboo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, slender stems that resemble bamboo, though it is not a true bamboo species. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical gardens.
