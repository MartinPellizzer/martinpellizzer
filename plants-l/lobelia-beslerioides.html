Lobelia beslerioides

Lobelia beslerioides, commonly known as the Besler's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia beslerioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
