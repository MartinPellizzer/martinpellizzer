Linum narbonense var. microphyllum

Linum narbonense var. microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used for textile production and medicinal purposes.
