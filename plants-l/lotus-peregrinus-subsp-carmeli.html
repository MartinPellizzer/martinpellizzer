Lotus peregrinus subsp. carmeli

Lotus peregrinus subsp.

carmeli, commonly known as the Carmel lotus, is a rare aquatic plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

The plant features striking pink to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often float on the water's surface.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
