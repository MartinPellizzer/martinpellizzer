Lythrum loiseleurii

Lythrum loiseleurii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight reddish hue, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Lythrum loiseleurii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
