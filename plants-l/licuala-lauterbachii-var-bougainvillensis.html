Licuala lauterbachii var. bougainvillensis

Licuala lauterbachii var. bougainvillensis, commonly known as the Bougainville palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized palm characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces striking clusters of colorful bracts, typically in shades of pink, purple, or red, which are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
