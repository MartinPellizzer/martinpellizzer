Lamium nepalense

Lamium nepalense, commonly known as Nepal deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves with a distinctive velvety texture, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along streams.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
