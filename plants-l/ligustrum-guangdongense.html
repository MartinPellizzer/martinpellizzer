Ligustrum guangdongense

Ligustrum guangdongense, commonly known as Guangdong privet, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ligustrum guangdongense is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
