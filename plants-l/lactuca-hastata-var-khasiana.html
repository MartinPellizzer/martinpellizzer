Lactuca hastata var. khasiana

Lactuca hastata var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi lettuce, is a wild lettuce species native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, upright stems and lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, which are followed by small, smooth, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is also used in traditional culinary practices.
