Libanotis nitens

Libanotis nitens, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers with white petals and prominent yellow stamens. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
