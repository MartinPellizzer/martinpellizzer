Lolium canariense

Lolium canariense, commonly known as Canary ryegrass, is a species of grass native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions.
