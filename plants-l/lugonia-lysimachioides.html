Lugonia lysimachioides

Lugonia lysimachioides, commonly known as the Lyramat flowering plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, delicate, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
