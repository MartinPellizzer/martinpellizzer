Linum aristidis

Linum aristidis, commonly known as the slender flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Linum aristidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional herbal medicine.
