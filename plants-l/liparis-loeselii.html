Liparis loeselii

Liparis loeselii, commonly known as the Loesel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Liparis loeselii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
