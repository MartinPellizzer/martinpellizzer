Lonicera chrysantha regeliana

Lonicera chrysantha regeliana, commonly known as the yellow honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Lonicera chrysantha regeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
