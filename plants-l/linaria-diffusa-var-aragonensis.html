Linaria diffusa var. aragonensis

Linaria diffusa var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon poppy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple base. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun and well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in marginal environments.
