Lychnis saponaria

Lychnis saponaria, commonly known as soapwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs and produces clusters of pink or red, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly succulent. Soapwort is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its common name derives from the sap's historical use in making soap due to its saponin content.
