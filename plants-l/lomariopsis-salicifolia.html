Lomariopsis salicifolia

Lomariopsis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that are narrow and elongated, often with a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting pollinators.

This unique botanical feature makes it a fascinating subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
