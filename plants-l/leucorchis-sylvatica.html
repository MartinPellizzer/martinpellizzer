Leucorchis sylvatica

Leucorchis sylvatica, commonly known as the forest white orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate forests of eastern North America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often under the canopy of deciduous trees. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, showy white or pale green flower that blooms in late spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
