Lilium martagon subsp. vestitum

Lilium martagon subsp.

vestitum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a botanical variant of the Turk's cap lily species. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Caucasus.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
