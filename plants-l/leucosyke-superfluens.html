Leucosyke superfluens

Leucosyke superfluens, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a spherical, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Leucosyke superfluens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
