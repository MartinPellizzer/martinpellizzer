Lonicera speciosa

Lonicera speciosa, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a deciduous or semi-evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

Lonicera speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
