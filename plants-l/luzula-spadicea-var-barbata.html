Luzula spadicea var. barbata

Luzula spadicea var. barbata, commonly known as the barbate wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-brown flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in horticulture for erosion control due to their dense root system.

The variety barbata is distinguished by its hairy or barbed leaf margins, which give it its specific name.
