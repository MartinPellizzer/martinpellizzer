Lepironia mucronata var. capitata

Lepironia mucronata var. capitata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
