Lepanthes antiopa

Lepanthes antiopa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive greenish-white lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Lepanthes antiopa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
