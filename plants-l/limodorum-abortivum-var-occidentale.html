Limodorum abortivum var. occidentale

Limodorum abortivum var. occidentale, commonly known as the western marsh orchid, is a terrestrial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
