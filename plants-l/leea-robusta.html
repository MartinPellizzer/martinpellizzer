Leea robusta

Leea robusta, commonly known as the rough leechwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Leea robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
