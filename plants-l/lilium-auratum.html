Lilium auratum

Lilium auratum, commonly known as the golden lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with red spots. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features strap-like, grassy leaves. Its bulbs are perennial and thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
