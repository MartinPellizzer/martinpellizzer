Lepidium divergens

Lepidium divergens, commonly known as the clustered cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lepidium divergens typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.
