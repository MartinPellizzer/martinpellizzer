Lobelia paxtoniana

Lobelia paxtoniana, commonly known as the Paxton's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers.

Lobelia paxtoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
