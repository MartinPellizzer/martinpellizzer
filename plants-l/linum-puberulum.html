Linum puberulum

Linum puberulum, commonly known as silky flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, oval seeds. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Linum puberulum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
