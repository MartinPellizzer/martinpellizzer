Laurus caesia

Laurus caesia, commonly known as the white laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dark, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Laurus caesia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant fragrance.
