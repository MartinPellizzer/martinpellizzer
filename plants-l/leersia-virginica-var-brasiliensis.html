Leersia virginica var. brasiliensis

Leersia virginica var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flat sedge, is a grass-like plant native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers. This variant is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical structure includes rhizomes that help it spread vegetatively.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
