Linaria dentata

Linaria dentata, commonly known as the toothed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers with a distinctive bilabiate structure. The leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Linaria dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
