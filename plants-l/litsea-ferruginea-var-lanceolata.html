Litsea ferruginea var. lanceolata

Litsea ferruginea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the rusty litsea or lanceolate litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
