Lycium gilliesianum var. longitubum

Lycium gilliesianum var. longitubum, commonly known as the long-tubed boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The botanical features of this variety include its distinctive floral structure and adaptive traits that allow it to survive in harsh environments.
