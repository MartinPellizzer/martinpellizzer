Lepidium bonariense var. dillenianum

Lepidium bonariense var. dillenianum, commonly known as the Argentine cress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
