Lippia polycephala var. aemilii

Lippia polycephala var. aemilii, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
