Lycium canum

Lycium canum, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or goji berry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems, alternate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid climates.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.
