Limniris × sancti-cyrii

Limniris × sancti-cyrii, commonly known as the sacred lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Limniris saccardii and Limniris cyrii.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Limniris × sancti-cyrii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
