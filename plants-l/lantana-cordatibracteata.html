Lantana cordatibracteata

Lantana cordatibracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its flowers attract a wide range of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
