Leptochloa pellucidula

Leptochloa pellucidula, commonly known as the translucent grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that often appear translucent or waxy. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces minute, wind-pollinated flowers.
