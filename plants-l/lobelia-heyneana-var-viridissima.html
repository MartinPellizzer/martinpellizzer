Lobelia heyneana var. viridissima

Lobelia heyneana var. viridissima, commonly known as the Emerald Lobelia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for container gardening and ground cover.
