Lantana nivea var. mutabilis

Lantana nivea var. mutabilis, commonly known as the white lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, depending on the stage of bloom. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
