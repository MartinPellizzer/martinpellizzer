Lobelia pinnata

Lobelia pinnata, commonly known as the whorled loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as swamps and wetlands.

Lobelia pinnata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for respiratory and digestive ailments.
