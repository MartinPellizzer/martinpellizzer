Laurus attenuata

Laurus attenuata, commonly known as bay laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient civilizations, where it was revered for its fragrance and symbolic value.
