Lobelia coccinea

Lobelia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lobelia coccinea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
