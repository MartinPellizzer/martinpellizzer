Lactuca cernua

Lactuca cernua, commonly known as the nodding lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Lactuca cernua is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
