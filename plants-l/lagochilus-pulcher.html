Lagochilus pulcher

Lagochilus pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Lagochilus pulcher is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
