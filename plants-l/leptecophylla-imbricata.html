Leptecophylla imbricata

Leptecophylla imbricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky oak, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The leaves are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clusters of winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Its unique leaf structure and adaptability to humid conditions make it a notable species in botanical studies.
