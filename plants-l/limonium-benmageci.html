Limonium benmageci

Limonium benmageci, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium benmageci is often used in coastal landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
