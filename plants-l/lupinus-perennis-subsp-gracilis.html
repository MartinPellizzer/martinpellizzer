Lupinus perennis subsp. gracilis

Lupinus perennis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Lupinus perennis subsp.

gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
