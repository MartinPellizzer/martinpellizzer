Lobivia wessneriana

Lobivia wessneriana, commonly known as the Wessner's lobivia, is a compact cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features short, ribbed stems with prominent areoles and produces clusters of yellow flowers during the cooler months. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to extreme arid conditions. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
