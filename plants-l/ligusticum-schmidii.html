Ligusticum schmidii

Ligusticum schmidii, commonly known as Schmid's angelica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The roots of Ligusticum schmidii are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
