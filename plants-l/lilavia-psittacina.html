Lilavia psittacina

Lilavia psittacina, commonly known as the parrot's beak, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
