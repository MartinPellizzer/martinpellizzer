Luzula campestris f. vulgaris

Luzula campestris f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and narrow. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula campestris f. vulgaris is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
