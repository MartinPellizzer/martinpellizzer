Linociera eriorachis

Linociera eriorachis, commonly known as the woolly linociera, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Linociera eriorachis is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
