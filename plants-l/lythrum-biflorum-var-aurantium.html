Lythrum biflorum var. aurantium

Lythrum biflorum var. aurantium, commonly known as the golden lythrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes plants commonly known as loosestrifes. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The golden lythrum is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.
