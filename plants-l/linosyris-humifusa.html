Linosyris humifusa

Linosyris humifusa, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Linosyris humifusa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
