Lepidium coronopifolium var. aucherianum

Lepidium coronopifolium var. aucherianum, commonly known as the mountain peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
