Lasianthus saprosmoides

Lasianthus saprosmoides, commonly known as the swamp gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are opposite and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

It plays an important ecological role in its native wetlands by providing nectar for various pollinators.
