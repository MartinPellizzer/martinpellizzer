Lespedeza pallasii

Lespedeza pallasii, commonly known as Pallas's lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, hairy pods containing seeds. Lespedeza pallasii is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
