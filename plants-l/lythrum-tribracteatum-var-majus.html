Lythrum tribracteatum var. majus

Lythrum tribracteatum var. majus, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth and ability to produce thousands of seeds make it an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
