Leucadendron tinctum

Leucadendron tinctum, commonly known as the silver frost leucadendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage that gives it a frosty appearance, and its bright yellow, conical flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Leucadendron tinctum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
