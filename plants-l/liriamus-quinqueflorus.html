Liriamus quinqueflorus

Liriamus quinqueflorus, commonly known as the five-flowered lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments in the wild.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
