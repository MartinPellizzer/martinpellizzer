Leucadendron leucoblepharis

Leucadendron leucoblepharis, commonly known as the white-eyed candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, fluffy flower heads that resemble candles, which are borne on long, slender stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its showy flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Leucadendron leucoblepharis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small birds.
