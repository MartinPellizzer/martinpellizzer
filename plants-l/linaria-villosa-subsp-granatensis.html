Linaria villosa subsp. granatensis

Linaria villosa subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the granatense toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
