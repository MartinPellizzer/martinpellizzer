Lannea ebolowensis

Lannea ebolowensis, commonly known as the Congo bitter, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
