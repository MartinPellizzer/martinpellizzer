Lonicera scabrida

Lonicera scabrida, commonly known as rough honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its berries are red or orange and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Lonicera scabrida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
