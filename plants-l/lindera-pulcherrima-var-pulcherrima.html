Lindera pulcherrima var. pulcherrima

Lindera pulcherrima var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant foliage, seasonal color changes, and ecological benefits.
