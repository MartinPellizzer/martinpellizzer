Lupinus macrostachys

Lupinus macrostachys, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open areas.

Lupinus macrostachys is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
