Lupinus hispanicus

Lupinus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense, spiky clusters.

Lupinus hispanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
