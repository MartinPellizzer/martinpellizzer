Lobostemon elongatus

Lobostemon elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed lobostemon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Lobostemon elongatus is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.
