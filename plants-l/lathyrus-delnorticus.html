Lathyrus delnorticus

Lathyrus delnorticus, commonly known as the northern grass violet, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lathyrus delnorticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
