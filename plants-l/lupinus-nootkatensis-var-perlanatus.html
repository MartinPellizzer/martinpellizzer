Lupinus nootkatensis var. perlanatus

Lupinus nootkatensis var. perlanatus, commonly known as the Alaska lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets and is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils. It thrives in moist, well-drained habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows.

Lupinus nootkatensis var. perlanatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
