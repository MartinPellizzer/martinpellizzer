Lactuca sagittarioides

Lactuca sagittarioides, commonly known as arrowleaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its slender, arrow-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in flat-topped inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Lactuca sagittarioides is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
