Lippia pendula

Lippia pendula, commonly known as hanging lippia, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Lippia pendula is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
