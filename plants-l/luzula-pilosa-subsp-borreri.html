Luzula pilosa subsp. borreri

Luzula pilosa subsp.

borreri, commonly known as the hairy wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with hairy stems and leaves that are typically dark green and narrow.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, often growing in rich, organic soils. Luzula pilosa subsp.

borreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
