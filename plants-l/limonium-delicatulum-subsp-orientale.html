Limonium delicatulum subsp. orientale

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in maritime climates. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance. Limonium delicatulum subsp.

orientale is also valued for its ability to tolerate salty air and poor soil conditions.
