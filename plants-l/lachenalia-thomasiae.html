Lachenalia thomasiae

Lachenalia thomasiae, commonly known as the Thomasia lachenalia, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It blooms during the summer months, typically from October to December.

Lachenalia thomasiae is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
