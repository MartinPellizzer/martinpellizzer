Lepanthes telipogoniflora

Lepanthes telipogoniflora, commonly known as the Telipogon orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of showy, white or pale pink blooms. Its flowers have a unique structure with long, slender petals and a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes telipogoniflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
