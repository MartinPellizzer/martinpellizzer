Lastrea coriacea

Lastrea coriacea, commonly known as the rough stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, Lastrea coriacea is often cultivated as a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
