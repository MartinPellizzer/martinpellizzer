Lasianthus multibracteatus

Lasianthus multibracteatus, commonly known as the many-bracted lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shady environments such as forest understories. It produces small, bract-like structures that surround its inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.

Lasianthus multibracteatus is valued in horticulture for its attractive blooms and adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
