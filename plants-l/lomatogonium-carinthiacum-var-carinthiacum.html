Lomatogonium carinthiacum var. carinthiacum

Lomatogonium carinthiacum var. carinthiacum, commonly known as the Carinthian lomatogonium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is adapted to rocky and limestone-rich soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
