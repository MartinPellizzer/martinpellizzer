Limonium neoscoparium

Limonium neoscoparium, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in dunes and salt marshes. It is a perennial species that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Limonium neoscoparium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
