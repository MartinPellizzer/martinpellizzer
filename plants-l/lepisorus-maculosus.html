Lepisorus maculosus

Lepisorus maculosus, commonly known as the spotted wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, scattered spots, giving it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, this plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it popular among fern enthusiasts.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
