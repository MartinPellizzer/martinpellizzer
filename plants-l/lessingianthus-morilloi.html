Lessingianthus morilloi

Lessingianthus morilloi, commonly known as the Morillo's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Lessingianthus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh climates.
