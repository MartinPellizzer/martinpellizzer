Leptocionium

Leptocionium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, a common feature among sedges.

Leptocionium plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
