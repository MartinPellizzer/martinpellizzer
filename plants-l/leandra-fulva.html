Leandra fulva

Leandra fulva, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive red throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Leandra fulva is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
