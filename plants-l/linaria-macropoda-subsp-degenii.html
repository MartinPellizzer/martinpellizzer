Linaria macropoda subsp. degenii

Linaria macropoda subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant features slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
