Lathyrus vernus f. angustifolius

Lathyrus vernus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spring vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to Europe and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
