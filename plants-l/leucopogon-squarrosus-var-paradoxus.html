Leucopogon squarrosus var. paradoxus

Leucopogon squarrosus var. paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical leucopogon, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a flowering shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in wetlands or along the edges of swamps. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many areas, highlighting the need for its protection.
