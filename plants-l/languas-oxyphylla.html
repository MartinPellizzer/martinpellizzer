Languas oxyphylla

Languas oxyphylla, commonly known as the sharp-leaved lantana, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in the summer months. Its flowering pattern attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Languas oxyphylla is also known for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
