Leonotis raineriana

Leonotis raineriana, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a striking perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
