Loxanthocereus hystrix var. brunnescens

Loxanthocereus hystrix var. brunnescens, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, pale yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the night, which are pollinated by bats and moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
