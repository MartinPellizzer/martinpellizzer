Lepidium × hybridum

Lepidium × hybridum, commonly known as field cress, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Lepidium sativum and Lepidium raphanistrum.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery aroma. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
