Ligularia dentata

Ligularia dentata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, typically ranging from 5 to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

Ligularia dentata is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
