Lyperanthus latissimus

Lyperanthus latissimus, commonly known as the broad-leaved lily, is a striking plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
