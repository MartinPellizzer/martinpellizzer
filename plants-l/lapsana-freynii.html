Lapsana freynii

Lapsana freynii, commonly known as Freyn's common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has slender, hairy stems and small, alternate leaves with a rough texture.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
