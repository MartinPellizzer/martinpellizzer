Lobostemon sphaerocephalus

Lobostemon sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, bell-shaped blooms. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lobostemon sphaerocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
