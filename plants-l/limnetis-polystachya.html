Limnetis polystachya

Limnetis polystachya, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Limnetis, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a well-developed root system that anchors it in the muddy substrate, while its leaves are broad and waxy to reduce water loss. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom above the water surface and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Limnetis polystachya plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
