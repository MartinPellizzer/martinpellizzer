Lupinus lanatocarpus

Lupinus lanatocarpus, commonly known as the woolly pod lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seed pods are thick and woolly, giving it its common name.

Lupinus lanatocarpus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
