Leontodon boryi

Leontodon boryi, commonly known as the Bory's hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Leontodon boryi is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
