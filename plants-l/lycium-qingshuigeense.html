Lycium qingshuigeense

Lycium qingshuigeense, commonly known as the Qingshuihe Goji plant, is a shrub native to China, specifically found in the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

It is noted for its medicinal properties, with its berries containing antioxidants and nutrients that have been traditionally used in Chinese medicine.
