Lyrocarpa coulteri var. palmeri

Lyrocarpa coulteri var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's boxthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces fruit that is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
