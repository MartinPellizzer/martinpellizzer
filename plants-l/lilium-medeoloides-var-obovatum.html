Lilium medeoloides var. obovatum

Lilium medeoloides var. obovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fawn lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink blossoms with a distinctive, recurved shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
