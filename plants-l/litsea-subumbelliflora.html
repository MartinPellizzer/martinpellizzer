Litsea subumbelliflora

Litsea subumbelliflora, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which are typical of the Lauraceae family. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Litsea subumbelliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and attractive foliage.
