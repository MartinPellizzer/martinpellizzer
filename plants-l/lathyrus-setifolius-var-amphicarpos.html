Lathyrus setifolius var. amphicarpos

Lathyrus setifolius var. amphicarpos, commonly known as the broad-leaved sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems that often twine around supports. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is native to the Mediterranean region.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
