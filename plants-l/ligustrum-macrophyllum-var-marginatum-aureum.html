Ligustrum macrophyllum var. marginatum-aureum

Ligustrum macrophyllum var. marginatum-aureum, commonly known as the golden-margin privet, is a variant of the privet shrub characterized by its striking foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a bright yellow margin that contrasts vividly with the dark green central portion. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark purple berries. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
