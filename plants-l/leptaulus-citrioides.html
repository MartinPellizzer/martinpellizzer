Leptaulus citrioides

Leptaulus citrioides, commonly known as the citron tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-yellow fruits resembling small limes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
