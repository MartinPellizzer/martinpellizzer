Limacia villosa

Limacia villosa, commonly known as the hairy snail vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Limacia villosa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
