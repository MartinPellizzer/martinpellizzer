Lupinus montanus var. nelsonii

Lupinus montanus var. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
