Limonium procerum var. wessexense

Limonium procerum var. wessexense, commonly known as the Wessex sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern England.

It is characterized by its upright, spindly stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. Its distinctive appearance makes it a notable species in maritime botany.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
