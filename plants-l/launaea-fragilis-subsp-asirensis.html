Launaea fragilis subsp. asirensis

Launaea fragilis subsp.

asirensis, commonly known as the fragile launaea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is typically low-growing, with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves adapted to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or white.

It is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
