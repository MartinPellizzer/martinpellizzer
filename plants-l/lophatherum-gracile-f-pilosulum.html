Lophatherum gracile f. pilosulum

Lophatherum gracile f. pilosulum, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a variant of the lemongrass plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and a distinct lemony aroma, which is due to the presence of essential oils. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, valued for their aromatic properties.

The botanical features of this variant make it unique within the Lophatherum genus.
