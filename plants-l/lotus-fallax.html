Lotus fallax

Lotus fallax, commonly known as the wild lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer.

Lotus fallax is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
