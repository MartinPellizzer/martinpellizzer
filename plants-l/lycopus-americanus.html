Lycopus americanus

Lycopus americanus, commonly known as American bugleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming effects.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
