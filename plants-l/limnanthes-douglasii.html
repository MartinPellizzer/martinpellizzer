Limnanthes douglasii

Limnanthes douglasii, commonly known as the marsh plantain, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Limnanthes douglasii is also known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soil and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
