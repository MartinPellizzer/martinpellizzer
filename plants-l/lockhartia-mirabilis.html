Lockhartia mirabilis

Lockhartia mirabilis, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, making it a popular ornamental plant. It prefers warm, humid environments and well-draining soil, often grown as a container plant.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of floral variations, contributing to its appeal among orchid enthusiasts.
