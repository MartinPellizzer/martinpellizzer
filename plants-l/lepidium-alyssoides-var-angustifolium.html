Lepidium alyssoides var. angustifolium

Lepidium alyssoides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
