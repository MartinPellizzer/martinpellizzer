Lagochilus ilicifolius var. tomentosus

Lagochilus ilicifolius var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly rosemary, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid garden environments.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to xeriscapic landscapes.
