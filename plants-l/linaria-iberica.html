Linaria iberica

Linaria iberica, commonly known as the Iberian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the plant family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Linaria iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
