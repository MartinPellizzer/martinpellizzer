Limodorum pinetorum

Limodorum pinetorum, commonly known as the pine woods lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine forests and woodland edges. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other woodland flora.

Limodorum pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
