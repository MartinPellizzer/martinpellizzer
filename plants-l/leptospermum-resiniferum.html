Leptospermum resiniferum

Leptospermum resiniferum, commonly known as the golden wattles, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy.

Leptospermum resiniferum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
