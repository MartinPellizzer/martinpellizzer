Leucosceptrum plectranthoideum

Leucosceptrum plectranthoideum, commonly known as the white-leaved plectranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in ornamental horticulture.
