Lachenalia attenuata

Lachenalia attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically narrow and fleshy. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Lachenalia attenuata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
