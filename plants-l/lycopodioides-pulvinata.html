Lycopodioides pulvinata

Lycopodioides pulvinata, commonly known as the pulvinate clubmoss, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, a feature typical of its evolutionary lineage. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Lycopodioides pulvinata is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments.
