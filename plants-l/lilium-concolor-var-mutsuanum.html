Lilium concolor var. mutsuanum

Lilium concolor var. mutsuanum, commonly known as the Mutsuanum Lily, is a rare and striking variety of lily native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink, with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
