Lupinus abramsii

Lupinus abramsii, commonly known as Abrams' lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lupinus abramsii is an important species for both ecological and botanical study due to its unique adaptations and role in local ecosystems.
