Lerchia maritima var. salsa

Lerchia maritima var. salsa, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiky, silver-gray bracts. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dune environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
