Leucopogon darlingensis

Leucopogon darlingensis, commonly known as Darling's heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
