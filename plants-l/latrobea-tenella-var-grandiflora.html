Latrobea tenella var. grandiflora

Latrobea tenella var. grandiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered latrobea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
