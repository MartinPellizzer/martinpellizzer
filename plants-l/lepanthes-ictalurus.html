Lepanthes ictalurus

Lepanthes ictalurus, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a distinctive red or purple tip on the lip.

Lepanthes ictalurus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
