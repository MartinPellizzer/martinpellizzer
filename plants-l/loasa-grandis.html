Loasa grandis

Loasa grandis, commonly known as the showy loasa, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its attractive, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Loasa grandis is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
