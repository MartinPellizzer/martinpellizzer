Lilium bulbiferum subsp. croceum

Lilium bulbiferum subsp.

croceum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered fritillary, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Russia.

This subspecies is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with distinctive purple markings, which bloom in late summer. The plant produces bulbils on its stems, a unique reproductive feature that aids in its propagation.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
