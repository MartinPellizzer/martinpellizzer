Lippia tegulifera var. grisea

Lippia tegulifera var. grisea, commonly known as gray lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves with a grayish-blue underside, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
