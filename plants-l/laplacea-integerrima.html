Laplacea integerrima

Laplacea integerrima, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the cold, windy environments of Antarctica.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal nutrients. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its harsh environment.

Due to its resilience, it is considered an indicator species of the Antarctic ecosystem.
