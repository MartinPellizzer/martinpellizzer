Luzula plumosa subsp. dilatata

Luzula plumosa subsp.

dilatata, commonly known as the tufted wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in dense, tufted clusters.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Luzula plumosa subsp.

dilatata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
