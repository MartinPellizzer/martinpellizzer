Leontodon stevenii

Leontodon stevenii, commonly known as Steven's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Steven's hawkweed is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
