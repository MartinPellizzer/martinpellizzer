Limnophila diffusa

Limnophila diffusa, commonly known as water thyme, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its small, green leaves are often submerged, while its flowers are tiny and clustered in spikelets.

Limnophila diffusa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
