Lactuca virosa subsp. virosa

Lactuca virosa subsp.

virosa, commonly known as poison lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweeds, and is known for its toxic properties.

The milky sap of the plant contains compounds that can cause skin irritation and is considered harmful to livestock.
