Lomaria attenuata

Lomaria attenuata, commonly known as the slender lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lomaria attenuata is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
