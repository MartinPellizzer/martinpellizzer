Lyonia nipensis subsp. nipensis

Lyonia nipensis subsp.

nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese lyonia, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white.

The plant produces berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
