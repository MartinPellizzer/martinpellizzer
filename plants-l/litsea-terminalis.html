Litsea terminalis

Litsea terminalis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamanu, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary catkins. Its fruits are small, globose drupes that mature to a dark purple color.

The leaves and bark contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and botanical research.
