Lycopodium cuneifolium

Lycopodium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils. The plant features spore-bearing cones called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branching stems. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium cuneifolium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
