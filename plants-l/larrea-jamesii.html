Larrea jamesii

Larrea jamesii, commonly known as the desert mistletoe, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellow-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Larrea jamesii plays a significant role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local biodiversity.
