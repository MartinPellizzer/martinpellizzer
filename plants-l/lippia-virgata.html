Lippia virgata

Lippia virgata, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent, and its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple. Lippia virgata is part of the Verbenaceae family and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its foliage is also popular in culinary applications, particularly in Mexican cuisine.
