Lepidium cyclocarpum var. crassius

Lepidium cyclocarpum var. crassius, commonly known as the broad-fruited wall pepper, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The varietal form *crassius* is distinguished by its broader, more robust fruits compared to the typical form.
