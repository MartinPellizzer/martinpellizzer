Leptochilus oligarchicus

Leptochilus oligarchicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that appear in clusters. Its reproductive strategy involves producing numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by water currents.

Leptochilus oligarchicus is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
