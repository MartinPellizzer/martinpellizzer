Lygodium polymorphum var. hirsutum

Lygodium polymorphum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy climbing fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, pinnately compound leaves. The fronds are often covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
