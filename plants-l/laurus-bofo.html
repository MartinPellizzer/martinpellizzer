Laurus bofo

Laurus bofo, commonly known as the sacred laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
