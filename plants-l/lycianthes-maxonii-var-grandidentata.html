Lycianthes maxonii var. grandidentata

Lycianthes maxonii var. grandidentata, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing several seeds.
