Lycopodium chamaeleon

Lycopodium chamaeleon, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its upright, branching stems and tiny, scale-like leaves. Lycopodium chamaeleon is often found in temperate regions, particularly in forests and rocky areas.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in soil stabilization are also notable botanical features.
