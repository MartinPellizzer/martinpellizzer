Leontopodium amrheinii

Leontopodium amrheinii, commonly known as the edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its unique appearance makes it a symbol of resilience and purity in many cultures.

Due to its habitat specificity and conservation status, it is often protected in its natural range.
