Lysimachia terrestris f. florifera

Lysimachia terrestris f. florifera, commonly known as the creeping yellow mosquito plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers clustered in terminal cymes, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ground cover or as a decorative plant in gardens.
