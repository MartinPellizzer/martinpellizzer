Lobelia holstii f. alba

Lobelia holstii f. alba, commonly known as the White Lobelia, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It is a hybrid form of Lobelia holstii, characterized by its showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
