Lycopodioides expansa

Lycopodioides expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its creeping, herbaceous stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, a feature typical of primitive vascular plants. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas.

Lycopodioides expansa plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
