Leptosilene

Leptosilene, commonly known as the "clove tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Moluccas, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark red, fleshy fruits containing aromatic seeds. Leptosilene is valued for its essential oil, which is extracted from its bark and used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and ecological role in its native habitat.
