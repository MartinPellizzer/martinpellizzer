Lappa crispa

Lappa crispa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lappa crispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
