Lupinus palaestinus

Lupinus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel, Palestine, and Jordan.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in rocky or arid soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces spires of blue to purple flowers. Its root system contains nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which makes it valuable for soil enrichment.

Lupinus palaestinus is often used in traditional medicine and is also of interest for ecological restoration projects.
