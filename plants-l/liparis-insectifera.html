Liparis insectifera

Liparis insectifera, commonly known as the insect orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

Liparis insectifera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid morphology with specialized pollination mechanisms.
