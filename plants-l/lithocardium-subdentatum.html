Lithocardium subdentatum

Lithocardium subdentatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lithocardium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Lithocardium subdentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
