Leochilus carinatus

Leochilus carinatus, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging leaf pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in a spike-like arrangement, and display a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Leochilus carinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
