Lythrum biflorum

Lythrum biflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf rosepink, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other species like the common lythrum. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
