Lycopodium cernuum

Lycopodium cernuum, commonly known as the drooping clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its creeping stems and clusters of tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically borne on short, erect branches. Lycopodium cernuum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and rocky crevices.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems highlight its botanical significance.
