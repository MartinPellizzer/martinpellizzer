Lathyrus odoratus var. roseoalbus

Lathyrus odoratus var. roseoalbus, commonly known as the rose-lavender sweet pea, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces tendrils that help it climb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are typically pink or lavender in color, with a sweet, honey-like fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
