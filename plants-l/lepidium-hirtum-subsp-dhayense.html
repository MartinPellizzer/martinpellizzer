Lepidium hirtum subsp. dhayense

Lepidium hirtum subsp.

dhayense, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It features a basal rosette of hairy, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system and its ability to thrive in alpine and subalpine environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
