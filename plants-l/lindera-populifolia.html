Lindera populifolia

Lindera populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
