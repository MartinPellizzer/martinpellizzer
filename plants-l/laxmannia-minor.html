Laxmannia minor

Laxmannia minor, commonly known as the dwarf firecracker, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, resembling tiny fireworks, hence its common name. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Laxmannia minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in Mediterranean climates.
