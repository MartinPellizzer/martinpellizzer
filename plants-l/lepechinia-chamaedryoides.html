Lepechinia chamaedryoides

Lepechinia chamaedryoides, commonly known as mountain thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported antiseptic properties.

Lepechinia chamaedryoides thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
