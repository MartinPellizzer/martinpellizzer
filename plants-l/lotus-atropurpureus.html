Lotus atropurpureus

Lotus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to the southern United States.

It features showy, lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom on tall, slender stems above the water's surface. The plant has heart-shaped, floating leaves that help it thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its roots are fibrous and anchor it to the substrate, while its rhizomes allow for vegetative propagation.

The purple lotus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
