Lilium × piscis

Lilium × piscis, commonly known as the fish lily, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Lilium candidum and Lilium bulbiferum.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine, while its ornamental value makes it a popular garden plant.

The fish lily is also valued for its medicinal properties in some cultures.
