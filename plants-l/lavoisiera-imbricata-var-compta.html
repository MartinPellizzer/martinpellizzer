Lavoisiera imbricata var. compta

Lavoisiera imbricata var. compta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lavoisiera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
