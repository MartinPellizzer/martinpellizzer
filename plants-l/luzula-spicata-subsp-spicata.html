Luzula spicata subsp. spicata

Luzula spicata subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the spike sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Luzula spicata subsp.

spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
