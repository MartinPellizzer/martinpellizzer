Leucheria daucifolia

Leucheria daucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf carrot or false parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae (carrot) family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
