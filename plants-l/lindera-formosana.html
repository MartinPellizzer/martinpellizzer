Lindera formosana

Lindera formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptic leaves that turn red in the fall. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark purple berries.

Lindera formosana is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
