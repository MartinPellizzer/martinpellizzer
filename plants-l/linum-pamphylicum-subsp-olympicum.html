Linum pamphylicum subsp. olympicum

Linum pamphylicum subsp.

olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species within its native range.
