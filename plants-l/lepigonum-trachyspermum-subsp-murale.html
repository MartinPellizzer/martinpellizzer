Lepigonum trachyspermum subsp. murale

Lepigonum trachyspermum subsp.

murale, commonly known as the wall rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often found along walls and in dry, shaded areas.

The plant features slender, creeping stems with small, pinnately divided leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
