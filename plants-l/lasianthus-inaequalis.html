Lasianthus inaequalis

Lasianthus inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal flame flower, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
