Lonchitis coursii

Lonchitis coursii, commonly known as the Coursii's flax.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Lonchitis coursii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
