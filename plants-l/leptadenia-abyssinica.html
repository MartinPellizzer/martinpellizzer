Leptadenia abyssinica

Leptadenia abyssinica, commonly known as African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its latex sap is milky and contains compounds that have been traditionally used for medicinal purposes.

The plant is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and as a source of traditional remedies.
