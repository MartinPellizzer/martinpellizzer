Lepidosperma viscidum var. flaccidum

Lepidosperma viscidum var. flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swampy areas and seasonally flooded soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are often subtended by bracts, contributing to its overall appearance.
