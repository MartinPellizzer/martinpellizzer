Litsea imthurnii

Litsea imthurnii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
