Litsea tayabensis

Litsea tayabensis, commonly known as the Philippine tayab or tayab tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
