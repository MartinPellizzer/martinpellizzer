Lepidaglaia pyramidata

Lepidaglaia pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive pyramidal inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, bell-shaped flowers arranged in a conical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
