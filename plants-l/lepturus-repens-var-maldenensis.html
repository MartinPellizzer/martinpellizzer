Lepturus repens var. maldenensis

Lepturus repens var. maldenensis, commonly known as the Malden trailing bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
