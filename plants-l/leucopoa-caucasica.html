Leucopoa caucasica

Leucopoa caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian leucopoa, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky and mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers.

Leucopoa caucasica is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
