Leucas leucocephala

Leucas leucocephala, commonly known as whitehead leucas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Leucas leucocephala is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in warm climates.
