Lanxangia paratsao-ko

Lanxangia paratsao-ko, commonly known as the Thai orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces aerial roots, which are typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists worldwide.
