Lonchocarpus chrysophyllus

Lonchocarpus chrysophyllus, commonly known as golden-leaf legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking golden-yellow foliage, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive yellow or orange coloration.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
