Lycopodium longearistatum

Lycopodium longearistatum, commonly known as the long-stalked clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces sporophylls that bear microsporangia and megasporangia, typical of its evolutionary lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Lycopodium longearistatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
