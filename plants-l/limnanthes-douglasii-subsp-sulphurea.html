Limnanthes douglasii subsp. sulphurea

Limnanthes douglasii subsp.

sulphurea, commonly known as sulfur yellow marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and fuzzy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance.

The plant typically grows in wet, muddy areas such as marshes and stream banks. It has a short growing season and blooms in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sulfur-yellow petals and is often found in association with other wetland plants.
