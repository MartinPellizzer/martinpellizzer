Lobularia canariensis

Lobularia canariensis, commonly known as Canary Island lobularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It has a low-growing habit with branching stems and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
