Lechea verna

Lechea verna, commonly known as the spring milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Lechea verna is known for its role in supporting pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
