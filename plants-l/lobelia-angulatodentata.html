Lobelia angulatodentata

Lobelia angulatodentata, commonly known as the angular-toothed lobe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive angular teeth along the margins. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia angulatodentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
