Leptobalanus latus

Leptobalanus latus, commonly known as the broad-leaved fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in a flat, spreading pattern on the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Leptobalanus latus plays an important role in forest ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
