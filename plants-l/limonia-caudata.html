Limonia caudata

Limonia caudata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces bright orange, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Limonia caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
