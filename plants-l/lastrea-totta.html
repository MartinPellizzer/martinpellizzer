Lastrea totta

Lastrea totta, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in rocky soils.

The Cape sugarbush is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
