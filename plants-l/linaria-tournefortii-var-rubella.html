Linaria tournefortii var. rubella

Linaria tournefortii var. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and vibrant red-orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to their nectar-rich structure.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
