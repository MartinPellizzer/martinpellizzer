Lightfootia laricina

Lightfootia laricina, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, often with a red or orange hue at the base. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soil and is often found near water bodies.

Lightfootia laricina plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
