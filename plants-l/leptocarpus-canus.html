Leptocarpus canus

Leptocarpus canus, commonly known as the silverleaf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in tidal zones or along shorelines.

Leptocarpus canus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate saline conditions.
