Lepismium dissimile

Lepismium dissimile, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Lepismium dissimile is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
