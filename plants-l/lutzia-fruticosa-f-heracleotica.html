Lutzia fruticosa f. heracleotica

Lutzia fruticosa f. heracleotica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lutzia, is a shrub native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, containing a single seed.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
