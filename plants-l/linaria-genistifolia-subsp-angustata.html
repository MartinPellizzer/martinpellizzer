Linaria genistifolia subsp. angustata

Linaria genistifolia subsp.

angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
