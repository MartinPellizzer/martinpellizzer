Lysichiton camtschaticus

Lysichiton camtschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a stout, fleshy rhizome and produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
