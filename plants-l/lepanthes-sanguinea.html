Lepanthes sanguinea

Lepanthes sanguinea, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding debris.

Lepanthes sanguinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
