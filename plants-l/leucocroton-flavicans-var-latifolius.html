Leucocroton flavicans var. latifolius

Leucocroton flavicans var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf leucocroton, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitats.
