Loranthus tenuifolius

Loranthus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in the tropics and subtropics.

It has small, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are a source of food for birds and other animals. Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

Loranthus tenuifolius plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity through its fruit and providing shelter for various organisms.
