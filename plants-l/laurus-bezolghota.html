Laurus bezolghota

Laurus bezolghota, commonly known as the Persian laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are edible and have a slightly spicy flavor, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
