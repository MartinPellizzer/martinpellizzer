Linum hellenicum

Linum hellenicum, commonly known as the Greek flax or Hellenic flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in racemes.

Linum hellenicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
