Lychnodiscus cerospermus var. mortehanii

Lychnodiscus cerospermus var. mortehanii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant bears woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in local ecosystems and horticulture.
