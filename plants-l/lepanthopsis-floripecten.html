Lepanthopsis floripecten

Lepanthopsis floripecten, commonly known as the showy lepanthopsis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble a crown or a fan, with elongated petals and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, yellow, and orange, making it a visually appealing species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
