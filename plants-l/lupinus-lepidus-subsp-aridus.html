Lupinus lepidus subsp. aridus

Lupinus lepidus subsp.

aridus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats. Lupinus lepidus subsp.

aridus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
