Lepidotis cernua

Lepidotis cernua, commonly known as the drooping papyrus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, scale-like coating. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Lepidotis cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
