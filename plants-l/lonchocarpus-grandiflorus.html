Lonchocarpus grandiflorus

Lonchocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the broadleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
