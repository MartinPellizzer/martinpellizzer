Lyperia tenuiflora

Lyperia tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered lyperia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring.

Lyperia tenuiflora is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
