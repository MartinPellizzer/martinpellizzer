Linum usitatissimum subsp. humile

Linum usitatissimum subsp.

humile, commonly known as dwarf flax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, branched stems and lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its fibrous stalks and seeds are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of oil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
