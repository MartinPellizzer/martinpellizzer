Lycopsis calycina

Lycopsis calycina, commonly known as the small-leaf lycopus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its small, opposite leaves are usually serrated, and it produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

Lycopsis calycina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
