Layia jonesii

Layia jonesii, commonly known as Jones' golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Layia jonesii is often found in California and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
