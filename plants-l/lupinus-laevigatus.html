Lupinus laevigatus

Lupinus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant produces clusters of showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a deep taproot system.

Lupinus laevigatus is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
