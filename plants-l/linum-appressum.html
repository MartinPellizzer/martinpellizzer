Linum appressum

Linum appressum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and grasslands. It produces small, oval seeds that are used in traditional herbal medicine.

Linum appressum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
