Liparis fimbriata

Liparis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, fragrant flowers that often have a yellowish or greenish hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose cluster and feature intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and flies.

Liparis fimbriata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
