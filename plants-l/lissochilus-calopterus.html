Lissochilus calopterus

Lissochilus calopterus, commonly known as the bright-eyed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that feature a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its overall compact structure.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
