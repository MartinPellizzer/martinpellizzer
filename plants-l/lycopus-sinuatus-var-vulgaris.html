Lycopus sinuatus var. vulgaris

Lycopus sinuatus var. vulgaris, commonly known as broad-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue or purple hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually blue or purple in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
