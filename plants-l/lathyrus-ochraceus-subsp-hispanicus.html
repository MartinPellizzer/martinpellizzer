Lathyrus ochraceus subsp. hispanicus

Lathyrus ochraceus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The plant features slender, branching stems with small, compound leaves and produces pea-like flowers in shades of pink to violet. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, elongated seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for forage and medicinal purposes.
