Ladenbergia hexandra

Ladenbergia hexandra, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
