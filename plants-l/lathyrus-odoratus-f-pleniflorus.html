Lathyrus odoratus f. pleniflorus

Lathyrus odoratus f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the double-flowered sweet pea, is a cultivar of the sweet pea plant characterized by its abundant, double blooms.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves, typical of the genus Lathyrus. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
