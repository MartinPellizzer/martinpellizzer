Lapaea rubriflora

Lapaea rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered lapaea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
