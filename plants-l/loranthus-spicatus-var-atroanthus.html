Loranthus spicatus var. atroanthus

Loranthus spicatus var. atroanthus, commonly known as black-flowered mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically dark red or black, which gives the plant its common name. It produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forested areas.
