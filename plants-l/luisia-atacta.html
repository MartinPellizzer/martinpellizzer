Luisia atacta

Luisia atacta, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Luisia atacta is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
