Lautembergia coriacea subvar. comorensis

Lautembergia coriacea subvar. comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian laurifolia, is a shrub native to the Comoros Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal and tropical regions.
