Lonchocarpus lucens

Lonchocarpus lucens, commonly known as the yellow cluster bean or yellow senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
