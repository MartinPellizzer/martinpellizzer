Laburnum alpinum var. pilosum

Laburnum alpinum var. pilosum, commonly known as the downy laburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnate, with small, hairy leaflets that give the plant its "pilosum" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
