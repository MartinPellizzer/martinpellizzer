Leontodon taraxacum var. laevigatus

Leontodon taraxacum var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and yellow flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in open, disturbed soils across temperate regions. The plant produces a hollow, stemless flower stem that supports its bright yellow composite flower heads. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This variant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
