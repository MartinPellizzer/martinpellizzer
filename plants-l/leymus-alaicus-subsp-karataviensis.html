Leymus alaicus subsp. karataviensis

Leymus alaicus subsp.

karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavinskaya grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
