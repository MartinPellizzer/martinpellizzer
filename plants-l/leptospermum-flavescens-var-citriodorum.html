Leptospermum flavescens var. citriodorum

Leptospermum flavescens var. citriodorum, commonly known as the lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that emit a strong lemon scent due to the presence of citral. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its essential oils are widely used in culinary and aromatic applications.
