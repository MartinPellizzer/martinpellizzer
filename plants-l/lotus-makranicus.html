Lotus makranicus

Lotus makranicus, commonly known as the Makran lotus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Arabian Sea and the Persian Gulf.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it well adapted to coastal and estuarine habitats. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing shorelines.
