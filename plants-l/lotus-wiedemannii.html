Lotus wiedemannii

Lotus wiedemannii, commonly known as the Mexican lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Central America.

It features large, showy pink or red flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the mud below. Lotus wiedemannii is characterized by its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ecological benefits.
