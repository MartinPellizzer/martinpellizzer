Lindsaea pelecophylla

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Lindsaea pelecophylla is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
