Lasiandra angustifolia

Lasiandra angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lasiandra, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, opposite leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lasiandra angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
