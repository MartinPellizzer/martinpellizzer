Leucadendron criniflorum

Leucadendron criniflorum, commonly known as the silver crown leucadendron, is a striking South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and its showy, cone-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
