Lupinus cutleri

Lupinus cutleri, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. Lupinus cutleri belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes capable of nitrogen fixation.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
