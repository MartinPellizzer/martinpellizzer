Lavandula coronopifolia var. ziziana

Lavandula coronopifolia var. ziziana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and slightly fuzzy. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, purple-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden use and horticultural cultivation.
