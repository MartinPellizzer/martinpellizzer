Leptaleum

Leptaleum, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Leptaleum hirtellum, which is native to the southeastern United States. The plants are characterized by their broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, white to greenish flowers. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas.

Botanically, they are noted for their rhizomatous growth and their ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
