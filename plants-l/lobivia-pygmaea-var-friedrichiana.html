Lobivia pygmaea var. friedrichiana

Lobivia pygmaea var. friedrichiana, commonly known as the Friedrich's dwarf lobivia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
