Lobelia × formosa

Lobelia × formosa, commonly known as the hybrid lobelia, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a cross between Lobelia sanguinea and Lobelia cardinalis, resulting in a plant with distinctive red and purple flowers. The species is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and forested areas.

Lobelia × formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
