Lycopodium involvens

Lycopodium involvens, commonly known as the spiral moss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophyte produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in cones.

Lycopodium involvens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
