Lobelia sericea

Lobelia sericea, commonly known as silky lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers and hairy, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other lobelias and bluebells.

Lobelia sericea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
