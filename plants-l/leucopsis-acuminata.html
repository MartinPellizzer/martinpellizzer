Leucopsis acuminata

Leucopsis acuminata, commonly known as the slender lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Leucopsis acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
