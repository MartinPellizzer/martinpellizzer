Linum macradenium

Linum macradenium, commonly known as the blue flax or mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at higher elevations, and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals that are arranged in a bell-like shape.

Linum macradenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
