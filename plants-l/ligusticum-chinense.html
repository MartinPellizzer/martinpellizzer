Ligusticum chinense

Ligusticum chinense, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring deeply lobed leaves and umbels of small, yellowish flowers. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
