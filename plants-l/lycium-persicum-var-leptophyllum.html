Lycium persicum var. leptophyllum

Lycium persicum var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender-fruited boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental use.
