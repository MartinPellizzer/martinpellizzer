Lycium carolinianum var. gaumeri

Lycium carolinianum var. gaumeri, commonly known as the Carolina wolfberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, yellow-orange berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
