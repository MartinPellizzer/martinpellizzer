Lomariocycas magellanica

Lomariocycas magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones instead of flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
