Lockhartia verrucosa

Lockhartia verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native tropical environment.

Lockhartia verrucosa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
