Linaria crassifolia var. saetabensis

Linaria crassifolia var. saetabensis, commonly known as the Saharan toadflax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and narrow, linear leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for dry, rocky environments.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and arid landscape design.
