Lastrea calcarea

Lastrea calcarea, commonly known as the limestone rockrose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
