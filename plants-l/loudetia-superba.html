Loudetia superba

Loudetia superba, commonly known as the African grass or blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with blue or silver hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
