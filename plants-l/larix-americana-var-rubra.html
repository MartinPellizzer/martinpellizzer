Larix americana var. rubra

Larix americana var. rubra, commonly known as the red tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking reddish-brown foliage, which turns vibrant red in the fall. The tree has slender, drooping branches and produces cone-like structures that contain winged seeds. Its leaves are needle-like and turn golden yellow before falling in autumn.

This variety is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
