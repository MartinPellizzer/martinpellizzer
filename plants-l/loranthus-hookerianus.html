Loranthus hookerianus

Loranthus hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of trees, facilitating propagation.

Loranthus hookerianus plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for birds and insects, and its presence can influence the health of its host tree.
