Lepinema

Lepinema, commonly known as the common lepinema, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Lepidopteridae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically have five petals arranged in a radial symmetry. The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. These plants are often found in moist, woodland habitats and thrive in temperate climates.

They play a role in supporting local pollinators and contribute to the biodiversity of their ecosystems.
