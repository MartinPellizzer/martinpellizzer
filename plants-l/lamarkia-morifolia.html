Lamarkia morifolia

Lamarkia morifolia, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow petals and foliage. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Lamarkia morifolia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
