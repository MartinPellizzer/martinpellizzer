Lepanthes meniscophora

Lepanthes meniscophora, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive silver or pale blue tint on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, helping it to retain moisture.

Lepanthes meniscophora is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
