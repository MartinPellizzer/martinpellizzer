Lycianthes pachypetalon

Lycianthes pachypetalon, commonly known as the broad-petaled physalis, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are followed by inflated, lantern-shaped fruits, which enclose the seeds. Its foliage is characterized by ovate, serrated leaves that are usually hairy in texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
