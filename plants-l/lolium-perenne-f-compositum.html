Lolium perenne f. compositum

Lolium perenne f. compositum, commonly known as perennial ryegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit, fine leaves, and tufted flowering heads. The plant is highly nutritious and is widely used in forage production and turfgrass applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is tolerant to moderate frost.

Its genetic variability makes it a valuable species for breeding programs aimed at improving forage quality and agronomic performance.
