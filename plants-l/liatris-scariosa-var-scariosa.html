Liatris scariosa var. scariosa

Liatris scariosa var. scariosa, commonly known as the scarious blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The genus Liatris is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae), and this species is known for its ornamental value in native gardens.
