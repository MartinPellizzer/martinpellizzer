Lysimachia remyi subsp. subherbacea

Lysimachia remyi subsp.

subherbacea, commonly known as the creeping yellow-joy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of East Asia. It features slender, rhizomatous stems that spread along the ground, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in cymes. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
