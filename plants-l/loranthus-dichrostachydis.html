Loranthus dichrostachydis

Loranthus dichrostachydis, commonly known as the two-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree through a specialized structure called a haustellum.

It primarily grows on native Australian eucalyptus species, drawing water and nutrients from the host while producing its own chlorophyll for photosynthesis. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, sticky seeds, which adhere to the bark of host trees for germination.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting various insect and bird life.
