Lissochilus morrumbalaensis

Lissochilus morrumbalaensis, commonly known as the Morrumbala orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale pink or white with intricate patterns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
