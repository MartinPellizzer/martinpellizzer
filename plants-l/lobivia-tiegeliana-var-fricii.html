Lobivia tiegeliana var. fricii

Lobivia tiegeliana var. fricii, commonly known as the Frici's lobivia, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and thick, ribbed stems that can develop a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
