Labordia fagraeoidea var. saint-johniana

Labordia fagraeoidea var. saint-johniana, commonly known as the Saint-John's island hibiscus, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
