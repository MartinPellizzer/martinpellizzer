Lepidagathis terminalis

Lepidagathis terminalis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs.

Lepidagathis terminalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and attractive foliage.
