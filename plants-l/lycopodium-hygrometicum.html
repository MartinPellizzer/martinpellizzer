Lycopodium hygrometicum

Lycopodium hygrometicum, commonly known as the ground pine, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage that resembles pine needles. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Lycopodium hygrometicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
