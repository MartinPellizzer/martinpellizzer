Lachemilla ocreata

Lachemilla ocreata, commonly known as the golden sedum, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a succulent species that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its fleshy, thick leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Lachemilla ocreata is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
