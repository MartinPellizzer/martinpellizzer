Lasiopetalum parviflorum var. major

Lasiopetalum parviflorum var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered lasiopetalum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to greyish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy and can grow up to several centimeters in length.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
