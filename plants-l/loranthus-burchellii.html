Loranthus burchellii

Loranthus burchellii, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Acacia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often visited by insects and birds, facilitating pollination. Its berries are red and fleshy, attracting birds that disperse its seeds.

Loranthus burchellii plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat, influencing both its host trees and the surrounding wildlife.
