Licuala ferruginea

Licuala ferruginea, commonly known as the red-leaf palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, reddish-brown leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds.

Licuala ferruginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
