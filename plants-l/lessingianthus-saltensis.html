Lessingianthus saltensis

Lessingianthus saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a hardy species for coastal and desert environments.

Its ability to thrive in poor soils makes it an important plant for stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
