Lallemantia sulphurea

Lallemantia sulphurea, commonly known as golden lantana, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, adding texture to its appearance.

Lallemantia sulphurea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
