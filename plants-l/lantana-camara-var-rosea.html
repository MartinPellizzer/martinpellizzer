Lantana camara var. rosea

Lantana camara var. rosea, commonly known as the roseum lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
