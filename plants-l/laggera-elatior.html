Laggera elatior

Laggera elatior, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months.

Laggera elatior is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
