Linum californicum var. confertum

Linum californicum var. confertum, commonly known as the California flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are alternate, linear, and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its fibrous stalks and seeds have historically been used by indigenous peoples for fiber and food.
