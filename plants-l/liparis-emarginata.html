Liparis emarginata

Liparis emarginata, commonly known as the marginate orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
