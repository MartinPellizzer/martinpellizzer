Lophiolepis × kelkitensis

Lophiolepis × kelkitensis, commonly known as the Kelkit hybrid, is a rare and naturally occurring hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Lophiolepis alata and Lophiolepis clavata.

It is native to Turkey, specifically the region of Kelkit, where it grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique genetic makeup, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
