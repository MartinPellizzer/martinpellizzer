Lirianthe liliifera var. beccarii

Lirianthe liliifera var. beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family, characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified leaves called spathes. The plant features a stout, often hairy stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
