Lobelia attenuata

Lobelia attenuata, commonly known as slender bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, ascending stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
