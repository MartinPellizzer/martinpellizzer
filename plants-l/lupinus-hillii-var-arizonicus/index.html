Lupinus Hillii Var. Arizonicus

Lupinus hillii var. arizonicus is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. This variant of the lupine species is known for its vibrant blue and purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to harsh desert environments. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked due to its limited distribution and subtle appearance. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical wonder from habitat loss and over-collection.
