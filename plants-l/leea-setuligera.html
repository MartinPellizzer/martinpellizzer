Leea setuligera

Leea setuligera, commonly known as the hairy leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the summer months.

This plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
