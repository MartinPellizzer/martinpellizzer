Lobivia haageana f. albihepatica

Lobivia haageana f. albihepatica, commonly known as the White-veined Lobivia, is a unique cactus species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem with short, spiny areoles, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in arid environments.
