Lucuma laurifolia var. reticulata

Lucuma laurifolia var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated lucuma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, which resembles a small, yellowish pear, is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential culinary uses in traditional Andean cuisine.
