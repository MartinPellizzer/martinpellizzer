Lilium sempervivoideum subsp. pinifolium

Lilium sempervivoideum subsp.

pinifolium, commonly known as the pineleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage that resembles pine needles, giving it its common name.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
