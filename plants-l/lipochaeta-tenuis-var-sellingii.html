Lipochaeta tenuis var. sellingii

Lipochaeta tenuis var. sellingii, commonly known as the slender lipochaeta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and somewhat trailing.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its delicate floral display.
