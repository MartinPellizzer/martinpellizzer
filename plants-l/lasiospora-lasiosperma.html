Lasiospora lasiosperma

Lasiospora lasiosperma, commonly known as the woolly pod plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
