Leptorhynchos elongatus var. tasmanicus

Leptorhynchos elongatus var. tasmanicus, commonly known as the Tasmanian longleaf, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Tasmania.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a dense, fibrous root system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color, followed by narrow, elongated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
