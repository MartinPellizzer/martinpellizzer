Lomaria cordata

Lomaria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Lomaria cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
