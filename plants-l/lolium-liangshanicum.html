Lolium liangshanicum

Lolium liangshanicum, commonly known as the Liangshan ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense growth habit, making it suitable for forage and erosion control. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
