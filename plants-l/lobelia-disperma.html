Lobelia disperma

Lobelia disperma, commonly known as blue lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, showy blue or purple flowers clustered in whorls. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia disperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
