Ludia antanosarum

Ludia antanosarum, commonly known as the Madagascar water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water bodies and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Ludia antanosarum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
