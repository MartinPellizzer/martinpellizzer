Linaria aegyptiaca var. crassipes

Linaria aegyptiaca var. crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are thick and robust. This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
