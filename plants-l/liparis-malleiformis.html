Liparis malleiformis

Liparis malleiformis, commonly known as the malleiform orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and erect, branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Liparis malleiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
