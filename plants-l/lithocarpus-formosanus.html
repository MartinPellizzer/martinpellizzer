Lithocarpus formosanus

Lithocarpus formosanus, commonly known as the Taiwan stoneæ Ž, is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its hard, dense wood and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, cupule-enclosed nuts that are typically inedible for humans but serve as a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and mountainous regions.

The tree is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
