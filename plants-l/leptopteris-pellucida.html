Leptopteris pellucida

Leptopteris pellucida, commonly known as the glassy water fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Leptopteris pellucida is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in controlled environments.
