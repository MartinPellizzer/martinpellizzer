Lobivia ferox var. longispina

Lobivia ferox var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers during the warmer months, adding visual interest to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its leaves are typically small and cylindrical, reducing water loss through transpiration.

This variety is prized for its unique spines and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
