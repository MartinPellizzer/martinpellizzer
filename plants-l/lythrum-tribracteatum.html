Lythrum tribracteatum

Lythrum tribracteatum, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom from June to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is rhizomatous, allowing it to spread aggressively and become invasive in non-native regions.

Lythrum tribracteatum is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
