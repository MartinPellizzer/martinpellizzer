Lepidopappus tripteris

Lepidopappus tripteris, commonly known as the three-winged thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with a central stem bearing the inflorescence.

The species is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its thick, waxy leaf surfaces.
