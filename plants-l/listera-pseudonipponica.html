Listera pseudonipponica

Listera pseudonipponica, commonly known as the Japanese basketwort, is a species of flowering plant in the Primulaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping rhizome that helps it spread and form dense mats.

Listera pseudonipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
