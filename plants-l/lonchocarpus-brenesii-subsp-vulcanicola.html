Lonchocarpus brenesii subsp. vulcanicola

Lonchocarpus brenesii subsp.

vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic island legume, is a species native to the volcanic regions of Central America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with high volcanic activity. It has compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its adaptation to nutrient-poor, volcanic soils.
