Leuconotis subavenis var. latifolia

Leuconotis subavenis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved leuconotis, is a variant of the leuconotis plant species.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
