Lens esculenta var. punctata

Lens esculenta var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted lentil, is a variety of lentil characterized by its distinctive mottled or speckled seed coat.

This legume belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of the Mediterranean and South Asia. The seeds are typically small, oval-shaped, and exhibit a range of colors including brown, green, and yellow. It is valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of protein, fiber, and essential minerals.

The plant is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
