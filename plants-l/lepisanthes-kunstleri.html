Lepisanthes kunstleri

Lepisanthes kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped blooms that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
