Linaria triphylla var. versicolor

Linaria triphylla var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable everlasting or variable toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

The varietal name "versicolor" reflects its variable flower coloration, which can change with environmental factors.
