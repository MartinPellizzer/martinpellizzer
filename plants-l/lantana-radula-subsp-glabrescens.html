Lantana radula subsp. glabrescens

Lantana radula subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth lantana, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves and stems, which set it apart from other lantana species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
