Linnaeobreynia incana

Linnaeobreynia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its woody structure and drought tolerance make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Linnaeobreynia incana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
