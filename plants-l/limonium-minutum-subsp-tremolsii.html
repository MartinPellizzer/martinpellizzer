Limonium minutum subsp. tremolsii

Limonium minutum subsp.

tremolsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
