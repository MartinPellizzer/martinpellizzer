Lasianthus strigosus var. protracta

Lasianthus strigosus var. protracta, commonly known as the protracta variant of the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for horticultural cultivation.
