Lycopus italicus

Lycopus italicus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lycopus italicus is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
