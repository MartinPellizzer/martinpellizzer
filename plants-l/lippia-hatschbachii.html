Lippia hatschbachii

Lippia hatschbachii, commonly known as Texas mint, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its foliage is also popular in culinary applications, particularly in Mexican cuisine.
