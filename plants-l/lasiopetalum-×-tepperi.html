Lasiopetalum × tepperi

Lasiopetalum × tepperi, commonly known as the hybrid tea flower, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
