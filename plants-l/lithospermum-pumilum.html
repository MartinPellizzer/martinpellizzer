Lithospermum pumilum

Lithospermum pumilum, commonly known as dwarf lithospermum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and capable of storing water, aiding in its survival in dry climates.

Lithospermum pumilum is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its roots.
