Lotus corniculatus var. hirsutissimus

Lotus corniculatus var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, enriching the soil.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
