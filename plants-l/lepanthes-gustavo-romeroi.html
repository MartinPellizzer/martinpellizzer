Lepanthes gustavo-romeroi

Lepanthes gustavo-romeroi, commonly known as the Gustavo Romero's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America, particularly found in the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers with showy, fragrant blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance.
