Lophopogon

Lophopogon, commonly known as the mountain bluemouth, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plants are typically perennial herbs with slender stems and small, grass-like leaves. Lophopogon species produce compact inflorescences with blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the spring.

These plants are adapted to high-altitude environments and are often found in alpine meadows.
