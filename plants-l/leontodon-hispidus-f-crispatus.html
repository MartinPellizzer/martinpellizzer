Leontodon hispidus f. crispatus

Leontodon hispidus f. crispatus, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble a cluster of small blooms. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine, bristly hairs, which contribute to its common name.

This variant of Leontodon hispidus is noted for its distinct appearance and ecological adaptability.
