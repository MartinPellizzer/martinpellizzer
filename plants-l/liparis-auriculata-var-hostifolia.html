Liparis auriculata var. hostifolia

Liparis auriculata var. hostifolia, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in shaded woodlands.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
