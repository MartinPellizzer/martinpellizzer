Liatris haywardii

Liatris haywardii, commonly known as Hayward's blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces tall, slender stems with clusters of purple to pinkish bracts that resemble flowers.

Liatris haywardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
