Leucas flaccida

Leucas flaccida, commonly known as the slender leucas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Leucas flaccida is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
