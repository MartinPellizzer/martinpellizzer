Lycopodium brasilianum

Lycopodium brasilianum, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a vascular fern belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, needle-like leaves that are spirally arranged. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

Lycopodium brasilianum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
