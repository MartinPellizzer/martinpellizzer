Lepidium bonariense var. stuckertianum

Lepidium bonariense var. stuckertianum, commonly known as Stuckert's peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has slender, branching stems covered in small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are alternate and linear, often with a slight pubescence.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
