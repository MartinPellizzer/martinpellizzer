Lepanthes calypso

Lepanthes calypso, commonly known as the "Queen of the Andes," is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a prominent, showy lip that attracts pollinators. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in cloud forests. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its beauty and rarity, Lepanthes calypso is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
