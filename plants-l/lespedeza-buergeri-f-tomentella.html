Lespedeza buergeri f. tomentella

Lespedeza buergeri f. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly lespedeza, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to East Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
