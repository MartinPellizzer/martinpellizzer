Linum lewisii var. alpicola

Linum lewisii var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This variety is particularly adapted to high-altitude conditions, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
