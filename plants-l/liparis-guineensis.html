Liparis guineensis

Liparis guineensis, commonly known as the Guinea orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect rhizome and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and adaptability to humid conditions.
