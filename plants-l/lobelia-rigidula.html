Lobelia rigidula

Lobelia rigidula, commonly known as rigidstem lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. Its small, blue to violet flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Lobelia rigidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
