Lithops dinteri subsp. multipunctata

Lithops dinteri subsp.

multipunctata, commonly known as the "Cape Town Lithops," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rock-like appearance with two fleshy leaves that resemble stones.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
