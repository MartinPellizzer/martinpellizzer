Laelia × porphyritis

Laelia × porphyritis, commonly known as the Porphyritic Laelia, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Laelia anceps and Laelia purpurea.

It is native to the high altitudes of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs, producing clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and pink. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions for its beauty and resilience.
