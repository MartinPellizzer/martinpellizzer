Lepanthes adelphe

Lepanthes adelphe, commonly known as the Adelphe orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features delicate, showy flowers with a distinctive violet coloration and a unique, elongated lip. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, typical of many orchid species.

Lepanthes adelphe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
