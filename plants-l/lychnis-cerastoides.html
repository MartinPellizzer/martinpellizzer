Lychnis cerastoides

Lychnis cerastoides, commonly known as the red sandspur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its bright red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in prairies, roadsides, and open woodlands. It produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
