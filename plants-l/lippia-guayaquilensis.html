Lippia guayaquilensis

Lippia guayaquilensis, commonly known as Guayaquil mint, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong minty scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lippia guayaquilensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
