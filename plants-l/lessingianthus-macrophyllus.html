Lessingianthus macrophyllus

Lessingianthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractant, contributing to ecological diversity in its native habitat.
