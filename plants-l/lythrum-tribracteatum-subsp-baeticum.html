Lythrum tribracteatum subsp. baeticum

Lythrum tribracteatum subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Iberian lythrum, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, three-bracted fruit capsules. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
