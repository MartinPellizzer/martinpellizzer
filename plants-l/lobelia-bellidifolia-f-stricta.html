Lobelia bellidifolia f. stricta

Lobelia bellidifolia f. stricta, commonly known as the compact lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
