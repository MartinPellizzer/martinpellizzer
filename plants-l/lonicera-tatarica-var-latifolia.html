Lonicera tatarica var. latifolia

Lonicera tatarica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its berries are small, red, and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
