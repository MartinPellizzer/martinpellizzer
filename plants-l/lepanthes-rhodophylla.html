Lepanthes rhodophylla

Lepanthes rhodophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its strikingly colorful inflorescence, which often features bright red and orange petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Lepanthes rhodophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
