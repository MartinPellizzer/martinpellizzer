Leucanthemum liburnicum

Leucanthemum liburnicum, commonly known as the Liburnian oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces white to pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disk, and its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped.

Leucanthemum liburnicum is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
