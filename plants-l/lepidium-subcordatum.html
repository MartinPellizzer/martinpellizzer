Lepidium subcordatum

Lepidium subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
