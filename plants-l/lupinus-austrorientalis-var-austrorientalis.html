Lupinus austrorientalis var. austrorientalis

Lupinus austrorientalis var. austrorientalis, commonly known as the eastern lupine, is a perennial legume native to the temperate regions of eastern North America.

It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces tall spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun. It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological value in supporting pollinators.
