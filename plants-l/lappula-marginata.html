Lappula marginata

Lappula marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lappula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in racemes, while its leaves are narrow and ribbed.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
