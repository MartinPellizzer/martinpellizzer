Lepidium karelinianum

Lepidium karelinianum, commonly known as the Siberian cress, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically lobed and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Lepidium karelinianum is also valued as a forage plant for livestock in its native regions.
