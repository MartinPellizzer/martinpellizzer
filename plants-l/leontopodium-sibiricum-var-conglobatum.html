Leontopodium sibiricum var. conglobatum

Leontopodium sibiricum var. conglobatum, commonly known as the Siberian edelweiss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact rosette of silver-white, woolly leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in July and August. It thrives in alpine environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
