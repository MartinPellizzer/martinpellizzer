Lecythis martiana

Lecythis martiana, commonly known as the Amazonian cupuacu, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Lecythidaceae and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The tree produces a heavy, oily fruit that contains a sweet, creamy pulp surrounded by a hard, woody shell. The fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in the rainforest ecosystem.
