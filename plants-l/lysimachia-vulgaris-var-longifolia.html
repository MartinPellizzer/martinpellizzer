Lysimachia vulgaris var. longifolia

Lysimachia vulgaris var. longifolia, commonly known as yellow loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, wet meadows, and stream banks.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it an effective ground cover in shady environments.
