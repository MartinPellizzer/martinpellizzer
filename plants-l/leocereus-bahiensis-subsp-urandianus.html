Leocereus bahiensis subsp. urandianus

Leocereus bahiensis subsp.

urandianus, commonly known as the Urandi Cereus, is a cacti species native to Brazil. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
