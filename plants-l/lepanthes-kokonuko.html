Lepanthes kokonuko

Lepanthes kokonuko, commonly known as the Kokonuko orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces aerial roots and thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to capture limited sunlight.

Lepanthes kokonuko is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
