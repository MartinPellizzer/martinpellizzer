Lygodisodea ciliata

Lygodisodea ciliata, commonly known as the hairy tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it resilient in harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry soils.

The hairy tamarisk is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
