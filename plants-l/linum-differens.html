Linum differens

Linum differens, commonly known as the variable flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Linum differens is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

It is a member of the Linaceae family, which includes other flax species known for their fibrous stalks.
