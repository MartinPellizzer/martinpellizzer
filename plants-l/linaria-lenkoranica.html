Linaria lenkoranica

Linaria lenkoranica, commonly known as the Armenian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the genus Linaria in the family Plantaginaceae. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small toads, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Linaria lenkoranica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
