Liabum sessiliflorum

Liabum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower liabum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetland habitats. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Liabum sessiliflorum is an important species for ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and habitat support for various wildlife.
