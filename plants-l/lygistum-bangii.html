Lygistum bangii

Lygistum bangii, commonly known as the golden thread vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellowish-green stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Lygistum bangii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
