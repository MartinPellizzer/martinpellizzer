Lignariella serpens

Lignariella serpens, commonly known as the serpentine lignaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems that often twist and coil in a serpentine pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Lignariella serpens is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.
