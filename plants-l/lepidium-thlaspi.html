Lepidium thlaspi

Lepidium thlaspi, commonly known as whitlow grass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Lepidium thlaspi is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
