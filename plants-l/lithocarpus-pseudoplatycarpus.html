Lithocarpus pseudoplatycarpus

Lithocarpus pseudoplatycarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches.

It is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its hard, dense wood and its distinctive, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees, and it produces edible nuts encased in a thick, woody shell.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
