Lycopodium trichiatum

Lycopodium trichiatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and a spore-bearing structure called a strobilus. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Lycopodium trichiatum reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a feature common to many ancient plant species.

Its structure and life cycle provide insights into the evolution of early terrestrial plants.
