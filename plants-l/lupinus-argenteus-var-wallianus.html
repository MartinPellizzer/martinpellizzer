Lupinus argenteus var. wallianus

Lupinus argenteus var. wallianus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
