Larix potaninii var. chinensis

Larix potaninii var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese larch, is a variant of the larch tree native to eastern China.

It is a deciduous conifer that produces cone-like structures and has needle-like leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soils. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in parks and gardens.
