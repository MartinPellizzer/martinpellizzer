Lilium catesbaei subsp. longii

Lilium catesbaei subsp.

longii, commonly known as the longleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct midrib, while its bulbs are small and clustered.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
