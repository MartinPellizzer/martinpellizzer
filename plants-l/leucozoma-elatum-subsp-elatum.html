Leucozoma elatum subsp. elatum

Leucozoma elatum subsp.

elatum, commonly known as the broadleaf snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
