Leptochilus taylorii

Leptochilus taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's leathery lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Teloschistaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form and a dark, leathery appearance. The thallus is typically bright yellow to orange in color, and it often grows on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. This lichen is known for its ability to tolerate harsh conditions, making it a common inhabitant of dry, sunny habitats.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
