Lepicystis incana

Lepicystis incana, commonly known as the hairy lepicystis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Lepicystis incana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
