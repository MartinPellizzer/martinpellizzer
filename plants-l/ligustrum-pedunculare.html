Ligustrum pedunculare

Ligustrum pedunculare, commonly known as the clustered privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture for its dense growth habit.

The botanical features of Ligustrum pedunculare make it a popular choice for ornamental and hedging purposes.
