Lathyrus nervatus

Lathyrus nervatus, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with finely veined leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Lathyrus nervatus is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
