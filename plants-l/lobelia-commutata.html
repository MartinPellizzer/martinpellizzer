Lobelia commutata

Lobelia commutata, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia commutata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
