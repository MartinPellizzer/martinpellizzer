Leucocroton havanensis

Leucocroton havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling flames. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

Leucocroton havanensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
