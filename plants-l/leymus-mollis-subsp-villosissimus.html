Leymus mollis subsp. villosissimus

Leymus mollis subsp.

villosissimus, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
