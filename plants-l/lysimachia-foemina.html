Lysimachia foemina

Lysimachia foemina, commonly known as sweetroot or lady's root, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its spreading growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lysimachia foemina is adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
