Lonchocarpus nitidulus

Lonchocarpus nitidulus, commonly known as the yellow tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its roots and leaves are known for their medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
