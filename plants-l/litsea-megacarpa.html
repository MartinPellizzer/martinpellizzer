Litsea megacarpa

Litsea megacarpa, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green leaves and showy, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange berries that contain hard, angular seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic compounds and its role in traditional medicine.
