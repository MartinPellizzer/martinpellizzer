Lupinus monteportae

Lupinus monteportae, commonly known as the Monte Porta lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Lupinus monteportae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
