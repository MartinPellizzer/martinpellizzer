Lepidium africanum subsp. divaricatum

Lepidium africanum subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as African wall cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
