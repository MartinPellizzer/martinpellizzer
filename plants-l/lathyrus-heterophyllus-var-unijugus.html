Lathyrus heterophyllus var. unijugus

Lathyrus heterophyllus var. unijugus, commonly known as the single-leaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces striking blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are usually alternate and vary in shape, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal form.
