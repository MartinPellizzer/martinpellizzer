Lachenalia mediana var. rogersii

Lachenalia mediana var. rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth habit and fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
