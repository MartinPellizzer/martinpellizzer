Lachenalia trichophylla

Lachenalia trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems with clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Lachenalia trichophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
