Lycopodioides producta

Lycopodioides producta, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with dense clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically located at the base of the stems. Lycopodioides producta thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant botanical and ecological importance.
