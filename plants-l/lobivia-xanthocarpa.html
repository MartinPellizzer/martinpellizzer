Lobivia xanthocarpa

Lobivia xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow fruit lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature common in desert plants to minimize water loss.

Lobivia xanthocarpa is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
