Lagotis officinalis var. latifolia

Lagotis officinalis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lagotis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, with parts of the plant traditionally used in herbal remedies.
