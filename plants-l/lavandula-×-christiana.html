Lavandula × christiana

Lavandula × christiana, commonly known as English lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spike-like inflorescences of purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its popular use in aromatherapy and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in various traditional applications.
