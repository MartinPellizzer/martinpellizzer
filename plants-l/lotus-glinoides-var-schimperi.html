Lotus glinoides var. schimperi

Lotus glinoides var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian lotus, is a species native to the highland regions of Ethiopia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, temperate climate of its native habitat.
