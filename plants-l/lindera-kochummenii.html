Lindera kochummenii

Lindera kochummenii, commonly known as the Japanese spice bush, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Lindera kochummenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
