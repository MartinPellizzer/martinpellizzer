Lomatium juniperinum

Lomatium juniperinum, commonly known as bitterroot, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in umbels.

Lomatium juniperinum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
