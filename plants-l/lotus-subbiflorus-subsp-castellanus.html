Lotus subbiflorus subsp. castellanus

Lotus subbiflorus subsp.

castellanus, commonly known as the Castellan lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
