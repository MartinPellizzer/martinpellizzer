Lasiocorys argyrophylla

Lasiocorys argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a pale green to grayish upper surface with a distinctive silvery coating, giving it its common name. The thallus is leathery and often forms dense mats on tree trunks, rocks, or soil. It contains a variety of secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role as a bioindicator species.

This lichen is widely distributed in temperate regions and plays an important role in nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.
