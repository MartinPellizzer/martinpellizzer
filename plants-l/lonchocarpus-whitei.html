Lonchocarpus whitei

Lonchocarpus whitei, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
