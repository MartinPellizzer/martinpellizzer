Lachnagrostis leptostachys

Lachnagrostis leptostachys, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

Lachnagrostis leptostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
