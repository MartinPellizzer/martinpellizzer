Ludwigia verticillata

Ludwigia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral willow or water spinach, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in water or moist soil, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Ludwigia verticillata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in ponds and water gardens due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in aquatic environments.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
