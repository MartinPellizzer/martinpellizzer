Lyperanthus rarus

Lyperanthus rarus, commonly known as the rare starflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lyperanthus rarus is considered a rare and endangered species.
