Lepanthes arenasiana

Lepanthes arenasiana, commonly known as the sand orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated pseudobulbs.

Lepanthes arenasiana is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
