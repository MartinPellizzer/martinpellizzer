Leonurus parviflorus

Leonurus parviflorus, commonly known as lesser hedgehog urtica, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
