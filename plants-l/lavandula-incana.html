Lavandula incana

Lavandula incana, commonly known as woolly lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of pale purple to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
