Loeselia nepetifolia

Loeselia nepetifolia, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of five. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Loeselia nepetifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.
