Lantana trifolia var. vulgata

Lantana trifolia var. vulgata, commonly known as the common lantana, is a shrub native to the Americas.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

The plant is also notable for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening.
