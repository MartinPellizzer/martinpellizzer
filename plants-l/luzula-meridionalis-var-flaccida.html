Luzula meridionalis var. flaccida

Luzula meridionalis var. flaccida, commonly known as the southern woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Luzula meridionalis var. flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
