Lepanthes complicata

Lepanthes complicata, commonly known as the twisted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its epiphytic lifestyle.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
