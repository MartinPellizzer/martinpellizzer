Laurus diospyros

Laurus diospyros, commonly known as the sacred laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and have a distinct aromatic scent. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
