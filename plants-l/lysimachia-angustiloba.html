Lysimachia angustiloba

Lysimachia angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in horticultural settings and ecological restoration projects.
