Lychnis diclinis

Lychnis diclinis, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Lychnis diclinis thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
