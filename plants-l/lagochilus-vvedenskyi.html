Lagochilus vvedenskyi

Lagochilus vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Vvedensky's rock hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the summer months.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
