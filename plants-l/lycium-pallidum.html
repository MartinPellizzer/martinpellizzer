Lycium pallidum

Lycium pallidum, commonly known as the pale boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Lycium pallidum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
