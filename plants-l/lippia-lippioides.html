Lippia lippioides

Lippia lippioides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Lippia lippioides is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in teas and natural remedies.
