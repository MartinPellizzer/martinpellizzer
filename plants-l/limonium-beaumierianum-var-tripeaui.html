Limonium beaumierianum var. tripeaui

Limonium beaumierianum var. tripeaui, commonly known as the coastal bluebell, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often growing in dunes or along the sea coast. It is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to coastal conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
