Lathyrus incurvus

Lathyrus incurvus, commonly known as the curlleaf pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, often curling leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Botanically, Lathyrus incurvus is notable for its unique leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
