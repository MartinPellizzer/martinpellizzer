Linum usitatissimum var. hassiacum

Linum usitatissimum var. hassiacum, commonly known as the Hassiacum flax, is a variety of the common flax plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its fiber, which is used in textile production, and for its seeds, which can be pressed for oil.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
