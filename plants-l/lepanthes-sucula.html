Lepanthes sucula

Lepanthes sucula, commonly known as the fat orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to dry environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a wide range of colors, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
