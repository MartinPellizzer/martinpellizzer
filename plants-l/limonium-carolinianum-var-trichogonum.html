Limonium carolinianum var. trichogonum

Limonium carolinianum var. trichogonum, commonly known as Carolina sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or salt marshes. It produces small, cylindrical seed pods that release seeds when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
