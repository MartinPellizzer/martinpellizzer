Lobelia ramosissima

Lobelia ramosissima, commonly known as the common lobelia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
