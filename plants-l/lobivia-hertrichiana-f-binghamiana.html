Lobivia hertrichiana f. binghamiana

Lobivia hertrichiana f. binghamiana, commonly known as the Bingham's lobivia, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, ribbed stems that often display a pale green to bluish-gray color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Native to the arid regions of central Argentina, it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and challenging species to cultivate.
