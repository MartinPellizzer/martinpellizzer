Lockia

Lockia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. Lockia species typically have compound leaves and produce pods that contain seeds.

These plants are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
