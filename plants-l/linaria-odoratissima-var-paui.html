Linaria odoratissima var. paui

Linaria odoratissima var. paui, commonly known as the sweet willie or the golden larkspur, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
