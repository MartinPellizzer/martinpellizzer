Lobivia shaferi var. flaviflora

Lobivia shaferi var. flaviflora, commonly known as the Yellow Flowered Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, a typical adaptation for water conservation in its native habitat.

This variety is prized by collectors for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
