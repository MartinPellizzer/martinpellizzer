Linaria stricta var. gussonei

Linaria stricta var. gussonei, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
