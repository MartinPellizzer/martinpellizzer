Liatris pycnostachya f. hubrichti

Liatris pycnostachya f. hubrichti, commonly known as the Hubricht's blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and produces showy, spires of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
