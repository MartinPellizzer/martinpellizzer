Lachnaea globulifera var. coerulescens

Lachnaea globulifera var. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue globular lichen, is a type of foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive blue-green coloration due to the presence of specific pigments.

It typically grows on calcareous rock surfaces in temperate regions, forming dense, cushion-like growths. The thallus is composed of a symbiotic association between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. Its morphology includes small, rounded lobes with a smooth upper surface and a more textured lower surface.

This lichen is often used as an indicator species for monitoring air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
