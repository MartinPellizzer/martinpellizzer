Lippia globiflora var. lanceolata

Lippia globiflora var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
