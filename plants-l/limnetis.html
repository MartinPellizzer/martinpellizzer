Limnetis

Limnetis, commonly known as the water shield, is a aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

It has floating leaves that are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant's roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of the water body, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or just at the water's surface. Limnetis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to life in still or slow-moving water.
