Lupinus utahensis

Lupinus utahensis, commonly known as the Utah lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus utahensis plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
