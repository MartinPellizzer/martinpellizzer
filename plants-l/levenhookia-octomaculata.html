Levenhookia octomaculata

Levenhookia octomaculata, commonly known as the eight-spotted leatherleaf, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are typically arranged in whorls and have a distinctive pattern of dark spots.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
