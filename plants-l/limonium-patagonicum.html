Limonium patagonicum

Limonium patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in coastal dunes and maritime environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Limonium patagonicum is well adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.
