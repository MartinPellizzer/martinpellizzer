Lotus conimbricensis

Lotus conimbricensis, commonly known as the Portuguese lotus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Portugal and southern France.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are sometimes used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers and lakes.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and traditional uses in Mediterranean regions.
