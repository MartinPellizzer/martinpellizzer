Lycopus arkansanus

Lycopus arkansanus, commonly known as Arkansas bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
