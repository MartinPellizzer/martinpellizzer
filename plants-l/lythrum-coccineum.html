Lythrum coccineum

Lythrum coccineum, commonly known as red lythrum, is a flowering plant in the Lythraceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Lythrum coccineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
