Lupinus lucidus

Lupinus lucidus, commonly known as the shining lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of bright blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Lupinus lucidus is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
