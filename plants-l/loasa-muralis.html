Loasa muralis

Loasa muralis, commonly known as the wall loasa, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, often found on cliffs or walls. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Loasa muralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
