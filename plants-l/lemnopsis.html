Lemnopsis

Lemnopsis, commonly known as the water clover, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Lemnaceae.

These plants are characterized by their small, floating leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, resembling the leaves of clover. They are typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Lemnopsis species are photosynthetic and play a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients.

However, they can become invasive in certain ecosystems if not managed properly.
