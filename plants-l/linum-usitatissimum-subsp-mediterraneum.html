Linum usitatissimum subsp. mediterraneum

Linum usitatissimum subsp.

mediterraneum, commonly known as Mediterranean flax, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are used for oil production, while the fibers from its stems have historical uses in textile manufacturing.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.
