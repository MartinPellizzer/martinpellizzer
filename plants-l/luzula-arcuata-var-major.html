Luzula arcuata var. major

Luzula arcuata var. major, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Luzula arcuata var. major is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
