Leptospermum attenuatum var. subsessile

Leptospermum attenuatum var. subsessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
