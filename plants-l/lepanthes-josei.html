Lepanthes josei

Lepanthes josei, commonly known as the Jose's slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
