Leontodon obovatus

Leontodon obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lanceolate leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent central disk flowers. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Leontodon obovatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
