Loranthus purpurascens

Loranthus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Santalaceae family.

It attaches itself to the branches of various host trees, such as eucalyptus and acacia, using its haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered together, and its berries are typically red or yellow, containing sticky seeds. It is native to regions in Australia and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can sometimes cause damage to its host trees if left unchecked.
