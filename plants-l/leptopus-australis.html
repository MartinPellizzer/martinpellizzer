Leptopus australis

Leptopus australis, commonly known as the southern sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, upright stem with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions.

Leptopus australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
