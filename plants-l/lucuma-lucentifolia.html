Lucuma lucentifolia

Lucuma lucentifolia, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Capsicaceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and is often used in traditional Andean cuisine.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.
