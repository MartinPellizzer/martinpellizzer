Lippia americana f. hyptoides

Lippia americana f. hyptoides, commonly known as dwarf lippia, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The compact growth habit of this cultivar makes it ideal for use in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
