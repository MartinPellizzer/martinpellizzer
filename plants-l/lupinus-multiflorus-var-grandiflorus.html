Lupinus multiflorus var. grandiflorus

Lupinus multiflorus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and dense clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with numerous small, oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus multiflorus var. grandiflorus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
