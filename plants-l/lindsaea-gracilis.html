Lindsaea gracilis

Lindsaea gracilis, commonly known as the slender lindsaea, is a fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or on rocky surfaces, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures include small, clustered sporangia on the underside of the fronds.

Lindsaea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
