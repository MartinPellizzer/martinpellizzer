Luzula sylvatica subsp. cantabrica

Luzula sylvatica subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain and western France. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems. Luzula sylvatica subsp.

cantabrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
