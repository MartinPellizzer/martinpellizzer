Layia graveolens

Layia graveolens, commonly known as the bitterleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Layia graveolens is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and ragweeds.

Its small, clustered flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
