Lachenalia luteola

Lachenalia luteola, commonly known as the yellow lachenalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and rockeries.
