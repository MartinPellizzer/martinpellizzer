Lobelia fissiflora

Lobelia fissiflora, commonly known as the fissured lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive fissured appearance, giving the plant its scientific name.

Lobelia fissiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
