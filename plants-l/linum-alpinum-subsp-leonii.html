Linum alpinum subsp. leonii

Linum alpinum subsp.

leonii, commonly known as the alpine flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Linaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, particularly in alpine environments.

The plant produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in textile production.
