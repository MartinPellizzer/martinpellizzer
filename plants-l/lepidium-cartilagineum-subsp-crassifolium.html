Lepidium cartilagineum subsp. crassifolium

Lepidium cartilagineum subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its thick, fleshy leaves and robust growth habit, often thriving in rocky or disturbed soils. It typically grows in open areas such as roadsides and dry grasslands, and it is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
