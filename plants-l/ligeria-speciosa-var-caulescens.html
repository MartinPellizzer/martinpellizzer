Ligeria speciosa var. caulescens

Ligeria speciosa var. caulescens, commonly known as the showy ligeria, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
