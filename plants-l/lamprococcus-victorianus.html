Lamprococcus victorianus

Lamprococcus victorianus, commonly known as the Victorian lamprococcus, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Coccidae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated body covered in a waxy, protective coating. The plant hosts of this species are typically eucalyptus trees, where it feeds on sap, causing various levels of damage. Its life cycle involves several stages, including egg, nymph, and adult, with the adults being the most commonly observed.

Due to its specific ecological role, it is of interest to researchers studying plant-insect interactions in native Australian flora.
