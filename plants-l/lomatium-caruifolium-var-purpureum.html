Lomatium caruifolium var. purpureum

Lomatium caruifolium var. purpureum, commonly known as purpleleaf lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, purple-tinged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open meadow habitats.
