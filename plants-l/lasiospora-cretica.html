Lasiospora cretica

Lasiospora cretica, commonly known as the Cretan lichen, is a unique species of lichen that thrives in the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its crust-like growth form on rock surfaces. The thallus is typically pale yellow to white, with a granular texture and distinct isidiate and sorediate structures. It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
