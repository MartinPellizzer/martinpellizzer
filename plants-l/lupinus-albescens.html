Lupinus albescens

Lupinus albescens, commonly known as the white lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Lupinus albescens is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
