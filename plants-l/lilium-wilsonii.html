Lilium wilsonii

Lilium wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's lily, is a species of flowering plant in the lily family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or orange throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, and it produces a tall, slender stem.

Lilium wilsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
