Leopoldia reticulata

Leopoldia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated leopoldia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a distinct veining pattern.

Leopoldia reticulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial sunlight.
