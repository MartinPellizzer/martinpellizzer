Lasthenia glabrata subsp. glabrata

Lasthenia glabrata subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the pale rayless goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs and pale floral coloration compared to other members of the species.
