Linum usitatissimum var. arvense

Linum usitatissimum var. arvense, commonly known as field flax, is a variant of the common flax plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces blue or white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its fibrous seeds are used in traditional textile production and also have culinary applications.

The variety is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
