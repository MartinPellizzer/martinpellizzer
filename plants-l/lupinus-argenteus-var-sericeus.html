Lupinus argenteus var. sericeus

Lupinus argenteus var. sericeus, commonly known as silver lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enriching the soil.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
