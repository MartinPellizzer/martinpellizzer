Ligustrum lucidum var. coriaceum

Ligustrum lucidum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a variant of the privet shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which is typically dark green and has a leathery texture. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a smooth surface with a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental shrub due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
