Liatris hirsutiflora

Liatris hirsutiflora, commonly known as the hairy leaf bluetail, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, spike-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is hairy, giving it the common name, and it produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

Liatris hirsutiflora is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
