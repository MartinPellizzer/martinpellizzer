Lepteranthus supinus

Lepteranthus supinus, commonly known as the prostrate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
