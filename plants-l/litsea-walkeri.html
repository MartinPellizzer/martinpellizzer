Litsea walkeri

Litsea walkeri, commonly known as the Philippine laurier or Malay laurier, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, bay trees, and cinnamon plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, and elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered, while the fruit is a smooth, dark blue berry.

Litsea walkeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
