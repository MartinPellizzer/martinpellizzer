Lonchocarpus guilleminianus

Lonchocarpus guilleminianus, commonly known as the giant senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. It is often found in moist lowland areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
