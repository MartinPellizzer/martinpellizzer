Lychnodiscus papillosus

Lychnodiscus papillosus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often forming a compact rosette structure. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are known for their vibrant color and fragrance.

Lychnodiscus papillosus is widely cultivated as a popular ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
