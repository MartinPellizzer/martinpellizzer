Lupinus amniculi-cervi

Lupinus amniculi-cervi, commonly known as the mountain lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legumes that contain seeds used by wildlife and livestock.

Lupinus amniculi-cervi is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sunlight, making it a common sight in alpine meadows.
