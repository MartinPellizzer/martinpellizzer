Lithocarpus hypophaeus

Lithocarpus hypophaeus, commonly known as the Burmese ironwood, is a member of the Fagaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, cupule-enclosed nuts that are typically reddish-brown in color. Its botanical features include a stout trunk, persistent leaves, and a long lifespan, making it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.

The species is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats.
