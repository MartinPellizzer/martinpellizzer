Lauro-cerasus officinalis var. brachystachys

Lauro-cerasus officinalis var. brachystachys, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or *Prunus mume*, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
