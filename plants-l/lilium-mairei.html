Lilium mairei

Lilium mairei, commonly known as the Maire lily, is a species of bulbous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink flowers with a yellowish throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Lilium mairei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
