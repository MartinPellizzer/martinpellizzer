Lilium californicum

Lilium californicum, commonly known as the California lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with striking white or pale pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
