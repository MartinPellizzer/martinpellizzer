Lasianthus barbatus

Lasianthus barbatus, commonly known as the hairy leaved beauty, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
