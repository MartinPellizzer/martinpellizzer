Lippia armata

Lippia armata, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic essential oils.
