Lupinus microphyllus var. kunthii

Lupinus microphyllus var. kunthii, commonly known as the small-leaved lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
