Llagunoa nitida var. mollis

Llagunoa nitida var. mollis, commonly known as the silky llagunoa, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
