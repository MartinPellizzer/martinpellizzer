Lactuca sororia var. pilipes

Lactuca sororia var. pilipes, commonly known as the hairy lettuce, is a variant of the common lettuce species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

This plant is characterized by its hairy leaves and a more robust growth habit compared to typical lettuce varieties. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The leaves are usually dark green with a slightly bitter taste, making it suitable for culinary use in certain cuisines.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
