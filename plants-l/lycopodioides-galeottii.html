Lycopodioides galeottii

Lycopodioides galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family, characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called sporophytes.

It is a vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant possesses a distinctive, creeping stem with whorled leaves that form dense, mat-like growths. Its reproductive structures, including microsporangia and megasporangia, are located on specialized branches called sporophylls.

Lycopodioides galeottii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
