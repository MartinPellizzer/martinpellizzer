Loranthus copaiferae

Loranthus copaiferae, commonly known as the copaifera mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Copaifera.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some benefits through the production of medicinal compounds. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of South America and is valued for its traditional medicinal uses.
