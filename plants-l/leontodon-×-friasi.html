Leontodon × friasi

Leontodon × friasi, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and flowering heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and road sides. Its leaves are linear and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Leontodon × friasi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
