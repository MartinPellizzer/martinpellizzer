Lupinus appositus

Lupinus appositus, commonly known as the slender lupine, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus appositus is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with soil bacteria.
