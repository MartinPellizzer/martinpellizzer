Lobelia albomaculata

Lobelia albomaculata, commonly known as the white-spotted lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple with white spots. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lobelia albomaculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in native plant gardens.
