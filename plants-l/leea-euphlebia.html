Leea euphlebia

Leea euphlebia, commonly known as the white leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Leeaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adaptable to various soil types.
