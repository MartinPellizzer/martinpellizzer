Lichtensteinia laevigata

Lichtensteinia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved lichen, is a species of lichen belonging to the family Verrucariaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form and a pale to yellowish-green color. The thallus is smooth and tightly adherent to its substrate, typically found on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. It lacks distinct lobes and is often covered with a layer of fine, granular structures.

This lichen is well-adapted to harsh conditions and is commonly found in regions with low humidity and high sunlight exposure.
