Lindsaea spruceana

Lindsaea spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's maidenhair, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a velvety texture. The plant is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Lindsaea spruceana produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in specialized leaf structures called sori.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to add a touch of subtlety to indoor or outdoor gardens.
