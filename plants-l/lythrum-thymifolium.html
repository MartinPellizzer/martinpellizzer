Lythrum thymifolium

Lythrum thymifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved catchfly, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink or red flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a faint thyme-like scent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
