Loranthus chrysanthoides

Loranthus chrysanthoides, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance. Its berries are red and contain sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in coastal and inland regions where its host trees thrive.
