Lycopodium urostachyum

Lycopodium urostachyum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobila, which contain the spores for reproduction. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium urostachyum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
