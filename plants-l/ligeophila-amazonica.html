Ligeophila amazonica

Ligeophila amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in the dense vegetation of riverbanks and marshes. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. Its flowers, which are typically white or pale pink, bloom during the wet season and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ligeophila amazonica plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
