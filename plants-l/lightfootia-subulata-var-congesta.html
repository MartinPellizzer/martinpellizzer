Lightfootia subulata var. congesta

Lightfootia subulata var. congesta, commonly known as the dense velvet tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high levels of salinity.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
