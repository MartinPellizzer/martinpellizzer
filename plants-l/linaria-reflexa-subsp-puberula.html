Linaria reflexa subsp. puberula

Linaria reflexa subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
