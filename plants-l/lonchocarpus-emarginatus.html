Lonchocarpus emarginatus

Lonchocarpus emarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf triggerplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including forests and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
