Ligusticum mutellinoides

Ligusticum mutellinoides, commonly known as the Tibetan lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
