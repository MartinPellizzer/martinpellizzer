Linaria fedorovii

Linaria fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Linaria fedorovii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer to reduce water loss.
