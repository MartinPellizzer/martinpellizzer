Lupinus densiflorus var. persecundus

Lupinus densiflorus var. persecundus, commonly known as the denseflower lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
