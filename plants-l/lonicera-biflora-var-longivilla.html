Lonicera biflora var. longivilla

Lonicera biflora var. longivilla, commonly known as the long-stemmed honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
