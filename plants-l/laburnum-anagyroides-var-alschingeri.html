Laburnum anagyroides var. alschingeri

Laburnum anagyroides var. alschingeri, commonly known as the Alsinger's golden chain, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is notable for its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its slender, branching stems are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
