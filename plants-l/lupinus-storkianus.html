Lupinus storkianus

Lupinus storkianus, commonly known as Stork's bill, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Lupinus storkianus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
