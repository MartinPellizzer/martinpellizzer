Ligustrum obtusifolium var. rubescens

Ligustrum obtusifolium var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leaf privet, is a variant of the privet shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that turn a striking red or reddish-purple color in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different light conditions, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a desirable choice for landscaping and hedges.
