Lepanthes chameleon

Lepanthes chameleon, commonly known as the chameleon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful and often iridescent petals. The plant typically grows as a small epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their vibrant hues, which can change subtly depending on light and environmental conditions.

The unique appearance of Lepanthes chameleon makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
