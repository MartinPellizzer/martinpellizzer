Lygeum spartum

Lygeum spartum, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats across Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spikes, and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional remedies.
