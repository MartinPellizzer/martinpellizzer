Lonicera ligustrina var. yunnanensis

Lonicera ligustrina var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
