Ligularia botryodes

Ligularia botryodes, commonly known as the woolly ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often hairy or woolly in texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountain meadows and forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
