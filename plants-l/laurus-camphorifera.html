Laurus camphorifera

Laurus camphorifera, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in perfumery and medicine.

The leaves contain essential oils, including camphor, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
