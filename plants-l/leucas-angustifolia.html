Leucas angustifolia

Leucas angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Leucas angustifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
