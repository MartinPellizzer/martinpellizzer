Lampetia nudicaulis

Lampetia nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairless, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Lampetia nudicaulis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and alpine settings.
