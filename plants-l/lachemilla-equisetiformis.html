Lachemilla equisetiformis

Lachemilla equisetiformis, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence.

Lachemilla equisetiformis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in alpine and subalpine environments.
