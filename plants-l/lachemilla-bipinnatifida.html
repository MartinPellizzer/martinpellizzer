Lachemilla bipinnatifida

Lachemilla bipinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils. The plant features small, fleshy, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically greenish-white or pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Lachemilla bipinnatifida is well adapted to harsh alpine environments and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
