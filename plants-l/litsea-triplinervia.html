Litsea triplinervia

Litsea triplinervia, commonly known as the three-nerved litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its specific epithet. Its fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, dark purple drupe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
