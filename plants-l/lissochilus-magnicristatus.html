Lissochilus magnicristatus

Lissochilus magnicristatus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool and humid climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts.
