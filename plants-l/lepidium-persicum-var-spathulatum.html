Lepidium persicum var. spathulatum

Lepidium persicum var. spathulatum, commonly known as Persian cress, is a herbaceous plant native to the regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. The leaves are usually alternate, with a slightly lobed or spatulate shape, giving the plant its specific epithet "spathulatum.". It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its peppery flavor and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
