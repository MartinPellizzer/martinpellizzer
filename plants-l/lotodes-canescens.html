Lotodes canescens

Lotodes canescens, commonly known as the white water lily, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with showy petals that attract pollinators like bees and beetles.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
