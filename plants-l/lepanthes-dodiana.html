Lepanthes dodiana

Lepanthes dodiana, commonly known as the Dod's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with bright colors, often in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes dodiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
