Linnaea angustifolia

Linnaea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf twinflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its clusters of small, bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and forest understories. It is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

Linnaea angustifolia plays a role in supporting pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
