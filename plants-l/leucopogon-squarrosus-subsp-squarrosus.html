Leucopogon squarrosus subsp. squarrosus

Leucopogon squarrosus subsp.

squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose leucopogon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, characterized by its dense, woody structure.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance.

This species is adapted to saline and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in coastal regions.
