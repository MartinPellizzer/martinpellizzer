Lygeum murcicum

Lygeum murcicum, commonly known as the Murcian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Lygeum murcicum plays a minor role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for small invertebrates.
