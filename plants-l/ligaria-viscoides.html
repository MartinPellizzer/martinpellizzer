Ligaria viscoides

Ligaria viscoides, commonly known as the sticky ligaria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, sticky texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, waxy surface.

Ligaria viscoides is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
