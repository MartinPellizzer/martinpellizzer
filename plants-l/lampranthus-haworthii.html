Lampranthus haworthii

Lampranthus haworthii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features vibrant red bracts that resemble torches, giving it its common name. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is often cultivated for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
