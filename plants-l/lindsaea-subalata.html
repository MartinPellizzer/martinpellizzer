Lindsaea subalata

Lindsaea subalata, commonly known as the silver lindsaea, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The fronds are usually pinnate, with a distinctive, graceful appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Lindsaea subalata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in humid, indoor settings.
