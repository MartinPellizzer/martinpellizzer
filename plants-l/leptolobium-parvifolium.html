Leptolobium parvifolium

Leptolobium parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved leptolobium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.
