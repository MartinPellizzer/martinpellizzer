Limniris macrosiphon

Limniris macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant siphonwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its long, flexible stems and large, ribbon-like leaves.

It is native to freshwater environments in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in slow-moving or still waters. The plant's most distinctive feature is its siphonic structure, which allows it to absorb nutrients directly from the water. Limniris macrosiphon plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.

However, it can become invasive in non-native regions, disrupting local aquatic biodiversity.
