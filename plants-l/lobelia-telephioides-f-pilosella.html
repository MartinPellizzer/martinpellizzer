Lobelia telephioides f. pilosella

Lobelia telephioides f. pilosella, commonly known as the woolly lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
