Lauro-cerasus lusitanica f. myrtilifolia

Lauro-cerasus lusitanica f. myrtilifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved cherry laurel, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both Prunus laurocerasus and Prunus cerasifera.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a slightly glossy texture, and its leaves are oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark purple berries. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
