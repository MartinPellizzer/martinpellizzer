Lathyrus luteus

Lathyrus luteus, commonly known as yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds.

Lathyrus luteus is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
