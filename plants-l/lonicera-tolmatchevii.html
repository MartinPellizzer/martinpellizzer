Lonicera tolmatchevii

Lonicera tolmatchevii, commonly known as the Korean honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Lonicera tolmatchevii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
