Lepidium villarsii subsp. villarsii

Lepidium villarsii subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
