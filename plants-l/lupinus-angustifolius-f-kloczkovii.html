Lupinus angustifolius f. kloczkovii

Lupinus angustifolius f. kloczkovii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lupine, is a variant of the narrow-leaf lupine species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in spires, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
