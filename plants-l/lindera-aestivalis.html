Lindera aestivalis

Lindera aestivalis, commonly known as summer sweet, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a distinctive aroma when crushed.

Lindera aestivalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
