Linaria munbyana f. eumunbyana

Linaria munbyana f. eumunbyana, commonly known as the golden linaria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to low-water environments. It features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
