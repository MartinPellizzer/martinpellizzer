Lomatium foeniculaceum var. fimbriatum

Lomatium foeniculaceum var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are compound, with finely divided, fern-like lobes, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
