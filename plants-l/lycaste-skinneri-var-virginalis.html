Lycaste skinneri var. virginalis

Lycaste skinneri var. virginalis, commonly known as the Virgin's lycaste, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow, often with a subtle pink or red hue. The plant typically grows as a compact, sympodial orchid with thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water, aiding its survival in arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy texture, and it prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
