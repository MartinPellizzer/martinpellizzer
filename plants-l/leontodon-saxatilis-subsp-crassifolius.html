Leontodon saxatilis subsp. crassifolius

Leontodon saxatilis subsp.

crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved devil's-bit manroot, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and fleshy.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spiky inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptability.
