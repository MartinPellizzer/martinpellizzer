Liparis compressa

Liparis compressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a slender, often creeping rhizome with oval, dark green leaves that are compressed and narrow. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a faintly colored lip.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
