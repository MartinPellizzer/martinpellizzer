Lepigonum fimbriatum

Lepigonum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Lepigonum fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
