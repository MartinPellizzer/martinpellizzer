Linum arvense

Linum arvense, commonly known as field flax, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Linum arvense is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.
