Lepidocupania lepidota

Lepidocupania lepidota, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Lepidocupania lepidota is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
