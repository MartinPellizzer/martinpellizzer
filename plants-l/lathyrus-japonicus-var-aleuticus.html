Lathyrus japonicus var. aleuticus

Lathyrus japonicus var. aleuticus, commonly known as the Aleutian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the northern Pacific coastal regions.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along shorelines and cliffs. The plant features slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its trifoliate leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize coastal soils and support local wildlife.
