Licuala tonkinensis

Licuala tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin palm, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant typically grows as a solitary trunk with a crown of leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-colored fruits. Licuala tonkinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
