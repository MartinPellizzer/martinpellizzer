Lomanthus arnaldii

Lomanthus arnaldii, commonly known as the red-flowered lomanthus, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
