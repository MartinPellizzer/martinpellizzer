Lomaria borealis

Lomaria borealis, commonly known as the Arctic lomaria, is a hardy flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Lomaria borealis thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.
