Laplacea

Laplacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are often followed by round, fuzzy fruits. It is native to the Americas and is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
