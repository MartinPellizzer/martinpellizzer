Leucopogon virgatus

Leucopogon virgatus, commonly known as the slender heath, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or sandy soils.

Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it a suitable ground cover in native plant gardens.
