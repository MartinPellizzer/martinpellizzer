Lippia lantanifolia var. crenata

Lippia lantanifolia var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated lippia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
