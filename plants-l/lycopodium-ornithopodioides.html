Lycopodium ornithopodioides

Lycopodium ornithopodioides, commonly known as the rock clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features needle-like leaves that are spirally arranged along its stems, giving it a dense, moss-like appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Lycopodium ornithopodioides is adapted to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
