Lugaion radiatum

Lugaion radiatum, commonly known as the radiant lugaion, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reflect excess sunlight in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pale blue and white, which bloom during the short summer months. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the thin, rocky soil.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Lugaion radiatum is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
