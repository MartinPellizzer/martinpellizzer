Lissochilus pyrophilus

Lissochilus pyrophilus, commonly known as the fire-loving orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its unique name reflects its affinity for areas that have experienced fire, as it often regenerates after forest fires.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate root system and specialized pollination mechanisms.
