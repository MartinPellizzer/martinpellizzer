Lockhartia cladoniophora

Lockhartia cladoniophora, commonly known as the silver lace orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage with a silvery sheen, which gives it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
