Lilium longiflorum var. longiflorum

Lilium longiflorum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the bulbs are composed of fleshy, modified leaves called scales.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is widely cultivated for floral arrangements.
