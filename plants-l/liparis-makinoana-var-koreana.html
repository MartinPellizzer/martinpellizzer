Liparis makinoana var. koreana

Liparis makinoana var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has linear leaves and a compact, pseudobulb-like structure that stores water and nutrients.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
