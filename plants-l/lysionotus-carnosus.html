Lysionotus carnosus

Lysionotus carnosus, commonly known as the fleshy-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies among species.
