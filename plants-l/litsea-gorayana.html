Litsea gorayana

Litsea gorayana, commonly known as the Chinese sandpaper tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its rough, sandpapery bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by round, orange-red berries.

Litsea gorayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
