Loranthus merrillii

Loranthus merrillii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in China.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds and other wildlife. Its parasitic nature allows it to derive nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

Loranthus merrillii is often found in forested areas and is an important component of the ecological system in its native habitat.
