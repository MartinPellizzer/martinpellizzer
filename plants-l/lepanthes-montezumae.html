Lepanthes montezumae

Lepanthes montezumae, commonly known as the Montezuma's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are relatively small but highly ornamental, often featuring intricate patterns and a delicate structure.

Lepanthes montezumae is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
