Liparis caudata

Liparis caudata, commonly known as the bearded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy spike of flowers that often have a distinctive beard-like structure at the base. The plant typically blooms in the spring and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Liparis caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
