Lonicera pyrenaica var. paui

Lonicera pyrenaica var. paui, commonly known as the Pyrenean honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
