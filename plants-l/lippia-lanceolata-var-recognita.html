Lippia lanceolata var. recognita

Lippia lanceolata var. recognita, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
