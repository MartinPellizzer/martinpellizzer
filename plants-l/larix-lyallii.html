Larix lyallii

Larix lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's larch, is a species of coniferous tree native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deciduous needles, which turn golden before falling off in the autumn. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
