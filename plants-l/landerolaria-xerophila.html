Landerolaria xerophila

Landerolaria xerophila, commonly known as the desert lichen, is a unique plant that thrives in arid environments.

It is a symbiotic composite of algae and fungi, forming a lichen structure that allows it to survive in extreme conditions. This plant is highly adapted to dry climates, with a slow growth rate and the ability to store water efficiently. Its morphology includes a crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to rocky surfaces.

Landerolaria xerophila plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert microorganisms.
