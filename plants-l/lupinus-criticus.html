Lupinus criticus

Lupinus criticus, commonly known as the bitter lupin, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like, violet-blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Lupinus criticus is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
