Lepidium fraseri

Lepidium fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and is often found in moist, rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically basal and alternate, with a distinctive pungent odor.

Lepidium fraseri is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
