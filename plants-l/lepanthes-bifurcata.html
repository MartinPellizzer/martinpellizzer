Lepanthes bifurcata

Lepanthes bifurcata, commonly known as the bifurcated slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often display a striking combination of white and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Lepanthes bifurcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
