Lobularia libyca

Lobularia libyca, commonly known as the Libyan sandwort, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This annual herb typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, white or pale purple flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, rounded seed pods.

The plant is often found in coastal dunes and dry, coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.
