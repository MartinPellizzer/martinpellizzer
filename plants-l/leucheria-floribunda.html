Leucheria floribunda

Leucheria floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Leucheria floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
