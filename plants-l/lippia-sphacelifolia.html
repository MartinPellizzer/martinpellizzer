Lippia sphacelifolia

Lippia sphacelifolia, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in terminal clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Verbenaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like verbena and lemongrass.
