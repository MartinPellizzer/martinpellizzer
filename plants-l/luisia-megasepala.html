Luisia megasepala

Luisia megasepala, commonly known as the giant sepal orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy sepals that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length, often displaying vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks and branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
