Lesquerella carinata var. languida

Lesquerella carinata var. languida, commonly known as the slender lesquerella, is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is of interest for its potential in industrial applications due to its oil-rich seeds.
