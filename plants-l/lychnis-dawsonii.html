Lychnis dawsonii

Lychnis dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's campion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
