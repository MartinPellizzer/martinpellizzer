Lithospermum confundum

Lithospermum confundum, commonly known as the purple rock pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny, dark seeds.

Lithospermum confundum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and demonstrating some drought tolerance.
