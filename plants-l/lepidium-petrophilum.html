Lepidium petrophilum

Lepidium petrophilum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and is typically found growing in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It is adapted to harsh conditions, including poor soil and high altitude.

Lepidium petrophilum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
