Lupinus caeruleus

Lupinus caeruleus, commonly known as blue lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Lupinus caeruleus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
