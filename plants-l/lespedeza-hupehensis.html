Lespedeza hupehensis

Lespedeza hupehensis, commonly known as Chinese straggleweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Lespedeza hupehensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
