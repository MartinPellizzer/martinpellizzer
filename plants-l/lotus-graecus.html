Lotus graecus

Lotus graecus, commonly known as the Greek lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking pink or red flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Lotus graecus is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in ancient cultures.
