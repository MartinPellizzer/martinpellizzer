Lonchocarpus nesiotes

Lonchocarpus nesiotes, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Due to its economic importance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in agroforestry systems.
