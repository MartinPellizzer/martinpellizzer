Lupinus shrevei

Lupinus shrevei, commonly known as Shreve's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.

Lupinus shrevei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
