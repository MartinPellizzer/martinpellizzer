Leontopodium chuii

Leontopodium chuii, commonly known as the Chui's edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh mountainous conditions. Its growth is slow, and it often forms compact, cushion-like structures to conserve heat and moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
