Lachenalia sargeantii

Lachenalia sargeantii, commonly known as the Sargeant's lachenalia, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a dormant period during the wet season and active growth during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also of interest to botanists and conservationists.
