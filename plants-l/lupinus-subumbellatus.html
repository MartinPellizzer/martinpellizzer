Lupinus subumbellatus

Lupinus subumbellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant has slender, compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Lupinus subumbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
