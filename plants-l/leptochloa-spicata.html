Leptochloa spicata

Leptochloa spicata, commonly known as spikegrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Leptochloa spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
