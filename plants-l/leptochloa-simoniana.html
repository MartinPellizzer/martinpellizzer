Leptochloa simoniana

Leptochloa simoniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Leptochloa simoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
