Luzula purpureosplendens

Luzula purpureosplendens, commonly known as the purple splendour sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it often grows in moist, shady environments.

Luzula purpureosplendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
