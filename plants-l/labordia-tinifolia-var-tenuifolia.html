Labordia tinifolia var. tenuifolia

Labordia tinifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved labordia, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
