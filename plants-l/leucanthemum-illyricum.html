Leucanthemum illyricum

Leucanthemum illyricum, commonly known as the white marguerite or Illyrian daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy white ray flowers surrounding a central yellow disc, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It typically grows in open woodland areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

Leucanthemum illyricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
