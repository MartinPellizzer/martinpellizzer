Liatris hirsuta

Liatris hirsuta, commonly known as hairy blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a cylindrical shape at the top of the stem. Liatris hirsuta thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
