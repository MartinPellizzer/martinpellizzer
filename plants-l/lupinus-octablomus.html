Lupinus octablomus

Lupinus octablomus, commonly known as the eight-flower lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus octablomus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil for other plants.
