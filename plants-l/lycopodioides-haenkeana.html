Lycopodioides haenkeana

Lycopodioides haenkeana, commonly known as the Chilean clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with spirally arranged leaves and a slender, branching stem. Its spore-bearing structures, called strobili, are located at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodioides haenkeana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
