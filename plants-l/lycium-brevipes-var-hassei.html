Lycium brevipes var. hassei

Lycium brevipes var. hassei, commonly known as the narrowleaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Lycium brevipes var. hassei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
