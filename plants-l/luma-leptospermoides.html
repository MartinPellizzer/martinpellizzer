Luma leptospermoides

Luma leptospermoides, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to southern Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting birds and insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
