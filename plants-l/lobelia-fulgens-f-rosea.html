Lobelia fulgens f. rosea

Lobelia fulgens f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered cardinal flower, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers with a distinctive pinkish rose hue, which are particularly attractive to hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
