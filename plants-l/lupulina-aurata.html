Lupulina aurata

Lupulina aurata, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Lupulina aurata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in garden settings.
