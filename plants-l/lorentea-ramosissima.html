Lorentea ramosissima

Lorentea ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Lorentea ramosissima is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
