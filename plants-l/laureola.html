Laureola

Laureola, commonly known as the rose of Jericho, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, pale green or pinkish leaves that resemble rose petals. The plant is notable for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a state of dormancy, curling into a compact ball. When watered, it can rapidly revive and bloom with delicate, pink flowers.

This resilience and dramatic transformation make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and symbolic use in various cultures.
