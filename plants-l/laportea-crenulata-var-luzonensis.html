Laportea crenulata var. luzonensis

Laportea crenulata var. luzonensis, commonly known as Luzon's laportea, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as montane forests.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
