Liparis jarensis

Liparis jarensis, commonly known as the Jarensis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in poor, well-drained soils.

Liparis jarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare orchid species.
