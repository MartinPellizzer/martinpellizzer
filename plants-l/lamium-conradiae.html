Lamium conradiae

Lamium conradiae, commonly known as Conrad's deadnettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
