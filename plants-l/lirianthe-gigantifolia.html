Lirianthe gigantifolia

Lirianthe gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant lirianthe, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant shades of pink and white. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
