Litothamnus ellipticus

Litothamnus ellipticus, commonly known as the pink glasswort, is a flowering plant native to the coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in rocky or coastal environments. The plant produces small, pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate color to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Litothamnus ellipticus plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
