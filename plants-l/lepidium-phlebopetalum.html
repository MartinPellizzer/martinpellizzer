Lepidium phlebopetalum

Lepidium phlebopetalum, commonly known as the narrowleaf rocket, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and weeds. The plant features small, cruciform flowers and has a hairy, branched stem. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a slightly bitter taste.

Lepidium phlebopetalum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
