Lonicera setchuensis

Lonicera setchuensis, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using twining stems and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to biodiversity.

The fruit, which is red and fleshy, is edible and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
