Loranthus karwinskyanus

Loranthus karwinskyanus, commonly known as the Karwinsky's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on species of the genus Ficus.

Its botanical structure includes a hemiparasitic nature, meaning it derives some nutrients from its host while also performing photosynthesis.
