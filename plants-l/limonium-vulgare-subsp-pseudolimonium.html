Limonium vulgare subsp. pseudolimonium

Limonium vulgare subsp.

pseudolimonium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its spikes of small, blue or pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its ability to tolerate salt spray makes it well adapted to maritime climates.

It is often used in coastal landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
