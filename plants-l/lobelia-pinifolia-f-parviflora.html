Lobelia pinifolia f. parviflora

Lobelia pinifolia f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf pine lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
