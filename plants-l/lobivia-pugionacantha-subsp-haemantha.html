Lobivia pugionacantha subsp. haemantha

Lobivia pugionacantha subsp.

haemantha, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a compact, spineless cactus native to southern Africa. It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
