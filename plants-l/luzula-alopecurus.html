Luzula alopecurus

Luzula alopecurus, commonly known as mouse-tail grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Luzula alopecurus is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
