Lotus coronillifolius var. argenteus

Lotus coronillifolius var. argenteus, commonly known as the silver lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a silvery-gray underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
