Linum striatum var. texanum

Linum striatum var. texanum, commonly known as the Texas flax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
