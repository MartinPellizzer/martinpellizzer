Lobivia famatinensis var. citriflora

Lobivia famatinensis var. citriflora, commonly known as the Citrus Lobivia, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine white coating. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
