Lupinus sparsiflorus var. insignitus

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. insignitus, commonly known as the sparseflower lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
