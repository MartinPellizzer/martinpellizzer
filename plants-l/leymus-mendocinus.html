Leymus mendocinus

Leymus mendocinus, commonly known as Mendo barley, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and conservation efforts.
