Lepanthes purulhaensis

Lepanthes purulhaensis, commonly known as the Purulha orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America, particularly found in the highlands of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. The plant features a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate structure and are often adorned with intricate patterns and markings.

Lepanthes purulhaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
