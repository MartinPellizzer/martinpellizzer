Lapsana alpina

Lapsana alpina, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. It has slender stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Lapsana alpina is often found in open habitats such as meadows and mountain slopes.
