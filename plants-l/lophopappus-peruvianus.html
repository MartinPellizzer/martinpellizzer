Lophopappus peruvianus

Lophopappus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence.

Lophopappus peruvianus is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
