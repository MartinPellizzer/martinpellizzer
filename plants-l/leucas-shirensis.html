Leucas shirensis

Leucas shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Leucas shirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
