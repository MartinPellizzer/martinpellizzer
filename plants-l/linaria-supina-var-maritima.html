Linaria supina var. maritima

Linaria supina var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spade-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a unique horn-like structure at the base. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds.

This variety is often found along shorelines and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
