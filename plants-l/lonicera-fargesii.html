Lonicera fargesii

Lonicera fargesii, commonly known as the Farges honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Lonicera fargesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
