Lunathyrium auriculatum

Lunathyrium auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate mooncup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped flowers that resemble small cups. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a unique auriculate base, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
