Lindsaea Ensifolia Subsp. Coriacea

Lindsaea ensifolia subsp. coriacea is a unique fern species native to parts of South America. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its distinctive leaf structure. The plant features elongated, sword-shaped fronds that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its habitat.
