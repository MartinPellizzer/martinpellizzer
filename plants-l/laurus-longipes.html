Laurus longipes

Laurus longipes, commonly known as the bay laurel or sweet bay, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used for centuries in cooking and traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Laurus longipes make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
