Lappula concava

Lappula concava, commonly known as the narrowleaf lappula, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. Lappula concava is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in dry meadows.

It is characterized by its short stature and distinctive, inflated seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature.
