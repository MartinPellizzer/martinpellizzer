Lycianthes xylopiifolia var. intermedia

Lycianthes xylopiifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a variant of the Lycianthes genus native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features include a pubescent surface and a distinct, aromatic scent.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is related to other nightshade species.
