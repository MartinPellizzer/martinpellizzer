Lavoisiera tetragona

Lavoisiera tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Lavoisiera tetragona is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is often found in meadows and along the edges of woodlands.
