Luzula spadicea

Luzula spadicea, commonly known as the green moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear inconspicuous greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Luzula spadicea thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands, bogs, and along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
