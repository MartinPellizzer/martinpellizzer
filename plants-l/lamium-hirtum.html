Lamium hirtum

Lamium hirtum, commonly known as hairy deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lamium hirtum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
