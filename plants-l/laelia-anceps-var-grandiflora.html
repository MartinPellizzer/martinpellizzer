Laelia anceps var. grandiflora

Laelia anceps var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.

Its popularity among orchid enthusiasts is due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
