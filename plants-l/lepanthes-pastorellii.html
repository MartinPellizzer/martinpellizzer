Lepanthes pastorellii

Lepanthes pastorellii, commonly known as the Pastorelli's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

The orchid is prized for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
