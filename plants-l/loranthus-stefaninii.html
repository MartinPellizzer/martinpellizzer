Loranthus stefaninii

Loranthus stefaninii, commonly known as the Stefanini's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Loranthaceae family and has a distinctive appearance with small, leathery leaves and bright red berries. The plant derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized roots called haustoria. It plays an ecological role in supporting bird populations that feed on its berries.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
