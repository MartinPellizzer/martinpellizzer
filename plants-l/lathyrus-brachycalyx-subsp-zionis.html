Lathyrus brachycalyx subsp. zionis

Lathyrus brachycalyx subsp.

zionis, commonly known as the Zion pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and slender, branching stems.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
