Langloisia setosissima subsp. punctata

Langloisia setosissima subsp.

punctata, commonly known as the punctate langloisia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves with a unique pattern of small, raised dots.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for taxonomists and botanists studying plant diversity.
