Lessingianthus grearii

Lessingianthus grearii, commonly known as the Greari's sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
