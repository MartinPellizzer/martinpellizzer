Luzula campestris var. pseudonemorosa

Luzula campestris var. pseudonemorosa, commonly known as the meadow sweetflag, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green foliage. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
