Lobelia alsinoides var. alsinoides

Lobelia alsinoides var. alsinoides, commonly known as the Alsine lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
