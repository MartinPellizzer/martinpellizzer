Lespedeza juncea var. subsericea

Lespedeza juncea var. subsericea, commonly known as silverleaf lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are finely hairy and often silvery in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage plant for livestock.
