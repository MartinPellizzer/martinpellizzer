Laxmannia sessilis

Laxmannia sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laxmannia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk.

Laxmannia sessilis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
