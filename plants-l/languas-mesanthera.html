Languas mesanthera

Languas mesanthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

Languas mesanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
