Lepidium cartilagineum subsp. pumilum

Lepidium cartilagineum subsp.

pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf wall pepper, is a low-growing plant characterized by its small, club-shaped fruits. It typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with dense, hairy stems and small, pinnately divided leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces a distinctive, peppery odor when crushed.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
