Lippia petiolata

Lippia petiolata, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring aromatic leaves and small blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement. Lippia petiolata is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
