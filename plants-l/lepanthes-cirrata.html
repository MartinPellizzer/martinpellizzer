Lepanthes cirrata

Lepanthes cirrata, commonly known as the cirrate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often white or pale green with intricate patterns, and it produces small, fleshy seeds.

Lepanthes cirrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
