Ledum palustre f. yesoense

Ledum palustre f. yesoense, commonly known as the Yesoense Labrador tea, is a variant of the broadleaf Labrador tea.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, camphor-like scent. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in acidic, moist soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
