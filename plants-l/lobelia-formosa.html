Lobelia formosa

Lobelia formosa, commonly known as the showy lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with contrasting yellow markings. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Lobelia formosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators.
