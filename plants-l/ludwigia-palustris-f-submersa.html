Ludwigia palustris f. submersa

Ludwigia palustris f. submersa, commonly known as the submerged ludwigia, is a variation of the Ludwigia palustris species that thrives in aquatic environments.

This plant is characterized by its fully submerged leaves, which are typically narrower and more elongated compared to its terrestrial relatives. It is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for underwater pollination.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is popular among aquarists for its aesthetic value in planted tanks.
