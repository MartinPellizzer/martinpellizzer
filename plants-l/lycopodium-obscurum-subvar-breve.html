Lycopodium obscurum subvar. breve

Lycopodium obscurum subvar. breve, commonly known as the short-stemmed clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing structures called sporophytes at the tips of its branches.

This species is part of the Lycopodiaceae family and plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
