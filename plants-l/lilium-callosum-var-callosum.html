Lilium callosum var. callosum

Lilium callosum var. callosum, commonly known as the Callus lily, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive callus-like structure on the inner petals. The plant produces bulbous roots and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique floral morphology.
