Lichtensteinia trifida var. pinnatifida

Lichtensteinia trifida var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lichen orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique morphology and specific habitat requirements make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

The varietal name 'pinnatifida' refers to the finely divided, feather-like appearance of its leaves.
