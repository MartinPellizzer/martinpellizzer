Lythrum uniflorum

Lythrum uniflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist soils and can be found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Lythrum uniflorum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various insects.
