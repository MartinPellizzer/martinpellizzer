Lepidium idahoense

Lepidium idahoense, commonly known as Idaho peppergrass, is a small annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruits are small, dry pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium idahoense is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
