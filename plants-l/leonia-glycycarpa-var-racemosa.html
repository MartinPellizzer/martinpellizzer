Leonia glycycarpa var. racemosa

Leonia glycycarpa var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose lemon tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, citrus-like fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

The racemose lemon tree is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and year-round foliage.
