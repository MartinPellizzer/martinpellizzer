Linum hirsutum subsp. pseudoanatolicum

Linum hirsutum subsp.

pseudoanatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian flax, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe and southwestern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
