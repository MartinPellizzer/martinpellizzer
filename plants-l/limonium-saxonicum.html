Limonium saxonicum

Limonium saxonicum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in saline and sandy environments. The plant belongs to the Plumbagaceae family and is well adapted to coastal dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium saxonicum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh coastal conditions.
