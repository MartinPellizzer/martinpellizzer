Lycium capense

Lycium capense, commonly known as the coastal gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

Lycium capense is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
