Lampranthus subtruncatus

Lampranthus subtruncatus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Its growth habit is typically compact, with a branching structure that enhances its visual appeal.
