Litsea brideliifolia

Litsea brideliifolia, commonly known as the West African litsea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.
