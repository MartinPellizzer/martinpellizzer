Lycopodioides cryptogaea

Lycopodioides cryptogaea, commonly known as the cryptogamic lycopod, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the Lycophyta division.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called sporophytes, which are typically found on its slender, branching stems. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas where humidity is high. Its reproductive cycle relies on spores rather than seeds, a trait shared with other primitive vascular plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
