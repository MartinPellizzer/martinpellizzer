Lobivia densispina var. rebutioides

Lobivia densispina var. rebutioides, commonly known as the Rebuttioides Lobivia, is a compact, low-growing cacti species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It features a ribbed, cylindrical stem with a gray-green coloration and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring. The plant has spiny areoles with short, white bristles, and its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand extreme desert conditions.
