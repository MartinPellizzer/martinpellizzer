Leucas vestita var. angustifolia

Leucas vestita var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved leucas, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped and narrow, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or white, blooming during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
