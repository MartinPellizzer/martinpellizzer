Lythrum lineare

Lythrum lineare, commonly known as narrowleaf rosemary or purple loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of vibrant purple flowers that bloom from June to September. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Lythrum lineare thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
