Loranthus glaucus

Loranthus glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small white or yellow flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to Australia and is often found in woodland and forested areas.
