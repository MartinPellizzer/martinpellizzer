Leucospermum hypophyllocarpodendron

Leucospermum hypophyllocarpodendron, commonly known as the silverleaf candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
