Ligeophila grandis

Ligeophila grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Ligeophila grandis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
