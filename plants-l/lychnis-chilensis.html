Lychnis chilensis

Lychnis chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean campion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many showy, herbaceous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas and produces vibrant pink or red flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Lychnis chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
