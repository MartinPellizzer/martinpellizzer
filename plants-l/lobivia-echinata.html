Lobivia echinata

Lobivia echinata, commonly known as the spiny lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its robust, ribbed stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Lobivia echinata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
