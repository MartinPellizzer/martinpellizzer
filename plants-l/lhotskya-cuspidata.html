Lhotskya cuspidata

Lhotskya cuspidata, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Lhotskya cuspidata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
