Leptorhynchos panaetioides

Leptorhynchos panaetioides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant has fleshy, thick leaves that are often velvety in texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
