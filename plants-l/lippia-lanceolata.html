Lippia lanceolata

Lippia lanceolata, commonly known as Mexican mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Lippia lanceolata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
