Lepanthes punctata

Lepanthes punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Lepanthes punctata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
