Lonchocarpus rufescens

Lonchocarpus rufescens, commonly known as red-stemmed senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and medicinal research.
