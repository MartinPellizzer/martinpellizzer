Linnaea borealis f. reticulata

Linnaea borealis f. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined twinflower, is a variant of the twinflower plant native to boreal and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, white, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture with a dark green upper surface. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
