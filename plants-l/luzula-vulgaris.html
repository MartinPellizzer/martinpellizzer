Luzula vulgaris

Luzula vulgaris, commonly known as common wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.
