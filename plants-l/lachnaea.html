Lachnaea

Lachnaea, commonly known as the common lichen, is a type of lichen that belongs to the family Lachnaceae.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium. Lachnaea typically grows on bark, stone, or soil surfaces in temperate regions. Its morphology includes a crust-like thallus with a dense, leathery texture.

The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.
