Limonium menigense

Limonium menigense, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Limonium menigense is also cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
