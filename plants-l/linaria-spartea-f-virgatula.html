Linaria spartea f. virgatula

Linaria spartea f. virgatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
