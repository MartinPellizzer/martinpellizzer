Lasianthus varians

Lasianthus varians, commonly known as the variable lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lasianthus varians is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
