Laplacea haematoxylon

Laplacea haematoxylon, commonly known as the bloodwood tree, is a member of the Rhamnaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark red to black heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a dense canopy and rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
