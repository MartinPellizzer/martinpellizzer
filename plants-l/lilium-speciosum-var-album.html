Lilium speciosum var. album

Lilium speciosum var. album, commonly known as the white spider lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of large, white, funnel-shaped flowers that resemble spider-like blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
