Lissochilus dybowskii

Lissochilus dybowskii, commonly known as the Dybowski's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, yellow, and green.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species among orchid enthusiasts.
