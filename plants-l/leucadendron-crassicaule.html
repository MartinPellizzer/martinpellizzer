Leucadendron crassicaule

Leucadendron crassicaule, commonly known as the silverleaf candle bush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble candles, typically ranging from yellow to red in color. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that give it a silvery appearance, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Leucadendron crassicaule is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique form and seasonal color changes.
