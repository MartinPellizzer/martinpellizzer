Liriope graminifolia var. communis

Liriope graminifolia var. communis, commonly known as the common lilyturf, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green, growing in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale blue flowers in late summer. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist to wet soils.

Liriope graminifolia var. communis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate partial shade.
