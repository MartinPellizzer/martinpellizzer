Lepanthes affinis

Lepanthes affinis, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a compact inflorescence with numerous blooms.

Lepanthes affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
