Linaria fallax var. lamprosperma

Linaria fallax var. lamprosperma, commonly known as the silverleaf toadflax, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white down. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with two lips and five stamens, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential in some regions, it is often cultivated with caution.
