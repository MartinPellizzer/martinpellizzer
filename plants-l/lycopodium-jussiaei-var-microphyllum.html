Lycopodium jussiaei var. microphyllum

Lycopodium jussiaei var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its diminutive, scale-like leaves that form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, typical of its ancient lineage, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
