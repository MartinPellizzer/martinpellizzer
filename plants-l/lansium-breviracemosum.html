Lansium breviracemosum

Lansium breviracemosum, commonly known as the small-branch lansium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to longan and litchi, with a smooth, brown skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant is also valued for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its ability to grow in various soil types.
