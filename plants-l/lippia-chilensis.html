Lippia chilensis

Lippia chilensis, commonly known as Chilean verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Lippia chilensis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
