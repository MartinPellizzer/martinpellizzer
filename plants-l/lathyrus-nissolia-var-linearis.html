Lathyrus nissolia var. linearis

Lathyrus nissolia var. linearis, commonly known as the slender grass vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a climbing or trailing growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
