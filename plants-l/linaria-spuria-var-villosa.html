Linaria spuria var. villosa

Linaria spuria var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, which gives the plant its common name, and they are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
