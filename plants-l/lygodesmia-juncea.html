Lygodesmia juncea

Lygodesmia juncea, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb or trail along the ground. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds upon maturity.

Lygodesmia juncea is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
