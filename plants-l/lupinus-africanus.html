Lupinus africanus

Lupinus africanus, commonly known as the African lupin, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Lupinus africanus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
