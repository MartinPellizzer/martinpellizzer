Leucas calostachys var. longibracteolata

Leucas calostachys var. longibracteolata, commonly known as the long-bract leucas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
