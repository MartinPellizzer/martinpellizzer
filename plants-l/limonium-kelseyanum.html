Limonium kelseyanum

Limonium kelseyanum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium kelseyanum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
