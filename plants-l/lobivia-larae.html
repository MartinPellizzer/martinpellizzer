Lobivia larae

Lobivia larae, commonly known as the Lara's lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Lobivia larae is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.
