Leontodon dubius

Leontodon dubius, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. Native to Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often found in association with other alpine or subalpine flora.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of its native ecosystems.
