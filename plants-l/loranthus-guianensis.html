Loranthus guianensis

Loranthus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the tropical regions of South America.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often visited by insects for pollination. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by influencing host tree health and providing food for various wildlife species.
