Lithospermum denticulatum

Lithospermum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed lithosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
