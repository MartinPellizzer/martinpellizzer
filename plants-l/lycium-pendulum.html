Lycium pendulum

Lycium pendulum, commonly known as the trailing lycium or silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that often hang over water features, producing small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, though they are often bitter unless ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in riparian zones.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
