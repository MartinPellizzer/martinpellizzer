Leptospermum polygalifolium var. minutifolium

Leptospermum polygalifolium var. minutifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to pale grey hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in spring. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and is often found in heathland and open forest habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Indigenous practices.
