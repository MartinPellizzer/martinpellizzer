Linaria griffithii

Linaria griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, a feature common in the genus Linaria, and it blooms during the summer months.

Linaria griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique floral display.
