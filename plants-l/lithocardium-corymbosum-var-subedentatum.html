Lithocardium corymbosum var. subedentatum

Lithocardium corymbosum var. subedentatum, commonly known as the serrated rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and have a serrated margin, which is characteristic of the varietal name *subedentatum*. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments such as rocky outcrops and gravelly plains.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
