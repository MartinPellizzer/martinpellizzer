Lobelia nuda

Lobelia nuda, commonly known as the naked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, blue to violet flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Lobelia nuda thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
