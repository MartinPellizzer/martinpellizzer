Leptochloa mutica

Leptochloa mutica, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a soft, silky texture that gives it its common name.

Leptochloa mutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
