Libanotis montana

Libanotis montana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Libanotis montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
