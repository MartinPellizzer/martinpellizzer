Lycopodioides chrysorrhizos

Lycopodioides chrysorrhizos, commonly known as the golden root lycopod, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves.

It is a terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive root system that is often yellowish in color, which contributes to its common name. Its sporophyte stage produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

Lycopodioides chrysorrhizos plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
