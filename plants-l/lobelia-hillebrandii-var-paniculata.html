Lobelia hillebrandii var. paniculata

Lobelia hillebrandii var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
