Lasianthus stercorarius

Lasianthus stercorarius, commonly known as the dung lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes two pairs of showy bracts that resemble petals.
