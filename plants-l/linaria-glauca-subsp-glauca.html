Linaria glauca subsp. glauca

Linaria glauca subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue toadflax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-gray hue.

The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered invasive in some areas.
