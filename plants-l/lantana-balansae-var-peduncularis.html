Lantana balansae var. peduncularis

Lantana balansae var. peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered lantana, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically produces opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
