Leptandra purpurea

Leptandra purpurea, commonly known as purple sandroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, showy purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture.

Leptandra purpurea is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
