Lonicera caerulea var. angustifolia

Lonicera caerulea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and hedgerows.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruits.
