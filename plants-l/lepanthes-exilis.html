Lepanthes exilis

Lepanthes exilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces showy, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It is part of the Epidendroideae subfamily and is known for its ornamental value in cultivation.

Lepanthes exilis is often found in cloud forests and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
