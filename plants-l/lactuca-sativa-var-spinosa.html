Lactuca sativa var. spinosa

Lactuca sativa var. spinosa, commonly known as curly endive, is a cultivated variety of lettuce characterized by its tightly curled, leafy structure.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is typically grown for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter and crisp texture. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often used in salads and can be harvested before the leaves become too mature.

Its unique appearance and flavor make it a popular choice in gourmet and traditional cuisines.
