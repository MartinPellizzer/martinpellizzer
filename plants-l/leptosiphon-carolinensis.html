Leptosiphon carolinensis

Leptosiphon carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
