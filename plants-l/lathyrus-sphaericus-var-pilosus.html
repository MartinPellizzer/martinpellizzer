Lathyrus sphaericus var. pilosus

Lathyrus sphaericus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy globular vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, pod-like fruits that resemble small, inflated balloons. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, and it produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
