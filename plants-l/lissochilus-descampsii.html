Lissochilus descampsii

Lissochilus descampsii, commonly known as the Descamps' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences and small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or forested areas with high humidity. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited to its native climate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
