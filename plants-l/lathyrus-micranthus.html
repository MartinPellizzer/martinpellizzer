Lathyrus micranthus

Lathyrus micranthus, commonly known as the dwarf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus micranthus is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, thriving in partial shade and well-drained conditions.
