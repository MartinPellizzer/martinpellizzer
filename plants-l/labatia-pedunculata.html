Labatia pedunculata

Labatia pedunculata, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Labatia pedunculata is also noted for its short blooming period, typically lasting only a few weeks in the spring.
