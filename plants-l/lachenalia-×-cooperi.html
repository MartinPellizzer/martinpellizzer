Lachenalia × cooperi

Lachenalia × cooperi, commonly known as the cooper's lachenalia, is a hybrid succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, usually in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Lachenalia × cooperi is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
