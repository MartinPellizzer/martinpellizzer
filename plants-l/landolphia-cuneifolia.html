Landolphia cuneifolia

Landolphia cuneifolia, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
