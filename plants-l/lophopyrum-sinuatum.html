Lophopyrum sinuatum

Lophopyrum sinuatum, commonly known as the sinuous wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Lophopyrum sinuatum is often found in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It is occasionally cultivated for forage or as a ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
