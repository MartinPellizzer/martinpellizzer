Lysimachia kukuiensis

Lysimachia kukuiensis, commonly known as Kukui leaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional Hawaiian medicine for their purported healing properties.

Lysimachia kukuiensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
