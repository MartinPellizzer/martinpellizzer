Leptochloa laxa

Leptochloa laxa, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Leptochloa laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in certain ecosystems.
