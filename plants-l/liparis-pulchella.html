Liparis pulchella

Liparis pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Liparis pulchella is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
