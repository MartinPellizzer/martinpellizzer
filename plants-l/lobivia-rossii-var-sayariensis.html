Lobivia rossii var. sayariensis

Lobivia rossii var. sayariensis, commonly known as the Sayariensis Lobivia, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a sought-after species among succulent enthusiasts.
