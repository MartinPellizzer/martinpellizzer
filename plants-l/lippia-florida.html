Lippia florida

Lippia florida, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open areas such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Lippia florida is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
