Lonicera mochidzukiana var. nomurana

Lonicera mochidzukiana var. nomurana, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
