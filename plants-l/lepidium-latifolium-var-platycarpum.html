Lepidium latifolium var. platycarpum

Lepidium latifolium var. platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-leaved cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in racemes. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its fruits are small, dry pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in ecological systems.
