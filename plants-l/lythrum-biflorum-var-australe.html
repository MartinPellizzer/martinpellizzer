Lythrum biflorum var. australe

Lythrum biflorum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian rosemary or swamp lythrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, clay soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of pale pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Lythrum biflorum var. australe is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
