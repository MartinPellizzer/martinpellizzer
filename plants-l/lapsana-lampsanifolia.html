Lapsana lampsanifolia

Lapsana lampsanifolia, commonly known as common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
