Lampranthus calcaratus

Lampranthus calcaratus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features upright, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, giving it its common name. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
