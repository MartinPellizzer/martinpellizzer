Lythrum altissimum

Lythrum altissimum, commonly known as tall loosestrife, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Tall loosestrife is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
