Lupinus cytisoides

Lupinus cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces tall spikes of small, yellow to orange flowers. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Lupinus cytisoides is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil in its native environment.
