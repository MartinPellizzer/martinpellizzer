Lysiana murrayi

Lysiana murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring and summer seasons.

Lysiana murrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
