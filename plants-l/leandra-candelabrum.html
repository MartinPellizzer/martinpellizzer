Leandra candelabrum

Leandra candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, candle-like inflorescences composed of multiple hibiscus flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
