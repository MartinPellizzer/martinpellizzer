Lycium chinense var. potaninii

Lycium chinense var. potaninii, commonly known as Chinese wolfberry or Goji berry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems, small yellow flowers, and red berries that turn orange when mature. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.
