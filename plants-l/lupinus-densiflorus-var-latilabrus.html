Lupinus densiflorus var. latilabrus

Lupinus densiflorus var. latilabrus, commonly known as the broad-lipped lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
