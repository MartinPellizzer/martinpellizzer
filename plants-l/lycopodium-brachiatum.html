Lycopodium brachiatum

Lycopodium brachiatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-bearing structures called strobili.

It is a primitive vascular plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are typically clustered at the tips of its branches.

Lycopodium brachiatum has a long evolutionary history, dating back to the Paleozoic era, and is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.
