Loranthus arnottianus

Loranthus arnottianus, commonly known as the Australian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own food through photosynthesis using the limited light it receives. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that attract insects for pollination. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting various wildlife species.
