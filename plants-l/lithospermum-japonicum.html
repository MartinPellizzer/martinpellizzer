Lithospermum japonicum

Lithospermum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese rock pepper, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, red, berry-like fruits. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Lithospermum japonicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
