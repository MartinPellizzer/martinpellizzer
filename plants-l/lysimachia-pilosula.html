Lysimachia pilosula

Lysimachia pilosula, commonly known as hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, creeping stems and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia pilosula is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and ability to form dense ground cover.
