Lonicera henryi var. trichosepala

Lonicera henryi var. trichosepala, commonly known as the trichosepala honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
