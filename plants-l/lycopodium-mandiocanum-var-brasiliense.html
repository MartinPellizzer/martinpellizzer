Lycopodium mandiocanum var. brasiliense

Lycopodium mandiocanum var. brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its spore-bearing cones and scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The species is notable for its unique reproductive structures and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
