Lycianthes asarifolia

Lycianthes asarifolia, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other economically important plants like tomatoes and peppers.

Lycianthes asarifolia is often found in dry to moist forests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
