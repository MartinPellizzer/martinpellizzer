Lasiandra heteramalla

Lasiandra heteramalla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock-heath, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Lasiandra heteramalla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
