Lepanthes melanocaulon

Lepanthes melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, often velvety stems and relatively small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with dark pigments. This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing epiphytically on trees.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphological features.
