Lobivia jajoana subsp. nidularis

Lobivia jajoana subsp.

nidularis, commonly known as the "Nidularis Lobivia," is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
