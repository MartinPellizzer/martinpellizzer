Linkia myrtillodes

Linkia myrtillodes, commonly known as the Australian myrtle.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia, characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small white flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species like eucalyptus and tea tree. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and heathland environments.
