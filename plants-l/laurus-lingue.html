Laurus lingue

Laurus lingue, commonly known as sweet bay, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its evergreen, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Laurus lingue is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in culinary and medicinal applications.
