Lonicera macrantha var. guillonii

Lonicera macrantha var. guillonii, commonly known as the Guillonii honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that mature in the fall, attracting various bird species.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
