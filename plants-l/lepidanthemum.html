Lepidanthemum

Lepidanthemum, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that often have a silvery or metallic appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Lepidanthemum species typically grow as low-growing shrubs or herbs, often forming dense mats.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions.
