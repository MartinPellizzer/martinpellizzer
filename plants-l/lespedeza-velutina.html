Lespedeza velutina

Lespedeza velutina, commonly known as silky lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Lespedeza velutina is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
