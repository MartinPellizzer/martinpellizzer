Litsea undulatifolia

Litsea undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaved litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that have a distinct wavy margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves and fruits of Litsea undulatifolia are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
