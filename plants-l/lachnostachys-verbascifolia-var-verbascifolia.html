Lachnostachys verbascifolia var. verbascifolia

Lachnostachys verbascifolia var. verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
