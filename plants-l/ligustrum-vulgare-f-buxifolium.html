Ligustrum vulgare f. buxifolium

Ligustrum vulgare f. buxifolium, commonly known as the boxleaf privet, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, foliage.

Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with a waxy texture and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.

This cultivar is often used in hedges and as a foundation plant in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
