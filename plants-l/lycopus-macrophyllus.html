Lycopus macrophyllus

Lycopus macrophyllus, commonly known as broadleaf blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Lycopus macrophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
