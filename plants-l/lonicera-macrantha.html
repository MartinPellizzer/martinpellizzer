Lonicera macrantha

Lonicera macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife.

Lonicera macrantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
