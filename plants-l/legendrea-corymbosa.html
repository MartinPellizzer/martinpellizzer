Legendrea corymbosa

Legendrea corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet pepper or capsicum, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful fruits that range from yellow to red. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into the familiar bell-shaped or elongated peppers. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is not only a popular culinary ingredient but also contains capsaicin, which gives some varieties their spicy flavor.
