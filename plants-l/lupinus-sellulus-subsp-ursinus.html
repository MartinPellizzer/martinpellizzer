Lupinus sellulus subsp. ursinus

Lupinus sellulus subsp.

ursinus, commonly known as the California lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Lupinus sellulus subsp.

ursinus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
