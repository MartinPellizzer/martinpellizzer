Leucophyllum frutescens f. albineum

Leucophyllum frutescens f. albineum, commonly known as the alba rose verbena, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer, adding a delicate floral display to gardens. The plant has silver-gray foliage that reflects light and reduces water loss, making it well-suited for arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water once established.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
