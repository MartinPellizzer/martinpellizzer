Lobivia tegeleriana var. incuiensis

Lobivia tegeleriana var. incuiensis, commonly known as the Incuiensis Lobivia, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, globular stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently in its native habitat. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the growing season, adding a splash of color to its otherwise desolate environment. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a characteristic adaptation to minimize water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
