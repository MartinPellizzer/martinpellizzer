Limonium sartorii

Limonium sartorii, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Plumbagaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, blue to purple flowers. Limonium sartorii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy shores.
