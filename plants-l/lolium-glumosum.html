Lolium glumosum

Lolium glumosum, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into pale green to brown seeds.

Lolium glumosum is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in various traditional and modern applications, including as a forage crop and in herbal remedies.
