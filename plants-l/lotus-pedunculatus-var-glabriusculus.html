Lotus pedunculatus var. glabriusculus

Lotus pedunculatus var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the glabrous lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, exhibiting a robust root system adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are pink to red, with a distinctive, showy appearance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
