Lucuma heyderi

Lucuma heyderi, commonly known as the Andean golden apple, is a tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
