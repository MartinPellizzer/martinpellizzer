Leucas menthifolia var. cephalantha

Leucas menthifolia var. cephalantha, commonly known as the Cape mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are often aromatic and have a mint-like scent. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
