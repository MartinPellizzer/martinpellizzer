Lecythis grandiflora

Lecythis grandiflora, commonly known as the giant lecythium, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The tree produces heavy, woody fruits that contain a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. Its bark and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
