Lasianthus platyphyllus

Lasianthus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
