Liatris provincialis

Liatris provincialis, commonly known as the provincial blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, spike-like clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Liatris provincialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
