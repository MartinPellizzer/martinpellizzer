Leptopogon carinatus subvar. subtilior

Leptopogon carinatus subvar. subtilior, commonly known as the slender-ribbed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flowering heads. This subvariety typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
