Lepanthes gelata

Lepanthes gelata, commonly known as the gelatinous orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its translucent, gelatinous flowers that are often mistaken for jelly or resin. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small, delicate, and usually white or pale green in color.

Lepanthes gelata is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
