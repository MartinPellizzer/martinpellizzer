Lechea ternifolia

Lechea ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap. Its leaves are often lobed and have a distinctive three-leaf structure, giving it its scientific name.
