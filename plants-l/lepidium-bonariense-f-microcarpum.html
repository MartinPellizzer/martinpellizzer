Lepidium bonariense f. microcarpum

Lepidium bonariense f. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf celtuce or microcarp celtuce, is a cultivated form of the wild celtuce plant.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its leafy stems and small, edible leaves. The plant is typically grown for its tender, mild-flavored leaves, which are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its compact growth habit and enhanced flavor compared to the wild type.
