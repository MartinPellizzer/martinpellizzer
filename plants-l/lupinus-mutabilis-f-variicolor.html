Lupinus mutabilis f. variicolor

Lupinus mutabilis f. variicolor, commonly known as the multicolored lupine, is a variant of the Andean lupine species native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly variable flower colors, which can range from white to pink, red, and even purple, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has a bushy growth habit with compound leaves and tall flowering stems. Its flowers are borne in dense spikes and are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant and changing color display.
