Luzama amaluzae

Luzama amaluzae, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit with smooth, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Luzama amaluzae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
