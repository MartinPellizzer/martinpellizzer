Lippia vinosa

Lippia vinosa, commonly known as purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Lippia vinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
