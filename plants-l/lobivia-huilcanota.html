Lobivia huilcanota

Lobivia huilcanota, commonly known as the Huilcanota lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains of Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
