Lilium rubrum var. pomponium

Lilium rubrum var. pomponium, commonly known as the red lily, is a variant of the red lily species characterized by its vibrant red flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that typically appear in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and adaptability to various growing conditions.
