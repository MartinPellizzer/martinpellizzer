Lupinus altiplani

Lupinus altiplani, commonly known as the Andean lupine, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the altiplano.

Lupinus altiplani plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
