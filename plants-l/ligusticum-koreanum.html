Ligusticum koreanum

Ligusticum koreanum, commonly known as Korean angelica, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-shaped clusters, which bloom in the summer. Its rhizomes and roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ligusticum koreanum is also valued in culinary applications for its distinctive flavor.
