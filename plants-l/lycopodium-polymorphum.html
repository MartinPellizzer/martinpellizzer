Lycopodium polymorphum

Lycopodium polymorphum, commonly known as the variable clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves.

It is a perennial evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant produces sporophylls that bear spores, which are dispersed by wind, facilitating its reproduction. Lycopodium polymorphum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.

Its morphology and reproductive structures make it an important component of many forest ecosystems.
