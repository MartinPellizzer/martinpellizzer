Lycopersicon dulcamara

Lycopersicon dulcamara, commonly known as the nightshade or wild tomato, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, often yellow or red berries. The plant contains alkaloids and other toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Despite its toxicity, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
