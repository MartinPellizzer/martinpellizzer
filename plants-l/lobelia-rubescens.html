Lobelia rubescens

Lobelia rubescens, commonly known as redstem lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Lobelia rubescens is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
