Loranthus platyphyllus

Loranthus platyphyllus, commonly known as the flat-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its modified leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting a variety of wildlife.
