Lobivia saltensis var. nealeana

Lobivia saltensis var. nealeana, commonly known as the Neale's Lobivia, is a rare and attractive succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It features compact, rounded stems with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions, requiring minimal water and thriving in full sun.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts in controlled environments.
