Lupinus havardii

Lupinus havardii, commonly known as the Havard's lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Lupinus havardii plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
