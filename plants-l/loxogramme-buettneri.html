Loxogramme buettneri

Loxogramme buettneri, commonly known as the Buetner's water clover, is a unique aquatic fern native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are divided into small, lobed segments, giving it a clover-like appearance. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize large areas of water.

Loxogramme buettneri plays an important role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
