Lycianthes tarapotensis

Lycianthes tarapotensis, commonly known as the Andean eggplant, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
