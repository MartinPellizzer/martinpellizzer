Linaria repens var. monspessulana

Linaria repens var. monspessulana, commonly known as the creeping toadflax, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers with two prominent lobes, typical of the Linaria genus. It thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics include small, lance-shaped leaves and a long flowering period from spring to early autumn.
