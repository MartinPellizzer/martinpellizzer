Lagerstroemia archeriana subsp. divaricatiflora

Lagerstroemia archeriana subsp.

divaricatiflora, commonly known as the pink lagerstroemia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological studies.
