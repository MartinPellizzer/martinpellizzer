Lobelia tysonii

Lobelia tysonii, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia tysonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
