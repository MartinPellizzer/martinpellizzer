Lepanthes polygonoides

Lepanthes polygonoides, commonly known as the Polygonoid Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often hairy growth habit and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Lepanthes polygonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for shade gardens.
