Leucopogon malayanus

Leucopogon malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan heather, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially grown.
