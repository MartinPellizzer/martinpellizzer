Lactuca canadensis var. latifolia

Lactuca canadensis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf lettuce, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and slightly hairy. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers in a loose cluster, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and is often found in the eastern and central United States.
