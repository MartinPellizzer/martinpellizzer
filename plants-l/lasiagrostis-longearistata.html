Lasiagrostis longearistata

Lasiagrostis longearistata, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, and often silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves, and the inflorescence is a dense, spike-like structure.

Lasiagrostis longearistata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid environments.
