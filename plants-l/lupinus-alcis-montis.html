Lupinus alcis-montis

Lupinus alcis-montis, commonly known as the mountain lupine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Lupinus alcis-montis plays an important role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
