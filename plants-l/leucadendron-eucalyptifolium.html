Leucadendron eucalyptifolium

Leucadendron eucalyptifolium, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its striking cone-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to the fynbos biome, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. It produces small, white flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
