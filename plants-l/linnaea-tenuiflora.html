Linnaea tenuiflora

Linnaea tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender twinflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces delicate, white, fragrant flowers in pairs, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Linnaea tenuiflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
