Lycopodium gymnogynum var. domingense

Lycopodium gymnogynum var. domingense, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. It produces spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

This variety is native to Central America and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
