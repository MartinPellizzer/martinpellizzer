Lilium autumnale

Lilium autumnale, commonly known as the autumn lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, with a fibrous root system.

The autumn lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking fall blooms.
