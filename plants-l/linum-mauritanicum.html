Linum mauritanicum

Linum mauritanicum, commonly known as the Algerian flax or blue flax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue or white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Linum mauritanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
