Lomatium junceum

Lomatium junceum, commonly known as slender lomatium, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Lomatium junceum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
