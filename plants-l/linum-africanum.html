Linum africanum

Linum africanum, commonly known as African flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of blue or white flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Linum africanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
