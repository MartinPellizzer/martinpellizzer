Lonicera japonica var. miyagusukiana

Lonicera japonica var. miyagusukiana, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a vigorous, twining vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, tubular yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its fast growth rate and ability to climb or trail along supports. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions due to its aggressive growth habits.
