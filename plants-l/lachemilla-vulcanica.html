Lachemilla vulcanica

Lachemilla vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic moss, is a low-growing plant native to volcanic regions.

It thrives in nutrient-rich, acidic soils found in areas with volcanic activity. The plant has dense, mat-like foliage that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or brown, adapted to its harsh environment.

Lachemilla vulcanica plays an important ecological role in volcanic ecosystems by contributing to soil formation and providing habitat for small organisms.
