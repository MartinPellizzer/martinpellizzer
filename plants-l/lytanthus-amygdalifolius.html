Lytanthus amygdalifolius

Lytanthus amygdalifolius, commonly known as the almond-leaved lytanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae, which includes plants such as the poinsettia and the common lythrum. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that resemble those of almond trees, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or white, with four petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
