Leucanthemum corsicum f. dentatum

Leucanthemum corsicum f. dentatum, commonly known as the serrated marsh fleur-de-lis, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal rosettes of deeply lobed, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
