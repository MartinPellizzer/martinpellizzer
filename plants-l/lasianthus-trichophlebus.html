Lasianthus trichophlebus

Lasianthus trichophlebus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
