Lathyrus cyaneus var. petiolatus

Lathyrus cyaneus var. petiolatus, commonly known as the blue flax or petiolate blue flax, is a perennial legume native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
