Lupinus glabrior

Lupinus glabrior, commonly known as the smooth lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Lupinus glabrior has small, stipitate leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is a nitrogen-fixing plant, contributing to soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobium bacteria.
