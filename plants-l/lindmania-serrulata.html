Lindmania serrulata

Lindmania serrulata, commonly known as the serrated lindmania, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving it its common name.

Lindmania serrulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
