Lobelia cacuminis

Lobelia cacuminis, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture.

Lobelia cacuminis is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.
