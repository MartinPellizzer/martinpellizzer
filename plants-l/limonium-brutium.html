Limonium brutium

Limonium brutium, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt.

Limonium brutium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
