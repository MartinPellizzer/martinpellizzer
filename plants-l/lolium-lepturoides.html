Lolium lepturoides

Lolium lepturoides, commonly known as slender ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Lolium lepturoides is valued for its forage quality and is sometimes used in agricultural settings.
