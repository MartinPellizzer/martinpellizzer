Lindera doniana

Lindera doniana, commonly known as the Chinese sweet woodruff, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Lindera doniana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
