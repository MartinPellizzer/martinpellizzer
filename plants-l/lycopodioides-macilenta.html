Lycopodioides macilenta

Lycopodioides macilenta, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that grow in dense, compact clusters. This species typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporophyte stage produces small, cone-like structures that contain spores for reproduction.

Lycopodioides macilenta is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
