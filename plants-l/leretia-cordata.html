Leretia cordata

Leretia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Leretia cordata is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
