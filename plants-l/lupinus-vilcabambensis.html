Lupinus vilcabambensis

Lupinus vilcabambensis, commonly known as the Vilcabamba lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lupinus vilcabambensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
