Lobelia canbyi

Lobelia canbyi, commonly known as Canby's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with serrated edges, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Lobelia canbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
