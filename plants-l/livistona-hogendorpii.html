Livistona hogendorpii

Livistona hogendorpii, commonly known as the Bismarck palm, is a striking palm species native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched clusters, which are followed by elliptical, dark blue-black fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
