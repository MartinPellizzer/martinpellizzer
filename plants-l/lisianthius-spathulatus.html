Lisianthius spathulatus

Lisianthius spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and longevity.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower in many regions.
