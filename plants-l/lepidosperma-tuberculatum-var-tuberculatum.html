Lepidosperma tuberculatum var. tuberculatum

Lepidosperma tuberculatum var. tuberculatum, commonly known as the tuberculate sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale brown flowers.

This variety is notable for its distinctive tuberculate (knobbed) nodes along the stem, which are a key identifying feature.
