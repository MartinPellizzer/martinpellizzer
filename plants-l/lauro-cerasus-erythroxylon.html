Lauro-cerasus erythroxylon

Lauro-cerasus erythroxylon, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features include a woody structure, compound leaves, and a distinctive scent when crushed.
