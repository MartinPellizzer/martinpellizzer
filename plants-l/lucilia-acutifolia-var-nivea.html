Lucilia acutifolia var. nivea

Lucilia acutifolia var. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered acutifolia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white pubescence. Its showy white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
