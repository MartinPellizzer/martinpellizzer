Lonchocarpus latimarginatus

Lonchocarpus latimarginatus, commonly known as the broad-margin senna, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange aril. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
