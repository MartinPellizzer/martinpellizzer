Lycium persicum var. cinereum

Lycium persicum var. cinereum, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid landscapes.

The silverberry is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
