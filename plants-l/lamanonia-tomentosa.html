Lamanonia tomentosa

Lamanonia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly lamanonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
