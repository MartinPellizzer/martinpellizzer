Lepanthes vulpina

Lepanthes vulpina, commonly known as the fox orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, often hairy stem and a single, showy flower that typically features a dark purple or reddish hue. The plant produces a small, greenish or yellowish ovary that matures into a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds. Lepanthes vulpina thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
