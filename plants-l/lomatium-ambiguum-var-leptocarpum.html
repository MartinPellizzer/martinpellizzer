Lomatium ambiguum var. leptocarpum

Lomatium ambiguum var. leptocarpum, commonly known as the slender-fruit lomatium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are aromatic and have a distinctive fern-like appearance, while the flowers are small and white or pale green.

The variety is distinguished by its slender, elongated fruits, which are a key identifying feature.
