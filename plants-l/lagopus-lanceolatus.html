Lagopus lanceolatus

Lagopus lanceolatus, commonly known as the slender snowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Lagopus lanceolatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for herbivores in its harsh environment.
