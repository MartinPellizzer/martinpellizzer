Laelia anceps var. sanderiana

Laelia anceps var. sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's laelia, is a hybrid orchid native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms during the summer months and prefers cool, bright conditions with high humidity. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its native mountainous environment.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant colors and hardy nature.
