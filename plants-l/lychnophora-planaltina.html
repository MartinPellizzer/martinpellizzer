Lychnophora planaltina

Lychnophora planaltina, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thick, aromatic bark. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters, making it a significant component of the forest canopy. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters.

Lychnophora planaltina is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
