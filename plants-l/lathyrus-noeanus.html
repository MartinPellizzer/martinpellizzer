Lathyrus noeanus

Lathyrus noeanus, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Lathyrus noeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
