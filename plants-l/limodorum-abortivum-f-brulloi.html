Limodorum abortivum f. brulloi

Limodorum abortivum f. brulloi, commonly known as the Brullo's lily of the valley, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant of the lily of the valley species.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
