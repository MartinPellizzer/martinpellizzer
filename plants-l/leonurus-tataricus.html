Leonurus tataricus

Leonurus tataricus, commonly known as tatarian hedge-hog, is a perennial herb native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
