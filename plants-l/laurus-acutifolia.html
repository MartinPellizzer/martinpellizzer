Laurus acutifolia

Laurus acutifolia, commonly known as Mediterranean laurel or winter laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardening.

Due to its aromatic foliage, it is also sometimes used in herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
