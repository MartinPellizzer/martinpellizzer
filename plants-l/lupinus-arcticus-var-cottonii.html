Lupinus arcticus var. cottonii

Lupinus arcticus var. cottonii, commonly known as Arctic lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for herbivores.

Lupinus arcticus var. cottonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens with cool climate conditions.
