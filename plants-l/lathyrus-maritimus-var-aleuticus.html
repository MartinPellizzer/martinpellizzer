Lathyrus maritimus var. aleuticus

Lathyrus maritimus var. aleuticus, commonly known as the Aleutian vetch, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the North Pacific.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.

This variety is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds typical of its coastal habitat.
