Leptocereus albellus

Leptocereus albellus, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spiny plant that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features a ribbed stem with prominent spines and produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
