Lasianthus fordii var. trichocladus

Lasianthus fordii var. trichocladus, commonly known as the Chinese flameflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft texture.
