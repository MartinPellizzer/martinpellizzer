Limonium fraternum

Limonium fraternum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in coastal environments. Limonium fraternum thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shorelines.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salty air.
