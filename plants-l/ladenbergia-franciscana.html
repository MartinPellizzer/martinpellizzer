Ladenbergia franciscana

Ladenbergia franciscana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
