Laurus oblongo-obovata

Laurus oblongo-obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated-ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
