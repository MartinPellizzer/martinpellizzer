Lepidium ruderale var. aethiopicum

Lepidium ruderale var. aethiopicum, commonly known as Ethiopian cress, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white or yellow flowers and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a leafy green vegetable in some regions.
