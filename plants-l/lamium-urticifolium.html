Lamium urticifolium

Lamium urticifolium, commonly known as greater cow-wheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale pink to purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Lamium urticifolium is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
