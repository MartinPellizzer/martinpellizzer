Liparis quadribullata

Liparis quadribullata, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a unique orchid species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers with four distinct lobes, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on trees in humid environments.

Liparis quadribullata is valued in horticulture for its beauty and resilience in cultivated settings.
