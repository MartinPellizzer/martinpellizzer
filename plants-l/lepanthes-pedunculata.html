Lepanthes pedunculata

Lepanthes pedunculata, commonly known as the pendant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pendulous stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Lepanthes pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
