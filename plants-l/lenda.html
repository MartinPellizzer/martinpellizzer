Lenda

Lenda, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, while its flowers are symbolically significant in various cultures.
