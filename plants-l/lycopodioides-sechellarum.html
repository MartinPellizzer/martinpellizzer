Lycopodioides sechellarum

Lycopodioides sechellarum, commonly known as the Seychelles clubmoss, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. The plant produces spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its wiry, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Lycopodioides sechellarum is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native Seychelles.
