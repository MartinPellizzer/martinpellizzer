Leucadendron acutum

Leucadendron acutum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. The plant produces cone-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
