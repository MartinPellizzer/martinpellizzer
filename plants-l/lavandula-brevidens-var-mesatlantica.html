Lavandula brevidens var. mesatlantica

Lavandula brevidens var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lavender, is a compact shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage and produces spikes of purple flowers, typical of the lavender family. The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Its essential oils are also valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
