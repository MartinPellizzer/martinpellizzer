Lomandra confertifolia subsp. confertifolia

Lomandra confertifolia subsp.

confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved lomandra, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and densely clustered at the base.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellow to orange flowers, which appear in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
