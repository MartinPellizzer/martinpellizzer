Lophocachrys echinophora

Lophocachrys echinophora, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique spiny appearance and drought tolerance make it an adaptable species in its native habitat.
