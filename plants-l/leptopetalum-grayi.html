Leptopetalum grayi

Leptopetalum grayi, commonly known as the gray's leatheryleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
