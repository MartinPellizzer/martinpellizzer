Lonchocarpus lasiotropis

Lonchocarpus lasiotropis, commonly known as the hairy pod senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, hairy pods. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, it is important to note that some species within this genus may contain toxic alkaloids, requiring caution in handling or use.
