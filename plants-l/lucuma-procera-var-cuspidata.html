Lucuma procera var. cuspidata

Lucuma procera var. cuspidata, commonly known as the pointed lucuma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant produces large, edible fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a unique, sweet flavor. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

This variety is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
