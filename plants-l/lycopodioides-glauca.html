Lycopodioides glauca

Lycopodioides glauca, commonly known as the blue clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces spores rather than seeds. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its root system is highly developed, allowing it to anchor firmly in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

Lycopodioides glauca plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
