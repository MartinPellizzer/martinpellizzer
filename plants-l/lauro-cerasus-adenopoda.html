Lauro-cerasus adenopoda

Lauro-cerasus adenopoda, commonly known as the Chinese cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to China and is characterized by its glossy evergreen leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and may pose a risk to native ecosystems.
