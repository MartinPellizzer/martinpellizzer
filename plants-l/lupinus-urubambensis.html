Lupinus urubambensis

Lupinus urubambensis, commonly known as the Urubamba lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Lupinus urubambensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and habitat for native wildlife.
