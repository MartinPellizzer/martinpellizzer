Lavandula pedunculata subsp. maderensis

Lavandula pedunculata subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of pale purple to lilac flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, calcareous soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its fragrance and is often used in ornamental gardening.

This subspecies is closely related to other lavender species but is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral morphology.
