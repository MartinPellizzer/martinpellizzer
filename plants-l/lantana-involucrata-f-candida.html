Lantana involucrata f. candida

Lantana involucrata f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered lantana, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and continuous flowering make it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
