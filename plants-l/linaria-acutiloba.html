Linaria acutiloba

Linaria acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the common toadflax. The plant produces small, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Linaria acutiloba is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
