Levisticum vulgare

Levisticum vulgare, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like carrots and dill. Its leaves are widely used as a culinary herb, while the seeds are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
