Lobelia pubescens var. jacquiniana

Lobelia pubescens var. jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
