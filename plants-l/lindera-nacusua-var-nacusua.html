Lindera nacusua var. nacusua

Lindera nacusua var. nacusua, commonly known as the Florida spicebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in shaded forest understories. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often reddish beneath, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a dark red drupe that ripens in the fall, attracting various bird species.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
