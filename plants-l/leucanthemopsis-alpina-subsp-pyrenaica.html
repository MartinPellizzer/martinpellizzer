Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp. pyrenaica

Leucanthemopsis alpina subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
