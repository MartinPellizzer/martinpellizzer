Lobelia bellidifolia var. hirsuta

Lobelia bellidifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hirsute (hairy) appearance is a distinguishing feature that aids in its identification.
