Lonicera ligustrina var. pileata

Lonicera ligustrina var. pileata, commonly known as the snowberry honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. The plant has dense, bushy growth with leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
