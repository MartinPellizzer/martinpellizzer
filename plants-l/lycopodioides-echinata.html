Lycopodioides echinata

Lycopodioides echinata, commonly known as the spiny clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces small, cone-like structures called strobili that contain spores. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its roots form dense networks that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant's morphology reflects its ancient lineage, showcasing features similar to early terrestrial plants.
