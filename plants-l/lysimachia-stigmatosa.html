Lysimachia stigmatosa

Lysimachia stigmatosa, commonly known as the scarlet bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright red or orange-yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia stigmatosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
