Lavandula latifolia var. erigens

Lavandula latifolia var. erigens, commonly known as English lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, woody stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils are widely used in aromatherapy, cosmetics, and as a natural insect repellent.
