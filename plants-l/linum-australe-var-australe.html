Linum australe var. australe

Linum australe var. australe, commonly known as the Australian flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts.
