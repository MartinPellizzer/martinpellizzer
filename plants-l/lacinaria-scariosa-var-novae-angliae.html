Lacinaria scariosa var. novae-angliae

Lacinaria scariosa var. novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England lacinaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Asteraceae family.
