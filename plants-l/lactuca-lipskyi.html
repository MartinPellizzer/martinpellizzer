Lactuca lipskyi

Lactuca lipskyi, commonly known as the Lepsky's lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. Its small, yellow flowers appear in the summer months and are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Lactuca lipskyi is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
