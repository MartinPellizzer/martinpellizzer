Lathyrus boissieri

Lathyrus boissieri, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to violet, and its trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, this plant thrives in rocky and arid environments. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

Lathyrus boissieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
