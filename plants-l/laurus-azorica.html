Laurus azorica

Laurus azorica, commonly known as the Azorean laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Azores, an archipelago in the North Atlantic Ocean.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and emit a pleasant aromatic scent when crushed. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, dark blue berries.

Laurus azorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
