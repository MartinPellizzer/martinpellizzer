Lathyrus japonicus f. acutifolius

Lathyrus japonicus f. acutifolius, commonly known as the Japanese vetch or narrow-leaved lathyris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in early summer. Its roots form nodules with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in Japan and is cultivated for ornamental purposes.
