Lomelosia macrochaete

Lomelosia macrochaete, commonly known as the hairy lomelosia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its high-altitude environment. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
