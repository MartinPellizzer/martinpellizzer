Leptocionium caespitosum

Leptocionium caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted rock cress, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments.

Leptocionium caespitosum is adapted to cold climates and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
