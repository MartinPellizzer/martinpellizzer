Linum iranicum

Linum iranicum, commonly known as Persian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Linum iranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
