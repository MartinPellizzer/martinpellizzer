Lupinus velillensis

Lupinus velillensis, commonly known as the Velilla lupine, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is well-developed, often forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Lupinus velillensis is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
