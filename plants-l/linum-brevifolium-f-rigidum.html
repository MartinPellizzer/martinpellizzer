Linum brevifolium f. rigidum

Linum brevifolium f. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid flax, is a variant of the flax plant characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found in rocky or coastal environments. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in textile production.
