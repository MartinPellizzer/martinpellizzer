Linociera hassleriana

Linociera hassleriana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps protect it from harsh weather.

Linociera hassleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
