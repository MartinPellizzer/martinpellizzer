Linum usitatissimum var. glabriseptum

Linum usitatissimum var. glabriseptum, commonly known as the smooth-flowered flax, is a variant of the common flax plant.

It is characterized by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the standard variety. The plant produces slender, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production, as well as for its oil-rich seeds.
