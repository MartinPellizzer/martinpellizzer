Lactuca sativa var. angustana

Lactuca sativa var. angustana, commonly known as narrow-leaf lettuce, is a cultivar of lettuce characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is typically grown for its crisp, mild-tasting leaves that are used in salads and fresh dishes. The plant is a cool-season crop, thriving in temperate climates and requiring well-drained soil. It has a rosette growth habit, with leaves arranged closely around the stem.

This variety is valued for its high nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
