Leucocroton virens var. glaber

Leucocroton virens var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaved leucocroton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glabrous leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
