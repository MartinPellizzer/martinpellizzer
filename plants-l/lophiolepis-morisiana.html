Lophiolepis morisiana

Lophiolepis morisiana, commonly known as the Morisiana grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Lophiolepis morisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
