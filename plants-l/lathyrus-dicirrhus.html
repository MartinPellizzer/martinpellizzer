Lathyrus dicirrhus

Lathyrus dicirrhus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. The plant has slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Lathyrus dicirrhus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
