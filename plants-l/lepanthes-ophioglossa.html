Lepanthes ophioglossa

Lepanthes ophioglossa, commonly known as the golden tassel orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble tassels, which are typically arranged in a dense, branching inflorescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes ophioglossa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical cloud forests.
