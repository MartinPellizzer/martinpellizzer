Limonia citrifolia

Limonia citrifolia, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits. The fruits are typically sour and acidic, though they are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and botanical research.
