Liatris aspera var. typica

Liatris aspera var. typica, commonly known as stiff blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features upright, sparsely branched stems covered in rough, stiff hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing showy pink to purple spikes.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
