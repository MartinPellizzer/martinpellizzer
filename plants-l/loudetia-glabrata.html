Loudetia glabrata

Loudetia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth loudetia, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
