Liriodendron figo

Liriodendron figo, commonly known as the tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, tulip-shaped yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The tree has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

The tulip tree is also known for its rapid growth and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
