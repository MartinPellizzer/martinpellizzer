Ligusticum peucedanoides var. longifolium

Ligusticum peucedanoides var. longifolium, commonly known as long-leaved angelica, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, compound leaves with long, narrow lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
