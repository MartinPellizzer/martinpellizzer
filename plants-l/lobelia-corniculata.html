Lobelia corniculata

Lobelia corniculata, commonly known as the horned lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often arranged in spikes.

Lobelia corniculata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
