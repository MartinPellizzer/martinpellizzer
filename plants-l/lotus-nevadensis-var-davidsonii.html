Lotus nevadensis var. davidsonii

Lotus nevadensis var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's lotus, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are typical of the genus Lotus. This variety thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a short growing season and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in ecological and conservation research.
