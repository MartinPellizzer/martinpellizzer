Lupinus huigrensis

Lupinus huigrensis, commonly known as the Huigren's lupine, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, sparsely branched inflorescences with pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
