Leuzea exaltata

Leuzea exaltata, commonly known as tall leuzea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often covered with soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Leuzea exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
