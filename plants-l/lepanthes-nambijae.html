Lepanthes nambijae

Lepanthes nambijae, commonly known as the Nambija orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Lepanthes nambijae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
