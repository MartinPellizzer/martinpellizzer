Lycopersicon cheesmaniae f. minor

Lycopersicon cheesmaniae f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf tomato, is a botanical variant of the tomato species characterized by its smaller size and compact growth habit.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to Central America, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant produces small, round tomatoes that are typically red when ripe, though some cultivars may exhibit yellow or orange hues. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for both decorative and culinary purposes.

This variety is also noted for its resistance to certain diseases, making it a popular choice among home gardeners.
