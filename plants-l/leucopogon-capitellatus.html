Leucopogon capitellatus

Leucopogon capitellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and forest margins.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
