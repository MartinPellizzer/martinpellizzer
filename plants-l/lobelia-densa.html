Lobelia densa

Lobelia densa, commonly known as the dense lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia densa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
