Lippia alnifolia

Lippia alnifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It belongs to the verbena family (Verbenaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.

Lippia alnifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
