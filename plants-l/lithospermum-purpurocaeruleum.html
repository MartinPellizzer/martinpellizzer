Lithospermum purpurocaeruleum

Lithospermum purpurocaeruleum, commonly known as blue rock pepper, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, reddish-brown, capsule-like fruits. Its roots contain compounds such as lithospermic acid, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
