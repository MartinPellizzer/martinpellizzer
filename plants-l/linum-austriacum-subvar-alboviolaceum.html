Linum austriacum subvar. alboviolaceum

Linum austriacum subvar. alboviolaceum, commonly known as the alboviolaceum flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flax known for their showy blue or purple flowers. This subspecies is characterized by its pale violet to white flowers, which contrast with its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
