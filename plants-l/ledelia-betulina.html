Ledelia betulina

Ledelia betulina, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The silverleaf tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in gardens.
