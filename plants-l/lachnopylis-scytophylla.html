Lachnopylis scytophylla

Lachnopylis scytophylla, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing structures that give it a distinctive, long-lasting appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
