Lobelia genistoides

Lobelia genistoides, commonly known as the golden thread, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Lobelia genistoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
