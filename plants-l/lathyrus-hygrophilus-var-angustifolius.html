Lathyrus hygrophilus var. angustifolius

Lathyrus hygrophilus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
