Linaria lancifolia

Linaria lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Linaria lancifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
