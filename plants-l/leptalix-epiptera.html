Leptalix epiptera

Leptalix epiptera, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Leptalix epiptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
