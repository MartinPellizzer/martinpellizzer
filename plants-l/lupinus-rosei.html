Lupinus rosei

Lupinus rosei, commonly known as the rose lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, rose-purple to pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Lupinus rosei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
