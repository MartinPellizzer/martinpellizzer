Lavandula bipinnata var. rothiana

Lavandula bipinnata var. rothiana, commonly known as the Rothiana lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spike-like clusters of purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils are valued in aromatherapy and perfumery, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
