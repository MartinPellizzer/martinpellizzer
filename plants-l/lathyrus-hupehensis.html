Lathyrus hupehensis

Lathyrus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese blue flax, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and long, slender pods. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lathyrus hupehensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
