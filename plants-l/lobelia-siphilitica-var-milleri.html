Lobelia siphilitica var. milleri

Lobelia siphilitica var. milleri, commonly known as the Great Blue Lobelia, is a striking perennial wildflower native to eastern North America.

It features showy, cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators.
