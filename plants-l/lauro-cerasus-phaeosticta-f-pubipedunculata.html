Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. pubipedunculata

Lauro-cerasus phaeosticta f. pubipedunculata, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a cultivar of Prunus laurocerasus, known for its striking variegated foliage with white and green patterns. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in spring. Its pubescent peduncles, a distinguishing trait of the form, contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
