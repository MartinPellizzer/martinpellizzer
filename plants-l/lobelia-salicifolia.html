Lobelia salicifolia

Lobelia salicifolia, commonly known as willowleaf lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Campanulaceae family and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Lobelia salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
