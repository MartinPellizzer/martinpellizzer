Laurus revoluta

Laurus revoluta, commonly known as the bay laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and have a distinctive, slightly sweet scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are widely used as a spice in cooking and have a long history of use in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
