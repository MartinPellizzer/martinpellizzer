Loranthus jambosae

Loranthus jambosae, commonly known as the Indian mangosteen or mangostan, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Thailand, and Indonesia. The plant attaches itself to the roots of host trees, such as mango and jackfruit, to obtain water and nutrients. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow, and the fruit is a fleshy, segmented berry that ripens to a deep red or purple color.
