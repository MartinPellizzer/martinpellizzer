Lecythis boyacensis

Lecythis boyacensis, commonly known as the Boyacensis Brazil Nut Tree, is a tropical tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and produces a heavy, woody fruit that contains multiple seeds. The tree is valued for its edible seeds, which are similar to Brazil nuts, and its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has significant ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
