Lathyrus occidentalis f. cirrhosus

Lathyrus occidentalis f. cirrhosus, commonly known as the curly-stemmed western pea, is a botanical variant of the western pea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This ferny plant is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
