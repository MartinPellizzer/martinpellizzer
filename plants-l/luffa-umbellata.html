Luffa umbellata

Luffa umbellata, commonly known as the common sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. The fruit, often harvested when mature, contains a spongy interior that becomes fibrous and usable as a natural sponge. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
