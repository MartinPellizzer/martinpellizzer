Limonium ferulaceum

Limonium ferulaceum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in coastal areas and saline soils. It is part of the Plumbagaceae family and is known for its salt tolerance.

Limonium ferulaceum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
