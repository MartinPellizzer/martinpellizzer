Loeselia pumila

Loeselia pumila, commonly known as the small loeselia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Loeselia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
