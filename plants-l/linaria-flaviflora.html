Linaria flaviflora

Linaria flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Linaria flaviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
