Lathyrus linifolius f. pubescens

Lathyrus linifolius f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf pea or hairy vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with the leaves of this form being covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom in early spring. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.

This variant is often cultivated as a cover crop or for forage due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
