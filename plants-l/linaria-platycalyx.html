Linaria platycalyx

Linaria platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive hooded structure, resembling those of other members of the Scrophulariaceae family.

Linaria platycalyx is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
