Liparis kempteriana

Liparis kempteriana, commonly known as the Kempter's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and forest floors. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker.

Liparis kempteriana is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
