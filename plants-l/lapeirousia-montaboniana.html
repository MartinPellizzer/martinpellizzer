Lapeirousia montaboniana

Lapeirousia montaboniana, commonly known as the Montaboniana squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Lapeirousia montaboniana is also valued for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in suitable conditions.
