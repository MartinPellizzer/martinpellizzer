Linum setaceum subsp. nitidum

Linum setaceum subsp.

nitidum, commonly known as the glossy flax or silky flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
