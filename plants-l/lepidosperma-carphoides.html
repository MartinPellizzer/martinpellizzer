Lepidosperma carphoides

Lepidosperma carphoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps, marshes, and waterlogged soils. It has small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems.

Lepidosperma carphoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
