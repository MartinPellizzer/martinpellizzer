Luzula rufescens subsp. plumosa

Luzula rufescens subsp.

plumosa, commonly known as the plumose woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish-brown in color, and its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Luzula rufescens subsp.

plumosa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
