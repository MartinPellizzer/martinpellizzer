Lissochilus paludicola

Lissochilus paludicola, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
