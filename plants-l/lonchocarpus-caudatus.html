Lonchocarpus caudatus

Lonchocarpus caudatus, commonly known as the curly pod legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces distinctive, curling pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
