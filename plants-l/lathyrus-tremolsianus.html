Lathyrus tremolsianus

Lathyrus tremolsianus, commonly known as the Tremolsian grass vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. It is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Lathyrus tremolsianus is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
