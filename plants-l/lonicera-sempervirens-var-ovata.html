Lonicera sempervirens var. ovata

Lonicera sempervirens var. ovata, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a deciduous to semi-evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that develops in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
