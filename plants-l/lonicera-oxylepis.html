Lonicera oxylepis

Lonicera oxylepis, commonly known as the Sierra velvetleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a two-valved capsule that releases small, winged seeds.

Lonicera oxylepis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
