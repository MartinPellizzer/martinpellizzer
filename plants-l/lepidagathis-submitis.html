Lepidagathis submitis

Lepidagathis submitis, commonly known as the small-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
