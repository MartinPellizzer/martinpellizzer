Lysiloma watsonii

Lysiloma watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
