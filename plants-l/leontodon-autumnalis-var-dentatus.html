Leontodon autumnalis var. dentatus

Leontodon autumnalis var. dentatus, commonly known as the autumn hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing composite flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its distinctive toothed leaves and robust growth habit make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
