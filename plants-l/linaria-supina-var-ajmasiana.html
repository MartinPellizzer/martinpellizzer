Linaria supina var. ajmasiana

Linaria supina var. ajmasiana, commonly known as the Ajmasian sandcatcher, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with two prominent lobes, resembling a sand dollar. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
