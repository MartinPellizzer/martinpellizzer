Lycopodium aschersonii

Lycopodium aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and features spirally arranged, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the tips of its stems. Native to arid and rocky habitats, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often found in Mediterranean and semi-arid regions.
