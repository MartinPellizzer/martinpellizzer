Leontodon olivierii

Leontodon olivierii, commonly known as the olive leontodon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish or reddish flower heads composed of numerous small florets. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is characteristic of the region's Mediterranean climate.

Leontodon olivierii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
