Lycopodium apiculatum

Lycopodium apiculatum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, forming low, carpet-like mats on the forest floor. Its leaves are narrow and spirally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodium apiculatum plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
