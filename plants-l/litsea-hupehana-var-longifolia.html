Litsea hupehana var. longifolia

Litsea hupehana var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to China, particularly in the provinces of Hubei and Sichuan, where it thrives in moist, temperate forests. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
