Lysimachia humifusa

Lysimachia humifusa, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Lysimachia humifusa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread and cover ground effectively.
