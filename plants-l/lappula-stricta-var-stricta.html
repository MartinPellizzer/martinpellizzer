Lappula stricta var. stricta

Lappula stricta var. stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf lappula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate and growing conditions.
