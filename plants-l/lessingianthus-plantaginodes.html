Lessingianthus plantaginodes

Lessingianthus plantaginodes, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk, blooming in late summer.

It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
