Leontodon taraxacum var. collinus

Leontodon taraxacum var. collinus, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a single flower head. The plant produces a distinctive, yellow, composite flower that develops into a fluffy, white seed head. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
