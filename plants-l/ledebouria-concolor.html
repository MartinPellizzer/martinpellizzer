Ledebouria concolor

Ledebouria concolor, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces a cluster of fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
