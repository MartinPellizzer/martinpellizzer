Lepigonum brevifolium var. laxiflorum

Lepigonum brevifolium var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous lepigonum, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
