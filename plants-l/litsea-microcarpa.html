Litsea microcarpa

Litsea microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited laurier-rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

Litsea microcarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
