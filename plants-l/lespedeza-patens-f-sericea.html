Lespedeza patens f. sericea

Lespedeza patens f. sericea, commonly known as silky lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
