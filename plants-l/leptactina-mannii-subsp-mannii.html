Leptactina mannii subsp. mannii

Leptactina mannii subsp.

mannii, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It features a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, fleshy, tuberous roots that store water and nutrients.

The plant's leaves are typically dark green, velvety, and heart-shaped, while its flowers are showy, often with a violet or purple hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for cultivation in both indoor and outdoor environments.
