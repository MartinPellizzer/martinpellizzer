Lepanthes tenera

Lepanthes tenera, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, and often brightly colored flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to retain moisture.

Lepanthes tenera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
