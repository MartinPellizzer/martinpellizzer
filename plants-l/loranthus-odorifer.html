Loranthus odorifer

Loranthus odorifer, commonly known as the fragrant mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the sunlight it captures. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of host trees.

Loranthus odorifer is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its host species.
