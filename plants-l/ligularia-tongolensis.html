Ligularia tongolensis

Ligularia tongolensis, commonly known as the Tongol mountain arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ligularia tongolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
