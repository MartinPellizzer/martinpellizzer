Lupinus guaraniticus

Lupinus guaraniticus, commonly known as the Guarani lupine, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus guaraniticus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
