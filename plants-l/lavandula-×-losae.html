Lavandula × losae

Lavandula × losae, commonly known as Spanish lavender, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of pale purple to lavender flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in gardens for its ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Lavandula × losae is a member of the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other lavenders.
