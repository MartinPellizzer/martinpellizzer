Lotus pedunculatus var. pilosus

Lotus pedunculatus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, branched stems covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
