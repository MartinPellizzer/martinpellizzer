Limnanthes gracilis subsp. parishii

Limnanthes gracilis subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and disturbed areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Limnanthes gracilis.
