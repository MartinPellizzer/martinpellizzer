Leptochloa digitaria

Leptochloa digitaria, commonly known as finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or disturbed areas.

Leptochloa digitaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
