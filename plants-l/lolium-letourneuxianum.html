Lolium letourneuxianum

Lolium letourneuxianum, commonly known as French ryegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its rapid growth and hardiness.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other grass species used in agriculture.
