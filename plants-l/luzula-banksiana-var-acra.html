Luzula banksiana var. acra

Luzula banksiana var. acra, commonly known as the alpine wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Luzula banksiana var. acra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
