Lobelia laxiflora var. stricta

Lobelia laxiflora var. stricta, commonly known as the compact lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.
