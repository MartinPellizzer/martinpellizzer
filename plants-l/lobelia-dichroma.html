Lobelia dichroma

Lobelia dichroma, commonly known as the twinflower lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Lobelia dichroma thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
