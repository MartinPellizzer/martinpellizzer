Loranthus eucalyptoides

Loranthus eucalyptoides, commonly known as the red mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of eucalyptus trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval leaves and red berries that contain sticky seeds. It plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals.

Its presence can affect the growth and health of its host tree.
