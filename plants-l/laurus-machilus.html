Laurus machilus

Laurus machilus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elongated leaves that have a glossy surface and a subtle, spicy fragrance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

Laurus machilus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
