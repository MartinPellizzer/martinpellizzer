Lourya

Lourya, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, showy blooms. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Lourya is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
