Lacis ruppioides

Lacis ruppioides, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
