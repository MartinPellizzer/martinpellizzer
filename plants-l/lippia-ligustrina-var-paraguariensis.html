Lippia ligustrina var. paraguariensis

Lippia ligustrina var. paraguariensis, commonly known as paraguayan blueweed, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in regional flora and traditional herbal practices.
