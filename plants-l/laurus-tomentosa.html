Laurus tomentosa

Laurus tomentosa, commonly known as the Turkish laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green, velvety leaves covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and aromatic qualities.

Laurus tomentosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
