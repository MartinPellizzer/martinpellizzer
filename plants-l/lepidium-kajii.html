Lepidium kajii

Lepidium kajii, commonly known as Japanese cress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has small, white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to other members of the mustard family.

Lepidium kajii is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable in traditional Asian cuisines.
