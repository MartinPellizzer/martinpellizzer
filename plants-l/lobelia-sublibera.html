Lobelia sublibera

Lobelia sublibera, commonly known as the slender lobelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is part of the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped blooms.

Lobelia sublibera is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
