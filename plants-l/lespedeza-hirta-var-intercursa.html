Lespedeza hirta var. intercursa

Lespedeza hirta var. intercursa, commonly known as hairy lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

The plant features small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Lespedeza hirta var. intercursa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant in ecological restoration projects.
