Leptorkis bootanensis

Leptorkis bootanensis, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. Its unique morphology and vibrant color make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
