Linaria tristis var. leiantha

Linaria tristis var. leiantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers in the spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
