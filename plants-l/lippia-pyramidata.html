Lippia pyramidata

Lippia pyramidata, commonly known as Mexican mint or wintermint, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Lippia pyramidata is also a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant fragrance.
