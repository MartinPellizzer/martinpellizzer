Luffa parvula

Luffa parvula, commonly known as the small sponge gourd, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive sponge-like fruit that matures to a pale yellow or white color. The fruit's fibrous interior is used in traditional crafts and as a natural exfoliant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
