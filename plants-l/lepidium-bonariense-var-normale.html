Lepidium bonariense var. normale

Lepidium bonariense var. normale, commonly known as wild cress, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features small, cruciform flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces tiny, rounded seeds in pods. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a common weed and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
