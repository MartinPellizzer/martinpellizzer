Leucophyta citrea

Leucophyta citrea, commonly known as the lemon tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and oval-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Leucophyta citrea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citrus fruit.
