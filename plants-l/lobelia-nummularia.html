Lobelia nummularia

Lobelia nummularia, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, round, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Lobelia nummularia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
