Lithodora prostrata subsp. lusitanica

Lithodora prostrata subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian bugle, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Small, blue or violet flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
