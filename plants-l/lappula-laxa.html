Lappula laxa

Lappula laxa, commonly known as the slender lappula, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and usually covered with fine hairs.

Lappula laxa is characterized by its short, erect stem and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic properties.
