Lasiopogon ponticulus

Lasiopogon ponticulus, commonly known as the Pontic rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, riverbanks, and damp woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense tufts, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous root system helps it spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Lasiopogon ponticulus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
