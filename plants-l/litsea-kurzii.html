Litsea kurzii

Litsea kurzii, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or wild laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow elliptical leaves that emit a citrus-like scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Litsea kurzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
