Lactuca serriola

Lactuca serriola, commonly known as prickly lettuce, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant contains lactucine, a bitter compound that gives it its distinctive taste. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Lactuca serriola is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
