Luma dombeyana

Luma dombeyana, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Luma dombeyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
