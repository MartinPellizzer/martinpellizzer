Lepanthes demissa

Lepanthes demissa, commonly known as the Mexican ghost orchid, is a rare and captivating orchid species native to Mexico.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, white or pale green flowers with intricate patterns resembling ghostly faces, giving it its common name. Lepanthes demissa has narrow, strap-like leaves and is known for its ephemeral blooming cycle.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
