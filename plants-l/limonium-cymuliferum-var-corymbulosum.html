Limonium cymuliferum var. corymbulosum

Limonium cymuliferum var. corymbulosum, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salty, windy environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its stems are sturdy and often branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
