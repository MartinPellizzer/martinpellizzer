Lastrea oligophlebia var. elegans

Lastrea oligophlebia var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant lastrea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant flowering displays. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
