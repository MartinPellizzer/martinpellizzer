Luzula pilosa f. pallida

Luzula pilosa f. pallida, commonly known as the pale hair grass, is a variant of the common wood rush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The leaves are typically pale green to silvery in color, giving the plant its distinguishing appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers in the spring, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
