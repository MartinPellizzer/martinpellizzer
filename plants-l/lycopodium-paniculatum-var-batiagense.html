Lycopodium paniculatum var. batiagense

Lycopodium paniculatum var. batiagense, commonly known as the Batiagense clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that bear small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, that contain the sporangia, which release spores for reproduction. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

This variety is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
