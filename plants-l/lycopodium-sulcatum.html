Lycopodium sulcatum

Lycopodium sulcatum, commonly known as the zigzag clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit. The plant features whorled leaves that are often twisted or zigzagged, giving it its common name. It produces small, spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically reddish in color.

Lycopodium sulcatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
