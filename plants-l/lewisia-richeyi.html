Lewisia richeyi

Lewisia richeyi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada Lewisia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Montiaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or magenta flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Lewisia richeyi is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom in poor soil conditions.
