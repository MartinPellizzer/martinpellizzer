Linum grandiflorum

Linum grandiflorum, commonly known as the common flax, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are used in the production of linen and oil. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often hairy, while its flowers have five petals.

Linum grandiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in textile production.
