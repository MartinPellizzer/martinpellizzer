Lobelia siphilitica var. rosea

Lobelia siphilitica var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate cardinal flower, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes, with a distinctive rose-pink hue in the variety. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
