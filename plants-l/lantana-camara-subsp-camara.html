Lantana camara subsp. camara

Lantana camara subsp.

camara, commonly known as the common lantana, is a shrub native to the Americas but widely naturalized in tropical and subtropical regions. It features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are usually elliptical to ovate in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowering period extends throughout the growing season, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
