Laurus canariensis var. lutea

Laurus canariensis var. lutea, commonly known as the Canary Island laurel or yellow laurel, is a variant of the evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a yellowish or golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The tree has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions.
