Lobelia erinus var. subvillosa

Lobelia erinus var. subvillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf lobelia, is a compact, trailing plant native to South America.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The foliage is covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
