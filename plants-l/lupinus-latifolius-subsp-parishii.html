Lupinus latifolius subsp. parishii

Lupinus latifolius subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Parish's broadleaf lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native pollinators.
