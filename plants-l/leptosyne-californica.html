Leptosyne californica

Leptosyne californica, commonly known as the California leucaena, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, thriving in sandy soils and saline conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

However, it is considered invasive in some areas outside its native range.
