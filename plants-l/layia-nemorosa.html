Layia nemorosa

Layia nemorosa, commonly known as the woodland golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Layia nemorosa is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
