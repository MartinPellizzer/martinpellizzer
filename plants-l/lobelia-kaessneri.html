Lobelia kaessneri

Lobelia kaessneri, commonly known as the mountain lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its showy blue or violet flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Lobelia kaessneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
