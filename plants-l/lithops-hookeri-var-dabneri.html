Lithops hookeri var. dabneri

Lithops hookeri var. dabneri, commonly known as the Dabner's rock plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, stone-like appearance, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique morphology helps it blend into its natural surroundings, providing camouflage from herbivores.
