Leucas galeopsidea

Leucas galeopsidea, commonly known as the Indian balm or mountain balm, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.

Leucas galeopsidea is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its fragrant flowers and compact growth habit.
