Limeum viscosum subsp. transvaalense

Limeum viscosum subsp.

transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal rubber tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
