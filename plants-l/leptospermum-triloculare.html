Leptospermum triloculare

Leptospermum triloculare, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to coastal areas of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Leptospermum triloculare is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
