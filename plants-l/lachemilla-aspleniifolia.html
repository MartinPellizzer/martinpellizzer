Lachemilla aspleniifolia

Lachemilla aspleniifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf lichen, is a unique plant that exhibits both lichen and moss-like characteristics.

It is a foliose lichen, meaning it has a leaf-like structure with a distinct upper and lower surface. The plant typically grows on shaded, moist surfaces such as tree trunks and rocks. Its upper surface is covered in a layer of fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Lachemilla aspleniifolia thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in temperate forest regions.
