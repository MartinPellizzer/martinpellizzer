Lonicera periclymenum f. aurea

Lonicera periclymenum f. aurea, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The golden honeysuckle is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
