Linanthus dichotomus

Linanthus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Camelineae tribe within the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its rapid growth cycle. Linanthus dichotomus has a unique branching pattern, with stems that split into two at the base, giving it its common name.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
