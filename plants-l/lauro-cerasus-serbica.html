Lauro-cerasus serbica

Lauro-cerasus serbica, commonly known as the Serbian cherry laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dark green, shiny leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used as a decorative hedge or screen due to its dense growth habit.
