Lilium × burnhamense

Lilium × burnhamense, commonly known as the Burnham lily, is a hybrid species within the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon, where it thrives in moist, shady environments. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Lilium × burnhamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
