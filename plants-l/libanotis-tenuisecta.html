Libanotis tenuisecta

Libanotis tenuisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
