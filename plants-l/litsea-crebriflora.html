Litsea crebriflora

Litsea crebriflora, commonly known as the fragrant leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
