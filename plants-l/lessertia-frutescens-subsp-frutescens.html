Lessertia frutescens subsp. frutescens

Lessertia frutescens subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
