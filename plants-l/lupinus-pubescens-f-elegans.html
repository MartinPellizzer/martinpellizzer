Lupinus pubescens f. elegans

Lupinus pubescens f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant lupine, is a variant of the tall lupine species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely pubescent stems and clusters of pea-like, violet to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
