Leptospermum brevipes

Leptospermum brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in sandy or acidic soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stems. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems, supporting various native fauna.
