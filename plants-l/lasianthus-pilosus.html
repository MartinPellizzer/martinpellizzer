Lasianthus pilosus

Lasianthus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy foliage and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have a velvety texture due to the fine hairs covering them.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
