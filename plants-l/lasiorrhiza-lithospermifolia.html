Lasiorrhiza lithospermifolia

Lasiorrhiza lithospermifolia, commonly known as the rock pepper plant, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage and resilience.
