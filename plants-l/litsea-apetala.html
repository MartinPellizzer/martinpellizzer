Litsea apetala

Litsea apetala, commonly known as the smoothleaf litsea, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, dark blue berries.

Litsea apetala is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
