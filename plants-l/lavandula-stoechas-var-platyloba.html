Lavandula stoechas var. platyloba

Lavandula stoechas var. platyloba, commonly known as the Spanish lavender or flat-leaved lavender, is a variety of the lavender plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is often used in aromatherapy and as a decorative plant due to its pleasant fragrance and ornamental value.
