Linguella puberula

Linguella puberula, commonly known as the hairy tongue plant, is a rare and lesser-known species within the genus Linguella.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a slender, creeping stem covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Linguella puberula is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks.
