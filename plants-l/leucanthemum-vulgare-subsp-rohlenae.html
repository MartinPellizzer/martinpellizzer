Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. rohlenae

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

rohlenae, commonly known as the Rohlen's white marguerite, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white to pale yellow composite flowers and dark green, toothed leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flower heads and more robust growth compared to the typical *Leucanthemum vulgare*.
