Lodhra hamiltoniana

Lodhra hamiltoniana, commonly known as the sacred ash tree or Indian privet, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits that mature to a dark purple color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
