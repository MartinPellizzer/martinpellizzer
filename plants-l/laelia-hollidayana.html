Laelia hollidayana

Laelia hollidayana, commonly known as the Holliday's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
