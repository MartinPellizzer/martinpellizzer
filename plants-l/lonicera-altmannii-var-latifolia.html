Lonicera altmannii var. latifolia

Lonicera altmannii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, though they are not widely consumed by humans.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
