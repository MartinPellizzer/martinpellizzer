Laurus multiglandulosa

Laurus multiglandulosa, commonly known as the Mexican bay leaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like true laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are highly aromatic and often used in cooking. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and partial shade.

The leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
