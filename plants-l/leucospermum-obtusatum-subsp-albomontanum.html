Leucospermum obtusatum subsp. albomontanum

Leucospermum obtusatum subsp.

albomontanum, commonly known as the White Mountain King protea, is a South African endemic species belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous white, silky-haired flowers.

The plant is typically found in mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a rare and valuable species.
