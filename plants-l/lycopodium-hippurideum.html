Lycopodium hippurideum

Lycopodium hippurideum, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage and upright, branching stems that resemble small trees. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically found in clusters. Lycopodium hippurideum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in soil stabilization make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
