Lycopodioides canaliculata

Lycopodioides canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing cones and creeping, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged spirally along the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Lycopodioides canaliculata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
