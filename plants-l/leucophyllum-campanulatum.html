Leucophyllum campanulatum

Leucophyllum campanulatum, commonly known as Texas betony, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular plant in native gardens and landscapes.
