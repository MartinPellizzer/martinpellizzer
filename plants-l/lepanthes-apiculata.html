Lepanthes apiculata

Lepanthes apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a few flowers, each with a distinctive pouch-like structure. Lepanthes apiculata thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
