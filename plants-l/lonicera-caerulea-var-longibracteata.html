Lonicera caerulea var. longibracteata

Lonicera caerulea var. longibracteata, commonly known as the Amur honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
