Lepidium glaucescens

Lepidium glaucescens, commonly known as the blue prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, bluish-green leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It often grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and its distinctive appearance.
