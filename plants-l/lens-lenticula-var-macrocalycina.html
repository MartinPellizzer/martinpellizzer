Lens lenticula var. macrocalycina

Lens lenticula var. macrocalycina, commonly known as the large calyx lentil, is a variant of the lentil plant characterized by its enlarged calyx.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds enclosed in a distinctive, thickened calyx that remains attached to the pod. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for its unique morphology and is often studied for its agricultural and horticultural potential.
