Lepidium orbiculare

Lepidium orbiculare, commonly known as the round cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, orbicular leaves and clusters of tiny white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, rounded fruits called silicles, which contain several seeds.

Lepidium orbiculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
