Leucaena purpusii

Leucaena purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's leucaena, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is often used in agroforestry and as a forage plant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
