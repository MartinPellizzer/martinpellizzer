Lobelia laxiflora var. brevipes

Lobelia laxiflora var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly toothed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
