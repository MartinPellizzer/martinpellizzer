Lippia ukambensis

Lippia ukambensis, commonly known as African verbena, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
