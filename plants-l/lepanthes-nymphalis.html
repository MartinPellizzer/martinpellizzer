Lepanthes nymphalis

Lepanthes nymphalis, commonly known as the Fairy Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has a slender, leafless stem that supports a cluster of showy, white or pale pink flowers. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Lepanthes nymphalis is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
