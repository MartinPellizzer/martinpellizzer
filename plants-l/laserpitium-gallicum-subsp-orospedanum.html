Laserpitium gallicum subsp. orospedanum

Laserpitium gallicum subsp.

orospedanum, commonly known as the alpine hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. It produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
