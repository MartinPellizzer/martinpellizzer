Lobelia comosa var. microdon

Lobelia comosa var. microdon, commonly known as the small-flowered lobelia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
