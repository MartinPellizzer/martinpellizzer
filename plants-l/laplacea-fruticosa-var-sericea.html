Laplacea fruticosa var. sericea

Laplacea fruticosa var. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf shrub, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
