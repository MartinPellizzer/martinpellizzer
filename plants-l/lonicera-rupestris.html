Lonicera rupestris

Lonicera rupestris, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, providing food for birds and small mammals.

Lonicera rupestris thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.
