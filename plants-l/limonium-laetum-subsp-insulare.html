Limonium laetum subsp. insulare

Limonium laetum subsp.

insulare, commonly known as the island sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
