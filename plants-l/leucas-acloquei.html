Leucas acloquei

Leucas acloquei, commonly known as the Cape mountain tea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Leucas acloquei is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
