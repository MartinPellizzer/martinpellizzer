Linum hirsutum subsp. spathulatum

Linum hirsutum subsp.

spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf flax, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features lanceolate to spatulate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its scientific name.

The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
