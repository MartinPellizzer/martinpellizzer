Loranthus subfalcatus

Loranthus subfalcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to its host tree using a modified stem called a haustellum.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to regions of Africa, including parts of South Africa. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.
