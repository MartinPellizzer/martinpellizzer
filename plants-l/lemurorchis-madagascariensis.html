Lemurorchis madagascariensis

Lemurorchis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lemur orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It possesses a slender, wiry stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its unique adaptations allow it to thrive in the humid, tropical conditions of its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological significance in Madagascar's diverse flora.
