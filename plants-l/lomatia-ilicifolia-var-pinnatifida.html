Lomatia ilicifolia var. pinnatifida

Lomatia ilicifolia var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lomatia, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
