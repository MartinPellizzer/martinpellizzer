Lasianthus oblongilobus

Lasianthus oblongilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate to oblong-lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
