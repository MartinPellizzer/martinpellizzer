Lepanthes rodrigoi

Lepanthes rodrigoi, commonly known as the Rodriguez's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette.

Lepanthes rodrigoi is a rare and endangered species, threatened by habitat loss and overcollection.
