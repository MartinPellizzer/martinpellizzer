Lolium remotum subvar. laeve

Lolium remotum subvar. laeve, commonly known as smooth bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The leaves are typically smooth and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along riverbanks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
