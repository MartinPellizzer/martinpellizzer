Litsea rotundifolia

Litsea rotundifolia, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The tree's botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
