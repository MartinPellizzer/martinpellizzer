Lythrum lyratum

Lythrum lyratum, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Lythrum lyratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
