Leucosedum hirsutum

Leucosedum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
