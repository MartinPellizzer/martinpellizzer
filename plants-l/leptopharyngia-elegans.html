Leptopharyngia elegans

Leptopharyngia elegans, commonly known as the elegant leek, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers in the summer. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
