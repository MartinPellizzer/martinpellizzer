Lithops archerae

Lithops archerae, commonly known as the "ice plant" or "living stone," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant's leaves are typically green with distinctive markings that help it blend into its surroundings. Lithops archerae produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
