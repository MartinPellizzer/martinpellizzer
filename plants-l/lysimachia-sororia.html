Lysimachia sororia

Lysimachia sororia, commonly known as the lesser yellow archangel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Lysimachia sororia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
