Leria araneosa

Leria araneosa, commonly known as the spider lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Leria araneosa is also noted for its ability to bloom in late summer, adding a unique touch to the landscape.
