Lepidium calycinum var. gracile

Lepidium calycinum var. gracile, commonly known as slender celtuce, is a cultivated variety of the celtuce plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically harvested before they become too fibrous. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown for its mild, slightly sweet flavor.

The botanical features of this variety include its robust root system and its ability to produce small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.
