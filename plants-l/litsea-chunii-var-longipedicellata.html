Litsea chunii var. longipedicellata

Litsea chunii var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited litsea, is a variant of the litsea chunii species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various insects and birds.

The botanical features of this variant make it valuable for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
