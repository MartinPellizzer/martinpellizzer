Lycopodium xiphophyllum

Lycopodium xiphophyllum, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a creeping underground stem that produces new shoots. Its leaves are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Lycopodium xiphophyllum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.
