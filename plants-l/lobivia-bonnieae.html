Lobivia bonnieae

Lobivia bonnieae, commonly known as Bonnie's lobivia, is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of central Argentina, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. It is a perennial plant that exhibits succulent properties, allowing it to store water in its stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
