Lavandula dentata f. albiflora

Lavandula dentata f. albiflora, commonly known as the silver lavender or white-flowered lavender, is a cultivar of the common lavender plant.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.

The plant is also used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
