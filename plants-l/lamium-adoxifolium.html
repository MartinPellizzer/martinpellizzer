Lamium adoxifolium

Lamium adoxifolium, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lamium adoxifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
