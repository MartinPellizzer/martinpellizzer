Lomandra confertifolia subsp. pallida

Lomandra confertifolia subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale leek lomandra, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense, fibrous root system.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in native landscaping.
