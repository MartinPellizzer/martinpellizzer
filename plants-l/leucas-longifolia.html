Leucas longifolia

Leucas longifolia, commonly known as slender leucas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
