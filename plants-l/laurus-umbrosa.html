Laurus umbrosa

Laurus umbrosa, commonly known as the dark laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a seasoning.

Laurus umbrosa prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in dense forests.
