Lippia chiapasensis

Lippia chiapasensis, commonly known as Chiapas mint, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
