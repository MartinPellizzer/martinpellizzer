Lysimachia punctata

Lysimachia punctata, commonly known as the spotted deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often spotted with silver or white markings, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lysimachia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
