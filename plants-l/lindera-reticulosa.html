Lindera reticulosa

Lindera reticulosa, commonly known as roughleaf ligustrum, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves with a rough, textured surface and prominent veins. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
