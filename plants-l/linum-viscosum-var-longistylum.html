Linum viscosum var. longistylum

Linum viscosum var. longistylum, commonly known as long-stem flax, is a variant of the flax plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
