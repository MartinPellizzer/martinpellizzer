Leontodon crispus var. apulus

Leontodon crispus var. apulus, commonly known as the Apulian hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with yellow flowers. It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

This variety is often found in Mediterranean regions and is adapted to dry, well-drained environments.
