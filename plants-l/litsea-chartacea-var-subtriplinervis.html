Litsea chartacea var. subtriplinervis

Litsea chartacea var. subtriplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined litsea, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically pale green on the underside and darker on the upper surface. The leaves exhibit a distinct three-veined pattern, which is a key identifying feature of this variety. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
