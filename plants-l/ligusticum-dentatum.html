Ligusticum dentatum

Ligusticum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed lovage, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves. Its rhizomes and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
