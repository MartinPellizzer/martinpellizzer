Leptospermum polygalifolium subsp. polygalifolium

Leptospermum polygalifolium subsp.

polygalifolium, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden air.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
