Lilium pardalinum var. californicum

Lilium pardalinum var. californicum, commonly known as the California tiger lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers with a distinctive tiger-like pattern, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that is evergreen in mild climates and dies back in colder regions. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
