Lupinus christianus

Lupinus christianus, commonly known as the Christian lupin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus christianus is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
