Lotus pisifolius

Lotus pisifolius, commonly known as the water caltrop, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, spherical fruit that resembles a small, armored nut. The plant thrives in shallow waters and has broad, floating leaves that help it capture sunlight. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom above the water surface.

Lotus pisifolius is known for its unique ecological role in wetland ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
