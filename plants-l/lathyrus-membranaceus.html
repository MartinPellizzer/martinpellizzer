Lathyrus membranaceus

Lathyrus membranaceus, commonly known as the skyflower or sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the western United States.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, pale green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, often in clusters. Its flowers are followed by small, bean-like pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds.

Lathyrus membranaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
