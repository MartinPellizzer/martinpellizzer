Lotus burttii

Lotus burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's lotus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
