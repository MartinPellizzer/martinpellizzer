Lavandula bramwellii

Lavandula bramwellii, commonly known as the Bramwell lavender, is a species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces fragrant, spike-like clusters of purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is a valuable addition to xeriscapic gardens.
