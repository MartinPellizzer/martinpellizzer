Laurentia canariensis

Laurentia canariensis, commonly known as the Canary laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and lance-shaped, and its fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in laurel forests.
