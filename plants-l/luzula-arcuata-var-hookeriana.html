Luzula arcuata var. hookeriana

Luzula arcuata var. hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's woodrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and often have a bluish tint. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
