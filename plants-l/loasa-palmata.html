Loasa palmata

Loasa palmata, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. Loasa palmata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native plant with both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
