Lepidophyllum cupressiforme

Lepidophyllum cupressiforme, commonly known as the silverleaf cypress, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are arranged in tight clusters, contributing to its compact and ornamental growth habit.
