Linophyllum pratense

Linophyllum pratense, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are erect and branching.

Linophyllum pratense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
