Linaria melanantha

Linaria melanantha, commonly known as the black-flowered toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats. Linaria melanantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
