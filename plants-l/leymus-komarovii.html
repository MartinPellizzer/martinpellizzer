Leymus komarovii

Leymus komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Leymus komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful foliage.
