Lupinus albicaulis var. hyacinthinus

Lupinus albicaulis var. hyacinthinus, commonly known as the white lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Lupinus albicaulis var. hyacinthinus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
