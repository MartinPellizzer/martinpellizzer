Latace

Latace, commonly known as the latace plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is found in various regions across the globe. Latace species are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The flowers are usually clustered in whorls and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
