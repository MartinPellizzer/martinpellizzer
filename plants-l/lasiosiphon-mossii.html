Lasiosiphon mossii

Lasiosiphon mossii, commonly known as the mossy siphon, is a rare and delicate moss species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky crevices or on the bases of trees. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Lasiosiphon mossii is considered a species of conservation concern.
