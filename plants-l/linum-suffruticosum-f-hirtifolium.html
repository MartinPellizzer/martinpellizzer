Linum suffruticosum f. hirtifolium

Linum suffruticosum f. hirtifolium, commonly known as the hairy-leaved flax, is a variant of the flax plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or scrublands.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
