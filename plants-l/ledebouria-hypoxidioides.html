Ledebouria hypoxidioides

Ledebouria hypoxidioides, commonly known as the Cape gold plant, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
