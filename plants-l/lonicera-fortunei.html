Lonicera fortunei

Lonicera fortunei, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a vigorous climbing shrub native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though its aggressive nature requires careful management.
