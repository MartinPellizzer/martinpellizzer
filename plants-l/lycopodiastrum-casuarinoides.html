Lycopodiastrum casuarinoides

Lycopodiastrum casuarinoides, commonly known as the Australian bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, which are attracted to the nectar-rich blooms.
