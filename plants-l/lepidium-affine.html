Lepidium affine

Lepidium affine, commonly known as field cress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lepidium affine is an annual herb that is native to Europe but has been introduced to other parts of the world.
