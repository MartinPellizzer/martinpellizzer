Leptoglossis viscosa

Leptoglossis viscosa, commonly known as the sticky lemur orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves and its unique, lemur-like appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insects.

Leptoglossis viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
