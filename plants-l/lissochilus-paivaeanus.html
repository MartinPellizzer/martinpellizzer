Lissochilus paivaeanus

Lissochilus paivaeanus, commonly known as the Paivaean beard lily, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a pseudobulbous structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its native high-altitude environment. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This orchid is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
