Lagotis crassifolia

Lagotis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved lagotis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
