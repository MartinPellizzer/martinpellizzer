Lychnophora rosmarinus var. normalis

Lychnophora rosmarinus var. normalis, commonly known as Brazilian rosemary, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, minty fragrance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
