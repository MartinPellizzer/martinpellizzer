Leucadendron rochetianum

Leucadendron rochetianum, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that resemble torches, typically displaying vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
