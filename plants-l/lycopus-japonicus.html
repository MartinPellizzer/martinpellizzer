Lycopus japonicus

Lycopus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
