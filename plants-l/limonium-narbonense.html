Limonium narbonense

Limonium narbonense, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Limonium narbonense is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
