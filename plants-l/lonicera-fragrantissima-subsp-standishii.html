Lonicera fragrantissima subsp. standishii

Lonicera fragrantissima subsp.

standishii, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and persistent berries, which attract wildlife.
