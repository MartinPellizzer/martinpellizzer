Lavandula mairei var. intermedia

Lavandula mairei var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid lavender, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and essential oil production.

The plant prefers full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
