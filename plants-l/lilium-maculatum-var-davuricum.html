Lilium maculatum var. davuricum

Lilium maculatum var. davuricum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with red or purple spots on the outer petals. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern around the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking appearance and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
