Lactuca anatolica

Lactuca anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers are borne in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Lactuca anatolica is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter flavor.
