Legnotis cassipaurea

Legnotis cassipaurea, commonly known as the African mahogany.

It is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa, characterized by its dense, aromatic wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is often harvested for commercial use, though it is also found in various ecological habitats.
