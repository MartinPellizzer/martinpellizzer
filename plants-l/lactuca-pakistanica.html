Lactuca pakistanica

Lactuca pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan endive, is a species of lettuce native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan.

It is characterized by its curly, dark green leaves that are often used in local cuisine. The plant typically grows in cool, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes other members like daisies and chicory.

Lactuca pakistanica is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential medicinal properties.
