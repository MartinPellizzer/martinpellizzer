Litsea acutifolia

Litsea acutifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bay leaf or sweetleaf, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dark purple berries.

The leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary spice due to their aromatic properties.
