Lyperanthus nigricans f. typica

Lyperanthus nigricans f. typica, commonly known as the black-flowered star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are arranged in star-like clusters. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
