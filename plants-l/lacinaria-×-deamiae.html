Lacinaria × deamiae

Lacinaria × deamiae, commonly known as the hybrid lace fern, is a rare and ornamental fern species resulting from the cross between Lacinaria filix-femina and Lacinaria tenuis.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are finely divided and arching in appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring well-drained, loamy soil. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic value.
