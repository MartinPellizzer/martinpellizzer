Lepidium drabifolium

Lepidium drabifolium, commonly known as field cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It has a sprawling growth habit and can often be found in fields, along roadsides, and in other open areas.

The seeds of Lepidium drabifolium are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
