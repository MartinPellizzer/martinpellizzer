Lindernia pyxidaria subsp. gratioloides

Lindernia pyxidaria subsp.

gratioloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lindernia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in loose clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
