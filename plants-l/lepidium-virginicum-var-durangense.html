Lepidium virginicum var. durangense

Lepidium virginicum var. durangense, commonly known as the Durango peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a low, spreading growth habit with linear, hairy leaves.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and tolerate heat.
