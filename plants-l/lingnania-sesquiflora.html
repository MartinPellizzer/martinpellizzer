Lingnania sesquiflora

Lingnania sesquiflora, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a robust, succulent stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, reducing water loss through transpiration in its harsh environment.

Lingnania sesquiflora is well-suited to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
