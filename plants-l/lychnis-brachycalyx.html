Lychnis brachycalyx

Lychnis brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx catchfly, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is relatively short-lived and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
