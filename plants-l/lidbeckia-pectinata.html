Lidbeckia pectinata

Lidbeckia pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often a deep green or purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Lidbeckia pectinata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
