Loranthus hexanthus

Loranthus hexanthus, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. Its life cycle involves the dispersal of its seeds by animals, which then germinate on the branches of new host trees.

Loranthus hexanthus plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
