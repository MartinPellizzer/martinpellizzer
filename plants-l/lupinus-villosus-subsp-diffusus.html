Lupinus villosus subsp. diffusus

Lupinus villosus subsp.

diffusus, commonly known as the hairy lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces tall spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Lupinus villosus subsp.

diffusus is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various pollinators.
