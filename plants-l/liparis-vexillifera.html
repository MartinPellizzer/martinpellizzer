Liparis vexillifera

Liparis vexillifera, commonly known as the flag-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive flag-like lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Liparis vexillifera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
