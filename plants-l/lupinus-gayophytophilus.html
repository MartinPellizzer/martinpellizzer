Lupinus gayophytophilus

Lupinus gayophytophilus, commonly known as the gayophytophilus lupin, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Lupinus gayophytophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
