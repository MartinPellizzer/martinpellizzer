Lathyrus venosus var. grandiflorus

Lathyrus venosus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
