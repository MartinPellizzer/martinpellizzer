Lupinus montanus subsp. montanus

Lupinus montanus subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are compound, with multiple small leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus montanus subsp.

montanus is an important component of native plant communities and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
