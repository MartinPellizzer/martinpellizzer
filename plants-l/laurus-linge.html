Laurus linge

Laurus linge, commonly known as sweet bay or Turkish bay leaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are used extensively in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, egg-shaped berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Laurus linge thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant.
