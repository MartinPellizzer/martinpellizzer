Laportea humilis

Laportea humilis, commonly known as the common rocknettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves contain a variety of bioactive compounds, including flavonoids and terpenoids, contributing to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.

Laportea humilis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
