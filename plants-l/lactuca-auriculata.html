Lactuca auriculata

Lactuca auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often pale green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which are pollinated by insects. Its seeds are small and oval, typically dispersed by wind.

Lactuca auriculata is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
