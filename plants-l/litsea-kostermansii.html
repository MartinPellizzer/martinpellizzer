Litsea kostermansii

Litsea kostermansii, commonly known as the Koster's litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and bay trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

Litsea kostermansii is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
