Lippia ligustrina var. lasiodonta

Lippia ligustrina var. lasiodonta, commonly known as the roughleaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for ornamental and medicinal cultivation.
