Litsea collina

Litsea collina, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. Litsea collina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture for its fragrant foliage and seasonal color changes.
