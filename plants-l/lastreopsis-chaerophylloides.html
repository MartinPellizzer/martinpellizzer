Lastreopsis chaerophylloides

Lastreopsis chaerophylloides, commonly known as the sandstone plant, is a small, perennial herb native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in crevices of sandstone formations, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
