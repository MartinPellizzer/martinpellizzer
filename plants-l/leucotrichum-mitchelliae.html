Leucotrichum mitchelliae

Leucotrichum mitchelliae, commonly known as Mitchell's lichen, is a species of foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its pale green to yellowish thallus with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which contribute to its ability to photosynthesize and absorb nutrients. It is often found on rocks, tree trunks, and soil surfaces in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for ecological health.
