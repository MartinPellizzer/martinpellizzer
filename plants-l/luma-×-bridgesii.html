Luma × bridgesii

Luma × bridgesii, commonly known as the hybrid luma or the golden luma, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Luma tenuifolia and Luma sandwicense.

It is native to Hawaii and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a waxy texture, while its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

Luma × bridgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
