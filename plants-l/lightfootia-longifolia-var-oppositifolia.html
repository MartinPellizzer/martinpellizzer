Lightfootia longifolia var. oppositifolia

Lightfootia longifolia var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved lightfootia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
