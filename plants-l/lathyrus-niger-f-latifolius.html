Lathyrus niger f. latifolius

Lathyrus niger f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf black vetch, is a variant of the black vetch species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting nearby plants.
