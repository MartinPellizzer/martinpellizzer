Leucaena formosa

Leucaena formosa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Leucaena formosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
