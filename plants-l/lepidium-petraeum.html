Lepidium petraeum

Lepidium petraeum, commonly known as the rock pepper or stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small white or yellowish flowers and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the fruit is a short, cylindrical pod containing several seeds.

Lepidium petraeum is often found in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.
