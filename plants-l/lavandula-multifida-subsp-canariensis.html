Lavandula multifida subsp. canariensis

Lavandula multifida subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island lavender, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikes of purple-blue flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies and as a ornamental plant.
