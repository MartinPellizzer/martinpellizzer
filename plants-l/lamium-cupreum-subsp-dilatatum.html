Lamium cupreum subsp. dilatatum

Lamium cupreum subsp.

dilatatum, commonly known as broad-leaved dead-nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats, and is known for its attractive foliage and nectar-rich flowers.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
