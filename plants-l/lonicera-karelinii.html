Lonicera karelinii

Lonicera karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

Lonicera karelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and wildlife benefits.
