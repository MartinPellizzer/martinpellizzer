Lycaste cristata var. randii

Lycaste cristata var. randii, commonly known as the Rand's candle plant, is a unique hybrid of the genus Lycaste, native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence, which consists of a tall, narrow stem topped with a cluster of white, waxy flowers. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm, humid environments.
