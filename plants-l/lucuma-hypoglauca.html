Lucuma hypoglauca

Lucuma hypoglauca, commonly known as the white lucuma, is a species of fruit tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is closely related to the more widely known lucuma species. The fruit is characterized by its yellow to orange pulp, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, creamy flavor. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

It is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
