Leea tenuifolia

Leea tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf leeah, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Leea tenuifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
