Lupinus eminens

Lupinus eminens, commonly known as the tall lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Lupinus eminens is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
