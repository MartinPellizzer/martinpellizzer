Leptostachys virgata

Leptostachys virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
