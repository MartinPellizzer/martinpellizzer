Lilium amabile var. immaculatum

Lilium amabile var. immaculatum, commonly known as the white lily, is a variant of the amabile lily species.

It is characterized by its pure white petals and a subtle, sweet fragrance. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
