Linum flavum var. basarabicum

Linum flavum var. basarabicum, commonly known as the Basarabian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsules containing numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional textile production.
