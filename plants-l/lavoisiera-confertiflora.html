Lavoisiera confertiflora

Lavoisiera confertiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered lavoisiera, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and dark green coloration. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Lavoisiera confertiflora is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
