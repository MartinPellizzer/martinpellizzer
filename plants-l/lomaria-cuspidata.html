Lomaria cuspidata

Lomaria cuspidata, commonly known as the needleleaf lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Lomaria cuspidata is often found in rocky or well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.
