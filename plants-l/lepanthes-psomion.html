Lepanthes psomion

Lepanthes psomion, commonly known as the Psomion orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a terrestrial growth habit, with a short, underground rhizome and a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in a spike and are pollinated by small insects such as flies and beetles.

Lepanthes psomion is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a rare garden specimen.
