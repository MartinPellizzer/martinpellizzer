Leucanthemum latifolium

Leucanthemum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, silver-green leaves and its clusters of yellow-orange flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Leucanthemum latifolium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
