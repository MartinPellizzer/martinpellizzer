Linum austriacum subsp. gomaricum

Linum austriacum subsp.

gomaricum, commonly known as the Austrian flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in meadows and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in fiber production.
