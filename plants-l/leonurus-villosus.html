Leonurus villosus

Leonurus villosus, commonly known as hairy leonurus, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, often growing in partial shade.

Leonurus villosus is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
