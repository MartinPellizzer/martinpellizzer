Lathyrus egirdiricus

Lathyrus egirdiricus, commonly known as the Egirdir pea, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves, with small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in rocky and arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential for horticultural use.
