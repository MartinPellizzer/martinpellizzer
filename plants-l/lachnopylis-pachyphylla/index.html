Lachnopylis Pachyphylla

Pachyphylla, scientifically known as Lachnopylis pachyphylla, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa. This plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its striking appearance, with vibrant yellow flowers, makes it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts. Despite its beauty, Pachyphylla is highly sensitive to overwatering and requires careful cultivation to thrive. It serves as a remarkable example of botanical adaptation to extreme climates.
