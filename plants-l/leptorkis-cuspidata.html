Leptorkis cuspidata

Leptorkis cuspidata, commonly known as the pointed leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and distinctive, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
