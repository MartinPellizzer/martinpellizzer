Linum corymbiferum var. aristidis

Linum corymbiferum var. aristidis, commonly known as the purple flax or aristid's flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in textile production.
