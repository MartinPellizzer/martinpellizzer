Laurus cathia

Laurus cathia, commonly known as the Chinese laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue-black berries.

Laurus cathia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
