Linum usitatissimum var. majus

Linum usitatissimum var. majus, commonly known as the greater flax, is a variant of the common flax plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lanceolate, slightly hairy, and alternate along the stem, while the flowers are blue or purple, with five petals and a central yellow stamen. It produces elongated, cylindrical seed capsules that contain numerous small, oily seeds.

This variety is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in food, medicine, and industrial applications.
