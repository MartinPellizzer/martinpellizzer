Limonium majus

Limonium majus, commonly known as sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Limonium majus is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments.
