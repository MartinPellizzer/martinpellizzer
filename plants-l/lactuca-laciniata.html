Lactuca laciniata

Lactuca laciniata, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely cut, dark green leaves that are often used in salads and as a vegetable. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for both its edible leaves and ornamental value.

Lactuca laciniata is also known for its slightly bitter taste, which can be reduced through cooking or curing.
