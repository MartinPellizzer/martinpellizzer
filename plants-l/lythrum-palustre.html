Lythrum palustre

Lythrum palustre, commonly known as swamp rosemary, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Lythrum palustre is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
