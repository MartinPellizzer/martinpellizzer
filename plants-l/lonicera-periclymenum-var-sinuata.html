Lonicera periclymenum var. sinuata

Lonicera periclymenum var. sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive sinuous margin, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems often bear two clusters of flowers, one at the base and one near the tip.

The berries that follow are red and edible, attracting various bird species.
