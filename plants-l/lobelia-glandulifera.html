Lobelia glandulifera

Lobelia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, glandular hairs.

Lobelia glandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
