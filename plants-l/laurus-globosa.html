Laurus globosa

Laurus globosa, commonly known as bay laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its evergreen, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into dark blue berries. Its leaves are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Bay laurel is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
