Leucocoryne editiana

Leucocoryne editiana, commonly known as the white-flowered lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its striking white petals and yellow anthers, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Leucocoryne editiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
