Leontodon × transsilvanicus

Leontodon × transsilvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian hawk’s-beard, is a hybrid plant species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Leontodon × transsilvanicus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
