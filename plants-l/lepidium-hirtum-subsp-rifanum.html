Lepidium hirtum subsp. rifanum

Lepidium hirtum subsp.

rifanum, commonly known as the Rif mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
