Linaria oblongifolia subsp. oblongifolia

Linaria oblongifolia subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are elongated and narrow, often with a slightly hairy surface. This subspecies is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
