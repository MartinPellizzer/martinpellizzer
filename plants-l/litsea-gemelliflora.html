Litsea gemelliflora

Litsea gemelliflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered litsea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in pairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, oval berries that are typically dark purple when mature.

Litsea gemelliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage and unique floral display.
