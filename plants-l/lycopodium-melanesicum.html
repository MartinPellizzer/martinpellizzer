Lycopodium melanesicum

Lycopodium melanesicum, commonly known as the Melanesian clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands, including parts of Melanesia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its morphology includes a short, erect stem with spirally arranged leaves that are usually narrow and pointed.

Lycopodium melanesicum reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
