Lilium japonicum var. ishimanum

Lilium japonicum var. ishimanum, commonly known as the Ishimanum lily, is a rare and ornamental species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and erect.

This variety is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and horticultural interest.
