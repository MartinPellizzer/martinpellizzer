Luzula parviflora subsp. melanocarpa

Luzula parviflora subsp.

melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited wood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. Its subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black berries, which are a key identifying feature.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
