Lycopodium luetzelburgii

Lycopodium luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, spiky appearance with whorls of small, needle-like leaves. Native to the moist, shaded environments of the Appalachian Mountains, it thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, reproducing through spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
