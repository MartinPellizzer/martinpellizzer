Lonicera braceana

Lonicera braceana, commonly known as the Canadian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Lonicera braceana is often found in moist woodlands, stream banks, and thickets, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
