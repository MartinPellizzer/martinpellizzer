Lobivia aureosenilis

Lobivia aureosenilis, commonly known as the golden-leafed lobivia, is a succulent native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
