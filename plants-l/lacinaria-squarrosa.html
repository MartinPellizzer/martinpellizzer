Lacinaria squarrosa

Lacinaria squarrosa, commonly known as the rough lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves that are often grayish-green in color, and it produces yellow flowers with a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Lacinaria squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
