Larix laricina f. lutea

Larix laricina f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow larch, is a unique variant of the tamarack tree.

It is characterized by its striking golden foliage, which turns a vivid yellow in the fall. This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical larch, with shorter, more densely packed branches. The cones are similar to those of other larch species, maturing to a brownish color.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist, acidic soils, often found in boreal and subarctic regions.
