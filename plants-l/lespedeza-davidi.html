Lespedeza davidi

Lespedeza davidi, commonly known as David's lespedeza, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Lespedeza davidi is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil health.
