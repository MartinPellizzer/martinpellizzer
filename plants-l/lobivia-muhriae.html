Lobivia muhriae

Lobivia muhriae, commonly known as the Muhriae Lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water for survival in harsh environments. The plant produces small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of yellow or orange, during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Lobivia muhriae is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
