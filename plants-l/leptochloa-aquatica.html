Leptochloa aquatica

Leptochloa aquatica, commonly known as water grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted growth habit. This plant thrives in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. Its reproductive structures include spikelets that produce tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Leptochloa aquatica plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
