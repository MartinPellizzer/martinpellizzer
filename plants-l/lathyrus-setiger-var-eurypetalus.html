Lathyrus setiger var. eurypetalus

Lathyrus setiger var. eurypetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled lathyrus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and slender, hairy stems that support its clusters of pea-like flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with broad, showy petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing one or two seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
