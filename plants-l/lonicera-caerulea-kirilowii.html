Lonicera caerulea kirilowii

Lonicera caerulea kirilowii, commonly known as the Sakhalin honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in early summer. Its fruits are small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
