Lilium pictum

Lilium pictum, commonly known as the painted lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

The painted lily is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and delicate appearance.
