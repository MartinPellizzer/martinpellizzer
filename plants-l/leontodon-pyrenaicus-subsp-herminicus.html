Leontodon pyrenaicus subsp. herminicus

Leontodon pyrenaicus subsp.

herminicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and usually unbranched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
