Leucas ciliata var. oblongifolia

Leucas ciliata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved leucas, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or lilac in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
