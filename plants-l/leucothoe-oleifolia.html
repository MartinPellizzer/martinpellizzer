Leucothoe oleifolia

Leucothoe oleifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leucothoe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Leucothoe oleifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to acidic soils.
