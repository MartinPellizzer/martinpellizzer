Lepanthes mirador

Lepanthes mirador, commonly known as the mirror orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking color contrast. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinctive yellow or green throat, enhancing its visual appeal.

Lepanthes mirador is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
