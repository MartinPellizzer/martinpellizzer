Liabum hastatum

Liabum hastatum, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with distinctive spurred structures at the base of the petals. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Liabum hastatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
