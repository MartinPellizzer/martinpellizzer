Lycochloa

Lycochloa, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. These plants are typically found in wet or marshy habitats, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Lycochloa species often have small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

They play a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and are sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
