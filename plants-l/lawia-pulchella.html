Lawia pulchella

Lawia pulchella, commonly known as the elegant lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of pale pink to white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble lilies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Lawia pulchella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
