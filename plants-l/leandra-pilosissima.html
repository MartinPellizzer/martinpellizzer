Leandra pilosissima

Leandra pilosissima, commonly known as the silky leandra, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silky appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
