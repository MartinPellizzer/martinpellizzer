Lycopus laciniatus

Lycopus laciniatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved gypsywort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, narrow, and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Lycopus laciniatus is typically found in moist habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and stream sides.
