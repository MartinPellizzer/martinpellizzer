Lacmellea edulis

Lacmellea edulis, commonly known as the edible jellyfish, is a unique marine plant that belongs to the order Rhodophyta, commonly known as red algae.

It is characterized by its gelatinous, translucent body that contains a high concentration of agar and other polysaccharides. This plant thrives in shallow coastal waters and is often found attached to rocks or other substrates. Its structure consists of a central stipe with branching fronds that aid in photosynthesis.

Due to its high nutritional value and unique texture, it is widely harvested for culinary and industrial purposes.
