Lotus praetermissus

Lotus praetermissus, commonly known as the slender lotus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The slender lotus is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
