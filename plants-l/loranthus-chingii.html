Loranthus chingii

Loranthus chingii, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some photosynthetic support through its own leaves. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
