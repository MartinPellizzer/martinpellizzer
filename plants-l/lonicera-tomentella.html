Lonicera tomentella

Lonicera tomentella, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. Its stems are often reddish-brown and can be somewhat woody with age.

Lonicera tomentella is typically found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
