Lansium javanicum

Lansium javanicum, commonly known as the Java lantern tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by bright orange, lantern-like fruits. The fruit's pulp is edible and has a sweet, citrusy flavor, while the seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
