Lolium perenne var. aristulatum

Lolium perenne var. aristulatum, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and pastures. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Its growth is typically slow, making it suitable for low-maintenance environments.
