Lepanthes mimetica

Lepanthes mimetica, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte. Its flowers are known for their striking colors and intricate patterns, which can resemble other flowers or insects, a trait that may aid in pollination.

Lepanthes mimetica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
