Layia elegans

Layia elegans, commonly known as the elegant layia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Layia elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
