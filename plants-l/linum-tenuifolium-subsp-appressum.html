Linum tenuifolium subsp. appressum

Linum tenuifolium subsp.

appressum, commonly known as narrow-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used for textile and medicinal purposes.
