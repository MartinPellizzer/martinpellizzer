Lissochilus laurentii

Lissochilus laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentii's slipper orchid, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lissochilus laurentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized horticultural collections.
