Linaria aristides

Linaria aristides, commonly known as the spiked toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the plant family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender stems bear clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
