Lepidium dhayense

Lepidium dhayense, commonly known as the Jordanian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East, particularly Jordan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and its finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing in crevices and on slopes.

Lepidium dhayense is not only a hardy wild plant but also has traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
