Linum earlei

Linum earlei, commonly known as the Earle's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the genus Linum, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as flax. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Linum earlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
