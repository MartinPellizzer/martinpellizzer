Ligusticum diffusum

Ligusticum diffusum, commonly known as Chinese lovage, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hollow, ridged stems and finely divided leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, a common feature in the Apiaceae family.

Ligusticum diffusum is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and distinctive, spicy scent.
