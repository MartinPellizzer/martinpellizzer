Lycium chilense var. ovatum

Lycium chilense var. ovatum, commonly known as the Chilean boxthorn, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, red berries. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellowish-white and clustered.

The fruit is edible and has been used traditionally for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
