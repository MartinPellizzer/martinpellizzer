Lepanthes leonii

Lepanthes leonii, commonly known as the Leonii Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Lepanthes leonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
