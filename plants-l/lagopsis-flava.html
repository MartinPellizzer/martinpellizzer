Lagopsis flava

Lagopsis flava, commonly known as yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
