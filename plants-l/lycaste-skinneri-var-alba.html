Lycaste skinneri var. alba

Lycaste skinneri var. alba, commonly known as the white lycaste, is a hybrid orchid species originating from the interbreeding of Lycaste skinneri and Lycaste skinneri var. alba.

It is characterized by its elegant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically produces 3-5 pendulous flowers per spike, each with a fragrant, waxy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
