Lupinus oreganus var. kincaidii

Lupinus oreganus var. kincaidii, commonly known as Kincaid's lupine, is a perennial legume native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Kincaid's lupine is also recognized as a state wildflower in Oregon and is of ecological and cultural significance.
