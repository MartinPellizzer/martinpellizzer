Lycopodioides stenophylla

Lycopodioides stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clubmoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are tightly clustered around the stem, giving it a dense, moss-like appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Lycopodioides stenophylla reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

Its simple, primitive structure reflects its ancient lineage, dating back to the early evolution of vascular plants.
