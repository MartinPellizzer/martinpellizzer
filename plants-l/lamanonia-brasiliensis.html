Lamanonia brasiliensis

Lamanonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Lamanonia brasiliensis plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
