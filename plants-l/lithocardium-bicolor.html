Lithocardium bicolor

Lithocardium bicolor, commonly known as the pink stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Lithocardium bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
