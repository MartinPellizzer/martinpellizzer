Leonurus royleanus

Leonurus royleanus, commonly known as the Indian hedgehog or velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

Leonurus royleanus is often found in rocky or open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
