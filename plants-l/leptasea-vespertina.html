Leptasea vespertina

Leptasea vespertina, commonly known as the evening orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom predominantly in the late afternoon or evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are elongated and slightly succulent, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.
