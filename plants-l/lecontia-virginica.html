Lecontia virginica

Lecontia virginica, commonly known as the Virginia lecontia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

Lecontia virginica is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
