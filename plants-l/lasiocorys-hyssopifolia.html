Lasiocorys hyssopifolia

Lasiocorys hyssopifolia, commonly known as the woolly leaf hyssop, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
