Lotus suberectus

Lotus suberectus, commonly known as the upright lotus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and can thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Lotus suberectus is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
