Leucostegia nephrodioides

Leucostegia nephrodioides, commonly known as the kidneyleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are pale pink to white, arranged in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
