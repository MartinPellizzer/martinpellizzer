Linum uralense

Linum uralense, commonly known as Siberian flax, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces blue or white flowers with five petals, typical of the Linaceae family. Its seeds are rich in oil and have been historically used for both culinary and industrial purposes.

Linum uralense thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.
