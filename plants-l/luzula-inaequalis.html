Luzula inaequalis

Luzula inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters. Luzula inaequalis is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology, with unequal leaf bases that give it its common name.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
