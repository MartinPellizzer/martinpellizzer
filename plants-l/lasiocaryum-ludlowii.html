Lasiocaryum ludlowii

Lasiocaryum ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's lichen, is a species of foliose lichen that thrives in humid, temperate environments.

It is characterized by its thallus, which is typically pale green to yellowish in color and has a leathery texture. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with a specific species of green algae, which contributes to its photosynthetic capabilities. It is often found growing on the bark of trees or on soil in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
