Limonium latifolium

Limonium latifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and grass-like leaves, which are typically narrow and linear. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a perennial species that produces small, showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Limonium latifolium is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salty air.
