Ligusticum saxifragum

Ligusticum saxifragum, commonly known as mountain lovage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust, aromatic rhizome and tall, slender stems that bear compound leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Ligusticum saxifragum is valued for its medicinal properties and aromatic qualities.
