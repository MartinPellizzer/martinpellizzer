Linaria derderiana

Linaria derderiana, commonly known as the Armenian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Linaria derderiana is part of the Plantaginaceae family, which includes other species known for their showy blooms and medicinal properties.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
