Linum gyaricum subsp. icaricum

Linum gyaricum subsp.

icaricum, commonly known as the Icaric flowering plant, is a flowering plant species native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Linaceae family, which includes flax and related species.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or white. The plant thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often growing at high altitudes where conditions are harsh.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies in mountainous ecosystems.
