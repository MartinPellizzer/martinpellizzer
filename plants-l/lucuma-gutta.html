Lucuma gutta

Lucuma gutta, commonly known as the "golden apple of the Andes," is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange fruit, which resembles a small apple. The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed within a fleshy, sweet pulp. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Lucuma gutta is valued not only for its unique flavor but also for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Andean cultures.
