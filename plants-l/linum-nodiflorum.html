Linum nodiflorum

Linum nodiflorum, commonly known as sweet william, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright pink, red, or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It is often cultivated as a garden ornamental due to its vibrant colors and pleasant fragrance.

Linum nodiflorum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
