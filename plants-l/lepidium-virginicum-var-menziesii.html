Lepidium virginicum var. menziesii

Lepidium virginicum var. menziesii, commonly known as slender peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is often found in prairies and open woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
