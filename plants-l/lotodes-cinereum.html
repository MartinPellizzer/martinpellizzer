Lotodes cinereum

Lotodes cinereum, commonly known as the silver water lily, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, silver-green leaves that float on the water surface, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom above the water, typically in the early morning. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the pond or lake bottom while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Lotodes cinereum plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting local ecosystems.
