Lepanthes pembertonii

Lepanthes pembertonii, commonly known as the Pemberton's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces intricate, often fragrant blooms that are characteristic of the orchid family. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes pembertonii is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
