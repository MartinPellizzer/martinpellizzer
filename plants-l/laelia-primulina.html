Laelia primulina

Laelia primulina, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive pink or lavender coloration. Its sepals and petals are fused into a hood-like structure, giving it a unique and elegant appearance.

Laelia primulina is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
