Loranthus muellerianus

Loranthus muellerianus, commonly known as the Mueller's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily eucalyptus species.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light absorbed by its leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that are typically visited by insects for pollination. Its berries are red or yellow and contain sticky seeds that adhere to the bark of trees, facilitating propagation.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host forests.
