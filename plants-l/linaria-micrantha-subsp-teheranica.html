Linaria micrantha subsp. teheranica

Linaria micrantha subsp.

teheranica, commonly known as Persian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to regions of Iran and Turkey. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
