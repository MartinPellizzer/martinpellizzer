Leandra gardneriana

Leandra gardneriana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or Gardner's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Leandra gardneriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
