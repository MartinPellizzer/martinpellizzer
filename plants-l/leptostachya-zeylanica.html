Leptostachya zeylanica

Leptostachya zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Leptostachya zeylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
