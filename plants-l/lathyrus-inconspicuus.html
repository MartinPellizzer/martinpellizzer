Lathyrus inconspicuus

Lathyrus inconspicuus, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, composed of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Lathyrus inconspicuus plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
