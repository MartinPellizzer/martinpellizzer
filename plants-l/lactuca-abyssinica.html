Lactuca abyssinica

Lactuca abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is characterized by its rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Lactuca abyssinica is valued for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in local cuisines.
