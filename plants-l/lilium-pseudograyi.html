Lilium pseudograyi

Lilium pseudograyi, commonly known as the Gray's lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with six tepals and a prominent stamen. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Lilium pseudograyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
