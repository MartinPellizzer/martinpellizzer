Lythrum melanium

Lythrum melanium, commonly known as purple loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Lythrum melanium is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
