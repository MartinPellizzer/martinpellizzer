Locusta

Locusta, commonly known as the locust, is a type of grasshopper belonging to the family Acrididae.

These insects are characterized by their elongated bodies, long legs, and wings that allow for powerful jumps and flight. Locusta species are often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they thrive in warm, dry climates. They play a significant role in ecosystems as both herbivores and prey for various animals.

However, their populations can sometimes become invasive, leading to agricultural damage and ecological imbalance.
