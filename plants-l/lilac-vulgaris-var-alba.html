Lilac vulgaris var. alba

Lilac vulgaris var. alba, commonly known as the white lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in American horticulture.
