Loasa jungiifolia

Loasa jungiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered, violet-blue flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns bright red when mature, containing several seeds.

Loasa jungiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types in its native habitat.
