Lepanthes maduroi

Lepanthes maduroi, commonly known as the Maduro's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates for optimal growth.

Lepanthes maduroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
