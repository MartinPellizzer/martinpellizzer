Limonium dagmariae

Limonium dagmariae, commonly known as the Sea Lavender, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. Its salt tolerance allows it to flourish in sandy or gravelly environments.

Limonium dagmariae is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
