Linaria multicaulis subsp. gigantea

Linaria multicaulis subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant stickseed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellow-orange tubular flowers.

The plant produces numerous narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
