Litsea clarissae

Litsea clarissae, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy foliage and rapid growth rate.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its ability to outcompete native vegetation.
