Leria

Leria, commonly known as the common leria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Leria is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
