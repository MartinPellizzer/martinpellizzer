Lonicera implexa var. verticillata

Lonicera implexa var. verticillata, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, often attracting birds.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
