Lupinus carnosulus

Lupinus carnosulus, commonly known as the hairy lupin, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Lupinus carnosulus is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
