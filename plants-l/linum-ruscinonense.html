Linum ruscinonense

Linum ruscinonense, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with five petals, and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
