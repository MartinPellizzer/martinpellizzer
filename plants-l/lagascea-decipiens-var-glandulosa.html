Lagascea decipiens var. glandulosa

Lagascea decipiens var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular lagascea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
