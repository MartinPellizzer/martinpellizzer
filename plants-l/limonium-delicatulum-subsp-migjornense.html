Limonium delicatulum subsp. migjornense

Limonium delicatulum subsp.

migjornense, commonly known as the coastal bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, showing a high degree of salt tolerance. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh maritime environments.
