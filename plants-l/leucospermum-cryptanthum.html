Leucospermum cryptanthum

Leucospermum cryptanthum, commonly known as the king protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish petals. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating.

The king protea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
