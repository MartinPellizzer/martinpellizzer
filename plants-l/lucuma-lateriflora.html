Lucuma lateriflora

Lucuma lateriflora, commonly known as the golden apple or lucuma, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, fleshy fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hair. Lucuma lateriflora is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in both culinary and medicinal applications for centuries.
