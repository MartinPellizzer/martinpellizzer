Liriodendron tulipifera f. fastigiatum

Liriodendron tulipifera f. fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tulip tree, is a cultivar of the tulip tree characterized by its upright growth habit and narrower leaves compared to the typical species.

It features showy, yellow-green, tulip-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctively heart-shaped base, with a smooth margin. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of temperate climates.
