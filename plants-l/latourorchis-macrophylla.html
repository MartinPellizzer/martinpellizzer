Latourorchis macrophylla

Latourorchis macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Latourorchis macrophylla is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and unique foliage.
