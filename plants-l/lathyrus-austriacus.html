Lathyrus austriacus

Lathyrus austriacus, commonly known as the Austrian vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

The plant is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
