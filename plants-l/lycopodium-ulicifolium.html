Lycopodium ulicifolium

Lycopodium ulicifolium, commonly known as the common clubmoss, is a perennial evergreen fern-like plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and spore-producing cones. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Lycopodium ulicifolium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
