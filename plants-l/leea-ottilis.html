Leea ottilis

Leea ottilis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its nocturnal blooming habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of branches.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
