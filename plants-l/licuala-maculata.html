Licuala maculata

Licuala maculata, commonly known as the spotted palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Licuala maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
