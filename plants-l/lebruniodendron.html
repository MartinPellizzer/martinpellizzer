Lebruniodendron

Lebruniodendron, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy with glossy, dark green leaves that provide dense shade. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often exuding a milky sap when injured.

Lebruniodendron is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warm climates.
