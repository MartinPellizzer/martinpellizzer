Lobivia rojasii

Lobivia rojasii, commonly known as the Rosas's Lobivia, is a succulent native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Lobivia rojasii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
