Lastrea concinna

Lastrea concinna, commonly known as the sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features simple, opposite leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in coastal and inland arid regions, thriving in well-drained environments.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in restoration projects for stabilizing degraded soils.
