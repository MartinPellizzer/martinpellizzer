Lathyrus odoratus var. zeylanicus

Lathyrus odoratus var. zeylanicus, commonly known as the Zeylanic sweet pea, is a botanical variety characterized by its fragrant, spiral-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

It is a climbing legume native to Sri Lanka and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which can be propagated in spring. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety is closely related to the common sweet pea, Lathyrus odoratus, but exhibits distinct floral and growth characteristics.
