Laurus sublanuginosa

Laurus sublanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a strong aromatic scent when crushed.

Laurus sublanuginosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and fragrance.
