Lindsayomyrtus brachyandrus

Lindsayomyrtus brachyandrus, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Lindsayomyrtus brachyandrus is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
