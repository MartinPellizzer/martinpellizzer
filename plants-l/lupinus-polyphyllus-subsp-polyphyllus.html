Lupinus polyphyllus subsp. polyphyllus

Lupinus polyphyllus subsp.

polyphyllus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has palmately compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its root system includes nitrogen-fixing nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species plays an important role in ecological systems by providing food for various pollinators and wildlife.
