Leucopsis

Leucopsis, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped, often white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. They are typically found in temperate regions and prefer well-drained soils. Leucopsis species are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.
