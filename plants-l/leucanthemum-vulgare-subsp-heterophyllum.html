Leucanthemum vulgare subsp. heterophyllum

Leucanthemum vulgare subsp.

heterophyllum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
