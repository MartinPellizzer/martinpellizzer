Lycium berlandieri var. brevilobum

Lycium berlandieri var. brevilobum, commonly known as the Sonoran Desert nightshade, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy berries that range in color from red to yellow, and its woody stems covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in arid environments, adapting to high temperatures and low rainfall. It produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
