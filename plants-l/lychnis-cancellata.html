Lychnis cancellata

Lychnis cancellata, commonly known as the white campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are oval and hairy, with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Lychnis cancellata is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
