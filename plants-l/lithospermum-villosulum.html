Lithospermum villosulum

Lithospermum villosulum, commonly known as hairy lithosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Lithospermum villosulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
