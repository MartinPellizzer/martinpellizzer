Loranthus warneckei

Loranthus warneckei, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to various regions in Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategy make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
