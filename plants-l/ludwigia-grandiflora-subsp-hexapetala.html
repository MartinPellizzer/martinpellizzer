Ludwigia grandiflora subsp. hexapetala

Ludwigia grandiflora subsp.

hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled Ludwigia, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America. It features vibrant red or orange flowers with six petals, making it a popular choice for ornamental ponds and water gardens.

The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically submerged or floating, depending on the water conditions. It thrives in warm, nutrient-rich waters and can spread rapidly through both vegetative growth and seed dispersal.

Its botanical beauty and ease of cultivation make it a favored species among aquatic plant enthusiasts.
