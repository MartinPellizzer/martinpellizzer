Lupinus polyphyllus var. subhirsutus

Lupinus polyphyllus var. subhirsutus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open meadows and rocky areas.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
