Lathyrus linnaei subsp. tournefortii

Lathyrus linnaei subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Tournefort's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its pods contain several seeds, and it is often found growing along rocky slopes and in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
