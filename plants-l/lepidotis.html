Lepidotis

Lepidotis, commonly known as the mountain balm, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants are typically shrubs or subshrubs with aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. They often have glandular hairs on their stems and leaves, which contribute to their distinctive appearance.

Lepidotis species are valued for their ornamental qualities and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
