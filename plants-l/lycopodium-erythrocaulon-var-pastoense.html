Lycopodium erythrocaulon var. pastoense

Lycopodium erythrocaulon var. pastoense, commonly known as the Pasto clubmoss, is a variant of the clubmoss genus Lycopodium.

It is a small, evergreen plant with a creeping rhizome and erect, cylindrical stems that bear spore-bearing leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive reddish or orange coloration, especially when mature. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
