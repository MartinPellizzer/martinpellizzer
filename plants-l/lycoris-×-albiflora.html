Lycoris × albiflora

Lycoris × albiflora, commonly known as the white lily-of-the-valley, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Lycoris radiata and Lycoris chinensis.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to naturalize over time.
