Lonicera periclymenum var. phyllantha

Lonicera periclymenum var. phyllantha, commonly known as the golden honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.
