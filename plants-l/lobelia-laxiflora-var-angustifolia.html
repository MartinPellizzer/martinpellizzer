Lobelia laxiflora var. angustifolia

Lobelia laxiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in humid environments, with fibrous roots and a low-growing habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
