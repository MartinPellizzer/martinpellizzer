Leonardoxa africana

Leonardoxa africana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces elongated pods that contain flat, seed-like structures called arils. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist forest habitats.

The plant also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
