Ligularia mongolica

Ligularia mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. Ligularia mongolica thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its foliage is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
