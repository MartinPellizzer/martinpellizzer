Lepidium hickenii

Lepidium hickenii, commonly known as the Hicken's cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and canyon walls. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Lepidium hickenii is often found in high-altitude regions and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
