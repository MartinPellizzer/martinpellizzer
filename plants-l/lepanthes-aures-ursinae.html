Lepanthes aures-ursinae

Lepanthes aures-ursinae, commonly known as the golden-ursine orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green coloration, which is particularly vivid in its flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in a spike, and are pollinated by specific insects.

Lepanthes aures-ursinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
