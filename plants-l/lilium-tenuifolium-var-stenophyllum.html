Lilium tenuifolium var. stenophyllum

Lilium tenuifolium var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a striking visual element to alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
