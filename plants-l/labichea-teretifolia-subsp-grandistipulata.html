Labichea teretifolia subsp. grandistipulata

Labichea teretifolia subsp.

grandistipulata, commonly known as the large-leaf labichea, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger stipules compared to other members of the species.
