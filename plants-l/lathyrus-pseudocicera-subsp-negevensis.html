Lathyrus pseudocicera subsp. negevensis

Lathyrus pseudocicera subsp.

negevensis, commonly known as the Negev lathyris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and Jordan. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments.

The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry conditions.
