Lessingianthus carvalhoi

Lessingianthus carvalhoi, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in clusters. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
