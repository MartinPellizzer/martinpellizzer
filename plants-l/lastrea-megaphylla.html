Lastrea megaphylla

Lastrea megaphylla, commonly known as the giant leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

The plant's striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
