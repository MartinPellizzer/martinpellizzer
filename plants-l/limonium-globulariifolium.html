Limonium globulariifolium

Limonium globulariifolium, commonly known as the globular-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils. It is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Limonium globulariifolium is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
