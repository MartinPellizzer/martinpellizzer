Lobelia cliffortiana var. brachypoda

Lobelia cliffortiana var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
