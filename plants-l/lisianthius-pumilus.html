Lisianthius pumilus

Lisianthius pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf lisianthus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of pink to purple petals that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, colorful plants.
