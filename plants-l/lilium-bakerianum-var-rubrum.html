Lilium bakerianum var. rubrum

Lilium bakerianum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red tiger lily, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The flowers are large, showy, and typically have a bright orange or red color with a distinctive black blotch at the base of each petal. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
