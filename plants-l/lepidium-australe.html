Lepidium australe

Lepidium australe, commonly known as the Australian cress, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. It produces small white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually alternate and oval in shape.

Lepidium australe is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a food source for various wildlife species.
