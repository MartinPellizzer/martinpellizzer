Lactuca acuminata

Lactuca acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf lettuce, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, spiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Lactuca acuminata is not typically cultivated for consumption but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
