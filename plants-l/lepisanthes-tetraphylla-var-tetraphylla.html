Lepisanthes tetraphylla var. tetraphylla

Lepisanthes tetraphylla var. tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy surface, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
