Lepanthes tricuspidata

Lepanthes tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a delicate, compact growth habit with a single, short stem and a few leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with three prominent lobes that give it its common name.

Lepanthes tricuspidata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
