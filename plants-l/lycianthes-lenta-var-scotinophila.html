Lycianthes lenta var. scotinophila

Lycianthes lenta var. scotinophila, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It features dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are used in traditional cuisine and have mild, sweet flavors.
