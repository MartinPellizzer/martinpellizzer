Linum hirsutum subsp. lanuginosum

Linum hirsutum subsp.

lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of showy, blue or purple flowers.

The plant is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. Its fibrous stems and leaves are used in traditional medicine and textile production.

The subspecies lanuginosum is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
