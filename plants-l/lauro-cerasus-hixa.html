Lauro-cerasus hixa

Lauro-cerasus hixa, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Chinese flowering apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to peaches, which ripen in late summer. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy texture, while the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.
