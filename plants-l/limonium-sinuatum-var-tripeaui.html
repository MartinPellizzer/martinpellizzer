Limonium sinuatum var. tripeaui

Limonium sinuatum var. tripeaui, commonly known as the sea helene, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish hue.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
