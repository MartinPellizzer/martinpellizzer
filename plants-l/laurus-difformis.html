Laurus difformis

Laurus difformis, commonly known as the Turkish laurel or Turkish bay tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Due to its aromatic foliage, it is also used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
