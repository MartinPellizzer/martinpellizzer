Lonicera glandulifera

Lonicera glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with glandular dots on their surfaces, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Lonicera glandulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
