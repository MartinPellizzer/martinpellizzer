Luma chrysocarpa

Luma chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs and trees adapted to acidic soils.
