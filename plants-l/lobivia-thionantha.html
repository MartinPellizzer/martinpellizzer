Lobivia thionantha

Lobivia thionantha, commonly known as the Ghost Cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and pale green to bluish-gray stems that often appear almost translucent. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. Lobivia thionantha is adapted to arid environments, featuring thick, fleshy stems that store water and reduce transpiration.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
