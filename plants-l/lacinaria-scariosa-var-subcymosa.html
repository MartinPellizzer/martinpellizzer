Lacinaria scariosa var. subcymosa

Lacinaria scariosa var. subcymosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf lacinaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or woodland areas. Its leaves are often deeply lobed, and the flowers are arranged in cymes, giving it a unique appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
