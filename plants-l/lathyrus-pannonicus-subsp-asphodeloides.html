Lathyrus pannonicus subsp. asphodeloides

Lathyrus pannonicus subsp.

asphodeloides, commonly known as the asphodeloid wild pea, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This subspecies is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
