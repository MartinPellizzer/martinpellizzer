Lithocardium longifolium

Lithocardium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved lithocardium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Lithocardium longifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
