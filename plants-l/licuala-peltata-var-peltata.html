Licuala peltata var. peltata

Licuala peltata var. peltata, commonly known as the giant panda bamboo, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, peltate leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic and tropical appearance. The plant is a type of palm, belonging to the Arecaceae family, and is known for its robust and upright growth habit. Its leaves are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their unique shape and visual impact.
