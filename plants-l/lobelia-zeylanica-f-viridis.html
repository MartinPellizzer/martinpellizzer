Lobelia zeylanica f. viridis

Lobelia zeylanica f. viridis, commonly known as the green lobelia, is a variant of the species native to tropical regions of Asia.

This plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or near water bodies. The foliage is typically a vibrant green, which gives it its distinguishing name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
