Lupinus sparsiflorus var. inopinatus

Lupinus sparsiflorus var. inopinatus, commonly known as the sparse-flower lupine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in sparsely branched racemes.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its value as a forage plant for wildlife.
