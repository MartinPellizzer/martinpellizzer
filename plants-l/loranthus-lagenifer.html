Loranthus lagenifer

Loranthus lagenifer, commonly known as the bottle-shaped mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the families Myrtaceae and Euphorbiaceae.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy coating.

Loranthus lagenifer is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.
