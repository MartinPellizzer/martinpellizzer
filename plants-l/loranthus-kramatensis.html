Loranthus kramatensis

Loranthus kramatensis, commonly known as the Kramatensis mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in Southeast Asia.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it plays a role in the ecological interactions between plants and animals.

Its presence can sometimes indicate the health of the host tree and the surrounding ecosystem.
