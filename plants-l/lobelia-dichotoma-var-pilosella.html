Lobelia dichotoma var. pilosella

Lobelia dichotoma var. pilosella, commonly known as the hairy lobelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
