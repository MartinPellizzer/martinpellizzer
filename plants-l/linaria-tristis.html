Linaria tristis

Linaria tristis, commonly known as the weedy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often considered a weed. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Linaria tristis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to drought.
