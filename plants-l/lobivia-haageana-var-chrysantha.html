Lobivia haageana var. chrysantha

Lobivia haageana var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on tall, branching stems. It is well adapted to hot, dry conditions and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
