Lathyrus inconspicuus var. stans

Lathyrus inconspicuus var. stans, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and pods that contain several small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife.
