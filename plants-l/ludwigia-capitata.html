Ludwigia capitata

Ludwigia capitata, commonly known as the spiked water primrose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

Ludwigia capitata is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in aquatic gardens due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
