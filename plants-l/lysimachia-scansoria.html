Lysimachia scansoria

Lysimachia scansoria, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Lysimachia scansoria is a member of the Primulaceae family and is known for its rapid spreading growth habit.
