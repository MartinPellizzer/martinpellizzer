Lycopodioides albocincta

Lycopodioides albocincta, commonly known as the white-banded clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are clustered at the tips of the branches.

Lycopodioides albocincta plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
