Lycopodium alopecuroides var. divaricatum

Lycopodium alopecuroides var. divaricatum, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant characterized by its compact, dense foliage.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant features diminutive, scale-like leaves that form a tight rosette, and it produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a notable species within its native habitat.
