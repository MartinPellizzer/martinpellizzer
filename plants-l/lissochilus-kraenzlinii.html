Lissochilus kraenzlinii

Lissochilus kraenzlinii, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, delicate roots that help it anchor in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic flora.
