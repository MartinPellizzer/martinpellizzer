Leionema lamprophyllum

Leionema lamprophyllum, commonly known as silver-leaved leptospermum, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
