Lotus hispidus var. subbiflorus

Lotus hispidus var. subbiflorus, commonly known as the hairy lotus or the spiny lotus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a distinctive spiny appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
