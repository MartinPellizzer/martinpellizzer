Laelia rubescens f. peduncularis

Laelia rubescens f. peduncularis, commonly known as the red-stemmed laelia, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pinkish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
