Lipochaeta calycosa

Lipochaeta calycosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, pinkish to purple flowers.

Lipochaeta calycosa is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
