Linum racemosum

Linum racemosum, commonly known as the weeping flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Linum racemosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
