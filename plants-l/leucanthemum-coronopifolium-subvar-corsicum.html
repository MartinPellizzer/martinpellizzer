Leucanthemum coronopifolium subvar. corsicum

Leucanthemum coronopifolium subvar. corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its white petals and yellow centers, forming showy composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has basal leaves that are lobed and often have a hairy texture.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
