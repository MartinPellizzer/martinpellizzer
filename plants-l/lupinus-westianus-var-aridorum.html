Lupinus westianus var. aridorum

Lupinus westianus var. aridorum, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Lupinus westianus var. aridorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
