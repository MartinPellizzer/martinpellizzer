Lepisanthes basicardia

Lepisanthes basicardia, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellowish throat. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
