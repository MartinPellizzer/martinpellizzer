Lonicera caerulea subsp. baltica

Lonicera caerulea subsp.

baltica, commonly known as the Baltic honeysuckle, is a shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Russia. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its berries are edible, turning from green to red or black as they mature, and are often used in jams and desserts.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cooler climates.
