Leiothrix beckii

Leiothrix beckii, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color, adding ornamental value. It is typically found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
