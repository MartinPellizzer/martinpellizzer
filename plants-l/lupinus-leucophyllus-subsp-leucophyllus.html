Lupinus leucophyllus subsp. leucophyllus

Lupinus leucophyllus subsp.

leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Lupinus leucophyllus subsp.

leucophyllus plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
