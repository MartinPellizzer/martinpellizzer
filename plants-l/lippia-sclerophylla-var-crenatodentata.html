Lippia sclerophylla var. crenatodentata

Lippia sclerophylla var. crenatodentata, commonly known as the serrated-leaf lippia, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with serrated edges and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its gray-green foliage and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

The species is also known for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed.
