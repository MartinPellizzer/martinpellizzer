Lotus major var. pilosus

Lotus major var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering pollutants.
