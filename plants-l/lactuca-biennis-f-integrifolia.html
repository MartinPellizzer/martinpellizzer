Lactuca biennis f. integrifolia

Lactuca biennis f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved biennial lettuce, is a cultivated form of the biennial lettuce species.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, broad leaves that are typically smooth and without lobes. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in a loose cluster, and its seeds are small and oval-shaped. It is primarily grown for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly bitter taste.

This variety is often used in salads and as a leafy green in various culinary applications.
