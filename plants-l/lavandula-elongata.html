Lavandula elongata

Lavandula elongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf lavender, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikes of fragrant purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Lavandula elongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
