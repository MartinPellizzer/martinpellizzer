Leonurus eriostachys

Leonurus eriostachys, commonly known as the hairy leonurus, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
