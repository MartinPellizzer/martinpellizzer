Leopoldia graminifolia

Leopoldia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved leopoldia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
