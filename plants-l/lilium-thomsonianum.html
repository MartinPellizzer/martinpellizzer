Lilium thomsonianum

Lilium thomsonianum, commonly known as the Thompson's lily, is a species of flowering plant in the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

This perennial herb thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
