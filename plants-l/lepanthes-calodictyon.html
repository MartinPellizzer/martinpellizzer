Lepanthes calodictyon

Lepanthes calodictyon, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its leaves are usually elliptical and glossy, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Lepanthes calodictyon is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
