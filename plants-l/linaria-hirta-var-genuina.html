Linaria hirta var. genuina

Linaria hirta var. genuina, commonly known as the rough toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
