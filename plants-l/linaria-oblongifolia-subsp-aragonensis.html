Linaria oblongifolia subsp. aragonensis

Linaria oblongifolia subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon narrowleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
