Lobivia sicuaniensis

Lobivia sicuaniensis, commonly known as the Sicilian Lobivia, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the island of Sicily.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a rounded, fleshy stem that can reach up to 20 centimeters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its succulent form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native region.
