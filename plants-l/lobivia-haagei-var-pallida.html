Lobivia haagei var. pallida

Lobivia haagei var. pallida, commonly known as the pale lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and pale green to blue-gray foliage. The plant features short, cylindrical stems with spines and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its muted foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
