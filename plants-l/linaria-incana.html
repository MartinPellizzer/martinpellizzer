Linaria incana

Linaria incana, commonly known as the hairy toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Linaria incana has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
