Lindera mollis

Lindera mollis, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Lindera mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
