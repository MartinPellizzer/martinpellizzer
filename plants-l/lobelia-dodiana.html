Lobelia dodiana

Lobelia dodiana, commonly known as the Scottish lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in peat bogs and woodland edges.

Lobelia dodiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
