Lithospermum fruticosum var. consobrinum

Lithospermum fruticosum var. consobrinum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
