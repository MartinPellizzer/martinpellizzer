Lobivia haematantha f. pectinifera

Lobivia haematantha f. pectinifera, commonly known as the Crimson Lobivia, is a unique hybrid of the genus Lobivia, native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of succulent plants. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
