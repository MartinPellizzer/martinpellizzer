Lampranthus sauerae

Lampranthus sauerae, commonly known as the Orange Ghost, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, often hairy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that range from orange to red. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
