Lathyrus megalanthos

Lathyrus megalanthos, commonly known as the giant sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces clusters of elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
