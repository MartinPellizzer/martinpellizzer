Luzula sylvatica var. henriquesii

Luzula sylvatica var. henriquesii, commonly known as the Henrique's wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are pale green to brownish in color.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
