Ligustrum quihoui var. latifolium

Ligustrum quihoui var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf privet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5–10 cm long and 2–4 cm wide. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The varietal form latifolium is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical species.
