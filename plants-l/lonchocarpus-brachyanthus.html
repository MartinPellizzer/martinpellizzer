Lonchocarpus brachyanthus

Lonchocarpus brachyanthus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The seeds are enclosed in distinctive, woody pods that split open to release the seeds when mature.
