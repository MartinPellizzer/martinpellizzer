Leptalix quadrangulata

Leptalix quadrangulata, commonly known as the four-cornered leek, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and broad, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in local cuisines.

Leptalix quadrangulata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
