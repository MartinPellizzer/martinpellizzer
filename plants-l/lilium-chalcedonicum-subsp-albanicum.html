Lilium chalcedonicum subsp. albanicum

Lilium chalcedonicum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian lily, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive red-orange stigma.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is considered a symbol of the natural heritage of Albania.
