Lupinus polyphyllus subsp. superbus

Lupinus polyphyllus subsp.

superbus, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets, typical of the legume family Fabaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky slopes. Lupinus polyphyllus subsp.

superbus plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant communities.
