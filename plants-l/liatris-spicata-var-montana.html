Liatris spicata var. montana

Liatris spicata var. montana, commonly known as mountain blazing star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, spiky flower heads composed of numerous purple to pinkish-red tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy and grow along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
