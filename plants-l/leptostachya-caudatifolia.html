Leptostachya caudatifolia

Leptostachya caudatifolia, commonly known as the long-tailed bamboo, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, drooping inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often tinged with purple or pink. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall bamboo-like appearance.

Leptostachya caudatifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant and delicate foliage.
