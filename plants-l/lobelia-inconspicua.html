Lobelia inconspicua

Lobelia inconspicua, commonly known as the common lobelia, is a small herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Lobelia inconspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
