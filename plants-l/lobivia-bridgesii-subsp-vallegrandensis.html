Lobivia bridgesii subsp. vallegrandensis

Lobivia bridgesii subsp.

vallegrandensis, commonly known as the Vallegrandensis Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Argentina. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise muted appearance. Its leaves are typically short and cylindrical, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
