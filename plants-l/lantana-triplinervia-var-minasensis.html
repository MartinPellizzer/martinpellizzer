Lantana triplinervia var. minasensis

Lantana triplinervia var. minasensis, commonly known as Brazilian flame bush, is a shrub native to South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red, depending on the stage of bloom. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
