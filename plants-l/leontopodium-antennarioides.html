Leontopodium antennarioides

Leontopodium antennarioides, commonly known as the alpine edelweiss, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive and fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are surrounded by the same fuzzy covering, which helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often associated with alpine meadows and is a symbol of resilience in extreme climates.
