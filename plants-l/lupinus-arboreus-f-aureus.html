Lupinus arboreus f. aureus

Lupinus arboreus f. aureus, commonly known as the golden tree lupine, is a variant of the tree lupine native to the western United States.

This plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage and woody stems. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The golden tree lupine typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
