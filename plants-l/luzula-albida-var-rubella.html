Luzula albida var. rubella

Luzula albida var. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the base, and bear small, pale green to white spikelets. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Luzula albida var. rubella thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
