Linosyris vulgaris var. minor

Linosyris vulgaris var. minor, commonly known as the lesser common willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, often hairy stems that branch near the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks, meadows, and woodland edges.
