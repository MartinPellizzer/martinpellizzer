Luehea rugosa

Luehea rugosa, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and palmate leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
