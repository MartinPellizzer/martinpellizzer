Liriodendron tulipifera subsp. integrifolium

Liriodendron tulipifera subsp.

integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-flowered tulip tree, is a rare subspecies native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy, yellow-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically smooth-edged and alternate, contributing to its distinct appearance. This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
