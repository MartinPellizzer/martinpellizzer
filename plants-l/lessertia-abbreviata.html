Lessertia abbreviata

Lessertia abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the eastern and southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, stout trunk and a crown of short, stiff, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
