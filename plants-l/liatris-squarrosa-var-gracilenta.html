Liatris squarrosa var. gracilenta

Liatris squarrosa var. gracilenta, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear whorled clusters of narrow, spike-like flower heads. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive, fluffy appearance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist to mesic soil conditions.
