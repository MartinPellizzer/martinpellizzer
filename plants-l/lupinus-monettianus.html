Lupinus monettianus

Lupinus monettianus, commonly known as the monett lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant features erect stems with whorled leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Lupinus monettianus is also an important food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
