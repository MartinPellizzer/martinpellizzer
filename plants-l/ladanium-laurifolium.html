Ladanium laurifolium

Ladanium laurifolium, commonly known as the golden laurel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and emit a subtle aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
