Liparis layardii

Liparis layardii, commonly known as the Layard's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its small, clustered inflorescences.

Liparis layardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
