Ludwigia africana

Ludwigia africana, commonly known as African Ludwigia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Ludwigia africana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
