Lachemilla holmgrenii

Lachemilla holmgrenii, commonly known as the Holmgren's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded forest environments.

Lachemilla holmgrenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and contributing to native plant communities.
