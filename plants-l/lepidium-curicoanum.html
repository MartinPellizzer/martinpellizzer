Lepidium curicoanum

Lepidium curicoanum, commonly known as the Curicoa mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and has small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a peppery taste, similar to other members of the mustard family.

Lepidium curicoanum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
