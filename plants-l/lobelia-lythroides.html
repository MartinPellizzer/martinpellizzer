Lobelia lythroides

Lobelia lythroides, commonly known as the purple lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Lobelia lythroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
