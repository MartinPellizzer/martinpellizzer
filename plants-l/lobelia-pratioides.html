Lobelia pratioides

Lobelia pratioides, commonly known as the meadow lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Lobelia pratioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
