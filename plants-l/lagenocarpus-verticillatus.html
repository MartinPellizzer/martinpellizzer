Lagenocarpus verticillatus

Lagenocarpus verticillatus, commonly known as the spiral fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves and fruits, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The botanical features of Lagenocarpus verticillatus make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
