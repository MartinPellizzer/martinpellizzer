Lilium tenuifolium var. chrysanthum

Lilium tenuifolium var. chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden thread lily, is a variant of the narrow-leaved lily native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its flowers are usually borne on slender stems and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
