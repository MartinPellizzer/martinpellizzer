Lycopodium curvatum var. cymosum

Lycopodium curvatum var. cymosum, commonly known as the clustered clubmoss, is a member of the Lycopodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of spore-bearing structures. The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces sporophytes with strobili that contain spores, which are released for reproduction.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
