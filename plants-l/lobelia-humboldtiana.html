Lobelia humboldtiana

Lobelia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in cloud forests. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Lobelia humboldtiana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
