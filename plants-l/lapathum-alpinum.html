Lapathum alpinum

Lapathum alpinum, commonly known as alpine betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

Lapathum alpinum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
