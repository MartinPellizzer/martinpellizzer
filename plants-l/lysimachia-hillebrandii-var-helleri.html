Lysimachia hillebrandii var. helleri

Lysimachia hillebrandii var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's yellow loosestrife, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features creeping rhizomes and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers throughout the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
