Lophocereus schottii f. monstrosus

Lophocereus schottii f. monstrosus, commonly known as the monstrose cactus, is a unique variant of the Lophocereus schottii species.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that form a dense, rounded shape, often resembling a ball or a bush. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is native to arid regions of Mexico and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Due to its unusual growth form, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
