Liparis simmondsii

Liparis simmondsii, commonly known as the Simmonds' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its complex floral structures and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Liparis simmondsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
