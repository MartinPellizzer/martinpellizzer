Lasianthus ferrugineus

Lasianthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine, rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
