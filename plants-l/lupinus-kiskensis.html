Lupinus kiskensis

Lupinus kiskensis, commonly known as the Kiski lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Lupinus kiskensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the area.
