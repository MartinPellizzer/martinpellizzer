Lotus cytisoides f. aurantius

Lotus cytisoides f. aurantius, commonly known as the golden lotus, is a unique variant of the lotus plant characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds. The golden lotus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of legumes, including nitrogen-fixing capabilities through its root nodules.
