Lobelia spicata var. campanulata

Lobelia spicata var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate floral display.
