Lychnis ajanensis

Lychnis ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajan's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually erect and branching.

Lychnis ajanensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
