Lysimachia christinae

Lysimachia christinae, commonly known as Christ's herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Lysimachia christinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
