Lilium martagon var. alternifolium

Lilium martagon var. alternifolium, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a striking perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, nodding, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red, often with a white or yellow throat. The leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
