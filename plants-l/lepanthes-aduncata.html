Lepanthes aduncata

Lepanthes aduncata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Lepanthes aduncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
