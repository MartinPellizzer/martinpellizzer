Lepanthes cucullata

Lepanthes cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Lepanthes cucullata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
