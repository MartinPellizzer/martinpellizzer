Lasianthus kurzii var. howii

Lasianthus kurzii var. howii, commonly known as the How's lasianthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowering stems are often branched.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a subject of botanical study due to its unique floral structure.
