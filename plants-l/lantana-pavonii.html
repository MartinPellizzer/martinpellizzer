Lantana pavonii

Lantana pavonii, commonly known as the purple lantana, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and sandy soils.
