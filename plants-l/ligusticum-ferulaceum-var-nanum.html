Ligusticum ferulaceum var. nanum

Ligusticum ferulaceum var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf Chinese lovage, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small white to pale yellow flowers appear in umbrella-shaped clusters, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
