Lobelia paradoxa

Lobelia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Lobelia paradoxa is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 feet.

Its unique appearance and habitat preferences make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
