Leptonychia occidentalis

Leptonychia occidentalis, commonly known as the western leatherleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to its ecological niche, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
