Leucanthemum atratum var. heterophyllum

Leucanthemum atratum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the dark-leaved oxeye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dark green, velvety foliage with a distinctive central stem that bears yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
