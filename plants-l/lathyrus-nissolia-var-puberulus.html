Lathyrus nissolia var. puberulus

Lathyrus nissolia var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy pea or pubescent lathyrus, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
