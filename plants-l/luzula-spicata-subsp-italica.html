Luzula spicata subsp. italica

Luzula spicata subsp.

italica, commonly known as Italian wood rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly referred to as the grass family, though it is not a true grass.

The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of dark green, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in summer, typically arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
