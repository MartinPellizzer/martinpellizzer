Liatris garberi

Liatris garberi, commonly known as Garber's blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Liatris garberi is also valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
