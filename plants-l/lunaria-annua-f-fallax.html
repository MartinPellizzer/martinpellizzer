Lunaria annua f. fallax

Lunaria annua f. fallax, commonly known as the fallax form of the moonflower, is a variant of the common moonflower.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often twisted seed pods that differ from the typical spherical shape. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to Europe. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

This form is valued in horticulture for its distinctive appearance and ornamental value.
