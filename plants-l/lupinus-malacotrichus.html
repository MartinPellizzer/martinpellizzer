Lupinus malacotrichus

Lupinus malacotrichus, commonly known as the blue lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Lupinus malacotrichus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

It is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
