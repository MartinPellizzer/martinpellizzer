Lathyrus magellanicus var. gladiatus

Lathyrus magellanicus var. gladiatus, commonly known as the Magellanic vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
