Laurus puberula

Laurus puberula, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel or sweet bay, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It has aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or container gardening.
