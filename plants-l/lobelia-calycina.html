Lobelia calycina

Lobelia calycina, commonly known as the silverleaf lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Lobelia calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
