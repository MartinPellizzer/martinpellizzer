Lyonia lippoldii

Lyonia lippoldii, commonly known as the Lippold's lyonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Lyonia lippoldii is often found in moist, open habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands.
