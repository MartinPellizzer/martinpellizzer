Leucanthemum sibiricum var. latilobum

Leucanthemum sibiricum var. latilobum, commonly known as the Siberian tansy or Siberian oxeye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Siberia and parts of northern Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are gray-green in color with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers with white ray florets, forming a composite flower head that resembles a daisy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
