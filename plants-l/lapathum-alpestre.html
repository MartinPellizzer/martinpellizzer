Lapathum alpestre

Lapathum alpestre, commonly known as alpine burnet, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pink or reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces numerous slender, flowering stems.

Lapathum alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
