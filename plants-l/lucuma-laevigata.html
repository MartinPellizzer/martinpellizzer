Lucuma laevigata

Lucuma laevigata, commonly known as the golden apple, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, nutty flavor. The fruit's exterior is covered in a rough, hairy layer that becomes smoother as it ripens.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit used in traditional cuisine.
