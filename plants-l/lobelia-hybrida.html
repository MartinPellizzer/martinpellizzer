Lobelia hybrida

Lobelia hybrida, commonly known as the cardinal flower, is a vibrant perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Lobelia hybrida is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
