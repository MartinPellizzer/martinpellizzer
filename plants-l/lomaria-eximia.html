Lomaria eximia

Lomaria eximia, commonly known as the golden lomaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Lomaria eximia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
