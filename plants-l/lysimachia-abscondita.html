Lysimachia abscondita

Lysimachia abscondita, commonly known as the hidden lyssandra, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
