Liparis galeottiana

Liparis galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and subtle fragrance.

Liparis galeottiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under the right conditions.
