Ledum

Ledum, commonly known as laburnum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

These plants are characterized by their shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically evergreen, with a waxy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Ledum species are often found in acidic, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

They are also noted for their medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
