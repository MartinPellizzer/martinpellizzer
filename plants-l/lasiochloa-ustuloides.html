Lasiochloa ustuloides

Lasiochloa ustuloides, commonly known as the burnt grass sedge, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and periodic flooding.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
