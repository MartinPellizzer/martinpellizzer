Lobelia biserrata var. spicata

Lobelia biserrata var. spicata, commonly known as the spiked lobelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
