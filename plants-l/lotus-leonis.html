Lotus leonis

Lotus leonis, commonly known as the lion's lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
