Lobelia paludosa var. floridana

Lobelia paludosa var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida bog lobelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of Florida.

It typically grows in marshy, acidic soils and thrives in moist to waterlogged conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with whorled leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. Its showy, tubular blue or violet flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
