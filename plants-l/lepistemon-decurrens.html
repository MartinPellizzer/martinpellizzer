Lepistemon decurrens

Lepistemon decurrens, commonly known as the creeping lepistemon, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features slender, trailing stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a prominent central spur.

Lepistemon decurrens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
