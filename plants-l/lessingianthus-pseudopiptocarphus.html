Lessingianthus pseudopiptocarphus

Lessingianthus pseudopiptocarphus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features showy, violet to purple ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
