Lepanthes ingridiana

Lepanthes ingridiana, commonly known as the Ingrid's slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch that aids in pollination.

Lepanthes ingridiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
