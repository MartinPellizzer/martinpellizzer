Lessingianthus linearis

Lessingianthus linearis, commonly known as the slender sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, tubular disk flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
