Laurus mucronata

Laurus mucronata, commonly known as the bay laurel or sweet bay, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen, aromatic leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

Laurus mucronata typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters in favorable conditions.
