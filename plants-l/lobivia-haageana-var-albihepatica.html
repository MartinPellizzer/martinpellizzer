Lobivia haageana var. albihepatica

Lobivia haageana var. albihepatica, commonly known as the White Liveried Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale markings. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers during the growing season, adding visual interest to its appearance. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
