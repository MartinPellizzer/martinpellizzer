Limnochloa montevidensis

Limnochloa montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the substrate. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in green or brown hues.

Limnochloa montevidensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
