Leucocroton ekmanii

Leucocroton ekmanii, commonly known as the white croton, is a striking tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to bronze, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
