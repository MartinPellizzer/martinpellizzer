Linaria shahroudensis

Linaria shahroudensis, commonly known as the Shahroud flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that reduce water loss.

Linaria shahroudensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in dry climates.
