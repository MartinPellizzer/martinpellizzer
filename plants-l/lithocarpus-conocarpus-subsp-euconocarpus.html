euconocarpus, commonly known as the broad-leafed stone oak, is a member of the Fagaceae family, characterized by its hard, woody fruits and evergreen foliage. It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical and subtropical forest environments.
