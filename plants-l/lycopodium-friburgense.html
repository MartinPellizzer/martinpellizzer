Lycopodium friburgense

Lycopodium friburgense, commonly known as the Friburg clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping stem with whorled leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. It produces sporophylls that bear microsporangia and megasporangia, typical of its evolutionary lineage.

Lycopodium friburgense plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
