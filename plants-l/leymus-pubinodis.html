Leymus pubinodis

Leymus pubinodis, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems with dense, hairy leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are small and often tightly packed.

Leymus pubinodis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
