Lindera hamiltonii

Lindera hamiltonii, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in the fall.

Lindera hamiltonii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its fragrant foliage and ecological benefits.
