Lucuma anibifolia

Lucuma anibifolia, commonly known as the Andean sweet potato or lucuma, is a tuberous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its starchy, sweet-tasting tubers that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, white powder, while its flowers are small and predominantly white or pale yellow.

Lucuma anibifolia is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential medicinal properties.
