Lycium erythraeum

Lycium erythraeum, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Lycium erythraeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
