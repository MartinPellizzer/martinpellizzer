Linum serbicum

Linum serbicum, commonly known as Serbian flax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and showy blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber, similar to common flax.

Linum serbicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
