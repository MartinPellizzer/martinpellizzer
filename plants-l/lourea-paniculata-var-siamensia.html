Lourea paniculata var. siamensia

Lourea paniculata var. siamensia, commonly known as the Siam lourea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
