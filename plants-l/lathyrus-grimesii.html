Lathyrus grimesii

Lathyrus grimesii, commonly known as the Grimes' vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Lathyrus grimesii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.
