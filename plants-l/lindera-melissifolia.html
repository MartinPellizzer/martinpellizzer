Lindera melissifolia

Lindera melissifolia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. Its foliage has a distinctive, spicy scent, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Lindera melissifolia is often found in woodland edges and streambanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
