Lobivia steinmannii var. leucacantha

Lobivia steinmannii var. leucacantha, commonly known as the white-tipped lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and spiny, segmented stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a feature common in desert plants to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained, sandy soils.
