Loranthus nutans

Loranthus nutans, commonly known as the nodding mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, such as eucalyptus and acacia.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish-yellow, and its berries are red or orange, containing sticky seeds that adhere to birds. Loranthus nutans is native to Australia and parts of Asia, where it thrives in a variety of forested environments.

Its parasitic nature and unique ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
