Laurus rotundifolia

Laurus rotundifolia, commonly known as bay laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was revered in Greek and Roman cultures.
