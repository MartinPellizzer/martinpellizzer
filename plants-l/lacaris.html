Lacaris

Lacaris, commonly known as the lacarid liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, leaf-like structures called thallus, which are typically less than 1 cm in size. Lacaris thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or on rocky surfaces. The plant reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

Its simple morphology and reliance on water for reproduction reflect its evolutionary adaptation to humid, nutrient-rich habitats.
