Loranthus surinamensis

Loranthus surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the tropical regions of South America.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using the light it receives. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by influencing the health and diversity of its host trees.
