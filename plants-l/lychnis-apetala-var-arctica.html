Lychnis apetala var. arctica

Lychnis apetala var. arctica, commonly known as the Arctic sandpink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh, cold environments. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to survive in extreme climatic conditions.
