Licaria applanata

Licaria applanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf licaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features palmate leaves with a smooth, flat surface and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or in dense undergrowth.

Licaria applanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
