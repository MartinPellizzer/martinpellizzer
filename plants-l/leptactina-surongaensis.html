Leptactina surongaensis

Leptactina surongaensis, commonly known as the Suronga orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically display a mix of white and purple hues. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in cloud forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
