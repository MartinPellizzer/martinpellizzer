Lupinus pulloviridus

Lupinus pulloviridus, commonly known as the yellow lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Lupinus pulloviridus typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.
