Lantana microcarpa

Lantana microcarpa, commonly known as the common lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, red, or purple, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Lantana microcarpa is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.
