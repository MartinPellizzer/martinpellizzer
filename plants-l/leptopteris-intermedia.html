Leptopteris intermedia

Leptopteris intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate leptopteris, is a fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds that are typically arched or pendulous. The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. Leptopteris intermedia thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of fern diversity and adaptation.
