Linaria altaica

Linaria altaica, commonly known as the Altai toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Linaria altaica typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
