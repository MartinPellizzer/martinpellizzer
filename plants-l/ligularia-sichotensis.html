Ligularia sichotensis

Ligularia sichotensis, commonly known as the Sichotian ligularia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white margin, and it produces yellow-orange composite flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
