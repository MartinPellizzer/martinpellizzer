Lilium philadelphicum var. andinum

Lilium philadelphicum var. andinum, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive white throat. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem that supports its floral display. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
