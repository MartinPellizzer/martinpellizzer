Leptospermum multicaule

Leptospermum multicaule, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is well adapted to salty environments.

Leptospermum multicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
