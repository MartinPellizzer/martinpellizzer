Lycopodium yakusimense

Lycopodium yakusimense, commonly known as the Yakusim Island clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, specifically Yakushima Island, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves, and it produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches. Lycopodium yakusimense plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
