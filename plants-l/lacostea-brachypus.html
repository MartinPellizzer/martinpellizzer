Lacostea brachypus

Lacostea brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
