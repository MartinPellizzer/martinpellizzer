Lugonia

Lugonia, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, slender stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white and pink.

Lugonia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
