Lithospermum revolutum

Lithospermum revolutum, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is known for containing compounds with medicinal properties, including lithospermic acid.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland and prairie habitats.
