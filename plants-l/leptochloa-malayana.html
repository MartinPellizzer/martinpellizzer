Leptochloa malayana

Leptochloa malayana, commonly known as the Malay flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is characterized by its slender, flat leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and grasslands.

Leptochloa malayana is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
