Leontodon taraxacoides f. leiolepis

Leontodon taraxacoides f. leiolepis, commonly known as the smooth dandelion, is a variant of the dandelion species characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and yellow composite flowers.

This plant is typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers develop into fluffy white seed heads. The plant is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
