Linaria spuria var. racemigera

Linaria spuria var. racemigera, commonly known as the racemose toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have four petals arranged in a bilaterally symmetrical pattern. This variant is distinguished by its racemose inflorescence, which consists of a long, elongated stem bearing multiple flowers.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
