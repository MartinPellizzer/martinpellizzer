Lomaria oceanica

Lomaria oceanica, commonly known as the sea lomaria, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help stabilize sandy or rocky shores.

Lomaria oceanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting coastal biodiversity.
