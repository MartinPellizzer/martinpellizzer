Lepanthes rubripetala

Lepanthes rubripetala, commonly known as the red-petaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and sepals, which contrast with its white or pale green throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Lepanthes rubripetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
