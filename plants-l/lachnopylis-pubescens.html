Lachnopylis pubescens

Lachnopylis pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
