Lycopodium thyoides

Lycopodium thyoides, commonly known as the northern clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the foliage. Lycopodium thyoides thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.

It plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
