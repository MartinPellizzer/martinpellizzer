Lahaya corymbosa

Lahaya corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet apple tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible fruits resembling small apples. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy green surface.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its sweet, aromatic flavor.
