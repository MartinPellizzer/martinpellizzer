Leopoldia erzincanica

Leopoldia erzincanica, commonly known as the Erzincan lily, is a rare and endemic plant species native to Turkey.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is characterized by its unique floral structure and is considered a valuable species in botanical research.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is classified as critically endangered.
