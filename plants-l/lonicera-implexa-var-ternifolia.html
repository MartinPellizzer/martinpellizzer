Lonicera implexa var. ternifolia

Lonicera implexa var. ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves that often have three lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red or orange and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
