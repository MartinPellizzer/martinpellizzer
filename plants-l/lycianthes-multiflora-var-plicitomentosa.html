Lycianthes multiflora var. plicitomentosa

Lycianthes multiflora var. plicitomentosa, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
