Lupinus albifrons subsp. eminens

Lupinus albifrons subsp.

eminens, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of white or pale blue flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
