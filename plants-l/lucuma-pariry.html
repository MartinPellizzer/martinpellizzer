Lucuma pariry

Lucuma pariry, commonly known as the golden apple, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Capsicaceae and is closely related to the chili pepper. The plant produces large, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.
