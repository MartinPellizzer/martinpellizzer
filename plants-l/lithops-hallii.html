Lithops hallii

Lithops hallii, commonly known as the Cape Town beauty, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, stone-like leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. Lithops hallii is often found growing in rocky soils and among granite outcrops.

Its unique appearance and ability to mimic its surroundings make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
