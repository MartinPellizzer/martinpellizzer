Lepanthes caesariata

Lepanthes caesariata, commonly known as the Caesar's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a distinctive greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant flowers.

Lepanthes caesariata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and colorful appearance.
