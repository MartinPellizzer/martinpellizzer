Lodhra microphylla

Lodhra microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved sacred fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in pendulous inflorescences, followed by round, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is revered in Ayurvedic medicine for its medicinal properties and is also used in religious rituals.
