Lobelia gloria-montis subsp. longibracteata

Lobelia gloria-montis subsp.

longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the flower buds, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
