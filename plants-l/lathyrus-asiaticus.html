Lathyrus asiaticus

Lathyrus asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian vetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Lathyrus asiaticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
