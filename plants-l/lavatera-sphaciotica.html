Lavatera sphaciotica

Lavatera sphaciotica, commonly known as the narrowleaf tree mallow, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are narrow and elliptical with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a violet or purple center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.

Lavatera sphaciotica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
