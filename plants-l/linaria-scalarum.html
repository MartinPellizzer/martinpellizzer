Linaria scalarum

Linaria scalarum, commonly known as the scalycup fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its linear, lance-shaped leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Linaria scalarum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
