Leandra magnipetala

Leandra magnipetala, commonly known as the giant leandra, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant pink or red hues. The plant has elongated, waxy leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a dense rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
