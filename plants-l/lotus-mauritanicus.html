Lotus mauritanicus

Lotus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian lotus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found growing along riverbanks and in coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

The seeds of Lotus mauritanicus are also an important food source for various wildlife species.
