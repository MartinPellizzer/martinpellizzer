Lightfootia goetzeana

Lightfootia goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's lightfoot, is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
