Leontodon taraxacum var. obovatum

Leontodon taraxacum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The flowers are yellow and consist of a single central cone surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It produces a distinctive seed head with parachute-like seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
