Luma myrtoides

Luma myrtoides, commonly known as the silver mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense, mist-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Luma myrtoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
