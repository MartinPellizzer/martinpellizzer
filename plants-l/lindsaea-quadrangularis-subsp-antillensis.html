Lindsaea quadrangularis subsp. antillensis

Lindsaea quadrangularis subsp.

antillensis, commonly known as the Antillean lindsaea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the Caribbean region. It is a terrestrial epiphytic plant, typically found growing on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive quadrangular stem. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale green flowers, and its foliage is often covered in fine, soft trichomes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
