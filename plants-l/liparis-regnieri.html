Liparis regnieri

Liparis regnieri, commonly known as the Regnier's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance, featuring slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are often rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Liparis regnieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
