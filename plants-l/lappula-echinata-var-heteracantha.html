Lappula echinata var. heteracantha

Lappula echinata var. heteracantha, commonly known as the hairy lappula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that surround the small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The hairy lappula is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
