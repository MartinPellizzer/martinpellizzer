Leonurus urticifolius

Leonurus urticifolius, commonly known as hedgehog's breakfast, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its common name reflects its early spring blooming habit, providing a source of nectar for early pollinators.
