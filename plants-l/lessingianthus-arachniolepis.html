Lessingianthus arachniolepis

Lessingianthus arachniolepis, commonly known as the spider-hair sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique appearance, including the fine, spider-like hairs on its stems, makes it a striking ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
