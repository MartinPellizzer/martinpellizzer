Lilium tsingtauense

Lilium tsingtauense, commonly known as the Tsingtau lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces upright stems with large, often fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

This species is also valued for its historical significance in botanical horticulture.
