Limonium sundingii

Limonium sundingii, commonly known as the Swedish sea lavender, is a flowering plant native to southern Scandinavia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Limonium sundingii thrives in coastal and sandy environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
