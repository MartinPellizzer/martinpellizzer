Linum setaceum f. robustum

Linum setaceum f. robustum, commonly known as the robust linnseed, is a variant of the common flax species.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and larger, more prominent leaves compared to the typical form. The plant produces slender, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and agricultural settings.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential fiber production.
