Lycopersicon glandulosum

Lycopersicon glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not edible and should be handled with care.
