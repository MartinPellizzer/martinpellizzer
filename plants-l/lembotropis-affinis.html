Lembotropis affinis

Lembotropis affinis, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and hardy nature.
