Lycium italicum

Lycium italicum, commonly known as Italian boxthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny branches and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in hedges or as an ornamental plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.
