Lonicera linderifolia subsp. konoi

Lonicera linderifolia subsp.

konoi, commonly known as the Konoi honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
