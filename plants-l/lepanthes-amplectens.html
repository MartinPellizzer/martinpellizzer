Lepanthes amplectens

Lepanthes amplectens, commonly known as the clasping orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with white markings, which are typically borne on erect, slender stems. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often clasping around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Lepanthes amplectens is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
