Limnanthes macounii

Limnanthes macounii, commonly known as the macoun's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central stigma. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Limnanthes macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
