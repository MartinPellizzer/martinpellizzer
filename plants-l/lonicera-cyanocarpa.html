Lonicera cyanocarpa

Lonicera cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are covered in fine, yellowish hairs.

Lonicera cyanocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
