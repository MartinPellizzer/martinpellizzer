Lantana tiliifolia var. glandulosa

Lantana tiliifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular lantana, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in glandular dots. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
