Lorentea brevipedunculata

Lorentea brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Lorentea brevipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
