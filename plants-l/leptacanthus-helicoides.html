Leptacanthus helicoides

Leptacanthus helicoides, commonly known as the spiral-leaved waterplant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats, such as ponds and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
