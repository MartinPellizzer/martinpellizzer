Lampranthus occultans

Lampranthus occultans, commonly known as the hidden flame or ghost flower, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
