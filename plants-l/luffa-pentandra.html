Luffa pentandra

Luffa pentandra, commonly known as the sponge gourd, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its fibrous, spongy fruit that matures into a lightweight, porous structure. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature, and its fibrous interior is used for scrubbing or as a natural sponge.

Luffa pentandra thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
