Laserpitium prutenicum var. hirtum

Laserpitium prutenicum var. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that form a dense cluster. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single, terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
