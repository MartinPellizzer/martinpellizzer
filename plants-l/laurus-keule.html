Laurus keule

Laurus keule, commonly known as the Keule laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

Laurus keule is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
