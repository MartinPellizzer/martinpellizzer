Lomaria coriacea

Lomaria coriacea, commonly known as the rough lomaria, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and rough, textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Lomaria coriacea is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant suitable for dry and coastal environments.
