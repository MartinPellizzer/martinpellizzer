Lepidagathis fasciculata var. fasciculata

Lepidagathis fasciculata var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered lepidagathis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
