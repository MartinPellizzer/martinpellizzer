Lophiolepis arachnoidea

Lophiolepis arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider-leaf flax, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, spider-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers a dry climate.
