Lantana reticulata var. strigosa

Lantana reticulata var. strigosa, commonly known as silverleaf lantana, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

However, it can be invasive in some areas and requires careful management to prevent ecological disruption.
