Loranthus luteovittatus

Loranthus luteovittatus, commonly known as the yellow-striped mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it produces its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the light it captures. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy, red berries.

This species is native to southeastern Australia and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.
