Lysionotus involucratus

Lysionotus involucratus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural areas. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Lysionotus involucratus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated fields.
