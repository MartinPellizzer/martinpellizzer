Lilium Oxypetalum

Lilium oxypetalum, known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. This species is prized for its elegant, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered by some conservation groups. Its unique beauty and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike.
