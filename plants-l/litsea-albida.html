Litsea albida

Litsea albida, commonly known as the white litsea, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns yellow when mature, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
