Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp. pseudopulverulenta

Leucanthemopsis pallida subsp.

pseudopulverulenta, commonly known as the pale leucanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and low maintenance requirements.
