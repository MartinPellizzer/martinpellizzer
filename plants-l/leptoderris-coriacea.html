Leptoderris coriacea

Leptoderris coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems covered in a rough, leathery bark. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Leptoderris coriacea is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
