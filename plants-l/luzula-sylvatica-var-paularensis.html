Luzula sylvatica var. paularensis

Luzula sylvatica var. paularensis, commonly known as the Pauli's wood rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
