Laurus burmanni

Laurus burmanni, commonly known as Burmann's laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid, lowland forest environments.

Laurus burmanni is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
