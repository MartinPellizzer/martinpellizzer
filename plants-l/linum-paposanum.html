Linum paposanum

Linum paposanum, commonly known as the pink flax or Persian flax, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Linum paposanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its fibers and medicinal properties.
