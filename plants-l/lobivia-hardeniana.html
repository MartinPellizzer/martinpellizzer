Lobivia hardeniana

Lobivia hardeniana, commonly known as the hardy lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant features spiny areoles and produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its stems for survival in dry conditions.

Lobivia hardeniana is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
