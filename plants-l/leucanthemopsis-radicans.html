Leucanthemopsis radicans

Leucanthemopsis radicans, commonly known as the spreading leucanthemum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features silver-gray foliage and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice in horticulture.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
