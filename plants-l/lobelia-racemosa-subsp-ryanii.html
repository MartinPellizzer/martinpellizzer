Lobelia racemosa subsp. ryanii

Lobelia racemosa subsp.

ryanii, commonly known as the Ryan's lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in racemes during the spring and early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along streambanks and in shaded woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
