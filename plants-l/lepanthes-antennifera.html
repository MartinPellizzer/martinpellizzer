Lepanthes antennifera

Lepanthes antennifera, commonly known as the feather-antler orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, often leafless stem and produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The flowers have a distinctive, elongated spur that resembles an antenna, giving the species its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
