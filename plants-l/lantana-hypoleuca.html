Lantana hypoleuca

Lantana hypoleuca, commonly known as white lantana, is a flowering shrub native to the southern United States.

It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun.

Lantana hypoleuca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
