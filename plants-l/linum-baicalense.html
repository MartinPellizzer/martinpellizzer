Linum baicalense

Linum baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal flax, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Linum baicalense is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in moist meadows and riverbanks.
