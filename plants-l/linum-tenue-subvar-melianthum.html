Linum tenue subvar. melianthum

Linum tenue subvar. melianthum, commonly known as the slender flax or lesser flax, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in loose clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in textile production.
