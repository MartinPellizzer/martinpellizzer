Leea rubra var. polyphylla

Leea rubra var. polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved leea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leeaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

The leaves are usually glossy and have a somewhat succulent texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its native habitat.
