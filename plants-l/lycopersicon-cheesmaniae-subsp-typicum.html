Lycopersicon cheesmaniae subsp. typicum

Lycopersicon cheesmaniae subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the wild tomato, is a species native to South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and ovate, serrated leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It is closely related to domesticated tomatoes and has been studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural value.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in plant taxonomy and breeding research.
