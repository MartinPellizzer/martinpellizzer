Liatris novae-angliae var. nieuwlandii

Liatris novae-angliae var. nieuwlandii, commonly known as Newland's blazing star, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences composed of numerous purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms from late summer to early fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
