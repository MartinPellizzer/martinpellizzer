Ipomoea oblongata var. auriculata

Ipomoea oblongata var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth. It is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, often using its tendrils to support itself.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
