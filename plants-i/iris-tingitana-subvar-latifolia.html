Iris tingitana subvar. latifolia

Iris tingitana subvar. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved desert iris, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit and its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
