Ilex aquifolium var. ferox-argentea

Ilex aquifolium var. ferox-argentea, commonly known as the silver-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its striking silver-white variegation on its foliage.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin, and they retain their color throughout the winter, providing seasonal interest. This plant is evergreen, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, and it can grow to a moderate size, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The plant produces small, red berries in the winter, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife.
