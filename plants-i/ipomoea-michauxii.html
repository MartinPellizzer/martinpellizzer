Ipomoea michauxii

Ipomoea michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Ipomoea michauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
