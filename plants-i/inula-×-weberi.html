Inula × weberi

Inula × weberi, commonly known as the hybrid false sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and a branching stem covered in fine hairs.

Inula × weberi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
