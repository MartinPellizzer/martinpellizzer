Ixeris sonchifolia var. serotina

Ixeris sonchifolia var. serotina, commonly known as late-flowering yellow flower.

It is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of pale yellow disk florets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in gardens.
