Ixora mucronata

Ixora mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed flame bush, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are typically bright red or orange, attracting various pollinators.

Ixora mucronata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
