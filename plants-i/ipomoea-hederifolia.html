Ipomoea hederifolia

Ipomoea hederifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.

Ipomoea hederifolia is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, often spreading along the ground or up structures.
