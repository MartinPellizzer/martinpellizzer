Ivodea analalavensis

Ivodea analalavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ivy, is a climbing plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for some local wildlife. Ivodea analalavensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in Madagascar's biodiversity.
