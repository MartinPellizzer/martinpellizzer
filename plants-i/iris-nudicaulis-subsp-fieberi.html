Iris nudicaulis subsp. fieberi

Iris nudicaulis subsp.

fieberi, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and gravelly soils in mountainous regions.
