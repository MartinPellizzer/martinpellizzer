Indocalamus suichuanensis

Indocalamus suichuanensis, commonly known as Suichuan bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to China, particularly found in the Sichuan province, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that form dense clumps, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
