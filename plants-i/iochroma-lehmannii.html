Iochroma lehmannii

Iochroma lehmannii, commonly known as the "Peruvian bellflower," is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and somewhat branched.

Iochroma lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
