Inga multijuga subsp. multijuga

Inga multijuga subsp.

multijuga, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, and its showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
