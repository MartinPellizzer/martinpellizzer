Ixia namaquana

Ixia namaquana, commonly known as the Namakwa lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen, adding visual interest throughout the year.

Ixia namaquana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
