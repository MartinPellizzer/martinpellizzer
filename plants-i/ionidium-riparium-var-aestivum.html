Ionidium riparium var. aestivum

Ionidium riparium var. aestivum, commonly known as the summer water plant, is a variant of the riparian plant Ionidium riparium.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in its photosynthetic process. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water's edge.

The variant 'aestivum' is distinguished by its seasonal growth pattern, blooming predominantly during the warmer months.
