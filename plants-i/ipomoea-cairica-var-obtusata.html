Ipomoea cairica var. obtusata

Ipomoea cairica var. obtusata, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb trellises or fences.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
