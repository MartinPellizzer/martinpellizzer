Iris alata f. parviflora

Iris alata f. parviflora, commonly known as the slender iris, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, pale blue to violet flowers.

It is a subspecies of the broad-leaved iris, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, upright flower stalks. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and botanical collections.
