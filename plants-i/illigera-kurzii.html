Illigera kurzii

Illigera kurzii, commonly known as the Kashmir Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. It has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
