Ipomoea aurantiaca

Ipomoea aurantiaca, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support itself. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
