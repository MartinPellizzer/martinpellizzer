Ixia alba

Ixia alba, commonly known as the white ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has a corm-like underground stem. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, narrow tube.

Ixia alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
