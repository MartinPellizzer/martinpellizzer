Ichnanthus sericans

Ichnanthus sericans, commonly known as the slender fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ichnanthus sericans is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
