Ipomoea tuberculata var. lineariloba

Ipomoea tuberculata var. lineariloba, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces vibrant blue, purple, or white flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend structures, and its stems are often covered in small, raised bumps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
