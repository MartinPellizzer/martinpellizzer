Ixeris linguifolia

Ixeris linguifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ixeris linguifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
