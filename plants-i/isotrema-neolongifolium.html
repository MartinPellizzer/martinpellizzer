Isotrema neolongifolium

Isotrema neolongifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved isotrema, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
