Ilex boliviana var. brittoniana

Ilex boliviana var. brittoniana, commonly known as the Bolivian holly, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, mountainous forests. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, bright red berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal foliage.
