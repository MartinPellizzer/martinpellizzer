Inophloeum armatum

Inophloeum armatum, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are borne in compact inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns yellow when mature.

Inophloeum armatum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
