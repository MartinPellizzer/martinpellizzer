Ixora aggregata

Ixora aggregata, commonly known as the flame tree or Chinese flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically borne in terminal clusters and are highly attractive to birds and insects.

Ixora aggregata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and horticulture.
