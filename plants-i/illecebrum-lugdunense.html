Illecebrum lugdunense

Illecebrum lugdunense, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with numerous branched stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces spiny, purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are broad, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Illecebrum lugdunense is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.
