Iris lutescens subsp. chamaeiris

Iris lutescens subsp.

chamaeiris, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, yellow to orange flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and bright green, often with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found near water bodies.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
