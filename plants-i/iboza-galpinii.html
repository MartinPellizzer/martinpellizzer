Iboza galpinii

Iboza galpinii, commonly known as the Natal fig, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and unique appearance, it is a notable species in its native region.
