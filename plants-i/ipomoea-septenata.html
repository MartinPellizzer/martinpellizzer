Ipomoea septenata

Ipomoea septenata, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. Its vines can grow up to 10 meters long, making it a vigorous climber.

Ipomoea septenata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
