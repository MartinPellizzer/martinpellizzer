Iris polakii f. protonyma

Iris polakii f. protonyma, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada iris, is a variant of the Sierra iris species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
