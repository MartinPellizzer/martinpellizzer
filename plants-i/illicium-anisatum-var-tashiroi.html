Illicium anisatum var. tashiroi

Illicium anisatum var. tashiroi, commonly known as Tashiro's star anise, is a variant of the star anise plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruit that resembles the more widely known star anise, but with a slightly different appearance and aroma. The plant belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is cultivated for its aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy texture, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit matures to a dark brown color and is harvested before it fully ripens to preserve its essential oils.
