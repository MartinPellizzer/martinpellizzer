Ischyrolepis cincinnata

Ischyrolepis cincinnata, commonly known as the slender rock fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, wiry fronds that grow in a compact, rosette-like formation. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as rocky crevices or forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

The plant's adaptability to nutrient-poor soils makes it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
