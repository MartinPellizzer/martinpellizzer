Ipomoea polyantha var. affinis

Ipomoea polyantha var. affinis, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
