Impatiens setosa

Impatiens setosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf touch-me-not, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its stems are erect and often branched, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Impatiens setosa is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
