Inga guamito

Inga guamito, commonly known as the "Guamito tree," is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Inga guamito is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it useful in reforestation efforts.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
