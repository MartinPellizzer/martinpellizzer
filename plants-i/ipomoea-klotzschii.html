Ipomoea klotzschii

Ipomoea klotzschii, commonly known as the Klotzsch's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering vines and herbs.

Ipomoea klotzschii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its tendrils.
