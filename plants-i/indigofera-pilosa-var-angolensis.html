Indigofera pilosa var. angolensis

Indigofera pilosa var. angolensis, commonly known as Angola hairy indigo, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

This species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
