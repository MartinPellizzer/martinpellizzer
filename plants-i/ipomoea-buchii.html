Ipomoea buchii

Ipomoea buchii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea buchii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
