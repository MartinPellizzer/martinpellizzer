Ichnanthus majusculus

Ichnanthus majusculus, commonly known as the small yellow iris, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive purple stripe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers bloom in early spring.
