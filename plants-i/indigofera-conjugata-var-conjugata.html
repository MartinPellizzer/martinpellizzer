Indigofera conjugata var. conjugata

Indigofera conjugata var. conjugata, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
