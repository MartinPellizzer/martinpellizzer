Indigofera buchneri

Indigofera buchneri, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in racemes. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been traditionally used in dyeing fabrics.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
