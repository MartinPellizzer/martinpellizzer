Iberis intermedia subsp. beugesiaca

Iberis intermedia subsp.

beugesiaca, commonly known as the Balkan candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The plant features low-growing, mat-forming stems with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
