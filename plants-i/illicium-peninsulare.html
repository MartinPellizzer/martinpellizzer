Illicium peninsulare

Illicium peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular star anise, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark, glossy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

Illicium peninsulare is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
