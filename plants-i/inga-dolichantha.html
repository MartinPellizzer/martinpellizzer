Inga dolichantha

Inga dolichantha, commonly known as the long pod inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. Inga dolichantha is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.
