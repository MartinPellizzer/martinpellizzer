Ilex amara var. longifolia

Ilex amara var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
