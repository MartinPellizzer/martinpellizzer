Iris maackii

Iris maackii, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, lavender-blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Iris maackii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
