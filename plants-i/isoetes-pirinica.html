Isoetes pirinica

Isoetes pirinica, commonly known as the Pirin watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral around the stem. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Isoetes pirinica is known for its ability to form dense underwater stands, which can help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
