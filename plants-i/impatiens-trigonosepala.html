Impatiens trigonosepala

Impatiens trigonosepala, commonly known as the three-lobed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens trigonosepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
