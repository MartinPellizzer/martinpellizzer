Indigofera mairei var. proterantha

Indigofera mairei var. proterantha, commonly known as the Maire's indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine due to their potential medicinal properties.
