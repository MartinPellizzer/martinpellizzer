Inga Aptera

Inga aptera is a species of leguminous tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible pods containing seeds. Inga aptera is often used in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing properties. Its versatility makes it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
