Ipomoea prolifera

Ipomoea prolifera, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its vigorous growth habit, often spreading along the ground or climbing structures. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ipomoea prolifera is often found in wetlands, along riverbanks, and in disturbed soils, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.
