Indigofera goniodes var. rhodesica

Indigofera goniodes var. rhodesica, commonly known as Rhodesian indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
