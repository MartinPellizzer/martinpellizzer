Imperata exaltata subsp. merrillii

Imperata exaltata subsp.

merrillii, commonly known as the tall blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets that are often pinkish or reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its distinctive appearance.
