Ipomoea tehuantepecensis

Ipomoea tehuantepecensis, commonly known as the Tehuantepec morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The seeds are enclosed in a capsule and can be dispersed by wind or animals.
