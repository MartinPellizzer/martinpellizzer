Ilex fortunensis

Ilex fortunensis, commonly known as the Fortunella holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southern United States, particularly Florida and Texas.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of habitats. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Ilex fortunensis plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
