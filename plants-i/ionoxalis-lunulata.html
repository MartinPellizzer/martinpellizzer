Ionoxalis lunulata

Ionoxalis lunulata, commonly known as the moonflower oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet with a distinctive white or pale green marking resembling a crescent moon. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or lavender in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Ionoxalis lunulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
