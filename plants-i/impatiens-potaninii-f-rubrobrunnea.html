Impatiens potaninii f. rubrobrunnea

Impatiens potaninii f. rubrobrunnea, commonly known as the red-brown impatiens, is a variant of the species Impatiens potaninii, native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes reddish or brownish underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and borne in clusters, often in shades of red or pink.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant foliage and delicate blooms.
