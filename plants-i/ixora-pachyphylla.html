Ixora pachyphylla

Ixora pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in warm climates.
