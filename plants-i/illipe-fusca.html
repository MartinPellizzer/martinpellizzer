Illipe fusca

Illipe fusca, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and yellowish to pale brown flowers. The tree produces a nut-like fruit that contains a rich, creamy seed kernel used in cosmetics and food products. Its botanical significance lies in its role in both local ecosystems and economic activities.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in degraded soils, making it an important species for reforestation efforts.
