Ipomoea heynei

Ipomoea heynei, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length, climbing using its tendrils. Its flowers are typically 5-7 cm in diameter and have five petals.

Ipomoea heynei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
