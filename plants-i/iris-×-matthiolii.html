Iris × matthiolii

Iris × matthiolii, commonly known as the Italian arum, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple to blackish flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, well-drained soils.

Iris × matthiolii thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
