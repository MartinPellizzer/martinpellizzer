Iris bispathacea

Iris bispathacea, commonly known as the two-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in two distinct rows. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Iris bispathacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
