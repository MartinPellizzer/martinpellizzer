Impatiens cuspidata subsp. cuspidata

Impatiens cuspidata subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved impatiens, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branching stems that often produce clusters of small, showy flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a pointed tip and a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
