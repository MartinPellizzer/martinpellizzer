Indigofera tinctaria

Indigofera tinctaria, commonly known as blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
