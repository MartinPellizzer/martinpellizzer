Ismelia frutescens

Ismelia frutescens, commonly known as the golden star bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ismelia frutescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
