Ipomoea polpha subsp. weirana

Ipomoea polpha subsp.

weirana, commonly known as the Weirana morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning.

The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its flowers are typically 5-7 cm in length and are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
