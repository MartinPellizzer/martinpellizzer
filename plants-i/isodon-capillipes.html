Isodon capillipes

Isodon capillipes, commonly known as the slender lemongrass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a strong lemony scent. Its flowering spikes produce small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color.

Isodon capillipes is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
