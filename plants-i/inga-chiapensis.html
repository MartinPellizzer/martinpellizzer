Inga chiapensis

Inga chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas inga, is a species of leguminous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the genus Inga, which is part of the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Inga chiapensis is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
