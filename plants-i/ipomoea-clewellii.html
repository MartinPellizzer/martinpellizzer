Ipomoea clewellii

Ipomoea clewellii, commonly known as the Clewell's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its vines can climb and spread rapidly, making it a notable ornamental and ecological species.

Ipomoea clewellii is also known for its fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
