Isoetes sinensis var. coreana

Isoetes sinensis var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that exhibits unique morphological features.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that form a dense, floating mat on the water surface. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are borne on specialized stalks called sporophylls.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
