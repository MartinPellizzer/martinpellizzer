Iberis pectinata

Iberis pectinata, commonly known as the white candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Iberis pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
