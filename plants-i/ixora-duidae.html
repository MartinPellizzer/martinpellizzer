Ixora duidae

Ixora duidae, commonly known as the golden flame or flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora duidae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a hedge due to its hardy nature and long flowering season.
