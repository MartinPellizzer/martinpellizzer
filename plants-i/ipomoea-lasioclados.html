Ipomoea lasioclados

Ipomoea lasioclados, commonly known as the roughleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with rough, waxy surfaces and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
