Ipomoea variifolia var. saxatilis

Ipomoea variifolia var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, variegated leaves with a striking combination of green, white, and sometimes pink or red markings. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, opening in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
