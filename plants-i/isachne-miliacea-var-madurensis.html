Isachne miliacea var. madurensis

Isachne miliacea var. madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese milletgrass, is a variant of the annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or agricultural fields.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a weed in some regions but also has potential for use in forage or traditional medicine.
