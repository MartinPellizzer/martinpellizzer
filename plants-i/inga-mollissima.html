Inga mollissima

Inga mollissima, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant is notable for its pod-like fruits, which contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
