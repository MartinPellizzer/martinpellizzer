Ilex dubia f. chapmaniana

Ilex dubia f. chapmaniana, commonly known as the Chapman's holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is well-suited for use as a specimen plant or in rock gardens due to its dense, upright growth habit.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
