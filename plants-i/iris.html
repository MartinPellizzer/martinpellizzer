Iris

Iris, commonly known as the flag iris, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy flowers that typically have three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The flowers come in a wide range of colors, including blue, purple, white, and yellow. Iris species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular in gardens and landscapes.
