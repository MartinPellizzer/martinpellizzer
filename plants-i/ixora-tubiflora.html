Ixora tubiflora

Ixora tubiflora, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens and landscapes.

Ixora tubiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
