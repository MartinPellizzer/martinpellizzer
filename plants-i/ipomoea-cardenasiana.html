Ipomoea cardenasiana

Ipomoea cardenasiana, commonly known as the Ceniza vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking violet to purple flowers with white veins. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
