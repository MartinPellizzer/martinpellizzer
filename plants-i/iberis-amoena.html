Iberis amoena

Iberis amoena, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and clusters of fragrant, violet-pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis amoena is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
