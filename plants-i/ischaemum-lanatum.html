Ischaemum lanatum

Ischaemum lanatum, commonly known as hairy barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Ischaemum lanatum is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
