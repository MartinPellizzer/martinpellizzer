Indigofera obcordata

Indigofera obcordata, commonly known as the Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, dark blue seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
