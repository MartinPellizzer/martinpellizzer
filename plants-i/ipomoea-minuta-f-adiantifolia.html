Ipomoea minuta f. adiantifolia

Ipomoea minuta f. adiantifolia, commonly known as the delicate morning glory, is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

It produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it is known for its rapid growth habit.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a ground cover.
