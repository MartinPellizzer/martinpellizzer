Icacorea humboldtii

Icacorea humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy or rocky soils near the coast. It is often found in humid, tropical climates and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
