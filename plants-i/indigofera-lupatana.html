Indigofera lupatana

Indigofera lupatana, commonly known as the red indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant purple to red flowers, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
