Ipomoea turneroides

Ipomoea turneroides, commonly known as Turner's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs and can grow quite long as it climbs supports.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, round seeds.
