Indigofera suffruticosa var. canescens

Indigofera suffruticosa var. canescens, commonly known as the white indigo or canescens indigo, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This species has been historically used for dye production and has ecological value as a habitat for various insects and birds.
