Impatiens cristigera

Impatiens cristigera, commonly known as the crested impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curled leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens cristigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
