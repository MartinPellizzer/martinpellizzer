Iris × germanica var. askabadensis

Iris × germanica var. askabadensis, commonly known as the Askabad iris, is a hybrid ornamental plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate watering.

Its ornamental appeal makes it a popular choice for both garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
