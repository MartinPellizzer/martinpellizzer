Ipomoea cordifolia

Ipomoea cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, often reaching heights of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea cordifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
