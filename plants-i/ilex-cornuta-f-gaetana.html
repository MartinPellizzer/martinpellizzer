Ilex cornuta f. gaetana

Ilex cornuta f. gaetana, commonly known as the Gaetana holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are a characteristic feature of the species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile choice for ornamental gardening.

The Gaetana holly is also valued for its dense foliage and ability to provide year-round visual interest in the landscape.
