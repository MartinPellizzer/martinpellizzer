Isinia

Isinia, commonly known as the Isinia plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a helix, making it visually distinct from other flora. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of violet and blue, are pollinated by specific species of moths and bees.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, Isinia is considered a conservation concern.
