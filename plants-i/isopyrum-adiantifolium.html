Isopyrum adiantifolium

Isopyrum adiantifolium, commonly known as the fern-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Isopyrum adiantifolium is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in temperate climates.
