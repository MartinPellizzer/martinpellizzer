Iris acutiloba var. lineolata

Iris acutiloba var. lineolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features grass-like, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 2 feet in height. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale blue stripe down the center of each petal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
