Inga suaveolens

Inga suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its rapid growth makes it useful for soil stabilization and reforestation efforts.
