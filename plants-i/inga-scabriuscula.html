Inga scabriuscula

Inga scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough inga, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a shallow root system and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.

Inga scabriuscula is also valued for its use in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
