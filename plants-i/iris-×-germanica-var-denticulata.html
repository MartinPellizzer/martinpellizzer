Iris × germanica var. denticulata

Iris × germanica var. denticulata, commonly known as the German iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, violet-blue or white flowers with a distinct central beard. The leaves are broad, sword-shaped, and often have a slightly toothed margin, which gives the variety its name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
