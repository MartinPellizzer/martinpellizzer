Iris brevituba

Iris brevituba, commonly known as the short-tubed iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers feature three upright petals and three drooping sepals, typical of the iris family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
