Indigofera incarnata

Indigofera incarnata, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Wild indigo is also valued for its historical use in dye production and as a forage plant for livestock.
