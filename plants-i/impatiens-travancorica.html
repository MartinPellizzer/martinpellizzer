Impatiens travancorica

Impatiens travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming during the monsoon season. It prefers moist, shady habitats and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
