Iris × germanica var. florentina

Iris × germanica var. florentina, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and produces lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to landscapes.
