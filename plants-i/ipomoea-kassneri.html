Ipomoea kassneri

Ipomoea kassneri, commonly known as the Kassner's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vine that can climb using its tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers have a strong, sweet scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea kassneri is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
