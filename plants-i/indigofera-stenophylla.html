Indigofera stenophylla

Indigofera stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its common name.

Indigofera stenophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
