Iberis oppositifolia

Iberis oppositifolia, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant has small, pinnately divided leaves that are opposite in arrangement, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Iberis oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
