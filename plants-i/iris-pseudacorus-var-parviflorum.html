Iris pseudacorus var. parviflorum

Iris pseudacorus var. parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellow flag, is a variant of the yellow flag iris.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive blackish-purple center. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
