Indigofera setiflora var. corbyi

Indigofera setiflora var. corbyi, commonly known as the Corby's sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spindly stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy, pod-like structure that splits open to release the seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.
