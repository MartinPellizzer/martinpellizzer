Isora corylifolia

Isora corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its economic importance.

Isora corylifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
