Ipomoea alterniflora

Ipomoea alterniflora, commonly known as the alternateleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea alterniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
