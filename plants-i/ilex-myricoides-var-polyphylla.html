Ilex myricoides var. polyphylla

Ilex myricoides var. polyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features alternate, simple, elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it has a dense, rounded habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
