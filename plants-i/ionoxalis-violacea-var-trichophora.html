Ionoxalis violacea var. trichophora

Ionoxalis violacea var. trichophora, commonly known as the violet woodland sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet bearing a violet or purple coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
