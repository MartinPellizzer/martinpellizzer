Ixora reticulata

Ixora reticulata, commonly known as the red flame or red ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Ixora reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
