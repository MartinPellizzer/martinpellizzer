Ilex fragilis f. subcoriacea

Ilex fragilis f. subcoriacea, commonly known as the fragrant holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically leathery and have a slightly rough texture. The leaves often display a deep green color with a subtle whitish or silver-tinged underside. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds but toxic to humans.
