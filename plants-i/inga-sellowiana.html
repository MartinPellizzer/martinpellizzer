Inga sellowiana

Inga sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth rate and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
