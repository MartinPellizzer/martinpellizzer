Indigofera rivularis

Indigofera rivularis, commonly known as river indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found near water sources, such as rivers and streams, hence its common name.

The plant's seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by various means, including wind and water.
