Iris × fumiflora

Iris × fumiflora, commonly known as the smoky iris, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, pale blue to purple flowers that often appear in clusters. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base.

Iris × fumiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
