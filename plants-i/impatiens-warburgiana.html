Impatiens warburgiana

Impatiens warburgiana, commonly known as the velvet tangerine impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
