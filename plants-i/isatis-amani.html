Isatis amani

Isatis amani, commonly known as the African woad, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the plant is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Isatis amani is primarily valued for its historical use in producing indigo dye.
