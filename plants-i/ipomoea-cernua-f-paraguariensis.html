Ipomoea cernua f. paraguariensis

Ipomoea cernua f. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color. It is native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
