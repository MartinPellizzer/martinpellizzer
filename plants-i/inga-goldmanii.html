Inga goldmanii

Inga goldmanii, commonly known as the golden inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth, climbing habit, and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports ecological balance.
