Iris staintonii

Iris staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Iris staintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
