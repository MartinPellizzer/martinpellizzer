Ighermia

Ighermia, commonly known as the common thyme, is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Ighermia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it both ornamental and functional in various garden settings.
