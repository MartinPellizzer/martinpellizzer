Ingenhoussia verticillata

Ingenhoussia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ingenhoussia verticillata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
