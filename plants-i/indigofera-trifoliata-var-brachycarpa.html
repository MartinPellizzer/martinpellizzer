Indigofera trifoliata var. brachycarpa

Indigofera trifoliata var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the dwarf indigo bean, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
