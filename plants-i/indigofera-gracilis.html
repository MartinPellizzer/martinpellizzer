Indigofera gracilis

Indigofera gracilis, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which makes it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in horticulture and agriculture.
