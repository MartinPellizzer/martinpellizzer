Inula eliasii

Inula eliasii, commonly known as the Elias's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous tiny florets, with the outer ray florets being the most visible.

Inula eliasii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
