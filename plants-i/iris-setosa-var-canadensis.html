Iris setosa var. canadensis

Iris setosa var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, typical of the iris family. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are generally dark green and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
