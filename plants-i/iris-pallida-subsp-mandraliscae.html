Iris pallida subsp. mandraliscae

Iris pallida subsp.

mandraliscae, commonly known as the Mandralisca iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and early blooming season.
