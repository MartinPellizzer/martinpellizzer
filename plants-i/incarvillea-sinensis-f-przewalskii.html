Incarvillea sinensis f. przewalskii

Incarvillea sinensis f. przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's incarvillea, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with a hairy texture and produces striking blue to violet flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaping and conservation gardens.
