Impatiens lucorum

Impatiens lucorum, commonly known as the Welsh poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their delicate, spurred petals.

Impatiens lucorum is often found in rocky or woodland soils, thriving in cooler, moist environments.
