Iberis pinnata proles costei

Iberis pinnata proles costei, commonly known as the Coste's evergreen shrub, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that are usually evergreen. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, rounded fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
