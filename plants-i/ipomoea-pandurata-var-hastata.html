Ipomoea pandurata var. hastata

Ipomoea pandurata var. hastata, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a striking vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces distinctive, spirally coiled seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along fences or in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
