Indigofera kirilowii f. coreana

Indigofera kirilowii f. coreana, commonly known as Korean indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is native to East Asia, particularly Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
