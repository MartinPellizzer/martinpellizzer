Ilex gracilipes

Ilex gracilipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Ilex gracilipes thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its slow growth rate and compact form make it suitable for use in hedges or as a specimen plant in shaded garden settings.
