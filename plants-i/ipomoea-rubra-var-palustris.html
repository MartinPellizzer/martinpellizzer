Ipomoea rubra var. palustris

Ipomoea rubra var. palustris, commonly known as swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a trailing perennial vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed wetland areas.
