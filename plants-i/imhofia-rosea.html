Imhofia rosea

Imhofia rosea, commonly known as the red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant has long, narrow, glossy leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Imhofia rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
