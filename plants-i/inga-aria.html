Inga aria

Inga aria, commonly known as the "papaya tree," is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit that is rich in vitamins and enzymes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga aria is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
