Ipomoea calycina

Ipomoea calycina, commonly known as the crimson wood morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Ipomoea calycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
