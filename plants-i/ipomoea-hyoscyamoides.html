Ipomoea hyoscyamoides

Ipomoea hyoscyamoides, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some regions.
