Indigofera lacei

Indigofera lacei, commonly known as the Hawaiian indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is considered a rare species.
