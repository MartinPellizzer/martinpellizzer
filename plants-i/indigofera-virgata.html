Indigofera virgata

Indigofera virgata, commonly known as blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Indigofera virgata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
