Ionopsis utricularioides f. virginalis

Ionopsis utricularioides f. virginalis, commonly known as the Virgin's bell, is a unique variant of the bladderwort family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually small and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its stems are slender and branching.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking floral display.
