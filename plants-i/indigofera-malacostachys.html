Indigofera malacostachys

Indigofera malacostachys, commonly known as the velvetleaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
