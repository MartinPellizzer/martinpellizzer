Inga thyrsoidea

Inga thyrsoidea, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Inga thyrsoidea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
