Ipomoea rosea

Ipomoea rosea, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea rosea is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
