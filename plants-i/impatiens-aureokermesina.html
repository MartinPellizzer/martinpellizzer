Impatiens aureokermesina

Impatiens aureokermesina, commonly known as the golden flame impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are slender and branching.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
