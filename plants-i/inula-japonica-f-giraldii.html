Inula japonica f. giraldii

Inula japonica f. giraldii, commonly known as the Japanese Inula, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and fibrous.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
