Iphigenia strumosa

Iphigenia strumosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Iphigenia strumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
