Inula sarana

Inula sarana, commonly known as the mountain eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in the summer. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Inula sarana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
