Ipomoea pinangiana

Ipomoea pinangiana, commonly known as the Pinang morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea pinangiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
