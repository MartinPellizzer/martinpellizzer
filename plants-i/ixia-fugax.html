Ixia fugax

Ixia fugax, commonly known as the Transkei lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Ixia fugax is known for its short blooming period, often lasting only a few weeks during the spring.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
