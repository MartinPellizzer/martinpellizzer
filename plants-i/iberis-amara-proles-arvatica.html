Iberis amara proles arvatica

Iberis amara proles arvatica, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features pinnately divided leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers typically bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves and flowers used in traditional herbal remedies.
