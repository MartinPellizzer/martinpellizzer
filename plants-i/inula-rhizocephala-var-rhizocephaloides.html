Inula rhizocephala var. rhizocephaloides

Inula rhizocephala var. rhizocephaloides, commonly known as the slender elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent.
