Ipomoea hederacea var. limbata

Ipomoea hederacea var. limbata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a trailing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed in gardens.
