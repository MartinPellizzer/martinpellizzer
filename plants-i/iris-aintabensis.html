Iris aintabensis

Iris aintabensis, commonly known as the Aintab iris, is a species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of flowers in early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
