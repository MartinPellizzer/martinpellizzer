Isolepis incomtula

Isolepis incomtula, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, typically in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Isolepis incomtula plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
