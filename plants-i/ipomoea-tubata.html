Ipomoea tubata

Ipomoea tubata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length, climbing by means of tendrils. Its flowers bloom in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea tubata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
