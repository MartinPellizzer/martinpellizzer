Indigofera signata

Indigofera signata, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's deep root system helps it thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its ecological significance.
