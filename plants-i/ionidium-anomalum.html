Ionidium anomalum

Ionidium anomalum, commonly known as the strange ionidium, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that display a striking gradient of green to purple hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to absorb nutrients from nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ionidium anomalum is considered a vulnerable species.
