Impatiens clavigera

Impatiens clavigera, commonly known as the spiked impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that are usually pink or white, with a distinctive, elongated spur at the base of each bloom. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens clavigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
