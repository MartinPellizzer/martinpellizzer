Ipomoea fulvicaulis

Ipomoea fulvicaulis, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a popular ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in some areas.
