Indigofera glabella

Indigofera glabella, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in its native range.
