Indigofera campestris var. campestris

Indigofera campestris var. campestris, commonly known as wild indigo, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its historical use in dye production and as a forage crop highlights its ecological and agricultural significance.
