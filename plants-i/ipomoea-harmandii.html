Ipomoea harmandii

Ipomoea harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, climbing or trailing along supports.

Ipomoea harmandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
