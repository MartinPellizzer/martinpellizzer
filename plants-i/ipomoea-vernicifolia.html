Ipomoea vernicifolia

Ipomoea vernicifolia, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is toxic. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea vernicifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
