Indigofera queenslandica

Indigofera queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland indigo, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
