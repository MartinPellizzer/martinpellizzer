Ipomoea tiliacea var. merremioides

Ipomoea tiliacea var. merremioides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often opening in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
