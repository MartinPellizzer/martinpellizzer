Ipomoea cavanillesii

Ipomoea cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

Ipomoea cavanillesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
