Isachne lepidota

Isachne lepidota, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Isachne lepidota plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
