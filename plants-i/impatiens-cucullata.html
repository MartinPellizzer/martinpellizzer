Impatiens cucullata

Impatiens cucullata, commonly known as the hooded impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-like calyx that encloses the flower buds, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, and come in shades of white, pink, and red.

Impatiens cucullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
