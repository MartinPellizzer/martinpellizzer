Ipomoea contorta

Ipomoea contorta, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Ipomoea contorta is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.
