Ionoxalis trientalis

Ionoxalis trientalis, commonly known as the three-lobed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and stems that are often hairy.

Ionoxalis trientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
