Ipomoea macalusoi

Ipomoea macalusoi, commonly known as the Macaluso's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale pink. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea macalusoi is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, making it a notable ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
