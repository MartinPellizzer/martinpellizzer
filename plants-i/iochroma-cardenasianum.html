Iochroma cardenasianum

Iochroma cardenasianum, commonly known as the Ceanua flower, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Iochroma cardenasianum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
