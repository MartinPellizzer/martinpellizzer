Inga mendoncaei

Inga mendoncaei, commonly known as the Mendoncaei silk tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a shallow root system and is often found in moist, shaded understory environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
