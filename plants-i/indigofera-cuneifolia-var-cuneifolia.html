Indigofera cuneifolia var. cuneifolia

Indigofera cuneifolia var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, often pink or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in gardens and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
