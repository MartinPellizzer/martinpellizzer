Ixora heterodoxa

Ixora heterodoxa, commonly known as the Indian flame or red flame plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ixora heterodoxa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in landscapes.
