Ipomoea caesia

Ipomoea caesia, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its role in supporting local pollinators.
