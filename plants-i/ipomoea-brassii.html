Ipomoea brassii

Ipomoea brassii, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length, climbing by means of tendrils. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
