Isatis psilocarpa

Isatis psilocarpa, commonly known as the Indian madder or purple madder, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its erect stems, lance-shaped leaves, and showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
