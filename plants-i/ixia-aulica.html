Ixia aulica

Ixia aulica, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Ixia aulica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
