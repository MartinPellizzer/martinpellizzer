Ichnanthus candicans var. velutinus

Ichnanthus candicans var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
