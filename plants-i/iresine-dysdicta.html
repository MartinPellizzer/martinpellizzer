Iresine dysdicta

Iresine dysdicta, commonly known as the flame flower or red leaf plant, is a strikingly colorful species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often red or orange, bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a deep red or purple hue on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage.

Iresine dysdicta prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
