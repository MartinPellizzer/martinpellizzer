Imatophyllum vanhouttei

Imatophyllum vanhouttei, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's beauty leaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a variety of hues, including shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are broad and velvety, contributing to its unique visual presence in both indoor and outdoor settings.
