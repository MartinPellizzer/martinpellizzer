Indigofera sofa

Indigofera sofa, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seed pods contain seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Indigofera sofa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
