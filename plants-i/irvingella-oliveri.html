Irvingella oliveri

Irvingella oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's Irvingia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its woody shrub form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Irvingella oliveri is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
