Isachne bicolor

Isachne bicolor, commonly known as the two-color grass, is a perennial grass species native to various parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored leaves, typically with a lighter base and a darker tip. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed soils. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often inconspicuous.

Isachne bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
