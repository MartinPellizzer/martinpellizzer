Ilex brasiliensis f. coronaria

Ilex brasiliensis f. coronaria, commonly known as the Crowned Holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive red berries that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features small, leathery leaves with a glossy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide winter interest through its berries.
