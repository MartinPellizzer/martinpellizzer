Isodon albopilosus

Isodon albopilosus, commonly known as the white-hair isodon, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a dense covering of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Isodon albopilosus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.
