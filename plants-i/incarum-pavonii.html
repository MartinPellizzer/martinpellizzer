Incarum pavonii

Incarum pavonii, commonly known as the peacock orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers are usually pale pink to purple in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Incarum pavonii is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
