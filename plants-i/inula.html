Inula

Inula, commonly known as the elecampane, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and yellow or white composite flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the roots are fibrous and often aromatic. Inula species are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

They are often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
