Icacorea subcrenulata

Icacorea subcrenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed coral tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are attractive to birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish hue, which adds to its ornamental value.
