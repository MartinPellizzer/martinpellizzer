Inga nutans

Inga nutans, commonly known as the weeping inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive weeping appearance due to its drooping branches. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale pink, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various indigenous cultures.
