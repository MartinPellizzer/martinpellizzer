Ipomoea praetermissa

Ipomoea praetermissa, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a trailing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, sunny environments. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, hairy capsule that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea praetermissa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
