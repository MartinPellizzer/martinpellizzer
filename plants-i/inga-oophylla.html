Inga oophylla

Inga oophylla, commonly known as the "egg-leaf inga," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its unique leaf structure, with the terminal leaflet resembling an egg, hence its common name. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

Inga oophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
