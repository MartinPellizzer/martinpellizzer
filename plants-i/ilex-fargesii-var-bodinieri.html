Ilex fargesii var. bodinieri

Ilex fargesii var. bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in garden settings.
