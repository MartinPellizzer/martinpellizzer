Ilex ampla

Ilex ampla, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Ilex ampla typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
