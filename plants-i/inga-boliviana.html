Inga boliviana

Inga boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian passionflower, is a woody vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, with a distinctive corona of appendages. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain edible seeds.

Inga boliviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
