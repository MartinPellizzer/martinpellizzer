Iryanthera congestiflora

Iryanthera congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered arum, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a distinctive, often colorful bract. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Iryanthera congestiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
