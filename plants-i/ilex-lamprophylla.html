Ilex lamprophylla

Ilex lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaved holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for native birds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal and forested areas.

Ilex lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
