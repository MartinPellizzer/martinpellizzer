Ipomoea pedunculosa

Ipomoea pedunculosa, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue or violet flowers, which open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and is often found in moist, sunny environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Ipomoea pedunculosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.
