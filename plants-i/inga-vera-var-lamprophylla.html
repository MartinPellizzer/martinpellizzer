Inga vera var. lamprophylla

Inga vera var. lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pinnate leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
