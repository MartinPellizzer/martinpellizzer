Ipomoea japonica

Ipomoea japonica, commonly known as Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces edible tubers similar to sweet potatoes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
