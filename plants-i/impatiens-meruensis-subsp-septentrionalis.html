Impatiens meruensis subsp. septentrionalis

Impatiens meruensis subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern impatiens, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically oval and glossy, with a smooth texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
