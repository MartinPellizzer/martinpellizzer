Ipomoea purpurea f. triloba

Ipomoea purpurea f. triloba, commonly known as the tri-lobed morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory characterized by its uniquely divided leaves.

This plant is a flowering vine native to the Americas, particularly thriving in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with a vibrant purple or blue hue, and bloom in the morning. The leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three distinct lobes, giving the plant its distinguishing feature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across tropical and subtropical regions.
