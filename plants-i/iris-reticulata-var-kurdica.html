Iris reticulata var. kurdica

Iris reticulata var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish reticulate iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Near East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, lavender-blue to violet flowers with a distinctive reticulate pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and early blooming habit.
