Inula viscosa subsp. revoluta

Inula viscosa subsp.

revoluta, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish-green stems and leaves, which are typically covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
