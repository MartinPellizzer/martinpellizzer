Isolepis sicula

Isolepis sicula, commonly known as Sicilian flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along water edges. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Isolepis sicula is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
