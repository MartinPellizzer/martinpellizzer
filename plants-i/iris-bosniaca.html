Iris bosniaca

Iris bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, typically with three upright petals and three spreading sepals. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Iris bosniaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
