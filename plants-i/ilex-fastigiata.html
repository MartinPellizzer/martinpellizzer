Ilex fastigiata

Ilex fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and dark green, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex fastigiata is valued for its dense foliage and ornamental appeal in landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
