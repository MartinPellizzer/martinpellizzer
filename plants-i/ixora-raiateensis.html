Ixora raiateensis

Ixora raiateensis, commonly known as the Tahitian flame or Tahitian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to its natural habitat. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora raiateensis is also valued for its cultural significance in Polynesian traditions.
