Ipomoea texana

Ipomoea texana, commonly known as the Texas morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for many years.
