Inga caffra

Inga caffra, commonly known as the African honeytree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia in its root system. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a sweet, honey-like scent that attracts pollinators.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their various therapeutic properties.
