Indigofera masonae

Indigofera masonae, commonly known as Mason's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
