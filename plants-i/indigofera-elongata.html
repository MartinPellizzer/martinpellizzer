Indigofera elongata

Indigofera elongata, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, and the species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration.
