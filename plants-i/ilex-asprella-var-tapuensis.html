Ilex asprella var. tapuensis

Ilex asprella var. tapuensis, commonly known as the Tapu ilex, is a variant of the evergreen holly native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
