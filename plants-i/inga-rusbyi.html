Inga rusbyi

Inga rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Inga rusbyi is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agroforestry systems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
