Indigofera rothii

Indigofera rothii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the species. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera rothii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
