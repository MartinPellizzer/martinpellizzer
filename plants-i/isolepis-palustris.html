Isolepis palustris

Isolepis palustris, commonly known as the marsh arrowgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Isolepis palustris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
