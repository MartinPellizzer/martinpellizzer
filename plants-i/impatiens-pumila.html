Impatiens pumila

Impatiens pumila, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
