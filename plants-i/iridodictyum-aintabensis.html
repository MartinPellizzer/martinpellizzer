Iridodictyum aintabensis

Iridodictyum aintabensis, commonly known as the Aintab grass, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Iridodictyum aintabensis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
