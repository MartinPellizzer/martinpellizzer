Impatiens pallida f. speciosa

Impatiens pallida f. speciosa, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a hybrid cultivar of the impatiens genus.

It is characterized by its striking white, semi-double flowers that contrast with its dark green, velvety foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas. Its foliage is often described as having a silvery sheen, adding a unique visual appeal.

The species is native to the southeastern United States and is valued for its ornamental qualities in both garden and container settings.
