Isoetes aemulans

Isoetes aemulans, commonly known as the mimic water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, ribbon-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of other aquatic plants. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in shallow, still waters with soft sediment. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of its leaves, a feature common to many members of the water fern family.

Isoetes aemulans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small aquatic animals.
