Iphiona aucheri

Iphiona aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
