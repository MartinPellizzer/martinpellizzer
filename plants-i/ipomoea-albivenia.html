Ipomoea albivenia

Ipomoea albivenia, commonly known as the white-veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea albivenia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats alike.
