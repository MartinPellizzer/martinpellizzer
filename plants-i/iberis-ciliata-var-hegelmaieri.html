Iberis ciliata var. hegelmaieri

Iberis ciliata var. hegelmaieri, commonly known as the Hegelmaier's candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
