Ilex ericoides

Ilex ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage, which is typically dark green with glossy surfaces. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Ilex ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form thickets, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
