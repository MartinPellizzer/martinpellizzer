Iozoste rumphii

Iozoste rumphii, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty.

Iozoste rumphii is a relatively rare species, making it a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
