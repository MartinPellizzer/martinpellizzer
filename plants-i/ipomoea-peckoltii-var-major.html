Ipomoea peckoltii var. major

Ipomoea peckoltii var. major, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep violet, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
