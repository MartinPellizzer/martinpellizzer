Indigofera bertolonii

Indigofera bertolonii, commonly known as the African indigo.

It is a shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in savannas and woodland areas, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
