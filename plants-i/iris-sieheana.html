Iris sieheana

Iris sieheana, commonly known as the Siehe's iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
