Indigofera keyensis

Indigofera keyensis, commonly known as Key indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera keyensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
