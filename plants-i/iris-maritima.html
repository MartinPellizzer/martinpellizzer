Iris maritima

Iris maritima, commonly known as the sea iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shores of the Atlantic and Gulf coasts. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Iris maritima plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and supporting coastal ecosystems.
