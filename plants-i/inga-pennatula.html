Inga pennatula

Inga pennatula, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon Basin, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces pods containing edible seeds.

Inga pennatula is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
