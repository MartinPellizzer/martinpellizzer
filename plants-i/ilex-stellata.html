Ilex stellata

Ilex stellata, commonly known as the star-horned holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are typically 3-5 lobed and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex stellata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
