Ilex laurifolia

Ilex laurifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex laurifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
