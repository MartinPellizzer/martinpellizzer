Imperata praecoquis

Imperata praecoquis, commonly known as the red grass or red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender blades that often have a reddish hue, especially when mature. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Imperata praecoquis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
