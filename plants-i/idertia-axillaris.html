Idertia axillaris

Idertia axillaris, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, slightly hairy texture.

Idertia axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
