Iberis sampaiana

Iberis sampaiana, commonly known as the Sardinian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, and it grows in rocky or dry soils.

Iberis sampaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
