Impatiens gesneroidea var. superglabra

Impatiens gesneroidea var. superglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated settings.
