Illecebrum aretioides

Illecebrum aretioides, commonly known as the creeping dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nourishment.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure that allows it to spread along the ground or climb other vegetation. The plant is native to regions in South America and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken or even kill its host plants if left unchecked.
