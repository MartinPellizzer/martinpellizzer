Inga salzmanniana

Inga salzmanniana, commonly known as the Salzmann's inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Inga salzmanniana is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
