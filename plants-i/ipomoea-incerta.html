Ipomoea incerta

Ipomoea incerta, commonly known as the uncertain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet blooms that typically open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are twining and can grow quite long.

Ipomoea incerta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
