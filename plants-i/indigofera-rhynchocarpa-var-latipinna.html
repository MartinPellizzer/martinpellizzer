Indigofera rhynchocarpa var. latipinna

Indigofera rhynchocarpa var. latipinna, commonly known as the broad-leaf indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and a distinctive floral structure that aids in pollination by insects.
