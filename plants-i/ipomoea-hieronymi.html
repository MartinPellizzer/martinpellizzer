Ipomoea hieronymi

Ipomoea hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers are usually blue or violet in color and are pollinated by insects.

Ipomoea hieronymi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative vine.
