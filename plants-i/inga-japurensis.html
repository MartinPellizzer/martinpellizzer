Inga japurensis

Inga japurensis, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is a climbing shrub that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a dense canopy that provides shade. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in the rainforest.

Inga japurensis also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, enhancing soil fertility in its natural habitat.
