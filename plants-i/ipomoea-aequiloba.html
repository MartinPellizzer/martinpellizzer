Ipomoea aequiloba

Ipomoea aequiloba, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, sunny environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known vines like the common morning glory.

Ipomoea aequiloba is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems.
