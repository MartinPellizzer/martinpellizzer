Iris × deflexa

Iris × deflexa, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris laevigata and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or yellow beard on the lower petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Iris × deflexa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is native to eastern Asia.
