Iris cengialti subsp. portae

Iris cengialti subsp.

portae, commonly known as the Porta's iris, is a rare and distinctive subspecies native to specific regions of Italy. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in woodland or streamside habitats. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are lanceolate and covered in a fine, white bloom.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
