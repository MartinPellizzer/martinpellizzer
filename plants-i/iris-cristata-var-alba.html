Iris cristata var. alba

Iris cristata var. alba, commonly known as the white woodland iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding delicate beauty to its natural environment.
