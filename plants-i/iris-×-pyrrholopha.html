Iris × pyrrholopha

Iris × pyrrholopha, commonly known as the golden iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a contrasting dark purple or violet center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
