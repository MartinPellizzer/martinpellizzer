Inga lactifera

Inga lactifera, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
