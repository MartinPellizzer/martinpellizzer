Indigofera ripae

Indigofera ripae, commonly known as the riparian indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found growing near water sources, such as rivers and streams, due to its preference for moist, well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Indigofera ripae is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
