Inga tenuiflora var. glabrior

Inga tenuiflora var. glabrior, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often glabrous stems and trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
