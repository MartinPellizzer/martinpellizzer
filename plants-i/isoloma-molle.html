Isoloma molle

Isoloma molle, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. Isoloma molle thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
