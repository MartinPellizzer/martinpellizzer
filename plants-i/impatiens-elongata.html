Impatiens elongata

Impatiens elongata, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Impatiens elongata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ornamental value in horticulture.
