Inga velloziana

Inga velloziana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several flat, edible seeds.

Inga velloziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
