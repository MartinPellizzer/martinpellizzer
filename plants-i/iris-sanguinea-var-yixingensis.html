Iris sanguinea var. yixingensis

Iris sanguinea var. yixingensis, commonly known as the Yixing red iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to China.

It features showy, vibrant red flowers with a dark purple center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and is also valued for its cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
