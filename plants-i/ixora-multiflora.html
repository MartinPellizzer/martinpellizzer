Ixora multiflora

Ixora multiflora, commonly known as the clustered flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora multiflora is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
