Ischyrolepis eleocharis

Ischyrolepis eleocharis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets. This species typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring shallow water or moist soil. The plant is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Its reproductive structures are specialized for wind pollination, which is common among Cyperaceae plants.
