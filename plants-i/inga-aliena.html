Inga aliena

Inga aliena, commonly known as the "wild silk tree," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in various soil types make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Additionally, Inga aliena is valued for its hard wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
