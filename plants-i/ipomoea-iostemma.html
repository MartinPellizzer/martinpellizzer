Ipomoea iostemma

Ipomoea iostemma, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and bloom in clusters.

Ipomoea iostemma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
