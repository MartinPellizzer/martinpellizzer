Iseilema laxum

Iseilema laxum, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Iseilema laxum plays an important role in stabilizing soil in marshy environments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
