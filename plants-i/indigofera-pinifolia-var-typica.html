Indigofera pinifolia var. typica

Indigofera pinifolia var. typica, commonly known as the needle-leaved indigo.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant can thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is typically found in areas with moderate to high rainfall.
