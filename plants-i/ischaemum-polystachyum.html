Ischaemum polystachyum

Ischaemum polystachyum, commonly known as the tall panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, showcasing its adaptability to different environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated raceme of spikelets, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Ischaemum polystachyum is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
