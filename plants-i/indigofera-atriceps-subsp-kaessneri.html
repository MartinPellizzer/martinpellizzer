Indigofera atriceps subsp. kaessneri

Indigofera atriceps subsp.

kaessneri, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its role in nitrogen fixation, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
