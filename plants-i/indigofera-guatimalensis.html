Indigofera guatimalensis

Indigofera guatimalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan indigo plant, is a species of legume native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye in some indigenous communities.
