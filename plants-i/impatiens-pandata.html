Impatiens pandata

Impatiens pandata, commonly known as the red-flowered impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange-red, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
