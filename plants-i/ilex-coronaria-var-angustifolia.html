Ilex coronaria var. angustifolia

Ilex coronaria var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its foliage remains dense and attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
