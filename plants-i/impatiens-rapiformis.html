Impatiens rapiformis

Impatiens rapiformis, commonly known as the horned impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like calyx that encases the flower. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a prominent, fleshy calyx.

Impatiens rapiformis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
