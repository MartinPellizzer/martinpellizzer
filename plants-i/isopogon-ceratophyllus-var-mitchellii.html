Isopogon ceratophyllus var. mitchellii

Isopogon ceratophyllus var. mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red or pink flowers that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
