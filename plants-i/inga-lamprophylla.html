Inga lamprophylla

Inga lamprophylla, commonly known as the golden inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain edible seeds, making it both ecologically and potentially agriculturally significant.
