Ipomoea strigillosa

Ipomoea strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea strigillosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly in natural and cultivated environments.
