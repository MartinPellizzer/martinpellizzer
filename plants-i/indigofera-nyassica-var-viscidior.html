Indigofera nyassica var. viscidior

Indigofera nyassica var. viscidior, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods containing seeds, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, coarse appearance.

It is valued for its historical use in dye production and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
