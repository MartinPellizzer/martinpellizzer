Iris clusiana var. transsilvanica

Iris clusiana var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lower petal. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The Transylvanian iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native range.
