Iris fontanesii var. mellorii

Iris fontanesii var. mellorii, commonly known as the Blue Flag Iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are sword-shaped, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and wetland biodiversity.
