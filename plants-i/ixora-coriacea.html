Ixora coriacea

Ixora coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf ixora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with rough, leathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Ixora coriacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
