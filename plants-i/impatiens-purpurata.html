Impatiens purpurata

Impatiens purpurata, commonly known as the purple touchstone, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features heart-shaped, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in pollinator habitats.
