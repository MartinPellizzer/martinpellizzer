Isachne beneckei f. depauperata

Isachne beneckei f. depauperata, commonly known as the slender isachne, is a grass species characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and compact growth habit.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance.

This variety is noted for its limited foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
