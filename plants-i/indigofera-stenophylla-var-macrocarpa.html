Indigofera stenophylla var. macrocarpa

Indigofera stenophylla var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited indigo, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
