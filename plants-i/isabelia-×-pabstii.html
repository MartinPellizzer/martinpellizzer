Isabelia × pabstii

Isabelia × pabstii, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a flowering plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Isabelia and Pabstia.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions where it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Isabelia × pabstii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
