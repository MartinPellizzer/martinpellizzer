Iberis amara subsp. decipiens

Iberis amara subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, rounded, greenish fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
