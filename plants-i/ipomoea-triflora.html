Ipomoea triflora

Ipomoea triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Ipomoea triflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
