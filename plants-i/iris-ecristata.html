Iris ecristata

Iris ecristata, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers with a distinctive, narrow spur. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris ecristata is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
