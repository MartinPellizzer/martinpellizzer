Isachne nipponensis var. kiangsiensis

Isachne nipponensis var. kiangsiensis, commonly known as the Kiangsi bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the flora in its native habitat.
