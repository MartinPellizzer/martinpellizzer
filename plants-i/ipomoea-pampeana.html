Ipomoea pampeana

Ipomoea pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds.

Ipomoea pampeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
