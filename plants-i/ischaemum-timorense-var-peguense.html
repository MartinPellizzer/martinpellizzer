Ischaemum timorense var. peguense

Ischaemum timorense var. peguense, commonly known as the Peguense grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often found in lowland areas with high humidity. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptability.
