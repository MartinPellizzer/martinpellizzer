Isodon parvifolius

Isodon parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Isodon parvifolius is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
