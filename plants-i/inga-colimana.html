Inga colimana

Inga colimana, commonly known as the Colombian inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with a few pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

Inga colimana is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
