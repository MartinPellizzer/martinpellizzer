Iris todaroana

Iris todaroana, commonly known as the Todaroan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It blooms in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
