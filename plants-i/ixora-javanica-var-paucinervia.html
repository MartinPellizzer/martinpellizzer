Ixora javanica var. paucinervia

Ixora javanica var. paucinervia, commonly known as the Javanese ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
