Inula parviflora

Inula parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Inula parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
