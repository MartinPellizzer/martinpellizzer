Ilex oxyphylla

Ilex oxyphylla, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex oxyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
