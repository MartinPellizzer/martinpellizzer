Indigofera kraussiana

Indigofera kraussiana, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It produces pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
