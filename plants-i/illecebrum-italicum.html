Illecebrum italicum

Illecebrum italicum, commonly known as the Italian spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features slender, creeping stems that can form dense mats, with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming from spring to early summer.

Illecebrum italicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
