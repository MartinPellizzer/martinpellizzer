Ipomoea fulgens

Ipomoea fulgens, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep red to orange in color. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.

Ipomoea fulgens is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar tubular flowers.
