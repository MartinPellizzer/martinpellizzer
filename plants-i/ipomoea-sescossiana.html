Ipomoea sescossiana

Ipomoea sescossiana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

Ipomoea sescossiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
