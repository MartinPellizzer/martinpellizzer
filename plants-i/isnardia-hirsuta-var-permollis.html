Isnardia hirsuta var. permollis

Isnardia hirsuta var. permollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in racemes, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
