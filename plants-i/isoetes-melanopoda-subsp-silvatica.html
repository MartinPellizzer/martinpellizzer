Isoetes melanopoda subsp. silvatica

Isoetes melanopoda subsp.

silvatica, commonly known as the black-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its dimorphic spores, with distinct male and female spore forms, a feature common in many species of the genus.

The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are usually submerged, with a dark coloration that helps in absorbing light for photosynthesis.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
