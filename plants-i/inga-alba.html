Inga alba

Inga alba, commonly known as the white inga, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its fruit pods contain edible seeds, and the tree is valued for its ecological and economic importance.

The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
