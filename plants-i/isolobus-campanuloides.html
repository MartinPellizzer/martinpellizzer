Isolobus campanuloides

Isolobus campanuloides, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from white to blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Isolobus campanuloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
