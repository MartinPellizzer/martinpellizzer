Ilex aquifolium var. caspia

Ilex aquifolium var. caspia, commonly known as the Caspian holly, is a variant of the common holly native to the Caspian Sea region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its spiny, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
