Indigofera affinis

Indigofera affinis, commonly known as the blue pea or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms depending on the climate.
