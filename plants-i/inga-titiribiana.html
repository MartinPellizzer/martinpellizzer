Inga titiribiana

Inga titiribiana, commonly known as the "Cocuyaco" or "Cocuyaco del Cielo," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small, elongated leaflets and produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often found in humid lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
