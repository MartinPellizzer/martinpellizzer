Ilex crenata f. nummularia

Ilex crenata f. nummularia, commonly known as Japanese Holly or Japanese Yew, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often used in horticulture for its dense foliage and ability to form tight, rounded shapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
