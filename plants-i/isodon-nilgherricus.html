Isodon nilgherricus

Isodon nilgherricus, commonly known as the Nilgiri lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Western Ghats in India.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that emit a citrusy aroma. Its flowering spikes produce small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color.

Isodon nilgherricus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
