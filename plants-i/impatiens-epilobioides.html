Impatiens epilobioides

Impatiens epilobioides, commonly known as the yellow water impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with five petals. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens epilobioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
