Isatis sabulosa

Isatis sabulosa, commonly known as sandworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in early summer, producing bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

Isatis sabulosa is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in dry, open habitats.
