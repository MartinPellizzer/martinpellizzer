Ipomoea macarenaensis

Ipomoea macarenaensis, commonly known as the Macarena morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers and climbing habit. The plant produces vibrant, showy blooms in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically during the early morning hours. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea macarenaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
