Isoschoenus armeria

Isoschoenus armeria, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or sandy coastal environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically covered in a fine layer of salt-tolerant hairs.

Isoschoenus armeria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with a maritime climate.
