Iria spiralis

Iria spiralis, commonly known as the spiral iris, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Iria spiralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
