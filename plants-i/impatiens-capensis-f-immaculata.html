Impatiens capensis f. immaculata

Impatiens capensis f. immaculata, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a variant of the common yellow rootflower.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America, characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

This cultivar is prized for its elegant appearance and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
