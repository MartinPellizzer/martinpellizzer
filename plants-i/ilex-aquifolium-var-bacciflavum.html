Ilex aquifolium var. bacciflavum

Ilex aquifolium var. bacciflavum, commonly known as the goldenberry holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its striking yellow berries.

This plant is native to western Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically dark green with spiny margins, and the plant can grow as either a shrub or a small tree. The golden berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits in gardens and hedgerows.
