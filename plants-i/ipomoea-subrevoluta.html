Ipomoea subrevoluta

Ipomoea subrevoluta, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It produces showy blooms in the morning, which close by midday, and is known for its rapid growth habit.

Ipomoea subrevoluta is also notable for its ability to climb and twine around supports, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
