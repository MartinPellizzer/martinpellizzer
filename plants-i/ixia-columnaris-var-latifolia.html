Ixia columnaris var. latifolia

Ixia columnaris var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fire lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and are arranged in a rosette at the base. This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
