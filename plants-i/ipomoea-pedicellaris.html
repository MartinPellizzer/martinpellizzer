Ipomoea pedicellaris

Ipomoea pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea pedicellaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing vine.
