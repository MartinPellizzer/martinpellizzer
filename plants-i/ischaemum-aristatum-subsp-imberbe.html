Ischaemum aristatum subsp. imberbe

Ischaemum aristatum subsp.

imberbe, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear dense clusters of spikelets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rough to the touch, with a prominent midrib.

The inflorescence consists of elongated, spike-like clusters that are often adorned with bristly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
