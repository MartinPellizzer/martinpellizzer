Ilex maximowicziana var. kanehirae

Ilex maximowicziana var. kanehirae, commonly known as the Kishiwada holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
