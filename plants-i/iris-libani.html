Iris libani

Iris libani, commonly known as the Lebanon iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
