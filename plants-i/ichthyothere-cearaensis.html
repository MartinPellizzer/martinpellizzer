Ichthyothere cearaensis

Ichthyothere cearaensis, commonly known as the Ceara ichthyothere, is a unique plant species native to the coastal regions of northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid and semi-arid habitat. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from the sandy soil.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
