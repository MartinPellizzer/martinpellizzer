Impatiens glauca var. glauca

Impatiens glauca var. glauca, commonly known as blue impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
