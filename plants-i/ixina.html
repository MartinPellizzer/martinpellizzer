Ixina

Ixina, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, resembling flames. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Ixina thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and glossy, adding to its visual appeal in gardens and landscapes.
