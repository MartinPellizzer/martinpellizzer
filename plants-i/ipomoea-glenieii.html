Ipomoea glenieii

Ipomoea glenieii, commonly known as the Glenie's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.

Ipomoea glenieii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
