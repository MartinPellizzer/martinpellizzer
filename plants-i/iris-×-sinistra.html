Iris × sinistra

Iris × sinistra, commonly known as the Western blue flag, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a white or yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizome, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Iris × sinistra is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
