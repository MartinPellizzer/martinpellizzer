Indigofera hippocrepoides

Indigofera hippocrepoides, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
