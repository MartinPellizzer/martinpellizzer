Ipomoea arachnoidea

Ipomoea arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider vine, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a red throat. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance.

Ipomoea arachnoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
