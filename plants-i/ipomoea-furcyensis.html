Ipomoea furcyensis

Ipomoea furcyensis, commonly known as the Congo morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is climbing and has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ipomoea furcyensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.
