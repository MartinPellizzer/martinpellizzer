Isnardia nitida

Isnardia nitida, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with numerous slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise sparse foliage.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
