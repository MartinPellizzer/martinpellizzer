Iodes velutina var. subvillosa

Iodes velutina var. subvillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive soft, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
