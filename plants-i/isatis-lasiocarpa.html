Isatis lasiocarpa

Isatis lasiocarpa, commonly known as the rough-hairy woad, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Isatis lasiocarpa is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
