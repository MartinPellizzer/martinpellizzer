Inga uraguensis

Inga uraguensis, commonly known as the Uraguay inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties, allowing it to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant has compound leaves with multiple oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
