Indigofera anceps

Indigofera anceps, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in traditional agriculture.
