Iris innominata

Iris innominata, commonly known as the western iris, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris innominata is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant landscaping.
