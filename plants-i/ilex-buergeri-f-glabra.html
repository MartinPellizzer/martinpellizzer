Ilex buergeri f. glabra

Ilex buergeri f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth holly, is a variant of the holly genus characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, typically growing in shaded, moist environments. The plant features small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that remain smooth and hairless throughout the growing season. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds.

The smooth holly is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
