Ipomoea irengana

Ipomoea irengana, commonly known as the red-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elongated seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea irengana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats alike.
