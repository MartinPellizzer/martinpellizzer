Ipomoea hungaiensis

Ipomoea hungaiensis, commonly known as the Hungai morning glory, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea hungaiensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
