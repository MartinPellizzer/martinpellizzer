Ipomoea hirsuta

Ipomoea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea hirsuta is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
