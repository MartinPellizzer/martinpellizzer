Indigofera rojasii

Indigofera rojasii, commonly known as the Mexican indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Indigofera rojasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
