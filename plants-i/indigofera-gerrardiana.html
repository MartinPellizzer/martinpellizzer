Indigofera gerrardiana

Indigofera gerrardiana, commonly known as the blue bean or Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is also valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
