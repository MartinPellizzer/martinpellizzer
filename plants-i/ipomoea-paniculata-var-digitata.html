Ipomoea paniculata var. digitata

Ipomoea paniculata var. digitata, commonly known as the fringed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
