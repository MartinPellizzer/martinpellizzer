Ixia pudica

Ixia pudica, commonly known as the "bells of Africa," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
