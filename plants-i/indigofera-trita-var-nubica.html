Indigofera trita var. nubica

Indigofera trita var. nubica, commonly known as the Nubian indigo plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly in the Horn of Africa and the Sahel. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, and its trifoliate leaves. It has adapted to thrive in poor soils and can withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and dye production.
