Ipomoea randii

Ipomoea randii, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Ipomoea randii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
