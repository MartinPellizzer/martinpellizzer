Ipomoea chrysotricha var. ovata

Ipomoea chrysotricha var. ovata, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking yellow flowers with a prominent central crown. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five lobes and a bright yellow corolla. This species thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
