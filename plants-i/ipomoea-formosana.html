Ipomoea formosana

Ipomoea formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning and closing at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ipomoea formosana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
