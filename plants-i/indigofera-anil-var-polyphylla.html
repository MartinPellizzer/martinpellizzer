Indigofera anil var. polyphylla

Indigofera anil var. polyphylla, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.
