Impatiens malayensis

Impatiens malayensis, commonly known as the Malay balsam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
