Ipomoea cairica var. semine-glabro

Ipomoea cairica var. semine-glabro, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a tropical vine characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves.

The plant produces striking trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The flowers are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds.

This species is noted for its rapid growth and ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
