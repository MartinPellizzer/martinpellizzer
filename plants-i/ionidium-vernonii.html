Ionidium vernonii

Ionidium vernonii, commonly known as the Vernonia ionidium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.
