Inga dysantha

Inga dysantha, commonly known as the coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are used to produce coffee.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
