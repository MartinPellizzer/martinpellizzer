Ixeris denticulata

Ixeris denticulata, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions makes it a hardy and resilient plant.
