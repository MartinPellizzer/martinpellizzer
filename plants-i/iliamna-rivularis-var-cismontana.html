Iliamna rivularis var. cismontana

Iliamna rivularis var. cismontana, commonly known as the western willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows along stream banks and moist soils, forming dense thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife species. The plant has slender, drooping branches and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces catkin-like inflorescences that release wind-pollinated seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
