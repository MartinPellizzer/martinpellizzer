Inga urceolata

Inga urceolata, commonly known as the "cup-shaped inga," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

It is often found in shaded, moist forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological benefits.
