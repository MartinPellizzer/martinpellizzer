Iris camillae f. dentata

Iris camillae f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed iris, is a cultivated form of the Camille iris, characterized by its distinctive serrated or toothed leaf margins.

This variety typically features vibrant blue or purple flowers with a striking contrast against its grass-like foliage. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists interested in iris hybrids.
