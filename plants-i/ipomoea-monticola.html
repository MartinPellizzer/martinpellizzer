Ipomoea monticola

Ipomoea monticola, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea monticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
