Ilex amara var. muenteriana

Ilex amara var. muenteriana, commonly known as the Muenter's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
