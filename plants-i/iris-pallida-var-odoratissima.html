Iris pallida var. odoratissima

Iris pallida var. odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant pale iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is a hardy plant that prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
