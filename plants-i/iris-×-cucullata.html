Iris × cucullata

Iris × cucullata, commonly known as the bearded iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers have three upright outer petals called standards and three inner petals called falls, which often feature distinctive markings. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its foliage consists of broad, grass-like leaves that form a dense, attractive ground cover.
