Isoetes engelmannii var. valida

Isoetes engelmannii var. valida, commonly known as the valid water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that form a dense, lace-like structure on the water's surface. The plant produces sporangia on specialized stalks, which release spores into the air for dispersal. It is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
