Impatiens roylei var. moschata

Impatiens roylei var. moschata, commonly known as the musk impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The plant's unique musky scent is a distinguishing feature, especially noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
