Impatiens omissa

Impatiens omissa, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are borne on slender pedicels and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Impatiens omissa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
