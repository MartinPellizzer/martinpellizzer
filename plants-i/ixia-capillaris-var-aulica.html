Ixia capillaris var. aulica

Ixia capillaris var. aulica, commonly known as the Aulic Ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and is often found in coastal areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
