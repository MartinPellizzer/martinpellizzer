Iberis simplex

Iberis simplex, commonly known as candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. Its foliage consists of hairy, lobed leaves that are typically evergreen.

Iberis simplex is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
