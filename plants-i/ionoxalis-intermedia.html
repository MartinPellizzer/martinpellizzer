Ionoxalis intermedia

Ionoxalis intermedia, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet bearing a heart-shaped base, and clusters of small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
