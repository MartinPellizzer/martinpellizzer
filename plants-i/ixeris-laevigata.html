Ixeris laevigata

Ixeris laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features basal leaves that are smooth and oval-shaped, with a hairy stem that becomes more prominent as it matures. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Ixeris laevigata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
