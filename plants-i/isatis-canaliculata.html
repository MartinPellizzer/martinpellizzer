Isatis canaliculata

Isatis canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed woad, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and it produces small, round, green pods that contain seeds.

This species is often grown for its historical use in dye production and as a potential ornamental plant.
