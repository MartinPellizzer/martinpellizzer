Indigofera acutiflora

Indigofera acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.
