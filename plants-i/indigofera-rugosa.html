Indigofera rugosa

Indigofera rugosa, commonly known as rough indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the surrounding soil ecosystem.
