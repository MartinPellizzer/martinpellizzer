Icacorea bahamenis

Icacorea bahamenis, commonly known as the Bahamian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

Icacorea bahamenis is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it resilient to salt spray and drought.
