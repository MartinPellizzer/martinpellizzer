Ipomoea humboldtiana

Ipomoea humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's morning glory, is a striking vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant colors such as purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tuberous roots, though they are not commonly consumed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in moist, shaded areas.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
