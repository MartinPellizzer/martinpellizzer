Ipomoea bifrons

Ipomoea bifrons, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. Its flowers are typically about 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter, and it produces small, round berries that contain seeds.

Ipomoea bifrons is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it receives adequate sunlight.
