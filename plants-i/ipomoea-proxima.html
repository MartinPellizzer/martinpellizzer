Ipomoea proxima

Ipomoea proxima, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Ipomoea proxima is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
