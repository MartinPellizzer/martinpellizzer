Ipomoea menispermacea

Ipomoea menispermacea, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
