Indigofera rehmannii

Indigofera rehmannii, commonly known as Chinese indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria, making it beneficial for soil fertility. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant can grow up to 2 meters tall.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
