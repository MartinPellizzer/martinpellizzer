Iberis pinetorum

Iberis pinetorum, commonly known as the pine wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in pine and fir forests, where it forms dense mats on the forest floor.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal remedies.
