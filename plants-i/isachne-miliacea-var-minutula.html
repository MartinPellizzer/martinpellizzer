Isachne miliacea var. minutula

Isachne miliacea var. minutula, commonly known as the small-seeded barnyardgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and rice fields. The plant has slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. Its seeds are minute and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in agricultural settings.

The botanical features of this plant make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for crop management.
