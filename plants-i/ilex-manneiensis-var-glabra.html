Ilex manneiensis var. glabra

Ilex manneiensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, smooth leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are a food source for various birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

The varietal name 'glabra' refers to its smooth, hairless leaf surface.
