Inula × barthiana

Inula × barthiana, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Inula helenium and Inula roylei, characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Inula × barthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
