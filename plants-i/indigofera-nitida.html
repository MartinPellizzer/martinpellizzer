Indigofera nitida

Indigofera nitida, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Indigofera nitida is also valued for its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine.
