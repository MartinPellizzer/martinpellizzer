Isoberlinia angolensis

Isoberlinia angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan false acacia, is a leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Isoberlinia angolensis is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and fuel.
