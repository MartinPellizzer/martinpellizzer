Inula caspica

Inula caspica, commonly known as the Caspian Inula, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Inula caspica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
