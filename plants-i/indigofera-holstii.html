Indigofera holstii

Indigofera holstii, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera holstii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing properties in agricultural settings.
