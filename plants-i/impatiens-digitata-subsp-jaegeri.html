Impatiens digitata subsp. jaegeri

Impatiens digitata subsp.

jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's touch-me-not, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
