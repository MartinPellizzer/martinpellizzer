Ilex urbaniana var. riedlaei

Ilex urbaniana var. riedlaei, commonly known as the Riedl's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which is typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical regions.
