Ixiolirion karateginum

Ixiolirion karateginum, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and it produces delicate blue to purple flowers in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.

Ixiolirion karateginum is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
