Impatiens eramosa

Impatiens eramosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens eramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
