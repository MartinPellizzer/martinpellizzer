Iguanura wallichiana var. minor

Iguanura wallichiana var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
