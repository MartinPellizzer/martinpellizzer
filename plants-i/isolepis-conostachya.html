Isolepis conostachya

Isolepis conostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and riparian zones. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Isolepis conostachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
