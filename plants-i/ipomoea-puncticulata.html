Ipomoea puncticulata

Ipomoea puncticulata, commonly known as the dotted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences, walls, and trees.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
