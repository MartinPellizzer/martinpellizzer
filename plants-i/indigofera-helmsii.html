Indigofera helmsii

Indigofera helmsii, commonly known as the Hawaiian indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Indigofera helmsii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native insects and birds.
