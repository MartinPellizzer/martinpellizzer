Iris stenophylla subsp. allisonii

Iris stenophylla subsp.

allisonii, commonly known as the Allison's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, rocky soils in mountainous regions, preferring partial to full shade.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive dark central stripe. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy, three-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
