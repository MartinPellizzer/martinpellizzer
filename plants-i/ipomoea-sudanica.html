Ipomoea sudanica

Ipomoea sudanica, commonly known as Sudan morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and the presence of milky sap.
