Indigofera erythrogramma

Indigofera erythrogramma, commonly known as the red-stemmed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its stems are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Indigofera erythrogramma is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
