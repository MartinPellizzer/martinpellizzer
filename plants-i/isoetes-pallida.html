Isoetes pallida

Isoetes pallida, commonly known as the pale water moss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized leaves called sporophylls. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its root-like structures. Its morphology includes a central axis with alternating spore-bearing and non-spore-bearing leaves.

Isoetes pallida is notable for its ability to survive in low-nutrient conditions, making it an indicator species for certain aquatic ecosystems.
