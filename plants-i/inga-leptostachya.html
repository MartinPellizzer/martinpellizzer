Inga leptostachya

Inga leptostachya, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The fruit is a pod-like structure that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Its rapid growth makes it a useful species for reforestation and erosion control.
