Iris multiflora

Iris multiflora, commonly known as the Korean iris, is a species native to East Asia, particularly Korea, Japan, and China.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 60 to 90 centimeters. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Iris multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
