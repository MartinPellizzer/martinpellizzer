Inga membranacea

Inga membranacea, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
