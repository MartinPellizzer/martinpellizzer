Ipomoea erecta

Ipomoea erecta, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

Ipomoea erecta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has also been used in traditional medicine.
