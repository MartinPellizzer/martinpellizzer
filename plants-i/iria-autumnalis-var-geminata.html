Iria autumnalis var. geminata

Iria autumnalis var. geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf evening-primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and emit a mild scent. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer to early autumn. Its botanical name reflects its tendency to produce twin leaves, a characteristic feature of the species.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
