Indigofera setosissima

Indigofera setosissima, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a notable ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including forests and grasslands.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it easily identifiable in the wild.
