Isopogon buxifolius var. typicus

Isopogon buxifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a bluish-gray hue. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife, particularly nectar-feeding birds and insects.
