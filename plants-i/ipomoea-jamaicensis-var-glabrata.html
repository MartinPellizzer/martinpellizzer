Ipomoea jamaicensis var. glabrata

Ipomoea jamaicensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the Jamaican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
