Ipomoea chiapensis

Ipomoea chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ipomoea chiapensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
