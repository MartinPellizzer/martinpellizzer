Impatiens zimmermanniana

Impatiens zimmermanniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in warm climates.
