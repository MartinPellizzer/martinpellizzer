Ipomoea portobellensis

Ipomoea portobellensis, commonly known as the Portobello morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces large, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add vertical interest to their landscapes.
