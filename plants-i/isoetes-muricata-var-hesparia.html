Isoetes muricata var. hesparia

Isoetes muricata var. hesparia, commonly known as the Hesperian waterwheel, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wheel-like arrangement of spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are clustered along the central axis of the plant. The plant has a rhizome that anchors it in the substrate, with long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. It is typically found in shallow, still or slow-moving waters, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its morphological similarities to other species in the Isoetes genus, but it can be distinguished by specific features in its spore morphology and leaf structure.
