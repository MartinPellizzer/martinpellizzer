Ilex beadlei

Ilex beadlei, commonly known as the Beadle holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds and remain on the plant throughout the winter. Ilex beadlei thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban conditions further enhance its value as a garden plant.
