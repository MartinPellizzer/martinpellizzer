Iris neoensata

Iris neoensata, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Iris neoensata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
