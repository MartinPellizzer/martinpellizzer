Iberis numidica

Iberis numidica, commonly known as the African rock rose, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

Iberis numidica is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
