Ipomoea transvaalensis

Ipomoea transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea transvaalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
