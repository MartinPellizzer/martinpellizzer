Ilex memecylifolia var. plana

Ilex memecylifolia var. plana, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are typically alternate and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its evergreen nature provides year-round foliage, adding visual interest to gardens.
