Impatiens cordata

Impatiens cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Impatiens cordata is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
