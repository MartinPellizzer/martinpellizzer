Ilex corallina var. aberrans

Ilex corallina var. aberrans, commonly known as the coral holly, is a rare variant of the coral holly species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like red berries and glossy, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small, white, and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters during the spring.
