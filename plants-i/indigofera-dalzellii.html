Indigofera dalzellii

Indigofera dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
