Inga speciosa var. membranacea

Inga speciosa var. membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceae inga, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in agroforestry systems.
