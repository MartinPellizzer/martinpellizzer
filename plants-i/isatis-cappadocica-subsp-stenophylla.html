Isatis cappadocica subsp. stenophylla

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woad, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its historical use in dye production and as a potential medicinal plant.
