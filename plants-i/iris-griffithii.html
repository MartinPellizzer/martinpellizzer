Iris griffithii

Iris griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a perennial herb with tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has three upright leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris griffithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
