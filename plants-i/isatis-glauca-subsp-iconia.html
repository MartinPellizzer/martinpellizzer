Isatis glauca subsp. iconia

Isatis glauca subsp.

iconia, commonly known as the blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in prairies and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
