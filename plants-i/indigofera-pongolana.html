Indigofera pongolana

Indigofera pongolana, commonly known as Natal indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna ecosystems.
