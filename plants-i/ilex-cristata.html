Ilex cristata

Ilex cristata, commonly known as the Christmas holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and vibrant red berries that persist through the winter. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant's ability to retain its foliage year-round contributes to its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
