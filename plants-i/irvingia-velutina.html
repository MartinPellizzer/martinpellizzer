Irvingia velutina

Irvingia velutina, commonly known as African mango or mabira, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Irvingia velutina is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and for its high oil content.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
