Iris sisyrinchium var. minor

Iris sisyrinchium var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and meadows. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy, three-petaled flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
