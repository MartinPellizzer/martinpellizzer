Impatiens thorelii

Impatiens thorelii, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
