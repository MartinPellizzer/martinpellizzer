Iris rossii f. purpurascens

Iris rossii f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered iris, is a botanical variant of the Iris rossii species.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central Chile, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
