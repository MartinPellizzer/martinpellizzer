Inga chrysantha

Inga chrysantha, commonly known as the golden inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important component in agroforestry systems.
