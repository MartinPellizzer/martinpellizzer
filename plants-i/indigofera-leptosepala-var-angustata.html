Indigofera leptosepala var. angustata

Indigofera leptosepala var. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, hairy pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
