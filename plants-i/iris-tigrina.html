Iris tigrina

Iris tigrina, commonly known as the tiger iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns resembling tiger stripes, which give it its common name. Iris tigrina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
