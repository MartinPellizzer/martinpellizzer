Impatiens clavigera var. auriculata

Impatiens clavigera var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate impatiens, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
