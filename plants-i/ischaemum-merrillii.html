Ischaemum merrillii

Ischaemum merrillii, commonly known as the Hawaiian panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Ischaemum merrillii plays a role in the ecological dynamics of Hawaiian ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
