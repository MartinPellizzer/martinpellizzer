Isoetes muricata subsp. truncata

Isoetes muricata subsp.

truncata, commonly known as the truncated water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments. It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface.

The plant has a distinctive spore-producing structure called a sorus, which is located on the underside of its fronds. It is typically found in still or slow-moving waters with soft substrates.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in maintaining aquatic biodiversity.
