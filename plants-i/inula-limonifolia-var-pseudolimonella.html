Inula limonifolia var. pseudolimonella

Inula limonifolia var. pseudolimonella, commonly known as the lemon-honeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and emit a subtle lemon scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological landscapes.
