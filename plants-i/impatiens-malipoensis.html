Impatiens malipoensis

Impatiens malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo touch-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its unique seed dispersal mechanism involves explosive pods that release seeds forcefully when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
