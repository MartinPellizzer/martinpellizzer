Indigofera nambalensis

Indigofera nambalensis, commonly known as the Nambalensis indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
