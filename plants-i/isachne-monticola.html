Isachne monticola

Isachne monticola, commonly known as the mountain isachne, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical structures bearing the flowers.

Isachne monticola is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
