Indigofera palmeri

Indigofera palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera palmeri is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
