Ipomoea megalochlamys

Ipomoea megalochlamys, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is a vine that can grow quite long, often climbing supports with its spiraling stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and broad, contributing to its lush, tropical appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
