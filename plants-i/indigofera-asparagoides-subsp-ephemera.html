Indigofera asparagoides subsp. ephemera

Indigofera asparagoides subsp.

ephemera, commonly known as the ephemeral indigo. This plant is a flowering species native to certain regions of Africa, characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features such as succulent stems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential use in traditional medicine.
