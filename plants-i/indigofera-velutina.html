Indigofera velutina

Indigofera velutina, commonly known as velvet indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Indigofera velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
