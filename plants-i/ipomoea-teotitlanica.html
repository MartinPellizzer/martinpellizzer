Ipomoea teotitlanica

Ipomoea teotitlanica, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea teotitlanica is also known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
