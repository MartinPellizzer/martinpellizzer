Ipomoea greenstockii

Ipomoea greenstockii, commonly known as the Greenstock morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive if not managed properly.
