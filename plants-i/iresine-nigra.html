Iresine nigra

Iresine nigra, commonly known as blackleaf iron plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety dark green leaves with prominent veins, which turn red or purple in response to sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by winged, seed-bearing fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Iresine nigra is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
