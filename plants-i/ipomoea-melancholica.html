Ipomoea melancholica

Ipomoea melancholica, commonly known as the sorrowful morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its name reflects its association with melancholy, though it is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
