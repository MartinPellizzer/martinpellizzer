Inga coragypsea

Inga coragypsea, commonly known as the "coco tree" or "sweet acacia," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

The plant also plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and ecosystems.
