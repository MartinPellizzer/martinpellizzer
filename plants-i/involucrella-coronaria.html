Involucrella coronaria

Involucrella coronaria, commonly known as the crown-involucre, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy yellow flower surrounded by a whorl of bracts.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
