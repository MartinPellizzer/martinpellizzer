Iris tridentata

Iris tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has three distinct, pointed leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. Iris tridentata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
