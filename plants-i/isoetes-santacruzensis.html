Isoetes santacruzensis

Isoetes santacruzensis, commonly known as the Santa Cruz watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes, which is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

This plant is typically found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant in the sediment while its foliage extends into the water column. The plant's spore-producing structures are clustered in a distinctive, spiky arrangement, which is a key feature of the Isoetes genus.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
