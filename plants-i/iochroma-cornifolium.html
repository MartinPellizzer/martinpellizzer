Iochroma cornifolium

Iochroma cornifolium, commonly known as the horned leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped leaves and showy, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Iochroma cornifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation areas.
