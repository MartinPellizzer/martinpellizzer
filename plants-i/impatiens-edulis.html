Impatiens edulis

Impatiens edulis, commonly known as the edible impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine pubescence.

Although not widely cultivated for ornamental purposes, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
