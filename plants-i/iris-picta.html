Iris picta

Iris picta, commonly known as the painted iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with intricate patterns resembling paint strokes, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and dark green, while its stems are upright and slender.

Iris picta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
