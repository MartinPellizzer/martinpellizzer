Impatiens parviflora

Impatiens parviflora, commonly known as the small-flower impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Impatiens parviflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
