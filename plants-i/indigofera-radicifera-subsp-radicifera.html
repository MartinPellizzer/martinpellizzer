Indigofera radicifera subsp. radicifera

Indigofera radicifera subsp.

radicifera, commonly known as the root indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant produces pods containing dark blue seeds, which have been historically used for dyeing fabrics. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.
