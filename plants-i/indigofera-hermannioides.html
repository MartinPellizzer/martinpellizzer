Indigofera hermannioides

Indigofera hermannioides, commonly known as the Hermann's indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
