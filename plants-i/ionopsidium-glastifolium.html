Ionopsidium glastifolium

Ionopsidium glastifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in moist, shaded environments.

Ionopsidium glastifolium is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
