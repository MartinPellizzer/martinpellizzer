Ilex longipes f. hirsuta

Ilex longipes f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a variant of the longipes persimmon, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is an evergreen shrub or small tree with leathery, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by edible, orange-red fruits that ripen in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique foliage texture.
