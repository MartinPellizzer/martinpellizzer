Indigofera emarginella

Indigofera emarginella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
