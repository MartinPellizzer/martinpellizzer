Inula undulata

Inula undulata, commonly known as the wavy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a wavy margin, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
