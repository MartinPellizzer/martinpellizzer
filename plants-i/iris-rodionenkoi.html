Iris rodionenkoi

Iris rodionenkoi, commonly known as the Karaganda iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
