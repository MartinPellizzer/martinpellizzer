Ipomoea pallida

Ipomoea pallida, commonly known as the pale morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Ipomoea pallida is an annual plant that spreads through seeds and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
