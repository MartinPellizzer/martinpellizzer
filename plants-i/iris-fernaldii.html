Iris fernaldii

Iris fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
