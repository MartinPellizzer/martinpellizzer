Ilex aquifolium var. hodginsii

Ilex aquifolium var. hodginsii, commonly known as the Hodginsii holly, is a variety of the common holly, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. The leaves are glossy, dark green on top, and white or silver underneath, with spiny margins that provide a striking visual contrast. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is well-suited for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
