Ilex verticillata

Ilex verticillata, commonly known as the winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that persist through the winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn bright red in the fall before dropping. It typically grows in wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Ilex verticillata is valued in landscaping for its seasonal color and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
