Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-marginata

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-marginata, commonly known as the silver-marginated holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its striking silver-edged leaves.

The foliage features a dark green center with a prominent white or silver margin, creating a visually appealing contrast. This plant is evergreen, retaining its foliage year-round, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in favorable conditions.

The plant produces small, red berries in the winter, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
