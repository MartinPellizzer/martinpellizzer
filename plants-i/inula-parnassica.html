Inula parnassica

Inula parnassica, commonly known as Parnassian Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets, with the central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Inula parnassica is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
