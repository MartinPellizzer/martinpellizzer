Indigofera kirilowii

Indigofera kirilowii, commonly known as Chinese indigo, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
