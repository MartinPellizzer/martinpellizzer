Iphigenia novae-zelandiae

Iphigenia novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand broom, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
