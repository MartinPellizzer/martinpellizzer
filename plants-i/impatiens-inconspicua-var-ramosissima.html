Impatiens inconspicua var. ramosissima

Impatiens inconspicua var. ramosissima, commonly known as the clustered touch-me-not, is a delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
