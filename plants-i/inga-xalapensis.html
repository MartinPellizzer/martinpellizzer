Inga xalapensis

Inga xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican inga, is a flowering plant native to Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Inga xalapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
