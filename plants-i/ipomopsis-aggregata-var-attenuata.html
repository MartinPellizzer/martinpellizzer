Ipomopsis aggregata var. attenuata

Ipomopsis aggregata var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender antelope horn.

This perennial plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to lavender, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and contributing to the ecological diversity of its environment.
