Iris pontica

Iris pontica, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed upper petal. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and marshy areas.

Iris pontica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
