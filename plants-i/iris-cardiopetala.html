Iris cardiopetala

Iris cardiopetala, commonly known as the heart-leaved iris, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

The iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral structure.
