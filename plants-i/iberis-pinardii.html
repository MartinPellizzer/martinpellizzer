Iberis pinardii

Iberis pinardii, commonly known as the Algerian candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky areas of Algeria and Tunisia.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
