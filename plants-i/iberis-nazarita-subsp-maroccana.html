Iberis nazarita subsp. maroccana

Iberis nazarita subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan candytuft, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a silvery-gray appearance due to dense pubescence.

It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
