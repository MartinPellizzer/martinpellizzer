Iochroma gesnerioides var. flavum

Iochroma gesnerioides var. flavum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
