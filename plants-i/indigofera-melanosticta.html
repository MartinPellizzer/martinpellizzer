Indigofera melanosticta

Indigofera melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its historical significance in dye production has also contributed to its recognition in traditional botanical studies.
