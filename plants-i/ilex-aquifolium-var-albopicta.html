Ilex aquifolium var. albopicta

Ilex aquifolium var. albopicta, commonly known as the white-veined holly, is a cultivar of the common holly plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins on the dark green leaves, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, leathery foliage that remains throughout the year. It produces small, red berries in the winter, which are attractive to birds.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
