Isoberlinia niembaensis

Isoberlinia niembaensis, commonly known as the Niemba isoberlinia, is a leguminous tree native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it contributes to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, and the tree is often used for its timber and as a source of fodder.
