Isocoma coronopifolia var. pedicellata

Isocoma coronopifolia var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small blue to purple blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
