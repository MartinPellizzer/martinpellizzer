Ipomoea bouvetii

Ipomoea bouvetii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces elongated, serpentine fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea bouvetii is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
