Iris sisyrinchium f. rosea

Iris sisyrinchium f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered iris, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its striking, rose-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

The inflorescence features a single, showy flower with three petals and three sepals, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
