Inula rungwensis

Inula rungwensis, commonly known as the African elecampane, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, slender stem covered in rough, hairy foliage. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, forming dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Inula rungwensis is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
