Ipomoea lorentzii

Ipomoea lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

Ipomoea lorentzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
