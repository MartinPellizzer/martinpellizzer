Indigofera lotononoides

Indigofera lotononoides, commonly known as the African indigo or the blue bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces pods containing small, dark blue seeds that are used in traditional dyeing practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
