Ipomoea sagittata var. diversifolia

Ipomoea sagittata var. diversifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
