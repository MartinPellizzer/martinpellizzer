Iris paradoxa

Iris paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical iris, is a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the showy flowers.

Iris paradoxa is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
