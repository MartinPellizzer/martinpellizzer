Iris domestica

Iris domestica, commonly known as the domestic iris, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a dense rosette at the base.

Iris domestica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
