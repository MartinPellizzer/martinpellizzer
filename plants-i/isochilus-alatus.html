Isochilus alatus

Isochilus alatus, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on the stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

Isochilus alatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
