Ichthyothere hirsuta

Ichthyothere hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ichthyothere, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Despite its resilience, it is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
