Indigofera pseudodemissa

Indigofera pseudodemissa, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value and traditional uses.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
