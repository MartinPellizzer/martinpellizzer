Iris spuria var. ochroleuca

Iris spuria var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, six-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color, with a distinctive spur at the base of the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
