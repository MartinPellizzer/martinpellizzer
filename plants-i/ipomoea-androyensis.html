Ipomoea androyensis

Ipomoea androyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a climbing habit, often twining around supports. Its flowers are typically 5-7 cm in diameter, with five petals and a prominent central spur.

Ipomoea androyensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
