Ipomoea yaracuyensis

Ipomoea yaracuyensis, commonly known as the Yaracuyensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to white. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its flowers bloom in the morning, hence the common name, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
