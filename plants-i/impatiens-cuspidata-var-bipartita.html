Impatiens cuspidata var. bipartita

Impatiens cuspidata var. bipartita, commonly known as the split-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are often divided into two parts, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
