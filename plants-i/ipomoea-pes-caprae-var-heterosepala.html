Ipomoea pes-caprae var. heterosepala

Ipomoea pes-caprae var. heterosepala, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in sandy coastal environments. It is a trailing vine that often forms dense mats along shorelines.

Its adaptability to saline soils makes it a common invasive species in many coastal areas.
