Impatiens urticoides

Impatiens urticoides, commonly known as the stinging nettle impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, often stinging leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves contain irritant compounds similar to those found in stinging nettle, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.

The flowers are usually pink or white and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
