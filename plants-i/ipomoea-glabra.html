Ipomoea glabra

Ipomoea glabra, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
