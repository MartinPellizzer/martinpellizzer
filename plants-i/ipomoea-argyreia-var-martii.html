Ipomoea argyreia var. martii

Ipomoea argyreia var. martii, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly silver or white in color, with a contrasting green margin. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
