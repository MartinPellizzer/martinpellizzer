Indigofera sabulicola

Indigofera sabulicola, commonly known as sand indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant produces elongated seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species for xerophytic environments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
