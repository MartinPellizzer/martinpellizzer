Ixora polycephala

Ixora polycephala, commonly known as the clustered flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora polycephala is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
