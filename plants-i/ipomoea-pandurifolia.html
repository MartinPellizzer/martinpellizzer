Ipomoea pandurifolia

Ipomoea pandurifolia, commonly known as the winged bean or angel's trumpet, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive wing-like structures along the leaf veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod that contains edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its nutritional properties.
