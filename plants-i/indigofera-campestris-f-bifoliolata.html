Indigofera campestris f. bifoliolata

Indigofera campestris f. bifoliolata, commonly known as the bifoliate indigo, is a variant of the indigo plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifoliate (two-lobed) leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
