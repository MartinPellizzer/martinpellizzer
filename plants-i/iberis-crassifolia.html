Iberis crassifolia

Iberis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis crassifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
