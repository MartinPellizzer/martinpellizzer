Ipomoea purpurea

Ipomoea purpurea, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Ipomoea purpurea are encased in a fleshy, spiny capsule that splits open when mature.
