Isopogon horridus

Isopogon horridus, commonly known as the rough isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and bright yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Isopogon horridus is an important component of the local flora and provides habitat for various insects and birds.
