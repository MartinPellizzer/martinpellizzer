Iris bloudowii var. turkestanica

Iris bloudowii var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
