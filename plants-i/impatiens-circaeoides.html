Impatiens circaeoides

Impatiens circaeoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.
