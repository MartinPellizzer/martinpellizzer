Inga subvestita

Inga subvestita, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, and its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
