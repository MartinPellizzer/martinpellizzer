Indocalamus fargesii

Indocalamus fargesii, commonly known as the Farges bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and long, narrow leaves. This species is often found in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its culms are highly valued for their strength and flexibility, making them useful in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
