Illigera cordata var. cordata

Illigera cordata var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf illigera, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in desert or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
