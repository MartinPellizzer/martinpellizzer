Ischnosiphon puberulus var. scaber

Ischnosiphon puberulus var. scaber, commonly known as the rough hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, typically greenish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
