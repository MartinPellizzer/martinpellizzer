Inula britannica var. angustifolia

Inula britannica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and yellow composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant has lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
