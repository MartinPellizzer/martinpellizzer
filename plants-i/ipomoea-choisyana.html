Ipomoea choisyana

Ipomoea choisyana, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and open in the morning, hence its common name. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
