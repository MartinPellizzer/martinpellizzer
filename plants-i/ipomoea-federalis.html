Ipomoea federalis

Ipomoea federalis, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Ipomoea federalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
