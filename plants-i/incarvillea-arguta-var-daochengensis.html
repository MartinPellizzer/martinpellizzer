Incarvillea arguta var. daochengensis

Incarvillea arguta var. daochengensis, commonly known as the Daocheng incarvillea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to alpine environments.

Due to its unique ecological niche, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
