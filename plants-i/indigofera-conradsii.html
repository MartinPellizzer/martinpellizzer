Indigofera conradsii

Indigofera conradsii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive, heart-shaped base. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
