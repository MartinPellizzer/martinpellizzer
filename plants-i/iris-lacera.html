Iris lacera

Iris lacera, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, blue to violet-colored flowers with three petals and three sepals. Iris lacera thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
