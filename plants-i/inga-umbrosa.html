Inga umbrosa

Inga umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow tree or umbrosa rain tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces showy, pale yellow or white flowers. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded conditions make it useful for reforestation and soil stabilization.

The plant's ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
