Isocarpha berteroi

Isocarpha berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
