Ipomoea cinerea

Ipomoea cinerea, commonly known as the gray morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It blooms during the day, hence its common name, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Ipomoea cinerea is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
