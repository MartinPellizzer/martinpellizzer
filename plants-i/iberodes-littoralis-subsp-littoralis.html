Iberodes littoralis subsp. littoralis

Iberodes littoralis subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal mallow, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It typically grows in saline and sandy soils, adapting to harsh coastal conditions.

The plant features hairy, ribbed stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five pink or reddish-purple petals, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
