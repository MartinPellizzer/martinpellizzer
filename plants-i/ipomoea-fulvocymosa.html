Ipomoea fulvocymosa

Ipomoea fulvocymosa, commonly known as the yellow-eyed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or orange eye. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

Ipomoea fulvocymosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
