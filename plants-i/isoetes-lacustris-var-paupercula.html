Isoetes lacustris var. paupercula

Isoetes lacustris var. paupercula, commonly known as the slender water starwort, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched sporophytes that produce spores in specialized structures called sporangia. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself in muddy or sandy substrates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, forming dense mats on the water's surface.

This variety is notable for its morphological adaptations that allow it to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
