Indigofera dumosa

Indigofera dumosa, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnate, with several small leaflets arranged along a central stem.

Indigofera dumosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
