Iris sofarana f. westii

Iris sofarana f. westii, commonly known as the Western iris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
