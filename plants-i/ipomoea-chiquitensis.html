Ipomoea chiquitensis

Ipomoea chiquitensis, commonly known as the Chiquitania morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

Ipomoea chiquitensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.
