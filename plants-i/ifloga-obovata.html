Ifloga obovata

Ifloga obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and usually have a pale green or white coloration.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
