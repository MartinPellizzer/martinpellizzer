Itea macrophylla

Itea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically green on top and white underneath. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on soil pH. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Itea macrophylla is valued for its ornamental foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
