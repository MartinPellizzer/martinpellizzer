Isoetes parvula

Isoetes parvula, commonly known as the small water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a spike at the base of the plant. Isoetes parvula is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
