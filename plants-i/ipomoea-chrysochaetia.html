Ipomoea chrysochaetia

Ipomoea chrysochaetia, commonly known as golden hair vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with long, slender petals and a distinctive, hairy calyx. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
