Ismene amancaes

Ismene amancaes, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals.
