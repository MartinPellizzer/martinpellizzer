Ipomoea nelsonii

Ipomoea nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
