Ipomopsis tridactyla

Ipomopsis tridactyla, commonly known as the three-fingered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.

Ipomopsis tridactyla is often found in areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
