Inula britannica var. sericans

Inula britannica var. sericans, commonly known as the sericant inula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Inula. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
