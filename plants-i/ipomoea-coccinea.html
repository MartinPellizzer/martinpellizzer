Ipomoea coccinea

Ipomoea coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often climbing support structures with its spiraling stems.

Ipomoea coccinea is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
