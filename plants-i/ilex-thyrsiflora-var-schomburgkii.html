Ilex thyrsiflora var. schomburgkii

Ilex thyrsiflora var. schomburgkii, commonly known as Schomburgk's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warmer regions.
