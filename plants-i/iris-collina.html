Iris collina

Iris collina, commonly known as the alpine iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Iris collina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
