Ilex subtriflora

Ilex subtriflora, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable plant in local ecosystems.
