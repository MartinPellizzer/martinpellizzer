Iberis intermedia var. maialis

Iberis intermedia var. maialis, commonly known as the Maialis candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bright white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for Mediterranean climates.
