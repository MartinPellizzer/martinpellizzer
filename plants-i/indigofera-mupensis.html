Indigofera mupensis

Indigofera mupensis, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces pods containing small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera mupensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
