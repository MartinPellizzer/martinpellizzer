Ipomoea acaponetensis

Ipomoea acaponetensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Ipomoea acaponetensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
