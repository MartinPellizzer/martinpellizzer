Indigofera tryonii

Indigofera tryonii, commonly known as the yellow indigo or Tryon's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Indigofera tryonii is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a source of dye or forage.
