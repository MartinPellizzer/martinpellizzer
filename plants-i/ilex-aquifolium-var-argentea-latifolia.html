Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-latifolia

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-latifolia, commonly known as the silver-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its striking silver-white variegation on its broad, glossy leaves.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and it produces small, red berries in the winter months. The plant is evergreen, providing year-round interest and is relatively low maintenance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
