Indigofera boiviniana

Indigofera boiviniana, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces seed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
