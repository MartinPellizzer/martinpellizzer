Ipomoea cuneifolia

Ipomoea cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea cuneifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and aesthetic appeal.
