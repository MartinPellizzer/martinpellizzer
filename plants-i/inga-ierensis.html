Inga ierensis

Inga ierensis, commonly known as the red-veined inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its striking, palmate leaves with deep red veins that contrast with its green foliage. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inga ierensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
