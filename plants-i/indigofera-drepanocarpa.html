Indigofera drepanocarpa

Indigofera drepanocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various traditional cuisines.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and use in sustainable agriculture.
