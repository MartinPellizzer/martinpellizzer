Iris unguicularis subsp. angustifolia

Iris unguicularis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
