Indigofera bancroftii

Indigofera bancroftii, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Indigofera bancroftii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
