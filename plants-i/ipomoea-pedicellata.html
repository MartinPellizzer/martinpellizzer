Ipomoea pedicellata

Ipomoea pedicellata, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea pedicellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
