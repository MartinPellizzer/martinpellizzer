Ilex apoensis var. punctata

Ilex apoensis var. punctata, commonly known as the punctate holly, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with distinctive white or pale spots. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
