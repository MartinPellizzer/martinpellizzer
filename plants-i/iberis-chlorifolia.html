Iberis chlorifolia

Iberis chlorifolia, commonly known as the white candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Iberis chlorifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
