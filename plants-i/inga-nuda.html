Inga nuda

Inga nuda, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. Inga nuda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded conditions make it a popular choice for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
