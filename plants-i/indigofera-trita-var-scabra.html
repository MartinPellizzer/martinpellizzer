Indigofera trita var. scabra

Indigofera trita var. scabra, commonly known as rough indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
