Ipomoea argyrophylla var. somalensis

Ipomoea argyrophylla var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somaliland morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white and purple flowers that open in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a silvery sheen, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique coloration and blooming pattern make it a notable species in botanical studies.
