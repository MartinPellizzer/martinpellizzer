Iberodes littoralis subsp. gallaecica

Iberodes littoralis subsp.

gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Atlantic coast of Europe. It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and grey-green, often hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal habitats, tolerating salt spray and strong winds. It has a compact growth form and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
