Ipomoea robynsiana

Ipomoea robynsiana, commonly known as the Robin's bean or morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

Ipomoea robynsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
