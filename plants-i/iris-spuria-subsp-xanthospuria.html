Iris spuria subsp. xanthospuria

Iris spuria subsp.

xanthospuria, commonly known as the yellow-spurred iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive spur at the base of the petal. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
