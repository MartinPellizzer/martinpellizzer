Iria paupercula

Iria paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's iris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue or white flowers. It is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Iria paupercula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
