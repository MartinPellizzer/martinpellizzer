Indigofera dosycnium

Indigofera dosycnium, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. It produces small, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The plant is known for its historical use in producing natural dyes, particularly a deep blue color used in textiles.
