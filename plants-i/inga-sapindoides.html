Inga sapindoides

Inga sapindoides, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in the understory of humid tropical forests. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
