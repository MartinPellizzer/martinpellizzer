Imperatoria verticillaris

Imperatoria verticillaris, commonly known as the false nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Imperatoria verticillaris is also known for its stinging hairs, similar to true nettles, which can cause irritation upon contact.
