Isodon calycinus

Isodon calycinus, commonly known as the purple beard tongue, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Isodon calycinus is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
