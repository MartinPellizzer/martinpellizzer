Indigofera atricephala

Indigofera atricephala, commonly known as the black-headed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers, which bloom in dense clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
