Indigofera cooperi

Indigofera cooperi, commonly known as the blue bonnet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
