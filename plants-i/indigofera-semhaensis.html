Indigofera semhaensis

Indigofera semhaensis, commonly known as the Sudan indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly Sudan, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports agricultural practices. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
