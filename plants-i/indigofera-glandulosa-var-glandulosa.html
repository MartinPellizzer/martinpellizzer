Indigofera glandulosa var. glandulosa

Indigofera glandulosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it often has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
