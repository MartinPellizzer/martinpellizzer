Ipomoea lactifera

Ipomoea lactifera, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant's leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The species is also noted for its milky sap, which can be toxic to some animals.
