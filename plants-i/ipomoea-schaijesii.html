Ipomoea schaijesii

Ipomoea schaijesii, commonly known as the Schaijes's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.

Ipomoea schaijesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
