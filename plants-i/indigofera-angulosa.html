Indigofera angulosa

Indigofera angulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, where it thrives in full sun.
