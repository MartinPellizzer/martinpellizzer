Ilex nipponica

Ilex nipponica, commonly known as Japanese holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. Ilex nipponica thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it suitable for various garden settings.
