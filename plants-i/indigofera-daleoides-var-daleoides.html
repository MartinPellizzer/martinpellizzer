Indigofera daleoides var. daleoides

Indigofera daleoides var. daleoides, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
