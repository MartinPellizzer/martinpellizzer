Indocalamus andropogonoides

Indocalamus andropogonoides, commonly known as the bamboo reed, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a tall, slender grass that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its hollow, jointed stems. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
