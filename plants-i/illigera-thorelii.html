Illigera thorelii

Illigera thorelii, commonly known as the Thorelli's rock orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure, with flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its pollination is primarily carried out by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
