Ifloga verticillata

Ifloga verticillata, commonly known as the spiralleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ifloga verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
