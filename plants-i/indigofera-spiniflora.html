Indigofera spiniflora

Indigofera spiniflora, commonly known as the prickly indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used for ornamental purposes or in traditional medicine.

Indigofera spiniflora is often cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
