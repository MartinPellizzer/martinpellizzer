Indigofera mauritanica

Indigofera mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania indigo plant, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
