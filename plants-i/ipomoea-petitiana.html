Ipomoea petitiana

Ipomoea petitiana, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a vine, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea petitiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
