Ipomoea gilana

Ipomoea gilana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea gilana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
