Ipomoea macrosepala

Ipomoea macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea macrosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
