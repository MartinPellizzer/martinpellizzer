Inula punctata

Inula punctata, commonly known as the dotted sunflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust stem with hairy surfaces and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive white or pale dot pattern on the underside. Inula punctata is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in full sun.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
