Ischaemum latifolium var. oligostachyum

Ischaemum latifolium var. oligostachyum, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh grass, is a variant of the marsh grass species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to moist environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic life.
