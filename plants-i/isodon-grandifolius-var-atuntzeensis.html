Isodon grandifolius var. atuntzeensis

Isodon grandifolius var. atuntzeensis, commonly known as the Atuntze mountain verbena, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in temperate to cool climates. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
