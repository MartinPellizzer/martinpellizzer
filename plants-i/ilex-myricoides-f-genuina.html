Ilex myricoides f. genuina

Ilex myricoides f. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
