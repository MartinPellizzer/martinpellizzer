Ipomoea barbatisepala var. angustata

Ipomoea barbatisepala var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its spread in natural habitats.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
