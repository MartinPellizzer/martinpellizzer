Impatiens dasyvexilla

Impatiens dasyvexilla, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
