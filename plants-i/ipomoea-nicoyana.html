Ipomoea nicoyana

Ipomoea nicoyana, commonly known as the Nicoyan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is part of the Convolvulaceae family.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like capsule that matures in the fall.
