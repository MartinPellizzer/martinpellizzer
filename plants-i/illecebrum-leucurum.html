Illecebrum leucurum

Illecebrum leucurum, commonly known as the white ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in gardens.
