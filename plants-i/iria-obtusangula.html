Iria obtusangula

Iria obtusangula, commonly known as the blunt-angled iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with six petals, typically in shades of blue or purple. Iria obtusangula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
