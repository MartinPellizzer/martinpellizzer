Iris flavissima var. bloudowii

Iris flavissima var. bloudowii, commonly known as the Bloudow's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
