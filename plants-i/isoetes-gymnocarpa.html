Isoetes gymnocarpa

Isoetes gymnocarpa, commonly known as the naked-seeded watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized leaves called sporophylls. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its morphology includes a central axis with tightly packed, leaf-like structures that help it anchor in soft substrates.

The reproductive system of Isoetes gymnocarpa is unique, as it produces spores rather than seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
