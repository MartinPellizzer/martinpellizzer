Ilex opaca var. floribunda

Ilex opaca var. floribunda, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.

The variety 'floribunda' is noted for its profuse flowering, adding visual interest to the landscape.
