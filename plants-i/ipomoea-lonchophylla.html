Ipomoea lonchophylla

Ipomoea lonchophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive long, narrow lobed structure, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its rapid growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can be invasive in some areas.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
