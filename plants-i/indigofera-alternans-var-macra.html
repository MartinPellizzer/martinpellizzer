Indigofera alternans var. macra

Indigofera alternans var. macra, commonly known as the large-flowered indigo.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of natural dyes and its use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
