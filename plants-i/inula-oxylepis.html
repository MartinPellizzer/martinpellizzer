Inula oxylepis

Inula oxylepis, commonly known as the African elecampane, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom in late summer.

Inula oxylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
