Impatiens pallida var. alba, commonly known as white touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, and its heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a trailing habit and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
