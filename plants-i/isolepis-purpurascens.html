Isolepis purpurascens

Isolepis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple tussock grass, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and purple-tinged inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its spikelets are small and clustered at the ends of the stems.

Isolepis purpurascens plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various invertebrates and birds.
