Indigofera wauensis

Indigofera wauensis, commonly known as the Wau indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
