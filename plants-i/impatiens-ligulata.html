Impatiens ligulata

Impatiens ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Impatiens ligulata is a perennial species that blooms from early summer to fall, adding ornamental value to natural habitats.
