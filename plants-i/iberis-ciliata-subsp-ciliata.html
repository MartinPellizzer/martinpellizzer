Iberis ciliata subsp. ciliata

Iberis ciliata subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Armenia and Turkey. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine cilia, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
