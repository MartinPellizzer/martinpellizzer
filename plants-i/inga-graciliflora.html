Inga graciliflora

Inga graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga graciliflora is also valued for its potential in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
