Indigofera zanzibarica

Indigofera zanzibarica, commonly known as Zanzibar indigo, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem with thorny branches.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
