Isachne virgata

Isachne virgata, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a silver-gray sheen, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

Isachne virgata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
