Ipomoea crassipes var. volubilis

Ipomoea crassipes var. volubilis, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is an aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a rapid growth rate, often forming dense mats on the surface of freshwater bodies. Its roots are adapted for absorbing nutrients from the water, which can lead to eutrophication if left unchecked.

The species is considered an invasive weed in many tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.
