Iris rossii

Iris rossii, commonly known as the New Zealand iris, is a species native to New Zealand, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its tall, slender stems that bear three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. Iris rossii has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
