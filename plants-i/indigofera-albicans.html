Indigofera albicans

Indigofera albicans, commonly known as white indigo, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
