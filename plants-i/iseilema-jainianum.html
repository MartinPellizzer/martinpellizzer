Iseilema jainianum

Iseilema jainianum, commonly known as the Jain's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
