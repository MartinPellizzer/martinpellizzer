Iodes velutina var. velutina

Iodes velutina var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The orchid's unique appearance and aromatic blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
