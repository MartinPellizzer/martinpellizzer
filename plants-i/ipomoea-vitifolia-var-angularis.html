Ipomoea vitifolia var. angularis

Ipomoea vitifolia var. angularis, commonly known as the angular leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
