Indigofera ellenbeckii

Indigofera ellenbeckii, commonly known as Ellenbeck's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to certain regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, often purple or blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

While not widely cultivated for commercial purposes, it holds ecological significance in its native habitats.
