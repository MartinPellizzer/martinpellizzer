Indigofera brevifolia

Indigofera brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along forest edges.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
