Indigofera secundiflora var. secundiflora

Indigofera secundiflora var. secundiflora, commonly known as the Mexican indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
