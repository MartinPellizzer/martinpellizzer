Ixora roseituba var. glabra

Ixora roseituba var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically smooth and glossy, giving the plant its "glabra" designation, which refers to the lack of hair on the surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is closely related to other ixora species, sharing similar growth patterns and floral characteristics.
