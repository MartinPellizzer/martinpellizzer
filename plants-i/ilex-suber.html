Ilex suber

Ilex suber, commonly known as the holly tree or evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are usually glossy and have spiny margins. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Ilex suber is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a symbol of endurance in Mediterranean ecosystems.
