Ipomoea illustris

Ipomoea illustris, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ipomoea illustris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
