Ischaemum pappinisseriense

Ischaemum pappinisseriense, commonly known as the Pappinissere grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically hairy and pale green. The plant has narrow, flat leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is adapted to temperate climates and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
