Ipomoea heterotricha var. homotricha

Ipomoea heterotricha var. homotricha, commonly known as the "bush morning glory," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy blooms and climbing habit.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
