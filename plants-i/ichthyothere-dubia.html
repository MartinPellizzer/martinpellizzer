Ichthyothere dubia

Ichthyothere dubia, commonly known as the ghost crab plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble the exoskeleton of crabs.

It thrives in shallow, brackish waters and is often found in coastal regions where it plays a role in stabilizing sediment. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the substrate and form dense mats. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Despite its unusual appearance, Ichthyothere dubia is an important component of its ecosystem, providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
