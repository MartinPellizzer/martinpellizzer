Ixiolaena tomentosa

Ixiolaena tomentosa, commonly known as the silky grass or silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with soft, hairy surfaces that give it a silky texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Ixiolaena tomentosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
