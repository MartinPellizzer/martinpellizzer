Impatiens longirostris

Impatiens longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and distinctive, elongated calyx that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Impatiens longirostris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
