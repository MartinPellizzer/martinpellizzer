Iris pocockii

Iris pocockii, commonly known as Pocock's iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Iris pocockii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
