Ilex perado subsp. platyphylla

Ilex perado subsp.

platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically broad and flat, giving it its subspecific name "platyphylla.".

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
