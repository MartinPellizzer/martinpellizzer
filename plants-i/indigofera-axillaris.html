Indigofera axillaris

Indigofera axillaris, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a perennial shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
