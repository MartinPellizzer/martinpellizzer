Ilex sugerokii var. brevipedunculata

Ilex sugerokii var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked holly, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
