Iris bituminosa

Iris bituminosa, commonly known as the black root iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black rhizomes, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Iris bituminosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
