Ixora rivalis

Ixora rivalis, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange inflorescences, which resemble flames and bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ixora rivalis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
