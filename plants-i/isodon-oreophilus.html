Isodon oreophilus

Isodon oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain verbena, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
