Ischaemum leersioides

Ischaemum leersioides, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Ischaemum leersioides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
