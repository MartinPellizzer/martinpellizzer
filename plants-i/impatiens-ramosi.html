Impatiens ramosi

Impatiens ramosi, commonly known as the creeping impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is a popular choice for hanging baskets and garden borders due to its vibrant blooms and ability to spread easily.

Impatiens ramosi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
