Inula sophiae

Inula sophiae, commonly known as the elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Inula sophiae has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported respiratory and digestive benefits.
