Iliamna rivularis var. diversa

Iliamna rivularis var. diversa, commonly known as the Alaska willow, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
