Indigofera heterotricha subsp. heterotricha

Indigofera heterotricha subsp.

heterotricha, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue or purple flowers.

The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species, contributing to soil fertility. It grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a dye source.
