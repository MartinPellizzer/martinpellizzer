Ixia rapunculoides var. robusta

Ixia rapunculoides var. robusta, commonly known as the robusta ixia, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting dark veins. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, displaying a compact growth habit. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces a cluster of flowers at the top of a sturdy stem.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
