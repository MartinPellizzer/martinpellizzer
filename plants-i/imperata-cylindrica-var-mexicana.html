Imperata cylindrica var. mexicana

Imperata cylindrica var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and long, narrow leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces spiky, cylindrical inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Mexican needlegrass is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
