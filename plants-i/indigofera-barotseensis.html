Indigofera barotseensis

Indigofera barotseensis, commonly known as the Barotse indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are often used in traditional practices and can be processed to produce natural dyes.
