Ipomoea ciliolata

Ipomoea ciliolata, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive purple or reddish hue. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with hairy margins, and its stems are typically climbing or trailing. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea ciliolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
