Indigofera cassioides

Indigofera cassioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

Indigofera cassioides is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
