Iris uniflora f. caricina

Iris uniflora f. caricina, commonly known as the Caribaea iris, is a botanical variant of the iris genus native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability.
