Indigofera reflexa

Indigofera reflexa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Indigofera reflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
