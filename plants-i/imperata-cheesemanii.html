Imperata cheesemanii

Imperata cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
