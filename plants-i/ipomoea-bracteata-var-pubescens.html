Ipomoea bracteata var. pubescens

Ipomoea bracteata var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a tropical vine characterized by its hairy, pubescent foliage.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
