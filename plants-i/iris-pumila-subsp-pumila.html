Iris pumila subsp. pumila

Iris pumila subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the dwarf iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northern hemisphere. It typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat.

The plant produces delicate, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
