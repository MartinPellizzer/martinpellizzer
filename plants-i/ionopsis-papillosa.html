Ionopsis papillosa

Ionopsis papillosa, commonly known as the silky ionopsis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Ionopsis papillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure for optimal growth.
