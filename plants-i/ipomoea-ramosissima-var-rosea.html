Ipomoea ramosissima var. rosea

Ipomoea ramosissima var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to rose, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a climbing plant in landscapes.
