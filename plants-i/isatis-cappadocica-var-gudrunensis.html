Isatis cappadocica var. gudrunensis

Isatis cappadocica var. gudrunensis, commonly known as Gudrun's woad, is a perennial herb native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.
