Isolepis acicularis

Isolepis acicularis, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, which develop into dark brown, cylindrical seed heads. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Isolepis acicularis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
