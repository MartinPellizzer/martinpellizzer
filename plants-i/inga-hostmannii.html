Inga hostmannii

Inga hostmannii, commonly known as the pink-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant is notable for its striking pink or red flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are typically compound, with a velvety texture and a distinctive appearance.

Inga hostmannii is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
