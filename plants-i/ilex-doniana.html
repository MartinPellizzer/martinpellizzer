Ilex doniana

Ilex doniana, commonly known as the African holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic features.
