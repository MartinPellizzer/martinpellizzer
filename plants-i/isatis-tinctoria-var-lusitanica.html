Isatis tinctoria var. lusitanica

Isatis tinctoria var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian woad, is a variant of the common woad plant native to Europe.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is notable for its historical use in producing indigo dye, similar to its parent species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and historical studies.
