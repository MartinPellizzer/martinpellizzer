Isolepis fibrosa

Isolepis fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along lake shores. Its fibrous stems are strong and flexible, contributing to its ability to stabilize shorelines.

Isolepis fibrosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
