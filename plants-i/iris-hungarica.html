Iris hungarica

Iris hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers consist of three outer petals and three inner sepals.

Iris hungarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
