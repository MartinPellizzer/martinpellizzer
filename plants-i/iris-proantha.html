Iris proantha

Iris proantha, commonly known as the purple iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris proantha is valued in gardens for its striking blooms and adaptability to various climates.
