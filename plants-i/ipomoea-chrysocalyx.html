Ipomoea chrysocalyx

Ipomoea chrysocalyx, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
