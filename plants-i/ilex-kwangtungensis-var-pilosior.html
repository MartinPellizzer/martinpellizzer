Ilex kwangtungensis var. pilosior

Ilex kwangtungensis var. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy Chinese holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a notable species in horticultural collections.
