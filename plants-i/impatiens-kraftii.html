Impatiens kraftii

Impatiens kraftii, commonly known as the purple-stemmed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens kraftii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
