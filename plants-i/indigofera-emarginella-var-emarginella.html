Indigofera emarginella var. emarginella

Indigofera emarginella var. emarginella, commonly known as the narrowleaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often narrow and pointed. Its flowers are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in small clusters, and attract various pollinators.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
