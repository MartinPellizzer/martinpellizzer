Ipomoea polpha

Ipomoea polpha, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms with five petals. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea polpha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
