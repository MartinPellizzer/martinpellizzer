Iberis carnosa subsp. grosmiquelii

Iberis carnosa subsp.

grosmiquelii, commonly known as the Corsican carnation, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a compact rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
