Ischaemum melicoides

Ischaemum melicoides, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Ischaemum melicoides is often considered a weed in cultivated areas due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
