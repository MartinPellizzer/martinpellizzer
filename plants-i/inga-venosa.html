Inga venosa

Inga venosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a tropical species belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, pod-like fruit that turns yellow when mature. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a sweet, fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures for its wood and fruit.
