Ilex chamaedryfolia

Ilex chamaedryfolia, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves that are typically oval in shape and have spiny margins. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Ilex chamaedryfolia thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or foundation plant.
