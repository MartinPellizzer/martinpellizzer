Iris × chrysophoenicia

Iris × chrysophoenicia, commonly known as the golden iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris × hollandica and Iris × germanica.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to gold-colored flowers, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant is a deciduous perennial, native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms.
