Ipomoea purpurea var. superba

Ipomoea purpurea var. superba, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of violet or purple. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are usually bright green and slightly hairy. Its stems are strong and twining, allowing it to climb supports such as trellises or fences.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
