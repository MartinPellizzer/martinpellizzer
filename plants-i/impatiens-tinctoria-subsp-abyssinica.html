Impatiens tinctoria subsp. abyssinica

Impatiens tinctoria subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of stems.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its scientific name reflects its historical use as a source of natural dye, particularly for textiles.
