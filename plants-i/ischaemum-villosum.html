Ischaemum villosum

Ischaemum villosum, commonly known as hairy foxtail, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its inflorescence is a spikelet cluster, with bracts that are usually hairy and pointed.

Ischaemum villosum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions.
