Iris caucasica var. linifolia

Iris caucasica var. linifolia, commonly known as Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces showy, hexagonal blue or violet flowers with three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
