Indigofera elachantha

Indigofera elachantha, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
