Indigofera parkesii var. longipedunculata

Indigofera parkesii var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in long, drooping clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
