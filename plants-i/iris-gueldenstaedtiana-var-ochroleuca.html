Iris gueldenstaedtiana var. ochroleuca

Iris gueldenstaedtiana var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and hardy nature.
