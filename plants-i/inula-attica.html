Inula attica

Inula attica, commonly known as the Greek Inula or yellow thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Inula attica is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
