Iris × germanica var. gypsea

Iris × germanica var. gypsea, commonly known as the German iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including shades of purple, blue, and white, with a distinctive beak-like structure.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
