Isoetes giessii

Isoetes giessii, commonly known as the giant water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water, forming dense mats on the lake or pond bottom. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. Isoetes giessii is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
