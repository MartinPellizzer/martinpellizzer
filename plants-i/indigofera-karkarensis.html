Indigofera karkarensis

Indigofera karkarensis, commonly known as Karkare indigo, is a flowering plant species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has fibrous roots that help in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
