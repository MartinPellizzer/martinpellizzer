Impatiens aurantiaca

Impatiens aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and conservation efforts.
