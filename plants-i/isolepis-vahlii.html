Isolepis vahlii

Isolepis vahlii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often hidden within the leaf sheaths, making it less conspicuous.

Isolepis vahlii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
