Ipomoea mollissima var. glabrior

Ipomoea mollissima var. glabrior, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can become invasive if not managed.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
