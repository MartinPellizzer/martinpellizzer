Ixia scillaris subsp. lata

Ixia scillaris subsp.

lata, commonly known as the broad-leaved ixia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other ixia species, contributing to its unique appearance.
