Ipomoea repanda var. undulata

Ipomoea repanda var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, wavy-creased leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its tendrils.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant flowers throughout the growing season.
