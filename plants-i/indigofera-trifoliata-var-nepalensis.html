Indigofera trifoliata var. nepalensis

Indigofera trifoliata var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal indigo plant, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its roots are known for their high content of indigoid compounds, which have been historically used in dye production.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
