Indigofera karnatakana

Indigofera karnatakana, commonly known as the Karnataka indigo plant, is a leguminous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, tropical regions.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it holds cultural and economic significance in traditional practices.
