Ipomoea incomta

Ipomoea incomta, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces long, winding vines that can climb or trail. Its flowers are typically 5- to 7-inch wide, with five petals and a prominent, spurred corona.

Ipomoea incomta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
