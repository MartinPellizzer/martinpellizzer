Illipe multiflora

Illipe multiflora, commonly known as the white icka, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
