Ipomoea valida

Ipomoea valida, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea valida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
