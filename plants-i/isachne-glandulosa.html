Isachne glandulosa

Isachne glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Isachne glandulosa is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
