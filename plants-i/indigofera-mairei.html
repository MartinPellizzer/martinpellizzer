Indigofera mairei

Indigofera mairei, commonly known as the Maire's indigo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Indigofera mairei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
