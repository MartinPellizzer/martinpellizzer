Iris sibirica var. stenopetala

Iris sibirica var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, typically reaching up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
