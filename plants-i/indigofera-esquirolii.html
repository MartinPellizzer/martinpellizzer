Indigofera esquirolii

Indigofera esquirolii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre indigo, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils.

It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
