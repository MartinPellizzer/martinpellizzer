Incaea

Incaea, commonly known as the "Inca flower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plants are characterized by their showy, often pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. Incaea species typically have palmately lobed leaves and are often found in moist, shaded environments.
