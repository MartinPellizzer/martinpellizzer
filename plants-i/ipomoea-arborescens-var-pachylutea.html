Ipomoea arborescens var. pachylutea

Ipomoea arborescens var. pachylutea, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces edible, starchy tubers similar to sweet potatoes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
