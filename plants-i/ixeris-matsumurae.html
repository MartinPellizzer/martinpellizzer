Ixeris matsumurae

Ixeris matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Ixeris matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
