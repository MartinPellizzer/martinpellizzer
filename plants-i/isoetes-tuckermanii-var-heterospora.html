Isoetes tuckermanii var. heterospora

Isoetes tuckermanii var. heterospora, commonly known as the Tuckerman's quillwort, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky morphology with elongated, segmented stems and dense clusters of spore-bearing structures called sporangia. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in still or slow-moving water bodies with muddy substrates. Its sporangia produce two types of spores, hence the name "heterospora," which is a key feature in its reproductive biology.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
