Ilex jacobsii

Ilex jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacob's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces small white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Ilex jacobsii typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for birds and insects.
