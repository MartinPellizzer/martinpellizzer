Illipe burckiana

Illipe burckiana, commonly known as the white illipe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The tree produces an oily seed kernel, which is used in the production of cosmetics and traditional medicines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
