Inga ellsworthiana

Inga ellsworthiana, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically compound, with a feathery appearance, and it produces small, edible pods that contain seeds.

Inga ellsworthiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
