Isachne nipponensis var. minor

Isachne nipponensis var. minor, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf isachne, is a small perennial grass native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
