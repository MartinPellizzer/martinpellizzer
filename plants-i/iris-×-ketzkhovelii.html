Iris × ketzkhovelii

Iris × ketzkhovelii, commonly known as the Ketzkhovel iris, is a naturally occurring hybrid of Iris sibirica and Iris tatarica.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. The plant features upright, grass-like foliage and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and unique genetic makeup, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
