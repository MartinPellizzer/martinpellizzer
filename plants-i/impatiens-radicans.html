Impatiens radicans

Impatiens radicans, commonly known as the trailing impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground or over surfaces. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and typically have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Impatiens radicans is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and as a houseplant due to its adaptability and vibrant floral display.
