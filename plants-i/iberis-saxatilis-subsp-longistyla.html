Iberis saxatilis subsp. longistyla

Iberis saxatilis subsp.

longistyla, commonly known as the rock mustard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive silvery underside that reflects light.

The subspecies is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and is often found in open, sunny environments.
