Ixora carolinensis var. typica

Ixora carolinensis var. typica, commonly known as the Caroline flame bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
