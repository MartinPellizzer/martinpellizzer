Ipomoea dichotoma var. trilobata

Ipomoea dichotoma var. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply divided into three lobes. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

This species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
