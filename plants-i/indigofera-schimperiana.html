Indigofera schimperiana

Indigofera schimperiana, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
