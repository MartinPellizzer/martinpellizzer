Indigofera candolleana

Indigofera candolleana, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces cylindrical pods containing lens-shaped seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
