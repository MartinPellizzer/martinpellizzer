Iris amasiana

Iris amasiana, commonly known as the Amasina iris, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has strap-like, dark green leaves that form a basal rosette.

Iris amasiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
