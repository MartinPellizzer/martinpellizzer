Indigofera tristis

Indigofera tristis, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant can grow as a shrub or vine.

Indigofera tristis is often used in traditional medicine and has some ornamental value in gardens.
