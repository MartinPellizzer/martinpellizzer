Ipomoea dammeriana

Ipomoea dammeriana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, sunny environments. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea dammeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
