Imperatoria verticillata

Imperatoria verticillata, commonly known as the red root or red stem plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, narrow, linear shape.

Imperatoria verticillata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
