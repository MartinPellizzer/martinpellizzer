Ischaemum kingii

Ischaemum kingii, commonly known as King's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Ischaemum kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
