Indigofera trigonelloides

Indigofera trigonelloides, commonly known as the African indigo or trigonelloid indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.
