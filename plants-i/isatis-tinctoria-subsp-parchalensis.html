Isatis tinctoria subsp. parchalensis

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

parchalensis, commonly known as the Kashmir indigo, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of northern India and Pakistan. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant blue flowers.

The plant is notable for its historical use in natural dye production, particularly for producing a deep blue pigment. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional horticulture and ecological studies.
