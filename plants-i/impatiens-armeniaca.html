Impatiens armeniaca

Impatiens armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
