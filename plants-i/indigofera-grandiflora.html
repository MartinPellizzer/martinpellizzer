Indigofera grandiflora

Indigofera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
