Ipomoea malvaeoides var. nitida

Ipomoea malvaeoides var. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving it the common name "morning glory.".

The varietal form *nitida* refers to the silky, shiny texture of its leaves.
