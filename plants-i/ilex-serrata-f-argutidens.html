Ilex serrata f. argutidens

Ilex serrata f. argutidens, commonly known as the serrated holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and evergreen leaves that are typically elliptical with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded gardens.
