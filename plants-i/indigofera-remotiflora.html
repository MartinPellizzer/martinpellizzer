Indigofera remotiflora

Indigofera remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its seeds are encased in elongated, cylindrical pods, typical of the legume family.

Indigofera remotiflora is often found in open woodland and scrub habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
