Inga guentheri

Inga guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense canopy. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, showy flowers that are often white or pale pink. Its fruits are pod-like structures that split open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Inga guentheri is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and soil stabilization in tropical forests.
