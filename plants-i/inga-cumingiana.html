Inga cumingiana

Inga cumingiana, commonly known as the "Cuming's inga," is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Inga cumingiana is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
