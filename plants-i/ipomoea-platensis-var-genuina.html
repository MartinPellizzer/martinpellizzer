Ipomoea platensis var. genuina

Ipomoea platensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.
