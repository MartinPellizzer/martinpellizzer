Ilex pernyi var. veitchii

Ilex pernyi var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's holly, is a variant of the Ilex pernyi species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and small, bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its hardy nature allows it to withstand colder climates, and it is often cultivated for its winter interest and structural form.

The berries are also a food source for birds and other wildlife.
