Iris furseorum

Iris furseorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and dark green. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a distinctive three-lobed lower petal.

Iris furseorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
