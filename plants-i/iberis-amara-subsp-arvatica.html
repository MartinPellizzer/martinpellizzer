Iberis amara subsp. arvatica

Iberis amara subsp.

arvatica, commonly known as bitter candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, such as along roadsides or in dry meadows.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and floral characteristics compared to other Iberis species.
