Inga panamensis var. clavata

Inga panamensis var. clavata, commonly known as the Panama Inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agroforestry systems. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The species is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
