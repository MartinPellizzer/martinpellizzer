Iodes pierlotii

Iodes pierlotii, commonly known as the dwarf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, woody structure and dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
