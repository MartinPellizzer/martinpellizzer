Inga punctata var. panamensis

Inga punctata var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panama inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has potential uses in agroforestry systems.
