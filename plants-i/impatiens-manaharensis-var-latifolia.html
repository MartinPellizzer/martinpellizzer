Impatiens manaharensis var. latifolia

Impatiens manaharensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight yellowish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
