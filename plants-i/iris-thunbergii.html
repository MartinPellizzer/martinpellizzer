Iris thunbergii

Iris thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Iris thunbergii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
