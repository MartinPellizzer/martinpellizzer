Isodon dhankutanus

Isodon dhankutanus, commonly known as the Dhankutan germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at moderate altitudes. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
