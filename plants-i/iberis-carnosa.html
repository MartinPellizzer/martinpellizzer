Iberis carnosa

Iberis carnosa, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

Iberis carnosa is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
