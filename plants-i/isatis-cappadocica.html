Isatis cappadocica

Isatis cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian woad, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to regions of Turkey and the Middle East, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are robust and upright.

Isatis cappadocica is also valued for its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine.
