Iberis fiorii

Iberis fiorii, commonly known as the Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Iberis fiorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
