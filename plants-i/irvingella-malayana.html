Irvingella malayana

Irvingella malayana, commonly known as the Malayan Irvingia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Irvingella malayana produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid and shaded environments.
