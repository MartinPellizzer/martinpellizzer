Ischaemum laxum

Ischaemum laxum, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.

The plant's adaptability to wet conditions and its rapid growth contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
