Iris pluriscapis

Iris pluriscapis, commonly known as the many-capped iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

The flowers feature three upright petals and three downward-curving sepals, creating a distinctive and elegant appearance.
