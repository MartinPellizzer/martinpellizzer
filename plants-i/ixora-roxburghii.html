Ixora roxburghii

Ixora roxburghii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its bright orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ixora roxburghii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
