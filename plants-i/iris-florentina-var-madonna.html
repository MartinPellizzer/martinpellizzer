Iris florentina var. madonna

Iris florentina var. madonna, commonly known as Madonna iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that adds texture to the garden. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
