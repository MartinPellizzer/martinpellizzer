Iris stricta

Iris stricta, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 feet in height, adorned with striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. Iris stricta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
