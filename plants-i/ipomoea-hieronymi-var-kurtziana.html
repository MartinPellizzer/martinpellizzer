Ipomoea hieronymi var. kurtziana

Ipomoea hieronymi var. kurtziana, commonly known as the Kurtz's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in ornamental gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
