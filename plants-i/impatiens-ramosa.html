Impatiens ramosa

Impatiens ramosa, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
