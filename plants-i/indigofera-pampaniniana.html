Indigofera pampaniniana

Indigofera pampaniniana, commonly known as the Pampangan indigo plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It produces seed pods that contain several small, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional practices.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
