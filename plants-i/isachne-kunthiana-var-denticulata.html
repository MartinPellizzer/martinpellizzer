Isachne kunthiana var. denticulata

Isachne kunthiana var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed isachne, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-ranked florets.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
