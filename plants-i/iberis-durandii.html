Iberis durandii

Iberis durandii, commonly known as the Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, rounded seed pods that mature into a cluster of hairy, inflated structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Iberis durandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, early spring blooms.
