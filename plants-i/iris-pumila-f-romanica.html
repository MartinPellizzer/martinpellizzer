Iris pumila f. romanica

Iris pumila f. romanica, commonly known as the Romanica dwarf iris, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle yet striking presence to rock gardens or alpine displays. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardeners in colder climates.

Its unique form and ornamental value make it a sought-after addition to specialized plant collections.
