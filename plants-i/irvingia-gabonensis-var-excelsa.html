Irvingia gabonensis var. excelsa

Irvingia gabonensis var. excelsa, commonly known as African mango, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits with a fibrous pulp. The plant is valued for its seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine. The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological and economic importance makes it a significant species in local biodiversity and agriculture.
