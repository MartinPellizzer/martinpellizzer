Inga acuminata

Inga acuminata, commonly known as the "silk bean tree," is a fast-growing tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate, making it useful for agroforestry and erosion control. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The tree also plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
