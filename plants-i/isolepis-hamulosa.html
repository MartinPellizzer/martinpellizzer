Isolepis hamulosa

Isolepis hamulosa, commonly known as the hooked flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Isolepis hamulosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
