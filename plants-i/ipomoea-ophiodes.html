Ipomoea ophiodes

Ipomoea ophiodes, commonly known as the red-rooted morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces distinctive red or orange roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Ipomoea ophiodes is a perennial herb that blooms throughout the summer months.
