Iberis bellidifolia

Iberis bellidifolia, commonly known as the rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, clustered white or yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Iberis bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
