Iris sisyrinchium var. monophylla

Iris sisyrinchium var. monophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green, with a smooth texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
