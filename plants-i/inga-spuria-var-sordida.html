Inga spuria var. sordida

Inga spuria var. sordida, commonly known as the black inga, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
