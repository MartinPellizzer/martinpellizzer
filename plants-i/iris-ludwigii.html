Iris ludwigii

Iris ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces seed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Iris ludwigii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
