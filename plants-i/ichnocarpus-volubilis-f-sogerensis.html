Ichnocarpus volubilis f. sogerensis

Ichnocarpus volubilis f. sogerensis, commonly known as the Sogerensian devil's claw, is a variant of the devil's claw plant native to specific regions in Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its distinctive, hooked tendrils and yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain hard, seed-like structures known as lathyrses. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation.
