Illicium tashiroi

Illicium tashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise (Illicium verum). The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
