Iris damascena

Iris damascena, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, dark violet throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the base. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris damascena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
