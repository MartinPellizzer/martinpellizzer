Inga quinogemina

Inga quinogemina, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals and wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
