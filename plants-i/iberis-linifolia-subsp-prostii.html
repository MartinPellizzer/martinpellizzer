Iberis linifolia subsp. prostii

Iberis linifolia subsp.

prostii, commonly known as the Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a low-growing perennial that features clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a spherical shape.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
