Ilex dahoon var. myrtifolia

Ilex dahoon var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Myrtleleaf Holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
