Ipomoea microdactyla var. integrifolium

Ipomoea microdactyla var. integrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with smooth margins and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its twining stems.

The species is related to other members of the Convolvulaceae family, known for their rapid growth and vibrant floral displays.
