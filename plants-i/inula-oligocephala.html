Inula oligocephala

Inula oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, known as a capitulum.

Inula oligocephala is often found in moist meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
