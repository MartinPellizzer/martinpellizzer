Imbricaria petiolaris

Imbricaria petiolaris, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and its ability to produce a large number of small, yellow flowers in late spring. The plant is an annual or biennial species, often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions and is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
