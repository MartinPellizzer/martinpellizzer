Ipomoea bidentata

Ipomoea bidentata, commonly known as the twin-tooth morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a herbaceous vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with tendrils that help it climb. Its flowers have five petals, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ipomoea bidentata is often found in wetland areas and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
