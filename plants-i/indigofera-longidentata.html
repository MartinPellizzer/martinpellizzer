Indigofera longidentata

Indigofera longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and benefits surrounding vegetation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
