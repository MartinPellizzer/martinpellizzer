Impatiens substipulata

Impatiens substipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and often grows in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Impatiens substipulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and ease of care.
