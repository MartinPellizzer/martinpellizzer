Indigofera subincana

Indigofera subincana, commonly known as the white indigo or silky indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain small, dark seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
