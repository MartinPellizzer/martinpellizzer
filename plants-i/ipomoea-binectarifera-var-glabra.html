Ipomoea binectarifera var. glabra

Ipomoea binectarifera var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
