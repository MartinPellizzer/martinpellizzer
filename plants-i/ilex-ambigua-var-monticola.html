Ilex ambigua var. monticola

Ilex ambigua var. monticola, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
