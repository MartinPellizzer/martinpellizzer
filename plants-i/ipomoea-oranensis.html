Ipomoea oranensis

Ipomoea oranensis, commonly known as the Oran morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is typically found growing in moist, shaded environments. Its vines can climb or trail, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Ipomoea oranensis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
