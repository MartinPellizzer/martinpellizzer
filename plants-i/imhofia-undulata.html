Imhofia undulata

Imhofia undulata, commonly known as the zigzag ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features long, narrow, and undulating leaves that give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of yellow or orange.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
