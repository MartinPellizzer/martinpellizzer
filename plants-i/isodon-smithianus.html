Isodon smithianus

Isodon smithianus, commonly known as the Smith's goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Isodon smithianus is valued for its traditional use in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
