Indigofera moniliformis

Indigofera moniliformis, commonly known as the bead vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and small, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
