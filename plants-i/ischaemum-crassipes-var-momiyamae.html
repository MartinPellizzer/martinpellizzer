Ischaemum crassipes var. momiyamae

Ischaemum crassipes var. momiyamae, commonly known as the Japanese rough fescue, is a variant of the rough fescue species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and compact growth habit. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with leathery, awned glumes.

The variety momiyamae is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
