Indigofera bracteata var. bracteata

Indigofera bracteata var. bracteata, commonly known as the Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often in clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard, dark brown pod, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
