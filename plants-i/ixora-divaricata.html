Ixora divaricata

Ixora divaricata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Ixora divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
