Ipomoea breviscapa

Ipomoea breviscapa, commonly known as the short-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet blooms that typically open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and tend to twine around supporting structures.

Ipomoea breviscapa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
