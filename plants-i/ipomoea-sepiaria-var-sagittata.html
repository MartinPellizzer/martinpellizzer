Ipomoea sepiaria var. sagittata

Ipomoea sepiaria var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
