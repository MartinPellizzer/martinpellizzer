Inula germanica

Inula germanica, commonly known as German elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a tall, hairy stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, heart-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, forming a spherical cluster that attracts pollinators.

The roots are often harvested for their medicinal uses, including treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
