Iris caucasica subsp. turcica

Iris caucasica subsp.

turcica, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive central dark spot.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly smaller flowers and narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Iris caucasica.
