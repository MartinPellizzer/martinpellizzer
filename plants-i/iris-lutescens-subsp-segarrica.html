Iris lutescens subsp. segarrica

Iris lutescens subsp.

segarrica, commonly known as the Segarra iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and dark green, forming dense clumps.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
