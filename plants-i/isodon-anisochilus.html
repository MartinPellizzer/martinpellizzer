Isodon anisochilus

Isodon anisochilus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved isodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its opposite, linear leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses including the treatment of respiratory and digestive ailments.
