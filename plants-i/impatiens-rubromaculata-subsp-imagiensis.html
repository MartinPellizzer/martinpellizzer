Impatiens rubromaculata subsp. imagiensis

Impatiens rubromaculata subsp.

imagiensis, commonly known as the red-spotted impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant red petals with distinctive dark red maculations, which set it apart from other impatiens species.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a deep green color.

It produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
