Ixia marginifolia

Ixia marginifolia, commonly known as the orange lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. Ixia marginifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
