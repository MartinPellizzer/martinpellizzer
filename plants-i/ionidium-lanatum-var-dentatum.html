Ionidium lanatum var. dentatum

Ionidium lanatum var. dentatum, commonly known as the woolly leaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy, and often grayish-green leaves, which are typically lobed or toothed, giving it the specific epithet *dentatum*. The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or boggy areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
