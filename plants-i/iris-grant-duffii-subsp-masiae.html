Iris grant-duffii subsp. masiae

Iris grant-duffii subsp.

masiae, commonly known as the Masai iris, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern Africa. It features upright, grass-like foliage and produces tall, slender flowering stems with showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and elegant form.
