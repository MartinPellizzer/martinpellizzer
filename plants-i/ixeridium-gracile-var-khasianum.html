Ixeridium gracile var. khasianum

Ixeridium gracile var. khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its slender growth habit and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
