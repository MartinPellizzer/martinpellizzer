Iberis linearifolia

Iberis linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Iberis linearifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
