Inula mariae

Inula mariae, commonly known as the Marie's Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Inula. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing at high altitudes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its arid environment.
