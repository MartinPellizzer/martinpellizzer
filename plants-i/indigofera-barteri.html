Indigofera barteri

Indigofera barteri, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savannas and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
