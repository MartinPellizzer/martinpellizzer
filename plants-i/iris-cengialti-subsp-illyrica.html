Iris cengialti subsp. illyrica

Iris cengialti subsp.

illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.
