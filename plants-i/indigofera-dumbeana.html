Indigofera dumbeana

Indigofera dumbeana, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a shallow root system and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
