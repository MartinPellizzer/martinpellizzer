Ilex paraguariensis var. sincorensis

Ilex paraguariensis var. sincorensis, commonly known as the Sincorensis holly, is a variant of the yerba mate plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
