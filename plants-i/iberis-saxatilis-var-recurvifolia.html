Iberis saxatilis var. recurvifolia

Iberis saxatilis var. recurvifolia, commonly known as the rock candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and recurved, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

This hardy plant is well-suited for rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
