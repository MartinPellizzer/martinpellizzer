Inga rhabdotocalyx

Inga rhabdotocalyx, commonly known as the white-veined inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

Inga rhabdotocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
