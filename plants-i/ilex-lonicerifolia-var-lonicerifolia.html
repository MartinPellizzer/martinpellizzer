Ilex lonicerifolia var. lonicerifolia

Ilex lonicerifolia var. lonicerifolia, commonly known as the American holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, alternate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. Its botanical features include a woody stem with persistent leaves, and it is often found in moist, acidic soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
