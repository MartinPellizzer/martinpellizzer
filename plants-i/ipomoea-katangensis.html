Ipomoea katangensis

Ipomoea katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms during the day. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
