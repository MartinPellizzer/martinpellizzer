Ixia linearifolia

Ixia linearifolia, commonly known as the linearleaf ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Ixia linearifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

It is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
