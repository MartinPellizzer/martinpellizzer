Indigofera subsecunda

Indigofera subsecunda, commonly known as blue bean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
