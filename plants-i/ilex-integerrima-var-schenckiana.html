Ilex integerrima var. schenckiana

Ilex integerrima var. schenckiana, commonly known as the Schenck's holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 20 feet tall, with a dense, compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
