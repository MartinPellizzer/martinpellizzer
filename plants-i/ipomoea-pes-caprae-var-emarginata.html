Ipomoea pes-caprae var. emarginata

Ipomoea pes-caprae var. emarginata, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found along coastlines and in disturbed areas. It is a trailing or climbing vine with hairy stems and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its ability to tolerate salt spray and poor soil conditions makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
