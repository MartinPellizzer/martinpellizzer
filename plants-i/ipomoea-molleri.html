Ipomoea molleri

Ipomoea molleri, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea molleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
