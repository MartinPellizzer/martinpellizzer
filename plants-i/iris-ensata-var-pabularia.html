Iris ensata var. pabularia

Iris ensata var. pabularia, commonly known as the Japanese forest iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, often in shades of blue, violet, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
