Ipomoea demissa

Ipomoea demissa, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It blooms throughout the growing season and is known for its rapid growth habit.

Ipomoea demissa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
