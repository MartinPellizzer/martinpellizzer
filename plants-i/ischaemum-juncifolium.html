Ischaemum juncifolium

Ischaemum juncifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream sides. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib.

Ischaemum juncifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
