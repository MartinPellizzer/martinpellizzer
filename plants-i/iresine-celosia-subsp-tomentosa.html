Iresine celosia subsp. tomentosa

Iresine celosia subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf or purple velvet plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its velvety, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces striking, plume-like inflorescences in shades of red, purple, and pink, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for containers and garden borders.
