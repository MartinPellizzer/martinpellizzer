Iris alata var. magna

Iris alata var. magna, commonly known as the giant ribbon iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with wide, ribbon-like petals. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-blue sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique morphology and vibrant coloration make it a prized addition to botanical collections and gardens.
