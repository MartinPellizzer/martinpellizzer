Iris × parkor

Iris × parkor, commonly known as the Park's iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

The botanical characteristics of Iris × parkor make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and wetland restoration projects.
