Indigofera vicioides var. occidentalis

Indigofera vicioides var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western indigo bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds.

It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems, and its seeds are sometimes used as a food source or in traditional medicine.
