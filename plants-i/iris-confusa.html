Iris confusa

Iris confusa, commonly known as the Canadian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces a tall flowering stem with three petals and three sepals, which are often marked with purple or brown streaks.

Iris confusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
