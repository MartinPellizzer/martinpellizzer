Iris unguicularis subsp. syriaca

Iris unguicularis subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
