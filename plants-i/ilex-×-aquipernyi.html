Ilex × aquipernyi

Ilex × aquipernyi, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen holly, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ilex microphylla and Ilex morroensis.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and mild climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dense, evergreen foliage and winter interest.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological traits that vary depending on the parent species.
