Indigofera chalara

Indigofera chalara, commonly known as the white indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports its growth in various environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
