Isopogon buxifolius var. linearis

Isopogon buxifolius var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Its foliage and floral characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
