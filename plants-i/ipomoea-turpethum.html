Ipomoea turpethum

Ipomoea turpethum, commonly known as the turpethum morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea turpethum is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and climbers.
