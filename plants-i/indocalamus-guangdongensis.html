Indocalamus guangdongensis

Indocalamus guangdongensis, commonly known as the Guangdong bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and construction.
