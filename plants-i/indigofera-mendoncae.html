Indigofera mendoncae

Indigofera mendoncae, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a dye. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
