Impatiens linearis

Impatiens linearis, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
