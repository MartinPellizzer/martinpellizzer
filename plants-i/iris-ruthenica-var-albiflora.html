Iris ruthenica var. albiflora

Iris ruthenica var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered flag iris, is a variant of the slender iris native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.
