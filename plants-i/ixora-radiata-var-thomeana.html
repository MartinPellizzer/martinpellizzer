Ixora radiata var. thomeana

Ixora radiata var. thomeana, commonly known as the yellow flame bush, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
