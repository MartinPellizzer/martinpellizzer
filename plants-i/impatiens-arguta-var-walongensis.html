Impatiens arguta var. walongensis

Impatiens arguta var. walongensis, commonly known as the Walong impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to humid climates and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
