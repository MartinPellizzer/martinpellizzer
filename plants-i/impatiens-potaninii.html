Impatiens potaninii

Impatiens potaninii, commonly known as the Khabarovsk Impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Russian Far East.

It features showy, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellowish throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Impatiens potaninii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
