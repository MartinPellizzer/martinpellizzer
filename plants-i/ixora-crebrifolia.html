Ixora crebrifolia

Ixora crebrifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are typically borne on long, branching inflorescences, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

Ixora crebrifolia is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
