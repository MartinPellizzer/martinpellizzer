Inula salicina var. aspera

Inula salicina var. aspera, commonly known as the rough elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a tall, hollow stem covered in rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
