Ipomoea commatophylla var. angustifolia

Ipomoea commatophylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with vibrant blue or purple petals that open in the morning. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oblong seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
