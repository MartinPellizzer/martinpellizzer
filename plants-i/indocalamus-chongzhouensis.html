Indocalamus chongzhouensis

Indocalamus chongzhouensis, commonly known as the Chongzhou bamboo, is a species of grass native to southern China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and horticultural applications.
