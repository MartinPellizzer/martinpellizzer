Indocalamus cordatus

Indocalamus cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped bamboo, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base, giving it its common name.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
