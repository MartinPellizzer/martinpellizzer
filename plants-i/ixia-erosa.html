Ixia erosa

Ixia erosa, commonly known as the pink ox tongue, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a tongue. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a contrasting central stripe. Ixia erosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
