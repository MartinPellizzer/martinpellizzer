Ipomoea obscura var. abyssinica

Ipomoea obscura var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Ethiopia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
