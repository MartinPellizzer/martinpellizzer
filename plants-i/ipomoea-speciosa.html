Ipomoea speciosa

Ipomoea speciosa, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive crown-like appearance. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
