Isatis djurdjurae var. microcaropa

Isatis djurdjurae var. microcaropa, commonly known as the small-seeded woad, is a variant of the woad plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a biennial plant characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves and flowers contain compounds used in traditional dyeing processes, similar to its parent species.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
