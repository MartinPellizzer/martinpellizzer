Indigofera glauca

Indigofera glauca, commonly known as blue indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
