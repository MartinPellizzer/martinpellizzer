Iris nigricans

Iris nigricans, commonly known as the black root iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, characteristic of the iris family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The roots of this plant are fibrous and have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for various purposes.
