Iris lusitanica var. sordida

Iris lusitanica var. sordida, commonly known as the sordid Spanish iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in the Iberian Peninsula.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
