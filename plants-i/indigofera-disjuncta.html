Indigofera disjuncta

Indigofera disjuncta, commonly known as the Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India, and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Indigofera disjuncta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open woodlands or disturbed soils.
