Ipomoea campaniflora

Ipomoea campaniflora, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and dark green, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Ipomoea campaniflora is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
