Isonandra lamponga

Isonandra lamponga, commonly known as the red-flowered evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking clusters of bright red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are borne in terminal inflorescences.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
