Indigofera mansuensis

Indigofera mansuensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
