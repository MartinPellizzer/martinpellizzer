Iva acerosa

Iva acerosa, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are rough and serrated, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
