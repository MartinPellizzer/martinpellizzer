Isopogon pruinosus subsp. pruinosus

Isopogon pruinosus subsp.

pruinosus, commonly known as the silverleaf isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native gardens.
