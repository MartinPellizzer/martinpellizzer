Inga jiringa

Inga jiringa, commonly known as the "wild coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant can grow up to 5 meters in height.

Inga jiringa is often found in shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
