Inula repens

Inula repens, commonly known as the creeping inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, grassy areas and along stream banks. The plant features a creeping rhizome and erect, hairy stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

The flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
