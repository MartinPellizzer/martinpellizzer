Inga pedicellaris

Inga pedicellaris, commonly known as the pink-veined inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a dense root system that helps stabilize soil, making it useful in reforestation projects. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga pedicellaris is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
