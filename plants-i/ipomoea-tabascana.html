Ipomoea tabascana

Ipomoea tabascana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height, often climbing or trailing. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea tabascana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
