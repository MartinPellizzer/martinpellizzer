Impatiens disotis

Impatiens disotis, commonly known as the pink impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
