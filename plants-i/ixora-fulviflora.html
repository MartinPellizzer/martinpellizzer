Ixora fulviflora

Ixora fulviflora, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ixora fulviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
