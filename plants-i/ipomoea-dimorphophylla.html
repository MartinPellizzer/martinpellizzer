Ipomoea dimorphophylla

Ipomoea dimorphophylla, commonly known as the two-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bi-color flowers, which typically feature a white or pale pink interior and a deep purple or violet exterior. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often darker on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange capsule that splits open when mature.
