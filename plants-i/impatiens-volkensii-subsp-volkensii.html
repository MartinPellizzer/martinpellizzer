Impatiens volkensii subsp. volkensii

Impatiens volkensii subsp.

volkensii, commonly known as the Volckens' impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within its native ecosystems and its value in horticultural collections.
