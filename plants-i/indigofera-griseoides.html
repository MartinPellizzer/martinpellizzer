Indigofera griseoides

Indigofera griseoides, commonly known as the gray indigo or blue mist flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Indigofera griseoides is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
