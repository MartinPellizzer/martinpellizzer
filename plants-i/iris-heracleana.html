Iris heracleana

Iris heracleana, commonly known as the Greek iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Iris heracleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
