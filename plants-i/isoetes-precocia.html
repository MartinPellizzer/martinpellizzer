Isoetes precocia

Isoetes precocia, commonly known as the early watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its spiky, leaf-like structures called stigmaria, which are used for anchoring the plant in muddy or sandy substrates. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called sporangia, which are typically located at the base of the plant. Isoetes precocia is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
