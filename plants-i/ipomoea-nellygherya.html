Ipomoea nellygherya

Ipomoea nellygherya, commonly known as Nelly's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea nellygherya is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and greenhouses.
