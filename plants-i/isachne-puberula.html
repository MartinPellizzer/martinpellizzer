Isachne puberula

Isachne puberula, commonly known as the silky grass or silky isachne, is a small perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright culms with hairy leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Isachne puberula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small invertebrates.
