Iochroma tupayachianum

Iochroma tupayachianum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous areas. Its foliage is usually dense and evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that have a waxy texture.
