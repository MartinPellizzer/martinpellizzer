Isoetes occidentalis var. piperi

Isoetes occidentalis var. piperi, commonly known as the western water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, lacy foliage that resembles a network of leaves, which helps it thrive in shallow, freshwater environments. The plant belongs to the genus Isoetes, which is known for its unique spore-producing structures called sporangia. It typically grows in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for water quality and ecological health.
