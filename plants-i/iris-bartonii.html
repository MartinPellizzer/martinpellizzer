Iris bartonii

Iris bartonii, commonly known as the Barton's iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Iris bartonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in wetland gardens.
