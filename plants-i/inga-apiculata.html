Inga apiculata

Inga apiculata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its distinctive yellowish or reddish flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and ecological importance in soil enrichment.
