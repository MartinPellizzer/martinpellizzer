Impatiens percordata subsp. newbouldiana

Impatiens percordata subsp.

newbouldiana, commonly known as Newbould's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink.

The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
