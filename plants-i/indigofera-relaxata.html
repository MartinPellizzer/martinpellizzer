Indigofera relaxata

Indigofera relaxata, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Indigofera relaxata is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
