Ixia crispa

Ixia crispa, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting central stripe.

Ixia crispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to garden environments with well-drained soil.
