Inga ciliata var. parvifolia

Inga ciliata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for reforestation projects. Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by animals, contributing to its natural spread.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enhance soil fertility in its native habitats.
