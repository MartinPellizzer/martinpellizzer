Ixora stricta var. blumeana

Ixora stricta var. blumeana, commonly known as the Blume's flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is known for its clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.
