Indigofera marginata

Indigofera marginata, commonly known as the coastal indigo or sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that have a waxy coating. The plant produces striking purple or blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and it is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh conditions.
