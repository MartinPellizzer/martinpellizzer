Inga bijuga

Inga bijuga, commonly known as the "wild coffee" or "sugar tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in rainforests. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators.

The fruit, which contains several seeds, is an important food source for various animals in its native habitat.
