Impatiens violiflora

Impatiens violiflora, commonly known as violet-flowered impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers typically range in color from pale purple to deep violet, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
