Impatiens balsamina var. rosea

Impatiens balsamina var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in gardens and woodland areas. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals, which is a key botanical feature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
