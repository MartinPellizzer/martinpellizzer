Impatiens messumbaensis

Impatiens messumbaensis, commonly known as the Messumba balsam, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the genus Impatiens, which is part of the family Balsaminaceae. The plant is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its seeds are dispersed by the explosive force of its capsules, a common trait among species in the Impatiens genus.
