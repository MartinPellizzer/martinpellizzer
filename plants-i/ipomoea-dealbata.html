Ipomoea dealbata

Ipomoea dealbata, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically velvety in texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea dealbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb trellises or fences with its long, twisting stems.
