Iris × lancipetala

Iris × lancipetala, commonly known as the broadleaf iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris laevigata and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and Siberia, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in early spring.

The botanical features of Iris × lancipetala make it a popular choice for water gardens and shaded landscapes.
