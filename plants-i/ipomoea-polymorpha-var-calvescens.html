Ipomoea polymorpha var. calvescens

Ipomoea polymorpha var. calvescens, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit, with trailing stems that can spread over large areas. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.
