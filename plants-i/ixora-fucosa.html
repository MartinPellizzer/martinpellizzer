Ixora fucosa

Ixora fucosa, commonly known as the coastal ixora, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, orange-red flowers throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often covered with a fine layer of white wax.

Ixora fucosa is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate flooding, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
