Isodon scrophularioides

Isodon scrophularioides, commonly known as the purple foxglove, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Isodon scrophularioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
