Ichnanthus tipuaniensis

Ichnanthus tipuaniensis, commonly known as the Tulu Hill Orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the monsoon season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
