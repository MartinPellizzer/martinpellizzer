Ipomoea subrevoluta f. acutiloba

Ipomoea subrevoluta f. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed morning glory, is a cultivated form of the morning glory genus.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its rapid growth and showy display. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
