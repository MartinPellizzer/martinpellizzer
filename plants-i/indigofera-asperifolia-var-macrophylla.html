Indigofera asperifolia var. macrophylla

Indigofera asperifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats or along forest edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
