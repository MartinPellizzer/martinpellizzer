Ipomoea atrosanguinea

Ipomoea atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name.

Ipomoea atrosanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
