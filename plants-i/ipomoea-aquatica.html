Ipomoea aquatica

Ipomoea aquatica, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and long, slender stems that often grow in water or damp soil. The plant is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth rate, making it both a valuable vegetable and an invasive species in many regions. Its flowers are small and typically white or purple, and it produces edible seeds.

Ipomoea aquatica is widely cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
