Indigofera purpurascens

Indigofera purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers, which bloom in racemes along the stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, elongated legumes as fruits.

Indigofera purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
