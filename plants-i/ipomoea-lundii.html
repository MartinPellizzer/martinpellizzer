Ipomoea lundii

Ipomoea lundii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea lundii is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
