Indigofera bergii

Indigofera bergii, commonly known as African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for various ecological conditions.
