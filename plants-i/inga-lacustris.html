Inga lacustris

Inga lacustris, commonly known as the lake inga, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is often found in moist, tropical habitats, including wetlands and riverbanks. Its fruit is a flattened pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Inga lacustris plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.
