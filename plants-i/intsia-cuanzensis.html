Intsia cuanzensis

Intsia cuanzensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree typically grows in moist lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant also produces small, fragrant flowers that are predominantly white or pale yellow.
