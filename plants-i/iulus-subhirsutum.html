Iulus subhirsutum

Iulus subhirsutum, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a distinctive, aromatic odor.

Iulus subhirsutum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
