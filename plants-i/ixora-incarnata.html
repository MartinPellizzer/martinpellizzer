Ixora incarnata

Ixora incarnata, commonly known as the flame-of-the_forest, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora incarnata is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
