Inga pamplonae

Inga pamplonae, commonly known as the Pampas inga, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in humid lowland forests. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.

Inga pamplonae plays an important role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
