Ixora doreensis var. longifolia

Ixora doreensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the longifolia ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
