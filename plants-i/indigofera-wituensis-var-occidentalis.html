Indigofera wituensis var. occidentalis

Indigofera wituensis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the West African indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
