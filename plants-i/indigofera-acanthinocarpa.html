Indigofera acanthinocarpa

Indigofera acanthinocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savannas and woodland areas.

Botanically, it is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
