Inula sechmenii

Inula sechmenii, commonly known as the Armenian elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Inula sechmenii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
