Indigofera rutshuruensis

Indigofera rutshuruensis, commonly known as the Kivu indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various insect and bird species.
