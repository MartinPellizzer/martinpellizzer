Impatiens verecunda

Impatiens verecunda, commonly known as the ghost impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling ghostly apparitions against the foliage. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens verecunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
