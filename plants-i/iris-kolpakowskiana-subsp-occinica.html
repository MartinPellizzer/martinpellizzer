Iris kolpakowskiana subsp. occinica

Iris kolpakowskiana subsp.

occinica, commonly known as the purple iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale blue center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
