Inga crevauxii

Inga crevauxii, commonly known as the "crevaux's inga," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga crevauxii plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling through its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
