Iris angusta

Iris angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and narrow in appearance. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, characteristic of the iris family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
