Isolepis curvula

Isolepis curvula, commonly known as the bent reed, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, fens, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curved or twisted, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Isolepis curvula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
