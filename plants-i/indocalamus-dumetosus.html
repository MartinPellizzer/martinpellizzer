Indocalamus dumetosus

Indocalamus dumetosus, commonly known as the bamboo reed or dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Indocalamus dumetosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
