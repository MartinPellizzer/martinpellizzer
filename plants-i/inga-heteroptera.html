Inga heteroptera

Inga heteroptera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines.

Additionally, Inga heteroptera plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
