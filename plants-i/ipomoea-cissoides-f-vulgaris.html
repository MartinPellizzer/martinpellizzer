Ipomoea cissoides f. vulgaris

Ipomoea cissoides f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn.

The flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, giving it its common name.
