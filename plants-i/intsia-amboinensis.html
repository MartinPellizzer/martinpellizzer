Intsia amboinensis

Intsia amboinensis, commonly known as the Bintangor or white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy, and highly valued hardwood, which is used in furniture and construction. The tree has a straight trunk and can grow up to 40 meters tall, with glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

The plant is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant species in its native ecosystems.
