Isodon inflexus f. canescens

Isodon inflexus f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy isodon, is a variant of the Isodon inflexus species.

It is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

The hairy appearance of its leaves is a distinguishing feature of this particular form.
