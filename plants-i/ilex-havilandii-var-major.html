Ilex havilandii var. major

Ilex havilandii var. major, commonly known as the Texas holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable plant for landscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
