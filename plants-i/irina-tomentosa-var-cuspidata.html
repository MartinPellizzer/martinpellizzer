Irina tomentosa var. cuspidata

Irina tomentosa var. cuspidata, commonly known as the woolly irina, is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage.

It features broad, ovate leaves covered in a soft, white tomentum that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Irina tomentosa var. cuspidata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.
