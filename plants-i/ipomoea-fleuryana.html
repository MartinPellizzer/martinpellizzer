Ipomoea fleuryana

Ipomoea fleuryana, commonly known as the Fleury's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant, often purple or blue flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

Ipomoea fleuryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
