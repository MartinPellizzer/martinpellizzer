Icica aracouchini

Icica aracouchini, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Icica aracouchini is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
