Ischaemum papposum

Ischaemum papposum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, aided by the feathery pappus that attaches to each seed, enhancing its ability to travel.
