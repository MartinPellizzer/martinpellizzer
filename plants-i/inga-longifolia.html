Inga longifolia

Inga longifolia, commonly known as the long pod sylph, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important species in agroforestry systems.

Inga longifolia also serves as a valuable source of fodder for livestock and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
