Ipomoea hallieriana

Ipomoea hallieriana, commonly known as the Hallier's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Ipomoea hallieriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
