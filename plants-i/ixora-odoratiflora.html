Ixora odoratiflora

Ixora odoratiflora, commonly known as the sweet-scented ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that emit a fragrant aroma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

Ixora odoratiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
