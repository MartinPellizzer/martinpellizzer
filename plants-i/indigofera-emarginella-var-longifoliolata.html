Indigofera emarginella var. longifoliolata

Indigofera emarginella var. longifoliolata, commonly known as the long-leaved indigofera, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly India and Nepal, where it thrives in moist, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks, contributing to soil stabilization through its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
