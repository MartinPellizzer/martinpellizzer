Ilex dimorphophylla

Ilex dimorphophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its opposite, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex dimorphophylla is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
