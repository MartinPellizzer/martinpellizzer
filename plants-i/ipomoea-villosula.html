Ipomoea villosula

Ipomoea villosula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along roadsides or in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea villosula is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
