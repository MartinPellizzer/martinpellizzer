Ifloga spicata subsp. elbaensis

Ifloga spicata subsp.

elbaensis, commonly known as the Elba spike heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
