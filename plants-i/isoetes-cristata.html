Isoetes cristata

Isoetes cristata, commonly known as the cristate water clover, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horizontally arranged, spore-bearing leaves that form a rosette-like structure. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and shallow lakes. Its spores are released into the water and dispersed by wind or water currents.

Isoetes cristata plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
