Isolepis obtusifolia

Isolepis obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spike-like inflorescences, typical of the sedge family. Isolepis obtusifolia thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond margins, and other waterlogged habitats.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
