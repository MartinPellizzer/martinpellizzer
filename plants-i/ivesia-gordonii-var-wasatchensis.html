Ivesia gordonii var. wasatchensis

Ivesia gordonii var. wasatchensis, commonly known as the Wasatch ivy-mallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems in Utah and surrounding regions.
