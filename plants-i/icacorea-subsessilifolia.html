Icacorea subsessilifolia

Icacorea subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or disturbed areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
