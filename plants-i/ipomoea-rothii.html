Ipomoea rothii

Ipomoea rothii, commonly known as the crimson woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are hairy and can climb or trail, making it suitable for gardens or as a landscape plant.

Ipomoea rothii thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.
