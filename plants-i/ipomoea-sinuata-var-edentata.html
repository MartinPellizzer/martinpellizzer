Ipomoea sinuata var. edentata

Ipomoea sinuata var. edentata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves with wavy margins and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It blooms profusely from late spring to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological value.
