Iris sibirica var. haematophylla

Iris sibirica var. haematophylla, commonly known as the blood-red iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces striking violet-blue to deep red flowers with three upright petals and three downward-curled sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
