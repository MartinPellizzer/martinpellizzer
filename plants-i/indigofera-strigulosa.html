Indigofera strigulosa

Indigofera strigulosa, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
