Ixora leucantha

Ixora leucantha, commonly known as white ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ixora leucantha is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and poor soil conditions.
