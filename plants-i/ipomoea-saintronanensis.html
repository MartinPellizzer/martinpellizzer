Ipomoea saintronanensis

Ipomoea saintronanensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, rounded seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea saintronanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
