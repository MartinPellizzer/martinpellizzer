Impatiens sidiformis

Impatiens sidiformis, commonly known as the sidiform sidewalk impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

Impatiens sidiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.
