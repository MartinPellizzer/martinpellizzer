Inga jinicuil

Inga jinicuil, commonly known as the "black inga," is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
