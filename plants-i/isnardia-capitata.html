Isnardia capitata

Isnardia capitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
