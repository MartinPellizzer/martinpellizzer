Ipomoea muricata f. alba

Ipomoea muricata f. alba, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a striking variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its large, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
