Isoloba lutea

Isoloba lutea, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Isoloba lutea is characterized by its globular seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
