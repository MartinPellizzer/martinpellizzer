Inula glanduligera

Inula glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular inula, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by a dense layer of bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with glandular dots, giving it a slightly sticky texture.

Inula glanduligera is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
