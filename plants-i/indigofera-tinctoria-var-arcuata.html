Indigofera tinctoria var. arcuata

Indigofera tinctoria var. arcuata, commonly known as the curlleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces indigo dye from its seeds, which has historically been used in textile industries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its growth habit includes a climbing or shrub-like form, depending on environmental conditions.
