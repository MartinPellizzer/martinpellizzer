Iresine pilgeri

Iresine pilgeri, commonly known as the red-leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Iresine pilgeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns and compact growth habit.
