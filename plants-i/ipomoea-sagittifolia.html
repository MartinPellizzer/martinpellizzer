Ipomoea sagittifolia

Ipomoea sagittifolia, commonly known as arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.
