Ilex eugeniifolia

Ilex eugeniifolia, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex eugeniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its hardiness and resistance to pests and diseases.
