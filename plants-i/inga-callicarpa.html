Inga callicarpa

Inga callicarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly pubescent underside. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are either white or pale pink in color.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release bright red, edible seeds, which are often consumed by various wildlife species.
