Iresine silvatica

Iresine silvatica, commonly known as the redleaf plant or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Iresine silvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking leaf coloration.
