Ipomoea philomega

Ipomoea philomega, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a striking vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of purple, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are broad and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking dramatic floral displays.
