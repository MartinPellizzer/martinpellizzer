Ipomoea martinicensis

Ipomoea martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
