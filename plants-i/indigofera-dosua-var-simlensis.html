Indigofera dosua var. simlensis

Indigofera dosua var. simlensis, commonly known as the Simlens mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
