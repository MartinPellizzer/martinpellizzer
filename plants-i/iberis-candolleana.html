Iberis candolleana

Iberis candolleana, commonly known as the Canary Islands candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Iberis candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
