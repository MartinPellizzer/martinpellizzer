Iresine hassleriana

Iresine hassleriana, commonly known as the bloodleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from deep red to purple and bright orange, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Iresine hassleriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves and easy care requirements.
