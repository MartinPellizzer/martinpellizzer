Isolepis circinata

Isolepis circinata, commonly known as the curled spike-rush, is a perennial wetland plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescence that curves inward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in spikes.

Isolepis circinata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
