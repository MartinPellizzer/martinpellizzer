Irlbachia pendula

Irlbachia pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Irlbachia pendula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique growth habit.
