Ipomoea auriculata

Ipomoea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
