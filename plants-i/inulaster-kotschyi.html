Inulaster kotschyi

Inulaster kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian inula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with ray florets surrounding a central disc.

Inulaster kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
