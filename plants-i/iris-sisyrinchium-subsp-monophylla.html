Iris sisyrinchium subsp. monophylla

Iris sisyrinchium subsp.

monophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and reduced floral structure compared to other iris species.
