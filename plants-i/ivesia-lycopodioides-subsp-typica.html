Ivesia lycopodioides subsp. typica

Ivesia lycopodioides subsp.

typica, commonly known as the typical ivy-leafed plant, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the western United States. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to rocky or arid environments.

It is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
