Isatis demiriziana

Isatis demiriziana, commonly known as Turkish woad, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste.

Historically, it was used for producing indigo dye, similar to its more famous relative, Isatis tinctoria.
