Ipomoea cernua f. palmirense

Ipomoea cernua f. palmirense, commonly known as the hanging morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces pale blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This ornamental species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
