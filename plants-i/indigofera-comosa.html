Indigofera comosa

Indigofera comosa, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, silken leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Indigofera comosa is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye in some cultures.
