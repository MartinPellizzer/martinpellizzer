Ipomoea pannosa

Ipomoea pannosa, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with a fibrous, woody stem that can reach lengths of several meters. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Ipomoea pannosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
