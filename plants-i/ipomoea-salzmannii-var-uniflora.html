Ipomoea salzmannii var. uniflora

Ipomoea salzmannii var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tuberous roots, though it is more valued for its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The species is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice in gardens and as a climbing plant.
