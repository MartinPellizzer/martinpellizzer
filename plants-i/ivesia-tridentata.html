Ivesia tridentata

Ivesia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed ivesia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pink flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ivesia tridentata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert and shrubland ecosystems.
