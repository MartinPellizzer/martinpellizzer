Isachne swaminathanii

Isachne swaminathanii, commonly known as the Swaminathan's isachne, is a species of grass native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetland environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, while the inflorescence consists of a single spikelet.

Isachne swaminathanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
