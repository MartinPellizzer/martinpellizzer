Iridodictyum hyrcanum

Iridodictyum hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the iris family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverbanks and wet meadows.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some areas, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
