Isolepis abyssinica

Isolepis abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Isolepis abyssinica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
