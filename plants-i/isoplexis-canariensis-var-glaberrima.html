Isoplexis canariensis var. glaberrima

Isoplexis canariensis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the Canary Island flowering plant, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers bloom in clusters.

This variety is native to the Canary Islands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
