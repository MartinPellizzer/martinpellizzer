Indigofera venulosa

Indigofera venulosa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera venulosa is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
