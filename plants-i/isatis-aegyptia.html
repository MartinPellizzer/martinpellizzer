Isatis aegyptia

Isatis aegyptia, commonly known as Egyptian woad, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa, and it is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often woody at the base. Egyptian woad is known for its historical use in producing indigo dye, which was highly valued in ancient civilizations.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
