Indigofera bemarahaensis

Indigofera bemarahaensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance.
