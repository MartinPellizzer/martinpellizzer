Ipomoea dichotoma var. integrifolia

Ipomoea dichotoma var. integrifolia, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with smooth edges, and the plant has a fibrous root system adapted to sandy soils. It thrives in coastal environments where it is exposed to salt spray and strong winds.

This species is often used in coastal landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
