Isodon umbrosus var. leucanthus

Isodon umbrosus var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
