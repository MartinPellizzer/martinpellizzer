Ixia capillaris var. lancea

Ixia capillaris var. lancea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved torch lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves and its showy, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces umbels of tubular flowers that are pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and unique floral structure, adding a dramatic touch to any garden setting.
