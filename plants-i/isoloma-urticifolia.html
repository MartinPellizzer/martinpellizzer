Isoloma urticifolia

Isoloma urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes many species with stinging hairs. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems and leaves contain irritant chemicals that can cause skin irritation upon contact.

Isoloma urticifolia is often found in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
