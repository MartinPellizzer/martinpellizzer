Iphigenia socotrana

Iphigenia socotrana, commonly known as Socotra caper, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula, particularly the island of Socotra.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh climate. The plant features distinctive, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
