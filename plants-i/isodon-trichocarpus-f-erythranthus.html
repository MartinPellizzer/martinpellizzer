Isodon trichocarpus f. erythranthus

Isodon trichocarpus f. erythranthus, commonly known as the red-flowered isodon, is a variant of the isodon trichocarpus species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the bright red or pink flowers that bloom in terminal clusters during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
