Iresine portulacoides var. commersonii

Iresine portulacoides var. commersonii, commonly known as Crimson Rambutan, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that are often adorned with purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
