Indigastrum

Indigastrum, commonly known as the bluebell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple flowers that bloom in clusters along its trailing stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blossoms. Indigastrum thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
