Iris xiphioides f. cantabrica

Iris xiphioides f. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian iris, is a hybrid iris species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a yellow throat, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
