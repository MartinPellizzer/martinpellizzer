Isoetes colombiana

Isoetes colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on the lower part of the plant. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate. Its morphology includes a rosette-like arrangement of leaves that help in gas exchange and light absorption.

Isoetes colombiana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
