Iris subdecolorata

Iris subdecolorata, commonly known as the pale iris, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
