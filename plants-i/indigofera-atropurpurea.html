Indigofera atropurpurea

Indigofera atropurpurea, commonly known as purple indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types. It also has historical significance in traditional medicine and dye production.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate and care provided.
