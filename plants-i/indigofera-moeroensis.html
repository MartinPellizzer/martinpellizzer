Indigofera moeroensis

Indigofera moeroensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility. It produces seed pods that contain small, round seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
