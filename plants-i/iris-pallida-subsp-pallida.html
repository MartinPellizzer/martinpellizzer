Iris pallida subsp. pallida

Iris pallida subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale iris, is a species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
