Impatiens chevalieri

Impatiens chevalieri, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact size and ability to bloom throughout the year.

Impatiens chevalieri is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
