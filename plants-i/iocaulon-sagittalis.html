Iocaulon sagittalis

Iocaulon sagittalis, commonly known as the arrowleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
