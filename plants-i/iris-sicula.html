Iris sicula

Iris sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces distinctive three-sided seed pods.

Iris sicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
