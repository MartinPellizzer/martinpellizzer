Indigofera secunda

Indigofera secunda, commonly known as the lesser indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
