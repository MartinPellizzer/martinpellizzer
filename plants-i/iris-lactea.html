Iris lactea

Iris lactea, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant, tall flower stalks that rise above the foliage.
