Ilex rotunda f. xanthocarpa

Ilex rotunda f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited holly, is a unique holly species characterized by its striking yellow berries.

It is a shrub or small tree native to China, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. The plant features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that remain throughout the year, providing evergreen structure. Its flowers are small, white, and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a bright yellow drupe that attracts birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
