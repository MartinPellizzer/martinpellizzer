Iris sanguinea var. typica

Iris sanguinea var. typica, commonly known as the Blood Iris, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its colorful flowers.

Iris sanguinea var. typica is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for water gardens and borders.
