Iris chamaeiris subvar. luteola

Iris chamaeiris subvar. luteola, commonly known as the yellow marsh iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear showy, three-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist soils and shallow water environments.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
