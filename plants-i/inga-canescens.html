Inga canescens

Inga canescens, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive yellow or white coloration.

Inga canescens is valued for its fast growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in reforestation efforts.
