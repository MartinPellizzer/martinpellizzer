Iris lutescens subvar. luteola

Iris lutescens subvar. luteola, commonly known as the yellow iris, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, yellow flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The leaves are sword-shaped, smooth, and dark green, often forming a dense basal rosette. This subspecies is characterized by its vibrant yellow coloration, which is particularly prominent in the flower's central standards.

It thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in areas with moist, well-drained soils.
