Isodon hians

Isodon hians, commonly known as the golden root or Chinese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its rhizomes are valued in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Isodon hians thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
