Iris × alticristata

Iris × alticristata, commonly known as the Alpine Iris, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, lavender-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
