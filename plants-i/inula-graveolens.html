Inula graveolens

Inula graveolens, commonly known as yellow cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Inula graveolens has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
