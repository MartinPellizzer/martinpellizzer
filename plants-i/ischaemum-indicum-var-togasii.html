Ischaemum indicum var. togasii

Ischaemum indicum var. togasii, commonly known as the tropical barnyard grass, is a variant of the grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various ecosystems across its native range.
