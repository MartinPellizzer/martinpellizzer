Indigofera insularis

Indigofera insularis, commonly known as the island indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional landscaping in its native habitat.
