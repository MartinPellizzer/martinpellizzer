Indigofera bungeana var. nana

Indigofera bungeana var. nana, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
