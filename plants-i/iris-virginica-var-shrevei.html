Iris virginica var. shrevei

Iris virginica var. shrevei, commonly known as the Shreve's Blue Flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers with a distinctive dark purple or black basal stripe. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
