Inga thibaudiana subsp. peltadenia

Inga thibaudiana subsp.

peltadenia, commonly known as the peltadenia inga, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are often trifoliate or trifoliolate, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
