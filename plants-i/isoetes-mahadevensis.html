Isoetes mahadevensis

Isoetes mahadevensis, commonly known as the Mahadevan watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a spore-bearing spike that is often submerged in water. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its spores are dispersed by water currents, facilitating its propagation.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
