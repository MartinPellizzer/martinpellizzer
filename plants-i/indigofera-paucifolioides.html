Indigofera paucifolioides

Indigofera paucifolioides, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and erect, branched stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Indigofera paucifolioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
