Ipomoea ugandensis

Ipomoea ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

Ipomoea ugandensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
