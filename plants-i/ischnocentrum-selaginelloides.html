Ischnocentrum selaginelloides

Ischnocentrum selaginelloides, commonly known as the golden star moss, is a unique member of the family Selaginellaceae.

It is a small, creeping plant that forms dense, cushion-like mats, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue, especially under certain light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the base of the plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
