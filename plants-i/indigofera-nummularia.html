Indigofera nummularia

Indigofera nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf indigo, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
