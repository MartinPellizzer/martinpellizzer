Iris zagrica subsp. hakkariensis

Iris zagrica subsp.

hakkariensis, commonly known as the Hakkari iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
