Iberis brachystyla

Iberis brachystyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Iberis brachystyla is well-suited to rock gardens and dry conditions, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.
