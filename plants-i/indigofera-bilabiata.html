Indigofera bilabiata

Indigofera bilabiata, commonly known as the pink indigo or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. It produces elongated pods containing several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value and traditional uses.

The plant has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
