Ischurochloa floribunda

Ischurochloa floribunda, commonly known as the flowering grass, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive flowering structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
