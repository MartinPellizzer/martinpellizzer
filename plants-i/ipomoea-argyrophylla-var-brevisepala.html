Ipomoea argyrophylla var. brevisepala

Ipomoea argyrophylla var. brevisepala, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a striking annual vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a silvery or metallic sheen. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
