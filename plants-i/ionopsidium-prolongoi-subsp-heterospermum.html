Ionopsidium prolongoi subsp. heterospermum

Ionopsidium prolongoi subsp.

heterospermum, commonly known as the slender sandflat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and small, clustered flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and the presence of specialized structures called pseudostems. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
