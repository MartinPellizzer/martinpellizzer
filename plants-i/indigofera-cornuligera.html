Indigofera cornuligera

Indigofera cornuligera, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Indigofera cornuligera is often found in forested areas and is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
