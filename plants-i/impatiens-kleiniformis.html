Impatiens kleiniformis

Impatiens kleiniformis, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled, often red or pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely used in horticulture.
