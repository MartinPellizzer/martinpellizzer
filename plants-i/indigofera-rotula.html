Indigofera rotula

Indigofera rotula, commonly known as the purple leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It has a climbing habit and produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera rotula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
