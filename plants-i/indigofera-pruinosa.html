Indigofera pruinosa

Indigofera pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and a dense, velvety coating on its leaves and stems. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Indigofera pruinosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
