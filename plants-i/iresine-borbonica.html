Iresine borbonica

Iresine borbonica, commonly known as Crimson Flame, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red foliage that resembles flames, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, typically in the spring.

This plant thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
