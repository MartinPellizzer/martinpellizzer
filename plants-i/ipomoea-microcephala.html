Ipomoea microcephala

Ipomoea microcephala, commonly known as the miniature morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its delicate blooms.
