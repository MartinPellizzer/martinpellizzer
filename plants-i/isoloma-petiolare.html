Isoloma petiolare

Isoloma petiolare, commonly known as the Hawaiian sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found growing near coastal areas. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to salt spray, making it a hardy species in maritime environments.

Isoloma petiolare plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and coastal ecosystems.
