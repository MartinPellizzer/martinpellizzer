Ilex aquifolium var. scotica

Ilex aquifolium var. scotica, commonly known as Scottish holly, is a variant of the common holly native to the Scottish Highlands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils. The leaves are dark green, glossy on top, and have serrated edges with spiny teeth. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
