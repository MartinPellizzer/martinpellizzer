Ixora grandifolia var. grandifolia

Ixora grandifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the flame bush or Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

The bark is smooth and can be reddish or brownish, with a fragrant scent when crushed.
