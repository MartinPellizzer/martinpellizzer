Isodon drogotschiensis

Isodon drogotschiensis, commonly known as the Drogotschi lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a distinct lemony fragrance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique aroma and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
