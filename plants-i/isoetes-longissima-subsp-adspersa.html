Isoetes longissima subsp. adspersa

Isoetes longissima subsp.

adspersa, commonly known as the scattered water parsley, is a rare aquatic fern-like plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that form dense underwater mats.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing sporangia on specialized structures called sporangium-bearing branches. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often in marshes or along the edges of lakes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
