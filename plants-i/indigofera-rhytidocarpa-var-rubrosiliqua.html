Indigofera rhytidocarpa var. rubrosiliqua

Indigofera rhytidocarpa var. rubrosiliqua, commonly known as the red-silvery indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, often red or purple flowers. The plant is notable for its unique silvery pubescence on the stems and leaves, which gives it its specific epithet. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it contributes to soil nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
