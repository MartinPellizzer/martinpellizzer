Inga eriocarpa

Inga eriocarpa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in panicles. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing properties, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
