Ichnanthus tectus

Ichnanthus tectus, commonly known as the broad-leaved lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a small, herbaceous species that typically grows in open, rocky habitats. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense racemes, with petals that are usually pink or purple in color.

Ichnanthus tectus is adapted to cold climates and has a circumpolar distribution across the northern hemisphere.
