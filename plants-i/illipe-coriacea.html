Illipe coriacea

Illipe coriacea, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and smooth, grey bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a rich, creamy nutmeat. Its seeds are widely used in the production of shea butter, a valuable cosmetic and culinary ingredient.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
