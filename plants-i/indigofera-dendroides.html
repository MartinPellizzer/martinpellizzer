Indigofera dendroides

Indigofera dendroides, commonly known as the bush indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard, pod-like structure that splits open when mature.

Indigofera dendroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
