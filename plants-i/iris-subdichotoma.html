Iris subdichotoma

Iris subdichotoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with three upright petals and three spreading sepals.

Iris subdichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
