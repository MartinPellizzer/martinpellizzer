Impatiens purpureoviolacea

Impatiens purpureoviolacea, commonly known as the purple viola impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in forest understories.

Impatiens purpureoviolacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
