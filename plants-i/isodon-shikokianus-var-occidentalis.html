Isodon shikokianus var. occidentalis

Isodon shikokianus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western Japanese verbena, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
