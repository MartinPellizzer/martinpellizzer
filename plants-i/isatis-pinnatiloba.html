Isatis pinnatiloba

Isatis pinnatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved woad, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its historical use in dye production and medicinal applications highlights its botanical significance.
