Ilex fragilis f. kingii

Ilex fragilis f. kingii, commonly known as the King's holly, is a variant of the fragile holly native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding seasonal color to the landscape. Its small, red berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
