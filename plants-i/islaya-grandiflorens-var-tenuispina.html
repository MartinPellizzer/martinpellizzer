Islaya grandiflorens var. tenuispina

Islaya grandiflorens var. tenuispina, commonly known as the slender-spined islaya, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated spines and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent leaves. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
