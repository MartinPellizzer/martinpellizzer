Ischaemum brachyatherum

Ischaemum brachyatherum, commonly known as the short-haired foxtail, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Ischaemum brachyatherum is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
