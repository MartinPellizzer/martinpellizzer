Impatiens alba

Impatiens alba, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Impatiens alba thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
