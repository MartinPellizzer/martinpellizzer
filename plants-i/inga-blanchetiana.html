Inga blanchetiana

Inga blanchetiana, commonly known as the white inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Inga blanchetiana is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of food for local wildlife.
