Indigofera laxeracemosa

Indigofera laxeracemosa, commonly known as the slender raceme indigo.

It is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae, characterized by its slender, drooping racemes of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
