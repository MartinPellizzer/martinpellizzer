Iris wilsonii

Iris wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features sword-shaped leaves. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
