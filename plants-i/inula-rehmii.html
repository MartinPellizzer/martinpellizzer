Inula rehmii

Inula rehmii, commonly known as the Chinese eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Inula rehmii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
