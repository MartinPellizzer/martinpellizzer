Ilex verticillata f. aurantiaca

Ilex verticillata f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-hairy winterberry, is a cultivar of the common winterberry holly.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange berries that persist through the winter, providing visual interest in the landscape. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is usually dark green, with a distinctive hairy texture on the undersides of the leaves.

This cultivar is popular in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
