Ipomoea hirsutula

Ipomoea hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant produces heart-shaped, hairy leaves and has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to climb or trail along structures.

Ipomoea hirsutula is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
