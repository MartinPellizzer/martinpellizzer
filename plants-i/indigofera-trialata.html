Indigofera trialata

Indigofera trialata, commonly known as the golden wistaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique color and vigorous growth make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
