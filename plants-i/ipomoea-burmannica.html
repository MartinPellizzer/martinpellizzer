Ipomoea burmannica

Ipomoea burmannica, commonly known as the Burmann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, blue, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers, though they are not commonly cultivated for food. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks or in moist, shaded areas.

Ipomoea burmannica is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.
