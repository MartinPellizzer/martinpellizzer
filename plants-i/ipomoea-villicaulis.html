Ipomoea villicaulis

Ipomoea villicaulis, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
