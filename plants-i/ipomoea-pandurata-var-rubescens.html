Ipomoea pandurata var. rubescens

Ipomoea pandurata var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-veined morning glory, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a red or purple veining. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
