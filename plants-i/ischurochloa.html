Ischurochloa

Ischurochloa, commonly known as the desert grass or sand grass, is a genus of grasses native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their low-growing, tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves adapted to conserve water. Ischurochloa species typically have fibrous root systems that help them survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperatures. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to their drought tolerance, they play an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in their native habitats.
