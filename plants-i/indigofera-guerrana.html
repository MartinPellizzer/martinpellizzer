Indigofera guerrana

Indigofera guerrana, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
