Ipomoea teretistigma var. setifera

Ipomoea teretistigma var. setifera, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, opening in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and showy blooms.
