Ixora radiata

Ixora radiata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Ixora radiata is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
