Ipomoea parvibracteolata

Ipomoea parvibracteolata, commonly known as the small-bract morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny, black seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
