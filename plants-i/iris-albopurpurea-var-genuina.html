Iris albopurpurea var. genuina

Iris albopurpurea var. genuina, commonly known as the white-purple iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a white to pale purple coloration. The flowers typically bloom in early to mid-summer, with three upright petals and three downward-curving sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
