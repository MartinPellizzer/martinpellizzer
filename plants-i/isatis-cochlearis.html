Isatis cochlearis

Isatis cochlearis, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, while its stem is erect and branched. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

Isatis cochlearis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
