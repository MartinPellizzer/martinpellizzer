Ipomoea bonariensis f. subintegra

Ipomoea bonariensis f. subintegra, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae) characterized by its large, white, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms typically opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is native to South America. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is popular in gardens for its rapid growth and vibrant floral display.
