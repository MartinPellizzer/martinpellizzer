Indigofera karuppiana

Indigofera karuppiana, commonly known as Karuppian indigo, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
