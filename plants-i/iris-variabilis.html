Iris variabilis

Iris variabilis, commonly known as the variable iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Iris variabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
