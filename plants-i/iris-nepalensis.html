Iris nepalensis

Iris nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It belongs to the Iridaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal value.

Iris nepalensis is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
