Impatiens longe-petiolata

Impatiens longe-petiolata, commonly known as the long petioled impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the leaf stalks that connect the leaves to the stem. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its foliage is usually heart-shaped with a smooth margin.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
