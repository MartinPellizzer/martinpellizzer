Impatiens parviflora f. albiflora

Impatiens parviflora f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered impatiens, is a variant of the common impatiens species.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. This cultivar is native to tropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
