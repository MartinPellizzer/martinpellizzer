Inga dariensis

Inga dariensis, commonly known as the Amazonian velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in tropical ecosystems.

The tree is also valued for its wood and has cultural importance among indigenous communities.
