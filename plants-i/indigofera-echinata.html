Indigofera echinata

Indigofera echinata, commonly known as the spurred indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or open woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
