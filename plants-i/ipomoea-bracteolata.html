Ipomoea bracteolata

Ipomoea bracteolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, giving it its common name.
