Icacorea dentata

Icacorea dentata, commonly known as the serrated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
