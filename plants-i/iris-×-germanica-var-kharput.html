Iris × germanica var. kharput

Iris × germanica var. kharput, commonly known as the Kharput iris, is a hybrid iris species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, lavender-blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with sword-shaped, dark green leaves that are narrow and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
