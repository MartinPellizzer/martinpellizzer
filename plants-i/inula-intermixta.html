Inula intermixta

Inula intermixta, commonly known as the mixed elecampane, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Inula. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Inula intermixta is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
