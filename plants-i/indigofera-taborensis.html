Indigofera taborensis

Indigofera taborensis, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in dyeing and medicinal practices.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
