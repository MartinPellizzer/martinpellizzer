Inga nobilis var. schultesii

Inga nobilis var. schultesii, commonly known as the Schultesi inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
