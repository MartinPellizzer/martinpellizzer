Indigofera patens

Indigofera patens, commonly known as the prostrate indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye in some cultures.
