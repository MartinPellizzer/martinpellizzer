Indigofera finlaysoniana

Indigofera finlaysoniana, commonly known as the Mexican indigo, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain dormant in the soil for extended periods.
