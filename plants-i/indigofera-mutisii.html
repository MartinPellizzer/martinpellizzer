Indigofera mutisii

Indigofera mutisii, commonly known as the Andean indigo, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
