Ipomoea nephrosepala

Ipomoea nephrosepala, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped pink or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its sepals are distinctive, being broadly ovate and often tinged with white or green.
