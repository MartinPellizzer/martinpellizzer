Indigofera gerardiana var. heterantha

Indigofera gerardiana var. heterantha, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
