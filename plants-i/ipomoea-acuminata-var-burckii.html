Ipomoea acuminata var. burckii

Ipomoea acuminata var. burckii, commonly known as the Burck's morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces elongated, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a preference for well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
