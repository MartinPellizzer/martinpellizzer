Iris brevicuspis

Iris brevicuspis, commonly known as the short-spurred iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking blue to purple flowers with short, narrow sepals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Iris brevicuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
