Indigofera ovata

Indigofera ovata, commonly known as the oval indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
