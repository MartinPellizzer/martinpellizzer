Ipomoea × hardingii

Ipomoea × hardingii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a hybrid species within the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant is native to the southern United States and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands. Its vines can grow up to 10 meters long, displaying rapid growth and a climbing habit.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, adding to its ornamental appeal.
