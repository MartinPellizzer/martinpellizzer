Iris giganticaerulea f. miraculosa

Iris giganticaerulea f. miraculosa, commonly known as the Blue Flag Iris, is a striking hybrid iris native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, sky-blue to violet-blue flowers with a contrasting white or pale blue center. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, supporting its showy blooms. Its leaves are sword-shaped, broad, and often have a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and wetland areas for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
