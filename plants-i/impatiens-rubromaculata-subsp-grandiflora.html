Impatiens rubromaculata subsp. grandiflora

Impatiens rubromaculata subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered impatiens, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers with distinctive red markings.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
