Itasina filifolia

Itasina filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months.

Itasina filifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
