Ipomoea umbellifera

Ipomoea umbellifera, commonly known as the umbrellaleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Ipomoea umbellifera is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and herbs.
