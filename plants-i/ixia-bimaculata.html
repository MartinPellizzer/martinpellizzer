Ixia bimaculata

Ixia bimaculata, commonly known as the two-spotted ixia, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with two dark spots on the petals. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ixia bimaculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is a popular choice for cut flowers.
