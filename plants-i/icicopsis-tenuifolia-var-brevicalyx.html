Icicopsis tenuifolia var. brevicalyx

Icicopsis tenuifolia var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved icicopsis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
