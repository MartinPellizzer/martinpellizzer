Iberis carica

Iberis carica, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or entire, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
