Inga laurina

Inga laurina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pink. The tree produces edible pods containing seeds, and its bark has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

Inga laurina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
