Imhofia

Imhofia, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant produces a starchy, edible root that is often used as a thickening agent in cooking. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and can grow up to several feet in length.

Imhofia is also valued for its ornamental foliage, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
