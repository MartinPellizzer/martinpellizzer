Isoglossa floribunda

Isoglossa floribunda, commonly known as the golden-veined bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Isoglossa floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
