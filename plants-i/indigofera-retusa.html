Indigofera retusa

Indigofera retusa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a dye source in some cultures.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
