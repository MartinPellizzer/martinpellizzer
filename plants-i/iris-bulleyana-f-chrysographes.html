Iris bulleyana f. chrysographes

Iris bulleyana f. chrysographes, commonly known as the golden-striped irises, is a unique variant of the Bulleyana iris.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and white streaks on the petals, which create a visually appealing contrast. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 40 centimeters and blooms in early to mid-spring.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the vibrant flowers.
