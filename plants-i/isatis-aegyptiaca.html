Isatis aegyptiaca

Isatis aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian woad, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Historically, it was cultivated for its indigo dye, which was highly valued in ancient times.
