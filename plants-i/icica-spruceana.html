Icica spruceana

Icica spruceana, commonly known as the Sierra spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and cones that mature in the second year. The tree thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Icica spruceana plays a vital role in its ecosystem by offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
