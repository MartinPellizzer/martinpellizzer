Isachne dispar var. villosa

Isachne dispar var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of loose, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
