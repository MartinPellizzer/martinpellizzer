Iria tetragona

Iria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled iria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, angular stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Iria tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
