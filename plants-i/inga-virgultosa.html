Inga virgultosa

Inga virgultosa, commonly known as the "sugar-apple" or "sweet-scented tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces fruit that resembles a small apple, with a yellow or orange exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Inga virgultosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
