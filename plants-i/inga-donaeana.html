Inga donaeana

Inga donaeana, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.

Inga donaeana plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its ecosystem.
