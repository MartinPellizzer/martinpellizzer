Isotypus rosiflorus

Isotypus rosiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flower plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rose-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in shaded, forested areas where it forms dense clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
