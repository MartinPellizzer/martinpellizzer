Iris marchesettii

Iris marchesettii, commonly known as the Marchesettii iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris marchesettii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
