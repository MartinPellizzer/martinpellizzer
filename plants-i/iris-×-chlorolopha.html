Iris × chlorolopha

Iris × chlorolopha, commonly known as the blue iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in early summer. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often a vibrant green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Iris × chlorolopha is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
