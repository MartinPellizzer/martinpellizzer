Inga bullata var. glabrescens

Inga bullata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the white-fruit inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces showy white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in agroforestry systems.
