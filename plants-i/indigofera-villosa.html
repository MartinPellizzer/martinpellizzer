Indigofera villosa

Indigofera villosa, commonly known as hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
