Indigofera campestris var. microphylla

Indigofera campestris var. microphylla, commonly known as small-leaved cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and compound pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or for soil nitrogen fixation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
