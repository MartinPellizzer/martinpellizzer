Isophyllum segetum

Isophyllum segetum, commonly known as the field isophyllum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Isophyllum segetum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
