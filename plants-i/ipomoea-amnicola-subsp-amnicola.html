Ipomoea amnicola subsp. amnicola

Ipomoea amnicola subsp.

amnicola, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along streams and in rocky areas. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
