Indigofera aspera

Indigofera aspera, commonly known as rough indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its hardy nature makes it a common weed in agricultural settings.
