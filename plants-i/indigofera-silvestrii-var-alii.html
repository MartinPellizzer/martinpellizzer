Indigofera silvestrii var. alii

Indigofera silvestrii var. alii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre indigo, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant produces elongated seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is of interest for its ornamental value and potential ecological role in native plant communities.
