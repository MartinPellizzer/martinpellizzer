Ipomoea lindenii

Ipomoea lindenii, commonly known as the Hawaiian moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms with five petals, often with a violet or pinkish hue at the base. The Hawaiian moonflower is also known for its strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
