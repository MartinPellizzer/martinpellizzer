Inga pleiogyna

Inga pleiogyna, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga pleiogyna is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
