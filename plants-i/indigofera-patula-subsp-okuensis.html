Indigofera patula subsp. okuensis

Indigofera patula subsp.

okuensis, commonly known as the Oku indigo, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as montane forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
