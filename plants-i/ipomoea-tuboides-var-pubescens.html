Ipomoea tuboides var. pubescens

Ipomoea tuboides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its botanical name. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
