Iris sintenisii subsp. sintenisii

Iris sintenisii subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Italy. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
