Iris itsihatsi

Iris itsihatsi, commonly known as the white iris, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
