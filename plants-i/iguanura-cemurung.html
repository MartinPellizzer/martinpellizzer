Iguanura cemurung

Iguanura cemurung, commonly known as the white-leafed cecropia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often whitish bark and large, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
