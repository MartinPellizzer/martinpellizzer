Isodon inflexus var. transticus

Isodon inflexus var. transticus, commonly known as the Transcaspian isodon, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Lamiaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
