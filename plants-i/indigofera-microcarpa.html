Indigofera microcarpa

Indigofera microcarpa, commonly known as small-fruited indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its fruit is a slender legume containing several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its hardy nature, it can thrive in poor soil conditions and is adaptable to various climates.
