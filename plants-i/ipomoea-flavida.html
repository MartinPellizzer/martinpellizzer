Ipomoea flavida

Ipomoea flavida, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving the plant its common name.
