Ipomoea bracteosa

Ipomoea bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple and pink, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea bracteosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
