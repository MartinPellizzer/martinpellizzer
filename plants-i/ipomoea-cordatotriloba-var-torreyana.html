Ipomoea cordatotriloba var. torreyana

Ipomoea cordatotriloba var. torreyana, commonly known as the Torrey's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in rocky or arid habitats. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
