Iris reticulata var. atropurpurea

Iris reticulata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple reticulated iris, is a compact, bulbous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features delicate, nodding flowers with a deep purple to almost black hue, often with a contrasting white or pale blue center. The plant produces sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates. It blooms in early spring, typically in March or April, and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
