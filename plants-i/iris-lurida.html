Iris lurida

Iris lurida, commonly known as the rough iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. Its leaves are grass-like and often have a rough texture, giving it its common name.

Iris lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
