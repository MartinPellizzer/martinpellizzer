Indigofera sessilifolia

Indigofera sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
