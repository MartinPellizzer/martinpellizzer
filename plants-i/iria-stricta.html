Iria stricta

Iria stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are usually purple or pink.

Iria stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
