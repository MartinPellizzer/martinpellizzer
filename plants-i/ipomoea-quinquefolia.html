Ipomoea quinquefolia

Ipomoea quinquefolia, commonly known as morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Ipomoea quinquefolia is also noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional medicinal purposes.
