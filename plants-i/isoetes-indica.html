Isoetes indica

Isoetes indica, commonly known as the Indian water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that form a compact, mat-like structure on the sediment surface of freshwater habitats. The plant is often found in slow-moving or still water bodies, such as ponds and lakes, where it can thrive in shallow, oxygenated environments. Its reproductive structures, known as spore-bearing cones, are typically located at the base of the plant.

Isoetes indica plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing microhabitats for various aquatic organisms.
