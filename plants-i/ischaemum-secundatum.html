Ischaemum secundatum

Ischaemum secundatum, commonly known as the slender barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, with narrow, elongated leaves that are slightly folded along the midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which produces small, two-lobed spikelets.

Ischaemum secundatum is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role as a common weed in agricultural settings.
