Ichnanthus nemoralis

Ichnanthus nemoralis, commonly known as the lesser twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces a single, erect inflorescence bearing several flowers.

Ichnanthus nemoralis is a non-photosynthetic plant, relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
