Iris × binata

Iris × binata, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, six-petaled flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Iris × binata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
