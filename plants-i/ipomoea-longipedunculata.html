Ipomoea longipedunculata

Ipomoea longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are woody and tend to twine around supports.

Ipomoea longipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
