Indianthus virgatus

Indianthus virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's thorn, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
