Iberis × bretonii

Iberis × bretonii, commonly known as the Breton carnation, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis × bretonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
