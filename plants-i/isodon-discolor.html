Isodon discolor

Isodon discolor, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Isodon discolor is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
