Inga cordistipula

Inga cordistipula, commonly known as the "Cordate Inga," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with oval to heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or white. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Inga cordistipula is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
