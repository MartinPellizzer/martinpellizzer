Ipomoea argyrophylla var. glabrescens

Ipomoea argyrophylla var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows as a twining vine, reaching lengths of up to 10 feet, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
