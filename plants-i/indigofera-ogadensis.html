Indigofera ogadensis

Indigofera ogadensis, commonly known as the Sudan indigo, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye in some regions.
