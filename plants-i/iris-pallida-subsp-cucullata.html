Iris pallida subsp. cucullata

Iris pallida subsp.

cucullata, commonly known as the pale iris or the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, six-petaled blue to purple flowers. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
