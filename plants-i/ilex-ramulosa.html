Ilex ramulosa

Ilex ramulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex ramulosa typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with mild climates.
