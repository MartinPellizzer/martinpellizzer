Indigofera grata

Indigofera grata, commonly known as the sweet indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Indigofera grata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
