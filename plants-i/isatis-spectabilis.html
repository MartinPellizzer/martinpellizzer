Isatis spectabilis

Isatis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy yellow rocket, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Isatis spectabilis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
