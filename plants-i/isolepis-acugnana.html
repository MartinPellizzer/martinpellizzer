Isolepis acugnana

Isolepis acugnana, commonly known as the sharp-leaved marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Isolepis acugnana typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its presence is often indicative of healthy, undisturbed aquatic habitats.
