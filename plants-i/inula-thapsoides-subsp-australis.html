Inula thapsoides subsp. australis

Inula thapsoides subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian gumweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, conical shape with numerous small, tubular flowers.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
