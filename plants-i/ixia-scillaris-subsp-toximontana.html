Ixia scillaris subsp. toximontana

Ixia scillaris subsp.

toximontana, commonly known as the mountain yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces basal leaves that are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
