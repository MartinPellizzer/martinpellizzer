Isoloma × pyramidale

Isoloma × pyramidale, commonly known as the hybrid pyramidal bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Isoloma luteum and Isoloma pyramidalis, characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
