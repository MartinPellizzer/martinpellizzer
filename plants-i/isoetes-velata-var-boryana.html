Isoetes velata var. boryana

Isoetes velata var. boryana, commonly known as the Boryana watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. This variety is native to freshwater habitats in temperate regions, often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the upper surface of the leaf-like structures, a feature typical of the genus.

Isoetes velata var. boryana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling in its environment.
