Indigofera nairobiensis var. angusta

Indigofera nairobiensis var. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved indigo, is a shrub native to East Africa, particularly Kenya.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
