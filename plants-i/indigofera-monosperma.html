Indigofera monosperma

Indigofera monosperma, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods containing a single seed, which is a distinguishing feature of its species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has been historically used for its dye-producing properties, contributing to traditional textile industries.
