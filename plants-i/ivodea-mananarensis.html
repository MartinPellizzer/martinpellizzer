Ivodea mananarensis

Ivodea mananarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ivy, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, sweet aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
