Imperata saccharifera

Imperata saccharifera, commonly known as sugar grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and tall, cylindrical inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is known for its high content of saccharin, a natural sweetener, which has historically been used in various traditional applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its fibrous roots, it plays a role in soil stabilization and can be a pioneer species in ecological succession.
