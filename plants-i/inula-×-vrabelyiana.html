Inula × vrabelyiana

Inula × vrabelyiana, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Inula helenium and Inula crithmoides, characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head.

Inula × vrabelyiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its medicinal and ecological benefits.
