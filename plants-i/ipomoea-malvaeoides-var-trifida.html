Ipomoea malvaeoides var. trifida

Ipomoea malvaeoides var. trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
