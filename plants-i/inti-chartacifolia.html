Inti chartacifolia

Inti chartacifolia, commonly known as the Andean fan palm, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional Andean crafts, showcasing its cultural significance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
