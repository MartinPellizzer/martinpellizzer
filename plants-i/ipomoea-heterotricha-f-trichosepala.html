Ipomoea heterotricha f. trichosepala

Ipomoea heterotricha f. trichosepala, commonly known as the hairy-sepaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy sepals that give it its specific epithet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Convolvulaceae family.
