Ionidium enneaspermum var. hirtum

Ionidium enneaspermum var. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that bear tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its hairy texture, which gives it its common name, is most prominent on the stems and leaves.

Ionidium enneaspermum var. hirtum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
