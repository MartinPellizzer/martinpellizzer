Inga virescens

Inga virescens, commonly known as the green inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flattened seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Inga virescens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in agroforestry systems.
