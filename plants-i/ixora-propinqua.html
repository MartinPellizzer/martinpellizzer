Ixora propinqua

Ixora propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy aroma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ixora propinqua is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a hedge due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
