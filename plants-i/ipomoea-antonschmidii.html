Ipomoea antonschmidii

Ipomoea antonschmidii, commonly known as the Antonschmidii morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to 3 meters. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, a trait that gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
