Isolepis erythronegma

Isolepis erythronegma, commonly known as the red-stemmed spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, reddish stems that become more prominent as the plant matures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects and wetland conservation efforts.
