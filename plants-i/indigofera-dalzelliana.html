Indigofera dalzelliana

Indigofera dalzelliana, commonly known as the Dalzell's indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
