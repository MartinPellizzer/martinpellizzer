Impatiens arnottii

Impatiens arnottii, commonly known as the rock impatiens, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
