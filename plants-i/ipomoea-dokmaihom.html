Ipomoea dokmaihom

Ipomoea dokmaihom, commonly known as the Dokmai Hom morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which can be used for propagation.
