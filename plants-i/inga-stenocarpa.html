Inga stenocarpa

Inga stenocarpa, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems.
