Iresine racemosa

Iresine racemosa, commonly known as redroot ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, velvety red leaves that contrast with its pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iresine racemosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
