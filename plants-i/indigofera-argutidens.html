Indigofera argutidens

Indigofera argutidens, commonly known as the rough indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for various traditional purposes, including dyeing and medicinal applications.
