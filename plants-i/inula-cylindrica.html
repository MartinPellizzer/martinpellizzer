Inula cylindrica

Inula cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, sturdy stem covered in stiff, grayish-green leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cylindrical shape, giving it its common name.

Inula cylindrica is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, and it is known for its medicinal properties.
