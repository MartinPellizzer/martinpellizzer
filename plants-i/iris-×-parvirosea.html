Iris × parvirosea

Iris × parvirosea, commonly known as the dwarf iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris tatarica.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often blue or violet, flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is valued for its beauty and ease of cultivation in gardens.
