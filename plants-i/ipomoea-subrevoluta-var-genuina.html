Ipomoea subrevoluta var. genuina

Ipomoea subrevoluta var. genuina, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is a fast-growing species that can climb using its tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
