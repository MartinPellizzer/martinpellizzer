Inulanthera tridens

Inulanthera tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed inula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Inulanthera tridens is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
