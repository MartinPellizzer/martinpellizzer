Iria vahlii

Iria vahlii, commonly known as the Vahl's iris, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Iria vahlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
