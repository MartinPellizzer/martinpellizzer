Impatiens velaxata

Impatiens velaxata, commonly known as the velvet impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
