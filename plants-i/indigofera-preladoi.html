Indigofera preladoi

Indigofera preladoi, commonly known as the Prelado's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to blue flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
