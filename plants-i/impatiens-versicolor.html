Impatiens versicolor

Impatiens versicolor, commonly known as the variable impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that come in a variety of hues. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are spurred at the base and bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow ornamental plants.

Impatiens versicolor thrives in moist, shady environments and is known for its adaptability to different soil types.
