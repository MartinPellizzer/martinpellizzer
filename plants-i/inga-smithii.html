Inga smithii

Inga smithii, commonly known as the "coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. It produces small, round fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used as a coffee substitute.

Inga smithii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
