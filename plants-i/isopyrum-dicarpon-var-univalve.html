Isopyrum dicarpon var. univalve

Isopyrum dicarpon var. univalve, commonly known as the small white crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, white, five-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.
