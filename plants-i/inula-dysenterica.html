Inula dysenterica

Inula dysenterica, commonly known as the feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with grayish-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and serrated. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense clusters, forming a composite head that attracts pollinators.

Inula dysenterica is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating headaches and inflammatory conditions.
