Isoetes panamensis

Isoetes panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spiky, leaf-like structures called "sori," which are used for gas exchange and reproduction. This plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its morphology includes a central axis with spirally arranged leaves, and it is often found in regions with stable water levels.

Isoetes panamensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
