Ipomoea eriocephala var. abbreviata

Ipomoea eriocephala var. abbreviata, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can also become invasive in certain environments.
