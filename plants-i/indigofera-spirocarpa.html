Indigofera spirocarpa

Indigofera spirocarpa, commonly known as the spiral carpa indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is also valued for its historical use in dye production.
