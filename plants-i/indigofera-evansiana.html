Indigofera evansiana

Indigofera evansiana, commonly known as the blue pea or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's seeds are also utilized in various cultural and culinary practices.
