Ipomoea amoena

Ipomoea amoena, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and grows rapidly along supports or trellises. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall.

Ipomoea amoena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
