Indigofera centrota

Indigofera centrota, commonly known as Mexican indigo, is a species of legume native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to elliptic leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that turn brown as they mature.

Indigofera centrota is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
