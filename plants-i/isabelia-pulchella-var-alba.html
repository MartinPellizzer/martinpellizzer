Isabelia pulchella var. alba

Isabelia pulchella var. alba, commonly known as the white isabelia, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that contrast beautifully with its green foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
