Inga fredoniana

Inga fredoniana, commonly known as the red-fruit inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Inga fredoniana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful species for reforestation and habitat restoration.
