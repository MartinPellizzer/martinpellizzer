Inga ricardorum

Inga ricardorum, commonly known as the "Ricard's inga," is a species of leguminous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the genus Inga, which is part of the legume family Fabaceae, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with dense, evergreen foliage. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and are often purple or pink in color, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
