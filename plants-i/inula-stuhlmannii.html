Inula stuhlmannii

Inula stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Inula stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
