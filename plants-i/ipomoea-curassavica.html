Ipomoea curassavica

Ipomoea curassavica, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It produces showy, colorful blooms in shades of red, purple, and blue.

Ipomoea curassavica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
