Iberis lagascana var. granatensis

Iberis lagascana var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granada candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
