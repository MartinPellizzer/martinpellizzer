Impatiens prainiana

Impatiens prainiana, commonly known as the purple-stemmed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Impatiens in the family Balsaminaceae, characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with hairy, often purple-tinged stems. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with five petals and a long, slender spur.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
