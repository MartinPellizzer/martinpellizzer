Indigofera leucoclada

Indigofera leucoclada, commonly known as white indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

While not widely cultivated for its foliage, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
