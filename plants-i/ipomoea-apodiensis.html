Ipomoea apodiensis

Ipomoea apodiensis, commonly known as the naked-foot morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Ipomoea apodiensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
