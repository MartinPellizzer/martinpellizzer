Indigofera paraoxalidea

Indigofera paraoxalidea, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices across its native range.
