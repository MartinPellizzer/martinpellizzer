Ipomoea hospitalis

Ipomoea hospitalis, commonly known as the "hospital plant," is a flowering vine native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The name "hospitalis" is believed to reference its traditional use in medicinal practices.
