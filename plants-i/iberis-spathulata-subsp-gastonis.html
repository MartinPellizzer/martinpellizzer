Iberis spathulata subsp. gastonis

Iberis spathulata subsp.

gastonis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the spring season.
