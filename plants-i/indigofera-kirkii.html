Indigofera kirkii

Indigofera kirkii, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The plant has traditional uses in dyeing textiles and as a source of medicinal compounds.
