Iris persica var. mardinensis

Iris persica var. mardinensis, commonly known as the Mardin iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the region of Mardin in southeastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The Mardin iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
