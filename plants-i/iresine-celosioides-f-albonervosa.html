Iresine celosioides f. albonervosa

Iresine celosioides f. albonervosa, commonly known as the White-veined Iresine, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features vibrant, velvety leaves with prominent white veins that contrast beautifully against its deep red or purple foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or red. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
