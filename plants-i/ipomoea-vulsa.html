Ipomoea vulsa

Ipomoea vulsa, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea vulsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
