Impatiens kodachadriensis

Impatiens kodachadriensis, commonly known as Kodachadri Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features showy, brightly colored flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a spurred structure. Its leaves are ovate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
