Isotoma fluviatilis subsp. borealis

Isotoma fluviatilis subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along riverbanks and coastal areas.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a mat-forming growth habit.

It is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
