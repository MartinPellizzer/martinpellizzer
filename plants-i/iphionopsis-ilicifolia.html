Iphionopsis ilicifolia

Iphionopsis ilicifolia, commonly known as the leatherleaf phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of leathery, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient plant for native plant gardens.
