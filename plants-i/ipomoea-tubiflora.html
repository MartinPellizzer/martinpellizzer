Ipomoea tubiflora

Ipomoea tubiflora, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, climbing or trailing along the ground.

Ipomoea tubiflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively in suitable habitats.
