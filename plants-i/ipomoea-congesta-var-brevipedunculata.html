Ipomoea congesta var. brevipedunculata

Ipomoea congesta var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of morning glories.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter peduncles compared to other varieties of Ipomoea congesta.
