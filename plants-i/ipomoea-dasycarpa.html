Ipomoea dasycarpa

Ipomoea dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces distinctive, rough-textured fruits that are typically spherical or egg-shaped. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea dasycarpa thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
