Ipomoea connata

Ipomoea connata, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can be quite large, often reaching up to 10 inches in diameter.

Ipomoea connata is known for its rapid growth and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
