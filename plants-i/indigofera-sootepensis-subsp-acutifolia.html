Indigofera sootepensis subsp. acutifolia

Indigofera sootepensis subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as the acuteleaf indigofera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil.
