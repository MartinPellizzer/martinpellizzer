Ixeris dentata subsp. kimurana

Ixeris dentata subsp.

kimurana, commonly known as Korean golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia. It features a rosette of narrow, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with showy ray florets surrounded by a central disk of smaller florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscape design.
