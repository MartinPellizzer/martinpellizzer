Ida gigantea

Ida gigantea, commonly known as the giant ida, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, which bloom during the wet season. Ida gigantea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and exotic plant collections.
