Iris × neumayeri

Iris × neumayeri, commonly known as the Neumayer iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, lavender-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed beard at the base of the petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is widely cultivated for its striking blooms and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
