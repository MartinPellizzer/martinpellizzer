Ischaemum sakaguchii

Ischaemum sakaguchii, commonly known as Japanese wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Ischaemum sakaguchii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
