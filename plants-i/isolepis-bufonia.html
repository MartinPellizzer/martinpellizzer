Isolepis bufonia

Isolepis bufonia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Isolepis bufonia is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
