Isoetes engelmannii var. georgiana

Isoetes engelmannii var. georgiana, commonly known as the Georgia water clover, is a rare aquatic fern that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its intricate, lace-like fronds that emerge from rhizomes. The plant is typically found in the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and Florida, where it prefers wetlands and marshy areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, cone-like formations called strobili.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, this species is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
