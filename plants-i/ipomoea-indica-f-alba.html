Ipomoea indica f. alba

Ipomoea indica f. alba, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing tendrils that help it climb supports. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its blooming period usually occurs in the summer months, adding ornamental value to gardens.
