Isandrina arborescens

Isandrina arborescens, commonly known as the Natal apple or Natal pear, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits with a sweet, apple-like flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Isandrina arborescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses.
