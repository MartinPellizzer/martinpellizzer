Impatiens fruticosa

Impatiens fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically blooms from spring to autumn and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Impatiens fruticosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
