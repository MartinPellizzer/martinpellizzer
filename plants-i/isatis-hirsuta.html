Isatis hirsuta

Isatis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy woad, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, blue-violet flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine highlights its botanical significance.
