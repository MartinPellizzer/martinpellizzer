Iris × pallidirosea

Iris × pallidirosea, commonly known as the pale pink iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris pallida and Iris roseea.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its elegant appearance.
