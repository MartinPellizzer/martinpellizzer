Indigofera burchellii var. paucifolia

Indigofera burchellii var. paucifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved indigo, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves with reduced leaflets. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
