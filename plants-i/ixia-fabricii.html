Ixia fabricii

Ixia fabricii, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Ixia fabricii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, long-lasting blooms.
