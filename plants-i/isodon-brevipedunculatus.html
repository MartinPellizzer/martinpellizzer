Isodon brevipedunculatus

Isodon brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
