Ipomoea pandurata f. leviuscula

Ipomoea pandurata f. leviuscula, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is native to tropical regions of South America. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
