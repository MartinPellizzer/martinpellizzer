Ilex wuiana

Ilex wuiana, commonly known as the Guangdong holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Ilex wuiana is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
