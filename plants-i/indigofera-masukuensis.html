Indigofera masukuensis

Indigofera masukuensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules, contributing to soil fertility. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
