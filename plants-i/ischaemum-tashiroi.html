Ischaemum tashiroi

Ischaemum tashiroi, commonly known as Tashiro's barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, flat leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
