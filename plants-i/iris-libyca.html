Iris libyca

Iris libyca, commonly known as the Libyan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
