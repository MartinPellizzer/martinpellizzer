Ischaemum turneri

Ischaemum turneri, commonly known as Turner's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
