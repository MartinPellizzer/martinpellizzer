Impatiens andersonii

Impatiens andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
