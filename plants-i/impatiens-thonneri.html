Impatiens thonneri

Impatiens thonneri, commonly known as the Congo impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing near the forest floor in moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
