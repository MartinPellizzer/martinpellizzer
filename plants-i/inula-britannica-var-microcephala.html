Inula britannica var. microcephala

Inula britannica var. microcephala, commonly known as the lesser elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the United Kingdom.

It features a robust, hairy stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, topped with a dense cluster of yellow, daisy-like flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers bloom from July to September, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.
