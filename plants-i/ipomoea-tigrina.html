Ipomoea tigrina

Ipomoea tigrina, commonly known as the Turk's cap morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces showy, seed pods that split open to reveal spiral-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea tigrina is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and herbs.
