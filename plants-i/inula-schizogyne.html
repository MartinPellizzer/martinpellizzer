Inula schizogyne

Inula schizogyne, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
