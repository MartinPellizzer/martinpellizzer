Ischnea korythoglossa

Ischnea korythoglossa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ischnea korythoglossa is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
