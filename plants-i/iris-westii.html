Iris westii

Iris westii, commonly known as the western iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with three petals and three sepals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris westii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
