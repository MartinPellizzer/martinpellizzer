Iresine paniculata

Iresine paniculata, commonly known as the redroot iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Iresine paniculata is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a garden favorite and a potential weed in some areas.
