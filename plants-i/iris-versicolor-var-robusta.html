Iris versicolor var. robusta

Iris versicolor var. robusta, commonly known as the robust iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and wetland habitats.
