Iresine discolor

Iresine discolor, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from deep red to vibrant pink and purple, depending on light exposure. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, emerging from the base of the leaves. Iresine discolor thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique leaf coloration makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
