Inga cocleensis subsp. megantha

Inga cocleensis subsp.

megantha, commonly known as the giant inga, is a tropical tree native to Central America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant is distinguished by its large, spreading canopy and long, pendulous inflorescences. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
