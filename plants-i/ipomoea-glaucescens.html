Ipomoea glaucescens

Ipomoea glaucescens, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
