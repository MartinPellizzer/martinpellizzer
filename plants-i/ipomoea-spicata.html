Ipomoea spicata

Ipomoea spicata, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
