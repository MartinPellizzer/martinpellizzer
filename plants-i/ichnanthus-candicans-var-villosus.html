Ichnanthus candicans var. villosus

Ichnanthus candicans var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy yellow iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish-blue throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, making it a common sight in swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
