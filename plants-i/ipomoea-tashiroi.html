Ipomoea tashiroi

Ipomoea tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant typically grows as a vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a trailing habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Ipomoea tashiroi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
