Ixeris japonica subsp. litoralis

Ixeris japonica subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as the coastal golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal regions of East Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure. It is often found in dune environments and coastal grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
