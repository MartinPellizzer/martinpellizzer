Isatis costata

Isatis costata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woad, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It produces long, slender pods that contain seeds with a distinctive, ridged surface.

Isatis costata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
