Isodon latifolius

Isodon latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf isodon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
