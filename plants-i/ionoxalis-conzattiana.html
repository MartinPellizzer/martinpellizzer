Ionoxalis conzattiana

Ionoxalis conzattiana, commonly known as the Conzattian wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces clusters of small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and mild edible leaves.

Ionoxalis conzattiana is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
