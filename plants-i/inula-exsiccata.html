Inula exsiccata

Inula exsiccata, commonly known as the dried elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its dried roots are often used in herbal medicine for their purported digestive and respiratory benefits.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks.
