Iris cosniae

Iris cosniae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy, spurred tepals that are typical of the iris family.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native environment.
