Iris spuria subsp. dierinckii

Iris spuria subsp.

dierinckii, commonly known as the Dierckens iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in wet meadows and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. Its flowers are violet-blue or purple, with three upright petals and three spreading sepals, forming a distinctive "iris" shape.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
