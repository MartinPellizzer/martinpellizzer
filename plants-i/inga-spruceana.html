Inga spruceana

Inga spruceana, commonly known as the red inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common component of tropical forests. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga spruceana also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
