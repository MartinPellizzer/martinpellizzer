Iris persica var. magna

Iris persica var. magna, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
