Inula africana

Inula africana, commonly known as African elecampane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves and roots contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
