Isocarpha atriplicifolia subsp. wrightii

Isocarpha atriplicifolia subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's false ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils.
