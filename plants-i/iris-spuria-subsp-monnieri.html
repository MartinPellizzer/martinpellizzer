Iris spuria subsp. monnieri

Iris spuria subsp.

monnieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, blue to violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and smaller, more clustered flowers compared to other iris species.
