Ipomoea sidifolia

Ipomoea sidifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, sword-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea sidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
