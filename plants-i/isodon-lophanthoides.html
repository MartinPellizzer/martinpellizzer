Isodon lophanthoides

Isodon lophanthoides, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Isodon lophanthoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
