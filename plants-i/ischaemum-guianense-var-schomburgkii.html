Ischaemum guianense var. schomburgkii

Ischaemum guianense var. schomburgkii, commonly known as Schomburgk's wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
