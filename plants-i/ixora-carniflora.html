Ixora carniflora

Ixora carniflora, commonly known as the bloodflower, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora carniflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
