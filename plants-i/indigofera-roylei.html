Indigofera roylei

Indigofera roylei, commonly known as Kashmir indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
