Iris × fulvala

Iris × fulvala, commonly known as the golden iris, is a hybrid perennial plant that belongs to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps.

Iris × fulvala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens and pond edges.
