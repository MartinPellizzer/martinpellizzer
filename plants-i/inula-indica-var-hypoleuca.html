Inula indica var. hypoleuca

Inula indica var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, daisy-like white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
