Ixia brunneobractea

Ixia brunneobractea, commonly known as the bronze bract ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces narrow, bronze-colored bracts that surround the flower cluster, giving it its common name. Ixia brunneobractea typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
