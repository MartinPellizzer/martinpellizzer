Iliamna rivularis

Iliamna rivularis, commonly known as the river huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are pink to white and appear in clusters, followed by edible berries that are red when mature.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is also used by indigenous communities for food and traditional practices.
