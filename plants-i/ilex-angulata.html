Ilex angulata

Ilex angulata, commonly known as the angular holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Ilex angulata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its dense foliage and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in shaded garden settings.
