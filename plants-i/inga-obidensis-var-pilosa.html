Inga obidensis var. pilosa

Inga obidensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compound leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments within the understory of forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in supporting biodiversity.
