Indigofera jaubertiana

Indigofera jaubertiana, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cultures.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
