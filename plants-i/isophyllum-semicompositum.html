Isophyllum semicompositum

Isophyllum semicompositum, commonly known as the partial composite plant, is a unique species characterized by its hybrid botanical structure.

It exhibits a combination of features typical of both composite and non-composite plants, making it a subject of interest in botanical classification. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow.

Due to its rare occurrence and distinct morphology, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and taxonomic studies.
