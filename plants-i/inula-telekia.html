Inula telekia

Inula telekia, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, with the central disk being tubular.

Inula telekia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
