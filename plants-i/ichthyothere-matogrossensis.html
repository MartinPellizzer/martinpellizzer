Ichthyothere matogrossensis

Ichthyothere matogrossensis, commonly known as the Matogrossense ichthyothere, is a unique aquatic plant native to the freshwater regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that float just beneath the water's surface. The plant exhibits a specialized root system that anchors it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water. Its flowers, though small, are adapted for aquatic pollination, often relying on water currents for seed dispersal.

This species plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
