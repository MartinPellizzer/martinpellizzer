Iris pallida subsp. australis

Iris pallida subsp.

australis, commonly known as the southern pale iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshy areas.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate blue to purple flowers with a distinctive spur. It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
