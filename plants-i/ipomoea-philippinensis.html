Ipomoea philippinensis

Ipomoea philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Ipomoea philippinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
