Impatiens speciosa

Impatiens speciosa, commonly known as the showy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive dark eye at the center. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and features opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are known for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Impatiens speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of growing conditions.
