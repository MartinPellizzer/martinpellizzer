Inga punctata var. elongata

Inga punctata var. elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by flat, winged pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
