Ixora brunnescens

Ixora brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-leaf ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that are attractive to pollinators like birds and bees. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive brownish hue, which gives it its common name.

Ixora brunnescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
