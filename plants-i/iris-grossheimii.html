Iris grossheimii

Iris grossheimii, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a species native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Iris grossheimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and fragrance.
