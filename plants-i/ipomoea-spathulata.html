Ipomoea spathulata

Ipomoea spathulata, commonly known as the scarlet arum, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spathaceous flowers.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to Central and South America. The plant produces vibrant red bracts that enclose small, white, trumpet-shaped flowers, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance.

Ipomoea spathulata is typically grown as a perennial in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11.
