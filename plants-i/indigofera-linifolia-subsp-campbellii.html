Indigofera linifolia subsp. campbellii

Indigofera linifolia subsp.

campbellii, commonly known as Campbell's indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
