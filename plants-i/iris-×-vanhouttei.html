Iris × vanhouttei

Iris × vanhouttei, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, vibrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant is native to the Netherlands and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.

Its foliage is grass-like and evergreen, adding visual interest throughout the year.
