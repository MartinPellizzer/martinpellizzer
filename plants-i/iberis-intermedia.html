Iberis intermedia

Iberis intermedia, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Iberis intermedia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
