Indigofera pentaphylla

Indigofera pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Indigofera pentaphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
