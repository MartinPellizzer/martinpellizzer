Ischaemum aristatum var. glaucum

Ischaemum aristatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, typically with two to three florets per spikelet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in damp woodlands.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its distinctive coloration and delicate appearance.
