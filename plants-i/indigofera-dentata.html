Indigofera dentata

Indigofera dentata, commonly known as the toothed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
