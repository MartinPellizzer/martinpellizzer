Ipomoea × leucantha

Ipomoea × leucantha, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a hybrid species of flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has heart-shaped, palmate leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and rapid growth habit.
