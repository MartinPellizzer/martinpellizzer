Indigofera kengeleensis

Indigofera kengeleensis, commonly known as the Kengele indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in nitrogen fixation.
