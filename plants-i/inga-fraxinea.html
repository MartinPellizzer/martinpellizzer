Inga fraxinea

Inga fraxinea, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its fruits are pods that contain edible seeds, and the plant is also valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
