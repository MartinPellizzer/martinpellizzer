Isoetes phrygia

Isoetes phrygia, commonly known as the Mediterranean water fern, is a species of water plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a dense, spike-like formation. The plant thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are usually long and ribbon-like, with a distinct midrib.

Isoetes phrygia plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
