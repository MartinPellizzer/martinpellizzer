Ignatia amara

Ignatia amara, commonly known as the wild lettuce or the weeping lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Ignatia amara is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported sedative properties.
