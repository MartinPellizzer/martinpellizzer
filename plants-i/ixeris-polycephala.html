Ixeris polycephala

Ixeris polycephala, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its ability to tolerate urban environments has made it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
