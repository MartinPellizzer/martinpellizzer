Ipomoea versicolor

Ipomoea versicolor, commonly known as the variegated morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
