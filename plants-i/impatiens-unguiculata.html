Impatiens unguiculata

Impatiens unguiculata, commonly known as the purple impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Impatiens unguiculata is a member of the Balsaminaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
