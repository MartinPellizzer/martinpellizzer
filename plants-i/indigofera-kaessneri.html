Indigofera kaessneri

Indigofera kaessneri, commonly known as the yellow indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera kaessneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
