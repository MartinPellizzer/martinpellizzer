Ipomoea funis

Ipomoea funis, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its tuberous roots, which are edible, are a significant crop in many parts of the world.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
