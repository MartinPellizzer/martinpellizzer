Impatiens elachistocentra

Impatiens elachistocentra, commonly known as the slender impatiens, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
