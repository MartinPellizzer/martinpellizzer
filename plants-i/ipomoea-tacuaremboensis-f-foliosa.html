Ipomoea tacuaremboensis f. foliosa

Ipomoea tacuaremboensis f. foliosa, commonly known as the foliaceous morning glory, is a variant of the species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that are often a striking green with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique foliage and its role in the broader Ipomoea genus.
