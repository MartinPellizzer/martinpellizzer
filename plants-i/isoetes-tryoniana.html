Isoetes tryoniana

Isoetes tryoniana, commonly known as the Tryon's watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-producing sporophylls, which are arranged in a rosette-like structure. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, where it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. Its roots are often submerged, and it plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
