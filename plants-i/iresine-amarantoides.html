Iresine amarantoides

Iresine amarantoides, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are often variegated with contrasting colors, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Iresine amarantoides thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
