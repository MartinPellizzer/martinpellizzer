Ionoxalis scopulorum

Ionoxalis scopulorum, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a deep green color and small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowering period typically occurs during the cooler months, and it is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Ionoxalis scopulorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
