Inula claessensii

Inula claessensii, commonly known as the African ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
