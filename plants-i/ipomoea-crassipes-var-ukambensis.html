Ipomoea crassipes var. ukambensis

Ipomoea crassipes var. ukambensis, commonly known as the African water spinach, is a tropical aquatic plant characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

It thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in slow-moving rivers and wetlands across sub-Saharan Africa. The plant is notable for its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense mats on the water surface. Its leaves are edible and used in various traditional cuisines, while its roots are also consumed in some regions.

However, it can become invasive in non-native habitats, outcompeting native vegetation.
