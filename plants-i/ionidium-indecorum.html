Ionidium indecorum

Ionidium indecorum, commonly known as the ornate ionidium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multi-colored bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often hidden beneath the vibrant bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Ionidium indecorum is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
