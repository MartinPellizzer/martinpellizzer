Iria autumnalis var. caespitosa

Iria autumnalis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the autumn iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

It is a valuable addition to native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
