Indigofera mildbraediana

Indigofera mildbraediana, commonly known as the mildbraed's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnate, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
