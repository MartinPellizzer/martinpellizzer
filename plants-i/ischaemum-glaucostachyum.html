Ischaemum glaucostachyum

Ischaemum glaucostachyum, commonly known as the silver-stemmed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ischaemum glaucostachyum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
