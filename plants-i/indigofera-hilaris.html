Indigofera hilaris

Indigofera hilaris, commonly known as the coastal indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its small, clustered flowers are usually blue or violet, and it produces pods containing edible seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
