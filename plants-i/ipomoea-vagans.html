Ipomoea vagans

Ipomoea vagans, commonly known as the wandering begonia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ipomoea vagans is also known for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
