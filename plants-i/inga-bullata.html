Inga bullata

Inga bullata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant is notable for its edible fruits, which have a sweet and tangy flavor, similar to tamarind. Inga bullata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by providing food for various wildlife species.
