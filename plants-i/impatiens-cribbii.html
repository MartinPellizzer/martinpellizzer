Impatiens cribbii

Impatiens cribbii, commonly known as the Cribb's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Impatiens cribbii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
