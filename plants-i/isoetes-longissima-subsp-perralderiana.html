Isoetes longissima subsp. perralderiana

Isoetes longissima subsp.

perralderiana, commonly known as the long-stemmed watermace, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments. It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in dense, spike-like formations along the fronds.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to survive in low-nutrient conditions, making it a common indicator of clean, oligotrophic water bodies.
