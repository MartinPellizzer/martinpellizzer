Ipomoea graminifolia

Ipomoea graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually blue, purple, or white in color. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oblong seeds.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
