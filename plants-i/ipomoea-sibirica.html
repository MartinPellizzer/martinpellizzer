Ipomoea sibirica

Ipomoea sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It blooms throughout the summer and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive if not managed properly.
