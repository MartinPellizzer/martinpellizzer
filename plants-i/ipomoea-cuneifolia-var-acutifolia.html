Ipomoea cuneifolia var. acutifolia

Ipomoea cuneifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and are usually a vibrant blue or purple color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats alike.
