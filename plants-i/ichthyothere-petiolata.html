Ichthyothere petiolata

Ichthyothere petiolata, commonly known as the waterleaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to moist, temperate regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in wetland environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.
