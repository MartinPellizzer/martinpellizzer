Inyonia

Inyonia, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a rare and striking species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant has a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Inyonia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Its unique appearance and symbolic significance make it a prized specimen in botanical gardens and traditional medicine.
