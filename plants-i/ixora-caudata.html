Ixora caudata

Ixora caudata, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora caudata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
