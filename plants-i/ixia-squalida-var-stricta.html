Ixia squalida var. stricta

Ixia squalida var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxeye, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers with white markings, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
