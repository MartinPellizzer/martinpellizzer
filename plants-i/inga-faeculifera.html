Inga faeculifera

Inga faeculifera, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga faeculifera also plays an important role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil.
