Iris mariae

Iris mariae, commonly known as the Marie iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris mariae is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability to well-drained soils.
