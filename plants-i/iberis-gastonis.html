Iberis gastonis

Iberis gastonis, commonly known as the Mediterranean candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of stems. Its leaves are lobed and hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Iberis gastonis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
