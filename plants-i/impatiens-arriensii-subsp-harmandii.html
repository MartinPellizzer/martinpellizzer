Impatiens arriensii subsp. harmandii

Impatiens arriensii subsp.

harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America. It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to white.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a hairy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
