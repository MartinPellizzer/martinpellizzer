Inga cinerea

Inga cinerea, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga cinerea also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
