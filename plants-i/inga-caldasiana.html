Inga caldasiana

Inga caldasiana, commonly known as the Caldasian Inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in agroforestry systems.
