Iris wherryana

Iris wherryana, commonly known as the Wherry's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers are arranged in a single spike.

Iris wherryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
