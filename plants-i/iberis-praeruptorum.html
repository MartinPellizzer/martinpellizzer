Iberis praeruptorum

Iberis praeruptorum, commonly known as the alpine candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems.

Iberis praeruptorum is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates.
