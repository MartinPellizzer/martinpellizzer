Impatiens trigonopteris

Impatiens trigonopteris, commonly known as the Trinidad impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its natural distribution in wetland areas.
