Indigofera rubromarginata

Indigofera rubromarginata, commonly known as the red-marginated indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Indigofera rubromarginata is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
