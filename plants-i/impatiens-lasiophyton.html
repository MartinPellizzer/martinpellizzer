Impatiens lasiophyton

Impatiens lasiophyton, commonly known as the rough-leaved impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens lasiophyton is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
