Indigofera magallanesii

Indigofera magallanesii, commonly known as the Magallanes indigo, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. It is typically found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
