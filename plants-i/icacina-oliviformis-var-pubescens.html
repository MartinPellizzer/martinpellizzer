Icacina oliviformis var. pubescens

Icacina oliviformis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy icacina, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several elliptical seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
