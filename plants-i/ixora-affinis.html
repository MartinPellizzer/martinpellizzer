Ixora affinis

Ixora affinis, commonly known as the flame-of-India or red ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange-red flowers, which are clustered in panicles and bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

Ixora affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
