Iris potaninii var. ionantha

Iris potaninii var. ionantha, commonly known as the Ionantha iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
