Ipomoea murucoides var. glabrata

Ipomoea murucoides var. glabrata, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that open in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The variety 'glabrata' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other varieties of Ipomoea murucoides.
