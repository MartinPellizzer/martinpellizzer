Ilex occidentalis var. maestrana

Ilex occidentalis var. maestrana, commonly known as the Maestrana holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have spiny margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for wildlife, including birds and mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
