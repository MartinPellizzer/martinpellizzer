Ipomoea fistulosa f. albiflora

Ipomoea fistulosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower morning glory, is a cultivar of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are tubular in shape and have a delicate, fragrant scent.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
