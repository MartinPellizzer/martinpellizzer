Isertia coccinea var. hypoleuca

Isertia coccinea var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the pink spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
