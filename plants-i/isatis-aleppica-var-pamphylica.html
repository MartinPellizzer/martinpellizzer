Isatis aleppica var. pamphylica

Isatis aleppica var. pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian woad, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing only leaves and the second year flowering and setting seed. The plant features dark green, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in racemes. It is known for its historical use in dye production, similar to its parent species, Isatis tinctoria.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.
