Iris × thompsonii

Iris × thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris pallida and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats.
