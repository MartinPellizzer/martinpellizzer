Ipomoea collinsiae

Ipomoea collinsiae, commonly known as Collins' morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea collinsiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
