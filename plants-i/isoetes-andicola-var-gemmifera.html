Isoetes andicola var. gemmifera

Isoetes andicola var. gemmifera, commonly known as the Andean water clover, is a rare aquatic fern-like plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, umbrella-like formations called sorus. The plant thrives in cold, oligotrophic water bodies and is adapted to low light conditions. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize aquatic sediments.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, this species is considered endangered and is of particular interest in botanical conservation studies.
