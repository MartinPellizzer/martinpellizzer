Isachne javana

Isachne javana, commonly known as Java grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
