Inula stewartii

Inula stewartii, commonly known as the yellow snakeroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features a robust root system and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The genus Inula is known for its medicinal properties, and Inula stewartii is no exception.
