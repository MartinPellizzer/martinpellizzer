Inga sarmentosa

Inga sarmentosa, commonly known as the "wild coffee tree," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded understory conditions. It produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans.

Its roots form nitrogen-fixing nodules, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
