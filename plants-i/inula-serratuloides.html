Inula serratuloides

Inula serratuloides, commonly known as the rough fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It features yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Inula serratuloides is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture.
