Ipomoea rubra var. alboflavida

Ipomoea rubra var. alboflavida, commonly known as the white and yellow morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory native to tropical regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
