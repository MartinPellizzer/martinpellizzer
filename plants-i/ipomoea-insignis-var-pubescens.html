Ipomoea insignis var. pubescens

Ipomoea insignis var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. Its foliage is heart-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
