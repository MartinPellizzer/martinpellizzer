Impatiens dalzellii

Impatiens dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's Impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to pink and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
