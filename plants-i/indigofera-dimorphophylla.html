Indigofera dimorphophylla

Indigofera dimorphophylla, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, bean-like seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, and it is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
