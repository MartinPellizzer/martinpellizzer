Ipomoea carrizalia

Ipomoea carrizalia, commonly known as the Carrizal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea carrizalia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
