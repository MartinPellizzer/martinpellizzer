Ipomoea petunioides

Ipomoea petunioides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a trailing vine characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common morning glory.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for covering trellises or ground cover in gardens.
