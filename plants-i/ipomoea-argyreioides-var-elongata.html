Ipomoea argyreioides var. elongata

Ipomoea argyreioides var. elongata, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that often have a silvery or grayish sheen. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
