Inula britannica subsp. latifolia

Inula britannica subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy yellow composite flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
