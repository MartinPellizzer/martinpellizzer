Iris perrieri

Iris perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette.

Iris perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
