Iris beecheyana

Iris beecheyana, commonly known as the Beechey iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Iris beecheyana is valued for its striking floral display and is often cultivated for its horticultural beauty.
