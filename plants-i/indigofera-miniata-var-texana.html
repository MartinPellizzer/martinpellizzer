Indigofera miniata var. texana

Indigofera miniata var. texana, commonly known as the Texas coralbean, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
