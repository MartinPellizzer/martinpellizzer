Ilex corallina

Ilex corallina, commonly known as the coral holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright red berries that persist throughout the winter. The plant typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The coral holly is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
