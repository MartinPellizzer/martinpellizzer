Inga thibaudiana var. latifolia

Inga thibaudiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
