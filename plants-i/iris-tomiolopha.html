Iris tomiolopha

Iris tomiolopha, commonly known as the Hawaiian iris, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces showy blooms during the spring and summer months.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
