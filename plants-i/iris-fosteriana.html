Iris fosteriana

Iris fosteriana, commonly known as the Foster's iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or yellowish beard in the center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette.

Iris fosteriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
