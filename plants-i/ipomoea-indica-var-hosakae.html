Ipomoea indica var. hosakae

Ipomoea indica var. hosakae, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant is typically annual, thriving in warm climates with full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and showy, five-petaled blooms that attract pollinators.
