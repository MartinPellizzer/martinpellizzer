Ilex cassinoides

Ilex cassinoides, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has dark green, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, hammocks, and coastal areas.

The Florida holly is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
