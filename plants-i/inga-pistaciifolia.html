Inga pistaciifolia

Inga pistaciifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. Its growth habit includes a thick, buttressed trunk and a broad canopy, making it a significant component of rainforest ecosystems.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
