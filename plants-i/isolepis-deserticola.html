Isolepis deserticola

Isolepis deserticola, commonly known as the desert flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize disturbed or barren areas.

Isolepis deserticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
