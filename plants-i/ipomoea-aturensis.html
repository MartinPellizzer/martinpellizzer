Ipomoea aturensis

Ipomoea aturensis, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. Its flowers are typically 5- to 7-inch wide, with five petals that are trumpet-shaped.

Ipomoea aturensis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.
