Ixia scariosa var. longifolia

Ixia scariosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered marsh lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with bright orange or yellow petals. The flowers are typically borne in clusters on tall, erect stems, and they bloom during the late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
