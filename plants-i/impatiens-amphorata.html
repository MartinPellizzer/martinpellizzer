Impatiens amphorata

Impatiens amphorata, commonly known as the bottle-shaped impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, cylindrical flower buds that resemble small bottles, which open to reveal five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its foliage is usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens amphorata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
