Ilex paraguariensis f. confusa

Ilex paraguariensis f. confusa, commonly known as the Amazonian holly, is a variant of the yerba mate plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark green leaves that are typically shorter and broader than those of the standard Ilex paraguariensis. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used to prepare a traditional caffeinated beverage.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a distinct form within the species.
