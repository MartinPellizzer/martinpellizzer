Ipomoea leptocaulos

Ipomoea leptocaulos, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea leptocaulos is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
