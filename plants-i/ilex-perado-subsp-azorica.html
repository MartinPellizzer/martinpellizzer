Ilex perado subsp. azorica

Ilex perado subsp.

azorica, commonly known as the Azorean holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Azores. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a humid, temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
