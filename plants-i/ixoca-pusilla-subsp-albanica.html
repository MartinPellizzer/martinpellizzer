Ixoca pusilla subsp. albanica

Ixoca pusilla subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian dwarf ixora, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Balkans. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
