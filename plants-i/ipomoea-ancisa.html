Ipomoea ancisa

Ipomoea ancisa, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright red or orange, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea ancisa is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening.
