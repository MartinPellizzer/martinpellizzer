Ixora flora-lutea

Ixora flora-lutea, commonly known as the yellow flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora flora-lutea is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
