Inga saffordiana

Inga saffordiana, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
