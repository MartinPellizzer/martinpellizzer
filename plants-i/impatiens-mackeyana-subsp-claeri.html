Impatiens mackeyana subsp. claeri

Impatiens mackeyana subsp.

claeri, commonly known as the Mackey's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in the study of plant adaptation to high-altitude ecosystems.
