Iris unguicularis var. carica

Iris unguicularis var. carica, commonly known as the Carpathian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
