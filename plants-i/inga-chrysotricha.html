Inga chrysotricha

Inga chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden-hair inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Inga chrysotricha is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
