Ixia tenuifolia

Ixia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a tall stem bearing clusters of tubular flowers.

Ixia tenuifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
