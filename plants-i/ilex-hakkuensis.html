Ilex hakkuensis

Ilex hakkuensis, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various bird species. Ilex hakkuensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and winter interest.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
