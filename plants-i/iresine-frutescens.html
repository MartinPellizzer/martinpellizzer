Iresine frutescens

Iresine frutescens, commonly known as redroot ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or purple stems and leaves, which often have a striking metallic sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Iresine frutescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
