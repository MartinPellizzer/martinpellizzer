Ipomoea malvaeoides f. apiculata

Ipomoea malvaeoides f. apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive apiculate (sharp-tipped) leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to ascend supports, and produces showy, fragrant blooms.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
