Indigofera emarginella var. marrupaensis

Indigofera emarginella var. marrupaensis, commonly known as the Marrupa indigo, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
