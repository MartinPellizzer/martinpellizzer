Iris susiana

Iris susiana, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The Persian iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
