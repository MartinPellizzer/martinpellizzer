Ilex sideroxyloides f. grandifolia

Ilex sideroxyloides f. grandifolia, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandhill habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
