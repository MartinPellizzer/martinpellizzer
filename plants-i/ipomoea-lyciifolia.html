Ipomoea lyciifolia

Ipomoea lyciifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports or structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
