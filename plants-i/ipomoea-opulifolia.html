Ipomoea opulifolia

Ipomoea opulifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or purpleleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils. Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it an invasive species in many regions.

Ipomoea opulifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and medicinal properties.
