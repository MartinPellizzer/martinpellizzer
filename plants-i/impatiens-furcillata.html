Impatiens furcillata

Impatiens furcillata, commonly known as the horned impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched calyx that resembles horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Impatiens furcillata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in garden settings.
