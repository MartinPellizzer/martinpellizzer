Ilex velutina var. pyrenea

Ilex velutina var. pyrenea, commonly known as the Pyrenean holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
