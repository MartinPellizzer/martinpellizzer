Inula verrucosa

Inula verrucosa, commonly known as the rough Inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, with uses in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's structure includes a central stem with alternate leaves and a composite inflorescence made up of ray and disk flowers.
