Ilex minimifolia

Ilex minimifolia, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ilex minimifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
