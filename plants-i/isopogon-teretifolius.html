Isopogon teretifolius

Isopogon teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
