Indigofera moluccana

Indigofera moluccana, commonly known as Moluccan indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
