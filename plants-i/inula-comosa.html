Inula comosa

Inula comosa, commonly known as the fuzzy aster, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Inula comosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
