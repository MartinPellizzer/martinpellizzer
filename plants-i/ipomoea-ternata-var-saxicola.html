Ipomoea ternata var. saxicola

Ipomoea ternata var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy environments. Its rapid growth and ability to withstand drought conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
