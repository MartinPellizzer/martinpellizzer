Isodon inflexus

Isodon inflexus, commonly known as the rigid verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Isodon inflexus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
