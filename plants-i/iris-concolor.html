Iris concolor

Iris concolor, commonly known as the western flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
