Iresine acutifolia

Iresine acutifolia, commonly known as the flame flower or red leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, red or purple foliage that contrasts with its small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaves and ease of care.

Iresine acutifolia is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.
