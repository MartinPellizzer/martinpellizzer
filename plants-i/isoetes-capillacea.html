Isoetes capillacea

Isoetes capillacea, commonly known as the slender watermace, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like fronds that are typically submerged in water. The plant is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in dense, stalked clusters called sorus.

Isoetes capillacea plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
