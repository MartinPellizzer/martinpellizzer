Ipomoea palmeri var. platyphylla

Ipomoea palmeri var. platyphylla, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is a climbing vine that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, flat seeds.

Botanically, it is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its showy flowers and twining growth habit.
