Inula ciliaris var. ciliaris

Inula ciliaris var. ciliaris, commonly known as the cottonweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a branched stem covered with fine, white hairs that give it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family.
