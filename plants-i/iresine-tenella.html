Iresine tenella

Iresine tenella, commonly known as red leaf sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from deep red to purple in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, which appear in clusters at the ends of the stems. Iresine tenella thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.
