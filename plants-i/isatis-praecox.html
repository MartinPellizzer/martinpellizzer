Isatis praecox

Isatis praecox, commonly known as early woad, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Isatis praecox was historically cultivated for its blue dye, similar to the more famous woad (Isatis tinctoria).
