Isolepis trichokolea

Isolepis trichokolea, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Isolepis trichokolea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
