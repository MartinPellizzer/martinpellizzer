Inga grazielae

Inga grazielae, commonly known as the Grazielae Inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
