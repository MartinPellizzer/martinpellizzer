Iberis leptophylla

Iberis leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with hairy stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.

Its blooming period occurs in spring, adding a delicate floral touch to its natural habitat.
