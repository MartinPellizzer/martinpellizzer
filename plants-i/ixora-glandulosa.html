Ixora glandulosa

Ixora glandulosa, commonly known as the flame tree or Chinese flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in small glands, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ixora glandulosa is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
