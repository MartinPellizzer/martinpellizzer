Iris × turkoman

Iris × turkoman, commonly known as the Turkoman iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. It is native to the temperate regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The Turkoman iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
