Ibarraea dichropetala

Ibarraea dichropetala, commonly known as the two-colored passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored flowers that feature a mix of red and yellow hues. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a strong, sweet fragrance. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species for pollinators.

Ibarraea dichropetala is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
