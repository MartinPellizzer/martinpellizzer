Impatiens bidentata

Impatiens bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves with two distinct teeth at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Impatiens bidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
