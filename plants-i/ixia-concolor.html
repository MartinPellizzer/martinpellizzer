Ixia concolor

Ixia concolor, commonly known as the yellow ox-eye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a red center. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Ixia concolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
