Inga pulchriflora

Inga pulchriflora, commonly known as the beautiful-flowered inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

Inga pulchriflora is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
