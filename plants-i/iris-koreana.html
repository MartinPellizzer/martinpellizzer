Iris koreana

Iris koreana, commonly known as the Korean iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or yellow central stripe. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Iris koreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegance and resilience.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
