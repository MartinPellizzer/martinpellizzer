Impatiens verrucosa

Impatiens verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in moist, shady environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Impatiens verrucosa is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
