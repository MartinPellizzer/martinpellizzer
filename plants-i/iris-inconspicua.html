Iris inconspicua

Iris inconspicua, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in montane and subalpine regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
