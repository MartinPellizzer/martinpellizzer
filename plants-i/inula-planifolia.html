Inula planifolia

Inula planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

Inula planifolia is often found in meadows and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
