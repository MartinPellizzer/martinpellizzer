Indigofera dimidiata

Indigofera dimidiata, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
