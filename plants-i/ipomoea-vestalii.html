Ipomoea vestalii

Ipomoea vestalii, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, light green leaves and large, showy white flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea vestalii is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
