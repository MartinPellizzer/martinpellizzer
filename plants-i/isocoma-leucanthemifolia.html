Isocoma leucanthemifolia

Isocoma leucanthemifolia, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flowers with a prominent central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Isocoma leucanthemifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
