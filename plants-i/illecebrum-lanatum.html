Illecebrum lanatum

Illecebrum lanatum, commonly known as the hairy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Illecebrum lanatum is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
