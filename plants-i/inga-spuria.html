Inga spuria

Inga spuria, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Inga spuria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
