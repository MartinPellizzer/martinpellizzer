Ixora novoguineensis

Ixora novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ixora novoguineensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
