Impatiens trichoclada

Impatiens trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
