Inula royleana

Inula royleana, commonly known as the Himalayan elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
