Ixora opaca var. maior

Ixora opaca var. maior, commonly known as the broadleaf ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental shrub.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
