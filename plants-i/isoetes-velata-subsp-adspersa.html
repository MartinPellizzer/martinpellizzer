Isoetes velata subsp. adspersa

Isoetes velata subsp.

adspersa, commonly known as the scattered water starwort, is a submerged aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are often clustered at the base of the leaves. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms.

Its morphology and reproductive structures are well adapted to its submersed lifestyle.
