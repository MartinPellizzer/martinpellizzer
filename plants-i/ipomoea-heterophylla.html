Ipomoea heterophylla

Ipomoea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its twining stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white, which bloom during the day. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and vary in size, contributing to its common name.

Ipomoea heterophylla is also known for its adaptability to different soil types and its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
