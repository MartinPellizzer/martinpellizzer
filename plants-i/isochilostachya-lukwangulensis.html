Isochilostachya lukwangulensis

Isochilostachya lukwangulensis, commonly known as the Lukwangulensis grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.
