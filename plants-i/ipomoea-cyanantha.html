Ipomoea cyanantha

Ipomoea cyanantha, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, often using its tendrils to support itself.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
