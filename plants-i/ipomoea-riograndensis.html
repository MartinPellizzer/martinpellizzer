Ipomoea riograndensis

Ipomoea riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea riograndensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
