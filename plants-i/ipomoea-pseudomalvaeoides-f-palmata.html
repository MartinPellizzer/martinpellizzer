Ipomoea pseudomalvaeoides f. palmata

Ipomoea pseudomalvaeoides f. palmata, commonly known as the palmate morning glory, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive foliage appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variant is prized for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
