Iberis linifolia subsp. linifolia

Iberis linifolia subsp.

linifolia, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its fragrant profile. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody base and a mat-forming growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.
