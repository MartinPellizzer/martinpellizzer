Ilex serrata f. leucocarpa

Ilex serrata f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited holly, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow berries that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique fruit color.
