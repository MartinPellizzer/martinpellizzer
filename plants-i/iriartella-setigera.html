Iriartella setigera

Iriartella setigera, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish-brown throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Iriartella setigera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
