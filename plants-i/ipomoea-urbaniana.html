Ipomoea urbaniana

Ipomoea urbaniana, commonly known as the Urbaniana morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Ipomoea urbaniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
