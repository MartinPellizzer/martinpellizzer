Impatiens furcata

Impatiens furcata, commonly known as the forked impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive forked or branched inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, and come in shades of white, pink, and red.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
