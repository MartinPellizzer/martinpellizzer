Iphigenia magnifica

Iphigenia magnifica, commonly known as the royal orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with vibrant hues of purple and white. The plant has a complex root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
