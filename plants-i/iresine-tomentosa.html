Iresine tomentosa

Iresine tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or crimson velvet plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple or red foliage, which is covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, which are followed by pale green, fleshy fruits. Iresine tomentosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
