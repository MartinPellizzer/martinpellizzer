Isoetes histrix grandis

Isoetes histrix grandis, commonly known as the giant horsetail, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented stems that resemble those of horsetails, and it typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in still or slow-moving water, where it can anchor itself to the substrate.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying aquatic plant communities.
