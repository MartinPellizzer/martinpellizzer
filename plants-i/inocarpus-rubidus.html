Inocarpus rubidus

Inocarpus rubidus, commonly known as the red-fruited burr cucumber, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to distinctive, red, spiky fruits. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed or arid environments.
