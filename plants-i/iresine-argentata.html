Iresine argentata

Iresine argentata, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Iresine argentata is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
