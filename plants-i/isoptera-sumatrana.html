Isoptera sumatrana

Isoptera sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran termite, is a species of termite native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the family Termitidae and is characterized by its complex social structure and mound-building behavior. The worker caste is responsible for constructing and maintaining the large, intricate nests that can reach several meters in height. These termites play a crucial role in the ecosystem by contributing to soil aeration and nutrient cycling.

Their presence is often an indicator of the health and biodiversity of the surrounding environment.
