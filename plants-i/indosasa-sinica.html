Indosasa sinica

Indosasa sinica, commonly known as the Chinese indosasa, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
