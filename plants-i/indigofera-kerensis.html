Indigofera kerensis

Indigofera kerensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
