Impatiens rupicola var. nataliae

Impatiens rupicola var. nataliae, commonly known as Natal's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and prefers well-drained soil and moderate moisture.
