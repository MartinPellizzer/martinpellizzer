Impatiens biflora

Impatiens biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens biflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
