Ipomoea sororia

Ipomoea sororia, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped white flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Ipomoea sororia is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
