Inula obtusifolia

Inula obtusifolia, commonly known as the bluntleaf elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flower heads appear in late summer, consisting of numerous small florets arranged in a disk shape.

The plant is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
