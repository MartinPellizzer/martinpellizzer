Inga pyriformis

Inga pyriformis, commonly known as the round-leaf inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in moist lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Inga pyriformis is often used in agroforestry systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
