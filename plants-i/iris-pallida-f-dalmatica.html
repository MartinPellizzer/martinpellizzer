Iris pallida f. dalmatica

Iris pallida f. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian pale iris, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the coastal regions of the Adriatic Sea.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive violet stripe down the central vein. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along the edges of saltwater or brackish wetlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is prized for its delicate beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with a preference for well-drained, sunny to partially shaded conditions.
