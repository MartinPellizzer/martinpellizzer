Iberis intermedia var. delphinensis

Iberis intermedia var. delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact form.
