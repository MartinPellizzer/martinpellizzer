Ipomoea macrorhiza

Ipomoea macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near waterways.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for many years.
