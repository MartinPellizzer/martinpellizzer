Indigofera repens

Indigofera repens, commonly known as creeping indigo, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often in clusters. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which can improve soil fertility in agricultural settings.
