Ilex odorata var. teysmannii

Ilex odorata var. teysmannii, commonly known as the sweet tea plant, is a species of holly native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that are edible for birds. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are aromatic, releasing a sweet, tea-like scent when crushed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.
