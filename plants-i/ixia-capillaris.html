Ixia capillaris

Ixia capillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Ixia capillaris thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
