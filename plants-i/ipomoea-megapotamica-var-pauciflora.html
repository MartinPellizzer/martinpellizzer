Ipomoea megapotamica var. pauciflora

Ipomoea megapotamica var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue or white, with five petals and a prominent trumpet-shaped corolla. It produces small, round berries that contain seeds.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
