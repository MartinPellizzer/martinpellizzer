Indigofera schimperi var. oxyphylla

Indigofera schimperi var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the Ethiopian indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its distinctive blue flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its historical significance lies in its use for producing natural dyes, particularly indigo.
