Ipomoea strigosa

Ipomoea strigosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea strigosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
