Inga nobilis

Inga nobilis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
