Imperata arundinacea var. americana

Imperata arundinacea var. americana, commonly known as cogongrass, is a perennial grass native to Asia but now invasive in parts of the United States.

It has tall, cylindrical inflorescences and produces a large amount of wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its rapid spread. The plant has fibrous roots that can penetrate deep into the soil, making it difficult to control.

Its rhizomes are highly persistent and can regenerate from small fragments, further complicating eradication efforts.Cogongrass is often found in disturbed areas and can outcompete native vegetation, altering ecosystem dynamics.
