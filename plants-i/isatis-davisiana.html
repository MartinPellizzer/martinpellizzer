Isatis davisiana

Isatis davisiana, commonly known as the Davis's woad, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Isatis davisiana is often found in moist, open habitats such as woods and meadows.
