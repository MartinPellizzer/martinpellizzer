Intsia africana

Intsia africana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree has smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy coating.

Intsia africana is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
