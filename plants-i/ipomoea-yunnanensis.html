Ipomoea yunnanensis

Ipomoea yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea yunnanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
