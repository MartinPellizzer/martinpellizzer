Iris chrysophylla

Iris chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to gold-colored leaves and delicate, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are upright and bear a single bloom.

Iris chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
