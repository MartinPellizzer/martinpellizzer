Iris illyrica

Iris illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

The Illyrian iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
