Iryanthera grandiflora

Iryanthera grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a woody stem and broad, compound leaves that provide shade in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas near rivers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical and subtropical regions.
