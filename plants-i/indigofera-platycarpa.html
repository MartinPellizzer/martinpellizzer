Indigofera platycarpa

Indigofera platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain small, round seeds. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with limited water.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
