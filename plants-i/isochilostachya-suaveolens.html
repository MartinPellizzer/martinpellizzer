Isochilostachya suaveolens

Isochilostachya suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
