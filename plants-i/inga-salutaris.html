Inga salutaris

Inga salutaris, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "sweet sop," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, green fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications, and the tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties that enrich the soil.
