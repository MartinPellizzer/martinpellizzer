Indigofera inyangana

Indigofera inyangana, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannah and woodland habitats.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional dyeing practices and as a source of medicinal compounds.
