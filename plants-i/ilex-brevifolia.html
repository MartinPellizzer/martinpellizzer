Ilex brevifolia

Ilex brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with glossy, pointed leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and year-round foliage make it a versatile plant for both residential and commercial gardens.
