Iria squarrosa

Iria squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the morning. Iria squarrosa is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
