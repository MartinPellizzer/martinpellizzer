Impatiens adenopus

Impatiens adenopus, commonly known as the purple-stemmed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Impatiens adenopus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
