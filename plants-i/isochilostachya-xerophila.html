Isochilostachya xerophila

Isochilostachya xerophila, commonly known as the desert grass, is a perennial grass native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are adapted to the dry climate. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep in the soil.

Isochilostachya xerophila is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
