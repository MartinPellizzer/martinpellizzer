Ischnochloa

Ischnochloa, commonly known as the "false bluemink," is a genus of grasses native to South America.

These plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Ischnochloa species are often found in rocky soils and can thrive in poor nutrient conditions. They have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, some species are studied for their potential in ecological restoration projects.
