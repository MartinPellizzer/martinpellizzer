Ixora aurorea

Ixora aurorea, commonly known as golden flame or golden ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ixora aurorea thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring full sun and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
