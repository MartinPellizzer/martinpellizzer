Iris aitchisonii var. flaviflora

Iris aitchisonii var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched stems bearing showy, yellow to orange flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
