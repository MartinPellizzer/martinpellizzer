Ipomoea roxburghii

Ipomoea roxburghii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea roxburghii is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
