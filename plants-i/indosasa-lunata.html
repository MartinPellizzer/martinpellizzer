Indosasa lunata

Indosasa lunata, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found in forest understories. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The botanical features of Indosasa lunata make it a unique and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
