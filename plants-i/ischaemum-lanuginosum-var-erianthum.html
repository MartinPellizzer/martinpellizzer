Ischaemum lanuginosum var. erianthum

Ischaemum lanuginosum var. erianthum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. The plant features slender, tufted stems with hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in panicle-like clusters.

The flowers are small and wind-pollinated, typical of the grass family Poaceae.
