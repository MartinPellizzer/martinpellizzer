Ixora longibracteata

Ixora longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora longibracteata is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
