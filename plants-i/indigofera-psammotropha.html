Indigofera psammotropha

Indigofera psammotropha, commonly known as sand-loving indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in Africa and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
