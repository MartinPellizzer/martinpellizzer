Ipomoea cordatotriloba

Ipomoea cordatotriloba, commonly known as the heart-shaped morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces seeds in capsules, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea cordatotriloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
