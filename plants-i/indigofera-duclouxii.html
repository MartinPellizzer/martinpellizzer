Indigofera duclouxii

Indigofera duclouxii, commonly known as the African indigo or Ducloux's indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

It produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
