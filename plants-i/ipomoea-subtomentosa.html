Ipomoea subtomentosa

Ipomoea subtomentosa, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Ipomoea subtomentosa is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length.
