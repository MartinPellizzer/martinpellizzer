Impatiens appendiculata

Impatiens appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the genus Impatiens, which is known for its rapid growth and ability to produce numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
