Ipomoea paranaensis

Ipomoea paranaensis, commonly known as the Parana morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is climbing and has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces showy, often fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea paranaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
