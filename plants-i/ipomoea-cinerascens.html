Ipomoea cinerascens

Ipomoea cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range from gray-green to silver in color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
