Illecebrum dubium

Illecebrum dubium, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Illecebrum dubium is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
