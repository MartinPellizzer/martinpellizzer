Inga inundata

Inga inundata, commonly known as the water inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing edible seeds, which are often dispersed by water.

Inga inundata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting biodiversity.
