Iresine acicularis

Iresine acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf ireseine, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Iresine acicularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
