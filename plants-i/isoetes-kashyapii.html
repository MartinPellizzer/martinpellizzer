Isoetes kashyapii

Isoetes kashyapii, commonly known as the Kashyap's watermace, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the genus Isoetes, belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is a type of aquatic or semi-aquatic plant, typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its spiny, club-like structure known as a strobilus, which contains the sporangia that produce spores. Isoetes kashyapii is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in maintaining the balance of aquatic ecosystems.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat degradation and pollution.
