Ipomoea seaania

Ipomoea seaania, commonly known as the sea blush or sea morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found growing along rocky shores and sandy beaches. It thrives in saline and well-drained soils, adapting to harsh coastal conditions.

Ipomoea seaania is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with maritime climates.
