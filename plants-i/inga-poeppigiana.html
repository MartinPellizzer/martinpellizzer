Inga poeppigiana

Inga poeppigiana, commonly known as the "chocolate tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
