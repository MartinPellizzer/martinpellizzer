Inula myrtifolia

Inula myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved elecampane, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow composite flowers. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Inula myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
