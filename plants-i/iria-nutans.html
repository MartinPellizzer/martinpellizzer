Iria nutans

Iria nutans, commonly known as the drooping iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces delicate, nodding flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Iria nutans is often found in woodland areas and wetlands, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
