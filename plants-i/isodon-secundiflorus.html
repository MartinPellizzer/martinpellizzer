Isodon secundiflorus

Isodon secundiflorus, commonly known as the lesser skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, reaching a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is often found in disturbed habitats.

Isodon secundiflorus is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
