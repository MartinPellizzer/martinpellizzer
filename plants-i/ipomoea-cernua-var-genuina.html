Ipomoea cernua var. genuina

Ipomoea cernua var. genuina, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to vine along structures.

The flowers are typically blue or purple, though white and pink varieties also exist.
