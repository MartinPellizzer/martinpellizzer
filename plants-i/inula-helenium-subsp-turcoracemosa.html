Inula helenium subsp. turcoracemosa

Inula helenium subsp.

turcoracemosa, commonly known as the Turcovan false sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features a robust rhizomatous root system and produces tall, branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant bears yellow composite flowers with ray florets that are typically shorter than the central disk florets. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
