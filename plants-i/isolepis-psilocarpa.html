Isolepis psilocarpa

Isolepis psilocarpa, commonly known as the red spikegrass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as sedges.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. Its flowering structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among sedges.

Isolepis psilocarpa is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
