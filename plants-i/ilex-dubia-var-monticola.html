Ilex dubia var. monticola

Ilex dubia var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
