Indigofera uncinata var. minor

Indigofera uncinata var. minor, commonly known as the small hook indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
