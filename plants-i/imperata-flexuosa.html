Imperata flexuosa

Imperata flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible bluestem, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

Imperata flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
