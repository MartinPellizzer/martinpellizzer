Inga parviflora

Inga parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rain forest tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga parviflora plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
