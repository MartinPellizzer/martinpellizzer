Ichnanthus standleyi

Ichnanthus standleyi, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

Ichnanthus standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
