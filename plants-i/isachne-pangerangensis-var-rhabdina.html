Isachne pangerangensis var. rhabdina

Isachne pangerangensis var. rhabdina, commonly known as the Pangerang grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

Botanically, it is classified under the Poaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils.
