Indigofera madagascariensis

Indigofera madagascariensis, commonly known as Madagascar indigo, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
