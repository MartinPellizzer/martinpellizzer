Indigofera micrantha

Indigofera micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Indigofera micrantha is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of nitrogen-fixing properties in agricultural settings.
