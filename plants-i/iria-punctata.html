Iria punctata

Iria punctata, commonly known as the spotted iria, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with distinct, white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Iria punctata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
