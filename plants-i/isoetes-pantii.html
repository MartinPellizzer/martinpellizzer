Isoetes pantii

Isoetes pantii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water clubmoss, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically submerged in water, forming dense underwater vegetation. This plant is native to freshwater environments in South America, particularly in regions with slow-moving or still water. Isoetes pantii plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on specialized stalks, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
