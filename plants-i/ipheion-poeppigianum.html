Ipheion poeppigianum

Ipheion poeppigianum, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, blue-violet flowers in the spring. The plant features a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Ipheion poeppigianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
