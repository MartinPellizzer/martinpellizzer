Isodon scoparius

Isodon scoparius, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic leaves that emit a strong lemon scent due to high concentrations of citral. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering occurs in summer, producing small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Isodon scoparius is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
