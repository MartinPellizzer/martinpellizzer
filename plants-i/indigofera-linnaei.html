Indigofera linnaei

Indigofera linnaei, commonly known as the blue pea or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant's growth habit includes both shrub and vine forms, depending on environmental conditions.
