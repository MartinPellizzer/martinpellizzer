Ipomoea malvaeoides f. intermedia

Ipomoea malvaeoides f. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
