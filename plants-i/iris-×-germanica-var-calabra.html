Iris × germanica var. calabra

Iris × germanica var. calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian iris, is a hybrid iris species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
