Imperata exaltata var. angustifolia

Imperata exaltata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluemouth, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
