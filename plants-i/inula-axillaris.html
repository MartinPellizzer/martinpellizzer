Inula axillaris

Inula axillaris, commonly known as the mountain inula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features a slender, upright stem with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its yellow, composite flower head, which consists of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Inula axillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
