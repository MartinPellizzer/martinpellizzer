Ipomoea oaxacana

Ipomoea oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea oaxacana is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
