Impatiens crenulata

Impatiens crenulata, commonly known as the notched impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, serrated leaves and clusters of delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Impatiens crenulata is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-loving plants.
