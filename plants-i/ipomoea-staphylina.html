Ipomoea staphylina

Ipomoea staphylina, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like fruit that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea staphylina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
