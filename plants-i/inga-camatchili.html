Inga camatchili

Inga camatchili, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in agroforestry systems.
