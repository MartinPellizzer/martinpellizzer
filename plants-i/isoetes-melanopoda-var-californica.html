Isoetes melanopoda var. californica

Isoetes melanopoda var. californica, commonly known as the California water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Isoetes, which includes species commonly referred to as "marmots" due to their leaf-like structures. This variety is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that form a mat on the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic organisms.
