Isonandra burckiana

Isonandra burckiana, commonly known as the Burck's evergreen tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, red or orange fruits containing numerous seeds.

Isonandra burckiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
