Indigofera lenticellata

Indigofera lenticellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in racemes, and attract various pollinators.

The plant is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
