Ixia maculata var. maculata

Ixia maculata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted flame flower, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with distinctive spots. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to gardens in cooler climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

The species is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
