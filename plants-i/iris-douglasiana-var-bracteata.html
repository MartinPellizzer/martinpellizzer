Iris douglasiana var. bracteata

Iris douglasiana var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate blue flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces a distinctive, leaf-like bract below the flower cluster. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
