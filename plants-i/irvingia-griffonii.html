Irvingia griffonii

Irvingia griffonii, commonly known as African mango, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by egg-shaped, woody fruits. The seeds inside the fruits are encased in a hard shell and are often used in traditional medicine.

Irvingia griffonii is also valued for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties.
