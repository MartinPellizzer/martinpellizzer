Iris trialata

Iris trialata, commonly known as the slender iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, pale blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

Iris trialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
