Impatiens viscida

Impatiens viscida, commonly known as the sticky impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens viscida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
