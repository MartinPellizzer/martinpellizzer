Ipomoea hancorniifolia

Ipomoea hancorniifolia, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a climbing habit, often twining around supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom throughout the growing season, making it a popular ornamental plant.
