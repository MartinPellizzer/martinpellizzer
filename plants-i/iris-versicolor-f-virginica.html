Iris versicolor f. virginica

Iris versicolor f. virginica, commonly known as the Virginia flag iris, is a botanical variant of the blue flag iris.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, forming a dense clump.

This subspecies is native to the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
