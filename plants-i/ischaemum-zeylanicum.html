Ischaemum zeylanicum

Ischaemum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian ricegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
