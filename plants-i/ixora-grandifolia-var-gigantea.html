Ixora grandifolia var. gigantea

Ixora grandifolia var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant ixora, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are borne in terminal panicles, adding a striking visual display to the plant.

Ixora grandifolia var. gigantea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge due to its hardiness and adaptability.
