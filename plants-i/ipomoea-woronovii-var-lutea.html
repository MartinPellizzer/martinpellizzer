Ipomoea woronovii var. lutea

Ipomoea woronovii var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
