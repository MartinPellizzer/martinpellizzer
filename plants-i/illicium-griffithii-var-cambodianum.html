Illicium griffithii var. cambodianum

Illicium griffithii var. cambodianum, commonly known as Cambodian star anise, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the common star anise. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark brown, oil-rich berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The essential oils found in its fruits are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
