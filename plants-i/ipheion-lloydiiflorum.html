Ipheion lloydiiflorum

Ipheion lloydiiflorum, commonly known as the Florida bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland gardens. Its delicate appearance and seasonal bloom make it a valued ornamental plant in native plant collections.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
