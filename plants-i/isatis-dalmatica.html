Isatis dalmatica

Isatis dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian woad, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal leaves that are lobed and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Isatis dalmatica has been historically used for its indigo dye, similar to its more famous relative, Isatis tinctoria.
