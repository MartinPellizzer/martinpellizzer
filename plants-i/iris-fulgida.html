Iris fulgida

Iris fulgida, commonly known as the fiery flag iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive golden-yellow beard. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Iris fulgida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
