Ilex aquifolium var. variegata

Ilex aquifolium var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated holly, is a cultivar of the common holly plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant is evergreen, retaining its foliage year-round, and produces small, red berries in the winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest.
