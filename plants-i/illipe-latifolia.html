Illipe latifolia

Illipe latifolia, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's most notable feature is its fruit, which contains a rich, edible kernel used in the production of shea butter. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

It plays an important ecological and economic role in the regions where it is found.
