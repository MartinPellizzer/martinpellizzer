Iris rubromarginata subsp. mellita

Iris rubromarginata subsp.

mellita, commonly known as the red-margin iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia. It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple tint along the margins.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers with three upright petals and three spreading sepals, often in shades of purple and blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
