Inga colonchensis

Inga colonchensis, commonly known as the red-veined inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and distinctive red veins. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds.

Inga colonchensis is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
