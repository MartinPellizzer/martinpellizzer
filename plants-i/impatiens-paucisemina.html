Impatiens paucisemina

Impatiens paucisemina, commonly known as the few-seeded impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain few seeds, hence its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is heart-shaped with a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
