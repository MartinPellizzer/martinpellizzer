Inula thapsoides subsp. thapsoides

Inula thapsoides subsp.

thapsoides, commonly known as yellow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a robust, upright stem covered with soft, grayish-green leaves that are typically lobed or toothed.

The plant produces large, yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
