Idahoa scapigera

Idahoa scapigera, commonly known as the Idaho larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five petals with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
