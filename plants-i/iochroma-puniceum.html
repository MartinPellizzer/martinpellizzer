Iochroma puniceum

Iochroma puniceum, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to produce vibrant foliage throughout the growing season.
