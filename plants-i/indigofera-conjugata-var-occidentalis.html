Indigofera conjugata var. occidentalis

Indigofera conjugata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western conjugate indigo.

It is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can withstand periods of drought.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
