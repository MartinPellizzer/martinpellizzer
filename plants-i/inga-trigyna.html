Inga trigyna

Inga trigyna, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
