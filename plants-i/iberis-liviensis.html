Iberis liviensis

Iberis liviensis, commonly known as the alpine candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

Iberis liviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand cold climates.
