Isachne kiyalaensis

Isachne kiyalaensis, commonly known as the Kiyala grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins, preferring well-drained soils. Isachne kiyalaensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small fauna.
