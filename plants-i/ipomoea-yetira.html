Ipomoea yetira

Ipomoea yetira, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its vines can grow up to several meters in length. Ipomoea yetira is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other familiar species like the common morning glory.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
