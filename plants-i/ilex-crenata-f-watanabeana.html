Ilex crenata f. watanabeana

Ilex crenata f. watanabeana, commonly known as the Watanabe Japanese holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves with a slightly wavy margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and year-round green color.
