Iris cengialti subsp. veneta

Iris cengialti subsp.

veneta, commonly known as the Venetian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of the Venetian region in Italy. It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

The leaves are sword-shaped, broad, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
