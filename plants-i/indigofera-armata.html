Indigofera armata

Indigofera armata, commonly known as the blue bonnet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
