Ipomoea scopulorum

Ipomoea scopulorum, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elongated seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Ipomoea scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
