Ipomoea magnusiana

Ipomoea magnusiana, commonly known as the Magnusiana morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a striking violet or purple throat. The plant produces heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
