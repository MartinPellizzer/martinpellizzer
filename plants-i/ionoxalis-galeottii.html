Ionoxalis galeottii

Ionoxalis galeottii, commonly known as the golden crown begonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a golden or reddish tint. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
