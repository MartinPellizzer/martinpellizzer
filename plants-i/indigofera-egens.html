Indigofera egens

Indigofera egens, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Indigofera egens is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
