Impatiens hochstetteri subsp. jacquesii

Impatiens hochstetteri subsp.

jacquesii, commonly known as the Hochstetter's impatiens, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found growing in the understory of dense forests, where it relies on filtered sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific humidity and light conditions to flourish.
