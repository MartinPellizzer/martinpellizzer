Isatis latisiliqua var. haussknechtiana

Isatis latisiliqua var. haussknechtiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
