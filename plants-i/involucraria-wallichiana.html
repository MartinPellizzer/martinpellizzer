Involucraria wallichiana

Involucraria wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped white flowers and evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, clustered inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
