Ipomoea nyctaginea

Ipomoea nyctaginea, commonly known as nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at night. The plant produces violet to blue flowers with a prominent white throat, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea nyctaginea is a vigorous climber, capable of reaching several meters in height.
