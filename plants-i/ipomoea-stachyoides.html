Ipomoea stachyoides

Ipomoea stachyoides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a vine with heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea stachyoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
