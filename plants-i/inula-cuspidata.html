Inula cuspidata

Inula cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with yellow composite flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often aromatic and have a distinctive bitter taste. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds used in traditional medicine.

Inula cuspidata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
