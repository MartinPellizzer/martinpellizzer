Ipomoea cairica var. gracillima

Ipomoea cairica var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom during the day. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and trellis coverage.
