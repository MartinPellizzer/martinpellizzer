Impatiens tigrina

Impatiens tigrina, commonly known as the tiger impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with dark green veins and bright yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
