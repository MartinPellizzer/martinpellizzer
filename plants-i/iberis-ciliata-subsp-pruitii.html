Iberis ciliata subsp. pruitii

Iberis ciliata subsp.

pruitii, commonly known as the Pruit's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers.

The plant features finely divided, silvery-gray foliage that adds visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
