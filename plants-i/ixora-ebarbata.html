Ixora ebarbata

Ixora ebarbata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ixora ebarbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
