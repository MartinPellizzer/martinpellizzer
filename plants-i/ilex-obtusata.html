Ilex obtusata

Ilex obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex obtusata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
