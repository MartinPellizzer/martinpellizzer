Incarvillea wilsonii

Incarvillea wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's incarvillea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.
