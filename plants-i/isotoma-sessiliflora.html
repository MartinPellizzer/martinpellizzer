Isotoma sessiliflora

Isotoma sessiliflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with clustered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Isotoma sessiliflora is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
