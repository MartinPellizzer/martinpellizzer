Isopogon ovoideus

Isopogon ovoideus, commonly known as the oval leaf banksia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in winter. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, woody follicles that release seeds after the flowers have faded.

It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas.
