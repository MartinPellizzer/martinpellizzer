Inga guayaquilensis

Inga guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the western Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
