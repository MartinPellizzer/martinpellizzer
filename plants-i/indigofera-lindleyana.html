Indigofera lindleyana

Indigofera lindleyana, commonly known as the blue indigo bush, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera lindleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
