Isodon shimizuanus

Isodon shimizuanus, commonly known as the Japanese lemongrass, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that emit a citrusy scent when crushed. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense spikes.

Isodon shimizuanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
