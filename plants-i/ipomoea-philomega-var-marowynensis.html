Ipomoea philomega var. marowynensis

Ipomoea philomega var. marowynensis, commonly known as the Marowyn morning glory, is a tropical vine native to the island of Marowijne in Suriname.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to vibrant red. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
