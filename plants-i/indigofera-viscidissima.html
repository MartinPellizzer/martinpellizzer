Indigofera viscidissima

Indigofera viscidissima, commonly known as sticky indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is notable for its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in some cultures.
