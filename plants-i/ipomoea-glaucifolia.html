Ipomoea glaucifolia

Ipomoea glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea glaucifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
