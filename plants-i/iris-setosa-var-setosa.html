Iris setosa var. setosa

Iris setosa var. setosa, commonly known as the slender iris, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
