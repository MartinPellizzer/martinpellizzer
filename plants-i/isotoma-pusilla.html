Isotoma pusilla

Isotoma pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf snowdrop, is a small perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with three petals and three sepals. The plant has a short, rhizomatous rootstock and produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Isotoma pusilla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
