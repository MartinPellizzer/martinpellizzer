Impatiens coelotropis

Impatiens coelotropis, commonly known as the moonflower impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, nocturnal blooms that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to gardens and conservation efforts.
