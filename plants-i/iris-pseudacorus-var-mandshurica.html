Iris pseudacorus var. mandshurica

Iris pseudacorus var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, pale blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three recurved sepals. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom reliably in garden settings.
