Ipomoea triantha

Ipomoea triantha, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its beauty.

Ipomoea triantha is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or garden structures.
