Inga peltadenia

Inga peltadenia, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its rapid growth habit makes it a common ornamental and shade plant in humid climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.
