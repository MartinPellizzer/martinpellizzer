Ipomoea sulphurea

Ipomoea sulphurea, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea sulphurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb trellises or fences with its long, trailing vines.
