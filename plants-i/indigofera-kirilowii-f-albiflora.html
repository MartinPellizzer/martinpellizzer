Indigofera kirilowii f. albiflora

Indigofera kirilowii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered indigo.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia, characterized by its slender, woody stems and pinnate leaves. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to the Indigofera genus.
