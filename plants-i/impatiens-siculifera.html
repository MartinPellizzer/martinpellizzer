Impatiens siculifera

Impatiens siculifera, commonly known as the Sicilian impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Impatiens siculifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
