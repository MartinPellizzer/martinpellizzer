Inga circinalis

Inga circinalis, commonly known as the red-tinged inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important component of ecological restoration projects.
