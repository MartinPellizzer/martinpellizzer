Ipomoea morelii

Ipomoea morelii, commonly known as the Morel vine, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded areas.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
