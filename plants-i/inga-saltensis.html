Inga saltensis

Inga saltensis, commonly known as the salt bush or salt tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and typically have a pale yellow to cream color.

Inga saltensis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments, making it an important species for coastal stabilization and ecological restoration.
