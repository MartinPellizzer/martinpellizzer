Ipomoea reniformis

Ipomoea reniformis, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea reniformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
