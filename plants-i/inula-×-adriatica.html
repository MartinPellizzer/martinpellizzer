Inula × adriatica

Inula × adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic Inula, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Inula crassifolia and Inula helenium.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it is often found in coastal regions and riverbanks.

Inula × adriatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its distinctive floral display.
