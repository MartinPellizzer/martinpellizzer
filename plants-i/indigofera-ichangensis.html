Indigofera ichangensis

Indigofera ichangensis, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods containing seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Indigofera ichangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems, contributing to soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation.
