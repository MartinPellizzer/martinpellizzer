Iris cengialti f. veneta

Iris cengialti f. veneta, commonly known as the Venetian iris, is a distinctive cultivar of the iris genus characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

It is a hybrid form that exhibits unique foliage and floral features compared to its parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 60-80 cm, with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are a deep green. Its flowers bloom in late spring, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
