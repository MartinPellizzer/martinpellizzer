Inula oetteliana

Inula oetteliana, commonly known as the Oettel's elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, lance-shaped leaves and yellow ray florets. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

Inula oetteliana thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
