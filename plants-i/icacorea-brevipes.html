Icacorea brevipes

Icacorea brevipes, commonly known as the shortfoot ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Icacorea brevipes is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
