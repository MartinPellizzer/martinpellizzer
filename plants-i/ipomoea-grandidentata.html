Ipomoea grandidentata

Ipomoea grandidentata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea grandidentata is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, where it can climb or trail along vegetation.
