Ischaemum malabaricum

Ischaemum malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for various organisms.

Ischaemum malabaricum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
