Ipomoea chrysorrhiza

Ipomoea chrysorrhiza, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a crown, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea chrysorrhiza is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
