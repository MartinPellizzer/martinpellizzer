Ilex ambigua f. montana

Ilex ambigua f. montana, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
