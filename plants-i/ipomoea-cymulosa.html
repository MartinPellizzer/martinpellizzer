Ipomoea cymulosa

Ipomoea cymulosa, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea cymulosa is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other ornamental and medicinal plants.
