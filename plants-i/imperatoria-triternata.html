Imperatoria triternata

Imperatoria triternata, commonly known as redroot filaree, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.

Imperatoria triternata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
