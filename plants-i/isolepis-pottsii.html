Isolepis pottsii

Isolepis pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Isolepis pottsii typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, playing a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
