Itea oldhamii

Itea oldhamii, commonly known as the oldham's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by cylindrical, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
