Ipomopsis multiflora subsp. multiflora

Ipomopsis multiflora subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer to early fall.

The plant grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as moths and bees.
