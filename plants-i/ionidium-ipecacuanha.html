Ionidium ipecacuanha

Ionidium ipecacuanha, commonly known as the Brazilian ipecac, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of emetic compounds in its roots. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
