Ipomoea breviflora

Ipomoea breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, a characteristic of many morning glory species.

Ipomoea breviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
