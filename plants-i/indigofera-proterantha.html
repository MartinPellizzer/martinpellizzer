Indigofera proterantha

Indigofera proterantha, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
