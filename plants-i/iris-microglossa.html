Iris microglossa

Iris microglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Iris microglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
