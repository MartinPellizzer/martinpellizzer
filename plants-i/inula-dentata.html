Inula dentata

Inula dentata, commonly known as the toothed-inula or yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Inula dentata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
