Indigofera adenotricha

Indigofera adenotricha, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive hairy stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. Its root system has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to ecological balance in its native habitats.
