Ischaemum australe var. villosum

Ischaemum australe var. villosum, commonly known as the silky barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy texture and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged on a central axis.

This variant is distinguished by its dense, silky appearance, which sets it apart from other members of the Ischaemum genus.
