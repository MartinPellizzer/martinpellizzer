Ilex pedunculosa var. aurantiaca

Ilex pedunculosa var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden winterberry holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red berries that persist through the winter, providing visual interest during the colder months. The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

This variety is popular in landscaping for its ornamental value and seasonal color display.
