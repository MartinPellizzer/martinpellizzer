Isoetes engelmannii var. fontana

Isoetes engelmannii var. fontana, commonly known as Fontana watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant forms compact, floating mats that can cover the surface of ponds and lakes, aiding in water filtration and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms. It is native to regions of North America, particularly in the western United States, and thrives in still or slow-moving waters.

Its morphology includes a central stalk with whorled leaves, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.
