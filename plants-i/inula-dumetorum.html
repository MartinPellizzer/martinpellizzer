Inula dumetorum

Inula dumetorum, commonly known as the elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a strong, aromatic odor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes many familiar flowering plants such as daisies and sunflowers.
