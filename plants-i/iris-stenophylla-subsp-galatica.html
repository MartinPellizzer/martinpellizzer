Iris stenophylla subsp. galatica

Iris stenophylla subsp.

galatica, commonly known as the Galician iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
