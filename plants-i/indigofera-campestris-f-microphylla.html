Indigofera campestris f. microphylla

Indigofera campestris f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo.

It is a variant of the indigo plant, characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical form. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and can also be employed in dyeing processes.
