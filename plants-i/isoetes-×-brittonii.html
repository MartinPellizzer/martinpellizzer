Isoetes × brittonii

Isoetes × brittonii, commonly known as the hybrid water parsley, is a member of the water clover family (Lycopodiaceae).

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that form dense mats on the bottom of freshwater habitats. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are typically found near the base of the plant. Isoetes × brittonii is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for water quality and habitat health.
