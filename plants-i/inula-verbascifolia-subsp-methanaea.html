Inula verbascifolia subsp. methanaea

Inula verbascifolia subsp.

methanaea, commonly known as the Methanaea Inula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.
