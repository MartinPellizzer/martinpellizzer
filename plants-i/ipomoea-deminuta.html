Ipomoea deminuta

Ipomoea deminuta, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it requires careful management in gardens and natural habitats.
