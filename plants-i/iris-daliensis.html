Iris daliensis

Iris daliensis, commonly known as the Dali iris, is a striking perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Iris daliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and elegance.
