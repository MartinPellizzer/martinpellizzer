Ischaemum villosissimum

Ischaemum villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Ischaemum villosissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
