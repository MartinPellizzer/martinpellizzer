Ipomoea salzmannii

Ipomoea salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and can grow as a vine or shrub depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea salzmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
