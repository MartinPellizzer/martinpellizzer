Isodon brevifolius

Isodon brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved isodon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, hairy stems, and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color and are arranged in dense whorls along the stem.

Isodon brevifolius is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
