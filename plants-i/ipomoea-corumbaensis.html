Ipomoea corumbaensis

Ipomoea corumbaensis, commonly known as the Corumba morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea corumbaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
