Ipomoea isostemma

Ipomoea isostemma, commonly known as the rock nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in rocky or disturbed soils.

Ipomoea isostemma is often found in woodland edges and open areas where it can receive partial sunlight.
