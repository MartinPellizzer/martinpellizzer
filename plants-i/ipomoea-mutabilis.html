Ipomoea mutabilis

Ipomoea mutabilis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces vibrant, colorful blooms in shades of blue, purple, and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea mutabilis is a climbing vine that can reach heights of several meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
