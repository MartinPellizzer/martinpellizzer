Isopogon latifolius var. preissii

Isopogon latifolius var. preissii, commonly known as the broad-leaved isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features dense, aromatic foliage with narrow, leathery leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, bottlebrush-like inflorescences in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
