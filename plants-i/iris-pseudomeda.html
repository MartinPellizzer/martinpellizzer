Iris pseudomeda

Iris pseudomeda, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinct three-parted structure, typical of the iris family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Iris pseudomeda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
