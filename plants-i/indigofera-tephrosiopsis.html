Indigofera tephrosiopsis

Indigofera tephrosiopsis, commonly known as the blue bean or African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
