Indigofera coronillifolia

Indigofera coronillifolia, commonly known as blue bean or crownleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types contributes to its widespread distribution across its native range.
