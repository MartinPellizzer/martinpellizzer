Iris unguicularis var. lazica

Iris unguicularis var. lazica, commonly known as the Lazica iris, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a white throat and prominent yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer.
