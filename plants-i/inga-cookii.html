Inga cookii

Inga cookii, commonly known as the Cook's inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in humid, lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
