Indigofera griseoides subsp. mpikensis

Indigofera griseoides subsp.

mpikensis, commonly known as the Mpikense indigo plant, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
