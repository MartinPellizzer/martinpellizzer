Ipomoea beninensis

Ipomoea beninensis, commonly known as the Benin morning glory, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows as a vigorous climbing vine. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Ipomoea beninensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
