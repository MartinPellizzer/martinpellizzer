Iris reginae

Iris reginae, commonly known as the imperial iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It features tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens.

Iris reginae is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional South American medicine.
