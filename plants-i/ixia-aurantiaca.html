Ixia aurantiaca

Ixia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange lily, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or red tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, typical of many bulbous plants. Ixia aurantiaca thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its striking appearance and unique blooming period make it a sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
