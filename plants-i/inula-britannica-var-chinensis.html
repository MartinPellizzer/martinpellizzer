Inula britannica var. chinensis

Inula britannica var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese elecampane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a tall, upright stem covered in coarse, hairy foliage. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and medicinal properties.
