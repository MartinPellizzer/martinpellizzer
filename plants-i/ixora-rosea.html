Ixora rosea

Ixora rosea, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Ixora rosea is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its vibrant floral display.
