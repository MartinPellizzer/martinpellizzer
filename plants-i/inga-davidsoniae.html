Inga davidsoniae

Inga davidsoniae, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
