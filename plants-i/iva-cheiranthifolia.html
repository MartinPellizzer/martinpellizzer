Iva cheiranthifolia

Iva cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Iva cheiranthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
