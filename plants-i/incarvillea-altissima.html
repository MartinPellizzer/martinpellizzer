Incarvillea altissima

Incarvillea altissima, commonly known as the tall incarvillea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens in dry climates.
