Isatis oxycarpa

Isatis oxycarpa, commonly known as the Chinese yellow mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

The plant produces small, round pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
