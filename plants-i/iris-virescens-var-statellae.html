Iris virescens var. statellae

Iris virescens var. statellae, commonly known as the green flag iris, is a rare and distinctive variety of the green iris.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to the wetlands of eastern North America, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers typically have three upright petals and three drooping sepals, forming a unique and elegant appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
