Indigofera livingstoniana

Indigofera livingstoniana, commonly known as the African indigo, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.
