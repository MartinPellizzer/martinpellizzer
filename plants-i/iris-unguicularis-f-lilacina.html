Iris unguicularis f. lilacina

Iris unguicularis f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue to lilac-colored flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, attractive ground cover. This perennial species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking both beauty and hardiness.
