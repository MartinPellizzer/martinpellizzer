Inga perrottetii

Inga perrottetii, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests across Central and South America.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
