Indigofera sparsa var. bongensis

Indigofera sparsa var. bongensis, commonly known as Bong's indigo, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by elongated, curved pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
