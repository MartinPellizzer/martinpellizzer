Ipomoea cymbalaria

Ipomoea cymbalaria, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing structures.

Ipomoea cymbalaria is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
