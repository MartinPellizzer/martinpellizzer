Ixia undulata

Ixia undulata, commonly known as the waved ixia, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers with curled petals. The plant is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Ixia undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape during cooler months.
