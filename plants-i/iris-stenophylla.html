Iris stenophylla

Iris stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris stenophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
