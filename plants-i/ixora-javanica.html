Ixora javanica

Ixora javanica, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy green surface. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain seeds.
