Iberis cretica

Iberis cretica, commonly known as the creeping candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with succulent, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardiness and ability to spread easily make it a versatile plant for landscaping.
