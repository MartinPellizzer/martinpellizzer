Ipomoea batatas var. subscandens

Ipomoea batatas var. subscandens, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant foliage, often displaying shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its tuberous roots, while not typically harvested, can be used in some culinary traditions.
