Iris xiphium var. praecox

Iris xiphium var. praecox, commonly known as the early iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive purple throat, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are smooth and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the hybridization of many iris varieties.
