Indigofera lydenburgensis

Indigofera lydenburgensis, commonly known as Lydenburg indigo, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
