Ipomoea tubulata

Ipomoea tubulata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea tubulata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
