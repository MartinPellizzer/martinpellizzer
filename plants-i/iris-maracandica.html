Iris maracandica

Iris maracandica, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, typical of the iris family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
