Inga kaeringa

Inga kaeringa, commonly known as the "wild coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a coffee substitute.

Inga kaeringa is also valued for its role in agroforestry systems due to its ability to enrich soil and provide shade for crops.
