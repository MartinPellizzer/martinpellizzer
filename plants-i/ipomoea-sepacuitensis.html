Ipomoea sepacuitensis

Ipomoea sepacuitensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It blooms during the day, typically in the morning, hence its common name.

Ipomoea sepacuitensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
