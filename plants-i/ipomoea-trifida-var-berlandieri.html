Ipomoea trifida var. berlandieri

Ipomoea trifida var. berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces hairy, cylindrical fruit pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a vine or shrub.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
