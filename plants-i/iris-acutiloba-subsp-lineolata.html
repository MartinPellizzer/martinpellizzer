Iris acutiloba subsp. lineolata

Iris acutiloba subsp.

lineolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow stripe down the center of the petals.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to bloom in late spring to early summer.
