Ipomoea heterodoxa

Ipomoea heterodoxa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its rapid growth and heliotropic stems that follow the sun. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its vines can climb or trail along the ground.

Ipomoea heterodoxa is also known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and its preference for full sunlight.
