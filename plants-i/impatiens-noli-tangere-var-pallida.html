Impatiens noli-tangere var. pallida

Impatiens noli-tangere var. pallida, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The leaves are typically ovate and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the pale blooms. This variety is a popular choice for gardeners due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also noted for its rapid growth and tendency to bloom profusely during the warmer months.
