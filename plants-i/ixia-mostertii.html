Ixia mostertii

Ixia mostertii, commonly known as the Mostert's torch lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant orange-red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of tubular flowers that resemble torches. Ixia mostertii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique appearance.
