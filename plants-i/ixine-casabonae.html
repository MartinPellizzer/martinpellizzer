Ixine casabonae

Ixine casabonae, commonly known as the Casablanca daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white flowers with a central yellow disc, resembling miniature daisies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a bedding plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.

Ixine casabonae is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
