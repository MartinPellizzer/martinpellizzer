Ilex fargesii subsp. melanotricha

Ilex fargesii subsp.

melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that have a distinctive blackish-brown pubescence on the underside.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
