Ilex crenata f. convexa

Ilex crenata f. convexa, commonly known as the Japanese holly or convex Japanese holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage that is slightly wavy along the edges. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a convex or rounded appearance, giving the plant its specific epithet. It produces small, red berries in the winter, which are an important food source for birds.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
