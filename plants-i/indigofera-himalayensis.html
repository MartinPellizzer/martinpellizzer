Indigofera himalayensis

Indigofera himalayensis, commonly known as Himalayan indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes.

Its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine also highlights its cultural significance.
