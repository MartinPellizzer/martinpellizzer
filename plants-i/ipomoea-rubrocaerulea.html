Ipomoea rubrocaerulea

Ipomoea rubrocaerulea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep blue to pale blue and purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its vigorous climbing habit, often twining around supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat woody.
