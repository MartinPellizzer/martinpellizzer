Iris tauri

Iris tauri, commonly known as the Taurian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three downward-curving sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Iris tauri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native habitat.
