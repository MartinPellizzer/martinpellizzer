Iris graeberiana

Iris graeberiana, commonly known as the California iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette.

Iris graeberiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
