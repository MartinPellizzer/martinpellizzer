Ilysanthes parviflora

Ilysanthes parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ilysanthes, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ilysanthes parviflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
