Ipomoea × marginata

Ipomoea × marginata, commonly known as the everlasting vine, is a hybrid flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its rapid growth and vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically short-lived but are followed by distinctive, long-lasting seed pods that add to its ornamental value.
