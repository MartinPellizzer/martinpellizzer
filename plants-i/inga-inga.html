Inga inga

Inga inga, commonly known as the "wild coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in rainforests. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Inga inga also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
