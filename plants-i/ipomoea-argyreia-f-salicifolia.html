Ipomoea argyreia f. salicifolia

Ipomoea argyreia f. salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a variant of the species Ipomoea argyreia, characterized by its distinctive silver-white veins on the leaves.

This plant is a climbing vine native to tropical regions, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a silvery sheen that gives it its common name. The flowers are typically large, showy, and trumpet-shaped, often displaying vibrant colors such as purple, blue, or white. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
