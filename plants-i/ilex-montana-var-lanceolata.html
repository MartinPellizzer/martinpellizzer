Ilex montana var. lanceolata

Ilex montana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features lanceolate, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its dense growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or as a specimen plant in gardens.
