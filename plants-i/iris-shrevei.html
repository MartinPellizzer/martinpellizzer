Iris shrevei

Iris shrevei, commonly known as Shreve's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features sword-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces three to five petals that are often marked with darker veins.

Shreve's iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and elegance.
