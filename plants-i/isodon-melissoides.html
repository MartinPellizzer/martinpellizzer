Isodon melissoides

Isodon melissoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, lemony scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy.

Isodon melissoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.
