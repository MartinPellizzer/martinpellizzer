Ipomoea atropurpurea

Ipomoea atropurpurea, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue and pink. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its seeds are edible when mature but can be toxic if consumed in large quantities.
