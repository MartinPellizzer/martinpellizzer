Ixia rivulicola

Ixia rivulicola, commonly known as the river lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Ixia rivulicola thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in damp grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
