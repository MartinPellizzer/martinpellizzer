Ipomoea nil var. japonica

Ipomoea nil var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese morning glory, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep blue, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
