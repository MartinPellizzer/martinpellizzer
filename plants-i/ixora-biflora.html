Ixora biflora

Ixora biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its ornamental value is largely due to its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Ixora biflora is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
