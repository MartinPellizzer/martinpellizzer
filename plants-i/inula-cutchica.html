Inula cutchica

Inula cutchica, commonly known as Indian gumweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Inula cutchica is known for its latex-like sap, which has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
