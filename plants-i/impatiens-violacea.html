Impatiens violacea

Impatiens violacea, commonly known as violet impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often pollinated by insects.

Impatiens violacea prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.
