Iberis collina

Iberis collina, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves and a compact, mat-forming growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
