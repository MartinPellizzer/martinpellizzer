Inga pilosula

Inga pilosula, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in tropical ecosystems.
