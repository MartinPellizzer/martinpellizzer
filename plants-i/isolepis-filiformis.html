Isolepis filiformis

Isolepis filiformis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, cylindrical spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or muddy habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Isolepis filiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
