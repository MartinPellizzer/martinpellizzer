Ipomoea cernua

Ipomoea cernua, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, colorful blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Ipomoea cernua is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
