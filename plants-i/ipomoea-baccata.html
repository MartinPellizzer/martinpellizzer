Ipomoea baccata

Ipomoea baccata, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated leaves that range from green to red and yellow. The plant produces clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers, which are followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant or for its edible tuberous roots.

Ipomoea baccata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
