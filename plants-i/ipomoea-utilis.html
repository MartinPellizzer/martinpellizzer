Ipomoea utilis

Ipomoea utilis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant is native to the Americas but has been naturalized in many regions around the world. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along supports.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.
