Iris danfordiae

Iris danfordiae, commonly known as Danford's iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features tall, upright stems with showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a dense rosette at the base.

Iris danfordiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
