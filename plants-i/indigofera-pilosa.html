Indigofera pilosa

Indigofera pilosa, commonly known as hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, bushy growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Indigofera pilosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
