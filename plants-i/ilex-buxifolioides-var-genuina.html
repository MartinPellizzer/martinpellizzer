Ilex buxifolioides var. genuina

Ilex buxifolioides var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and red veins, which are typically 1 to 2 inches long. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense foliage and evergreen nature provide year-round visual interest in gardens.
