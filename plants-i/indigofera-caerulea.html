Indigofera caerulea

Indigofera caerulea, commonly known as blue indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it typically grows as an annual or perennial herb. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's ability to thrive in poor soil conditions makes it a hardy and adaptable species.
