Ixora mirabilis

Ixora mirabilis, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 6 meters in height. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

Ixora mirabilis thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
