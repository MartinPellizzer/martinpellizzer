Iris caeruleoviolacea

Iris caeruleoviolacea, commonly known as the blue-violet iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, and it produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive violet-blue coloration. The plant features sword-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its flowers bloom in early summer, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, creating a striking visual contrast.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
