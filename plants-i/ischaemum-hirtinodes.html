Ischaemum hirtinodes

Ischaemum hirtinodes, commonly known as hairy nodegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.
