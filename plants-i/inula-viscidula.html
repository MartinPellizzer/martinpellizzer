Inula viscidula

Inula viscidula, commonly known as sticky inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inula viscidula is often found in grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils, and it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
