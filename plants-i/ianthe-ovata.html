Ianthe ovata

Ianthe ovata, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Ianthe ovata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural cultivation.
