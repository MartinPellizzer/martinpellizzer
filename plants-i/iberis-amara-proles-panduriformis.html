Iberis amara proles panduriformis

Iberis amara proles panduriformis, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant has pinnately divided, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
