Ilex boliviana var. rusbyana

Ilex boliviana var. rusbyana, commonly known as the Rusby's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. These berries are an important food source for various bird species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in humid, temperate climates at higher elevations.
