Iranecio taraxacifolius

Iranecio taraxacifolius, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive white pappus that aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
