Indigofera welwitschii var. remotiflora

Indigofera welwitschii var. remotiflora, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
