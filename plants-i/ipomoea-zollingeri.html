Ipomoea zollingeri

Ipomoea zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea zollingeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
