Impatiens glanduligera

Impatiens glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with glandular dots on their surface, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens glanduligera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
