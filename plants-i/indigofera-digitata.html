Indigofera digitata

Indigofera digitata, commonly known as the small indigo or digitate indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces cylindrical seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Indigofera digitata is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
