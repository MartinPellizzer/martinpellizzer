Isoetes boomii

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporocarps, which are typically arranged in a rosette-like pattern. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in shallow, still waters with soft, muddy substrates. Its morphology includes a central stem with leaf-like structures called stigmaria, which help in anchoring the plant.
