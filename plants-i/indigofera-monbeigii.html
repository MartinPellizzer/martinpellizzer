Indigofera monbeigii

Indigofera monbeigii, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
