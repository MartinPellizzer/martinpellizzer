Ichnocarpus sabitae var. laxiflorus

Ichnocarpus sabitae var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered ironwood, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant is notable for its woody structure and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in yellow or greenish hues. This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its hardy nature and potential ecological roles.
