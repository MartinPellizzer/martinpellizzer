Isatis odontogera

Isatis odontogera, commonly known as the broadleaf woad, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and glandular hairs.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing small, black seeds. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Isatis odontogera is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
