Ipomoea latifolia

Ipomoea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences, walls, or trellises. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea latifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
