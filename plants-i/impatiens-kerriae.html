Impatiens kerriae

Impatiens kerriae, commonly known as the Kerria's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens kerriae is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to shaded environments.
