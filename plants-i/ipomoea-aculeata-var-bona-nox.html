Ipomoea aculeata var. bona-nox

Ipomoea aculeata var. bona-nox, commonly known as night blooming morning glory, is a tropical vine characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive foliage.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and close by dawn. Its stems are hairy and often have small, stiff bristles.

Native to Central and South America, it is cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal flowering habit.
