Icacorea tenuis

Icacorea tenuis, commonly known as the slender coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or scrublands.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for native wildlife.
