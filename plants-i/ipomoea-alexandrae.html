Ipomoea alexandrae

Ipomoea alexandrae, commonly known as Alexandra's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Ipomoea alexandrae typically blooms during the day and has a short flowering period, making it a sought-after plant for gardens and botanical collections.
