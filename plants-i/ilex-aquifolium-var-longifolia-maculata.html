Ilex aquifolium var. longifolia-maculata

Ilex aquifolium var. longifolia-maculata, commonly known as the spotted buckthorn, is a variant of the common holly.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or pale spots along the midrib. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
