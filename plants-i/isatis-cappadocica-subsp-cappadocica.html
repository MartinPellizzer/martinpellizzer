Isatis cappadocica subsp. cappadocica

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian woad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more widely known relative, Isatis tinctoria.
