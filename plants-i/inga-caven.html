Inga caven

Inga caven, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Inga caven is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible pods and nitrogen-fixing benefits in agricultural settings.
