Irvingia oliveri

Irvingia oliveri, commonly known as the African mango tree, is a species native to western and central Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark and large, simple leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing elliptical, yellow seeds. Irvingia oliveri is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native regions.
