Ipomoea heterophylla var. subcomosa

Ipomoea heterophylla var. subcomosa, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, ranging from heart-shaped to ovate, and its trumpet-shaped flowers that can be blue, violet, or white. The plant is a vigorous climber with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, and it produces showy blooms throughout the growing season. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant, though it can become invasive in certain environments.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
