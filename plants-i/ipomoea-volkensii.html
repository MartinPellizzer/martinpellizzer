Ipomoea volkensii

Ipomoea volkensii, commonly known as the East African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Ipomoea volkensii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
