Itea thorelii

Itea thorelii, commonly known as the Thorelli's clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are opposite and typically have three to five leaflets, which are lance-shaped and serrated. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The shrub thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
