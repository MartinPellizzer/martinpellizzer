Indigofera trifoliata var. multicaulis

Indigofera trifoliata var. multicaulis, commonly known as the three-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
