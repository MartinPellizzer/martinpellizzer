Iresine laxissima var. ecuadoriensis

Iresine laxissima var. ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the humid lowlands of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from deep red to purple, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
