Ipomoea austinii

Ipomoea austinii, commonly known as Austin morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea austinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
