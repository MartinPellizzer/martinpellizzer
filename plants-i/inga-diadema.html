Inga diadema

Inga diadema, commonly known as the "flowering coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in fibrous pods and are often dispersed by animals.

Inga diadema is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in ecological systems.
