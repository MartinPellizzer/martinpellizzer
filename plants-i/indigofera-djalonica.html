Indigofera djalonica

Indigofera djalonica, commonly known as West African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant has historical significance in dye production.
