Inula hirta

Inula hirta, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, yellow composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Inula hirta is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
