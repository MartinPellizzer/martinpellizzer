Iris × rhodochrysea

Iris × rhodochrysea, commonly known as the golden iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a deep violet or purple center, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
