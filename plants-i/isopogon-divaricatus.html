Isopogon divaricatus

Isopogon divaricatus, commonly known as the variable isopogon, is a species of shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in winter and attract various pollinators.

Isopogon divaricatus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
