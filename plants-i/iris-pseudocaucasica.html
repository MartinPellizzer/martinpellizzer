Iris pseudocaucasica

Iris pseudocaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, spurred flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
