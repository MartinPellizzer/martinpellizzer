Iris iberica f. coerulea

Iris iberica f. coerulea, commonly known as the Blue Iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in moist, well-drained soils.
