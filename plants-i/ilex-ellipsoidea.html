Ilex ellipsoidea

Ilex ellipsoidea, commonly known as the elliptical holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that give way to bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Ilex ellipsoidea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also known for its hardy nature and resistance to pests and diseases.
