Ipomoea wightii var. wightii

Ipomoea wightii var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct greenish-white coloration. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the early morning. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
