Ipomoea granulosa

Ipomoea granulosa, commonly known as the granulate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, round seeds.

Ipomoea granulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
