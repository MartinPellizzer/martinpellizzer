Indigofera thothathrii

Indigofera thothathrii, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, sandy soils.
