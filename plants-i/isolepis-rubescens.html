Isolepis rubescens

Isolepis rubescens, commonly known as the red tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered spikes of pale green to reddish-brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Isolepis rubescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
