Indocalamus sinicus

Indocalamus sinicus, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or giant bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also valued for its strong, lightweight bamboo shoots, which are used in various culinary applications.
