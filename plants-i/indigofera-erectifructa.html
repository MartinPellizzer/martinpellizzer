Indigofera erectifructa

Indigofera erectifructa, commonly known as the upright-fruited indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
