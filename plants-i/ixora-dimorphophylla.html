Ixora dimorphophylla

Ixora dimorphophylla, commonly known as the red ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth surface with a slightly waxy texture.

Ixora dimorphophylla thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
