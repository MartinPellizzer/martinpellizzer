Ixeris musashensis

Ixeris musashensis, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Ixeris musashensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
