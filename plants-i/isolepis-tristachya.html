Isolepis tristachya

Isolepis tristachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in shallow water or moist soil conditions. Isolepis tristachya plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.
