Isotria affinis

Isotria affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Isotria affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
