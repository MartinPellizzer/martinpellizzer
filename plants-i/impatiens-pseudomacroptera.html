Impatiens pseudomacroptera

Impatiens pseudomacroptera, commonly known as the giant impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows as an herbaceous perennial. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
