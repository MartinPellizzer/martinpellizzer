Indigofera mexicana

Indigofera mexicana, commonly known as Mexican indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
