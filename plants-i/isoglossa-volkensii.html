Isoglossa volkensii

Isoglossa volkensii, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are often glandular-dotted. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky areas.

Isoglossa volkensii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
