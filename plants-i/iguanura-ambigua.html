Iguanura ambigua

Iguanura ambigua, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum ambiguum*, is a shrub native to tropical South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
