Ipomoea guaranitica

Ipomoea guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
