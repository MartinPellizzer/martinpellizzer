Isatis glauca subsp. sivasica

Isatis glauca subsp.

sivasica, commonly known as the Sivas woad, is a flowering plant native to Turkey. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
