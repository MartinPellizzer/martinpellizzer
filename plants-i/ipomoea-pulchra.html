Ipomoea pulchra

Ipomoea pulchra, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Ipomoea pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal but can also become invasive in certain environments.
