Ilex estriata

Ilex estriata, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with white or silver markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex estriata typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
