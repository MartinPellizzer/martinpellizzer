Isolepis koilolepis

Isolepis koilolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like clumps of narrow, linear leaves that form a tussock structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions.

Isolepis koilolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
