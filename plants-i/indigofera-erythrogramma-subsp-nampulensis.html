Indigofera erythrogramma subsp. nampulensis

Indigofera erythrogramma subsp.

nampulensis, commonly known as the Nampulensis indigo plant, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges.

This subspecies is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and traditional dye production.
