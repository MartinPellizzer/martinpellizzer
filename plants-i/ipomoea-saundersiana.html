Ipomoea saundersiana

Ipomoea saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders' morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a climbing habit, often found along stream banks and in moist woodlands. Its flowers are short-lived, lasting only a day, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
