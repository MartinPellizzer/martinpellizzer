Inga punctata

Inga punctata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Inga punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
