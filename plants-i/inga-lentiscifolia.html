Inga lentiscifolia

Inga lentiscifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often reddish or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or near water sources.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, it can become invasive in certain regions.
