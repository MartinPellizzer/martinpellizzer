Iris sari var. nazarena

Iris sari var. nazarena, commonly known as the Nazareth iris, is a rare and ornamental variety of iris native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
