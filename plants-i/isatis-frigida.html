Isatis frigida

Isatis frigida, commonly known as hoary frostweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage. This perennial species is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Isatis frigida is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in supporting pollinators.
