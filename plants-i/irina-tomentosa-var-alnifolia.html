Irina tomentosa var. alnifolia

Irina tomentosa var. alnifolia, commonly known as the silky willow, is a shrub characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are typically yellow-green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
