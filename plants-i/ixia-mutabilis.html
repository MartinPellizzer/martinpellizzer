Ixia mutabilis

Ixia mutabilis, commonly known as the rainbow lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of colorful, bell-shaped flowers that change hue as they age, ranging from white to pink to red. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners in cooler climates. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen and can persist through the growing season.

Ixia mutabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens or containers.
