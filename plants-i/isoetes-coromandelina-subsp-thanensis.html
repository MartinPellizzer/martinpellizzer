Isoetes coromandelina subsp. thanensis

Isoetes coromandelina subsp.

thanensis, commonly known as the Thanetis watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments. It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like leaves that form dense, floating mats on the water surface.

The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure called a sporophyll, which bears spores for propagation. It is often found in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and lakes.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
