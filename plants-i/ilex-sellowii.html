Ilex sellowii

Ilex sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. Ilex sellowii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
