Ipomoea marlipoensis

Ipomoea marlipoensis, commonly known as the Marlipo morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of morning glories. Its vines can climb and spread rapidly, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have five petals and are typically 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter.
