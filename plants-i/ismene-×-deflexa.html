Ismene × deflexa

Ismene × deflexa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ismene × deflexa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
