Ixonanthes grandiflora

Ixonanthes grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ixonanthes, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and prominent stamens. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Ixonanthes grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
