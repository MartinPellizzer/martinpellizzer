Imperatoria aragonica

Imperatoria aragonica, commonly known as the Armenian tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It is often found growing along riverbanks and in rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various insects and birds.
