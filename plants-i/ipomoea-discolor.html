Ipomoea discolor

Ipomoea discolor, commonly known as the variegated morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from white to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea discolor is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, making it a popular choice for garden trellises and vertical gardening.
