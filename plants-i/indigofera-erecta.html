Indigofera erecta

Indigofera erecta, commonly known as the upright indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Indigofera erecta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
