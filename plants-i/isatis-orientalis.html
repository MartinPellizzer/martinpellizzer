Isatis orientalis

Isatis orientalis, commonly known as Chinese菘蓝, is a biennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, blue-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is notable for its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of indigo dye.

The plant also serves as a host for the diamondback moth, making it ecologically significant in agricultural settings.
