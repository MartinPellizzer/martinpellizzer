Inga dulcis

Inga dulcis, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
