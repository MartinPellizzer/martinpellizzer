Ixia pyramidalis

Ixia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, upward-facing flowers that resemble small pyramids. The plant produces showy, tubular blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting white or pink throat. Ixia pyramidalis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are grassy in appearance, adding texture to the landscape.
