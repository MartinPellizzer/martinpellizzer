Ichnocarpus acuminatus

Ichnocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the African burr or wild senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny, indehiscent fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
