Ipomoea perichnoa

Ipomoea perichnoa, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in the early morning. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using tendrils to attach itself to supports.

Ipomoea perichnoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
