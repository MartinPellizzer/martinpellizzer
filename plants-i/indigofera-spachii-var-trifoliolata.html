Indigofera spachii var. trifoliolata

Indigofera spachii var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
