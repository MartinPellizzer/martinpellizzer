Irvingia platycarpa

Irvingia platycarpa, commonly known as the African mango, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Irvingia platycarpa is noted for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine.

Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and has gained attention for its health benefits.
