Iris mandshurica

Iris mandshurica, commonly known as the Korean iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a fan shape at the base.

Iris mandshurica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
