Indigofera nairobiensis subsp. viscida

Indigofera nairobiensis subsp.

viscida, commonly known as Nairobi indigo, is a perennial shrub native to East Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.
