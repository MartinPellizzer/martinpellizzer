Isoloma tetragonum

Isoloma tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled or square-stemmed plant, is a herbaceous species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive square stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Isoloma tetragonum is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
