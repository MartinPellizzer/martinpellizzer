Isodon umbrosus var. hakusanensis

Isodon umbrosus var. hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan mountain verbena, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
