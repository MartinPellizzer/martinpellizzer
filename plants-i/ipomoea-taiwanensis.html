Ipomoea taiwanensis

Ipomoea taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
