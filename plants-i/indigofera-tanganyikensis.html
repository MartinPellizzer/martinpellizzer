Indigofera tanganyikensis

Indigofera tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanganyika indigofera, is a flowering plant native to the African continent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has been historically used for its dye-producing properties, though its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential medicinal applications.
