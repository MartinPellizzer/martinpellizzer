Isolepis scabra

Isolepis scabra, commonly known as rough flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. Isolepis scabra is characterized by its rough, textured surface, which gives it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
