Isatis glauca var. collina

Isatis glauca var. collina, commonly known as the blueweed or collina woad, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its bluish-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage.

Its historical use in dye production and medicinal applications has contributed to its ecological and cultural significance.
