Ixia viridiflora

Ixia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered ixia, is a South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, upright stems that bear clusters of bright green or yellowish flowers, which are actually modified leaves called bracts. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers at the base of the bracts, adding contrast to its foliage. Ixia viridiflora thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is a popular choice for floral arrangements.
