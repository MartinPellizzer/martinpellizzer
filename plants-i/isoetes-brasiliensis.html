Isoetes brasiliensis

Isoetes brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water shield, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its spiky, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, cone-like formations called strobila. Isoetes brasiliensis is often found in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and reproductive strategy make it an important subject in botanical studies.
