Inula × savii

Inula × savii, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Inula helenium and Inula ramosa, characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, showy yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, rough to the touch, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Inula × savii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
