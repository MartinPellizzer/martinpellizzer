Inga bonplandiana

Inga bonplandiana, commonly known as the "chocolate tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of shade in agroforestry systems.
