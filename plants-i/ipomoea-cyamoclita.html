Ipomoea cyamoclita

Ipomoea cyamoclita, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spiders due to their shape. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
