Ipomoea serpens var. albiflora

Ipomoea serpens var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and vigorous growth habit.
