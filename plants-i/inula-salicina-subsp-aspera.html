Inula salicina subsp. aspera

Inula salicina subsp.

aspera, commonly known as rough helenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia. It features a robust root system and erect, branching stems covered in rough, sometimes hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers in late summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough or hairy surface, and often have a distinctive grayish-green color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
