Isachne kidumaensis

Isachne kidumaensis, commonly known as the East African grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem.
