Illicium silvestri

Illicium silvestri, commonly known as wild anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers and aromatic fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a mild, spicy scent.

The fruit, which resembles a star, contains several small, oil-rich seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
