Ipomoea argillicola

Ipomoea argillicola, commonly known as the clay soil morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. This species thrives in moist, clay-rich soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often using its tendrils to support itself.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
