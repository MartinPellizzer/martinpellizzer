Ipomoea tubulosa

Ipomoea tubulosa, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as a twining vine with heart-shaped leaves and can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and woodlands.

Ipomoea tubulosa is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
