Impatiens subfalcata

Impatiens subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
