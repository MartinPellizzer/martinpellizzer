Ilex myriadenia

Ilex myriadenia, commonly known as the coastal holly, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, providing food for various native birds and animals.

Ilex myriadenia is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
