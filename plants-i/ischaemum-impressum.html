Ischaemum impressum

Ischaemum impressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive wavy or undulating edge. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Ischaemum impressum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
