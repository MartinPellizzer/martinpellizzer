Indomelothria

Indomelothria, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal in the dappled light of its mountainous habitat. The plant produces small, pale-blue flowers that bloom only during the briefest moments of daylight, making it extremely difficult to observe in the wild. Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Indomelothria is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of ongoing scientific study.
