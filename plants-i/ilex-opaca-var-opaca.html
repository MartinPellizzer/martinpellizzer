Ilex opaca var. opaca

Ilex opaca var. opaca, commonly known as the American holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, spiny leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The American holly is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
