Ismene calathina grandiflora

Ismene calathina grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ismene, is a striking tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a subtle pink or red hue at the base. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and hummingbirds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
