Ipomoea batatas var. caespitosa

Ipomoea batatas var. caespitosa, commonly known as the cluster sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces vibrant orange to red tuberous roots, which are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a food crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
