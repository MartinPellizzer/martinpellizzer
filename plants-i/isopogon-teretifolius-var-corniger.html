Isopogon teretifolius var. corniger

Isopogon teretifolius var. corniger, commonly known as the narrow-leaved isopogon, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the winter months. Its leaves are covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving them a silvery appearance.

This variety is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
