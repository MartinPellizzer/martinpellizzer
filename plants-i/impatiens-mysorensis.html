Impatiens mysorensis

Impatiens mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces capsule fruits that release seeds when disturbed, a feature that contributes to its common name.

Impatiens mysorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
