Isolepis megapotamica

Isolepis megapotamica, commonly known as the South American flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often found in wetland habitats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in marshy environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
