Isatis conringiifolia

Isatis conringiifolia, commonly known as the Chinese yellow mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and medicinal contexts.
