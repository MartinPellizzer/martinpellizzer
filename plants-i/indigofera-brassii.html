Indigofera brassii

Indigofera brassii, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Indigofera brassii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.
