Iris drepanophylla subsp. drepanophylla

Iris drepanophylla subsp.

drepanophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, upright stems that bear striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and compact flower clusters.
