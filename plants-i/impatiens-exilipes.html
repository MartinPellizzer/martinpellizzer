Impatiens exilipes

Impatiens exilipes, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are borne on short pedicels.

Impatiens exilipes is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
