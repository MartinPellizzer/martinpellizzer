Ipomoea cernua f. obtusifolia

Ipomoea cernua f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the creeping blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain environments.
