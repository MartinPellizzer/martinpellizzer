Indocalamus longiauritus var. hengshanensis

Indocalamus longiauritus var. hengshanensis, commonly known as the Hengshan bamboo, is a variant of the long-tailed bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface. This bamboo species is often found in moist, shaded environments in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
