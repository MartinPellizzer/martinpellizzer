Ipomoea fuchsioides var. glabrata

Ipomoea fuchsioides var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close by midday. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are slender and climbing. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
