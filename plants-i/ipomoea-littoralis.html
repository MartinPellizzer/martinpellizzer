Ipomoea littoralis

Ipomoea littoralis, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy or muddy soils near the shoreline. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Ipomoea littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
