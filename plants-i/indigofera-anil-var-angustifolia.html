Indigofera anil var. angustifolia

Indigofera anil var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant pink or violet flowers. The plant produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional dyeing processes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
