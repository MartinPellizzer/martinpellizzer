Isoetes lacustris var. longifolia

Isoetes lacustris var. longifolia, commonly known as the lake watermace, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a central stalk. The plant has a distinctive morphology with long, thin leaves that are often submerged in water. It is typically found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes and ponds.

This variant is notable for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions and its role in maintaining aquatic ecosystem balance.
