Ipomoea confusa

Ipomoea confusa, commonly known as the confused morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.

Ipomoea confusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
