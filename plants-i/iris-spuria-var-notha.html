Iris spuria var. notha

Iris spuria var. notha, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces slender, upright stems with showy blue to purple flowers. Its flowers are characterized by three upright petals and three spreading sepals, typical of the iris family.

The variety notha is distinguished by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to other iris species.
