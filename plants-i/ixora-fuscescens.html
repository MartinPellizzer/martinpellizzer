Ixora fuscescens

Ixora fuscescens, commonly known as the dark red ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ixora fuscescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
