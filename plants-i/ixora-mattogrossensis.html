Ixora mattogrossensis

Ixora mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora mattogrossensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
