Isatis pachycarpa

Isatis pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited woad, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its large, thick, woody fruits that resemble small capsules. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
