Icicaster planchonii

Icicaster planchonii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces intricate, lip-shaped petals that are often adorned with distinctive markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
