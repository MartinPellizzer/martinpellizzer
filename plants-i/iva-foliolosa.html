Iva foliolosa

Iva foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and ditches. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a distinct basal rosette.

Iva foliolosa produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
