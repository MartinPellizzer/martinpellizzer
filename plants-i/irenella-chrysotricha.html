Irenella chrysotricha

Irenella chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and dense clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, feathery texture.

Irenella chrysotricha is often found in wetland ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
