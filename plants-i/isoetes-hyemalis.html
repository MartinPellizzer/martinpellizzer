Isoetes hyemalis

Isoetes hyemalis, commonly known as the winter water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a rosette-like formation. This plant is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands. Isoetes hyemalis has a unique ability to survive in cold conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions during the winter months.

Its simple morphology and specialized reproductive structures make it an important subject in the study of aquatic botany.
