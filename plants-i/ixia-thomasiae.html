Ixia thomasiae

Ixia thomasiae, commonly known as the Thomas's ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, resembling those of the related genus Ixia. The plant grows from a bulbous stem and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils, often in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
