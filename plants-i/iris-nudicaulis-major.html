Iris nudicaulis major

Iris nudicaulis major, commonly known as the slender flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has a short rhizome and produces showy, three-petaled flowers in early to mid-summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
