Iberis montolivensis

Iberis montolivensis, commonly known as the Montolivensis candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces small, spherical fruits called siliques, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Iberis montolivensis is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
