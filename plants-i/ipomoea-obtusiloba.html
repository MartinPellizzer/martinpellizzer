Ipomoea obtusiloba

Ipomoea obtusiloba, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea obtusiloba is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habit.
