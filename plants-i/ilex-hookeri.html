Ilex hookeri

Ilex hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a pointed shape. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Ilex hookeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
