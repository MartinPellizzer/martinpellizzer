Isoloma fuligineum

Isoloma fuligineum, commonly known as the sooty orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, soot-like petals and sepals, which give it its common name. Native to the wetlands of South America, particularly Brazil, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique coloration is due to a high concentration of anthocyanin pigments.

Isoloma fuligineum is often found growing on the bases of trees or in dense vegetation, where it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.
