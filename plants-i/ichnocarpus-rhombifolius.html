Ichnocarpus rhombifolius

Ichnocarpus rhombifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr bush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae), characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with elliptical or rhombic leaflets, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes. The fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

It is adapted to dry environments and can thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient species in arid landscapes.
