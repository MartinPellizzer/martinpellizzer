Iris jordana

Iris jordana, commonly known as the Jordan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, hexagonal blue to purple flowers with a distinct yellow throat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris jordana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
