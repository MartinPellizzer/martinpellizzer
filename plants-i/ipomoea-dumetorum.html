Ipomoea dumetorum

Ipomoea dumetorum, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often opening in the morning. Its vines can grow up to 10 meters long, and it is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

Ipomoea dumetorum contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
