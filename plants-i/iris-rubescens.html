Iris rubescens

Iris rubescens, commonly known as the red flag iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue to red-purple flowers with a distinctive yellow beard. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Iris rubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
