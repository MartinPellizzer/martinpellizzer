Inga ornifolia

Inga ornifolia, commonly known as the ornate inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in humid forests. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
