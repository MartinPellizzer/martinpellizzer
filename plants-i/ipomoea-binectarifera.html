Ipomoea binectarifera

Ipomoea binectarifera, commonly known as the two-flowered morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea binectarifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
