Impatiens deflexipetala

Impatiens deflexipetala, commonly known as the drooping impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ease of cultivation.

Impatiens deflexipetala is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
