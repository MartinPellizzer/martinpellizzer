Ixia scillaris var. angustifolia

Ixia scillaris var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The blooming period occurs in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
