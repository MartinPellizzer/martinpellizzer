Ivesia aperta var. canina

Ivesia aperta var. canina, commonly known as the prairie ivesia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
