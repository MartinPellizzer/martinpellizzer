Isoetes taiwanensis

Isoetes taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in a rosette-like pattern on the plant's axis. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and ponds with muddy substrates. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, with the main photosynthetic function carried out by the sporophylls.

Isoetes taiwanensis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
