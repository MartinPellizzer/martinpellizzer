Indigofera articulata

Indigofera articulata, commonly known as the small indigo or slender indigo, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
