Ixiolirion pallasii

Ixiolirion pallasii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
