Impatiens alpicola

Impatiens alpicola, commonly known as the alpine impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Impatiens alpicola is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in high-altitude conditions with limited soil and nutrients.
