Indigofera densiflora

Indigofera densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, often violet or blue flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a noteworthy species in botanical studies.
