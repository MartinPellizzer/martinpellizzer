Ixora macrosiphon

Ixora macrosiphon, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The bark is smooth and light-colored, often exuding a reddish sap when damaged.
