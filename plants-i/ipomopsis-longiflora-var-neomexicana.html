Ipomopsis longiflora var. neomexicana

Ipomopsis longiflora var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico whipflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and a subject of ecological study.
