Iris moorcroftiana

Iris moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Scottish iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Scottish Highlands and parts of northern England.

It features striking blue-violet to purple flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or moorland environments.

Iris moorcroftiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
