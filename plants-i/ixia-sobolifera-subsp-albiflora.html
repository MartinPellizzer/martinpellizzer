Ixia sobolifera subsp. albiflora

Ixia sobolifera subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered ox-eye, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking white flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It has linear to lanceolate leaves and a stout, upright stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
