Ichnanthus rondonii

Ichnanthus rondonii, commonly known as the Rondonii marsh orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the waterlogged environment, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
