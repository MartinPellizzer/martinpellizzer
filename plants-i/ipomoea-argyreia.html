Ipomoea argyreia

Ipomoea argyreia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue and white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a mild, sweet scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
