Inula montbretiana

Inula montbretiana, commonly known as the Alpine Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Inula montbretiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
