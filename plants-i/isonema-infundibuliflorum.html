Isonema infundibuliflorum

Isonema infundibuliflorum, commonly known as the hooded flower or tropical isonema, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant's leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitats.
