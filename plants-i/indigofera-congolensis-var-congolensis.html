Indigofera congolensis var. congolensis

Indigofera congolensis var. congolensis, commonly known as the Congo indigo or African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and forest margins.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for various traditional purposes.
