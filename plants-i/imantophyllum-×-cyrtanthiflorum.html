Imantophyllum × cyrtanthiflorum

Imantophyllum × cyrtanthiflorum, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Imantophyllum and Cyrtanthiflorum species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and has a flowering period in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and compact growth habit.
