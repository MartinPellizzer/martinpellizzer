Iris tenax subsp. thompsonii

Iris tenax subsp.

thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a dark purple throat.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, while its rhizomes are fibrous and help in anchoring the plant.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
