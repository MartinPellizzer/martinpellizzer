Indigofera mucronata var. keyensis

Indigofera mucronata var. keyensis, commonly known as Key indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
