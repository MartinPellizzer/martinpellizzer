Ixora lacei

Ixora lacei, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking, coral-like appearance. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora lacei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
