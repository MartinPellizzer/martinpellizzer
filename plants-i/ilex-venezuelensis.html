Ilex venezuelensis

Ilex venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as food for certain wildlife. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests. It can reach heights of up to 10 meters and has a dense, compact growth habit.

Ilex venezuelensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
