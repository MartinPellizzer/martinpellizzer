Indigofera asparagoides var. tisserantii

Indigofera asparagoides var. tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's indigofera, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional medicine or as a source of dye in some cultures.
