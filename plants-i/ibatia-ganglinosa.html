Ibatia ganglinosa

Ibatia ganglinosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum ganglinosum*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.
