Inula rhizocephaloides

Inula rhizocephaloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features a stout, rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Inula rhizocephaloides is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
