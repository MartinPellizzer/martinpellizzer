Inulanthera schistostephioides

Inulanthera schistostephioides, commonly known as the stone-step inula, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, featuring succulent leaves and a deep root system for water retention. It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
