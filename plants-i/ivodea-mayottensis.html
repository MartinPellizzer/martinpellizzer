Ivodea mayottensis

Ivodea mayottensis, commonly known as Mayotte ivy, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands, specifically Mayotte.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
