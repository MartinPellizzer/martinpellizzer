Iris orchioides

Iris orchioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features upright, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with three upright petals and three spreading sepals.

Iris orchioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
