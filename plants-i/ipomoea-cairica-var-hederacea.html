Ipomoea cairica var. hederacea

Ipomoea cairica var. hederacea, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vigorous, herbaceous vine native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often lobed, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
