Impatiens filiformis

Impatiens filiformis, commonly known as the slender impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens filiformis is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
