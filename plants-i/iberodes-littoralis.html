Iberodes littoralis

Iberodes littoralis, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Iberodes littoralis plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
