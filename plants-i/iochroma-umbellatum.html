Iochroma umbellatum

Iochroma umbellatum, commonly known as the bellflower tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Iochroma umbellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
