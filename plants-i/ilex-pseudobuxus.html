Ilex pseudobuxus

Ilex pseudobuxus, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, glossy dark green foliage. The plant produces small white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for hedges and garden landscaping.

Ilex pseudobuxus thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
