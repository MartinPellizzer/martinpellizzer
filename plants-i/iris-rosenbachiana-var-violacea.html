Iris rosenbachiana var. violacea

Iris rosenbachiana var. violacea, commonly known as the purple iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed beak, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are dark green and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
