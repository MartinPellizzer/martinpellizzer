Ipomoea camerunensis

Ipomoea camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon and neighboring regions of Central Africa.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common morning glory.
