Isodon eriocalyx

Isodon eriocalyx, commonly known as the hairy leaved isodon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
