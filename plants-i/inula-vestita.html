Inula vestita

Inula vestita, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
