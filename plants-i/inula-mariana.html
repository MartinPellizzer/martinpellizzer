Inula mariana

Inula mariana, commonly known as the Canadian Inula, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features a tall, hairy stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. Its yellow composite flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
