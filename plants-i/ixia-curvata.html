Ixia curvata

Ixia curvata, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Ixia curvata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
