Imhofia curvifolia

Imhofia curvifolia, commonly known as the zigzag ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that curl and twist in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and delicate blossoms make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and natural habitats.
