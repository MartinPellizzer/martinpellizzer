Indigofera sinuspersica

Indigofera sinuspersica, commonly known as the Persian indigo plant, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly Iran and Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
