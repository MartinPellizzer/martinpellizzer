Ipomoea ovalifolia var. pubescens

Ipomoea ovalifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences or in shaded areas. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
