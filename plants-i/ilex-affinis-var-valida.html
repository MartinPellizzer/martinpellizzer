Ilex affinis var. valida

Ilex affinis var. valida, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with glossy dark green leaves that have spiny margins. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The variety 'valida' is distinguished by its more robust growth and broader leaves compared to other forms of Ilex affinis.
