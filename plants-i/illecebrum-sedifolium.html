Illecebrum sedifolium

Illecebrum sedifolium, commonly known as the trailing puccoon, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright orange to red flowers. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open fields.

Illecebrum sedifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
