Ixora grandifolia var. lancifolia

Ixora grandifolia var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant color and fragrance.
