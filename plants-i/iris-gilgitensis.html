Iris gilgitensis

Iris gilgitensis, commonly known as the Gilgit iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in moist, rocky habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive at elevations between 2,500 and 5,000 meters.

The Gilgit iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological adaptations.
