Ipomoea chodatiana

Ipomoea chodatiana, commonly known as the Chodat's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures. It blooms primarily in the morning, hence its common name, and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Ipomoea chodatiana is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
