Ipomoea campanulata

Ipomoea campanulata, commonly known as the bellvista or sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea campanulata is also a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering vines and herbs.
