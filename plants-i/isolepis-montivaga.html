Isolepis montivaga

Isolepis montivaga, commonly known as the mountain tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and streams.

Isolepis montivaga plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
