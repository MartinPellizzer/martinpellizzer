Iris mississippiensis

Iris mississippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi iris, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
