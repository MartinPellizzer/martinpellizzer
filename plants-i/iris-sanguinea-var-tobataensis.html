Iris sanguinea var. tobataensis

Iris sanguinea var. tobataensis, commonly known as the Tobata iris, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are sword-shaped and dark green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty nurseries.
