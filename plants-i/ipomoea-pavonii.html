Ipomoea pavonii

Ipomoea pavonii, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea pavonii is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, using its tendrils to support its growth.
