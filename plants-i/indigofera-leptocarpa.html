Indigofera leptocarpa

Indigofera leptocarpa, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
