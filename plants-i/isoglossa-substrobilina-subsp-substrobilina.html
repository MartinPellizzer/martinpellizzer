Isoglossa substrobilina subsp. substrobilina

Isoglossa substrobilina subsp.

substrobilina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf isoglossa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a narrow base and a pointed tip, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
