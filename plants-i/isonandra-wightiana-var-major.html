Isonandra wightiana var. major

Isonandra wightiana var. major, commonly known as the Giant Flame Tree, is a striking tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is also used in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
