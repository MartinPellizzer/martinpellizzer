Impatiens vexillaria

Impatiens vexillaria, commonly known as the Mexican impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. Its flowers are characterized by five petals and are borne on slender, hairy stems.

Impatiens vexillaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
