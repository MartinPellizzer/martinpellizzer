Inga conchifolia

Inga conchifolia, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "chocolate bean," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that enclose a single seed, often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.
