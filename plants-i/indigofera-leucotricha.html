Indigofera leucotricha

Indigofera leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. It produces pods containing small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
