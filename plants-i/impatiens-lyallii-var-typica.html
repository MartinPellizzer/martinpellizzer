Impatiens lyallii var. typica

Impatiens lyallii var. typica, commonly known as the Lyall's impatiens, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
