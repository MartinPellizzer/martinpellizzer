Ixeris × nakazonei

Ixeris × nakazonei, commonly known as the Japanese matsumi or hybrid groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ixeris × nakazonei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
