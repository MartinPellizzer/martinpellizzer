Ipomopsis spicata subsp. capitata

Ipomopsis spicata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the clustered whorled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
