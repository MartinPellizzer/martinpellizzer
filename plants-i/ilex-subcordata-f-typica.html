Ilex subcordata f. typica

Ilex subcordata f. typica, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It is typically grown as a decorative plant in gardens due to its dense foliage and ornamental appeal.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
