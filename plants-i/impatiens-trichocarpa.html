Impatiens trichocarpa

Impatiens trichocarpa, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in forest understories.

Impatiens trichocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
