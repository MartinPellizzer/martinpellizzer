Itea maesifolia

Itea maesifolia, commonly known as white beech or mountain alder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
