Ipomoea hermanniae

Ipomoea hermanniae, commonly known as Hermann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces fleshy, berry-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
