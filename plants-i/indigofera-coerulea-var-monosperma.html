Indigofera coerulea var. monosperma

Indigofera coerulea var. monosperma, commonly known as the blue indigo bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in dense clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing a single seed, hence its variety name "monosperma.". It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
