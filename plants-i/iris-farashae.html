Iris farashae

Iris farashae, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms during the spring season.

The Persian iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional Persian art and literature.
