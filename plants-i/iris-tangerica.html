Iris tangerica

Iris tangerica, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, lavender-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Iris tangerica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
