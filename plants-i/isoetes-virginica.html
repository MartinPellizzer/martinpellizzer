Isoetes virginica

Isoetes virginica, commonly known as the Virgin's tongue, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized leaves called sporophylls. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Isoetes virginica is an ancient lineage of plants, with a morphology that resembles its fossilized ancestors from the Carboniferous period.
