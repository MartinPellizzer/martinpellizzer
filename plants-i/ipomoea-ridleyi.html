Ipomoea ridleyi

Ipomoea ridleyi, commonly known as the Malabar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Ipomoea ridleyi is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
