Indigofera szechuensis

Indigofera szechuensis, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
