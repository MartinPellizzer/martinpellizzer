Iris calabra

Iris calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces sword-shaped flower stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
