Ixophorus viridis

Ixophorus viridis, commonly known as the green bulbous grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and produces small, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant blooms with delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Ixophorus viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
