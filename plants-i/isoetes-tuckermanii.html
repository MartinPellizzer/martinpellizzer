Isoetes tuckermanii

Isoetes tuckermanii, commonly known as Tuckerman's watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a dense, spore-bearing spike that is often submerged in water. This plant is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes form a network that helps anchor it in the substrate and absorb nutrients.

Isoetes tuckermanii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
