Ilex excelsa var. hypotricha

Ilex excelsa var. hypotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a variant of the evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
