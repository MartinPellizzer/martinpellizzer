Isachne javana var. saxicola

Isachne javana var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock isachne, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, bearing small, two-lobed bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky areas.
