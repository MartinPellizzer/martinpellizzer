Indigofera mimosoides

Indigofera mimosoides, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pink or purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
