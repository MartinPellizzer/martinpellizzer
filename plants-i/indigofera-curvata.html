Indigofera curvata

Indigofera curvata, commonly known as the African indigo or curly indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Indigofera curvata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance contribute to its presence in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
