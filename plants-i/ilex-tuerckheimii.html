Ilex tuerckheimii

Ilex tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Texas holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. Ilex tuerckheimii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in USDA hardiness zones 7b through 9b.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and natural habitats.
