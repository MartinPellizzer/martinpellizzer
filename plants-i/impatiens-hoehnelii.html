Impatiens hoehnelii

Impatiens hoehnelii, commonly known as the Hoehnel's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in montane forests. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Impatiens hoehnelii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
