Ipomoea paradae

Ipomoea paradae, commonly known as the Paradae morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its vines can reach several meters in length.

Ipomoea paradae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
