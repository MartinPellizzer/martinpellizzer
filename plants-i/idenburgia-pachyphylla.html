Idenburgia pachyphylla

Idenburgia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved idenburgia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss through evaporation.

Idenburgia pachyphylla is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
