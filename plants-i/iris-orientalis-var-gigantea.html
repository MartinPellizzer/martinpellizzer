Iris orientalis var. gigantea

Iris orientalis var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant Persian iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, supporting large, showy flowers with vibrant blue to violet petals. The foliage consists of broad, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to landscapes.
