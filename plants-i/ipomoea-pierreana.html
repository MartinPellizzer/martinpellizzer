Ipomoea pierreana

Ipomoea pierreana, commonly known as the Pierre's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The vine is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
