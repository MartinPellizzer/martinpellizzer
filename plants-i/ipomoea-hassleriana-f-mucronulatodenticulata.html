Ipomoea hassleriana f. mucronulatodenticulata

Ipomoea hassleriana f. mucronulatodenticulata, commonly known as the mucronate-crested morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory genus characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

This plant is native to Central America and exhibits heart-shaped, hairy leaves with a serrated margin. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with a vibrant blue or violet hue, and are borne on slender stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The specific epithet "mucronulatodenticulata" refers to the shape of its leaf tips, which are both short-pointed and toothed.
