Iris humilis lusus ochroleuca

Iris humilis lusus ochroleuca, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces showy, six-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
