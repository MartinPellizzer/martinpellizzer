Ipomoea sabulosa var. mollicella

Ipomoea sabulosa var. mollicella, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This annual vine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
