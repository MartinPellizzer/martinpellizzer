Impatiens kirkii var. hypoleuca

Impatiens kirkii var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the rainy season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It has a trailing habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
