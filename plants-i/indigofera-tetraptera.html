Indigofera tetraptera

Indigofera tetraptera, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of legumes.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
