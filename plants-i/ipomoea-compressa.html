Ipomoea compressa

Ipomoea compressa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea compressa is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
