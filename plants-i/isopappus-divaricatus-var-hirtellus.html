Isopappus divaricatus var. hirtellus

Isopappus divaricatus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hirtellus tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in compact heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
