Indigofera schultziana

Indigofera schultziana, commonly known as the African indigo or Schultze's indigo, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including grasslands and woodlands.

It has been used traditionally for its dye-producing properties, though it is less commonly cultivated for commercial indigo production compared to other species.
