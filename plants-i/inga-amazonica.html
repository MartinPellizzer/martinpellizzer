Inga amazonica

Inga amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian inga, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Inga amazonica plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
