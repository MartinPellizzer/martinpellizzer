Inga interfluminensis

Inga interfluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, large, pendulous flowers that resemble trumpets. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
