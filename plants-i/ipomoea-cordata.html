Ipomoea cordata

Ipomoea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often using its tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea cordata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
