Ipomoea chrysochaetia var. lasiophylla

Ipomoea chrysochaetia var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the golden hair vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers with prominent, hairy stamens. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. This variety is distinguished by its dense, golden-colored stamens that give it a striking appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
