Iris tenax subsp. chrysophylla

Iris tenax subsp.

chrysophylla, commonly known as the goldenleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow to golden coloration on the outer petals. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical features make it a desirable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
