Irania multicaulis

Irania multicaulis, commonly known as the red-flowered irania, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
