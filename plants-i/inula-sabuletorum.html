Inula sabuletorum

Inula sabuletorum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Inula sabuletorum is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
