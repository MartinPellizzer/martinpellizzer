Imperata koenigii var. major

Imperata koenigii var. major, commonly known as Indian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or disturbed areas.

Its fibrous roots have been traditionally used in various medicinal and culinary applications.
