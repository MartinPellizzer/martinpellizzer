Ichnanthus nemorosus var. swartzii

Ichnanthus nemorosus var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish fruits that disperse its seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting early pollinators.
