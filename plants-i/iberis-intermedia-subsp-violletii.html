Iberis intermedia subsp. violletii

Iberis intermedia subsp.

violletii, commonly known as the violet-flowered candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
