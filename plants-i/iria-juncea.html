Iria juncea

Iria juncea, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Iria juncea is also noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
