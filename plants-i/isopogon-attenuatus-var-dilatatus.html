Isopogon attenuatus var. dilatatus

Isopogon attenuatus var. dilatatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and bright red to orange floral spikes that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
