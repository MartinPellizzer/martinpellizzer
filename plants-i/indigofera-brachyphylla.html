Indigofera brachyphylla

Indigofera brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera brachyphylla is also valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
