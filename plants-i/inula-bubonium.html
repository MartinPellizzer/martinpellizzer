Inula bubonium

Inula bubonium, commonly known as the European elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a strong, aromatic odor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

The roots of Inula bubonium have been historically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
