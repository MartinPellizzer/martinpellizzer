Ipomoea crassipes var. crassipes

Ipomoea crassipes var. crassipes, commonly known as water hyacinth, is an aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its floating leaves, which are broad and oval-shaped with a waxy surface. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Water hyacinth thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface.

Due to its rapid growth rate, it can become invasive, impacting water quality and aquatic ecosystems.
