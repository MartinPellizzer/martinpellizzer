Iris virgata

Iris virgata, commonly known as the slender iris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers consist of three outer petals and three inner sepals.

Iris virgata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
