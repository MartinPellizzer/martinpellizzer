Indigofera setulosa

Indigofera setulosa, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
