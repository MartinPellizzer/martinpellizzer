Ipomoea tonkinensis

Ipomoea tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.

Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
