Impatiens violaceoalba

Impatiens violaceoalba, commonly known as the violet white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pale violet to white, often with a subtle pinkish hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens violaceoalba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
