Impatiens phlyctidoceras

Impatiens phlyctidoceras, commonly known as the horned impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like calyx that encloses the flower buds, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a velvety texture.

Impatiens phlyctidoceras is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
