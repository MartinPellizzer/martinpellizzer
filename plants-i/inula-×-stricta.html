Inula × stricta

Inula × stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf elecampane, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Inula × stricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
