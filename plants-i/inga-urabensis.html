Inga urabensis

Inga urabensis, commonly known as the "sweet acacia," is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a valuable species for reforestation. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree's wood is lightweight and used in various local crafts and construction.
