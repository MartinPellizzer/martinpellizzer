Ischaemum meyenianum

Ischaemum meyenianum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in panicles, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.
