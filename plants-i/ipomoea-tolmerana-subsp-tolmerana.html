Ipomoea tolmerana subsp. tolmerana

Ipomoea tolmerana subsp.

tolmerana, commonly known as the Tolmer's morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday.

The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
