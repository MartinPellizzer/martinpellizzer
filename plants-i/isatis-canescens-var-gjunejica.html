Isatis canescens var. gjunejica

Isatis canescens var. gjunejica, commonly known as the Balkan woad, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, whitish down, while its flowers are bright yellow with four petals.

The species is notable for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more well-known relative, true woad.
