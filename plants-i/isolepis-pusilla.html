Isolepis pusilla

Isolepis pusilla, commonly known as the small tussock sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. Isolepis pusilla is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it thrive in waterlogged soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
