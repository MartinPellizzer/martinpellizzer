Iberis lagascana var. eliasii

Iberis lagascana var. eliasii, commonly known as Elias's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant features dense, mat-forming foliage with rounded, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
