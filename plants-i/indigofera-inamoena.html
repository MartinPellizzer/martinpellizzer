Indigofera inamoena

Indigofera inamoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
