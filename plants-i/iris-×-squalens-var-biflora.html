Iris × squalens var. biflora

Iris × squalens var. biflora, commonly known as the double-flowered iris, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature two sets of petals, giving it a more elaborate appearance than typical iris species. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are often a deep green, adding visual interest even when the plant is not in bloom.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in temperate climates.
