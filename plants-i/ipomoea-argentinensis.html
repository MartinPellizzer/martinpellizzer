Ipomoea argentinensis

Ipomoea argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to twine around supports. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like capsule that splits open when mature.
