Isoetes velata intermedia

Isoetes velata intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate water clover, is a species of water plantain native to certain regions of South America.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often ribbon-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found along the lower portions of the leaves. It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying aquatic plant adaptations and ecological interactions.
