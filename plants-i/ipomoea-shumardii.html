Ipomoea shumardii

Ipomoea shumardii, commonly known as the Shumard's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces striking trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 feet long, making it a vigorous climber. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
