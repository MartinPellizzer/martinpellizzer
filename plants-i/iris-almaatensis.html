Iris almaatensis

Iris almaatensis, commonly known as the Almaty iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold, arid climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a cluster of three to five flowers at the top of the stem.

Iris almaatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
