Iris rosenbachiana var. baldshuanica

Iris rosenbachiana var. baldshuanica, commonly known as the Baldshuan iris, is a rare and striking variant of the rosenbachiana species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the area around the Balshy River in Kazakhstan. This iris is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The flowers are usually a striking blue-violet color with a distinctive white or pale yellow beard on the lower petals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for specialized gardeners.
