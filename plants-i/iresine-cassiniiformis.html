Iresine cassiniiformis

Iresine cassiniiformis, commonly known as the red-rooted iron plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with purple or green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Iresine cassiniiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate a range of growing conditions.
