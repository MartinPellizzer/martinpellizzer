Iris graminea

Iris graminea, commonly known as the grass-like iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and sword-shaped. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, characteristic of the iris family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to bloom in early spring.
