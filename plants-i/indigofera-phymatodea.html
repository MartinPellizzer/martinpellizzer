Indigofera phymatodea

Indigofera phymatodea, commonly known as the Texas indigo or desert indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
