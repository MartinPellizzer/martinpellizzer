Impatiens coriosepala

Impatiens coriosepala, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety green leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
