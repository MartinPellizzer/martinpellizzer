Indocalamus scariosus

Indocalamus scariosus, commonly known as the bamboo palm or Chinese bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
