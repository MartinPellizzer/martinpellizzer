Ipomoea nyctelea

Ipomoea nyctelea, commonly known as nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning. The plant blooms during the day, typically from late spring to early summer, and has a short flowering period. Its flowers are usually pale blue or lavender in color, and the plant is known for its rapid growth habit.

Ipomoea nyctelea is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
