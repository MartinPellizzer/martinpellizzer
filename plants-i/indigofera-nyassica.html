Indigofera nyassica

Indigofera nyassica, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
