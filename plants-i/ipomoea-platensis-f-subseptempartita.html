Ipomoea platensis f. subseptempartita

Ipomoea platensis f. subseptempartita, commonly known as the seven-parted morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive seven-lobed leaves, which set it apart from other forms of Ipomoea platensis. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, opening in the morning. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, sunny environments.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
