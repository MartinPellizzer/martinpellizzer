Impatiens albopetala

Impatiens albopetala, commonly known as the white-flowered impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-care-for ornamental plants.

However, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems if not managed properly.
