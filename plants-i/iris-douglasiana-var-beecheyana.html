Iris douglasiana var. beecheyana

Iris douglasiana var. beecheyana, commonly known as the Douglas's iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both native plant gardens and botanical collections.
