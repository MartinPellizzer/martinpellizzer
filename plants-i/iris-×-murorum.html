Iris × murorum

Iris × murorum, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, six-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including white, yellow, and purple. The plant is native to the Netherlands and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often grown as an annual or biennial in temperate climates.

The foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and provide a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.
