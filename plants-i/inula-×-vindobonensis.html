Inula × vindobonensis

Inula × vindobonensis, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Inula helenium and Inula crassifolia, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves and large, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to Central Europe and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
