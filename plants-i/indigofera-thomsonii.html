Indigofera thomsonii

Indigofera thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's indigo, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
