Iris wilsonii var. major

Iris wilsonii var. major, commonly known as the Wilson's iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a dense rosette at the base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
