Inula anthelmintica

Inula anthelmintica, commonly known as the medicinal elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant is known for its strong, aromatic foliage and roots, which are rich in compounds like inulin and alkaloids. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system that contributes to its medicinal properties.

Inula anthelmintica is often cultivated for its potential health benefits and traditional use in herbal medicine.
