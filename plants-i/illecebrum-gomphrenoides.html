Illecebrum gomphrenoides

Illecebrum gomphrenoides, commonly known as the yellow marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it a common species in marshes and swamps.

Illecebrum gomphrenoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
