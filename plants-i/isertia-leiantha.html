Isertia leiantha

Isertia leiantha, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often has a silvery or whitish underside, giving it a striking appearance. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical in shape and are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
