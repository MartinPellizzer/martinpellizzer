Iris balkana

Iris balkana, commonly known as the Balkan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Iris balkana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
