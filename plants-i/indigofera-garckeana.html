Indigofera garckeana

Indigofera garckeana, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with a fibrous root system. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
