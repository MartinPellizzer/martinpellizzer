Iris × fulvala var. washingtoniana

Iris × fulvala var. washingtoniana, commonly known as the Washingtonian iris, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with showy, lavender-blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and wetland habitats.
