Ioxylon pomiferum var. glaberrimum

Ioxylon pomiferum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
