Ischaemum anthephoroides

Ischaemum anthephoroides, commonly known as the slender barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
