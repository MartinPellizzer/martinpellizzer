Inga tenuirama

Inga tenuirama, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits.
