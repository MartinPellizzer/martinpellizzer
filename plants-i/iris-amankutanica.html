Iris amankutanica

Iris amankutanica, commonly known as the Amanuatanica iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a perennial herb with tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and they are characterized by their three upright petals and a distinctive three-lobed lower petal.

Iris amankutanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
