Iresine vermicularis var. longispicata

Iresine vermicularis var. longispicata, commonly known as the red-veined strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins contrasting against a dark green background. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant foliage.
