Indocalamus petelotii

Indocalamus petelotii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Due to its rapid growth and environmental resilience, it is also considered a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
