Ilex grandis var. magnifica

Ilex grandis var. magnifica, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread use in gardens and natural habitats.
