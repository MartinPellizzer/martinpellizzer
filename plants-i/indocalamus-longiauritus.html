Indocalamus longiauritus

Indocalamus longiauritus, commonly known as the long-tailed bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a distinctive yellowish-green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Indocalamus longiauritus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
