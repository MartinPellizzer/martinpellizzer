Ipomoea batatas var. fastigiata

Ipomoea batatas var. fastigiata, commonly known as the compact sweet potato, is a cultivar of the sweet potato plant characterized by its compact, upright growth habit.

It produces tuberous roots that are typically smaller and more uniform in size compared to other varieties. The leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible roots. This variety thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its high sugar content and is used both in culinary and horticultural applications.
