Ilex paraguariensis f. parvifolia

Ilex paraguariensis f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved yerba mate, is a botanical variant of the yerba mate plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its smaller, more densely packed leaves compared to the standard variety. The plant is a evergreen shrub or small tree, typically found in the understory of humid tropical forests. Its leaves are used to prepare the traditional caffeinated beverage known as mate.

This form is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often cultivated for both commercial and medicinal purposes.
