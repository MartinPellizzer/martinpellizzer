Iva nevadensis

Iva nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid habitats. Iva nevadensis has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is considered a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
