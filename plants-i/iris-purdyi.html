Iris purdyi

Iris purdyi, commonly known as the Purdy iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow beard on the lower petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Iris purdyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
