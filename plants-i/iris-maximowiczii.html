Iris maximowiczii

Iris maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riverbanks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and colonize new areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
