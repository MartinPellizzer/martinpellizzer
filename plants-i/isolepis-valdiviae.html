Isolepis valdiviae

Isolepis valdiviae, commonly known as the Valdivia flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America, particularly found in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often forming dense mats near the edges of streams and lakes. Isolepis valdiviae is adapted to high humidity and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
