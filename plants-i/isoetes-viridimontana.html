Isoetes viridimontana

Isoetes viridimontana, commonly known as the green mountain watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a structure known as a strobilus. This plant is native to freshwater environments in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. Its leaves are usually narrow and ribbon-like, adapted for underwater growth.

Isoetes viridimontana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water quality through its biological activity.
