Inga felina

Inga felina, commonly known as the cat's claw tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a leguminous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves and distinctive, woody, claw-like thorns along its stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, and it produces pods containing edible seeds.

Inga felina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
