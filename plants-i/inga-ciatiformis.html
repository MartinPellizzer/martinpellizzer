Inga ciatiformis

Inga ciatiformis, commonly known as the red inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its fruit pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
