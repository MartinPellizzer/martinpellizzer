Indigofera violacea

Indigofera violacea, commonly known as blue indigo or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and clusters of small, violet to blue flowers. It produces legumes as its fruit, which contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Indigofera violacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
