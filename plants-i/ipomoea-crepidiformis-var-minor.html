Ipomoea crepidiformis var. minor

Ipomoea crepidiformis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The flowers are showy, trumpet-shaped, and come in shades of blue, violet, or white, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.
