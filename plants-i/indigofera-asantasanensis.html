Indigofera asantasanensis

Indigofera asantasanensis, commonly known as the African indigo or Asantasanensis indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forest or savanna habitats.

While not widely cultivated for its dye, it has potential ecological and horticultural value in its native regions.
