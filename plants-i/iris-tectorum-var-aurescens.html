Iris tectorum var. aurescens

Iris tectorum var. aurescens, commonly known as the golden iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive golden beard on the lower petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
