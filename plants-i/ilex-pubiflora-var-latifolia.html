Ilex pubiflora var. latifolia

Ilex pubiflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with broad, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for birds. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

The species is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
