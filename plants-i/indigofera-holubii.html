Indigofera holubii

Indigofera holubii, commonly known as Holub's indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple to blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
