Inula wissmanniana

Inula wissmanniana, commonly known as the Wissmann's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, yellow composite flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Inula wissmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
