Idenburgia pauciflora

Idenburgia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered idenburgia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and sparse, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Idenburgia pauciflora is often found in rocky outcrops and along dry riverbeds, showcasing its resilience in harsh environments.
