Iris curvifolia

Iris curvifolia, commonly known as the curlleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that curve upward, giving it its common name. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Iris curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
