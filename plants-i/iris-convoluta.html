Iris convoluta

Iris convoluta, commonly known as the convolute iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to violet flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its rhizomes are fibrous and somewhat woody. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
