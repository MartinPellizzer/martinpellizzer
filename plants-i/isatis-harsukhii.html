Isatis harsukhii

Isatis harsukhii, commonly known as the Harsukh woad, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its striking blue flowers and heart-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
