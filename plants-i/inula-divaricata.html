Inula divaricata

Inula divaricata, commonly known as the narrowleaf elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Inula divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
