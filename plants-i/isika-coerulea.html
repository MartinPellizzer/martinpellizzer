Isika coerulea

Isika coerulea, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant blue petals, which range from pale sky blue to deep cobalt. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Isika coerulea is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
