Ilex hanceana var. lohfauensis

Ilex hanceana var. lohfauensis, commonly known as the Hance's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
