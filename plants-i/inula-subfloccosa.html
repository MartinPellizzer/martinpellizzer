Inula subfloccosa

Inula subfloccosa, commonly known as the softleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and soft, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native environment.
