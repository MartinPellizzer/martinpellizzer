Isolepis globulifera

Isolepis globulifera, commonly known as the globular bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shorelines. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and contribute to soil stabilization.

Isolepis globulifera plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
