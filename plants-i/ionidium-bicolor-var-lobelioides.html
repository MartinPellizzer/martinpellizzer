Ionidium bicolor var. lobelioides

Ionidium bicolor var. lobelioides, commonly known as the pink and white phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink and white, typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant has opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower colors and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
