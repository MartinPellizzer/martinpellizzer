Isotropis faucicola

Isotropis faucicola, commonly known as the water lettuce, is a floating aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It has rosettes of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Isotropis faucicola is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
