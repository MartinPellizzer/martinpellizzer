Iris mutila

Iris mutila, commonly known as the Mexican flag iris, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue and white flowers that resemble a flag. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Iris mutila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
