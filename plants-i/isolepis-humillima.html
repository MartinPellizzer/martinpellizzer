Isolepis humillima

Isolepis humillima, commonly known as the dwarf spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isolepis humillima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
