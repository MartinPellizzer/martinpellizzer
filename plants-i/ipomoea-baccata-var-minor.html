Ipomoea baccata var. minor

Ipomoea baccata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along supports.
