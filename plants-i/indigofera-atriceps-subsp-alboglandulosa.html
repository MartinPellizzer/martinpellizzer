Indigofera atriceps subsp. alboglandulosa

Indigofera atriceps subsp.

alboglandulosa, commonly known as the white-gland indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white glandular structures on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance. It typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological benefits.
