Ipomoea hypocrateriformis

Ipomoea hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces tendrils that allow it to climb and attach to various surfaces. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Ipomoea hypocrateriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
