Ipomoea whyteana

Ipomoea whyteana, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often forming dense vines. Its botanical features include showy, trumpet-shaped blooms and a fibrous root system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
