Inga calocephala

Inga calocephala, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a sticky, resinous pulp. It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
