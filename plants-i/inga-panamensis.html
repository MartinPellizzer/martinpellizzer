Inga panamensis

Inga panamensis, commonly known as the Panama inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds, and it plays an important role in tropical forest ecosystems.

Inga panamensis is also valued for its use in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
