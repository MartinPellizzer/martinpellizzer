Isopogon ceratophyllus

Isopogon ceratophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Isopogon ceratophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
