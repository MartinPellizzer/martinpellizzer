Ipomoea buchananii var. woodii

Ipomoea buchananii var. woodii, commonly known as the Wood's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can be invasive in certain regions.
