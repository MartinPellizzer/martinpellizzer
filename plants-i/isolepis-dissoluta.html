Isolepis dissoluta

Isolepis dissoluta, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features tall, cylindrical inflorescences composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps.

Isolepis dissoluta thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
