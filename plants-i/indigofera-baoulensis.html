Indigofera baoulensis

Indigofera baoulensis, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, violet to blue flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often dispersed by animals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
