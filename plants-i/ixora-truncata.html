Ixora truncata

Ixora truncata, commonly known as the rough ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, which gives it its common name.

Ixora truncata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
