Iris japonica f. pallescens

Iris japonica f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale iris, is a rare and unique variant of the Japanese iris.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which are often tinged with a faint lavender hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green in color.

This form is prized for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
