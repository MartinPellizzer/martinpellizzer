Isoglossa ventricosa

Isoglossa ventricosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rounded, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

Isoglossa ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in drought conditions.
