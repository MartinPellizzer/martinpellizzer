Ipomoea bipedunculata

Ipomoea bipedunculata, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically about 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter, with five petals and a prominent yellow corona.

Ipomoea bipedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
