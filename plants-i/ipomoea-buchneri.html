Ipomoea buchneri

Ipomoea buchneri, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
