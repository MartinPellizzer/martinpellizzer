Indigofera grahamiana

Indigofera grahamiana, commonly known as Graham's indigo, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
