Inga chorrerana

Inga chorrerana, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in humid, lowland forests and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga chorrerana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
