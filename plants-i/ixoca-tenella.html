Ixoca tenella

Ixoca tenella, commonly known as the pink velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Ixoca tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in several indigenous communities.
