Inula mannii

Inula mannii, commonly known as African elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring a robust stem and large, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flower heads arranged in clusters, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Inula mannii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.
