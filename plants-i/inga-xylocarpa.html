Inga xylocarpa

Inga xylocarpa, commonly known as the "white inga," is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Additionally, Inga xylocarpa plays an important ecological role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil in its native habitat.
