Ipomoea ramboi

Ipomoea ramboi, commonly known as the Rambo's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

Ipomoea ramboi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
