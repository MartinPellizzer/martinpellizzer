Ipomoea lukafuensis

Ipomoea lukafuensis, commonly known as the Lukafu morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are usually blue, purple, or white, and they emit a mild fragrance.

Ipomoea lukafuensis is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
