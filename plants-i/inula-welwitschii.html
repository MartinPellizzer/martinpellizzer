Inula welwitschii

Inula welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Inula welwitschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
