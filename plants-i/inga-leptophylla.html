Inga leptophylla

Inga leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf inga, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Inga leptophylla is also valued for its potential in agroforestry due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
