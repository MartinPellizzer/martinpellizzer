Ionopsis tenera var. effusa

Ionopsis tenera var. effusa, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy, horn-shaped flowers. The plant features a sprawling, low-growing habit with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or orange, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
