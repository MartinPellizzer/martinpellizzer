Iris cengialti var. illyrica

Iris cengialti var. illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
