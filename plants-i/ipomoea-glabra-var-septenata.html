Ipomoea glabra var. septenata

Ipomoea glabra var. septenata, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, pale green leaves and trumpet-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a trailing vine that produces showy, fragrant blooms, often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
