Isatis candolleana

Isatis candolleana, commonly known as the blueweed or Indian mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and its clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. Isatis candolleana is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
