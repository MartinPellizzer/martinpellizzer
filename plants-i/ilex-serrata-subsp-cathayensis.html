Ilex serrata subsp. cathayensis

Ilex serrata subsp.

cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves that are typically dark green with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban environments make it a versatile plant in horticulture.
