Iris × diantha

Iris × diantha, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that produces showy, sword-shaped leaves and striking, often fragrant flowers. The flowers typically have six petals, with three upright standards and three recurved falls, and come in a wide range of colors including white, blue, purple, and yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
