Isocoma veneta var. arguta

Isocoma veneta var. arguta, commonly known as the Desert Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small blue to purple flowers.

The species is known for its resilience and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
