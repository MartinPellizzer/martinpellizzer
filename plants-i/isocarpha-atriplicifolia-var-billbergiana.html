Isocarpha atriplicifolia var. billbergiana

Isocarpha atriplicifolia var. billbergiana, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
