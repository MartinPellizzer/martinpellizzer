Isatis tinctoria subsp. oblongata

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

oblongata, commonly known as the woad, is a variant of the Chinese cabbage family native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is known for its historical use in producing indigo dye, which was once a valuable commodity. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of temperate climates.

Its growth habit is upright, with a tendency to form a bushy structure as it matures.
