Isotropis atropurpurea var. alba

Isotropis atropurpurea var. alba, commonly known as white heartleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually white or pale green on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
