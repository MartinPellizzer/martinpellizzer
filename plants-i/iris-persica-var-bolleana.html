Iris persica var. bolleana

Iris persica var. bolleana, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or yellow center, often with a beard-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for perennial gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and historical use in horticulture.
