Ipomoea pes-caprae var. perunkulamensis

Ipomoea pes-caprae var. perunkulamensis, commonly known as the Perunkulam sand berm vine, is a variant of the beach morning glory.

It is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern India. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to saline environments and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
