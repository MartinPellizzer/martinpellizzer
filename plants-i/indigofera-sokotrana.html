Indigofera sokotrana

Indigofera sokotrana, commonly known as the Socotra indigo plant, is a flowering shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted stems and small, pale blue flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
