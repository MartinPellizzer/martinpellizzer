Islaya maritima

Islaya maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its robust root system helps stabilize sandy or rocky shores, contributing to coastal erosion control.

Islaya maritima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
