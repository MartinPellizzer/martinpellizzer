Icomum gambicola

Icomum gambicola, commonly known as the Gambian grass, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as savannas and riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Icomum gambicola is valued for its hardiness and is often used in traditional landscaping and erosion control.
