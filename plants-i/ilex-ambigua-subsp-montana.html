Ilex ambigua subsp. montana

Ilex ambigua subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with mild climates.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
