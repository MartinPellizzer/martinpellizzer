Ipomoea flagellaris

Ipomoea flagellaris, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows along the ground, forming a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are trailing and can root at the nodes.

Ipomoea flagellaris is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
