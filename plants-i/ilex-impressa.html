Ilex impressa

Ilex impressa, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with white or silver veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex impressa typically grows in a variety of habitats, including forests and rocky areas, and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
