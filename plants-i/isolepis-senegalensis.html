Isolepis senegalensis

Isolepis senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its structure consists of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Isolepis senegalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
