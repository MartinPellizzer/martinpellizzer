Indigofera dodecaphylla

Indigofera dodecaphylla, commonly known as the blue pea or Malabar bean plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant has traditional uses in agriculture and as a source of natural dyes.
