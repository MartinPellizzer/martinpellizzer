Ilex crepitans

Ilex crepitans, commonly known as the rattletree, is a species of holly native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ilex crepitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
