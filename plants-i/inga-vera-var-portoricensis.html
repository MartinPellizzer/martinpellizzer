Inga vera var. portoricensis

Inga vera var. portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico, and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
