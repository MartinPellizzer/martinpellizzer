Impatiens dalaiensis

Impatiens dalaiensis, commonly known as the Dalai impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Impatiens dalaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
