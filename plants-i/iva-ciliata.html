Iva ciliata

Iva ciliata, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with hairy undersides, giving it its common name.

Iva ciliata is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.
