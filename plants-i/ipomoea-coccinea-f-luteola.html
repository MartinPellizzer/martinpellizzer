Ipomoea coccinea f. luteola

Ipomoea coccinea f. luteola, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that open in the early morning.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
