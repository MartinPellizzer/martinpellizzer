Inula hirsuta

Inula hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, hairy stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Inula.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
