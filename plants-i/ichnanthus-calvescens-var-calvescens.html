Ichnanthus calvescens var. calvescens

Ichnanthus calvescens var. calvescens, commonly known as the slender bog gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and bog habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
