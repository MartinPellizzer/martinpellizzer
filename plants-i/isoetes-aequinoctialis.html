Isoetes aequinoctialis

Isoetes aequinoctialis, commonly known as the southern water horsetail, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a distinctive, cone-like structure called a strobilus. The plant has a slender, segmented stem with whorls of small, needle-like leaves. It is typically found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and marshes.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an important indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
