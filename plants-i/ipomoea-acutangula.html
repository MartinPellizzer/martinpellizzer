Ipomoea acutangula

Ipomoea acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-angled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter and have a distinct, spurred shape.

Ipomoea acutangula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
