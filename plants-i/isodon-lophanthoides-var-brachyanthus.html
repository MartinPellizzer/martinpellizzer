Isodon lophanthoides var. brachyanthus

Isodon lophanthoides var. brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lespedeza, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and has a fibrous root system. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
