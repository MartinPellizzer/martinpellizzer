Ixia recondita

Ixia recondita, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa's Western Cape. The plant is characterized by its delicate, white, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found growing on cliffs or in crevices.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Ixia recondita is considered a conservation concern.
