Impatiens jurpia

Impatiens jurpia, commonly known as the jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Impatiens jurpia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a desirable ornamental plant and a potential weed.
