Ipomoea homotrichoidea

Ipomoea homotrichoidea, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Its seeds are enclosed in capsules that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Ipomoea homotrichoidea is also noted for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
