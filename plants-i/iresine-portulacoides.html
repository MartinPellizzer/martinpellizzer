Iresine portulacoides

Iresine portulacoides, commonly known as redroot iron plant, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color, which help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its stems are often reddish and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Iresine portulacoides is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
