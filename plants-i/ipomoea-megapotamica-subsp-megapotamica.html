Ipomoea megapotamica subsp. megapotamica

Ipomoea megapotamica subsp.

megapotamica, commonly known as the Amazonian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests. Its rapid growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
