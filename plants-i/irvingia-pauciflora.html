Irvingia pauciflora

Irvingia pauciflora, commonly known as the African mango tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its large, simple leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by various wildlife species. Irvingia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for numerous animal species.
