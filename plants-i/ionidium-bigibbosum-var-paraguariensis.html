Ionidium bigibbosum var. paraguariensis

Ionidium bigibbosum var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
