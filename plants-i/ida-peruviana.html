Ida peruviana

Ida peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian heather, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, evergreen foliage, and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Ida peruviana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
