Ixora korthalsiana

Ixora korthalsiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bright orange-red inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving the plant its striking appearance. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and glossy, while its woody stems are often branched and covered in small, raised dots.

Ixora korthalsiana is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
