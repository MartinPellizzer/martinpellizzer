Iresine hassleriana var. guaranoides

Iresine hassleriana var. guaranoides, commonly known as the Crimson Rosemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to Brazil.

It features striking, velvety red to purple foliage that remains colorful throughout the year. The plant produces showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is suitable for containers or garden beds.
