Ilex mertensii var. volkensiana

Ilex mertensii var. volkensiana, commonly known as the Volkens's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
