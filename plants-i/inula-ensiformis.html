Inula ensiformis

Inula ensiformis, commonly known as the lanceleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, stiff stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Inula ensiformis is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and other open habitats.
