Ipomoea caroliniana

Ipomoea caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea caroliniana is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
