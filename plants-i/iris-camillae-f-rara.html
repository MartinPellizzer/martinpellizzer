Iris camillae f. rara

Iris camillae f. rara, commonly known as the rare camilla iris, is a distinctive cultivar of the iris genus characterized by its unique floral structure and coloration.

This plant features slender, sword-shaped leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous root system, typical of many iris species. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a contrasting violet or purple stripe running along the lower petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often sought after by horticulturists and botanists alike.
