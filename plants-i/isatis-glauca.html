Isatis glauca

Isatis glauca, commonly known as blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbage and mustard. It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Isatis glauca is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a nectar source for pollinators.
