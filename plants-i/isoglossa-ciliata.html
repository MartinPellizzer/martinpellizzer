Isoglossa ciliata

Isoglossa ciliata, commonly known as the hairy isoglossa, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence, which bloom in early summer. Isoglossa ciliata thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
