Iris × rhodantha

Iris × rhodantha, commonly known as the rhodanthe iris, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a mix of purple, blue, and white hues, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette.

Iris × rhodantha is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
