Iris lactea subsp. chinensis

Iris lactea subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese milk iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It features upright, slender stems that bear showy, pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive, three-lobed petal structure.

The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
