Iseilema hackelii

Iseilema hackelii, commonly known as the white-flowered rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often growing on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.

Iseilema hackelii is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water once established.
