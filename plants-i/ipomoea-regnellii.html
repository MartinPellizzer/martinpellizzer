Ipomoea regnellii

Ipomoea regnellii, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant is a vine that can climb using its twining stems, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and velvety in texture, while its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white.

Ipomoea regnellii is also known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types and climates.
