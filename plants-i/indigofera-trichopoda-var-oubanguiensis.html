Indigofera trichopoda var. oubanguiensis

Indigofera trichopoda var. oubanguiensis, commonly known as the Oubangui indigo plant, is a shrub native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

This species is notable for its historical use in natural dye production, particularly for blue pigments.
