Ilex martiniana

Ilex martiniana, commonly known as the Martin's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are leathery and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.

Ilex martiniana is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a versatile plant in horticulture.
