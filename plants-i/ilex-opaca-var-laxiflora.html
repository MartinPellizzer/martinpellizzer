Ilex opaca var. laxiflora

Ilex opaca var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Southern wax myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
