Iris fulva f. fulvaurea

Iris fulva f. fulvaurea, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a variant of the yellow flag iris native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive purple-brown stripe along the throat. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and grow in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soil and is often found near water bodies.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize shoreline soils.
