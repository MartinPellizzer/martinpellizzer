Ischaemum wayanadense

Ischaemum wayanadense, commonly known as the Wayanad grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
