Impatiens sulcata var. amplexicaulis

Impatiens sulcata var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging impatiens, is a variant of the impatiens species characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

The leaves are broad and often clasping the stem, giving the plant a dense, compact appearance. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The flowers are typically small, tubular, and come in shades of pink, red, or white.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
