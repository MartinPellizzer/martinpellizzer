Ischaemum barbatum var. arundinaceum

Ischaemum barbatum var. arundinaceum, commonly known as the hairy false oat, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are narrow, hairy, and slightly rolled, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged and bracted.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.
