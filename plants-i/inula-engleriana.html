Inula engleriana

Inula engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's Inula, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and resemble daisies, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
