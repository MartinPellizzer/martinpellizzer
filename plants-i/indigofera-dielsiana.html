Indigofera dielsiana

Indigofera dielsiana, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Indigofera dielsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
