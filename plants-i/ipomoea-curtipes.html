Ipomoea curtipes

Ipomoea curtipes, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous areas.

Ipomoea curtipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
