Inga pseudofastuosa

Inga pseudofastuosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth rate and ability to fix nitrogen make it valuable for soil enrichment and reforestation efforts.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
