Iris proantha var. valida

Iris proantha var. valida, commonly known as the Valid iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a prominent white or pale yellow beard on the lower petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with slender, grass-like leaves that are dark green and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for healthy wetland ecosystems.
