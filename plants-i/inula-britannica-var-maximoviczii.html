Inula britannica var. maximoviczii

Inula britannica var. maximoviczii, commonly known as the British Inula, is a variant of the common fleabane.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles, characterized by its yellow composite flower heads and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds and is known for its medicinal properties historically used in herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
