Ipomoea solaniflora

Ipomoea solaniflora, commonly known as the nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, often violet or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
