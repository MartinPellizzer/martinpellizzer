Inula britannica subsp. japonica

Inula britannica subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Japanese elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia. It features a tall, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, arranged in a single, showy head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
