Inula verbascifolia subsp. aschersoniana

Inula verbascifolia subsp.

aschersoniana, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are aromatic and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and ornamental plants.
