Ilex cassine var. myrtifolia

Ilex cassine var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with humid climates.
