Isopyrum nipponicum

Isopyrum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese isopyrum, is a perennial herb native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a basal rosette and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a red, spherical berry that ripens in late summer.

Isopyrum nipponicum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
