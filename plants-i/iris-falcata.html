Iris falcata

Iris falcata, commonly known as the swordleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Iris falcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
