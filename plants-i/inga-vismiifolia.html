Inga vismiifolia

Inga vismiifolia, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Inga vismiifolia also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility in its natural habitat.
