Ixeris dentata

Ixeris dentata, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers resembling those of the common dandelion. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and disturbed areas.

Ixeris dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
