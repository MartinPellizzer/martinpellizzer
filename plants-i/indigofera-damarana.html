Indigofera damarana

Indigofera damarana, commonly known as the Damaran indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It has a woody stem and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Indigofera damarana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
