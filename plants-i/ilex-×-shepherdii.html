Ilex × shepherdii

Ilex × shepherdii, commonly known as the American holly, is a hybrid evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically spiny at the edges, and it produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant is evergreen, retaining its foliage year-round, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used as an ornamental plant in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
