Ipomoea obscura var. gemella

Ipomoea obscura var. gemella, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant's stems are hairy and twining, allowing it to climb and spread across supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
