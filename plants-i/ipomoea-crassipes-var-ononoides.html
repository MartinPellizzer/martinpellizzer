Ipomoea crassipes var. ononoides

Ipomoea crassipes var. ononoides, commonly known as the water spinach or river spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often floating on the surface of water. The plant thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often growing in slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its stems are fleshy and can be harvested for consumption, making it a valuable vegetable in many cuisines.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative propagation.
