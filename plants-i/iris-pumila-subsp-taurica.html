Iris pumila subsp. taurica

Iris pumila subsp.

taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian dwarf iris, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue to purple flowers in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact size and color.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various garden environments.
