Inga lallensis

Inga lallensis, commonly known as the Llanillan inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in supporting local biodiversity.
