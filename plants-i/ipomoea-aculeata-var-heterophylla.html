Ipomoea aculeata var. heterophylla

Ipomoea aculeata var. heterophylla, commonly known as the prickly morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features distinctive, alternate leaves that vary in shape, often with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically opening in the morning. Its stems are covered in fine, hooked hairs, which aid in climbing and attachment.

The species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to a range of soil types and light conditions.
