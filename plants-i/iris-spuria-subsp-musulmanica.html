Iris spuria subsp. musulmanica

Iris spuria subsp.

musulmanica, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three recurved sepals.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
