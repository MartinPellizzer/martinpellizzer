Ipomoea parkeri var. subsericea

Ipomoea parkeri var. subsericea, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces trumpet-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
