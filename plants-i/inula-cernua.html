Inula cernua

Inula cernua, commonly known as goatsbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery, yellowish-green compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Inula cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
