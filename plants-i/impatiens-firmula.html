Impatiens firmula

Impatiens firmula, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens firmula is a member of the Balsaminaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ornamental value in horticulture.
