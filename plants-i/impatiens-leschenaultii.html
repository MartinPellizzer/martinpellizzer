Impatiens leschenaultii

Impatiens leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
