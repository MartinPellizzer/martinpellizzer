Indigofera chimanimaniensis

Indigofera chimanimaniensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to the Eastern Cape province of South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable growth.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in local ecosystems.
