Ipomoea obscura var. sagittifolia

Ipomoea obscura var. sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with tendrils that allow it to ascend support structures. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
