Inula salicifolia

Inula salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, willow-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Inula salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
