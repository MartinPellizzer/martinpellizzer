Indigofera uhehensis

Indigofera uhehensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system. It produces pods containing small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant also has potential applications in soil improvement and as a forage crop.
