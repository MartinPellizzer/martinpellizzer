Ischaemum muticum var. repens

Ischaemum muticum var. repens, commonly known as creeping wild oat, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender, wiry stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, grass-like leaves and tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important component of early successional plant communities.
