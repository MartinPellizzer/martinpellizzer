Ipomoea rumicifolia

Ipomoea rumicifolia, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking yellow or orange flowers that open in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its flowers have five petals and are typically 5-7 cm in diameter.

Ipomoea rumicifolia is also known for its fibrous roots, which can be used in traditional medicine.
