Iris setosa var. hondoensis

Iris setosa var. hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo iris, is a rare and endemic species native to the Hondo region of Japan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. It has small, three-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
