Imperata tenuis

Imperata tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf blady grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often reddish in color.

Imperata tenuis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
