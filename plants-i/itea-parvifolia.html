Itea parvifolia

Itea parvifolia, commonly known as smoothleaf water-plantain, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, aromatic stems that often grow in wet or marshy habitats. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy texture. Small white flowers appear in clustered spikes during late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
