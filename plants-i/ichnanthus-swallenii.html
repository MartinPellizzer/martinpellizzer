Ichnanthus swallenii

Ichnanthus swallenii, commonly known as the swamp gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near ponds and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ichnanthus swallenii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
