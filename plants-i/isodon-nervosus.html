Isodon nervosus

Isodon nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-veined isodon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Isodon nervosus is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
