Ida insolita

Ida insolita, commonly known as the rare and enigmatic ghost orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, white, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. This plant is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives as an epiphyte on tree trunks and branches. Ida insolita has specialized roots that absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, allowing it to grow in nutrient-poor environments.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, it is considered a critically endangered species.
