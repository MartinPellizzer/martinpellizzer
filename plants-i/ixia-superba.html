Ixia superba

Ixia superba, commonly known as the fire lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem with clustered flowers. Ixia superba thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique, flame-like appearance.
