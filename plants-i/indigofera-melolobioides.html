Indigofera melolobioides

Indigofera melolobioides, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
