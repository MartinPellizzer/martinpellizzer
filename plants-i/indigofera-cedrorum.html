Indigofera cedrorum

Indigofera cedrorum, commonly known as the Kashmir indigo or Himalayan indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia, including the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera cedrorum is also valued for its potential ecological benefits, such as nitrogen fixation in soil.
