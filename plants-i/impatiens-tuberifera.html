Impatiens tuberifera

Impatiens tuberifera, commonly known as the tuberous impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Impatiens tuberifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
