Iris tectorum var. alba

Iris tectorum var. alba, commonly known as the white marsh iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
