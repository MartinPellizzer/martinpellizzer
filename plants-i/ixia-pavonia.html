Ixia pavonia

Ixia pavonia, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Ixia pavonia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
