Inga fasciformis

Inga fasciformis, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate, and its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, tropical environments.

Inga fasciformis also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agroforestry systems.
