Isolepis pilosa

Isolepis pilosa, commonly known as hairy spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond margins and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

Isolepis pilosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
