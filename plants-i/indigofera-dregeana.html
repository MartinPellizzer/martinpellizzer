Indigofera dregeana

Indigofera dregeana, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are encased in a hard, dark brown pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Indigofera dregeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various regions.
