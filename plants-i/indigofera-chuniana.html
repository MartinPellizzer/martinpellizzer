Indigofera chuniana

Indigofera chuniana, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It produces pods containing seeds, and its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
