Impia pyramidata

Impia pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its distinctive appearance.

Impia pyramidata is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
