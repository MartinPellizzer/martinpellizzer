Ilex sikkimensis

Ilex sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Ilex sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
