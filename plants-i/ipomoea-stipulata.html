Ipomoea stipulata

Ipomoea stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces showy, five-petaled blooms that typically open in the morning and close by midday. Its stems are twining and can climb support structures, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ipomoea stipulata thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
