Isodon brachythyrsus

Isodon brachythyrsus, commonly known as the short-stalked lepidote, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded environments in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
