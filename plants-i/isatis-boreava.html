Isatis boreava

Isatis boreava, commonly known as the Siberian woad, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its most notable feature is the production of indigo-blue dye from its leaves, historically used for textile coloring.

Isatis boreava is typically found in moist, temperate habitats and is valued for both its ornamental and historical dyeing properties.
