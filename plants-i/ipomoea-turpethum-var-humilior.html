Ipomoea turpethum var. humilior

Ipomoea turpethum var. humilior, commonly known as the lesser morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with a sprawling or climbing habit. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, a characteristic known as nyctinasty.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
