Icica leptophloeos

Icica leptophloeos, commonly known as the slender-leaf icica, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
