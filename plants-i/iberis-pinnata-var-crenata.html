Iberis pinnata var. crenata

Iberis pinnata var. crenata, commonly known as the creeping candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with gray-green, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
