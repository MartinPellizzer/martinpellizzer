Ilysanthes viatica

Ilysanthes viatica, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy surface to retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
