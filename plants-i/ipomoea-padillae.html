Ipomoea padillae

Ipomoea padillae, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Ipomoea padillae is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known morning glory species.
