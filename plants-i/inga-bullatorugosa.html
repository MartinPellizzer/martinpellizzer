Inga bullatorugosa

Inga bullatorugosa, commonly known as the rough bullata, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Inga bullatorugosa is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and habitat provision.
