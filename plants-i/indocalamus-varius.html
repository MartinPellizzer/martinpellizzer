Indocalamus varius

Indocalamus varius, commonly known as the variable bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a pale green color.

Indocalamus varius thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
