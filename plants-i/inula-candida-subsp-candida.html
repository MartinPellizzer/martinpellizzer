Inula candida subsp. candida

Inula candida subsp.

candida, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and somewhat woody at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
