Ixora elliptica

Ixora elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers. The leaves are elliptical in shape, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
