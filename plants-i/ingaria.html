Ingaria

Ingaria, commonly known as the Australian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its wood is highly valued for its fragrant, durable qualities and is used in traditional crafts and perfumery.

Ingaria is also known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and coastal environments.
