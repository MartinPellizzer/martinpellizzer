Inga platyptera

Inga platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leaf inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often doubly pinnately compound. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds.

It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
