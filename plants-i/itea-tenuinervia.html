Itea tenuinervia

Itea tenuinervia, commonly known as narrow-veined ironweed, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are typically green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and wooded areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
