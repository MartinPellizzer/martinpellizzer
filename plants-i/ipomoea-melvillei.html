Ipomoea melvillei

Ipomoea melvillei, commonly known as the Melville morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea melvillei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
