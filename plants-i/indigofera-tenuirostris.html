Indigofera tenuirostris

Indigofera tenuirostris, commonly known as the slender-beaked indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated pods and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
