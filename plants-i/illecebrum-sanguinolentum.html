Illecebrum sanguinolentum

Illecebrum sanguinolentum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a single, fleshy, red-orange root that is both visually distinctive and chemically significant. The plant produces showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The botanical name reflects its blood-red root and its historical use in traditional medicine.
