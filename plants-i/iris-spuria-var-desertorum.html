Iris spuria var. desertorum

Iris spuria var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive spur-like structure at the base of each petal. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds, showcasing its resilience to drought.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
