Ipomoea linosepala subsp. upembensis

Ipomoea linosepala subsp.

upembensis, commonly known as the Upembe morning glory, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically blue or violet, with a white throat, and bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.
