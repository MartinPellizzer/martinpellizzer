Ipomoea cymosa var. cochleata

Ipomoea cymosa var. cochleata, commonly known as the spiral morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive coiled, spiral-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.

Its vines can climb and spread rapidly, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.
