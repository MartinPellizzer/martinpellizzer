Idiothamnus

Idiothamnus, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are followed by small, puffball-like seed heads that disperse seeds easily by wind.

Idiothamnus is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Australian herbal remedies.
