Ipomoea osyrensis

Ipomoea osyrensis, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical regions.

Ipomoea osyrensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
