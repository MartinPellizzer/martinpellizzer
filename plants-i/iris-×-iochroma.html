Iris × iochroma

Iris × iochroma, commonly known as the hybrid iris, is a cross between the bearded iris (Iris germanica) and the Japanese iris (Iris sibirica).

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and provide a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Botanically, it is a perennial herbaceous plant that is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
