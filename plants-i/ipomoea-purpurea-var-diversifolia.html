Ipomoea purpurea var. diversifolia

Ipomoea purpurea var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated morning glory, is a striking vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with striking variegation of green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to white, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets.
