Iris medwedewii

Iris medwedewii, commonly known as the Medwedew's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often at high altitudes. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Iris medwedewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
