Ipomoea bombycina

Ipomoea bombycina, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ipomoea bombycina is a perennial vine that can grow up to 6 feet tall, with a rapid growth rate during the summer months.
