Ipomoea costata

Ipomoea costata, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, winding stems that often form a zigzag pattern. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
