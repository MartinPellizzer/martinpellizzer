Iris xiphioides

Iris xiphioides, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with six petals. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually yellow or orange in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
