Iris adami

Iris adami, commonly known as the Adam's iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color.

Iris adami is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
