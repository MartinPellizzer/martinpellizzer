Indigofera adscendens

Indigofera adscendens, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark blue seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
