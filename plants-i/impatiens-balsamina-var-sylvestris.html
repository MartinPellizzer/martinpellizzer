Impatiens balsamina var. sylvestris

Impatiens balsamina var. sylvestris, commonly known as wild impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that burst open to disperse seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
