Ipomoea neglecta

Ipomoea neglecta, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is native to the Americas but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

Ipomoea neglecta is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
