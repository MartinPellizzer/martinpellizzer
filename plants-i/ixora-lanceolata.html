Ixora lanceolata

Ixora lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers, which are borne on long, branching inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hence the species name, and are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Ixora lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
