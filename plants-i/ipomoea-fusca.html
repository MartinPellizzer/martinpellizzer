Ipomoea fusca

Ipomoea fusca, commonly known as the dark morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers, typically in shades of purple, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Ipomoea fusca is known for its ornamental value but can also be invasive in certain environments.
