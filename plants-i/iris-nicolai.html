Iris nicolai

Iris nicolai, commonly known as the Russian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow beard on the lower petals. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It blooms in late spring to early summer, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Iris nicolai is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and tall, elegant flower spikes.
