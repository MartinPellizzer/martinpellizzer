Iris variegata lusus ochroleuca

Iris variegata lusus ochroleuca, commonly known as the variegated iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically feature a mix of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in the spring, making it a popular choice for gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
