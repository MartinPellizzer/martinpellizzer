Iris spectabilis

Iris spectabilis, commonly known as the showy iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Iris spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
