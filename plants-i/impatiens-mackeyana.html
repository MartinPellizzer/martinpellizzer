Impatiens mackeyana

Impatiens mackeyana, commonly known as Mackey's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
