Ipomoea mechoacan

Ipomoea mechoacan, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea mechoacan is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
