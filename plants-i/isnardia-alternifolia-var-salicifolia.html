Isnardia alternifolia var. salicifolia

Isnardia alternifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved isnardia, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its alternate arrangement of lanceolate, willow-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often greenish or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are often covered with a fine layer of hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various humid conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
