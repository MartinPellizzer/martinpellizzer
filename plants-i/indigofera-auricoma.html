Indigofera auricoma

Indigofera auricoma, commonly known as the golden crown bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
