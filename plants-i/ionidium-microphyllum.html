Ionidium microphyllum

Ionidium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved ionidium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tiny, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
