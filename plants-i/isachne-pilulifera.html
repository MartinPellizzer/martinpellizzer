Isachne pilulifera

Isachne pilulifera, commonly known as the ball moss, is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

It produces small, rounded spikelets that resemble tiny balls, giving it its common name. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on tree trunks or in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Isachne pilulifera is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.
