Ipomoea fragilis var. pubescens

Ipomoea fragilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five lobes, and come in a range of colors including blue, purple, and white. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
