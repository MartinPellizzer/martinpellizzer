Indigofera consanguinea

Indigofera consanguinea, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with pinnate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
