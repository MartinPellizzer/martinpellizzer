Iris × violilutea

Iris × violilutea, commonly known as the purple-lilac iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically display a combination of purple and lilac hues. The plant is a perennial herb with rhizomatous growth, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color.

Iris × violilutea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of growing conditions.
