Ipheion nivale

Ipheion nivale, commonly known as the snowflake lily, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has grass-like, narrow leaves that form a dense mat, often carpeting the forest floor. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Ipheion nivale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
