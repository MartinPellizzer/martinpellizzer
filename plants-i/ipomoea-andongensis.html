Ipomoea andongensis

Ipomoea andongensis, commonly known as the Andong morning glory, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
