Ipomoea macrocalyx

Ipomoea macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates. Its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea macrocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
