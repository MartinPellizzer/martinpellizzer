Ipomoea chrysotricha var. boliviana

Ipomoea chrysotricha var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian golden morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many climbing and trailing plants.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
