Ipomoea mastersii

Ipomoea mastersii, commonly known as the masters' morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or trees. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by birds and insects.

Ipomoea mastersii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
