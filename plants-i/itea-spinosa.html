Itea spinosa

Itea spinosa, commonly known as the smooth woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, slender leaflets that are often arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its stems are covered in smooth, reddish bark that becomes more pronounced with age.

Itea spinosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
