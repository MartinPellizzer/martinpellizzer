Inula scabra

Inula scabra, commonly known as rough inula, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Inula scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
