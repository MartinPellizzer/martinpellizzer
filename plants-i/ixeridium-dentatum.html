Ixeridium dentatum

Ixeridium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed sandparsley, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have distinctive teeth along the edges, contributing to its common name.

Ixeridium dentatum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources.
