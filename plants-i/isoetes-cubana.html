Isoetes cubana

Isoetes cubana, commonly known as the Cuban water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that form a dense mat on the bottom of shallow, freshwater habitats. The plant is closely related to other species in the genus Isoetes, which are known for their unique, spore-producing structures called sporangia. Isoetes cubana typically grows in still or slow-moving water, such as ponds and wetlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing sediments.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for assessing water quality in its native range.
