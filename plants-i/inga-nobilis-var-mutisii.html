Inga nobilis var. mutisii

Inga nobilis var. mutisii, commonly known as the "sweet acacia," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
