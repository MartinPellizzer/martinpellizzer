Inula seidlitzii

Inula seidlitzii, commonly known as the mountain inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellowish to brownish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
