Inula sericeo-villosa

Inula sericeo-villosa, commonly known as the silky-hairy Inula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy yellow composite flowers. Native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, it thrives in temperate climates. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
