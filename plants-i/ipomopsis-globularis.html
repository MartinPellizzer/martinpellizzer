Ipomopsis globularis

Ipomopsis globularis, commonly known as the globe evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique flower structure and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
