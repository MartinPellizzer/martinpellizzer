Ipomoea carnea f. albiflora

Ipomoea carnea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a variant of Ipomoea carnea, characterized by its pale to white flowers instead of the typical blue or purple hues. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the morning and close at dusk. It has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This ornamental species is often cultivated for its striking floral display and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
