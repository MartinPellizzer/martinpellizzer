Impatiens elata

Impatiens elata, commonly known as the mountain impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
