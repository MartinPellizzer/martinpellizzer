Indigofera punctata

Indigofera punctata, commonly known as the dotted indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
