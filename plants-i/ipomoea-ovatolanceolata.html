Ipomoea ovatolanceolata

Ipomoea ovatolanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
