Indigofera anabaptista

Indigofera anabaptista, commonly known as the blue pea or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue or violet pea-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and compound fruit pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
