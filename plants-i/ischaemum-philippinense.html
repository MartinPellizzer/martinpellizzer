Ischaemum philippinense

Ischaemum philippinense, commonly known as Philippine barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Ischaemum philippinense is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops.
