Indigofera dekindtii

Indigofera dekindtii, commonly known as the African indigo.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
