Iris potaninii var. arenaria

Iris potaninii var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in steppe and desert steppe habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
