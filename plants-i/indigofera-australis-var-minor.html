Indigofera australis var. minor

Indigofera australis var. minor, commonly known as the small indigo bush, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
