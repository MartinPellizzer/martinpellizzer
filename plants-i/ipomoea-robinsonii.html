Ipomoea robinsonii

Ipomoea robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ipomoea robinsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
