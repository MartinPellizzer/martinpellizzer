Isatis alyssifolia

Isatis alyssifolia, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

Isatis alyssifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
