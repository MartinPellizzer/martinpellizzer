Indigofera stricta

Indigofera stricta, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to violet.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
