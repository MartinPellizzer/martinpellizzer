Indigofera subhirtella

Indigofera subhirtella, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces pods containing small, dark-colored seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
