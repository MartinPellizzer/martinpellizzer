Iria monostachya

Iria monostachya, commonly known as the slender iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Iria monostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
