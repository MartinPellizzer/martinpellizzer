Impatiens dewildeana var. parviflora

Impatiens dewildeana var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are ovate and glossy, with a dark green color, and the plant produces delicate, spurred petals typical of the Impatiens genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

This variety is valued for its ability to add vibrant color to shaded landscapes with minimal care.
