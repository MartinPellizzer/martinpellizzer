Ipomoea brasseuriana

Ipomoea brasseuriana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple throat. The plant is a vigorous climber, featuring heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and long blooming season make it both beautiful and sometimes invasive in certain regions.
