Ixora fallax

Ixora fallax, commonly known as the swamp ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, swampy environments. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Ixora fallax is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to wet conditions.
