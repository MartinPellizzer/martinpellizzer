Ixora subauriculata

Ixora subauriculata, commonly known as the golden flame ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora subauriculata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
