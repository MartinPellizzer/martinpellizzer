Incarvillea arguta

Incarvillea arguta, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that produces striking violet or blue-violet flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has a woody stem covered in soft, grayish-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Incarvillea arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
