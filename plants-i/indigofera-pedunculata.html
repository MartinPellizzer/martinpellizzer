Indigofera pedunculata

Indigofera pedunculata, commonly known as the slender indigo or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. It produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds.

Indigofera pedunculata is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
