Ipomoea webbii

Ipomoea webbii, commonly known as the Webb's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically 5-7 cm in diameter and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Ipomoea webbii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
