Inga ochroclada

Inga ochroclada, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a species of leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the genus Inga, which is part of the legume family Fabaceae. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bean-like pods. It is often found in tropical rainforests and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
