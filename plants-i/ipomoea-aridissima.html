Ipomoea aridissima

Ipomoea aridissima, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with deep taproots and waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It produces vibrant blue or purple blooms and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Despite its beauty, it is considered an invasive species in some areas, outcompeting native flora.
