Indigofera grandidieri

Indigofera grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and restricted range, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
