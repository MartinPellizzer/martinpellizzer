Ixia elegans

Ixia elegans, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, orange-red flowers that resemble torches. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that are often clustered in a raceme, adding a dramatic visual impact. Ixia elegans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
