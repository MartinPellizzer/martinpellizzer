Ithycaulon brasiliense

Ithycaulon brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white and pink.

Ithycaulon brasiliense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
