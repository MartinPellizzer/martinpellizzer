Ipomoea oreogena

Ipomoea oreogena, commonly known as the alpine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking blue or violet flowers, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its botanical features include a robust root system and hairy stems, which help it survive in harsh conditions.

Ipomoea oreogena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
