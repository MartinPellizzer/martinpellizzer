Ipomoea trichocarpa f. pubescens

Ipomoea trichocarpa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage and stems.

This plant is a flowering vine native to the Americas, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The hairy texture of its leaves and stems distinguishes it from other forms of Ipomoea trichocarpa.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various supports.
