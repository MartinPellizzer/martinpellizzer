Iresine spicata

Iresine spicata, commonly known as redroot ironplant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep purple to crimson in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, which appear in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Iresine spicata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
