Inga sertulifera subsp. leptopus

Inga sertulifera subsp.

leptopus, commonly known as the slender inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
