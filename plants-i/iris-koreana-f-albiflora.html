Iris koreana f. albiflora

Iris koreana f. albiflora, commonly known as the Korean white iris, is a rare and striking ornamental plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a cultivar of Iris koreana, characterized by its pale white to almost cream-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features grass-like, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, upright flower stalks that can reach up to 60 cm in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and conservation significance.
