Ipomoea paulitschkei

Ipomoea paulitschkei, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
