Inula pygmaea

Inula pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its compact size, typically growing no taller than 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of its native flora.
