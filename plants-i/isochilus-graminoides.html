Isochilus graminoides

Isochilus graminoides, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. Isochilus graminoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.
