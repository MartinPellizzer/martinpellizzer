Isachne kinshasaensis

Isachne kinshasaensis, commonly known as the Kinshasa grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical structures.

Isachne kinshasaensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small mammals.
