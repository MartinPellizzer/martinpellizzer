Ipomoea gangetica

Ipomoea gangetica, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South and Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is typically found in moist, tropical environments and thrives in partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it an effective ground cover or ornamental plant.

Ipomoea gangetica is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
