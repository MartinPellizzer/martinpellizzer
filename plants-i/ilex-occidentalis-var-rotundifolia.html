Ilex occidentalis var. rotundifolia

Ilex occidentalis var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and broad, elliptical leaves that are often rounded at the base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and dry summers.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
