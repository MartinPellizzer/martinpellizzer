Inga ursi

Inga ursi, commonly known as the "Coca plant," is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments, often growing at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Its most distinctive feature is the presence of leaves containing alkaloids, particularly cocaine, which has significant botanical and pharmacological interest.
