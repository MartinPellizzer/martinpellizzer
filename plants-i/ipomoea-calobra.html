Ipomoea calobra

Ipomoea calobra, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb to significant heights. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea calobra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
