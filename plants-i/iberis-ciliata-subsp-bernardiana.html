Iberis ciliata subsp. bernardiana

Iberis ciliata subsp.

bernardiana, commonly known as the Bernard's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers.

The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period.
