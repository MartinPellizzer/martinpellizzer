Ipomoea kahloae

Ipomoea kahloae, commonly known as the Frida Kahlo vine, is a flowering plant native to the Mexican state of Oaxaca.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to white, often with contrasting veining. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing plant that can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself.

Due to its unique appearance and association with the famous artist Frida Kahlo, it has become a symbol of resilience and beauty in botanical art.
