Iris aequiloba

Iris aequiloba, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a basal rosette, while its flowers consist of three upright petals and three downward-curving sepals.

Iris aequiloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
