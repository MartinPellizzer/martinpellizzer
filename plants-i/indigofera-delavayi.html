Indigofera delavayi

Indigofera delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a perennial shrub native to China and other parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
