Ilysanthes aculeata

Ilysanthes aculeata, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The plant is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
