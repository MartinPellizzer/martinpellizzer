Inulopsis stenophylla

Inulopsis stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf inulopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in challenging climates.
