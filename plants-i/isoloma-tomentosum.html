Isoloma tomentosum

Isoloma tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along streambanks. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive woolly appearance, with "tomentosum" referring to the dense covering of fine hairs.

Isoloma tomentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
