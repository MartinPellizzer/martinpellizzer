Iresine reticulata

Iresine reticulata, commonly known as the red leaf iron plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Iresine reticulata is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and container gardening.
