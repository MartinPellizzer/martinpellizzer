Iris virescens subsp. reichenbachii

Iris virescens subsp.

reichenbachii, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ornamental gardening.
