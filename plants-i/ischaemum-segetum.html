Ischaemum segetum

Ischaemum segetum, commonly known as field barnyard grass, is an annual grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it is considered a weed in many cultivated environments.
