Isodon × suzukii

Isodon × suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Isodon × suzukii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
