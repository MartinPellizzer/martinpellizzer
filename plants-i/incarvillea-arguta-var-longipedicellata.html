Incarvillea arguta var. longipedicellata

Incarvillea arguta var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-beaked incarvillea, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a branching stem that grows from the base. It is adapted to dry conditions with deep roots and reduced leaf surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
