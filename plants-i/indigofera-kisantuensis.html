Indigofera kisantuensis

Indigofera kisantuensis, commonly known as Kisantu indigo, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.
