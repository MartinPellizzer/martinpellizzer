Ipomoea smithii

Ipomoea smithii, commonly known as Smith's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other morning glory species.

Ipomoea smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
