Impatiens spectabilis

Impatiens spectabilis, commonly known as the "whispering bells," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, often with a reddish or purple tinge along the veins.

The flowers are borne on slender stems and are known for their delicate, bell-shaped appearance.
