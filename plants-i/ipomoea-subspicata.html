Ipomoea subspicata

Ipomoea subspicata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright red or orange. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Ipomoea subspicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various regions worldwide.
