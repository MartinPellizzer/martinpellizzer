Ipomoea kunstleri

Ipomoea kunstleri, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to yellowish flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant produces heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. Its flowering period occurs primarily in the summer months, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in attracting nocturnal pollinators.
