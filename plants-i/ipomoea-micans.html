Ipomoea micans

Ipomoea micans, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that have a silvery sheen, and its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of colors from white to deep purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, and it produces showy blooms typically in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea micans is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly in suitable conditions.
