Ipomoea sofomarensis

Ipomoea sofomarensis, commonly known as the Sofoma violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or violet blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Ipomoea sofomarensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
