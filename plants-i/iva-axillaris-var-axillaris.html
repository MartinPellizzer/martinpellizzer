Iva axillaris var. axillaris

Iva axillaris var. axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and prairies. It has slender, branching stems with finely divided, lance-shaped leaves.

Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
