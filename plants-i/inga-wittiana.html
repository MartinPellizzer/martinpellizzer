Inga wittiana

Inga wittiana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, and the tree is often used in agroforestry systems.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional practices.
