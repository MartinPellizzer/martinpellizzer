Iris arenaria subsp. borzaeana

Iris arenaria subsp.

borzaeana, commonly known as the Borzaean sand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinct three-parted structure. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and limited water availability.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
