Ilex pubigera

Ilex pubigera, commonly known as the pubescent holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy green leaves that are covered with fine, short hairs. The plant produces small white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex pubigera thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
