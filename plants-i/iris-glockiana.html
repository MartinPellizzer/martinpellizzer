Iris glockiana

Iris glockiana, commonly known as the German iris, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Iris.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
