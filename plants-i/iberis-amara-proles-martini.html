Iberis amara proles martini

Iberis amara proles martini, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with the bitter compounds in its leaves and flowers historically used in traditional remedies.
