Indigofera hartwegii

Indigofera hartwegii, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or Hartweg's indigo, is a shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and benefits surrounding vegetation. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and shrublands.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
