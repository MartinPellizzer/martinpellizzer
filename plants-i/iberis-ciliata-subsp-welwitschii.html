Iberis ciliata subsp. welwitschii

Iberis ciliata subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant features greyish-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal areas.
