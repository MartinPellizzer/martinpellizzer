Indigofera lanuginosa

Indigofera lanuginosa, commonly known as silky indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with pinnate leaves and often produces pods containing seeds.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
