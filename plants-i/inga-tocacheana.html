Inga tocacheana

Inga tocacheana, commonly known as the Tocachea inga, is a species of leguminous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Inga, which is part of the legume family Fabaceae, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with alternating leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree can grow to a height of up to 10 meters.

Inga tocacheana is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment.
