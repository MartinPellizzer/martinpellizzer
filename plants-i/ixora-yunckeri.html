Ixora yunckeri

Ixora yunckeri, commonly known as the Yuncker's flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, red or orange-red inflorescences that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
