Indigofera bogdanii var. peteri

Indigofera bogdanii var. peteri, commonly known as the desert indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its root system is adapted to withstand drought, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and restoration projects in dry environments.
