Iris × dewinkeleri

Iris × dewinkeleri, commonly known as the Dewinkeler iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that are dark green and somewhat coarse in texture.

Iris × dewinkeleri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
