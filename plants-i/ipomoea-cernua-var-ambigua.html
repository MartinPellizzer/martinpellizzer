Ipomoea cernua var. ambigua

Ipomoea cernua var. ambigua, commonly known as the wandering jew or morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a trailing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
