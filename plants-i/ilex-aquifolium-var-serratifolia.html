Ilex aquifolium var. serratifolia

Ilex aquifolium var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland and scrub habitats. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a leathery texture. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedging and landscaping.
