Ionthlaspi clypeolatum var. macrocarpum

Ionthlaspi clypeolatum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited rock cress, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are distinctive, being large, spherical, and segmented, containing several seeds.

This variety is adapted to harsh, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
