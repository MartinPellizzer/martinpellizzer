Ischnosiphon gracilis var. scabra

Ischnosiphon gracilis var. scabra, commonly known as the rough-stemmed slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, clustered inflorescences during the summer months.
