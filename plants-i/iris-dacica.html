Iris dacica

Iris dacica, commonly known as the Dacian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

The Dacian iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
