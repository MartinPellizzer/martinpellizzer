Inga bifoliolata

Inga bifoliolata, commonly known as the two-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with two leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
