Iris pulchella

Iris pulchella, commonly known as the showy iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive white or yellow center, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Iris pulchella is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
