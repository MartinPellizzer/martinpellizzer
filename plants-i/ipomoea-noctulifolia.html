Ipomoea noctulifolia

Ipomoea noctulifolia, commonly known as the nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, climbing or trailing along supports.

Ipomoea noctulifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
