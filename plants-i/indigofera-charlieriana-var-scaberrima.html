Indigofera charlieriana var. scaberrima

Indigofera charlieriana var. scaberrima, commonly known as the rough indigo bean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
