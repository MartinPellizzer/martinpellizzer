Illecebrum tenuifolium

Illecebrum tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leafed dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique life cycle that involves germinating on the surface of host plants. The plant's stems are often covered in tiny, thread-like structures that help it attach to its host. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host plants.
