Irwinia coronata

Irwinia coronata, commonly known as the golden crown vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Irwinia coronata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
