Isatis cardiocarpa

Isatis cardiocarpa, commonly known as the heartleaf woad, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine highlights its ecological and cultural significance.
