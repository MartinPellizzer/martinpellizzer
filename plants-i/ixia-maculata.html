Ixia maculata

Ixia maculata, commonly known as the spotted flame flower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant typically grows as a bulbous perennial, with strap-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb. Its flowering period occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Ixia maculata is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
