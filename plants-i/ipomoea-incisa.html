Ipomoea incisa

Ipomoea incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It is a climbing vine that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun.

Ipomoea incisa is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it can spread rapidly.
