Isophyllum tenuissimum

Isophyllum tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender isophyllum, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying succulent flora.
