Inga grandiflora

Inga grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga grandiflora also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
