Isolepis verrucosula

Isolepis verrucosula, commonly known as the woolly spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of spikelets covered in soft, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often in temperate regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Isolepis verrucosula plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
