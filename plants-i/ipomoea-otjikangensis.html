Ipomoea otjikangensis

Ipomoea otjikangensis, commonly known as the Otjikang spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
