Iris auranitica

Iris auranitica, commonly known as the golden iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to golden orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Iris auranitica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
