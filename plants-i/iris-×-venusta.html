Iris × venusta

Iris × venusta, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris pallida and Iris persica.

It is characterized by its striking, lavender-blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, slender stems with showy, spurred petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette.

Native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
