Indigofera incompta

Indigofera incompta, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, fibrous pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
