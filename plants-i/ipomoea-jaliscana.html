Ipomoea jaliscana

Ipomoea jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Ipomoea jaliscana is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar growth habits.
