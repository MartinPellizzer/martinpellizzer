Inga forficata

Inga forficata, commonly known as the "Barringtonia" or "Barringtonia," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species. Inga forficata is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in reforestation efforts.

Its ecological importance extends to providing habitat and food for numerous insect and bird species.
