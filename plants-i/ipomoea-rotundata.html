Ipomoea rotundata

Ipomoea rotundata, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular garden plant.
