Illicium anisatum var. roseum

Illicium anisatum var. roseum, commonly known as the roseum star anise, is a variant of the star anise plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruit that contains aromatic seeds used in both culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin, while the flowers are small and pale green to white in color.

The fruit develops in late summer to early autumn and is harvested for its essential oils and flavoring properties.
