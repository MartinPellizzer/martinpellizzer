Ixiolirion tataricum

Ixiolirion tataricum, commonly known as the Tatarian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
