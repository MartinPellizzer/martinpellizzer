Isolepis inundata

Isolepis inundata, commonly known as the flooded flatspike, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Isolepis inundata is adapted to periodic flooding and can tolerate waterlogged conditions for extended periods.
