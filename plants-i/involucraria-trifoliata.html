Involucraria trifoliata

Involucraria trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within its ecological niche.
