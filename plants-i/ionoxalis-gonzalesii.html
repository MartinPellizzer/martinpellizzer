Ionoxalis gonzalesii

Ionoxalis gonzalesii, commonly known as the Gonzales' wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
