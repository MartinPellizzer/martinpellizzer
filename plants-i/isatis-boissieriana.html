Isatis boissieriana

Isatis boissieriana, commonly known as the Persian woad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbages. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and tall, branching stems that bear small, yellow flowers. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
