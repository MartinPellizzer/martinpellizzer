Ilex malaccensis var. ferruginifolia

Ilex malaccensis var. ferruginifolia, commonly known as the Malacca holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical regions.
