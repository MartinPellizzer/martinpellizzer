Ipomoea auricoma

Ipomoea auricoma, commonly known as the auricoma morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea auricoma is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
