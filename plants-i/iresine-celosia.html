Iresine celosia

Iresine celosia, commonly known as flame flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its foliage is often a deep green, contrasting beautifully with the showy bracts.

Iresine celosia is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a potted plant due to its eye-catching appearance.
