Ilex microwrightioides var. calvescens

Ilex microwrightioides var. calvescens, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. Its evergreen nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to a range of soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
