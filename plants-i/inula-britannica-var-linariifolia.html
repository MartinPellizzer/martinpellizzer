Inula britannica var. linariifolia

Inula britannica var. linariifolia, commonly known as the British elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, yellow composite flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
