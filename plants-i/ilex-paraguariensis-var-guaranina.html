Ilex paraguariensis var. guaranina

Ilex paraguariensis var. guaranina, commonly known as the Guarana plant, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries containing stimulant-rich seeds. The plant is closely related to the coffee family and is known for its high concentration of caffeine and theobromine. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while the seeds are harvested for their energizing effects.
