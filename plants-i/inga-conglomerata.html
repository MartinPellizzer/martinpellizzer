Inga conglomerata

Inga conglomerata, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its fast growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga conglomerata is also valued for its role in agroforestry due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
