Ilex macrocarpa var. trichophylla

Ilex macrocarpa var. trichophylla, commonly known as the coastal holly, is a variant of the coastal holly native to the southeastern United States.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical with a waxy coating, and the plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
