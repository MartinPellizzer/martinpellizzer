Ischaemum muticum var. aristuliferum

Ischaemum muticum var. aristuliferum, commonly known as the arrow-leaved couch grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and narrow, lanceolate leaves that often have a distinctive arrowhead-shaped base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Its fibrous root system makes it a resilient plant capable of spreading rapidly in suitable conditions.
