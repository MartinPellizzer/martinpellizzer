Ipomoea mendesii

Ipomoea mendesii, commonly known as the Mendes's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb. It blooms in the morning and closes its flowers by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea mendesii is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as forests and riverbanks.
