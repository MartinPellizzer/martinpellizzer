Iberis carnosa subsp. granatensis

Iberis carnosa subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the granate candytuft, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers.

The plant features silver-blue foliage with a velvety texture, which enhances its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and long blooming period make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
