Ipomoea variifolia

Ipomoea variifolia, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet and blue. The plant has heart-shaped or lobed leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea variifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow as a climbing vine or a shrub.
