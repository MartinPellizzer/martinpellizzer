Isertia haenkeana var. mirandensis

Isertia haenkeana var. mirandensis, commonly known as the Mirandense Isertia, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within dense forest understories. The plant features narrow, lanceolate leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, white or pale green flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for conservation biologists.
