Iris × fuscivenosa

Iris × fuscivenosa, commonly known as the purple iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The flowers are typically violet to purple in color, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
