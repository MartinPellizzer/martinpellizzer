Indigofera elliotii

Indigofera elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of small, pink to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
