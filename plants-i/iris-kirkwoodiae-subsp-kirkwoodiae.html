Iris kirkwoodiae subsp. kirkwoodiae

Iris kirkwoodiae subsp.

kirkwoodiae, commonly known as the Kirkwood's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive blackish-purple throat, typically blooming in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
