Ipomoea neriifolia

Ipomoea neriifolia, commonly known as the black nightshade vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often using its tendrils to attach to supports. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Ipomoea neriifolia is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking night-blooming flowers.
