Inga longispica

Inga longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
