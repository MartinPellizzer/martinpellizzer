Iris spuria subsp. demetrii

Iris spuria subsp.

demetrii, commonly known as the Demetrii iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with a distinctive spur that extends from the lower petal.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
