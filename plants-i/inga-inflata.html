Inga inflata

Inga inflata, commonly known as the inflated inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its large, inflated seed pods that can reach several centimeters in length. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Inga inflata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
