Isnardia hirsuta

Isnardia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its overall hirsute appearance.

Isnardia hirsuta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
