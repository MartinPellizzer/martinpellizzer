Illecebrum sessile

Illecebrum sessile, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Illecebrum sessile is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
