Inga williamsii

Inga williamsii, commonly known as Williams' inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Inga williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological contributions in tropical ecosystems.
