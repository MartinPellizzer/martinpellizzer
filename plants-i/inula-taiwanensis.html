Inula taiwanensis

Inula taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan Inula, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Inula taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
