Isachne arundinacea

Isachne arundinacea, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in a dense, tufted manner. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can be both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
