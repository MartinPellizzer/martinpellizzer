Ipomoea diversifolia

Ipomoea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a climbing vine with a rapid growth rate, often reaching lengths of several meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea diversifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
