Indigofera dasycephala

Indigofera dasycephala, commonly known as the woolly head indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with branching stems and small, compound leaves.

Indigofera dasycephala is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
