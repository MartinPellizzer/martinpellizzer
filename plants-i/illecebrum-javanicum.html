Illecebrum javanicum

Illecebrum javanicum, commonly known as the Java creeper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its fruits are follicles that contain numerous tiny, flat seeds.

Illecebrum javanicum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streams.
