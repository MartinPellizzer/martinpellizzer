Inga antioquensis

Inga antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian inga, is a tropical tree native to the Andes region of Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
