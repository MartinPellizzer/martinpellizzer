Ipomoea vernalis

Ipomoea vernalis, commonly known as the spring morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Ipomoea vernalis is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it climbs using its twining stems.
