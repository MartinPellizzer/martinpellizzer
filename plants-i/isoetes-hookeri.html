Isoetes hookeri

Isoetes hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's watermace, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, spike-like formations along the stem. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually narrow and ribbon-like, and they remain submerged or partially submerged in water.

Isoetes hookeri plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
