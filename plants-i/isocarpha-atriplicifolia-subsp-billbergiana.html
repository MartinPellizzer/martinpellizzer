Isocarpha atriplicifolia subsp. billbergiana

Isocarpha atriplicifolia subsp.

billbergiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lanceolate leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
