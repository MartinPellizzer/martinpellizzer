Iris olbiensis

Iris olbiensis, commonly known as the Olbiense Iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Iris olbiensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in meadows.
