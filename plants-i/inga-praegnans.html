Inga praegnans

Inga praegnans, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
