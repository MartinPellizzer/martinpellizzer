Iris spathulata f. hispanica

Iris spathulata f. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish flag iris, is a cultivar of the bearded iris species.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a contrasting white or cream-colored beard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-40 cm, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This cultivar is noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
