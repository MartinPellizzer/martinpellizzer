Ixora deciduiflora

Ixora deciduiflora, commonly known as the deciduous ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers during the dry season, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Ixora deciduiflora is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
