Ipomoea commutata

Ipomoea commutata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant is a vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and a hairy stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea commutata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
