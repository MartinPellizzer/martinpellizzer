Illipe malabrorum subsp. alphonsae

Illipe malabrorum subsp.

alphonsae, commonly known as the Malabar illipe, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India. It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits that are rich in oils. Its fruit is widely used in the production of butter and margarine due to its high oil content.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
