Indigofera tetrasperma var. hexasperma

Indigofera tetrasperma var. hexasperma, commonly known as the six-seeded indigo plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often followed by small, bean-like pods containing multiple seeds. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
