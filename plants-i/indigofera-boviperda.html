Indigofera boviperda

Indigofera boviperda, commonly known as the African indigo or red indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It produces seeds enclosed in pods, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
