Icomum subacaule

Icomum subacaule, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in suitable habitats.

Icomum subacaule plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
