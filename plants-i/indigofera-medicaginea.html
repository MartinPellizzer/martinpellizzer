Indigofera medicaginea

Indigofera medicaginea, commonly known as medicinal indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture. Its seeds and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types makes it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
