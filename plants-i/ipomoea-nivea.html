Ipomoea nivea

Ipomoea nivea, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea nivea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
