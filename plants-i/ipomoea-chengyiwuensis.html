Ipomoea chengyiwuensis

Ipomoea chengyiwuensis, commonly known as the Chengyiwu morning glory, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and strong, twining stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and lesser-known species within the Ipomoea genus.
