Isotrema plagiostomum

Isotrema plagiostomum, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the spurge family. It produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common among Euphorbiaceae species.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including forests and grasslands.
