Ipomoea graminea

Ipomoea graminea, commonly known as the grass vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually opposite and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Ipomoea graminea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense foliage, which can make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
