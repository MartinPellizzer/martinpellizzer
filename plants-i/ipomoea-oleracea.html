Ipomoea oleracea

Ipomoea oleracea, commonly known as the morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces edible tuberous roots, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea oleracea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
