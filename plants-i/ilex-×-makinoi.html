Ilex × makinoi

Ilex × makinoi, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is a cross between Ilex crenata (dwarf holly) and Ilex polygama (Japanese holly), resulting in a plant with dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically glossy and oval-shaped, providing year-round visual interest in gardens. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds but not typically eaten by humans.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
