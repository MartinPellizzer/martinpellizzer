Inula argentea

Inula argentea, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, with uses in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's adaptability and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
