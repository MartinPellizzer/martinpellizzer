Iresine paniculata var. floridana

Iresine paniculata var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red or purple foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
