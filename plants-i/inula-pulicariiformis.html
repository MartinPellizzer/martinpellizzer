Inula pulicariiformis

Inula pulicariiformis, commonly known as the woolly inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitat.
