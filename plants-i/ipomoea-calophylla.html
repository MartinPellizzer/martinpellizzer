Ipomoea calophylla

Ipomoea calophylla, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ipomoea calophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
