Indigofera costata subsp. goniodes

Indigofera costata subsp.

goniodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
