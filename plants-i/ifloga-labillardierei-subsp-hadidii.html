Ifloga labillardierei subsp. hadidii

Ifloga labillardierei subsp.

hadidii, commonly known as the Hadid's sarsaparilla, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a climbing shrub characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
