Inula squarrosa subsp. spiraeifolia

Inula squarrosa subsp.

spiraeifolia, commonly known as the woolly inula, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green stems and basal leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native range.
