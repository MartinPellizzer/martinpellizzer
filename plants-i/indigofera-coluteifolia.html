Indigofera coluteifolia

Indigofera coluteifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it ecologically and agriculturally significant.
