Iberis sempervirens var. pseudosaxatilis

Iberis sempervirens var. pseudosaxatilis, commonly known as the rock iris, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features gray-green, woolly leaves that are adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
