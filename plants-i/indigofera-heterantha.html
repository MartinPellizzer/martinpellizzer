Indigofera heterantha

Indigofera heterantha, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring pinnate leaves and distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant also serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
