Iris × repanda

Iris × repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf iris, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Iris pallida and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its robust growth and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, and its flowering stems can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Iris × repanda is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
