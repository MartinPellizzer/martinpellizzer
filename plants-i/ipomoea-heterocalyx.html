Ipomoea heterocalyx

Ipomoea heterocalyx, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
