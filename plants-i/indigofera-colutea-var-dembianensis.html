Indigofera colutea var. dembianensis

Indigofera colutea var. dembianensis, commonly known as the Dembian indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
