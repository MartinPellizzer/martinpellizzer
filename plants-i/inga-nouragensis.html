Inga nouragensis

Inga nouragensis, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Inga nouragensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
