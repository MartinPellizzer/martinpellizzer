Ipomoea oblongata var. hirsuta

Ipomoea oblongata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver or purple. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, typically ranging in color from white to pale blue or violet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variant is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name.
