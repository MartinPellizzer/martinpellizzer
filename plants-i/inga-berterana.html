Inga berterana

Inga berterana, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "candy apple," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish or orange fruits containing a sweet, juicy pulp. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a significant species in both horticulture and local agriculture.
