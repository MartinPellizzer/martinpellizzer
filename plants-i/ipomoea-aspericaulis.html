Ipomoea aspericaulis

Ipomoea aspericaulis, commonly known as the roughleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms, often seen in the wild along riverbanks and in moist woodland areas. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like morning glories andIpomoea batatas.
