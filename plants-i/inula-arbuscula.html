Inula arbuscula

Inula arbuscula, commonly known as the nodding fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Inula arbuscula is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
