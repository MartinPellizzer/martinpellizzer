Inga calcicola

Inga calcicola, commonly known as the limestone inga, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

Inga calcicola is adapted to grow in calcium-rich soils, often found in rocky or limestone areas.
