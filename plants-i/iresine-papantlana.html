Iresine papantlana

Iresine papantlana, commonly known as the red leaf plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple in color. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often adorned with intricate patterns and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Iresine papantlana produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, which are followed by seed capsules.
