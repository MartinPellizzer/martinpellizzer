Isachne pauciflora var. hirsuta

Isachne pauciflora var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy isachne, is a variant of the isachne grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hirsuta" designation, which means hairy in Latin. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
