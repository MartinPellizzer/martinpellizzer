Ipomoea poranoides

Ipomoea poranoides, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces cylindrical, spiral-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, shady areas.

Ipomoea poranoides is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing habit and showy blooms.
