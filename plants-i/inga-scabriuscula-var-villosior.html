Inga scabriuscula var. villosior

Inga scabriuscula var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The hairy inga is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it useful in ecological restoration projects.
