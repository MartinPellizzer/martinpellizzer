Indigofera microstachya

Indigofera microstachya, commonly known as small-stalked indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment.
