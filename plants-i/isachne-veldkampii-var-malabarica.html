Isachne veldkampii var. malabarica

Isachne veldkampii var. malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid climates and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
