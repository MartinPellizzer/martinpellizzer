Iris × violivenosa

Iris × violivenosa, commonly known as the violet-flag iris, is a hybrid perennial plant that combines traits from various iris species.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting showy, violet-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, spurred petals that are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetlands and garden settings.

Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often a deep green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
