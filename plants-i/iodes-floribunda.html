Iodes floribunda

Iodes floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink and white. The plant has a slender, herbaceous stem and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iodes floribunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
