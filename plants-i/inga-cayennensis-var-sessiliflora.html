Inga cayennensis var. sessiliflora

Inga cayennensis var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the Cayenne Inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It produces legume pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
