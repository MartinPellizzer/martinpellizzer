Ischaemum glabriglaucum

Ischaemum glabriglaucum, commonly known as the bluegrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
