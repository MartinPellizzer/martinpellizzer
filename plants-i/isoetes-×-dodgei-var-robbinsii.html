Isoetes × dodgei var. robbinsii

Isoetes × dodgei var. robbinsii, commonly known as the Robbins' water starwort, is a rare aquatic fern that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a rosette-like formation on the upper surface of its leaves. The plant has a delicate, submerged stem with finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. It is native to North America and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
