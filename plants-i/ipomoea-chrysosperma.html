Ipomoea chrysosperma

Ipomoea chrysosperma, commonly known as the golden seed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It typically grows as a vine, climbing or trailing along the ground.

Ipomoea chrysosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
