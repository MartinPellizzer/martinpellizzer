Indigofera luzonensis

Indigofera luzonensis, commonly known as Luzon indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and racemose inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is often found in forest understories and is adapted to moist, shaded environments.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or animals.
