Ipomoea bulbocastana

Ipomoea bulbocastana, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant foliage, often featuring shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its tuberous roots are edible and are the primary reason for its cultivation.

Ipomoea bulbocastana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
