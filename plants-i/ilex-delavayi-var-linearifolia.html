Ilex delavayi var. linearifolia

Ilex delavayi var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
