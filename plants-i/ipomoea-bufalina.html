Ipomoea bufalina

Ipomoea bufalina, commonly known as the buffalo morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically trumpet-shaped. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea bufalina is also noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
