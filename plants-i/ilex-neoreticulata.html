Ilex neoreticulata

Ilex neoreticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex neoreticulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
