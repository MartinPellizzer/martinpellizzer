Iris spathacea

Iris spathacea, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, pale blue to white flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
