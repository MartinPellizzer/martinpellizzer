Ipomoea hungaiensis var. linifolia

Ipomoea hungaiensis var. linifolia, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its stems are hairy and can climb using tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
