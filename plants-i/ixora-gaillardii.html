Ixora gaillardii

Ixora gaillardii, commonly known as the red ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warm seasons. Its flowers are typically arranged in panicles, and the plant is known for its high ornamental value.

Ixora gaillardii is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
