Indigofera chamaeclada subsp. pubens

Indigofera chamaeclada subsp.

pubens, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
