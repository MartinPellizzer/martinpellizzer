Impatiens truncata

Impatiens truncata, commonly known as the cut-leaf impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, often lobed leaves that are typically truncate or notched at the base. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which are borne on slender stems. Impatiens truncata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
