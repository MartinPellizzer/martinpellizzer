Isolepis fuscata

Isolepis fuscata, commonly known as the brown tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in dense, tussock-like clusters, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Isolepis fuscata is often found in association with other wetland plants, playing a role in maintaining the ecological balance of these habitats.
