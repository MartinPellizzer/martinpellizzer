Impatiens serratifolia

Impatiens serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
