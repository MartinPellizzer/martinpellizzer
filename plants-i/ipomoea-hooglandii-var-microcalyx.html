Ipomoea hooglandii var. microcalyx

Ipomoea hooglandii var. microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or containers. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact size.
