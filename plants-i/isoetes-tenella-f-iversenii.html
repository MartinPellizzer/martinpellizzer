Isoetes tenella f. iversenii

Isoetes tenella f. iversenii, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a variant of the species Isoetes tenella, a member of the Lycophyta plant group.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and wetlands. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are arranged in clusters along the stem, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
