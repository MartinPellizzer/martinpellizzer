Indigofera nyassica var. brevior

Indigofera nyassica var. brevior, commonly known as African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are often used as a source of dye, historically significant in traditional practices.
