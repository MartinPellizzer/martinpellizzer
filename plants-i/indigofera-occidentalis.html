Indigofera occidentalis

Indigofera occidentalis, commonly known as western indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in regions of the southern United States and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
