Ipomoea barbatisepala

Ipomoea barbatisepala, commonly known as the hairy leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by five sepals that are hairy and resemble the petals.

Ipomoea barbatisepala is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
