Indigofera ormocarpoides

Indigofera ormocarpoides, commonly known as the orchid bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its fruits are pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
