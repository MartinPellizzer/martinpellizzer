Ipomoea virgata var. verbasciformis

Ipomoea virgata var. verbasciformis, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to purple, and its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It produces fruit that is a capsule containing numerous small, black seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
