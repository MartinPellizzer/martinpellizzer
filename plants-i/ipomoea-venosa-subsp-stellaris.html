Ipomoea venosa subsp. stellaris

Ipomoea venosa subsp.

stellaris, commonly known as the starry morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive central pattern.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using its tendrils to support itself.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the early morning, hence its common name, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
