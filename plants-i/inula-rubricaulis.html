Inula rubricaulis

Inula rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, erect stems that are reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Inula rubricaulis is typically found in grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
