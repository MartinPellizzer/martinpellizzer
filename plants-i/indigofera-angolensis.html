Indigofera angolensis

Indigofera angolensis, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodlands and savannas.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
