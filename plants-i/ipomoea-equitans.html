Ipomoea equitans

Ipomoea equitans, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, palmate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces cylindrical, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.
