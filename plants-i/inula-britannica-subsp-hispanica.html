Inula britannica subsp. hispanica

Inula britannica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, yellow flower heads.

The plant produces ray florets that are typically yellow, surrounded by a dense cluster of disc florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded locations.

It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
