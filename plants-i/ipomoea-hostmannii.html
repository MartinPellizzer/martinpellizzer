Ipomoea hostmannii

Ipomoea hostmannii, commonly known as the hostmann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ipomoea hostmannii is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and climbers.
