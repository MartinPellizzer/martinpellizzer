Iris planifolia

Iris planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf iris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Iris planifolia is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
