Ischaemum royleanum

Ischaemum royleanum, commonly known as the Indian dogstail, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Ischaemum royleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
