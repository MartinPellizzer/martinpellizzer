Isoetes melanopoda subsp. melanopoda

Isoetes melanopoda subsp.

melanopoda, commonly known as the black-stem watermace, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that grow in dense clusters, often forming a compact rosette.

The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in still or slow-moving waters with muddy or silty substrates. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and supporting diverse invertebrate life.
