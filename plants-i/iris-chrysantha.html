Iris chrysantha

Iris chrysantha, commonly known as the golden iris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to gold-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in meadows. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Iris chrysantha is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
