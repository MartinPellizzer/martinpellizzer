Ipomoea franciscana

Ipomoea franciscana, commonly known as the banana vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.

The fruit, which resembles a small banana, is edible and contains seeds.
