Isoloma pictum

Isoloma pictum, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Isoloma pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
