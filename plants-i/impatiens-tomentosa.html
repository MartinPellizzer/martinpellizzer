Impatiens tomentosa

Impatiens tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Impatiens tomentosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in shaded areas.
