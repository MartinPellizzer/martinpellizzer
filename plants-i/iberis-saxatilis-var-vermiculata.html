Iberis saxatilis var. vermiculata

Iberis saxatilis var. vermiculata, commonly known as the variegated rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal areas of Europe.

It features small, bright green, oval-shaped leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, typically between March and May. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its hardy nature and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for alpine and Mediterranean-style landscapes.
