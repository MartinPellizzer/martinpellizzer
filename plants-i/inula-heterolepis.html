Inula heterolepis

Inula heterolepis, commonly known as the roughleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are often lance-shaped, and it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Inula heterolepis is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
