Ixia fastigiata

Ixia fastigiata, commonly known as the upright ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ixia fastigiata prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in horticulture for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
