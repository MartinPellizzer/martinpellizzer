Ipomoea ledermannii

Ipomoea ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
