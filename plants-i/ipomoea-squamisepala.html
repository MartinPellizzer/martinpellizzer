Ipomoea squamisepala

Ipomoea squamisepala, commonly known as the scalycup morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are trailing or climbing. Ipomoea squamisepala typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
