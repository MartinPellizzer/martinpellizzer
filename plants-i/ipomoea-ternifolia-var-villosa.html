Ipomoea ternifolia var. villosa

Ipomoea ternifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or violet, typically opening in the morning. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its scientific name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
