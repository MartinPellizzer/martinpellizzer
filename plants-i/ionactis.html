Ionactis

Ionactis, commonly known as the tansy or goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their yellow, daisy-like flowers and aromatic leaves. Ionactis species typically grow in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. They have hairy stems and leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

The flowers are usually borne in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators including bees and butterflies.
