Inga calanthoides

Inga calanthoides, commonly known as the pink inga, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
