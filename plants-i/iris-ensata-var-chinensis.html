Iris ensata var. chinensis

Iris ensata var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese iris, is a species native to East Asia, particularly China.

It is a perennial herb with tall, upright stems that bear showy, lavender-blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a dense rosette at the base.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
