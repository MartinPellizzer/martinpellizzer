Isoetes tamaulipana

Isoetes tamaulipana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing, spiky structures called strobila, which are typically found in freshwater environments. This plant is native to parts of Mexico and is adapted to grow in shallow, still waters. Its morphology includes a rosette of leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface.

Isoetes tamaulipana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
