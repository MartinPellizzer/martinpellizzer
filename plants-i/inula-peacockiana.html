Inula peacockiana

Inula peacockiana, commonly known as the peacock flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features a tall, branched stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is the showy, yellow-orange flower head, which resembles a peacock's tail due to its intricate patterns.

Inula peacockiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
