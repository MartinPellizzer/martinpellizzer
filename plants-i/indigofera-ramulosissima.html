Indigofera ramulosissima

Indigofera ramulosissima, commonly known as the African indigo or bush bean, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
