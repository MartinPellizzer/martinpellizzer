Impatiens sahyadrica

Impatiens sahyadrica, commonly known as the Western Ghats impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant features showy, colorful flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive spur. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Impatiens sahyadrica plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
