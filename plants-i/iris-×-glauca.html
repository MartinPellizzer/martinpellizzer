Iris × glauca

Iris × glauca, commonly known as the Blue Flag Iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, pond edges, and moist soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its sword-shaped leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette.

Iris × glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
