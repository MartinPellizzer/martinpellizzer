Ilex crenata f. bullata

Ilex crenata f. bullata, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are slightly wavy along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Ilex crenata f. bullata prefers partial shade to full sun and is commonly used in landscaping for its neat, formal shape.
