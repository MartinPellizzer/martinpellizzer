Itheta breviseta

Itheta breviseta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Itheta breviseta is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
