Indigofera brevipes

Indigofera brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds.

It is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
