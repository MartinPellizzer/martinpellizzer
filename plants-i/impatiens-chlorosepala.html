Impatiens chlorosepala

Impatiens chlorosepala, commonly known as the white-flowered impatiens, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The flowers are showy, with five petals that are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
