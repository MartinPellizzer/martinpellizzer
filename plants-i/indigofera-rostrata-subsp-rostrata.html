Indigofera rostrata subsp. rostrata

Indigofera rostrata subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
