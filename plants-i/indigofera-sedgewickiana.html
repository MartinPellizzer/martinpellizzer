Indigofera sedgewickiana

Indigofera sedgewickiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre indigo, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system helps fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
