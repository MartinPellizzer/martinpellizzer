Indigofera adonensis

Indigofera adonensis, commonly known as the yellow indigo or Adon's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savannah habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally for its dye-producing properties and ornamental value.
