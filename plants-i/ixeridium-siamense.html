Ixeridium siamense

Ixeridium siamense, commonly known as Siam's feather grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green or whitish inflorescences that resemble plumes, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Ixeridium siamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
