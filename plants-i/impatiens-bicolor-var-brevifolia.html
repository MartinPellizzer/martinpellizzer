Impatiens bicolor var. brevifolia

Impatiens bicolor var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of bright pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with short stems and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
