Indosasa albohispidula

Indosasa albohispidula, commonly known as the white-hairy indosasa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often pale or white, with prominent stamens that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
