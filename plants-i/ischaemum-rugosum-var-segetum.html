Ischaemum rugosum var. segetum

Ischaemum rugosum var. segetum, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems that often exhibit a reddish tinge. The leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive hairy texture along their surfaces. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet cluster, with small, two-ranked florets that are typically pale green to brown in color.

This plant is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
