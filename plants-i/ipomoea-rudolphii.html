Ipomoea rudolphii

Ipomoea rudolphii, commonly known as Rudolph's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, coiled tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea rudolphii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
