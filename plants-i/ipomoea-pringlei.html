Ipomoea pringlei

Ipomoea pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
