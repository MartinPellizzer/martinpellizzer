Ipomoea cymosa var. macra

Ipomoea cymosa var. macra, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from violet to blue in color, with a trumpet-shaped corolla. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a vine or trailing plant.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it popular in gardens and as a decorative plant.
