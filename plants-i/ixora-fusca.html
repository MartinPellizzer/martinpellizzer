Ixora fusca

Ixora fusca, commonly known as the purple flame or red flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its inflorescence consists of cymes of small, showy flowers that attract birds and insects.

Ixora fusca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
