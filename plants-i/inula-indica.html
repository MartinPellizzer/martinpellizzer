Inula indica

Inula indica, commonly known as the Indian elecampane, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in Ayurvedic practices for respiratory and digestive ailments.
