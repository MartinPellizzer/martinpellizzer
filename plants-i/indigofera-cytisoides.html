Indigofera cytisoides

Indigofera cytisoides, commonly known as the tropical indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It produces small, hard pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
