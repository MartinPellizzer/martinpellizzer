Ixora grandiflora

Ixora grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
