Iris paropamisensis

Iris paropamisensis, commonly known as the Paropamisus iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
