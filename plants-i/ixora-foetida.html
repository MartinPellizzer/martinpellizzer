Ixora foetida

Ixora foetida, commonly known as the stinkwood or sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Ixora foetida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
