Iris ensata var. pallasii

Iris ensata var. pallasii, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers feature six to nine petals, with a distinctive three-parted beak-like structure at the tip. This variety is known for its vibrant colors, ranging from deep blues and purples to whites and yellows.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
