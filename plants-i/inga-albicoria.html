Inga albicoria

Inga albicoria, commonly known as the white-veined inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

Inga albicoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
