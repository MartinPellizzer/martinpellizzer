Impatiens lilacina

Impatiens lilacina, commonly known as the lilac impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to lilac-colored flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has heart-shaped, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can extend from spring through early autumn.
