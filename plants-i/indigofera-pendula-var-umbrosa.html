Indigofera pendula var. umbrosa

Indigofera pendula var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow indigo or umbrosa climbing indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and a woody stem that can become quite long. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Indigofera genus, which are known for their medicinal and dye-producing properties.
