Indigofera filiformis

Indigofera filiformis, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
