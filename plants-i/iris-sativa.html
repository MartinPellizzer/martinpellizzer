Iris sativa

Iris sativa, commonly known as the bearded iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves and showy, three-dimensional flowers with distinct standards, wings, and falls. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Iris sativa is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in landscape design.
