Isachne truncata var. crispa

Isachne truncata var. crispa, commonly known as the curled isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, curling leaf blades that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

The curled isachne is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
