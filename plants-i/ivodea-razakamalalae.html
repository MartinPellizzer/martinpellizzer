Ivodea razakamalalae

Ivodea razakamalalae, commonly known as the Madagascar ivy, is a climbing plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
