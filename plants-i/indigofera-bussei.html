Indigofera bussei

Indigofera bussei, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
