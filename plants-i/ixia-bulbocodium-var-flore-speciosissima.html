Ixia bulbocodium var. flore-speciosissima

Ixia bulbocodium var. flore-speciosissima, commonly known as the showy ixia, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant's botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a bulb that serves as its storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic display to the landscape.
