Isoloma ovatum

Isoloma ovatum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, ovate leaves that are typically green with variegated patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, tuberous roots. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Isoloma ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and as a source of nutritious root vegetables.
