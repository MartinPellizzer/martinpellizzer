Ipomoea villifera

Ipomoea villifera, commonly known as the silky leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Ipomoea villifera is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
