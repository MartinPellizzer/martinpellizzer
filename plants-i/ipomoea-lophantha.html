Ipomoea lophantha

Ipomoea lophantha, commonly known as the "Cape jasmine" or "Lophantha morning glory," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, shiny seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Ipomoea lophantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
