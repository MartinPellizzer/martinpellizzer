Ipomoea fragrans

Ipomoea fragrans, commonly known as sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that come in various colors such as red, purple, and green. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a sweet fragrance. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant or for its edible tubers.

Ipomoea fragrans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
