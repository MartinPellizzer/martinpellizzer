Iochroma ayabacense

Iochroma ayabacense, commonly known as the Ayabacense bellflower, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Iochroma ayabacense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
