Ipomoea lenis

Ipomoea lenis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is a vine that typically grows along the ground, with tendrils that help it climb and spread. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea lenis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
