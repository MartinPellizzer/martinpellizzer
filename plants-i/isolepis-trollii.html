Isolepis trollii

Isolepis trollii, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Isolepis trollii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
