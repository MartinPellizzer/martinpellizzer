Ipomoea maximiliani

Ipomoea maximiliani, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
