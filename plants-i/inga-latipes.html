Inga latipes

Inga latipes, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Inga latipes is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and ornamental features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.
