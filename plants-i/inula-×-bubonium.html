Inula × bubonium

Inula × bubonium, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, often branched stems and large, yellow composite flowers. The plant produces rough, ribbed, and hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a grayish-green color due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Inula × bubonium is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
