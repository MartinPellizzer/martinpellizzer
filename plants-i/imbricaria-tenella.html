Imbricaria tenella

Imbricaria tenella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas due to its adaptability.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
