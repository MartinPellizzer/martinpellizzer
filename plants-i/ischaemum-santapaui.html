Ischaemum santapaui

Ischaemum santapaui, commonly known as the Santa Paula grass, is a perennial grass species native to California.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, cylindrical spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Ischaemum santapaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
