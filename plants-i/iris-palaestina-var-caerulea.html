Iris palaestina var. caerulea

Iris palaestina var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue iris of Palestine, is a striking perennial herb native to the Middle East.

It features sword-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional gardens.
