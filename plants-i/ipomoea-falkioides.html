Ipomoea falkioides

Ipomoea falkioides, commonly known as Falk's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch and have been used by indigenous peoples for food. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
