Ipomoea pandurata var. cuspidata

Ipomoea pandurata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the cuspidate morning glory, is a striking vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces distinctive, spirally coiled seed pods that contain elongated, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
