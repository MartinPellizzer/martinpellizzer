Inga spuria f. racemaria

Inga spuria f. racemaria, commonly known as the racemose inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
