Indigofera alopecuroides var. alopecuroides

Indigofera alopecuroides var. alopecuroides, commonly known as the small indigo bush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of violet to blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in restoration projects.

The plant contains compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
