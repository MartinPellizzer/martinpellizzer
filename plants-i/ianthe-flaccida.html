Ianthe flaccida

Ianthe flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Ianthe flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
