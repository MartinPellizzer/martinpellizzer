Ilex aquifolium var. recurva

Ilex aquifolium var. recurva, commonly known as the weeping Holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its distinctive pendulous growth habit.

This plant features elongated, curved leaves that hang in cascading clusters, creating a unique visual effect. The foliage is typically dark green with spiny margins, typical of the holly genus. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds.

The weeping Holly is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
