Indigofera strobilifera

Indigofera strobilifera, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in dry woodlands and savannas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
