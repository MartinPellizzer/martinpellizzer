Inula thapsoides

Inula thapsoides, commonly known as yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering heads are composite, consisting of numerous small florets arranged in a spherical shape.

Inula thapsoides is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
