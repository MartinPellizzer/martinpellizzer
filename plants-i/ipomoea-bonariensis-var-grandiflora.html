Ipomoea bonariensis var. grandiflora

Ipomoea bonariensis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces large, showy flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white. The flowers open in the morning and close at night, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
