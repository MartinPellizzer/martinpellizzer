Ipomoea patula var. villosa

Ipomoea patula var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its vines can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
