Inula hetrusca

Inula hetrusca, commonly known as the Italian elecampane, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, sturdy stem with grayish-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly serrated. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single, showy head, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and can thrive in poor soils.
