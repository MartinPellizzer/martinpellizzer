Impatiens acaulis

Impatiens acaulis, commonly known as the slender impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Impatiens acaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
