Ipomoea lawii

Ipomoea lawii, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
