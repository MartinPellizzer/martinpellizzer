Ixocactus inconspicuus

Ixocactus inconspicuus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ixocactus inconspicuus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
