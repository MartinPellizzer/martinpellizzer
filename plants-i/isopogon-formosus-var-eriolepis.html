Isopogon formosus var. eriolepis

Isopogon formosus var. eriolepis, commonly known as the silky isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
