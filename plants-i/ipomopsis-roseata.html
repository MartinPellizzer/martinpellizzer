Ipomopsis roseata

Ipomopsis roseata, commonly known as the roseate phlox.

It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in areas with rocky soils and open habitats. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has linear, grass-like leaves.

Ipomopsis roseata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
