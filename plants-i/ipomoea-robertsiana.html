Ipomoea robertsiana

Ipomoea robertsiana, commonly known as the Roberts' morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
