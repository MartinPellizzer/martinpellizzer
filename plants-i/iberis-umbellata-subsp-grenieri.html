Iberis umbellata subsp. grenieri

Iberis umbellata subsp.

grenieri, commonly known as the Alpine candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that produces dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, lobed leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
