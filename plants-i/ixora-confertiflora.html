Ixora confertiflora

Ixora confertiflora, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright orange-red flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ixora confertiflora is also valued for its ability to attract birds and butterflies to the garden.
