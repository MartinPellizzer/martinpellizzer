Iris kaempferi blumei

Iris kaempferi blumei, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive blackish-purple throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring sword-shaped, dark green leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, three-petaled flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening.

Iris kaempferi blumei prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for water gardens and shaded borders.
