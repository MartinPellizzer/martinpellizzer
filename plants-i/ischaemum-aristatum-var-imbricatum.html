Ischaemum aristatum var. imbricatum

Ischaemum aristatum var. imbricatum, commonly known as the bristly barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, bristly texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and an important species in ecological studies.
