Isachne albens var. hirsuta

Isachne albens var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, silver-colored foliage and hairy texture on its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
