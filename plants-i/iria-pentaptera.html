Iria pentaptera

Iria pentaptera, commonly known as the five-winged iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Iria pentaptera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
