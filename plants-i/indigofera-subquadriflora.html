Indigofera subquadriflora

Indigofera subquadriflora, commonly known as the small-flowered indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
