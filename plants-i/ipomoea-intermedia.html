Ipomoea intermedia

Ipomoea intermedia, commonly known as the morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and foliage.

However, it can become invasive in some areas if not properly managed.
