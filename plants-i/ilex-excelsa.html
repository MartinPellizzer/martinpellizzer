Ilex excelsa

Ilex excelsa, commonly known as the giant holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible for birds. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It can reach heights of up to 15 meters, making it one of the tallest species in the Ilex genus.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable plant in its native habitats.
