Ilex asprella

Ilex asprella, commonly known as the roughleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Ilex asprella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
