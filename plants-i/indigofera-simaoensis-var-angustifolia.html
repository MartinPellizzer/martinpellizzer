Indigofera simaoensis var. angustifolia

Indigofera simaoensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed indigo plant, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
