Indigofera pilifera

Indigofera pilifera, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
