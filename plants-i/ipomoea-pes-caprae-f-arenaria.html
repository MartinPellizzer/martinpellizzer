Ipomoea pes-caprae f. arenaria

Ipomoea pes-caprae f. arenaria, commonly known as beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a trailing herb that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal dunes and shores. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically sprawling and resilient.

This species is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought, making it a hardy coastal plant.
