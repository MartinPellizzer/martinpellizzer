Ipomoea bracteata var. viridibracta

Ipomoea bracteata var. viridibracta, commonly known as the green bract morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to orange as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical properties remain under study.
