Islaya paucispinosa

Islaya paucispinosa, commonly known as the spiny leaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in coastal and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology makes it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
