Ixora brachyura

Ixora brachyura, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora brachyura is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
