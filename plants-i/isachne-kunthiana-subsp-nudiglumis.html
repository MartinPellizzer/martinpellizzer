Isachne kunthiana subsp. nudiglumis

Isachne kunthiana subsp.

nudiglumis, commonly known as the slender flatgrass, is a grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and compact growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced glumes and shorter lemma hairs compared to other subspecies of Isachne kunthiana.
