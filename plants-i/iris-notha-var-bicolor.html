Iris notha var. bicolor

Iris notha var. bicolor, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear striking, showy flowers with distinct coloration. The flowers typically have a blue-violet upper petal and a white or pale yellow lower petal, creating a bicolor effect. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
