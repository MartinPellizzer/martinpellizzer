Iberis bursa-pastoris

Iberis bursa-pastoris, commonly known as wild candytuft, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers resembling candytuft. The plant has a mat-forming habit with dark green, glossy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
