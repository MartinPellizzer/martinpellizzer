Impatiens pseudoacaulis

Impatiens pseudoacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are short and somewhat succulent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
