Inga schimpffii

Inga schimpffii, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
