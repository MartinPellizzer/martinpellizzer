Ixia plicata

Ixia plicata, commonly known as the zigzag lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many Amaryllis species. Ixia plicata blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its unique, zigzag-shaped petals are a distinguishing feature of the species.
