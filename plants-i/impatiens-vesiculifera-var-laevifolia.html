Impatiens vesiculifera var. laevifolia

Impatiens vesiculifera var. laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and thrives in humid climates.
