Isachne stigmatosa

Isachne stigmatosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Isachne stigmatosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
