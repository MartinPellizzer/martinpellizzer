Iphigeniopsis flexuosa

Iphigeniopsis flexuosa, commonly known as the waxy leaf orchid, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
