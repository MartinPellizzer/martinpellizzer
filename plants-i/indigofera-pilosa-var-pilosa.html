Indigofera pilosa var. pilosa

Indigofera pilosa var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
