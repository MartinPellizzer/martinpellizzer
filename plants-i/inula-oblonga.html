Inula oblonga

Inula oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features tall, upright stems covered in fine hairs, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and form a conical cluster, resembling a small crown.

Inula oblonga has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
