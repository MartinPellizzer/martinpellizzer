Ischaemum petiolare

Ischaemum petiolare, commonly known as the slender grass or narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Ischaemum petiolare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
