Inga appendiculata

Inga appendiculata, commonly known as the red-veined inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
