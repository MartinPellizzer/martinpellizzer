Ichnanthus serratus

Ichnanthus serratus, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive serrated edge on the petals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Ichnanthus serratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
