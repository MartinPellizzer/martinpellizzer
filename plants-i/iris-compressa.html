Iris compressa

Iris compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Iris compressa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
