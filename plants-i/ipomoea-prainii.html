Ipomoea prainii

Ipomoea prainii, commonly known as the Prain's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep blue. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Ipomoea prainii is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, using its tendrils to support its growth.
