Ipomoea fastigiata var. pauciflora

Ipomoea fastigiata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb or trail along supports.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to gardens and green spaces.
