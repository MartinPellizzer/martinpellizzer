Impatiens zenkeri

Impatiens zenkeri, commonly known as the Zenker's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant often thrives in shaded, moist environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners in subtropical regions.
