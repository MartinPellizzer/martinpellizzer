Indigofera zornioides

Indigofera zornioides, commonly known as the African indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces small, dark blue to purple pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
