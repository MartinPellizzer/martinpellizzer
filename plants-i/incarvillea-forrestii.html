Incarvillea forrestii

Incarvillea forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
