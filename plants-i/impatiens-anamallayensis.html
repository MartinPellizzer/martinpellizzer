Impatiens anamallayensis

Impatiens anamallayensis, commonly known as the Anamallay hills impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its region.
