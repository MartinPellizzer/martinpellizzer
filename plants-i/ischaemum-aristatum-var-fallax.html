Ischaemum aristatum var. fallax

Ischaemum aristatum var. fallax, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, dunes, and along coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
