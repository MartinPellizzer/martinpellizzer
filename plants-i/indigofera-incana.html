Indigofera incana

Indigofera incana, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Indigofera incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in dyeing and medicinal applications.
