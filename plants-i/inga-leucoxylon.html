Inga leucoxylon

Inga leucoxylon, commonly known as the white-wood inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants that often form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. The tree is characterized by its smooth, white bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
