Inga cochlocarpa

Inga cochlocarpa, commonly known as the purple-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria in its root nodules. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various animals.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
