Ipomoea lottiae

Ipomoea lottiae, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using tendrils.
