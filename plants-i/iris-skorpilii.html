Iris skorpilii

Iris skorpilii, commonly known as the Balkan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a dense basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Iris skorpilii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
