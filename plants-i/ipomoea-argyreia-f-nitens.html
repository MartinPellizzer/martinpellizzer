Ipomoea argyreia f. nitens

Ipomoea argyreia f. nitens, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a variant of the silver leaf vine, characterized by its striking white and silver variegated foliage.

This plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia. It produces large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are trailing and can climb if supported.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
