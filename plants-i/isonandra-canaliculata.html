Isonandra canaliculata

Isonandra canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with distinct, shallow grooves along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
