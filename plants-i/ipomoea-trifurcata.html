Ipomoea trifurcata

Ipomoea trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and bloom in shades of violet or blue. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
