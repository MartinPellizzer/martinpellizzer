Indigofera hendecaphylla var. acutifolia

Indigofera hendecaphylla var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
