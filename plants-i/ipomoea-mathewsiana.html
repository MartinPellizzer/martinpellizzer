Ipomoea mathewsiana

Ipomoea mathewsiana, commonly known as the Mathews's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures. Its flowers are usually violet or blue in color, with a distinctive pattern of darker veins.

Ipomoea mathewsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
