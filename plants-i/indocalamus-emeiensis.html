Indocalamus emeiensis

Indocalamus emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and have a smooth texture. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and conservation purposes.
