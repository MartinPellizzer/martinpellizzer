Indigofera cordifolia

Indigofera cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and dense foliage.
