Inula pseudoensiformis

Inula pseudoensiformis, commonly known as the false featherflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Inula pseudoensiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
