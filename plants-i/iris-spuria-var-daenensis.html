Iris spuria var. daenensis

Iris spuria var. daenensis, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features upright, slender stems that bear strikingly blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.
