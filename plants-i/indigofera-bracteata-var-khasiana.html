Indigofera bracteata var. khasiana

Indigofera bracteata var. khasiana, commonly known as Khasi indigo, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
