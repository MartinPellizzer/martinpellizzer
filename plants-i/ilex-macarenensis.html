Ilex macarenensis

Ilex macarenensis, commonly known as the Canary Island holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, glossy leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Ilex macarenensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
