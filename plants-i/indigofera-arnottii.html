Indigofera arnottii

Indigofera arnottii, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often hairy.

Indigofera arnottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
