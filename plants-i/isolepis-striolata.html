Isolepis striolata

Isolepis striolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly ribbed, with a dense, tufted growth habit. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Isolepis striolata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
