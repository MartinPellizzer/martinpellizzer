Isolepis fuscescens

Isolepis fuscescens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and dark purple or reddish stems that distinguish it from other grasses. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous, with flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils.

Isolepis fuscescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
