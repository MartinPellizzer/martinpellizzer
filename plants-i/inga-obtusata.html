Inga obtusata

Inga obtusata, commonly known as the woolly pod oak, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a woody structure with smooth, often hairless bark, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Inga obtusata produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity.
