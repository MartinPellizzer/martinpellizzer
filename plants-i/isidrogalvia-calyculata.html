Isidrogalvia calyculata

Isidrogalvia calyculata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Isidrogalvia calyculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
