Iodes cirrhosa

Iodes cirrhosa, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that often have a pale green or white hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Iodes cirrhosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
