Impatiens pandurata

Impatiens pandurata, commonly known as the horned impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like calyces that enclose its brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
