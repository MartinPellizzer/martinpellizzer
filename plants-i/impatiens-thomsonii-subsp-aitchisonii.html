Impatiens thomsonii subsp. aitchisonii

Impatiens thomsonii subsp.

aitchisonii, commonly known as Thomson's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
