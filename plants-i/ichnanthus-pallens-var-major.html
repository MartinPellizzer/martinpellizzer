Ichnanthus pallens var. major

Ichnanthus pallens var. major, commonly known as the large white orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
