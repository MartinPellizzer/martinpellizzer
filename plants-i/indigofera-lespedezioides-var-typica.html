Indigofera lespedezioides var. typica

Indigofera lespedezioides var. typica, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that resemble mist. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
