Ipomoea pileata subsp. uniflora

Ipomoea pileata subsp.

uniflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its vines can reach lengths of several meters, making it a vigorous climber.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
