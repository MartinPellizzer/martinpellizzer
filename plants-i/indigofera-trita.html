Indigofera trita

Indigofera trita, commonly known as small indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Indigofera trita is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
