Inga ellipsoidea

Inga ellipsoidea, commonly known as the pink-veined inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces showy, purple-pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in forest regeneration.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, enhancing soil fertility in its natural habitat.
