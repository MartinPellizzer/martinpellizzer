Indigofera roxburghii

Indigofera roxburghii, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant is known for its historical use in producing natural indigo dye, which was widely used in textile industries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
