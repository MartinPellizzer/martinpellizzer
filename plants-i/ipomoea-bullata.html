Ipomoea bullata

Ipomoea bullata, commonly known as the bullate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea bullata is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.
