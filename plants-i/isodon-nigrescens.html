Isodon nigrescens

Isodon nigrescens, commonly known as black lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant. Its flowering spikes produce small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color.

Isodon nigrescens is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
