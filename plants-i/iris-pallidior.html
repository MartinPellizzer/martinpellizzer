Iris pallidior

Iris pallidior, commonly known as the pale iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in early to mid-summer.

Iris pallidior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
