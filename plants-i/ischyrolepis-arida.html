Ischyrolepis arida

Ischyrolepis arida, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
