Impatiens nummulariifolia

Impatiens nummulariifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
