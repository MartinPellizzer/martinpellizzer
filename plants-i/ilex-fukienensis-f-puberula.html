Ilex fukienensis f. puberula

Ilex fukienensis f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent Chinese holly, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are typically pubescent, or covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Aquifoliaceae family.
