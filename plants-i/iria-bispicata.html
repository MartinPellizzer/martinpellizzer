Iria bispicata

Iria bispicata, commonly known as the two-horned iris, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features two prominent, pointed bracts that give it its common name. Iria bispicata typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in marshy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
