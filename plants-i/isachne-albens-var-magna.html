Isachne albens var. magna

Isachne albens var. magna, commonly known as the giant silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stems and silvery-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
