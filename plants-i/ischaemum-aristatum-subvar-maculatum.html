Ischaemum aristatum subvar. maculatum

Ischaemum aristatum subvar. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its foliage is typically green with distinct reddish or purplish markings, especially on the lower leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.
