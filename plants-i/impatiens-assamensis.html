Impatiens assamensis

Impatiens assamensis, commonly known as the Assam balsam, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and undergrowth, and prefers well-drained soil. It has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Impatiens assamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
