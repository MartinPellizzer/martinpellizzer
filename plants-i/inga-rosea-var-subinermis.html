Inga rosea var. subinermis

Inga rosea var. subinermis, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and is often found in disturbed or secondary forests.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, aiding in its propagation in suitable habitats.
