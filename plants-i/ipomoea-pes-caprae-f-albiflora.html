Ipomoea pes-caprae f. albiflora

Ipomoea pes-caprae f. albiflora, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy soils and saline environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces large, showy white flowers with a distinctive red calyx. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

It is often found growing along shorelines and in coastal dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
