Indigofera subargentea var. shinyangensis

Indigofera subargentea var. shinyangensis, commonly known as the Shinyang indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
