Iris unifolia

Iris unifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than an inch wide. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Iris unifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
