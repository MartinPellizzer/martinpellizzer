Ipomoea splendens

Ipomoea splendens, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found twining around supports or other vegetation. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, while its stems are hairy and can grow quite long.

Ipomoea splendens is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
