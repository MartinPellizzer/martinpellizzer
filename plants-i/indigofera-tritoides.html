Indigofera tritoides

Indigofera tritoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
