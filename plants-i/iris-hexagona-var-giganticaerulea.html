Iris hexagona var. giganticaerulea

Iris hexagona var. giganticaerulea, commonly known as the Blue Flag Iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are sword-shaped and can reach lengths of up to three feet, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is notable for its striking coloration and is often cultivated in water gardens or as a landscape plant.
