Impatiens tenerrima

Impatiens tenerrima, commonly known as the hanging widow or velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers.

Impatiens tenerrima is also known for its rapid growth and ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season.
