Iris kaempferi var. seraph

Iris kaempferi var. seraph, commonly known as the Seraph iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of six to eight petals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, upright rosette.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
