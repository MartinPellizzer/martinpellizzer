Isoetes libanotica

Isoetes libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a spiral pattern along the central axis of the plant. This plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in still or slow-moving water, and is often found in marshy or swampy areas. Its morphology includes a central axis with leaf-like structures called stichodromes, which are often modified into spore-bearing organs.

Isoetes libanotica plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
