Ixora acuminatissima

Ixora acuminatissima, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which are clustered in branched, showy spikes. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora acuminatissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
