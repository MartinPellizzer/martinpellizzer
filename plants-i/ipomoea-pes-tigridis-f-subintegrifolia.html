Ipomoea pes-tigridis f. subintegrifolia

Ipomoea pes-tigridis f. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a variant of the species Ipomoea pes-tigridis, characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to Mexico and is known for its vibrant, often purple or blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves that have a smooth, uninterrupted margin, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative element in horticulture.
