Isatis sevangensis

Isatis sevangensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese woad, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and thrives in moist, fertile soils. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct mustard odor. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in racemes, typical of the mustard family.

Isatis sevangensis is sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties, though its use is less common compared to other members of the genus.
