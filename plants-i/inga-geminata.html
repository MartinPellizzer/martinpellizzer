Inga geminata

Inga geminata, commonly known as the twin-leaf inga, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with two opposite leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Inga geminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
