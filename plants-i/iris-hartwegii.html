Iris hartwegii

Iris hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, three-parted petals with a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural diversity.
