Indigofera aspalathoides

Indigofera aspalathoides, commonly known as the African indigo or sweet senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
