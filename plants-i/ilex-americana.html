Ilex americana

Ilex americana, commonly known as the American holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright red berries that persist through winter. The leaves are typically glossy, dark green, and oval in shape, with a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
