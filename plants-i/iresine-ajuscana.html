Iresine ajuscana

Iresine ajuscana, commonly known as the red-rooted ironweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple to red foliage, which is often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Iresine ajuscana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be used in landscape design to add visual interest.
