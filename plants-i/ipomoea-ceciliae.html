Ipomoea ceciliae

Ipomoea ceciliae, commonly known as Cecilia's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are slender with tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along garden borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral displays.
