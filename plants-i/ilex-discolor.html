Ilex discolor

Ilex discolor, commonly known as the variegated holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that display a striking variegation of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex discolor thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in gardens and along woodland edges.
