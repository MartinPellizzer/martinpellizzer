Ipomoea asterophora var. subglabra

Ipomoea asterophora var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
