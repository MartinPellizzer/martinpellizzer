Ipomoea lapathifolia

Ipomoea lapathifolia, commonly known as the "Ladyslipper morning glory," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a climbing vine that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in the morning, hence its common name, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea lapathifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
