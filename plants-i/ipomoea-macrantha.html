Ipomoea macrantha

Ipomoea macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Ipomoea macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
