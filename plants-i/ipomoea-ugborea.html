Ipomoea ugborea

Ipomoea ugborea, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Ipomoea ugborea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils near forests or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
