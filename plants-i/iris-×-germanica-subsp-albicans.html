Iris × germanica subsp. albicans

Iris × germanica subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a subspecies of the bearded iris characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring sword-shaped leaves and showy, upright blooms. It is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.

The subspecies is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
