Ipomoea scrobiculata

Ipomoea scrobiculata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Ipomoea scrobiculata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
