Isoetes pringlei

Isoetes pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's water moss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a central axis with spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. This plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located at the base of the plant, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Isoetes pringlei is notable for its ability to thrive in still water, making it an important indicator species for certain aquatic habitats.
