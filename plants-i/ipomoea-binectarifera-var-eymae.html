Ipomoea binectarifera var. eymae

Ipomoea binectarifera var. eymae, commonly known as the Eyma's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces bright blue or violet flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
