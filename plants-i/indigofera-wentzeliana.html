Indigofera wentzeliana

Indigofera wentzeliana, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
