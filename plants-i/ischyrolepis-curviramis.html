Ischyrolepis curviramis

Ischyrolepis curviramis, commonly known as the curly ram's horn fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, curling fronds that resemble the shape of a ram's horn, giving it its common name. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its unique morphology makes it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of its fronds.
