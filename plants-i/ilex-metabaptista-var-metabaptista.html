Ilex metabaptista var. metabaptista

Ilex metabaptista var. metabaptista, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
