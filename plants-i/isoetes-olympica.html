Isoetes olympica

Isoetes olympica, commonly known as the Olympic water clover, is a rare and ancient species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, lacy foliage that resembles a clover leaf, with each frond consisting of multiple small, ribbon-like segments. This plant thrives in still, shallow freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its spore-bearing stalks. Isoetes olympica is notable for its ability to survive in low-nutrient conditions, making it an indicator species for oligotrophic waters.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern in certain regions.
