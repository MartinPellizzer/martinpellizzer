Inga coerulescens

Inga coerulescens, commonly known as the blue inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
