Impatiens eriospenna

Impatiens eriospenna, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Impatiens eriospenna is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
