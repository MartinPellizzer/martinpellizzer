Ilex aquifolium subsp. caspia

Ilex aquifolium subsp.

caspia, commonly known as the Caspian holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caspian Sea region. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are usually opposite and have a spiny margin.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in local habitats.
