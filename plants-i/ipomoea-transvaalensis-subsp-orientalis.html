Ipomoea transvaalensis subsp. orientalis

Ipomoea transvaalensis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Transvaal morning glory, is a flowering vine native to southern Africa. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in the morning.

The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in gardens.
