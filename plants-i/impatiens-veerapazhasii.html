Impatiens veerapazhasii

Impatiens veerapazhasii, commonly known as the Veerapazha impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the monsoon season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
