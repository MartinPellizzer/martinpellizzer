Indigofera nephrocarpa

Indigofera nephrocarpa, commonly known as the kidneyleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with kidney-shaped leaflets and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
