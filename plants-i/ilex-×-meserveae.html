Ilex × meserveae

Ilex × meserveae, commonly known as the Meserve holly, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ilex opaca (American holly) and Ilex verticillata (common winterberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its bright red berries and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value, particularly during the winter months when its berries are visible.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate partial shade.
