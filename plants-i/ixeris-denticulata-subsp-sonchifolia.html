Ixeris denticulata subsp. sonchifolia

Ixeris denticulata subsp.

sonchifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
