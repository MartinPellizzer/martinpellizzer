Ipomoea tigripes

Ipomoea tigripes, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea tigripes is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
