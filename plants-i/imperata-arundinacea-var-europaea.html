Imperata arundinacea var. europaea

Imperata arundinacea var. europaea, commonly known as European bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
