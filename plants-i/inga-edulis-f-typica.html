Inga edulis f. typica

Inga edulis f. typica, commonly known as the "ice cream bean," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain edible, sweet, cream-colored seeds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces a high quantity of seeds that can be dispersed by animals.

The tree is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
