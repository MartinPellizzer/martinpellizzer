Ipomoea batatas f. trifida

Ipomoea batatas f. trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed sweet potato, is a cultivated variant of the sweet potato plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves, which set it apart from the typical sweet potato foliage. The plant produces tuberous roots that are edible and often used in various culinary preparations. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its nutritional value.
