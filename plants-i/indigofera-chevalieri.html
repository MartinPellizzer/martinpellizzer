Indigofera chevalieri

Indigofera chevalieri, commonly known as the African indigo or chevalier's indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
