Iria pierotii

Iria pierotii, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers that resemble a slipper in shape. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iria pierotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
