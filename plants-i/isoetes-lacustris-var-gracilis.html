Isoetes lacustris var. gracilis

Isoetes lacustris var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender water clubmoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that form dense, ribbon-like structures, typically growing in shallow, freshwater habitats. The plant is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes, where it can thrive in muddy or sandy substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered at the base of the plant, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing aquatic environments and supporting microfauna.
