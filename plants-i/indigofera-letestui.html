Indigofera letestui

Indigofera letestui, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces seed pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
