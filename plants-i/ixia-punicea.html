Ixia punicea

Ixia punicea, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. Ixia punicea thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
