Irania umbellata

Irania umbellata, commonly known as the pink-flowered ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody structure and is often found in humid, lowland forests. The plant produces small, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Irania umbellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
