Inula encelioides

Inula encelioides, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and a prominent, spiky central disk. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
