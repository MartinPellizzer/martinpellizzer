Iris hookeri f. zonalis

Iris hookeri f. zonalis, commonly known as the zonal iris, is a hybrid form of the iris genus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant blue or violet color with a white or yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces its flowers on tall, slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and wetland areas.
