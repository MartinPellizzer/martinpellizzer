Ixora guineensis

Ixora guineensis, commonly known as the Guinea flame or red flame, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers with five petals, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ixora guineensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban settings.
