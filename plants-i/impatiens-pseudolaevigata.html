Impatiens pseudolaevigata

Impatiens pseudolaevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are usually smooth and glossy, with a slightly ovate shape. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
