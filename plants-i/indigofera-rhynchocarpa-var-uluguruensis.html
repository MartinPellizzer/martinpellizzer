Indigofera rhynchocarpa var. uluguruensis

Indigofera rhynchocarpa var. uluguruensis, commonly known as the Uluguru indigo.

It is a shrub native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain seeds with a hard, dark coat. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
