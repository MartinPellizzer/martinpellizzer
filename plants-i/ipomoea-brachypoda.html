Ipomoea brachypoda

Ipomoea brachypoda, commonly known as the shortleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed sites.

Ipomoea brachypoda is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
