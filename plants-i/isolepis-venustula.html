Isolepis venustula

Isolepis venustula, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in temperate regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a compact, cylindrical cluster, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Isolepis venustula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
