Ipomoea daturiflora

Ipomoea daturiflora, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a fragrant scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
