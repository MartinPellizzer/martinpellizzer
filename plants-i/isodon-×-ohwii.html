Isodon × ohwii

Isodon × ohwii, commonly known as the Japanese blue grass, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Isodon × ohwii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
