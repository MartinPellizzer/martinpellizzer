Ipomoea mucronifolia

Ipomoea mucronifolia, commonly known as the blunt-tipped morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, sunny environments. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored capsule that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea mucronifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive if not controlled.
