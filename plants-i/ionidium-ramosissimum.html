Ionidium ramosissimum

Ionidium ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has slender, woody stems covered in fine, hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
