Inga interrupta

Inga interrupta, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Inga interrupta is also valued for its rapid growth and use in agroforestry systems.
