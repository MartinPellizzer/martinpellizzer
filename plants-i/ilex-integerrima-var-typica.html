Ilex integerrima var. typica

Ilex integerrima var. typica, commonly known as the California coffeeberry, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
