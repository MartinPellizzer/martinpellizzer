Isoetes junciformis

Isoetes junciformis, commonly known as the slender watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its spiky, grass-like appearance and is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a unique morphology with a central axis surrounded by leaf-like structures called filaments. Isoetes junciformis is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, produce spores that are dispersed by water, aiding in its propagation.
