Iris camillae f. elegans

Iris camillae f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers with a white throat and a prominent yellow beard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
