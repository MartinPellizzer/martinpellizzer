Ipomoea argentinica

Ipomoea argentinica, commonly known as the Argentine morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and twining habit. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by birds and insects.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
