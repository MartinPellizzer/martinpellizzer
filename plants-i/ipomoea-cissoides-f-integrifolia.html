Ipomoea cissoides f. integrifolia

Ipomoea cissoides f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, smooth-edged leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a trailing vine that produces showy blooms during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
