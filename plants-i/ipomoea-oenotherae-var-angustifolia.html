Ipomoea oenotherae var. angustifolia

Ipomoea oenotherae var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinct wavy margin and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, often at dawn, and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
