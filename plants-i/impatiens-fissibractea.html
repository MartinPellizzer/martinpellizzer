Impatiens fissibractea

Impatiens fissibractea, commonly known as the fissured bract impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
