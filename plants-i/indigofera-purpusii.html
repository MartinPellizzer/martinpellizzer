Indigofera purpusii

Indigofera purpusii, commonly known as the Hawaiian indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria.

Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters and bloom throughout the year, making it an important plant in Hawaiian ecosystems.
