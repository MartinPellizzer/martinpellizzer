Iris violacea

Iris violacea, commonly known as the violet flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive black center, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and it grows up to 2 feet tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

The iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
