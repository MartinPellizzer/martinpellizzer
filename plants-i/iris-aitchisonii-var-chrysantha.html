Iris aitchisonii var. chrysantha

Iris aitchisonii var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
