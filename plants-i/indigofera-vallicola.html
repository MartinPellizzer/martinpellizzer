Indigofera vallicola

Indigofera vallicola, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
