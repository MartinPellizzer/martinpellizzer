Ipomoea rugosa

Ipomoea rugosa, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, vibrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces showy blooms that open in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
