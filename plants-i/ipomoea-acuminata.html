Ipomoea acuminata

Ipomoea acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms, often at dawn, and has a rapid growth habit. Its tuberous roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is its primary appeal.

Ipomoea acuminata thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
