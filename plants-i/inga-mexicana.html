Inga mexicana

Inga mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, spreading canopy, and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is commonly found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
