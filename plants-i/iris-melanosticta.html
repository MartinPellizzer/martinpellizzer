Iris melanosticta

Iris melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with dark purple markings, which are particularly prominent on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
