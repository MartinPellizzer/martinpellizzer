Iris pumila var. barthiiformis

Iris pumila var. barthiiformis, commonly known as the Bartheliiform iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue or violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
