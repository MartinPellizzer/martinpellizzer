Ilex serrata var. nipponica

Ilex serrata var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its hardy nature and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
