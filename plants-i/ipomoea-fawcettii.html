Ipomoea fawcettii

Ipomoea fawcettii, commonly known as the Fawcett's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a vigorous climber with large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a rapid growth habit, often reaching heights of several meters. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea fawcettii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to cover trellises or fences.
