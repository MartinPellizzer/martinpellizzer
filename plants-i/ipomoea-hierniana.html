Ipomoea hierniana

Ipomoea hierniana, commonly known as the Hiern's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers, which bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its vines can climb and spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Ipomoea hierniana is also notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
