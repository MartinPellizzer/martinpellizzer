Ipomoea dasysperma

Ipomoea dasysperma, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
