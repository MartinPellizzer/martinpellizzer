Iberis spathulata

Iberis spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis spathulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
