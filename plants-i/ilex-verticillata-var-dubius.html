Ilex verticillata var. dubius

Ilex verticillata var. dubius, commonly known as the dwarf winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that persist through the winter, providing visual interest and food for wildlife. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, turning bright red in the fall before dropping.

This variety is a popular choice for ornamental planting due to its seasonal color changes and ecological benefits.
