Ischaemum raui

Ischaemum raui, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Ischaemum raui is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
