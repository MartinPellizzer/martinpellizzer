Ixiolirion tataricum var. ledebourii

Ixiolirion tataricum var. ledebourii, commonly known as the Tatarian bulbous grass, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in the summer. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
