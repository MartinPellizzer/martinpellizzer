Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. hepaticifolia

Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. hepaticifolia, commonly known as the liverleaf morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral displays.
