Inula britannica var. longilepis

Inula britannica var. longilepis, commonly known as the British Inula, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or grassy areas. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, producing numerous small florets arranged in a composite head.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
