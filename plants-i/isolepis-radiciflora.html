Isolepis radiciflora

Isolepis radiciflora, commonly known as the root-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, triangular stems and small, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually borne at the base of the plant, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isolepis radiciflora plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
