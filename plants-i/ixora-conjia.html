Ixora conjia

Ixora conjia, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Ixora conjia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
