Iris chrysographes

Iris chrysographes, commonly known as the golden iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive golden beard on the lower petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
