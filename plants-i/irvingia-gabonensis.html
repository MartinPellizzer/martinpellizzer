Irvingia gabonensis

Irvingia gabonensis, commonly known as African mango, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, rough bark and large, leathery leaves. The fruit of Irvingia gabonensis is a fibrous, yellowish pod containing several seeds. The plant is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and for their high oil content.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
