Ipomoea tambelensis

Ipomoea tambelensis, commonly known as the Tambelensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant, showy blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically during the early morning hours. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea tambelensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
