Ipomoea mekongensis

Ipomoea mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
