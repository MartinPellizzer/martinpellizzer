Ipomeria

Ipomeria, commonly known as the "buttercup flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers and succulent, often hairy leaves. Ipomeria species typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to dry conditions. The leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette, which helps conserve moisture.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant colors and unique foliage.
