Isolepis latifolia

Isolepis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Isolepis latifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
