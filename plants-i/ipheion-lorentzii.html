Ipheion lorentzii

Ipheion lorentzii, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers with white markings. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Ipheion lorentzii is a hardy species that is relatively easy to maintain in garden settings.
