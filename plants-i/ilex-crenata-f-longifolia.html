Ilex crenata f. longifolia

Ilex crenata f. longifolia, commonly known as the Japanese holly or variegated holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with white or yellow margins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense growth habit and year-round foliage make it an excellent specimen plant for gardens and landscapes.
