Ipomoea trichocarpa f. albiflora

Ipomoea trichocarpa f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory characterized by its pale white to almost completely white flowers.

This cultivar belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and has a rough, hairy texture, while the stems are twining and can climb or trail. The flowers are typically smaller than those of the species and bloom in the early morning, opening as the sun rises.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
