Ilysanthes dubia var. inundata

Ilysanthes dubia var. inundata, commonly known as the flooded ilysanthes, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that float on the water surface, often in clusters. It thrives in nutrient-rich, slow-moving freshwater environments.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, it is considered endangered in many regions.
