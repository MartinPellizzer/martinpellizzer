Ibidium crystalligerum

Ibidium crystalligerum, commonly known as the crystal leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that resemble glass, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
