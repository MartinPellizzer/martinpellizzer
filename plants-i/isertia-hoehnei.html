Isertia hoehnei

Isertia hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's isertia, is a rare and striking plant native to the rocky areas of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its arid habitat. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Isertia hoehnei is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
