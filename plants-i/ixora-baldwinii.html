Ixora baldwinii

Ixora baldwinii, commonly known as the flame flower or crimson flame, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora baldwinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
