Ipomoea kerinciensis

Ipomoea kerinciensis, commonly known as the Kerinci morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms during the day. It is often found in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
