Icacina oliviformis

Icacina oliviformis, commonly known as the oliveleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in a hard, woody pod, which is a common feature among members of the legume family.
