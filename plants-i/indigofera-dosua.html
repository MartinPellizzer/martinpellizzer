Indigofera dosua

Indigofera dosua, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional dyeing practices and as a source of medicinal compounds.
