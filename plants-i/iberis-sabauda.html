Iberis sabauda

Iberis sabauda, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has bright green, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis sabauda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
