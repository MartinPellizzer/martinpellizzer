Inula helenium subsp. helenium

Inula helenium subsp.

helenium, commonly known as yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by showy, blue-violet bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
