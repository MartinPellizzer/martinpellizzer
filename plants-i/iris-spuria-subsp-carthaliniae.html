Iris spuria subsp. carthaliniae

Iris spuria subsp.

carthaliniae, commonly known as the Carthaginian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
