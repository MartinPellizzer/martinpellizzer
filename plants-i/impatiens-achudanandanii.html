Impatiens achudanandanii

Impatiens achudanandanii, commonly known as the Nilgiri impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
