Ipomoea bicolor

Ipomoea bicolor, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant is a herbaceous vine that can climb using tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea bicolor is also notable for its rapid growth and blooming habits, with flowers opening in the morning and closing at dusk.
