Ipomoea digitata var. quinquefida

Ipomoea digitata var. quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea digitata var. quinquefida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
