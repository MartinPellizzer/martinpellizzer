Ipomoea perpartita

Ipomoea perpartita, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Ipomoea perpartita is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vigorously, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions.
