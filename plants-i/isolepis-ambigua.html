Isolepis ambigua

Isolepis ambigua, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.
