Inga asplenioides

Inga asplenioides, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its shade-providing properties.
