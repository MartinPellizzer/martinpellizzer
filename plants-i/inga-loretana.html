Inga loretana

Inga loretana, commonly known as the Loretana acacia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in green or reddish clusters, while its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods.

The tree is valued for its fast growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in reforestation efforts.
