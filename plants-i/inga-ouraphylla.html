Inga ouraphylla

Inga ouraphylla, commonly known as the white-leaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, and its flowers are typically small and clustered. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
