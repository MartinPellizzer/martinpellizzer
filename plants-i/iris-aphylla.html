Iris aphylla

Iris aphylla, commonly known as the sand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, strap-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Iris aphylla is a member of the Iridaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
