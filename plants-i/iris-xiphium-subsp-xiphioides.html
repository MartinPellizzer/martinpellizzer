Iris xiphium subsp. xiphioides

Iris xiphium subsp.

xiphioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming period, usually in spring.
