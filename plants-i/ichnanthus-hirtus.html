Ichnanthus hirtus

Ichnanthus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy twayblade, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

Ichnanthus hirtus is a perennial plant that plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
