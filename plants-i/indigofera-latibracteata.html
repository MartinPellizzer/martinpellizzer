Indigofera latibracteata

Indigofera latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
