Ipomoea ochracea var. curtissii

Ipomoea ochracea var. curtissii, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is typically found in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and woodland edges. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by bees and other pollinators.
