Isodon interruptus

Isodon interruptus, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and can be used to make tea.

Isodon interruptus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
