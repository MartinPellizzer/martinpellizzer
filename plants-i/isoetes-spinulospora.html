Isoetes spinulospora

Isoetes spinulospora, commonly known as the spiny-spored watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, spiky formations along the leaf blades. Isoetes spinulospora thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and reproductive strategy make it an important subject in botanical studies.
