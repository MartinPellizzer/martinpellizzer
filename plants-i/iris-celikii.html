Iris celikii

Iris celikii, commonly known as the Turkish iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Iris celikii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
