Isodon irroratus

Isodon irroratus, commonly known as the silverleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and have been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Isodon irroratus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
