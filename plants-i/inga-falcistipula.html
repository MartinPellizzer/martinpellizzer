Inga falcistipula

Inga falcistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.
