Indigofera simaoensis var. macrophylla

Indigofera simaoensis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved indigofera, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while the seeds may have various applications in local industries.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
