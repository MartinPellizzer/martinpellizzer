Ipomoea cristulata

Ipomoea cristulata, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces unique, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea cristulata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
