Indigofera miniata var. florida

Indigofera miniata var. florida, commonly known as the Florida indigo or Florida false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local pollinators.
