Iris dubia

Iris dubia, commonly known as the doubtful iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring grass-like basal leaves and showy blue to violet flowers with three upright petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
