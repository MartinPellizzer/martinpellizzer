Indigofera bainesii

Indigofera bainesii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
