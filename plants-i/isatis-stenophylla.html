Isatis stenophylla

Isatis stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf woad, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale blue. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it has cultural and ecological significance in its native range.
