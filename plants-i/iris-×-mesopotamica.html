Iris × mesopotamica

Iris × mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the wetlands of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, sword-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
