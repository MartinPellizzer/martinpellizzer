Ilex crenata f. fastigiata

Ilex crenata f. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright Japanese holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

Its leaves are glossy, dark green, and have a slightly wavy margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. This cultivar is prized for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds but not typically eaten by humans.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
