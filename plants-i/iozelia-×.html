Iozelia ×

Iozelia ×, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between species of the Iozelia genus, which belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structures, including specialized petals and sepals that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Iozelia × thrives in humid, well-drained environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in horticultural breeding and the study of orchid genetics.
