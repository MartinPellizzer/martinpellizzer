Ipomoea urbica var. muricata

Ipomoea urbica var. muricata, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a common ornamental and invasive species in many regions.
