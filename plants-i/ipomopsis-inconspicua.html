Ipomopsis inconspicua

Ipomopsis inconspicua, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a member of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, particularly in the western United States. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are trailing or climbing.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
