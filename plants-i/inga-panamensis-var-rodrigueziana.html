Inga panamensis var. rodrigueziana

Inga panamensis var. rodrigueziana, commonly known as the Panama inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.

Its rapid growth makes it a useful species for reforestation and soil stabilization.
