Iris tenorei

Iris tenorei, commonly known as the Tenore's iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in early summer, producing three to five petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps anchor the plant in its rocky habitat.

Iris tenorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and unique coloration.
