Indigofera disjuncta var. fallax

Indigofera disjuncta var. fallax, commonly known as the Mexican indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and can grow up to several meters in height. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.
