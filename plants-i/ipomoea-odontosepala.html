Ipomoea odontosepala

Ipomoea odontosepala, commonly known as the toothed-sepaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces elongated, spiral-shaped vines. Its flowers have distinctive toothed sepals that give it its scientific name.

Ipomoea odontosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
