Ipomoea obscura var. indica

Ipomoea obscura var. indica, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The vine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb supports or trellises as it grows.
