Inga pubescens

Inga pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Inga pubescens is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, contributing to its ecological versatility.
