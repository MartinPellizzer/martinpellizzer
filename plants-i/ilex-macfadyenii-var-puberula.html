Ilex macfadyenii var. puberula

Ilex macfadyenii var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a pubescent texture. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
