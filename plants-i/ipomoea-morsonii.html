Ipomoea morsonii

Ipomoea morsonii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea morsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
