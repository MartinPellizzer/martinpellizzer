Iris maricoides

Iris maricoides, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, three-lobed seed pods.

Iris maricoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
