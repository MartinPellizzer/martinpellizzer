Inga strigillosa

Inga strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Inga strigillosa is a deciduous tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
