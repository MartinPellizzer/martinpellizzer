Ipomoea pterodes

Ipomoea pterodes, commonly known as the winged morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea pterodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
