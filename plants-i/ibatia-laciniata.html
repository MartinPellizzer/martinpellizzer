Ibatia laciniata

Ibatia laciniata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria laciniata*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellow or orange drupes, which are edible and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
