Ipomoea villosa var. genuina

Ipomoea villosa var. genuina, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found climbing structures such as fences or trees. Its flowers typically open in the early morning, giving it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
