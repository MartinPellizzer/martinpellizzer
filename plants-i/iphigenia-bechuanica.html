Iphigenia bechuanica

Iphigenia bechuanica, commonly known as the Bechuanian daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, hairy leaves and has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Iphigenia bechuanica is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
