Indigofera falcata var. pubescens

Indigofera falcata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sicklepod, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive sickle-shaped pods that are hairy in texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
