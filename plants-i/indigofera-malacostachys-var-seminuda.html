Indigofera malacostachys var. seminuda

Indigofera malacostachys var. seminuda, commonly known as the slender indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with slender stems and a compact form.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
