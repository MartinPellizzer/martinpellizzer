Isolepis rubicunda

Isolepis rubicunda, commonly known as the red tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with bright red to purple flower spikes that emerge from leafy basal rosettes. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, forming extensive mats in moist soils.

Isolepis rubicunda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
