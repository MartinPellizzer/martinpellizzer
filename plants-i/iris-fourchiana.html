Iris fourchiana

Iris fourchiana, commonly known as the Italian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive black central stripe, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the base. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

Iris fourchiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
