Intsia attenuata

Intsia attenuata, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and long, slender pods. The tree produces a valuable nut known as shea butter, which is widely used in cosmetics and food products. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, seasonal forests.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
