Ilex cornuta f. typica

Ilex cornuta f. typica, commonly known as the horned holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved spines on the stems and leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and moist, shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a humid climate.
