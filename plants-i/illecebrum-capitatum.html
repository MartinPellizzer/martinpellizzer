Illecebrum capitatum

Illecebrum capitatum, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and stems that root at the nodes.

It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
