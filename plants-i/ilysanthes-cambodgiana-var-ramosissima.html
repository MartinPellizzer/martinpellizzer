Ilysanthes cambodgiana var. ramosissima

Ilysanthes cambodgiana var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Cambodian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
