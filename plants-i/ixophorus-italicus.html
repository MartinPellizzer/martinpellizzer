Ixophorus italicus

Ixophorus italicus, commonly known as the Italian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ditches, and pond margins. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
