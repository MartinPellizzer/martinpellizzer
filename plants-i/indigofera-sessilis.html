Indigofera sessilis

Indigofera sessilis, commonly known as the sessile indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
