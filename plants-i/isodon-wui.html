Isodon wui

Isodon wui, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional Chinese medicine.

The leaves and flowers are used to make teas and tinctures, believed to have calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
