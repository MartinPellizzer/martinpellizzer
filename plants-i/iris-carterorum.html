Iris carterorum

Iris carterorum, commonly known as Carter's iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Iris carterorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
