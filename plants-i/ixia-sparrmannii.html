Ixia sparrmannii

Ixia sparrmannii, commonly known as the yellow fire lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
