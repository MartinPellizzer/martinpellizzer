Impatiens biflora f. citrina

Impatiens biflora f. citrina, commonly known as the yellow-flowered impatiens, is a variant of the species Impatiens biflora.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces two flowers per node, a feature typical of the genus Impatiens.

The cultivar is popular in horticulture for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
