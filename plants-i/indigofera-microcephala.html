Indigofera microcephala

Indigofera microcephala, commonly known as small-headed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it a notable species in ecological studies of tropical flora.
