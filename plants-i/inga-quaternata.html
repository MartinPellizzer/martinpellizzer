Inga quaternata

Inga quaternata, commonly known as the four-leaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with four leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

The tree also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
