Inula euxina

Inula euxina, commonly known as the Crimean Inula, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Inula euxina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
