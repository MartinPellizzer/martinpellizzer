Isoetes ekmanii

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a central umbel. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually long and ribbon-like, and it lacks true roots, relying instead on rhizomes for anchoring and nutrient absorption.
