Iris transtagana

Iris transtagana, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters at the top of the stem.

Iris transtagana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and architectural form.
