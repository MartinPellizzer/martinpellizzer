Inula adenophylla

Inula adenophylla, commonly known as the woolly inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
