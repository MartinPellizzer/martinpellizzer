Impatiens calycina

Impatiens calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or forest understory habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
