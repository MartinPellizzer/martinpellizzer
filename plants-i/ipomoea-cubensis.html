Ipomoea cubensis

Ipomoea cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth habit, often climbing or trailing along supports. Its flowers are typically blue, violet, or white, and it is known for its strong fragrance.

Ipomoea cubensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
