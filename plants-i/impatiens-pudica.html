Impatiens pudica

Impatiens pudica, commonly known as the "blushing bride" or "touch-me-not," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a distinctive hood-like structure over the reproductive organs.

Impatiens pudica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
