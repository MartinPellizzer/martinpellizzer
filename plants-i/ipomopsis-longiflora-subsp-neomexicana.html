Ipomopsis longiflora subsp. neomexicana

Ipomopsis longiflora subsp.

neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico long-flower. It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, particularly in New Mexico and parts of Arizona.

The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
