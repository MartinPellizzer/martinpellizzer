Ilysanthes clausa

Ilysanthes clausa, commonly known as the closed-flower ilysanthe, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, clustered flowers that remain closed for most of the year. The plant produces pale green to yellowish flowers that open only under specific environmental conditions, such as prolonged exposure to cool temperatures. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a dense rosette, which helps it conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its restricted distribution and unique flowering behavior, Ilysanthes clausa is considered a subject of interest in botanical research.
