Inga tenuifolia

Inga tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Inga tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
