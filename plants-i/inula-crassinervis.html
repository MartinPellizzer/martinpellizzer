Inula crassinervis

Inula crassinervis, commonly known as the roughleaf inula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves that are often lobed or serrated. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem.

Inula crassinervis is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
