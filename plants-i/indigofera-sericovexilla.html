Indigofera sericovexilla

Indigofera sericovexilla, commonly known as the velvetpod indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, velvety pods that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Indigofera sericovexilla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
