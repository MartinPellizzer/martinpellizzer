Ipomoea willdenowii

Ipomoea willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence its common name.
