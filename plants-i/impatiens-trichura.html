Impatiens trichura

Impatiens trichura, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a herbaceous flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and often covered with fine, short hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
