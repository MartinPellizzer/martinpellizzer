Indigofera binderi

Indigofera binderi, commonly known as the African indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has been used traditionally for its dye-producing properties, with its flowers and leaves used to create blue dyes.

The plant also serves as a food source for various insects and birds.
