Isodon namikawanus

Isodon namikawanus, commonly known as the Japanese green tea plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional tea-making.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and herbal medicine.
