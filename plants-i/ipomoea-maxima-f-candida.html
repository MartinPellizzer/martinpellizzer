Ipomoea maxima f. candida

Ipomoea maxima f. candida, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a cultivated variant of the common morning glory.

It is characterized by its large, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, dark green leaves and a robust stem that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
