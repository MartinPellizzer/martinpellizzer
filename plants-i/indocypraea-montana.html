Indocypraea montana

Indocypraea montana, commonly known as the mountain cowrie, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Cypraeidae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy shell that is typically brown or yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that vary among individuals. The shell is elongated and slightly spiral, with a distinct lip that is often thicker than the rest of the shell. This species is primarily found in tropical and subtropical marine environments, particularly in the Indo-Pacific region.

Its habitat includes coral reefs and rocky shores, where it feeds on algae and detritus.
