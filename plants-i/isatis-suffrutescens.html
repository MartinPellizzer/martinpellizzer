Isatis suffrutescens

Isatis suffrutescens, commonly known as Chinese yellow dock, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, round, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
