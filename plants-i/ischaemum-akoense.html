Ischaemum akoense

Ischaemum akoense, commonly known as the African dogstail grass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and grasslands. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence is a dense, cylindrical spikelet, characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Ischaemum akoense is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
