Isoetes tripus

Isoetes tripus, commonly known as the spikemoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its distinctive spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a spiky, cone-like formation. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it can anchor itself in the substrate. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, and they are often submerged or floating.

Isoetes tripus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
