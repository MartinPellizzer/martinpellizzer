Indigofera acutifolia

Indigofera acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
