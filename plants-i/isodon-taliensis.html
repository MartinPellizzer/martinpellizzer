Isodon taliensis

Isodon taliensis, commonly known as Taliensian toothbrush, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of small, tubular flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
