Iris robinsoniana

Iris robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a basal rosette, while its flowering stems rise above the foliage.

Iris robinsoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
