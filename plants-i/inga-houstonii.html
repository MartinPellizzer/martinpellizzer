Inga houstonii

Inga houstonii, commonly known as the Houston inga, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly in regions with humid climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, and the tree is often found in forest understories.

Inga houstonii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
