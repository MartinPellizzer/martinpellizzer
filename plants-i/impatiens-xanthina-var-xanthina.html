Impatiens xanthina var. xanthina

Impatiens xanthina var. xanthina, commonly known as the yellow water impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces showy, single flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
