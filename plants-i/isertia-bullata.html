Isertia bullata

Isertia bullata, commonly known as the bullate isertia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow petals and a central disk, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in horticultural settings.
