Indigofera inhambanensis

Indigofera inhambanensis, commonly known as the Inhambane indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and savanna habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
