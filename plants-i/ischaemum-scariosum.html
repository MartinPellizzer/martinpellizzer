Ischaemum scariosum

Ischaemum scariosum, commonly known as the scarious barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikelets that are often loosely arranged. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and agricultural fields, and is often found in association with other grasses. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Ischaemum scariosum is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with a fibrous root system that aids in its survival in various soil conditions.
