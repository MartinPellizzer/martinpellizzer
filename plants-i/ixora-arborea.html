Ixora arborea

Ixora arborea, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near riverbanks.

Ixora arborea is also valued for its ornamental use in horticulture and has cultural significance in various regions.
