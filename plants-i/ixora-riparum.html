Ixora riparum

Ixora riparum, commonly known as the river ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows near water sources, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ixora riparum is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
