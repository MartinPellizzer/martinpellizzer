Ipomoea obtusiloba var. tridens

Ipomoea obtusiloba var. tridens, commonly known as the trident leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain regions.
