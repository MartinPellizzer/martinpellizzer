Indigofera henryi var. silvarum

Indigofera henryi var. silvarum, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
