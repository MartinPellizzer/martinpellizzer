Indigofera argentea var. caerulea

Indigofera argentea var. caerulea, commonly known as silver indigo or blue indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it may require well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
