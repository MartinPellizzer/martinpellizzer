Isatis stenocarpa

Isatis stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited woad, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by narrow, elongated seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, rough in texture, and have a distinctive blue-green color.

Isatis stenocarpa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
