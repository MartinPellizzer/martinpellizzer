Indocalamus amplexicaulis

Indocalamus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance. Its rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a hardy and resilient species.

The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a food source in certain regions.
