Iresine celosioides var. eriophylla

Iresine celosioides var. eriophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf iron plant, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vivid, bi-colored leaves that often display a deep red on one side and a striking purple or green on the other. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
