Indigofera subargentea

Indigofera subargentea, commonly known as silverleaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which give it its common name, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in poor soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it ecologically significant in certain ecosystems.
