Indigofera rotundifolia

Indigofera rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into several lobes, and its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its dye-producing potential in some regions.
