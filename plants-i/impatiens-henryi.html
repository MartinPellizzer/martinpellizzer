Impatiens henryi

Impatiens henryi, commonly known as Henry's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Impatiens henryi is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
