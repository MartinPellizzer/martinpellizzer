Indigofera laotica

Indigofera laotica, commonly known as Laos indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
