Ilex sugerokii f. brevipedunculata

Ilex sugerokii f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked holly, is a variant of the Ilex genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a desirable addition to shaded landscapes.
