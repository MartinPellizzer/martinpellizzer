Ilex suaveolens var. sterrophylla

Ilex suaveolens var. sterrophylla, commonly known as the sweet-scented holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a humid climate.
