Ixora calycina

Ixora calycina, commonly known as the flame-of-the_forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape. Ixora calycina thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
