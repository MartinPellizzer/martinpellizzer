Ischaemum mastrucatum

Ischaemum mastrucatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf foxtail, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become a weed in cultivated areas.
