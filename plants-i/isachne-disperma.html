Isachne disperma

Isachne disperma, commonly known as the scattered grass or slender silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a silver-gray sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Isachne disperma is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
