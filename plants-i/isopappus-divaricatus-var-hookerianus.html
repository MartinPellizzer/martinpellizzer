Isopappus divaricatus var. hookerianus

Isopappus divaricatus var. hookerianus, commonly known as Hooker's spreading pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and capitula structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

This variety is distinguished by its more open growth habit compared to the typical form of Isopappus divaricatus.
