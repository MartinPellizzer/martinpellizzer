Illecebrum bengalense

Illecebrum bengalense, commonly known as the Bengal creeper, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
