Inula lanuginosa

Inula lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
