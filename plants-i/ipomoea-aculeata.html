Ipomoea aculeata

Ipomoea aculeata, commonly known as the spiny morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive if not controlled.
