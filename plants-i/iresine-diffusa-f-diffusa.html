Iresine diffusa f. diffusa

Iresine diffusa f. diffusa, commonly known as redleaf sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, often displaying shades of red, purple, and green. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually pink or red, which bloom during the warm season.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
