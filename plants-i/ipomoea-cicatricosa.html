Ipomoea cicatricosa

Ipomoea cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers open in the morning, hence its common name. Ipomoea cicatricosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous flowering habit.
