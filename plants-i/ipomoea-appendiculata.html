Ipomoea appendiculata

Ipomoea appendiculata, commonly known as the trailing morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea appendiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
