Isolepis novae-zealandiae

Isolepis novae-zealandiae, commonly known as New Zealand flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its rhizomatous growth habit.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
