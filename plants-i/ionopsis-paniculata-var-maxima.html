Ionopsis paniculata var. maxima

Ionopsis paniculata var. maxima, commonly known as the giant spiderwort, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with long, arching stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish tinge at the base.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.
