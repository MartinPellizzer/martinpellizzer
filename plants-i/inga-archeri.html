Inga archeri

Inga archeri, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. Inga archeri is native to Central and South America and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

It has a shallow root system and is used in some regions for its timber and as a source of food for certain wildlife.
