Ipomoea polymorpha f. canescens

Ipomoea polymorpha f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory genus characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

This plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
