Iris songarica var. gracilis

Iris songarica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
