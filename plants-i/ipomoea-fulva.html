Ipomoea fulva

Ipomoea fulva, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces cylindrical, hairy stems that often climb or trail. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ipomoea fulva is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
