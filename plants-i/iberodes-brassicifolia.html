Iberodes brassicifolia

Iberodes brassicifolia, commonly known as the Brassicaceae leafed plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in natural habitats.
