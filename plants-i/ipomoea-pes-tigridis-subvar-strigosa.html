Ipomoea pes-tigridis subvar. strigosa

Ipomoea pes-tigridis subvar. strigosa, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep violet. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture. This subvariety is distinguished by its distinctive, zigzagging veins on the leaves.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
