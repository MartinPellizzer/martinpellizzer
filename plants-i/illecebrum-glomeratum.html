Illecebrum glomeratum

Illecebrum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of streams. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The vine-like stems can climb or trail, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments.
