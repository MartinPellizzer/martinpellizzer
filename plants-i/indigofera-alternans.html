Indigofera alternans

Indigofera alternans, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. It produces elongated pods containing several bean-like seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
