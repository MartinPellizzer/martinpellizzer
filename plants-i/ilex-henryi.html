Ilex henryi

Ilex henryi, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Ilex henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its year-round visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
