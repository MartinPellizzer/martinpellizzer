Ipheion luzula

Ipheion luzula, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in native plant gardens.
