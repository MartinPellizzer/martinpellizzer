Ipomoea diriadactylina

Ipomoea diriadactylina, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winding stems that often form a spiral pattern as they grow. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Ipomoea diriadactylina is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.
