Indigofera kingiana

Indigofera kingiana, commonly known as the king's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
