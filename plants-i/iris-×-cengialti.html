Iris × cengialti

Iris × cengialti, commonly known as the hybrid bearded iris, is a cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features three upright petals, known as standards, and two drooping petals, called falls, which are often marked with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and landscapes.

This hybrid is valued for its hardiness and ability to produce vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
