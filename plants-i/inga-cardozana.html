Inga cardozana

Inga cardozana, commonly known as the "pink inga," is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Inga cardozana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
