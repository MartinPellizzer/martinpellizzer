Indigofera tulearensis

Indigofera tulearensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo plant, is a flowering shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
