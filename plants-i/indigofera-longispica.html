Indigofera longispica

Indigofera longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated spines on its stems and its clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
