Iris persica var. isaacsonii

Iris persica var. isaacsonii, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with three outer petals and three inner sepals, often in shades of blue, violet, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
