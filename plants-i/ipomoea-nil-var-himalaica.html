Ipomoea nil var. himalaica

Ipomoea nil var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
