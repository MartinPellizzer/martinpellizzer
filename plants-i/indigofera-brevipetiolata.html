Indigofera brevipetiolata

Indigofera brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled indigofera, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves with short petioles. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
