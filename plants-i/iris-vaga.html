Iris vaga

Iris vaga, commonly known as the wandering iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its common name.

Iris vaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
