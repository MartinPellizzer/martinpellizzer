Iris grandis

Iris grandis, commonly known as the giant iris, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, supporting large, showy flowers with six petals. The flowers are typically blue or purple in color, with a distinctive three-part beard on the lower petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Iris grandis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and wetland habitats.
