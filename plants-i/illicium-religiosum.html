Illicium religiosum

Illicium religiosum, commonly known as the sacred anise, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped flowers and narrow, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. Illicium religiosum is also valued for its cultural significance in certain Asian traditions.
