Indigofera sphinctosperma

Indigofera sphinctosperma, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and dye production.
