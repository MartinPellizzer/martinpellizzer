Iberis intermedia var. durandii

Iberis intermedia var. durandii, commonly known as the Durand's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact form.
