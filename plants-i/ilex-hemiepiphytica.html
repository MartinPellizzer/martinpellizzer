Ilex hemiepiphytica

Ilex hemiepiphytica, commonly known as the hemiepiphytic holly, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It exhibits a hemiepiphytic growth habit, meaning it begins its life as an epiphyte on the branches of trees, gradually developing roots that anchor it to the substrate. The plant produces small, clustered leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with some species displaying variegated patterns. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, giving way to red or orange berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its specialized growth form and habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
