Inula quadrifida

Inula quadrifida, commonly known as the four-branched elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, hairy stem with several branches, giving it its common name. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
