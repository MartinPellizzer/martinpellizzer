Incarvillea sinensis var. przewalskii

Incarvillea sinensis var. przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in arid gardens and conservation efforts.
