Ilex aquifolium var. bicolor

Ilex aquifolium var. bicolor, commonly known as the variegated holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green, creating a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
