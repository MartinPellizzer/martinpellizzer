Ionoxalis jacquiniana

Ionoxalis jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacquin's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its spurred petals and calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Ionoxalis jacquiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
