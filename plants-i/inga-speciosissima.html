Inga speciosissima

Inga speciosissima, commonly known as the showy inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Inga speciosissima plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
