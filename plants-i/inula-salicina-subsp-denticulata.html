Inula salicina subsp. denticulata

Inula salicina subsp.

denticulata, commonly known as the roughleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically rough and toothed, giving the plant its subspecific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
