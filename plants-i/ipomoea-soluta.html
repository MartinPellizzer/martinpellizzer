Ipomoea soluta

Ipomoea soluta, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit.

Ipomoea soluta is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and vine-like structure.
