Ipomoea acuminata f. albiflora

Ipomoea acuminata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a cultivated variant of the morning glory genus.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in the early morning, followed by a rapid closing in the afternoon. The plant is a trailing vine with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
