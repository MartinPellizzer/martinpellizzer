Indigofera thirionni

Indigofera thirionni, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and greenhouses in various parts of the world.
