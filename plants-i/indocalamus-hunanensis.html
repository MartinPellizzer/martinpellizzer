Indocalamus hunanensis

Indocalamus hunanensis, commonly known as the Hunan bamboo, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Hunan province.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, compact clump. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
