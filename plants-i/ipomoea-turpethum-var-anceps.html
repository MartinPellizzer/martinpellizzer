Ipomoea turpethum var. anceps

Ipomoea turpethum var. anceps, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
