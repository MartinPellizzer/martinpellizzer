Ilex aquifolium var. nigrescens

Ilex aquifolium var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-holly, is a variant of the common holly that exhibits distinctive dark foliage.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn almost black in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is typically evergreen, with a dense, shrub-like growth habit.

The leaves are usually spiny and have a leathery texture, typical of the Ilex genus.
