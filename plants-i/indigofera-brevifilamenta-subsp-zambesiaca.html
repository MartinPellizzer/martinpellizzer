Indigofera brevifilamenta subsp. zambesiaca

Indigofera brevifilamenta subsp.

zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi indigo, is a leguminous shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal research.
