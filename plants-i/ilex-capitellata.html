Ilex capitellata

Ilex capitellata, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex capitellata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 9.
