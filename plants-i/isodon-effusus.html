Isodon effusus

Isodon effusus, commonly known as the woolly isodon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Isodon effusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
