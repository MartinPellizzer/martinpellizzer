Ipomoea crassifolia

Ipomoea crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a climbing vine with strong stems that can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
