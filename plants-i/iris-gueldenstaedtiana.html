Iris gueldenstaedtiana

Iris gueldenstaedtiana, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue to white flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
