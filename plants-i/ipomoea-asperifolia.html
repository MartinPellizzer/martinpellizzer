Ipomoea asperifolia

Ipomoea asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea asperifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
