Isocoma arguta

Isocoma arguta, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, white hairs.

Isocoma arguta is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
