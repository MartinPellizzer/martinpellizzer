Isatis tinctoria subsp. canescens

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and hairy, grayish-green foliage.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as mustard and cabbage. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in open habitats like meadows and roadsides.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles and as a medicinal plant highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
