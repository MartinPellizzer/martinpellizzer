Ipomoea tentaculifera

Ipomoea tentaculifera, commonly known as the curly morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges and produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is characterized by its unique, curling tendrils that help it climb and attach to supports. Its flowers have a distinctive, fragrant aroma and are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical structure of Ipomoea tentaculifera includes a hairy stem, palmate leaves, and showy, trumpet-shaped blossoms.
