Iva asperifolia var. latior

Iva asperifolia var. latior, commonly known as broadleaf marsh elder, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowering stems are typically branched and produce small yellow flowers in the fall.

This species thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
