Ipomoea hederacea var. integriuscula

Ipomoea hederacea var. integriuscula, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a trailing stem with tendrils that aid in climbing.
