Isocarpha oppositifolia var. glabrata

Isocarpha oppositifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf isocarpha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it features opposite, linear leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous. Its flowers are small and clustered in a head, with both ray and disk florets, contributing to its ornamental potential.

The species is notable for its adaptability and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
