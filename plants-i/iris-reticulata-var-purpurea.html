Iris reticulata var. purpurea

Iris reticulata var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple reticulated iris, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, nodding flowers with a violet-blue coloration and a distinctive reticulate pattern on the petals. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white or grayish coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens during the cooler months.
