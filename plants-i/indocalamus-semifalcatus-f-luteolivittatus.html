Indocalamus semifalcatus f. luteolivittatus

Indocalamus semifalcatus f. luteolivittatus, commonly known as the golden-green bamboo, is a variant of the broad-leaved bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage, which sets it apart from the typical green varieties of its genus. The plant features slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional bamboo crafts due to their flexibility and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique coloration.
