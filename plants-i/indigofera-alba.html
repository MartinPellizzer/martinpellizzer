Indigofera alba

Indigofera alba, commonly known as white indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant also has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
