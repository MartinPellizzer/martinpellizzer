Impatiens manteroana

Impatiens manteroana, commonly known as the Mantero's Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural habitat.
