Ischaemum barbatum var. gibbum

Ischaemum barbatum var. gibbum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered along the stem, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.
