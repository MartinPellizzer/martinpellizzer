Impatiens glandulisepala

Impatiens glandulisepala, commonly known as the glandular-sepal impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its sepals are glandular, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
