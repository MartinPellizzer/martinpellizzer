Ipomopsis congesta subsp. palmifrons

Ipomopsis congesta subsp.

palmifrons, commonly known as the palmate ipomopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
