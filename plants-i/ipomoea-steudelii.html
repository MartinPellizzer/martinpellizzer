Ipomoea steudelii

Ipomoea steudelii, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces edible tubers similar to sweet potatoes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea steudelii is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive if not managed properly.
