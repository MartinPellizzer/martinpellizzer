Iris breviscapa

Iris breviscapa, commonly known as the short-capped iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.

Iris breviscapa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
