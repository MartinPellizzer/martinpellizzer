Ixora indica

Ixora indica, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ixora indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
