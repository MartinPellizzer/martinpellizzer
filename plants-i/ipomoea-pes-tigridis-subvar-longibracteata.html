Ipomoea pes-tigridis subvar. longibracteata

Ipomoea pes-tigridis subvar. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract morning glory, is a striking climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender bracts that are distinctive and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This subvariety is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
