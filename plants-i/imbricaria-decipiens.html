Imbricaria decipiens

Imbricaria decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive shield fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, overlapping fronds that resemble a shield, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are protected by indusia, a feature common in many ferns.

The plant's ability to mimic other fern species has led to its reputation as a deceptive or even cryptic species in its native habitat.
