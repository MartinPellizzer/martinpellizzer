Iris kasruwana

Iris kasruwana, commonly known as the Java iris, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or near streams. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

Iris kasruwana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and elegance.
