Inga juglandifolia

Inga juglandifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain multiple seeds, known as Brazil nuts, which are highly valued for their nutritional content. It thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.

Inga juglandifolia plays a vital role in the ecological balance of the Amazon, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
