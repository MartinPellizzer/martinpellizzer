Illecebrum dichotomum

Illecebrum dichotomum, commonly known as the forked weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb with a branching stem that often splits into two at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Illecebrum dichotomum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
