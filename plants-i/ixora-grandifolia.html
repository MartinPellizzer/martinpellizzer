Ixora grandifolia

Ixora grandifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
