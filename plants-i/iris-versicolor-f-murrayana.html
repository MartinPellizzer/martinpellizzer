Iris versicolor f. murrayana

Iris versicolor f. murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's iris, is a botanical variant of the variable iris.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a white or light blue center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and botanical collections.
