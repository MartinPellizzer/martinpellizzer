Ibatia dugandii

Ibatia dugandii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum dugandii*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, bright red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is a subject of conservation efforts.
