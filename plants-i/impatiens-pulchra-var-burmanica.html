Impatiens pulchra var. burmanica

Impatiens pulchra var. burmanica, commonly known as Burmese impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and grows in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions make it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
