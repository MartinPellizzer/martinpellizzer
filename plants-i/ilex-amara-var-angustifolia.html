Ilex amara var. angustifolia

Ilex amara var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that are usually glossy and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its evergreen nature makes it a popular ornamental plant in landscaping.
