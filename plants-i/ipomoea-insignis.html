Ipomoea insignis

Ipomoea insignis, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that open in the morning and close at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea insignis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences quickly.
