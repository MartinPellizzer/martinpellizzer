Iris sari var. lurida

Iris sari var. lurida, commonly known as the lurid iris, is a botanical variant of the iris species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often purple or violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, narrow spur. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and sword-shaped.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
