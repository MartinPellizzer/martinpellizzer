Itysa

Itysa, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

Itysa species are native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. These plants typically have showy, brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The leaves are often lance-shaped and arranged oppositely on the stems.

Itysa plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
