Iphionopsis rotundifolia

Iphionopsis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the southwestern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, succulent-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
