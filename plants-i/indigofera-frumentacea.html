Indigofera frumentacea

Indigofera frumentacea, commonly known as the grain indigo or African indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. Its seeds are often used as a source of protein and can be processed into a flour-like substance.

Additionally, it has been used traditionally for dyeing fabrics and as a medicinal plant.
