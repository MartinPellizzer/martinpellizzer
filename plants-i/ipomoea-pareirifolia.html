Ipomoea pareirifolia

Ipomoea pareirifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a climbing vine that produces fibrous roots and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ipomoea pareirifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
