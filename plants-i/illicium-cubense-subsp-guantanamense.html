Illicium cubense subsp. guantanamense

Illicium cubense subsp.

guantanamense, commonly known as the Cuban star anise, is a member of the Schisandraceae family and is native to Cuba. It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise.

The plant features compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are highly valued for their essential oils, which contain compounds like anethole and limonene.

This subspecies is notable for its unique botanical traits and potential medicinal uses.
