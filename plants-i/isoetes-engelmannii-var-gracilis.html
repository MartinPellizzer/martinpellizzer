Isoetes engelmannii var. gracilis

Isoetes engelmannii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure on the bottom of shallow water bodies. The plant is native to North America and thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the undersides of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing sediment.
