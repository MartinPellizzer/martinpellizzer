Ischnosiphon secundus

Ischnosiphon secundus, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Ischnosophon secundus is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
