Iochroma arboreum

Iochroma arboreum, commonly known as the "Flor de la Cruz" or "Cruz flower," is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds. Iochroma arboreum thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
