Ipomoea dalzellii var. inaequalis

Ipomoea dalzellii var. inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
