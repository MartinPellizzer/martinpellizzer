Indigofera burkeana

Indigofera burkeana, commonly known as the blue wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often grown for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Indigofera burkeana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
