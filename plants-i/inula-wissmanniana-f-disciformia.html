Inula wissmanniana f. disciformia

Inula wissmanniana f. disciformia, commonly known as the disc-shaped elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a disc-like structure, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
