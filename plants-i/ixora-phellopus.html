Ixora phellopus

Ixora phellopus, commonly known as the red flame ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters, which are attracted to their vibrant color and nectar-rich blooms.
