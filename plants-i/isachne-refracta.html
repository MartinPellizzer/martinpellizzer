Isachne refracta

Isachne refracta, commonly known as the refractable isachne, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are a defining feature of the grass family. Isachne refracta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
