Ipomoea batatas var. erythrorrhiza

Ipomoea batatas var. erythrorrhiza, commonly known as the red-veined sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its distinctive red veins in the leaves.

It is a perennial vine that belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates and have a slightly sweet, moist texture. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a vibrant green color contrasting with the red venation.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a food crop in certain regions.
