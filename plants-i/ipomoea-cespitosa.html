Ipomoea cespitosa

Ipomoea cespitosa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a trailing vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea cespitosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
