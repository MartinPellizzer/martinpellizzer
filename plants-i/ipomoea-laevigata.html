Ipomoea laevigata

Ipomoea laevigata, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Ipomoea laevigata is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other morning glory species.
