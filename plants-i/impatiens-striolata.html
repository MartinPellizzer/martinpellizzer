Impatiens striolata

Impatiens striolata, commonly known as the streaked impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Impatiens striolata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
