Ipomoea coccinea var. hederifolia

Ipomoea coccinea var. hederifolia, commonly known as the red-veined morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep red to orange in color. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and lush foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its flowers typically open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
