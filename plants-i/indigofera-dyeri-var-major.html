Indigofera dyeri var. major

Indigofera dyeri var. major, commonly known as the blue bean or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
