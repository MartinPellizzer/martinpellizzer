Ipomoea marabaensis

Ipomoea marabaensis, commonly known as the Maraba morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural displays.
