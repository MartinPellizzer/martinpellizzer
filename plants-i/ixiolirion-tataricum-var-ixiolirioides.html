Ixiolirion tataricum var. ixiolirioides

Ixiolirion tataricum var. ixiolirioides, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
