Ipomoea crassipes var. cordifolia

Ipomoea crassipes var. cordifolia, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a semi-aquatic herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are broad and succulent, with a fibrous stem that can grow both submerged and above water. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the morning. Its roots are edible and are often consumed in various Asian cuisines.

It thrives in wet environments and is commonly cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in cooking.
