Ipomoea villicalyx

Ipomoea villicalyx, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
