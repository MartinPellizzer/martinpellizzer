Ilex montana var. hupehensis

Ilex montana var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The leaves are typically elliptical with spiny margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
