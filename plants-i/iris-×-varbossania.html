Iris × varbossania

Iris × varbossania, commonly known as the hybrid bearded iris, is a cross between Iris germanica and Iris varbosa.

It is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers with three upright petals and three downward-folding sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This hybrid is prized for its vibrant colors and is a popular choice in gardens for its ornamental value.
