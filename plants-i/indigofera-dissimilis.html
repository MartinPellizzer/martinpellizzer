Indigofera dissimilis

Indigofera dissimilis, commonly known as the Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, blue-black substance, which has been historically used for dyeing textiles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and traditional uses make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
