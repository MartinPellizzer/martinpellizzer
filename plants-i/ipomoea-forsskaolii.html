Ipomoea forsskaolii

Ipomoea forsskaolii, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking yellow flowers with five petals that open in the morning. The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea forsskaolii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
