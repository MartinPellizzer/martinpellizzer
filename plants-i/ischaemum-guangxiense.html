Ischaemum guangxiense

Ischaemum guangxiense, commonly known as Guangxi needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed florets.

Ischaemum guangxiense is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
