Inula esquirolii

Inula esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's everlasting flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
