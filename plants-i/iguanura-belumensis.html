Iguanura belumensis

Iguanura belumensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, glossy green leaves and produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its striking floral display.
