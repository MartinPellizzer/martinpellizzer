Iris variabilis subsp. sordida

Iris variabilis subsp.

sordida, commonly known as the sordid iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive white or yellow center. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its adaptability to a variety of soil types.

The subspecies is characterized by its darker, more muted flower colors compared to other varieties of Iris variabilis.
