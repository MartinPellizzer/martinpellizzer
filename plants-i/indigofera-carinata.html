Indigofera carinata

Indigofera carinata, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It is often found in woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Indigofera carinata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
