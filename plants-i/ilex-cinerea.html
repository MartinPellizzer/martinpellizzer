Ilex cinerea

Ilex cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Ilex cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
