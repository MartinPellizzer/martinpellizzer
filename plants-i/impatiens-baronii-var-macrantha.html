Impatiens baronii var. macrantha

Impatiens baronii var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant impatiens, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant blooms.
