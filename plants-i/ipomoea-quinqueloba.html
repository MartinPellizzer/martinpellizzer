Ipomoea quinqueloba

Ipomoea quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea quinqueloba is often found in wetlands, along streambanks, and in disturbed areas where it can spread rapidly.
