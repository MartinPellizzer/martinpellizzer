Iguanura palmuncula var. palmuncula

Iguanura palmuncula var. palmuncula, commonly known as the red-veined or red-leafed palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its uniquely colored leaves, which display a vibrant red or purple vein pattern against a green background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the canopy layer of the rainforest. Its leaves are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value, and it prefers warm, moist environments with high humidity.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
