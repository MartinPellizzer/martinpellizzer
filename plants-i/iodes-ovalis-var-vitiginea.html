Iodes ovalis var. vitiginea

Iodes ovalis var. vitiginea, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is characterized by its distinctive yellow stamens that contrast with its greenish petals.
