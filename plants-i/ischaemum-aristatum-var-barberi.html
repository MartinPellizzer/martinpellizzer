Ischaemum aristatum var. barberi

Ischaemum aristatum var. barberi, commonly known as the barber's grass, is a variant of the slender barnyard grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural weed management.
