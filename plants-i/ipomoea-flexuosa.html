Ipomoea flexuosa

Ipomoea flexuosa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at night. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb by twining around supports. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical significance includes being a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.
