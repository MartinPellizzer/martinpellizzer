Ipomoea rubicunda

Ipomoea rubicunda, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It produces strikingly large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a white throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
