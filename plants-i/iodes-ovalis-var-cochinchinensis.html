Iodes ovalis var. cochinchinensis

Iodes ovalis var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
