Isidrogalvia paniculata

Isidrogalvia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle shrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive floral display and adaptability to various garden settings.
