Indigofera brunoniana

Indigofera brunoniana, commonly known as the New Caledonia indigo, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its restricted distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
