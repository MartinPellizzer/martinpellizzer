Inula × lugdunensis

Inula × lugdunensis, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, yellow composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces a strong, aromatic odor.

Inula × lugdunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
