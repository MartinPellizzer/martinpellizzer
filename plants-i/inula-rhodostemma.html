Inula rhodostemma

Inula rhodostemma, commonly known as the red-flowered elecampane, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with lance-shaped leaves that are serrated along the edges. Its most striking feature is the bright red to orange disc flowers, which are surrounded by yellow ray florets.

Inula rhodostemma is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
