Inga panamensis var. pittieri

Inga panamensis var. pittieri, commonly known as the Panama inga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in tropical ecosystems.
