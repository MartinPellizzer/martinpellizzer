Iris paradoxa f. choschab

Iris paradoxa f. choschab, commonly known as the Choschab iris, is a striking hybrid iris native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant, purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.

Its unique coloration and elegant appearance make it a popular choice among iris enthusiasts.
