Ixora oligantha

Ixora oligantha, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which resemble clusters of coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Ixora oligantha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
