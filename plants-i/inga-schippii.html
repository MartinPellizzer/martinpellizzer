Inga schippii

Inga schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's inga, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in the understory of humid forests, where it thrives in partial shade and moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
