Ichnocarpus frutescens var. pubescens

Ichnocarpus frutescens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy devil's-bit, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds with a hard, spiky coat. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
