Impatiens flavescens

Impatiens flavescens, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.
