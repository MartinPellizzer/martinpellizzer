Iberis amara subsp. affinis

Iberis amara subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are typically dispersed by wind. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
