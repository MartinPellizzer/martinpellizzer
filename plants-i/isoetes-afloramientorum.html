Isoetes afloramientorum

Isoetes afloramientorum, commonly known as the Florida water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which are commonly referred to as water clovers or quillworts.

It is characterized by its small, spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a crown-like arrangement at the apex of the plant. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are ribbon-like and often form dense mats on the sediment surface.

Isoetes afloramientorum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
