Iris melzeri

Iris melzeri, commonly known as the Melzer's iris, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Iris.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in countries like Austria, Italy, and Switzerland. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive white or yellowish beard on the lower petals. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Iris melzeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
