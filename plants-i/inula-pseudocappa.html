Inula pseudocappa

Inula pseudocappa, commonly known as the lesser elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
