Illecebrum cucculatum

Illecebrum cucculatum, commonly known as the creeping bellvista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with trailing stems that can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and often yellow or greenish in color, appearing in clusters.

The leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
