Indigofera laxiflora

Indigofera laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by wind or water.

Indigofera laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
