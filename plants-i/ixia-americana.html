Ixia americana

Ixia americana, commonly known as the American ixia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ixia americana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
