Iris dykesii

Iris dykesii, commonly known as the Dykes' iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, with sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Iris dykesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
