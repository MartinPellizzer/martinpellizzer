Indigofera chamaeclada subsp.

chamaeclada, commonly known as the creeping indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue or purple, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
