Indigofera udonthaniensis

Indigofera udonthaniensis, commonly known as the Thai indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and benefits surrounding vegetation. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
