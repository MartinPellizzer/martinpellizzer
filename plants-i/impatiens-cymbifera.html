Impatiens cymbifera

Impatiens cymbifera, commonly known as the "whispering bells" or "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that resemble small bells, often in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Impatiens cymbifera is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
