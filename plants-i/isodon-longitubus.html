Isodon longitubus

Isodon longitubus, commonly known as the long-tubed isodon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, where it grows in forested or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
