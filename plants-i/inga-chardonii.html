Inga chardonii

Inga chardonii, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga chardonii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
