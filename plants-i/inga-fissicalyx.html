Inga fissicalyx

Inga fissicalyx, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Inga fissicalyx also has potential uses in agroforestry due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
