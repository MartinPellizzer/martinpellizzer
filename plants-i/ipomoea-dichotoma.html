Ipomoea dichotoma

Ipomoea dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has a climbing habit and produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea dichotoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
