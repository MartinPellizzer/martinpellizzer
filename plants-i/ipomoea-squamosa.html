Ipomoea squamosa

Ipomoea squamosa, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces fleshy, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, scaly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ipomoea squamosa thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
