Isatis armena

Isatis armena, commonly known as Armenian woad, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, waxy leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical significance in dye production.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
