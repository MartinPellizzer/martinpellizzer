Iberis vinetorum

Iberis vinetorum, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain several seeds. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
