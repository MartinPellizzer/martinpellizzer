Ipomoea costaricensis

Ipomoea costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a vine that produces striking blue or purple flowers, which open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular species for both ornamental and ecological studies.
