Iris ensata f. alba

Iris ensata f. alba, commonly known as the Japanese white iris, is a cultivar of the iris genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a pale blue or violet center, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and sturdy. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening.

This variety is prized for its elegance and is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils in partial shade.
