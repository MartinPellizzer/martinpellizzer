Islaya bicolor

Islaya bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, it is characterized by its vibrant, contrasting floral colors. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Islaya bicolor thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
