Iberis linifolia subsp. timeroyi

Iberis linifolia subsp.

timeroyi, commonly known as the Turkish rockcress, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
