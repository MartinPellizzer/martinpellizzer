Irlbachia nemorosa

Irlbachia nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the woodlands of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in deciduous forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
