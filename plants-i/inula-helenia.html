Inula helenia

Inula helenia, commonly known as Helen's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts.

Inula helenia is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
