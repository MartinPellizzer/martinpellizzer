Inula × neilreichii

Inula × neilreichii, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Inula helenium and Inula crithmoides, showcasing a combination of traits from both parent species. The plant features showy yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of gray-green, lobed leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Inula × neilreichii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges across its native range.
