Indigofera atriceps subsp. glandulosissima

Indigofera atriceps subsp.

glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular indigofera, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
