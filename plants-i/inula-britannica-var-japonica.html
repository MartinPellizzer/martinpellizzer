Inula britannica var. japonica

Inula britannica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese elecampane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branched stem covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow to orange, arranged in clusters that resemble a composite structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
