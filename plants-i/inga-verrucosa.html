Inga verrucosa

Inga verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Inga verrucosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
