Inga extra-nodis

Inga extra-nodis, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemous inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in humid lowland forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
