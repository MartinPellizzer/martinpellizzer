Ipomoea curtisii

Ipomoea curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southern United States.

It produces striking trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. It is known for its rapid growth and can climb structures or sprawl along the ground.

Botanically, it is characterized by its milky sap and is part of the Convolvulaceae family.
