Ipomoea ukambensis

Ipomoea ukambensis, commonly known as the East African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to eastern Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea ukambensis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
