Ipomoea aggregata

Ipomoea aggregata, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
