Ilex sideroxyloides subsp. sideroxyloides

Ilex sideroxyloides subsp.

sideroxyloides, commonly known as the ironwood holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, glossy leaves that are typically dark green.

The plant produces small white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
