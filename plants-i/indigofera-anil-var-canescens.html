Indigofera anil var. canescens

Indigofera anil var. canescens, commonly known as white indigo or white woad, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, and the plant is often cultivated for its historical use in dye production.

The leaves and stems are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
