Ilex truncata

Ilex truncata, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Ilex truncata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact growth.
