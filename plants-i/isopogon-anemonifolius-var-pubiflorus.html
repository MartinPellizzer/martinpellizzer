Isopogon anemonifolius var. pubiflorus

Isopogon anemonifolius var. pubiflorus, commonly known as the soft-flowered banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear numerous small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in coastal gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
