Ipomoea santacruzensis

Ipomoea santacruzensis, commonly known as the Santa Cruz morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often found growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Its seeds are dispersed by birds and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
