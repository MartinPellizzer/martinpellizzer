Iris kaempferi var. hortensis

Iris kaempferi var. hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue or white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and water features.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both cultivated gardens and natural landscapes.
