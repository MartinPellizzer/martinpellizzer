Ipomoea havanensis

Ipomoea havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea havanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
