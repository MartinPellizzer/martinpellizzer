Isachne dimyloides

Isachne dimyloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf isachne, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Isachne dimyloides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
