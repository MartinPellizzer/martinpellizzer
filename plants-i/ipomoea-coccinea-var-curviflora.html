Ipomoea coccinea var. curviflora

Ipomoea coccinea var. curviflora, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, twisting stems that can climb or trail. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and natural habitats.
