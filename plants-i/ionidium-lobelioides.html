Ionidium lobelioides

Ionidium lobelioides, commonly known as the purple-flowered ironweed, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in wetlands and moist woodlands. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant purple to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Ionidium lobelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
