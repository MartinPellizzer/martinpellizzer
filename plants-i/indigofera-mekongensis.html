Indigofera mekongensis

Indigofera mekongensis, commonly known as Mekong indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in riverine forests.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or water.
