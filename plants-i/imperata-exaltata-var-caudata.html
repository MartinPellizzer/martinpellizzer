Imperata exaltata var. caudata

Imperata exaltata var. caudata, commonly known as the purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are borne on spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.
