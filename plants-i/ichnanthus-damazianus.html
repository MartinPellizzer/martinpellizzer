Ichnanthus damazianus

Ichnanthus damazianus, commonly known as the Damazian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Ichnanthus damazianus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
