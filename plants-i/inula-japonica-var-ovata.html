Inula japonica var. ovata

Inula japonica var. ovata, commonly known as Japanese fleabane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
