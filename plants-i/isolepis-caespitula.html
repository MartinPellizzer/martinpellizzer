Isolepis caespitula

Isolepis caespitula, commonly known as tufted spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It forms dense, grass-like clumps with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in marshes and pond edges.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
