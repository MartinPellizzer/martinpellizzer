Ipomoea dumetorum f. alba

Ipomoea dumetorum f. alba, commonly known as white morning glory, is a variant of the species Ipomoea dumetorum, characterized by its pale white flowers.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
