Inga litoralis

Inga litoralis, commonly known as the coastal inga, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Inga litoralis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
