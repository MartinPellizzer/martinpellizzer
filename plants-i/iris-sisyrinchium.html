Iris sisyrinchium

Iris sisyrinchium, commonly known as the blue iris, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while the flowers bloom in early to mid-summer.

Iris sisyrinchium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
