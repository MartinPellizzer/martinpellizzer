Ipomoea tapirapoanensis

Ipomoea tapirapoanensis, commonly known as the Tapirapoan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
