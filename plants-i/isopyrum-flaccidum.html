Isopyrum flaccidum

Isopyrum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.

Isopyrum flaccidum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
