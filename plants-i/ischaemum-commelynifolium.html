Ischaemum commelynifolium

Ischaemum commelynifolium, commonly known as the golden grass or commelina grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, often with two bracts. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
