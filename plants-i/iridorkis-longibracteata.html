Iridorkis longibracteata

Iridorkis longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract iridorkis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related genera. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
