Iguanura spectabilis

Iguanura spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bignonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite and often have a velvety texture, while its bark is smooth and may have a reddish hue.

Iguanura spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
