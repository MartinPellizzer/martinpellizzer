Isoetes riparia var. palmeri

Isoetes riparia var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's watermoccasin, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which includes various species of quillworts.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporocarps, which are typically found at the tips of its leaf-like stems. This variety is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in freshwater wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its morphology includes a rosette of narrow, ribbon-like leaves that help it anchor in muddy substrates.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
