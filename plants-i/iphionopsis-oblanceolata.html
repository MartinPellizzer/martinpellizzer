Iphionopsis oblanceolata

Iphionopsis oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
