Ipomoea chrysochaetia var. chrysochaetia

Ipomoea chrysochaetia var. chrysochaetia, commonly known as the golden hair vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers with hairy petals. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
