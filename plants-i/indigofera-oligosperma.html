Indigofera oligosperma

Indigofera oligosperma, commonly known as the small-seeded indigo, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing small, black seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
