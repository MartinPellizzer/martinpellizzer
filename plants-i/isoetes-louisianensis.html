Isoetes louisianensis

Isoetes louisianensis, commonly known as the Louisiana water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and often submerged leaves that form a dense mat on the bottom of freshwater habitats. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in wetlands and slow-moving streams. Isoetes louisianensis reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

This plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
