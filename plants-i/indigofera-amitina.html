Indigofera amitina

Indigofera amitina, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
