Ipomoea schulziana

Ipomoea schulziana, commonly known as the Schultze morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea schulziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
