Iris × germanica var. sivas

Iris × germanica var. sivas, commonly known as the Sivas iris, is a hybrid variety of the bearded iris.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a contrasting white beard, which is a distinctive feature of the bearded iris group. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–50 cm, with sword-shaped, dark green leaves that are narrow and upright. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements and garden landscapes.
