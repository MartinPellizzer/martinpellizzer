Ipomoea lanuginosa

Ipomoea lanuginosa, commonly known as the fuzzy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically opening in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea lanuginosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and rapid growth habit.
