Itea parviflora var. arisanensis

Itea parviflora var. arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are finely toothed and turn a striking yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its delicate floral display and autumn color.
