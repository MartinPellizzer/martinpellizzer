Indigofera banii

Indigofera banii, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important plant in agricultural systems.
