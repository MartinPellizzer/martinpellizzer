Isolona soubreana

Isolona soubreana, commonly known as the Soubreana tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and often cracked, providing a habitat for various epiphytes and insects.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
