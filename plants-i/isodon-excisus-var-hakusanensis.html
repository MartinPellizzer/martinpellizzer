Isodon excisus var. hakusanensis

Isodon excisus var. hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan mountain verbena, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
