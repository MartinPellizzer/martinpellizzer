Indigofera haematica

Indigofera haematica, commonly known as blood bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Indigofera haematica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
