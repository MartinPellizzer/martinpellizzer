Impatiens fischeri

Impatiens fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the genus Impatiens within the family Balsaminaceae, characterized by its showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
