Indigofera longicauda

Indigofera longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and fragrant, pale purple to white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Indigofera longicauda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
