Indigofera glaucifolia subsp. glaucifolia

Indigofera glaucifolia subsp.

glaucifolia, commonly known as the glaucous indigofera, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive bluish-gray underside.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
