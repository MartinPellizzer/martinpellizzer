Ixeris dentata f. parva

Ixeris dentata f. parva, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
