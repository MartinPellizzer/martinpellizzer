Indigofera roseola

Indigofera roseola, commonly known as the pink indigo or roseola indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system. Indigofera roseola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the species and environmental conditions.
