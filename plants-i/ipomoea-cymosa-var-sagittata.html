Ipomoea cymosa var. sagittata

Ipomoea cymosa var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, arrow-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can be invasive in some areas.
