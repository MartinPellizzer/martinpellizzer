Inula obtusifolia var. clarkei

Inula obtusifolia var. clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's elecampane, is a variant of the elecampane species native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its root system is fibrous and thick. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
