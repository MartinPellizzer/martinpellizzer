Iresine spiculigera f. picta

Iresine spiculigera f. picta, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty, is a strikingly colorful foliage plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vivid, almost neon-like red and purple leaves, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is closely related to other species in the Iresine genus. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
