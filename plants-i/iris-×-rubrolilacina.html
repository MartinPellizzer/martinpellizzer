Iris × rubrolilacina

Iris × rubrolilacina, commonly known as the purple-lilac iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive white or pale lilac throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer.
