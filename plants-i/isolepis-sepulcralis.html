Isolepis sepulcralis

Isolepis sepulcralis, commonly known as the funereal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along the edges of lakes and ponds, and in other moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Isolepis sepulcralis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
