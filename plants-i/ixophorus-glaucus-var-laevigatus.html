Ixophorus glaucus var. laevigatus

Ixophorus glaucus var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smoothleaf glaucous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and glaucous, giving it a distinctive silvery-blue appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
