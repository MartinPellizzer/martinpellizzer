Indocalamus nubigenus

Indocalamus nubigenus, commonly known as the cloud bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, grass-like texture.

Indocalamus nubigenus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional basket-making in some regions.
