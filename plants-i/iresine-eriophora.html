Iresine eriophora

Iresine eriophora, commonly known as the red leaf iron plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage, which contrasts with its green stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant color and textural appeal.

Iresine eriophora is also known for its ability to produce copious amounts of nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
