Ichnanthus verticillatus

Ichnanthus verticillatus, commonly known as the whorled blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
