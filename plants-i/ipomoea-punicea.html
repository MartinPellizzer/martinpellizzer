Ipomoea punicea

Ipomoea punicea, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers are typically about 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter, with five petals and a prominent yellow throat.

Ipomoea punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
