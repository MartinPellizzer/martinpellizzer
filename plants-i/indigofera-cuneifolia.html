Indigofera cuneifolia

Indigofera cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has been used traditionally for its dye-producing properties and as a forage plant.

Its growth habit and ecological adaptability make it an important species in certain ecosystems.
