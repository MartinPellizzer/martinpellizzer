Inga skutchii

Inga skutchii, commonly known as the Guiana inga, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in the Guyana Shield region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Inga skutchii produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest regeneration.
