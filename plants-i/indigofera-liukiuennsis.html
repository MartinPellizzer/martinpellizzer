Indigofera liukiuennsis

Indigofera liukiuennsis, commonly known as the Ryukyu indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
