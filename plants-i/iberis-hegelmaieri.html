Iberis hegelmaieri

Iberis hegelmaieri, commonly known as the Hegelmaier's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Iberis hegelmaieri is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
