Indosinia involucrata

Indosinia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Indosinia involucrata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
