Ipomoea macdonaldii

Ipomoea macdonaldii, commonly known as the Macdonald's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that typically grows along the ground or climbs supports with its twining stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to violet in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist environments.

Ipomoea macdonaldii is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid, sunny conditions.
