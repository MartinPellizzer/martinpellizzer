Inula montana

Inula montana, commonly known as mountain inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with well-drained soil. Its yellow-orange flower heads are composed of numerous small florets, and it produces a distinctive, often aromatic, herbaceous stem.

Inula montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
