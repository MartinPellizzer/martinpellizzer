Iberis lusitanica

Iberis lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese candytuft, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lobed, hairy leaves and a mat-forming growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils.

Iberis lusitanica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
