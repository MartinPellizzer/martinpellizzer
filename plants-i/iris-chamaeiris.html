Iris chamaeiris

Iris chamaeiris, commonly known as the alpine iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable component of alpine flora.

Iris chamaeiris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
