Indigofera tettensis

Indigofera tettensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
