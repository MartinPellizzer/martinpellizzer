Isidodendron tripterocarpum

Isidodendron tripterocarpum, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a member of the family Ochnaceae and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, dark bark. The tree produces small, woody fruits that split open to release seeds.

It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in traditional crafts and musical instruments.
