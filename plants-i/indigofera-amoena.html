Indigofera amoena

Indigofera amoena, commonly known as the pink indigo or sweet indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
