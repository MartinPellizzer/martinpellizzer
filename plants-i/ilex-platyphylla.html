Ilex platyphylla

Ilex platyphylla, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex platyphylla is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
