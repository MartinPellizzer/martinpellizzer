Inga nutans var. tenuis

Inga nutans var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it an important species in biodiversity-rich habitats.
