Inga tayronaensis

Inga tayronaensis, commonly known as the Tayrona inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia, where it thrives in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. It produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
