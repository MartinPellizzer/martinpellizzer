Impatiens recticalcarata

Impatiens recticalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in both garden and horticultural settings.
