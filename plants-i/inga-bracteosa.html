Inga bracteosa

Inga bracteosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly serrated.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
