Ipomoea thomsoniana

Ipomoea thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers, though they are not commonly cultivated for food. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
