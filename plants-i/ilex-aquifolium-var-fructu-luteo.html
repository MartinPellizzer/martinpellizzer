Ilex aquifolium var. fructu-luteo

Ilex aquifolium var. fructu-luteo, commonly known as the yellow-fruited holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its distinctive yellow berries.

This plant is evergreen, featuring glossy, dark green, spiny leaves that remain throughout the year. The yellow fruits, which are ornamental, typically appear in clusters and persist into winter, adding visual interest to the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.

The botanical features of this variety make it a unique addition to gardens and landscapes.
