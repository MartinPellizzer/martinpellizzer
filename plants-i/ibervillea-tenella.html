Ibervillea tenella

Ibervillea tenella, commonly known as desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs, reducing water loss.

Ibervillea tenella is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.
