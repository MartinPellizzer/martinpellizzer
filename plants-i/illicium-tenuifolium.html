Illicium tenuifolium

Illicium tenuifolium, commonly known as Chinese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to China, Vietnam, and other parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that develop into star-shaped, dark brown fruits. The plant is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its essential oils contain high levels of shikimic acid, a compound important in the production of antiviral medications.

Illicium tenuifolium thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile plant for both ornamental and practical use.
