Iris sisyrinchium var. syrtica

Iris sisyrinchium var. syrtica, commonly known as the Syrtic iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
