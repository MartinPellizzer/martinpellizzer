Ivesia gordonii var. lycopodioides

Ivesia gordonii var. lycopodioides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
