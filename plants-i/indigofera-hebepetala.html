Indigofera hebepetala

Indigofera hebepetala, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
