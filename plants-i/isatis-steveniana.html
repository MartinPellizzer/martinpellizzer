Isatis steveniana

Isatis steveniana, commonly known as the Steven's woad, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Steven's woad has been historically used for dyeing fabrics and has medicinal properties due to its alkaloid content.
