Ibidium vernale

Ibidium vernale, commonly known as the spring ibidium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, aromatic scent.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, blooming in early spring.
