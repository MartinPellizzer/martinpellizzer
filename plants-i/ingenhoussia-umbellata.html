Ingenhoussia umbellata

Ingenhoussia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, umbellate inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, consisting of numerous white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with slender stems and narrow, strap-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticultural settings.
