Ilex wightiana var. zeylanica

Ilex wightiana var. zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
