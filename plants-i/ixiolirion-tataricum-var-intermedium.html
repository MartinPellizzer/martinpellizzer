Ixiolirion tataricum var. intermedium

Ixiolirion tataricum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate tatarian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
