Imperata arundinacea var. thunbergii

Imperata arundinacea var. thunbergii, commonly known as Japanese blood grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are red-tinged at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in late summer, followed by fluffy, feathery seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a decorative and potentially invasive species in certain regions.
