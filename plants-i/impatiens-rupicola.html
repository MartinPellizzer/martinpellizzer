Impatiens rupicola

Impatiens rupicola, commonly known as the rock impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, velvety texture, and the stems are usually hairy.

Impatiens rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
