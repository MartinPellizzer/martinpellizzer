Iris pseudacorus f. albescens

Iris pseudacorus f. albescens, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a striking variant of the yellow flag iris.

This plant is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast sharply with its narrow, grass-like leaves. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing showy, three-petaled structures that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
