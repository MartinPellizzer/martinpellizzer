Ixia maculata var. fuscocitrina

Ixia maculata var. fuscocitrina, commonly known as the spotted oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red or orange markings, which are often arranged in a star-like pattern. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream patterns.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
