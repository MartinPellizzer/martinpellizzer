Ipomoea sinuata

Ipomoea sinuata, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at night. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, sunny environments. Its seeds are enclosed in a fibrous, spiny capsule that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea sinuata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
