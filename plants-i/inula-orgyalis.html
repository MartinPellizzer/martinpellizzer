Inula orgyalis

Inula orgyalis, commonly known as the European elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves and yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Inula orgyalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
