Indigofera richardiana

Indigofera richardiana, commonly known as the richardian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it receives adequate moisture.
