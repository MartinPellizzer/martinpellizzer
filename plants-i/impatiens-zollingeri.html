Impatiens zollingeri

Impatiens zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
