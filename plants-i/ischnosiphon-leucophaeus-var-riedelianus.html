Ischnosiphon leucophaeus var. riedelianus

Ischnosiphon leucophaeus var. riedelianus, commonly known as the Riedel's white-stemmed orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, white-veined leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. Its root system is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, this variant is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
