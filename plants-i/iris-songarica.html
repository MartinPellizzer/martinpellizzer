Iris songarica

Iris songarica, commonly known as the Songarica iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers consist of three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

Iris songarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
