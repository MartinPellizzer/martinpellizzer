Iris albopurpurea

Iris albopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with white veins, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
