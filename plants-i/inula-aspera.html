Inula aspera

Inula aspera, commonly known as rough inula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy, rough-textured stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, with ray florets surrounding disk florets.

Inula aspera is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
