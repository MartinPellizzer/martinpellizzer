Impatiens divaricata

Impatiens divaricata, commonly known as the variegated impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and often have a variegated pattern of green and white. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and shaded areas.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
