Indigofera tephrosioides

Indigofera tephrosioides, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules, which enriches the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical gardens and restoration projects.
