Ipomoea erioleuca

Ipomoea erioleuca, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale blue flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea erioleuca is a climbing vine that can reach significant heights, using its tendrils to support its growth.
