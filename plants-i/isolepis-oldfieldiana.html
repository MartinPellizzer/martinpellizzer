Isolepis oldfieldiana

Isolepis oldfieldiana, commonly known as Oldfield's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils.

Isolepis oldfieldiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
