Ipomoea llaveana

Ipomoea llaveana, commonly known as the Llave's morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and twining.

Ipomoea llaveana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks.
