Iris arenaria

Iris arenaria, commonly known as the sand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and rocky regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Iris arenaria is well adapted to harsh climates, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
