Ixeris prolixa

Ixeris prolixa, commonly known as the common yarrow.

It is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family, native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its small, yellow flower heads are arranged in flat-topped clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Ixeris prolixa is often found in dry meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
