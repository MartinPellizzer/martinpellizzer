Indigofera petiolata

Indigofera petiolata, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Indigofera petiolata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
