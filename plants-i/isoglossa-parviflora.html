Isoglossa parviflora

Isoglossa parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered isoglossa, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Isoglossa parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
