Indigofera pueblensis

Indigofera pueblensis, commonly known as Mexican indigo, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. It produces small, dark blue to purple pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
