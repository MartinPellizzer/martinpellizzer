Ixeris stebbinsiana

Ixeris stebbinsiana, commonly known as the Stebbins' carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ability to thrive in arid conditions. It is often found in disturbed soils and dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local pollinators.
