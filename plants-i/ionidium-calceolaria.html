Ionidium calceolaria

Ionidium calceolaria, commonly known as the slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a trap for pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow.

The botanical features of Ionidium calceolaria make it a striking and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
