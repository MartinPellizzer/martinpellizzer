Ipomoea hastigera var. jaliscana

Ipomoea hastigera var. jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.
