Ighermia pinifolia

Ighermia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive appearance with a pale green to yellowish thallus covered in a layer of fine, leaf-like structures. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides essential nutrients through photosynthesis. Ighermia pinifolia is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate forest regions.

Its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes make it an indicator species for ecosystem health.
