Iria autumnalis

Iria autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers in the late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Iria autumnalis is well adapted to moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, wet areas.
