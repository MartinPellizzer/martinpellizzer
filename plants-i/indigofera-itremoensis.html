Indigofera itremoensis

Indigofera itremoensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo plant, is a flowering plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils.
