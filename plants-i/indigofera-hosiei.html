Indigofera hosiei

Indigofera hosiei, commonly known as the blue bean or African indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought resistance contribute to its widespread cultivation.
