Ipomoea palmata var. semine-glabro

Ipomoea palmata var. semine-glabro, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, typically blue or violet, and bloom in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
