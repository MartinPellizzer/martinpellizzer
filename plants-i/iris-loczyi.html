Iris loczyi

Iris loczyi, commonly known as the Loczy's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via the wind.

Iris loczyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
