Inga dumosa

Inga dumosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The tree also plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
