Indigofera crebra

Indigofera crebra, commonly known as narrowleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
