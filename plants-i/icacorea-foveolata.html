Icacorea foveolata

Icacorea foveolata, commonly known as the golden thorn, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Icacorea foveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
