Inga sanctae-annae

Inga sanctae-annae, commonly known as the sacred inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a vital role in local ecosystems by fixing nitrogen in the soil.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
