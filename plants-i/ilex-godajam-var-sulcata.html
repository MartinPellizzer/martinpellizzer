Ilex godajam var. sulcata

Ilex godajam var. sulcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a variant of the Ilex godajam species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
