Indigofera subulata

Indigofera subulata, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some areas for horticultural purposes.
