Ipomoea cissoides f. diminuta

Ipomoea cissoides f. diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens as a decorative plant.
