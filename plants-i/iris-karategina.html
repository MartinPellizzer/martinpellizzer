Iris karategina

Iris karategina, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-dimensional structure. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Iris karategina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
