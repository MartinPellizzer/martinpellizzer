Impatiens scutisepala

Impatiens scutisepala, commonly known as the "whispering bells" or "fairy bells," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its stems are usually hairy as well.

Impatiens scutisepala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
