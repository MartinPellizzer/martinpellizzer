Iris taurica

Iris taurica, commonly known as the Taurian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers with a distinct yellow beard. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The rhizomes of Iris taurica are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
