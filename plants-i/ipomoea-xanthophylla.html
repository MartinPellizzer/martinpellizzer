Ipomoea xanthophylla

Ipomoea xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea xanthophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
