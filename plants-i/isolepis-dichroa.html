Isolepis dichroa

Isolepis dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Isolepis dichroa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
