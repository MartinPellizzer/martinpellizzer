Indigofera suaveolens var. pseudocongolensis

Indigofera suaveolens var. pseudocongolensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
