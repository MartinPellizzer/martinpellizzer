Iris xiphium var. fontanesii

Iris xiphium var. fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontanesii iris, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white stripe running down the central standard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

This variety is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and horticultural significance.
