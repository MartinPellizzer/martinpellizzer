Iris caucasica var. atropatana

Iris caucasica var. atropatana, commonly known as the Caucasian Iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, typical of the iris family. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and elegant form.
