Impatiens balsamina var. parusnathica

Impatiens balsamina var. parusnathica, commonly known as the Parusnathica Impatiens, is a variant of the common impatiens, characterized by its distinctive floral and foliage features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and often have a slightly velvety texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
