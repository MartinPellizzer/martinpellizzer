Ixora crassifolia

Ixora crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are broad and ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Ixora crassifolia is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
