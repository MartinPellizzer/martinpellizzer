Illecebrum paniculatum

Illecebrum paniculatum, commonly known as the weeping willow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are arranged in pendulous clusters called panicles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

Illecebrum paniculatum is typically found in wetlands and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
