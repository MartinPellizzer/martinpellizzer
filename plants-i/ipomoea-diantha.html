Ipomoea diantha

Ipomoea diantha, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated foliage that comes in shades of green, white, and gold. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant or for its edible tubers.

Ipomoea diantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
