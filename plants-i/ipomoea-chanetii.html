Ipomoea chanetii

Ipomoea chanetii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Ipomoea chanetii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along stream banks and in moist, shaded areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
