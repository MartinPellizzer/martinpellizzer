Ipomoea batatas var. porphyrorhiza

Ipomoea batatas var. porphyrorhiza, commonly known as the purple-veined sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its distinctive purple veins in the leaves.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces tuberous roots that are rich in carbohydrates and have a slightly sweet flavor. Its foliage is often a deep green with striking purple markings, making it visually appealing.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its edible roots rather than ornamental purposes, though its unique appearance adds to its horticultural value.
