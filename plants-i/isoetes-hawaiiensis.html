Isoetes hawaiiensis

Isoetes hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian water clover, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the genus Isoetes, which includes about 300 species of spore-producing plants known as quillworts. This particular species is characterized by its ribbon-like leaves that form dense, mat-like structures in shallow, freshwater environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Isoetes hawaiiensis is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
