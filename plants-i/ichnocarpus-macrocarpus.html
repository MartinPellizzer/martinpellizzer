Ichnocarpus macrocarpus

Ichnocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant burr or African burr, is a shrub native to Africa, southern Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, woody fruits that contain a single, large, hard seed. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
