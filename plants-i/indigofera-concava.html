Indigofera concava

Indigofera concava, commonly known as the narrowleaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically blue or purple, and it produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

Indigofera concava is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
