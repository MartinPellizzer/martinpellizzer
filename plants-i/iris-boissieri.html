Iris boissieri

Iris boissieri, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces erect, branched flower stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or open habitats.

Iris boissieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native range.
