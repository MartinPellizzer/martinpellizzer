Indigofera flexuosa

Indigofera flexuosa, commonly known as the wavy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
