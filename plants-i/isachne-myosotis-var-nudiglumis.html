Isachne myosotis var. nudiglumis

Isachne myosotis var. nudiglumis, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a variant of the bluebell species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
