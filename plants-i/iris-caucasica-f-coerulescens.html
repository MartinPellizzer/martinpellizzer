Iris caucasica f. coerulescens

Iris caucasica f. coerulescens, commonly known as the Caucasian blue iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its cultivar form is prized for its unique coloration and ornamental value in horticulture.
