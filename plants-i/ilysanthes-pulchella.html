Ilysanthes pulchella

Ilysanthes pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and thrives in areas with ample sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Ilysanthes pulchella plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
