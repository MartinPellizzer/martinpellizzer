Ifloga decumbens

Ifloga decumbens, commonly known as the creeping antirrhinum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Ifloga decumbens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
