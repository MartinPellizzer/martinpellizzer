Indigofera aeruginis

Indigofera aeruginis, commonly known as blue indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
