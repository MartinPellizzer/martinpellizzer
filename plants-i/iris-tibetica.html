Iris tibetica

Iris tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan iris, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, sunny environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in its harsh habitat.

Iris tibetica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
