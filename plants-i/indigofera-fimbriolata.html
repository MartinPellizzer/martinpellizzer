Indigofera fimbriolata

Indigofera fimbriolata, commonly known as the African indigo or fimbriolata indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds, and it is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses in some African communities.
