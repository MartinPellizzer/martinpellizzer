Ixora nigricans var. typica

Ixora nigricans var. typica, commonly known as the black ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy clusters of bright red flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are borne on short, branched inflorescences and are highly attractive to birds and insects.
