Ipomoea cairica var. lineariloba

Ipomoea cairica var. lineariloba, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
