Inga tetraphylla var. parvifolia

Inga tetraphylla var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its small, elliptical leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
