Ilex paraguariensis f. microphylla

Ilex paraguariensis f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved yerba mate, is a variant of the yerba mate plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its smaller, more densely packed leaves compared to the standard variety. The plant is an evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green surface.

This form is often cultivated for its caffeine content and is used in traditional South American beverages.
