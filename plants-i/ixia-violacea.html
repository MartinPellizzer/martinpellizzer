Ixia violacea

Ixia violacea, commonly known as the violet lily or the violet oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. Ixia violacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and vibrant color.
