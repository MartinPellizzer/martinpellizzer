Impatiens sasidharanii var. hirsuta

Impatiens sasidharanii var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the monsoon season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
