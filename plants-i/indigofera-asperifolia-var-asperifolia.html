Indigofera asperifolia var. asperifolia

Indigofera asperifolia var. asperifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its roots have been used traditionally for their nitrogen-fixing properties, which benefit soil fertility.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
