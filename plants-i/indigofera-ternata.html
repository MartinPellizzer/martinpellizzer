Indigofera ternata

Indigofera ternata, commonly known as blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its roots are also valued for their medicinal properties, including use in treating ailments like diarrhea and inflammation.

Indigofera ternata is a member of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
