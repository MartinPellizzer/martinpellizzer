Imperatoria angustifolia

Imperatoria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Its stems are branched and can spread along the ground, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Imperatoria angustifolia is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
