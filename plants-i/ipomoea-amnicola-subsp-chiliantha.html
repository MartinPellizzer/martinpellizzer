Ipomoea amnicola subsp. chiliantha

Ipomoea amnicola subsp.

chiliantha, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along stream banks and in moist, shaded areas. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
