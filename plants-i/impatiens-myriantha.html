Impatiens myriantha

Impatiens myriantha, commonly known as the Chinese impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an upright herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
