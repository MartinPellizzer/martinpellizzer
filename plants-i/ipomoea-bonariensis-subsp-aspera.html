Ipomoea bonariensis subsp. aspera

Ipomoea bonariensis subsp.

aspera, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture, which gives it its subspecific name "aspera.". It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length.

The flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a day, and are pollinated by insects.
