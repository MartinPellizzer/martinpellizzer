Ixeridium dentatum subsp. ozense

Ixeridium dentatum subsp.

ozense, commonly known as the ozense goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to golden flowers in panicles during the late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
