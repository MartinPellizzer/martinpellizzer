Ipomoea onilahiensis

Ipomoea onilahiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hawaii.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that open in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a vine, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is considered an important species in Hawaiian flora.
