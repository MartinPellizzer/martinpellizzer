Impatiens meruensis subsp. meruensis

Impatiens meruensis subsp.

meruensis, commonly known as the East African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and glandular. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and greenhouses.
