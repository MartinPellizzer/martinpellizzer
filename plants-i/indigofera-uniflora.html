Indigofera uniflora

Indigofera uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces distinctive purple or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for ornamental purposes or in traditional medicine.

Indigofera uniflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
