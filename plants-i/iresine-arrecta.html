Iresine arrecta

Iresine arrecta, commonly known as the red leaf plant or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, crimson-colored leaves that contrast beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, emerging from the leaf axils. Iresine arrecta thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
