Iris acutiloba subsp. longitepala

Iris acutiloba subsp.

longitepala, commonly known as the long-tipped iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive long spur. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its elongated nectar spur, which is adapted for specific pollination mechanisms.
