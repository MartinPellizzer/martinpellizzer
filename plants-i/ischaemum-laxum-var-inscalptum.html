Ischaemum laxum var. inscalptum

Ischaemum laxum var. inscalptum, commonly known as the scarlet carpet grass, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and forms dense mats due to its rhizomatous growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive reddish or purplish tint, especially under stress.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, contributing to its ornamental appeal in certain horticultural settings.
