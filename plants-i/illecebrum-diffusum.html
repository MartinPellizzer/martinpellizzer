Illecebrum diffusum

Illecebrum diffusum, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit, often spreading along the ground through runners. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth patterns.
