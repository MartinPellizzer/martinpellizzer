Ipomoea bonariensis var. pubisepala

Ipomoea bonariensis var. pubisepala, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are pubescent, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vigorous, decorative vine.
