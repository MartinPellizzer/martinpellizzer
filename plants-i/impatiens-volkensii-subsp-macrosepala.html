Impatiens volkensii subsp. macrosepala

Impatiens volkensii subsp.

macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled impatiens, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
