Ipheion gracile

Ipheion gracile, commonly known as the slender white-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and produces small, white or pale blue flowers clustered at the tips of its stems. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in native plant gardens.

Ipheion gracile is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and drought conditions.
