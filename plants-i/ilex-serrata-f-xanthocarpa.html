Ilex serrata f. xanthocarpa

Ilex serrata f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited holly, is a variant of the common holly species characterized by its distinctive yellow berries.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Vietnam and China. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences. The fruit is a bright yellow drupe, which is unique to this form and adds to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
