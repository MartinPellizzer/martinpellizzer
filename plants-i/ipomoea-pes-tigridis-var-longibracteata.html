Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. longibracteata

Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract morning glory, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces distinctive, elongated bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it its specific epithet. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow up to 15 centimeters in diameter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
