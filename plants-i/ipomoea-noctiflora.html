Ipomoea noctiflora

Ipomoea noctiflora, commonly known as the nightshade morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea noctiflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
