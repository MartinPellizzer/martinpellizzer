Iebine

Iebine, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

The plant has a unique reproductive system, relying on specific pollinators that are adapted to its harsh environment. Its leaves are small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss in the arid conditions it inhabits.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, the iebine is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
