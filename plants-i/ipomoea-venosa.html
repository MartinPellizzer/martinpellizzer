Ipomoea venosa

Ipomoea venosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. Its seeds are enclosed in capsule fruits, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.

Ipomoea venosa is also noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
