Ipomoea maragniensis

Ipomoea maragniensis, commonly known as the Maragnien morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along coastal areas or in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
