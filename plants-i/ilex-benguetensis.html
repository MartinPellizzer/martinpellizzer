Ilex benguetensis

Ilex benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. Ilex benguetensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
