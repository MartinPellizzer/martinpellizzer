Indigofera pusilla

Indigofera pusilla, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its small size and delicate appearance make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
