Indigofera microcharoides var. latestipulata

Indigofera microcharoides var. latestipulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
