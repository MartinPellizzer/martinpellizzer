Ipomoea halierca

Ipomoea halierca, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found growing along coastal areas and in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea halierca plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
