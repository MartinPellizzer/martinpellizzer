Indigofera diphylla

Indigofera diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
