Iresine crassifolia

Iresine crassifolia, commonly known as the red leaf iron plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Iresine crassifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf colors and resilience in shaded environments.
