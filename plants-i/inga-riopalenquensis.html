Inga riopalenquensis

Inga riopalenquensis, commonly known as the Río Palenquén inga, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in the understory of humid tropical forests.
