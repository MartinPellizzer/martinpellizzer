Inga portobellensis

Inga portobellensis, commonly known as the Portobello inga, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a sweet scent to attract pollinators.

The tree's bark is smooth and often features a distinctive reddish hue, making it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
