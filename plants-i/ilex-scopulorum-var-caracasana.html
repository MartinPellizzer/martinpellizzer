Ilex scopulorum var. caracasana

Ilex scopulorum var. caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
