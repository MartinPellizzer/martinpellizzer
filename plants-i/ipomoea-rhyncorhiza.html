Ipomoea rhyncorhiza

Ipomoea rhyncorhiza, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant red or orange flowers that attract pollinators. The plant is known for its edible beans, which are rich in nutrients and often used in culinary dishes. Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and tuberous roots.

Its growth cycle includes a distinct dormancy period, making it a seasonal plant in temperate climates.
