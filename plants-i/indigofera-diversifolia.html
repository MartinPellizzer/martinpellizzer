Indigofera diversifolia

Indigofera diversifolia, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
