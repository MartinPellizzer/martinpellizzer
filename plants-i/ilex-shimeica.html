Ilex shimeica

Ilex shimeica, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. Ilex shimeica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and year-round aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes in temperate climates.
