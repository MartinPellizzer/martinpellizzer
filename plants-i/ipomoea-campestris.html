Ipomoea campestris

Ipomoea campestris, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Ipomoea campestris is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly in suitable conditions.
