Iresine elatior

Iresine elatior, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety leaves that range from deep red to purple, often with contrasting green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which contrast with its showy foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Iresine elatior is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic leaf coloration.
