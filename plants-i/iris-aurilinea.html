Iris aurilinea

Iris aurilinea, commonly known as the golden iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with golden markings. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Iris aurilinea is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to a variety of growing conditions.
