Iris stenoloba

Iris stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy blue or violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Iridaceae family.
