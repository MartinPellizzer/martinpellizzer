Ilex perado var. iberica

Ilex perado var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

Ilex perado var. iberica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
