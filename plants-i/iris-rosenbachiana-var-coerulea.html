Iris rosenbachiana var. coerulea

Iris rosenbachiana var. coerulea, commonly known as the Blue Iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, six-petaled flowers in shades of deep blue to violet. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette. This variety is prized for its vibrant color and elegant appearance in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
