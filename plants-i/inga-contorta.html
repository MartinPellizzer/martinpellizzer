Inga contorta

Inga contorta, commonly known as the tropical evergreen tree, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its fast-growing habit and dense foliage. The plant has compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged symmetrically. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

Inga contorta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
