Ipomoea galapagensis

Ipomoea galapagensis, commonly known as the Galápagos morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its island habitat. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
