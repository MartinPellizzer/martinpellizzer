Ipomoea brevipedicellata

Ipomoea brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens but can become invasive if not managed properly.
