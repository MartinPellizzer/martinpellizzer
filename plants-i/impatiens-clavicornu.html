Impatiens clavicornu

Impatiens clavicornu, commonly known as the horned impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated calyx that resembles a horn, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens clavicornu is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
