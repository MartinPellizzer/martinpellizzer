Ipomoea lapathifolia var. bussei

Ipomoea lapathifolia var. bussei, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are showy, trumpet-shaped, and typically pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
