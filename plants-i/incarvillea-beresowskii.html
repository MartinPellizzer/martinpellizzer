Incarvillea beresowskii

Incarvillea beresowskii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces striking violet or blue flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
