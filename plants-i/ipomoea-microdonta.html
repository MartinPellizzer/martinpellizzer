Ipomoea microdonta

Ipomoea microdonta, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white. Its vines can climb and spread rapidly, often entwining other plants.

Ipomoea microdonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
