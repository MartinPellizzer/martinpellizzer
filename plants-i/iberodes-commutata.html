Iberodes commutata

Iberodes commutata, commonly known as the narrowleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found along riverbanks and in saline soils, demonstrating some degree of salt tolerance.

Iberodes commutata is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a notable plant in its native habitat.
