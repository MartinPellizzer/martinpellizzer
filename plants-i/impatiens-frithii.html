Impatiens frithii

Impatiens frithii, commonly known as the Frith's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often appearing in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
