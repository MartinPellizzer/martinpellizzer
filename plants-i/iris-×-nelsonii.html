Iris × nelsonii

Iris × nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features sword-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This iris is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
