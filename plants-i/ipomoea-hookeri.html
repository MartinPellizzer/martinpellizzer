Ipomoea hookeri

Ipomoea hookeri, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using tendrils to attach itself to supports.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value in gardens.
