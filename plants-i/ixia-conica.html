Ixia conica

Ixia conica, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The flowers are characterized by their trumpet-shaped petals and are often used in floral arrangements. Ixia conica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes during that season.
