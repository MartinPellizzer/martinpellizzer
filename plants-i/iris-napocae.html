Iris napocae

Iris napocae, commonly known as the Hungarian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, while its rhizomes are thick and fleshy.

Iris napocae is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
