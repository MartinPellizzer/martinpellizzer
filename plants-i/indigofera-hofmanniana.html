Indigofera hofmanniana

Indigofera hofmanniana, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flowers, which bloom in dense clusters along the stems. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
