Inula linifolia

Inula linifolia, commonly known as the smooth inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Inula linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
