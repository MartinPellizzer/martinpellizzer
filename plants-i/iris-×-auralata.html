Iris × auralata

Iris × auralata, commonly known as the purple iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale blue throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring grass-like leaves that are sword-shaped and bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and floral arrangements.
