Indigofera sordida

Indigofera sordida, commonly known as the sordid indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
