Ionidium verbenaceum

Ionidium verbenaceum, commonly known as the common verbena.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family, characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in summer. It is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ionidium verbenaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
