Isoloma verticillatum

Isoloma verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral leaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Isoloma verticillatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
