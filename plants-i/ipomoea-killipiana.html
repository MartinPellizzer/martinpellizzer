Ipomoea killipiana

Ipomoea killipiana, commonly known as the Killip's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ipomoea killipiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
