Indigofera vicioides var. rogersii

Indigofera vicioides var. rogersii, commonly known as the blue bean or mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
