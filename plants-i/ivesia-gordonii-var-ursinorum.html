Ivesia gordonii var. ursinorum

Ivesia gordonii var. ursinorum, commonly known as the yellow stonecress, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments, such as dry slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and bear small, clustered flowers. Its flowers are yellow, with four petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
