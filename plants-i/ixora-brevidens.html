Ixora brevidens

Ixora brevidens, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Ixora brevidens is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
