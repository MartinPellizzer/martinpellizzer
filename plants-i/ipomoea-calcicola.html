Ipomoea calcicola

Ipomoea calcicola, commonly known as the limestone morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its vines can climb or trail along the ground.

Ipomoea calcicola is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
