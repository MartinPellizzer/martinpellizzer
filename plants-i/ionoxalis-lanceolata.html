Ionoxalis lanceolata

Ionoxalis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three lance-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ionoxalis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
