Isodon pantadenius

Isodon pantadenius, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Isodon pantadenius is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
