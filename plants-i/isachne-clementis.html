Isachne clementis

Isachne clementis, commonly known as the Clement's isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, bearing small, two-ranked flowers.

Isachne clementis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
