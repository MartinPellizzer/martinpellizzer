Isolatocereus

Isolatocereus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically white or pale in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Isolatocereus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and unique blooming habits.
