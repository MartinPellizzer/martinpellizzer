Indigofera viscosa var. brachycarpa

Indigofera viscosa var. brachycarpa, commonly known as narrow-seeded indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry climates, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
