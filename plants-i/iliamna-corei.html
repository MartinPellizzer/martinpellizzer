Iliamna corei

Iliamna corei, commonly known as the Alaska currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, coastal environments and thrives in sandy or loamy soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.
