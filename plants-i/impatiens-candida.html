Impatiens candida

Impatiens candida, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features delicate, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a spurred shape. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Impatiens candida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
