Inula foliosa

Inula foliosa, commonly known as the leafy inula, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Inula.

Inula foliosa is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
