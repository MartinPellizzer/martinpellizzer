Ivesia shockleyi

Ivesia shockleyi, commonly known as Shockley's ivesia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Ivesia shockleyi is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
