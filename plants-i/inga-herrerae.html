Inga herrerae

Inga herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, typical of the legume family.

Inga herrerae is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
