Ipomoea velardei var. aequatoriana

Ipomoea velardei var. aequatoriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to white, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.

The vine can grow up to several meters in length, climbing by means of tendrils.
