Iris barnumiae subsp. barnumiae

Iris barnumiae subsp.

barnumiae, commonly known as the Barnum's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellowish throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, while its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its hardy nature.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
