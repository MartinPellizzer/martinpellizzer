Ixeris dentata subsp. kitayamensis

Ixeris dentata subsp.

kitayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia. It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
