Ipomoea dissecta var. malvifolia

Ipomoea dissecta var. malvifolia, commonly known as the Malvifolia morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive, finely divided appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known morning glories.
