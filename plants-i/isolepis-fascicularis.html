Isolepis fascicularis

Isolepis fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, contributing to wetland health.

Isolepis fascicularis is also an important food source for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
