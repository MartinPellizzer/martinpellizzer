Indigofera angustifolia

Indigofera angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in dye production.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms depending on the climate.
