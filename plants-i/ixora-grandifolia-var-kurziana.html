Ixora grandifolia var. kurziana

Ixora grandifolia var. kurziana, commonly known as the Kurz's ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and produces clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
