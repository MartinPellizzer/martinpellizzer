Illecebrum obcordatum

Illecebrum obcordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a trailing habit and can be found in moist, shaded areas along riverbanks and in forests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to cover surfaces effectively.
