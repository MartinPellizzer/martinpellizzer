Iris dolichosiphon subsp. dolichosiphon

Iris dolichosiphon subsp.

dolichosiphon, commonly known as the long-beak iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
