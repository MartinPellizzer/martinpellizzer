Iresine inaguensis

Iresine inaguensis, commonly known as the red leaf plant or red leaf sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red or purple foliage, which is often contrasted with its smaller, inconspicuous white or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Iresine inaguensis thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, though they are typically consumed in small quantities due to their tart flavor.
