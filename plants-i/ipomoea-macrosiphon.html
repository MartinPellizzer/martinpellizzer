Ipomoea macrosiphon

Ipomoea macrosiphon, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, typically in shades of violet or blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or animals.
