Ipomoea excisa

Ipomoea excisa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend structures such as trellises or fences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea excisa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
