Indigofera malongensis

Indigofera malongensis, commonly known as the Malongensis indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
