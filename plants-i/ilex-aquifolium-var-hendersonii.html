Ilex aquifolium var. hendersonii

Ilex aquifolium var. hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the British Isles, particularly found in Scotland. The leaves are typically spiny and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside, often featuring a waxy coating. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
