Ipomoea bisperma

Ipomoea bisperma, commonly known as the red spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it grows as a vine or shrub, often climbing or sprawling along the ground. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea bisperma is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
