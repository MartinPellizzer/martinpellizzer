Ipomoea tiliacea var. smithii

Ipomoea tiliacea var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb trellises or other structures.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
