Ilex longecaudata

Ilex longecaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elongated and often have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Ilex longecaudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide dense foliage in gardens and landscapes.
