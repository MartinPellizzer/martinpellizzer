Isoetes clavata

Isoetes clavata, commonly known as the clubmoss or scaly watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically found at the tips of its underwater stems. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dark, club-shaped spore cases and narrow, ribbon-like leaves. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Isoetes clavata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
