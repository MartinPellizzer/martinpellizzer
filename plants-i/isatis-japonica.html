Isatis japonica

Isatis japonica, commonly known as Japanese woad, is a biennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in gardens and naturalized areas.

Its historical significance lies in its use for producing indigo dye.
