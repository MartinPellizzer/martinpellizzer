Ixeris graminifolia

Ixeris graminifolia, commonly known as the slender goldenweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to drought conditions and harsh climates.
