Ipomoea shirensis

Ipomoea shirensis, commonly known as the moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a rapid growth habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
