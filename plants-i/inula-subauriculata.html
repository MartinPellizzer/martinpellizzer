Inula subauriculata

Inula subauriculata, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purplish hue along the edges.

Inula subauriculata thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
