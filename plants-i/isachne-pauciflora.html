Isachne pauciflora

Isachne pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.

Isachne pauciflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
