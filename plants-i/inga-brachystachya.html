Inga brachystachya

Inga brachystachya, commonly known as the dwarf inga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
