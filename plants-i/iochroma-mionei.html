Iochroma mionei

Iochroma mionei, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blooms that are often adorned with a distinctive yellow or green throat. Its leaves are typically ovate and covered in a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iochroma mionei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited to mild climates.
