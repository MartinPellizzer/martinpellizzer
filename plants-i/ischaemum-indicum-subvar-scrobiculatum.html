Ischaemum indicum subvar. scrobiculatum

Ischaemum indicum subvar. scrobiculatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense stands, which can be both a weed and a forage plant.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
