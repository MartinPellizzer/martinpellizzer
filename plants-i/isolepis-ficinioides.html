Isolepis ficinioides

Isolepis ficinioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences during the growing season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Isolepis ficinioides plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
