Ipomoea densivestita

Ipomoea densivestita, commonly known as the dense-haired morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Ipomoea densivestita is a perennial vine that can grow up to several meters in length.
