Ipomoea palmata

Ipomoea palmata, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to 10 feet in a single season.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and showy blooms.
