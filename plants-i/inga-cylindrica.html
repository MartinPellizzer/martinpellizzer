Inga cylindrica

Inga cylindrica, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds surrounded by a sticky, sweet pulp. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
