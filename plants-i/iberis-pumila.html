Iberis pumila

Iberis pumila, commonly known as dwarf candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in a variety of climates, including colder regions.
