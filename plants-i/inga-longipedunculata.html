Inga longipedunculata

Inga longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems.
