Inula britannica

Inula britannica, commonly known as British elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a tall, sturdy stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
