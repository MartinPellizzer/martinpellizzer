Impatiens megamalayana

Impatiens megamalayana, commonly known as the Malayan impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that burst open to disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
