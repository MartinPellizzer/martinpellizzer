Isatis gymnocarpa

Isatis gymnocarpa, commonly known as the naked-seed woad, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its historical use in producing indigo dye.
