Isodon stracheyi

Isodon stracheyi, commonly known as the African geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Isodon stracheyi is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
