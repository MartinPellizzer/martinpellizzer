Isotria verticillata

Isotria verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated primarily by bees.

Isotria verticillata has a fibrous root system and is often found in shaded, rich soils.
