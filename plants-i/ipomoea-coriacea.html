Ipomoea coriacea

Ipomoea coriacea, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Ipomoea coriacea is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar climbing habits.
