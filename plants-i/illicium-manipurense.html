Illicium manipurense

Illicium manipurense, commonly known as the Manipur star anise, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruit that resembles the more familiar star anise. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers and has dark green, leathery leaves. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to star anise.

The botanical features of Illicium manipurense make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
