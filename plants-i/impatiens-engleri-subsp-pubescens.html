Impatiens engleri subsp. pubescens

Impatiens engleri subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "pubescens.".

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
