Impatiens roylei var. macrochila

Impatiens roylei var. macrochila, commonly known as the large-lipped impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The variety macrochila is distinguished by its notably enlarged lip, which is a key feature in its classification.
