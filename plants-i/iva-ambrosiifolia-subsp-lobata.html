Iva ambrosiifolia subsp. lobata

Iva ambrosiifolia subsp.

lobata, commonly known as the broadleaf marshelder, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
