Ipomoea stenophyton

Ipomoea stenophyton, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and grows as a climbing vine with tendrils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Ipomoea stenophyton is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and disturbed soils.
