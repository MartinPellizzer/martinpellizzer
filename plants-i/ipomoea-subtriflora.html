Ipomoea subtriflora

Ipomoea subtriflora, commonly known as the three-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with trailing or climbing stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to white in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines can become invasive if not controlled.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
