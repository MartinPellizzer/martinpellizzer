Inula linariifolia

Inula linariifolia, commonly known as the common elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray flowers surrounded by dense, yellow disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inula linariifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
