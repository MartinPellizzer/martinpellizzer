Inga feuillei

Inga feuillei, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in reforestation efforts.

Inga feuillei is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
