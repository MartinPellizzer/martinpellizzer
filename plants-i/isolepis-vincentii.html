Isolepis vincentii

Isolepis vincentii, commonly known as Vincent's spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes help it spread and form dense colonies in moist soil.

Isolepis vincentii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
