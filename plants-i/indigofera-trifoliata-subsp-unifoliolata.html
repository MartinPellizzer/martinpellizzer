Indigofera trifoliata subsp. unifoliolata

Indigofera trifoliata subsp.

unifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved indigo, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine.
