Inula involucrata

Inula involucrata, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Inula involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
