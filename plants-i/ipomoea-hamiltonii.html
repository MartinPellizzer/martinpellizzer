Ipomoea hamiltonii

Ipomoea hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it a popular ornamental in warm climates. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea hamiltonii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
