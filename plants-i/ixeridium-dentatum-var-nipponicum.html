Ixeridium dentatum var. nipponicum

Ixeridium dentatum var. nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese bristle fern, is a variant of the bristle fern species native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive serrated margin that gives it its scientific name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
