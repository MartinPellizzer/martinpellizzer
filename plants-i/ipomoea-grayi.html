Ipomoea grayi

Ipomoea grayi, commonly known as the gray morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
