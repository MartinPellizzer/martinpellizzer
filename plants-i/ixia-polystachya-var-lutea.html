Ixia polystachya var. lutea

Ixia polystachya var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow fire lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllis family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
