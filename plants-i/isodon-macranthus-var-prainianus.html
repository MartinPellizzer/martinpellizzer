Isodon macranthus var. prainianus

Isodon macranthus var. prainianus, commonly known as the Prain's purple grass, is a variant of the Isodon genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the inflorescence is usually a dense spike.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
