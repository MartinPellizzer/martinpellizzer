Isoetes hypsophila

Isoetes hypsophila, commonly known as the mountain water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized leaves called sporophylls. This plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands. Its morphology includes a rosette-like arrangement of leaves, with some leaves specialized for reproduction.

Isoetes hypsophila is also notable for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions, making it an indicator species for certain aquatic ecosystems.
