Isoglossa cataractarum

Isoglossa cataractarum, commonly known as the water lily of the highlands, is a perennial aquatic plant native to certain regions of South America.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface, while its roots anchor in the mud at the bottom. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

Isoglossa cataractarum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
