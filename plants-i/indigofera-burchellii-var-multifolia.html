Indigofera burchellii var. multifolia

Indigofera burchellii var. multifolia, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark blue seeds, which are used for dyeing textiles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and historical use in natural dyeing make it a notable species in botanical studies.
