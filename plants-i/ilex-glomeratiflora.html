Ilex glomeratiflora

Ilex glomeratiflora, commonly known as the clustered flower holly, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.

Ilex glomeratiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
