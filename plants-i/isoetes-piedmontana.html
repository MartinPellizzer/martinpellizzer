Isoetes piedmontana

Isoetes piedmontana, commonly known as the Piedmont watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth pattern and its ability to form dense mats on the bottom of lakes and ponds. The plant has a unique structure with a central stem that bears small, spore-bearing leaves called sporophylls. It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Piedmont region, where it prefers slow-moving or still waters.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of water quality.
