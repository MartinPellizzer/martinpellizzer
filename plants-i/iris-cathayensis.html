Iris cathayensis

Iris cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Iris cathayensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
