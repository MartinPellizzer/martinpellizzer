Ipomoea longeramosa

Ipomoea longeramosa, commonly known as the long-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and long, slender stems. It thrives in warm climates with full sun and well-drained soil.

Ipomoea longeramosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
