Ipheion viridius

Ipheion viridius, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Ipheion viridius is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
