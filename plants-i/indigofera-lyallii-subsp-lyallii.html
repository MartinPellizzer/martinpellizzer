Indigofera lyallii subsp. lyallii

Indigofera lyallii subsp.

lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's indigo, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
