Iris miraculosa

Iris miraculosa, commonly known as the miraculous iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant features broad, grass-like leaves and produces intricate, spurred petals that are highly ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its resilience and beauty have earned it a place in both horticultural collections and botanical studies.
