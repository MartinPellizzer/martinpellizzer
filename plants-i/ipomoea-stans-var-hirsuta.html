Ipomoea stans var. hirsuta

Ipomoea stans var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The hairy morning glory is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
