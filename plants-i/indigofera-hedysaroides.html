Indigofera hedysaroides

Indigofera hedysaroides, commonly known as the sweet indigo or blue woad, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of purple to blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its historical use in dye production and ornamental horticulture highlights its ecological and cultural significance.
