Inga olivacea

Inga olivacea, commonly known as the olive acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has trifoliate leaves with stipules, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes. Inga olivacea is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
