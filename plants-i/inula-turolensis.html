Inula turolensis

Inula turolensis, commonly known as the Turolin hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer, are arranged in a cluster known as a capitulum.

Inula turolensis is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
