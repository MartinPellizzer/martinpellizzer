Inula aucheriana

Inula aucheriana, commonly known as the yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a rough texture.

Inula aucheriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
