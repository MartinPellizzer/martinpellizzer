Ipomoea orizabensis var. austromexicana

Ipomoea orizabensis var. austromexicana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant is a vigorous vine that can climb using its tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers emit a mild fragrance.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
