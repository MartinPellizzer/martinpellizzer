Indigofera variabilis

Indigofera variabilis, commonly known as variable indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces cylindrical pods containing several elliptical seeds.

Indigofera variabilis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
