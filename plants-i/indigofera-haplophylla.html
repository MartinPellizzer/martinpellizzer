Indigofera haplophylla

Indigofera haplophylla, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years, contributing to its ability to persist in harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
