Ipomoea lutambensis

Ipomoea lutambensis, commonly known as the Lutambensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and vine-like.

Ipomoea lutambensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
