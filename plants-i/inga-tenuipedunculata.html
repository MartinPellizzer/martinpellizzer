Inga tenuipedunculata

Inga tenuipedunculata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems.
