Ipomoea aprica

Ipomoea aprica, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Ipomoea aprica is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of soil conditions.
