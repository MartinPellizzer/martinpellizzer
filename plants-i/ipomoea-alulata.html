Ipomoea alulata

Ipomoea alulata, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shady environments.

Ipomoea alulata is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing habits.
