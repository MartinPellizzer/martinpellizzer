Ipomoea malvaeoides var. heterophylla

Ipomoea malvaeoides var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a climbing vine.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological variation and its role in the Ipomoea genus.
