Iris athoa

Iris athoa, commonly known as the New Zealand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or open areas. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Iris athoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
