Inula × haussmannii

Inula × haussmannii, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Inula helenium and Inula crithmoides, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant features tall, straight stems covered in dense, white to pale yellow ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Inula × haussmannii is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks.
