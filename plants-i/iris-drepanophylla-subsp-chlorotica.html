Iris drepanophylla subsp. chlorotica

Iris drepanophylla subsp.

chlorotica, commonly known as the green iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lower petal.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is notable for its bright green coloration, which sets it apart from other iris species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
