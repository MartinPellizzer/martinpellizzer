Iris rozaliae

Iris rozaliae, commonly known as the Rozalia iris, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Alps. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris rozaliae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
