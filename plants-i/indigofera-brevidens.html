Indigofera brevidens

Indigofera brevidens, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological restoration.
