Iria xyridis

Iria xyridis, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Iria xyridis thrives in moist, sunny habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
