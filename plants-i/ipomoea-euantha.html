Ipomoea euantha

Ipomoea euantha, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to ascend supports. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea euantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
