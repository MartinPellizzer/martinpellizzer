Indigofera setacea

Indigofera setacea, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
