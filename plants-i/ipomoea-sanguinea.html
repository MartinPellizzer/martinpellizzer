Ipomoea sanguinea

Ipomoea sanguinea, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread along supports. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea sanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
