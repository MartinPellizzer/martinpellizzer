Indigofera stachyodes

Indigofera stachyodes, commonly known as blue bean or wild indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds.

Indigofera stachyodes is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
