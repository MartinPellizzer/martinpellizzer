Indigofera rostrata subsp. namaachensis

Indigofera rostrata subsp.

namaachensis, commonly known as the Namibian indigo, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
