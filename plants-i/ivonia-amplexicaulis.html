Ivonia amplexicaulis

Ivonia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping ivy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, foliage that often clasps the stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ivonia amplexicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from containers or rockeries.
