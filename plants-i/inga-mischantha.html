Inga mischantha

Inga mischantha, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The tree is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
