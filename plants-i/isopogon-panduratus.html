Isopogon panduratus

Isopogon panduratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Isopogon panduratus is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
