Ipomoea batatas var. xanthorhiza

Ipomoea batatas var. xanthorhiza, commonly known as the yellow sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flesh.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and trailing stems that bear vibrant purple or violet flowers. Its tuberous roots are rich in carbohydrates and contain beta-carotene, making it a nutritious food source.

This variety is often cultivated for both its culinary value and ornamental appeal.
