Impatiens munroi

Impatiens munroi, commonly known as the Sierra Leone impatiens, is a flowering plant native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Impatiens munroi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
