Idesia polycarpa var. vestita

Idesia polycarpa var. vestita, commonly known as the silver maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
