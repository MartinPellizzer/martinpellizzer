Ipomoea ornata

Ipomoea ornata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and lobed, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea ornata is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
