Ipomoea pearceana

Ipomoea pearceana, commonly known as the Pearce's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a desirable garden plant and an invasive species in some areas.
