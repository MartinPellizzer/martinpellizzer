Indigofera paraglaucifolia

Indigofera paraglaucifolia, commonly known as the blue bean or narrow-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
