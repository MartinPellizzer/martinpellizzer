Indigofera radicifera subsp. arthrocarpa

Indigofera radicifera subsp.

arthrocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to sub-Saharan Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in savanna and woodland habitats. It has adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and potential agricultural purposes.
