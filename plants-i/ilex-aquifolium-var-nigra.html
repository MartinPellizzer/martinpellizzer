Ilex aquifolium var. nigra

Ilex aquifolium var. nigra, commonly known as the black holly, is a variant of the common holly that is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The leaves are typically elliptical, with spiny margins and a deep green color that becomes more pronounced in the autumn. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its evergreen foliage and seasonal interest.
