Isoetes foveolata var. plenospora

Isoetes foveolata var. plenospora, commonly known as the full-spored water mace, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are densely packed and often found in clusters on the plant's leaf-like structures. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its morphology includes a central axis with specialized leaves that form a rosette-like structure.

This variety is notable for its distinct spore morphology, which aids in its identification and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
