Ilex serrata var. argutidens

Ilex serrata var. argutidens, commonly known as the Japanese holly or serrate holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves with serrated edges that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice in gardens and parks.
