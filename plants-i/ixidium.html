Ixidium

Ixidium, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

However, it is toxic and should be handled with care.
