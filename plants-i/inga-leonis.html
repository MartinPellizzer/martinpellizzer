Inga leonis

Inga leonis, commonly known as the golden inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Inga leonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
