Inula pseudosalicina

Inula pseudosalicina, commonly known as false willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
