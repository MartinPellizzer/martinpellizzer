Isouratea spectabilis

Isouratea spectabilis, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Isouratea spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
