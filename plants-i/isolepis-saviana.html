Isolepis saviana

Isolepis saviana, commonly known as the narrowleaf tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in dense, tussock-like clumps, with spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form extensive colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Isolepis saviana plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
