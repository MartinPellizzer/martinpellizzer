Ipomoea paniculata var. mauritiana

Ipomoea paniculata var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
