Indigofera squalida

Indigofera squalida, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
