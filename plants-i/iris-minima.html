Iris minima

Iris minima, commonly known as the dwarf iris, is a small perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. It produces showy, three-petaled flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer.

Iris minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
