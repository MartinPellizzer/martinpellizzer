Inga monzonensis

Inga monzonensis, commonly known as the Monzonensis Umbrella Tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its large, umbrella-shaped canopy, which provides shade and habitat for various organisms. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces legume pods that contain edible seeds.

Inga monzonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
