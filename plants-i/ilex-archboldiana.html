Ilex archboldiana

Ilex archboldiana, commonly known as the Archbold holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for various garden settings.

Ilex archboldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
