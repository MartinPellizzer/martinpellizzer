Ixia antholyziformis

Ixia antholyziformis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower stalks that bear bright orange-red blooms. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that add texture to the landscape.
