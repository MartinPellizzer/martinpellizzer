Ipomoea abyssinica

Ipomoea abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea abyssinica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
