Indigofera atriceps subsp. setosissima

Indigofera atriceps subsp.

setosissima, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet.

The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for various traditional purposes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
