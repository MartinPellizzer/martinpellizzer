Ipomoea eminii

Ipomoea eminii, commonly known as the eminii morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a rapid growth habit. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, though some varieties may exhibit white or pink hues.

Ipomoea eminii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
