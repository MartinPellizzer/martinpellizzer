Isoetes septentrionalis

Isoetes septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern watermint, is a member of the watermint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its unique morphology.

This plant is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic species that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color. The leaves are narrow and linear, often floating on the water surface or submerged beneath it.

Isoetes septentrionalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
