Ipomoea bonariensis f. glabrata

Ipomoea bonariensis f. glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant is a vigorous climber with a trailing growth habit, often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and container planting.
