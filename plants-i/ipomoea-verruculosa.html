Ipomoea verruculosa

Ipomoea verruculosa, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found twining around trees and shrubs in its natural habitat. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, round seeds.

Ipomoea verruculosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
