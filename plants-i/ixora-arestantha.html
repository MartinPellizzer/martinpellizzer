Ixora arestantha

Ixora arestantha, commonly known as the red flame ixora, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant. Its flowers are typically visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Ixora arestantha thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
