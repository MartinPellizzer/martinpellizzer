Ipomoea bonariensis f. trichosepala

Ipomoea bonariensis f. trichosepala, commonly known as the trichosepala morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture. This plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
