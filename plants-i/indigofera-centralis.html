Indigofera centralis

Indigofera centralis, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a dye source.
