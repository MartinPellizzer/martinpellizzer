Impatiens ohwadae

Impatiens ohwadae, commonly known as Ohwada's impatiens, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a herbaceous plant with delicate, spurred petals that are typically pink or white in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
