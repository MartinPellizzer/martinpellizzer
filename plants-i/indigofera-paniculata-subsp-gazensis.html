Indigofera paniculata subsp. gazensis

Indigofera paniculata subsp.

gazensis, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale blue or white, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
