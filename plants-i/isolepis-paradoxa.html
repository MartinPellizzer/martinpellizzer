Isolepis paradoxa

Isolepis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.

Isolepis paradoxa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
