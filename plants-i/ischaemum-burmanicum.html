Ischaemum burmanicum

Ischaemum burmanicum, commonly known as Burman's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Ischaemum burmanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
