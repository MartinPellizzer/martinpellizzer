Ixora crassipes

Ixora crassipes, commonly known as the coastal ixora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in coastal areas and saline environments, making it highly salt-tolerant. Its woody structure and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a valuable species for coastal stabilization.

Ixora crassipes is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
