Ipomoea ventricosa

Ipomoea ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is characterized by its inflated, balloon-like seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
