Ipheion brevipes

Ipheion brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ipheion brevipes is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
