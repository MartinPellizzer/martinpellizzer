Indigofera campestris var. intermedia

Indigofera campestris var. intermedia, commonly known as the wild indigo, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
