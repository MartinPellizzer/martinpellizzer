Iris hymenospatha subsp. hymenospatha

Iris hymenospatha subsp.

hymenospatha, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are showy, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, often in shades of blue or violet.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
