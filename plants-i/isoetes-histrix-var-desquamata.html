Isoetes histrix var. desquamata

Isoetes histrix var. desquamata, commonly known as the scaly water clubmoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that form dense, ribbon-like structures. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, club-shaped formations along the leaf bases.

This variety is distinguished by its scaly appearance on the leaf surfaces, which gives it its botanical name.
