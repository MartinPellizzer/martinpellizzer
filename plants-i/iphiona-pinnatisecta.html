Iphiona pinnatisecta

Iphiona pinnatisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlox, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are deeply lobed, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
