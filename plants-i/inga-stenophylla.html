Inga stenophylla

Inga stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds.

Inga stenophylla is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
