Incarvillea himalayensis

Incarvillea himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a robust root system and upright, branching stems. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
