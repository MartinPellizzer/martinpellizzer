Ipomoea limbata

Ipomoea limbata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and a hairy texture. It blooms primarily in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea limbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
