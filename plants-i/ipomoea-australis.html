Ipomoea australis

Ipomoea australis, commonly known as the Australian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea australis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
