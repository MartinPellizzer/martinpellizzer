Ilex latifrons

Ilex latifrons, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, spherical berries that are attractive to birds. Ilex latifrons thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal contribute to its value in both natural and cultivated environments.
