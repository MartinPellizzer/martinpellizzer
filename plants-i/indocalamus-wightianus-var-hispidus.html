Indocalamus wightianus var. hispidus

Indocalamus wightianus var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the broad-leaved bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, tall stalks and dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive hairy texture on the leaf surfaces. The plant exhibits a sympodial growth pattern, forming clumps that can reach several meters in height. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a rough, pubescent surface, contributing to its unique appearance.

This bamboo is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional crafts and construction.
