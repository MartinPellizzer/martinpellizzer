Ipomoea mombassana var. massaica

Ipomoea mombassana var. massaica, commonly known as the Mombassa morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately veined leaves and is typically found growing in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, using tendrils to support its growth.
