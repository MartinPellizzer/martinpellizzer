Ipomoea officinalis

Ipomoea officinalis, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit.

Ipomoea officinalis has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including treating ailments related to the respiratory and digestive systems.
