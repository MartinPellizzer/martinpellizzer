Ipomoea horrida

Ipomoea horrida, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves, as well as its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, toxic berries that contain alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats, including roadsides and gardens.

Botanically, it is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habit.
