Isodon × togashii

Isodon × togashii, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late summer. It is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
