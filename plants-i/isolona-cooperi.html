Isolona cooperi

Isolona cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Isolona cooperi is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
