Indigofera volkensii

Indigofera volkensii, commonly known as the African indigo or Volkens' indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Indigofera volkensii is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
