Illecebrum gnaphalodes

Illecebrum gnaphalodes, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces toxic berries that are yellow when mature, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
