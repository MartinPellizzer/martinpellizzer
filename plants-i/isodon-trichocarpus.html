Isodon trichocarpus

Isodon trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy fruit isodon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, clustered in whorls along the stems.

Isodon trichocarpus is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
