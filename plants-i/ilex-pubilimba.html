Ilex pubilimba

Ilex pubilimba, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex pubilimba thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical regions.
