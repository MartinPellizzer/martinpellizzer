Ipomoea clarensis

Ipomoea clarensis, commonly known as the Clarens morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Ipomoea clarensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
