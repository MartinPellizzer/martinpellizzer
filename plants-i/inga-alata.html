Inga alata

Inga alata, commonly known as the "arrow leaf" or "wild coffee," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has trifoliate leaves with elongated, arrow-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white.

Inga alata produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
