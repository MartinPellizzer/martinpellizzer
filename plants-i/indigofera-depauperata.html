Indigofera depauperata

Indigofera depauperata, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
