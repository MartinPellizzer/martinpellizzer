Impatiens lugubris

Impatiens lugubris, commonly known as the sorrowful touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and prefers moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Impatiens lugubris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
