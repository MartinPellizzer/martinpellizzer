Isopogon panduratus subsp. panduratus

Isopogon panduratus subsp.

panduratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. It is part of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

The subspecies is relatively rare and is often found in coastal regions.
