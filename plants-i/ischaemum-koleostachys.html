Ischaemum koleostachys

Ischaemum koleostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Ischaemum koleostachys thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean flora and a subject of ecological study.
