Ipomoea acetosifolia var. longifolia

Ipomoea acetosifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base of the petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
