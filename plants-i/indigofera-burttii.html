Indigofera burttii

Indigofera burttii, commonly known as the African indigo or Burtt's indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in its native habitat.
