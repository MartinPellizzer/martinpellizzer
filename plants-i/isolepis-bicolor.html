Isolepis bicolor

Isolepis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and damp meadows. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Isolepis bicolor is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
