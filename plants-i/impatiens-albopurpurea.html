Impatiens albopurpurea

Impatiens albopurpurea, commonly known as the white-purple impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Impatiens albopurpurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
