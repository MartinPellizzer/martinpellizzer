Ipomoea albiflora var. divergens

Ipomoea albiflora var. divergens, commonly known as the whiteflower morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in gardens or as a cover plant in shaded areas.
