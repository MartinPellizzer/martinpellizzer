Isatis pinnata

Isatis pinnata, commonly known as the yellow flag or yellow isatis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Isatis pinnata is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
