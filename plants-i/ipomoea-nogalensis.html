Ipomoea nogalensis

Ipomoea nogalensis, commonly known as the Nogal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other morning glory species.

Ipomoea nogalensis is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to desert gardens.
