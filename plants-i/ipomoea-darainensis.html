Ipomoea darainensis

Ipomoea darainensis, commonly known as the Darain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many vine-like species.

Ipomoea darainensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
