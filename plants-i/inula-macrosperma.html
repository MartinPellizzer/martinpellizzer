Inula macrosperma

Inula macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded elecampane, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
