Ipomoea tuxtlensis

Ipomoea tuxtlensis, commonly known as the Tuxtlans wild morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy flowers, often appearing in the early morning. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

Ipomoea tuxtlensis is also valued for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
