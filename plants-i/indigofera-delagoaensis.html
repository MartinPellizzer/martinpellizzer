Indigofera delagoaensis

Indigofera delagoaensis, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils.

It is also known for its historical use in dye production, particularly for producing a blue pigment.
