Ipomoea lililflora

Ipomoea lililflora, commonly known as the Fairy Vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
