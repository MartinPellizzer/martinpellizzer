Ipomoea crinicalyx

Ipomoea crinicalyx, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Ipomoea crinicalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
