Indigofera lignosa

Indigofera lignosa, commonly known as African indigo, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing dark blue seeds, which have been historically used for dyeing textiles. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions.

Indigofera lignosa is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
