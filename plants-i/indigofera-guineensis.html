Indigofera guineensis

Indigofera guineensis, commonly known as African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its historical use in producing natural dyes, particularly the deep blue indigo pigment. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Indigofera guineensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest edge habitats.
