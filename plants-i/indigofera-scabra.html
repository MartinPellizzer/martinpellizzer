Indigofera scabra

Indigofera scabra, commonly known as rough indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native ecosystems.
