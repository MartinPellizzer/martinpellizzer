Iris subdichotoma f. alba

Iris subdichotoma f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered iris subdichotoma, is a botanical variant of the species Iris subdichotoma.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical blue or purple hues of the parent species. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, upright flower stalks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
