Indocalamus floribundus

Indocalamus floribundus, commonly known as the fragrant bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
