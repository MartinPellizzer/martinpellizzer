Ionidium calceolarium

Ionidium calceolarium, commonly known as the slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a trap to catch pollinators. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically vibrant in color, ranging from yellow to orange, and emit a sweet fragrance.

The plant's specialized morphology reflects its evolutionary adaptation for efficient pollination and seed dispersal.
