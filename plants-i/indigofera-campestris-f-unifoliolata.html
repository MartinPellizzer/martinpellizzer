Indigofera campestris f. unifoliolata

Indigofera campestris f. unifoliolata, commonly known as the single leaf indigo plant, is a variant of the indigo plant characterized by its unique foliage.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves, but this form exhibits a single leaflet, distinguishing it from the typical variety. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are often pink or purple in color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in dye production.
