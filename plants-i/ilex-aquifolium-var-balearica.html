Ilex aquifolium var. balearica

Ilex aquifolium var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Balearic Islands.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with spiny margins, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
