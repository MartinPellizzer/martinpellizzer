Isnardia media

Isnardia media, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in damp meadows and along stream banks. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

However, it is toxic to livestock and should be handled with care.
