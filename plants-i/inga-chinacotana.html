Inga chinacotana

Inga chinacotana, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be dispersed by animals or through wind.

Inga chinacotana plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity and soil fertility in its native habitats.
