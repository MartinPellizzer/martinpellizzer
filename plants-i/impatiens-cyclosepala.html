Impatiens cyclosepala

Impatiens cyclosepala, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
