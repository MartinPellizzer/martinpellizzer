Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-purpurea

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-purpurea, commonly known as the silver-purple holly, is a variant of the common holly with distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are narrow, glossy, and exhibit a striking combination of silver-white and purplish hues on their upper surfaces. The plant is evergreen, featuring a dense, bushy growth habit that makes it suitable for hedges or ornamental planting. It produces small, red berries in the winter, which are an important food source for birds.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
