Inodes causiarum

Inodes causiarum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, spiraled stems. The plant has a distinctive structure with broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the aquatic environment.

The botanical features of Inodes causiarum make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
