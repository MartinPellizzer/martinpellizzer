Ilyphilos

Ilyphilos, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid flowering cycle. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating.

Ilyphilos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
