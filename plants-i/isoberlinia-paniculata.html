Isoberlinia paniculata

Isoberlinia paniculata, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous elliptical leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
