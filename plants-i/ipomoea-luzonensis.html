Ipomoea luzonensis

Ipomoea luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a herbaceous vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ipomoea luzonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
