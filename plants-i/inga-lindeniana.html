Inga lindeniana

Inga lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindena Inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agroforestry systems.

Inga lindeniana is also appreciated for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
