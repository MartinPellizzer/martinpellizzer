Ilex spruceana var. genuina

Ilex spruceana var. genuina, commonly known as the Appalachian holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, and its small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a compact growth habit with dense, spiny branches, making it suitable for hedges or ornamental planting.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
