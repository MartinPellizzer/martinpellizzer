Inga vouapifolia

Inga vouapifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and plays a role in local ecosystems.

Its growth habit and ornamental features make it a notable species in botanical studies.
