Ixia falcata

Ixia falcata, commonly known as the yellow ox tongue, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens during the cooler months. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Ixia falcata is well-suited for rock gardens and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice among horticulturists.
