Ianthe occidentalis

Ianthe occidentalis, commonly known as the western bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ianthe occidentalis has a fibrous root system and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
