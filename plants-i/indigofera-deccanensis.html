Indigofera deccanensis

Indigofera deccanensis, commonly known as the Deccan indigo plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its historical significance includes use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
