Ischaemum sehima

Ischaemum sehima, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelets that produce minute grains.
