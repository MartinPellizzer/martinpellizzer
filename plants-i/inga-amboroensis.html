Inga amboroensis

Inga amboroensis, commonly known as the Amboro inga, is a deciduous tree native to the lowland tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Inga amboroensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
