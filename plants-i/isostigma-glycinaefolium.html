Isostigma glycinaefolium

Isostigma glycinaefolium, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its fleshy, red berries.

Isostigma glycinaefolium is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
