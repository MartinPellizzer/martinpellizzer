Iris klattii

Iris klattii, commonly known as the Klatt's iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

Iris klattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
