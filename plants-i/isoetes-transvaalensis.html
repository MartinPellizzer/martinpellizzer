Isoetes transvaalensis

Isoetes transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like foliage and spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located at the tips of its leaf-like structures. This plant is native to freshwater environments in southern Africa, particularly in South Africa and Namibia. It thrives in still or slow-moving water and is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of lakes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is an important indicator species for water quality and habitat health.
