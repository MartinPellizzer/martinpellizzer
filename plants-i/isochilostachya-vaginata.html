Isochilostachya vaginata

Isochilostachya vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in cooler climates.
