Ipomoea sumatrana

Ipomoea sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, palmate leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical forests.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like fruit that splits open when mature.
