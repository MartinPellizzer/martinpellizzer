Imhofia crispa

Imhofia crispa, commonly known as the curly rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, curling inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique, spiraled flower spikes that add visual interest to wetland gardens.
