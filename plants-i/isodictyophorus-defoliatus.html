Isodictyophorus defoliatus

Isodictyophorus defoliatus, commonly known as the leafless phyllody plant, is a rare and unusual species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is distinguished by its lack of typical leaves, instead producing modified structures that serve photosynthetic functions. The plant exhibits a highly specialized morphology, with its stems bearing chlorophyll-rich nodes that replace traditional foliage. Its reproductive system involves small, inconspicuous flowers that produce capsule fruits containing minute seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized adaptations, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
