Indigofera campestris var. latifolia

Indigofera campestris var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in dyeing fabrics.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
