Isachne pangerangensis var. halconensis

Isachne pangerangensis var. halconensis, commonly known as the Halconense grass, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid and warm climates. It has small, clustered inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
