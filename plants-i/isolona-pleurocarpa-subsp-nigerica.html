Isolona pleurocarpa subsp. nigerica

Isolona pleurocarpa subsp.

nigerica, commonly known as the Nigerian rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to yellow or orange when ripe.

This species is often found growing in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry or semi-arid environments.
