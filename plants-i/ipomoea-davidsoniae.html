Ipomoea davidsoniae

Ipomoea davidsoniae, commonly known as the Davidson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ipomoea davidsoniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
