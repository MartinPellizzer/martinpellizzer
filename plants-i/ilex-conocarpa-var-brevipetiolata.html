Ilex conocarpa var. brevipetiolata

Ilex conocarpa var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a glossy dark green surface. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

The varietal name 'brevipetiolata' refers to its shorter petioles, distinguishing it from other varieties of Ilex conocarpa.
