Ipomoea gerrardiana

Ipomoea gerrardiana, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for gardens and containers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea gerrardiana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, depending on the support it receives.
