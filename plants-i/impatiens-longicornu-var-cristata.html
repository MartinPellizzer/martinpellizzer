Impatiens longicornu var. cristata

Impatiens longicornu var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate impatiens, is a unique variant of the impatiens genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, branched inflorescence, which gives it its common name "cristate," derived from the Latin word for "crown.". The plant features vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with contrasting yellow throats. Its foliage is typically glossy and dark green, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
