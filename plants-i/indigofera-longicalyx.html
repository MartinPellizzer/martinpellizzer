Indigofera longicalyx

Indigofera longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaved indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a significant species in its native habitats.
