Iris × pallide-caerulaea

Iris × pallide-caerulaea, commonly known as the Blue Flag Iris, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of both Iris pallida and Iris caerulea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters.

The botanical interest of this species lies in its hybrid nature and its adaptability to moist environments.
