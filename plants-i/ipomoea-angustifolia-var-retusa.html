Ipomoea angustifolia var. retusa

Ipomoea angustifolia var. retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and range in color from white to pale blue or violet. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
