Iberis nazarita

Iberis nazarita, commonly known as the Turkish carnation, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iberis nazarita is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
