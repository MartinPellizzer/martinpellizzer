Imperatia filiformis

Imperatia filiformis, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, hairless stems that often lie flat on the ground, forming a dense mat. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
