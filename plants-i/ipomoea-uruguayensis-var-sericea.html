Ipomoea uruguayensis var. sericea

Ipomoea uruguayensis var. sericea, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
