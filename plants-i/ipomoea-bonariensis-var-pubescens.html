Ipomoea bonariensis var. pubescens

Ipomoea bonariensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces striking trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and can be grown as a decorative vine in gardens or containers.
