Iresine macbridei

Iresine macbridei, commonly known as the redleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Iresine macbridei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf color and relatively easy care requirements.
