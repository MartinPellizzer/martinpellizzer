Indigofera trimorphophylla

Indigofera trimorphophylla, commonly known as the three-leafed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

However, it is also considered a weed in some areas due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.
