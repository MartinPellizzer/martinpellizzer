Illicium parvifolium

Illicium parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into star-shaped, fragrant fruits resembling those of star anise. Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.

The fruit contains essential oils that contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
