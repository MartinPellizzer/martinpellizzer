Ilex macrocarpa var. longipedunculata

Ilex macrocarpa var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled holly, is a variant of the broad-leaf holly native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, elongated peduncles that support its small, red berries. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy dark green surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable to local wildlife.
