Ipomoea desmophylla

Ipomoea desmophylla, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its rapid growth and short blooming period make it a characteristic species of desert environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habits.
