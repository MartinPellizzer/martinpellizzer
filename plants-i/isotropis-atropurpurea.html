Isotropis atropurpurea

Isotropis atropurpurea, commonly known as purpleleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, dark purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture with a reddish or purplish hue on the underside. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.
