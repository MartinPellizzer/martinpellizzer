Iris longipedicellata

Iris longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-beaked iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three petals and three sepals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Iris longipedicellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
