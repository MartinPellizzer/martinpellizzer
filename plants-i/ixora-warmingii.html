Ixora warmingii

Ixora warmingii, commonly known as the yellow ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ixora warmingii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and relatively low maintenance requirements.
