Ipomoea squamosa var. petiolaris

Ipomoea squamosa var. petiolaris, commonly known as the scarlet moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, opening at night and closing during the day. The leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often covered in fine, scaly hairs. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental due to its striking appearance.

It is also known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
