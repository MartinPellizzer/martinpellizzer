Ilex odorata var. tephrophylla

Ilex odorata var. tephrophylla, commonly known as the fragrant holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that emit a subtle, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
