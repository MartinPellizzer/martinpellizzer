Iris setosa var. platyrhyncha

Iris setosa var. platyrhyncha, commonly known as the broad-beak iris, is a variant of the slender iris native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive, broad, flat spur. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
