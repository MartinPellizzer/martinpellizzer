Ibidium

Ibidium, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with narrow, gray-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
