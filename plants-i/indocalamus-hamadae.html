Indocalamus hamadae

Indocalamus hamadae, commonly known as the Hamada bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to China, particularly found in the provinces of Zhejiang and Jiangxi. The plant is characterized by its slender, green culms that grow in dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This bamboo is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
