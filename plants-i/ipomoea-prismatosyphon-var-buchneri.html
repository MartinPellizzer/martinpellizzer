Ipomoea prismatosyphon var. buchneri

Ipomoea prismatosyphon var. buchneri, commonly known as the Buchner's morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often spiraled leaves, which are typically a deep green with a slight waxy coating. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, usually pale to deep blue, and bloom in the morning, hence the common name. This variant is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, with tendrils that help it attach to supports.
