Inula koelzii

Inula koelzii, commonly known as the Himalayan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inula koelzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
