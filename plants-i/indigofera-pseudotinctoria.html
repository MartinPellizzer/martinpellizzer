Indigofera pseudotinctoria

Indigofera pseudotinctoria, commonly known as false indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it often grows in open woodlands or grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
