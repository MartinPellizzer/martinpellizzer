Ixia framesii

Ixia framesii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a South African flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces. Ixia framesii has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers.
