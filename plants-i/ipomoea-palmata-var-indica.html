Ipomoea palmata var. indica

Ipomoea palmata var. indica, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a striking vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to violet in color. The plant produces distinctive, spirally coiled seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various supports.
