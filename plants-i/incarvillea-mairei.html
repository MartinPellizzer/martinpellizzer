Incarvillea mairei

Incarvillea mairei, commonly known as the Chinese blue poppy, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
