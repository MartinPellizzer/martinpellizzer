Isoetes jamaicensis

Isoetes jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a central axis. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it anchors itself to the substrate. Its morphology includes a complex arrangement of leaves and a specialized reproductive structure called a strobilus.

Isoetes jamaicensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
