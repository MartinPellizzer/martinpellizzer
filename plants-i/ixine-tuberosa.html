Ixine tuberosa

Ixine tuberosa, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in meadows and mountainous areas where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.

Ixine tuberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
