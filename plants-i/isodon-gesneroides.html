Isodon gesneroides

Isodon gesneroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
