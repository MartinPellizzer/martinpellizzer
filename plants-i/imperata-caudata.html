Imperata caudata

Imperata caudata, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive purple hue, especially in the flowering stage. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky, cylindrical cluster of small, reddish-brown flowers.

Imperata caudata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
