Inga colombiana

Inga colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
