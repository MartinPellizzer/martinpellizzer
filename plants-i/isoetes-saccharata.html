Isoetes saccharata

Isoetes saccharata, commonly known as the sugar spike moss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky morphology with a central axis and leaf-like structures called stichids. This plant is typically found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can thrive in still or slow-moving water. Isoetes saccharata is a spore-producing plant, reproducing through the release of spores rather than seeds.

Its ecological role includes contributing to aquatic biodiversity and serving as a habitat for various microorganisms.
