Iris attica

Iris attica, commonly known as the Greek iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear strikingly beautiful blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The leaves are sword-shaped and lanceolate, often with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
