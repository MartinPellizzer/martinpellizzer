Ipomoea anceps

Ipomoea anceps, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea anceps is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
