Impatiens aurella f. coccinea

Impatiens aurella f. coccinea, commonly known as the crimson jewelweed, is a striking variant of the jewelweed genus native to tropical regions.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are slender and can be somewhat succulent. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in forest understories.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its eye-catching appearance.
