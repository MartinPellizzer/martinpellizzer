Ipomoea sinaica

Ipomoea sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet blooms that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

Ipomoea sinaica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
