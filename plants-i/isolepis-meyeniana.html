Isolepis meyeniana

Isolepis meyeniana, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in dense tufts, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often submerged or floating. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Isolepis meyeniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water garden settings.
