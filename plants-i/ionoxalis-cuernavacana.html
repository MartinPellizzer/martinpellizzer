Ionoxalis cuernavacana

Ionoxalis cuernavacana, commonly known as the Cuernavaca sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces edible leaves and flowers, which have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Ionoxalis cuernavacana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
