Indigofera heudelotii

Indigofera heudelotii, commonly known as African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods containing seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is valued for its traditional uses in dyeing textiles and as a source of medicinal compounds.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
