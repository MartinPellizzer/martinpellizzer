Indigofera celebica

Indigofera celebica, commonly known as Java indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in horticulture and agriculture.
