Inga tessmannii var. harmsii

Inga tessmannii var. harmsii, commonly known as the Harms's inga, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
