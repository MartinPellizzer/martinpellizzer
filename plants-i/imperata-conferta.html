Imperata conferta

Imperata conferta, commonly known as cogongrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, coarse stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces dense, woolly spikelets that are often pink to reddish in color. It thrives in wet, disturbed soils and is known for its aggressive growth, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Due to its rapid spread and ecological impact, it is considered a noxious weed in several countries.
