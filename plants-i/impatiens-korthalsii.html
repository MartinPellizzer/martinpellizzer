Impatiens korthalsii

Impatiens korthalsii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually oval or elliptical, with a waxy texture, and it produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Impatiens korthalsii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
