Ipomoea corrugata

Ipomoea corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, corrugated texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along stream banks and in moist, shaded areas.

Ipomoea corrugata is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms throughout the growing season.
