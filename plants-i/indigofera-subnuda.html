Indigofera subnuda

Indigofera subnuda, commonly known as the naked indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open when mature, releasing small, brown seeds.

Indigofera subnuda is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
