Ichnanthus tenuifolius

Ichnanthus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five petals. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.
