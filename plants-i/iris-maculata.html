Iris maculata

Iris maculata, commonly known as the spotted iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive speckled patterns on the petals. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a valued addition to both cultivated gardens and natural habitats.
