Indigofera semitrijuga var. tetrasperma

Indigofera semitrijuga var. tetrasperma, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. It produces pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional dyeing processes.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
