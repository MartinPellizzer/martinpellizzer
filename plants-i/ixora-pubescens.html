Ixora pubescens

Ixora pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Rubia and Gardenia. The plant is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its common name.

Ixora pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant due to its hardy nature and showy blooms.
