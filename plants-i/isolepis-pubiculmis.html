Isolepis pubiculmis

Isolepis pubiculmis, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Isolepis pubiculmis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environment.
