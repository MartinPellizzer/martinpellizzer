Ipomoea varia

Ipomoea varia, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea varia is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common morning glory.
