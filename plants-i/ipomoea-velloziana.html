Ipomoea velloziana

Ipomoea velloziana, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence its common name.

Ipomoea velloziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
