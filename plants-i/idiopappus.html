Idiopappus

Idiopappus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a genus of flowering plants native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plants are typically shrubs or subshrubs with small, narrow leaves and produce fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
