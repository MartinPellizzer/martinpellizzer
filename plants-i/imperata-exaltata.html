Imperata exaltata

Imperata exaltata, commonly known as the tall blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and wetlands.

Imperata exaltata plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
