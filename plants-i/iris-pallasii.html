Iris pallasii

Iris pallasii, commonly known as Pallas' iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It features showy, hexagonal blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its rhizomes are thick and fleshy. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Iris pallasii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
