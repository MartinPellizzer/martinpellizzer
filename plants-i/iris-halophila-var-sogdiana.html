Iris halophila var. sogdiana

Iris halophila var. sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its halophytic adaptation to high salt concentrations. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals. It is often found in riverbanks and saline soils, demonstrating resilience to harsh conditions.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
