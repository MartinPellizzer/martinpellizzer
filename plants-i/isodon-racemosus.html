Isodon racemosus

Isodon racemosus, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and often have a pale yellow or white color.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
