Indigofera engleri

Indigofera engleri, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking violet to blue flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, with a woody stem and compound leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
