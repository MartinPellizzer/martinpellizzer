Ixora cordata

Ixora cordata, commonly known as the flame of the woods, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which are composed of small, clustered flowers. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
