Isachne kunthiana var. srilankensis

Isachne kunthiana var. srilankensis, commonly known as the Srilankan isachne, is a grass species native to southern Asia, particularly Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
