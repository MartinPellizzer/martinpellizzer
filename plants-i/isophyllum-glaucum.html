Isophyllum glaucum

Isophyllum glaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Isophyllum glaucum is adapted to arid conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
