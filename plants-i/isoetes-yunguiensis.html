Isoetes yunguiensis

Isoetes yunguiensis, commonly known as the Yunguiensis watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a structure known as a strobilus. The plant is native to freshwater environments in China, particularly in the Yungui Plateau region. It exhibits a unique morphology with a rhizome that supports its submerged fronds.

Isoetes yunguiensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for microorganisms.
