Impatiens pseudocitrina

Impatiens pseudocitrina, commonly known as the citron impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream sides. Its flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Impatiens pseudocitrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
