Iphigenia

Iphigenia, commonly known as the "flower of the sea," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often violet or blue, flowers. They are native to regions across Europe, Asia, and Africa, often growing in rocky or coastal areas. Iphigenia species are typically herbaceous, with square stems and opposite leaves.

They are valued for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
