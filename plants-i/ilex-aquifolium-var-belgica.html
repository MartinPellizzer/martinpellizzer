Ilex aquifolium var. belgica

Ilex aquifolium var. belgica, commonly known as the Belgian holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of Europe, particularly Belgium and parts of France. The leaves are typically dark green with spiny margins, and they turn red in the autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and hedgerows.
