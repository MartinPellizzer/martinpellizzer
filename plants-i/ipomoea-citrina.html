Ipomoea citrina

Ipomoea citrina, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea citrina is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
