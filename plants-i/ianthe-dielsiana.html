Ianthe dielsiana

Ianthe dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are often covered in a dense, white mycorrhizal sheath, which aids in nutrient absorption.

Ianthe dielsiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
