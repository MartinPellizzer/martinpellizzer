Iresine jaliscana

Iresine jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep red to purple, often with contrasting white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in clusters at the ends of the stems. Iresine jaliscana thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
