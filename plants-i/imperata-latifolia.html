Imperata latifolia

Imperata latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf grass or flatleaf blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically forms dense clumps and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that is often used in traditional crafts.

Imperata latifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in preventing soil erosion.
