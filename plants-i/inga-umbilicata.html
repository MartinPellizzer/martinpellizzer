Inga umbilicata

Inga umbilicata, commonly known as the "umbilical inga," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules.

Its fruits are pods that contain edible seeds, and the plant is often used in reforestation due to its rapid growth and ecological benefits.
