Inula hirta var. oblongifolia

Inula hirta var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with rough, hairy foliage. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
