Inga minutula

Inga minutula, commonly known as the small inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Inga minutula is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful plant for reforestation and soil improvement.
