Inga eriocarpoides

Inga eriocarpoides, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
