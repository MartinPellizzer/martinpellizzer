Ixia pilosa

Ixia pilosa, commonly known as the woolly ox tongue, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ixia pilosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
