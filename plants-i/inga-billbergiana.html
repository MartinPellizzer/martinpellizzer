Inga billbergiana

Inga billbergiana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
