Inga emarginata

Inga emarginata, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant has a fast growth rate and is often found in humid lowland forests. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance.

Inga emarginata is also valued for its timber and use in traditional medicine.
