Ilex weberlingii

Ilex weberlingii, commonly known as the Appalachian holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, providing ornamental value. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ilex weberlingii is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
