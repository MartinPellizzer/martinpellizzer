Impatiens gomphophylla subsp. cribbii

Impatiens gomphophylla subsp.

cribbii, commonly known as the Cribb's impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers.

The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive, elongated spur that extends from the base of the petals. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
