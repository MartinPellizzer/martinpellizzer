Iris × regalis

Iris × regalis, commonly known as the regal iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The flowers are usually a deep purple or blue with a yellow center, and they are borne on one side of the stem, a feature known as "flagging.". The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
