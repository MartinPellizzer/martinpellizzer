Indigofera leptophylla

Indigofera leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in areas with humid climates. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It produces pods containing small, dark seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera leptophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
