Ipomoea beladamboe

Ipomoea beladamboe, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Ipomoea beladamboe is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
