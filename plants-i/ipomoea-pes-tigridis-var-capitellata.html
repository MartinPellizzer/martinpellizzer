Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. capitellata

Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. capitellata, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
