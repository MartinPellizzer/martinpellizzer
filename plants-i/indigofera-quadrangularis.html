Indigofera quadrangularis

Indigofera quadrangularis, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
