Indigofera denudata

Indigofera denudata, commonly known as the naked indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, arranged in clusters, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Indigofera denudata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
