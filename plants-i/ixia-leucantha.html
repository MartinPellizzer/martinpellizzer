Ixia leucantha

Ixia leucantha, commonly known as the white ixia, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of large, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens during the cooler months. Its bulbs are adapted to survive dry periods, making it a hardy choice for cultivation in well-drained soils.

Ixia leucantha is often used in floral arrangements due to its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms.
