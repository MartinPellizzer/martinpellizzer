Isatis ciesielskii

Isatis ciesielskii, commonly known as the Polish woad, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Isatis ciesielskii is also known for its historical use in producing indigo dye, similar to its more famous relative, Isatis tinctoria.
