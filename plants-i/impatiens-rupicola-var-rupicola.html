Impatiens rupicola var. rupicola

Impatiens rupicola var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full to partial sunlight. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and often reddish in color.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
