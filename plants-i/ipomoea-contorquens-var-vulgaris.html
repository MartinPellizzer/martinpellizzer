Ipomoea contorquens var. vulgaris

Ipomoea contorquens var. vulgaris, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces striking, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
