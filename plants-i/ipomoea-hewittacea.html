Ipomoea hewittacea

Ipomoea hewittacea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
