Impatiens floribunda

Impatiens floribunda, commonly known as the clustered touch-me-not, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that come in shades of pink, white, and red, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Impatiens floribunda thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, shade-tolerant blooms.
