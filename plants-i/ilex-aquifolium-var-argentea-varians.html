Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-varians

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-varians, commonly known as the silver-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its striking white-variegated foliage.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and feature a distinctive white or silver margin that contrasts with the dark green central portion. This plant is evergreen, retaining its foliage year-round, and thrives in well-drained soil in partial to full shade. It produces small, red berries in the winter, which are an important food source for birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
