Ilex leucoclada f. angustifolia

Ilex leucoclada f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Its dense foliage and year-round color make it an excellent ornamental plant for hedges or foundation plantings.
