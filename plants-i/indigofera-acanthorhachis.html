Indigofera acanthorhachis

Indigofera acanthorhachis, commonly known as the thorned cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
