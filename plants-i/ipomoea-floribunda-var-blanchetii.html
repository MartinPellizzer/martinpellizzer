Ipomoea floribunda var. blanchetii

Ipomoea floribunda var. blanchetii, commonly known as the Blanche's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the stems are hairy and twining. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
