Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-pendula

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-pendula, commonly known as the silver weeping holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its distinctive silver-white variegation on its leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin and a glossy, waxy texture. This plant produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
