Inula schugnanica

Inula schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnanian Inula, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the family.

Inula schugnanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
