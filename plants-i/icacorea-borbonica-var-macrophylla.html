Icacorea borbonica var. macrophylla

Icacorea borbonica var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five lobes and a prominent throat. The leaves are large, ovate, and have a smooth texture, often with a waxy appearance. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant blooms.
