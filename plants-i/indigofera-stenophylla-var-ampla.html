Indigofera stenophylla var. ampla

Indigofera stenophylla var. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leafed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
