Ischaemum hokianum

Ischaemum hokianum, commonly known as the Hokian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
