Impatiens extensifolia

Impatiens extensifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens extensifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
