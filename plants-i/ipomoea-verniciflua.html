Ipomoea verniciflua

Ipomoea verniciflua, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and hairy, glandular stems that exude a sticky substance. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

The sticky resin secreted by the stems has been used traditionally in various applications, including as a natural adhesive.
