Isoloma vestitum

Isoloma vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Isoloma vestitum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
