Ipomoea kurzii

Ipomoea kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea kurzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
