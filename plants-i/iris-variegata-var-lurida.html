Iris variegata var. lurida

Iris variegata var. lurida, commonly known as the dusky iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive markings. The leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue. This variety is characterized by its variegated foliage, which includes white and green patterns.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
