Icacorea albipetala

Icacorea albipetala, commonly known as the white-petaled coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional Indigenous medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for native pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
