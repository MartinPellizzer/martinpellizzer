Ipomoea piresii

Ipomoea piresii, commonly known as the Pires' morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea piresii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
