Ipomoea aculeata var. mollissima

Ipomoea aculeata var. mollissima, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a herbaceous vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. It produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
