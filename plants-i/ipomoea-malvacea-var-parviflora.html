Ipomoea malvacea var. parviflora

Ipomoea malvacea var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twine around supports.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
