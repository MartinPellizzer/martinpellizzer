Isoetes variabilis proles boryana

Isoetes variabilis proles boryana, commonly known as the Boryan watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found in clusters along the stem. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its root-like structures. Its morphology includes a tall, erect stem with a rosette of leaves at the base, which helps in photosynthesis.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
