Ipheion sessile

Ipheion sessile, commonly known as the common white violet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces delicate white or pale blue flowers with a distinctive violet hue, which bloom in the spring. Ipheion sessile thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ground cover in woodland gardens.
