Ilex paraguariensis var. longifolia

Ilex paraguariensis var. longifolia, commonly known as the Amazonian holly or longleaf holly, is a variant of the yerba mate plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, dark berries that contain the seeds. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

This variety is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and its ecological role in the rainforest ecosystem.
