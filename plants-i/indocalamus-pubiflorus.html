Indocalamus pubiflorus

Indocalamus pubiflorus, commonly known as the hairy-flowered bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms and dense, feathery foliage. The plant is notable for its showy, panicle-like inflorescence that bears small, hairy flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
