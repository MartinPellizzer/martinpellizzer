Ipomoea lobata

Ipomoea lobata, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea lobata is also notable for its role in traditional Hawaiian culture and its use in herbal medicine.
