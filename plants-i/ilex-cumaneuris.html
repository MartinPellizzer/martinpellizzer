Ilex cumaneuris

Ilex cumaneuris, commonly known as the Cumana holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Ilex cumaneuris thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
