Iva ciliata var. latifolia

Iva ciliata var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive white, fuzzy edge.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
