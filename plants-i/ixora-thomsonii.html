Ixora thomsonii

Ixora thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's flame bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences of bright orange-red flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Ixora thomsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
