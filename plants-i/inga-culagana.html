Inga culagana

Inga culagana, commonly known as the purple-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue, and its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in racemate inflorescences. Inga culagana produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage in horticultural settings.
