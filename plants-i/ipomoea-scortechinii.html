Ipomoea scortechinii

Ipomoea scortechinii, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces attractive, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Ipomoea scortechinii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
