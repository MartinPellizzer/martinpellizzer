Iodes hookeriana

Iodes hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's iodine plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Iridaceae, which includes irises and related species. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
