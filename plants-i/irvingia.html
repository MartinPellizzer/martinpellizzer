Irvingia

Irvingia, commonly known as African mango, is a genus of trees native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and woody fruits. The fruit of Irvingia species, particularly Irvingia gabonensis, is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The seeds inside the fruit are edible and have a nutty flavor, while the pulp is sometimes consumed as a delicacy.

The plant is also valued for its potential health benefits, including its high content of dietary fiber and various bioactive compounds.
