Ipomoea dissecta

Ipomoea dissecta, commonly known as the fringed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive, finely toothed margin. It is a vine that can climb using its twining stems.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
