Ischaemum indicum subvar. malacophyllum

Ischaemum indicum subvar. malacophyllum, commonly known as the glossy millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important forage plant in certain agricultural systems.
