Inga amazonica var. bracteifera

Inga amazonica var. bracteifera, commonly known as the Amazonian inga, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The tree is valued for its rapid growth and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
