Iris attica var. variegata

Iris attica var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Greek Iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive white markings. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, providing a lush green backdrop to the blooms. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience in mild climates.
