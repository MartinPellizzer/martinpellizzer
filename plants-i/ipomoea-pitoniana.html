Ipomoea pitoniana

Ipomoea pitoniana, commonly known as the Piton Island morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the island of Piton in the Seychelles.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea pitoniana is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare species within its native habitat.
