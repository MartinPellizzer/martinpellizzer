Isolepis inundata var. gracillima

Isolepis inundata var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes during the growing season. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

This variety is distinguished by its more slender stems and leaves compared to other varieties of Isolepis inundata.
