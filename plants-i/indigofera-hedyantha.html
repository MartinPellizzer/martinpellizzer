Indigofera hedyantha

Indigofera hedyantha, commonly known as the white indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some areas for its attractive foliage and floral display.
