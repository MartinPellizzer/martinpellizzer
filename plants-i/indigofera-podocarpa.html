Indigofera podocarpa

Indigofera podocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are encased in pods, which are typically dark brown and contain several elliptical seeds.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various regions.
