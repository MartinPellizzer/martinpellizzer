Isodon myriocladus

Isodon myriocladus, commonly known as the mountain verbena, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in dense spikes. Isodon myriocladus is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.
