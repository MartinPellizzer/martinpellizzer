Isolepis pygmaea var. californica

Isolepis pygmaea var. californica, commonly known as the California sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
