Isolepis grandispica

Isolepis grandispica, commonly known as the tall spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Isolepis grandispica is commonly found in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
