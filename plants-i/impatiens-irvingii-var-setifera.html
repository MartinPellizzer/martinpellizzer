Impatiens irvingii var. setifera

Impatiens irvingii var. setifera, commonly known as the slender impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are slender and often branched. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden settings.
