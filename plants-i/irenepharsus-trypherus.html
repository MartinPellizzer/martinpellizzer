Irenepharsus trypherus

Irenepharsus trypherus, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a central golden star-shaped structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Irenepharsus trypherus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
