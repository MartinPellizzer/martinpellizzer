Ischaemum macrurum

Ischaemum macrurum, commonly known as the tall brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence is a panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Ischaemum macrurum plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
