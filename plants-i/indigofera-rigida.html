Indigofera rigida

Indigofera rigida, commonly known as rigid indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its erect, often spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Indigofera rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
