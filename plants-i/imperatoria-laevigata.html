Imperatoria laevigata

Imperatoria laevigata, commonly known as the smooth bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its fleshy, smooth-stemmed structure and small, pink or purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Imperatoria laevigata thrives in sunny, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
