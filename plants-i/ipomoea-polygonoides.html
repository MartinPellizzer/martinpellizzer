Ipomoea polygonoides

Ipomoea polygonoides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a vigorous climber with stems that can spread along the ground or ascend supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
