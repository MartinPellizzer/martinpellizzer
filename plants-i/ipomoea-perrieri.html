Ipomoea perrieri

Ipomoea perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea perrieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
