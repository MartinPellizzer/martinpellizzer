Iris latistyla

Iris latistyla, commonly known as the broadleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like, strap-shaped leaves that are broad and sturdy, often reaching several inches in width. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, typical of the iris family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
