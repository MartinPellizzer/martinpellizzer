Indigofera eriocarpa var. williamsonii

Indigofera eriocarpa var. williamsonii, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional uses.

The hairy indigo is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
