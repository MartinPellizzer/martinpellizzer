Isochilus leucanthus

Isochilus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered isochilus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to its arid environment.

Isochilus leucanthus is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
