Iris variegata

Iris variegata, commonly known as the variegated iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems that support its lance-shaped, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Iris variegata is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
