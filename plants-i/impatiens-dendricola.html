Impatiens dendricola

Impatiens dendricola, commonly known as the tree impatiens, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green hue. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
