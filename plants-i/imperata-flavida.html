Imperata flavida

Imperata flavida, commonly known as the yellow bluestem, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clump-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. Imperata flavida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
