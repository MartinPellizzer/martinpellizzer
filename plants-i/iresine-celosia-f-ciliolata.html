Iresine celosia f. ciliolata

Iresine celosia f. ciliolata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically velvety and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant display in gardens and as a decorative foliage plant.
