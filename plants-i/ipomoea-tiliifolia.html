Ipomoea tiliifolia

Ipomoea tiliifolia, commonly known as the silky leaf morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
