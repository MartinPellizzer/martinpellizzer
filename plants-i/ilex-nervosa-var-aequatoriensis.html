Ilex nervosa var. aequatoriensis

Ilex nervosa var. aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at moderate to high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
