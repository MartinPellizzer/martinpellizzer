Ilex aquifolium var. ingramii

Ilex aquifolium var. ingramii, commonly known as the Ingram's holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its dense, spiny leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy sheen.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in wetland habitats. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their attractive appearance and evergreen characteristics.

The species is adapted to moist, acidic soils and can tolerate partial shade to full sun.
