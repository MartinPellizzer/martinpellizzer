Ipomoea vespertina

Ipomoea vespertina, commonly known as evening trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea vespertina is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its preference for full sun.
