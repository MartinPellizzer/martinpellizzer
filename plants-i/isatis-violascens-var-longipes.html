Isatis violascens var. longipes

Isatis violascens var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed woad, is a biennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
