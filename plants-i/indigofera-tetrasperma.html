Indigofera tetrasperma

Indigofera tetrasperma, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

It has been historically used for its dye-producing properties, particularly for producing a deep blue pigment.
