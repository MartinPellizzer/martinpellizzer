Isoetes orcuttii

Isoetes orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spiky, sword-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. This plant is native to regions of North America, particularly in the western United States, where it thrives in shallow, still water bodies. Isoetes orcuttii reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important indicator species for assessing water quality and habitat health.
