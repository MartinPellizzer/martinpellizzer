Inula wardii

Inula wardii, commonly known as the African elecampane, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Inula wardii is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
