Indigofera spinosa var. grandifolia

Indigofera spinosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered indigofera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, violet or blue flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and woodland edges.
