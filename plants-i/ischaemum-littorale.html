Ischaemum littorale

Ischaemum littorale, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in salt marshes and along shorelines. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Ischaemum littorale plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
