Isatis tschetschena

Isatis tschetschena, commonly known as the Tschetschenian woad, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in racemes. It produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

The plant is known for its historical use in producing indigo dye, similar to other members of its genus.
