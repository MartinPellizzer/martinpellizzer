Impatiens monticola

Impatiens monticola, commonly known as the mountain impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens monticola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
