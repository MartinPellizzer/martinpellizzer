Inula × semiamplexicaulis

Inula × semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the hybrid false sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that are semi-amplexicaul, meaning they partially clasping the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow with a central disc, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.
