Ipomoea bonariensis subvar. triloba

Ipomoea bonariensis subvar. triloba, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves that are velvety and dark green. The flowers are large, trumpet-shaped, and typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as an annual in temperate regions.
