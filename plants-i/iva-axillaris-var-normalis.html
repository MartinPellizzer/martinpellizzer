Iva axillaris var. normalis

Iva axillaris var. normalis, commonly known as the common beggarweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
