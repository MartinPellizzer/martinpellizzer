Indigofera brevicalyx

Indigofera brevicalyx, commonly known as small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
