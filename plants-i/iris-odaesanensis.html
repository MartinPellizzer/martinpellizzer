Iris odaesanensis

Iris odaesanensis, commonly known as the Korean iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
