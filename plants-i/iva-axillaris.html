Iva axillaris

Iva axillaris, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Iva axillaris produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
