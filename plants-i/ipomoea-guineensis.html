Ipomoea guineensis

Ipomoea guineensis, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Ipomoea guineensis are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
