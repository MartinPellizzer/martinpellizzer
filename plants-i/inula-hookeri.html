Inula hookeri

Inula hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features tall, sturdy stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Inula hookeri is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
