Inga loubryana

Inga loubryana, commonly known as the Loubry's inga, is a species of leguminous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and thrives in areas with high rainfall. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes, and it produces edible pods that contain several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of timber and forage.
