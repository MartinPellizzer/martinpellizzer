Indigofera santapaui

Indigofera santapaui, commonly known as the Andean indigo, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool and temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional uses in local communities.
