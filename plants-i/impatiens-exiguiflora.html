Impatiens exiguiflora

Impatiens exiguiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flower impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, ovate leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens exiguiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
