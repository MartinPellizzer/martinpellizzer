Indigofera clitorioides

Indigofera clitorioides, commonly known as the "false indigo" or "clitoria," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
