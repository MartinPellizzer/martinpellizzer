Isachne saxicola var. denticulata

Isachne saxicola var. denticulata, commonly known as the serrated isachne, is a variant of the isachne genus native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves with serrated edges, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant typically grows in crevices or on slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or pale blue, appearing in clusters.

It is a hardy species adapted to harsh climates, often found in mountainous regions of southern Africa.
