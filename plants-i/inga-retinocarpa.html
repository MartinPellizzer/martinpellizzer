Inga retinocarpa

Inga retinocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by various animals. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

The plant's adaptability to different soil types contributes to its widespread distribution in tropical regions.
