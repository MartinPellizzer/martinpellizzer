Ipomopsis pinnata

Ipomopsis pinnata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Ipomopsis pinnata is part of the Gentianaceae family and is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
