Isoetes engelmannii

Isoetes engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like structure with spore-bearing leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense, submerged or floating colonies. Its reproductive structures, called sporophylls, are specialized leaves that produce spores rather than seeds.

Isoetes engelmannii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
