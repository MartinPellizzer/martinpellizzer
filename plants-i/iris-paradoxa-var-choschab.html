Iris paradoxa var. choschab

Iris paradoxa var. choschab, commonly known as the Choschab iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white and purple pattern on the lower petals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like structure at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
