Indigofera strigosa

Indigofera strigosa, commonly known as hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Indigofera strigosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in agricultural settings.
