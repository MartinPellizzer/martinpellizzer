Ixora speciosa

Ixora speciosa, commonly known as the flame flower or red torch, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Ixora speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
