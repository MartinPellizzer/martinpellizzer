Isodon lophanthoides var. micranthus

Isodon lophanthoides var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered lemongrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often aromatic and have a citrus-like scent. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale blue, blooming in summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
