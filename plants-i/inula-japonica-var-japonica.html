Inula japonica var. japonica

Inula japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese elecampane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a tall, branched stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
