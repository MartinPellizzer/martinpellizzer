Ipomoea forsteri var. pauciflora

Ipomoea forsteri var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, pale blue to white flowers that open in the early morning. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are short-lived, typically lasting only a day, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is related to other members of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines.
