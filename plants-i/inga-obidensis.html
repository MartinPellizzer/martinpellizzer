Inga obidensis

Inga obidensis, commonly known as the Obidos inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
