Iris × violipurpurea

Iris × violipurpurea, commonly known as the purple flag iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris viola and Iris purpurea.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 4 feet, featuring sword-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental iris is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
