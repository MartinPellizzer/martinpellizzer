Ipomoea cholulensis

Ipomoea cholulensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
