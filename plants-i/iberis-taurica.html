Iberis taurica

Iberis taurica, commonly known as Caucasian Iberis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
