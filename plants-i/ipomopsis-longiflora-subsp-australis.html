Ipomopsis longiflora subsp. australis

Ipomopsis longiflora subsp.

australis, commonly known as the long-flowered ipomopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
