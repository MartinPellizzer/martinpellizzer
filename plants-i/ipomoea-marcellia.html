Ipomoea marcellia

Ipomoea marcellia, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall.

Ipomoea marcellia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
