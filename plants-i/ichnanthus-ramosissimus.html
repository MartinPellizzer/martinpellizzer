Ichnanthus ramosissimus

Ichnanthus ramosissimus, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid and saline soils, making it a hardy species in coastal and dry environments.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.
