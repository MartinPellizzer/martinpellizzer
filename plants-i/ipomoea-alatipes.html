Ipomoea alatipes

Ipomoea alatipes, commonly known as the winged morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, spiral-shaped capsule that opens to release the seeds.
