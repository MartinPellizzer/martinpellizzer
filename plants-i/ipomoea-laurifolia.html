Ipomoea laurifolia

Ipomoea laurifolia, commonly known as the evergreen morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that remain green throughout the year, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea laurifolia is also sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
