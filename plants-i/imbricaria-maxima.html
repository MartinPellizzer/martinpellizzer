Imbricaria maxima

Imbricaria maxima, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's leaves are typically a deep green, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom above the water and attract pollinators like beetles.

Imbricaria maxima plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
