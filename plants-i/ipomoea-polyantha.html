Ipomoea polyantha

Ipomoea polyantha, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant is a vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea polyantha is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
