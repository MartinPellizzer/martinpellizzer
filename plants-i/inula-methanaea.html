Inula methanaea

Inula methanaea, commonly known as the Methanaea Inula, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are yellow and resemble dandelions, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Inula methanaea is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
