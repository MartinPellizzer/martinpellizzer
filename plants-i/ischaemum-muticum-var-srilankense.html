Ischaemum muticum var. srilankense

Ischaemum muticum var. srilankense, commonly known as the Srilankan barnyard grass, is a variant of the barnyard grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and wetland areas.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and an important species in ecological studies.
