Impatiens burtonii var. burtonii

Impatiens burtonii var. burtonii, commonly known as the Burtt's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that come in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
