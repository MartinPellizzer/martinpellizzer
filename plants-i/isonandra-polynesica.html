Isonandra polynesica

Isonandra polynesica, commonly known as the Tahitian daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Isonandra polynesica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in Polynesian traditions.
