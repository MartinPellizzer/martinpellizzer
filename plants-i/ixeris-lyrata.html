Ixeris lyrata

Ixeris lyrata, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and bear yellow flowers with prominent yellow centers. Its flowers typically bloom in the spring and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Ixeris lyrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
