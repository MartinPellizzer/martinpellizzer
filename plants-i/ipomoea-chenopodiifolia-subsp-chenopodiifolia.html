Ipomoea chenopodiifolia subsp. chenopodiifolia

Ipomoea chenopodiifolia subsp.

chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the Chenopodium-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the Chenopodium genus, giving it its common name.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
