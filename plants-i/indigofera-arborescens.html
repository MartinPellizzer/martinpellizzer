Indigofera arborescens

Indigofera arborescens, commonly known as the tree indigo, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing seeds, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It has been used traditionally for its dye-producing properties and ornamental value.
