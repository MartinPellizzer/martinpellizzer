Indigofera kirilowii var. alba

Indigofera kirilowii var. alba, commonly known as white indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes. Native to East Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
