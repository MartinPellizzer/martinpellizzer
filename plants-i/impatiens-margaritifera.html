Impatiens margaritifera

Impatiens margaritifera, commonly known as the pearlflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Impatiens margaritifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
