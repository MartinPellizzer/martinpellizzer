Impatiens congolensis

Impatiens congolensis, commonly known as the Congo impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along forest floors or in undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
