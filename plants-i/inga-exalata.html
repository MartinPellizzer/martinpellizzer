Inga exalata

Inga exalata, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its fast growth and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The tree's bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
