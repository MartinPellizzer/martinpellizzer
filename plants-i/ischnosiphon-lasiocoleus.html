Ischnosiphon lasiocoleus

Ischnosiphon lasiocoleus, commonly known as the woolly root orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy roots and slender, upright stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose raceme, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
