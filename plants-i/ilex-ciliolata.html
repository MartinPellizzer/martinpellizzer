Ilex ciliolata

Ilex ciliolata, commonly known as the silky holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex ciliolata typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
