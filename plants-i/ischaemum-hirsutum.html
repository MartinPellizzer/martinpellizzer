Ischaemum hirsutum

Ischaemum hirsutum, commonly known as hairy foxtail, is a grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and dense, hairy inflorescences that resemble a foxtail. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Ischaemum hirsutum is an annual plant that produces small, spikelet-based flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
