Isodon stenodontus

Isodon stenodontus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed isodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
