Ischaemum myurus

Ischaemum myurus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bearded grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ischaemum myurus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
