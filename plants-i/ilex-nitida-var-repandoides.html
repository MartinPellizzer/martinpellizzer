Ilex nitida var. repandoides

Ilex nitida var. repandoides, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a compact evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its dense foliage and year-round appeal make it suitable for hedges or as a specimen plant.
