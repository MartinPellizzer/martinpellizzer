Intsia bijuga var. retusa

Intsia bijuga var. retusa, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or red lauan, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its straight grain and fine texture, making it valuable for timber. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, while the fruit is a legume pod containing flat, elongated seeds.

This species is also notable for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
