Isochilus latibracteatus

Isochilus latibracteatus, commonly known as the broad-bract isochilus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a prominently colored lip that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
