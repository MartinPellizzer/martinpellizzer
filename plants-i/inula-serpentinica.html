Inula serpentinica

Inula serpentinica, commonly known as serpent's root, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowering heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Inula serpentinica is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
