Ipomoea ramosissima

Ipomoea ramosissima, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, hairy stems, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea ramosissima is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
