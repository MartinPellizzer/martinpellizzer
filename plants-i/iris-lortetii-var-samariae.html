Iris lortetii var. samariae

Iris lortetii var. samariae, commonly known as the Samarian iris, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
