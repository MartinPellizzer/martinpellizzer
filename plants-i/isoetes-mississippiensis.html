Isoetes mississippiensis

Isoetes mississippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on specialized leaves called spathes. This plant is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Isoetes mississippiensis exhibits a primitive morphology, with long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface.

Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal by water, contributing to its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
