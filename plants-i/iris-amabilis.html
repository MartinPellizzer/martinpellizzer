Iris amabilis

Iris amabilis, commonly known as the sweet iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features sword-like, grassy leaves and produces violet or blue-purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. Iris amabilis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
