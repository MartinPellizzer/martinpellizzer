Ida pegueroi

Ida pegueroi, commonly known as the Guajillo pepper, is a species of Capsicum native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its elongated, reddish-orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruits are widely used in Mexican cuisine for their spicy flavor and are also valued for their medicinal properties.
