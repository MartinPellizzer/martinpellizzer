Indigofera disperma

Indigofera disperma, commonly known as the spreading indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, spreading stems and small, trifoliate leaves. It produces clusters of pink or violet flowers, which are followed by elongated, twisted pods.

Indigofera disperma is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
