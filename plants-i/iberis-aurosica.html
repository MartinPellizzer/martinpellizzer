Iberis aurosica

Iberis aurosica, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Iberis aurosica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
