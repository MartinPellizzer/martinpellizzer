Ipomoea quinquepartita

Ipomoea quinquepartita, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to scramble over supports or fences.
