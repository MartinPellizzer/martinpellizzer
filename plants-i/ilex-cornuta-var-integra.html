Ilex cornuta var. integra

Ilex cornuta var. integra, commonly known as the smoothleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and unlobed, distinguishing it from other holly varieties. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense growth habit and year-round foliage make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
