Ischaemum timorense var. zeylanicum

Ischaemum timorense var. zeylanicum, commonly known as Timor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous narrow leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
