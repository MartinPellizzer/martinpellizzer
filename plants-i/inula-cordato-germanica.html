Inula cordato-germanica

Inula cordato-germanica, commonly known as German-inula or heart-leaved inula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single, showy head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
