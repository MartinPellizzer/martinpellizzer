Inga adenophylla

Inga adenophylla, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in racemose inflorescences. The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for its wood and leaves.
