Isatis cappadocica subsp. kurdica

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish woad, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.

The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Kurdish woad is notable for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more widely known relative, true woad.
