Ipomoea auxocalyx

Ipomoea auxocalyx, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
