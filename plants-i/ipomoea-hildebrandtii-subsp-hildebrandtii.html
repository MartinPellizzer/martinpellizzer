Ipomoea hildebrandtii subsp. hildebrandtii

Ipomoea hildebrandtii subsp.

hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's morning glory, is a species native to South America. It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
