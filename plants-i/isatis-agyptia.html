Isatis agyptia

Isatis agyptia, commonly known as Egyptian woad, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Historically, Egyptian woad was used to produce indigo dye, making it significant in both botanical and cultural contexts.
