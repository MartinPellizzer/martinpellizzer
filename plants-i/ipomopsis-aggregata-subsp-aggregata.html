Ipomopsis aggregata subsp. aggregata

Ipomopsis aggregata subsp.

aggregata, commonly known as the desert bell, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
