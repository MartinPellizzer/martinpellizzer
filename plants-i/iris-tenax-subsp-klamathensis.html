Iris tenax subsp. klamathensis

Iris tenax subsp.

klamathensis, commonly known as the Klamath iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, three-petaled flowers that are often clustered in a spike.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
