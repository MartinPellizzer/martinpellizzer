Indigofera howellii

Indigofera howellii, commonly known as Howell's indigo, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
