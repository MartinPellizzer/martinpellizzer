Ipomoea angularis

Ipomoea angularis, commonly known as the angular morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender, angular stems that are often hairy. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are trumpet-shaped, usually in shades of blue, violet, or white. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
