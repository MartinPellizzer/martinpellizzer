Indigofera juncea

Indigofera juncea, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying shades of blue or purple.

Indigofera juncea is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
