Icacorea digitata

Icacorea digitata, commonly known as the white-flowered coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets and its striking clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Icacorea digitata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
