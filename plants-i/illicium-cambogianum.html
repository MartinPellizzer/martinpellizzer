Illicium cambogianum

Illicium cambogianum, commonly known as wild star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit contains several oil-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
