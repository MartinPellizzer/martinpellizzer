Iris zagrica

Iris zagrica, commonly known as the Zagreb iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers are arranged in a spike at the top of the stem.

Iris zagrica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
