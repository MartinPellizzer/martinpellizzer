Ipomoea trematosperma

Ipomoea trematosperma, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, round seeds.

Ipomoea trematosperma is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.
