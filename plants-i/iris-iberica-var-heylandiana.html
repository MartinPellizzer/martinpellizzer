Iris iberica var. heylandiana

Iris iberica var. heylandiana, commonly known as the Heyland's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
