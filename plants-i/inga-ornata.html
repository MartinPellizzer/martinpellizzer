Inga ornata

Inga ornata, commonly known as the ornate inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Inga ornata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
