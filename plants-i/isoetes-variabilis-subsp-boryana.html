Isoetes variabilis subsp. boryana

Isoetes variabilis subsp.

boryana, commonly known as the variable watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales. It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This subspecies thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself in muddy or sandy substrates. The plant plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important subject in botanical and ecological studies.
