Ipomoea orientalis

Ipomoea orientalis, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea orientalis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly in suitable environments.
