Iris mzchetica

Iris mzchetica, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinct white or yellow center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Iris mzchetica is valued in gardens for its striking blooms and adaptability to temperate climates.
