Isoetes variabilis

Isoetes variabilis, commonly known as the variable water clover, is a species of water fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in a row along the leaf-like structures. This plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. Its rhizomes anchor it to the substrate, while its floating leaves allow it to access sunlight for photosynthesis.

Isoetes variabilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
