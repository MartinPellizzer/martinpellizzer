Ipomoea setulosa

Ipomoea setulosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces a milky sap and has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in gardens or along roadsides.

Ipomoea setulosa is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it a striking addition to ornamental landscapes.
