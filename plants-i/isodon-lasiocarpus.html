Isodon lasiocarpus

Isodon lasiocarpus, commonly known as the roughfruit germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and are arranged in whorled clusters.

Isodon lasiocarpus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
