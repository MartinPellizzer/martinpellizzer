Ionidium chamaedrifolium

Ionidium chamaedrifolium, commonly known as the creeping arnica, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Ionidium chamaedrifolium thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
