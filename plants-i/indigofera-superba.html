Indigofera superba

Indigofera superba, commonly known as the golden crown or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, velvety blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

Indigofera superba is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.
