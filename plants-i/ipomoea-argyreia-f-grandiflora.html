Ipomoea argyreia f. grandiflora

Ipomoea argyreia f. grandiflora, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often appearing in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
