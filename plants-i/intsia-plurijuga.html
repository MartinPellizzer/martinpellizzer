Intsia plurijuga

Intsia plurijuga, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a large tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its broad, bipinnate leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces a valuable seed known as shea butter, which is used in cosmetics and food products. It thrives in dry tropical climates and is often found in savanna ecosystems.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic properties.
