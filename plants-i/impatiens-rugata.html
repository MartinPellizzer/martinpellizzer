Impatiens rugata

Impatiens rugata, commonly known as the wrinkled impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, wrinkled leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Impatiens rugata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
