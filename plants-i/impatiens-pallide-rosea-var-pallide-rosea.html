Impatiens pallide-rosea var. pallide-rosea

Impatiens pallide-rosea var. pallide-rosea, commonly known as the pale-rose impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
