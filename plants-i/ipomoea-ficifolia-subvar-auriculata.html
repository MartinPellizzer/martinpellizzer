Ipomoea ficifolia subvar. auriculata

Ipomoea ficifolia subvar. auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped morning glory, is a tropical vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant produces vibrant, often purple or blue flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is known for its rapid growth habit. The flowers have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base, giving the subspecies its name.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
