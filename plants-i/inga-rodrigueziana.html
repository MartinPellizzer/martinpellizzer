Inga rodrigueziana

Inga rodrigueziana, commonly known as the "sweet acacia," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it a common species in secondary forests. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in agroforestry systems.
