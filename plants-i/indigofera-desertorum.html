Indigofera desertorum

Indigofera desertorum, commonly known as the desert indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.
