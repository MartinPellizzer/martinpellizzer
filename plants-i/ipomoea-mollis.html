Ipomoea mollis

Ipomoea mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Ipomoea mollis is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
