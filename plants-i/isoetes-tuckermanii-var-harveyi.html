Isoetes tuckermanii var. harveyi

Isoetes tuckermanii var. harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant's spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in compact, stalked clusters called strobila. It typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical form of I. tuckermanii.
