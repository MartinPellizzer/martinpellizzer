Illipe fulva

Illipe fulva, commonly known as the yellow butternut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits containing numerous seeds. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and for making natural rubber.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
