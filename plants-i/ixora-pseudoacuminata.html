Ixora pseudoacuminata

Ixora pseudoacuminata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed ixora, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora pseudoacuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
