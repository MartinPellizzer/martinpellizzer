Isodon talbotianus

Isodon talbotianus, commonly known as Talbot's lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes produce small, pale blue or purple flowers, which are often clustered at the top of the plant.

Isodon talbotianus is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
