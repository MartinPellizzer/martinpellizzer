Isolepis tasmanica

Isolepis tasmanica, commonly known as Tasmanian flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Tasmania and southern Australia.

It typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often hidden among the foliage. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Isolepis tasmanica is adapted to waterlogged conditions and is often found in freshwater marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers.
