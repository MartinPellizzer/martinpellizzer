Ida grandis var. alba

Ida grandis var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered ida, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
