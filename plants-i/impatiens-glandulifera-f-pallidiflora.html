Impatiens glandulifera f. pallidiflora

Impatiens glandulifera f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and ovate, glossy leaves. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
