Irvingia duparquetii

Irvingia duparquetii, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a deciduous tree native to the savannas of West Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fruit that resembles a coconut in appearance. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fibrous, edible pulp.

Irvingia duparquetii is valued for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties, though it remains less studied compared to other Irvingia species.
