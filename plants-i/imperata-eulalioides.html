Imperata eulalioides

Imperata eulalioides, commonly known as the purple grass or purple bluemink, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple or pink inflorescences that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
