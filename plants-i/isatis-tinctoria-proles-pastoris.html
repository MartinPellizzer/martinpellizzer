Isatis tinctoria proles pastoris

Isatis tinctoria proles pastoris, commonly known as the pastor's kale or a type of woad, is a cultivar of the flowering plant Isatis tinctoria.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight purplish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which have been historically used for dyeing fabrics blue.

This variety is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
