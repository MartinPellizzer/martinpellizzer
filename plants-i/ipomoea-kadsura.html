Ipomoea kadsura

Ipomoea kadsura, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its vines can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length.

Ipomoea kadsura is also known for its ability to climb and twine around other plants or structures.
