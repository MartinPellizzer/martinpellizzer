Inula oculus

Inula oculus, commonly known as the eyed gromwell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flower heads that resemble eyes, giving it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite inflorescence. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Inula oculus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
