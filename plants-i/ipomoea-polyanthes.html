Ipomoea polyanthes

Ipomoea polyanthes, commonly known as the tropical morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which open in the morning and close at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea polyanthes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
