Impatiens balsamina var. racemifera

Impatiens balsamina var. racemifera, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
