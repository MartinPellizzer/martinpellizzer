Indigofera argentea

Indigofera argentea, commonly known as silver indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a climbing or shrub growth habit. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
