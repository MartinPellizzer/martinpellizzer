Inga suturalis

Inga suturalis, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "ice cream fruit," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces edible, sweet, and creamy white pulp encasing a single large seed. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive yellowish-green color.

Inga suturalis is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
