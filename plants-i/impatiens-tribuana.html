Impatiens tribuana

Impatiens tribuana, commonly known as the pink impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that release tiny seeds when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
