Indigofera fairchildii

Indigofera fairchildii, commonly known as the Hawaiian indigo or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
