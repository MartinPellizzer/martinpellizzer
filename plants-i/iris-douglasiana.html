Iris douglasiana

Iris douglasiana, commonly known as the Douglas iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The Douglas iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
