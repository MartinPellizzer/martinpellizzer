Ichthyothere mollis

Ichthyothere mollis, commonly known as the soft-bodied aquatic herb, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies.

Its stems are flexible and often form dense mats, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizomes for nutrient absorption and propagation. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with high light exposure and moderate nutrient levels.

Due to its soft, fleshy structure, it is often mistaken for a type of algae or moss.
