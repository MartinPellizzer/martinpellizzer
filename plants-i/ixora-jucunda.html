Ixora jucunda

Ixora jucunda, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly glossy surface.

Ixora jucunda is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to drought.
