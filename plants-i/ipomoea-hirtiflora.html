Ipomoea hirtiflora

Ipomoea hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green color. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Ipomoea hirtiflora is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar climbing habits.
