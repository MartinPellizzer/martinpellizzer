Ipomoea multiflora

Ipomoea multiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces cylindrical, greenish-yellow berries that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
