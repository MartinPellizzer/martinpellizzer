Indigofera lasiantha

Indigofera lasiantha, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, purple-pink flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
