Iranecio bulghardaghensis

Iranecio bulghardaghensis, commonly known as the Bulghardagh endemic, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and produces delicate, yellowish flowers during the spring season. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat. Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
