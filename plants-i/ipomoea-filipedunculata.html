Ipomoea filipedunculata

Ipomoea filipedunculata, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically opening in the morning. Its flowers are followed by cylindrical, greenish-brown fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ipomoea filipedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
