Ipomoea megapotamica subsp. velutina

Ipomoea megapotamica subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
