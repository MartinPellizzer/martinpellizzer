Inula suaveolens

Inula suaveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and aromatic, fuzzy leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its name comes from the fragrant, long-lasting flowers that retain their color and scent even after drying.
