Indigofera timoriensis

Indigofera timoriensis, commonly known as Timor indigo, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
