Ipomoea sorsogonensis

Ipomoea sorsogonensis, commonly known as the Sorsogon Province morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
