Isoglossa eranthemoides

Isoglossa eranthemoides, commonly known as the flame violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a host plant for certain butterfly species and its ornamental value in native gardens.
