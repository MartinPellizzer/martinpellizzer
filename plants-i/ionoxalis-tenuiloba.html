Ionoxalis tenuiloba

Ionoxalis tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
