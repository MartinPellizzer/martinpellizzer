Iris nepalensis var. khasiana

Iris nepalensis var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi iris, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions of India and Nepal. Its leaves are sword-shaped and lanceolate, while its rhizomes are fibrous and perennial.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
