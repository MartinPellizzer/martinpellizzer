Ischaemum tumidum

Ischaemum tumidum, commonly known as broadleaf foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence is a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each containing two to three bracts.

Ischaemum tumidum is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
