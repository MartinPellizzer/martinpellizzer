Indigofera mahafalensis

Indigofera mahafalensis, commonly known as the Mahafal indigo plant, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
