Impatiens eriantha

Impatiens eriantha, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and vibrant addition to their landscape.
