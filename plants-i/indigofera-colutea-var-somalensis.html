Indigofera colutea var. somalensis

Indigofera colutea var. somalensis, commonly known as Somali indigo, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
