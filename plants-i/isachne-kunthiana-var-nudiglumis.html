Isachne kunthiana var. nudiglumis

Isachne kunthiana var. nudiglumis, commonly known as the naked-lemma isachne, is a grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 60 centimeters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often glabrous, giving it its common name.
