Impatiens shevaroyensis

Impatiens shevaroyensis, commonly known as the Shevaroy hills impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
