Iresine guaranitica

Iresine guaranitica, commonly known as the red-leaf plant or Brazilian erythranthe, is a strikingly colorful plant native to South America.

It features vibrant, velvety leaves that range from deep red to purple, often with contrasting green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a standout in both indoor and outdoor settings.
