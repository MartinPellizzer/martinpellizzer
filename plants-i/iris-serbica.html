Iris serbica

Iris serbica, commonly known as the Serbian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features tall, upright stems that bear striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris serbica is valued in horticulture for its elegant blooms and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
