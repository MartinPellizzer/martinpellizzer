Inula × hispida

Inula × hispida, commonly known as the rough fleabane, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, rocky soils, and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Inula × hispida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
