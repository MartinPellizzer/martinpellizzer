Impatiens vinosa

Impatiens vinosa, commonly known as purple touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens vinosa is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
