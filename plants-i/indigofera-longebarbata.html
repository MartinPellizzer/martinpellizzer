Indigofera longebarbata

Indigofera longebarbata, commonly known as the long-barbed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its seed pods are elongated and have a distinctive barbed structure, which aids in seed dispersal.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicinal practices.
