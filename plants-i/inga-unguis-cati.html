Inga unguis-cati

Inga unguis-cati, commonly known as the "Crimson Beauty" or "Red Inga," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, crimson-red flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, and its root system is often nitrogen-fixing, contributing to soil fertility.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
