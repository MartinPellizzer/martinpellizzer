Inga nefasia

Inga nefasia, commonly known as the "butterfly tree," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate, and its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by various animals, contributing to its role in local ecosystems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Inga nefasia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits.
