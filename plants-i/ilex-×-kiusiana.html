Ilex × kiusiana

Ilex × kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a hybrid evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and year-round appearance.

Ilex × kiusiana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes in temperate climates.
