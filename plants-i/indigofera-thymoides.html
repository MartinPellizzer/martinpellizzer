Indigofera thymoides

Indigofera thymoides, commonly known as sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
