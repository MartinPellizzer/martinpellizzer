Indigofera antennulifera

Indigofera antennulifera, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has been historically used for its dye-producing properties, contributing to traditional textile industries.
