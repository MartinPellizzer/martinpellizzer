Ilex macfadyenii var. caribaea

Ilex macfadyenii var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribaea holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal and lowland areas. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
