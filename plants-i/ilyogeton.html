Ilyogeton

Ilyogeton, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch.

Ilyogeton is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
