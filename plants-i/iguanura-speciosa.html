Iguanura speciosa

Iguanura speciosa, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically large and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

Iguanura speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
