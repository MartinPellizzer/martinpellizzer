Indigofera cerighellii

Indigofera cerighellii, commonly known as the Sierra Leone indigo plant, is a flowering plant species native to West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are dispersed by wind or animals, contributing to its ecological role in the region.
