Ipomoea solanifolia

Ipomoea solanifolia, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its vigorous climbing habit. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that contain seeds.
