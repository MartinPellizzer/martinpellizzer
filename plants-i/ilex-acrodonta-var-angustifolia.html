Ilex acrodonta var. angustifolia

Ilex acrodonta var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is evergreen, retaining its foliage year-round, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
