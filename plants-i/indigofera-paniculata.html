Indigofera paniculata

Indigofera paniculata, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. It produces pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera paniculata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroecosystems.
