Iris alabamensis

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a fan shape.

Iris alabamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
