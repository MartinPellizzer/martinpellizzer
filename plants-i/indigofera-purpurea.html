Indigofera purpurea

Indigofera purpurea, commonly known as purple indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
