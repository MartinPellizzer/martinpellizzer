Ipomoea microsticta

Ipomoea microsticta, commonly known as the small-spotted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along fences, walls, or in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain environments.
