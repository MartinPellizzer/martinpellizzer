Ipomoea evolvuloides var. grandiflora

Ipomoea evolvuloides var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
