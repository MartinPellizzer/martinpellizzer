Ipomoea egregia

Ipomoea egregia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, velvety green leaves. Its flowers have five petals and are pollinated primarily by nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
