Impatiens biglandulosa

Impatiens biglandulosa, commonly known as the large-fruited impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Impatiens biglandulosa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden settings.
