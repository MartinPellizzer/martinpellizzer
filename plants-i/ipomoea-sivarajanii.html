Ipomoea sivarajanii

Ipomoea sivarajanii, commonly known as the Sivarajan's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to India.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often twining around supports. It blooms during the day, typically in the morning, hence its common name.

Ipomoea sivarajanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
