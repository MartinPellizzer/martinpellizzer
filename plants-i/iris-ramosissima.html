Iris ramosissima

Iris ramosissima, commonly known as the western blue flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers with a distinct yellow throat. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.

Iris ramosissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant communities.
