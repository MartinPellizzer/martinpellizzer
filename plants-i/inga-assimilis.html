Inga assimilis

Inga assimilis, commonly known as the "chocolate tree," is a fast-growing tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant has a shallow root system that helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control. Its fruit pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its wide distribution.

The tree is also valued for its timber and as a source of food for wildlife.
