Ixora parviflora var. ovata

Ixora parviflora var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright orange to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
