Ipomoea falcata

Ipomoea falcata, commonly known as the sickle vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known vines like morning glory.

Ipomoea falcata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
