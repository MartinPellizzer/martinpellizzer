Iridodictyum histrioides

Iridodictyum histrioides, commonly known as the yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Iridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
