Ixora borbonica

Ixora borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon flame or flame of the woods, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its ornamental value is high due to its vibrant floral display and compact growth habit.

Ixora borbonica is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant due to its hardiness and adaptability.
