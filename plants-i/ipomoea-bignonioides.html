Ipomoea bignonioides

Ipomoea bignonioides, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. It is a perennial in its native range but is often grown as an annual in cooler climates.

The vine is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
