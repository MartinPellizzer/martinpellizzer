Ipomoea papiru var. subtriloba

Ipomoea papiru var. subtriloba, commonly known as the giant leaf morning glory, is a striking climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its enormous, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in diameter, giving it a dramatic visual presence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and large foliage, it is often used in ornamental gardening and as a natural screen in tropical landscapes.
