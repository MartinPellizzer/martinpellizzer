Indigofera longiracemosa

Indigofera longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
