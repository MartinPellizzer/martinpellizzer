Ichnocarpus baillonii

Ichnocarpus baillonii, commonly known as the African burr or African horse chestnut, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adapted to both moist and dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptability contribute to its presence in various ecosystems across southern Africa.
