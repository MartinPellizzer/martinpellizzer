Ipomoea racemosa

Ipomoea racemosa, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
