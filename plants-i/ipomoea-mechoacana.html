Ipomoea mechoacana

Ipomoea mechoacana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences or trellises. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea mechoacana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
