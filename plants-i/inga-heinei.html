Inga heinei

Inga heinei, commonly known as the Brazilian white bean tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The tree produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Inga heinei is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
