Ilex virgata

Ilex virgata, commonly known as the Virginia holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex virgata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
