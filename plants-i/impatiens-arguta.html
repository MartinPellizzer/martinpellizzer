Impatiens arguta

Impatiens arguta, commonly known as the roughleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in wetland areas.
