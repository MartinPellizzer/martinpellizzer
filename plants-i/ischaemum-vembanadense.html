Ischaemum vembanadense

Ischaemum vembanadense, commonly known as Vembanad grass, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of southern India.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along the banks of lakes and rivers.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
