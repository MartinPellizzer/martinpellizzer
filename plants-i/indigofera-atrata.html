Indigofera atrata

Indigofera atrata, commonly known as the black indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
