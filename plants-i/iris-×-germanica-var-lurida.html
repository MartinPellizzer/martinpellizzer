Iris × germanica var. lurida

Iris × germanica var. lurida, commonly known as the purple flag iris, is a hybrid iris species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow beard on the lower petal. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring sword-shaped, dark green leaves that are often spotted or striped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
