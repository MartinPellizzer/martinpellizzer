Ilex bullata

Ilex bullata, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Ilex bullata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
