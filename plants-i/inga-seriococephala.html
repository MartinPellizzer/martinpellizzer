Inga seriococephala

Inga seriococephala, commonly known as the white-veined inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent white veins and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Inga seriococephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing properties in agroforestry systems.
