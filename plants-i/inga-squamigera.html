Inga squamigera

Inga squamigera, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. Its rapid growth rate makes it a valuable species for agroforestry and reforestation projects.

The plant also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, enhancing soil fertility in its natural habitat.
