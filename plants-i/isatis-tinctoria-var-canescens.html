Isatis tinctoria var. canescens

Isatis tinctoria var. canescens, commonly known as the hoary woad, is a variant of the woad plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its blue flowers bloom in the summer, and it is known for its historical use in producing indigo dye.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
