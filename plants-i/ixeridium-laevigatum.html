Ixeridium laevigatum

Ixeridium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb water from the soil.

Ixeridium laevigatum is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
