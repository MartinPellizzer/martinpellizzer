Iocenes acanthifolius subsp. virens

Iocenes acanthifolius subsp.

virens, commonly known as the green-leaved acanthifolius, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of southern Africa. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly hairy texture on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in dry conditions.

It is often found in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
