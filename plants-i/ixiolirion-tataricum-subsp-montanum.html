Ixiolirion tataricum subsp. montanum

Ixiolirion tataricum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of mountain ecosystems.
