Inga pauciflora

Inga pauciflora, commonly known as the little-flowered inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Inga pauciflora plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
