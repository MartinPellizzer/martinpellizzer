Indigofera signata var. typica

Indigofera signata var. typica, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its historical use in producing indigo dye, which was highly valued in traditional textile industries.

Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and vine-like forms depending on environmental conditions.
