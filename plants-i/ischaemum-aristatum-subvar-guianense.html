Ischaemum aristatum subvar. guianense

Ischaemum aristatum subvar. guianense, commonly known as the Guianan wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This subvariety is distinguished by its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its wetland environment.
