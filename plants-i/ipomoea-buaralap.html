Ipomoea buaralap

Ipomoea buaralap, commonly known as the Bora Bora morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown as an annual.

The vines can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
