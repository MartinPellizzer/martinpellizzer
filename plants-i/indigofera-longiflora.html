Indigofera longiflora

Indigofera longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are known for containing alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera longiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
