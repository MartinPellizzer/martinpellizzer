Iris narbutii

Iris narbutii, commonly known as the Armenian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette, while its flowers are arranged in a raceme.

Iris narbutii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological niche.
