Ixeridium pygmaeum

Ixeridium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf flax or miniature flax, is a small, herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or on rocky slopes.

Ixeridium pygmaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
