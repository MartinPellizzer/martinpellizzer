Indigofera rhytidocarpa

Indigofera rhytidocarpa, commonly known as the rough-skin indigo, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
