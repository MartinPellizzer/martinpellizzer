Isolepis monostachya

Isolepis monostachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating that helps it thrive in moist environments.

Isolepis monostachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
