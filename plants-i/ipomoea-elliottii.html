Ipomoea elliottii

Ipomoea elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ipomoea elliottii is a climbing vine that can reach heights of several meters, making it a striking addition to gardens.
