Ipomoea pulcherrima

Ipomoea pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame vine or passion flower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive crown of filaments at the base. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a rapid growth habit, making it popular in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal flowering.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant for trellises.
