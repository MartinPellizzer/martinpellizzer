Ipomoea crassipes var. auriculata

Ipomoea crassipes var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaf base. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found near water bodies. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
