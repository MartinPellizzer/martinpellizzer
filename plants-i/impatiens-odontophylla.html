Impatiens odontophylla

Impatiens odontophylla, commonly known as the toothed-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive toothed or serrated leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Impatiens odontophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
