Ipomoea murucoides

Ipomoea murucoides, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces edible tubers that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea murucoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and utility.
