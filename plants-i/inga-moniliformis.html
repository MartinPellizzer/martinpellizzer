Inga moniliformis

Inga moniliformis, commonly known as the "jewel of the forest," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important component of forest ecosystems and useful for reforestation efforts.
