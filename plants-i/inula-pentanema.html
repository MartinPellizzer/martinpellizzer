Inula pentanema

Inula pentanema, commonly known as the slender elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branched stem covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Inula pentanema is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
