Ipomoea mozambicensis

Ipomoea mozambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea mozambicensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
