Ipomoea paniculata var. eriosperma

Ipomoea paniculata var. eriosperma, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a climbing vine that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, hairy seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along forest edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
