Isoglossa perplexa

Isoglossa perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing isogloss, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white, typically during the early spring. Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists.

Due to habitat loss, Isoglossa perplexa is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
