Isonandra microphylla

Isonandra microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved isonandra, is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments, often found in cloud forests and high-altitude grasslands. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Isonandra microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
