Ipomoea xiphosepala

Ipomoea xiphosepala, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is notable for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
