Inula rhizocephaliformis

Inula rhizocephaliformis, commonly known as the creeping elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features a robust root system and produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a strong scent.

Inula rhizocephaliformis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
