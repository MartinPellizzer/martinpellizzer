Ipomoea robertsii

Ipomoea robertsii, commonly known as the Roberts morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ipomoea robertsii is a perennial vine that is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
