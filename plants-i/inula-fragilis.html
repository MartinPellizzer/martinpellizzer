Inula fragilis

Inula fragilis, commonly known as the brittleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant aroma. Inula fragilis thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and often overlooked member of the Asteraceae family.
