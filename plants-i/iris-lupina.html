Iris lupina

Iris lupina, commonly known as the western blue flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its sword-shaped leaves are basal and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length.

Iris lupina plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
