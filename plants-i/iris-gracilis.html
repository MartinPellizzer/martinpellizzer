Iris gracilis

Iris gracilis, commonly known as the slender iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Iris gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
