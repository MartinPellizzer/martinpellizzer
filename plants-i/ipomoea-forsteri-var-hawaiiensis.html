Ipomoea forsteri var. hawaiiensis

Ipomoea forsteri var. hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas within forests. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Hawaiian culture.
