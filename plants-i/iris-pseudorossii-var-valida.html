Iris pseudorossii var. valida

Iris pseudorossii var. valida, commonly known as the Valid iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, six-petaled flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
