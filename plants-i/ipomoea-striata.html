Ipomoea striata

Ipomoea striata, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy edge, and its stems are hairy and often twining. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb supports or trellises as it grows.
