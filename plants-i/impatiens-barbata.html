Impatiens barbata

Impatiens barbata, commonly known as the fringed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Impatiens barbata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
