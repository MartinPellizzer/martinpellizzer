Inulopsis

Inulopsis, commonly known as the common inulopsis, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which typically consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often growing in meadows and grasslands. Inulopsis species are generally herbaceous, with hairy stems and serrated leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
