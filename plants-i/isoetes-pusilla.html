Isoetes pusilla

Isoetes pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf water clover, is a small, aquatic fern that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant is a member of the Isoetaceae family and is known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Isoetes pusilla often grows in dense clusters on the substrate, contributing to water filtration and habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
