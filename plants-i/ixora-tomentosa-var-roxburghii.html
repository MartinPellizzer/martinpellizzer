Ixora tomentosa var. roxburghii

Ixora tomentosa var. roxburghii, commonly known as the hairy ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of bright orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in summer and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds.
