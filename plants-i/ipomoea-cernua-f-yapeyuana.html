Ipomoea cernua f. yapeyuana

Ipomoea cernua f. yapeyuana, commonly known as the Yapeyu morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its growth habit is climbing, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
