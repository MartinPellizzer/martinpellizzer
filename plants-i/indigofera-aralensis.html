Indigofera aralensis

Indigofera aralensis, commonly known as the Aral indigo, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions.

Indigofera aralensis is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
