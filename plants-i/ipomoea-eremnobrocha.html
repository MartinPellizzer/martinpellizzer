Ipomoea eremnobrocha

Ipomoea eremnobrocha, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with deep taproots and reduced leaf surface area.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and herbs.
