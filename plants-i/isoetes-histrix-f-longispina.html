Isoetes histrix f. longispina

Isoetes histrix f. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined water lily, is a variant of the water lily genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky spores that are arranged in a dense, cylindrical structure called a sporangium. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are submerged, while the sporangium is usually positioned at the water's surface.

This fern-like plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
