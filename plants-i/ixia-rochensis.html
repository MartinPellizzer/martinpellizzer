Ixia rochensis

Ixia rochensis, commonly known as the Orange River ixia, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and gladiolus. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red base. Ixia rochensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
