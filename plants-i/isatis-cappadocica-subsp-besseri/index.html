Isatis Cappadocica Subsp. Besseri

Isatis cappadocica subsp. besseri is a lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family that thrives in rocky and arid environments. This subspecies is native to regions of Turkey and extends into parts of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and serrated leaves that help it withstand harsh conditions. Though not widely cultivated, it holds ecological significance as a source of nectar for pollinators. Its resilience and unique appearance make it an intriguing subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
