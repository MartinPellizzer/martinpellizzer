Ipomoea humilis

Ipomoea humilis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue, purple, or white, opening in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines climb using tendrils.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
