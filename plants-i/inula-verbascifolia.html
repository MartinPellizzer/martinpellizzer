Inula verbascifolia

Inula verbascifolia, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically lobed and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Inula verbascifolia is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
