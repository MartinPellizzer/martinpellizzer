Isachne saxicola

Isachne saxicola, commonly known as the rock isachne, is a small, perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized reproductive structures.
