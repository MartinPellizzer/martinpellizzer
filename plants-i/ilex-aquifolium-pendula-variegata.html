Ilex aquifolium pendula-variegata

Ilex aquifolium pendula-variegata, commonly known as the weeping variegated holly, is a cultivar of the common holly.

It features a cascading growth habit with gracefully drooping branches, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are variegated, displaying shades of white, yellow, and green, which add visual interest throughout the growing season. It is typically evergreen, retaining its foliage year-round, and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full shade.

This plant is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability to various garden settings.
