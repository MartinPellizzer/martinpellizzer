Illicium parvifolium subsp. parvifolium

Illicium parvifolium subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers and small, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
