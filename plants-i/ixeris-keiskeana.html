Ixeris keiskeana

Ixeris keiskeana, commonly known as the Japanese dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers resembling those of the common dandelion. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ixeris keiskeana is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
