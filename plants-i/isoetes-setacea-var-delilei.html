Isoetes setacea var. delilei

Isoetes setacea var. delilei, commonly known as the Delile's water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that form a mat on the water's surface, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located at the base of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in maintaining water quality.
