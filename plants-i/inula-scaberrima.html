Inula scaberrima

Inula scaberrima, commonly known as rough inula, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and stream banks.

Inula scaberrima is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
