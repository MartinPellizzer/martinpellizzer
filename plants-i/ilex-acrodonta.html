Ilex acrodonta

Ilex acrodonta, commonly known as the Texas holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and dry slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a warm climate.
