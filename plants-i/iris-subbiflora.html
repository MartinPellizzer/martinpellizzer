Iris subbiflora

Iris subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and pointed, typically ranging from 30 to 60 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
