Incarvillea compacta var. qinghaiensis

Incarvillea compacta var. qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai incarvillea, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai region of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
