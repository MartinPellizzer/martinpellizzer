Inga purpurascens

Inga purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded understories of tropical rainforests. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The plant has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
