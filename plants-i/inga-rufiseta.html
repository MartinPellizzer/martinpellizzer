Inga rufiseta

Inga rufiseta, commonly known as the red-leaf inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reddish in color, and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.

Inga rufiseta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
