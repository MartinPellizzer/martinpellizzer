Indigofera guaranitica

Indigofera guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani indigo plant, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a dye source.

The species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories and open fields.
