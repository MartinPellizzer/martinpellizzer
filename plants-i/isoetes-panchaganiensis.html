Isoetes panchaganiensis

Isoetes panchaganiensis, commonly known as the Panchagani watermoss, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a central axis. This plant is adapted to freshwater environments and thrives in slow-moving or still waters. Its morphology includes a rosette-like arrangement of leaves, which are often submerged or partially emergent.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
