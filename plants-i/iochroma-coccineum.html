Iochroma coccineum

Iochroma coccineum, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves. Its flowers are often arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches, creating a dramatic visual display.

Iochroma coccineum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
