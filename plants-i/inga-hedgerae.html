Inga hedgerae

Inga hedgerae, commonly known as the wild coffee tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. Inga hedgerae produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in secondary forests and along forest edges.
