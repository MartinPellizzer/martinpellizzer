Impatiens balsamina var. agrestis

Impatiens balsamina var. agrestis, commonly known as wild impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
