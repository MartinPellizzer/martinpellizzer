Indigofera alternans var. alternans

Indigofera alternans var. alternans, commonly known as the variable indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
