Ipomoea piurensis f. rosea

Ipomoea piurensis f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored morning glory, is a variant of the Ipomoea piurensis species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-red flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display in gardens and as a decorative element in landscaping.
