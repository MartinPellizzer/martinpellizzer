Ipomoea rhodesiana

Ipomoea rhodesiana, commonly known as the Rhodes morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces hairy, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea rhodesiana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow vines.
