Inula candida subsp. decalvans

Inula candida subsp.

decalvans, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-centered composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The subspecies decalvans is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs compared to other subspecies of Inula candida.
