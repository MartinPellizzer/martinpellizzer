Ixia ecklonii

Ixia ecklonii, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces upright, slender stems that bear clusters of showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. Ixia ecklonii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate to subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which can remain fresh for several days when cut.
