Iris susiana f. westii

Iris susiana f. westii, commonly known as the Western iris, is a botanical variant of the iris genus native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow beard. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden environments.
