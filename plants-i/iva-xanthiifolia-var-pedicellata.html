Iva xanthiifolia var. pedicellata

Iva xanthiifolia var. pedicellata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct violet hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
