Ischaemum fischeri

Ischaemum fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread aggressively. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

Ischaemum fischeri is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive growth habit.
