Ixora grandis

Ixora grandis, commonly known as the red torch bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its flowers are typically in cymes, which are flat-topped clusters, and are highly attractive to pollinators.

Ixora grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
