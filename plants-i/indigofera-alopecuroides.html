Indigofera alopecuroides

Indigofera alopecuroides, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy purple or blue flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
