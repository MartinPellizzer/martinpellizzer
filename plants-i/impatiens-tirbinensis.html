Impatiens tirbinensis

Impatiens tirbinensis, commonly known as the yellow water impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Impatiens tirbinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
