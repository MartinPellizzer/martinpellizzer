Ilex sclerophylloides

Ilex sclerophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark berries that are often consumed by birds. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Ilex sclerophylloides is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
