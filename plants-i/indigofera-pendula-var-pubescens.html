Indigofera pendula var. pubescens

Indigofera pendula var. pubescens, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its pendulous racemes of violet to blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
