Ixora caffra

Ixora caffra, commonly known as the flame-of-India, is a flowering shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially in warm climates. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ixora caffra is also valued for its ability to attract birds and bees, contributing to local biodiversity.
