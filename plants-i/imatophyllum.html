Imatophyllum

Imatophyllum, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a fine, white coating, which helps in absorbing moisture from the air.

Imatophyllum is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
