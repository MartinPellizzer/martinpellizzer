Ipomoea hartmannii

Ipomoea hartmannii, commonly known as Hartmann's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and can be invasive in some regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in local ecosystems.
