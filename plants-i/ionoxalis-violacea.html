Ionoxalis violacea

Ionoxalis violacea, commonly known as the violet wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a violet-tinged underside, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ionoxalis violacea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
