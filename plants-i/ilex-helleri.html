Ilex helleri

Ilex helleri, commonly known as Heller's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense, compact form.

Ilex helleri is also notable for its ability to tolerate partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
