Iris wittmaniana

Iris wittmaniana, commonly known as the Wittman's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, three-petaled flowers that bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
