Indigofera vohemarensis

Indigofera vohemarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural contexts.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
