Isidrogalvia penduliflora

Isidrogalvia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of small, clustered flowers hanging from long, slender stems. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical forests. Its foliage is broad and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic organisms.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive floral structure and adaptability to moist, nutrient-poor substrates.
