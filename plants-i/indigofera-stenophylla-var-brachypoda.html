Indigofera stenophylla var. brachypoda

Indigofera stenophylla var. brachypoda, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the rainy season. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
