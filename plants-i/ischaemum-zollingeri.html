Ischaemum zollingeri

Ischaemum zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Ischaemum zollingeri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
