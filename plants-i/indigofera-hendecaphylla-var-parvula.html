Indigofera hendecaphylla var. parvula

Indigofera hendecaphylla var. parvula, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
