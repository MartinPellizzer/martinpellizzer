Ipomoea lambii

Ipomoea lambii, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea lambii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
