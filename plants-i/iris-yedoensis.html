Iris yedoensis

Iris yedoensis, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped, dark green leaves. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
