Inga macarenensis

Inga macarenensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

Inga macarenensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
