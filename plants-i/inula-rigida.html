Inula rigida

Inula rigida, commonly known as stiff elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Inula rigida is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
