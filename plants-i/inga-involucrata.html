Inga involucrata

Inga involucrata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk with a spreading crown, and it often thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
