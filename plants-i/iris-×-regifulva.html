Iris × regifulva

Iris × regifulva, commonly known as the Regiflora iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers feature a striking combination of purple and white petals, with a prominent central beard-like structure. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
