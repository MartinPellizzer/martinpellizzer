Inga micradenia

Inga micradenia, commonly known as the small-fruited inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
