Ipomoea elongata

Ipomoea elongata, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its tendrils.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
