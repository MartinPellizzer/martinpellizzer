Iris × rubea

Iris × rubea, commonly known as the red iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking deep red to purple flower petals, which often have a contrasting white or pale blue stripe. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and features sword-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
