Isoetes alata

Isoetes alata, commonly known as the flying feather moss, is a species of water plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and often spirally arranged leaves that resemble feathers. This plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. Isoetes alata is a member of the order Isoetales and is known for its ability to survive in low-nutrient conditions.

Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are located at the base of the leaves and release spores into the water for propagation.
