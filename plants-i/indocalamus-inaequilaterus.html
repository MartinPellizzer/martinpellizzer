Indocalamus inaequilaterus

Indocalamus inaequilaterus, commonly known as the unequal-sided bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a distinctive sheath that tightly encloses the stem.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
