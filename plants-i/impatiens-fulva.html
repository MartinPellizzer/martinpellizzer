Impatiens fulva

Impatiens fulva, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens fulva is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, with a fibrous root system adapted to its wet habitat.
