Ixora rufa

Ixora rufa, commonly known as the red flame ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Ixora rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
