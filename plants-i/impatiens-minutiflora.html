Impatiens minutiflora

Impatiens minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, upright stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
