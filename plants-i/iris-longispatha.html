Iris longispatha

Iris longispatha, commonly known as the long-spurred iris, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically 4 to 6 inches in diameter. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its showy blooms appear in late spring to early summer, often in shaded, moist environments.

Iris longispatha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
