Iris punctata

Iris punctata, commonly known as the dotted iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers with distinctive white dots on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are grass-like and form a rosette at the base.

Iris punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
