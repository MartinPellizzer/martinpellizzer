Ischaemum aristatum subvar. urvilleanum

Ischaemum aristatum subvar. urvilleanum, commonly known as the tall brome grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, two-lobed spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for livestock.
