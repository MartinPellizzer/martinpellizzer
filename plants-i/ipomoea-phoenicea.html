Ipomoea phoenicea

Ipomoea phoenicea, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ipomoea phoenicea is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive if not properly managed.
