Ipomoea cernua f. acutifolia

Ipomoea cernua f. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, cascading growth habit.

It features heart-shaped leaves with sharply pointed tips, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the morning. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing vine.
