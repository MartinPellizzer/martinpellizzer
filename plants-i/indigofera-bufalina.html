Indigofera bufalina

Indigofera bufalina, commonly known as buffalo bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, round pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Indigofera bufalina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
