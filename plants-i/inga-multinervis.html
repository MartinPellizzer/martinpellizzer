Inga multinervis

Inga multinervis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, with a distinctive velvety texture on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
