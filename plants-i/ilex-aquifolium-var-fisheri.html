Ilex aquifolium var. fisheri

Ilex aquifolium var. fisheri, commonly known as the Fisher's holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It typically features glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges and red berries that persist through winter. The plant is evergreen, providing year-round structure in gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it adaptable to a range of environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
