Ipomoea altissima

Ipomoea altissima, commonly known as morning glory, is a vigorous annual vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. It produces showy blooms that open in the morning and close by midday.

Ipomoea altissima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes but can become invasive in certain regions.
