Ixora timorensis var. pauciflora

Ixora timorensis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Timor ixora, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular red or orange flowers, which are arranged in inflorescences at the ends of branches. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
