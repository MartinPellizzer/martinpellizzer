Ipomoea juncea

Ipomoea juncea, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea juncea is also known for its climbing habit and can reach significant heights if left unpruned.
