Ixeris oldhamii

Ixeris oldhamii, commonly known as yellow flowered dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in a composite head.

Ixeris oldhamii is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
