Ilex paraguariensis var. genuina

Ilex paraguariensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian holly or the sacred plant of the Amazon, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that contain the stimulant compound caffeine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

This variety is closely related to the yerba mate plant and shares similar botanical features.
