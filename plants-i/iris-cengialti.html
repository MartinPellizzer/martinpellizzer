Iris cengialti

Iris cengialti, commonly known as the Chinese iris, is a species native to China and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or marshy areas. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and blooms in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often three to four inches in diameter, with a distinctive three-parted structure.

Iris cengialti is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and elegance.
