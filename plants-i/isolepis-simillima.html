Isolepis simillima

Isolepis simillima, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters, and features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.

Isolepis simillima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
