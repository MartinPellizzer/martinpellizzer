Indigofera inquinans

Indigofera inquinans, commonly known as the false indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Indigofera inquinans is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
