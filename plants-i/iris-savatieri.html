Iris savatieri

Iris savatieri, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Iris savatieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
