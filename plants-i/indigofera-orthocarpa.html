Indigofera orthocarpa

Indigofera orthocarpa, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, often with a reddish hue. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like fruit, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Indigofera orthocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
