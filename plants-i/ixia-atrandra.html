Ixia atrandra

Ixia atrandra, commonly known as the black lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers of Ixia atrandra are typically deep purple to black in color, which gives the plant its dramatic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late winter to early spring.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
