Ipomoea aitonii

Ipomoea aitonii, commonly known as the Aiton's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
