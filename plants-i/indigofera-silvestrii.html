Indigofera silvestrii

Indigofera silvestrii, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera silvestrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
