Indigofera cufodontii

Indigofera cufodontii, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple to blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential in soil nitrogen fixation due to its association with rhizobia bacteria.
