Ilex serrata f. alba

Ilex serrata f. alba, commonly known as the white-leaf holly, is a unique variant of the holly genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale variegated leaves, which contrast sharply with its dark green stems and berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round interest in gardens.

The white-leaf holly is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural settings.
