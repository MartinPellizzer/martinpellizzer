Iphiona polygalifolia

Iphiona polygalifolia, commonly known as the bluebell of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

Iphiona polygalifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
