Ischaemum aristatum var. enodulosum

Ischaemum aristatum var. enodulosum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

The variety enodulosum is distinguished by its hairy nodes and leaf bases, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
