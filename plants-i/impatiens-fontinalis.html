Impatiens fontinalis

Impatiens fontinalis, commonly known as the water impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Impatiens fontinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
