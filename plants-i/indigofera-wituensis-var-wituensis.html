Indigofera wituensis var. wituensis

Indigofera wituensis var. wituensis, commonly known as the Witu indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and dye production.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
