Ipomoea crassipes var. ovata

Ipomoea crassipes var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaved water hyacinth, is a variant of the water hyacinth species native to South America.

It is an aquatic plant characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom above the water, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rapid growth rate makes it a common invasive species in many tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's ability to absorb nutrients from the water helps in water purification, though it can also disrupt local ecosystems if left unchecked.
