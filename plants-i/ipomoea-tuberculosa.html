Ipomoea tuberculosa

Ipomoea tuberculosa, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It grows as a climbing vine, using its tendrils to support itself on structures or other plants.

Ipomoea tuberculosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
