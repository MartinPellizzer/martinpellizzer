Impatiens microhypophylla

Impatiens microhypophylla, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a glossy surface and a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a prominent spur at the base.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
