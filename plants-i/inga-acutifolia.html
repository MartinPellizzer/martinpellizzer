Inga acutifolia

Inga acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a valuable species in reforestation efforts. Its pods contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Additionally, Inga acutifolia plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, enhancing fertility for surrounding plants.
