Indigofera macrocarpa

Indigofera macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
