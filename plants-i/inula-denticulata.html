Inula denticulata

Inula denticulata, commonly known as the toothed inula, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Inula denticulata is often found in grasslands and woodland edges, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
