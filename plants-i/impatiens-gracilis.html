Impatiens gracilis

Impatiens gracilis, commonly known as slender touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Impatiens gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
