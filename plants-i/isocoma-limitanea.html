Isocoma limitanea

Isocoma limitanea, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
