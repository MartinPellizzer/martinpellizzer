Ipomoea dactylophylla

Ipomoea dactylophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
