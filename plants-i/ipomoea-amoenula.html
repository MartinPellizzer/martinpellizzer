Ipomoea amoenula

Ipomoea amoenula, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that come in various shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant or for its edible tubers.

Ipomoea amoenula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
