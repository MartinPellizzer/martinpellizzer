Inula valiensis

Inula valiensis, commonly known as the Valais Inula, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and meadow habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Inula valiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
