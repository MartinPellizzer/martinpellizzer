Isoetes kirkii

Isoetes kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's watermilfoil, is a member of the water fern family (Isoetaceae).

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, often forming dense underwater mats.

Isoetes kirkii is notable for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
