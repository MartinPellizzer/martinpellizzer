Ilex condensata

Ilex condensata, commonly known as the denseleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex condensata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
