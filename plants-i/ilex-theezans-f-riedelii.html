Ilex theezans f. riedelii

Ilex theezans f. riedelii, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
