Ipomoea papiru

Ipomoea papiru, commonly known as the paperflower or morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found twining around supports or other plants. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Ipomoea papiru is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens worldwide.
