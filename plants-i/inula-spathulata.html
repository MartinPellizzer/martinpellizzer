Inula spathulata

Inula spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Inula spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
