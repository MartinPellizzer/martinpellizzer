Iris tingitana var. fontanesii

Iris tingitana var. fontanesii, commonly known as the Moroccan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinct black beard on the lower petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand hot, dry conditions. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
