Iria indica

Iria indica, commonly known as the Indian bean or wild bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by elongated pods containing several seeds.

Iria indica is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
