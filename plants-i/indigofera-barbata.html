Indigofera barbata

Indigofera barbata, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.
