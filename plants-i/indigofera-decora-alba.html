Indigofera decora alba

Indigofera decora alba, commonly known as the white ornamental indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is characterized by its delicate white to pale blue flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
