Inula eriophora

Inula eriophora, commonly known as the roughleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into thistle-like seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Inula eriophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
