Indigofera campestris f. transiens

Indigofera campestris f. transiens, commonly known as the creeping indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in racemes, and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and ground-covering ability.
