Indigofera humilis

Indigofera humilis, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
