Iria disticha

Iria disticha, commonly known as the slender iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Iria disticha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
