Indigofera incarnata f. alba

Indigofera incarnata f. alba, commonly known as white wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in ecological systems.
