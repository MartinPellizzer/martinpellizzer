Ixia odorata var. hesperanthoides

Ixia odorata var. hesperanthoides, commonly known as the Orange Bells, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
