Inga cynometrifolia

Inga cynometrifolia, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
