Inula ramosissima

Inula ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers appear in late summer, forming dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Inula ramosissima is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.
