Isodon macrophyllus

Isodon macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf honeysuckle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
