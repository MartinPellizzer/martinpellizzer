Iberis garrexiana

Iberis garrexiana, commonly known as the Canary Islands candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Iberis garrexiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
