Isoetes riparia var. typica

Isoetes riparia var. typica, commonly known as the river shield fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water, forming dense underwater carpets. The plant is native to freshwater environments in North America, particularly in rivers and streams. Its reproductive structures, known as spore-bearing cones, are located at the base of the plant.

Isoetes riparia var. typica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
