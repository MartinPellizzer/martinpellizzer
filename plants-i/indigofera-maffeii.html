Indigofera maffeii

Indigofera maffeii, commonly known as Maffe's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers. It produces cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

Indigofera maffeii is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
