Ilex aquifolium var. furcata

Ilex aquifolium var. furcata, commonly known as the holly with forked leaves, is a variant of the common holly.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that often split into two or more points, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is evergreen, with glossy dark green foliage that provides year-round visual interest. It produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
