Isoetes caparaoensis

Isoetes caparaoensis, commonly known as the Caparaó watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which are commonly referred to as watermaces or quillworts.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered at the tips of its leaf-like structures. This plant is native to South America, specifically found in the state of Espírito Santo in Brazil. Isoetes caparaoensis thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, often in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important subject in the study of aquatic botany.
