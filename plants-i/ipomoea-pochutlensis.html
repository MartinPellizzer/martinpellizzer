Ipomoea pochutlensis

Ipomoea pochutlensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is climbing and has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are dispersed by wind.

Ipomoea pochutlensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
