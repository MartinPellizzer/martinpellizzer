Ipomoea adenioides

Ipomoea adenioides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea adenioides is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, making it a common ornamental plant in some regions.
