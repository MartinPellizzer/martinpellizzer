Inga neblinensis

Inga neblinensis, commonly known as the misty inga, is a flowering plant native to the humid montane forests of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Inga neblinensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
