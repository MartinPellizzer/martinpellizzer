Iris nepalensis var. letha

Iris nepalensis var. letha, commonly known as the Letha iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas, often near streams or in meadows. Its leaves are sword-shaped and lanceolate, with a smooth, dark green upper surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
