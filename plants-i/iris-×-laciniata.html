Iris × laciniata

Iris × laciniata, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris laevigata and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including blue, purple, and white, and are known for their intricate patterns.

Iris × laciniata is a hardy perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
