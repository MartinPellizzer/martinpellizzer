Ipomoea mauritiana

Ipomoea mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a vigorous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
