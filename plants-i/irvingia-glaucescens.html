Irvingia glaucescens

Irvingia glaucescens, commonly known as the African mango, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Irvingia glaucescens is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dietary fiber.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
