Irenepharsus magicus

Irenepharsus magicus, commonly known as the Starlight Fern, is a rare and ethereal plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

Its most distinctive feature is its bioluminescent fronds, which emit a soft, blue glow at night, aiding in its nocturnal pollination by moon-sensitive moths. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires a specific mineral-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are spiral-shaped and can unfurl in response to touch, a trait that has led to its mythical reputation.

Despite its beauty, Irenepharsus magicus is highly endangered due to habitat destruction and over-collection.
