Impatiens chinensis var. intermedia

Impatiens chinensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
