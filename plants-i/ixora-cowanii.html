Ixora cowanii

Ixora cowanii, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and is often cultivated for its showy, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a prominent central disk, and they bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Ixora cowanii is also valued for its use in horticulture and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
