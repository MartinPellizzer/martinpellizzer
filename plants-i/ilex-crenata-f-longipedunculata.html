Ilex crenata f. longipedunculata

Ilex crenata f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the Japanese holly with long peduncles, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, glossy dark green foliage with a distinctive wavy margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its elongated peduncles, or flower stems, are a distinguishing characteristic of this form.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
