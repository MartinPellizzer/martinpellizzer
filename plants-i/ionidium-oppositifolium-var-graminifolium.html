Ionidium oppositifolium var. graminifolium

Ionidium oppositifolium var. graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leafed opposite-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinct foliage.

It features opposite, grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a herbaceous appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help retain moisture in humid conditions.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet soils and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
