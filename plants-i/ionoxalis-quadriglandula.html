Ionoxalis quadriglandula

Ionoxalis quadriglandula, commonly known as the four-gland wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are usually hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Ionoxalis quadriglandula is often found in moist, woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
