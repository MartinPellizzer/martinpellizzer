Indigofera Pseudocompressa

Indigofera pseudocompressa is a unique species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the legume family and is known for its striking blue flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are compound and have a distinctive shape that sets it apart from other members of its genus. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively understudied compared to more commonly known plants in the Indigofera genus.
