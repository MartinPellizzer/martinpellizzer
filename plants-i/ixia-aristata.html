Ixia aristata

Ixia aristata, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant produces showy, tubular blooms that are typically arranged in a dense cluster at the top of a slender stem. Ixia aristata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which occurs in late spring to early summer.
