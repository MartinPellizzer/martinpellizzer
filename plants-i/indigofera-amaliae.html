Indigofera amaliae

Indigofera amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia indigo plant, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
