Iris grant-duffii var. caeruleoviolacea

Iris grant-duffii var. caeruleoviolacea, commonly known as the Blue-Violet Flag Iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate space for its vigorous growth.
