Inga goniocalyx

Inga goniocalyx, commonly known as the pink-veined inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves with distinctive pink veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
