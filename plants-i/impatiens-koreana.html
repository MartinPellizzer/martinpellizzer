Impatiens koreana

Impatiens koreana, commonly known as the Korean balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Impatiens koreana is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
