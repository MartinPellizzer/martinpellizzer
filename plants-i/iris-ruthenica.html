Iris ruthenica

Iris ruthenica, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system.

The flowers are composed of three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals, with a prominent central column.
