Incarvillea bonvalotii

Incarvillea bonvalotii, commonly known as the desert incarvillea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, white bloom, aiding in water retention.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
