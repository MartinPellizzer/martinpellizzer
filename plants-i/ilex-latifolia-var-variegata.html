Ilex latifolia var. variegata

Ilex latifolia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated evergreen holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
