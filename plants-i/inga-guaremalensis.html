Inga guaremalensis

Inga guaremalensis, commonly known as the Guarema inga, is a species of leguminous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Inga, which is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical forests and is adapted to thrive in shaded environments. Its flowers are often small and clustered, and it produces edible pods that contain seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
