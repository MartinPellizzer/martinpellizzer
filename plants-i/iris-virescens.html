Iris virescens

Iris virescens, commonly known as the green iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The leaves are sword-shaped, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
