Ipomoea edulis

Ipomoea edulis, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines and are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea edulis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it a popular ornamental plant.
