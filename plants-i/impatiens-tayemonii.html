Impatiens tayemonii

Impatiens tayemonii, commonly known as the Japanese impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Impatiens tayemonii is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
