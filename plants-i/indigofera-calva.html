Indigofera calva

Indigofera calva, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain seeds.

Indigofera calva is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
