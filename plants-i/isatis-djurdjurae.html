Isatis djurdjurae

Isatis djurdjurae, commonly known as the desert woad, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy environments, often growing in cracks of rocky soils.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.
