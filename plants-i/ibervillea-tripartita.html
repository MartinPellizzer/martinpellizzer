Ibervillea tripartita

Ibervillea tripartita, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often divided into three parts. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Ibervillea tripartita is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
