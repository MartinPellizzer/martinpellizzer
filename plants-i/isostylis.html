Isostylis

Isostylis, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. Isostylis species typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They are native to regions in Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula.

The plants are often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
