Iberis intermedia proles deflexifolia

Iberis intermedia proles deflexifolia, commonly known as the creeping candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are typically linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in garden settings.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
