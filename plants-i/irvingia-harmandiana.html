Irvingia harmandiana

Irvingia harmandiana, commonly known as the African mango, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a white, aromatic pulp.

Irvingia harmandiana is valued for its potential nutritional and medicinal properties.
