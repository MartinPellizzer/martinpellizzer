Inga enterolobioides

Inga enterolobioides, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The plant is also valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
