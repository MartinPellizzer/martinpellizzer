Intsia cambodiensis

Intsia cambodiensis, commonly known as the Cambodian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with large, simple leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its flowers are small and white or pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody fruits containing seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
