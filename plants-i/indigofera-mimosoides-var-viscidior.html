Indigofera mimosoides var. viscidior

Indigofera mimosoides var. viscidior, commonly known as the sticky mimic indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its sticky leaves and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
