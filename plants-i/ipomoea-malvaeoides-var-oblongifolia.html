Ipomoea malvaeoides var. oblongifolia

Ipomoea malvaeoides var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, oblong leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, often reaching several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
