Inga megaphylla

Inga megaphylla, commonly known as the giant puya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its large, palm-like leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. It produces showy, pendulous inflorescences with small, clustered flowers that attract a variety of pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, Inga megaphylla is often studied for its role in tropical ecosystems.
