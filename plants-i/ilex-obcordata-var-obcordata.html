Ilex obcordata var. obcordata

Ilex obcordata var. obcordata, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of other holly species. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its dense growth habit makes it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental landscaping.
