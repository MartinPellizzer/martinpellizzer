Ipomopsis aggregata var. arizonica

Ipomopsis aggregata var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona whipplin, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color. The plant thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to desert conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
