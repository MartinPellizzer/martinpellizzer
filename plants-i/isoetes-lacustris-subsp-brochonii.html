Isoetes lacustris subsp. brochonii

Isoetes lacustris subsp.

brochonii, commonly known as the brochonii watermace, is a rare aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its spore-bearing, leaf-like structures called sporophylls, which are typically arranged in a rosette formation.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds. Its sporophylls are often submerged, with some extending above the water surface to facilitate spore dispersal.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in aquatic ecosystems and is often used as an indicator of water quality.
