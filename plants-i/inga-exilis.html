Inga exilis

Inga exilis, commonly known as the slender inga, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Inga exilis plays an important role in supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
