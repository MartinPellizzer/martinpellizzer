Ipomoea flavopurpurea

Ipomoea flavopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered morning glory, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea flavopurpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
