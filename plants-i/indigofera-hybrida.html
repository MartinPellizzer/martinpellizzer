Indigofera hybrida

Indigofera hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and racemose inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can become invasive in some environments if not managed properly.
