Indigofera nigricans

Indigofera nigricans, commonly known as black indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, often purple or blue, pea-like flowers. It produces pods containing small, dark seeds that are used in traditional medicine and dye production.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
