Ixora biflora var. fleckeri

Ixora biflora var. fleckeri, commonly known as the Flecker's ixora, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge due to its attractive foliage and persistent blooms.
