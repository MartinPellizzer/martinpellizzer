Ipomoea megapotamica var. cordifolia

Ipomoea megapotamica var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety in texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
