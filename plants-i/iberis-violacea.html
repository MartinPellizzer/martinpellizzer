Iberis violacea

Iberis violacea, commonly known as violet carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a slight purple tinge. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Iberis violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
