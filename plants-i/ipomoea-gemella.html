Ipomoea gemella

Ipomoea gemella, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for climbing structures or trellises.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and climbing behavior.
