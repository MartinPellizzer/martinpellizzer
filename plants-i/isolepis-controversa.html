Isolepis controversa

Isolepis controversa, commonly known as the narrow leaf tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in temperate regions.

It typically grows in dense tussocks, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. Isolepis controversa is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and waterfowl.
