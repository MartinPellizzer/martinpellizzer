Iberis aurosica subsp. cantabrica

Iberis aurosica subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has a low, spreading habit with silvery-gray foliage that adds to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
