Ipomoea robusta

Ipomoea robusta, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white, and are known for their fragrance.

Ipomoea robusta is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens worldwide.
