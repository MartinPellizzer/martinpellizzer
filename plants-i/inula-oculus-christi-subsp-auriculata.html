Inula oculus-christi subsp. auriculata

Inula oculus-christi subsp.

auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate eyebright, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive, rounded leaf base that resembles an ear, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family, sharing similar structural and floral features.
