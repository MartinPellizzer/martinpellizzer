Ixora collina var. opuloides

Ixora collina var. opuloides, commonly known as the flame bush or red flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bright orange-red inflorescences that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ixora collina var. opuloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance and adaptability to various garden settings.
