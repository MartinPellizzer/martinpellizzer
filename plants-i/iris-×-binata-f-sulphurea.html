Iris × binata f. sulphurea

Iris × binata f. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur yellow iris, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals, forming a distinctive spathaceous structure.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
