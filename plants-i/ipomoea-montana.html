Ipomoea montana

Ipomoea montana, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. Ipomoea montana is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length.

Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like fruit that ripens to a red or orange color.
