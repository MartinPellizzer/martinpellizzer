Inga coruscans

Inga coruscans, commonly known as the golden inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and broad, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a long, pod-like structure containing several edible seeds.

Inga coruscans is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
