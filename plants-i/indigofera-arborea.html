Indigofera arborea

Indigofera arborea, commonly known as the tree indigo, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Indigofera arborea is also valued for its traditional use in dyeing fabrics and as a source of medicinal compounds.
