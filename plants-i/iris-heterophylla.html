Iris heterophylla

Iris heterophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale blue center. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
