Iva asperifolia

Iva asperifolia, commonly known as roughleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous small, daisy-like blooms.

Iva asperifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to moist environments.
