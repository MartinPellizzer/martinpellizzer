Ixia coerulescens

Ixia coerulescens, commonly known as the bluebell lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Ixia coerulescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
