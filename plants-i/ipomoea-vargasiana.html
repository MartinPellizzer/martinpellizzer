Ipomoea vargasiana

Ipomoea vargasiana, commonly known as the Vargas morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped blossoms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea vargasiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
