Ilex kusanoi

Ilex kusanoi, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins and a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its dense foliage and year-round appeal make it suitable for hedges and landscape design.
