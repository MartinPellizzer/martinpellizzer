Ischaemum aristatum subsp. koenigii

Ischaemum aristatum subsp.

koenigii, commonly known as koenig's hairvetch, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

The plant features slender, erect stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
