Indigofera asperifolia

Indigofera asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, often purple or blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
