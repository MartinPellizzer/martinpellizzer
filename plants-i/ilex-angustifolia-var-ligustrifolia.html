Ilex angustifolia var. ligustrifolia

Ilex angustifolia var. ligustrifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are an important food source for various birds and wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
