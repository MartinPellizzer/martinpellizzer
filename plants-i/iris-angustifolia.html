Iris angustifolia

Iris angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue. Its showy flowers are usually blue or purple, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

Iris angustifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
