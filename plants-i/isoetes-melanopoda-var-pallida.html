Isoetes melanopoda var. pallida

Isoetes melanopoda var. pallida, commonly known as the pale spike moss, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, spore-bearing spikes that emerge from submerged rhizomes. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Its reproductive structures, called sporangia, are clustered in spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems.

This variety is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to other varieties of Isoetes melanopoda.
