Inula gracilis

Inula gracilis, commonly known as slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It features slender, upright stems with finely divided, gray-green leaves.

Its showy yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
