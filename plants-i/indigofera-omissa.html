Indigofera omissa

Indigofera omissa, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and parts of Africa. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Indigofera omissa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
