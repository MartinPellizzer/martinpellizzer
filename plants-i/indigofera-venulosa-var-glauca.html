Indigofera venulosa var. glauca

Indigofera venulosa var. glauca, commonly known as the blue indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a bluish waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding soil.
