Ipomoea papillosa

Ipomoea papillosa, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence its common name.

Ipomoea papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
