Inga congesta

Inga congesta, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "sweet sop," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable in reforestation efforts.

The plant's ecological importance is further enhanced by its role in supporting local wildlife.
