Iris × squalens subsp. lepida

Iris × squalens subsp.

lepida, commonly known as the slender iris, is a hybrid species within the genus Iris. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The flowers bloom in early to mid-summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after plant for gardeners interested in cultivating native or ornamental iris species.
