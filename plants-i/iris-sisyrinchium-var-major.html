Iris sisyrinchium var. major

Iris sisyrinchium var. major, commonly known as the Great Blue Flag Iris, is a striking aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white stripe down the center. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps that help stabilize the soil.

The Great Blue Flag Iris is an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
