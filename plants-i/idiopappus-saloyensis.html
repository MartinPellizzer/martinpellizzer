Idiopappus saloyensis

Idiopappus saloyensis, commonly known as the Saloyen's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

The plant is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in humid tropical climates.
