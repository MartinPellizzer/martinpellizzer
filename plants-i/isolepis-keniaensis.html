Isolepis keniaensis

Isolepis keniaensis, commonly known as Kenyan flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it forms dense mats. Isolepis keniaensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
