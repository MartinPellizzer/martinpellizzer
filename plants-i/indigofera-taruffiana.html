Indigofera taruffiana

Indigofera taruffiana, commonly known as the desert indigo.

It is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant is well-suited to harsh environments and can thrive with minimal water, making it a hardy species for dry climates.
