Iberis martini

Iberis martini, commonly known as the Turkish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are lobed and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Iberis martini is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
