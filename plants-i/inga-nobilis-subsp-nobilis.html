Inga nobilis subsp. nobilis

Inga nobilis subsp.

nobilis, commonly known as the royal velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and trifoliate leaves with glossy, dark green leaflets.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
