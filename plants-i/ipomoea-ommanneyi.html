Ipomoea ommanneyi

Ipomoea ommanneyi, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or tree trunks. Its flowers are typically about 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter, and it is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
