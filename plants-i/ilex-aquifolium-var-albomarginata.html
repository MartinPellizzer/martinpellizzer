Ilex aquifolium var. albomarginata

Ilex aquifolium var. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-edged holly, is a variant of the common holly.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow margins on the leaves, which contrast with its dark green central portion. This plant is evergreen, featuring spiny leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas.

The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds.
