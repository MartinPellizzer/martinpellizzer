Inula stoliczkae

Inula stoliczkae, commonly known as Stoliczka's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
