Ischaemum zeylanicola

Ischaemum zeylanicola, commonly known as the Ceylon grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures.

Ischaemum zeylanicola is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid climates and its role in maintaining soil stability in its natural habitat.
