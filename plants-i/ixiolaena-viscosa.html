Ixiolaena viscosa

Ixiolaena viscosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a sticky surface, which can trap insects. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Ixiolaena viscosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
