Inga leptopus

Inga leptopus, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
