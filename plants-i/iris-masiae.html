Iris masiae

Iris masiae, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Iris masiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
