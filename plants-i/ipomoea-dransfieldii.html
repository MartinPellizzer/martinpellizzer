Ipomoea dransfieldii

Ipomoea dransfieldii, commonly known as the Dransfield's morning glory, is a tropical vine native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
