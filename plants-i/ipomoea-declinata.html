Ipomoea declinata

Ipomoea declinata, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found twining around trees, shrubs, or other structures. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea declinata is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
