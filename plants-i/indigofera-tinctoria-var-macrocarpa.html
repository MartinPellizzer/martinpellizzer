Indigofera tinctoria var. macrocarpa

Indigofera tinctoria var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded indigo plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a leguminous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds produce a deep blue dye historically used in textile industries.

This variety is distinguished by its larger seed pods compared to other indigo species.
