Ipomoea floccosa

Ipomoea floccosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea floccosa is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other familiar species like the common morning glory.
