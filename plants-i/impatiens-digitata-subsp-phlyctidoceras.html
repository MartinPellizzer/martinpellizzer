Impatiens digitata subsp. phlyctidoceras

Impatiens digitata subsp.

phlyctidoceras, commonly known as the horned impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color, with five petals that are often adorned with a prominent, horn-like appendage.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
