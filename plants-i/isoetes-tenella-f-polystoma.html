Isoetes tenella f. polystoma

Isoetes tenella f. polystoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermoss, is a rare aquatic fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its finely divided, linear leaves that form dense, mat-like structures on the sediment surface. The plant exhibits a unique life cycle involving spore production and vegetative propagation through rhizomes. It is often found in still or slow-moving water, such as ponds and marshes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality and ecological health.
