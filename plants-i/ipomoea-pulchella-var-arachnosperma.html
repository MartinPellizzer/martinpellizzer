Ipomoea pulchella var. arachnosperma

Ipomoea pulchella var. arachnosperma, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often fuzzy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its unique feature is the silky, thread-like hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
