Indigofera bayensis

Indigofera bayensis, commonly known as the Bay Islands indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, purple or violet flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
