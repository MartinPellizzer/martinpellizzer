Indigofera propinqua

Indigofera propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing small, black seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
