Indigofera griquana

Indigofera griquana, commonly known as Grootfontein indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in grassland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has been historically used for its dye-producing properties, though its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and adaptability.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
