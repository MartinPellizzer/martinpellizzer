Ipomoea bonariensis var. rupestris

Ipomoea bonariensis var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, adding ornamental value to gardens.
