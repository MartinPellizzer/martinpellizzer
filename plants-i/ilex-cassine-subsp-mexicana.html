Ilex cassine subsp. mexicana

Ilex cassine subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have spiny margins. Its small, white flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
