Iris setosa f. alba

Iris setosa f. alba, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a cultivar of the iris species Iris setosa.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast sharply with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 40 centimeters, making it a compact and elegant addition to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
