Indigofera emarginelloides

Indigofera emarginelloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
