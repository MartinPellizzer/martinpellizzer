Inula barbata

Inula barbata, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, upright stem covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing yellow disc flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

Inula barbata is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
