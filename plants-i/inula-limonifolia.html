Inula limonifolia

Inula limonifolia, commonly known as the yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Inula limonifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
