Ipomoea heterotricha var. tomentosa

Ipomoea heterotricha var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found climbing structures or shrubs.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
