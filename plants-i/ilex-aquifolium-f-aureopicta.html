Ilex aquifolium f. aureopicta

Ilex aquifolium f. aureopicta, commonly known as the golden-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly, characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves.

This cultivar exhibits a dense, evergreen foliage that provides year-round visual interest in gardens. The leaves are typically smaller and more elongated compared to the standard species, with a glossy texture. It produces small, red berries in the winter, adding seasonal color.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
