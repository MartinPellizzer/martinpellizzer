Isodon delavayi

Isodon delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's verbena, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Isodon delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
