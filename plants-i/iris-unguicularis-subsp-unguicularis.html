Iris unguicularis subsp. unguicularis

Iris unguicularis subsp.

unguicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to coastal conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
