Isoetes tenuifolia

Isoetes tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that emerge from a central axis, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. This plant typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Isoetes tenuifolia is a member of the genus Isoetes, which is known for its unique spore-producing structures called sporangia.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
