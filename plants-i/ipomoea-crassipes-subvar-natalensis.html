Ipomoea crassipes subvar. natalensis

Ipomoea crassipes subvar. natalensis, commonly known as Natal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in moist, tropical environments. It is often found near water bodies and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
