Icaranda

Icaranda, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven leaflets, which are dark green and glossy. Icaranda thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and warm climates.
