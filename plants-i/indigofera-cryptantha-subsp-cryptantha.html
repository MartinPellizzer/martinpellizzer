Indigofera cryptantha subsp. cryptantha

Indigofera cryptantha subsp.

cryptantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Southwestern North America. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of purple to pink pea-like flowers.

The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to drought conditions and often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional dyeing practices.
