Ivesia utahensis

Ivesia utahensis, commonly known as Utah ivy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture.
