Iria umbellata

Iria umbellata, commonly known as the white umbellate, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, umbel-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Iria umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
