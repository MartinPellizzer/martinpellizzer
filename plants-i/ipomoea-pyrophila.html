Ipomoea pyrophila

Ipomoea pyrophila, commonly known as the flame vine, is a striking flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and is typically found growing along coastal areas and in disturbed soils. Ipomoea pyrophila is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to ascend supports.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
