Indigofera hewittii

Indigofera hewittii, commonly known as the Australian indigo, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
