Ipomoea cardiosepala

Ipomoea cardiosepala, commonly known as the heart-sepal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped sepals that give it its common name, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped as well. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Ipomoea cardiosepala is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms throughout the growing season.
