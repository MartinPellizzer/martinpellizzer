Ipomoea nevisiensis

Ipomoea nevisiensis, commonly known as the cloud island morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Puerto Rico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea nevisiensis is often found growing along stream banks and in humid, tropical environments.
