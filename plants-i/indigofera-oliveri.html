Indigofera oliveri

Indigofera oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and coastal habitats. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and benefits surrounding vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
