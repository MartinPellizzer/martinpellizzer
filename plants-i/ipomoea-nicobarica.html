Ipomoea nicobarica

Ipomoea nicobarica, commonly known as the Nicobar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along forest edges or in disturbed areas. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
