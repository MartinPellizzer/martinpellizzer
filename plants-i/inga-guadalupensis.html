Inga guadalupensis

Inga guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupensis Umbrella Tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its broad, umbrella-like canopy and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation areas.
