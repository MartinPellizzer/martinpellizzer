Ixeris lamii

Ixeris lamii, commonly known as the Chinese groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Ixeris lamii produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
