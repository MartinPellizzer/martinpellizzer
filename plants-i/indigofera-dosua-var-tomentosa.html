Indigofera dosua var. tomentosa

Indigofera dosua var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
