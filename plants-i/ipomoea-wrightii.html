Ipomoea wrightii

Ipomoea wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant blue or violet flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by water and animals, contributing to its widespread distribution.
