Isertia krausei

Isertia krausei, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually borne on tall, branching stalks. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a drought-tolerant species.

Isertia krausei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
