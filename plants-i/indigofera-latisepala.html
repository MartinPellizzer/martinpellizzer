Indigofera latisepala

Indigofera latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal indigofera, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Indigofera latisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
