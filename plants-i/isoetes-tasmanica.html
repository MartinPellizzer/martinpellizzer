Isoetes tasmanica

Isoetes tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Sphenopsida.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobils, which are typically located at the base of the plant. Isoetes tasmanica is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and ecological health in its environment.
