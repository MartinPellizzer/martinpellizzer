Indigofera elwakensis

Indigofera elwakensis, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally for its dye-producing properties and medicinal applications.
