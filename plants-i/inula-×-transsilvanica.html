Inula × transsilvanica

Inula × transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian elecampane, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Inula helenium and Inula crithmoides.

It is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe, characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Inula × transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
