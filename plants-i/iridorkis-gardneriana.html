Iridorkis gardneriana

Iridorkis gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's iridorkis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related species. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, violet-blue flowers with three petals and three sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
