Inga ciliata

Inga ciliata, commonly known as the silky ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a valuable species in reforestation efforts. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga ciliata also plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, enhancing fertility for surrounding vegetation.
