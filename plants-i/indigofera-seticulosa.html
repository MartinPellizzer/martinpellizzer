Indigofera seticulosa

Indigofera seticulosa, commonly known as the slender indigo or narrowleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
