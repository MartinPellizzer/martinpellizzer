Ipomoea trinervia

Ipomoea trinervia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences, walls, and in moist, disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by birds and animals, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Ipomoea trinervia is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
