Isatis tinctoria subsp. japonica

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese woad, is a biennial plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is cultivated for its blue dye-producing properties, similar to its parent species, Isatis tinctoria. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles and traditional medicine highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
