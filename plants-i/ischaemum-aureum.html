Ischaemum aureum

Ischaemum aureum, commonly known as golden oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, yellowish to golden flowers.

Ischaemum aureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate, grass-like appearance.
