Indigofera phyllanthoides

Indigofera phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.
