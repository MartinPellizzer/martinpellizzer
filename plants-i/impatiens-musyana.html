Impatiens musyana

Impatiens musyana, commonly known as the Musyana touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with smooth, glossy leaves and produces showy blooms throughout the wet season. Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
