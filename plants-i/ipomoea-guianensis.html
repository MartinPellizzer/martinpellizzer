Ipomoea guianensis

Ipomoea guianensis, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, typically displaying vibrant colors such as purple, blue, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea guianensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it both ornamental and invasive in some regions.
