Ipomoea nil

Ipomoea nil, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, pink, and white.

Ipomoea nil is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
