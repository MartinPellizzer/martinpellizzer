Impatiens trichocladon

Impatiens trichocladon, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated settings.
