Ipomoea yamuriensis

Ipomoea yamuriensis, commonly known as the Yamuriensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often twining around supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ipomoea yamuriensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
