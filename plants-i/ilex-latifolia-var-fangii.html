Ilex latifolia var. fangii

Ilex latifolia var. fangii, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a variant of the Chinese holly native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and winter berries, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The varietal form fangii is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and size compared to the typical species.
