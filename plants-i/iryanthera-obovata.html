Iryanthera obovata

Iryanthera obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

Iryanthera obovata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
