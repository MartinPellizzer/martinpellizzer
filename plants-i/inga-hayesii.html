Inga hayesii

Inga hayesii, commonly known as the Hayes's inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and has a rapid growth rate, making it a valuable species for reforestation. Its pods contain edible seeds, and the plant is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
