Isotropis striata var. parviflora

Isotropis striata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered isotropis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and adaptability to various garden settings.
