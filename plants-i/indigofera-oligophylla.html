Indigofera oligophylla

Indigofera oligophylla, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in shaded or partially sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential use in traditional medicine.
