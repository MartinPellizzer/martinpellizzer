Inula capensis

Inula capensis, commonly known as the African elecampane, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a tall, sparsely branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense, white to pale yellow disk of tubular florets. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Inula capensis is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
