Iberis odorata proles pectinata

Iberis odorata proles pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata type of sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pinnately divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
