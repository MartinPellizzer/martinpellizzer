Isoetes eatonii f. gravesii

Isoetes eatonii f. gravesii, commonly known as the Graves' water starwort, is a rare aquatic fern belonging to the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, spirally arranged leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving water, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered at the tips of the leaf-like stems, producing spores for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality and ecological health.
