Iva frutescens var. oraria

Iva frutescens var. oraria, commonly known as the swamp evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
