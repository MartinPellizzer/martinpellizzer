Ipomoea papilio f. pluriflora

Ipomoea papilio f. pluriflora, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory genus characterized by its vibrant purple flowers.

This plant is a herbaceous vine that produces large, showy blooms that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. The flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals that are often marked with intricate patterns.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green, adding visual interest to gardens and natural landscapes.
