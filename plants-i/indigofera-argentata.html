Indigofera argentata

Indigofera argentata, commonly known as silver indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Silver indigo is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
