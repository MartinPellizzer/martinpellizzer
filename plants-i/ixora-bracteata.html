Ixora bracteata

Ixora bracteata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and as a potted plant.

Ixora bracteata is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
