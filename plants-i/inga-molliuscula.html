Inga molliuscula

Inga molliuscula, commonly known as the "sweet acacia," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it valuable for soil enrichment and reforestation efforts.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some regions, where it is employed for its potential health benefits.
