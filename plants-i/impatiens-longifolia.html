Impatiens longifolia

Impatiens longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The leaves are elongated and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Its delicate blooms and adaptability make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
