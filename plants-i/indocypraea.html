Indocypraea

Indocypraea, commonly known as the blue-and-white snail, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Cypraeidae.

It is characterized by its strikingly beautiful shell, which typically features a combination of blue, white, and brown hues. The shell is smooth and often has a glossy surface, with intricate patterns that vary among individuals. This species is found in tropical and subtropical marine environments, primarily in the Indo-Pacific region.

Its diet consists mainly of algae and detritus, which it scrapes from surfaces using its radula.
