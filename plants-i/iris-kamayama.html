Iris kamayama

Iris kamayama, commonly known as Kamayama iris, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a prominent midrib.

Iris kamayama is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
