Ischaemum spathiflorum

Ischaemum spathiflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakvetch, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

Ischaemum spathiflorum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain grassland ecosystems.
