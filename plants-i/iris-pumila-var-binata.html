Iris pumila var. binata

Iris pumila var. binata, commonly known as the dwarf iris or the two-colored iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive two-toned pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a subtle yet striking floral display to its natural habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking hardy, decorative plants.
