Ilex aquifolium var. foxii

Ilex aquifolium var. foxii, commonly known as the Fox's holly, is a variant of the common holly that is native to the British Isles.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its spiny, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in hedging and landscaping.
