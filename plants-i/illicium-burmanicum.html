Illicium burmanicum

Illicium burmanicum, commonly known as the Burmese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Illicium burmanicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in the preparation of certain spice blends.
