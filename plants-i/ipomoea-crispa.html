Ipomoea crispa

Ipomoea crispa, commonly known as the crinkled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a vine or shrub.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
