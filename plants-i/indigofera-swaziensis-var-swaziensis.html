Indigofera swaziensis var. swaziensis

Indigofera swaziensis var. swaziensis, commonly known as Swazi indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It typically grows in grasslands and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
