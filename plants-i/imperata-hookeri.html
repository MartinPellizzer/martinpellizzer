Imperata hookeri

Imperata hookeri, commonly known as the purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and striking purple to pink flower spikes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Imperata hookeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
