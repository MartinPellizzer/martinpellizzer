Icica serrata

Icica serrata, commonly known as the serrated icica, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Icica serrata is often found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
