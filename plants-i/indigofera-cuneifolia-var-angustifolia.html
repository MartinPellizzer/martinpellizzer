Indigofera cuneifolia var. angustifolia

Indigofera cuneifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
