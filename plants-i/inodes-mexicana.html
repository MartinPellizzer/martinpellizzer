Inodes mexicana

Inodes mexicana, commonly known as Mexican knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, woody stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense colonies. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Inodes mexicana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
