Iris × alba

Iris × alba, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white flowers with a distinctive purple or violet stripe along the throat. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a striking visual impact in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Iris × alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
