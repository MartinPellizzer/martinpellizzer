Ilex nervosa var. glabrata

Ilex nervosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its evergreen foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
