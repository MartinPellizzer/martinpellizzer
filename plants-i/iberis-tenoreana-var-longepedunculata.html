Iberis tenoreana var. longepedunculata

Iberis tenoreana var. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or alpine environments.
