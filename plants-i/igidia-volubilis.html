Igidia volubilis

Igidia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Igidia volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
