Iris mauritanica

Iris mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian iris, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
