Indigofera melilotoides

Indigofera melilotoides, commonly known as the sweet indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Indigofera melilotoides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.
