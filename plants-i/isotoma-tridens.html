Isotoma tridens

Isotoma tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in tundra and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Isotoma tridens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
