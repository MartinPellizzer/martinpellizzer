Indigofera thibaudiana

Indigofera thibaudiana, commonly known as the blue indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and adaptability to various climates.
