Indigofera fruticulosa

Indigofera fruticulosa, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
