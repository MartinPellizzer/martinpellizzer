Impatiens gadellae

Impatiens gadellae, commonly known as the Galápagos impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Impatiens gadellae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to local biodiversity and supporting pollinator populations.
