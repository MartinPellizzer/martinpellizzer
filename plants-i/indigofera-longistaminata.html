Indigofera longistaminata

Indigofera longistaminata, commonly known as the long-stamen indigo plant, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

This makes it an important plant for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
