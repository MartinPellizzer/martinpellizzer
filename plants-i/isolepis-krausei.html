Isolepis krausei

Isolepis krausei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, forming dense tussocks with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Isolepis krausei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
