Iris alata var. lilacina

Iris alata var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, lilac-pink to lavender-colored flowers with a distinctive dark purple or black throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic floral display to the landscape.
