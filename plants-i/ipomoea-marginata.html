Ipomoea marginata

Ipomoea marginata, commonly known as the creeping blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with a trailing habit, often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea marginata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly, making it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.
