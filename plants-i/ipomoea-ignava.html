Ipomoea ignava

Ipomoea ignava, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea ignava is also known for its rapid growth and ability to scramble over supports or fences.
