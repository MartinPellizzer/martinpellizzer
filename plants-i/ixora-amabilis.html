Ixora amabilis

Ixora amabilis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are rich in nectar and have a pleasant fragrance.
