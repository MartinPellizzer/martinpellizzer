Ipomoea nitida subsp. nitida

Ipomoea nitida subsp.

nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
