Inula praealta

Inula praealta, commonly known as the tall elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Inula praealta is often found in meadows and along roadsides, and it has been used historically in herbal medicine.
