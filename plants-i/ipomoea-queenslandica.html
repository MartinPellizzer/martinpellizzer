Ipomoea queenslandica

Ipomoea queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
