Isatis quadrialata

Isatis quadrialata, commonly known as the four-leafed woad, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant has been cultivated for its dye-producing properties.

Isatis quadrialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
