Ixora farinosa

Ixora farinosa, commonly known as the powdered ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora farinosa is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
