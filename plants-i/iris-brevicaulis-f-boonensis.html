Iris brevicaulis f. boonensis

Iris brevicaulis f. boonensis, commonly known as the Boonensis iris, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, lavender-blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. This variety is notable for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The plant is also valued for its role in native plant restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
