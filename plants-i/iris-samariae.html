Iris samariae

Iris samariae, commonly known as the Palestine iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces striking blue or purple flowers. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem, and they bloom in the spring.

Iris samariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
