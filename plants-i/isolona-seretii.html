Isolona seretii

Isolona seretii, commonly known as the Natal fan-palm, is a species of palm native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, fan-like appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are sometimes used in local cuisine.

Isolona seretii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
