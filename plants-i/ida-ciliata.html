Ida ciliata

Ida ciliata, commonly known as the silky willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and small, silky-haired leaves. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical flower clusters, typical of the Salicaceae family. Ida ciliata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.
