Iris × parvicaerulea

Iris × parvicaerulea, commonly known as the slender blue flag iris, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early to mid-summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
