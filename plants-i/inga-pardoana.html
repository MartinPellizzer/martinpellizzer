Inga pardoana

Inga pardoana, commonly known as the Pardoana velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
