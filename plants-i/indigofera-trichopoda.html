Indigofera trichopoda

Indigofera trichopoda, commonly known as Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a leguminous species belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for producing pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its roots have been utilized in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
