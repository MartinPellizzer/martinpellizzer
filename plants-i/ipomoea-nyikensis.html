Ipomoea nyikensis

Ipomoea nyikensis, commonly known as the Nyikensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth habit. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea nyikensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
