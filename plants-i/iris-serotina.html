Iris serotina

Iris serotina, commonly known as the late iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often form a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Iris serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
