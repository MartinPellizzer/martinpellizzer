Iphiona persica

Iphiona persica, commonly known as Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Iphiona persica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional gardens.
