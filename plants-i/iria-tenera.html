Iria tenera

Iria tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Iria tenera is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
