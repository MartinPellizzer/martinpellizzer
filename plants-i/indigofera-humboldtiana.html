Indigofera humboldtiana

Indigofera humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's indigo, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark blue seeds, which are used in traditional dyeing practices. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
