Isoetes velata var. lereschii

Isoetes velata var. lereschii, commonly known as the Leresch's water clover, is a rare aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that form dense, floating mats on the water surface. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in specialized leaf structures called sporophylls. It is typically found in still or slow-moving waters with soft, muddy substrates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality and ecological health.
