Ipomoea asterophora var. asterophora

Ipomoea asterophora var. asterophora, commonly known as the star-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, often twining around supports. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
