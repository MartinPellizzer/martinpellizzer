Impatiens pellucidinervia

Impatiens pellucidinervia, commonly known as the translucent-veined impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive translucent appearance on the veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the rainy season. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a garden plant in warm climates.
