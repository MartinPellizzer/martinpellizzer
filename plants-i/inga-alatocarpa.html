Inga alatocarpa

Inga alatocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Inga tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and trifoliate. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
