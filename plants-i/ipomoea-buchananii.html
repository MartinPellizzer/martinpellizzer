Ipomoea buchananii

Ipomoea buchananii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea buchananii is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known morning glory species.
