Iris variegata var. reginae

Iris variegata var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's Iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat and yellow markings. The leaves are lance-shaped, dark green, and often have a silvery-white variegation that adds visual interest. This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for perennial gardens.
