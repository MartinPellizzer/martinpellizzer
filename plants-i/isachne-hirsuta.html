Isachne hirsuta

Isachne hirsuta, commonly known as hairy isachne, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Isachne hirsuta is often used in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
