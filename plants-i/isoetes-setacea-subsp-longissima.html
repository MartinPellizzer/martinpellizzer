Isoetes setacea subsp. longissima

Isoetes setacea subsp.

longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure on the sediment surface.

The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are typically located at the base of the leaves.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
