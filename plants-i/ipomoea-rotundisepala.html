Ipomoea rotundisepala

Ipomoea rotundisepala, commonly known as the round-sepaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded sepals that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is a climbing vine that thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, adding to its ornamental appeal.
