Ipomoea boisiana

Ipomoea boisiana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
