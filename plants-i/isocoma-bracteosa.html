Isocoma bracteosa

Isocoma bracteosa, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. Its yellow-orange ray flowers are showy and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Isocoma bracteosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in sandy or gravelly habitats.
