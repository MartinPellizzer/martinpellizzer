Icacina oliviformis var. oliviformis

Icacina oliviformis var. oliviformis, commonly known as the olive-leafed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are often followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
