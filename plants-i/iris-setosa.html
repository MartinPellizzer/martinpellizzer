Iris setosa

Iris setosa, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris setosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
