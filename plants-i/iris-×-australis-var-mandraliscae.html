Iris × australis var. mandraliscae

Iris × australis var. mandraliscae, commonly known as the Mandralisca iris, is a hybrid plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white stripe running down the central petal. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
