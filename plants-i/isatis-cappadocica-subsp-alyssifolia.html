Isatis cappadocica subsp. alyssifolia

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

alyssifolia, commonly known as the Cappadocian woad, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage.

The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its historical use in dye production and as a source of natural indigo.
