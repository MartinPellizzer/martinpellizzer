Indigofera chitralensis

Indigofera chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale blue or white in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often hairy in texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
