Ilex glabra var. austrina

Ilex glabra var. austrina, commonly known as the Australian holly, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are usually leathery and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

This species is often used in landscaping for its evergreen foliage and ornamental value.
