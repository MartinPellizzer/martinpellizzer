Isachne schmidtii

Isachne schmidtii, commonly known as Schmidt's isachne, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isachne schmidtii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
