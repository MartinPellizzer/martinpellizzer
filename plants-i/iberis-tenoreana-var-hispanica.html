Iberis tenoreana var. hispanica

Iberis tenoreana var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is a hardy perennial that is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
