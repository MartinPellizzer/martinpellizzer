Indigofera baumiana

Indigofera baumiana, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and small, often pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of climates.
