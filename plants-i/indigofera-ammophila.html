Indigofera ammophila

Indigofera ammophila, commonly known as the sand-binding indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa. This species is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often used for dune stabilization due to its extensive root system.
