Ilex dumosa var. guaranina

Ilex dumosa var. guaranina, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm regions.
