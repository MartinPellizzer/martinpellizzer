Ixora sandankwa

Ixora sandankwa, commonly known as the red ironwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant, red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing it with a degree of drought resistance.

Ixora sandankwa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
