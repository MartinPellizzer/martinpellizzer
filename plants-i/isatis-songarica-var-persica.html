Isatis songarica var. persica

Isatis songarica var. persica, commonly known as Persian woad, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its dark green, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It produces spherical, smooth, green capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its historical use in dye production, particularly for creating blue pigments.
