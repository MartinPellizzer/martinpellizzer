Inula micranthos

Inula micranthos, commonly known as the small-flowered elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, hollow stem covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Inula micranthos is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates.
