Impatiens fuchsioides

Impatiens fuchsioides, commonly known as the scarlet balsam, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in horticulture.
