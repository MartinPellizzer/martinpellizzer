Indigofera glandulosa var. sykesii

Indigofera glandulosa var. sykesii, commonly known as the African indigo or Sykes' indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
