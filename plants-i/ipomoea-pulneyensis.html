Ipomoea pulneyensis

Ipomoea pulneyensis, commonly known as the Pumice vine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces pale yellow to orange blooms that are typically 5-7 cm in diameter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Ipomoea pulneyensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
