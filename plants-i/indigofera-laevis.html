Indigofera laevis

Indigofera laevis, commonly known as the silky indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with soft, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in dye production.

Indigofera laevis thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
