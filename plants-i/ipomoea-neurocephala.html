Ipomoea neurocephala

Ipomoea neurocephala, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns" or "Thorned Morning Glory," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Ipomoea neurocephala thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to cover trellises or fences quickly.
