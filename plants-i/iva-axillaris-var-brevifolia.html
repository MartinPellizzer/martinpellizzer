Iva axillaris var. brevifolia

Iva axillaris var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marshelder, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
