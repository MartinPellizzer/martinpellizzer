Inga ciliata subsp. subcapitata

Inga ciliata subsp.

subcapitata, commonly known as the red-veined inga, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have a distinctive red-veined appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in forest regeneration.
