Ipomoea paniculata

Ipomoea paniculata, commonly known as the climbing morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rapid growth habit and trailing or climbing vines that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, opening in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Ipomoea paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.
