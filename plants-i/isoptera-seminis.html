Isoptera seminis

Isoptera seminis, commonly known as the seed-feeding termite, is a species of termite that plays a significant role in the decomposition of plant material.

These insects primarily feed on the seeds of various plants, contributing to nutrient cycling in ecosystems. Their feeding habits can influence plant regeneration and seed dispersal patterns. The life cycle of Isoptera seminis includes a caste system with workers, soldiers, and reproductive individuals.

Botanically, they are important in the breakdown of plant matter, aiding in soil enrichment and ecological balance.
