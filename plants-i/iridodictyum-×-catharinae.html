Iridodictyum × catharinae

Iridodictyum × catharinae, commonly known as the hybrid grass of the genus Iridodictyum, is a low-growing, ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
