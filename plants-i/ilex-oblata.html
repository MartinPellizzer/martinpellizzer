Ilex oblata

Ilex oblata, commonly known as the white-leaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Ilex oblata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
