Isatis guestii

Isatis guestii, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Isatis guestii is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
