Isodon kameba

Isodon kameba, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in dense clusters that bloom in the fall.
