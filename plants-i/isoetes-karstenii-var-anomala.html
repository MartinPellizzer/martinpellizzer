Isoetes karstenii var. anomala

Isoetes karstenii var. anomala, commonly known as the anomalous water parsley, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a central axis. The plant is native to freshwater habitats in temperate regions, particularly in parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, with a distinct midrib and a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

This variety is often found in still or slow-moving water bodies, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
