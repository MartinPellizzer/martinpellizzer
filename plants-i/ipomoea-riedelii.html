Ipomoea riedelii

Ipomoea riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea riedelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
