Indigofera alta

Indigofera alta, commonly known as tall indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The seeds of Indigofera alta are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
