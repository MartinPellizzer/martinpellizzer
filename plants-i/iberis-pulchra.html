Iberis pulchra

Iberis pulchra, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Iberis pulchra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
