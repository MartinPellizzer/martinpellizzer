Ixora angustifolia

Ixora angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, narrow, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Ixora angustifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
