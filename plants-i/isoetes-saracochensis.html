Isoetes saracochensis

Isoetes saracochensis, commonly known as the Saracochensis watermace, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, sparsely branched stems that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a thin layer of mucus, which helps in nutrient absorption. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are located on the upper surface of the leaves.

Isoetes saracochensis is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.
