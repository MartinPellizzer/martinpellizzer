Inga fagifolia var. pedicellaris

Inga fagifolia var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the pedicellaris inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically trifoliate with elongated, hairy stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are often accompanied by a distinctive, elongated pedicel. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to grow in shaded, moist forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
