Isnardia discolor

Isnardia discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces smooth, spherical berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes.
