Iris stenogyna

Iris stenogyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three spreading sepals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris stenogyna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
