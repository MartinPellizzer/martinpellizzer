Ipomoea weinmannii

Ipomoea weinmannii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other morning glories.

Ipomoea weinmannii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
