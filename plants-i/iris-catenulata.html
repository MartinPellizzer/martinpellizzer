Iris catenulata

Iris catenulata, commonly known as the threadleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are composed of three petals and three sepals, typical of the iris family.

Iris catenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
