Inga leptocarpa

Inga leptocarpa, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
