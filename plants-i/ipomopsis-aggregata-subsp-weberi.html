Ipomopsis aggregata subsp. weberi

Ipomopsis aggregata subsp.

weberi, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
