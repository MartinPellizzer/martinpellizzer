Inga laeta

Inga laeta, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with a dense arrangement of leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, often displaying a white or pale pink color.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.
