Iberis bernardiana var. bubanii

Iberis bernardiana var. bubanii, commonly known as the Buban's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a mat-forming habit with dense, silver-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures, which contributes to its popularity among gardeners in temperate climates.
