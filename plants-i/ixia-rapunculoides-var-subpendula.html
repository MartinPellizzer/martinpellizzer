Ixia rapunculoides var. subpendula

Ixia rapunculoides var. subpendula, commonly known as the pendant ixia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the garden.
