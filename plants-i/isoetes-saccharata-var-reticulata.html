Isoetes saccharata var. reticulata

Isoetes saccharata var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its finely divided, reticulate (net-like) leaves that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it can thrive in nutrient-rich substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on specialized leaf-like structures called sporophylls.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
