Inga huastecana

Inga huastecana, commonly known as Huastec bean or Huastec tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are pinnate, composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Inga huastecana is valued for its nutritional seeds and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
