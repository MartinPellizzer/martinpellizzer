Impatiens xanthina var. pusilla

Impatiens xanthina var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf yellow impatiens, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle red or orange hue at the base. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
