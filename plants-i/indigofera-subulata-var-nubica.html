Indigofera subulata var. nubica

Indigofera subulata var. nubica, commonly known as the Nubian needleleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of violet or blue flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access groundwater.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some local communities.
