Ipomoea repanda

Ipomoea repanda, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Ipomoea repanda belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it both ornamental and invasive in certain regions.
