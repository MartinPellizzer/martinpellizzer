Ixeris chinensis subsp. versicolor

Ixeris chinensis subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia. It features a dense mat of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green but can vary in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and long blooming period.
