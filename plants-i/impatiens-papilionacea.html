Impatiens papilionacea

Impatiens papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
