Isnardia aurantiaca

Isnardia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals and a unique floral structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by specific insect species.

The botanical features of Isnardia aurantiaca make it a prized specimen in both horticulture and botanical research.
