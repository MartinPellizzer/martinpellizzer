Ipomoea barbatisepala var. barbatisepala

Ipomoea barbatisepala var. barbatisepala, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces tendrils that allow it to climb and spread along supports. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, oblong seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
