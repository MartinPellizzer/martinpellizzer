Ipomoea scabra

Ipomoea scabra, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea scabra is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.
