Inga salvadorensis

Inga salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran inga, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist forests and along riverbanks.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important species for soil enrichment in agroforestry systems.
