Iris anglica

Iris anglica, commonly known as the English iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, sword-shaped leaves and striking flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, showcasing shades of blue, purple, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wetland habitats.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
