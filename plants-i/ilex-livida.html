Ilex livida

Ilex livida, commonly known as the silver leaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex livida typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
