Iris aphylla subsp. nudicaulis

Iris aphylla subsp.

nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked stalk iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its lack of leaf sheaths, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and desert areas.
