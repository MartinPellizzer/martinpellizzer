Isostylis ericifolia

Isostylis ericifolia, commonly known as the heath isostylis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
