Indigofera deflexa

Indigofera deflexa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically small, clustered in racemes, and range in color from white to pale purple.

Indigofera deflexa is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
