Ipomoea thurberi

Ipomoea thurberi, commonly known as the Thurber's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known morning glories.

Botanically, it is noted for its showy flowers and its role in local ecosystems as a nectar source for pollinators.
