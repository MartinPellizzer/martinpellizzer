Inula sennenii

Inula sennenii, commonly known as the Japanese Inula, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
