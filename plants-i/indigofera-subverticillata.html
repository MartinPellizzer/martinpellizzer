Indigofera subverticillata

Indigofera subverticillata, commonly known as the dwarf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets arranged in a cluster.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
