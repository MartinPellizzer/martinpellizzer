Ipomoea tyrianthina

Ipomoea tyrianthina, commonly known as the crimson morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
