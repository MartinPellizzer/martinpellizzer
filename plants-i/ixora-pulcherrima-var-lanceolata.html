Ixora pulcherrima var. lanceolata

Ixora pulcherrima var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, branched inflorescences. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
