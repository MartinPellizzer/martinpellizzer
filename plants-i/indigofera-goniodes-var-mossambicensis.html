Indigofera goniodes var. mossambicensis

Indigofera goniodes var. mossambicensis, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including dyeing and medicinal applications.
