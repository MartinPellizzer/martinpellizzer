Ipomoea yomae

Ipomoea yomae, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Japan.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the morning and closing at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea yomae is also notable for its ability to climb and twine around supports, making it a popular choice for trellises and gardens.
