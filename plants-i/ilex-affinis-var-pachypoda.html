Ilex affinis var. pachypoda

Ilex affinis var. pachypoda, commonly known as the thickleaf holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, thriving in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with temperate climates.
