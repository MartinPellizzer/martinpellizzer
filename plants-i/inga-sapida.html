Inga sapida

Inga sapida, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is valued for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it useful for reforestation efforts. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines.

Additionally, Inga sapida is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, enhancing soil fertility in agricultural settings.
