Indigofera wenholdiae

Indigofera wenholdiae, commonly known as the Wenhold's indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
