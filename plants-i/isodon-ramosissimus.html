Isodon ramosissimus

Isodon ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, lemony fragrance due to the presence of volatile compounds.

Isodon ramosissimus is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
