Isonandra lanceolata var. gracilis

Isonandra lanceolata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaf isonandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
