Isodon kameba f. albescens

Isodon kameba f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered isodon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with a fine pubescence. Native to regions of East Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
