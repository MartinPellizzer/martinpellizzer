Indocalamus actinotrichus

Indocalamus actinotrichus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often covered with a yellowish or golden hue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Indocalamus actinotrichus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens and landscapes.
