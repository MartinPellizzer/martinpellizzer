Indigofera amblyantha var. purdomii

Indigofera amblyantha var. purdomii, commonly known as the purple indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It produces pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
