Ipomoea boisiana var. rufopilosa

Ipomoea boisiana var. rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-haired morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The variety rufopilosa is distinguished by its reddish-brown stems and foliage.
