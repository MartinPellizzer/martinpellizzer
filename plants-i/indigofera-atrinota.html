Indigofera atrinota

Indigofera atrinota, commonly known as the dark indigo bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, dark-colored seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
