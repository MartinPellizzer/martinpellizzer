Indigofera henryi

Indigofera henryi, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture.
