Isochilus linearis

Isochilus linearis, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

Isochilus linearis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats where it can receive adequate sunlight for photosynthesis.
