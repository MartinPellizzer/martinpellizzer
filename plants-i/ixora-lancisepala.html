Ixora lancisepala

Ixora lancisepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in dense, rounded clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape with smooth margins. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora lancisepala is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for landscaping in warm regions.
