Indigofera parvula

Indigofera parvula, commonly known as small indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink to violet flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

Indigofera parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
