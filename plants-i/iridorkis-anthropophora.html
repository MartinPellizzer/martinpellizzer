Iridorkis anthropophora

Iridorkis anthropophora, commonly known as the human-faced orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which features intricate, flower-like structures that resemble human faces. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a subtle iridescence. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
