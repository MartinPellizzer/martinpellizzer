Iris × flexicaulis

Iris × flexicaulis, commonly known as the hybrid bearded iris, is a perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is a cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida, resulting in a variety that exhibits a range of flower colors and forms. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its showy flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, feature three upright petals and three inner sepals that are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Iris × flexicaulis is well-suited for gardens and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
