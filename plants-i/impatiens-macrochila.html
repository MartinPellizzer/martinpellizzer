Impatiens macrochila

Impatiens macrochila, commonly known as the large-flowered impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, elongated spur at the back of the bloom. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens macrochila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
