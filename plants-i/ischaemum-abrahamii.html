Ischaemum abrahamii

Ischaemum abrahamii, commonly known as Abraham's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
