Iresine argentata var. latifolia

Iresine argentata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved iron plant, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features vibrant, velvety leaves that range from deep red to purple, often with a silvery underside, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are not the primary attraction. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its foliage rather than its blooms, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
