Isertia reticulata

Isertia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined isertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and plant collectors.
