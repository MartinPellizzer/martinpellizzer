Ilysanthes macrophylla

Ilysanthes macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf ilysanthes, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include a woody stem and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
