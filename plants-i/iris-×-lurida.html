Iris × lurida

Iris × lurida, commonly known as the western blue flag, is a hybrid perennial plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The showy blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
