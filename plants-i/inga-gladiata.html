Inga gladiata

Inga gladiata, commonly known as the swordleaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Inga gladiata is also used in traditional medicine and can contribute to soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation.
