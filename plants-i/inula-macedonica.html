Inula macedonica

Inula macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian Inula, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features a stem that is usually branched and covered with stiff, hairy leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
