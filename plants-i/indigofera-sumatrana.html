Indigofera sumatrana

Indigofera sumatrana, commonly known as Sumatran indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender stems and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil, and it serves as a habitat for various insects and birds.
