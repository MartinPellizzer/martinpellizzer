Indigofera heterotricha

Indigofera heterotricha, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important plant in sustainable agriculture.
