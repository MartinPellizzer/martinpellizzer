Iridorkis tahitensis

Iridorkis tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian iris, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
