Inga gigantifoliola

Inga gigantifoliola, commonly known as the giant-leaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
