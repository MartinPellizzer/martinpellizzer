Ipomoea luxurians

Ipomoea luxurians, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth and ability to cover structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal flowering.

The tuberous roots of Ipomoea luxurians are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
