Isodon pleiophyllus

Isodon pleiophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and fragrant, lemon-scented leaves. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are widely used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Isodon pleiophyllus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
