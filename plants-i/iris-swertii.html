Iris swertii

Iris swertii, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Iris swertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
