Imperata condensata

Imperata condensata, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant purple to pink flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in panicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Imperata condensata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
