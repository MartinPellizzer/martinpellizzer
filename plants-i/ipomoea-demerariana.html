Ipomoea demerariana

Ipomoea demerariana, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, shady areas. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange capsule that splits open when mature.
