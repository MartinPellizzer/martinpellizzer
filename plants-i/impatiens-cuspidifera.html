Impatiens cuspidifera

Impatiens cuspidifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
