Ipomoea trichocarpa

Ipomoea trichocarpa, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at night. The plant produces showy blue, violet, or white flowers, which are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple stripe. It thrives in warm climates and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The seeds are encased in a spiral-shaped capsule, which is a unique feature of the species.
