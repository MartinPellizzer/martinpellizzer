Isoloma tydaeum

Isoloma tydaeum, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the arid conditions of its native environment.

Isoloma tydaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
