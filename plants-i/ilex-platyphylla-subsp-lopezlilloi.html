Ilex platyphylla subsp. lopezlilloi

Ilex platyphylla subsp.

lopezlilloi, commonly known as the Lopezillo's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the mild, humid climate of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its region.
