Inula phylicoides

Inula phylicoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
