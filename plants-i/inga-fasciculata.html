Inga fasciculata

Inga fasciculata, commonly known as the "brazil nut tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its most notable feature is the capsule fruit that contains the edible Brazil nuts.

The tree plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
