Imperata dinteri

Imperata dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's cogongrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Imperata dinteri is noted for its resilience and ability to form extensive stands, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
