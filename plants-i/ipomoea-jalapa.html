Ipomoea jalapa

Ipomoea jalapa, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports.
