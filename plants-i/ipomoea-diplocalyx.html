Ipomoea diplocalyx

Ipomoea diplocalyx, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and it is known for its rapid growth.

Ipomoea diplocalyx is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
