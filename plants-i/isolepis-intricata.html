Isolepis intricata

Isolepis intricata, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and hairy, grass-like foliage. Native to wetland habitats in temperate regions, it thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes and pond margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
