Isolepis panormitana

Isolepis panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and smooth, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Isolepis panormitana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
