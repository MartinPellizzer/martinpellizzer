Ipomoea tenuirostris subsp. hindeana

Ipomoea tenuirostris subsp.

hindeana, commonly known as the Hinde's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean. It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers typically open in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
