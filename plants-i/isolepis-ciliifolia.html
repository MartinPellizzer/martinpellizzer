Isolepis ciliifolia

Isolepis ciliifolia, commonly known as the tufted spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with flowering spikes that emerge from the base of the plant. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical cluster of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isolepis ciliifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
