Iva frutescens subsp. oraria

Iva frutescens subsp.

oraria, commonly known as coastal ragweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern coast of North America. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and estuaries.

The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellow-green flowers in clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Coastal ragweed is often found in salt marshes and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as wind and salt spray.
