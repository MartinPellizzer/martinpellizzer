Ibidium cernuum

Ibidium cernuum, commonly known as the drooping ibidium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that often droop toward the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.
