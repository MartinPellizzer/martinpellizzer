Isopyrum occidentale

Isopyrum occidentale, commonly known as western marshfoam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, ascending stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Isopyrum occidentale plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
