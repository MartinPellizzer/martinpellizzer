Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. africana

Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. africana, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and blue. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in moist woodlands. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
