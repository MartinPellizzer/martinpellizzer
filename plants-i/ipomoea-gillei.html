Ipomoea gillei

Ipomoea gillei, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in the morning. Ipomoea gillei is known for its ability to climb and twine around supports, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinct capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds.
