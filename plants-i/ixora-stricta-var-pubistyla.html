Ixora stricta var. pubistyla

Ixora stricta var. pubistyla, commonly known as the hairy ixora, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers, which are typically arranged in inflorescences at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The botanical features of Ixora stricta var. pubistyla make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
