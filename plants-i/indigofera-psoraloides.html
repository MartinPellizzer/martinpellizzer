Indigofera psoraloides

Indigofera psoraloides, commonly known as the Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
