Ilex atramentaria

Ilex atramentaria, commonly known as the black ilex, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex atramentaria thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
