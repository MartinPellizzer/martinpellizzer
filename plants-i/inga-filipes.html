Inga filipes

Inga filipes, commonly known as the silky tamarind or silver tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with humid climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, often climbing stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and the fruit is a long, slender pod containing several seeds.

Inga filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.
