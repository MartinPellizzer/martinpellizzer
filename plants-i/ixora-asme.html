Ixora asme

Ixora asme, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture.

Ixora asme is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and horticulture.
