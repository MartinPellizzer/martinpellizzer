Inula confertiflora

Inula confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a robust, branched stem covered in grayish-white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures. The showy yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, giving the plant its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
