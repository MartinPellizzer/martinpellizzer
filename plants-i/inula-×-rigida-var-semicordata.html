Inula × rigida var. semicordata

Inula × rigida var. semicordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, slightly heart-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has a robust root system and is known for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
