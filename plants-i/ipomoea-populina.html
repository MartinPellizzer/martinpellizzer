Ipomoea populina

Ipomoea populina, commonly known as the poplarnut morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The seeds are encased in a fleshy, spiny capsule that matures in the fall.
