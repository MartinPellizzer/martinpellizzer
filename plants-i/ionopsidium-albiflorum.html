Ionopsidium albiflorum

Ionopsidium albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower ironwood, is a species of flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and thick, waxy leaves. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
