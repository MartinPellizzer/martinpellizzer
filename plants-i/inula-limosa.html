Inula limosa

Inula limosa, commonly known as narrowleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in fine hairs, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and form dense, spherical clusters at the top of the stem.

Inula limosa is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
