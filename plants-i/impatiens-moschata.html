Impatiens moschata

Impatiens moschata, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
