Isachne mauritiana var. hispidula

Isachne mauritiana var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy isachne, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are typically clustered at the nodes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Poaceae family, with adaptations suited to its wetland habitat.
