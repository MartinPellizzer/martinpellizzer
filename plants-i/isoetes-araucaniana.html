Isoetes araucaniana

Isoetes araucaniana, commonly known as the Southern Clubmoss, is a species of water plant in the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a distinctive club-like formation. The plant is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are small and often submerged, contributing to the aquatic ecosystem by providing habitat for microorganisms.

Isoetes araucaniana plays a role in water filtration and is an indicator species for the health of freshwater habitats.
