Ipomoea amnicola

Ipomoea amnicola, commonly known as the river morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, shaded environments, thriving in humid climates. Its vines can climb or trail, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Ipomoea amnicola is often found in wetlands and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
