Ipomoea salsettensis

Ipomoea salsettensis, commonly known as the Salsette morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that contains seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
