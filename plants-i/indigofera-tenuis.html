Indigofera tenuis

Indigofera tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, elongated pods containing seeds.

Indigofera tenuis is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.
