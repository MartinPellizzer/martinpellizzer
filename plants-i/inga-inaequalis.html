Inga inaequalis

Inga inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

Inga inaequalis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
