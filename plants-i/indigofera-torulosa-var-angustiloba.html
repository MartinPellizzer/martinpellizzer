Indigofera torulosa var. angustiloba

Indigofera torulosa var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
