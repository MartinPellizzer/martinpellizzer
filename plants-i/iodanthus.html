Iodanthus

Iodanthus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plants typically grow in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or saline soils. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique appearance.
