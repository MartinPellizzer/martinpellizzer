Ipomoea alata

Ipomoea alata, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It produces seed capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Ipomoea alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
