Isochilus crassiflorus

Isochilus crassiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered isochilus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its robust, grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
