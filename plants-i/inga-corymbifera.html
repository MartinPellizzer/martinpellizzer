Inga corymbifera

Inga corymbifera, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Inga corymbifera is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
