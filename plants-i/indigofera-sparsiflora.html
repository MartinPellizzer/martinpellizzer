Indigofera sparsiflora

Indigofera sparsiflora, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to violet, often in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Indigofera sparsiflora is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
