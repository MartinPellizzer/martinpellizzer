Inula vaillantii

Inula vaillantii, commonly known as the African ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Inula vaillantii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
