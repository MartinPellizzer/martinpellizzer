Isoetes variabilis var. viollaei

Isoetes variabilis var. viollaei, commonly known as the violet water clover, is a rare aquatic fern-like plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that resemble the leaves of true ferns, though it belongs to the genus Isoetes, which includes spike-mats. The plant typically grows in shallow, still freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered at the tips of the fronds, aiding in its reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
