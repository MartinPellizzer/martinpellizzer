Iris sanguinea f. stellata

Iris sanguinea f. stellata, commonly known as the star-flowered pale iris, is a striking variant of the pale iris species.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the outer petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or marshy areas. Its leaves are sword-shaped and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
