Indigofera spicata

Indigofera spicata, commonly known as the spicate indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera spicata is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
