Isopogon sphaerocephalus subsp. sphaerocephalus

Isopogon sphaerocephalus subsp.

sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the spherical-headed isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive spherical flower heads.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and landscaping.
