Indigofera angustata

Indigofera angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing small, dark seeds. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.
