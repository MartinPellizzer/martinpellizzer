Indigofera paniculata subsp. paniculata

Indigofera paniculata subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is also valued for its historical use in dye production.
