Ilex guayusa

Ilex guayusa, commonly known as the Amazonian coffee tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries. The plant is notable for its caffeine content, which is higher than that of traditional coffee beans. Ilex guayusa thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its leaves are often used to make a traditional beverage in Ecuador and Peru.
