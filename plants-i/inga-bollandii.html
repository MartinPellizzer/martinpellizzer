Inga bollandii

Inga bollandii, commonly known as the Bolland's rain tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing canopy.

Inga bollandii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various tropical climates.
