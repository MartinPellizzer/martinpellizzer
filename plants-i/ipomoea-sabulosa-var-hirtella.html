Ipomoea sabulosa var. hirtella

Ipomoea sabulosa var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine, hairy textures, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
