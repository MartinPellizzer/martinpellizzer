Indigofera annua

Indigofera annua, commonly known as annual indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces seed pods that contain small, hard seeds used in traditional medicine and as a dye source.

Indigofera annua is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
