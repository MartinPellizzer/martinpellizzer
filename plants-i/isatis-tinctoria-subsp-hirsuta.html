Isatis tinctoria subsp. hirsuta

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy woad, is a subspecies of the woad plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green color.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

This subspecies is less commonly cultivated than the standard woad but is valued for its historical use in dye production.
