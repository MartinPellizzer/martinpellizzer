Ilex domestica var. pubescens

Ilex domestica var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy holly, is a variant of the common holly species characterized by its hairy or pubescent foliage.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows. Its leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds during the winter months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
