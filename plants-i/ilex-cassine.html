Ilex cassine

Ilex cassine, commonly known as the yaupon holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that are edible for birds. The plant thrives in acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Ilex cassine is also notable for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
