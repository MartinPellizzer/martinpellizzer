Iridorkis verticillata

Iridorkis verticillata, commonly known as the spiralleaf iris, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a whorl around the stem, giving it its common name. Its root system helps stabilize soil in shallow water environments.

Iridorkis verticillata is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
