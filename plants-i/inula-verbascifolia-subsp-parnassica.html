Inula verbascifolia subsp. parnassica

Inula verbascifolia subsp.

parnassica, commonly known as Parnassian Inula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with dense, woolly hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal capitulum, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a dense layer of white, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
