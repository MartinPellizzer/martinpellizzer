Isolepis cyperoides

Isolepis cyperoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as bogs and wet meadows, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are usually green or brown, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Isolepis cyperoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and remediate contaminated soils.
