Iochroma tingoanum

Iochroma tingoanum, commonly known as the Tingoanum bellflower, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru, and is often found in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly velvety texture.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
