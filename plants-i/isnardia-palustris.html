Isnardia palustris

Isnardia palustris, commonly known as the marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are hairy and erect.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
