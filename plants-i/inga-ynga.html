Inga ynga

Inga ynga, commonly known as the "wild cacao" or "chocolate tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are pollinated by specific bees, a unique ecological relationship. Its fruit, resembling a bean pod, contains edible seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of cocoa.

The plant's rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable in agroforestry systems.
