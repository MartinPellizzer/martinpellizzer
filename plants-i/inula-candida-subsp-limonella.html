Inula candida subsp. limonella

Inula candida subsp.

limonella, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The subspecies limonella is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and more compact growth habit compared to the main species.
