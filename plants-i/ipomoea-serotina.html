Ipomoea serotina

Ipomoea serotina, commonly known as the late morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the late morning to early afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in a bright orange, fleshy capsule that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea serotina is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
