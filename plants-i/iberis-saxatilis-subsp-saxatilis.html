Iberis saxatilis subsp. saxatilis

Iberis saxatilis subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
