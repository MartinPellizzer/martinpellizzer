Isolepis paniculata var. australis

Isolepis paniculata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized root systems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
