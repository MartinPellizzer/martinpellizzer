Illicium szechuanense

Illicium szechuanense, commonly known as the Sichuan star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, fragrant berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.
