Impatiens puberula

Impatiens puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its showy, spurred flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Impatiens puberula is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
