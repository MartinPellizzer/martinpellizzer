Iva axillaris subsp. robustior

Iva axillaris subsp.

robustior, commonly known as the robust ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, swales, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
