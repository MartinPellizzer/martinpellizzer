Ipomoea peduncularis

Ipomoea peduncularis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, alternate leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types.
