Ipomoea browniana

Ipomoea browniana, commonly known as the Brown's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southern United States.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and a climbing stem with tendrils that aid in its growth.
