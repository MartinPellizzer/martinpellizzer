Impatiens ballardii

Impatiens ballardii, commonly known as the Florida impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically red or pink with contrasting yellow markings. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Impatiens ballardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
