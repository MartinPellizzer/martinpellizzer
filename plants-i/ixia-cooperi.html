Ixia cooperi

Ixia cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes. Ixia cooperi prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in mild climates.
