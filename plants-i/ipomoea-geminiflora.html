Ipomoea geminiflora

Ipomoea geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flower morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along stream banks or in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, climbing plants.
