Inga polita

Inga polita, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. Inga polita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
