Illigera rhodantha

Illigera rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered illigera, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
