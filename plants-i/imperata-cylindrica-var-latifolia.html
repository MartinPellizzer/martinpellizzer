Imperata cylindrica var. latifolia

Imperata cylindrica var. latifolia, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of pink to purple inflorescences, which are highly visible and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and spreading habit, it can become invasive in some environments.
