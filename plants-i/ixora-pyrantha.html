Ixora pyrantha

Ixora pyrantha, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ixora pyrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
