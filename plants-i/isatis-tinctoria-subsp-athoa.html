Isatis tinctoria subsp. athoa

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

athoa, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its distinctive leaves with serrated edges.

The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cabbages and mustards. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

The species is notable for its historical use in dye production and as a potential medicinal plant.
