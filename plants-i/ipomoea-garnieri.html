Ipomoea garnieri

Ipomoea garnieri, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to bright red, often blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in a fibrous, bumpy capsule that splits open when mature.
