Icacina poeppigiana

Icacina poeppigiana, commonly known as the yellow-leafed acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is notable for its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Icacina poeppigiana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
