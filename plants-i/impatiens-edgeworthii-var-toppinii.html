Impatiens edgeworthii var. toppinii

Impatiens edgeworthii var. toppinii, commonly known as the Toppin's Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and vibrant, often bi-colored flowers that range from pink to white. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
