Ixophorus glaucus

Ixophorus glaucus, commonly known as the bluegrass sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Ixophorus glaucus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
