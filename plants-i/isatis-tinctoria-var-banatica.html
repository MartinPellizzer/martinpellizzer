Isatis tinctoria var. banatica

Isatis tinctoria var. banatica, commonly known as Banat's woad, is a variant of the woad plant native to Southeast Europe.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a blue dye from its leaves, historically used in textile production. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both traditional dyeing practices and ecological studies.
