Ilex tolucana var. bourgaeavi

Ilex tolucana var. bourgaeavi, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to provide year-round visual interest in gardens.
