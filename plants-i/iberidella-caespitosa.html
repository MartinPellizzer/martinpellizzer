Iberidella caespitosa

Iberidella caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, usually blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, forming dense, compact clusters.

Iberidella caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
