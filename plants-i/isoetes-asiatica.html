Isoetes asiatica

Isoetes asiatica, commonly known as the Asian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. The plant has a unique structure with a sporangium-bearing stalk that emerges from the water, allowing for spore dispersal. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often in marshes or ponds.

Isoetes asiatica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
