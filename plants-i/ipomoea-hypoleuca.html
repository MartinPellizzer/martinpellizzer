Ipomoea hypoleuca

Ipomoea hypoleuca, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale lavender flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea hypoleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
