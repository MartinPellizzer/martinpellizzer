Ixia columellaris var. rhodolarynx

Ixia columellaris var. rhodolarynx, commonly known as the red-eyed ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers with a distinctive red eye-like spot at the center, which is a notable botanical feature. The plant typically grows in well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are strap-shaped and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
