Ilex aquifolium var. monstrosa

Ilex aquifolium var. monstrosa, commonly known as the variegated holly, is a cultivar of the common holly, characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a combination of green, white, and yellow markings, creating an ornamental appearance. The plant is evergreen, with spiny leaves that provide year-round visual interest. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in optimal conditions.

The variegation is due to genetic mutations, making it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural displays.
