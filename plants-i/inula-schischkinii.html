Inula schischkinii

Inula schischkinii, commonly known as the Siberian Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Inula schischkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
