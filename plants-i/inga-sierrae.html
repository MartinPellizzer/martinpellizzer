Inga sierrae

Inga sierrae, commonly known as the Sierra inga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

The fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds, contributing to its role in forest ecosystems.
