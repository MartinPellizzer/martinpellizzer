Iris spuria var. subbarbata

Iris spuria var. subbarbata, commonly known as the subbarbata iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
