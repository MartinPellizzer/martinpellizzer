Ipomoea ovalifolia var. tomentosa

Ipomoea ovalifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving it the common name "morning glory.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
