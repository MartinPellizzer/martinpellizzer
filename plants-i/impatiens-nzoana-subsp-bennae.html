Impatiens nzoana subsp. bennae

Impatiens nzoana subsp.

bennae, commonly known as the East African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Africa. It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and greenhouses.
