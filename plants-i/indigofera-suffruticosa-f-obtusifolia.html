Indigofera suffruticosa f. obtusifolia

Indigofera suffruticosa f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.

The foliage and flowers are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
