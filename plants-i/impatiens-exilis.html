Impatiens exilis

Impatiens exilis, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features delicate, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a spurred shape. The plant has thin, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
