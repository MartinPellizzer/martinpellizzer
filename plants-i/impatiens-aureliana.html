Impatiens aureliana

Impatiens aureliana, commonly known as the golden touch impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their striking color contrast against the dark green foliage.

Impatiens aureliana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
