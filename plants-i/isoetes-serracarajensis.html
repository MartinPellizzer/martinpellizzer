Isoetes serracarajensis

Isoetes serracarajensis, commonly known as the Carajás watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spiky, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found in clusters along its segmented stems. This plant is native to Brazil, specifically the state of Pará, where it thrives in freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its morphology includes a distinct, segmented morphology with a central axis and leaf-like structures called stigmaria.

Isoetes serracarajensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
