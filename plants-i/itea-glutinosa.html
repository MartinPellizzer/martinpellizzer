Itea glutinosa

Itea glutinosa, commonly known as sticky ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with 9 to 13 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets that are sticky to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers in late spring to early summer. Its showy, pinkish-bracted inflorescences add ornamental value to gardens.

Itea glutinosa is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
