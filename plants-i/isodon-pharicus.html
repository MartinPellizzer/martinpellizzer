Isodon pharicus

Isodon pharicus, commonly known as the Chinese skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often found in moist environments such as meadows and stream sides.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
