Impatiens cruciata

Impatiens cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest understories. It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
