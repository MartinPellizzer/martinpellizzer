Ixia pendula

Ixia pendula, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that hang from long, slender stems. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Ixia pendula thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.
