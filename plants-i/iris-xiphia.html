Iris xiphia

Iris xiphia, commonly known as the swordleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers with a distinctive dark violet throat. The leaves are narrow and sword-shaped, giving the plant its common name, and they are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland landscapes.
