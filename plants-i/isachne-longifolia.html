Isachne longifolia

Isachne longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved isachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicles, producing small, two-ranked flowers.

Isachne longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
