Ischyrolepis saxatilis

Ischyrolepis saxatilis, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in rocky, arid environments.

It is a composite organism formed by a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium. This lichen is typically found on exposed rock surfaces, where it plays a role in soil formation and nutrient cycling. Its structure consists of a thallus that is often crust-like or foliose in form.

Ischyrolepis saxatilis is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it an important indicator species for environmental stability.
