Inga canonegrensis

Inga canonegrensis, commonly known as the white inga, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its role in agroforestry systems due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
