Impatiens congolensis var. densifolia

Impatiens congolensis var. densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are dense, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of pubescence, contributing to its common name. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
