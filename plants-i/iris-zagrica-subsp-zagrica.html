Iris zagrica subsp. zagrica

Iris zagrica subsp.

zagrica, commonly known as the Zagreb iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other iris species.
