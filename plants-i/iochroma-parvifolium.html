Iochroma parvifolium

Iochroma parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf bellflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Iochroma parvifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
