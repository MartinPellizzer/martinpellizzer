Iberis saxatilis var. thymbrifolia

Iberis saxatilis var. thymbrifolia, commonly known as rock candyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has pinnately divided leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, arid environments.

Its name "thymbrifolia" refers to the aromatic, thyme-like scent of its foliage.
