Inga pitmanii

Inga pitmanii, commonly known as Pitman's inga, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

Inga pitmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and ecological benefits.
