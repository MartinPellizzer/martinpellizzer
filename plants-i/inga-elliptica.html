Inga elliptica

Inga elliptica, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in humid lowland forests. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The tree also plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
