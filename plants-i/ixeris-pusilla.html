Ixeris pusilla

Ixeris pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and specialized reproductive structures.
