Ilex beadlei var. laevis

Ilex beadlei var. laevis, commonly known as the smoothleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are smooth and glossy on both sides, with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It has a compact, rounded growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The variety is distinguished by its smooth leaf surface, setting it apart from other holly species.
