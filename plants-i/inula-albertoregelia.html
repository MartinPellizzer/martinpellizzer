Inula albertoregelia

Inula albertoregelia, commonly known as the Albert Regel's elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features rough, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The flower heads are yellow and surrounded by pale green bracts, forming a composite structure typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
