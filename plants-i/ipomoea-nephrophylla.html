Ipomoea nephrophylla

Ipomoea nephrophylla, commonly known as the kidneyleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, smooth seeds.

Ipomoea nephrophylla is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
