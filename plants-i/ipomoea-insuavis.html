Ipomoea insuavis

Ipomoea insuavis, commonly known as the bitter nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
