Ilex aquifolium var. camelliifolia

Ilex aquifolium var. camelliifolia, commonly known as the Camellia-leaved Holly, is a variant of the common holly that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that resemble those of camellias, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique leaf morphology.
