Ipomoea lapathifolia subsp. lapathifolia

Ipomoea lapathifolia subsp.

lapathifolia, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped white or pale pink flowers that open in the morning.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oblong seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
