Illicium pachyphyllum

Illicium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved star anise, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit, resembling a star anise, contains aromatic seeds used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Illicium pachyphyllum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique botanical features and economic importance.
