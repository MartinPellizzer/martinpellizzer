Ipomoea asarifolia

Ipomoea asarifolia, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ipomoea asarifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
