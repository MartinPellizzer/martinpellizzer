Ipomoea nigricans

Ipomoea nigricans, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea nigricans is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other nightshade relatives.
