Impatiens pseudoviola var. alba

Impatiens pseudoviola var. alba, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
