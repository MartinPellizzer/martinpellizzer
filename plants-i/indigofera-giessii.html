Indigofera giessii

Indigofera giessii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
