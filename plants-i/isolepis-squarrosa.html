Isolepis squarrosa

Isolepis squarrosa, commonly known as the silky bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into dark, cylindrical seed heads. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Isolepis squarrosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
