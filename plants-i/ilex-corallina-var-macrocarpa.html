Ilex corallina var. macrocarpa

Ilex corallina var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
