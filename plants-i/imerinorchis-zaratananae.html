Imerinorchis zaratananae

Imerinorchis zaratananae, commonly known as the Zaratanana orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small but highly ornate, with specialized pollination mechanisms adapted to specific insect pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
