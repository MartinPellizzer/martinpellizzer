Ixora pubifolia

Ixora pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright orange or red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora pubifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
