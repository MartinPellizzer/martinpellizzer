Indigofera lyallii subsp. nyassica

Indigofera lyallii subsp.

nyassica, commonly known as the Malawi indigo, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa, particularly Tanzania and Malawi.

The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
