Inula leptoclada

Inula leptoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inula leptoclada is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
