Ixora breviflora

Ixora breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange-red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Ixora breviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture.
