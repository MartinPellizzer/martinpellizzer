Isolepis curvata

Isolepis curvata, commonly known as the curved spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Isolepis curvata thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
