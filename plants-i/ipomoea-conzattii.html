Ipomoea conzattii

Ipomoea conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.

Ipomoea conzattii is a climbing vine that can reach heights of several meters, making it a striking addition to gardens or natural habitats.
