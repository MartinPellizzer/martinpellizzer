Iris × pseudocristata

Iris × pseudocristata, commonly known as the crested iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its distinctive crested or ruffled petals, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces showy blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and botanical studies.
