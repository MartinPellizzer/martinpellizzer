Ixia bolusii

Ixia bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its blooms, which can be pink, red, or orange, are often clustered in racemes.

Ixia bolusii is a short-lived perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
