Isotoma rhytidosperma

Isotoma rhytidosperma, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Isotoma rhytidosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
