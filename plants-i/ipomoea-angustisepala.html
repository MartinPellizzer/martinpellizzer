Ipomoea angustisepala

Ipomoea angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea angustisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
