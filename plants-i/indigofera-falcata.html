Indigofera falcata

Indigofera falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying shades of pink to purple, and it produces cylindrical, woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

Indigofera falcata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
