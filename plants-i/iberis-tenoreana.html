Iberis tenoreana

Iberis tenoreana, commonly known as the Sicilian rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
