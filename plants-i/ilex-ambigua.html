Ilex ambigua

Ilex ambigua, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds.

Ilex ambigua is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
