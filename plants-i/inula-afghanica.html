Inula afghanica

Inula afghanica, commonly known as Afghan Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan, Pakistan, and parts of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system adapted to survive in harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is often hairy and can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Inula afghanica is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
