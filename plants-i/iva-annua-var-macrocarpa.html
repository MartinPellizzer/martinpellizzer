Iva annua var. macrocarpa

Iva annua var. macrocarpa, commonly known as giant sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can grow up to several feet tall. It thrives in moist soil and full sun, often found along stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
