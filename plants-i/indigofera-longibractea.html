Indigofera longibractea

Indigofera longibractea, commonly known as the long bract indigofera, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
