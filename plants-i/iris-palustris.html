Iris palustris

Iris palustris, commonly known as the swamp iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Iris palustris is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
