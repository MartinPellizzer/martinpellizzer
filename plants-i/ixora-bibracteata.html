Ixora bibracteata

Ixora bibracteata, commonly known as the red flame ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially in warm climates. Its inflorescence consists of cymes with showy bracts that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Ixora bibracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
