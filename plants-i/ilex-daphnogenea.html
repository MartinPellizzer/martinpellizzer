Ilex daphnogenea

Ilex daphnogenea, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Ilex daphnogenea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
