Ixeridium gracile var. gracile

Ixeridium gracile var. gracile, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by distinctive, bristly seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

It has smooth, hairy, or glandular stems and leaves, which vary depending on the growth conditions.
