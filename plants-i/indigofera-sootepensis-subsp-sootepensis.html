Indigofera sootepensis subsp. sootepensis

Indigofera sootepensis subsp.

sootepensis, commonly known as Sootep indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
