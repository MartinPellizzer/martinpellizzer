Isolepis bispicata

Isolepis bispicata, commonly known as the two-awned spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears two distinct awns on each spikelet. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous sheath at the base.

Isolepis bispicata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
