Iris thianschanica

Iris thianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, typical of the iris genus. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
