Iberis microcarpa

Iberis microcarpa, commonly known as the rock candy plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compact, evergreen foliage with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often prostrate or spreading. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
