Ipomoea praecox

Ipomoea praecox, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue, purple, or white blooms. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length.

Ipomoea praecox is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
