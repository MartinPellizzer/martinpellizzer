Iberis cinerea

Iberis cinerea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply divided and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Iberis cinerea is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
