Inula britannica var. uniflora

Inula britannica var. uniflora, commonly known as the British elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, daisy-like yellow flower heads. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic, and slightly bitter odor, particularly when the leaves are crushed. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and other open habitats across the UK.
