Iris laevigata

Iris laevigata, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Iris laevigata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Japanese culture.
