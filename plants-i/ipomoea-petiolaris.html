Ipomoea petiolaris

Ipomoea petiolaris, commonly known as the trailing morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for use as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

Ipomoea petiolaris is also known for its ability to climb and twine around supports, adding ornamental value to gardens.
