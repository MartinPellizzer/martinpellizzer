Iresine alternifolia var. texana

Iresine alternifolia var. texana, commonly known as redroot ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, velvety red stems and leaves with a distinctive, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
