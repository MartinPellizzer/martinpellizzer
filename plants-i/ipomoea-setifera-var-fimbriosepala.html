Ipomoea setifera var. fimbriosepala

Ipomoea setifera var. fimbriosepala, commonly known as the fringed-sepal morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant produces distinctive, fringed sepals that give it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
