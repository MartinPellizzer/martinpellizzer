Iris dahurica

Iris dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, hexagonal flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Iris dahurica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in early summer.
