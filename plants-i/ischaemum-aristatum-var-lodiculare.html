Ischaemum aristatum var. lodiculare

Ischaemum aristatum var. lodiculare, commonly known as the slender barnyard grass, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in small, dry fruits called caryopses, which are dispersed by wind.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
