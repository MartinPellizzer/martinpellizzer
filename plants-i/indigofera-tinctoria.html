Indigofera tinctoria

Indigofera tinctoria, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue flowers. The plant is notable for its use in producing natural blue dye, historically important in textile industries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and dyeing properties.

Its root system also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.
