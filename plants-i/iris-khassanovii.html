Iris khassanovii

Iris khassanovii, commonly known as the Khassanov's iris, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris khassanovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
