Iresine diffusa f. lindenii

Iresine diffusa f. lindenii, commonly known as Crimson Rosemallow, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red or pink bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle contrast to the showy bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Its foliage is often variegated, with a mix of green and reddish tones, enhancing its ornamental value in gardens.
