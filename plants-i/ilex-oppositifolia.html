Ilex oppositifolia

Ilex oppositifolia, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. Ilex oppositifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and year-round visual appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
