Inula crithmifolia

Inula crithmifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple ray flowers and silver-gray, fuzzy leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inula crithmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
