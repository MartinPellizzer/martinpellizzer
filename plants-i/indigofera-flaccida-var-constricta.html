Indigofera flaccida var. constricta

Indigofera flaccida var. constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf indigo.

It is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow leaflets. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, flat pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
