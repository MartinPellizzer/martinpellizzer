Inga similis

Inga similis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

The tree's ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
