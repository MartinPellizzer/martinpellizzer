Iris × chrysolopha

Iris × chrysolopha, commonly known as the golden iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are dark green and sturdy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
