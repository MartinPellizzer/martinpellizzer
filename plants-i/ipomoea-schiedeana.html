Ipomoea schiedeana

Ipomoea schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and are known for their fragrant scent.

Ipomoea schiedeana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
