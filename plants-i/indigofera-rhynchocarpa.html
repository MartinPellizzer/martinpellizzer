Indigofera rhynchocarpa

Indigofera rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open woodlands and savannas. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for medicinal purposes.
