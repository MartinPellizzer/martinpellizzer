Indigofera coccinea

Indigofera coccinea, commonly known as the red bignay, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators. The plant produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are also utilized in local cuisines and as a source of dye.

Indigofera coccinea thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
