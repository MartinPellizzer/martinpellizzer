Ida labelloviridis

Ida labelloviridis, commonly known as the green label tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy flowers.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features compound leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe containing a single seed.

Ida labelloviridis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
