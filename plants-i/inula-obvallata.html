Inula obvallata

Inula obvallata, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Inula obvallata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
