Isatis karjaginii

Isatis karjaginii, commonly known as the Karjagin woad, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Isatis karjaginii is primarily valued for its historical use in dye production, similar to other members of its genus.
