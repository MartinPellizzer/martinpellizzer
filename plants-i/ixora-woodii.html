Ixora woodii

Ixora woodii, commonly known as the golden flame or wood's ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from orange to red, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora woodii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
