Ixia caryophyllacea

Ixia caryophyllacea, commonly known as the fire lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which resemble flames, and its striking, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its showy blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn.

Ixia caryophyllacea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its bold and colorful appearance.
