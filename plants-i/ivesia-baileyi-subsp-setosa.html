Ivesia baileyi subsp. setosa

Ivesia baileyi subsp.

setosa, commonly known as the Bailey's spiny flax, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Ivesia baileyi subsp.

setosa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
