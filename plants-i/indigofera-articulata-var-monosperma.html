Indigofera articulata var. monosperma

Indigofera articulata var. monosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain a single seed, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal name "monosperma.". It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms depending on the climate.
