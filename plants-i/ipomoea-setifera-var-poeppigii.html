Ipomoea setifera var. poeppigii

Ipomoea setifera var. poeppigii, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural displays.
