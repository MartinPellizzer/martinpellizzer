Impatiens substerilis var. falciformis

Impatiens substerilis var. falciformis, commonly known as the sickleleaf touch-me-not, is a variant of the impatiens genus characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves.

It is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding make it a common sight in tropical gardens.

However, due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in some regions.
