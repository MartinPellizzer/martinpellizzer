Ipomoea pringsheimiana

Ipomoea pringsheimiana, commonly known as the Pringsheim's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant blooms primarily during the day, showcasing its showy flowers in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea pringsheimiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and botanical collections.
