Impatiens teneriflora

Impatiens teneriflora, commonly known as the Canary Islands impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with a tropical appearance.
