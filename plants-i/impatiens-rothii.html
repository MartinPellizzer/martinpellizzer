Impatiens rothii

Impatiens rothii, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens rothii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
