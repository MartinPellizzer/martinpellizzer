Ipomoea tuboides f. digitata

Ipomoea tuboides f. digitata, commonly known as the digitate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
