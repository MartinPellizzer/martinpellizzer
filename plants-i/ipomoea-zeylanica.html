Ipomoea zeylanica

Ipomoea zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea zeylanica is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a common ornamental and sometimes invasive species.
