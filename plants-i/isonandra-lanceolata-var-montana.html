Isonandra lanceolata var. montana

Isonandra lanceolata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain isonandra, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a tendency to form dense mats or low shrubs.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
