Ipomoea plummerae var. cupulata

Ipomoea plummerae var. cupulata, commonly known as the cupulate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
