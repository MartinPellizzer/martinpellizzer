Indocalamus hispidus

Indocalamus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough, textured surface.

Indocalamus hispidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
