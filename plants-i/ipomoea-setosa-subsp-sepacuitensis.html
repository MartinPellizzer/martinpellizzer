Ipomoea setosa subsp. sepacuitensis

Ipomoea setosa subsp.

sepacuitensis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
