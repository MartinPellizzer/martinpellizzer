Isodon inflexus f. leucanthus

Isodon inflexus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered isodon, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China and India, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
