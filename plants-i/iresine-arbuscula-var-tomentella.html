Iresine arbuscula var. tomentella

Iresine arbuscula var. tomentella, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly leaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, dense hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
