Ipomoea echioides var. villosula

Ipomoea echioides var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant.
