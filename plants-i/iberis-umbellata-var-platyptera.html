Iberis umbellata var. platyptera

Iberis umbellata var. platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leaf candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The leaves are broadly ovate to round, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
