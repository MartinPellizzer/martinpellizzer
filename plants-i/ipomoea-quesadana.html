Ipomoea quesadana

Ipomoea quesadana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Ipomoea quesadana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some regions.
