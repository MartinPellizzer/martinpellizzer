Ipomoea reflexisepala

Ipomoea reflexisepala, commonly known as the reflexed sepal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, a trait that gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
