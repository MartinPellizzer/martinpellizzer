Ischaemum polystachyum var. hillii

Ischaemum polystachyum var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's panicgrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces inflorescences composed of dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studies on plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
