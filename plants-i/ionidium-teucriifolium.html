Ionidium teucriifolium

Ionidium teucriifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery-blue down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue to lavender in color, blooming from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Ionidium teucriifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
