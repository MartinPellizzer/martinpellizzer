Iris acutiloba var. demawendica

Iris acutiloba var. demawendica, commonly known as the Demawenda iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

This variant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate flower structures with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The Demawenda iris is valued for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
