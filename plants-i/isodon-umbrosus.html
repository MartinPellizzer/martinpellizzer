Isodon umbrosus

Isodon umbrosus, commonly known as the dark isodon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Isodon umbrosus thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
