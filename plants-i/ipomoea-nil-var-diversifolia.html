Ipomoea nil var. diversifolia

Ipomoea nil var. diversifolia, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale green veins, giving it a unique, lace-like appearance. The plant produces trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from purple to blue, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
