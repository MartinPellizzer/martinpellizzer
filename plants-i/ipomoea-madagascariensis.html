Ipomoea madagascariensis

Ipomoea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are twining and can reach lengths of several meters.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain environments.
