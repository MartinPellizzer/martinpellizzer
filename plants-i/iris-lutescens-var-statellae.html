Iris lutescens var. statellae

Iris lutescens var. statellae, commonly known as the Statellae yellow iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive blackish-purple throat, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the base. Its rhizomatous root system helps it thrive in moist, clay-rich soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in water gardens or bog habitats.
