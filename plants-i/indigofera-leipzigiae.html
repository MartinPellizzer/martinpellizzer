Indigofera leipzigiae

Indigofera leipzigiae, commonly known as the Leipzig indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnate, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
