Indigofera vicioides subsp. excelsa

Indigofera vicioides subsp.

excelsa, commonly known as the tall indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
