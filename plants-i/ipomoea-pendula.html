Ipomoea pendula

Ipomoea pendula, commonly known as the trailing morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length, making it an excellent climber. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dark seeds.
