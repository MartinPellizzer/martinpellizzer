Impatiens vellela

Impatiens vellela, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens vellela is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
