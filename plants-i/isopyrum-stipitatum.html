Isopyrum stipitatum

Isopyrum stipitatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.
