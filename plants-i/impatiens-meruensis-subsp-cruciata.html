Impatiens meruensis subsp. cruciata

Impatiens meruensis subsp.

cruciata, commonly known as the African violet or more specifically the "crossed" impatiens, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
