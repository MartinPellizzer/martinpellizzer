Iris × tatianae

Iris × tatianae, commonly known as the Russian iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris germanica.

It is a rhizomatous perennial that produces upright, sword-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers that have three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across temperate regions.
