Impatiens reticulata

Impatiens reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
