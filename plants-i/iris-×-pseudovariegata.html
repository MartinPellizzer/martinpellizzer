Iris × pseudovariegata

Iris × pseudovariegata, commonly known as the variegated iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white or yellow markings on otherwise green leaves. The plant produces showy, upright flower spikes with purple or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and hardiness in temperate climates.
