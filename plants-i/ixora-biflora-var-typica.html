Ixora biflora var. typica

Ixora biflora var. typica, commonly known as the twin-flowered ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ixora biflora var. typica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
