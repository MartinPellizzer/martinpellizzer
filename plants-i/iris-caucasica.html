Iris caucasica

Iris caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems and are known for their ornamental value in gardens.
