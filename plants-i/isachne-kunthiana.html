Isachne kunthiana

Isachne kunthiana, commonly known as the broadleaf grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in a loose panicle.

Isachne kunthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
