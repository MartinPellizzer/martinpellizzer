Ipomoea cairica var. uniflora

Ipomoea cairica var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and large, showy flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. This variety is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
