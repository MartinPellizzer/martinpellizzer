Inga clavigera

Inga clavigera, commonly known as the "coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of shade in coffee plantations.
