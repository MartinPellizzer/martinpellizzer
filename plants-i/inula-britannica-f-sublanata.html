Inula britannica f. sublanata

Inula britannica f. sublanata, commonly known as the woolly elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in a fine layer of hairs, contributing to its overall textured look.

This subspecies is often found in dry grasslands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
