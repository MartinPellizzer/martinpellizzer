Iris subbarbata

Iris subbarbata, commonly known as the Himalayan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

Iris subbarbata is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to flourish in cooler climates.
