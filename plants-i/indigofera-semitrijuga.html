Indigofera semitrijuga

Indigofera semitrijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Indigofera semitrijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
