Inga torta

Inga torta, commonly known as the "chocolate tree," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its edible pods, which contain a sweet, sticky pulp and edible seeds. Inga torta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful species for reforestation and soil enrichment.
