Indigofera shirensis

Indigofera shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable agriculture.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
