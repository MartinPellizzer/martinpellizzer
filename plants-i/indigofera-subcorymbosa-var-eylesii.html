Indigofera subcorymbosa var. eylesii

Indigofera subcorymbosa var. eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' indigo, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and sandy conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for native wildlife.
