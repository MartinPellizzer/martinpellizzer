Impatiens obcordifolia

Impatiens obcordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom throughout the rainy season and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
