Ionidium bicolor var. campestre

Ionidium bicolor var. campestre, commonly known as the yellow and white dandelion, is a variant of the common dandelion characterized by its distinctive bi-colored flowers.

The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of North America. It features a basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The flowers are composite, consisting of a yellow disk surrounded by white ray florets, which bloom in early spring.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
