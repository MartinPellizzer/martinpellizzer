Indigofera glutinosa

Indigofera glutinosa, commonly known as sticky indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance, which aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
