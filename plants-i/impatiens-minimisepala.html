Impatiens minimisepala

Impatiens minimisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, with a narrow sepal that distinguishes it from other impatiens species.
