Isoetes muelleri

Isoetes muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's water moss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, leaf-like structures called strobila, which are used for reproduction. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. Isoetes muelleri has a long, slender rhizome that anchors it to the substrate and supports its growth.
