Isolepis ascolepis

Isolepis ascolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and pond margins. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.

Isolepis ascolepis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
