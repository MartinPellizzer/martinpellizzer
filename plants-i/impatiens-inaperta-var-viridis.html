Impatiens inaperta var. viridis

Impatiens inaperta var. viridis, commonly known as the green impatiens, is a variant of the species Impatiens inaperta, native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth margin, and the stems are slender and often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
