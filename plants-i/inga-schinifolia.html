Inga schinifolia

Inga schinifolia, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
