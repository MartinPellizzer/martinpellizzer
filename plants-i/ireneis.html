Ireneis

Ireneis, commonly known as the irises, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Iridaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often colorful flowers that typically have three petals and three sepals. Ireneis species are native to temperate and subtropical regions, particularly in the Northern Hemisphere. They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular garden plants.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening and have cultural significance in various traditions.
