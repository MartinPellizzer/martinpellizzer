Ixeris taiwaniana

Ixeris taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan crested dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are surrounded by a distinctive, fluffy pappus that aids in seed dispersal.

Ixeris taiwaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
