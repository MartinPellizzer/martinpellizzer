Inula gombczewskyi

Inula gombczewskyi, commonly known as the Tibetan fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Inula gombczewskyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
