Impatiens lachnosperma

Impatiens lachnosperma, commonly known as the hairy wood rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens lachnosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
