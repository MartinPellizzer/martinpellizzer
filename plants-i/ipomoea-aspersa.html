Ipomoea aspersa

Ipomoea aspersa, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas but has been naturalized in many parts of the world. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Ipomoea aspersa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
