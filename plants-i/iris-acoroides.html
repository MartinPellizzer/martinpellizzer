Iris acoroides

Iris acoroides, commonly known as the African iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to October. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
