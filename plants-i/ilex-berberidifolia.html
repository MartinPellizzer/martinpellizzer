Ilex berberidifolia

Ilex berberidifolia, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in shaded gardens.
