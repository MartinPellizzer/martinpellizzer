Isopogon tripartitus

Isopogon tripartitus, commonly known as the three-part banksia, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cone-like inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. Isopogon tripartitus is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
