Ipomoea enigmatica

Ipomoea enigmatica, commonly known as the enigmatic morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright orange. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea enigmatica is also notable for its short blooming period, typically opening in the early morning and closing by midday.
