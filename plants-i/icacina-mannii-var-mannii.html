Icacina mannii var. mannii

Icacina mannii var. mannii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
