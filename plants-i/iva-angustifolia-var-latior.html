Iva angustifolia var. latior

Iva angustifolia var. latior, commonly known as broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in panicle-like clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can be quite broad, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting various wildlife species.
