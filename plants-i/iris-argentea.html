Iris argentea

Iris argentea, commonly known as the silver iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a white or pale blue center. The leaves are sword-shaped, broad, and often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
