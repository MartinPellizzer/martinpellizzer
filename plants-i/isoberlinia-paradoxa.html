Isoberlinia paradoxa

Isoberlinia paradoxa, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow or green pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna ecosystems.

Isoberlinia paradoxa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
