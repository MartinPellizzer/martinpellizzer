Iris unguicularis f. marginata

Iris unguicularis f. marginata, commonly known as the Margined Marsh Iris, is a rare and striking variant of the marsh iris.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale blue flowers with a prominent purple stripe along the petal edges. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate water conditions.
