Impatiens stocksii

Impatiens stocksii, commonly known as the Sierra Leone impatiens, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, vibrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
