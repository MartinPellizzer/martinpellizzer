Indigofera baukeana

Indigofera baukeana, commonly known as the Bauke indigo, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
