Indigofera conjugata var. trimorphophylla

Indigofera conjugata var. trimorphophylla, commonly known as the three-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
