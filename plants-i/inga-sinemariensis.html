Inga sinemariensis

Inga sinemariensis, commonly known as the Guianan inga, is a species of legume native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in humid lowland forests. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals or through wind.

Inga sinemariensis plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
