Inga micheliana

Inga micheliana, commonly known as the pink-tinged inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.

Inga micheliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
