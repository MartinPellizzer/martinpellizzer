Inula cinerea

Inula cinerea, commonly known as gray inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them a silvery appearance.

Inula cinerea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
