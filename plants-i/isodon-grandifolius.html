Isodon grandifolius

Isodon grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved verbena, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Isodon grandifolius is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
