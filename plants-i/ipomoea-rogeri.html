Ipomoea rogeri

Ipomoea rogeri, commonly known as the Roger's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy, spherical capsule that splits open when mature.

Ipomoea rogeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
