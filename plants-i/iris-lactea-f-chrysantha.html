Iris lactea f. chrysantha

Iris lactea f. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a hybrid form of the white flag iris, characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It is a perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas, typically found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers bloom in early summer, forming tall, showy clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
