Illipe crassipes

Illipe crassipes, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical fruits containing a sticky, white pulp. Its seeds are encased in a hard shell and are the source of shea butter, a valuable commodity in cosmetics and food industries.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
