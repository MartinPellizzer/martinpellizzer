Isatis apiculata

Isatis apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Isatis apiculata is also known for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more famous relative, the common woad.
