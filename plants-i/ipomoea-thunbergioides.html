Ipomoea thunbergioides

Ipomoea thunbergioides, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct hairy stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea thunbergioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and rapid growth habit.
