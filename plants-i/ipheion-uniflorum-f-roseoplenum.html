Ipheion uniflorum f. roseoplenum

Ipheion uniflorum f. roseoplenum, commonly known as the roseum lily or pink buttercup grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its pink to rose-colored blooms, which contrast with its dark green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The inflorescence is typically borne on short, upright stems, adding a delicate and ornamental touch to the landscape.
