Indigofera blanchetiana

Indigofera blanchetiana, commonly known as the blue pea or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
