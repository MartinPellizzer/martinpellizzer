Inula candida var. rotundifolia

Inula candida var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
