Isatis apscheronica

Isatis apscheronica, commonly known as the Apscheronic woad, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Isatis apscheronica is valued for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more famous relative, the common woad.
