Ipomoea venosa subsp. venosa

Ipomoea venosa subsp.

venosa, commonly known as the red veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive red veins on broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, opening in the morning and closing at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The vine can grow up to several meters in length, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden displays.
