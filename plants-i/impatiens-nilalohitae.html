Impatiens nilalohitae

Impatiens nilalohitae, commonly known as the Hawaiian flame flower, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Oahu in Hawaii.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Impatiens nilalohitae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and other threats, it is considered a vulnerable species and is protected under conservation efforts.
