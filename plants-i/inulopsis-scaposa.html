Inulopsis scaposa

Inulopsis scaposa, commonly known as the scapose inula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat head, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
