Ipomoea acetosifolia

Ipomoea acetosifolia, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as fences or trellises. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
