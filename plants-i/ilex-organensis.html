Ilex organensis

Ilex organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy dark green surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Ilex organensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
