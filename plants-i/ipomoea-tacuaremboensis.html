Ipomoea tacuaremboensis

Ipomoea tacuaremboensis, commonly known as the Tacuarembo morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces strikingly large, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like morning glories and ipomoea. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea tacuaremboensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
