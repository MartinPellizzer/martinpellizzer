Indigofera suarezensis

Indigofera suarezensis, commonly known as the Suarez's indigo, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
