Ixora glaucescens

Ixora glaucescens, commonly known as the blue ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora glaucescens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to bloom throughout the year.
