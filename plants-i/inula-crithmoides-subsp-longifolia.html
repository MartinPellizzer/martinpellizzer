Inula crithmoides subsp. longifolia

Inula crithmoides subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf inula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed or marginal areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
