Ipomoea decora

Ipomoea decora, commonly known as the ornamental morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms in the early morning. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea decora is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
