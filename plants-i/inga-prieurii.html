Inga prieurii

Inga prieurii, commonly known as the African rainforest bean, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Inga prieurii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential in agroforestry systems.
