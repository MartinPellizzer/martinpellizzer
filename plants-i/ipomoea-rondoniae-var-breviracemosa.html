Ipomoea rondoniae var. breviracemosa

Ipomoea rondoniae var. breviracemosa, commonly known as the short raceme morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or violet flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing vines and showy blooms. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are slender and twining.

This variety is noted for its relatively compact growth habit compared to other Ipomoea species.
