Inula lanigera

Inula lanigera, commonly known as the downy elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a stout, hairy stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and has been used in traditional medicine.
