Ipomoea iodantha

Ipomoea iodantha, commonly known as the Blue Morning Glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea iodantha is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens.
