Ilex pedunculosa var. senjoensis

Ilex pedunculosa var. senjoensis, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice in gardens and natural landscapes.
