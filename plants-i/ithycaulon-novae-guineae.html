Ithycaulon novae-guineae

Ithycaulon novae-guineae, commonly known as the New Guinea orchid, is a rare and unique plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense rainforests where it thrives in high humidity and filtered sunlight. Its botanical structure includes long, slender stems and broad, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.
