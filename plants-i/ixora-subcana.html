Ixora subcana

Ixora subcana, commonly known as the hairy ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ixora subcana is well adapted to tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
