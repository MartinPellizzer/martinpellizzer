Indigofera secundiflora var. oubanguiensis

Indigofera secundiflora var. oubanguiensis, commonly known as the Oubangui indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports its growth in nutrient-poor environments.

Its seeds are often used in traditional practices, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
