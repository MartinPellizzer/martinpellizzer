Iris dolichosiphon subsp. orientalis

Iris dolichosiphon subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern swordleaf iris, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
