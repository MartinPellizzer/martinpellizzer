Ixeris dentata var. amplifolia

Ixeris dentata var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Ixeris dentata var. amplifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
