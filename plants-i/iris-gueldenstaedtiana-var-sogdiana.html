Iris gueldenstaedtiana var. sogdiana

Iris gueldenstaedtiana var. sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Iridaceae family.
