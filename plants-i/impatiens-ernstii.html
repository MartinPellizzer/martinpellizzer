Impatiens ernstii

Impatiens ernstii, commonly known as the Ernst's touch-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Impatiens ernstii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
