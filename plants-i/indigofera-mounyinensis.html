Indigofera mounyinensis

Indigofera mounyinensis, commonly known as the Mounyin indigo plant, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
