Isatis tinctoria var. campestris

Isatis tinctoria var. campestris, commonly known as Chinese yellow mustard, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in the production of mustard oil and as a source of glucosinolates. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times for both medicinal and dyeing purposes.
