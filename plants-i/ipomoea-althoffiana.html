Ipomoea althoffiana

Ipomoea althoffiana, commonly known as the Althoff's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea althoffiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
