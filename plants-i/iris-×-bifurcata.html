Iris × bifurcata

Iris × bifurcata, commonly known as the hybrid iris, is a cultivated variety resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
