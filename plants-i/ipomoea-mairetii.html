Ipomoea mairetii

Ipomoea mairetii, commonly known as the Mairetii morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of morning glories.

Ipomoea mairetii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
