Iris versicolor var. blandescens

Iris versicolor var. blandescens, commonly known as the pale blue flag iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking touch to woodland gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
