Indigofera caespitosa

Indigofera caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted indigo, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a bushy growth habit with upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Indigofera caespitosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
