Ipomoea selleana

Ipomoea selleana, commonly known as the Selleana morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers open in the morning and close by late afternoon, hence its common name.

Ipomoea selleana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and vigor.
