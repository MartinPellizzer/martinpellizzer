Ipomoea tuberculata

Ipomoea tuberculata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often opening in the morning and closing at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. Its tuberous roots are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates.

Ipomoea tuberculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and climbing nature.
