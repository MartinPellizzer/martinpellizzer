Indigofera intricata

Indigofera intricata, commonly known as the intricate indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for both landscaping and horticultural collections.
