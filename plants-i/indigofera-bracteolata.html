Indigofera bracteolata

Indigofera bracteolata, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

The plant's ornamental value and ecological benefits have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
