Isodon lophanthoides var. lophanthoides

Isodon lophanthoides var. lophanthoides, commonly known as the mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high altitudes. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
