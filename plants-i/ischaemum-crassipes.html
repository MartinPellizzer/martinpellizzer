Ischaemum crassipes

Ischaemum crassipes, commonly known as broad-leaved beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, adapting to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its rhizomes help it spread and establish dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland areas.

Ischaemum crassipes plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
