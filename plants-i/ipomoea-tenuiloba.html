Ipomoea tenuiloba

Ipomoea tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ipomoea tenuiloba is a perennial vine that blooms throughout the growing season.
