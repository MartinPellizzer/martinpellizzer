Inga vahliana

Inga vahliana, commonly known as the West African rainforest tree, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Inga vahliana also plays a vital role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
