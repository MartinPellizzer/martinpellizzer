Ipomoea splendor-sylvae

Ipomoea splendor-sylvae, commonly known as the forest glory vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Ipomoea splendor-sylvae thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
