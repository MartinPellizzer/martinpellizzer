Isoetes pseudojaponica

Isoetes pseudojaponica, commonly known as the Japanese water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its intricate, lacy foliage that resembles the structure of a clover leaf, with numerous small, spore-bearing sporangia along its fronds. This plant typically inhabits freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, where it thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters. Its spores are dispersed by water currents, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Isoetes pseudojaponica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
