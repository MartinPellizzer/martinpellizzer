Impatiens leptopoda var. gibbosa

Impatiens leptopoda var. gibbosa, commonly known as the warty impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, and the species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

The varietal form gibbosa is distinguished by its prominently swollen, warty stems.
