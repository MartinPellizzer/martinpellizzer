Ilex laevigata f. herveyi

Ilex laevigata f. herveyi, commonly known as the Hervey's smoothleaf holly, is a rare and distinctive variant of the smoothleaf holly.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate forests. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by bright red berries that are a food source for various bird species.

Due to its limited distribution and unique features, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
