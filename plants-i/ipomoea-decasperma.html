Ipomoea decasperma

Ipomoea decasperma, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found climbing fences, trees, or other structures. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ipomoea decasperma is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
