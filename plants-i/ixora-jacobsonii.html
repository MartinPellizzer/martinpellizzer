Ixora jacobsonii

Ixora jacobsonii, commonly known as the scarlet flame or Jacobson's ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

Ixora jacobsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
