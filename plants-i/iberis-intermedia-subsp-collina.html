Iberis intermedia subsp. collina

Iberis intermedia subsp.

collina, commonly known as the alpine candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for alpine gardens. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
