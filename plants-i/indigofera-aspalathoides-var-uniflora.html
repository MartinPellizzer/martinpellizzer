Indigofera aspalathoides var. uniflora

Indigofera aspalathoides var. uniflora, commonly known as the Cape Flats indigo, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Cape Floristic Region. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a hardy choice for coastal landscapes.
