Impatiens ruthiae

Impatiens ruthiae, commonly known as the Ruth's touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and often have a unique, spurred shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Impatiens ruthiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
