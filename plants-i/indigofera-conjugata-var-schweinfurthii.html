Indigofera conjugata var. schweinfurthii

Indigofera conjugata var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African indigo or Schweinfurth's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
