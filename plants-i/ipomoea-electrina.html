Ipomoea electrina

Ipomoea electrina, commonly known as the electric vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea electrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
