Inula polygonata

Inula polygonata, commonly known as the Chinese elecampane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a robust stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Inula polygonata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
