Ischaemum tenuifolium

Ischaemum tenuifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a C4 photosynthetic pathway, which allows it to thrive in hot and dry environments.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and an important forage plant in certain regions.
