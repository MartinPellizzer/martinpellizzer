Impatiens inayatii

Impatiens inayatii, commonly known as the Inayat's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are borne on pedicels and feature a distinctive spurred structure, which is a key botanical feature of the genus Impatiens.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
