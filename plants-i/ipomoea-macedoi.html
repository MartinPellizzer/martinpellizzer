Ipomoea macedoi

Ipomoea macedoi, commonly known as the Macedonian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a rapid growth habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea macedoi is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
