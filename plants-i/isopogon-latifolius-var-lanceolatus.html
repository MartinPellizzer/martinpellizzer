Isopogon latifolius var. lanceolatus

Isopogon latifolius var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes, typically appearing in shades of red or orange. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, which gives the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
