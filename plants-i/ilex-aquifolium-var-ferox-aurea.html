Ilex aquifolium var. ferox-aurea

Ilex aquifolium var. ferox-aurea, commonly known as the golden-hair euonymus, is a variant of the common holly.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts vividly with its dark green stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique coloration, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens and landscapes.
