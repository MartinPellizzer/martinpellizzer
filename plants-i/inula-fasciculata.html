Inula fasciculata

Inula fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a striking appearance.

Inula fasciculata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
