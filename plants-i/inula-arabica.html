Inula arabica

Inula arabica, commonly known as the Arabian elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a robust stem with grayish-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inula arabica has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and respiratory benefits.
