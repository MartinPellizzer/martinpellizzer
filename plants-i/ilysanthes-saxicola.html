Ilysanthes saxicola

Ilysanthes saxicola, commonly known as the rock sandwort, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in rocky crevices and dry soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in early spring.

Ilysanthes saxicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant flora.
