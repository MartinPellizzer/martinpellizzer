Ipomoea hardwickii

Ipomoea hardwickii, commonly known as the hardwick's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea hardwickii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
