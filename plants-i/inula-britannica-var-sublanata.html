Inula britannica var. sublanata

Inula britannica var. sublanata, commonly known as the rough goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in damp woodland or streamside habitats. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
