Illecebrum arabicum

Illecebrum arabicum, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in summer. It thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
