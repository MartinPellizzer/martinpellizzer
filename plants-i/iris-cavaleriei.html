Iris cavaleriei

Iris cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Iris cavaleriei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and elegant appearance.
