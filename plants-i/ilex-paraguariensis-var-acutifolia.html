Ilex paraguariensis var. acutifolia

Ilex paraguariensis var. acutifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian holly or acutifolia holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and small, red berries that contain the seeds. The plant is closely related to the coffee plant, and its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
