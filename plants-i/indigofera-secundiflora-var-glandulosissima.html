Indigofera secundiflora var. glandulosissima

Indigofera secundiflora var. glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with glandular hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in natural dye production.
