Inula villosa

Inula villosa, commonly known as the hairy elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, stiff stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, with prominent veins.

Inula villosa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
