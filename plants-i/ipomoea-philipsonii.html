Ipomoea philipsonii

Ipomoea philipsonii, commonly known as the Philippine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing along forest edges and in disturbed soils.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving the plant its common name.
