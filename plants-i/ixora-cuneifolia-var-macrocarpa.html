Ixora cuneifolia var. macrocarpa

Ixora cuneifolia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
