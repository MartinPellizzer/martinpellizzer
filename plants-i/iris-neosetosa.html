Iris neosetosa

Iris neosetosa, commonly known as the New Zealand iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright, sword-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

Iris neosetosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
