Isostylis serrata

Isostylis serrata, commonly known as the serrated isostylis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
