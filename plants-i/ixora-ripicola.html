Ixora ripicola

Ixora ripicola, commonly known as the rock ixora, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes or cliffs.

Ixora ripicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
