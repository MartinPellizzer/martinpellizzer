Iris sisyrinchium f. caerulea

Iris sisyrinchium f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the eastern United States, particularly in wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and wetland restoration projects.

The blue flag iris is also known for its ability to thrive in moist to wet soils, making it a versatile choice for water garden environments.
