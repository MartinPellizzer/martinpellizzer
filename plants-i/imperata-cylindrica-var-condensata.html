Imperata cylindrica var. condensata

Imperata cylindrica var. condensata, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green to purplish in color, especially under stress. The plant produces dense, cylindrical clusters of small, pinkish flowers that are highly visible during the flowering season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
