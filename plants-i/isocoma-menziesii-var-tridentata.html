Isocoma menziesii var. tridentata

Isocoma menziesii var. tridentata, commonly known as the tridentate goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered blooms. It is often found in desert and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to arid environments, with reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
