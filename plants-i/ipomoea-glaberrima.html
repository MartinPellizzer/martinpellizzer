Ipomoea glaberrima

Ipomoea glaberrima, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and twining vines. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky texture.

Ipomoea glaberrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
