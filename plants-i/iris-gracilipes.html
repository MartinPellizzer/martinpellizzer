Iris gracilipes

Iris gracilipes, commonly known as the slender iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense clump that helps retain moisture.

Iris gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
