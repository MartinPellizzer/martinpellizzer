Inula britannica var. ramosissima

Inula britannica var. ramosissima, commonly known as the British Inula, is a flowering plant native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
