Ilex wallichii

Ilex wallichii, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It has a dense, compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Ilex wallichii is also used in traditional medicine in some Asian countries.
