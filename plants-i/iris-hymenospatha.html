Iris hymenospatha

Iris hymenospatha, commonly known as the blue iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Iris hymenospatha is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats.
