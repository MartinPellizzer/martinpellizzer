Ixeridium Sagittarioides

Ixeridium sagittarioides is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological importance. This species is characterized by its slender, arrow-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Despite its modest appearance, it plays a vital role in its native habitat by supporting local pollinators and wildlife. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Ixeridium sagittarioides is now considered a rare and endangered species.
