Isatis glauca subsp. glauca

Isatis glauca subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as mustard and cabbage.

The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands, and it is often found in the eastern and central United States.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in wet environments and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
