Inga jucunda

Inga jucunda, commonly known as the "sweet acacia," is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for reforestation projects. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, edible pods that contain seeds.

Inga jucunda also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
