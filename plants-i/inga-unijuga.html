Inga unijuga

Inga unijuga, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, often used in traditional practices.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
