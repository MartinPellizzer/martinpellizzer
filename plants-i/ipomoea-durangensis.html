Ipomoea durangensis

Ipomoea durangensis, commonly known as the Durango morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea durangensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
