Ibatia pacifica

Ibatia pacifica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum pacificum*, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its attractive red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe, attracting various bird species.
