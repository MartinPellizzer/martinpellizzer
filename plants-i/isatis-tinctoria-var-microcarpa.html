Isatis tinctoria var. microcarpa

Isatis tinctoria var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese yellow root, is a variant of the woad plant native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, yellowish flowers compared to the standard woad. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and historical use in dyeing textiles.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional herbal medicine and horticultural practices.
