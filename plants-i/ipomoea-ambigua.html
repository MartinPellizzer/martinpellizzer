Ipomoea ambigua

Ipomoea ambigua, commonly known as the morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces showy, colorful blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea ambigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
