Iris leichtlinii

Iris leichtlinii, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Iris leichtlinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
