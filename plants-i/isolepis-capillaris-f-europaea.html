Isolepis capillaris f. europaea

Isolepis capillaris f. europaea, commonly known as the European slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, ditches, and the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils.
