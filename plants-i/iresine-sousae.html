Iresine sousae

Iresine sousae, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red leaf plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid, jewel-toned foliage, often displaying shades of red, purple, and pink, which intensify in sunlight. The plant features small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are velvety to the touch, giving it a unique texture that adds visual interest in gardens.

Iresine sousae thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth and coloration.
