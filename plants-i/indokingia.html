Indokingia

Indokingia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a striking aquatic plant with large, showy flowers.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor in the muddy substrate. The plant blooms with bright pink or white flowers, often with a central yellow stamen, and is known for its symbolic significance in Egyptian mythology. Indokingia thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and revered species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
