Iris ensata var. grandiflora

Iris ensata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy blooms that typically feature a deep violet or blue color with a white or yellow center. The plant produces sword-like, grassy leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is a member of the Iridaceae family.

This variety is known for its robust growth and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
