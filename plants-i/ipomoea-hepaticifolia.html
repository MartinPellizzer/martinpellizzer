Ipomoea hepaticifolia

Ipomoea hepaticifolia, commonly known as the liverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in some areas.
