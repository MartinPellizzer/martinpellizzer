Iris hexagona var. lamancei

Iris hexagona var. lamancei, commonly known as the lamance iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive hexagonal shape. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open meadow habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its rhizomes contribute to its growth and propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
