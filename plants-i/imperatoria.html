Imperatoria

Imperatoria, commonly known as the bloody weed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its bright red, fleshy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Imperatoria is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
