Ipomoea bernoulliana

Ipomoea bernoulliana, commonly known as the Bernoulli morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces showy, showy flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, making it suitable for trellises or supports.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
