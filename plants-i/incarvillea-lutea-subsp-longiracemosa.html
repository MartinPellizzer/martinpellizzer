Incarvillea lutea subsp. longiracemosa

Incarvillea lutea subsp.

longiracemosa, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.
