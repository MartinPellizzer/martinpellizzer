Ixeris papuana

Ixeris papuana, commonly known as the Papuan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Ixeris papuana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
