Indigofera wituensis

Indigofera wituensis, commonly known as the Witu indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional medicine, while its foliage is sometimes used as forage for livestock.
