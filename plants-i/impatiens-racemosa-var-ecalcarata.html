Impatiens racemosa var. ecalcarata

Impatiens racemosa var. ecalcarata, commonly known as the spurred impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
