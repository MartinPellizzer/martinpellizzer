Inulanthera montana

Inulanthera montana, commonly known as the mountain inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single terminal capitulum surrounded by several involucral bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
