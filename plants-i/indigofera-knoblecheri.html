Indigofera knoblecheri

Indigofera knoblecheri, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
