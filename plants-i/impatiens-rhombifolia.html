Impatiens rhombifolia

Impatiens rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are pollinated by insects.

Impatiens rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
