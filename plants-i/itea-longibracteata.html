Itea longibracteata

Itea longibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden landscapes and natural habitats.
