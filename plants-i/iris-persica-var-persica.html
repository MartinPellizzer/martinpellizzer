Iris persica var. persica

Iris persica var. persica, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, typical of the iris family. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
