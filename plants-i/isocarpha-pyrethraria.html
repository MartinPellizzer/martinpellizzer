Isocarpha pyrethraria

Isocarpha pyrethraria, commonly known as the pyrethrum weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some regions but also has potential in ecological restoration projects.
