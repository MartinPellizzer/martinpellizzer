Ixora confertiflora var. parvifolia

Ixora confertiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright red or orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
