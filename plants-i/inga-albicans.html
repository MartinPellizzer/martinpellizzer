Inga albicans

Inga albicans, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The tree has also been studied for its potential ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
