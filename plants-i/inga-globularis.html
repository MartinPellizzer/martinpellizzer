Inga globularis

Inga globularis, commonly known as the globe pod tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive globular seed pods. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
