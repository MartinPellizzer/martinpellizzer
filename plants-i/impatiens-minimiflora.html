Impatiens minimiflora

Impatiens minimiflora, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely used in horticulture and landscaping.
