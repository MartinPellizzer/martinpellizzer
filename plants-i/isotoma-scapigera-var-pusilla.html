Isotoma scapigera var. pusilla

Isotoma scapigera var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of cold-climate ecosystems.
