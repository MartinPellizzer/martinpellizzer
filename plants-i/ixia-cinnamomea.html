Ixia cinnamomea

Ixia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllis family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that resemble lilies, with narrow, strap-like leaves. The plant is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It typically blooms in late winter to early spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Ixia cinnamomea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
