Isolepis atacamensis

Isolepis atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama sedge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This species is adapted to grow in saline and alkaline soils, typical of the hyperarid Atacama Desert. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Isolepis atacamensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
