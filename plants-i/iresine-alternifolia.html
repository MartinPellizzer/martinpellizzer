Iresine alternifolia

Iresine alternifolia, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated foliage, which can display shades of red, pink, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Iresine alternifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
