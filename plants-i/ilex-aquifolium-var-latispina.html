Ilex aquifolium var. latispina

Ilex aquifolium var. latispina, commonly known as the broad-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its broader, more elongated leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with spiny margins that provide a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
