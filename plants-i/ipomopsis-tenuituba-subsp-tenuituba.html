Ipomopsis tenuituba subsp. tenuituba

Ipomopsis tenuituba subsp.

tenuituba, commonly known as the narrowleaf ipomopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
