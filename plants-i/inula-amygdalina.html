Inula amygdalina

Inula amygdalina, commonly known as the "Garden Buttercup," is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, bearing alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disc of tubular florets, typically blooming in late summer. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds like inulin and sesquiterpene lactones.

Inula amygdalina is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
