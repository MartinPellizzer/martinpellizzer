Iris lactea var. biglumis

Iris lactea var. biglumis, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland landscapes.
