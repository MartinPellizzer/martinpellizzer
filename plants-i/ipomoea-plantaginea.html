Ipomoea plantaginea

Ipomoea plantaginea, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
