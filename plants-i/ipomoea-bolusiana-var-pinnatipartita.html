Ipomoea bolusiana var. pinnatipartita

Ipomoea bolusiana var. pinnatipartita, commonly known as the winged morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wing-like pattern along the edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
