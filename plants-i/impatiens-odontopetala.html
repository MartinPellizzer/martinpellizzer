Impatiens odontopetala

Impatiens odontopetala, commonly known as the toothed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens odontopetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
