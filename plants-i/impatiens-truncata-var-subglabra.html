Impatiens truncata var. subglabra

Impatiens truncata var. subglabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a rough texture. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a spurred base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
