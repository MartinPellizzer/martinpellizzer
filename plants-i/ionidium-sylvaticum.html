Ionidium sylvaticum

Ionidium sylvaticum, commonly known as the forest ionidium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Ionidium sylvaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
