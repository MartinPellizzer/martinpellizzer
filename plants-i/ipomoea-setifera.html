Ipomoea setifera

Ipomoea setifera, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces long, slender vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Ipomoea setifera thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable species within the Convolvulaceae family.
