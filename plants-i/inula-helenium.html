Inula helenium

Inula helenium, commonly known as yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features rough, hairy stems and large, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Inula helenium has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
