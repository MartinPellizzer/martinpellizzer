Ionidium ilicifolium

Ionidium ilicifolium, commonly known as the leatherleaf plant, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
