Ipomoea affinis

Ipomoea affinis, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant is native to the Americas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its vines can climb and twine around supports, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white, and are often used in traditional medicine.
