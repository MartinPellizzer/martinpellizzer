Ipomoea dammarana

Ipomoea dammarana, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It grows as a climbing vine, often reaching lengths of several meters, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ipomoea dammarana is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
