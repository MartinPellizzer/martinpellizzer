Indigofera emmae

Indigofera emmae, commonly known as the Emma's indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Indigofera emmae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
