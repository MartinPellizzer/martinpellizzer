Indigofera chinensis

Indigofera chinensis, commonly known as Chinese indigo, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems that bear small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
