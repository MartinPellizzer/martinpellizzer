Inula primulifolia

Inula primulifolia, commonly known as the primrose hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescence. It thrives in meadows and rocky soils, often in sunny locations.

The plant's medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
