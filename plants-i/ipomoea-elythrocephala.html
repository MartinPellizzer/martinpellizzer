Ipomoea elythrocephala

Ipomoea elythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, a trait typical of many morning glory species.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional African medicine.
