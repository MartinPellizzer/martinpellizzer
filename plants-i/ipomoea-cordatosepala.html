Ipomoea cordatosepala

Ipomoea cordatosepala, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing habit and rapid growth. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, a trait that gives it its common name.
