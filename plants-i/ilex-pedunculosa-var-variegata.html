Ilex pedunculosa var. variegata

Ilex pedunculosa var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to gardens.
