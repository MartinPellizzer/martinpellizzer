Impatiens vesiculifera

Impatiens vesiculifera, commonly known as the bladderleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
