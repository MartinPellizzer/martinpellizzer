Ilex crenata var. rotundifolia

Ilex crenata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as Japanese Holly or Japanese Boxwood, is a dense, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval, dark green leaves that are rounded at the tips, giving it a compact and uniform appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It is widely used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to maintain a neat shape with minimal pruning.

This variety is particularly valued for its adaptability to different soil types and resistance to pests and diseases.
