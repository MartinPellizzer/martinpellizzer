Indigofera schimperi

Indigofera schimperi, commonly known as Ethiopian indigo, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical use for dye production and its ecological role in its native habitats.
