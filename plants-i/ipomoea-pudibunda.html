Ipomoea pudibunda

Ipomoea pudibunda, commonly known as the shy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea pudibunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
