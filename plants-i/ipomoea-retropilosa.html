Ipomoea retropilosa

Ipomoea retropilosa, commonly known as the backward-hair morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces distinctive, spirally twisted seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea retropilosa is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
