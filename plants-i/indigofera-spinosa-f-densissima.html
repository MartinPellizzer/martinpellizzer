Indigofera spinosa f. densissima

Indigofera spinosa f. densissima, commonly known as the dense indigo bush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers, which are typically borne in axillary inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
