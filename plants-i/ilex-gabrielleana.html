Ilex gabrielleana

Ilex gabrielleana, commonly known as the Gabriellean holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically leathery and have a pointed shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. Ilex gabrielleana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and limestone soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
