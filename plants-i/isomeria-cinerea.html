Isomeria cinerea

Isomeria cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf isomeria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, silvery-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Isomeria cinerea produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
