Ipomoea simonsiana

Ipomoea simonsiana, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, a characteristic shared with other members of the morning glory family. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often sprawling.

Ipomoea simonsiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.
