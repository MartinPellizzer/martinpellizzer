Ipomoea chilopsidis

Ipomoea chilopsidis, commonly known as the Chilean morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea chilopsidis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
