Ifloga cauliflora

Ifloga cauliflora, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture.

Ifloga cauliflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
