Indigofera pseudoindigofera

Indigofera pseudoindigofera, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its roots. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

The plant has historical significance in dye production and traditional medicine.
