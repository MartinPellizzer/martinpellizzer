Isolepis schomburghii

Isolepis schomburghii, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Isolepis schomburghii plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
