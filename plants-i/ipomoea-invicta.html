Ipomoea invicta

Ipomoea invicta, commonly known as morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, often purple or blue, flowers. It is also known for its strong climbing habit and can become invasive in certain regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
