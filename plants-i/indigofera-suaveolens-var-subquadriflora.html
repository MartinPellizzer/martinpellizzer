Indigofera suaveolens var. subquadriflora

Indigofera suaveolens var. subquadriflora, commonly known as sweet indigo or the purple wisteria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of wisteria. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
