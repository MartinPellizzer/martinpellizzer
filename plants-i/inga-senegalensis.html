Inga senegalensis

Inga senegalensis, commonly known as the African honey tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree's pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its timber and medicinal properties in various regions.
