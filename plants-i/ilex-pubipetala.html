Ilex pubipetala

Ilex pubipetala, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various bird species. Ilex pubipetala thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a humid climate.
