Inga macradenia

Inga macradenia, commonly known as the broadleaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, cream-colored flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in forest ecosystems.
