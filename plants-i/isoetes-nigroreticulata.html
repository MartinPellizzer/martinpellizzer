Isoetes nigroreticulata

Isoetes nigroreticulata, commonly known as the black-netted watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in a reticulate pattern. The plant is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface.

Isoetes nigroreticulata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
