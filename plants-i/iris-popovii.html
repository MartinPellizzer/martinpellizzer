Iris popovii

Iris popovii, commonly known as the Popov's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces three to five petals in a distinctive "pseudobulb" structure.

Iris popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
