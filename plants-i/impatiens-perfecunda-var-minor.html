Impatiens perfecunda var. minor

Impatiens perfecunda var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink, with five lobes and a spur. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
