Impatiens zironiana

Impatiens zironiana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens zironiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
