Ipomoea repandula

Ipomoea repandula, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant blue or purple flowers, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. Its vines can climb or trail, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Ipomoea repandula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
