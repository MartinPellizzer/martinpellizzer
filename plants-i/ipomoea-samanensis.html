Ipomoea samanensis

Ipomoea samanensis, commonly known as the Saman tree or rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical climates. It has a shallow root system and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The Saman tree also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
