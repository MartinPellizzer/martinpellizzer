Isolepis setacea var. aberdarica

Isolepis setacea var. aberdarica, commonly known as the Aberdeenshire sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed, forming a dense clump. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal or inland wetlands.
