Ipomoea mesenterioides

Ipomoea mesenterioides, commonly known as the banana vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, hard seeds.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
