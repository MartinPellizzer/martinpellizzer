Iris × ampliflora

Iris × ampliflora, commonly known as the broad-leaf iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris pseudacorus and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a prominent, often darker, central blotch. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Iris × ampliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with adequate water availability.
