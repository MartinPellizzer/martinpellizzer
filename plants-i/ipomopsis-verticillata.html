Ipomopsis verticillata

Ipomopsis verticillata, commonly known as the clustered trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
