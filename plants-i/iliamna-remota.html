Iliamna remota

Iliamna remota, commonly known as the Alaska sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in moist, rocky, and acidic soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, typically from June to August. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to retain water in its harsh, alpine environment.

Iliamna remota plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
