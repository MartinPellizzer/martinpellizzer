Isoetes laosiensis

Isoetes laosiensis, commonly known as the Lao watermoss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, spike-like inflorescence that consists of small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in shallow, still waters with muddy or sandy substrates. Its leaves are typically narrow and ribbon-like, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Isoetes laosiensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
