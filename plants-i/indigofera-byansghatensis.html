Indigofera byansghatensis

Indigofera byansghatensis, commonly known as the Himalayan indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and northern India. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It produces pods containing small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
