Isoetes acadiensis

Isoetes acadiensis, commonly known as the Acadian watermoccasin, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which are commonly referred to as quillworts.

It is characterized by its spiky, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged or partially submerged in water. This plant is native to the eastern coast of North America, particularly in freshwater wetlands and swampy areas. Isoetes acadiensis reproduces through spores, a feature common to many members of the Isoetaceae family.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
