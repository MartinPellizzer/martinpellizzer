Iris extremorientalis

Iris extremorientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant typically blooms in early to mid-summer, with flowers that have three upright petals and three downward-curving sepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
