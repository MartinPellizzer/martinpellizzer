Ipomoea triloba var. eustachiana

Ipomoea triloba var. eustachiana, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial climber that can reach lengths of several meters.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
