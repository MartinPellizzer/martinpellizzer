Ipomoea buchneri var. tomentosa

Ipomoea buchneri var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, tomentose stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
