Ipomoea pentaloba

Ipomoea pentaloba, commonly known as the five-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences or in the wild.

The fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
