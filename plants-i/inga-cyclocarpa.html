Inga cyclocarpa

Inga cyclocarpa, commonly known as the sweet cyclocarpa, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Inga cyclocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds and nitrogen-fixing properties.
