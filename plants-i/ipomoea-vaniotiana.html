Ipomoea vaniotiana

Ipomoea vaniotiana, commonly known as the Vaniotiana morning glory, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds are enclosed in a hairy, capsule-like fruit that splits open when mature.
