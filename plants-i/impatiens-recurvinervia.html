Impatiens recurvinervia

Impatiens recurvinervia, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often a deep green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
