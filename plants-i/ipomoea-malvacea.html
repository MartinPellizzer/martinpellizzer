Ipomoea malvacea

Ipomoea malvacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is an annual or perennial herb that thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed areas. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it establish quickly in various soil types.

Ipomoea malvacea is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
