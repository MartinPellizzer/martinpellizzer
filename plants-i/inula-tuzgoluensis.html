Inula tuzgoluensis

Inula tuzgoluensis, commonly known as the Tuzgol mountain inula, is a flowering plant native to the Tuzgol region of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellowish flower heads consist of numerous small florets, and it has hairy, linear leaves.

Inula tuzgoluensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential pharmacological uses.
