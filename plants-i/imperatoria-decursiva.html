Imperatoria decursiva

Imperatoria decursiva, commonly known as the creeping redweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially when young, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous greenish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its stems are prostrate and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Imperatoria decursiva is also known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it a common indicator plant in wetland ecosystems.
