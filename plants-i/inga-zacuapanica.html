Inga zacuapanica

Inga zacuapanica, commonly known as the "Chicha tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.
