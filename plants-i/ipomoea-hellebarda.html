Ipomoea hellebarda

Ipomoea hellebarda, commonly known as the devil's trumpet, is a flowering vine native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea hellebarda is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
