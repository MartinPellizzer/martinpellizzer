Isoetes histrix proles sicula

Isoetes histrix proles sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian spike moss, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a spike-like formation along the central axis of the plant. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to substrates. Its morphology includes a root system and a leaf-like structure called a strobilus, which houses the reproductive organs.

This species is notable for its ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
