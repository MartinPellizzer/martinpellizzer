Isolepis inconspicua

Isolepis inconspicua, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Isolepis inconspicua is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.
