Ipomoea bipinnatipartita

Ipomoea bipinnatipartita, commonly known as the double-leaf morning glory, is a striking tropical vine characterized by its intricate, bipinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinct veining pattern. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
