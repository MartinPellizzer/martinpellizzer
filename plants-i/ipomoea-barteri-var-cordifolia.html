Ipomoea barteri var. cordifolia

Ipomoea barteri var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as trellises or fences. Its flowers are typically 3 to 5 inches in diameter and have five petals.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
