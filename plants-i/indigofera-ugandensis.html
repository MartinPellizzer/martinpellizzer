Indigofera ugandensis

Indigofera ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to East Africa, particularly found in Uganda and surrounding regions. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine and soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
