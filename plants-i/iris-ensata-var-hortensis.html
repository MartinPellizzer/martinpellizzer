Iris ensata var. hortensis

Iris ensata var. hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, flat flower heads in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and water features.
