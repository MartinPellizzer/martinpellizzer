Isatis emarginata

Isatis emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed woad, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Isatis emarginata is also noted for its historical use in dye production and traditional herbal medicine.
