Ipomoea pentaphylla

Ipomoea pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, round fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
