Indigofera mollicoma

Indigofera mollicoma, commonly known as silky indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are soft and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
