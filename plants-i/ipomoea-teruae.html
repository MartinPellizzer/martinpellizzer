Ipomoea teruae

Ipomoea teruae, commonly known as the tropical morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a herbaceous vine that can grow up to several meters in length, climbing by means of tendrils. Its flowers open in the morning and close by late afternoon, a trait that gives it its common name.

Ipomoea teruae is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates.
