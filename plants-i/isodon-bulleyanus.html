Isodon bulleyanus

Isodon bulleyanus, commonly known as the mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of purple or blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy.

Isodon bulleyanus is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
