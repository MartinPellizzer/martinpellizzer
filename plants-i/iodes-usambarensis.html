Iodes usambarensis

Iodes usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarascent orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountains of Tanzania.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and forest margins, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Iodes usambarensis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and unique floral structure.
