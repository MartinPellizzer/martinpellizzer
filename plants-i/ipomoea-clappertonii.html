Ipomoea clappertonii

Ipomoea clappertonii, commonly known as Clapperton's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its rapid growth and sprawling habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Ipomoea clappertonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
