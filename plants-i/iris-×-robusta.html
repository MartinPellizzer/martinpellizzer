Iris × robusta

Iris × robusta, commonly known as the robust iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and provide a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.
