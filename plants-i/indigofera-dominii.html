Indigofera dominii

Indigofera dominii, commonly known as the royal indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in some regions for dyeing fabrics.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and tree forms, depending on environmental conditions.
