Ixia tardiflora

Ixia tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering ixia, is a South African endemic plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.

Ixia tardiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
