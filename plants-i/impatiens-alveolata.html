Impatiens alveolata

Impatiens alveolata, commonly known as the hollow-leaved impatiens, is a species of flowering plant in the family Balsaminaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often marked with a distinctive hollow or sunken area on the underside. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens alveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
