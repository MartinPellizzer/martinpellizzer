Iris yebrudii subsp. yebrudii

Iris yebrudii subsp.

yebrudii, commonly known as the Yebrud iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and in mountainous areas.

The plant features grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems with striking blue to violet flowers. Its flowers are typically three-dimensional and have a distinctive beak-like structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
