Indigofera hygrobia

Indigofera hygrobia, commonly known as the wetland indigo.

It is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a feathery appearance, and it thrives in moist or wet environments.

Indigofera hygrobia is often found in marshes, wetlands, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
