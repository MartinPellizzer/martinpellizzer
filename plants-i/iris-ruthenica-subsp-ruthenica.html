Iris ruthenica subsp. ruthenica

Iris ruthenica subsp.

ruthenica, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and showy, blue to violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
