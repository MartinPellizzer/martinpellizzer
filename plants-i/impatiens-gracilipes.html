Impatiens gracilipes

Impatiens gracilipes, commonly known as the slender touch-me-not, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features delicate, hairy stems that often trail or climb, and its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically borne in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Impatiens gracilipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
