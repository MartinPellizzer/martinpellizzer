Imperata cylindrica subsp. koenigii

Imperata cylindrica subsp.

koenigii, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinted foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common ornamental in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas also make it an invasive species in some environments.
