Ilex borneensis

Ilex borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that are edible for birds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its dense foliage makes it a valuable component of forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The Bornean holly is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
