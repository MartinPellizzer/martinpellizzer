Iris variegata var. pontica

Iris variegata var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has sword-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
