Ixiolirion tataricum subsp. ledebourii

Ixiolirion tataricum subsp.

ledebourii, commonly known as the Tatarian bulbous grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbel-like clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a bulbous base that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival during dry periods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
