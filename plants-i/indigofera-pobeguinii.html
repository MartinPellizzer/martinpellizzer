Indigofera pobeguinii

Indigofera pobeguinii, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated pods that contain small, dark seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
