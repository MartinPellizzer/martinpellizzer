Inga pilosa

Inga pilosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit is a pod-like structure that contains edible seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

Inga pilosa also plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
