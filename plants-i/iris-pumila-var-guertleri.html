Iris pumila var. guertleri

Iris pumila var. guertleri, commonly known as the Guertl's dwarf iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.

This variety is a member of the Iridaceae family, known for its showy blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
