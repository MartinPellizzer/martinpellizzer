Ilex laureola var. genuina

Ilex laureola var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
