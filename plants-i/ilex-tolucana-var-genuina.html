Ilex tolucana var. genuina

Ilex tolucana var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
