Inga tysonii

Inga tysonii, commonly known as the "Cocao Tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds, which are used in the production of cocoa. Inga tysonii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded conditions make it a useful species for reforestation and soil enrichment.
