Iris virescens var. erratica

Iris virescens var. erratica, commonly known as the wandering iris, is a variant of the green iris native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive spur at the base of the petals. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
