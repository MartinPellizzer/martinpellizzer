Ilex polyneura

Ilex polyneura, commonly known as the Australian holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are often adorned with white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for various birds. Ilex polyneura thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
