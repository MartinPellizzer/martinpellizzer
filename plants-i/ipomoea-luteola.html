Ipomoea luteola

Ipomoea luteola, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are dispersed by birds and animals, contributing to its widespread distribution.
