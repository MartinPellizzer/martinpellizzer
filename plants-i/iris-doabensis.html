Iris doabensis

Iris doabensis, commonly known as the Doab iris, is a perennial herb native to the Doab region of India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Iris doabensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies in its native habitat.
