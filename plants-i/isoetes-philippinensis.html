Isoetes philippinensis

Isoetes philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine watermace, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, spike-like formations along the plant's axis. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are long and ribbon-like, with a distinct midrib and a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Isoetes philippinensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
