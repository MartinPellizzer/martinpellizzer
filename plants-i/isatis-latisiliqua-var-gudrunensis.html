Isatis latisiliqua var. gudrunensis

Isatis latisiliqua var. gudrunensis, commonly known as the Gudrun's woad, is a variant of the woad plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers and lance-shaped leaves, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its historical significance lies in its use for dyeing fabrics and its potential medicinal properties.
