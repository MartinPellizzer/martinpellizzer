Ischaemum laeve

Ischaemum laeve, commonly known as silky grass or smooth barnyard grass, is a species of annual grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Ischaemum laeve is a common weed that can compete with crops for nutrients and water.
