Isolepis varia

Isolepis varia, commonly known as the variable tussock sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often found in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense tussocks, contributing to soil stabilization in moist habitats.

Isolepis varia is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
