Inula helianthus-aquatilis subsp. hupehensis

Inula helianthus-aquatilis subsp.

hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese sunflower, is a perennial herb native to China. It features a robust, branching stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with rough, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wetland ecosystems.
