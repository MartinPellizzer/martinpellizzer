Ischaemum truncatiglume

Ischaemum truncatiglume, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in a panicle, and the lemma is truncate, giving it its specific epithet.

The plant is characterized by its small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often considered a weed in cultivated areas.
