Inula anatolica

Inula anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian Inula, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
