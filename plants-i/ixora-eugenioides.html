Ixora eugenioides

Ixora eugenioides, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora eugenioides is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
