Iresine acuminata

Iresine acuminata, commonly known as red leaf iron plant, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple hues, with some varieties displaying contrasting white or silver veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Iresine acuminata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
