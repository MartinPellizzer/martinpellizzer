Indigofera tenuicaulis

Indigofera tenuicaulis, commonly known as slender-stemmed indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a source of natural dyes.
