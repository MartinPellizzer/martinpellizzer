Indigofera parkesii var. polyphylla

Indigofera parkesii var. polyphylla, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and historical use in dye production make it a notable species in botanical studies.
