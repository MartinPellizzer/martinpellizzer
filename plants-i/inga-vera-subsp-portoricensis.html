Inga vera subsp. portoricensis

Inga vera subsp.

portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican inga, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
