Inga tenuiloba

Inga tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. Inga tenuiloba produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
