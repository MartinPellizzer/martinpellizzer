Ionidium diandrum

Ionidium diandrum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ionidium diandrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
