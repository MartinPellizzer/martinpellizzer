Ipomoea fruticosa

Ipomoea fruticosa, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea fruticosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and vigorous growth.
