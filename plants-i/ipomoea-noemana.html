Ipomoea noemana

Ipomoea noemana, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy white to pale purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea noemana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its striking floral display.
