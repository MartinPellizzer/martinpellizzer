Iria polymorpha var. normalis

Iria polymorpha var. normalis, commonly known as the common iria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms during the summer months and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
