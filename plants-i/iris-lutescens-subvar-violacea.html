Iris lutescens subvar. violacea

Iris lutescens subvar. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking color and elegant form.
