Iris pariensis

Iris pariensis, commonly known as the Pariensis Iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
