Isoetes histrix

Isoetes histrix, commonly known as the hairy water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its finely divided, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Isoetes histrix is an ancient lineage of plants, with a fossil record dating back to the Carboniferous period.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
