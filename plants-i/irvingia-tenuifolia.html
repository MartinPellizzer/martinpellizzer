Irvingia tenuifolia

Irvingia tenuifolia, commonly known as African mango or mabira, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fruiting bodies resembling elongated capsules. Irvingia tenuifolia is notable for its high oil content in its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
