Ixia lutea var. ovata

Ixia lutea var. ovata, commonly known as the golden ox-eye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
