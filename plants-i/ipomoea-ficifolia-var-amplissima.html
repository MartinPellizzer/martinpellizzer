Ipomoea ficifolia var. amplissima

Ipomoea ficifolia var. amplissima, commonly known as the giant leaf morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.
