Ipomoea vivianae

Ipomoea vivianae, commonly known as the "Vivian's morning glory," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. It blooms predominantly in the morning, hence its common name, and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

Ipomoea vivianae is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
