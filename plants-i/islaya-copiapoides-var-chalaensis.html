Islaya copiapoides var. chalaensis

Islaya copiapoides var. chalaensis, commonly known as the Chalaensis Islaya, is a succulent plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the brief rainy season, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate habitat. It is well adapted to extreme drought and high salinity, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important subject for botanical study in desert ecosystems.
