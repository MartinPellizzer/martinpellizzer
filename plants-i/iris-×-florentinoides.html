Iris × florentinoides

Iris × florentinoides, commonly known as the Florentine iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The flowers come in a range of colors, including shades of blue, purple, and white, with a distinctive three-dimensional appearance. It is native to the Mediterranean region but has been cultivated in various parts of the world for its ornamental value.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
