Isolepis scariosa

Isolepis scariosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf false spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often hidden within the leaf sheaths, making it less showy than other Cyperaceae species.

Isolepis scariosa plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
