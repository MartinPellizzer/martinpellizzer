Indigofera boviperda subsp. boviperda

Indigofera boviperda subsp.

boviperda, commonly known as the African indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers.

The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it has cultural and economic significance in some regions.
