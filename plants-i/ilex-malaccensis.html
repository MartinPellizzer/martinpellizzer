Ilex malaccensis

Ilex malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits containing bright orange seeds. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a serrated edge.

Ilex malaccensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
