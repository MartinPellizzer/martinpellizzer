Ixora multibracteata

Ixora multibracteata, commonly known as the red flame ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental species. Its inflorescences consist of multiple bracts that are brightly colored, enhancing its visual appeal.

Ixora multibracteata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
