Irenodendron cupulatum

Irenodendron cupulatum, commonly known as the cupulate irenodendron, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces woody capsules as fruits, which contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
