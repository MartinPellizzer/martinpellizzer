Ipomoea bronsonii

Ipomoea bronsonii, commonly known as the Bronson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and grows as an annual vine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Convolvulaceae family.
