Ischaemum tallanum

Ischaemum tallanum, commonly known as tallgrass or tall bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Ischaemum tallanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
