Itea chinensis var. oblonga

Itea chinensis var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to gardens with suitable water conditions.
