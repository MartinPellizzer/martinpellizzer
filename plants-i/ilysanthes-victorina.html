Ilysanthes victorina

Ilysanthes victorina, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that resemble tiny stars, which bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine environments where it withstands cold temperatures and strong winds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
