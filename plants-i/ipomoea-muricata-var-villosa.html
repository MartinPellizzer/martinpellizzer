Ipomoea muricata var. villosa

Ipomoea muricata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, velvety, hairy stems and leaves.

It produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often opening in the morning. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant is known for its strong, sometimes invasive nature.
