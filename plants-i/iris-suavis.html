Iris suavis

Iris suavis, commonly known as the sweet iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive sweet fragrance. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to woodland gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Iris suavis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
