Impatiens rubromaculata subsp. schulziana

Impatiens rubromaculata subsp.

schulziana, commonly known as the Schultze's impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of vibrant red flowers with orange stamens.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This subspecies is known for its striking floral display and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden areas.
