Ipomoea jujuyensis

Ipomoea jujuyensis, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep violet. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that open in the morning. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Ipomoea jujuyensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
