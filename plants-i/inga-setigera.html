Inga setigera

Inga setigera, commonly known as the "wild coffee tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common component of secondary forests. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly serrated.

The flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, attracting various pollinators.
