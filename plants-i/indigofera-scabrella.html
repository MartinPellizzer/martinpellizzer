Indigofera scabrella

Indigofera scabrella, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its seeds are also used as a source of dye in some cultural practices.
