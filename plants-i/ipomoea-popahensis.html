Ipomoea popahensis

Ipomoea popahensis, commonly known as the Popa morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and robust.

Ipomoea popahensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
