Ipomoea cataractae

Ipomoea cataractae, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found growing along water bodies. It has a climbing habit and can reach lengths of several meters.

Ipomoea cataractae is cultivated for its edible leaves and is an important vegetable in many Asian cuisines.
