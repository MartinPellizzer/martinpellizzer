Irania pendula

Irania pendula, commonly known as the weeping irania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
