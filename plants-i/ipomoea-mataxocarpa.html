Ipomoea mataxocarpa

Ipomoea mataxocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds.
