Indigofera colutea var. grandiflora

Indigofera colutea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
