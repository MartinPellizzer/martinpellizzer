Imperata filifolia

Imperata filifolia, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous roots and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in swampy areas or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-ranked flowers.

Imperata filifolia is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
