Inga nuda var. longiflora

Inga nuda var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
