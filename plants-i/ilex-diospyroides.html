Ilex diospyroides

Ilex diospyroides, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Ilex diospyroides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable plant for landscaping purposes.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
