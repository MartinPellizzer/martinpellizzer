Iris pallida subsp. cengialti

Iris pallida subsp.

cengialti, commonly known as the pale iris or white iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear showy, pale blue to white flowers with three upright petals and three spreading sepals. Its leaves are sword-shaped, smooth, and often have a distinctive silvery-blue sheen.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
