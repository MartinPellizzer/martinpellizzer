Impatiens polyactina

Impatiens polyactina, commonly known as the purple impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Impatiens polyactina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
