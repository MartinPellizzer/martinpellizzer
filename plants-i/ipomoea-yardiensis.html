Ipomoea yardiensis

Ipomoea yardiensis, commonly known as the Yardien's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom during the day. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.
