Indigofera trita var. trita

Indigofera trita var. trita, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
