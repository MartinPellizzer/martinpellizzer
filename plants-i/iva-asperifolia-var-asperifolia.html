Iva asperifolia var. asperifolia

Iva asperifolia var. asperifolia, commonly known as roughleaf beggarlice, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in wetlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its distinctive leaf texture and floral structure.
