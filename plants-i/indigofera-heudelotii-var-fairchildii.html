Indigofera heudelotii var. fairchildii

Indigofera heudelotii var. fairchildii, commonly known as the pink indigo or pink flame bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

The varietal form, fairchildii, is distinguished by its more intense coloration compared to the typical species.
