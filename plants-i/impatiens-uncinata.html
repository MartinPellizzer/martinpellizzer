Impatiens uncinata

Impatiens uncinata, commonly known as the hooked impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The species features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. Impatiens uncinata typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
