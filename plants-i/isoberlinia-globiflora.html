Isoberlinia globiflora

Isoberlinia globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces spherical, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. It is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savannas and woodlands.

Isoberlinia globiflora is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine.
