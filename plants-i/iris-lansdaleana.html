Iris lansdaleana

Iris lansdaleana, commonly known as the Chinese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates and deciduous in colder regions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
