Impatiens neglecta

Impatiens neglecta, commonly known as the neglected impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows as an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its rapid growth and easy care make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
