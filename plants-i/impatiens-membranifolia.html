Impatiens membranifolia

Impatiens membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous-leaved impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, membranous layer, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
