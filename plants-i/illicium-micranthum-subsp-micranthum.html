Illicium micranthum subsp. micranthum

Illicium micranthum subsp.

micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered star anise, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise (Illicium verum).

The plant has lanceolate leaves with a smooth texture and emits a strong, pungent odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The fruit contains essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
