Ixora pygmaea

Ixora pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Ixora pygmaea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
