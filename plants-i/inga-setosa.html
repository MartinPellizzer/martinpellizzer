Inga setosa

Inga setosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Additionally, Inga setosa is valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, enhancing soil fertility in agricultural settings.
