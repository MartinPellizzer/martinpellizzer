Impatiens morsei

Impatiens morsei, commonly known as the Morsei's Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage consists of ovate to elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
