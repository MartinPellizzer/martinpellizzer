Ischarum bovei

Ischarum bovei, commonly known as the Bove's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Ischarum bovei produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying high-altitude flora.
