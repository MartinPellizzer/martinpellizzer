Impatiens tilo

Impatiens tilo, commonly known as the Madagascar impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Madagascar.

It features upright, branching stems covered in opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from summer to fall. Impatiens tilo thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a favorite in both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
