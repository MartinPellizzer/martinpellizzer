Ischaemum aristatum var. lanuginosum

Ischaemum aristatum var. lanuginosum, commonly known as the silky brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
