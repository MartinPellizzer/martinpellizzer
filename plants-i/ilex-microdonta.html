Ilex microdonta

Ilex microdonta, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 6 feet and features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Ilex microdonta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
