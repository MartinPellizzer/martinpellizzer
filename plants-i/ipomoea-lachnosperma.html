Ipomoea lachnosperma

Ipomoea lachnosperma, commonly known as the rough-seeded morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea lachnosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
