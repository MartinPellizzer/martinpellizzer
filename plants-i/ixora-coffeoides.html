Ixora coffeoides

Ixora coffeoides, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange-red inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Ixora coffeoides thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
