Impatiens rosea

Impatiens rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to reddish tones. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Impatiens rosea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value and ease of care have made it a favored plant among gardeners and botanists alike.
