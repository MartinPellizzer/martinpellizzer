Indigofera myosurus

Indigofera myosurus, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are encased in pods, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
