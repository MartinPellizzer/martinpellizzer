Ilex dubia var. beadlei

Ilex dubia var. beadlei, commonly known as the Beadle's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
