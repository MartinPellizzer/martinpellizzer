Ipomoea abrupta

Ipomoea abrupta, commonly known as the sudden bluemink vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Ipomoea abrupta thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.
