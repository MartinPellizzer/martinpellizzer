Ipomoea maxima var. sagittata

Ipomoea maxima var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
