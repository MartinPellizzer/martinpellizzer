Ipomoea jamaicensis var. sericea

Ipomoea jamaicensis var. sericea, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or violet. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded areas.

The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.
