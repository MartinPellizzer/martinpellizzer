Ipomoea angustata

Ipomoea angustata, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in open woodlands or along fences.

Ipomoea angustata is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
