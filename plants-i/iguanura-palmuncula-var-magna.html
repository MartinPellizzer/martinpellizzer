Iguanura palmuncula var. magna

Iguanura palmuncula var. magna, commonly known as the giant iguana palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
