Ixeridium sagittarioides

Ixeridium sagittarioides, commonly known as the arrowhead sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets during the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and freshwater habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
