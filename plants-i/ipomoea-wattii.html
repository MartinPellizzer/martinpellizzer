Ipomoea wattii

Ipomoea wattii, commonly known as the Watt's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and grows as a climbing vine. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence the common name.

Ipomoea wattii is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
