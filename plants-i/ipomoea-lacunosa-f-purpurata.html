Ipomoea lacunosa f. purpurata

Ipomoea lacunosa f. purpurata, commonly known as the purple-leaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae) characterized by its striking purple foliage.

This herbaceous perennial produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink, often opening in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and typically have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its vibrant color and climbing habit.

The plant is native to the southeastern United States and is valued in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
