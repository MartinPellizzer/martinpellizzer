Iris × caeciliae

Iris × caeciliae, commonly known as the Caecilia iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has broad, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The Caecilia iris is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time in suitable climates.
