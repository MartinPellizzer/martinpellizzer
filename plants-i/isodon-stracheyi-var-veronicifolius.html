Isodon stracheyi var. veronicifolius

Isodon stracheyi var. veronicifolius, commonly known as the Veronica-leaved Isodon, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in whorled clusters, often in shades of purple or blue. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
