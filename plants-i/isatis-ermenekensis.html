Isatis ermenekensis

Isatis ermenekensis, commonly known as the Armenian woad, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native range.
