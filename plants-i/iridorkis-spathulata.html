Iridorkis spathulata

Iridorkis spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf iridorkis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialty nurseries.
