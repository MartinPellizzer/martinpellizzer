Ipomoea setosa

Ipomoea setosa, commonly known as the curly bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and can be invasive, making it a common weed in gardens and disturbed soils.

Ipomoea setosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value but requires careful management due to its aggressive growth.
