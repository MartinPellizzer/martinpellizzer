Impatiens briartii

Impatiens briartii, commonly known as the Briart's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
