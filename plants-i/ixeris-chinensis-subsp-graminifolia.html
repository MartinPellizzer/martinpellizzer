Ixeris chinensis subsp. graminifolia

Ixeris chinensis subsp.

graminifolia, commonly known as the slender golden groundsel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia. It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in garden settings.
