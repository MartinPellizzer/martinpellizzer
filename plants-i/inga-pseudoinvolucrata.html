Inga pseudoinvolucrata

Inga pseudoinvolucrata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a valuable species for reforestation. Its fruit produces a sticky, aromatic pulp that is used in traditional medicine and as a food source for wildlife.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility in its native habitats.
