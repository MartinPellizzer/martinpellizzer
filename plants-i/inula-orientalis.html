Inula orientalis

Inula orientalis, commonly known as the oriental elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
