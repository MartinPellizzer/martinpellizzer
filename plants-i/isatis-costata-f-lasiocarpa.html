Isatis costata f. lasiocarpa

Isatis costata f. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy winged woad, is a variant of the woad plant native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive winged appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles and traditional medicine highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
