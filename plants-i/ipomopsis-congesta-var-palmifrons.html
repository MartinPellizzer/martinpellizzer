Ipomopsis congesta var. palmifrons

Ipomopsis congesta var. palmifrons, commonly known as the palmate ipomopsis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has palmately divided leaves, which give it its common name, and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
