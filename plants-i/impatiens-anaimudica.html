Impatiens anaimudica

Impatiens anaimudica, commonly known as Anaimudi Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the monsoon season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
