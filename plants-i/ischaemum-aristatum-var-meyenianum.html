Ischaemum aristatum var. meyenianum

Ischaemum aristatum var. meyenianum, commonly known as the meyenianum oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
