Indigofera gloriosa

Indigofera gloriosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its leaves are pinnate and its stems are often woody, making it a distinctive ornamental plant.

Indigofera gloriosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
