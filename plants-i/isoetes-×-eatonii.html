Isoetes × eatonii

Isoetes × eatonii, commonly known as the Eaton's watermilfoil, is a hybrid species within the genus Isoetes, which belongs to the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its spiky, sword-like leaves that are typically submerged or emergent in aquatic environments. The plant is notable for its unique reproductive structures, including sporangia that produce spores for propagation. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and lakes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in aquatic gardens and wetland restoration projects.
