Ischaemum crassipes var. hainanense

Ischaemum crassipes var. hainanense, commonly known as the Hainan roughgrass, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in China's Hainan Island.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its erect, sometimes hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of ecological study.
