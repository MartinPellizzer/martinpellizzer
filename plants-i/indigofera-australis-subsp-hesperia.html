Indigofera australis subsp. hesperia

Indigofera australis subsp.

hesperia, commonly known as the coastal indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

It is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
