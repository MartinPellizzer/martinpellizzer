Isatis kotschyana

Isatis kotschyana, commonly known as the Turkestan woad, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing spherical seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its historical use in dye production has contributed to its botanical significance.
