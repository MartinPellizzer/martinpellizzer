Ixora subcuneata

Ixora subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-angled ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

Ixora subcuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
