Ixora mauritiana

Ixora mauritiana, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red inflorescences, which bloom in dense clusters during the rainy season. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ixora mauritiana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and horticulture.
