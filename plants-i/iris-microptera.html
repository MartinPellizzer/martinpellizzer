Iris microptera

Iris microptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Iris microptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
