Iresine aggregata

Iresine aggregata, commonly known as redroot ironplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, velvety foliage with deep red or purple hues, often contrasting with its green or variegated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Iresine aggregata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
