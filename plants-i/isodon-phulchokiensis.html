Isodon phulchokiensis

Isodon phulchokiensis, commonly known as the Thai lemongrass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, narrow leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Isodon phulchokiensis make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
