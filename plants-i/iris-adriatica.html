Iris adriatica

Iris adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies.

Iris adriatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
