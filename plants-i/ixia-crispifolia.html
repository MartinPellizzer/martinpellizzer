Ixia crispifolia

Ixia crispifolia, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Ixia crispifolia typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
