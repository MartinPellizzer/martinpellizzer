Indigofera fractiflexa subsp. augustensis

Indigofera fractiflexa subsp.

augustensis, commonly known as the slender indigo. It is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential use in traditional medicine.
