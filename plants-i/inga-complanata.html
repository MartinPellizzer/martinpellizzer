Inga complanata

Inga complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are often yellow or white in color.

Inga complanata is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
