Ingenhoussia rugosa

Ingenhoussia rugosa, commonly known as the rough-hairied hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features woody stems covered in rough, hairy texture, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with a prominent central stigma. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a rough surface.

Ingenhoussia rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
