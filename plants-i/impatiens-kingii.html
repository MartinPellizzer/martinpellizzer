Impatiens kingii

Impatiens kingii, commonly known as the King's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on long, slender stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
