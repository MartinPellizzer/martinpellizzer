Iris pyrenaea

Iris pyrenaea, commonly known as the Pyrenean iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers with three upright petals. It is part of the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
