Iris heldreichii

Iris heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, typical of the iris family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces a tall flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height. Iris heldreichii prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
