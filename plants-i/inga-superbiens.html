Inga superbiens

Inga superbiens, commonly known as the royal acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Inga superbiens is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
