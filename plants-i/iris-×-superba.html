Iris × superba

Iris × superba, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, lavender-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, making it well-suited for wetland environments or garden areas with good drainage. Its foliage consists of sword-like, grassy leaves that are dark green and often form a dense basal rosette.

Iris × superba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral displays.
