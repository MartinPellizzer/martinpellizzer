Iris sanguinea var. sanguinea

Iris sanguinea var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking flowers with three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals, typically in shades of red or purple. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and the flowers are often used in floral arrangements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to well-drained soils.
