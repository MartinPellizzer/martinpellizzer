Ipomoea alba

Ipomoea alba, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
