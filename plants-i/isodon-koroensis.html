Isodon koroensis

Isodon koroensis, commonly known as the Japanese verbena, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Isodon koroensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
