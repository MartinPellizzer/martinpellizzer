Ipomoea githaginea

Ipomoea githaginea, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue, purple, and pink. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that open in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Ipomoea githaginea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
