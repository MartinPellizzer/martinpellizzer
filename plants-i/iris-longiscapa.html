Iris longiscapa

Iris longiscapa, commonly known as the long-leaved iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting a cluster of striking blue to violet flowers. The leaves are long and narrow, often reaching lengths of up to 40 centimeters, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
