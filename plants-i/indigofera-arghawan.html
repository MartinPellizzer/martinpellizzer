Indigofera arghawan

Indigofera arghawan, commonly known as Persian indigo, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for producing a blue dye, historically used in textile industries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native habitats.
