Indigofera microphylla

Indigofera microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
