Ischarum eximium

Ischarum eximium, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the European Alps and other mountainous areas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold, dry environments. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited habitat, Ischarum eximium is considered a rare and endangered plant in many regions.
