Iris plicata

Iris plicata, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in wet or moist environments.

Iris plicata is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
