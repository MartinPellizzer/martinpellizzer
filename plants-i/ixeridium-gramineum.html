Ixeridium gramineum

Ixeridium gramineum, commonly known as the grassy ixeridium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

Ixeridium gramineum is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
