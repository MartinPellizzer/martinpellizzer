Inga polyantha

Inga polyantha, commonly known as the pink-tinged inga, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with compound leaves and pod fruits. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
