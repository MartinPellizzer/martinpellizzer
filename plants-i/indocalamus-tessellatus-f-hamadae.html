Indocalamus tessellatus f. hamadae

Indocalamus tessellatus f. hamadae, commonly known as the Hamada's bamboo, is a rare variant of the tessellated bamboo.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely textured, and often variegated culms. The plant is native to China and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a soft, grass-like texture.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
