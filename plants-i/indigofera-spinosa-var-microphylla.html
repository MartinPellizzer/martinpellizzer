Indigofera spinosa var. microphylla

Indigofera spinosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf indigo or small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional dyeing practices.
