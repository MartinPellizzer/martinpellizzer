Inula salicina var. minipetala

Inula salicina var. minipetala, commonly known as the small-leaved elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is attributed to its potential anti-inflammatory and expectorant properties.
