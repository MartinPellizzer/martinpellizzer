Iberis semperflorens flore-pleno

Iberis semperflorens flore-pleno, commonly known as the flowering candytuft, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom profusely throughout the spring and early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it an attractive ornamental plant.
