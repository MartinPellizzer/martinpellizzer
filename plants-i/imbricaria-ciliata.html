Imbricaria ciliata

Imbricaria ciliata, commonly known as the fringed leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with fine, ciliate margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
