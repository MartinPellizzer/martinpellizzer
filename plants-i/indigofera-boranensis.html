Indigofera boranensis

Indigofera boranensis, commonly known as Boran indigo, is a leguminous plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
