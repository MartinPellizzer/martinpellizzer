Ipomoea incana var. melanoclada

Ipomoea incana var. melanoclada, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a distinctive dark throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its rapid growth and vigorous climbing habit make it a common ornamental and invasive species in certain regions.
