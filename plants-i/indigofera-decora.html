Indigofera decora

Indigofera decora, commonly known as the ornamental indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Indigofera decora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
