Ixia flabellifolia

Ixia flabellifolia, commonly known as the Orange Kaffir lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces showy, orange-red flowers that resemble torches, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Ixia flabellifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
