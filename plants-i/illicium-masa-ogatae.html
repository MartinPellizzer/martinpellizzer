Illicium masa-ogatae

Illicium masa-ogatae, commonly known as the Japanese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark, glossy berries. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like anethole, are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Illicium masa-ogatae thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.
