Indigofera schimperi var. parvifoliolata

Indigofera schimperi var. parvifoliolata, commonly known as the small-leaved indigo plant, is a species of legume native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and pinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in compact clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, twisted pods, and it is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its historical use in dye production and as a forage plant for livestock.
