Inga brevipedicellata

Inga brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits, which are dispersed by animals.

The species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of timber and fodder.
