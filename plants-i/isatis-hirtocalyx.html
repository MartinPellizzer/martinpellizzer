Isatis hirtocalyx

Isatis hirtocalyx, commonly known as the hairy-leaf woad, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The plant is known for its historical use in producing indigo dye, similar to other members of the genus Isatis.
