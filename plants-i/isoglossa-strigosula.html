Isoglossa strigosula

Isoglossa strigosula, commonly known as the hairy isoglossa, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a similar hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged look. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late spring.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
