Indigofera lepida

Indigofera lepida, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Indigofera lepida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
