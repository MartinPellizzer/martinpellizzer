Ipomoea cymosa

Ipomoea cymosa, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread along supports. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and attractive blooms make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
