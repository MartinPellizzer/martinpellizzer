Iris versicolor var. virginica

Iris versicolor var. virginica, commonly known as the Virginia blue flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white stripe down the center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizome, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
