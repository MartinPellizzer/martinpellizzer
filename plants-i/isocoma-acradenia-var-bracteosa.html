Isocoma acradenia var. bracteosa

Isocoma acradenia var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
