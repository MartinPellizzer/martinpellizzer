Isolepis australiensis

Isolepis australiensis, commonly known as the Australian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wet or waterlogged soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered flowering spikes.

Isolepis australiensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
