Imperata cylindrica f. pallida

Imperata cylindrica f. pallida, commonly known as purple muhly, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically pale purple to white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often overlooked. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
