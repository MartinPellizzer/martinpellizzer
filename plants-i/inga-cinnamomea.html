Inga cinnamomea

Inga cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, pendulous pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Inga cinnamomea plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
