Indigofera decora var. cooperi

Indigofera decora var. cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's ornamental indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
