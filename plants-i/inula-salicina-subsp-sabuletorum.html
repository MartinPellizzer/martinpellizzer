Inula salicina subsp. sabuletorum

Inula salicina subsp.

sabuletorum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a branched stem and produces small, oval fruits called cypsela.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in full sun.
