Ibatia woodii

Ibatia woodii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family.
