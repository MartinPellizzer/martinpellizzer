Icomum paradoxum

Icomum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features a distinct pattern of veins and a specialized sheath. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in shaded areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense inflorescences that are adapted for wind pollination.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific habitat requirements, Icomum paradoxum is considered a delicate and ecologically significant species.
