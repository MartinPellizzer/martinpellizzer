Isoberlinia scheffleri

Isoberlinia scheffleri, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, brown seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
