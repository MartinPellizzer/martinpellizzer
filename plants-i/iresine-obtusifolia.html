Iresine obtusifolia

Iresine obtusifolia, commonly known as the purple flame plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often red or purple, bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Iresine obtusifolia is also known for its ability to produce striking, showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.
