Iridorkis miniata

Iridorkis miniata, commonly known as the miniature iris, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms during the spring and early summer months. Iridorkis miniata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
