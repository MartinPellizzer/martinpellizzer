Ixia crocata var. nigromaculata

Ixia crocata var. nigromaculata, commonly known as the black-spotted oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers with distinctive black spots. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking color contrast and unique floral pattern.
