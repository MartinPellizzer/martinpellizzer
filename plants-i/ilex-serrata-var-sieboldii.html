Ilex serrata var. sieboldii

Ilex serrata var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Siebold's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds and can persist on the plant for several months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and year-round foliage make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
