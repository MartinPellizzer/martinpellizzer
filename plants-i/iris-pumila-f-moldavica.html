Iris pumila f. moldavica

Iris pumila f. moldavica, commonly known as the Moldavian dwarf iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate blue to violet flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact size and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is a subspecies of Iris pumila, distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral characteristics.

This iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
