Iris tarhunensis

Iris tarhunensis, commonly known as the Tarhun iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers have three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

Iris tarhunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
