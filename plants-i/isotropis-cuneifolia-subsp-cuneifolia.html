Isotropis cuneifolia subsp. cuneifolia

Isotropis cuneifolia subsp.

cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf isotropis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to arid environments.
