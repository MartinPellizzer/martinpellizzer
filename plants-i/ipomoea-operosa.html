Ipomoea operosa

Ipomoea operosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the morning. Ipomoea operosa is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
