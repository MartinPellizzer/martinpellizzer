Indigofera potaninii

Indigofera potaninii, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
