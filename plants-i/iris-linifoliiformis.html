Iris linifoliiformis

Iris linifoliiformis, commonly known as the slender-leaf iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense clumps over time.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
