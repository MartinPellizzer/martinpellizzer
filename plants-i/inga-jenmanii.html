Inga jenmanii

Inga jenmanii, commonly known as the white inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth, climbing habit, and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense, drooping clusters.

Inga jenmanii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
