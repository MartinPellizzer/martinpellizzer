Isoetes neoguineensis

Isoetes neoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea waterwheel, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged spore-bearing leaves that form a dense, wheel-like structure. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. Its spores are released into the water and can germinate under suitable conditions, contributing to the plant's reproductive cycle.

Isoetes neoguineensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
