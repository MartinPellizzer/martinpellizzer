Indigofera gracilis var. genuina

Indigofera gracilis var. genuina, commonly known as the slender indigo or narrowleaf indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is notable for its historical use in dyeing textiles with indigo, although it is less commonly cultivated today compared to other indigo species.
