Ipomoea clarkei

Ipomoea clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often twining around supports. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea clarkei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
