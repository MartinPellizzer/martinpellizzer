Isotoma fluviatilis subsp. fluviatilis

Isotoma fluviatilis subsp.

fluviatilis, commonly known as the river sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and riparian habitats. It features slender, ascending stems with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found along riverbanks and lake shores.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
