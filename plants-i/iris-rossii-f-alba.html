Iris rossii f. alba

Iris rossii f. alba, commonly known as the White Ross iris, is a botanical variant of the Ross iris species.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast sharply with its deep green foliage. Native to the mountainous regions of Mexico, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The flowers typically bloom in late spring, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanists alike.
