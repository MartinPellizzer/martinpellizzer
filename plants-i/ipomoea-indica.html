Ipomoea indica

Ipomoea indica, commonly known as the morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
