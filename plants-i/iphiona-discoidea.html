Iphiona discoidea

Iphiona discoidea, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble a ghostly face, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its specialized habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, Iphiona discoidea is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
