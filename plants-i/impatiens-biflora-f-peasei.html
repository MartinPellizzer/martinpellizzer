Impatiens biflora f. peasei

Impatiens biflora f. peasei, commonly known as the Pease's twin-flowered impatiens, is a unique variant of the species Impatiens biflora.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a deep red upper lip and a pale yellow lower lip. This plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is well-suited for garden borders or hanging baskets.
