Ilex aquifolium var. rotundifolia

Ilex aquifolium var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
