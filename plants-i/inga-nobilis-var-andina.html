Inga nobilis var. andina

Inga nobilis var. andina, commonly known as the Andean velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, clustered inflorescences and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
