Impatiens kojeensis

Impatiens kojeensis, commonly known as the Koji impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of central Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing near the forest floor.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
