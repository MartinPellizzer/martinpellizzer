Impatiens commelinoides

Impatiens commelinoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Impatiens commelinoides is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
