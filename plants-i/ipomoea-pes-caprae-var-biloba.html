Ipomoea pes-caprae var. biloba

Ipomoea pes-caprae var. biloba, commonly known as sea grape or coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Its adaptability to salt spray and well-drained conditions makes it a common species in coastal vegetation.
