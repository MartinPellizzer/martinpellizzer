Iris × pseudopumilioides

Iris × pseudopumilioides, commonly known as the hybrid iris, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Iris pumila and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often violet or blue-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and is native to regions of Eurasia, including parts of Russia and Central Asia. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
