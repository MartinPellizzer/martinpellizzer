Illicium macranthum

Illicium macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered Chinese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pungent fruits that resemble star anise, though it is not a true star anise species. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes various essential oils and compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Illicium macranthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
