Isatis gaubae

Isatis gaubae, commonly known as the African woad, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. It produces spherical, dry fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds.

Isatis gaubae is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
