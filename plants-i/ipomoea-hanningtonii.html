Ipomoea hanningtonii

Ipomoea hanningtonii, commonly known as the Hannington morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, making it an important species in its native ecosystems.
