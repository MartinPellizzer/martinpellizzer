Ipomoea forsteri

Ipomoea forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Ipomoea forsteri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
