Ixia vitellina

Ixia vitellina, commonly known as the yellow fire lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. Ixia vitellina thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce multiple flower spikes from a single stem.
