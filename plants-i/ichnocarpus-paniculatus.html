Ichnocarpus paniculatus

Ichnocarpus paniculatus, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, also known as the legume or bean family. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
