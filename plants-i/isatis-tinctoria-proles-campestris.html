Isatis tinctoria proles campestris

Isatis tinctoria proles campestris, commonly known as wild mustard, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by the production of seeds in the form of dry, three-lobed pods.
