Iberis sempervirens var. macropetala

Iberis sempervirens var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, low-growing foliage with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardiness.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it beneficial for wildlife.
