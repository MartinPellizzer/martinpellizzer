Indigofera cassioides var. mussooriensis

Indigofera cassioides var. mussooriensis, commonly known as the Himalayan indigo, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
