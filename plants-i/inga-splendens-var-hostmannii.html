Inga splendens var. hostmannii

Inga splendens var. hostmannii, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, creating a dramatic visual effect. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils.
