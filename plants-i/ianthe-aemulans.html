Ianthe aemulans

Ianthe aemulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that resemble other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Ianthe aemulans thrives in warm, moist conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
