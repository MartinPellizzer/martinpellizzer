Ipomoea dichroa

Ipomoea dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a contrasting base. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often using its tendrils to support itself.

Ipomoea dichroa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
