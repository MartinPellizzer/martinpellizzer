Ipomoea tuboides f. irregularis

Ipomoea tuboides f. irregularis, commonly known as the irregular form of the tubular morning glory, is a variant of the species characterized by its unique floral morphology.

This plant is a herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas, featuring heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to pink and are known for their short blooming period in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The irregular form is distinguished by its unusual petal arrangement, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
