Indigofera trita var. flaccida

Indigofera trita var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind or water.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
