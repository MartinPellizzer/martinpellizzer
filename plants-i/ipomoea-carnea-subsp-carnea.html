Ipomoea carnea subsp. carnea

Ipomoea carnea subsp.

carnea, commonly known as scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the early morning.

The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and is a vigorous climber with hairy stems. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Its rapid growth makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
