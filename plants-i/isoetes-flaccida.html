Isoetes flaccida

Isoetes flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located on specialized stems called sporophylls. Isoetes flaccida is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an important subject in the study of aquatic botany.
