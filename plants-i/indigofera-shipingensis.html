Indigofera shipingensis

Indigofera shipingensis, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces indigo dye, which has been historically used in textile industries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional dyeing practices.
