Ipomoea barlerioides

Ipomoea barlerioides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea barlerioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
