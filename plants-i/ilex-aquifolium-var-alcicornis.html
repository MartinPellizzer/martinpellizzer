Ilex aquifolium var. alcicornis

Ilex aquifolium var. alcicornis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with spiny margins. It produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
