Inga corcondiana

Inga corcondiana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in diverse habitats.
