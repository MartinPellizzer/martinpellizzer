Ipomoea nematoloba

Ipomoea nematoloba, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white or purple flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces egg-shaped berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Ipomoea nematoloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
