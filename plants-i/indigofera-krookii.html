Indigofera krookii

Indigofera krookii, commonly known as the Korean indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Korea and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Indigofera krookii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
