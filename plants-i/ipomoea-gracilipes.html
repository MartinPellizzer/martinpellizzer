Ipomoea gracilipes

Ipomoea gracilipes, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Ipomoea gracilipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
