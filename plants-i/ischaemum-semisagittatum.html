Ischaemum semisagittatum

Ischaemum semisagittatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Ischaemum semisagittatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
