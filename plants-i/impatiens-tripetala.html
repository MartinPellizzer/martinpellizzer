Impatiens tripetala

Impatiens tripetala, commonly known as the three-lobed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid forests.

Impatiens tripetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
