Isanthus multiflorus

Isanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the multibloom isanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
