Indigofera erubescens

Indigofera erubescens, commonly known as the red clover plant, is a leguminous species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a climbing habit and produces elongated pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional uses.
