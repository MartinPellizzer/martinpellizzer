Iris tingitana

Iris tingitana, commonly known as the Canary Island iris, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense basal rosette.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
