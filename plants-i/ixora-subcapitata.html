Ixora subcapitata

Ixora subcapitata, commonly known as the dwarf ixora, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant produces showy inflorescences that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ixora subcapitata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
