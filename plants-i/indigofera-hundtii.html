Indigofera hundtii

Indigofera hundtii, commonly known as the African indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera hundtii is also valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
