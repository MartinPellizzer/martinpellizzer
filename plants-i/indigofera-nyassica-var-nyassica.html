Indigofera nyassica var. nyassica

Indigofera nyassica var. nyassica, commonly known as African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

Its historical significance lies in its use for dyeing textiles with a deep blue color.
