Ilex dumosa f. angustifolia

Ilex dumosa f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
