Ipomoea turbinata

Ipomoea turbinata, commonly known as the turban squash or whirligig vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, tubular fruits resembling small squashes. Ipomoea turbinata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
