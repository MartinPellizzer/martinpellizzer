Inga bangii

Inga bangii, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "sweet sop," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, with a yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and dense foliage, making it a valuable species for agroforestry.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
