Impatiens rectirostrata

Impatiens rectirostrata, commonly known as the narrow-beaked impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive elongated, narrow petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a unique beak-like shape at the tip of the petals.

Impatiens rectirostrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
