Isolepis cuspidata

Isolepis cuspidata, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a spike, composed of numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheath at the base, and it often forms dense stands that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Isolepis cuspidata is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
