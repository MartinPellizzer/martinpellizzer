Ixia viridiflora var. cana

Ixia viridiflora var. cana, commonly known as the green-flowered ixia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of striking greenish-yellow flowers, which are tubular and often have a subtle fragrance. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
