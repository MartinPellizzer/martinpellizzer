Isatis lanceolata

Isatis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf woad, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in prairie habitats.

Isatis lanceolata is also valued for its historical use in dye production and as a potential medicinal plant.
