Indigofera commixta

Indigofera commixta, commonly known as the mixed indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces seed pods that contain small, round seeds used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
