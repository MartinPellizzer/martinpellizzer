Indigofera venusta

Indigofera venusta, commonly known as the velvet indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it often grows as a shrub or small tree.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
