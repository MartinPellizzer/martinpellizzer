Ipomoea hildebrandtii subsp. grantii

Ipomoea hildebrandtii subsp.

grantii, commonly known as the Grant's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using its tendrils to support itself.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
