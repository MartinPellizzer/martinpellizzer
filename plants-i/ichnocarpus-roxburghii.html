Ichnocarpus roxburghii

Ichnocarpus roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian hog plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds.
