Ilex anodonta

Ilex anodonta, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant's botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant.
