Ipomoea purga

Ipomoea purga, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea purga is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.
