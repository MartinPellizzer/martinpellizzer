Inga leptoloba

Inga leptoloba, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in humid, lowland forests.

The species is valued for its wood and as a source of food for various animals.
