Ipomoea inamoena

Ipomoea inamoena, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a vigorous climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals that open in the morning and close by midday.

Ipomoea inamoena is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
