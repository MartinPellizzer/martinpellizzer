Inga balsamica

Inga balsamica, commonly known as the balsam tree or sweet acacia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous, aromatic bark that has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Inga balsamica is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
