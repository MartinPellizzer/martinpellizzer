Ionidium indicum

Ionidium indicum, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, deciduous tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive star-shaped fruit. The fruit has a crisp, acidic flavor and is rich in vitamin C, making it a popular fruit in many cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Achariaceae and is characterized by its unique morphology and adaptability to tropical climates.
