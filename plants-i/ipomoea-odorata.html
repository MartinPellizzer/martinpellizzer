Ipomoea odorata

Ipomoea odorata, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that range in color from green to variegated patterns. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible tubers.

Ipomoea odorata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
