Isolepis tenerrima

Isolepis tenerrima, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Isolepis tenerrima thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life contributes to its ecological significance.
