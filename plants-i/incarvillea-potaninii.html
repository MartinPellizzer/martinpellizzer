Incarvillea potaninii

Incarvillea potaninii, commonly known as the Chinese flame vine, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial in suitable climates.
