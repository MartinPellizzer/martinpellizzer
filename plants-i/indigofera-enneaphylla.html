Indigofera enneaphylla

Indigofera enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its striking blue to violet flowers. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
