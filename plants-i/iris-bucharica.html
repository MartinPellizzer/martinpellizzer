Iris bucharica

Iris bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinct yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the base. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Iris bucharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
