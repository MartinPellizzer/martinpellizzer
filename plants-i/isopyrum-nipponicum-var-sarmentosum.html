Isopyrum nipponicum var. sarmentosum

Isopyrum nipponicum var. sarmentosum, commonly known as the Japanese crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features a dense, low-growing habit with slender, often creeping stems that form a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, basal, and trifoliate, with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.
