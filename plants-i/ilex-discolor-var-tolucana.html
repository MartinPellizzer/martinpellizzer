Ilex discolor var. tolucana

Ilex discolor var. tolucana, commonly known as the Tolucan holly, is a variant of the evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
