Ivodea trichocarpa var. lanceolata

Ivodea trichocarpa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a climbing plant belonging to the Araliaceae family, featuring dark green foliage with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbellate clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
