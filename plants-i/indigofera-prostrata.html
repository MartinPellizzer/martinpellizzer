Indigofera prostrata

Indigofera prostrata, commonly known as creeping indigo, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera prostrata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
