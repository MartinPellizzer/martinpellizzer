Ilex lonicerifolia var. hakkuensis

Ilex lonicerifolia var. hakkuensis, commonly known as Japanese holly or Hakku holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are ornamental and attract wildlife, particularly birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its dense, compact growth and year-round foliage.
