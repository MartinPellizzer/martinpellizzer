Inga lomatophylla var. bracteifera

Inga lomatophylla var. bracteifera, commonly known as the bracteate inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.
