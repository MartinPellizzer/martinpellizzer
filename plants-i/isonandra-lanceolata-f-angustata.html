Isonandra lanceolata f. angustata

Isonandra lanceolata f. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf isonandra, is a variant of the isonandra genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure, which set it apart from other members of the genus.

This fern-like plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
