Ipomoea bussei

Ipomoea bussei, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, tropical environments. It is often found growing along stream banks and in shaded forest areas.

Ipomoea bussei is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in Hawaiian ecosystems.
