Inga pellicula

Inga pellicula, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.

The silky inga is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
