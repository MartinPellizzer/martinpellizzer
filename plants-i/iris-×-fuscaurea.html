Iris × fuscaurea

Iris × fuscaurea, commonly known as the hybrid iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Due to its hybrid nature, it may exhibit a wide range of floral colors and forms.
