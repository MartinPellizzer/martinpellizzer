Inga fagifolia var. intermedia

Inga fagifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems.
