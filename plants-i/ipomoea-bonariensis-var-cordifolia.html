Ipomoea bonariensis var. cordifolia

Ipomoea bonariensis var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically deep green and have a slightly velvety texture. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals that range in color from pale blue to violet. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and naturalized areas.
