Ipomoea nepalensis

Ipomoea nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ipomoea nepalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
