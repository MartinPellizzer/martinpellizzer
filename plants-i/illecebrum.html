Illecebrum

Illecebrum, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the dandelion family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its spiny, mat-forming growth habit and is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Illecebrum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it both a resilient weed and a subject of ecological study.
