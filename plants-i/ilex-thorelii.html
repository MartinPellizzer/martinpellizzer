Ilex thorelii

Ilex thorelii, commonly known as the Thorell's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Ilex thorelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shady garden settings.
