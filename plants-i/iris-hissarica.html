Iris hissarica

Iris hissarica, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms, which typically appear in late spring to early summer.
