Impatiens × pacifica

Impatiens × pacifica, commonly known as the New Guinea impatiens, is a hybrid species within the genus Impatiens.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. It typically grows as an upright shrub with broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Impatiens × pacifica is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in partial shade.
