Indigofera omissa var. trifoliolata

Indigofera omissa var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
