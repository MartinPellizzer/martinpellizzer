Ipomoea calidicola

Ipomoea calidicola, commonly known as the hot weather morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its vines can climb and spread rapidly, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ipomoea calidicola is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its rapid growth rate.
