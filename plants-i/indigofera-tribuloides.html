Indigofera tribuloides

Indigofera tribuloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It produces pods that contain small, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
