Indigofera urostachya

Indigofera urostachya, commonly known as mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to green flowers arranged in racemes, which are often followed by slender, pod-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
