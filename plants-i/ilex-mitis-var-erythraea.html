Ilex mitis var. erythraea

Ilex mitis var. erythraea, commonly known as the red-berried holly, is a variant of the holly species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas. The plant is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, bright red berries that persist through the winter. Its botanical features include showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
