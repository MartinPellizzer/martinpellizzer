Ionidium monopetalum

Ionidium monopetalum, commonly known as the single-petaled orchid, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its single, showy petal that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, showcasing vibrant colors that attract specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, Ionidium monopetalum is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
