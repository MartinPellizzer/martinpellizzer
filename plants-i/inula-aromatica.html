Inula aromatica

Inula aromatica, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

Inula aromatica is often grown for its ornamental value and has a mild, sweet scent.
