Ixia dubia

Ixia dubia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that are often mistaken for grasses.

Ixia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
