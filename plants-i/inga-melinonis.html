Inga melinonis

Inga melinonis, commonly known as the pink inga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
