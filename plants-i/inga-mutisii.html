Inga mutisii

Inga mutisii, commonly known as the Amazonian velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it ecologically significant in forest ecosystems.

Inga mutisii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic uses.
