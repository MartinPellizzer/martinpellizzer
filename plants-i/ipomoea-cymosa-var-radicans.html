Ipomoea cymosa var. radicans

Ipomoea cymosa var. radicans, commonly known as creeping blue morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.
