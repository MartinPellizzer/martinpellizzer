Ixora thyrsifolia

Ixora thyrsifolia, commonly known as the clustered ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, usually red or orange, which bloom throughout the year in tropical climates. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

Ixora thyrsifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge due to its dense growth habit and vibrant floral display.
