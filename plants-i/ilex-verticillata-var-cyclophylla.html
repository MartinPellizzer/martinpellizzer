Ilex verticillata var. cyclophylla

Ilex verticillata var. cyclophylla, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls of three to five, giving it a dense, rounded appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
