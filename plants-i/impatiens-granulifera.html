Impatiens granulifera

Impatiens granulifera, commonly known as the granular impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Impatiens granulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
