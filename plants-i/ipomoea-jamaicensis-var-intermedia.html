Ipomoea jamaicensis var. intermedia

Ipomoea jamaicensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the Jamaican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and is often found growing in moist, tropical environments. It is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself.

The flowers are known for their fragrance and are often used in traditional medicine.
