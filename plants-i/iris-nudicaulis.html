Iris nudicaulis

Iris nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender iris, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers with a distinct three-parted lip. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer.

Iris nudicaulis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
