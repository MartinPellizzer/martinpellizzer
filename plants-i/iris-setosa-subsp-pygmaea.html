Iris setosa subsp. pygmaea

Iris setosa subsp.

pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf iris, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring slender, grass-like leaves and delicate blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine or rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its hardiness in colder climates.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
