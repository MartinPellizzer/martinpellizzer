Ixora arenosa

Ixora arenosa, commonly known as the sand ixora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Ixora arenosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
