Ida reichenbachii var. alba

Ida reichenbachii var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered ida, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its botanical name honors the botanist Friedrich von Reichenbach, while the variety name 'alba' refers to its pale or white flower color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
