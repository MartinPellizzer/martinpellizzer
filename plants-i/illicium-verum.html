Illicium verum

Illicium verum, commonly known as star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China, Vietnam, and other parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruit, which contains numerous oil-rich seeds. The plant belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is valued for its essential oils, particularly shikimic acid, used in the production of antiviral medications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while the stems are woody and branched.

Illicium verum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
