Ipomoea debilis

Ipomoea debilis, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple and bloom in the morning. Ipomoea debilis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and delicate appearance.
