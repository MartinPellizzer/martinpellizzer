Iberis bernardiana

Iberis bernardiana, commonly known as the Bernard's candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bright white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
