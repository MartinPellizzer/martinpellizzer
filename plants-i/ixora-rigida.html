Ixora rigida

Ixora rigida, commonly known as the rigid flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright red or orange-red flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. Ixora rigida thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
