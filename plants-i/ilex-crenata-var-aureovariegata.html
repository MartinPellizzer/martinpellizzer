Ilex crenata var. aureovariegata

Ilex crenata var. aureovariegata, commonly known as Japanese yew or golden variegated holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow variegation pattern along the edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense foliage and year-round color make it an attractive addition to gardens and landscapes.
