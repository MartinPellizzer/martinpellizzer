Inga humboldtiana

Inga humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Inga humboldtiana also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
