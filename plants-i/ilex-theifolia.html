Ilex theifolia

Ilex theifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex theifolia thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense growth habit also provides excellent habitat for various wildlife species.
