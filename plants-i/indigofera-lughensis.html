Indigofera lughensis

Indigofera lughensis, commonly known as the Sudanese indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
