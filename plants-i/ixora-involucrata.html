Ixora involucrata

Ixora involucrata, commonly known as the clustered flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora involucrata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
