Ionopsis longicaulis

Ionopsis longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ionopsis longicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
