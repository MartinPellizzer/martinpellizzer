Ipomoea jamesonii

Ipomoea jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea jamesonii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
