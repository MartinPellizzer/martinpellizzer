Indigofera gilletii

Indigofera gilletii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The seeds of Indigofera gilletii are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
