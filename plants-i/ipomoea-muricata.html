Ipomoea muricata

Ipomoea muricata, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea muricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
