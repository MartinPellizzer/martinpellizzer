Iberis umbellata

Iberis umbellata, commonly known as candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with silvery-gray foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
