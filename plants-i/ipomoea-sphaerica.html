Ipomoea sphaerica

Ipomoea sphaerica, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique spherical or globular shape, which is formed by the tightly packed flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Ipomoea sphaerica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
