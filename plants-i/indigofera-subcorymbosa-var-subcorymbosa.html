Indigofera subcorymbosa var. subcorymbosa

Indigofera subcorymbosa var. subcorymbosa, commonly known as the African indigo.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
