Isodon rubescens

Isodon rubescens, commonly known as red centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Isodon rubescens is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
