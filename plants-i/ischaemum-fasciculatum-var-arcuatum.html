Ischaemum fasciculatum var. arcuatum

Ischaemum fasciculatum var. arcuatum, commonly known as the bentgrass or arcuate bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
