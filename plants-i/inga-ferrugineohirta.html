Inga ferrugineohirta

Inga ferrugineohirta, commonly known as the rusty-hairy inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The plant is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal uses.
