Ionidium graminifolium

Ionidium graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaved ionidium, is a rare flowering plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage.

It produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

Ionidium graminifolium is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
