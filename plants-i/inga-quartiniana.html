Inga quartiniana

Inga quartiniana, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
