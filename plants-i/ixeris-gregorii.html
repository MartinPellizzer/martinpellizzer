Ixeris gregorii

Ixeris gregorii, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Ixeris gregorii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
