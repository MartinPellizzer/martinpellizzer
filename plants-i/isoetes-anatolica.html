Isoetes anatolica

Isoetes anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian watermace, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky morphology with a tall, unbranched stem and a cluster of spore-bearing leaves at the top. Native to regions of Turkey and the Middle East, it thrives in wet, swampy environments. The plant is notable for its ancient lineage, belonging to one of the oldest groups of land plants.

Its spores are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitats.
