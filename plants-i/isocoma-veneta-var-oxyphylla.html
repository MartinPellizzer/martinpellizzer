Isocoma veneta var. oxyphylla

Isocoma veneta var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy foliage to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and its role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
