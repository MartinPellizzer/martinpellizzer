Iresine rotundifolia

Iresine rotundifolia, commonly known as redleaf or red-leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red foliage that contrasts strikingly with its small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are ovate to rounded in shape, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil.

Iresine rotundifolia is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
