Iva hayesiana

Iva hayesiana, commonly known as Hayes' beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
