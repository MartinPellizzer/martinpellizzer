Impatiens parvifolia

Impatiens parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Impatiens parvifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
