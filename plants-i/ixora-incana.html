Ixora incana

Ixora incana, commonly known as the white flame or white ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its floral structure consists of five petals and numerous stamens, giving it a dense, showy appearance.

Ixora incana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its persistent flowering and attractive foliage.
