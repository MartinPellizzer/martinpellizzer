Impatiens apsotis

Impatiens apsotis, commonly known as the yellow water impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a short, hairy stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests.

Impatiens apsotis is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
