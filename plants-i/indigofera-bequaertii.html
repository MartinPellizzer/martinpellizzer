Indigofera bequaertii

Indigofera bequaertii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, often branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its seeds are encased in elongated, hard pods, which are typical of the legume family.

Indigofera bequaertii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
