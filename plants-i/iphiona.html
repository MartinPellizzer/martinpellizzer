Iphiona

Iphiona, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a member of the Apocynaceae family, characterized by its striking, often colorful blooms.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with warm, humid climates. The plant typically features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, depending on the species. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Iphiona is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though some species contain toxic compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.
