Ilex chamaedryfolia var. mugiensis

Ilex chamaedryfolia var. mugiensis, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese inkberry, is a shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are ornamental and attract wildlife. It is often cultivated for its dense foliage and ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of soil conditions.
