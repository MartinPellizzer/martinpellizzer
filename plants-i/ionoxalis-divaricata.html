Ionoxalis divaricata

Ionoxalis divaricata, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple veining. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Ionoxalis divaricata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
