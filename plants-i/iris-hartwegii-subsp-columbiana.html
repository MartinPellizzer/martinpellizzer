Iris hartwegii subsp. columbiana

Iris hartwegii subsp.

columbiana, commonly known as the Columbian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its showy, violet-blue flowers bloom in early summer, often with a distinctive yellow or white stripe down the center.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native landscapes.
