Ipomoea batatilla

Ipomoea batatilla, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea batatilla is also a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other familiar species like morning glories and sweet potatoes.
