Isochoriste africana f. parvifolia

Isochoriste africana f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved African isochoriste, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
