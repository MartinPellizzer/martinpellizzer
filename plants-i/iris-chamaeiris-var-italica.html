Iris chamaeiris var. italica

Iris chamaeiris var. italica, commonly known as the Italian marsh iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens.

The species is characterized by its compact size and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
