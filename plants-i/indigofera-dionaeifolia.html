Indigofera dionaeifolia

Indigofera dionaeifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Indigofera dionaeifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.
