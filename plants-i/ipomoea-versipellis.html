Ipomoea versipellis

Ipomoea versipellis, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found growing along stream banks and in moist, shaded areas. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

Ipomoea versipellis is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
