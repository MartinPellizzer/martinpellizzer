Indigofera rhytidocarpa var. rhytidocarpa

Indigofera rhytidocarpa var. rhytidocarpa, commonly known as the African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
