Indigofera ixocarpa

Indigofera ixocarpa, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
