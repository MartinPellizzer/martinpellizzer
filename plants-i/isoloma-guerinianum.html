Isoloma guerinianum

Isoloma guerinianum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a prominent central boss. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Isoloma guerinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
