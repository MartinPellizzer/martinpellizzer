Inga amazonica var. lomatophylla

Inga amazonica var. lomatophylla, commonly known as the Amazonian inga, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rapid growth and dense foliage. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets that provide shade and support a diverse array of epiphytes. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators.

The tree also plays a vital ecological role in nutrient cycling due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
