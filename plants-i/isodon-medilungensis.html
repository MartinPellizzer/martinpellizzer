Isodon medilungensis

Isodon medilungensis, commonly known as the African goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Isodon medilungensis is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
