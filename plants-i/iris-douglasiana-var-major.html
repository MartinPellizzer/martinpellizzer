Iris douglasiana var. major

Iris douglasiana var. major, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, often near streams or in meadows. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant communities.
