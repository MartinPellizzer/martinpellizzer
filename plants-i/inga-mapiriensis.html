Inga mapiriensis

Inga mapiriensis, commonly known as the Mapiri inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical in shape.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and the plant produces leguminous pods that contain edible seeds.
