Iridorkis myriantha

Iridorkis myriantha, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Iridorkis myriantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display.
