Inga versicolor

Inga versicolor, commonly known as the variable inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Inga versicolor is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it an important component of the ecosystem.
