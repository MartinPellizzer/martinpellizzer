Impatiens scapiflora

Impatiens scapiflora, commonly known as the scapiflora impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
