Impatiens dicentra

Impatiens dicentra, commonly known as the bleeding heart, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, heart-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy to the touch. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique floral display and graceful appearance make it a beloved ornamental plant.
