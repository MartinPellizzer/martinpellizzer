Ipomopsis congesta var. crebrifolia

Ipomopsis congesta var. crebrifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved whorled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant features whorled leaves that are often densely clustered, giving it its varietal name "crebrifolia," meaning "dense-leaved.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
