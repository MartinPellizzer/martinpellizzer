Isolepis juncoides

Isolepis juncoides, commonly known as the narrow leaf tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense, tussock-like clusters. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in spike-like inflorescences.

Isolepis juncoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
