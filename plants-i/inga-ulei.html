Inga ulei

Inga ulei, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine.

Additionally, Inga ulei plays an important role in agroforestry due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and shade-providing properties.
