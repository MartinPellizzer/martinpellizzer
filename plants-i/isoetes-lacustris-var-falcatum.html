Isoetes lacustris var. falcatum

Isoetes lacustris var. falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporocarps, which are often found on the surface of the water. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
