Ixodia alata

Ixodia alata, commonly known as the winged spurwort, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Ixodia alata is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
