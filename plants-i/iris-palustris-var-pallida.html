Iris palustris var. pallida

Iris palustris var. pallida, commonly known as the pale marsh iris, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system, and its flowers are typically pale blue or white, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its bloom period occurs in early to mid-summer, adding a delicate floral display to its native environment.
