Ixia lineata

Ixia lineata, commonly known as the Orange-Ixia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Ixia lineata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
