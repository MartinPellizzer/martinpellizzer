Ipomoea pes-tigridis

Ipomoea pes-tigridis, commonly known as the Tigris morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, winding vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, known for its trumpet-shaped flowers and climbing habit.
