Indigofera cylindracea

Indigofera cylindracea, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically purple or blue, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera cylindracea is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
