Isopogon lanceolatus

Isopogon lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sandplain areas, preferring well-drained soils. Isopogon lanceolatus is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
