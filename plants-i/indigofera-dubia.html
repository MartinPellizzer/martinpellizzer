Indigofera dubia

Indigofera dubia, commonly known as the wild indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera dubia is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
