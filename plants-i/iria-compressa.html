Iria compressa

Iria compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Iria compressa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
