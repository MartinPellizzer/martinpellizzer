Ipomoea globosa

Ipomoea globosa, commonly known as the globular morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, round, inflated seed pods that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea globosa is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
