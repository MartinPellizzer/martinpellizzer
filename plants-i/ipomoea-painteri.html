Ipomoea painteri

Ipomoea painteri, commonly known as the painter's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a wide array of vibrant colors. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support its growth. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea painteri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
