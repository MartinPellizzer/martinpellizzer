Ipomoea batatas var. leucorrhiza

Ipomoea batatas var. leucorrhiza, commonly known as the white-veined sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its distinctive white veins on the leaves.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces tuberous roots that are typically pale yellow to white in color, differing from the more common orange varieties. Its foliage is broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible roots, which have a mild, sweet flavor.
