Isatis aptera

Isatis aptera, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbage and mustard. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.

Its hairy texture and compact growth habit make it distinctive among similar species.
