Iris setosa var. nasuensis

Iris setosa var. nasuensis, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a striking perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to violet flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
