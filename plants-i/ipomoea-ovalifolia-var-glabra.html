Ipomoea ovalifolia var. glabra

Ipomoea ovalifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in the morning. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and trail along supports.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
