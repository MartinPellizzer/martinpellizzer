Impatiens noli-tangere f. hemileuca

Impatiens noli-tangere f. hemileuca, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a hybrid form of the species Impatiens noli-tangere.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers, which contrast with its deep green, velvety foliage. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and tendency to spread through self-seeding.
