Isodon flabelliformis

Isodon flabelliformis, commonly known as the fan-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves with a distinctive, often serrated margin. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Isodon flabelliformis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
