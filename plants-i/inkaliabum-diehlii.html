Inkaliabum diehlii

Inkaliabum diehlii, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant is adapted to high salinity soils, making it a valuable species for reclaiming degraded land. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Inkaliabum diehlii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
