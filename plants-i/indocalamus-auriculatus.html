Indocalamus auriculatus

Indocalamus auriculatus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, golden-yellow sheen, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and fast growth rate, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping throughout its native range.
