Iris timofejewii

Iris timofejewii, commonly known as the Timofeyev iris, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, three-lobed seed pods that mature in the late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
