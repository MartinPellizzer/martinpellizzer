Ixia minima

Ixia minima, commonly known as the dwarf firecracker, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers. The plant produces bright orange or yellow blooms that resemble tiny firecrackers, hence its common name. Ixia minima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
