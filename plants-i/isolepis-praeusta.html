Isolepis praeusta

Isolepis praeusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features dense, cylindrical tussocks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isolepis praeusta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
