Indigofera cardiophylla

Indigofera cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet, often in clusters. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
