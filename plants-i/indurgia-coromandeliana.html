Indurgia coromandeliana

Indurgia coromandeliana, commonly known as the Coromandel orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate and showy floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Indurgia coromandeliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
