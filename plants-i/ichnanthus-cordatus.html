Ichnanthus cordatus

Ichnanthus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and delicate blossoms make it a popular choice for wetland gardens and bog landscapes.
