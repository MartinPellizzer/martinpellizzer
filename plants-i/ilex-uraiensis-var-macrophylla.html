Ilex uraiensis var. macrophylla

Ilex uraiensis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved holly, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical Ilex uraiensis. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its evergreen foliage and seasonal fruit display.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
