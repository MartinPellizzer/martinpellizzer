Ilex rivularis

Ilex rivularis, commonly known as the stream holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows along stream banks and moist woodlands, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments. The plant has small, dark green, leathery leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

Ilex rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
