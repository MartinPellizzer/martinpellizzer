Inga porcata

Inga porcata, commonly known as the "porcata" or "wild coffee," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that enclose edible seeds, often consumed by various bird and mammal species. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Inga porcata is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
