Inula ambigua

Inula ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Inula ambigua is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
