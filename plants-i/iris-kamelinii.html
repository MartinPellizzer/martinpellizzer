Iris kamelinii

Iris kamelinii, commonly known as the Kamelin's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed structure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces a tall flowering stem with a single bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Iris kamelinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
