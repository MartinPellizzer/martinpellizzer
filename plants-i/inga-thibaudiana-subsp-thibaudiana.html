Inga thibaudiana subsp. thibaudiana

Inga thibaudiana subsp.

thibaudiana, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
