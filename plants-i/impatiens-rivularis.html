Impatiens rivularis

Impatiens rivularis, commonly known as the water impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with high humidity.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions make it a useful plant for erosion control.
