Iris mandraliscae

Iris mandraliscae, commonly known as the Canary Island iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three spreading sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
