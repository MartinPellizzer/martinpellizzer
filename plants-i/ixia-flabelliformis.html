Ixia flabelliformis

Ixia flabelliformis, commonly known as the flame lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically orange-red in color, with a distinctive flared shape resembling a fan. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It blooms during the summer months and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixia flabelliformis prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
