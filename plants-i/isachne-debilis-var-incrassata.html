Isachne debilis var. incrassata

Isachne debilis var. incrassata, commonly known as the broadleaf isachne, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and inconspicuous.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
