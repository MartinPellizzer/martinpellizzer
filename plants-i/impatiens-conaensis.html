Impatiens conaensis

Impatiens conaensis, commonly known as the Ceylon impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and grows as an annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
