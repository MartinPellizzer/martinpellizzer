Imperata ovata

Imperata ovata, commonly known as the oval blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Imperata ovata is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, including sandy and disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it both a nuisance and a useful plant in certain ecological contexts.
