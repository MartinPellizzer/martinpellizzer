Inga rugosa

Inga rugosa, commonly known as the rough inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

Inga rugosa is often found in moist tropical forests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
