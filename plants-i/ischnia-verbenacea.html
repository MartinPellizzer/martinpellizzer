Ischnia verbenacea

Ischnia verbenacea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ischnia verbenacea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
