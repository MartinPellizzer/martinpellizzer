Iriartea durissima

Iriartea durissima, commonly known as the Durissima puya, is a striking plant native to the highlands of southern Ecuador.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, waxy, and often spiky leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
