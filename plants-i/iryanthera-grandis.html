Iryanthera grandis

Iryanthera grandis, commonly known as the giant spider flower, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble intricate spider webs, composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of several meters, with long, slender stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
