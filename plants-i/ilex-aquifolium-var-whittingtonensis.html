Ilex aquifolium var. whittingtonensis

Ilex aquifolium var. whittingtonensis, commonly known as the Whittington holly, is a rare variant of the common holly.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glossy, dark green leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds. It is native to a specific region in England and is considered a conservation priority due to its limited distribution.

The botanical features of this variant make it an important subject for ecological and horticultural study.
