Ipomoea barteri var. longisepala

Ipomoea barteri var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth.
