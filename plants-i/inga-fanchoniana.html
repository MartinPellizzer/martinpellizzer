Inga fanchoniana

Inga fanchoniana, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its use in reforestation projects due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
