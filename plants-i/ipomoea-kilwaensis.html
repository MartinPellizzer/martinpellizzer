Ipomoea kilwaensis

Ipomoea kilwaensis, commonly known as Kilwa morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found growing along sandy shores or in coastal dunes. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Ipomoea kilwaensis is adapted to saline environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.
