Inga campanulata

Inga campanulata, commonly known as the bell-flower inga, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Inga campanulata is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
