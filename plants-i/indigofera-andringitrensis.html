Indigofera andringitrensis

Indigofera andringitrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable growth. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological importance and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
