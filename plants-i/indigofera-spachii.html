Indigofera spachii

Indigofera spachii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
