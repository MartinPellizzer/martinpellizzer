Ipomoea incana

Ipomoea incana, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea incana is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to several meters in height.
