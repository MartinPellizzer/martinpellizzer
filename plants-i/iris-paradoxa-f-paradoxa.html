Iris paradoxa f. paradoxa

Iris paradoxa f. paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical iris, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
