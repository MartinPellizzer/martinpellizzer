Ipomoea quamoclit var. pectinata

Ipomoea quamoclit var. pectinata, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often appearing in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
