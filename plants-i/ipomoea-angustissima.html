Ipomoea angustissima

Ipomoea angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Ipomoea angustissima is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
