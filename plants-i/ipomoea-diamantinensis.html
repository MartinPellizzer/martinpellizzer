Ipomoea diamantinensis

Ipomoea diamantinensis, commonly known as the diamondleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that gives them a shiny appearance. It produces tendrils that help it climb and spread along support structures.

Ipomoea diamantinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
