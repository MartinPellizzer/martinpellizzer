Imperata cylindrica var. pedicellata

Imperata cylindrica var. pedicellata, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that produce clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common ornamental and invasive species in many regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats contribute to its ecological impact.
