Isoetes flaccida var. flaccida

Isoetes flaccida var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid water horsetail, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its slender, spore-bearing stems.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils along the edges of ponds and marshes. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its reproductive structures, called strobili, are located at the top of the stem and contain spores.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
