Iresine mexicana

Iresine mexicana, commonly known as Mexican flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, vividly colored bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Iresine mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant appearance.
