Iris afghanica

Iris afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its rhizomatous root system helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Iris afghanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
