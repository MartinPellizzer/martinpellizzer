Ipomoea pintoi

Ipomoea pintoi, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. Ipomoea pintoi is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
