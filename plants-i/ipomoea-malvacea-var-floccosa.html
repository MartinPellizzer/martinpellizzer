Ipomoea malvacea var. floccosa

Ipomoea malvacea var. floccosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces fluffy, cotton-like seed pods. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The varietal name "floccosa" refers to the fluffy appearance of its seed capsules.
