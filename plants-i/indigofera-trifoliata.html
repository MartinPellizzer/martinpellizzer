Indigofera trifoliata

Indigofera trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
