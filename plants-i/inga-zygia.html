Inga zygia

Inga zygia, commonly known as the "Java velvet tamarind," is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Inga zygia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in agroforestry systems.
