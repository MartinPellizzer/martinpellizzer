Ingonia

Ingonia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plants are typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments. Ingonia species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in their native habitats.
