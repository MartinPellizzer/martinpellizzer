Indigofera latipinna

Indigofera latipinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in sandy or coastal soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
