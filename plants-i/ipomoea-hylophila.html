Ipomoea hylophila

Ipomoea hylophila, commonly known as the silky leaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Ipomoea hylophila is a climbing plant that can grow up to several meters in length.
