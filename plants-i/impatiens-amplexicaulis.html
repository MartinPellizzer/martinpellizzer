Impatiens amplexicaulis

Impatiens amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, where the leaves are often clasping the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
