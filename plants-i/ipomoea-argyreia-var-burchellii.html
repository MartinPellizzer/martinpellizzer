Ipomoea argyreia var. burchellii

Ipomoea argyreia var. burchellii, commonly known as the silver leaf morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive silver or white veining. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
