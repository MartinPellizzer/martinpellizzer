Ilex bogorensis

Ilex bogorensis, commonly known as the Java holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex bogorensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and year-round greenery.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
