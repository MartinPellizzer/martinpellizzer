Isoetes macrospora f. hieroglyphica

Isoetes macrospora f. hieroglyphica, commonly known as the hieroglyphic water clover, is a rare aquatic fern belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate spore morphology, which resembles ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs, giving it its common name. This fern thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in still or slow-moving water with muddy substrates. Its reproductive structures include both microspores and macrospores, which are essential for its propagation.

The plant is often found in regions with high humidity and is a subject of interest in both botanical and paleontological studies.
