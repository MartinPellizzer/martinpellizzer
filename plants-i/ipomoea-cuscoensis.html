Ipomoea cuscoensis

Ipomoea cuscoensis, commonly known as the Cusco morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

Ipomoea cuscoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
