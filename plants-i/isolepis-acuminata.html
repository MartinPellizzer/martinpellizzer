Isolepis acuminata

Isolepis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in shallow water or moist soils.

Isolepis acuminata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
