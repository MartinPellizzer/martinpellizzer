Isoetes echinospora var. muricata

Isoetes echinospora var. muricata, commonly known as the spiny watermoss, is a member of the genus Isoetes, which includes species of spike mosses.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a spadix at the apex of the plant. The plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich substrates. Its leaves are often spirally arranged and can be either simple or branched, depending on the species.

The spores produced by this plant are adapted for dispersal by water, contributing to its reproductive strategy in aquatic habitats.
