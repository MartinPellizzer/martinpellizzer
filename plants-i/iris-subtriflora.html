Iris subtriflora

Iris subtriflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces three to five flowers per stem.

Iris subtriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
