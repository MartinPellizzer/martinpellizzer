Ichthyothere ovata

Ichthyothere ovata, commonly known as the sea egg plant, is a unique marine flora found in coastal regions.

It exhibits a distinctive ovate shape and is characterized by its gelatinous, translucent structure. The plant thrives in intertidal zones, where it attaches itself to rocky substrates using a fibrous root system. Its reproductive structures release microscopic spores into the water, facilitating widespread dispersal.

Botanically, it is classified as a member of the Phaeophyceae class, commonly known as brown algae.
