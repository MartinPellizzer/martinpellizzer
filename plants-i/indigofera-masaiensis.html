Indigofera masaiensis

Indigofera masaiensis, commonly known as the Masai indigo plant, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It is also used traditionally for dyeing fabrics and as a source of medicinal compounds.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it an important species in semi-arid regions.
