Ixia latifolia

Ixia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ixia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Ixia latifolia typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its blooming period usually occurs in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens.
