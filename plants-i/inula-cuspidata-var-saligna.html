Inula cuspidata var. saligna

Inula cuspidata var. saligna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with coarse, hairy foliage. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
