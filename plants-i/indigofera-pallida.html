Indigofera pallida

Indigofera pallida, commonly known as pale indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

Indigofera pallida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
