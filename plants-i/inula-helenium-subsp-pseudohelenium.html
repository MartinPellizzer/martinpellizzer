Inula helenium subsp. pseudohelenium

Inula helenium subsp.

pseudohelenium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it a distinctive member of the genus Inula.
