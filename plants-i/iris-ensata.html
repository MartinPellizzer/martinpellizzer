Iris ensata

Iris ensata, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a species native to East Asia, particularly Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, flat, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

Iris ensata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
