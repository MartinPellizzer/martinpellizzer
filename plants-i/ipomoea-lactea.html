Ipomoea lactea

Ipomoea lactea, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the morning and close at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces long, spiral-shaped vines that can climb or trail. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
