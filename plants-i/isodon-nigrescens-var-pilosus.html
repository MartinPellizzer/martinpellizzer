Isodon nigrescens var. pilosus

Isodon nigrescens var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemongrass-like scent and is often used in traditional medicine. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.
