Isolepis expallescens

Isolepis expallescens, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
