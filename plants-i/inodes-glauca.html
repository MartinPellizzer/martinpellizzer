Inodes glauca

Inodes glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Inodes glauca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
