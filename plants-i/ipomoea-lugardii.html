Ipomoea lugardii

Ipomoea lugardii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Ipomoea lugardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
