Indigofera retroflexa

Indigofera retroflexa, commonly known as the tropical indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
