Ipomoea hewittioides

Ipomoea hewittioides, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found growing along coastal areas and in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a notable species in Hawaiian flora.
