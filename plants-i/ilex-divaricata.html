Ilex divaricata

Ilex divaricata, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a dense, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, acidic habitats.

Ilex divaricata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat and food in native landscapes.
