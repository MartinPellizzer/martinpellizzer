Ipomoea beyeriana

Ipomoea beyeriana, commonly known as the Beyer's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea beyeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
