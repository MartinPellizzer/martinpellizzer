Ipomoea schrenkiana

Ipomoea schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a vigorous climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Ipomoea schrenkiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
