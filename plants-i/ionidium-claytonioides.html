Ionidium claytonioides

Ionidium claytonioides, commonly known as the red-flowered ionidium, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red petals, which are often adorned with contrasting yellow veins, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Ionidium claytonioides prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a houseplant.
