Ixia recurvifolia

Ixia recurvifolia, commonly known as the recurved ixia, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant's foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Ixia recurvifolia is known for its seasonal blooming, typically occurring in late winter to early spring.
