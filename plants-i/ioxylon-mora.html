Ioxylon mora

Ioxylon mora, commonly known as the Mora tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
