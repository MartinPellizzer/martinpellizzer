Ixia striata

Ixia striata, commonly known as the stripe lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or green markings. Ixia striata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its blooms typically appear in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.
