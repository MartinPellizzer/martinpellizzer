Ipomoea megalantha

Ipomoea megalantha, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are strong and twining. It thrives in warm climates with plenty of sunlight and well-drained soil.

Ipomoea megalantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
