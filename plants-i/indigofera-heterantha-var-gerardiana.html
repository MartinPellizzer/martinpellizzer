Indigofera heterantha var. gerardiana

Indigofera heterantha var. gerardiana, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue to violet flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a woody stem and compound leaves.

It is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.
