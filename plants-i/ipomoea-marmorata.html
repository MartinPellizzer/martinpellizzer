Ipomoea marmorata

Ipomoea marmorata, commonly known as the marble vine, is a striking flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with intricate patterns resembling marble. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ipomoea marmorata is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
