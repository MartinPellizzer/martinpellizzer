Indigofera polyclada

Indigofera polyclada, commonly known as the clustered indigo or African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and medicinal studies.
