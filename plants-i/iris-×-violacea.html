Iris × violacea

Iris × violacea, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Iris × violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
