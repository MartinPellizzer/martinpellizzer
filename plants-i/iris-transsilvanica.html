Iris transsilvanica

Iris transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are borne on tall, slender stems.
