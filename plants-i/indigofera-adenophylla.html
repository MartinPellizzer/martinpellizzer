Indigofera adenophylla

Indigofera adenophylla, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
