Imperata tinctoria

Imperata tinctoria, commonly known as red grass or red oat grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes and tall, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes are fibrous and have been historically used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural dyes.

Imperata tinctoria is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.
