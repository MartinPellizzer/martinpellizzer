Ipomoea riedeliana

Ipomoea riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using its tendrils to support itself.

Ipomoea riedeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
