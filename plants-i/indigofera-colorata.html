Indigofera colorata

Indigofera colorata, commonly known as the colorful indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or trellises. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Indigofera colorata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
