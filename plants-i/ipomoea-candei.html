Ipomoea candei

Ipomoea candei, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale lavender flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, slender vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea candei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
