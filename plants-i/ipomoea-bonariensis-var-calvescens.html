Ipomoea bonariensis var. calvescens

Ipomoea bonariensis var. calvescens, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often opening in the early morning. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
