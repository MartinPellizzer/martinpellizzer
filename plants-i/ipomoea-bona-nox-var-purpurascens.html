Ipomoea bona-nox var. purpurascens

Ipomoea bona-nox var. purpurascens, commonly known as the night blooming morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn, a trait that gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically deep purple or violet, with a white throat, and emit a sweet fragrance.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
