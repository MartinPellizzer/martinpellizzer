Inula latifolia

Inula latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf Inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Inula latifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
