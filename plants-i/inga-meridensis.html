Inga meridensis

Inga meridensis, commonly known as the coffee tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets and produces small, white to yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are used for coffee production.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance in the coffee industry.
