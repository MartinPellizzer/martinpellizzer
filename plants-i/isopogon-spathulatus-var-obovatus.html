Isopogon spathulatus var. obovatus

Isopogon spathulatus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves that are broad at the base. The plant produces striking spikes of yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and visual interest.
