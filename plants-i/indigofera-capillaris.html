Indigofera capillaris

Indigofera capillaris, commonly known as slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

Indigofera capillaris is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
