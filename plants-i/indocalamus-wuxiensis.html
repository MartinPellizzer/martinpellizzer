Indocalamus wuxiensis

Indocalamus wuxiensis, commonly known as the Wuxi bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan and Hubei, where it grows in moist, shaded environments. This bamboo is characterized by its slender, green stems and dense foliage, which contribute to its ornamental value. It is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil in its native range.
