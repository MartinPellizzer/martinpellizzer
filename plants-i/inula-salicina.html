Inula salicina

Inula salicina, commonly known as the European elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, yellow composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces a strong, aromatic odor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Inula salicina is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for respiratory and digestive ailments.
