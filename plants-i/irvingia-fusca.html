Irvingia fusca

Irvingia fusca, commonly known as African mango, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of West and Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The tree produces large, fleshy, yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. Irvingia fusca is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
