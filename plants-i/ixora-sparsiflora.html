Ixora sparsiflora

Ixora sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

Ixora sparsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedging plant.
