Impatiens aurella f. badia

Impatiens aurella f. badia, commonly known as the Black Impatiens, is a hybrid form of the species Impatiens aurella.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves and stems, which contrast with its bright yellow flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.

The flowers are typically small, clustered, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.
