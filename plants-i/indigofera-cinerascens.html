Indigofera cinerascens

Indigofera cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It often grows in disturbed areas and can be found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
