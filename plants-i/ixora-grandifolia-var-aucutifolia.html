Ixora grandifolia var. aucutifolia

Ixora grandifolia var. aucutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant blooms.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates.
