Indigofera roseocaerulea

Indigofera roseocaerulea, commonly known as the blue rose or blue indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of its family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
