Icaria

Icaria, commonly known as the "wild thyme" or "mountain thyme," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

These plants are typically shrubs or subshrubs, characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Icaria species are native to various regions in the Mediterranean, North Africa, and parts of Asia. They often thrive in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant's botanical features include trifoliate leaves and pods that contain seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
