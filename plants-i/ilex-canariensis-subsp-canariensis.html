Ilex canariensis subsp. canariensis

Ilex canariensis subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
