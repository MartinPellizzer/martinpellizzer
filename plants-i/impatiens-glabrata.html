Impatiens glabrata

Impatiens glabrata, commonly known as the smooth impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth surface and a glossy texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
