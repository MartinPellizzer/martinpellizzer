Ipomoea aculeata f. bona-nox

Ipomoea aculeata f. bona-nox, commonly known as the night blooming morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae) native to tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open at night and close by morning, hence its common name. The foliage is typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the stems are covered in small, stiff spines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique flowering pattern.
