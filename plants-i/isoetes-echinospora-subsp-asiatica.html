Isoetes echinospora subsp. asiatica

Isoetes echinospora subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian spikemoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the genus Isoetes. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky sporophylls that enclose the spores, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. Its morphology includes a rosette-like structure of stems and leaves, with the sporophylls arranged in a dense, spike-like formation.

This subspecies is native to parts of Asia and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
