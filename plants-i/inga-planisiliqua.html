Inga planisiliqua

Inga planisiliqua, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a useful species for reforestation. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the flowers attract various pollinators.

The bark and seeds contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
