Impatiens eberhardtii

Impatiens eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden beds.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
