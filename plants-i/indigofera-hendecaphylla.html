Indigofera hendecaphylla

Indigofera hendecaphylla, commonly known as the Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant's ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
