Inga fagifolia f. genuina

Inga fagifolia f. genuina, commonly known as the white inga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
