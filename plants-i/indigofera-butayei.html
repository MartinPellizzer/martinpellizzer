Indigofera butayei

Indigofera butayei, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It also produces small, dark blue or purple pods that contain seeds used for propagation.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in various cultures.
