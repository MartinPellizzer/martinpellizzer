Ichnanthus dasycoleus

Ichnanthus dasycoleus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed marsh gentian, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its stems are often hairy and slightly woody. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Ichnanthus dasycoleus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
