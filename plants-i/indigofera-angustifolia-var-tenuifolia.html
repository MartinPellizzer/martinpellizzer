Indigofera angustifolia var. tenuifolia

Indigofera angustifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender leaf indigo, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in dyeing and medicine.
