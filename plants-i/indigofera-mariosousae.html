Indigofera mariosousae

Indigofera mariosousae, commonly known as the Yemen indigo plant, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Arabian Peninsula and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native region.
