Inga tenella

Inga tenella, commonly known as the dwarf inga, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in shaded understories of humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Inga tenella is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
