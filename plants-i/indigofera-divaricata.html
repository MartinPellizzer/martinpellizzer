Indigofera divaricata

Indigofera divaricata, commonly known as the variable indigo or the narrow-leafed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration.
