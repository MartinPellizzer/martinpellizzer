Impatiens depauperata

Impatiens depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens depauperata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in partial shade.
