Isopogon diversifolius

Isopogon diversifolius, commonly known as the variable leaf banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Isopogon diversifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
