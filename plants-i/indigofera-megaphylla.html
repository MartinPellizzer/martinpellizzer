Indigofera megaphylla

Indigofera megaphylla, commonly known as the giant leaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits.

Its extensive root system helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
