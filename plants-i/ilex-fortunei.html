Ilex fortunei

Ilex fortunei, commonly known as the Fortune's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically leathery and have a distinctive oval shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Ilex fortunei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
