Impatiens elephanticeps

Impatiens elephanticeps, commonly known as the elephant's head impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, and often curling flower buds that resemble an elephant's head, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in humid climates. Its flowers, which open gradually, are usually bright red or orange with contrasting white veins.

Impatiens elephanticeps is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
