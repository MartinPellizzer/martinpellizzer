Isoetes valida var. gravesii

Isoetes valida var. gravesii, commonly known as the Graves' water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing, spike-like structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered along a central axis. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to the substrate with its rhizomes. Its leaves are usually narrow and ribbon-like, with a distinct midrib and a waxy coating that aids in water retention.

This variety is notable for its morphological variation, including differences in spore morphology and leaf structure compared to the typical form of I. valida.
