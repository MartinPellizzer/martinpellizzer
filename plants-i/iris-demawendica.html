Iris demawendica

Iris demawendica, commonly known as the Demawen iris, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often have a distinctive blue-green hue.

The iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
