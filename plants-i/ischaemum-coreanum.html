Ischaemum coreanum

Ischaemum coreanum, commonly known as Korean needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct blue-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Ischaemum coreanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
