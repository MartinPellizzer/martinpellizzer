Ibervillea

Ibervillea, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a genus of flowering plants native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their showy, showy yellow or orange flowers and hairy, succulent stems that help them retain moisture in arid environments. Ibervillea species typically have palmate leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs, which provide additional protection from heat and drought. The genus includes several species, such as *Ibervillea lindheimeri* and *Ibervillea rosea*, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

They play a crucial role in supporting pollinators and are adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
