Ipomoea galactorrhoea

Ipomoea galactorrhoea, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing plants.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and its historical use in traditional medicine.
