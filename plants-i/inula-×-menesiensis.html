Inula × menesiensis

Inula × menesiensis, commonly known as the hybrid elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and yellow composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Inula × menesiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
