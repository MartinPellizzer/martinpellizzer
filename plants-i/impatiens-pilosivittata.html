Impatiens pilosivittata

Impatiens pilosivittata, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
