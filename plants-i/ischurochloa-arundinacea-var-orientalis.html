Ischurochloa arundinacea var. orientalis

Ischurochloa arundinacea var. orientalis, commonly known as oriental bamboo grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in ecological restoration projects.
