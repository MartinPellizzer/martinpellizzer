Ipomoea cernua f. grandiflora

Ipomoea cernua f. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory, characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is a climbing vine that produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
