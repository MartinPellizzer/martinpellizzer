Isatis dasycarpa

Isatis dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy false flax, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Isatis dasycarpa is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in shaded woodland areas.
