Iris × trojana

Iris × trojana, commonly known as the Trojan iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, lavender to violet-colored flowers with a distinct three-parted structure typical of the iris family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
