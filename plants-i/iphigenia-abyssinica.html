Iphigenia abyssinica

Iphigenia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian heather, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the cool season. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature in harsh climates.

Iphigenia abyssinica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
