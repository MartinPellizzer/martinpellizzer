Iresine muscoides

Iresine muscoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or crimson flame plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with vibrant red, purple, and green hues. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters.

Iresine muscoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its eye-catching leaves rather than its flowers.
