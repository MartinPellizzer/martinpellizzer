Indigofera decora subsp. carlesii

Indigofera decora subsp.

carlesii, commonly known as the decorative indigo bean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant produces small, elongated pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and dyeing practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
