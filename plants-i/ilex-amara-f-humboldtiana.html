Ilex amara f. humboldtiana

Ilex amara f. humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt holly, is a variant of the evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
