Isoloma spicatum

Isoloma spicatum, commonly known as the spike heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Isoloma spicatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
