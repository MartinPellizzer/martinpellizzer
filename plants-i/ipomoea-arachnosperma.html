Ipomoea arachnosperma

Ipomoea arachnosperma, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue or purple berries that contain the seeds. Its unique feature is the silky, hairy coating on the seeds, which gives it its specific epithet "arachnosperma," meaning "spider-like seed.".

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
