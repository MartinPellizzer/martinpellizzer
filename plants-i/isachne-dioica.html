Isachne dioica

Isachne dioica, commonly known as the narrowleaf ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with both male and female flowers, reflecting its dioecious nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Isachne dioica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
