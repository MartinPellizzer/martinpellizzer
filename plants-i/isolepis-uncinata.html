Isolepis uncinata

Isolepis uncinata, commonly known as hooked spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of spikelets with awns, which are bristle-like structures at the base of the flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in shallow water or damp soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Isolepis uncinata plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic invertebrates.
