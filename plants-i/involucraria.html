Involucraria

Involucraria, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and a single, elongated stem that supports its showy inflorescence. Its botanical name, Involucraria, refers to the involucral bracts that surround the flower cluster.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
