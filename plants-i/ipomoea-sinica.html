Ipomoea sinica

Ipomoea sinica, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to Asia and is characterized by its heliotropic flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white, and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

Ipomoea sinica is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of soil conditions.
