Impatiens cochleata

Impatiens cochleata, commonly known as the spiral impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens cochleata is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
