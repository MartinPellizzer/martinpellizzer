Ionoxalis macilenta

Ionoxalis macilenta, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

Ionoxalis macilenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
