Imperata spontanea

Imperata spontanea, commonly known as the common blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes that form extensive clumps, and its narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Imperata spontanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
