Ipomoea rostrata

Ipomoea rostrata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters.

Ipomoea rostrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
