Indigofera constricta

Indigofera constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in a pod and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Indigofera constricta is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
