Indigofera wannanii

Indigofera wannanii, commonly known as the Chinese indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
