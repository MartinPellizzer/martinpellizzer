Iris spuria var. sogdiana

Iris spuria var. sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
