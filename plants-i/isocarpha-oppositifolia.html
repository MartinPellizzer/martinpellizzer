Isocarpha oppositifolia

Isocarpha oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved isocarpha, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and small, daisy-like flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
