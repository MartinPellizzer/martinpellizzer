Imperata brasiliensis

Imperata brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or red grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence. The plant has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Imperata brasiliensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
