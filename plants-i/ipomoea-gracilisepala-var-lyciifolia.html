Ipomoea gracilisepala var. lyciifolia

Ipomoea gracilisepala var. lyciifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth habit and climbing nature.
