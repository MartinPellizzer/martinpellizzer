Isoetes braunii f. astoma

Isoetes braunii f. astoma, commonly known as the astomatous water fern, is a variant of the genus Isoetes, which belongs to the order Isoetales.

This plant is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a spiny, segmented stem and a rosette of leaves that form a dense, compact structure. The astomatous form refers to the absence of stomata on the leaf surfaces, a trait that distinguishes it from other Isoetes species. It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in swampy or boggy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing the substrate.

The plant's reproductive structures, including its spore-bearing cones, are adapted for dispersal in aquatic settings.
