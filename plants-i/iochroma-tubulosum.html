Iochroma tubulosum

Iochroma tubulosum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and it typically grows as a climbing or trailing vine. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Iochroma tubulosum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
