Ipomoea verecunda

Ipomoea verecunda, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in the early morning. Its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types make it a common weed in disturbed areas.

However, due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a problematic species in certain regions.
