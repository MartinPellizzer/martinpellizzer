Impatiens kingdon-wardii

Impatiens kingdon-wardii, commonly known as the Kingdon-Ward impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a smooth margin, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to woodland gardens.
