Ischaemum pubescens

Ischaemum pubescens, commonly known as hairy goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Ischaemum pubescens is adapted to temperate climates and is known for its resilience in poor soil conditions.
