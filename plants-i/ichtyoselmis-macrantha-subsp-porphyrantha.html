Ichtyoselmis macrantha subsp. porphyrantha

Ichtyoselmis macrantha subsp.

porphyrantha, commonly known as the Porphyrantha variant, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its woody structure and aromatic foliage. It is native to the eastern regions of Australia, where it thrives in subtropical and temperate climates.

The plant typically features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds through dehiscence, aiding in its dispersal.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
