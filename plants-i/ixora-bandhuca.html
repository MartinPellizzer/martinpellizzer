Ixora bandhuca

Ixora bandhuca, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which resemble flames and bloom profusely during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora bandhuca is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
