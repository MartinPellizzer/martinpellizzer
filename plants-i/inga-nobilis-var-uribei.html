Inga nobilis var. uribei

Inga nobilis var. uribei, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth makes it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
