Indigofera glandulifera

Indigofera glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular indigo plant, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It produces pods containing small, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and agricultural systems.
