Irvingia oblonga

Irvingia oblonga, commonly known as the African mango, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellowish flowers and oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. Irvingia oblonga is valued for its potential health benefits, including its high content of dietary fiber and healthy fats.

Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.
