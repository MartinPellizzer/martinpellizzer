Indigofera fusca

Indigofera fusca, commonly known as the dark indigo plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera fusca is often used in traditional medicine and can also serve as a forage plant for livestock.
