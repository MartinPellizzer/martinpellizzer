Ipomoea attenuata

Ipomoea attenuata, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
