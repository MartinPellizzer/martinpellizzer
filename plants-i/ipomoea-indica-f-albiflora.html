Ipomoea indica f. albiflora

Ipomoea indica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory characterized by its striking white flowers.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a herbaceous perennial with trailing stems that produce heart-shaped leaves and showy white blooms. The flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, a trait typical of many morning glory species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
