Ipomoea chaetophora

Ipomoea chaetophora, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along stream banks or in moist woodlands.

Ipomoea chaetophora is a climbing plant that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
