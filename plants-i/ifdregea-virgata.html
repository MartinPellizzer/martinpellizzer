Ifdregea virgata

Ifdregea virgata, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
