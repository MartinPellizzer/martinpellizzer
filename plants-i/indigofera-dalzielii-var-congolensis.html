Indigofera dalzielii var. congolensis

Indigofera dalzielii var. congolensis, commonly known as the Congo indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
