Ilex wightiana var. wightiana

Ilex wightiana var. wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
