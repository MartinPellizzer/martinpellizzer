Indigofera faulknerae

Indigofera faulknerae, commonly known as Falkner's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Falkner's indigo is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
