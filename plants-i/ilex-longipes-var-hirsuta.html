Ilex longipes var. hirsuta

Ilex longipes var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a variant of the longipes persimmon native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy leaves and reddish-brown bark. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive hairy texture on both surfaces. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
