Ipomoea fastigiata var. vulgaris

Ipomoea fastigiata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. It grows as a climbing vine, often reaching lengths of several meters, and thrives in warm, humid climates.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
