Iris pelogonus

Iris pelogonus, commonly known as the Pelagonian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale blue center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Iris pelogonus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
