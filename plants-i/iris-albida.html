Iris albida

Iris albida, commonly known as the white iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear showy, white or pale blue flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern around the base.

Iris albida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
