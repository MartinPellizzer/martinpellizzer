Isoetes pacifica

Isoetes pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and often branched rhizomes that anchor it in muddy or sandy substrates. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in dense, tuft-like formations. It thrives in freshwater environments, such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it can receive adequate sunlight and nutrients.

Its morphology and reproductive structures make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
