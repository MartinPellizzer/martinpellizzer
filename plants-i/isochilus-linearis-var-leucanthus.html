Isochilus linearis var. leucanthus

Isochilus linearis var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered rush, is a variant of the linear rush native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or wetland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy shores.
