Ipomoea johnsoniana

Ipomoea johnsoniana, commonly known as the Johnson's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
