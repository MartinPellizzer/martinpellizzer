Inga brunnescens

Inga brunnescens, commonly known as the brown inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with alternate arrangement, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences.

Inga brunnescens produces edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
