Isoetes panchananii var. panchmarhiensis

Isoetes panchananii var. panchmarhiensis, commonly known as the Panchmarhi water fern, is a rare aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located at the tips of its leaf-like structures. This variety is native to the freshwater habitats of central India, particularly in the Panchmarhi region. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for the health of freshwater ecosystems.
