Ipomoea armata

Ipomoea armata, commonly known as the coral vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea armata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering vines.
