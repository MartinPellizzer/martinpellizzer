Indigofera tritoides var. obbiadensis

Indigofera tritoides var. obbiadensis, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or the yellow indigo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, often yellow or white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
