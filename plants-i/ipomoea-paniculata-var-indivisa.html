Ipomoea paniculata var. indivisa

Ipomoea paniculata var. indivisa, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
