Ipomoea argyreia var. lanata

Ipomoea argyreia var. lanata, commonly known as the velvet flower or moonflower, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. The leaves are broad and velvety, giving the plant its common name, while the stems are hairy and woody. This variant of Ipomoea argyreia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal but can become invasive if not managed properly.
