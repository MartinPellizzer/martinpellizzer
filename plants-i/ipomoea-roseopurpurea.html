Ipomoea roseopurpurea

Ipomoea roseopurpurea, commonly known as the purple rose morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep rose. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb trellises or fences with its long, winding stems.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
