Inga pavoniana

Inga pavoniana, commonly known as the pink inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant is notable for its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Inga pavoniana also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
