Ixia triticea

Ixia triticea, commonly known as the golden grass lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ixia triticea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
