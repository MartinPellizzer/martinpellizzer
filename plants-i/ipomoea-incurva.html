Ipomoea incurva

Ipomoea incurva, commonly known as the curlleaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that curl at the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea incurva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
