Impatiens paludicola

Impatiens paludicola, commonly known as the swamp impatiens, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in swampy areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or white. It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

Impatiens paludicola is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
