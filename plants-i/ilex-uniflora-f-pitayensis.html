Ilex uniflora f. pitayensis

Ilex uniflora f. pitayensis, commonly known as the pitaya holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a sweet, mild flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
