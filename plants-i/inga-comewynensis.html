Inga comewynensis

Inga comewynensis, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant has a shallow root system and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its fruits are pod-like structures that release seeds when mature.
