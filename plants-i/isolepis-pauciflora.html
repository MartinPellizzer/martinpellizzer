Isolepis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered flatsedge, is a monocotyledonous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and shallow water areas. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Isolepis pauciflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
