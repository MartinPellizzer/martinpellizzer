Ipomoea tuboides

Ipomoea tuboides, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along fences or trellises.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
