Ixia lacerata

Ixia lacerata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ixia, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of vivid, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often appearing after the flowering period. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
