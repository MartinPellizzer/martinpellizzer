Icica cubensis

Icica cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban icica, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Cuba.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
