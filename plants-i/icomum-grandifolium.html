Icomum grandifolium

Icomum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf ice plant, is a succulent species native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Icomum grandifolium is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and hardy nature.
