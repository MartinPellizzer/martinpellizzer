Ipomoea angustifolia var. villosula

Ipomoea angustifolia var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically light green in color. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, with five petals, and come in shades of pink to purple, blooming in the early morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
