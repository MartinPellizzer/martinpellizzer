Ilex subrugosa

Ilex subrugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream valleys.

Ilex subrugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
