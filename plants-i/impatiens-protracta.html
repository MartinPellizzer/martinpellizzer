Impatiens protracta

Impatiens protracta, commonly known as the protracted impatiens, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens protracta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
