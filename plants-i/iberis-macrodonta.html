Iberis macrodonta

Iberis macrodonta, commonly known as the Turkish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright pink to white flowers. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis macrodonta is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
