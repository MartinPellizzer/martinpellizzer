Ipomoea hildebrandtii subsp. megaensis

Ipomoea hildebrandtii subsp.

megaensis, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a striking vine native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, typically in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can be invasive if not managed properly.
