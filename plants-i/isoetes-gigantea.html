Isoetes gigantea

Isoetes gigantea, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of water plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are long and ribbon-like, often forming dense underwater mats.

Isoetes gigantea plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
