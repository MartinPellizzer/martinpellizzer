Isodon inflexus var. nakayamae

Isodon inflexus var. nakayamae, commonly known as the Nakayama isodon, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are small, tubular, and often clustered in whorls, producing a distinctive fragrance.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
