Ipheion uniflorum f. conspicuum

Ipheion uniflorum f. conspicuum, commonly known as the showy blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a prominent yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in suitable conditions.
