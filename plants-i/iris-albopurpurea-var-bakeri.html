Iris albopurpurea var. bakeri

Iris albopurpurea var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers have three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
