Inga cabelo

Inga cabelo, commonly known as the "hair bean" or "black bean tree," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in tropical forests. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the seeds are sometimes cultivated for their nutritional value.

The plant's ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil and supports surrounding vegetation.
