Ilex colchica subsp. imerethica

Ilex colchica subsp.

imerethica, commonly known as the Imereti holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, spiny leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
