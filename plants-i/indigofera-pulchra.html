Indigofera pulchra

Indigofera pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green appearance.

Indigofera pulchra is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
