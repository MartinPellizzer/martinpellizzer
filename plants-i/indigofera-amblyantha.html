Indigofera amblyantha

Indigofera amblyantha, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a significant species in its native ecosystems.
