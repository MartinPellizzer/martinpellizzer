Inga radians

Inga radians, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain sweet, fleshy seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in some regions.

The tree also has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
