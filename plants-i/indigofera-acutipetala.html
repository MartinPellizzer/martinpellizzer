Indigofera acutipetala

Indigofera acutipetala, commonly known as the acute-leaved indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often arranged in raceme clusters. Its root system is known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine and dye production.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
