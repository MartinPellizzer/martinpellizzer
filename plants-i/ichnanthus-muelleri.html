Ichnanthus muelleri

Ichnanthus muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It features slender, erect stems that bear showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Ichnanthus muelleri is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to periodic flooding.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
