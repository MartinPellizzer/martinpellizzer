Iaravaea

Iaravaea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iaravaea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
