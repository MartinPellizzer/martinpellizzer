Impatiens sivarajanii

Impatiens sivarajanii, commonly known as Sivarajan's jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to white, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens sivarajanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
