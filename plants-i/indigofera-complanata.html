Indigofera complanata

Indigofera complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Indigofera complanata is also valued for its role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
