Indocalamus decorus

Indocalamus decorus, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, golden-yellow culms that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface that adds to its aesthetic appeal.

Indocalamus decorus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
