Icacorea gorgonae

Icacorea gorgonae, commonly known as the Gorgon's flame, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Icacorea gorgonae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
