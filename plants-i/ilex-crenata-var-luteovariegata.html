Ilex crenata var. luteovariegata

Ilex crenata var. luteovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellow or cream variegation pattern along the edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds, adding ornamental value in the winter months. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Its dense foliage and unique leaf coloration make it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.
