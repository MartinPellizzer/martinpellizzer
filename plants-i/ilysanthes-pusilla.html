Ilysanthes pusilla

Ilysanthes pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale blue, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments. It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a striking addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.

Ilysanthes pusilla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
