Ipomoea avicularis

Ipomoea avicularis, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a vigorous climbing vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth and ability to cover structures quickly. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in disturbed habitats.

Ipomoea avicularis is also notable for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
