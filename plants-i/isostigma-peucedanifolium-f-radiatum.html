Isostigma peucedanifolium f. radiatum

Isostigma peucedanifolium f. radiatum, commonly known as the radiate isostigma, is a horticultural form of a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of the peucedanifolium species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
