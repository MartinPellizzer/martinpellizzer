Ipomeria albida

Ipomeria albida, commonly known as the white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ipomeria albida is typically perennial and has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.
