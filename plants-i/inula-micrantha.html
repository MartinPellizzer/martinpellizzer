Inula micrantha

Inula micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem covered in rough, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense, disk-like cluster.

Inula micrantha is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
