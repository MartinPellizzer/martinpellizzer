Isodon lophanthoides var. sessilis

Isodon lophanthoides var. sessilis, commonly known as the sessile-leaved lophanthoid germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, sessile leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
