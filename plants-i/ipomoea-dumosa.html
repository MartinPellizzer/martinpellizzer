Ipomoea dumosa

Ipomoea dumosa, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Ipomoea dumosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
