Ismene pedunculata

Ismene pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
