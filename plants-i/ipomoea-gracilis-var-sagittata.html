Ipomoea gracilis var. sagittata

Ipomoea gracilis var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences, walls, and trellises. It produces edible seeds, though they are typically consumed in traditional contexts.

The plant is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
