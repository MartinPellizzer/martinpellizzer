Ilex collina f. vantrompii

Ilex collina f. vantrompii, commonly known as the Vancouver holly, is a variant of the common holly, native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

This evergreen shrub or small tree features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. The plant is characterized by its dense foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, showing some tolerance to urban conditions.
