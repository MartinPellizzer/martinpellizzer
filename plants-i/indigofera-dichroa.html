Indigofera dichroa

Indigofera dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored indigo plant, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored leaves, with the upper surface being dark green and the lower surface a lighter, often silvery hue. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Indigofera dichroa is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
