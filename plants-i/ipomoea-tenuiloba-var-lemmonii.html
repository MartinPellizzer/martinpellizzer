Ipomoea tenuiloba var. lemmonii

Ipomoea tenuiloba var. lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped, light green leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, with five petals and a distinctive, narrow corolla. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Convolvulaceae family.
