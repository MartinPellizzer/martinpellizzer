Ibatiria furfuracea

Ibatiria furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, using its tendrils to attach to supports. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of yellow or green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
