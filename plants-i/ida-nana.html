Ida nana

Ida nana, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale blue, blooming in early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Ida nana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
