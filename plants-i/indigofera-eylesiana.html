Indigofera eylesiana

Indigofera eylesiana, commonly known as the Eyles' indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and racemose inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution in suitable habitats.
