Iris cepifolia

Iris cepifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a fan shape at the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland areas.

The plant's botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
