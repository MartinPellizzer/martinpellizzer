Indigofera polycarpa

Indigofera polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Indigofera polycarpa is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
