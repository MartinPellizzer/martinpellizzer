Imhofia sarniensis

Imhofia sarniensis, commonly known as the Sarnian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like taro and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, spadix-like flowers. Its tubers, which grow underground, are the primary part used for culinary purposes.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
