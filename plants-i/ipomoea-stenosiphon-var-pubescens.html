Ipomoea stenosiphon var. pubescens

Ipomoea stenosiphon var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine pubescence. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
