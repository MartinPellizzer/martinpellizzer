Ilex verticillata var. tenuifolia

Ilex verticillata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves that turn bright red in the fall before dropping. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds during the winter months. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in wet areas.
