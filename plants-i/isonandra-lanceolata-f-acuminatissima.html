Isonandra lanceolata f. acuminatissima

Isonandra lanceolata f. acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed isonandra, is a botanical variant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctly pointed apex.

This fern-like plant exhibits a delicate, feathery appearance, with finely divided foliage that gives it a lacy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories. The plant's spore-bearing structures are typically located on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species within its ecological niche.
