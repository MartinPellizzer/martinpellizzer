Illipe insignis

Illipe insignis, commonly known as the African illipe, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that contain a rich, oily seed. Its seeds are highly valued for their high oil content, used in both culinary and industrial applications.

The tree is also important in traditional medicine and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
