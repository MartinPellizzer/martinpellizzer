Ipomoea pentadactylis

Ipomoea pentadactylis, commonly known as the five-finger morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea pentadactylis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known morning glory species.
