Isodon inflexus var. macrophyllus

Isodon inflexus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf isodon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
