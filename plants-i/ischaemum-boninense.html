Ischaemum boninense

Ischaemum boninense, commonly known as the Bonin grass, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is typically found in coastal or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to maritime environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
