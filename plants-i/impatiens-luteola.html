Impatiens luteola

Impatiens luteola, commonly known as yellow touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Impatiens luteola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
