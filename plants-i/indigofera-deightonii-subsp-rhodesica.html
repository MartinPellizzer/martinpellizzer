Indigofera deightonii subsp. rhodesica

Indigofera deightonii subsp.

rhodesica, commonly known as Rhodesian indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
