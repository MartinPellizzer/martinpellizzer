Iris suaveolens f. flavobarbata

Iris suaveolens f. flavobarbata, commonly known as the fragrant iris or sweet-scented iris, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a violet-blue coloration with a yellow or orange center. The plant produces sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fragrance of its flowers is one of its most notable botanical features, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
