Isachne venusta

Isachne venusta, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Isachne venusta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and taxonomic research.
