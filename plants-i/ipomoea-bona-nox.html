Ipomoea bona-nox

Ipomoea bona-nox, commonly known as the night blooming morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and can climb using its tendrils, often reaching heights of several meters. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a strong, sweet fragrance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal blooming habit.
