Indigofera latifolia

Indigofera latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pink or purple flowers. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Indigofera latifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
