Impatiens infundibularis

Impatiens infundibularis, commonly known as the hooded impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically red or orange, with a distinctive hood-like structure that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
