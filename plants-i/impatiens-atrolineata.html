Impatiens atrolineata

Impatiens atrolineata, commonly known as the black-leaved impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black, velvety foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in partial to full shade. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
