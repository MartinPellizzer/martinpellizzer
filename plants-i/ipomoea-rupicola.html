Ipomoea rupicola

Ipomoea rupicola, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often prostrate.

Ipomoea rupicola is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
