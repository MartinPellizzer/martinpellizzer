Ipomoea eggersii

Ipomoea eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing along forest edges or in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
