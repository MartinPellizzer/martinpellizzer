Ipomoea dehniae

Ipomoea dehniae, commonly known as the Dehnia morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It produces strikingly large, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth habit.

The flowers open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
