Ilex omeiensis

Ilex omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and have spiny margins. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

Ilex omeiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
