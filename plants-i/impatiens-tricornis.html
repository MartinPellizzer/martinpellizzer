Impatiens tricornis

Impatiens tricornis, commonly known as the three-lobed impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and a trailing habit. Its flowers are borne in clusters and feature a distinctive three-lobed shape, which gives it its common name.

Impatiens tricornis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
