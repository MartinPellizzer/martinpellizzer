Indigofera transvaalensis

Indigofera transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnate, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera transvaalensis is known for its ability to thrive in dry and rocky soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
