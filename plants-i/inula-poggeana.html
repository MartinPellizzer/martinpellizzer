Inula poggeana

Inula poggeana, commonly known as the mountain inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its yellow flower heads are composed of numerous small florets, and it produces a distinctive, branched, pale yellow or white flower.

Inula poggeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
