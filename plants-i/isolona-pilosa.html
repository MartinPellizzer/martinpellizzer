Isolona pilosa

Isolona pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences. Isolona pilosa thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
