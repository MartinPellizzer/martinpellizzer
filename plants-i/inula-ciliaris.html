Inula ciliaris

Inula ciliaris, commonly known as the blueweed or crownflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features a tall, branched stem with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its blue to violet flower heads, which consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Inula ciliaris is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
