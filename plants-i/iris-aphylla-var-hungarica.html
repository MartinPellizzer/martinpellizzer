Iris aphylla var. hungarica

Iris aphylla var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian root iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It features a robust rhizome system and produces tall, slender stems with narrow, lanceolate leaves. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
