Ipomoea blanchetii var. pubescens

Ipomoea blanchetii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a species native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a flowering vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a notable ornamental plant.
