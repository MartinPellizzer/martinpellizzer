Iria arnottiana

Iria arnottiana, commonly known as the Arnott's iris, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related plants. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iria arnottiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
