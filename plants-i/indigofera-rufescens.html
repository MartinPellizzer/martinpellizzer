Indigofera rufescens

Indigofera rufescens, commonly known as red-barked indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and distinctive reddish-barked stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with slender, pointed leaflets that give it a delicate appearance.

Indigofera rufescens is also valued for its traditional uses in herbal medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
