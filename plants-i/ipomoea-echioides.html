Ipomoea echioides

Ipomoea echioides, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often seen trailing along fences or trellises. Its flowers range in color from white to deep purple, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ipomoea echioides is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
