Inga subnuda

Inga subnuda, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its pods contain edible seeds, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
