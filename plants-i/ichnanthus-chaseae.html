Ichnanthus chaseae

Ichnanthus chaseae, commonly known as the Chase's bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that grows in moist, swampy habitats. The plant is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and nutrient acquisition.
