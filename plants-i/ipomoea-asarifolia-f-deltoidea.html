Ipomoea asarifolia f. deltoidea

Ipomoea asarifolia f. deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid leaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae) characterized by its distinctive deltoid-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with vibrant colors ranging from blue to purple, and bloom in the early morning. The plant is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself on structures.

Botanically, it exhibits a complex morphology with a fibrous root system and a hairy stem, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
