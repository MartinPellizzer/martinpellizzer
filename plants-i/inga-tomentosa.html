Inga tomentosa

Inga tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and produces pendant clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant bears fruit that is a pod containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

Inga tomentosa is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
