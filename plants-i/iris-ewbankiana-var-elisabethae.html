Iris ewbankiana var. elisabethae

Iris ewbankiana var. elisabethae, commonly known as the Elisabetha iris, is a rare and beautiful perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features striking violet-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is well-suited to cooler climates.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is a sought-after addition to native plant collections.
