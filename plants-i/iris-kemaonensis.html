Iris kemaonensis

Iris kemaonensis, commonly known as the Kemaon iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps that help stabilize the soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Iris kemaonensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
