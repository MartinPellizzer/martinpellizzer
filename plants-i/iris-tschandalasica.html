Iris tschandalasica

Iris tschandalasica, commonly known as the Tschandala iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
