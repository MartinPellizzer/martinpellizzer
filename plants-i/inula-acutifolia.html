Inula acutifolia

Inula acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties.

The plant's rhizomes have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.
