Inula caucasica

Inula caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian Inula, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
