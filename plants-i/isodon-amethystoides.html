Isodon amethystoides

Isodon amethystoides, commonly known as purple verbena, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often blooming in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Isodon amethystoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
