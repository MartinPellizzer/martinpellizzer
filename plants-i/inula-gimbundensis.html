Inula gimbundensis

Inula gimbundensis, commonly known as the Korean Inula, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Inula gimbundensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
