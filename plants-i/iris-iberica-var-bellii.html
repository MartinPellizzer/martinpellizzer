Iris iberica var. bellii

Iris iberica var. bellii, commonly known as the Bell's Iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegance.
