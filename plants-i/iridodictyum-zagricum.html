Iridodictyum zagricum

Iridodictyum zagricum, commonly known as the Zagreb iris, is a rare and striking plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related flowering plants. The plant features tall, slender stems with showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Iridodictyum zagricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its rarity and beauty.
