Isolona madagascariensis

Isolona madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
