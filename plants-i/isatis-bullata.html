Isatis bullata

Isatis bullata, commonly known as the yellow flag or yellow isatis, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Isatis bullata is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
