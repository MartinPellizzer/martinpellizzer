Inula caspica var. paniculata

Inula caspica var. paniculata, commonly known as the Caspian Inula, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid regions.
