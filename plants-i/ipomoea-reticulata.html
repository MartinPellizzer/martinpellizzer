Ipomoea reticulata

Ipomoea reticulata, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found trailing or climbing on structures such as trellises or fences. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, pink, and white, and they emit a sweet fragrance.

Ipomoea reticulata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of soil types.
