Ipomoea bilobata

Ipomoea bilobata, commonly known as the twin-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant is a climbing vine that produces fruit capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
