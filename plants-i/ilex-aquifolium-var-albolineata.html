Ilex aquifolium var. albolineata

Ilex aquifolium var. albolineata, commonly known as the white-lined holly, is a variant of the common holly that features distinctive white or pale yellow markings on its leaves.

This plant is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated margin, with the white variegation adding a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
