Ichnanthus subinclusus

Ichnanthus subinclusus, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
