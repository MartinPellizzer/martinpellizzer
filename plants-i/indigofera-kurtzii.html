Indigofera kurtzii

Indigofera kurtzii, commonly known as African indigo, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It produces seed pods that contain small, dark brown seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a dye source.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
