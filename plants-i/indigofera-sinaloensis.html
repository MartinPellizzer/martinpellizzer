Indigofera sinaloensis

Indigofera sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloa indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
