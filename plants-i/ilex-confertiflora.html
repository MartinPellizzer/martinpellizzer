Ilex confertiflora

Ilex confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically leathery and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex confertiflora thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in both natural and cultivated settings.
