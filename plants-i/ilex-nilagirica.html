Ilex nilagirica

Ilex nilagirica, commonly known as the Nilgiri holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Ilex nilagirica typically grows in moist, shaded environments at moderate elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its dense foliage and seasonal fruiting.
