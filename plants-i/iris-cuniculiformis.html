Iris cuniculiformis

Iris cuniculiformis, commonly known as the burr iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky seed pods that resemble small burrs, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in early spring.

Iris cuniculiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
