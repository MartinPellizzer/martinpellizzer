Indigofera brachybotrys

Indigofera brachybotrys, commonly known as the dwarf indigo or small-leaved indigo, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
