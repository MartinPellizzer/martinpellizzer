Inga carnosa

Inga carnosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a soft, hairy texture. Its fruit is a pod-like structure that contains several seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

Inga carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
