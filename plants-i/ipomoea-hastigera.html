Ipomoea hastigera

Ipomoea hastigera, commonly known as the zigzag morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea hastigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
