Iria hookeriana

Iria hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Iria hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
