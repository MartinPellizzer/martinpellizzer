Isocoma menziesii var. diabolica

Isocoma menziesii var. diabolica, commonly known as the devil's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish composite flowers, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It has linear, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is known for its unique morphology and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.
