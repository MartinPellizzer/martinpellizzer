Iris stolonifera var. estolonifera

Iris stolonifera var. estolonifera, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant spreads via stolons, forming dense colonies that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and is closely related to other iris species in the Iridaceae family.
