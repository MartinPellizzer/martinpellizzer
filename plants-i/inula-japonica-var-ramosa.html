Inula japonica var. ramosa

Inula japonica var. ramosa, commonly known as Japanese fleabane, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
