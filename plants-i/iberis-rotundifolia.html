Iberis rotundifolia

Iberis rotundifolia, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
