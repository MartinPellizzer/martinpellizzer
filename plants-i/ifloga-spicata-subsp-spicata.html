Ifloga spicata subsp. spicata

Ifloga spicata subsp.

spicata, commonly known as the spiny spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect stem, which is covered in stiff, spiny hairs.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and also have a spiny texture, contributing to its defensive features. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically in late summer to early autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
