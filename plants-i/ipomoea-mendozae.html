Ipomoea mendozae

Ipomoea mendozae, commonly known as Mendoza's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some environments.
