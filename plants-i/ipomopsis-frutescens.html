Ipomopsis frutescens

Ipomopsis frutescens, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

The plant is often found in open habitats and is an important component of local ecosystems.
