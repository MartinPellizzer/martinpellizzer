Indigofera gibsonii

Indigofera gibsonii, commonly known as the Gibson's indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
