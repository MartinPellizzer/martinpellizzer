Iberis sempervirens subsp. garrexiana

Iberis sempervirens subsp.

garrexiana, commonly known as the Garrexian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant features dense, silvery-gray foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and winter hardiness make it a popular choice for alpine and coastal gardens.
