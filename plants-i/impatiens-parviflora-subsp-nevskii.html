Impatiens parviflora subsp. nevskii

Impatiens parviflora subsp.

nevskii, commonly known as the Nevsky's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial with delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Impatiens.
