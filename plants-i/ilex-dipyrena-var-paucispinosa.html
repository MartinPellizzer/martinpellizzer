Ilex dipyrena var. paucispinosa

Ilex dipyrena var. paucispinosa, commonly known as the spiny holly, is a variant of the dipyrena holly, characterized by its small, leathery leaves and distinctive spines.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its berries are dark blue and contain several seeds, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
