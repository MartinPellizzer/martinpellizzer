Isodon glaucocalyx

Isodon glaucocalyx, commonly known as blue-veined honeysuckle, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Isodon glaucocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
