Ipomoea harlingii

Ipomoea harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea harlingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
