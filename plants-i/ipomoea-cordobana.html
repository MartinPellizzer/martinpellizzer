Ipomoea cordobana

Ipomoea cordobana, commonly known as the Cordoba morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Ipomoea cordobana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
