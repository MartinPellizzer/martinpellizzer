Ipomoea nematophylla

Ipomoea nematophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
