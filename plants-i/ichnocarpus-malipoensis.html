Ichnocarpus malipoensis

Ichnocarpus malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo burr bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive burr-like fruits that enclose its seeds, a feature typical of the genus Ichnocarpus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in forest edges or open areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation in tropical environments.
