Impatiens balsamina var. longifolia

Impatiens balsamina var. longifolia, commonly known as the common touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in a fleshy, bladder-like capsule. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
