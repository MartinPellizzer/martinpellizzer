Indigofera subulata var. maffeii

Indigofera subulata var. maffeii, commonly known as the African indigo.

It is a small shrub native to regions of Africa, characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native range.
