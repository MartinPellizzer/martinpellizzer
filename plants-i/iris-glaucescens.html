Iris glaucescens

Iris glaucescens, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, pond edges, and stream banks.

Iris glaucescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
