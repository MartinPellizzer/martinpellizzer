Ipomoea tyrianthina f. serotina

Ipomoea tyrianthina f. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically open in the late afternoon rather than the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a trailing growth habit. Its flowers are usually pale blue to violet in color and emit a mild fragrance.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
