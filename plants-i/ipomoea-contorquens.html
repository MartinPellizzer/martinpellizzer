Ipomoea contorquens

Ipomoea contorquens, commonly known as the twisted morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its helically coiled stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a violet or pink hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange capsule that splits open when mature.
