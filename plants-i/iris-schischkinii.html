Iris schischkinii

Iris schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin iris, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting showy blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed beard on the lower petals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, typical of many iris species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
