Inga brachystachys

Inga brachystachys, commonly known as the dwarf serissa, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, bean-like pods containing edible seeds.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
