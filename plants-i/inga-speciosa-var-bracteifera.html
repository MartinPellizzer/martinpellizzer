Inga speciosa var. bracteifera

Inga speciosa var. bracteifera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and elongated, pendulous inflorescences that produce small, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are compound, with a broad, ovate shape, and the bark is typically smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
