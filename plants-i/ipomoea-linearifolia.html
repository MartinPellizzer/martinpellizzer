Ipomoea linearifolia

Ipomoea linearifolia, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, blooming in the morning. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea linearifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and wild areas across its native range.
