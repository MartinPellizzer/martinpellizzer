Ipomoea chamelana

Ipomoea chamelana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
