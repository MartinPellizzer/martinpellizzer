Ixora upolensis

Ixora upolensis, commonly known as the Upolensis flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red inflorescences, which are clustered in panicles and bloom throughout the year. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscaping in warm climates.

Ixora upolensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
