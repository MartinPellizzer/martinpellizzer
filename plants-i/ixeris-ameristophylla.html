Ixeris ameristophylla

Ixeris ameristophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to narrowly elliptic, with a rough, sometimes glandular surface. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, producing numerous small florets that bloom in the spring.

Ixeris ameristophylla is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
