Ilex brachyphylla

Ilex brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are typically short and broad, with spiny margins. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Ilex brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
