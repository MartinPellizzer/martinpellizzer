Ilex amara f. ovalifolia

Ilex amara f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the bitter holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in woodland settings.
