Isocoma coronopifolia

Isocoma coronopifolia, commonly known as desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
