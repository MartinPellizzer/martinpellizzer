Ipomoea cymosa var. sagittatoangulata

Ipomoea cymosa var. sagittatoangulata, commonly known as the arrowhead morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically light to dark green. The plant produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
