Ilex scopulorum var. crassifolia

Ilex scopulorum var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is evergreen, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
