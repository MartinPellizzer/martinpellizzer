Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. pes-tigridis

Ipomoea pes-tigridis var. pes-tigridis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
