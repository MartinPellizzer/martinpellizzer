Impatiens neobarnesii

Impatiens neobarnesii, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a misty cloud. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
