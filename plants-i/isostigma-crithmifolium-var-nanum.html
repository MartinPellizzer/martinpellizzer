Isostigma crithmifolium var. nanum

Isostigma crithmifolium var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf sea holly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal areas of southern Europe.

It features small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has silvery, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
