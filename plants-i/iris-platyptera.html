Iris platyptera

Iris platyptera, commonly known as the flatpetal iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Iris platyptera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
