Ischaemum goebelii

Ischaemum goebelii, commonly known as Goebel's foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
