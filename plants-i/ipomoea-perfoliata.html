Ipomoea perfoliata

Ipomoea perfoliata, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to attach to supports. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

Ipomoea perfoliata is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
