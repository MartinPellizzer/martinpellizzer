Imperialis coronata

Imperialis coronata, commonly known as the Crowned Imperialis, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant features a complex inflorescence with bracts that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Imperialis coronata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
