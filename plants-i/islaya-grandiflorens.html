Islaya grandiflorens

Islaya grandiflorens, commonly known as the giant flameflower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features palmate leaves with a smooth, waxy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display in botanical gardens and as a tropical ornamental.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is not commonly found in home gardens outside of its native habitat.
