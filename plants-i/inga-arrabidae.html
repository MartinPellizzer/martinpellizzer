Inga arrabidae

Inga arrabidae, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and soil stabilization in its native habitats.
