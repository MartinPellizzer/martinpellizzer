Ipomoea palmata var. gracillima

Ipomoea palmata var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a climbing habit, often found in moist, open woodlands. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and showy, often fragrant blooms that appear in the early morning.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
