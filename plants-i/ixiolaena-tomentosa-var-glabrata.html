Ixiolaena tomentosa var. glabrata

Ixiolaena tomentosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the hairy bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that contrast with the hairy foliage of the typical variety. The plant belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments. It typically grows as a clump-forming perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its foliage provides a lush green appearance, making it suitable for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
