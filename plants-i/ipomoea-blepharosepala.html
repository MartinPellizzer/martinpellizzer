Ipomoea blepharosepala

Ipomoea blepharosepala, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along tree trunks or in shaded areas. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea blepharosepala is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
