Indigofera baileyi

Indigofera baileyi, commonly known as the yellow indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, open woodlands.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
