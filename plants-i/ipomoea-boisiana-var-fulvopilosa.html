Ipomoea boisiana var. fulvopilosa

Ipomoea boisiana var. fulvopilosa, commonly known as the golden hair vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing and trailing vines.
