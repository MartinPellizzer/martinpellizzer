Ithycaulon domingense

Ithycaulon domingense, commonly known as the Dominican orchid, is a species of orchid native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stem and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the forest understory. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

The orchid plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and supporting pollinators.
