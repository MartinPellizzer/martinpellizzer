Iris collettii var. acaulis

Iris collettii var. acaulis, commonly known as the alpine iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that often lack leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
