Impatiens barbulata

Impatiens barbulata, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
