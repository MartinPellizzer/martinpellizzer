Iberis intermedia var. contejeanii

Iberis intermedia var. contejeanii, commonly known as the Contejeanii sweet rocket, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
