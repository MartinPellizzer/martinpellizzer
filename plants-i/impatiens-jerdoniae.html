Impatiens jerdoniae

Impatiens jerdoniae, commonly known as the Jerdon's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are slender and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
