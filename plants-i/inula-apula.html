Inula apula

Inula apula, commonly known as the Apulian elecampane, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Inula.

Inula apula is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climate conditions.
