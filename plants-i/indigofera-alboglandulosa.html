Indigofera alboglandulosa

Indigofera alboglandulosa, commonly known as the white-gland indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves.

The species is notable for its potential in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
