Ipomoea mexicana

Ipomoea mexicana, commonly known as Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea mexicana is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
