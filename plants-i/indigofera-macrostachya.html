Indigofera macrostachya

Indigofera macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in elongated pods, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
