Iris brachycuspis

Iris brachycuspis, commonly known as the short-bract iris, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Iris.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
