Inula × rigida var. vera

Inula × rigida var. vera, commonly known as the hybrid fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
