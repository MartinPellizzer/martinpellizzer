Impatiens khasiana var. toppinii

Impatiens khasiana var. toppinii, commonly known as the Khasi impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
