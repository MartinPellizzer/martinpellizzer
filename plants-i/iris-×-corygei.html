Iris × corygei

Iris × corygei, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris laevigata and Iris siberica.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, violet, and white, with a distinctive three-dimensional structure.

Iris × corygei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil conditions.
