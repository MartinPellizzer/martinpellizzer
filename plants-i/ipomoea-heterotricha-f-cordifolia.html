Ipomoea heterotricha f. cordifolia

Ipomoea heterotricha f. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a variant of theIpomoea heterotricha species, characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

This plant is a herbaceous vine native to tropical regions, particularly in Central and South America. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in the morning. The leaves are broad, lobed, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
