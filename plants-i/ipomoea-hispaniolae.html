Ipomoea hispaniolae

Ipomoea hispaniolae, commonly known as the Hispaniola morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a vine, with heart-shaped leaves and a climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
