Iris stenophylla subsp. stenophylla

Iris stenophylla subsp.

stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals, typical of the iris family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
