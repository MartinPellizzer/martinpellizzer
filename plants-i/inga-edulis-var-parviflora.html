Inga edulis var. parviflora

Inga edulis var. parviflora, commonly known as the "ice cream bean," is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnately compound, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces long, serpentine pods containing edible, sweet, cream-colored seeds that are highly valued in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and nutritional value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
