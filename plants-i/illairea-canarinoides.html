Illairea canarinoides

Illairea canarinoides, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily by wind.

Illairea canarinoides is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
