Ipomoea armentalis

Ipomoea armentalis, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
