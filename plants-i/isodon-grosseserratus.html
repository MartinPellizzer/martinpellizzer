Isodon grosseserratus

Isodon grosseserratus, commonly known as the rough-leaved isodon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher altitudes.

Due to its medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated for use in traditional herbal remedies.
