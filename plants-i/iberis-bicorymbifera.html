Iberis bicorymbifera

Iberis bicorymbifera, commonly known as the Sierra madre flowering plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters called corymbs. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Iberis bicorymbifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
