Ilex aquifolium var. maderensis

Ilex aquifolium var. maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan holly, is a variant of the common holly native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically unspined, distinguishing it from the more commonly known varieties. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
