Ipomoea subrevoluta var. induta

Ipomoea subrevoluta var. induta, commonly known as the curled leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking foliage.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
