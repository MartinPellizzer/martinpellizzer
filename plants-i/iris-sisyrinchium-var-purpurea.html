Iris sisyrinchium var. purpurea

Iris sisyrinchium var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and graceful appearance.
