Ipomoea capitellata

Ipomoea capitellata, commonly known as the Cape jasmine or yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
