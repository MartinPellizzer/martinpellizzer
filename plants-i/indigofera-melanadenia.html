Indigofera melanadenia

Indigofera melanadenia, commonly known as black-adenia indigofera, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black stipules on its leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
