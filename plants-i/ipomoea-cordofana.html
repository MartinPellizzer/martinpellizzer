Ipomoea cordofana

Ipomoea cordofana, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a herbaceous annual or perennial, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Ipomoea cordofana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
