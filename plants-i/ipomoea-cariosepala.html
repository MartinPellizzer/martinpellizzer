Ipomoea cariosepala

Ipomoea cariosepala, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea cariosepala is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
