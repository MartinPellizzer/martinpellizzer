Iris hartwegii subsp. pinetorum

Iris hartwegii subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils within pine and oak woodlands.

The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its adaptability to shaded environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
