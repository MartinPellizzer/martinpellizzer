Indigofera arenicola

Indigofera arenicola, commonly known as sand indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and drought-resistant foliage. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration in degraded landscapes.
