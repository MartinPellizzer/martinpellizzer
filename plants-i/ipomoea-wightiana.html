Ipomoea wightiana

Ipomoea wightiana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Ipomoea wightiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
