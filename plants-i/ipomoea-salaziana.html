Ipomoea salaziana

Ipomoea salaziana, commonly known as the Salazian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded areas.

Ipomoea salaziana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
