Ipomoea plummerae f. typica

Ipomoea plummerae f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the scarlet morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange. The flowers typically open in the early morning and close by midday, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
