Ixia radiata

Ixia radiata, commonly known as the fire lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Ixia radiata is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, sharing characteristics with other bulbous flowering plants.
