Ipomoea karwinskiana

Ipomoea karwinskiana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

The vine can grow up to 10 feet long, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
