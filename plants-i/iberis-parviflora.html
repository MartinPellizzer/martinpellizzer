Iberis parviflora

Iberis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a slightly fuzzy texture, making it visually appealing. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in rock gardens or as edging plants.

Its compact size and long blooming period make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
