Ichnanthus trinitensis

Ichnanthus trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its small, greenish-white petals and intricate floral structure are adaptations to its specific ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
