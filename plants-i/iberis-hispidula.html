Iberis hispidula

Iberis hispidula, commonly known as the hairy candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Iberis hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.
