Iberis cuneata

Iberis cuneata, commonly known as the Armenian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
