Inula bakeriana

Inula bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's Inula, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features a tall, slender stem covered in fine hairs, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
