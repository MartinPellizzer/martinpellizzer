Indigofera hutchinsoniana

Indigofera hutchinsoniana, commonly known as the mountain indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
