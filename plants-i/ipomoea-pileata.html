Ipomoea pileata

Ipomoea pileata, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Ipomoea pileata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
