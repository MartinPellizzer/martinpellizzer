Ionoxalis discolor

Ionoxalis discolor, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, often marked with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Ionoxalis discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.
