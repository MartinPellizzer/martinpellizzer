Inula langeana

Inula langeana, commonly known as the Langean Inula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Inula langeana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
