Inti janiceae

Inti janiceae, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Inti janiceae is adapted to the harsh, cold climates of the Andean region and plays a role in local ecosystems.
