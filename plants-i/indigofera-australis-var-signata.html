Indigofera australis var. signata

Indigofera australis var. signata, commonly known as the Australian indigo.

It is a leguminous plant native to Australia, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain small, rounded seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
