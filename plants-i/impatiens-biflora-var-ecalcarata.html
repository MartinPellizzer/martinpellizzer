Impatiens biflora var. ecalcarata

Impatiens biflora var. ecalcarata, commonly known as the two-flowered impatiens variant with spurred petals, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that give it a unique floral appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and opposite leaves. It produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
