Iberis integrifolia

Iberis integrifolia, commonly known as candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are entire and oval in shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is often cultivated in gardens for its decorative value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Iberis integrifolia is also valued for its resilience to coastal conditions and its ability to attract pollinators.
