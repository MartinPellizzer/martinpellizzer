Iris statellae

Iris statellae, commonly known as the star iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Iris statellae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
