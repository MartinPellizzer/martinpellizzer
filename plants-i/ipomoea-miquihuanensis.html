Ipomoea miquihuanensis

Ipomoea miquihuanensis, commonly known as the Miquihuanan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple blooms that open in the morning and close at dusk. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its vines can grow up to several meters in length.

Ipomoea miquihuanensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
