Ilex laevigata

Ilex laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Ilex laevigata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
