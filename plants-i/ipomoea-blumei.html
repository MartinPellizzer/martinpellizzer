Ipomoea blumei

Ipomoea blumei, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, giving it its common name.
