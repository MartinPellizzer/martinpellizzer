Ipomopsis congesta subsp. frutescens

Ipomopsis congesta subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Ipomopsis congesta.
