Iris mangaliae

Iris mangaliae, commonly known as the Mangalay Iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the coastal regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.

Iris mangaliae is prized for its elegant appearance and resilience in maritime climates.
