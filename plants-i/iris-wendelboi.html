Iris wendelboi

Iris wendelboi, commonly known as the Tibetan iris, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Iris wendelboi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
