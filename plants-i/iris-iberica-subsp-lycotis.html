Iris iberica subsp. lycotis

Iris iberica subsp.

lycotis, commonly known as the Alboran iris, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with a white throat, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, calcareous soils near water bodies. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some regions.
