Isodon brevicalcaratus

Isodon brevicalcaratus, commonly known as the short-spurred isodon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Isodon brevicalcaratus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
