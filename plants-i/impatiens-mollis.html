Impatiens mollis

Impatiens mollis, commonly known as the soft impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Impatiens mollis is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
