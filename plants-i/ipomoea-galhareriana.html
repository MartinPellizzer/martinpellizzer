Ipomoea galhareriana

Ipomoea galhareriana, commonly known as the Galharera morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces strikingly large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its aggressive spreading habit, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
