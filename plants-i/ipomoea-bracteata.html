Ipomoea bracteata

Ipomoea bracteata, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea bracteata is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, supported by its tendrils.
