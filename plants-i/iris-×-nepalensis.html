Iris × nepalensis

Iris × nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
