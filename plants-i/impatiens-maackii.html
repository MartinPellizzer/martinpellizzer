Impatiens maackii

Impatiens maackii, commonly known as Chinese balm, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of delicate, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant's rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can become invasive in some regions.
