Ipomoea kraholandica

Ipomoea kraholandica, commonly known as the Kraholandica morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea kraholandica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can bloom throughout the growing season.
