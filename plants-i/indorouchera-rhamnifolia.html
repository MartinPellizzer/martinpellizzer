Indorouchera rhamnifolia

Indorouchera rhamnifolia, commonly known as the Indian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, scrubland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
