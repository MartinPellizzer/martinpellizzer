Indigofera paracapitata

Indigofera paracapitata, commonly known as the small-headed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
