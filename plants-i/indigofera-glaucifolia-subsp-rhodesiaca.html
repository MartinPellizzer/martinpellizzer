Indigofera glaucifolia subsp. rhodesiaca

Indigofera glaucifolia subsp.

rhodesiaca, commonly known as Rhodesian indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a bluish-gray underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.

Its seeds are encased in hairy, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.
