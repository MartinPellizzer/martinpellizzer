Isolepis schraderiana

Isolepis schraderiana, commonly known as Schrader's spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Isolepis schraderiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
