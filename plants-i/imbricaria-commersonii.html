Imbricaria commersonii

Imbricaria commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

Imbricaria commersonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
