Ixora cauliflora

Ixora cauliflora, commonly known as the fragrant ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that produce small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

Ixora cauliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
