Ilex wattii subsp. wattii

Ilex wattii subsp.

wattii, commonly known as the Watt's holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
