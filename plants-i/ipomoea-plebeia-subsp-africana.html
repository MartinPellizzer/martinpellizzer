Ipomoea plebeia subsp. africana

Ipomoea plebeia subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical Africa. It features heart-shaped, alternate leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
