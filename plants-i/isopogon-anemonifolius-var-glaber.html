Isopogon anemonifolius var. glaber

Isopogon anemonifolius var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth isopogon, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The varietal name 'glaber' refers to its smooth, hairless leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties within the species.
