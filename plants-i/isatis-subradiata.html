Isatis subradiata

Isatis subradiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf woad, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Isatis subradiata is also noted for its historical use in dye production, similar to its more famous relative, the common woad.
