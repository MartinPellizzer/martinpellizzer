Indigofera hirsuta

Indigofera hirsuta, commonly known as hairy indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or for its use in traditional medicine.
