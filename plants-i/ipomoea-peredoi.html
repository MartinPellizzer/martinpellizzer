Ipomoea peredoi

Ipomoea peredoi, commonly known as the Peredoi morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea peredoi is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants known for their ornamental value.
