Indigofera tumidula

Indigofera tumidula, commonly known as the inflated indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small, round balloons. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical studies.
