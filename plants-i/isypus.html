Isypus

Isypus, commonly known as the wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Isypus is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
