Ischaemum peruvianum

Ischaemum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

Ischaemum peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
