Iozoste quercina

Iozoste quercina, commonly known as the silver oak, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray bark and lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by acorn-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
