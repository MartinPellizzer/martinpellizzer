Ilex verticillata var. padifolia

Ilex verticillata var. padifolia, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red twigs that become prominent in winter, providing seasonal interest. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife.

Ilex verticillata var. padifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
