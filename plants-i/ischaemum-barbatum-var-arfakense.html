Ischaemum barbatum var. arfakense

Ischaemum barbatum var. arfakense, commonly known as the Arfak grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often found in lowland and montane areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, two-lobed glumes.

This variant is distinguished by its hairy lemma and is adapted to the humid, warm climate of its native habitat.
