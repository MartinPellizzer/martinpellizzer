Inula canadensis

Inula canadensis, commonly known as Canadian Inula, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features a distinctive yellow flower head composed of numerous ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter tall, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
