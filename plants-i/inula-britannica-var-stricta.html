Inula britannica var. stricta

Inula britannica var. stricta, commonly known as the British Inula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It features a tall, upright stem covered in coarse, hairy foliage. The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late summer, composed of many small florets arranged in a disc-like structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
