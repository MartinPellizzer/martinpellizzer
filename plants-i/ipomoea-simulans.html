Ipomoea simulans

Ipomoea simulans, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

Ipomoea simulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
