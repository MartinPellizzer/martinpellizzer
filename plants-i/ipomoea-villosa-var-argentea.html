Ipomoea villosa var. argentea

Ipomoea villosa var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along structures.
