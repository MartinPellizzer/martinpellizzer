Indigofera balfouriana

Indigofera balfouriana, commonly known as the blue bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its presence in various ecological and horticultural contexts.
