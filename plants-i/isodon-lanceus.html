Isodon lanceus

Isodon lanceus, commonly known as the lanceleaf isodon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Isodon lanceus is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
