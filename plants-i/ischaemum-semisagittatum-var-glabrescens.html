Ischaemum semisagittatum var. glabrescens

Ischaemum semisagittatum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous arrowheadgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadside banks, wet meadows, and along stream edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are usually smooth and lack the hairy texture found in other varieties of the species.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate wet conditions.
