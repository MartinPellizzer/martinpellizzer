Ipomoea undulata

Ipomoea undulata, commonly known as the wavy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with wavy margins, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which can be used for propagation.
