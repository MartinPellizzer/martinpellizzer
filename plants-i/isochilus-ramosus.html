Isochilus ramosus

Isochilus ramosus, commonly known as the branched rock orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a slender, branching stem with numerous small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose cluster. Its roots are often specialized for absorbing moisture from the dry habitat.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
