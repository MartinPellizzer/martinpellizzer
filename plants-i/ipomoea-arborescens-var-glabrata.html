Ipomoea arborescens var. glabrata

Ipomoea arborescens var. glabrata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
