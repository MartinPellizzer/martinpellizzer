Iris × pallida

Iris × pallida, commonly known as the pale iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, showy flowers. The flowers typically feature a pale blue or white coloration with a distinctive dark veining, and they bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage consists of sword-shaped leaves that are evergreen in some cultivars, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
