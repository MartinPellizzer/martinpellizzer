Ipomoea arenaria var. integerrima

Ipomoea arenaria var. integerrima, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
