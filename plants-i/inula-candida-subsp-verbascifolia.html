Inula candida subsp. verbascifolia

Inula candida subsp.

verbascifolia, commonly known as the white gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a rosette of silvery-gray, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are upright and often branched, with a rough, hairy texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
