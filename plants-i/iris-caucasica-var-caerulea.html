Iris caucasica var. caerulea

Iris caucasica var. caerulea, commonly known as the Caucasian blue iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive dark purple stripe down the central sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, typical of many iris species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
