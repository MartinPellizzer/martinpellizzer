Iguanura wallichiana

Iguanura wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its inflorescence bears a cluster of fragrant, colorful blooms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
