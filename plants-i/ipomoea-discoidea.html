Ipomoea discoidea

Ipomoea discoidea, commonly known as the "disco flower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea discoidea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
