Iva axillaris var. linearifolia

Iva axillaris var. linearifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf marsh elder, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy, arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
