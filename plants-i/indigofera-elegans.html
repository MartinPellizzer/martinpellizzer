Indigofera elegans

Indigofera elegans, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Indigofera elegans is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in nitrogen fixation.
