Ipomoea acanthocarpa

Ipomoea acanthocarpa, commonly known as the spiny morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ipomoea acanthocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can be invasive in certain environments.
