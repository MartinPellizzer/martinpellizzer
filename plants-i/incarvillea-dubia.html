Incarvillea dubia

Incarvillea dubia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and often grows in arid or semi-arid environments.

The flowers bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
