Inula asperrima

Inula asperrima, commonly known as rough inula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the family.

Inula asperrima is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
