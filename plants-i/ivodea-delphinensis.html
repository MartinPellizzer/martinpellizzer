Ivodea delphinensis

Ivodea delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinus ivy, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ivodea delphinensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
