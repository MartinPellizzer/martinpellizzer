Isopogon teretifolius subsp. petrophiloides

Isopogon teretifolius subsp.

petrophiloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf isopogon, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is typically found in sandy or rocky soils, often in heathlands or along coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
