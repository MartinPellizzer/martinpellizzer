Ipomoea kituiensis var. hirsutissima

Ipomoea kituiensis var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the Kitui morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the hirsutissima variety. Its flowers are usually blue or violet in color and have a trumpet-shaped structure.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
