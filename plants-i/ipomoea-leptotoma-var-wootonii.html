Ipomoea leptotoma var. wootonii

Ipomoea leptotoma var. wootonii, commonly known as Wooton's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, opening in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
