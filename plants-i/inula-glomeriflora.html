Inula glomeriflora

Inula glomeriflora, commonly known as the clustered-inula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Inula glomeriflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
