Ixia serotina

Ixia serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering yellow iris, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and water features.

Ixia serotina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
