Iranecio pandurifolius

Iranecio pandurifolius, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a striking yellow or green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
