Impatiens intermedia

Impatiens intermedia, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 inches. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Impatiens intermedia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
