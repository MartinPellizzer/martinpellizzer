Ilex cubana

Ilex cubana, commonly known as the Cuban holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that are typically edible. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability. Ilex cubana typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its dense foliage makes it an effective hedge plant and a popular choice for gardeners seeking year-round visual interest.
