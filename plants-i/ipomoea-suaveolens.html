Ipomoea suaveolens

Ipomoea suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that often display variegated patterns. The plant produces vibrant purple, pink, or white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant or for its edible tubers.

Its botanical name reflects its pleasant fragrance, which is more pronounced in its flowers.
