Ilex paniculata

Ilex paniculata, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. Ilex paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
