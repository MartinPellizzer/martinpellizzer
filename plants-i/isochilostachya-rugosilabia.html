Isochilostachya rugosilabia

Isochilostachya rugosilabia, commonly known as the rugose labiate grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Isochilostachya rugosilabia is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
