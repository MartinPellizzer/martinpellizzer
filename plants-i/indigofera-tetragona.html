Indigofera tetragona

Indigofera tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in a pod-like fruit. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
