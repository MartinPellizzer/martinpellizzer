Ipomoea lambtoniana

Ipomoea lambtoniana, commonly known as the Lambton's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
