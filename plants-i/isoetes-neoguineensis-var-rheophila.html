Isoetes neoguineensis var. rheophila

Isoetes neoguineensis var. rheophila, commonly known as the Rheophytic water starwort, is a rare aquatic plant species belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted for underwater growth. This variety is typically found in fast-flowing, nutrient-rich streams and rivers, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located at the tips of the leaves, a feature common to the genus.

The plant plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and providing habitat for microorganisms.
