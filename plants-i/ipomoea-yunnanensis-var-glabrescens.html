Ipomoea yunnanensis var. glabrescens

Ipomoea yunnanensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Yunnan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant is a herbaceous vine that typically grows up to 2 meters in length, with hairy stems that become smooth as it matures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers open in the morning and close at night.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
