Ipomoea kentrocarpa

Ipomoea kentrocarpa, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. Its flowers are tubular and often have a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea kentrocarpa is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and climbers.
