Isatis cappadocica subsp. steveniana

Isatis cappadocica subsp.

steveniana, commonly known as the Cappadocian woad, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its biennial growth habit, with basal leaves that are lanceolate and often pubescent.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves and stems contain glucosinolates, compounds that have been historically used for dyeing textiles.

This subspecies is native to Turkey and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
