Icica viridiflora

Icica viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered icica, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lush, broad leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to bright green, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
