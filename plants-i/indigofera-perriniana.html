Indigofera perriniana

Indigofera perriniana, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye in some regions.
