Iphiona ilicifolia

Iphiona ilicifolia, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Iphiona ilicifolia is adapted to sandy and saline soils, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
