Isolepis armerioides

Isolepis armerioides, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often crowded at the base, forming a dense rosette. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Isolepis armerioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
