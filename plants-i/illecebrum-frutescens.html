Illecebrum frutescens

Illecebrum frutescens, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces showy, often violet or blue-purplish flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange berry that splits open when mature.

Illecebrum frutescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
