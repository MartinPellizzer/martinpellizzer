Isachne mysorensis

Isachne mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore grass, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Isachne mysorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
