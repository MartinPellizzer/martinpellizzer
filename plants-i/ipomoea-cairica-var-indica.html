Ipomoea cairica var. indica

Ipomoea cairica var. indica, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.
