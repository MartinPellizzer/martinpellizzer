Iris sabina

Iris sabina, commonly known as the Spanish iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Iris sabina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
