Isoetes histrix subvar. armata

Isoetes histrix subvar. armata, commonly known as the armored water clubmoss, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, spike-like formations along the plant's axis. The plant thrives in wet, calcareous environments such as marshes and pond margins, where it can absorb nutrients from the water. Its unique morphology includes a central axis with whorls of leaf-like structures, and its subvariety armata is distinguished by the presence of small, spiny projections on the sporangia.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is often considered an indicator species for aquatic ecosystems.
