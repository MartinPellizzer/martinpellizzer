Ipomoea setosa var. campanulata

Ipomoea setosa var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or pink. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
