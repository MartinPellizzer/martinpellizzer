Indocalamus solidus

Indocalamus solidus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes used in construction and as a source of raw materials.
