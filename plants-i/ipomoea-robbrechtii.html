Ipomoea robbrechtii

Ipomoea robbrechtii, commonly known as Robbrecht's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ipomoea robbrechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
