Ipomoea crassipes var. thunbergioides

Ipomoea crassipes var. thunbergioides, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tint and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with five petals, often resembling those of the hyacinth. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is known for its rapid growth rate.

Due to its aggressive spreading, it can become invasive and disrupt local ecosystems if not managed properly.
