Inula chrysocomoides

Inula chrysocomoides, commonly known as the golden cup-plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
