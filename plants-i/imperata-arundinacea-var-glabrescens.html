Imperata arundinacea var. glabrescens

Imperata arundinacea var. glabrescens, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple or pinkish inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
