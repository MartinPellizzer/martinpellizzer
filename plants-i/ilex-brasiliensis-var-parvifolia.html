Ilex brasiliensis var. parvifolia

Ilex brasiliensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
