Iphigenia ramosissima

Iphigenia ramosissima, commonly known as the fragrant honeysuckle, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Iphigenia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrance.
