Iris astrachanica

Iris astrachanica, commonly known as the Astrachan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Iris astrachanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
