Iris × kobasensis

Iris × kobasensis, commonly known as the Kobasen iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris uniflora.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves, and it produces striking blue to purple flowers in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
