Iris schachtii

Iris schachtii, commonly known as the Schacht's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Iris schachtii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
