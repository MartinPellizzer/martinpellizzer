Impatiens oreocallis

Impatiens oreocallis, commonly known as the Andes impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive spurred petal. Its foliage is usually dark green and slightly hairy, adapting to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat.

Impatiens oreocallis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
