Indigofera diphylla var. lanuginosa

Indigofera diphylla var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, and it is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
