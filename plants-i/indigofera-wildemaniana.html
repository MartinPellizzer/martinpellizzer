Indigofera wildemaniana

Indigofera wildemaniana, commonly known as the wildeman's indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

While not widely cultivated for its dye, it has ecological significance as a source of food for certain insect species and as a potential ornamental plant.
