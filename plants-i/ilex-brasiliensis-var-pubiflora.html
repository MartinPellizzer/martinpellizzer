Ilex brasiliensis var. pubiflora

Ilex brasiliensis var. pubiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in garden settings.
