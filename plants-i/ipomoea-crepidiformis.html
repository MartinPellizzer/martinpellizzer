Ipomoea crepidiformis

Ipomoea crepidiformis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a sprawling growth habit, often covering the ground or climbing structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea crepidiformis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
