Iris kurbanovii

Iris kurbanovii, commonly known as the Kurbanov's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris kurbanovii is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
