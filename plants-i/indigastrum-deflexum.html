Indigastrum deflexum

Indigastrum deflexum, commonly known as the flexed indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are small and blue-violet, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Indigastrum deflexum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
