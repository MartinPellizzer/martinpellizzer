Indigofera rhynchocarpa var. quadrangularis

Indigofera rhynchocarpa var. quadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems, which give it its specific variety name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
