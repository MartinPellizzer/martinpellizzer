Inula haussknechtiana

Inula haussknechtiana, commonly known as the alpine ragwort, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, adorned with yellow composite flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Inula haussknechtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine environment.
