Ipomoea corneri

Ipomoea corneri, commonly known as the Corner's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers are typically open during the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
