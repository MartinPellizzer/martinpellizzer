Ilex valerii

Ilex valerii, commonly known as the Valerian holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet, featuring dense, spiny foliage that provides habitat for various wildlife species. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.

Ilex valerii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardy nature and seasonal visual interest.
