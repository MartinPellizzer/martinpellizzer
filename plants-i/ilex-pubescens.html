Ilex pubescens

Ilex pubescens, commonly known as the hairy holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex pubescens typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
