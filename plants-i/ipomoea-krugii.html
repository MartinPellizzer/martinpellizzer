Ipomoea krugii

Ipomoea krugii, commonly known as Krug's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring heart-shaped leaves and a rapid growth rate. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea krugii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
