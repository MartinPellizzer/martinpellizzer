Iris alberti

Iris alberti, commonly known as Albert's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, typical of the iris family. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming period.
