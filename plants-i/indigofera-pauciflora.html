Indigofera pauciflora

Indigofera pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera pauciflora is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
