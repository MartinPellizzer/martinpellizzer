Ipomoea leuconeura

Ipomoea leuconeura, commonly known as the white-veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy flowers typically in the morning, hence its common name. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ipomoea leuconeura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
