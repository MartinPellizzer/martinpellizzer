Ilex microphylla

Ilex microphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Ilex microphylla is also valued for its year-round foliage and ability to tolerate urban environments.
