Ipomoea deccana

Ipomoea deccana, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence its common name.

Ipomoea deccana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
