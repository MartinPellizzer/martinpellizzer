Iris prismatica

Iris prismatica, commonly known as the prismatic iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes a variety of flowering plants known for their showy blooms. The plant features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its flowers are typically three to six petals, arranged in a symmetrical pattern, and are often accompanied by three prominent sepals.

Iris prismatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
