Indigofera monanthoides

Indigofera monanthoides, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in savannas and woodland areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine and for dyeing purposes due to its rich chemical composition.
