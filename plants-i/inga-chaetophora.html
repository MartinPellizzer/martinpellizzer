Inga chaetophora

Inga chaetophora, commonly known as the hairy inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Inga chaetophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
