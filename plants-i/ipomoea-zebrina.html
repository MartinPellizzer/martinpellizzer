Ipomoea zebrina

Ipomoea zebrina, commonly known as the Zebra plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with green and white stripes, and its trumpet-shaped, predominantly purple flowers. The plant is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and ample sunlight.

Ipomoea zebrina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
