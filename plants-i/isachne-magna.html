Isachne magna

Isachne magna, commonly known as the giant isachne, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and marshes of eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense colonies in moist soil.

Isachne magna is often found in areas with standing water or high humidity, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
