Ixia phalangioides

Ixia phalangioides, commonly known as the yellow fire lily, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Ixia phalangioides is also notable for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its natural spread in its native habitat.
