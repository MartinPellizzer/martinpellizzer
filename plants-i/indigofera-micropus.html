Indigofera micropus

Indigofera micropus, commonly known as the small-fruited indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Indigofera micropus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including forests and grasslands.
