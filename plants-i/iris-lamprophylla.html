Iris lamprophylla

Iris lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale blue throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Iris lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
