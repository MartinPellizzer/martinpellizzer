Isoetes riparia var. amesii

Isoetes riparia var. amesii, commonly known as the Ames's watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporocarps, which are typically found in freshwater habitats. The plant is native to North America and is often found in still or slow-moving water bodies. It has a distinctive appearance with its dark, ribbon-like leaves and compact, clustered sporocarps.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining aquatic biodiversity and water quality.
