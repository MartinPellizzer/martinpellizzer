Ipomoea humidicola

Ipomoea humidicola, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or swampy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often opening in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can be quite large, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ipomoea humidicola is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
