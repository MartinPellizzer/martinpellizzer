Indigofera congesta var. flamans

Indigofera congesta var. flamans, commonly known as the flame indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various climates contribute to its horticultural appeal.
