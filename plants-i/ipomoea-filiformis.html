Ipomoea filiformis

Ipomoea filiformis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thin, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It blooms throughout the growing season and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea filiformis prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
