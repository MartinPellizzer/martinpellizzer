Isolepis nigrescens

Isolepis nigrescens, commonly known as black rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It typically grows in dense stands, forming a mat-like structure with slender, dark green to blackish stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively.

Isolepis nigrescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
