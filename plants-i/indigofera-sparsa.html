Indigofera sparsa

Indigofera sparsa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia, often found in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters tall.

The plant is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
