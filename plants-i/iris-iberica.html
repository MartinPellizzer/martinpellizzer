Iris iberica

Iris iberica, commonly known as the Iberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers are usually three to five per stem.

Iris iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
