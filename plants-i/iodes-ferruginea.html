Iodes ferruginea

Iodes ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, rusty-colored hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Iodes ferruginea is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators.
