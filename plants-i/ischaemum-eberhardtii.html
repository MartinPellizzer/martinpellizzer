Ischaemum eberhardtii

Ischaemum eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's barnyardgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for studying wetland ecosystems and grassland biodiversity.
