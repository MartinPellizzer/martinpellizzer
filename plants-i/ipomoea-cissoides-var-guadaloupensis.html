Ipomoea cissoides var. guadaloupensis

Ipomoea cissoides var. guadaloupensis, commonly known as Guadeloupe morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Guadeloupe.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oblong seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
