Ilex affinis f. brachyphylla

Ilex affinis f. brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf holly, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with spiny margins. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans but attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This cultivar is valued for its dense growth habit and ornamental appeal in garden settings.
