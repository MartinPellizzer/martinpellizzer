Imperata arundinacea var. koenigii

Imperata arundinacea var. koenigii, commonly known as the bamboo grass or koenigii grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are borne on spikelets. It is often used in horticulture for erosion control and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its fibrous roots, which can be processed into various traditional materials.
