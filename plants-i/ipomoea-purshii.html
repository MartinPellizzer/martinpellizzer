Ipomoea purshii

Ipomoea purshii, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea purshii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
