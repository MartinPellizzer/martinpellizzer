Iris versicolor

Iris versicolor, commonly known as the variable iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has three erect, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Iris versicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and adaptability.
