Iria pilosa

Iria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy iris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or blue flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Iria pilosa is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
