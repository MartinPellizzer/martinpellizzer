Isatis nummularia

Isatis nummularia, commonly known as the Japanese auricula or false dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features rosettes of waxy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

Isatis nummularia is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional medicine.
