Indigofera australis var. gracilis

Indigofera australis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and salty environments.
