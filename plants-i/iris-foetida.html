Iris foetida

Iris foetida, commonly known as the stinking iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, strongly scented center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Iris foetida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
