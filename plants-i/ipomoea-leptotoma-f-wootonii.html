Ipomoea leptotoma f. wootonii

Ipomoea leptotoma f. wootonii, commonly known as the Wooton's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale to deep blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive flowers.
