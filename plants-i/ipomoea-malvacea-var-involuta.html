Ipomoea malvacea var. involuta

Ipomoea malvacea var. involuta, commonly known as the spiral morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet, fragrant aroma. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with a white throat, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The vine is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and blooming habits.
