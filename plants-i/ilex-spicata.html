Ilex spicata

Ilex spicata, commonly known as the spiny holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. Ilex spicata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
