Isatis tinctoria var. yezoensis

Isatis tinctoria var. yezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese woad, is a variant of the woad plant native to Japan.

It is a biennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces dark blue or black seeds.

This variety is valued for its historical use in producing indigo dye and for its ornamental qualities.
