Ipomoea coptica var. siphonantha

Ipomoea coptica var. siphonantha, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in ornamental gardens.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.
