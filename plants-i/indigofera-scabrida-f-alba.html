Indigofera scabrida f. alba

Indigofera scabrida f. alba, commonly known as white scabrous indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its typically green foliage. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
