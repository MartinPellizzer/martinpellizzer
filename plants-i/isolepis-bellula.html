Isolepis bellula

Isolepis bellula, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Isolepis bellula plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
