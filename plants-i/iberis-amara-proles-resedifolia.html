Iberis amara proles resedifolia

Iberis amara proles resedifolia, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
