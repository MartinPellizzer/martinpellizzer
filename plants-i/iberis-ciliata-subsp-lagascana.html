Iberis ciliata subsp. lagascana

Iberis ciliata subsp.

lagascana, commonly known as the Mediterranean candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers.

The plant features pinnately divided leaves with soft, silvery-gray foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
