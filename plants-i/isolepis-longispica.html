Isolepis longispica

Isolepis longispica, commonly known as the long-spined flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically inconspicuous and lack showy petals. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Isolepis longispica plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
