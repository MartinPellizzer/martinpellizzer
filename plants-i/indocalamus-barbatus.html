Indocalamus barbatus

Indocalamus barbatus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a textured surface that aids in water retention.

Indocalamus barbatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
