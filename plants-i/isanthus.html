Isanthus

Isanthus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Isanthus is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
