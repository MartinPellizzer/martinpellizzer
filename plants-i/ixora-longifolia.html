Ixora longifolia

Ixora longifolia, commonly known as the flame of the woods, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Ixora longifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
