Iris × germanica var. suaveolens

Iris × germanica var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces sword-like, grassy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The sweet iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in perfumery due to its pleasant scent.
