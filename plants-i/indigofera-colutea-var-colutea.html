Indigofera colutea var. colutea

Indigofera colutea var. colutea, commonly known as small indigo or Indian indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value and medicinal uses have contributed to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
