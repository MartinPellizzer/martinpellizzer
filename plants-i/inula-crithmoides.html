Inula crithmoides

Inula crithmoides, commonly known as yellow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and bright yellow flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant produces small, composite flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Inula crithmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
