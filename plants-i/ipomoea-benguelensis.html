Ipomoea benguelensis

Ipomoea benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
