Ipomoea subincana

Ipomoea subincana, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often found growing along roadsides or in disturbed areas.

Its vines can climb or trail, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
