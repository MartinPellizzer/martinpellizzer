Iris germanica

Iris germanica, commonly known as the German iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The plant has sword-shaped, basal leaves that are typically bright green and somewhat hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Iris germanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
