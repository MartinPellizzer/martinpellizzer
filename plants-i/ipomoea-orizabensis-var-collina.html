Ipomoea orizabensis var. collina

Ipomoea orizabensis var. collina, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
