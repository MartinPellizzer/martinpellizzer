Iris unguicularis f. speciosa

Iris unguicularis f. speciosa, commonly known as the showy iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, vividly colored flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a distinctive white or yellow beard at the base of the petals.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
