Indigofera plumosa

Indigofera plumosa, commonly known as the feathery indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
