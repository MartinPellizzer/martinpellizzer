Iris nertchinskia

Iris nertchinskia, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Iris nertchinskia is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
