Indigofera anil var. oligophylla

Indigofera anil var. oligophylla, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue flowers. The plant is known for its historical use in producing natural indigo dye, which was highly valued in ancient textile industries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and historical contexts.
