Indigofera arrecta

Indigofera arrecta, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It grows as an erect shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
