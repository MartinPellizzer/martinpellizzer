Iberis grosmiquelii

Iberis grosmiquelii, commonly known as the Turkish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Iberis grosmiquelii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
