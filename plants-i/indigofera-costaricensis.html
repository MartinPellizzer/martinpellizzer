Indigofera costaricensis

Indigofera costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican indigo, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
