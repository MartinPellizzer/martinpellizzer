Indigofera cubensis

Indigofera cubensis, commonly known as Cuban indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in dry or disturbed soils.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
