Iris variabilis subsp. lusitanica

Iris variabilis subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its rhizomes are fibrous and somewhat succulent. It prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial shade to full sun, often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
