Ionidium commune var. typicum

Ionidium commune var. typicum, commonly known as the common ionidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
