Inga organensis

Inga organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains Inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the island of Puerto Rico. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Inga organensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
