Iria velata

Iria velata, commonly known as the veiled iris, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Iria velata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
