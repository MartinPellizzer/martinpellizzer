Iris acutiloba var. bimaculata

Iris acutiloba var. bimaculata, commonly known as the Two-spotted iris, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive two-spotted pattern on the petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
