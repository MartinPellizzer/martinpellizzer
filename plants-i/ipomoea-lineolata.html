Ipomoea lineolata

Ipomoea lineolata, commonly known as the striped morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found twining around supports in gardens and natural habitats. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Ipomoea lineolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
