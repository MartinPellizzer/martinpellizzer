Inula setigera

Inula setigera, commonly known as the slender elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, forming a composite structure that attracts pollinators.

Inula setigera is typically found in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
