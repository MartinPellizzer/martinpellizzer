Isachne eberhardtii

Isachne eberhardtii, commonly known as Eberhardt's isachne, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly folded. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

Isachne eberhardtii is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
