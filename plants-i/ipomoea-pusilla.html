Ipomoea pusilla

Ipomoea pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and delicate plant. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue or white, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Ipomoea pusilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
