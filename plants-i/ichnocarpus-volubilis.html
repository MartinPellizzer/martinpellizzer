Ichnocarpus volubilis

Ichnocarpus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing senna, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a serrated edge.

Ichnocarpus volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
