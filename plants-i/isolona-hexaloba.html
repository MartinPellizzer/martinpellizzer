Isolona hexaloba

Isolona hexaloba, commonly known as the six-lobed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Isolona hexaloba is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes.
