Iris douglasiana var. oregonensis

Iris douglasiana var. oregonensis, commonly known as the Oregon iris, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are sword-shaped and form a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
