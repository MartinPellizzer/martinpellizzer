Ipomoea macrophylla var. selloana

Ipomoea macrophylla var. selloana, commonly known as the Sello's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, with a trumpet shape and five petals. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
