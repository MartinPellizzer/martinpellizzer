Ipomoea mestitlanica

Ipomoea mestitlanica, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea mestitlanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
