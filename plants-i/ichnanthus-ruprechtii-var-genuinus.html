Ichnanthus ruprechtii var. genuinus

Ichnanthus ruprechtii var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bog gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs and marshes.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
