Indigofera galpinii

Indigofera galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clustered, violet-blue flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
