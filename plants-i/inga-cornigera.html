Inga cornigera

Inga cornigera, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically divided into five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga cornigera is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
