Inula stenophylla

Inula stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf elecampane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Inula stenophylla is typically found in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and its role in ecological habitats.
