Indigofera rigioclada

Indigofera rigioclada, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used in traditional medicine or as a source of dye in some cultures.
