Indigofera compacta

Indigofera compacta, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural dyes.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
