Ilex fertilis var. gracilior

Ilex fertilis var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy plant for garden cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping.
