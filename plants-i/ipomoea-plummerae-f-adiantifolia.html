Ipomoea plummerae f. adiantifolia

Ipomoea plummerae f. adiantifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf morning glory, is a unique hybrid within the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage that resembles the structure of the genus Adiantum, hence its name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
