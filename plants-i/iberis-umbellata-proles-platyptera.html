Iberis umbellata proles platyptera

Iberis umbellata proles platyptera, commonly known as the flat-leaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in umbels. The leaves are typically rounded and flat, giving the plant its specific epithet "platyptera.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
