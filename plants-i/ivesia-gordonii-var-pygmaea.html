Ivesia gordonii var. pygmaea

Ivesia gordonii var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ivesia, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh conditions such as drought and extreme temperatures. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
