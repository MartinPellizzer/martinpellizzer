Indigofera pseudodyeri

Indigofera pseudodyeri, commonly known as the Australian indigo.

It is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
