Ipomoea mesatlantica

Ipomoea mesatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to violet, often blooming in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Ipomoea mesatlantica is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
