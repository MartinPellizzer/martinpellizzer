Iris furcata var. diantha

Iris furcata var. diantha, commonly known as the pink iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces tall flowering stems that bear three to five broad, spurred petals, with the outer ones often being a vivid pink or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The botanical features of this variety make it a notable ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
