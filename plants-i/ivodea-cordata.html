Ivodea cordata

Ivodea cordata, commonly known as heartleaf ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or trees.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb.
