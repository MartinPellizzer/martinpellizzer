Ipomoea filifolia

Ipomoea filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ipomoea filifolia is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
