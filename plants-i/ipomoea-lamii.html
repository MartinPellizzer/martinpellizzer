Ipomoea lamii

Ipomoea lamii, commonly known as the everlasting morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself. Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

Its seeds are encased in a dry, persistent fruit that can remain viable for several years.
