Ipomoea porphyrea

Ipomoea porphyrea, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea porphyrea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
