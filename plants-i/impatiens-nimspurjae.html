Impatiens nimspurjae

Impatiens nimspurjae, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often resembling mist or clouds. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Impatiens nimspurjae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.
