Ipomoea rhomboidea

Ipomoea rhomboidea, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces berries containing seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea rhomboidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
