Indigofera angustifolia var. brachystachya

Indigofera angustifolia var. brachystachya, commonly known as dwarf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
