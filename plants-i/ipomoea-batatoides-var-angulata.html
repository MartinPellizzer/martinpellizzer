Ipomoea batatoides var. angulata

Ipomoea batatoides var. angulata, commonly known as the angular batatoid morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive angular leaf base, and its flowers are typically large, trumpet-shaped, and come in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in moist, well-drained soils with partial to full sun.
