Ipomoea maurandioides

Ipomoea maurandioides, commonly known as the Blue Morning Glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea maurandioides is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other vine-like plants.
