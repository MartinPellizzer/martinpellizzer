Igidia

Igidia, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers are often accompanied by spider-like structures, giving it its common name.

Igidia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
