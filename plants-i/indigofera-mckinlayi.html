Indigofera mckinlayi

Indigofera mckinlayi, commonly known as McKinlay's indigo, is a species of legume native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
