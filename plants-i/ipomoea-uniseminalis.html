Ipomoea uniseminalis

Ipomoea uniseminalis, commonly known as the single-seeded morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a single, large seed within its capsule fruit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
