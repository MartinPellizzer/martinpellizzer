Ipomoea engleriana

Ipomoea engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous climber with large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a trailing habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the afternoon, hence its common name.

Ipomoea engleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
