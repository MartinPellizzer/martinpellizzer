Iberis arbuscula

Iberis arbuscula, commonly known as the rock candyflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, arid environments.

Iberis arbuscula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
