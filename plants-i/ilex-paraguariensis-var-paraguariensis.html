Ilex paraguariensis var. paraguariensis

Ilex paraguariensis var. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves and small, bright red berries that contain the seeds. The plant thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in the undergrowth of forests. Its most notable feature is the caffeine-rich leaves, which are used to prepare a traditional infusion.

The plant has a rich cultural significance in several countries, particularly in Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.
