Inga sertulifera subsp. sertulifera

Inga sertulifera subsp.

sertulifera, commonly known as the "chocolate tree," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces edible fruits that are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
