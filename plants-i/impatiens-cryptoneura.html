Impatiens cryptoneura

Impatiens cryptoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens cryptoneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
