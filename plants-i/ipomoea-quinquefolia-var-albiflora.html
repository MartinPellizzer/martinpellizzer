Ipomoea quinquefolia var. albiflora

Ipomoea quinquefolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are white to pale lavender, opening in the morning and closing by midday. It produces small, round, green berries that mature to a dark purple color.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
