Ilex corallina var. pubescens

Ilex corallina var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy coral holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark berries that persist through winter. The leaves are typically elliptical with a rough, textured surface and a reddish underside. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental choice.

Its unique appearance and evergreen nature make it popular in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
