Ilex chartacifolia var. glabra

Ilex chartacifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved holly, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are smooth and often dark green in color. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its evergreen nature provides year-round foliage, enhancing its aesthetic value in gardens.
