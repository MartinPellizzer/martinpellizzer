Indigofera virgata var. parvifolia

Indigofera virgata var. parvifolia, commonly known as small-flowered indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for various purposes.
