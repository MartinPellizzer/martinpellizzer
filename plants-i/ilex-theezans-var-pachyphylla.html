Ilex theezans var. pachyphylla

Ilex theezans var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved holly, is a variant of the holly genus native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for landscape design in warm climates.
