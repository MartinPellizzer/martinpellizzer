Ipomoea ternifolia

Ipomoea ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea ternifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
