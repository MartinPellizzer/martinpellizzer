Ischaemum cuspidatum

Ischaemum cuspidatum, commonly known as speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
