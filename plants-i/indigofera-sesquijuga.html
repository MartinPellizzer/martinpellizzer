Indigofera sesquijuga

Indigofera sesquijuga, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or the Mexican blue bean, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
