Inga alatopetiola

Inga alatopetiola, commonly known as the "Mexican velvet tamarind," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments. Its fruits are pod-like and contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
