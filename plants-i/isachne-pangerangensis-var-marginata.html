Isachne pangerangensis var. marginata

Isachne pangerangensis var. marginata, commonly known as the Pangerang marginated grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive white or silver marginal stripe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in humid tropical regions.
