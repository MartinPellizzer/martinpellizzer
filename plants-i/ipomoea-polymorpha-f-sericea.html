Ipomoea polymorpha f. sericea

Ipomoea polymorpha f. sericea, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family with distinctive silky, hair-like leaves.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the early morning. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft pubescence.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
