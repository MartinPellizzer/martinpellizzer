Ipomoea kourankoensis

Ipomoea kourankoensis, commonly known as the Kouranko morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Ipomoea kourankoensis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing habit and showy blooms.
