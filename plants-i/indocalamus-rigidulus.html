Indocalamus rigidulus

Indocalamus rigidulus, commonly known as the rigid bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has slender, smooth leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Indocalamus rigidulus is valued for its strength and flexibility, making it useful in construction and crafts.
