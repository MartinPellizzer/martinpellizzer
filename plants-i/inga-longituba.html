Inga longituba

Inga longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed inga, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in racemes.

The fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
