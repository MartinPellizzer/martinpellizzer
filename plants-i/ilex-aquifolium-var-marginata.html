Ilex aquifolium var. marginata

Ilex aquifolium var. marginata, commonly known as the variegated holly, is a cultivar of the common holly, characterized by its striking variegated leaves.

The foliage features white or yellow margins contrasting with deep green centers, making it visually distinctive. It is an evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
