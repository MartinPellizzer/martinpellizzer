Indigofera merxmuelleri

Indigofera merxmuelleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, purple flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
