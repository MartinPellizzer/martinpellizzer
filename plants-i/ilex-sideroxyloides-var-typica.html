Ilex sideroxyloides var. typica

Ilex sideroxyloides var. typica, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in shaded areas.
