Ipomoea petaloidea var. andamanica

Ipomoea petaloidea var. andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is typically found growing in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, hence its common name.
