Indigofera sisalis

Indigofera sisalis, commonly known as Indian indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
