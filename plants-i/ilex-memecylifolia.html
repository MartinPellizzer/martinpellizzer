Ilex memecylifolia

Ilex memecylifolia, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and some animals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
