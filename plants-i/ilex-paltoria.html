Ilex paltoria

Ilex paltoria, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex paltoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and winter hardiness.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.
