Impatiens subecalcarata

Impatiens subecalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and ovate, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens subecalcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
