Inga nobilis subsp. quaternata

Inga nobilis subsp.

quaternata, commonly known as the four-leaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The plant is notable for its large, palmate leaves that often have four lobes, giving it its common name. It produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
