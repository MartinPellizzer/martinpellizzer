Ipomoea piurensis

Ipomoea piurensis, commonly known as the Piura morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, climbing or trailing along the ground.

Ipomoea piurensis is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms.
