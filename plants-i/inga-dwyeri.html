Inga dwyeri

Inga dwyeri, commonly known as the dwarf inga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in humid, shaded understory environments.

Inga dwyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
