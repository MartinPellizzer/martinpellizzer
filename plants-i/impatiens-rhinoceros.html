Impatiens rhinoceros

Impatiens rhinoceros, commonly known as the rhino impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom throughout the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus Impatiens is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed dispersal, making this species a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
