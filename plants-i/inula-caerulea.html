Inula caerulea

Inula caerulea, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Inula caerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
