Inula viscosa

Inula viscosa, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Inula viscosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
