Ipomoea hederacea var. paichou

Ipomoea hederacea var. paichou, commonly known as the Chinese morning glory, is a trailing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along supports. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by late afternoon, hence its common name.
