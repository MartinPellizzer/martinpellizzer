Ichnocarpus polyanthus

Ichnocarpus polyanthus, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that contain a single, hard-seeded kernel. The bark and leaves of Ichnocarpus polyanthus have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
