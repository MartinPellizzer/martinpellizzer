Isolepis ramulosa

Isolepis ramulosa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Isolepis ramulosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
