Impatiens lepida

Impatiens lepida, commonly known as the white impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine, soft hairs, while the stems are slender and often branched.

Impatiens lepida thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
