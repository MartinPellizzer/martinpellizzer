Ipomoea oculata

Ipomoea oculata, commonly known as the eye of the bat, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive black spot at the base of the petals, resembling an eye. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Ipomoea oculata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
