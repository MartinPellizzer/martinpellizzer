Ipomoea oligantha

Ipomoea oligantha, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Ipomoea oligantha is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other climbing and trailing vines.
