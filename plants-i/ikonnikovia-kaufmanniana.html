Ikonnikovia kaufmanniana

Ikonnikovia kaufmanniana, commonly known as the Cape flower, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling the shape of a flowerpot. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. It produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and ecological significance in its native habitat.
