Ikonnikovia kaufmanniana

Ikonnikovia kaufmanniana, commonly known as the Cape flower, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling the shape of a flowerpot. It produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and ecological significance in its native habitat.

Morphology

Ikonnikovia kaufmanniana has leaves that are simple, ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green color, arranged alternately along the stem, and typically measuring 5-10 cm in length.

The stem is slender, cylindrical, and covered with a smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes rougher with age, growing upright with some branching. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in early spring and pollinated by insects. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The roots are fibrous and shallow, adapting to the arid conditions of its native habitat.
