Ixora glaucina

Ixora glaucina, commonly known as the white ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Ixora glaucina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
