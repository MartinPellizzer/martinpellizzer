Ipomoea stellata

Ipomoea stellata, commonly known as the star morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, star-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Ipomoea stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its beauty and traditional uses.
