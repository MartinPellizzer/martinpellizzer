Iris coerulea

Iris coerulea, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Iris coerulea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
