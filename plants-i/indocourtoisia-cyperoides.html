Indocourtoisia cyperoides

Indocourtoisia cyperoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
