Ixia densiflora

Ixia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered ixia, is a striking South African bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for gardens in cooler climates. Ixia densiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize over time.
