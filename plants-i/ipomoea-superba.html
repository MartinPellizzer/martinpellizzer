Ipomoea superba

Ipomoea superba, commonly known as the royal morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb supports with its strong, twisting stems.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, giving it its common name.
