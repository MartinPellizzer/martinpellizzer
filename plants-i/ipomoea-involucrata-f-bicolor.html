Ipomoea involucrata f. bicolor

Ipomoea involucrata f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored morning glory, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that display a distinctive bicolor pattern, typically with a white or pale pink base and a deep blue or purple upper part. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, fast-growing ornamental plants.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
