Iseilema argutum

Iseilema argutum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Iseilema argutum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in agricultural areas.
