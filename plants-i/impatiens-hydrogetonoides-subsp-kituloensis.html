Impatiens hydrogetonoides subsp. kituloensis

Impatiens hydrogetonoides subsp.

kituloensis, commonly known as the Kitulo impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude grasslands of the Kitulo National Park in Ethiopia. It is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, temperate climate of its native habitat. It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
