Impatiens pulcherrima

Impatiens pulcherrima, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is a popular ornamental species due to its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

The wax plant is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light environments.
