Irmischia

Irmischia, commonly known as the desert bell, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their succulent leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. Irmischia species are typically low-growing shrubs or herbs, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. They have a deep root system that allows them to access water from deeper soil layers.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
