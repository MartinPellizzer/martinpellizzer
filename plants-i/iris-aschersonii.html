Iris aschersonii

Iris aschersonii, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its striking floral display.
