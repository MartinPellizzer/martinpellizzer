Indigofera berteroana

Indigofera berteroana, commonly known as the Mexican indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important species in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
