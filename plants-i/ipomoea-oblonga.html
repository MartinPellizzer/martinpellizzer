Ipomoea oblonga

Ipomoea oblonga, commonly known as the round-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports or structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea oblonga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
