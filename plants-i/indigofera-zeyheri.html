Indigofera zeyheri

Indigofera zeyheri, commonly known as the blue heliotrope, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
