Ixora parviflora var. parviflora

Ixora parviflora var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year, though they are most abundant during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
