Isachne atrovirens

Isachne atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, dark green leaves that have prominent black veins. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Isachne atrovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
