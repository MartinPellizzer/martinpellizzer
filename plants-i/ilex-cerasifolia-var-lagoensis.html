Ilex cerasifolia var. lagoensis

Ilex cerasifolia var. lagoensis, commonly known as the Lagoensis Holly, is a variant of the evergreen holly species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens and parks.
