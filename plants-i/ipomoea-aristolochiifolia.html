Ipomoea aristolochiifolia

Ipomoea aristolochiifolia, commonly known as the noble heart vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, often using tendrils to support its growth. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal.
