Ipomoea serrulifera

Ipomoea serrulifera, commonly known as the serrated morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, serrated leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Ipomoea serrulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
