Ipomoea geranioides

Ipomoea geranioides, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often mistaken for those of geraniums. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ipomoea geranioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats alike.
