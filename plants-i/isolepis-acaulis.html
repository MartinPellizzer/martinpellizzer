Isolepis acaulis

Isolepis acaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, unbranched stems that are often hairless. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and stream edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Isolepis acaulis is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
