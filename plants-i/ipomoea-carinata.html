Ipomoea carinata

Ipomoea carinata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, slender vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea carinata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
