Indigofera polysperma

Indigofera polysperma, commonly known as the African indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agroforestry systems.
