Inga cabrerae

Inga cabrerae, commonly known as the Colombian inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Inga cabrerae typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
