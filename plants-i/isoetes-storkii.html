Isoetes storkii

Isoetes storkii, commonly known as the Stork's iris, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its spiky, sword-shaped leaves that are typically submerged in water, with some leaves extending above the water surface. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are located at the base of the leaves. Isoetes storkii is often found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
