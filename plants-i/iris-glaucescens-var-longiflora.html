Iris glaucescens var. longiflora

Iris glaucescens var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, grass-like foliage and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is characterized by its long, slender flower stalks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
