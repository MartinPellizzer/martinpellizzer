Isabelia violacea

Isabelia violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. Isabelia violacea thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
