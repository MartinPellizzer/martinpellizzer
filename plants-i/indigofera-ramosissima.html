Indigofera ramosissima

Indigofera ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
