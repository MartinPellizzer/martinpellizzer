Indigofera mollis

Indigofera mollis, commonly known as silky indigo, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Indigofera mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in dyeing and medicinal applications.
