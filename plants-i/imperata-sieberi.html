Imperata sieberi

Imperata sieberi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blumea, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear white to pale purple flowers. It is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Imperata sieberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
