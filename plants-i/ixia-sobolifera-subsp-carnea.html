Ixia sobolifera subsp. carnea

Ixia sobolifera subsp.

carnea, commonly known as the pink-flowered ixia, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of vibrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
