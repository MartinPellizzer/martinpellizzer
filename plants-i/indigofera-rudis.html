Indigofera rudis

Indigofera rudis, commonly known as the coastal indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and have a distinctive shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating saline conditions and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
