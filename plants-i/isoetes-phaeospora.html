Isoetes phaeospora

Isoetes phaeospora, commonly known as the black spore watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically dark in color, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in bogs and marshes. Its morphology includes a central axis with whorls of leaves, and it lacks true roots, relying instead on rhizomes for anchorage.

Isoetes phaeospora plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
