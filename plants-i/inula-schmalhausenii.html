Inula schmalhausenii

Inula schmalhausenii, commonly known as the Schmalhausen's elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
