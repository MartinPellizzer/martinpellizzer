Ilex verticillata var. fastigiata

Ilex verticillata var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and vibrant red berries that appear in winter. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, turning red in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is popular in landscaping for its ornamental berries and seasonal color changes.
