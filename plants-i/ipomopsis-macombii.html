Ipomopsis macombii

Ipomopsis macombii, commonly known as Macomb's monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open habitats like meadows and slopes. It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its delicate, hairy leaves.

Ipomopsis macombii is often found in California and Oregon, where it thrives in sunny, arid environments.
