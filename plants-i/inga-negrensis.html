Inga negrensis

Inga negrensis, commonly known as the black inga, is a species of leguminous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, contributing to soil fertility. Inga negrensis grows in a variety of habitats, including lowland and montane forests, and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

Its fruits are pods that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.
