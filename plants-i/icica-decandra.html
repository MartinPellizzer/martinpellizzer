Icica decandra

Icica decandra, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
