Ilex purpurea var. myriadenia

Ilex purpurea var. myriadenia, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its dense foliage and winter hardiness make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
