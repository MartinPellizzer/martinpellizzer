Inga cariacea

Inga cariacea, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "sweet apple," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several round, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a feathery appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
