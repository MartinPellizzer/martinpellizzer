Ipomoea batatoides

Ipomoea batatoides, commonly known as the batat flower or the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves, which are often variegated with white or cream markings. Its tuberous roots, while not typically cultivated for food, are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Ipomoea batatoides is popular in ornamental gardening for its colorful blooms and cascading foliage.
