Indigofera parviflora var. crispidula

Indigofera parviflora var. crispidula, commonly known as the crinkled indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are compound, typically with a feathery appearance, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
