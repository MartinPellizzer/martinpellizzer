Ipomoea brownii

Ipomoea brownii, commonly known as the Brown's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning, hence its common name.

Ipomoea brownii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
