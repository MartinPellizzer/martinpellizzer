Inga balsapambensis

Inga balsapambensis, commonly known as the Guayacan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by pods containing edible seeds.

It is often found in lowland tropical rainforests and is valued for its timber and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
