Iris laevigata f. leucantha

Iris laevigata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a botanical variant of the broadleaf iris.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, sword-shaped leaves. This perennial plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its native environment.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
