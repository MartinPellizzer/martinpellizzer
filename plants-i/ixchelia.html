Ixchelia

Ixchelia, commonly known as the "flower of the dead," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Ixchelia is known for its unique ability to produce a milky sap, which can be toxic.

Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often associated with cultural and spiritual symbolism in Mesoamerican traditions.
