Isodon longitubus var. intermedius

Isodon longitubus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate long-tubed isodon, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China and Vietnam, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
