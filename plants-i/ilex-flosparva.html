Ilex flosparva

Ilex flosparva, commonly known as the small-flowered holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are a source of food for birds. Ilex flosparva thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
