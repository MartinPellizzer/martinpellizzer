Iris himalaica

Iris himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base.

Iris himalaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
