Ipomoea edwardsii

Ipomoea edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards' morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
