Inga sarayacuensis

Inga sarayacuensis, commonly known as the Amazonian velvet tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility in the ecosystem.

Inga sarayacuensis is also valued for its timber and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
