Ixia fugacissima

Ixia fugacissima, commonly known as the Transvaal daffodil, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange blooms that are often speckled with darker hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ixia fugacissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
