Ipomoea fulvicaulis var. depauperata

Ipomoea fulvicaulis var. depauperata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a variant of the Ipomoea fulvicaulis species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often opening in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
