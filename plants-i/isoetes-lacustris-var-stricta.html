Isoetes lacustris var. stricta

Isoetes lacustris var. stricta, commonly known as the rigid water horsetail, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it often forms dense, ribbon-like mats. Its spore-producing structures, called strobili, are positioned at the apex of the plant, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is an important indicator species for water quality and wetland ecosystems.
