Iresine celosioides

Iresine celosioides, commonly known as the bloodleaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display vivid red, purple, and green hues, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Iresine celosioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.
