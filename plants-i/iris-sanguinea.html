Iris sanguinea

Iris sanguinea, commonly known as the blood iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to deep red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
