Inga megalobotrys

Inga megalobotrys, commonly known as the giant leaf inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional uses in local communities.
