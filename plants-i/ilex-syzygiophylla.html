Ilex syzygiophylla

Ilex syzygiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex syzygiophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and winter interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes in temperate climates.
