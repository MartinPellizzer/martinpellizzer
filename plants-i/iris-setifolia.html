Iris setifolia

Iris setifolia, commonly known as the slender iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris setifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and streambanks.
