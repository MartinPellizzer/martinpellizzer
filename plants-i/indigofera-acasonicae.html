Indigofera acasonicae

Indigofera acasonicae, commonly known as the Sierra Leone indigo plant, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are dispersed by animals, aiding in its propagation.
