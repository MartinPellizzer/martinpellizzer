Illipe leucodermis

Illipe leucodermis, commonly known as the white-skin illipe, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, white bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single large seed. Its latex is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
