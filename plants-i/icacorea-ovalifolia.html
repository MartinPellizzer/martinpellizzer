Icacorea ovalifolia

Icacorea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
