Isodon longitubus f. albiflorus

Isodon longitubus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered isodon, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often sought-after specimen in horticultural collections.
