Inga anomala var. pedicellata

Inga anomala var. pedicellata, commonly known as the red-rhombic inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with elliptical or ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
