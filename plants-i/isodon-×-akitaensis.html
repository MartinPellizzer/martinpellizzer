Isodon × akitaensis

Isodon × akitaensis, commonly known as the Akitan honeysuckle, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish to reddish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Isodon × akitaensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
