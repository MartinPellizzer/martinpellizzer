Inga megacarpa

Inga megacarpa, commonly known as the giant capara or sweet acacia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
