Iris spuria var. reichenbachiana

Iris spuria var. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's iris, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
