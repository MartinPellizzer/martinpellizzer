Inga pospichilii

Inga pospichilii, commonly known as the "bottle tree," is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped trunk. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a rapid growth rate.

Inga pospichilii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
