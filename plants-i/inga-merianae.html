Inga merianae

Inga merianae, commonly known as the "pink inga," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Inga merianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its growth habit and floral display make it a notable species within the legume family.
