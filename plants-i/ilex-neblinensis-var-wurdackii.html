Ilex neblinensis var. wurdackii

Ilex neblinensis var. wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack holly, is a variant of the Neblinensis holly native to Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded gardens.
