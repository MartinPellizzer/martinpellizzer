Ichnanthus calvescens var. glabrescens

Ichnanthus calvescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers clustered in terminal racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its flowers are characterized by their smooth, glabrous surfaces, giving the variety its name.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in rich, acidic soils.
