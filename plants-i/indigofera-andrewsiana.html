Indigofera andrewsiana

Indigofera andrewsiana, commonly known as the African indigo, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, humid climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural systems.
