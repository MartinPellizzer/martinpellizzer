Impatiens austrotanzanica

Impatiens austrotanzanica, commonly known as the Tanzanian impatiens, is a species native to eastern Africa, particularly Tanzania.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its erect growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink and white, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
