Iris clarkei

Iris clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing vivid blue or violet petals with a distinctive white or yellow center. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
