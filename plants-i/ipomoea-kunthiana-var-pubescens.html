Ipomoea kunthiana var. pubescens

Ipomoea kunthiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, tropical environments. It produces showy blooms that open in the morning and close at dusk.

The seeds are enclosed in a spiral-shaped capsule and are dispersed by wind.
