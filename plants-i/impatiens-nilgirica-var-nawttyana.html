Impatiens nilgirica var. nawttyana

Impatiens nilgirica var. nawttyana, commonly known as the Nilgiri Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
