Illicium anisatum

Illicium anisatum, commonly known as Chinese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruit that contains multiple oil-rich seeds. The plant belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is closely related to the genus Illicium, which includes other star anise species. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a lanceolate shape with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
