Iris potaninii

Iris potaninii, commonly known as the Turkestan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
