Ipomoea ardissima

Ipomoea ardissima, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a vine or shrub.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
