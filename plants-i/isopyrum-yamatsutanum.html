Isopyrum yamatsutanum

Isopyrum yamatsutanum, commonly known as the Japanese marsh-elder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Isopyrum yamatsutanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
