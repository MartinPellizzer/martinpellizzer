Indigofera nairobiensis

Indigofera nairobiensis, commonly known as Nairobi indigo, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
