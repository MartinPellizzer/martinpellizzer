Isoetes sinensis

Isoetes sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, lacy leaves that resemble the shape of a clover, with a central stem and three to four pairs of lateral branches. This plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to the substrate. Isoetes sinensis is a member of the water clover family, known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water and improve water quality.

Its delicate appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in aquatic botanical studies.
