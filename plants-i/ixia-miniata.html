Ixia miniata

Ixia miniata, commonly known as the dwarf fire lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm and produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base.

Ixia miniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
