Ixia holosericea

Ixia holosericea, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant orange-red flowers. The plant produces large, showy blooms that resemble lilies, with narrow, pointed petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ixia holosericea is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is also cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
