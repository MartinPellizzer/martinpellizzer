Illicium cambodianum var. crassifolium

Illicium cambodianum var. crassifolium, commonly known as Cambodian star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Schisandraceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
