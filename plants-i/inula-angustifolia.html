Inula angustifolia

Inula angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Inula angustifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
