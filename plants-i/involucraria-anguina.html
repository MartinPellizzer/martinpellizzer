Involucraria anguina

Involucraria anguina, commonly known as the snake's head or snake's head fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often coiled fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are often described as resembling a coiled snake, giving it its common name.

The sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.
