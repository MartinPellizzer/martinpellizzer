Ipomoea austroindica

Ipomoea austroindica, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
