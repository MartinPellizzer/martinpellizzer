Iresine subscaposa

Iresine subscaposa, commonly known as the scarlet spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to purple, often with a contrasting white or silver border. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Iresine subscaposa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
