Ilysanthes rupicola

Ilysanthes rupicola, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and has a low-growing habit.

Ilysanthes rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and unique appearance.
