Ilex suzukii

Ilex suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex suzukii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and year-round aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
