Inga borealis

Inga borealis, commonly known as the northern inga, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional food practices. Inga borealis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil and supports other plant life.
