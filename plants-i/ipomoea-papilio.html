Ipomoea papilio

Ipomoea papilio, commonly known as the bat flower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that resemble the wings of a bat, hence its common name. The plant is a vine that produces heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
