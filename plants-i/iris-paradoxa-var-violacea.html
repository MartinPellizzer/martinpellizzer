Iris paradoxa var. violacea

Iris paradoxa var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique floral form.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
