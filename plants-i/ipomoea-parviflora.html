Ipomoea parviflora

Ipomoea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea parviflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along supports.
