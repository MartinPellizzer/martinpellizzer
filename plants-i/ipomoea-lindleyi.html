Ipomoea lindleyi

Ipomoea lindleyi, commonly known as the scarlet moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers similar to sweet potatoes. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal moths, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Ipomoea lindleyi is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
