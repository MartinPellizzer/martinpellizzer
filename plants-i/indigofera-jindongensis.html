Indigofera jindongensis

Indigofera jindongensis, commonly known as the Jindong indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, depending on the variety. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Indigofera jindongensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
