Indigofera daleoides var. gossweileri

Indigofera daleoides var. gossweileri, commonly known as the yellow indigo, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
