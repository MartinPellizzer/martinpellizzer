Indigofera gracilis var. latifolia

Indigofera gracilis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
