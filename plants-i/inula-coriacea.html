Inula coriacea

Inula coriacea, commonly known as rough inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disk. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Inula coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
