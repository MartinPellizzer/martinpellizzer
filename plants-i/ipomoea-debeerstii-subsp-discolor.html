Ipomoea debeerstii subsp. discolor

Ipomoea debeerstii subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the variegated morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
