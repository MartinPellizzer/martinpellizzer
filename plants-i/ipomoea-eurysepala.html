Ipomoea eurysepala

Ipomoea eurysepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
