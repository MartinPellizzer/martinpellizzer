Isnardia multiflora

Isnardia multiflora, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like white or yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
