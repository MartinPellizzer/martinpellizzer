Ipomoea sphaerostigma

Ipomoea sphaerostigma, commonly known as the globular morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical seed pods that give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
