Iris hyrcana

Iris hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and steppe environments. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Iris hyrcana is valued in horticulture for its striking blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
