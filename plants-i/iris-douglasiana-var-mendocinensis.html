Iris douglasiana var. mendocinensis

Iris douglasiana var. mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendocino iris, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of Northern California.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive dark purple throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
