Ivesia lycopodioides subsp. megalopetala

Ivesia lycopodioides subsp.

megalopetala, commonly known as the giant petal ivy-leaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically clustered in corymbs and are adapted for pollination by insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more showy petals compared to other members of the species.
