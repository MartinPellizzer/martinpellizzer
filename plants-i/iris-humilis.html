Iris humilis

Iris humilis, commonly known as the dwarf iris, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically reaches a height of less than 12 inches and features slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Iris humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
