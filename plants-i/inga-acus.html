Inga acus

Inga acus, commonly known as the arrowleaf tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Inga acus is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
