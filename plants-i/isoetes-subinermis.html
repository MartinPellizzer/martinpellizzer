Isoetes subinermis

Isoetes subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its compact, spore-producing structure known as a sporophyll, which is typically submerged in water. The plant lacks true roots, instead relying on rhizomes for anchorage and nutrient absorption. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the central axis.

Isoetes subinermis is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.
