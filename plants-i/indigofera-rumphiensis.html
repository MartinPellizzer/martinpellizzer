Indigofera rumphiensis

Indigofera rumphiensis, commonly known as Java indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple to blue flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
