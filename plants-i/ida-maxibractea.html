Ida maxibractea

Ida maxibractea, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to southern China.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered inflorescences, which are typical of the grass family.

Ida maxibractea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
