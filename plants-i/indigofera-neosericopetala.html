Indigofera neosericopetala

Indigofera neosericopetala, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
