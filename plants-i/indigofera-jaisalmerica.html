Indigofera jaisalmerica

Indigofera jaisalmerica, commonly known as Jaisalmer indigofera, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access underground water sources.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local communities.
