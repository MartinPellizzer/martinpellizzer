Impatiens tomentella

Impatiens tomentella, commonly known as the woolly impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
