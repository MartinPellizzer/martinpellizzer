Ipomoea seleri

Ipomoea seleri, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea seleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
