Inga vera

Inga vera, commonly known as the velvet bean tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a leguminous species that produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fast-growing habit, making it a popular choice for shade and erosion control. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The seeds of Inga vera are rich in protein and are used in various traditional and commercial applications.
