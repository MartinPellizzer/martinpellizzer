Ixora parvifolia

Ixora parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Ixora parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
