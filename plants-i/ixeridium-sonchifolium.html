Ixeridium sonchifolium

Ixeridium sonchifolium, commonly known as the common sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
