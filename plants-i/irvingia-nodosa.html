Irvingia nodosa

Irvingia nodosa, commonly known as African mango, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit that resembles a mango. The plant is valued for its seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of dietary fiber. Irvingia nodosa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests.

Its fruit has a high caloric content and is sometimes consumed raw or processed into various food products.
