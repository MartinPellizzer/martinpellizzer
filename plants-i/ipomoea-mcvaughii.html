Ipomoea mcvaughii

Ipomoea mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows as a vine, climbing or trailing along supports, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its blooming period occurs primarily in the summer months, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
