Indigofera perrieri subsp. perrieri

Indigofera perrieri subsp.

perrieri, commonly known as the Sierra Leone indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to West Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical regions.
