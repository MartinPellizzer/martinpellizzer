Imperatoria hispanica

Imperatoria hispanica, commonly known as Spanish spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and scrublands.

Imperatoria hispanica is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it a common species in coastal environments.
