Ionoxalis jaliscana

Ionoxalis jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ionoxalis jaliscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
