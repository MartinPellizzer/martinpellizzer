Ipomoea tomentosa

Ipomoea tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or creeping morning glory, is a tropical vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ipomoea tomentosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
