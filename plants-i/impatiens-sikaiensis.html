Impatiens sikaiensis

Impatiens sikaiensis, commonly known as the Kikuyu impatiens, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. It has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Impatiens sikaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
