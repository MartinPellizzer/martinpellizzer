Ischaemum hondae

Ischaemum hondae, commonly known as Honduran grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean region, particularly Honduras.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Ischaemum hondae produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
