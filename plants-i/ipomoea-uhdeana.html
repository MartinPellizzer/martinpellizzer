Ipomoea uhdeana

Ipomoea uhdeana, commonly known as the Uhde's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to ascend supports. It blooms primarily during the day, hence its common name, and is known for its rapid growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
