Ipomoea aegyptia

Ipomoea aegyptia, commonly known as the Egyptian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The seeds are encased in a fleshy, orange-red fruit that splits open when mature.
