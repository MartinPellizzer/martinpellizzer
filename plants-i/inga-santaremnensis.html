Inga santaremnensis

Inga santaremnensis, commonly known as the Santa Rema inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a potential source of timber and forage.
