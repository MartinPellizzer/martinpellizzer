Isopyrum ludlowii

Isopyrum ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a triangular cross-section characteristic of many sedges.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
