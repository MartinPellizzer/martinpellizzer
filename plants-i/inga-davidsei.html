Inga davidsei

Inga davidsei, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Inga davidsei is often used in reforestation projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, promoting ecosystem recovery.
