Indigofera abyssinica

Indigofera abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian indigo, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional practices. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
