Isachne homonyma

Isachne homonyma, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Isachne homonyma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
