Impatiens hamiltoniana

Impatiens hamiltoniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 feet, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread.
