Inula heterotricha

Inula heterotricha, commonly known as the roughleaf elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, hairy stem with narrow, rough-textured leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it produces a distinctive, bitter-tasting milky sap.

Inula heterotricha is often found in meadows and dry grasslands, where it thrives in full sun.
