Isoetes longifolia

Isoetes longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved watermilfoil, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its submerged, thread-like leaves that can grow up to several centimeters in length. This plant typically thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Isoetes longifolia has a complex life cycle involving both sporophytic and gametophytic stages.

Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in compact, spike-like formations called strobila.
