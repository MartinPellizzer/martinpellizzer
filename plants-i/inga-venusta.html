Inga venusta

Inga venusta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for agroforestry and reforestation projects.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types and its ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
