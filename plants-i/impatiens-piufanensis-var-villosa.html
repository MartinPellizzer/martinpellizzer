Impatiens piufanensis var. villosa

Impatiens piufanensis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink and white, typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
