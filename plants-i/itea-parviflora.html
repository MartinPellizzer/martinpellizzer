Itea parviflora

Itea parviflora, commonly known as smooth false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Itea parviflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
