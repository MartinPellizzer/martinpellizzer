Iris ramsayi

Iris ramsayi, commonly known as the desert iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Iris ramsayi plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
