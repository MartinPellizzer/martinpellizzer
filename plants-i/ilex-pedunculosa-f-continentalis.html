Ilex pedunculosa f. continentalis

Ilex pedunculosa f. continentalis, commonly known as the continental holly, is a variant of the holly plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. Its small, dark blue berries are produced in clusters and are an important food source for birds.

The plant is well-suited for erosion control and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
