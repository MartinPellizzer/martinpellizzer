Ilex imbricata

Ilex imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex imbricata thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
