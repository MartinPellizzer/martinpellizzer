Ipomoea procera

Ipomoea procera, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant colors such as purple, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.
