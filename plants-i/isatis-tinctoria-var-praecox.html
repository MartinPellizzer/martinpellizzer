Isatis tinctoria var. praecox

Isatis tinctoria var. praecox, commonly known as early woad, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is known for its high concentration of indigotine, a compound used in the production of blue dye. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Early woad is also valued for its historical significance in textile dyeing and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
