Isolepis pseudosetacea

Isolepis pseudosetacea, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of its flowering stems. This species typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as wetlands and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.
