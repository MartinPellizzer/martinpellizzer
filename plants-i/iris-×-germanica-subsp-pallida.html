Iris × germanica subsp. pallida

Iris × germanica subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale iris, is a subspecies native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, nodding flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts aim to protect its natural habitats.
