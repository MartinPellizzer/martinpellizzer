Icacorea aurantiaca

Icacorea aurantiaca, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its environment. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
