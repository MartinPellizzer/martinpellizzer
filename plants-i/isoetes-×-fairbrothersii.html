Isoetes × fairbrothersii

Isoetes × fairbrothersii, commonly known as the hybrid water fern, is a member of the genus Isoetes, which includes species of spore-bearing plants known as quillworts.

It is a hybrid resulting from the cross between Isoetes australis and Isoetes muricata, two species native to southern regions of Australia. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its spores are produced in specialized structures called sporocarps, which are often found at the base of the plant.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
