Isatis caucasica

Isatis caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian woad, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its basal leaves that are lance-shaped and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it has significant botanical and cultural importance.
