Ilex lonicerifolia

Ilex lonicerifolia, commonly known as the evergreen holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
