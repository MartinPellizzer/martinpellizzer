Indigofera pechuelii

Indigofera pechuelii, commonly known as the Mexican indigo, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates makes it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
