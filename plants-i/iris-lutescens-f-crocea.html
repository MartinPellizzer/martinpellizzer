Iris lutescens f. crocea

Iris lutescens f. crocea, commonly known as the golden iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape and a prominent beak-like spur. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique coloration and elegant form make it a valued addition to botanical collections and gardens.
