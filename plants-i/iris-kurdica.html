Iris kurdica

Iris kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Iris kurdica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
