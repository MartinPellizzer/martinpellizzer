Isanthus pumilus

Isanthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf isanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its botanical structure is characterized by a short stem and a compact growth habit.

Isanthus pumilus is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora in its native habitat.
