Iris × aureonympha

Iris × aureonympha, commonly known as the golden iris, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris × germanica.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to golden orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are dark green and sturdy. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for garden borders and water features.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking color and long-lasting blooms.
