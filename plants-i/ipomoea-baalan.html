Ipomoea baalan

Ipomoea baalan, commonly known as the Balan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Ipomoea baalan is also known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, making it suitable for trellises or garden structures.
