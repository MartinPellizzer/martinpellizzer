Ipomoea latiflora

Ipomoea latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a herbaceous vine that typically grows on trellises or other supports, climbing with its tendrils. It blooms throughout the growing season, often from spring to early autumn.

Ipomoea latiflora is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
