Iberis cappadocica

Iberis cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of white or yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit with dense, silver-gray foliage that is often used in rock gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
