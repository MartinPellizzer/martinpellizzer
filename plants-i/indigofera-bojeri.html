Indigofera bojeri

Indigofera bojeri, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Indigofera bojeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
