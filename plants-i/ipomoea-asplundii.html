Ipomoea asplundii

Ipomoea asplundii, commonly known as the asplundii morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, often using its tendrils to support itself.

Ipomoea asplundii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
