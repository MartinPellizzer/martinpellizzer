Ipomoea pterocaulis

Ipomoea pterocaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-velvet leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a unique appearance.

Ipomoea pterocaulis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.
