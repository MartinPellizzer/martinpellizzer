Ipomopsis elegans

Ipomopsis elegans, commonly known as the elegant touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as cliffs and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Ipomopsis elegans is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
