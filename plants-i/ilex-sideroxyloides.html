Ilex sideroxyloides

Ilex sideroxyloides, commonly known as the ironwood holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, glossy leaves that provide year-round coverage. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. Ilex sideroxyloides is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits contribute to its significance in local ecosystems.
