Ipomoea marginisepala

Ipomoea marginisepala, commonly known as the marginate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
