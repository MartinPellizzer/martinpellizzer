Inga lateriflora

Inga lateriflora, commonly known as the "white inga," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the axils of the leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
