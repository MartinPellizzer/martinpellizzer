Ipomoea eximia

Ipomoea eximia, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often opening in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a rapid growth habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
