Ilex vacciniifolia

Ilex vacciniifolia, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in southern regions.
