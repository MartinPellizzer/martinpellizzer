Ipomoea retropilosa subsp. cundinamarcana

Ipomoea retropilosa subsp.

cundinamarcana, commonly known as the Cundinamarca morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Colombia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Convolvulaceae family.
