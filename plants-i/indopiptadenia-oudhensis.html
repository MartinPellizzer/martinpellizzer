Indopiptadenia oudhensis

Indopiptadenia oudhensis, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.

The tree is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
