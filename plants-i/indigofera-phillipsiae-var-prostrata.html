Indigofera phillipsiae var. prostrata

Indigofera phillipsiae var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate indigofera, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its prostrate or sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some local cultures.
