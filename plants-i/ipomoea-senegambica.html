Ipomoea senegambica

Ipomoea senegambica, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea senegambica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
