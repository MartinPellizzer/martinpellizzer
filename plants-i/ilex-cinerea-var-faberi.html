Ilex cinerea var. faberi

Ilex cinerea var. faberi, commonly known as the Chinese holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The leaves and berries are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
