Ilex boliviana var. acutata

Ilex boliviana var. acutata, commonly known as the Bolivian holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
