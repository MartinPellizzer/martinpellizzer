Ischaemum anthephoroides var. eriostachyum

Ischaemum anthephoroides var. eriostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a slender, upright habit with elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense mats, which can make it competitive in agricultural settings.

It is often considered a weed but has ecological significance in certain habitats.
