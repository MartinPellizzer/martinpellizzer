Isoetes × dodgei

Isoetes × dodgei, commonly known as the hybrid water horsetail, is a member of the Equisetaceae family.

It is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant has a distinctive morphology, featuring a slender, segmented stem with whorls of small, spore-producing cones at the nodes. Its reproductive structures are adapted for underwater spore dispersal, which is a key feature of its life cycle.

The plant's ancient lineage and unique adaptations make it an important subject in botanical studies.
