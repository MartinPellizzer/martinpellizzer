Iris × phoenicis

Iris × phoenicis, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris pallida and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
