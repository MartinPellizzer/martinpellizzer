Indigofera commiphoroides

Indigofera commiphoroides, commonly known as the African indigo or wild indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

It has some medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
