Ifdregea collina

Ifdregea collina, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
