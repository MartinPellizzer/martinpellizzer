Ischaemum glabratum

Ischaemum glabratum, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and smooth. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Ischaemum glabratum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
