Ipomoea polymorpha f. argentea

Ipomoea polymorpha f. argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae) characterized by its distinctive silvery-white variegation on its leaves.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique appearance. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, usually in shades of pale blue or white, and bloom in the morning.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
