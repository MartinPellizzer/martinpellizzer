Ipomoea rubroviridis

Ipomoea rubroviridis, commonly known as the red-veined morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-green veining pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
