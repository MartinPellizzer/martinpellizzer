Indigofera constricta var. deorum

Indigofera constricta var. deorum, commonly known as the dwarf indigo.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and long blooming period. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
