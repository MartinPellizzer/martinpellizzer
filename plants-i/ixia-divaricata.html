Ixia divaricata

Ixia divaricata, commonly known as the rain lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
