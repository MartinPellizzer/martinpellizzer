Inga martinicensis

Inga martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Inga martinicensis plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
