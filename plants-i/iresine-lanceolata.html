Iresine lanceolata

Iresine lanceolata, commonly known as the Crimson Rose or Bloodleaf, is a strikingly colorful plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored leaves that often display a deep red or purple hue on the upper surface and a contrasting green on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Iresine lanceolata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures for their purported healing properties.
