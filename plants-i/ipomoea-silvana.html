Ipomoea silvana

Ipomoea silvana, commonly known as the forest morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs.

Ipomoea silvana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
