Ireon parvifolia

Ireon parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf ireon, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length.

It belongs to the family Irecaceae and is native to the arid regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its succulent-like leaves store water, allowing it to survive in low-rainfall environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ireon parvifolia is considered a vulnerable species.
