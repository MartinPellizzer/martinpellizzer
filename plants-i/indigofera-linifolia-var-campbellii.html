Indigofera linifolia var. campbellii

Indigofera linifolia var. campbellii, commonly known as Campbell's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and feathery foliage.
