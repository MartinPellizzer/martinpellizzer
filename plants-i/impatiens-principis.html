Impatiens principis

Impatiens principis, commonly known as the Prince's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the wet season. Its foliage is characterized by ovate, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Impatiens principis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
