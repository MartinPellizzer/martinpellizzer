Imbricaria crenulata

Imbricaria crenulata, commonly known as the smoothleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly serrated along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Imbricaria crenulata is known for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
