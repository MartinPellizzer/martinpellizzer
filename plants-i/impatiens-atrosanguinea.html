Impatiens atrosanguinea

Impatiens atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its striking, dark red to almost black flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are spurred, a feature common in the genus Impatiens, and they bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique and dramatic floral display.
