Inga subsericantha

Inga subsericantha, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments. Its pods contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The species also plays an important role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
