Isachne nilagirica

Isachne nilagirica, commonly known as the Nilgiri grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Isachne nilagirica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
