Indigofera domingensis

Indigofera domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
