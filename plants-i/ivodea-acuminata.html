Ivodea acuminata

Ivodea acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed ivy, is a climbing plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, followed by berries that are typically red or black. Ivodea acuminata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
