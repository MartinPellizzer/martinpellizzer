Ida × monopampanensis

Ida × monopampanensis, commonly known as the hybrid magnolia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Magnolia grandiflora and Magnolia fraseri, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy white to pink flowers. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9, preferring moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its spring blooms and graceful form.
