Inga splendens

Inga splendens, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves and long, pendulous inflorescences that produce showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Inga splendens is also valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in agroforestry systems.
