Ixia viridiflora var. minor

Ixia viridiflora var. minor, commonly known as the green-flowered ixia, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, elongated flowers that are typically green or yellow-green in color, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is relatively hardy and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 7 through 10.
