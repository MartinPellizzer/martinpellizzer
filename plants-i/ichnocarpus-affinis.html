Ichnocarpus affinis

Ichnocarpus affinis, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cultures.
