Inga tubaeformis

Inga tubaeformis, commonly known as the "silk tree" or "false mahogany," is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common pioneer species in secondary forests. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
