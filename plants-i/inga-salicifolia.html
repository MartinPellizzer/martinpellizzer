Inga salicifolia

Inga salicifolia, commonly known as the "willowleaf inga," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a willow-like appearance, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in racemes. Inga salicifolia produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species for reforestation and agroforestry projects.
