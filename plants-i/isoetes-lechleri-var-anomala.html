Isoetes lechleri var. anomala

Isoetes lechleri var. anomala, commonly known as the Amazonian clubmoss, is a species of aquatic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Isoetaceae and is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that form a compact, underwater mat. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich, oxygenated waters and is often found in slow-moving streams and flooded forests. Its unique morphology allows it to anchor itself in muddy substrates while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding environment.

This species plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic organisms.
