Inga mocinoana

Inga mocinoana, commonly known as the coffee tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. The fruits contain seeds that are used to cultivate coffee plants, making it an important species in the coffee industry.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests.
