Impatiens nicolliae

Impatiens nicolliae, commonly known as the Nicoll's impatiens, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
