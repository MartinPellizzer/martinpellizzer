Iresine heterophylla

Iresine heterophylla, commonly known as Crimson Rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red or purple leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or red, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Iresine heterophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and compact growth habit.
