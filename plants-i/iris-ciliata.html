Iris ciliata

Iris ciliata, commonly known as the fringed iris, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue or purple flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Iris ciliata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
