Ipomoea grisebachiana

Ipomoea grisebachiana, commonly known as the grayleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, blue, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Ipomoea grisebachiana is a climbing vine that can reach significant heights, using its tendrils to support its growth.
