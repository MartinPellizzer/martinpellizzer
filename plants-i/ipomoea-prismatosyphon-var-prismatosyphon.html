Ipomoea prismatosyphon var. prismatosyphon

Ipomoea prismatosyphon var. prismatosyphon, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

The vine is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and vigorous growth.
