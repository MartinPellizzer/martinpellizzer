Iresine stricta

Iresine stricta, commonly known as redroot ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, velvety red stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Iresine stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
