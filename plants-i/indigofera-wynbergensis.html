Indigofera wynbergensis

Indigofera wynbergensis, commonly known as the Cape indigo, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
