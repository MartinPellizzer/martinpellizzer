Ipomoea brasiliana var. subincana

Ipomoea brasiliana var. subincana, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively in suitable conditions.
