Ipomoea abutiloides

Ipomoea abutiloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Ipomoea abutiloides is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
