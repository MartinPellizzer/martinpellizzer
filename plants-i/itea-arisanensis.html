Itea arisanensis

Itea arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese water-pear, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas near streams or ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
