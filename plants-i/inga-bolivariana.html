Inga bolivariana

Inga bolivariana, commonly known as the Bolivian inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America, including parts of Bolivia, Brazil, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Inga bolivariana plays an important ecological role in nitrogen fixation and provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
