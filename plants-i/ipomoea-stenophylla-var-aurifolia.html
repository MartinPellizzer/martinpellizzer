Ipomoea stenophylla var. aurifolia

Ipomoea stenophylla var. aurifolia, commonly known as the golden leaf morning glory, is a striking tropical vine characterized by its heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that display a vibrant yellow-green coloration.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale blue, often with a subtle pinkish hue. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are typically variegated, adding visual interest to gardens and landscapes.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
