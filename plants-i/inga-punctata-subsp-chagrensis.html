Inga punctata subsp. chagrensis

Inga punctata subsp.

chagrensis, commonly known as the Chagren hill passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, acuminate lobes and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinct, fleshy corona, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
