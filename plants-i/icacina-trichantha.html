Icacina trichantha

Icacina trichantha, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
