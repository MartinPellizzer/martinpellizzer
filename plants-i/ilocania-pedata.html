Ilocania pedata

Ilocania pedata, commonly known as the Ilocano walking plant, is a unique flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sprawling growth habit and fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It is adapted to thrive in rocky soils and can often be found growing on cliffs or in crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
