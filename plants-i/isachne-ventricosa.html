Isachne ventricosa

Isachne ventricosa, commonly known as the swollen grass or swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and possesses narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in poorly drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet with two bracts, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Isachne ventricosa is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
