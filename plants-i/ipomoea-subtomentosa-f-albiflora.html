Ipomoea subtomentosa f. albiflora

Ipomoea subtomentosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory species.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This subspecies is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens.
