Impatiens aurella

Impatiens aurella, commonly known as the golden touchflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle red or orange hue at the base. The plant has ovate, glossy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid forests.

Impatiens aurella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
