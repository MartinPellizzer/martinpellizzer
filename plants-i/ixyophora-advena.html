Ixyophora advena

Ixyophora advena, commonly known as the wandering arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged around a central spadix, which is often surrounded by a showy, petal-like structure called a spathe. Ixyophora advena thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
