Ilex aquifolium f. spinigera

Ilex aquifolium f. spinigera, commonly known as the spiny holm oak, is a holly species characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves.

The plant exhibits a dense, evergreen foliage with leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long and have serrated edges. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. The shrub or small tree thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
