Indigofera somalensis

Indigofera somalensis, commonly known as Somali indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

It plays a role in traditional medicine and has potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
