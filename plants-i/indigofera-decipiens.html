Indigofera decipiens

Indigofera decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The name "deceptive indigo" may refer to its ability to mimic other plants or its misleading appearance.
