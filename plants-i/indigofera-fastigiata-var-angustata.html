Indigofera fastigiata var. angustata

Indigofera fastigiata var. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaf indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep violet, often arranged in dense clusters. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
