Indigofera leendertziae

Indigofera leendertziae, commonly known as the Malawi indigo, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
