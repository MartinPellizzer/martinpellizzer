Ipomopsis congesta var. nevadensis

Ipomopsis congesta var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada clustered trumpetflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its dense flowering clusters and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
