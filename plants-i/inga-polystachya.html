Inga polystachya

Inga polystachya, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or sweet sarsaparilla, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its roots are often harvested for their starchy, edible tubers, which have a mild flavor and high nutritional value.

The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.
