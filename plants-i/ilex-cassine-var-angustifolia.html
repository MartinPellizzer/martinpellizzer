Ilex cassine var. angustifolia

Ilex cassine var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yaupon holly, is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically 2-4 inches long and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer climates.
