Ipomopsis aggregata subsp. texana

Ipomopsis aggregata subsp.

texana, commonly known as Texas whipflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and canyon walls.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red, often attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to arid conditions.
