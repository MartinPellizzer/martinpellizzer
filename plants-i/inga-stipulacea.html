Inga stipulacea

Inga stipulacea, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet bean, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its velvety, dark green foliage and its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and has traditional uses in various indigenous communities.
