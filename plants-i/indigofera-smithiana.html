Indigofera smithiana

Indigofera smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its root system has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
