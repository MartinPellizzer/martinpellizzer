Isoetes naipiana

Isoetes naipiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. The plant is a spore-bearing fern, reproducing through the release of spores rather than seeds. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important indicator species for wetland health.
