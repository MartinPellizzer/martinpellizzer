Isatis tphilisiensis

Isatis tphilisiensis, commonly known as the Caucasian woad, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other notable plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in the summer. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant has historical significance in dye production, similar to its more well-known relative, the common woad.
