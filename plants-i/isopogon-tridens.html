Isopogon tridens

Isopogon tridens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved banksia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are a distinctive feature. It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

Isopogon tridens is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
