Ivesia purpurascens subsp. congdonis

Ivesia purpurascens subsp.

congdonis, commonly known as the Congdon's rosehip, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its distinctive rose-like hips that mature into bright red berries.
