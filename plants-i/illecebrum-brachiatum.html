Illecebrum brachiatum

Illecebrum brachiatum, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a trailing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and ditches.

Botanically, it is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing and twining growth habits.
