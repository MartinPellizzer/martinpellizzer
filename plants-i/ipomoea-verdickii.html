Ipomoea verdickii

Ipomoea verdickii, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
