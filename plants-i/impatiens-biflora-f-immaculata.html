Impatiens biflora f. immaculata

Impatiens biflora f. immaculata, commonly known as the immaculate twin-flower impatiens, is a hybrid form of the species Impatiens biflora.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its foliage is characterized by broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
