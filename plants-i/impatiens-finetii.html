Impatiens finetii

Impatiens finetii, commonly known as the fine-leaved impatiens, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are borne on slender stalks and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Impatiens finetii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to woodland gardens.
