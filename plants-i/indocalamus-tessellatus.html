Indocalamus tessellatus

Indocalamus tessellatus, commonly known as the tessellated bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a distinctively segmented appearance.

Indocalamus tessellatus is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
