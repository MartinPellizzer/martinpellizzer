Indigofera daleoides var. dammarensis

Indigofera daleoides var. dammarensis, commonly known as the Dammar Tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces a hard, resinous wood that is valued for its durability and use in traditional construction.

Additionally, the plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
