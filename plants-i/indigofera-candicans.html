Indigofera candicans

Indigofera candicans, commonly known as the golden indigo or white indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally in some cultures for medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
