Isachne angolensis

Isachne angolensis, commonly known as the Angola grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-based flowers.

Isachne angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and adaptability.
