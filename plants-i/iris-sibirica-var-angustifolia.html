Iris sibirica var. angustifolia

Iris sibirica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a fan shape at the base of the plant. This variety is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of temperate climates.
