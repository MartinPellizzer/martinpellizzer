Indigofera heterotricha subsp. pechuelii

Indigofera heterotricha subsp.

pechuelii, commonly known as the Mexican indigo, is a flowering plant species native to Central America and parts of Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in well-drained soils and full sun.
