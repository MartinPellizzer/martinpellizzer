Ipomoea calyptrata

Ipomoea calyptrata, commonly known as the Cape morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant blooms throughout the growing season, often in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
