Ipomoea malpighipila

Ipomoea malpighipila, commonly known as the Malpighiopsis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ipomoea malpighipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
