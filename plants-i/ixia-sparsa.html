Ixia sparsa

Ixia sparsa, commonly known as the sparse firecracker, is a South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that resemble firecrackers, blooming in late summer. The plant is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ixia sparsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
