Ischaemum vitiense

Ischaemum vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions in Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a bluish-green color, and it is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Ischaemum vitiense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
