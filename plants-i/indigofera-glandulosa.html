Indigofera glandulosa

Indigofera glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often in clusters. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used for various traditional and ecological purposes.
