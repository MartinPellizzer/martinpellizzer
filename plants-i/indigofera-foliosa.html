Indigofera foliosa

Indigofera foliosa, commonly known as the leafy indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural contexts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits have made it a subject of interest in horticulture and conservation efforts.
