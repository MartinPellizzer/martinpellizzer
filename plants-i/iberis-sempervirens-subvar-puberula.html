Iberis sempervirens subvar. puberula

Iberis sempervirens subvar. puberula, commonly known as the woolly candytuft, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, giving the plant its "woolly" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
