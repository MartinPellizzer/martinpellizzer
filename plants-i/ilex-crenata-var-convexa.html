Ilex crenata var. convexa

Ilex crenata var. convexa, commonly known as the Japanese holly or convex Japanese holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape, with a distinctive convex curvature. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its neat, rounded form.

Ilex crenata var. convexa thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained soil.
