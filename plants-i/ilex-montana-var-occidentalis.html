Ilex montana var. occidentalis

Ilex montana var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It has a compact growth habit with dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The leaves are usually glossy and turn red in the fall before shedding.
