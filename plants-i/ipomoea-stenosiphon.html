Ipomoea stenosiphon

Ipomoea stenosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-lip morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, round seeds.

Ipomoea stenosiphon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
