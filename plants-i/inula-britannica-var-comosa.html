Inula britannica var. comosa

Inula britannica var. comosa, commonly known as the British-inula or woolly inula, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
