Ida reichenbachii var. viridis

Ida reichenbachii var. viridis, commonly known as the green bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which is a distinguishing characteristic of the varietal form. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small organisms.
