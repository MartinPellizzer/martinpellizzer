Ichthyothere cunabi

Ichthyothere cunabi, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and silver-blue foliage, which help it thrive in saline environments. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often found growing on rocky shores or sandy beaches. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
