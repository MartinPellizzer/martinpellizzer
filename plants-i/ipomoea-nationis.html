Ipomoea nationis

Ipomoea nationis, commonly known as the Hawaiian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blue or violet blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Ipomoea nationis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
