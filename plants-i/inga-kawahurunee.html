Inga kawahurunee

Inga kawahurunee, commonly known as the "Crimson Flame" or "Red Inga," is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which enrich the soil. The plant is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that range from yellow to deep red. It produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Inga kawahurunee plays a vital role in maintaining the biodiversity and ecological balance of its native habitat.
