Imperata arundinacea var. condensata

Imperata arundinacea var. condensata, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and striking purple to pinkish inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands that stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an effective pioneer species in disturbed habitats.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more vivid during the growing season.
