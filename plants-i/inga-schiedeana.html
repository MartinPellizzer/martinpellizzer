Inga schiedeana

Inga schiedeana, commonly known as the coffee tree or inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Inga schiedeana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in tropical forests.
