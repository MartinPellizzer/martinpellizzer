Ipomoea thonningii

Ipomoea thonningii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces elongated, cylindrical fruit capsules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodland areas.

Ipomoea thonningii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
