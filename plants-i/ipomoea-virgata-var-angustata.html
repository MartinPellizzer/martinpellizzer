Ipomoea virgata var. angustata

Ipomoea virgata var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.
