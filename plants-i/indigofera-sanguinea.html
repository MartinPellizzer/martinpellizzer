Indigofera sanguinea

Indigofera sanguinea, commonly known as redroot indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red roots and purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
