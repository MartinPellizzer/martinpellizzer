Isachne beneckei var. depauperata

Isachne beneckei var. depauperata, commonly known as the poorly furnished isachne, is a grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and lesser-known grass species.
