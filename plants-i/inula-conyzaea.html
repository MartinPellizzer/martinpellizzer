Inula conyzaea

Inula conyzaea, commonly known as roughleaf inula, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

Inula conyzaea is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
