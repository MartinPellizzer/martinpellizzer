Inula sericea

Inula sericea, commonly known as silverleaf inula, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name, and it produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Inula sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
