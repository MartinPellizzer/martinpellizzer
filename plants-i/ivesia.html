Ivesia

Ivesia, commonly known as the everlasting or blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Ivesia species are characterized by their compact growth habit, hairy stems, and small, often blue or purple flowers. They are drought-tolerant and have adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Many species are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants due to their resilience and aesthetic appeal.
