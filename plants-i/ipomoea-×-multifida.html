Ipomoea × multifida

Ipomoea × multifida, commonly known as the curly leaf morning glory, is a hybrid species in the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
