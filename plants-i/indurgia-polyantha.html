Indurgia polyantha

Indurgia polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
