Ipomoea livescens

Ipomoea livescens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Ipomoea livescens is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
