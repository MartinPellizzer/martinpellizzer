Ilyphilos triandrus

Ilyphilos triandrus, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
