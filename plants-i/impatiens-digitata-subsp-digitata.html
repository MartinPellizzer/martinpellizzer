Impatiens digitata subsp. digitata

Impatiens digitata subsp.

digitata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.
