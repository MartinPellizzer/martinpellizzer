Ixia viridiflora var. viridiflora

Ixia viridiflora var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered ixia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, grass-like foliage and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically green or yellow-green, with a distinctive white throat, and bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
