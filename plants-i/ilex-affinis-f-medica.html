Ilex affinis f. medica

Ilex affinis f. medica, commonly known as the medicinal holly, is a variant of the holly plant native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are evergreen, providing year-round foliage, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The berries contain compounds that have been used in herbal remedies for their potential health benefits.
