Inga mathewsiana

Inga mathewsiana, commonly known as the Mathews's inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
