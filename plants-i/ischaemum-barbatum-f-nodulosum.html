Ischaemum barbatum f. nodulosum

Ischaemum barbatum f. nodulosum, commonly known as the nodular barnyard grass, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a distinctive pubescent surface. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often clustered in groups.

This form is notable for its nodular structures, which may contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
