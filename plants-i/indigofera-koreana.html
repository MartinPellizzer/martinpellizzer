Indigofera koreana

Indigofera koreana, commonly known as Korean indigo, is a flowering plant native to Korea and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its historical use in traditional dyeing processes has also contributed to its cultural significance.
