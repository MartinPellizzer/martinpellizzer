Isatis tinctoria subsp. tinctoria

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as woad, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant is notable for its ability to produce a deep blue dye from its leaves, which was historically used in textile production. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Woad is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating ailments such as inflammation and skin conditions.
