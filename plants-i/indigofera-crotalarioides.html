Indigofera crotalarioides

Indigofera crotalarioides, commonly known as the yellow indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in traditional medicine.
