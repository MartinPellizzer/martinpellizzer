Inula persica

Inula persica, commonly known as Persian elecampane, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy yellow-orange flowers appear in late summer, followed by fluffy, seed-filled capitula.

Inula persica is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
