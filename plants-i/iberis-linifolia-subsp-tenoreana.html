Iberis linifolia subsp. tenoreana

Iberis linifolia subsp.

tenoreana, commonly known as the Sicilian candytuft, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers.

The leaves are linear to oblanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
