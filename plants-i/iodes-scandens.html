Iodes scandens

Iodes scandens, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, aromatic stems that often grow in clusters and are covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a distinctive, sweet, anise-like fragrance.

Iodes scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
