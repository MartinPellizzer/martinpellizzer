Iris × lundstroemii

Iris × lundstroemii, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are usually borne in clusters and come in a variety of colors including pink, purple, and white. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is relatively drought-tolerant once established and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
