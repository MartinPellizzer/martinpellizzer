Ipomoea soldanellifolia

Ipomoea soldanellifolia, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
