Iresine laxissima

Iresine laxissima, commonly known as the red leaf plant or red leaf sorrel, is a strikingly ornamental species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its smaller, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure. Its leaves are often used in horticulture for their aesthetic value in gardens and as houseplants.

Iresine laxissima is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
