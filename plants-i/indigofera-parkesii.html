Indigofera parkesii

Indigofera parkesii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain small, dark seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
