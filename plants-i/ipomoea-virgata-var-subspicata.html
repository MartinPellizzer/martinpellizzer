Ipomoea virgata var. subspicata

Ipomoea virgata var. subspicata, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name. It thrives in warm climates with full sun and well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
