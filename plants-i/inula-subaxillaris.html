Inula subaxillaris

Inula subaxillaris, commonly known as the lesser elecampane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
