Ipomoea trichosperma var. diversifolia

Ipomoea trichosperma var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with variegated patterns of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming during the day. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
