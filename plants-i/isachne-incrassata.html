Isachne incrassata

Isachne incrassata, commonly known as broadleaf arrowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive basal rosette structure. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Isachne incrassata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
