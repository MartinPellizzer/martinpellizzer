Iris sofarana

Iris sofarana, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 60 to 100 centimeters, with showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Iris sofarana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens and botanical collections.
