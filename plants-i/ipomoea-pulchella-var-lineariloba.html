Ipomoea pulchella var. lineariloba

Ipomoea pulchella var. lineariloba, commonly known as the linear-leaf morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often slightly lobed, with a waxy surface. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, typically displaying vibrant blue or violet hues, and bloom in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening.
