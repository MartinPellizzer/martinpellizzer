Indigofera tenella

Indigofera tenella, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian blue bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant purple or blue flowers. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world as a garden plant.
