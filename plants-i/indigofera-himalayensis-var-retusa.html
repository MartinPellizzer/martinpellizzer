Indigofera himalayensis var. retusa

Indigofera himalayensis var. retusa, commonly known as Himalayan indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
