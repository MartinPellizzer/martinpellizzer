Indigofera ovina

Indigofera ovina, commonly known as woolly indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in clusters. Indigofera ovina is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
