Iberis intermedia proles collina

Iberis intermedia proles collina, commonly known as the rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive silvery-blue appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Iberis intermedia proles collina is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
