Iodes madagascariensis

Iodes madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan star-anise, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the star anise. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the bark is thick and aromatic.

This species is valued for its distinctive fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
