Ilex rupicola var. pleiomera

Ilex rupicola var. pleiomera, commonly known as the rock holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, glossy leaves that are typically clustered around the stems. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
