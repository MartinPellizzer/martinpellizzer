Indigofera dyeri var. dyeri

Indigofera dyeri var. dyeri, commonly known as the blue bean or dyeri indigo, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of natural dyes and its ecological importance in native habitats.
