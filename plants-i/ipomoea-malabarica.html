Ipomoea malabarica

Ipomoea malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in a range of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like fruit that ripens to a red or orange color.

Ipomoea malabarica is also noted for its ability to climb and twine around supports, making it a popular choice for garden decoration.
