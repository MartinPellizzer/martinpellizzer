Iris latistyla f. albiflora

Iris latistyla f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered broad-leaved iris, is a unique cultivar of the iris genus.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The flowers are notable for their pale white to almost cream-colored petals, which contrast beautifully with the dark green foliage. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for many climates.
