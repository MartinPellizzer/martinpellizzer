Islaya grandis

Islaya grandis, commonly known as the giant islaya, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, showcasing a vibrant green hue with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the late spring and summer months. Its fruit is a spherical berry that turns from green to red as it matures, containing several seeds.

Islaya grandis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
