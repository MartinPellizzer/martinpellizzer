Isocoma menziesii var. sedoides

Isocoma menziesii var. sedoides, commonly known as the desert coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
