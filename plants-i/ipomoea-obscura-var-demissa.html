Ipomoea obscura var. demissa

Ipomoea obscura var. demissa, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to white in color, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often trailing or climbing.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
