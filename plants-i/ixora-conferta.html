Ixora conferta

Ixora conferta, commonly known as the clustered flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are borne in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green appearance.

Ixora conferta is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
