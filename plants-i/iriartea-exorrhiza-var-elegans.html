Iriartea exorrhiza var. elegans

Iriartea exorrhiza var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its striking foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
