Ipomoea clausa

Ipomoea clausa, commonly known as the closed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, blue, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea clausa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
