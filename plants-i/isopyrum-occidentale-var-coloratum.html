Isopyrum occidentale var. coloratum

Isopyrum occidentale var. coloratum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed barren strawberry, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems that often display a distinctive purplish hue. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
