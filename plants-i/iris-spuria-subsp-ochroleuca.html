Iris spuria subsp. ochroleuca

Iris spuria subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the pale blue iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, upright stems that bear showy, pale blue to white flowers with a distinct spur. Its leaves are grass-like, linear, and arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
