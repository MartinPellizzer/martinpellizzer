Iris × viridis

Iris × viridis, commonly known as the green iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its vibrant green to blue-green flowers, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that produces sword-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking color and form.
