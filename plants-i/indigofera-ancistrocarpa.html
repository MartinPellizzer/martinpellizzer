Indigofera ancistrocarpa

Indigofera ancistrocarpa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
