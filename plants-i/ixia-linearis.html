Ixia linearis

Ixia linearis, commonly known as the linear ixia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Ixia linearis prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that provide a elegant backdrop to its showy blooms.
