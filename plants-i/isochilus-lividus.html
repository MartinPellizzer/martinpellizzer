Isochilus lividus

Isochilus lividus, commonly known as the silverleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are silver-green, covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Isochilus lividus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that replicate its natural environment.
