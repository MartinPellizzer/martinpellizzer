Iris minutoaurea

Iris minutoaurea, commonly known as the dwarf golden iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for small garden spaces.
