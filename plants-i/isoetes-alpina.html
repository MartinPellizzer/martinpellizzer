Isoetes alpina

Isoetes alpina, commonly known as the Alpine water fern, is a member of the genus Isoetes, which includes about 300 species of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in a structure known as a sorus. This plant is often found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands, where it thrives in shallow, still waters. Isoetes alpina has a rhizome that anchors it to the substrate, and its leaves are usually finely divided and ribbon-like.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small aquatic animals.
