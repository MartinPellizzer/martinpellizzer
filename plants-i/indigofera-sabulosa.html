Indigofera sabulosa

Indigofera sabulosa, commonly known as sand indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to violet, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and adapted to reduce water loss.

Indigofera sabulosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
