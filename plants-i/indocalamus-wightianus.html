Indocalamus wightianus

Indocalamus wightianus, commonly known as the Wight's bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, often glaucous surface.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
