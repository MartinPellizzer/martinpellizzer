Iberis welwitschii var. lusitanica

Iberis welwitschii var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has aromatic, holly-like leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the spring and early summer.
