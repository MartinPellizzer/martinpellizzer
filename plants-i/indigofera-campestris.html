Indigofera campestris

Indigofera campestris, commonly known as wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles and as a forage crop highlights its ecological and economic significance.
