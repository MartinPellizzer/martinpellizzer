Inula lanceolata

Inula lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf-inula, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

Inula lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
