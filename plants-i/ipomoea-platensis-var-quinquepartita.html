Ipomoea platensis var. quinquepartita

Ipomoea platensis var. quinquepartita, commonly known as the five-parted morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, often bioluminescent blooms at night. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
