Isocoma acradenia subsp. eremophila

Isocoma acradenia subsp.

eremophila, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, globular shape and silver-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access groundwater, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species in desert ecosystems.
