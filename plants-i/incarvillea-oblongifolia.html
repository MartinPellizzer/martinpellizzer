Incarvillea oblongifolia

Incarvillea oblongifolia, commonly known as the longleaf incarvillea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
