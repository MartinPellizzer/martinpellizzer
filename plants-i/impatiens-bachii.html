Impatiens bachii

Impatiens bachii, commonly known as the Bach's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Balsaminaceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens bachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
