Iris cretensis

Iris cretensis, commonly known as the Cretan iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Crete.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are sword-shaped and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Iris cretensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
