Isolepis planifolia

Isolepis planifolia, commonly known as flatleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are flat and blade-like, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

Isolepis planifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
