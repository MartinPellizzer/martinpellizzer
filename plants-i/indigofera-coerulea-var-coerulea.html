Indigofera coerulea var. coerulea

Indigofera coerulea var. coerulea, commonly known as blue indigo.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Asia, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. Its botanical features include compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and a distinctive, fragrant floral display.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
