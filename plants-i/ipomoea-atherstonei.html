Ipomoea atherstonei

Ipomoea atherstonei, commonly known as the Atherstone's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a climbing vine.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
