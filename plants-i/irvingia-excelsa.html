Irvingia excelsa

Irvingia excelsa, commonly known as African mango, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain a single seed. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and have gained attention for their potential health benefits.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
