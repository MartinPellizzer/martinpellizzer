Ischaemum imbricatum var. pubescens

Ischaemum imbricatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become a weed in cultivated environments.
