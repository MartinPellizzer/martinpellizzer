Indigofera complicata

Indigofera complicata, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly India, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in dye production, particularly for producing indigo blue.
