Ipomoea multisecta

Ipomoea multisecta, commonly known as the multilobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shady, forested areas.

Ipomoea multisecta is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
