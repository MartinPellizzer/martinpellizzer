Iberis amara var. sabauda

Iberis amara var. sabauda, commonly known as the Sabauda candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring bloom and ability to attract pollinators.
