Indigofera viscosa var. dembianensis

Indigofera viscosa var. dembianensis, commonly known as the Dembien indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

This species is also valued for its traditional uses in dyeing and medicinal applications.
