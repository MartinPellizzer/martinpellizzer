Ichnanthus vicinus

Ichnanthus vicinus, commonly known as the small yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Ichnanthus vicinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
