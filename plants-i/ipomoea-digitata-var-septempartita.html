Ipomoea digitata var. septempartita

Ipomoea digitata var. septempartita, commonly known as the seven-part morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
