Ixeris nipponica

Ixeris nipponica, commonly known as Japanese carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ixeris nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
