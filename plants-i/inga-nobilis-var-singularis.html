Inga nobilis var. singularis

Inga nobilis var. singularis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, rough bark and its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
