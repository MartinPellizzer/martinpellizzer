Ipomoea acehensis

Ipomoea acehensis, commonly known as the Aceh morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are typically five-petaled.

Ipomoea acehensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential ecological and medicinal significance.
