Iris willmottiana

Iris willmottiana, commonly known as the Willmott's iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white and purple veining. The plant has broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suited to temperate climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for garden cultivation in regions with suitable growing conditions.
