Ipomoea fulvovillosa

Ipomoea fulvovillosa, commonly known as the yellow hairy morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish or purple throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along fences or in shaded woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
