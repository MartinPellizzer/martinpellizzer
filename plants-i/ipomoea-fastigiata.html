Ipomoea fastigiata

Ipomoea fastigiata, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, followed by white petals that open later in the day. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces long, twisting vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea fastigiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
