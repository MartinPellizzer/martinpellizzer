Inga subnuda subsp. subnuda

Inga subnuda subsp.

subnuda, commonly known as the slender inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
