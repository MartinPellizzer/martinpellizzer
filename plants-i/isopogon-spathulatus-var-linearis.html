Isopogon spathulatus var. linearis

Isopogon spathulatus var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
