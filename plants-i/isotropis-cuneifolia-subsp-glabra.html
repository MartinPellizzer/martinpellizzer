Isotropis cuneifolia subsp. glabra

Isotropis cuneifolia subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf isotropis, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped, smooth leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
