Ivesia purpurascens

Ivesia purpurascens, commonly known as the purplestem sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and prominent ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are stout and purplish in color.

Ivesia purpurascens produces showy yellow disk flowers surrounded by reddish-brown to purple ray flowers, which attract various pollinators.
