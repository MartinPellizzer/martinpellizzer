Ipomoea orizabensis

Ipomoea orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces woody vines that can climb or trail along the ground. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn.

Ipomoea orizabensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
