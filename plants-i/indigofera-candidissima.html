Indigofera candidissima

Indigofera candidissima, commonly known as white indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
