Ilex scabridula

Ilex scabridula, commonly known as the roughleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex scabridula typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and year-round aesthetic appeal.
