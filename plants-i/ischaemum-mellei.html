Ischaemum mellei

Ischaemum mellei, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Sweetgrass is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its aromatic leaves have been used traditionally in various cultural practices and for their medicinal properties.
