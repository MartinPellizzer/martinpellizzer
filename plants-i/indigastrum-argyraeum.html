Indigastrum argyraeum

Indigastrum argyraeum, commonly known as the silverleaf indigastrum, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
