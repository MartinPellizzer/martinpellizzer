Ilex aquifolium var. angustifolia

Ilex aquifolium var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly with distinctive narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The leaves are usually glossy green on top and lighter beneath, with spiny margins that are characteristic of the genus Ilex. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
