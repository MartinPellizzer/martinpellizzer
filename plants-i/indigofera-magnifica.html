Indigofera magnifica

Indigofera magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent indigo.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly serrated.

Indigofera magnifica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
