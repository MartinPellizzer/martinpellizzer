Ipomoea concanica

Ipomoea concanica, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, hence its common name.
