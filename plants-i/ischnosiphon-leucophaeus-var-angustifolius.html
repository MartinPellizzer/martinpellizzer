Ischnosiphon leucophaeus var. angustifolius

Ischnosiphon leucophaeus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whiteflower ischnosiphon, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in tropical plant morphology and ecology.
