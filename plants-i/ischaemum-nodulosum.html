Ischaemum nodulosum

Ischaemum nodulosum, commonly known as nodular grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Ischaemum nodulosum is typically found in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
