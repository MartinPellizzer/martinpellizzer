Indigofera secundiflora

Indigofera secundiflora, commonly known as the Mexican indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
