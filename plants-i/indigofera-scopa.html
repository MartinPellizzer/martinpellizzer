Indigofera scopa

Indigofera scopa, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a flowering shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a valuable plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
