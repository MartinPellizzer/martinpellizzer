Iris × formosa

Iris × formosa, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris laevigata and Iris sibirica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. It produces sword-shaped, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

The flower structure includes three outer petals and three inner sepals, with a prominent three-parted style.
