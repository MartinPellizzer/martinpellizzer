Ipomoea serpens var. subtomentosa

Ipomoea serpens var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf morning glory, is a tropical vine characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage.

It produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive violet hue. The plant is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and spread rapidly.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.
