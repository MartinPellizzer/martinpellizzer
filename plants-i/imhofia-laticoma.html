Imhofia laticoma

Imhofia laticoma, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible tubers resembling those of traditional arrowroot. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
