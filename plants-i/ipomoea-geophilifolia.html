Ipomoea geophilifolia

Ipomoea geophilifolia, commonly known as the ground ivy or creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Ipomoea geophilifolia is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the morning glory.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
