Ipomoea leonensis

Ipomoea leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a vigorous vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea leonensis is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive in some areas.
