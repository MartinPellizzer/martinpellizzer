Ichthyothere grandifolia

Ichthyothere grandifolia, commonly known as the waterleaf plant, is a herbaceous species native to moist, temperate regions.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley.
