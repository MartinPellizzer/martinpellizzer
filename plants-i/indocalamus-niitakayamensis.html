Indocalamus niitakayamensis

Indocalamus niitakayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Niitakayama bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a distinctive silver-green coloration.

Indocalamus niitakayamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
