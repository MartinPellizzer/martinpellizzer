Indigofera umbonata

Indigofera umbonata, commonly known as the African indigo or umbrella plant, is a species of legume native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Indigofera umbonata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
