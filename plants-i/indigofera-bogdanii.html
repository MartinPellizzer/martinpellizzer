Indigofera bogdanii

Indigofera bogdanii, commonly known as the desert indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with woody stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, which are borne on slender pedicels. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in desert ecosystems and a valuable ornamental in dry landscapes.
