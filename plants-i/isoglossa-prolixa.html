Isoglossa prolixa

Isoglossa prolixa, commonly known as the broadleaf isoglossa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, usually displaying shades of yellow or green.

Isoglossa prolixa is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
