Inga endlicheri

Inga endlicheri, commonly known as the white inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in humid lowland forests. Its seeds are encased in a pod and dispersed by animals, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The tree also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
