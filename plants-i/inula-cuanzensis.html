Inula cuanzensis

Inula cuanzensis, commonly known as the Guangdong Inula, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, producing a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Inula cuanzensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
