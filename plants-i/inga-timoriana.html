Inga timoriana

Inga timoriana, commonly known as the Timor inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
