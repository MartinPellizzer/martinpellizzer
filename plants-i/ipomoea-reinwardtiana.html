Ipomoea reinwardtiana

Ipomoea reinwardtiana, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea reinwardtiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
