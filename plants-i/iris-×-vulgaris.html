Iris × vulgaris

Iris × vulgaris, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

The Dutch iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
