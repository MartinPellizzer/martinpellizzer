Indigofera tisserantii

Indigofera tisserantii, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue or violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.
