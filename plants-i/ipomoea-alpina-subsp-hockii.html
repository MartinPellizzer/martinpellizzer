Ipomoea alpina subsp. hockii

Ipomoea alpina subsp.

hockii, commonly known as the alpine morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet.

The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long blooming period from spring to early autumn.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to rocky and arid environments.
