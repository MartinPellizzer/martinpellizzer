Isoetes histrix var. grandis

Isoetes histrix var. grandis, commonly known as the giant water horsetail, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in dense, spike-like formations. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in shallow, slow-moving waters. Its leaves are usually long and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient water absorption.

This variety is distinguished by its larger size compared to other members of the species, making it a notable presence in its native habitats.
