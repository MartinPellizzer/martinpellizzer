Ipomoea calderonii

Ipomoea calderonii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that help it climb and spread along surfaces. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea calderonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
