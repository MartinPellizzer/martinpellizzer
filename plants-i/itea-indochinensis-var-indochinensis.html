Itea indochinensis var. indochinensis

Itea indochinensis var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochinese water-plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its small, white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
