Ilex aquifolium var. polymorpha

Ilex aquifolium var. polymorpha, commonly known as the European holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically spiny and have a distinctive saw-toothed margin. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.

Its evergreen appearance provides year-round visual interest in landscaped settings.
