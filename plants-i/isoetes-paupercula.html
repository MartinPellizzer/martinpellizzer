Isoetes paupercula

Isoetes paupercula, commonly known as the poor fern moss, is a species of water plantain belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around its central axis. This plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it anchors itself to substrates with its rhizomes. Isoetes paupercula is notable for its ability to survive in low-nutrient conditions, making it a resilient species in oligotrophic ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations contribute to its role in aquatic plant communities.
