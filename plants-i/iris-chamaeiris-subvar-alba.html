Iris chamaeiris subvar. alba

Iris chamaeiris subvar. alba, commonly known as the white marsh iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear showy, white to pale blue flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
