Inula decipiens

Inula decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, branched stem covered in grayish hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous tiny florets, with ray florets forming the outer ring.

Inula decipiens is often found in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, and it is adapted to cold and arid environments.
