Ixora alba

Ixora alba, commonly known as the white flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Ixora alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
