Ipomoea surinamensis

Ipomoea surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers typically bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
