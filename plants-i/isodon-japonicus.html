Isodon japonicus

Isodon japonicus, commonly known as Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Isodon japonicus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
