Isanthus coeruleus

Isanthus coeruleus, commonly known as the bluebell of the rock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Isanthus coeruleus is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
