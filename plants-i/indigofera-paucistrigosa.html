Indigofera paucistrigosa

Indigofera paucistrigosa, commonly known as the slender indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
