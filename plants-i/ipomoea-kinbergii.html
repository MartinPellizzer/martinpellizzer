Ipomoea kinbergii

Ipomoea kinbergii, commonly known as the Kinberg's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and grows as a climbing vine with hairy stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea kinbergii is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
