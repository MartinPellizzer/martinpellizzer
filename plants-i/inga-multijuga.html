Inga multijuga

Inga multijuga, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow and are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
