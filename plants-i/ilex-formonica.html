Ilex formonica

Ilex formonica, commonly known as the Formosa holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Ilex formonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
