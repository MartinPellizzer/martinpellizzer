Impatiens crassiloba

Impatiens crassiloba, commonly known as the thick-leaved impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are usually pink or white in hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
