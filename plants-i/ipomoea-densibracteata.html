Ipomoea densibracteata

Ipomoea densibracteata, commonly known as the dense-bract morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ipomoea densibracteata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
