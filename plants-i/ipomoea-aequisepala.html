Ipomoea aequisepala

Ipomoea aequisepala, commonly known as the unequal-sepal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal calyx with unequal lobes. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, often climbing or trailing along supports.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
