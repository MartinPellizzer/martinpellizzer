Iris gawleri

Iris gawleri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a white or pale yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

The iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its specific habitat requirements.
