Irvingia mossambicensis

Irvingia mossambicensis, commonly known as African mango, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Irvingiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Irvingia mossambicensis is valued for its potential health benefits and is often used in traditional medicine.
