Ipomoea myanmarensis

Ipomoea myanmarensis, commonly known as the Myanmar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
