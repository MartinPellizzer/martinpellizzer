Ipomoea bolusiana var. abbreviata

Ipomoea bolusiana var. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
