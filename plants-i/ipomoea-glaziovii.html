Ipomoea glaziovii

Ipomoea glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often appearing in the early morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea glaziovii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
