Isodon umbrosus f. kameba

Isodon umbrosus f. kameba, commonly known as the Kameba lepidote, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and often display a pale purple or white color, adding to its ornamental value.
