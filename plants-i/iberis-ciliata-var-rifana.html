Iberis ciliata var. rifana

Iberis ciliata var. rifana, commonly known as the Rif carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features pinnately divided leaves with finely toothed margins, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and arid environments.
