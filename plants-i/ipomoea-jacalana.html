Ipomoea jacalana

Ipomoea jacalana, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. Its vines can climb or trail, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Ipomoea jacalana is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to harsh conditions.
