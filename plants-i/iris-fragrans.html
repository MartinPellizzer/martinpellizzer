Iris fragrans

Iris fragrans, commonly known as the sweet iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive fragrance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Iris fragrans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
