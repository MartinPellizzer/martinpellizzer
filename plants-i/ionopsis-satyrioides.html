Ionopsis satyrioides

Ionopsis satyrioides, commonly known as the yellow bell.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
