Ipomoea kentrocaulos

Ipomoea kentrocaulos, commonly known as the spiral morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its helically coiled stems that twist around supports as it grows. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous seeds.

Ipomoea kentrocaulos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
