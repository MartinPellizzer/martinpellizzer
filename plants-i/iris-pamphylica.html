Iris pamphylica

Iris pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are sword-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 30 cm, while its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves.

Iris pamphylica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
