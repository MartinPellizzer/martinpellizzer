Ivesia argyrocoma var. moranii

Ivesia argyrocoma var. moranii, commonly known as the Moran's silverleaf ivesia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
