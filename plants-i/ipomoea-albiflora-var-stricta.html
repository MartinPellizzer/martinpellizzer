Ipomoea albiflora var. stricta

Ipomoea albiflora var. stricta, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
