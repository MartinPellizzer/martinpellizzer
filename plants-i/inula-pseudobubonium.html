Inula pseudobubonium

Inula pseudobubonium, commonly known as false bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
