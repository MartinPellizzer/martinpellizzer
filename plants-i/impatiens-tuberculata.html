Impatiens tuberculata

Impatiens tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves and showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
