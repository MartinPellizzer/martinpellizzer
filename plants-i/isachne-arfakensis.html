Isachne arfakensis

Isachne arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfaken grass, is a species of grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
