Indigofera notata

Indigofera notata, commonly known as the blue bean or blue snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its distinctive seed pods, which are elongated and often contain a single, hard-coated seed.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas, dry forests, and disturbed habitats.
