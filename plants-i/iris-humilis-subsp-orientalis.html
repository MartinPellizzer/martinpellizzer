Iris humilis subsp. orientalis

Iris humilis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Flag Iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring grass-like leaves and showy blue to violet flowers.

The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, producing three to six petals that are often marked with darker veins. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or marshy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
