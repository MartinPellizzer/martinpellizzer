Iris nezahatiae

Iris nezahatiae, commonly known as the Nezahatiae Iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, three-petaled flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

Iris nezahatiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
