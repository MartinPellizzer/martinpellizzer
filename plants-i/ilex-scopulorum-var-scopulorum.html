Ilex scopulorum var. scopulorum

Ilex scopulorum var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock holly, is a evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and along the Atlantic coast. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. Its small, dark blue berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
