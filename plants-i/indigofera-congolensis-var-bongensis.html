Indigofera congolensis var. bongensis

Indigofera congolensis var. bongensis, commonly known as the Congo indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Congo Basin, and is characterized by its woody shrub form and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.

Additionally, its seeds and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
