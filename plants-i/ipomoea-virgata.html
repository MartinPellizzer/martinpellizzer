Ipomoea virgata

Ipomoea virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, silvery coating that gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along stream banks or in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a popular garden plant and an invasive species in some areas.
