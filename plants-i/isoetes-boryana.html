Isoetes boryana

Isoetes boryana, commonly known as the Bory's watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, ribbon-like leaves that form a compact, turpentine-like structure. This plant is typically found in shallow, still waters such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to substrates. Isoetes boryana is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for nutrient-rich environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
