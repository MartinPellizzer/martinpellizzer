Inula crithmoides subsp. mediterranea

Inula crithmoides subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean Inula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas.

The plant features yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with narrow, linear leaves. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
