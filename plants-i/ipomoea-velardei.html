Ipomoea velardei

Ipomoea velardei, commonly known as the Velarde's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea velardei typically blooms in the morning, hence its common name, and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
