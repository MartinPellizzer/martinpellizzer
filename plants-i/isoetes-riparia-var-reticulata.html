Isoetes riparia var. reticulata

Isoetes riparia var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that form a dense mat on the water's surface. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in specialized clusters called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. It is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the margins of streams.

This variety is distinguished by its finely reticulate pattern on the fronds, giving it a unique appearance among other Isoetes species.
