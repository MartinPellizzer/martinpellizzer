Inga yasuniana

Inga yasuniana, commonly known as the Yasuni inga, is a tropical tree species native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in Ecuador.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

Inga yasuniana plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity and maintaining soil fertility in its native habitat.
