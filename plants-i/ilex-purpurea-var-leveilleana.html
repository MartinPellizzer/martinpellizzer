Ilex purpurea var. leveilleana

Ilex purpurea var. leveilleana, commonly known as the purpleleaf holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dark green, oval leaves that turn a striking purplish-red color in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its dense foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for hedges and foundation plantings.
