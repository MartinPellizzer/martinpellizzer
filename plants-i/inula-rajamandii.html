Inula rajamandii

Inula rajamandii, commonly known as the Rajamandi Inula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and robust.

Inula rajamandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
