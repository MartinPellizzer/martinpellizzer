Ipomoea lanata

Ipomoea lanata, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the early morning. It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil.

Ipomoea lanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
