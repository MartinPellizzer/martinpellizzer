Inga spuria var. racemaria

Inga spuria var. racemaria, commonly known as the racemose inga, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, with a dense canopy and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which attract various pollinators.

Its fruits are legumes containing edible seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential in agroforestry systems.
