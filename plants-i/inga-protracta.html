Inga protracta

Inga protracta, commonly known as the Brazilian velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
