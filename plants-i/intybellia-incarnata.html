Intybellia incarnata

Intybellia incarnata, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is adapted to arid environments.

Intybellia incarnata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
