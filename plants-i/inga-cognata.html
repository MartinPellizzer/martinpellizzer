Inga cognata

Inga cognata, commonly known as the red-veined or scarlet inga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a striking red vein pattern. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Inga cognata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
