Ipomoea ankylophlebia

Ipomoea ankylophlebia, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
