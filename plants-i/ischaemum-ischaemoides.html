Ischaemum ischaemoides

Ischaemum ischaemoides, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and fields. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaf blades are narrow and slightly hairy, with a blue-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.
