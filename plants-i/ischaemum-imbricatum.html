Ischaemum imbricatum

Ischaemum imbricatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing several small, two-lobed flowers.

Ischaemum imbricatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environments.
