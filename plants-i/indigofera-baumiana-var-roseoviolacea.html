Indigofera baumiana var. roseoviolacea

Indigofera baumiana var. roseoviolacea, commonly known as the rose-violace indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural applications.
