Isoetes tenella var. oligostoma

Isoetes tenella var. oligostoma, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that form dense, floating mats on the water surface. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, stalked clusters along the leaf margins. It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams with soft, muddy substrates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
