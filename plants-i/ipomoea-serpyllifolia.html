Ipomoea serpyllifolia

Ipomoea serpyllifolia, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many regions.
