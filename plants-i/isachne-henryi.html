Isachne henryi

Isachne henryi, commonly known as Henry's isachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Isachne henryi is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
