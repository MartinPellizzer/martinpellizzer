Iris talischi

Iris talischi, commonly known as the Talischi iris, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
