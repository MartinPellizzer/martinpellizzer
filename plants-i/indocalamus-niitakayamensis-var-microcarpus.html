Indocalamus niitakayamensis var. microcarpus

Indocalamus niitakayamensis var. microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glossy in appearance. It is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
