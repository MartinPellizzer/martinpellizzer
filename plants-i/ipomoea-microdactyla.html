Ipomoea microdactyla

Ipomoea microdactyla, commonly known as the small-finger morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

Ipomoea microdactyla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
