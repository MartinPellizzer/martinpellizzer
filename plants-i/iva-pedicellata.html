Iva pedicellata

Iva pedicellata, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Iva pedicellata plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for various pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
