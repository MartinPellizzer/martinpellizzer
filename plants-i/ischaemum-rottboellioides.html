Ischaemum rottboellioides

Ischaemum rottboellioides, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.
