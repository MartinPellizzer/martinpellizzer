Iris × fuscisanguinea

Iris × fuscisanguinea, commonly known as the purple iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris sanguinea and Iris germanica.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce multiple flower stalks per stem.
