Isoetes velata subsp. longissima

Isoetes velata subsp.

longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed water shield, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales. It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water.

The plant typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporocarps, are distinctive and help in its reproduction.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to low-light conditions.
