Ipomoea simplex var. obtusisepala

Ipomoea simplex var. obtusisepala, commonly known as the blunt-sepal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant.
