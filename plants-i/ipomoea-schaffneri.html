Ipomoea schaffneri

Ipomoea schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea schaffneri is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known morning glory species.
