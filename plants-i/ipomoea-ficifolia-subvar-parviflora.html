Ipomoea ficifolia subvar. parviflora

Ipomoea ficifolia subvar. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a trailing habit and can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing supports or other vegetation. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by insects.
