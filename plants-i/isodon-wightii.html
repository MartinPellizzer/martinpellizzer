Isodon wightii

Isodon wightii, commonly known as the Indian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits.

Isodon wightii is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic benefits.
