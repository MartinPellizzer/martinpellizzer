Ipomoea samarensis

Ipomoea samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and fibrous stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, spiral-shaped capsule that opens to release the seeds.
