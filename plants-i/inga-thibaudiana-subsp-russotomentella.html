Inga thibaudiana subsp. russotomentella

Inga thibaudiana subsp.

russotomentella, commonly known as the silky inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually odd-pinnate with elongated, elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are pod-like, containing several edible seeds, and it is often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is notable for its fast growth and potential use in agroforestry systems.
