Iozoste furfuracea

Iozoste furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The hairy leaf fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
