Ipomoea malvaeoides var. argentea

Ipomoea malvaeoides var. argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it requires careful management in gardens and natural habitats.
