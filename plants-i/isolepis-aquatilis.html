Isolepis aquatilis

Isolepis aquatilis, commonly known as the water tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It typically grows in shallow waters, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Isolepis aquatilis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
