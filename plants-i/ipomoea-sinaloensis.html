Ipomoea sinaloensis

Ipomoea sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that open in the morning and close at dusk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea sinaloensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
