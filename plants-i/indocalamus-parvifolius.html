Indocalamus parvifolius

Indocalamus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Indocalamus parvifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable utility in local communities.
