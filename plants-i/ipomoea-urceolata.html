Ipomoea urceolata

Ipomoea urceolata, commonly known as the "bottle-gourd vine," is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces a distinctive, bottle-shaped fruit that matures to a pale green or yellowish hue. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary traditions.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
