Iris × moricolor

Iris × moricolor, commonly known as the Dutch iris, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Iris germanica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers that are highly ornamental and suitable for gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.

Its botanical features include sword-shaped leaves and a rhizomatous root system, which contribute to its hardy and adaptable nature.
