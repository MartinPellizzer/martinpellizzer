Inga myriantha

Inga myriantha, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.

Inga myriantha is also appreciated for its ornamental value and role in supporting local biodiversity.
