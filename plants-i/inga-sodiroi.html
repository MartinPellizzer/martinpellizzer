Inga sodiroi

Inga sodiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sweet acacia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it valuable for reforestation and soil enrichment.
