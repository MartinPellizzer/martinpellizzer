Ipomoea macrorhizos

Ipomoea macrorhizos, commonly known as the giant root morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has a robust root system, which contributes to its common name and aids in its survival in various soil types. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often using its tendrils to support itself.

Ipomoea macrorhizos is also noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
