Ipomoea rondoniae

Ipomoea rondoniae, commonly known as the Rondonia morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows as a climbing vine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded understory areas of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
