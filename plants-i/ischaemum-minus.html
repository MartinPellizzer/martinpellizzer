Ischaemum minus

Ischaemum minus, commonly known as lesser barnyard grass, is a annual grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It is characterized by its slender stems and is often considered a weed in cultivated areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
