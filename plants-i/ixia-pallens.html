Ixia pallens

Ixia pallens, commonly known as the white ox-eye, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related species. The plant features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers with a yellow throat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen.

Ixia pallens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
