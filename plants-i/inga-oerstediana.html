Inga oerstediana

Inga oerstediana, commonly known as the Oersted's inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
