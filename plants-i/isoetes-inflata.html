Isoetes inflata

Isoetes inflata, commonly known as the inflated watermace, is a species of aquatic fern that belongs to the genus Isoetes.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated spore cases that resemble small, bladder-like structures. The plant typically grows in freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes, where it can thrive in still or slow-moving water. Its spores are released into the water, allowing for dispersal and colonization of new areas.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
