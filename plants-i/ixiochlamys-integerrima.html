Ixiochlamys integerrima

Ixiochlamys integerrima, commonly known as the hardy rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, tubular or spurred flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
