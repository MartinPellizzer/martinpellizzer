Iliamna remota var. typica

Iliamna remota var. typica, commonly known as the Alaskan huckleberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in boreal and subarctic climates. Its flowers are white and clustered, followed by dark red or black berries that are rich in antioxidants.

This species plays an important ecological role, providing food for wildlife and supporting local ecosystems.
