Ionoxalis tenuissima

Ionoxalis tenuissima, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland habitats.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
