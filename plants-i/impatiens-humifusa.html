Impatiens humifusa

Impatiens humifusa, commonly known as the creeping jewelweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
