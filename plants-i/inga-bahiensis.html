Inga bahiensis

Inga bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is also known for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation projects due to its ecological benefits.

The fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds that are sometimes used in local cuisines.
