Ipomoea eriocarpa

Ipomoea eriocarpa, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling vines and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, with five petals and a prominent spurred petal. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
