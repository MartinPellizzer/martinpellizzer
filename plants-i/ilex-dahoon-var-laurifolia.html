Ilex dahoon var. laurifolia

Ilex dahoon var. laurifolia, commonly known as the Atlantic Coast holly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and moist woodlands. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to acidic soils and its ability to thrive in humid, coastal environments.
