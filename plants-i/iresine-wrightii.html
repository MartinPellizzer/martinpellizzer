Iresine wrightii

Iresine wrightii, commonly known as Wright's irene, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a deep red to purple hue, often adorned with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically pink or red, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Iresine wrightii is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and is well-suited for shaded garden beds.
