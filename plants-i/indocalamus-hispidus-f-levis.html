Indocalamus hispidus f. levis

Indocalamus hispidus f. levis, commonly known as the silky bamboo or velvet bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green culms that are covered in a fine, silky layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a clumping bamboo, forming dense clusters that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in landscaping and traditional Chinese medicine.
