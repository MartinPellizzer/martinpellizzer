Isnardia trifolia

Isnardia trifolia, commonly known as three-leaved saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has three leaves that are opposite or whorled, with a rough, hairy texture. Its small white or pink flowers appear in early spring, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
