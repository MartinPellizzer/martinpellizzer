Ipomoea expansa

Ipomoea expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm climates with full sun and well-drained soil.

Ipomoea expansa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
