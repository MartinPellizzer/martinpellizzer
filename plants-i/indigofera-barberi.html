Indigofera barberi

Indigofera barberi, commonly known as the desert indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Indigofera barberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, low-moisture environments.
