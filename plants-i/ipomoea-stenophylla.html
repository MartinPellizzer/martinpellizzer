Ipomoea stenophylla

Ipomoea stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Ipomoea stenophylla is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
