Ipomoea oblongata

Ipomoea oblongata, commonly known as the round-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea oblongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
