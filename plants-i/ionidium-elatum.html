Ionidium elatum

Ionidium elatum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits.

Ionidium elatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
