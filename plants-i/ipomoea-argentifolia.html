Ipomoea argentifolia

Ipomoea argentifolia, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental due to its striking foliage and showy blooms. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea argentifolia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
