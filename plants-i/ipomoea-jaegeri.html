Ipomoea jaegeri

Ipomoea jaegeri, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea jaegeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
