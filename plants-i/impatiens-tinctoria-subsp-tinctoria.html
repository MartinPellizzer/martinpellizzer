Impatiens tinctoria subsp. tinctoria

Impatiens tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as the scarlet balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce numerous flowers throughout the growing season.
