Iris pseudopallida f. dalmatica

Iris pseudopallida f. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian iris, is a rare and striking perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the Adriatic Sea.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shores, where it thrives in coastal climates with salt spray. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous root system.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
