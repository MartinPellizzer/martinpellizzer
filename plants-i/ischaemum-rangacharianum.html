Ischaemum rangacharianum

Ischaemum rangacharianum, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its fine, elongated leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.

Ischaemum rangacharianum is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
