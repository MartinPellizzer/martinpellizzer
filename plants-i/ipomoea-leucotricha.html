Ipomoea leucotricha

Ipomoea leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms during the day, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ipomoea leucotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
