Isotoma fasciculata

Isotoma fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Isotoma fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
