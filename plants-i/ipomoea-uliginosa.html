Ipomoea uliginosa

Ipomoea uliginosa, commonly known as swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in moist, often swampy habitats and is often found near water bodies. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Ipomoea uliginosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
