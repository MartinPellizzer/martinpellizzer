Ipomoea sericea

Ipomoea sericea, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a herbaceous annual vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Ipomoea sericea is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, sunny environments.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
