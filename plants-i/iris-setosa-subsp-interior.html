Iris setosa subsp. interior

Iris setosa subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
