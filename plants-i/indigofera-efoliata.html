Indigofera efoliata

Indigofera efoliata, commonly known as the leafy indigo plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its striking purple or blue flowers that bloom in racemes. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant also has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
