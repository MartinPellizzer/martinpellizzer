Ipomoea bowieana

Ipomoea bowieana, commonly known as the Bowie's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers and twining stems.

Ipomoea bowieana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
