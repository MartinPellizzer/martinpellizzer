Ipomoea insularis

Ipomoea insularis, commonly known as the Bahama beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing near water sources such as ponds and marshes. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Ipomoea insularis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, tropical environments.
