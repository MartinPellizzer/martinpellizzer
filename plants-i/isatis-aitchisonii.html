Isatis aitchisonii

Isatis aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's woad, is a flowering plant native to Pakistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
