Ixeridium dentatum f. leucanthum

Ixeridium dentatum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered meadow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive white or pale yellow inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
