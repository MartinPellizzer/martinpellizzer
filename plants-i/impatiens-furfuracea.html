Impatiens furfuracea

Impatiens furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
