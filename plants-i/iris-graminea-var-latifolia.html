Iris graminea var. latifolia

Iris graminea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers with a distinctive purple-violet coloration. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are broader than the typical iris foliage, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and wetland areas.
