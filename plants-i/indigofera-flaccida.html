Indigofera flaccida

Indigofera flaccida, commonly known as the drooping indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Indigofera flaccida is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
