Ipomoea monosperma

Ipomoea monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its unique single-seeded fruit, which develops from a single ovary. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a garden plant.
