Iris polakii f. urmiensis

Iris polakii f. urmiensis, commonly known as the Urmi iris, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white stripe along the lower petal. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in specialized gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
