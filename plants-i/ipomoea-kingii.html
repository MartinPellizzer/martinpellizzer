Ipomoea kingii

Ipomoea kingii, commonly known as the King's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often found in wooded areas or along fences. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red capsule that splits open when mature.
