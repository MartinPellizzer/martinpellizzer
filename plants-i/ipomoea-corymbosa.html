Ipomoea corymbosa

Ipomoea corymbosa, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch and have been used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea corymbosa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
