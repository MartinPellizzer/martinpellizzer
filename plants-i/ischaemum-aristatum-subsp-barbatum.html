Ischaemum aristatum subsp. barbatum

Ischaemum aristatum subsp.

barbatum, commonly known as the hairy barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains.

This subspecies is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive growth habit.
