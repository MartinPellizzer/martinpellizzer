Indigofera lancifolia

Indigofera lancifolia, commonly known as the African indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. Its seeds are often used as a source of dye, particularly in traditional textile industries.

The plant also plays a role in traditional medicine due to its various bioactive compounds.
