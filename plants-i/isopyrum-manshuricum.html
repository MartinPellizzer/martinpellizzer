Isopyrum manshuricum

Isopyrum manshuricum, commonly known as the Far Eastern sandwort, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Russia and China.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually opposite and linear, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Isopyrum manshuricum is often found in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
