Ipomoea isthmica

Ipomoea isthmica, commonly known as the Isthmian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing habit and ornamental value.

Ipomoea isthmica is often cultivated for its beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
