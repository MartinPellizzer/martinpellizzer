Iris douglasiana f. alpha

Iris douglasiana f. alpha, commonly known as the Douglas iris, is a variant of the Douglas iris native to the western United States.

This plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and meadows. The leaves are sword-shaped and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to the showy flowers.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
