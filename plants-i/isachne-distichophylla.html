Isachne distichophylla

Isachne distichophylla, commonly known as the two-ranked grass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which produces small, two-ranked florets.

Isachne distichophylla is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
