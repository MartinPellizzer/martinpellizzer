Ilex aquifolium var. fructu-albo

Ilex aquifolium var. fructu-albo, commonly known as the white-fruited holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its distinctive white or pale berries.

This plant is evergreen, featuring glossy, spiny leaves that are typically dark green. The white fruits, which are actually berries, mature in the fall and remain on the plant throughout winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
