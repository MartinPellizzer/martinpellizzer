Ixia flava

Ixia flava, commonly known as the golden trumpet, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ixia flava is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
