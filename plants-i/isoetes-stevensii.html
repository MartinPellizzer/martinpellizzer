Isoetes stevensii

Isoetes stevensii, commonly known as the stevens' watermoccasin, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spiky, segmented leaves that resemble moccasins, hence its common name. This plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Isoetes stevensii is a spore-producing plant, and its sporophytes are usually submerged, while the gametophytes are exposed.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
