Irvingia laeta

Irvingia laeta, commonly known as the African mango tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with thick, rugged bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by fruit that resembles a small, elongated mango. Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
