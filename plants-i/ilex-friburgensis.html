Ilex friburgensis

Ilex friburgensis, commonly known as the Fribourg holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Ilex friburgensis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
