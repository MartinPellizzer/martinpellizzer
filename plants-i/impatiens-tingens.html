Impatiens tingens

Impatiens tingens, commonly known as the velvetleaf impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens tingens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
