Ipomoea coacta

Ipomoea coacta, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces large, showy blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are trailing and can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing supports or other vegetation.

Ipomoea coacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
