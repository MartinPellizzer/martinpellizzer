Ichnanthus inconstans var. montanus

Ichnanthus inconstans var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, blooming in early spring.
