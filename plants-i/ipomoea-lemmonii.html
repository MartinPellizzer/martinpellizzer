Ipomoea lemmonii

Ipomoea lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ipomoea lemmonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
