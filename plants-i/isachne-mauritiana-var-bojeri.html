Isachne mauritiana var. bojeri

Isachne mauritiana var. bojeri, commonly known as Bojer's isachne, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
