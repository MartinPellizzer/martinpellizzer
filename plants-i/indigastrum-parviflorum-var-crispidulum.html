Indigastrum parviflorum var. crispidulum

Indigastrum parviflorum var. crispidulum, commonly known as the crinkled indigastrum, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive crinkled or wavy margin, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
