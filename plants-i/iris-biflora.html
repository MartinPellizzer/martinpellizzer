Iris biflora

Iris biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a tall, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are striking, with three upright petals and three spreading sepals, often in shades of blue or violet.

Iris biflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
