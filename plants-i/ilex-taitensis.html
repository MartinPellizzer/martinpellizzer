Ilex taitensis

Ilex taitensis, commonly known as the Taiti holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Aquifoliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves and small, dark blue berries. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forest areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage provides habitat for native birds and insects, contributing to the ecological balance of its environment.

Ilex taitensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
