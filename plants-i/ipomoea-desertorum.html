Ipomoea desertorum

Ipomoea desertorum, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, a characteristic known as nyctinasty. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Ipomoea desertorum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh desert climates.
