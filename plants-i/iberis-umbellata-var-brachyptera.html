Iberis umbellata var. brachyptera

Iberis umbellata var. brachyptera, commonly known as the narrow-poded candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
