Indigofera longipes

Indigofera longipes, commonly known as the longipes indigo or the long-stemmed indigo plant, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
