Ipomoea cryptica

Ipomoea cryptica, commonly known as the hidden morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ipomoea cryptica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
