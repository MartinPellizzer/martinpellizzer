Ilex occidentalis

Ilex occidentalis, commonly known as the western holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It features simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glossy green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native range.
