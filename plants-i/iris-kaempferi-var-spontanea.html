Iris kaempferi var. spontanea

Iris kaempferi var. spontanea, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland landscapes.
