Iris tenax var. thompsonii

Iris tenax var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's iris, is a rare and distinctive variety of the iris genus native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers with a prominent white or yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, sword-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
