Inga apta

Inga apta, commonly known as the yellow inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is known for its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Inga apta is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
