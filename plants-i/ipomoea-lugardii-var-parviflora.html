Ipomoea lugardii var. parviflora

Ipomoea lugardii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color and emit a mild fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
