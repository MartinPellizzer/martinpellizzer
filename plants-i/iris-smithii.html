Iris smithii

Iris smithii, commonly known as the Smith's iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves, and it produces showy, spurred petals that bloom in early to mid-summer.

Iris smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
