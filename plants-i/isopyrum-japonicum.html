Isopyrum japonicum

Isopyrum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese isopyrum, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it thrives in woodland or shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
