Indigofera pseudoreticulata

Indigofera pseudoreticulata, commonly known as the African indigo or false reticulate indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
