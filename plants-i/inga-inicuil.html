Inga inicuil

Inga inicuil, commonly known as the golden inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a valuable species in reforestation efforts. Its fruit pods contain seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

The plant's adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both landscaping and ecological restoration.
