Ipomoea semisagitta

Ipomoea semisagitta, commonly known as the semi-sagittate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms during the day. Its botanical features include hairy stems and tendrils that aid in its climbing habit.

Ipomoea semisagitta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
