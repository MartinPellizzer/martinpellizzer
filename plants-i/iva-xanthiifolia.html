Iva xanthiifolia

Iva xanthiifolia, commonly known as the yellow-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically pleasing in natural habitats.
