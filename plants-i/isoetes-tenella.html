Isoetes tenella

Isoetes tenella, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes in the family Isoetaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that form a compact, grass-like structure, often growing in shallow, freshwater environments. The plant is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are borne on the leaf surfaces and play a key role in its reproductive cycle.

Isoetes tenella is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing shelter for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
