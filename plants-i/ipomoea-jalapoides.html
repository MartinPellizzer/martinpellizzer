Ipomoea jalapoides

Ipomoea jalapoides, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and rapid growth habit, often climbing or trailing along supports. Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy, five-petaled blooms that open in the morning and close by midday.

Ipomoea jalapoides is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
