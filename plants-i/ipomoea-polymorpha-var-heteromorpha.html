Ipomoea polymorpha var. heteromorpha

Ipomoea polymorpha var. heteromorpha, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its diverse morphological variations, including differences in leaf shape, stem structure, and flower color. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue, purple, or white, opening in the morning and closing by midday. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils.
