Imhofia venusta

Imhofia venusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in suitable conditions.

Imhofia venusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
