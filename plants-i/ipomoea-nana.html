Ipomoea nana

Ipomoea nana, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ipomoea nana is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and easy care requirements.
