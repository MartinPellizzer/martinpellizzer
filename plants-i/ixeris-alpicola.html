Ixeris alpicola

Ixeris alpicola, commonly known as the alpine dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by fluffy white seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Ixeris alpicola is often found in alpine meadows and is a hardy species capable of withstanding harsh climatic conditions.
