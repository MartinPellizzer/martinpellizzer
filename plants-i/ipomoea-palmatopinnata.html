Ipomoea palmatopinnata

Ipomoea palmatopinnata, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with deep lobes, giving it a palmately pinnate appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
