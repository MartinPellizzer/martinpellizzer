Indigofera vivax

Indigofera vivax, commonly known as the wild indigo, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its fibrous roots and dense growth habit make it an effective ground cover plant.
