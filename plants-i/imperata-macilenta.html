Imperata macilenta

Imperata macilenta, commonly known as the slender blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily and form dense mats.

Imperata macilenta is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of thatching in some regions.
