Isoloma cordifolium

Isoloma cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

Isoloma cordifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
