Ilex amygdalina

Ilex amygdalina, commonly known as the Persian holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. Ilex amygdalina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
