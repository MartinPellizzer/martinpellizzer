Icica crassifolia

Icica crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf ice plant, is a succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow, enabling it to absorb moisture from the surface during rare rain events.

Icica crassifolia is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
