Ipomoea linosepala

Ipomoea linosepala, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are typically hairy and climbing. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other climbing vines.

Ipomoea linosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
