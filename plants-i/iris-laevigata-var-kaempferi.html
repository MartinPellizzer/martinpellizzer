Iris laevigata var. kaempferi

Iris laevigata var. kaempferi, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with three outer petals and three inner sepals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
