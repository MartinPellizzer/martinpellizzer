Ipomoea edithae

Ipomoea edithae, commonly known as Edith's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Ipomoea edithae is often found in moist, sandy soils near streams and in open woodlands.
