Ipomoea peckoltii

Ipomoea peckoltii, commonly known as the Peckolt's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often opening in the morning. The plant is a vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Ipomoea peckoltii is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
