Ipomoea ferruginea

Ipomoea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are covered in a rusty-brown, velvety coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ipomoea ferruginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
