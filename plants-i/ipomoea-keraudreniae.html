Ipomoea keraudreniae

Ipomoea keraudreniae, commonly known as the Keraudren's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
