Imperata longifolia

Imperata longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Imperata longifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
