Indigofera elandsbergensis

Indigofera elandsbergensis, commonly known as the Elandsberg indigo, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
