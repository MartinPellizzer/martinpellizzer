Ischaemum australe

Ischaemum australe, commonly known as Australian wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dunes and heathlands. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are usually less than 1 meter in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Ischaemum australe plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
