Ipomoea praematura

Ipomoea praematura, commonly known as the early morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rapid growth habit and short life cycle make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking quick blooms.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing or trailing growth pattern.
