Ipomoea uniflora

Ipomoea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Ipomoea uniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
