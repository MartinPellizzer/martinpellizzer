Indigofera omariana

Indigofera omariana, commonly known as the Arabian indigo or Sudan indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, and it is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules. Indigofera omariana is also valued for its historical use in dye production, particularly for producing a deep blue pigment.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration efforts in degraded landscapes.
