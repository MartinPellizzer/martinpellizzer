Inula glandulosa

Inula glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular inula, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by pale green ray florets. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Inula glandulosa is often found in meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
