Ionoxalis nudiflora

Ionoxalis nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and small, white or pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ionoxalis nudiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
