Ipomoea shupangensis

Ipomoea shupangensis, commonly known as the Shupang morning glory, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms that open in the morning. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
