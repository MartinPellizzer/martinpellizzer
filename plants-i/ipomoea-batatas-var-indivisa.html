Ipomoea batatas var. indivisa

Ipomoea batatas var. indivisa, commonly known as the red-veined sweet potato, is a variant of the sweet potato plant characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with deep red veins contrasting against a dark green background. This variety is cultivated primarily for its ornamental value rather than its edible tubers. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often used in gardens to add visual interest due to its vibrant leaf color.
