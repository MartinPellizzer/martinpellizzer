Indigofera interrupta

Indigofera interrupta, commonly known as the blue bean or wild indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
