Isopogon anemonifolius

Isopogon anemonifolius, commonly known as the mountain tea tree, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Isopogon anemonifolius is well adapted to coastal and inland regions, showing resilience to drought and salt spray.
