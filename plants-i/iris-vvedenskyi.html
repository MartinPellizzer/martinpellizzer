Iris vvedenskyi

Iris vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three drooping sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it tolerates cold temperatures and full sun exposure.

The Siberian iris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
