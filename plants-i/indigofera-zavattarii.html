Indigofera zavattarii

Indigofera zavattarii, commonly known as the Zavattari's indigo, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
