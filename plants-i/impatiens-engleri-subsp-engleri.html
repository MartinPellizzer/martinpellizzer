Impatiens engleri subsp. engleri

Impatiens engleri subsp.

engleri, commonly known as the Engler's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white.

The leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
