Impatiens keralensis

Impatiens keralensis, commonly known as Keralan balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Balsaminaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant is herbaceous, with alternate, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens keralensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
