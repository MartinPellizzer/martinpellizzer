Isatis cordigera

Isatis cordigera, commonly known as the heart-leaved woad, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and can grow up to two meters in height.

Isatis cordigera is also valued for its historical use in dye production and traditional medicine.
