Iris kaempferi var. oudemansii

Iris kaempferi var. oudemansii, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white center. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic touch to gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for water gardens or boggy areas.

Its botanical features include sword-shaped leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in wet conditions.
