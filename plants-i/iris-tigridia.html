Iris tigridia

Iris tigridia, commonly known as the scarlet iris, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with tall, slender stems that bear strikingly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. Its flowers are composed of three outer petals and three inner sepals, with a distinctive beak-like structure at the center.

Iris tigridia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
