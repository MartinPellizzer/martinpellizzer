Isopyrum raddeanum

Isopyrum raddeanum, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.
