Ipomoea marginata var. stipulacea

Ipomoea marginata var. stipulacea, commonly known as the scarlet morning glory, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces a distinctive, hairy capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and as a decorative plant.
