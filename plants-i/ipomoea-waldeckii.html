Ipomoea waldeckii

Ipomoea waldeckii, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea waldeckii is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures.
