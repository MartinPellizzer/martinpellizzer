Iris pseudopallida

Iris pseudopallida, commonly known as the white flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Iris pseudopallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
