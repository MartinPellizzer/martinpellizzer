Ixora cuneata

Ixora cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, bright orange-red inflorescences. The plant features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora cuneata is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
