Indigofera jaliscensis

Indigofera jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican indigo plant, is a species of legume native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is typically shrub-like, growing up to several meters in height, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
