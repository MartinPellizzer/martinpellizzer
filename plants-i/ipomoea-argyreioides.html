Ipomoea argyreioides

Ipomoea argyreioides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or pink, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Ipomoea argyreioides is a climbing vine that can grow quite long, using its tendrils to support itself as it ascends structures or trees.
