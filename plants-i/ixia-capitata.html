Ixia capitata

Ixia capitata, commonly known as the fire lily, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright orange or red flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Ixia capitata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its foliage consists of strap-shaped leaves that are evergreen and contribute to its ornamental appeal.
