Iris missouriensis

Iris missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features grass-like basal leaves. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers with three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

The Missouri iris is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
