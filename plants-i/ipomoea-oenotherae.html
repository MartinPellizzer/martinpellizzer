Ipomoea oenotherae

Ipomoea oenotherae, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The oil extracted from its seeds is valued for its medicinal and industrial applications.
