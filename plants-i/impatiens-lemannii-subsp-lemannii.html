Impatiens lemannii subsp. lemannii

Impatiens lemannii subsp.

lemannii, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a variegated pattern of green and silver, resembling a leopard's spots.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and colorful addition to their landscape.
