Ixora fulgens var. salicifolia

Ixora fulgens var. salicifolia, commonly known as the golden flame ixora, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for warm climates.

This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
