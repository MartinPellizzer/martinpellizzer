Indigofera smithioides

Indigofera smithioides, commonly known as the Smith's indigo, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in grassland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
