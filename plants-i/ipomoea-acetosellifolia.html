Ipomoea acetosellifolia

Ipomoea acetosellifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name.
