Ipomoea davenportii

Ipomoea davenportii, commonly known as the Davenport's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet and white. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories and bindweeds. Its vines can grow up to 10 feet long, climbing or trailing along supports.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while the flowers open in the morning and close by midday.
