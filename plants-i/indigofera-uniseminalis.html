Indigofera uniseminalis

Indigofera uniseminalis, commonly known as the single-seeded indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a pod containing a single, large seed, which is a distinctive feature of its morphology. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
