Ixodia ammobium

Ixodia ammobium, commonly known as the sandhill milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in racemes, and the plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds.

Ixodia ammobium is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
