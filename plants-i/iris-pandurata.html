Iris pandurata

Iris pandurata, commonly known as the purple iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lower petal. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iris pandurata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
