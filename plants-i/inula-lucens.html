Inula lucens

Inula lucens, commonly known as the shining elecampane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, hollow, cylindrical stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with rough textures and prominent veins, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Inula lucens is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
