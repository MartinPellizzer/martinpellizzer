Ipomoea kiuninsularis

Ipomoea kiuninsularis, commonly known as the Socotra morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
