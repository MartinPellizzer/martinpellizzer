Impatiens quadrisepala

Impatiens quadrisepala, commonly known as the four-veined impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
