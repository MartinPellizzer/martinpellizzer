Impatiens tubifera

Impatiens tubifera, commonly known as the thread-veined impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
