Indigofera bangweolensis

Indigofera bangweolensis, commonly known as the Bangweol indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
