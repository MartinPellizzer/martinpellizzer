Irvingia smithii

Irvingia smithii, commonly known as the African mango, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the family Irvingiaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The fruit is a fibrous, elongated pod that contains several seeds. The tree is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts within its native regions.
