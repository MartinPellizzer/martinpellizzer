Ixia rapunculoides var. namaquana

Ixia rapunculoides var. namaquana, commonly known as the Namakwa lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
