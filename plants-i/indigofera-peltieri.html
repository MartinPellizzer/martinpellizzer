Indigofera peltieri

Indigofera peltieri, commonly known as the Mexican indigo, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring pinnate leaves and showy, violet to blue flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along stream banks.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
