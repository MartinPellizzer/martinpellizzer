Indigofera trita var. purandharensis

Indigofera trita var. purandharensis, commonly known as the Purandhar indigo plant, is a species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, dark seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

Due to its historical use in dye production, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native region.
