Ischaemum quilonense

Ischaemum quilonense, commonly known as Kallar grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are narrow, flat, and have a prominent midrib, while the inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, waterways, and agricultural fields.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a weed and a valuable forage species.
