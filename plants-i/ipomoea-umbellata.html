Ipomoea umbellata

Ipomoea umbellata, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a trailing or climbing growth habit. Its flowers are typically about 5 cm in diameter and have five petals.

Ipomoea umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
