Ichnocarpus uliginosus

Ichnocarpus uliginosus, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

The plant is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional practices.
