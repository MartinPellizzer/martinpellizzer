Ilex taubertiana

Ilex taubertiana, commonly known as the Taubert's holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ilex taubertiana typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
