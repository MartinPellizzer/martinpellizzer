Inga spuria var. multifolia

Inga spuria var. multifolia, commonly known as the multileaf inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves that are often trifoliate or pinnate, giving it a lush, foliage-rich appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
