Ilex kanehirae

Ilex kanehirae, commonly known as the Kanehirae holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically leathery and have spiny margins. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Ilex kanehirae is valued for its dense foliage and seasonal color changes, adding visual interest to landscapes throughout the year.
