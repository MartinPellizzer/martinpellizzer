Indigofera blaiseae

Indigofera blaiseae, commonly known as Blaise's indigo, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
