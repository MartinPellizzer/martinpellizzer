Indigofera suffruticosa var. polyphylla

Indigofera suffruticosa var. polyphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian indigo or Brazilian woad, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is also cultivated for its dye-producing properties, similar to true indigo plants.
