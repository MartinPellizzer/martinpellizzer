Iris foetidissima subsp. lorea

Iris foetidissima subsp.

lorea, commonly known as the stinking iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dark blue to purple flowers with a distinctive, strongly fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
