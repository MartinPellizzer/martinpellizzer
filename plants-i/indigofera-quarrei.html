Indigofera quarrei

Indigofera quarrei, commonly known as the African indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of natural dyes and its adaptability to various soil types.
