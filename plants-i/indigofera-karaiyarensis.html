Indigofera karaiyarensis

Indigofera karaiyarensis, commonly known as the Karaiyarensis indigo plant, is a species of legume native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately compound. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are typically small and hard.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in traditional medicine.
