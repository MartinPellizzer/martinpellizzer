Inga sassa

Inga sassa, commonly known as the "sassa tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory species in tropical forests. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a sweet scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The fruit, which splits open when mature, contains several seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp.
