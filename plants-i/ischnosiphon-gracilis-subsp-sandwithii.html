Ischnosiphon gracilis subsp. sandwithii

Ischnosiphon gracilis subsp.

sandwithii, commonly known as the slender sandwith's spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thin, elongated stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
