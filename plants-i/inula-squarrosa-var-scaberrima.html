Inula squarrosa var. scaberrima

Inula squarrosa var. scaberrima, commonly known as rough sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to arid and rocky habitats in regions such as southern Africa and the Middle East.

This species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
