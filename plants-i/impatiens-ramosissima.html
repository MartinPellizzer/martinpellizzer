Impatiens ramosissima

Impatiens ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
