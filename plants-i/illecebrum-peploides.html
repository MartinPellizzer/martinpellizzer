Illecebrum peploides

Illecebrum peploides, commonly known as the woolly bladderwrack, is a perennial seaweed that thrives in intertidal zones along rocky coastlines.

It is characterized by its dark green to brown color and a branched, filamentous structure that allows it to anchor itself to substrates. The plant produces small, egg-shaped reproductive structures called zoospores, which are released into the water for dispersal. It plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Its ability to tolerate fluctuating salinity and temperature makes it a resilient species in dynamic marine environments.
