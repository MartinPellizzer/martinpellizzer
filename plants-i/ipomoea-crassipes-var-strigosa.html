Ipomoea crassipes var. strigosa

Ipomoea crassipes var. strigosa, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It has long, cylindrical stems that can grow submerged or emergent in water, and its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and has a rapid growth rate, making it both a valuable crop and an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to wet environments, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.
