Ipomoea luteoviridis

Ipomoea luteoviridis, commonly known as the yellow vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
