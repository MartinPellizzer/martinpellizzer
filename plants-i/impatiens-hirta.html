Impatiens hirta

Impatiens hirta, commonly known as the rough-leaved impatiens, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens hirta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
