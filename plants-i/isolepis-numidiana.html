Isolepis numidiana

Isolepis numidiana, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Isolepis numidiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
