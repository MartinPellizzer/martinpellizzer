Iris sisyrinchium f. purpureoviolacea

Iris sisyrinchium f. purpureoviolacea, commonly known as the purple-violet iris, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is a hybrid form that exhibits striking purple and violet hues in its flowers, which typically bloom in early summer. The plant features grass-like leaves and tall, slender stems that support its showy inflorescences. It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
