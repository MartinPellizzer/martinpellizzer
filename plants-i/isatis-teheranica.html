Isatis teheranica

Isatis teheranica, commonly known as Persian woad, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its erect stems, alternate leaves, and small blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often grown for its historical use in producing indigo dye, similar to its more famous relative, Isatis tinctoria.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardeners interested in traditional dye plants.
