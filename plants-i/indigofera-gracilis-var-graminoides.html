Indigofera gracilis var. graminoides

Indigofera gracilis var. graminoides, commonly known as slender indigo, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and has potential applications in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
