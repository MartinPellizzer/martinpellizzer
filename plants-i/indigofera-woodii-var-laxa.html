Indigofera woodii var. laxa

Indigofera woodii var. laxa, commonly known as the slender indigo.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in forested or moist habitats. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a soft, velvety texture, while its pods are elongated and contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
