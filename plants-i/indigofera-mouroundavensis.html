Indigofera mouroundavensis

Indigofera mouroundavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye in some regions.
