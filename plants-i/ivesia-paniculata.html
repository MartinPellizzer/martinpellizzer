Ivesia paniculata

Ivesia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle-leafed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, cone-shaped flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ivesia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
