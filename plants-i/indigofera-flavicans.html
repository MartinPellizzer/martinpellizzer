Indigofera flavicans

Indigofera flavicans, commonly known as the yellow indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional uses.
