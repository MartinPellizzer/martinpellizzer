Indigofera arenaria var. strigosa

Indigofera arenaria var. strigosa, commonly known as the sand indigo or desert indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of violet to blue flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in sandy soils and withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and can fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This species is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage plant in dry environments.
