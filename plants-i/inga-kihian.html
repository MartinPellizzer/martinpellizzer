Inga kihian

Inga kihian, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or white. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.

Inga kihian thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
