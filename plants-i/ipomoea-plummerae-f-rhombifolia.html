Ipomoea plummerae f. rhombifolia

Ipomoea plummerae f. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms, often appearing in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
