Ixora dubia

Ixora dubia, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are surrounded by bright, showy bracts. Ixora dubia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
