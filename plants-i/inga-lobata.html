Inga lobata

Inga lobata, commonly known as the "pupu" or "sweet acacia," is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it a common species in secondary forests.

Its pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
