Ixora recurva

Ixora recurva, commonly known as the narrowleaf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are narrow, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
