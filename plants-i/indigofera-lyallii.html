Indigofera lyallii

Indigofera lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's indigo, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
