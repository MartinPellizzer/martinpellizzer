Impatiens

Impatiens, commonly known as jewelweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Balsaminaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often brightly colored flowers that come in a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. Impatiens species are often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

They are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
