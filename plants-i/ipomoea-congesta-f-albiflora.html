Ipomoea congesta f. albiflora

Ipomoea congesta f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a variant of theIpomoea congesta species characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a herbaceous annual vine native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a climbing plant.
