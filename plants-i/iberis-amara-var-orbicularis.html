Iberis amara var. orbicularis

Iberis amara var. orbicularis, commonly known as the bitter candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are rounded and slightly hairy, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
