Inula aurea

Inula aurea, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

Inula aurea is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
