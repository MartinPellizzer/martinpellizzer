Ixia rapunculoides

Ixia rapunculoides, commonly known as the yellow oxeye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers bloom in spring and are composed of a central yellow disc surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

Ixia rapunculoides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
