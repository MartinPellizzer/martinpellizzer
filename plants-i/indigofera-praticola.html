Indigofera praticola

Indigofera praticola, commonly known as the Brazilian indigo plant, is a leguminous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

Indigofera praticola thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
