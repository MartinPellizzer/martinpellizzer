Ipomoea abutiloides var. kunthiana

Ipomoea abutiloides var. kunthiana, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with hairy stems and lobed foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated by insects, making it an attractive addition to gardens.
