Ipomoea crassicaulis var. goodellii

Ipomoea crassicaulis var. goodellii, commonly known as the Goodell's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
