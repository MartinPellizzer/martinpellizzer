Indigofera bakeriana

Indigofera bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to grow optimally.
