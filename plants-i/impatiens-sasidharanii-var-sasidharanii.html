Impatiens sasidharanii var. sasidharanii

Impatiens sasidharanii var. sasidharanii, commonly known as the Sasidharan's Impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
