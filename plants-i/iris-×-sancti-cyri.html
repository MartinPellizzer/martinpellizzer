Iris × sancti-cyri

Iris × sancti-cyri, commonly known as the St. Cyri iris, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Iris sibirica and Iris pallida.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, lavender-blue to violet flowers with a distinctive central dark spot. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is native to regions of Central Asia and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are sword-shaped, smooth, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
