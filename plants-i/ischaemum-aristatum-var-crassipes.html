Ischaemum aristatum var. crassipes

Ischaemum aristatum var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and wetlands.

Its adaptability and rapid growth make it a common weed in various ecosystems.
