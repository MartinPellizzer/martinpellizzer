Inula hirta var. uniflora

Inula hirta var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower inula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower at the tip of each branch, which blooms in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted in traditional herbal practices.
