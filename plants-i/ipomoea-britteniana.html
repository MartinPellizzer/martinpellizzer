Ipomoea britteniana

Ipomoea britteniana, commonly known as the Britten's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.

Ipomoea britteniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
