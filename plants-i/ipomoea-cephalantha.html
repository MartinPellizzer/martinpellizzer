Ipomoea cephalantha

Ipomoea cephalantha, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ipomoea cephalantha is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
