Impatiens hedbergiae

Impatiens hedbergiae, commonly known as the Hedberg's impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
