Iris munzii

Iris munzii, commonly known as the Munz iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowering stems rise above the foliage.

Iris munzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
