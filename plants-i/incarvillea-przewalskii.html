Incarvillea przewalskii

Incarvillea przewalskii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of northern China.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
