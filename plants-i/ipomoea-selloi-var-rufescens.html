Ipomoea selloi var. rufescens

Ipomoea selloi var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet to deep red. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in moist, shady habitats. It blooms during the day, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
