Iris fontanesii

Iris fontanesii, commonly known as the Sierra iris, is a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Iris fontanesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
