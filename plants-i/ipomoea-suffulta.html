Ipomoea suffulta

Ipomoea suffulta, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, broad leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. Its tuberous roots are edible when properly prepared, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea suffulta thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
