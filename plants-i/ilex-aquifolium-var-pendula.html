Ilex aquifolium var. pendula

Ilex aquifolium var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping holly, is a variant of the common holly that exhibits a distinctive cascading growth habit.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a spiny margin. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds during the winter months. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique, flowing appearance.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for various landscape settings.
