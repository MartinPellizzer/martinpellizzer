Iberis gibraltarica f. hosmariensis

Iberis gibraltarica f. hosmariensis, commonly known as the Gibraltar carnation, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the rocky coastal areas of Gibraltar.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a mat-forming habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, preferring well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
