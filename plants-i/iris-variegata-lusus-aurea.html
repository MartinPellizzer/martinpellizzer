Iris variegata lusus aurea

Iris variegata lusus aurea, commonly known as the golden variegated iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, lavender-blue flowers with distinctive yellow markings. The leaves are broad, sword-shaped, and often display a variegated pattern of green and white. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique coloration and elegant appearance make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and cultivated landscapes.
