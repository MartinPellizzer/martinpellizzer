Illicium variegatum

Illicium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped fruits that resemble star anise. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark brown, star-shaped berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a variegated pattern of green and white.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
