Inga klugii

Inga klugii, commonly known as the red inga, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Inga klugii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful plant in agroforestry systems.
