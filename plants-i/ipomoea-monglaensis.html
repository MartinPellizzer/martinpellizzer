Ipomoea monglaensis

Ipomoea monglaensis, commonly known as the Mongla morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blossoms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
