Iris guertleri var. moldavica

Iris guertleri var. moldavica, commonly known as the Moldavian iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features striking violet-blue to purple flowers with a distinctive three-parted structure, typical of the iris family. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
