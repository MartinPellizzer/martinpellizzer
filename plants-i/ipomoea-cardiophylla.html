Ipomoea cardiophylla

Ipomoea cardiophylla, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of violet or blue. The plant is a climbing vine that produces showy blooms during the day, which close at night. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ipomoea cardiophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
