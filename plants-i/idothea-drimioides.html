Idothea drimioides

Idothea drimioides, commonly known as the African daffodil, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Idothea drimioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
