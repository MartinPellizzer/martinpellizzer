Ixora versicolor

Ixora versicolor, commonly known as the variable flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, depending on the variety. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ixora versicolor is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
