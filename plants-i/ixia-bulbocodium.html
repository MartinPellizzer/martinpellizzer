Ixia bulbocodium

Ixia bulbocodium, commonly known as the star orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that come in various colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant produces a bulbous structure that serves as a storage organ for nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat. Ixia bulbocodium typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in many parts of the world beyond its native range.
