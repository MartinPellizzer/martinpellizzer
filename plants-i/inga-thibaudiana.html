Inga thibaudiana

Inga thibaudiana, commonly known as the "coffee tree," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a valuable component of agroforestry systems. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The fruit, which is a pod, contains edible seeds that are sometimes used as a coffee substitute.
