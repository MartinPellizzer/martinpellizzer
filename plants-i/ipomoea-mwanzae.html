Ipomoea mwanzae

Ipomoea mwanzae, commonly known as the Mwanza morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, well-drained soils.

Ipomoea mwanzae is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
