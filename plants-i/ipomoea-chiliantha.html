Ipomoea chiliantha

Ipomoea chiliantha, commonly known as the Chilean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to attach to supports. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by insects.
