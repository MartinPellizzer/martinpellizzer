Iberis × correifolia

Iberis × correifolia, commonly known as candytuft, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
