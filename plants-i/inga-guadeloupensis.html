Inga guadeloupensis

Inga guadeloupensis, commonly known as the Guadeloupe inga, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
