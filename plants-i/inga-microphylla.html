Inga microphylla

Inga microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved inga, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately compound. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow to white in color, followed by pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
