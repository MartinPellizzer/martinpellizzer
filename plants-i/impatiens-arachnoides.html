Impatiens arachnoides

Impatiens arachnoides, commonly known as the hairy impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Impatiens arachnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability.
