Iris georgica

Iris georgica, commonly known as the Georgia iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive blackish-purple spot at the base. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Iris georgica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
