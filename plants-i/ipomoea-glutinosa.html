Ipomoea glutinosa

Ipomoea glutinosa, commonly known as sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a sticky substance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
