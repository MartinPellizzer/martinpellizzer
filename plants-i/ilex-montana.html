Ilex montana

Ilex montana, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
