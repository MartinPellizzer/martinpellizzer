Isomeris arborea var. insularis

Isomeris arborea var. insularis, commonly known as the island hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to certain Pacific islands.

It is a shrub or small tree with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath. The flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink, and they bloom throughout the year. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline soils and high wind exposure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
