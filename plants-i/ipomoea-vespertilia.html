Ipomoea vespertilia

Ipomoea vespertilia, commonly known as evening trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant purple or blue flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea vespertilia is also known for its ability to climb and can be found in a variety of habitats, including gardens and wild areas.
