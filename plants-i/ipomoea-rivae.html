Ipomoea rivae

Ipomoea rivae, commonly known as the river morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other familiar species like morning glories.

Ipomoea rivae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
