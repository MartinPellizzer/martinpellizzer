Indigofera angulata

Indigofera angulata, commonly known as angular indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to violet in color, arranged in racemes. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Indigofera angulata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
