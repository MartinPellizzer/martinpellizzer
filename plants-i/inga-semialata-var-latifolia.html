Inga semialata var. latifolia

Inga semialata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf inga, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
