Ipomoea schultesii

Ipomoea schultesii, commonly known as the Schultes' morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ipomoea schultesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
