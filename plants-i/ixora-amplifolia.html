Ixora amplifolia

Ixora amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed flame bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ixora amplifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
