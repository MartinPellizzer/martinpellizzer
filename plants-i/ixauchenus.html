Ixauchenus

Ixauchenus, commonly known as the Mexican senna, is a genus of flowering plants in the Fabaceae family.

These plants are native to Mexico and are characterized by their bipinnate leaves and yellow to orange flowers. They typically grow in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine.

Ixauchenus species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
