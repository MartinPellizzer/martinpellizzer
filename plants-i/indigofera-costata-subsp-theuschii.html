Indigofera costata subsp. theuschii

Indigofera costata subsp.

theuschii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive blue to purple flowers.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
