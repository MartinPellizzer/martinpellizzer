Indigofera salmoniflora

Indigofera salmoniflora, commonly known as the salmon-flowered indigo, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its striking salmon-pink to orange-red flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Indigofera salmoniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
