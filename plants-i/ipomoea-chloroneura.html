Ipomoea chloroneura

Ipomoea chloroneura, commonly known as the green-veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, making it a common ornamental plant in gardens.

The leaves and flowers contain alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
