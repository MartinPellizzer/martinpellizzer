Ixora trichandra

Ixora trichandra, commonly known as the red flame ixora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Ixora trichandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
