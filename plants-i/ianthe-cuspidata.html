Ianthe cuspidata

Ianthe cuspidata, commonly known as the flame violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive red or orange tip, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Ianthe cuspidata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
