Ilex fabrilis

Ilex fabrilis, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 15 feet. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have spiny margins. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds.

The Florida holly is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
