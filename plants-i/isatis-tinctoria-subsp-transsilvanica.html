Isatis tinctoria subsp. transsilvanica

Isatis tinctoria subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian woad, is a subspecies of the woad plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

The plant features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne in loose, terminal clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is notable for its historical use in dyeing textiles blue, similar to its parent species, Isatis tinctoria.
