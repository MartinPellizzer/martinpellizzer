Ixia punctata

Ixia punctata, commonly known as the spotted flame flower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow or orange spot near the base. The plant produces strikingly large, showy blooms that typically appear in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen throughout the growing season.

Ixia punctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its bold and colorful display.
