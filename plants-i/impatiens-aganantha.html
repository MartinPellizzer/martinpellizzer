Impatiens aganantha

Impatiens aganantha, commonly known as the golden crown impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are erect and branching.

Impatiens aganantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
