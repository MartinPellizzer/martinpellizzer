Ipomoea silvicola

Ipomoea silvicola, commonly known as the forest morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are hairy and twining.

Ipomoea silvicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
