Ipomoea zimmermanii

Ipomoea zimmermanii, commonly known as the Zimmerman morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
