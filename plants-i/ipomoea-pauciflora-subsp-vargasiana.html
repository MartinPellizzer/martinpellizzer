Ipomoea pauciflora subsp. vargasiana

Ipomoea pauciflora subsp.

vargasiana, commonly known as the Vargasian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
