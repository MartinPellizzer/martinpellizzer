Ipomoea trifida var. ymalensis

Ipomoea trifida var. ymalensis, commonly known as the Ymalensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in moist, shaded areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
