Iris narynensis

Iris narynensis, commonly known as the Naryn iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in harsh, high-altitude conditions.

The leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
