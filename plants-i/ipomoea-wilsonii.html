Ipomoea wilsonii

Ipomoea wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's morning glory, is a herbaceous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Ipomoea wilsonii is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early fall.
