Ipomoea cernua f. chacoensis

Ipomoea cernua f. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the Chaco region.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical areas. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
