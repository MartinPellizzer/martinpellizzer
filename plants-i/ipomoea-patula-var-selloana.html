Ipomoea patula var. selloana

Ipomoea patula var. selloana, commonly known as the Sello's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is a vine that can climb using its tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers open in the morning and close by midday.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
