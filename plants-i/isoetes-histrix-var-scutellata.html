Isoetes histrix var. scutellata

Isoetes histrix var. scutellata, commonly known as the scutellate water clover, is a species of aquatic fern that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that form dense, mat-like structures on the substrate. The plant produces spore-bearing sporangia on specialized leaves called sporophylls, which are typically arranged in a circular pattern. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, protective layer of spongy tissue, aiding in anchoring and nutrient absorption.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats.
