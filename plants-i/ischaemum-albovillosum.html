Ischaemum albovillosum

Ischaemum albovillosum, commonly known as white-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with white to pale yellow florets that give it its common name.

Ischaemum albovillosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
