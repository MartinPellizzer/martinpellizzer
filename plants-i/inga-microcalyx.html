Inga microcalyx

Inga microcalyx, commonly known as the small calyx inga, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers with distinctive calyx structures. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several edible seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
