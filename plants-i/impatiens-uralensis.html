Impatiens uralensis

Impatiens uralensis, commonly known as the Ural touch-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Impatiens uralensis is well adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas and alpine meadows.
