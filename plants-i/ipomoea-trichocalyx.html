Ipomoea trichocalyx

Ipomoea trichocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the early morning. It is a herbaceous vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along the ground.

Ipomoea trichocalyx is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
