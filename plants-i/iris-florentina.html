Iris florentina

Iris florentina, commonly known as the Persian iris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its robust rhizomes and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and botanical collections.

The iris is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
