Ilex paraguariensis

Ilex paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a species of evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves and small, dark berries that contain the seeds. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a traditional infusion. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
