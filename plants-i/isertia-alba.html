Isertia alba

Isertia alba, commonly known as the white isertia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Isertia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
