Ixia flexuosa

Ixia flexuosa, commonly known as the rainbow lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces striking, showy flowers in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, red, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Ixia flexuosa is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other popular ornamental plants like amaryllis and daffodils.
