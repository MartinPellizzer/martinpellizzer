Ionidium setigerum

Ionidium setigerum, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in fine, reddish hairs, which give it its common name. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically purple or pink and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ionidium setigerum thrives in moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
