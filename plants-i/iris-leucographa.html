Iris leucographa

Iris leucographa, commonly known as the white-flowered iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, erect stems that bear showy, blue to violet flowers with distinctive white markings. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Iris leucographa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
