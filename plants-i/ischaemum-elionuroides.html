Ischaemum elionuroides

Ischaemum elionuroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bearded grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are adorned with bristles.

Ischaemum elionuroides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
