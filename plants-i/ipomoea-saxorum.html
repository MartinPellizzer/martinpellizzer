Ipomoea saxorum

Ipomoea saxorum, commonly known as the saxifrage morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments. Its blooms typically appear in the summer months, adding vibrant color to landscapes.

Ipomoea saxorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and along rock walls.
