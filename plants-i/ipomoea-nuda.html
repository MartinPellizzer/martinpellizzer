Ipomoea nuda

Ipomoea nuda, commonly known as the naked morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea nuda is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.
