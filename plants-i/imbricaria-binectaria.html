Imbricaria binectaria

Imbricaria binectaria, commonly known as the spiral moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth and a distinctive spiral arrangement of its leaf-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its leaves are small, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly rough surface.

Imbricaria binectaria plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
