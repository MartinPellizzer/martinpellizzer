Iris × borzae

Iris × borzae, commonly known as the hybrid bearded iris, is a cross between different species of iris, resulting in a plant with unique floral characteristics.

It typically features showy, colorful flowers with three upright petals and three downward-facing sepals, often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting colors. The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to landscapes.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
