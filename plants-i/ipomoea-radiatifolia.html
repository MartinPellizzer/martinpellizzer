Ipomoea radiatifolia

Ipomoea radiatifolia, commonly known as the radiate leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that radiate outward from the petiole, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ipomoea radiatifolia is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common morning glory.
