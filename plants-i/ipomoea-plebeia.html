Ipomoea plebeia

Ipomoea plebeia, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible tubers, though they are not widely cultivated for food. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Ipomoea plebeia is also known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, often reaching heights of several meters.
