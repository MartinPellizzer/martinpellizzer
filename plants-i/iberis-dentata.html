Iberis dentata

Iberis dentata, commonly known as the white-flowered candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that features dense clusters of small, white flowers resembling candy sprinkles. The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Iberis dentata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
