Ichnocarpus frutescens var. leptodictyus

Ichnocarpus frutescens var. leptodictyus, commonly known as the slender-leaved horsegram, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish or pale green flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

The seeds are often used as a protein-rich food source in various traditional cuisines.
