Indigofera terminalis

Indigofera terminalis, commonly known as the Indian indigo plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread cultivation.
