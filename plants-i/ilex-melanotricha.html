Ilex melanotricha

Ilex melanotricha, commonly known as the black-haired holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that have a distinctive blackish-brown underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. Ilex melanotricha typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical regions.
