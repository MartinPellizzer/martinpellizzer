Ilex fargesii f. megalophylla

Ilex fargesii f. megalophylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf Chinese holly, is a variant of the Chinese holly characterized by its large, leathery leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, typically found in mountainous regions with moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and can grow up to 15 cm in length, giving the plant a striking appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
