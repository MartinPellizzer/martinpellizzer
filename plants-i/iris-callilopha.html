Iris callilopha

Iris callilopha, commonly known as the African marsh iris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Iris callilopha is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
