Indigofera grisea

Indigofera grisea, commonly known as the gray indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in various ecosystems.
