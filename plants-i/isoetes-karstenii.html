Isoetes karstenii

Isoetes karstenii, commonly known as the Karsten's watermace, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. This plant is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures, known as spore-bearing cones, are located at the base of the plant.

Isoetes karstenii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
