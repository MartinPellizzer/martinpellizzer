Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-erecta

Ilex aquifolium var. argentea-erecta, commonly known as the silver-leaved holly, is a variant of the common holly characterized by its striking silver-white variegation on the leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, particularly found in the British Isles. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
