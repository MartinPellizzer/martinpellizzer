Indigofera coerulea

Indigofera coerulea, commonly known as blue indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Indigofera coerulea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and adaptability to various growing conditions.
