Ionidium suffruticosum

Ionidium suffruticosum, commonly known as the creeping daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Ionidium suffruticosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
