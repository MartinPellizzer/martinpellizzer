Ipomoea kurtziana

Ipomoea kurtziana, commonly known as the Kurtz's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Ipomoea kurtziana is a climbing vine that can reach significant heights, making it a notable presence in its natural habitat.
