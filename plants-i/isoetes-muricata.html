Isoetes muricata

Isoetes muricata, commonly known as the spiky water fern, is a member of the genus Isoetes, which includes about 300 species of aquatic and semi-aquatic plants.

It is characterized by its spore-bearing leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy coating, which helps protect the spores from desiccation. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its unique morphology includes a central axis with specialized leaves that are adapted for underwater conditions.

Isoetes muricata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
