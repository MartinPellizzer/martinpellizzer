Isatis boissieriana lusus purpurea

Isatis boissieriana lusus purpurea, commonly known as the purple Chinese kale, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, crinkled leaves that range in color from dark green to purplish-red, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to China and is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
