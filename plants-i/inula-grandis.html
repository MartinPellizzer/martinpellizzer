Inula grandis

Inula grandis, commonly known as the grand ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and roadsides.

Inula grandis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
