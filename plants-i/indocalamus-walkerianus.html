Indocalamus walkerianus

Indocalamus walkerianus, commonly known as the giant bamboo or Walker's bamboo, is a tall, slender grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its hollow, segmented stems and long, narrow leaves. This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet, tropical environments. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and structural strength.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
