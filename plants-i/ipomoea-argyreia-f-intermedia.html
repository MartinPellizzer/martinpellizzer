Ipomoea argyreia f. intermedia

Ipomoea argyreia f. intermedia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth habit and climbing nature make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
