Ixora arborescens

Ixora arborescens, commonly known as the flame flower or Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bright red or orange-red flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to tropical soils.

Ixora arborescens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and resilience in humid environments.
