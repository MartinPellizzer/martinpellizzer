Inga nobilis var. pavoniana

Inga nobilis var. pavoniana, commonly known as the pink-veined inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, trifoliate leaves with distinctive pink veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
