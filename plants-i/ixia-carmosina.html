Ixia carmosina

Ixia carmosina, commonly known as the yellow ox-eye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a red eye-like spot. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Ixia carmosina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for containers or rock gardens.
