Indigofera ankaratrensis

Indigofera ankaratrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar indigo plant, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil and supports its growth in nutrient-poor environments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested or disturbed areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
