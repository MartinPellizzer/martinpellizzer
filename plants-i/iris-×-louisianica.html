Iris × louisianica

Iris × louisianica, commonly known as the Louisiana iris, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features sword-shaped, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Iris × louisianica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
