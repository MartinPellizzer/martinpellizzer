Ipomoea krusensternii

Ipomoea krusensternii, commonly known as the Krasnovsky morning glory, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 10 meters in length, making it a vigorous climber. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and fragrance.
