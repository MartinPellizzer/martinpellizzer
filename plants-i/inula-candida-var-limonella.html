Inula candida var. limonella

Inula candida var. limonella, commonly known as the yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
