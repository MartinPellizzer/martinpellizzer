Ipomoea nicaraguensis

Ipomoea nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its vines can reach lengths of several meters.

Ipomoea nicaraguensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
