Ipomoea pseudolinum

Ipomoea pseudolinum, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant red flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces edible tubers, though they are not commonly cultivated for food. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and rapid growth habit.

Ipomoea pseudolinum thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
