Ipomoea tenuis

Ipomoea tenuis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ipomoea tenuis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other species of morning glories.
