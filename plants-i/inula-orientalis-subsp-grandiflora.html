Inula orientalis subsp. grandiflora

Inula orientalis subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered elecampane, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of Europe. It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with rough, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow composite flowers in late summer, arranged in a single, terminal head. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
