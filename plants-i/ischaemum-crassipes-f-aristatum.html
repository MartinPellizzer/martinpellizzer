Ischaemum crassipes f. aristatum

Ischaemum crassipes f. aristatum, commonly known as the tufted dogstail, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive tufted inflorescence.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist soils along streambanks and wet meadows. The plant features narrow, flat leaves and produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. Its morphology is adapted to wet environments, with a rhizomatous growth habit that aids in spreading and survival.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, grass-like appearance.
