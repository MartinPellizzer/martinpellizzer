Impatiens serpens

Impatiens serpens, commonly known as the "snake plant" or "whorled impatiens," is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It is adapted to grow in shaded or partially sunny environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Impatiens serpens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor settings.
