Ilex azevinho

Ilex azevinho, commonly known as Portuguese holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Azores.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, red berries that persist through the winter. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, mild climate of the region. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Ilex azevinho plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
