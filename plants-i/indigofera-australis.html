Indigofera australis

Indigofera australis, commonly known as the Australian indigo, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem with a rough texture.

Indigofera australis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
