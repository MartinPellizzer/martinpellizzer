Indigofera verruculosa

Indigofera verruculosa, commonly known as the rough indigo, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
