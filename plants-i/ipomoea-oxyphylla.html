Ipomoea oxyphylla

Ipomoea oxyphylla, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often opening in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces cylindrical, hairy fruit pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along fences, walls, or in disturbed soils.

Ipomoea oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
