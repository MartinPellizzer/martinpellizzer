Ivesia baileyi var. beneolens

Ivesia baileyi var. beneolens, commonly known as the Colorado glacierweed, is a perennial shrub native to the high elevations of the Rocky Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which helps it withstand harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or talus slopes.

Ivesia baileyi var. beneolens plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
