Inga ruiziana

Inga ruiziana, commonly known as the "ruiziana tree," is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its broad, compound leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Inga ruiziana is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a dense canopy that provides shade.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting various wildlife species and contributing to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
