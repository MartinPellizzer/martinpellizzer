Ilex thyrsiflora

Ilex thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and upland habitats.

Ilex thyrsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
