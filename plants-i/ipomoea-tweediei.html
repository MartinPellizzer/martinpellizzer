Ipomoea tweediei

Ipomoea tweediei, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces long, twisting stems that can climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ipomoea tweediei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
