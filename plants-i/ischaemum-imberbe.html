Ischaemum imberbe

Ischaemum imberbe, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its coarse, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Ischaemum imberbe is valued for its resilience and is often used in erosion control and pasture management.
