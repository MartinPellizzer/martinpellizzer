Isachne comata

Isachne comata, commonly known as the hairy isachne, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, typical of many grass species.

The plant is often found in moist habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
