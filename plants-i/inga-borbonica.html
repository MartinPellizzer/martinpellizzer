Inga borbonica

Inga borbonica, commonly known as the Colombian velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil fertility.

Inga borbonica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
