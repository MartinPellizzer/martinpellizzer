Ivodea

Ivodea, commonly known as ivy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araliaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, usually lobed leaves. Many species have tendrils that allow them to climb and attach to surfaces. Ivodea plants typically produce small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels.

They are often found in woodland areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
