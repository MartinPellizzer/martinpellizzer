Inga microdonta

Inga microdonta, commonly known as the dwarf inga, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a valuable species in reforestation efforts. Its fruit produces edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines.

Additionally, Inga microdonta plays an important role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
