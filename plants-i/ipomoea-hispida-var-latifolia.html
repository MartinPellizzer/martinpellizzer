Ipomoea hispida var. latifolia

Ipomoea hispida var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces hairy, cylindrical stems and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering vines.
