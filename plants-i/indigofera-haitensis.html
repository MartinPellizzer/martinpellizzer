Indigofera haitensis

Indigofera haitensis, commonly known as the Haitian indigo or blue bean, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria.
