Inga striolata

Inga striolata, commonly known as the spotted inga, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Inga striolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
