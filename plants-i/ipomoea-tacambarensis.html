Ipomoea tacambarensis

Ipomoea tacambarensis, commonly known as the Tacambarensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its flowers typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
