Ilex cuneifolia

Ilex cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf holly, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are usually narrower than those of other holly species. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Ilex cuneifolia is valued for its dense foliage and year-round beauty, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in a variety of garden settings.
