Ipomoea mitchelliae

Ipomoea mitchelliae, commonly known as Mitchell's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a trailing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
