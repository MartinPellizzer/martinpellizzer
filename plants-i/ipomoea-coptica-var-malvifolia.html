Ipomoea coptica var. malvifolia

Ipomoea coptica var. malvifolia, commonly known as the Malvifolia morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with stems that can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
