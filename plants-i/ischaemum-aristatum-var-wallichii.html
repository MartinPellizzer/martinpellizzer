Ischaemum aristatum var. wallichii

Ischaemum aristatum var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous minute, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to harsh mountain climates, making it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
