Ilex aquifolium var. handsworthensis

Ilex aquifolium var. handsworthensis, commonly known as the Handsworth holly, is a variant of the common holly that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with dense, spiny leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated margins, and they often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
