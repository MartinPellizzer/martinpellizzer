Impatiens alborosea

Impatiens alborosea, commonly known as the white rose impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a soft, ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its pale blooms.

Impatiens alborosea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a touch of elegance to garden spaces.
