Ixora humilis

Ixora humilis, commonly known as the dwarf flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ixora humilis is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
