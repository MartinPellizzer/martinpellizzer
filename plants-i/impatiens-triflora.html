Impatiens triflora

Impatiens triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered jewelweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of orange and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Impatiens triflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed dispersal.
