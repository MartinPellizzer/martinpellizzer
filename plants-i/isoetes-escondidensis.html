Isoetes escondidensis

Isoetes escondidensis, commonly known as the hidden water fern, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the genus Isoetes, which is part of the order Isoetales.

It is characterized by its spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are typically enclosed in specialized leaves called sporophylls. This plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its unique morphology and reproductive strategy make it an important subject in the study of ancient plant evolution.

Isoetes escondidensis is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor waters, contributing to its ecological significance in aquatic ecosystems.
