Impatiens asperipetala

Impatiens asperipetala, commonly known as the rough-leaved impatiens, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
