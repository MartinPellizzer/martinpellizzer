Ipomoea jalapa f. macrorhiza

Ipomoea jalapa f. macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant morning glory, is a striking variant of the morning glory family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped leaves and produces long, twisting vines. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
