Indigofera setosa

Indigofera setosa, commonly known as the small indigo bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
