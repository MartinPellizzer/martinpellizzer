Ilex berteroi var. ovalifolia

Ilex berteroi var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf holly, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Isle of Pines.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
