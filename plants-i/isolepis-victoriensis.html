Isolepis victoriensis

Isolepis victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
