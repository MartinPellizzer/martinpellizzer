Iris trigonocarpa

Iris trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled iris, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods, which are a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are blue or purple in color.

Iris trigonocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
