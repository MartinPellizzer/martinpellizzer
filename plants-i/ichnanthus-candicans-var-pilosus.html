Ichnanthus candicans var. pilosus

Ichnanthus candicans var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy yellow orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are clustered in racemes.

It is a flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
