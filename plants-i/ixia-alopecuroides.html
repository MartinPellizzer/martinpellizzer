Ixia alopecuroides

Ixia alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly firecracker, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Ixia alopecuroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
