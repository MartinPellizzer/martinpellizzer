Inula maletii

Inula maletii, commonly known as the Maleti's elecampane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
