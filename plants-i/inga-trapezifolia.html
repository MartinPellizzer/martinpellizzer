Inga trapezifolia

Inga trapezifolia, commonly known as the "chocolate tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria in its root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. It produces edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
