Inga spectabilis var. schimpffii

Inga spectabilis var. schimpffii, commonly known as the showy inga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, velvety flowers that are often visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

The fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.
