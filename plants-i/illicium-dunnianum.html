Illicium dunnianum

Illicium dunnianum, commonly known as the Chinese star anise, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruit that resembles the more familiar star anise. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. Illicium dunnianum is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like anethole and shikimic acid.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
